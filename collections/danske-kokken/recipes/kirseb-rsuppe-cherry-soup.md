## Ingredients

- 2 pounds black preserved cherries
- 3.5 pots water
- several sticks cinnamon
- a couple of lemon slices
- a little clove
- a little potato flour
- small bread balls
- sugar to taste

## Directions

1. From 2 pounds of black preserved cherries, select a plateful of the best ones.
2. Crush the remainder so that the pits break apart; use more or fewer pits according to how much you want the kernel flavor to come through.
3. Put them over the fire with 3.5 pots of water, or enough so the water just covers the berries, and cook them until all the strength is extracted, which will take about a quarter hour.
4. Strain them, and put the strained soup back over the fire together with the cherries set aside the first time, several sticks of cinnamon, a couple of lemon slices without pits, and a little clove, and cook it; then thicken it with a little potato flour and top with small bread balls.
5. Before serving, sweeten to taste with sugar.
6. Alternatively, instead of bread balls, you can serve it with small rusks or baked wheat bread cubes.
7. Dried cherries can also be used for this soup.
