## Ingredients

- 2 woodcocks
- Fresh bacon slices
- Butter
- Red wine
- Egg yolks
- Salt
- Pepper
- Parsley
- French bread
- Heart, gizzard, and liver of the woodcocks

## Directions

1. Pluck the birds, singe them, remove the eyes, and only flay the head.
2. When the birds are cleaned, collect the hearts, gizzards, and livers to make a forcemeat.
3. Dry the birds thoroughly inside with a cloth, cut off the feet, and truss using their long, pointed beaks.
4. Press the thighs tightly against the body and bend the head back, then insert the beak through the thigh and body and out again through the other thigh.
5. Lard or wrap the breast with thin bacon slices.
6. Brown the birds and roast them like partridges, preferably in a pot and not much more than half an hour.
7. Serve garnished with woodcock bread made as follows: Finely chop the liver, gizzard, and heart with a little fresh bacon and parsley.
8. Sauté this in a slice of butter, then stir in a little red wine, a couple of egg yolks along with salt and pepper.
9. Spread this forcemeat on slices of French bread from which the crust has been removed and which have been fried in butter in a pan.
10. Place these in a well-heated oven so the forcemeat bakes quickly.
11. Serve with sauce as for pheasant.
