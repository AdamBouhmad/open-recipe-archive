## Ingredients

- Fresh string beans
- Salt

## Directions

1. The beans must be freshly picked so they are fresh and crisp. Dry them in a cloth, remove the strings and cut them finely.
2. In a crock spread a thin layer of salt, then a layer of beans about three fingers thick, then a layer of salt thick enough to cover the beans, then beans, and so forth; press each layer firmly and the last must be salt
3. Cover with a piece of cloth, place a disk on top and on this a fairly heavy weight, which after some time, when the beans have become firm, can be removed
4. Tie the crocks, place them in a dry spot and they will keep until summer
5. When they are to be used, put them with the salt in sufficiently much boiling water and cook until tender, then strain through a sieve where the water is pressed out well, and stew them
6. If the beans, which is especially the case in early spring, should be too salty when the water is pressed from them, they can be placed for a short time in cold water with a little powdered sugar in it
7. The beans can also be salted whole in the same way after they are dried and have the strings removed. By adding a little prepared soda to the water in which the beans are cooked, they become tender quickly
