## Ingredients

- Half of a slaughtered lamb
- Salt
- Butter
- Water
- Parsley
- White writing paper
- Flour
- Potatoes
- Salad

## Directions

1. While lambs are small, it does not matter if you choose meat from a young ewe or wether lamb; but later the latter often acquire a less pleasant woolly taste, so the former are to be preferred.
2. For roasting, choose the loin with attached back.
3. Remove the shank, break the bones and back, then beat the roast lightly with a meat mallet, rub with salt and lard with fine parsley.
4. Place it in a warm oven in a pan with salt, butter and a little water.
5. Lay a piece of white writing paper, dipped in butter or water, over the roast.
6. When the roast has cooked somewhat and the heat is less strong, remove the paper and baste it with the sauce.
7. Roasting takes 1 to 2 hours depending on the size of the roast, and you must be careful that it does not burn.
8. Skim the fat from the sauce, thicken it with a little flour, strain and serve to the roast together with potatoes and salad.
