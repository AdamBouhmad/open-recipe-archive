## Ingredients

- Beautiful, fully ripe raspberries
- Melis, equal weight to the berries

## Directions

1. Extract the juice from the raspberries using a linen cloth
2. Let stand overnight for sediment to settle
3. The next day strain the clear juice (but not the sediment) through a jelly bag and weigh it
4. Pour into an enameled pot with an equal weight of Melis
5. Boil and skim until it passes the jelly test, determined by dripping a few drops of hot jelly onto a cold tin plate; if they quickly become stiff, the jelly is sufficiently boiled
6. Pour into small jelly cups or glasses that are widest at the top so the jelly can later be turned out onto a glass plate like a small pudding when serving
7. Let the jelly stand until stiff, then cover with a piece of wax paper dipped in cognac
8. Bind over as with other preserves using a bladder or parchment paper
