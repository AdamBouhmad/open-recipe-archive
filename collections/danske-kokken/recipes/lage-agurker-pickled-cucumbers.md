## Ingredients

- Medium-sized, long cucumbers without seeds
- Dill
- Bay leaves
- Grape leaves
- Unripe, green grapes (if available)
- Vinegar (1/4 liter per liter of water)
- Water
- Kitchen salt

## Directions

1. Choose medium-sized, long cucumbers without seeds rather than small, thick ones.
2. Rinse in lukewarm water and dry thoroughly.
3. Line the bottom of a barrel or jar with dill, bay leaves, grape leaves, and if available, unripe green grapes.
4. Layer cucumbers on top, continuing in layers.
5. Make a brine of 1/4 liter vinegar to each liter of water, bring to a boil, and dissolve enough kitchen salt to make a very strong brine.
6. Only when the brine is completely cold, pour it over the cucumbers, which should be covered by it.
7. Spread a cloth over the barrel, place a perforated wooden bottom fitting the size on top, and lastly a light press to push the cucumbers down.
8. For the first couple of weeks, place the barrel in a warm location until fermentation begins, then move to a cooler place.
9. The cucumbers are ready to eat only when they become clear.
