## Ingredients

- Veal loin
- Pork fat for larding
- Butter
- Water or meat stock
- Cream (optional)
- Cold butter (optional)
- Ginger and pepper (optional)

## Directions

1. Take the finest loin veal and cut pieces about 13 inches long, two fingers thick and somewhat wider than the thickness.
2. Beat the pieces gently so they do not fall apart and lard them well with pork fat so that only one side remains bare.
3. Brown butter in a flat pot and brown the pieces on all the sides that have fat until the fat is cooked through.
4. Turn the bare side down and when it is brown, add a little water or meat stock and cook on this side until cooked through.
5. They are best when not too well cooked but still somewhat juicy.
6. When removing from the pot, stir a little cream sauce; alternatively, dip the pieces in ginger and pepper before larding, in which case use only a piece of cold butter to thicken the sauce instead of cream.
7. These can be served with vegetables like a carbonade.
