## Ingredients

- Potatoes
- Salt
- Butter
- Powdered sugar
- Fat or oil
- Milk
- Flour
- Water

## Directions

1. For raw-peeled potatoes: Peel potatoes raw before cooking to make them more mealy and remove the unpleasant taste of sprouted potatoes. Let raw-peeled potatoes stand a day in cold water, then boil in a pot without a lid. Start them in cold salted water. When they begin to boil, skim the water, and when almost tender, drain the water and add a piece of butter to the pot. Shake the potatoes and cook tender. For browned potatoes: Choose small round potatoes, cook and peel them, then brown in lightly browned butter. When potatoes are brown, add powdered sugar. Potatoes can also be browned in slices: Peel raw potatoes and cut into very thin slices. Place in very hot but not burning fat in a pan. The fat should be a couple of inches high in the pan, and stir the potatoes so they do not stick together. Do not add too many at once for the same reason. When potatoes have taken on a nice brown color, remove and sprinkle with a little salt. This method is used much in France, where potatoes are baked in fine oil instead of fat. For stewed potatoes: Cook potatoes first, peel and cut into suitable pieces. Make a sauce of melted butter, flour and milk, and when smooth, give the potatoes one boil. For mashed potatoes: add a little melted butter, milk or broth to these potatoes.
