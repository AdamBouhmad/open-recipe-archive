## Ingredients

- Salmon or sea trout
- Cold water
- Salt
- Bay leaves (a pair)
- Peppercorns
- Parsley
- Boiled potatoes, freshly peeled
- Sour butter sauce or caper sauce

## Directions

1. Rinse well with cold water
2. Scrape along the scales from head toward tail with a knife that is not too sharp
3. Rinse the fish again
4. Remove the innards and bile very carefully
5. For a large fish, lay it on the kitchen table with back outward along the table's edge, head to the left
6. Cut the tail at the small hole visible on the belly
7. With a large sharp knife that is rounded like a common butcher's knife, cut the fish open along the back so the knife follows along the backbone; also cut through the head
8. Remove the innards, being especially careful the bile bladder does not break
9. The roe can be used for false caviar
10. Rub the whole salmon inside with coarse salt and rinse if it is to be cooked
11. Cut into pieces and place in boiling water with salt, a pair of bay leaves, and peppercorns
12. Cook until tender over fairly brisk heat, then remove the pot from heat and let stand without cooking on the stovetop for about 10 minutes
13. Arrange on a platter with skin side up and can be garnished with parsley and freshly peeled boiled potatoes
14. Serve with sour butter sauce or caper sauce
