## Ingredients

- One melon, not fully ripe
- One pound melon (prepared)
- One pound white sugar
- One-third pint water
- One egg (for testing brine density)

## Directions

1. Peel the melon, remove the seeds, and cut into finger-length pieces, about one inch wide and half as thick.
2. Weigh the pieces and place in a brine that is strong enough to float an egg; let sit for 4 days.
3. Remove the pieces and soak in cold water for 3 days, changing the water daily.
4. Place on heat in cold water and cook covered until tender enough to pierce with a stick; remove and dry thoroughly in cloth.
5. For 1 pound of melon, take 1 pound white sugar and cook with one-third pint water to make a syrup.
6. Add the melon to the syrup, cook for 5 to 6 minutes, pour into a jar and cover; let stand for 4 days.
7. Pour off the syrup, cook it for a few minutes, and pour hot over the melon; let stand for three days.
8. Spread on a sieve to drain for a few hours, then place on a plate.
9. Cook the syrup again until thick enough to pour over the melon; turn the melon in it and let stand several days in an airy place to dry.
10. Store in a dry place.
