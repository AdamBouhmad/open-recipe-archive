## Ingredients

- A couple of pounds of fish
- 1½ quart water
- Salt
- 1 gill vinegar
- 3 or 4 laurel leaves
- Whole pepper
- Some cloves
- Gelatine
- A couple of egg whites
- Rind of a lemon or vinegar
- Beets (optional for coloring)

## Directions

1. Clean the fish, cut it into pieces and cook in 1½ quart water with salt, 1 gill vinegar, 3 or 4 laurel leaves, whole pepper and some cloves
2. Remove the tender pieces of fish when cooked, while other pieces cook longer for the stock
3. Strain the stock and add a little gelatine, a couple of egg whites and lemon rind or vinegar
4. Pour through a flannel cloth
5. For pike, color the aspic red by cooking beets in it; for other sorts keep it as white as possible
6. Pour a thin layer of aspic over the bottom of the dish, arrange the most beautiful fish pieces on it when set, and place the aspic between them
7. For salmon with red aspic, cut flowers from cooked egg whites; for white aspic cut flowers from beets
8. Place these decorations between the pieces or form a circle inside the bottom of the dish with these flowers and fresh parsley leaves
9. Pour more aspic over, which should not be more than lukewarm
10. When all is set, hold the dish briefly over hot water and turn out onto another dish
