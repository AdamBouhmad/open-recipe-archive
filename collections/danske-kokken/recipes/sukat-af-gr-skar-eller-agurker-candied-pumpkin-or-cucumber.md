## Ingredients

- Pumpkin or large cucumbers, still green
- Puddered sugar
- Vinegar
- Ginger

## Directions

1. Cut into large pieces, give one boiling in water, and place on a clean cloth to dry. The outer skin should remain on, where it is not on the pumpkin too thick and hard
2. Dark powdered sugar is moistened with so much vinegar that it can melt; it is cooked until it is skimmed and clear, with 1 teaspoon ground ginger to each 2 pounds
3. Now the pieces are given a good boiling in the sugar, upon which they are taken up, placed in a crock, and the sauce is poured over warm. If it does not stand over, then more must be cooked
4. Since the pieces tend to float up, press them down with a tablespoon or a small round plate that is placed in the crock with some weight on it. After 8 days take them up to place the top ones at the bottom. When they have stood until you can taste that they are penetrated with sugar, they are taken up and placed in a dry crock or a deep vessel, and covered between each layer with dry powdered sugar
5. This also becomes syrup, and both this and the first can be used to candy other pumpkins with. The candied pieces are now placed in a light press between two large spoons with paper over and under. When they are pressed, thread a string through them and let them hang in an airy place to dry, but not in the sun
6. Finally place them in a crock with ground white sugar between each layer. The fruit must not be cooked too tender
