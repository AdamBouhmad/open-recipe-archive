## Ingredients

- 1 pound beef, preferably from the inner part, cut into strips
- a little over 1/4 pound beef fat (30 quints), picked apart and without membrane
- salt to taste
- ground pepper to taste
- 1 shallot
- 3 tablespoons wheat flour
- 1 pail sweet milk or cream
- ground spices such as nutmeg, ginger, allspice or cloves to taste
- 2 eggs or egg whites (optional if cream is used)
- marrow cut into small cubes (optional)

## Directions

1. Cut the beef into strips and scrape along the sinews to remove them before chopping (or omit if using a meat grinder)
2. During chopping, blend the beef with the fat
3. When the beef and fat are well worked together and chopped fine enough to hold together, chop in salt, pepper, shallot, and wheat flour
4. Allow the mixture to rest for several hours or longer before use
5. After resting, work it with a spoon and gradually add, at long intervals, a good pail of sweet milk or cream
6. Blend in ground spices such as nutmeg, ginger, allspice or cloves to taste
7. Stir the mixture strongly and continuously, making it more cohesive by adding a couple of eggs or egg whites if cream was not used
8. If desired, the mixture may rest for a few hours and then be stirred with a little more milk to make it firmer
9. If marrow is available, cut some into small cubes and blend these with the mixture
10. Test the consistency by forming one ball and cooking it in soup
11. To form balls: take a larger portion of dough on a large spoon, and using a small spoon dipped in soup between each time, form the balls and place them in the soup
