## Ingredients

- Asparagus
- Carrots
- Water
- Bouillon
- Butter
- Flour
- Salt
- Parsley
- Nutmeg

## Directions

1. Scrape both parts, cut them into short pieces and cook them for a short time in half water, half bouillon; of the asparagus cook only what is tender. Asparagus in this country is on the whole not good, and one must nearly always cut a piece of the hardest part away. The heads become tender faster than the rest and should therefore be added later. They are not cooked in more water and bouillon than is needed for the sauce. When they are tender, make a roux of butter and flour and a little salt. You can stew both parts each separately in the same way, and then add a little parsley to the carrots and nutmeg to the asparagus.
