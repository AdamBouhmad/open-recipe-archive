## Ingredients

- Goose liver
- Pork fat (lard)
- Truffles
- 2 finely chopped shallots
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped parsley
- Grated nutmeg
- Ground pepper
- Salt
- Butter
- Goose or pork offal (fat)

## Directions

1. Clean the truffles well, peel and cut into thick slices, then the shell is finely chopped.
2. Remove any spots carefully, and 1/2 pound of the goose liver is pounded into a fine farce with an equal amount of pork fat, an onion, a spoonful of parsley, the truffle shells and a little of the seasonings.
3. Cut the rest of the liver into 6 to 8 pieces, which are larded with slices of truffle that have been previously fried in butter with the rest of the onion and parsley.
4. Line the bottom of a fairly tall, cylindrical cooking vessel of porcelain or faience with thin slices of butter, over it a finger-thick layer of farce, then the larded slices of liver, which are sprinkled with seasonings, then another finger-thick layer of farce, thin slices of butter, and on top slices of pork fat. Cover the cooking vessel with paper and a lid, place on a baking sheet and bake in the oven for 2.5 to 3 hours.
5. Remove the paper and the pork fat slices.
6. Fill the cooking vessel completely with goose or pork offal, and a paper is placed anew under the lid.
7. Keeps for a long time in a cool place. All butter and offal used should be unsalted.
