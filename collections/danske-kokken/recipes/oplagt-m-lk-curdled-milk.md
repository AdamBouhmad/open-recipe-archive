## Ingredients

- 5 pots milk, preferably sweet
- 1 pot sour cream
- Rennet (4 drops, obtained from the apothecary)

## Directions

1. Mix 1 pot of sour cream into 5 pots of milk in an enameled pot, cover with a tight-fitting lid, and place in a lukewarm oven so the milk becomes warm (15 degrees), but not hot, as it would then become grainy.
2. The pot stands this way overnight (14 to 16 hours).
3. Then remove the lid, place a thin, clean linen cloth, dipped in cold water and wrung out, over a colander.
4. Place the curdled milk in the cloth and leave for 5 to 6 hours so the whey can drain completely.
5. Shortly before serving, turn the milk from the cloth onto a dish and serve with whipped cream on top, and sweet cream and powdered sugar on the side.
6. Alternatively, reduce the amount of sour cream and take a corresponding amount of buttermilk instead. The faster the milk curdles, the better. For this reason, 4 drops of rennet (obtained from the apothecary) can be added; the milk is warmed to 15 degrees, left to stand, and treated as previously mentioned.
