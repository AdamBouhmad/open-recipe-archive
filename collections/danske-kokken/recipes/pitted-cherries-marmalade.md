## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg pitted cherries
- 375 gr powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Remove the stems from the cherries and pit them using a cherry pitter, which can be purchased at kitchen supply stores.
2. Pour boiling water over the pits and rub the flesh that adheres to them well, then strain off the juice.
3. Spread a cloth on the table, pour the pits onto it, rub them well in it, then place them on a baking sheet in a warm oven or on a frying pan over low heat to dry completely.
4. When crushed with a meat mallet, place them either between paper or in a cloth, and remove only the whole kernels.
5. Pour the juice that has collected from the cherries into a pot and melt the sugar in it before adding the fruit to be boiled quite evenly and simultaneously skimmed well for at most 10 minutes.
6. Remove the cherries with a slotted spoon or place them on a colander, and when the juice has drained well, pour them into a jar.
7. Boil the juice until it is very thick, and give the kernels a final boil in it, then pour it over the cherries.
8. If there is plenty of juice, some of it can be poured into dry bottles and sealed with parchment.
