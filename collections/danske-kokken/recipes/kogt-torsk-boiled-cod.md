## Ingredients

- cod (1/4 kg per person)
- salt
- vinegar
- parsley

## Directions

1. Large cod should be cooked whole and placed in cold water with salt and a little vinegar to make the flesh firmer, while smaller cod are cut in half and placed in boiling water.
2. When the cod is slaughtered, make a cut at the nape and one at the tail, then hold it under running water, letting the water stream rinse it well, which causes the muscles to contract and the fish to become firmer.
3. Open the belly, remove the innards, and cook the liver, roe, or milt together with the fish. Remove the gall bladder carefully.
4. Scrape the fish from the tail upward, clean it well inside, and after sufficient rinsing, make 2 or 3 cuts on each side of the skin so it does not burst during cooking.
5. Let it stand with salt for a couple of hours before cooking, which must take place without a lid, otherwise the fish becomes loose and falls apart.
6. When plating, place a fish rack on the platter and over it a folded napkin, upon which the cod is laid.
7. Garnish at the head and tail with liver, roe, or sliced milt, between which parsley is inserted.
8. Place boiled potatoes on the sides of the cod.
