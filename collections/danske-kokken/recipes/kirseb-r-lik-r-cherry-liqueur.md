## Ingredients

- 4 kg (8 S) culinary cherries
- 4 l (4 pots) white rum
- 1 1/2 kg (3 S) ground icing sugar
- 1/4 l (3 pægles) water

## Directions

1. Pit and crush 4 kg (8 S) culinary cherries in a wooden or stone mortar.
2. Transfer the crushed cherries to a jug or jar and pour 4 l (4 pots) white rum over them, well corked or covered.
3. Over the course of a month, shake or stir regularly.
4. Strain the rum through a clear piece of cloth.
5. Boil 1 1/2 kg (3 S) ground icing sugar with 1/4 l (3 pægles) water to make a syrup and blend with the cherry rum.
6. Pour into bottles, cork them, and seal with gypsum.
7. Pour 2 l (2 pots) rum over the remaining solids and store in a well-corked jug for the next year. The rum will have extracted the remaining juice from the berries and should be measured and used the following year together with fresh rum to pour over freshly crushed berries.
