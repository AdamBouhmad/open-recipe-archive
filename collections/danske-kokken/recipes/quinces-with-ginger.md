## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg quinces, peeled
- 1/2 kg powdered sugar
- 1/4 l water
- 75 gr whole ginger

## Directions

1. Both apple and pear quinces are found; however, the latter are considered to be unconditionally the best.
2. They should be beautifully yellow and preferably have been exposed to frost before picking.
3. Place the quinces in boiling water and boil until they begin to soften, then remove them, cool, cut into 4 to 8 pieces, and cut away all the hard core.
4. Weigh the fruit now.
5. Boil sugar and water to a syrup, in which the quinces are boiled completely soft.
6. Remove the fruit with a slotted spoon and place it in a jar, then reduce the juice until it is thick, together with the ginger that has been previously scalded and scraped, which is removed before the boiling juice is poured over the fruit, which is then poured out and the glass sealed.
