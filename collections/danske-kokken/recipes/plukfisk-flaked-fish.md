## Ingredients

- Leftover cooked fish
- Butter
- Flour
- Malt
- Ground nutmeg or pepper
- Sugar
- Salt
- Egg yolk
- French bread

## Directions

1. Plukfisk is usually made when you have leftover fish from the day before.
2. Pick the meat from the bones.
3. Melt a good piece of butter, stir in flour; gradually stir in malt until it has a suitable thickness, then add the fish and ground nutmeg or pepper, cook a little, finally add a small piece of sugar and salt.
4. Before the salt is added, you can bind the fish with an egg yolk, which gives it a good flavor.
5. Then place slices of French bread, which have first been baked in butter on a plate, and pour the fish over.
6. You can also place the warm plukfisk in a deep plate, sprinkle a layer of ground bread a finger's thickness over it, pour melted butter over it and place it in the oven where it is baked light brown.
7. To prevent the bread from sinking into the fish, it must be fairly thick and is therefore best made by binding it with two egg yolks.
8. You can of course also use freshly cooked fish. Cooked salt cod can also be used, but it must be chopped fine and requires much butter.
9. If you do not want to use bread for the salt fish, small diced carrots and pepper can be added.
