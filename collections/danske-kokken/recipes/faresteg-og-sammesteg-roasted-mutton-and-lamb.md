## Ingredients

- Mutton or lamb roast
- Parsley
- Butter
- Bread dough (made from French bread, milk, butter, eggs, sugar, salt)
- Flour

## Directions

1. There is a skin on the sides of the mutton roast which must be removed before roasting.
2. The roast is washed, after which many lard it with parsley, which is done by making holes with a knife, one beside the other down the back of the leg of the roast, and pressing picked and washed parsley together with a little butter into each hole.
3. The roast can be filled with parsley and butter or with bread dough, which is made as follows: some slices of French bread are dipped in milk, left standing for about an hour, and then pounded into pieces with a spoon. A piece of cold butter is stirred until creamy, into which the bread is stirred, along with 1 or 2 eggs, a little sugar and salt.
4. When well mixed, it is filled into the roast, after this is opened from one side, so that a hand can be gotten in and with this the skin can be loosened as much as possible on the roast.
5. If there are holes in the roast they are sewn together, and when the dough is in the roast, it is sewn shut.
6. The roast is dusted with flour and roasted in a good hour.
7. Lamb is treated in all respects as mutton roast.
