## Ingredients

- Calf's head
- Water
- Salt
- Browned roast bones
- Broth
- Vegetables and onion
- Butter
- Onion
- Flour
- Cayenne pepper

## Directions

1. When a calf's head of medium size is scalded, clean it well; remove the tongue for better cleaning; this and the head, which is flocked, are cooked in water and salt until it is so tender that the meat can be separated from the bones, which must be done while it is warm
2. The broth should be skimmed carefully
3. Spread it on a platter, carefully remove all bones, and cut the meat along with the tongue into small square pieces
4. Then take bouillon, which can be made from browned roast bones and the water in which the head was cooked, but then it must be cooked in just water with vegetables and onion — but it is better when you directly make brown broth for the same purpose
5. Brown a good piece of butter, in which cook a finely grated onion, but carefully so it does not burn
6. Then add 3 heaping tablespoons flour and a little Cayenne pepper
