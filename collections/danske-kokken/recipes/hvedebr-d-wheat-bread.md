## Ingredients

- 4 pounds fine wheat flour
- 6 cups sweet, lukewarm milk
- a little salt
- 2 tablespoons thick yeast (or 20 grams pressed yeast)

## Directions

1. Knead the wheat flour with the lukewarm milk, a little salt, and yeast until the dough releases from the hands.
2. Let it rise well covered for 3 or 4 hours.
3. If baking in the morning, the dough can be laid out in the evening and left overnight in cold water to rise in a moderately warm place.
4. Knead the dough again and shape into 3 or 4 loaves, which should rise a little more.
5. Brush with egg white, water, or beer and place in a well-heated oven that is not so hot that the crust burns.
6. Since the heat is strongest at the back of the oven, it may be right to turn the pan once during baking, which takes 1 hour.
7. If the loaves should be smooth, make one or two cuts lengthwise or crosswise; the deeper the cut, the thicker the crust becomes.
8. The crust will be thinner by beginning baking with slightly less heat; for this purpose, you can leave the oven door slightly ajar.
9. When the loaves are taken out, it is good to cover them slightly so the cooling proceeds more slowly.
10. The milk used can also be skimmed, but it must in no way be sour, nor must the yeast be bitter.
