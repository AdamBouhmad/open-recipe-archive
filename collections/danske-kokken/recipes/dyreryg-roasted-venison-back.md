## Ingredients

- Venison back
- Butter, margarine or fat
- Flour
- Skimmed milk
- Lard
- Cream or melted margarine or butter browned with flour
- Stock, soup, thin cream or milk
- Raw cream or condensed milk
- Cold butter
- Currant jelly
- Potatoes
- Tartlets
- Herb salad

## Directions

1. The animal must hang at least 4 to 5 days before preparation.
2. Remove the back as described, and with a sharp knife remove all skin at once without losing any meat.
3. Rinse the back in skimmed milk and lard in close rows.
4. Place the back in a roasting pan, cover well with small pieces of butter, margarine or fat.
5. Put in a hot oven for a quarter hour before basting with cream or with a mixture of melted margarine or butter browned with flour, diluted with stock, soup, thin cream or milk.
6. The length of roasting depends on size, but in all cases not less than 1½ hours.
7. Strain the pan drippings for the sauce, which is either browned with butter and flour or reduced and finished with raw cream or condensed milk (483), which must boil well with it.
8. Finally add a piece of cold butter and some currant jelly.
9. At serving, cut the meat free from the ribs and backbone, then cut crosswise into slices, which are again gathered and placed on the back in the order they were cut.
10. Venison back can be garnished with browned potatoes and tartlets filled with currant jelly or with herb salad in gratin shells (see Nr. 316).
11. Fasten a skewer at the neck, passed through a lettuce head.
12. If both back and legs are to be roasted, it is best to roast the legs a good hour before placing the back in the roasting pan.
