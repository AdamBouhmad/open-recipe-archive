## Ingredients

- Swans, eiders, wild geese, or wild ducks
- Butter
- Cream
- Potatoes
- Currant jelly
- Milk

## Directions

1. These birds have a more or less fishy taste. Those with a broad, flat bill are the best, especially young ducks raised in fresh water.
2. Young ducks need not be flayed; before roasting, they may be boiled for 1 hour.
3. More fishy species must, after plucking and singeing, be flayed, then have the head, feet, and tips of the wings cut off.
4. Then drain and wash them well in cold water and place them in milk for several hours.
5. Lard them and roast for about an hour until tender in butter and cream.
6. Sharpen the sauce with a little cream.
7. Truly only the breast is worth serving.
8. Serve with browned potatoes and currant jelly.
