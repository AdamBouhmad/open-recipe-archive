## Ingredients

- 50 medium crayfish
- salt
- water
- 1/4 pound butter
- wheat flour (1 tablespoon)
- crayfish or lobster bouillon
- good bouillon
- fish forcemeat
- 4 egg yolks
- 6 tablespoons good sweet cream
- pepper

## Directions

1. Wash the crayfish and place in boiling salted water. Cook for approximately a quarter hour until done.
2. Remove and let cool, then shell them. Set aside the tails (removing the intestine) and some of the largest claws. Clean the 20 to 24 finest heads well and place in cold water.
3. Finely pound all remaining debris and shells with 1/4 pound of butter.
4. Place in a saucepan and sauté with constant stirring so it does not burn.
5. Dilute with the crayfish bouillon so it stands well above the shells and cook until the red butter floats on top. Skim this during cooking as it appears.
6. Place the crayfish butter in a saucepan with 1 tablespoon of wheat flour and cook together, then dilute with the strained crayfish bouillon and good bouillon until it makes between 4 and 5 pots total.
7. Fill the cleaned empty head shells with fish forcemeat, boil in salted water, clean them and add to the soup along with the tails and large claws. Small fish balls may also be added.
8. In the tureen, beat 4 egg yolks with 6 tablespoons of good sweet cream, then carefully add the soup a little at a time under continuous beating.
9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
10. For lobster soup, prepare in the same manner but also add 1 pound of asparagus cut into pieces about one inch long, along with a little of the asparagus water, which is brought to a boil with the soup.
