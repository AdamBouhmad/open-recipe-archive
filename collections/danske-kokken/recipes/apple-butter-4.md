## Ingredients

- Cider
- All sorts of apples, if only they are juicy
- Cinnamon and cloves (optional)

## Directions

1. Fill a kettle with cider and let it boil until about one-third is boiled away.
2. Peel the apples, cut them into small pieces, free them from the cores and put so many in the kettle that the cider covers them.
3. Let it boil evenly with frequent stirring. When the apples are fully soft, take them up with a fish slice so that no juice comes with.
4. Put a new batch of chopped apples in the cider in the same way as before, and treat them in the same manner.
5. Then remove the kettle from the fire and pour everything into a spacious crock, where it stands until the next day under a well-fitting lid.
6. Then pour it back into the kettle and cook again with constant stirring until it is brown and like soft soap. Can be seasoned with cinnamon and cloves to taste.
7. Pour into stone crocks and will, in a dry, cool place, keep through the winter.
