## Ingredients

- 1/2 kilogram sifted rye flour
- 1/2 kilogram cake flour
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 tablespoon caraway seeds
- 625 grams cold, stiff margarine
- 1 small glass vinegar
- 3 3/4 deciliters cold water

## Directions

1. Place the rye flour and cake flour on a pastry board with the salt and caraway seeds
2. Take half of the cold margarine and chop it together with the flour using a knife until very finely crumbled
3. Work the vinegar and cold water into the dough with the knife
4. Gather the dough together with your hands and roll out with a rolling pin into a large sheet
5. Distribute the remaining margarine in small pieces over the dough
6. Fold the dough together and roll out 3-4 times as instructed for puff pastry (Nr. 22)
7. Let the dough rest in a cold place for a quarter of an hour
8. Roll out as thin as possible, dusted with flour without adding to the weight
9. Cut the biscuits into squares with a pastry cutter, prick with a fork, and place close together on a baking sheet
10. Bake light brown over good heat. Makes approximately 1 1/2 kilograms of excellent crispy biscuits that keep well for a long time, but should be placed briefly in a warm oven before serving
