## Ingredients

- 3 Pints sweet cream
- 0.5 Pound whole sugar
- 3 Pounds sweet almonds
- 1 Ounce bitter almonds
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 2 Ounces isinglass, dissolved in warm water
- 1 Quart raspberry juice
- 3 Gills cherry juice
- 1 Pint water
- 1.5 Pounds white sugar
- 1 Ounce gelatine

## Directions

1. Heat the sweet cream with the whole sugar, finely pounded sweet almonds, finely pounded bitter almonds, and lemon zest
2. When it has boiled for 2 to 3 minutes, add the isinglass dissolved in warm water
3. Cook gently for 10 minutes with constant stirring
4. Strain through a fine cloth into a stone bowl
5. For the red jelly layer: mix together the raspberry juice, cherry juice, water, white sugar, and gelatine
6. Bring to a boil, stirring, and stir in a bowl until it begins to set
7. When both parts are ready, pour some of the white mixture into the oiled mold to a height of about half an inch
8. Insert a clean stick and make a cut where the mixture ends
9. When completely cold and stiff, warm some of the red jelly so it is just fluid and carefully pour it over with a spoon so it does not fall hard on one spot and break the white
10. Hold the stick, which must not be pointed, down to the solid layer and pour, filling to the cut
11. Continue in this way as long as there is something left
12. Keep the mold in cold water the entire time, changing it often
13. This pudding is always turned out
14. Always test each mixture before putting it in the mold by pouring a little onto a saucer and letting it cool and set as much as possible
15. The white never fails, but the red depends much on the quality of the juice; homemade juice should be used; if it does not set, use more gelatine
