## Ingredients

- pork intestines or beef intestines
- medium-fine barley grains
- 1½ gallons pork blood
- 3 spoonfuls dried, finely chopped thyme and marjoram leaves
- 1 spoonful crushed allspice
- 3 spoonfuls crushed cloves
- ¼ pound powdered sugar
- salt
- 2 pounds raisins
- pork lard, cut into small cubes

## Directions

1. Clean the intestines completely inside and out by scraping, turning and filling them repeatedly in lukewarm water
2. When they are completely smooth and clear, place them in cold water with celery leaves and let them stand overnight
3. The same day the intestines are cleaned, pour boiling water over a suitable quantity of medium-fine barley grains (or half barley grains and half rye flour)
4. After stirring well, drain off almost all the water and let the grains stand until the next day
5. The next day, pour 1½ gallons of pork blood, still warm from slaughter, through a sieve over them, and let them stand again until the next day, stirring regularly
6. Add 3 spoonfuls of dried, finely chopped and sieved thyme and marjoram leaves to the blood, along with 1 spoonful crushed allspice, 3 spoonfuls crushed cloves, ¼ pound powdered sugar, a little salt and 2 pounds raisins
7. Stir the whole to a thick consistency
8. Drain the water from the intestines and lay them in lukewarm water
9. Blow down into them to see where there are holes, and cut them there
10. Tie one end, put the sausage horn in the other end, and through it fill the sausage with a spoon while stirring often
11. For each spoonful add a couple of cubes of pork lard
12. Fill the intestines only about half full, tie them off and press the sausage mixture around in them so that the raisins and meat cubes are well distributed
13. Place them in lukewarm water, and when 4 or 6 are filled, place them in a kettle with boiling water
14. Add the thyme stalks to the boiling water
15. During cooking prick them here and there
16. Cook for a good half hour or until a fine stick inserted in them no longer becomes colored from blood
17. Serve with powdered white sugar and powdered cinnamon or sieved cloves in melted butter
