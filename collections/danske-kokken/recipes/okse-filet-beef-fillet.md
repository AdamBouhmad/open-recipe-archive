## Ingredients

- 1½ kg beef fillet (calculated as ¼ kg per person)
- Speck (larding fat), in thin strips
- Butter for browning
- Gravy or stock
- Flour
- Tomato purée or Maggi bouillon cubes, if needed
- French bread or household bread for the base
- Egg white
- Vegetables for garnish

## Directions

1. Remove the sinews and fat from the fillet before larding it.
2. Lard the fillet thickly in neat rows with fine, fresh speck strips.
3. Brown the fillet in butter on a roasting pan or on the stovetop, without piercing it as this causes it to lose juice.
4. When brown on both sides, place it in a roasting pan in a hot oven, pour some gravy over it, and roast with frequent basting for half an hour.
5. When the fillet is roasted, strain off the gravy and make a strong, smooth gravy sauce as for roast beef.
6. If the gravy is not strong enough, improve it with tomato purée or Maggi bouillon cubes.
7. For presentation, take a slice of French bread or household bread, cut off the upper crust, and attach it to the center of an elongated platter with a mixture of flour and egg white.
8. On this base, arrange the roasted fillet, sliced thinly and overlapped together.
9. Surround the base with vegetables and arrange herb salads in shells or bird's nests around the presentation.
