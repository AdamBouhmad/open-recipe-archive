## Ingredients

- Cooked calf tongues, skin removed
- Veal or pork forcemeat
- Bread crumbs
- Butter, melted
- Broth or thin soup
- Sharp, brown sauce made from the cooking broth
- Optional: mushrooms, pickles, capers, or chutney

## Directions

1. Split the cooked, skinned tongues lengthwise so they remain joined together.
2. Spread a layer of forcemeat, 1 to 2 centimeters (1 inch) thick, on the split tongues.
3. Sprinkle with bread crumbs and drip with melted butter.
4. Place the tongues carefully in a roasting pan, the bottom of which is covered with broth or thin soup to prevent sticking.
5. Set in a hot oven until browned on top and the forcemeat is cooked.
6. Serve with a sharp, brown sauce made from the cooking broth, which may be seasoned with mushrooms, pickles, capers, or chutney.
