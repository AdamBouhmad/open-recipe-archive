## Ingredients

- Salmon or other fish
- Cold water
- Bay leaves
- Whole peppercorns
- Isinglass (husblas)
- Egg whites, whipped stiff
- Lemon slices
- Eel or other fish as alternatives

## Directions

1. Clean the fish and cut it into appropriate slices across the grain, keeping the skin with each piece, as the skin is fatty and helps the piece hold its shape
2. Rinse the slices only once in cold water, as more rinsing will cause loss of fat
3. Bring the fish to a boil; when boiling, skim the broth and add some bay leaves and whole peppercorns; do not let it boil for too long
4. When the fish is tender, place it in a flat glass or porcelain dish
5. Cook the broth with the necessary isinglass, skim it, clarify it with whipped stiff egg whites, and strain it through a gelée sieve over the fish, laying some lemon slices among it
6. Eel and other fish may be arranged this way as well; eel must first be skinned
