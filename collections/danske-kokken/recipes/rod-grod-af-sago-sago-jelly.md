## Ingredients

- best large Sago grains
- 4 to 5 Quart Water
- Juice
- Sugar

## Directions

1. The best large sago grains are particularly suited for rod-grod, in which no starch is then needed.
2. If you get the best sago, which swells the most, you can reckon 1 pound grains to 4 to 5 quarts water.
3. Add juice and sugar to taste.
4. The grains should be cooked more in the water before juice and sugar are added, and thereafter the whole gets a good boil.
5. Spoon a spoonful onto a plate to see if it is sufficiently thick.
6. If it is too thick, it can be thinned with a little boiling water, but if it is too thin, there is nothing else to do but smooth it with a spoonful of corn-starch dissolved in cold water.
7. The best pearl sago can cook out to a clear jelly, and it becomes best with juice; but if preferred, it can also be made with red wine and lemon juice.
