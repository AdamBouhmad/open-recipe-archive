## Ingredients

- Pork roast, 5 to 6 kg (10 to 12 pounds)
- Salt
- Boiling water
- Bay leaves (optional)
- Whole peppercorns (optional)
- Cloves (optional)
- Flour for sauce
- Food coloring
- Maggi bouillon cubes

## Directions

1. A pork roast of 5 to 6 kg must roast for approximately 4 hours.
2. The ham is the piece of pork usually used for a larger roast, but in smaller households the front rib is often roasted.
3. First, remove the shank (see its use in Nr. 263 and 868), and then scrape the roast well on the skin side.
4. With a sharp knife, score the skin either in strips crosswise or in squares, but not too deeply into the meat.
5. Wash the skin in cold water and then rub it well with a little salt to make it crispy.
6. Place the roast in the roasting pan, which is placed in a well-heated oven, and only after a few minutes pour boiling water to it along with salt.
7. A pork roast must not be basted, as this prevents the skin from becoming crispy.
8. Many use a couple of bay leaves, some whole peppercorns, and a few cloves with the roast, but this depends on taste.
9. Half an hour before the roast is done, strain off some of the gravy.
10. Skim off the fat, and some of it can be used to cook the flour in for the sauce, which is diluted with the gravy and must then cook well.
11. Season with salt, give it color with food coloring, and improve it with Maggi bouillon cubes.
