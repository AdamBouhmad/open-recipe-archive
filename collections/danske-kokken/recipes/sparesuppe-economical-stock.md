## Ingredients

- Chopped raw, cooked or roasted beef, veal, lamb or pork bones
- Tendons
- Bacon scraps
- Meat scraps from raw, cooked and roasted meat
- Poultry carcasses
- Leftovers of white, smooth sauces
- Roll sausage and pork head soup and other soup leftovers
- Water
- Salt

## Directions

1. Place all bones, scraps, and leftovers in a pot with water and salt (if not already salted)
2. Add smoked items with caution as the flavor can become too overpowering and make the stock less versatile for all dishes
3. Use the same caution with herbs and spices, as their flavors do not always harmonize well with all dishes and the stock is better preserved without these additions
4. Roots and herbs should preferably only be added when the stock is to be used and the recipe requires it
5. When the stock is boiling and well-skimmed, it can be placed in a hay box for several hours, but in summer it should not sit overnight
6. Things that do not suit white, smooth soups can be used for brown stock instead
