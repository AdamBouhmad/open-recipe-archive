## Ingredients

- 40 pounds currants
- 25 pounds good, light powdered sugar
- Alternatively, 4 pounds of the currants may be replaced with 4 pounds red raspberries
- 13½ pails water

## Directions

1. Place the sugar in a clean bucket
2. The currants, which are not stripped from their stems, are wrung out in a clean linen cloth and the juice is poured over the sugar, stirred well, and left until the next day
3. Pour 13½ pails water over the wrung-out berries, which likewise stands until the next day, then strain through a sieve and press well
4. Pour the juice into the barrel along with the water from the wrung-out berries, and if this is not sufficient to fill the barrel, fill it with water
5. To make room for the juice to ferment, draw off 2 bottles of juice from the barrel and set aside loosely corked
6. Shake the barrel vigorously to promote mixing and place it in a cellar or other cool place
7. Seal it only loosely and leave it thus for 6 weeks to ferment
8. When fermentation has taken place, add the 2 bottles of juice previously removed, which have also fermented, then seal the barrel well and seal with clay
9. It then rests completely until around February, when the wine can be drawn off and corked well
10. To serve warm, heat the wine over the fire with 1 crushed cardamom per pail, a little whole cinnamon, a couple of cloves and sugar to taste; when the wine is about to boil, light it briefly and then remove from the fire
