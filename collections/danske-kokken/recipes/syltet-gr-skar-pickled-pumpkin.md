## Ingredients

- pumpkin
- wine vinegar
- 1 pound sugar (Melis) per pound pumpkin
- ginger
- cinnamon sticks
- cloves
- cognac and white wine (optional)

## Directions

1. Cut the pumpkin in half and then into smaller pieces.
2. Peel the skin well and remove the soft interior.
3. Cut the pieces into smaller, approximately equal-sized cubes.
4. Cook them tender in good wine vinegar until they can be pierced with a straw.
5. For each pound of pumpkin, take 1 pound sugar, dip it in cold water and cook to a thick syrup.
6. Cool it and pour cold over the pumpkin pieces.
7. Add small pieces of ginger, some cinnamon sticks and a little cloves to taste to the cold syrup.
8. Let the pumpkin stand thus for 8 days.
9. Give the syrup a brief boil, if needed with more sugar, until it becomes very smooth.
10. Pour it cold over the pumpkin pieces again.
11. Alternatively, when cooking the sugar the first time, dip it in half cognac and half white wine instead of water.
