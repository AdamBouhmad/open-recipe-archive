## Ingredients

- butter
- flour
- milk or water (boiling hot)

## Directions

1. The butter must boil before the flour is stirred into it, and the flour must be well baked before the milk or water, which must be boiling hot, is added little by little.
2. Bake it again over an even fire.
3. If there is too little butter to mix with the flour, a little milk or water can be added to the butter before the flour is stirred in.
4. Do not remove it from the fire during baking, as the dough would become heavy.
5. The common sign that the dough is baked enough is when it releases from the pan and spoon, but if this happens very quickly, bake it until the butter shows mostly on the outside and until it becomes lighter and rises again when pressed with a spoon.
6. To be safest that the dough turns out good, when everything is baked, let it bake another hour in the oven, so you only need to turn it occasionally.
