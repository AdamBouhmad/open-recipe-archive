## Ingredients

- 1 L good soup, either meat or fish
- a little vinegar
- whole white pepper
- salt
- a couple of bay leaves
- 12 leaves of isinglass, rinsed in cold water
- 2 to 3 cleaned and crushed eggshells, beaten together with egg whites
- tomatoes
- cucumbers
- lemon slices
- red and white carved beets
- parsley

## Directions

1. Add a little vinegar, whole white pepper, salt, and a couple of bay leaves to 1 L of good soup, either meat or fish
2. When it boils, add 12 leaves of isinglass that have first been rinsed in cold water, and 2 to 3 cleaned and crushed eggshells that have been beaten together with egg whites
3. Stir constantly with a whisk and bring the aspic to a boil again and let it stand for a few minutes on the heat so that it does not set but remains clear
4. Pour it either into an aspic bag or through a fairly tight cloth laid in a sieve, and whatever is used for this must be wrung out in boiling water before the aspic is poured into it
5. If the first of the aspic that runs through is not sufficiently clear, pour it back again, and this must sometimes be repeated a few times
6. Pour a thin layer of aspic into the bottom of the mold; when it is set, arrange figures over it made from tomatoes, cucumbers, lemon slices, or red and white carved beets, with parsley as garnish
7. Pour another thin layer of aspic over this, and only when this is again set, place the meat or fish in it
8. Pour some of the aspic over it, which during the entire preparation has been kept near enough to the heat so that it is not set but only kept lukewarm
9. Make the aspic the day before
10. At serving time, dip the mold in boiling water if it does not otherwise release
11. Any leftover aspic can be filled into small molds, which when turned out are placed around the larger mold, or can be chopped coarsely and laid in a ring around the dish
