## Ingredients

- dried yellow peas
- water
- a small piece of soda
- yellow carrot (optional)
- large onion
- parsley roots
- celery
- meat or sausage broth (optional)
- rolled sausage (optional)
- small peeled onions
- salt
- butter
- baked bread or meat balls

## Directions

1. Shell and rinse the peas and soak overnight in cold, soft water or water with a small piece of soda about the size of a large pea
2. The next day boil them in the same water, which should stand well above them when placed on the heat
3. Cooking a yellow carrot with them gives the peas a good color, but it can also be grated and added later
4. When they are skimmed and the clear shells that rise are removed, add a large bunch, whole parsley roots and celery, which are removed again when soft, rinsed, cut into pieces and placed in the terrine
5. When the peas are cooked soft, mash them and rub through a sieve with fresh or salt broth, if available, until the shells are removed
6. Since broth is not always on hand, one usually boils a small piece of meat or meat with the peas, and if desired, a rolled sausage, whose preparation will be explained later, and then the peas must be rubbed through with water
7. Set the finished peas back on the heat with the roots, some small peeled onions, and a little salt
8. At serving, stir a piece of butter into them
9. Serve either with baked bread or small meat balls, which are then boiled in the finished peas
