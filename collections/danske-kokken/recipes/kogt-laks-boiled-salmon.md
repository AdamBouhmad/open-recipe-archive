## Ingredients

- ½ kg salmon per 3 persons
- Salt
- Cold water
- Whole peppercorns
- Bay leaves

## Directions

1. Before cleaning, place the salmon in cold water. Scrape it with a knife from the head toward the tail without loosening the scales.
2. Make a small crosswise cut at the vent fin nearest the tail, and remove the entrails from the opening near the head without opening the belly.
3. Rinse the fish thoroughly in several changes of cold water and clean the inside well.
4. Sprinkle with salt and let stand for a couple of hours before cooking.
5. The salmon is best when cooked whole, but may also be cut into round slices about an inch thick, which are reassembled into the original form when served. Alternatively, the fish may be split while raw by following the backbone from the tail through the head with a sharp knife for a beautiful presentation on two platters.
6. For cooking time, allow 5 to 6 minutes per inch of thickness.
7. If cooking whole without a fish kettle, use a fish rack or lay a coarse cloth in the bottom of the pot large enough to hang over the sides. Place a plate on the cloth and lay the fish on it for easy removal when plated.
8. For whole fish, place in enough cold water to just cover. For cut fish, place in boiling water with vinegar (2½ dl or 1 pint per 5 to 6 liters of water), whole peppercorns, bay leaves, and salt.
9. Keep the cooking at an even temperature, skim well during cooking, and let the fish rest in the water for a few minutes before serving.
10. Serve on a warm platter lined with a fish rack and a folded napkin. Garnish the head and tail with parsley and lemon slices or boiled crayfish. The platter may be further garnished with croutons filled with a ragout of shrimp, crayfish, lobster, or oysters, or the ragout may be served in gratinée shells arranged around the edge of the platter.
