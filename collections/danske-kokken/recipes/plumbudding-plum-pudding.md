## Ingredients

- 8 French breads (4-øre pieces)
- rum
- sweet milk
- a small handful of flour
- 4 pounds finely chopped kidney fat
- 4 pounds beef marrow
- 19 pounds cleaned raisins without pits
- 1 pound washed currants
- a little mace and nutmeg
- 1 tablespoon powdered sugar
- a little salt
- 14 whole eggs
- 13 pægle sweet cream
- 1 pægle good rum
- sugar for glazing

## Directions

1. Remove the crusts from 8 French breads.
2. Cut the breads into slices, dip them in rum, then soak in sweet milk until soaked through.
3. Take them out and add a small handful of flour, 4 pounds finely chopped kidney fat, 4 pounds beef marrow, 19 pounds cleaned raisins without pits, 1 pound washed currants, a little mace and nutmeg, 1 tablespoon powdered sugar, and a little salt.
4. Beat 14 whole eggs with 13 pægle sweet cream and 1 pægle good rum and add to the mixture, beating well together.
5. Pour the entire mixture into a sugar-glazed mold.
6. Place in boiling water and cook for approximately 4 hours.
7. Turn out onto a serving platter.
8. Pour 1 pægle warm rum over it and set it alight, so the pudding is served flaming.
9. Serve with Chantilly sauce.
