## Ingredients

- Pig blood
- Barley groats (3 measures of groats per pot of blood)
- Dried herbs (Basil, Marjoram and Thyme)
- Ground cloves
- Allspice
- 18 Quintals sugar
- 2 Pounds washed raisins or dried fruit
- Salt
- Ground cinnamon
- Finely chopped onion
- 4 Pounds pork lard cut into fairly large dice
- Intestines

## Directions

1. When the blood runs from the pig, stir it well so it does not solidify.
2. Then pour in medium-fine barley groats while stirring constantly, 3 measures of groats for each pot of blood.
3. Let the blood stand until the next day and then mix it with 2 tablespoons dried herbs (Basil, Marjoram and Thyme) that are rubbed to pieces between the hands and sifted, furthermore with 1 tablespoon ground cloves, the same amount of allspice, 18 Quintals sugar, 2 Pounds washed raisins or dried fruit, a little salt, ground cinnamon and finely chopped onion.
4. 4 Pounds of pork lard are cut into fairly large dice and mixed in with the rest.
5. The sausage mixture is then stuffed into well-cleaned intestines and boiled in water with a little salt in it.
6. They can be used both boiled and roasted on a grill or cut into slices and fried in a pan in butter, to which a little syrup can be added.
7. In the first two cases they are served with melted butter and white, ground sugar and cinnamon alongside, in the last the sauce from the pan is served with it.
8. Apple compote or apple porridge can also be served with black sausages.
