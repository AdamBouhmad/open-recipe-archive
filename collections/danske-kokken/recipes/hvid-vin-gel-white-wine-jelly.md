## Ingredients

- 1/4 kg (1/2 S) chopped powdered sugar
- 1/4 L (1 pint) water
- 1 bottle Rhenish wine or Graves (with a little Madeira or Sherry optionally added)
- 12 sheets isinglass
- 1 1/4 dl (1/2 pint) water
- fresh grapes (optional)

## Directions

1. Cook the chopped powdered sugar with 1/4 L (1 pint) water to a syrup.
2. Pour in 1 bottle Rhenish wine or Graves, but the wine must not boil.
3. Melt 12 sheets of isinglass in 1 1/4 dl (1/2 pint) water without boiling and stir into the jelly.
4. Pour the jelly through a clean cloth into a mold moistened with water and strewn with sugar.
5. Fresh grapes can be layered in the mold: pour a little jelly into the bottom, and when it is set, lay some grapes, then jelly again and more grapes, until the mold is full.
6. While the mold is being filled, keep the remaining jelly near heat so it does not set.
7. The jelly can be arranged in small mounds on pineapple slices, decorated with grapes and foam peaks, and garnished with petits fours.
8. Prepared this way, it is not necessary to clarify the jelly.
9. At serving, turn the jelly out and serve with cream sauce.
