## Ingredients

- 1 uncut goose, approximately 5 kg (10 lb)
- Cold water with salt
- Bunch of herbs
- 4 carrots
- 1 celery
- 4 leeks
- 10 whole peppercorns
- Flour dumplings (recipe No. 497)
- 1 kg (2 lb) good apples
- Sugar
- 1/4 kg (1/2 lb) washed prunes
- Maggis Bouillon cubes (optional)
- Vinegar and sugar (optional)

## Directions

1. After the goose is plucked, singed, cleaned, and freed from feather follicles, cut off the feet at the knee joint
2. Cut off the neck and wings, then cut up the goose and remove the innards
3. Rinse the goose thoroughly
4. Place in a pot with enough cold salted water to reach the level of the meat
5. Scald the feet well, cut off the claws, remove the outer skin, and add to the soup along with the neck, cleaned gizzard, and heart
6. Save the liver for liver pâté
7. Once the soup is well skimmed, add bunch of herbs, 4 carrots, 1 celery, 4 leeks, and 10 whole peppercorns
8. While the soup cooks, prepare flour dumplings (recipe No. 497)
9. Peel 1 kg (2 lb) good apples, cut into 4 pieces, remove the core, cook in water with sugar until tender but whole
10. Cook 1/4 kg (1/2 lb) rinsed prunes in the same water as the apples
11. When the vegetables are tender, remove them, cut into pieces, and place in the tureen with the apples, prunes, and flour dumplings (which should be cooked separately in salted water to keep the soup clear)
12. When the meat is tender, strain most of the soup off, leaving only a little in the pot to keep the goose warm
13. Skim the fat from the soup carefully and add the water in which the apples and prunes were cooked
14. If necessary, strengthen the soup with Maggis Bouillon cubes
15. Season with vinegar and sugar to taste, if desired
16. Serve the boiled goose garnished with parsley along with the soup and gizzards
