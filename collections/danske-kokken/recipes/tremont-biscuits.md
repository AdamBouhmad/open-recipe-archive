## Ingredients

- 1 3/8 pints warm milk
- 1 tablespoon fat
- 2 tablespoons white sugar
- salt
- 1/2 cake compressed yeast or 1 cup homemade yeast
- 2 quarts sifted flour
- melted butter
- 1/2 teaspoon soda

## Directions

1. Make a well in the center of 2 quarts sifted flour.
2. Add 1 3/8 pints warm milk, 1 tablespoon fat, 2 tablespoons white sugar, salt, and 1/2 cake compressed yeast or 1 cup homemade yeast, and blend together to form a dough.
3. Let it rise. When light, knead again and set to rise again.
4. When risen again, knead lightly and roll out 1 inch thick.
5. Cut with a biscuit cutter; in the absence of one, use a small glass.
6. Dampen the corners slightly with melted butter and fold together with the point to the center.
7. Place on a bread pan with about 1 inch spacing.
8. Let them rise about half an hour in a warm place, then bake in a quick oven for 10 to 15 minutes.
9. If making for afternoon tea, set the dough in the morning. If for breakfast, set around 9 p.m., and as soon as you rise in the morning, add 1/2 teaspoon soda. They will then be ready for baking. They are considered very delicate when well made.
