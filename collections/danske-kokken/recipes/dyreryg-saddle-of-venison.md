## Ingredients

- One saddle of venison, approximately 10 pounds
- Strips of salted meat, very thin and approximately 2 inches long
- Two carrots
- One onion
- Salted meat, sliced thin
- Two bay leaves
- Two cloves
- Five grains of allspice
- Half a lemon, sliced
- Salt
- Pepper
- One quarter soup stock (Claret)
- A small lump of butter

## Directions

1. Take one approximately 10 pounds in weight and lard it with strips of salted meat, very thin and approximately 2 inches long, two rows on each side.
2. Slice two carrots and one onion and season with a little salted meat, sliced thin, in a large roasting pan.
3. Add two bay leaves, two cloves, five grains of allspice, half a lemon sliced thin, a little salt and pepper.
4. Place the venison saddle in the pan with one quarter soup stock (Claret) and a small lump of butter, and let it simmer approximately one quarter of an hour on top of the stove.
5. Set it then in a hot oven to roast, and drip with a little butter every fifth minute, until it is half-roasted, so the blood runs out when you cut into the meat.
6. Serve with wine sauce. Others prefer it completely tender. Then it must be roasted much longer. Serve with cream sauce.
