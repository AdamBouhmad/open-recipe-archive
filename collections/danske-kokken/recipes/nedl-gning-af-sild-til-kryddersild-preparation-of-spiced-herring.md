## Ingredients

- 1 barrel of herring
- 1/2 kg (1 pound) kitchen salt
- 1 L (1 pot) vinegar
- 1 L (1 pot) water
- 1 kg (2 pounds) Lüneburg salt
- 1/4 kg (1/2 pound) powdered sugar
- 15 grams (3 quintals) Spanish hops
- 20 bay leaves
- 25 grams (5 quintals) cloves
- 25 grams (5 quintals) allspice
- 50 grams (10 quintals) pepper
- 15 grams (3 quintals) saltpeter

## Directions

1. Use herring from autumn if possible
2. Between 1 barrel of herring (which must not be cleaned beforehand), sprinkle 1/2 kg (1 pound) of kitchen salt and let it stand for 12 hours under a light press
3. Take each herring from the brine, scrape off the scales, lay them again and cover with 1 L (1 pot) of vinegar mixed with 1 L (1 pot) of water
4. Let stand for another 24 hours
5. Take up the herrings and let the vinegar drain well before laying them again with the spices
6. For 1 barrel of herring, combine 1 kg (2 pounds) of Lüneburg salt, 1/4 kg (1/2 pound) of powdered sugar, 15 grams (3 quintals) of Spanish hops, 20 bay leaves, and of ground spices: 25 grams (5 quintals) of cloves, 25 grams (5 quintals) of allspice, 50 grams (10 quintals) of pepper and 15 grams (3 quintals) of saltpeter
7. Lay the herrings with the back downward in a butter barrel
