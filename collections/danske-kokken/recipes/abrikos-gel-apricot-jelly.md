## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg apricots, not fully ripe
- 2 1/2 dl water
- 1/2 kg sugar (or corresponding weight after straining)

## Directions

1. Split the apricots in half; remove the pits, and place the fruit over heat with 2 1/2 dl water until it begins to release its juices.
2. Pour the pulp into a scalded and tightly wrung jelly cloth.
3. The next day, weigh the drained juice, and after the sugar has been cooked dry (see Sugar Cooking for Jelly), add the juice to it.
4. Skim during cooking, which lasts approximately 10 minutes, and fill immediately into jelly cups.
5. Jelly may be tied up as soon as it begins to set. White paper dipped in cognac is usually used to place over the jelly, but it may be omitted.
6. Tie with parchment paper.
7. What remains in the jelly cloth is pressed through a sieve or Passermachine, cooked with sugar, and used for Russian marmalade, see No. 146.
