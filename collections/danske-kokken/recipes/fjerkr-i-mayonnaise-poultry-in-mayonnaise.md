## Ingredients

- Cooked poultry
- Mayonnaise
- Herb salad
- Soft or hard-cooked eggs
- Radishes
- Parsley
- Olives
- Capers
- Peas
- Carved yellow and white roots or cress
- Fruit coloring
- Gelatin
- Bordure ring

## Directions

1. When the meat is cooked and cold, cut it from the bones into small fillets (the bones can be used again for broth or sauce).
2. Dip the fillets on a fork in the mayonnaise and arrange them pyramidically on a platter.
3. Smooth the arrangement with a spoon, and use the remaining mayonnaise for herb salad, arranged as a garnish around the edge of the platter (see recipe 316).
4. The mayonnaise can also be decorated with soft or hard-cooked eggs, arranged alternately with radishes and parsley in a wreath around the platter.
5. Various other items can be used to decorate the pyramid, such as olives, capers, peas, carved yellow and white roots, or cress.
6. Mayonnaise can be served in a ring made of water, colored bright red with fruit coloring and stiffened with dissolved gelatin.
7. The ring is not eaten but provides an attractive presentation with the mayonnaise in the center.
8. A Bordure ring can also be used (see recipe 334).
