## Ingredients

- Tomatoes
- Salt (1/2 kilogram per 7 1/2 liters of water)
- Water

## Directions

1. Make a good brine of salt and water, bring to a boil, then cool completely.
2. Place well-dried tomatoes in the cooled brine; to keep them well, it is necessary that some of the stem remains attached and that they are kept submerged under the brine with the aid of a light press.
3. Tomatoes can be preserved in salt brine until well into summer.
4. Even if a fungal growth forms on them toward spring, this is of no consequence—simply remove it and the tomatoes are still good.
5. They can be used in soup, ragout, sauce, etc., but the dish for which they are used should not be salted too much, since the tomatoes are already salted.
