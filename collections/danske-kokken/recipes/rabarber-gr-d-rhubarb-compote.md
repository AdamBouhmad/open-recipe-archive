## Ingredients

- 6 to 8 rhubarb stalks
- 2 1/2 L (2 1/2 pots) water
- 30 g (6 quintal) potato flour per liter (pot) of juice
- 30 g (6 quintal) sago flour per liter (pot) of juice
- sugar to taste
- vanilla essence

## Directions

1. Cut the stalks into smaller pieces, rinse them, and cook them over the fire in 2 1/2 L (2 1/2 pots) water.
2. Strain the juice and measure it before pouring it back into the pot to be brought to the boil and sweetened.
3. For each liter (pot) of juice, take 30 g (6 quintal) potato flour and 30 g (6 quintal) sago flour, stirred in a little of the juice, and as soon as it boils, stir the thickening in.
4. Remove the pot from the fire as soon as the compote has boiled, otherwise it becomes stringy.
5. Stir vanilla essence into the finished compote.
6. If it is to be poured into glass bowls, these must first be rinsed in lukewarm water and the compote must be slightly cooled.
7. It is sprinkled with powdered sugar so that it does not form a skin.
8. Serve with cream or compote.
