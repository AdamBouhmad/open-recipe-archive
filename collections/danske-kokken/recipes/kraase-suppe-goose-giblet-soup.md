## Ingredients

- 3 times goose giblets
- apples
- prunes
- 100 g (20 skein) butter or vegetable margarine
- 100 g (20 skein) flour
- vinegar
- sugar
- Maggi bouillon cubes

## Directions

1. After the giblets are cleaned, split the heads, cut off the beaks, and remove the brains and eyes.
2. Chop the wings into 2 pieces and the neck with skin into 3 pieces.
3. Remove the windpipe and gullet, and clean the heart of blood.
4. Split the giblets, clean, and scald together with the feet (claws removed) and remove the outer skin, as well as the thick membrane on the inside of the giblets.
5. When sufficiently cleaned and washed, place in cold water with salt so it reaches the level of the giblets.
6. When boiling, skim the soup, then add whisks and vegetables as mentioned for goose soup.
7. As soon as the giblets are tender, which takes 2-3 hours, strain most of the soup away, leaving just a little in the pot to keep the giblets warm.
8. Bake 100 g (20 skein) flour in 100 g (20 skein) butter or vegetable margarine and thin with the strained soup and the water in which the apples and prunes were cooked.
9. Season with vinegar and sugar to taste and can be mixed with Maggi bouillon cubes.
10. Pour the boiling soup into the tureen over rolls, apples, prunes, and the cut vegetables.
11. The giblets can be served either in the soup or separately; garnish the platter with parsley.
