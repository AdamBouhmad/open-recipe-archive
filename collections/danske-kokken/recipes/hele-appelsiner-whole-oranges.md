## Ingredients

- 2 kg powdered loaf sugar
- 3¾ dl water
- oranges

## Directions

1. Score the orange peel with a sharp knife in uniform broad strips, removing every other strip so the oranges have a striped appearance.
2. Pierce the oranges several times with a pointed sharp knife or with a larding needle and lay them in cold water for one day.
3. Remove from water and cook in fresh water under a lid until the peels are tender to pierce.
4. Remove the fruit and lay on a sieve, and only when it has drained does it go into the jar and is covered with the hot, well-skimmed syrup, which is made from ½ kg sugar and 3¾ dl water to 1/10 kg fruit.
5. Cover the jar and set aside until the next day.
6. Pour off the syrup, boil it, and pour it hot over the fruit, which is again covered well.
7. After 3 to 4 days, pour everything into the pot and the fruit is given a boil, after which it is removed and placed in the jar.
8. Skim the syrup and reduce it until it is smooth; then pour it hot over the oranges.
9. Seal the jar immediately.
