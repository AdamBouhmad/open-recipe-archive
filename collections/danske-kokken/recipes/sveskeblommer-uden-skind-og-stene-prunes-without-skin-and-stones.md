## Ingredients

- ¾ kg Demerara sugar
- ½ l water
- ripe prunes
- 50 gr succade
- 50 gr candied orange peel
- peel of 1 lemon

## Directions

1. Place ripe prunes in boiling water until the skin comes off easily.
2. With a knife, cut a slit lengthwise along the prunes, through which the stones are removed; these can be crushed and the kernels cooked together with the prunes.
3. Cook sugar and water to syrup together with 50 gr succade, 50 gr candied orange peel, and the peel of 1 lemon, all cut into very fine, narrow strips.
4. When the syrup is cooked and skimmed, add the prunes, but not too many at once.
5. They must only just come to a boil and remain whole when removed with a slotted spoon and placed in the jar.
6. Reduce the syrup until smooth; then pour it over the prunes and seal.
7. After a week, check them, and should the liquid be too thin, pour off only the top, which is reduced and poured hot over the prunes. At the bottom of the jar the liquid will always be sufficiently thick.
