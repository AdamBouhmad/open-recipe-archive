## Ingredients

- ½ pound beef marrow
- 2 old 10-øre French breads, crusts scraped off
- sweet milk or thin cream
- ½ pound butter
- 15 egg yolks
- finely chopped marrow
- a little sugar
- salt
- cardamom
- ½ pound raisins
- egg whites

## Directions

1. Cut the beef marrow into pieces and place in cold water, then on a colander to drain, then on a clean linen cloth.
2. Soak the two French breads (with crusts removed) in sweet milk or thin cream, press them out and mix with ½ pound butter.
3. Pour the whole mixture into a dish to cool.
4. Stir in 15 egg yolks, one at a time, the finely chopped marrow, a little sugar, salt, cardamom, and ½ pound raisins.
5. Fold in the egg whites, beaten to stiff peaks.
6. Cook for 2½ hours and serve with sweet fruit sauce with red wine, or with melted butter.
