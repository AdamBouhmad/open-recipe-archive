## Ingredients

- whole apples
- water
- sugar
- corn starch (if needed)

## Directions

1. Peel the apples, then cook them in water or preferably by steaming
2. As the apples are peeled, cast them into water to prevent them from turning red
3. Discard this water
4. When the apples are soft, press them through a coarse sieve so the core remains behind
5. Place them back on the fire to prepare with sugar
6. Alternatively, slice the peeled apples into small pieces after they have lain in cold water, and place them over the fire with little water added as the apples themselves are watery
7. Let them cook quite evenly under a covered lid without stirring until they are fully cooked
8. Then stir vigorously and add white sugar according to the apples' sourness
9. Stir briskly during the cooking the sauce now undergoes, and reserve it to be eaten cold with sugar and cream
10. If it becomes too thin, take a couple of spoonfuls of corn starch mixed in a little cold water, which is added after the sugar and allowed to cook only a couple of minutes
11. To prevent it from sticking to the bottom, while the pot is clean and dry, grease it inside with cold butter and then rinse it with cold water
12. Do not place it over a flame fire
