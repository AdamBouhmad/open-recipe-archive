## Ingredients

- half a Pound Wheat Flour
- sweet Milk as needed
- a small piece melted Butter
- 3 Eggs
- a small handful Sugar
- a very little Cinnamon
- grated Lemon Peel

## Directions

1. The pan must be hot before beginning to bake, and a little Butter is added for the first cakes, later not
2. Sift half a Pound Wheat Flour and stir in as much sweet Milk that the batter becomes a little thicker than it should be
3. Add a small piece melted Butter, 3 Eggs (one at a time), a small handful Sugar, and a very little Cinnamon and grated Lemon Peel
4. Pour the batter thin on the pan and when the underside is baked enough, turn the cake, which is best done by loosening it all around the edge with a Knife, taking it at two places at the edge and turning quickly, after which it is baked finished on the other side
5. To make the cakes hard, they must be baked slowly over coals, not flames, which give too strong a fire, causing the cakes to become dark and not good; these should not be placed warm one on top of another
6. If you desire the cakes soft, a little Butter is added to the pan for each cake, they are baked faster and placed warm one on top of another
7. If the batter becomes too thick during baking, it can be thinned with a little Milk
8. The first cakes are rarely whole, but when several are baked on the pan, it goes better
