## Ingredients

- 5 L (5 pots) veal or lamb stock, or economical stock
- 1 kg (2.5 lb) fresh, medium asparagus
- 125 g (1/4 lb) butter
- 125 g (1/4 lb) flour
- 1 bunch of herbs
- 6 egg yolks
- 1 spoonful cold water

## Directions

1. Brown the butter with the flour and dilute with the stock in which a bunch of herbs has been cooked
2. Peel the asparagus and cut into pieces 3 centimeters (about 1 inch) in length and cook for a quarter hour in the stock
3. Add the asparagus heads a few minutes later, as they require less cooking than the rest of the asparagus
4. Whisk the egg yolks with a spoonful of cold water and then blend with some of the hot stock before stirring it into the pot with constant stirring
5. Do not allow the soup to boil while the eggs are being stirred in, nor afterwards, as it will curdle
6. If using canned asparagus, use 1 kilo (2.5 lb) tin, blend the water with the stock, and add the asparagus to the pot just before serving
7. Serve with toasted or baked bread
