## Ingredients

- 4 pounds beef
- 6 quarts water
- 1 carrot
- 1 onion
- Salt
- Celery leaves
- Parsley
- Celery root
- Parsnip root

## Directions

1. Rinse the meat in several changes of water, but do not let it soak. Place the meat in cold water with a carrot, onion, and a little salt over the fire. Skim when the broth first comes to a boil. Make a bouquet of celery leaves, leeks, and parsley. When the fat has been removed from the broth, which happens about an hour before the meat is tender, add the bouquet along with carrots, celery, and parsley roots that have been scraped and washed but not cut into pieces. The roots should be cooked in the broth at the same time as the meat; both should become tender at approximately the same time. Remove both the meat and roots, clean the roots, cut them into pieces, and place them in the serving dish to receive the broth. Remove the bouquet before it falls apart. Use slow cooking with steady, even heat for the best broth; wood fire cannot be used well. The lid must close well and should only be removed when the broth needs skimming. Add water for thinning only with boiling water if necessary. When everything is removed, strain the broth through a fine sieve. Let it stand briefly to settle, remove the fat, and pour it back into the pot carefully so the sediment does not come along. When the broth comes to a boil again, it will throw off a little more scum, and this should also be removed if you want it perfectly clear. After this, add the dumplings, though it is best to cook these in water or thin broth separately, as the broth becomes less clear and diminishes somewhat by cooking them in it. When serving the broth, you can add a piece of white sugar, which gives it a pleasant flavor. This portion will require approximately 2-3 hours of cooking.
