## Ingredients

- Five pounds brown sugar
- One gallon raw cider vinegar
- Peaches
- Whole cloves
- A piece of cinnamon
- Peach stones (optional)

## Directions

1. Take 5 pounds brown sugar and one gallon raw cider vinegar; boil strongly for 30 minutes, constantly skimming until clear.
2. Throw the peaches into boiling water, immediately remove, and rub the skin off quickly with a flannel cloth.
3. Add some whole cloves and a piece of cinnamon to the boiling syrup.
4. If the stones are in the peaches, add them to the syrup and cook for 15 to 20 minutes; if the stones have been removed, place the peaches in jars and pour the hot syrup over them.
5. Place a small saucer or plate over them to keep them submerged in the syrup and tie them.
6. After four days, check them; if necessary, cook the syrup again and pour hot over them.
7. Store in a cool place while the weather is warm to prevent them from becoming sour.
