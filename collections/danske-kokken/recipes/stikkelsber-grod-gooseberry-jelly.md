## Ingredients

- Gooseberries
- Water
- Sugar
- Cinnamon
- Corn-Starch (if needed)

## Directions

1. If the berries are unripe and very sour, they should stand a while and infuse in hot water, which is then strained off and fresh water placed on the fire.
2. For 2 quarts berries take 3 quarts water.
3. The berries must be cooked well out and often stirred from the bottom, after which the jelly is made with sugar and cinnamon.
4. Light powdered sugar can well be used for gooseberry jelly, but white sugar gives finer taste and prettier appearance.
5. Alternatively, the berries can be cooked without letting them extract first; more water is taken for the same portion of berries, and as it thereby becomes too thin, it is smoothed with a couple of spoonfuls of corn-starch dissolved in cold water.
6. This saves both berries and sugar.
7. A third way is to take more berries and not let them cook quite to jelly, but strain them when it is like a purée.
8. The juice is then placed over again, made with sugar and cinnamon, and smoothed with corn-starch.
9. Gooseberries kept in bottles can also be used for jelly, but always need a thickening of corn-starch as they lose the jelly-like quality by being kept.
