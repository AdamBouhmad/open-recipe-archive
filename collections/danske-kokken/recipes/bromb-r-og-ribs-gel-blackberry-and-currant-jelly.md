## Ingredients

- Blackberries
- Red currant juice, equal weight to blackberry juice
- 1/2 kg sugar (or corresponding weight to combined juices)
- 1 1/4 dl water

## Directions

1. Since blackberries are quite sweet, the best jelly is obtained by mixing blackberry and currant juice, half of each.
2. Place the blackberries in a pot and set over heat until they begin to release their juices well; then lift the pot off and crush the berries with a spoon before pouring into a scalded and tightly wrung jelly cloth.
3. The next day, weigh the juice and mix with an equal weight of completely clear currant juice without sugar.
4. After the sugar has been weighed and cooked as mentioned in No. 93, pour the juice into it: cook and skim for 10 minutes.
5. Fill the jelly into cups, which are tied up when it is partially cold and has begun to set.
6. The pulp remaining in the cloth is pressed through a very coarse sieve and used for Russian marmalade.
