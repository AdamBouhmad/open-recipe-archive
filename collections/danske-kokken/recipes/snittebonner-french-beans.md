## Ingredients

- French beans
- Water
- Salt
- Butter
- Wheat flour
- Cream or milk
- Parsley
- Sugar
- Nutmeg

## Directions

1. Using the side of a knife, take hold of the stem and you can draw out a long thread all the way to the tip of the bean. This is called stringing the beans. Thereafter cut them on the bias, quite fine; cook them for a short time in water without salt, then press them dry in a sieve. They should not cook for more than just a short time, as they must not fall apart. Melt butter, stir in wheat flour and spread it with cream or milk and let it cook to a smooth sauce, after which the French beans get a brief cooking in this with a little chopped parsley, a piece of sugar, a little nutmeg and finally salt. A good spoonful of butter, half an ounce of wheat flour and a small pint of cream is approximately sufficient for a deep plate full of firmly pressed beans.
