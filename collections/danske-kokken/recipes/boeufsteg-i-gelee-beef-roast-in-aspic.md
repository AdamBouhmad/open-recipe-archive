## Ingredients

- Beef, hung several days after slaughter
- Salt
- Pepper
- Grated onion
- Flour
- Butter
- Mustard
- Tarragon vinegar
- Vinegar of preserved lemons
- Gelatine
- Egg yolks
- Egg whites
- Lemon or lemon juice

## Directions

1. Cut beef into finger-thick but small pieces and beat them slightly
2. Season with salt, pepper, grated onion and a little flour
3. Heat the roasting pan and add a good amount of butter in proportion to the meat
4. When it begins to brown, place the meat with the seasoned side down and fry briskly, being careful not to burn it
5. Season the other side similarly, turn when the underside is brown, and fry briskly until the meat is cooked and red inside, but not bloody
6. Remove the meat and set aside to cool
7. Put the pan back on the heat with the sauce, stirring in a little mixed mustard, a little tarragon vinegar and a little vinegar of preserved lemons until it is well-flavored but not sour
8. Stir well until it boils and becomes smooth, then pour through a sieve onto a not entirely flat dish and place the meat in it
9. Make an aspic as for roast in aspic, but without vinegar and laurel leaves
10. When this aspic has become so cold that it begins to set, pour it over the beef roast
11. When serving, decorate with egg yolks and whites laid in strips on top of the aspic, as well as with a little chopped lemon or lemon juice
