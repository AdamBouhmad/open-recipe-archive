## Ingredients

- 2.5 to 3 kg beef from the sirloin
- Butter
- Claret or Palmin
- Onion slices
- Salt
- Pepper
- Water or gravy or broth
- Coloring

## Directions

1. The meat must have hung at least 1 week after slaughter
2. Cut the meat with the grain into thumb-thick slices, then cut across into 2 to 3 pieces
3. Brown a good portion of onion slices in butter, claret or Palmin on the frying pan
4. While this is happening, give each piece of beef a blow with a meat hammer
5. Scrape the crisp, brown onions off the pan
6. Place the pan back over the heat and on this dry, hot pan lay the beef to quickly turn it on the other side and then immediately remove from the pan
7. Brown a good slice of butter on the pan and place the beef in it, sprinkled with salt and pepper, to brown quickly on both sides
8. Place the beef in a strip on a hot plate and distribute the browned onions on the pieces
9. Deglaze the pan with a little water, gravy or broth so the meat juices can be loosened
10. Stir a little coloring and salt and about 1/4 kg cold butter into this gravy
11. After it boils, pour it over the beef on the plate
