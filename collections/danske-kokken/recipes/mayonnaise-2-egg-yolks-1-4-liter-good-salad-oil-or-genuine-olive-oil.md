## Ingredients

- Egg yolks
- Salt
- Salad oil or olive oil
- Lemon juice or tarragon vinegar
- Water
- Ground white pepper
- Vinegar

## Directions

1. Pour the egg yolks, well separated from the whites, into a large bowl and stir with a wooden spoon, adding a tiny amount of salt.
2. Stir the yolks constantly in the same direction until they become a silky light yellow mass.
3. Only then begin to add the oil drop by drop under constant stirring, occasionally adding a few drops of lemon juice or tarragon vinegar.
4. If the mayonnaise becomes too thick, thin it with a little water, which often also helps to bring the mayonnaise together if it shows a tendency to separate.
5. If it does separate, take a fresh egg yolk with a little salt and stir well; gradually stir in the failed mayonnaise.
6. Season with ground white pepper, vinegar, and salt to taste.
7. The mayonnaise can be colored red with ground lobster roe and green by adding a little juice from ground spinach, squeezed out in a cloth.
8. In winter it can be made the day before; in summer it should preferably be made the same day and kept on ice until needed.
9. Duck eggs can be used.
