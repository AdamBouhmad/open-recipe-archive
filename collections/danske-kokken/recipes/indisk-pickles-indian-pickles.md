## Ingredients

- 5 l (5 pots) vinegar
- 125 gr (1/4 lb) ground ginger
- 125 gr (1/4 lb) mustard (Colman's Mustard)
- 60 gr (12 drams) whole black pepper
- 50 gr (10 drams) long pepper
- 10 gr (2 drams) Cayenne pepper
- 6 cloves garlic
- A little Indian saffron to give it a suitable yellow color, or 2 tablespoons curry
- Small carrots (scraped)
- Small cucumbers (dried)
- Sugar peas (ribboned and cut into smaller pieces)
- String beans (ribboned and cut into smaller pieces)
- Pearl beans (ribboned and cut into smaller pieces)
- Cauliflower (divided into small florets)
- Pearl onions (peeled)
- Shallots (peeled)
- Red onions (peeled)
- Celery (peeled and cut into pieces)
- Nasturtium seeds
- A medium-sized white cabbage head
- Salt

## Directions

1. Place the spices in a larger jar and pour the boiling vinegar over them.
2. Cover the jar and let it stand for the next day to be tied shut.
3. As the vegetables mentioned in the previous recipe appear during the course of the summer, rinse them in a little cold vinegar and dry them in a cloth before placing them in the jar.
4. For the cabbage, cut a medium-sized white cabbage head into strips and sprinkle this cabbage together with the chopped cauliflower with salt and let it stand in a dish for a couple of days; then pour off the brine and press the cabbage dry in a cloth before filling it into the jar.
5. When you have collected what you want for the pickles jar, pour off the vinegar and mix the vegetables together well as they are placed in smaller glasses or jars.
6. Give the vinegar a boil and pour it boiling over the vegetables in the glasses, which, as soon as the vinegar is cold, are tied shut either with bladders, parchment paper, or, in the case of the smaller glasses, corked and sealed.
7. There must be plenty of vinegar over the vegetables; if, after it has boiled, there is not enough, some more must be boiled, which is added spices in proportion to the amount of vinegar.
8. This pickles can keep for years, but must stand for 1 year before being used; otherwise the boiling of the vinegar must be repeated a couple of times to soften the vegetables.
