## Ingredients

- goose breasts
- salt
- sugar
- ground saltpeter
- thin cloth

## Directions

1. Remove the breasts from the geese and rub well with salt mixed with a little sugar and ground saltpeter.
2. Lay them in a barrel skin-side down on the bottom, with each successive layer skin-side up.
3. Place a weight on top and let them stand for 5 to 6 days, turning them daily so the top layer moves to the bottom.
4. When ready to smoke, sew the breast together like a sausage and sew it into a very thin cloth.
5. Hang in smoke for 5 to 6 days or send to a smokehouse.
6. Serve the smoked goose breast cut into thin slices with vegetables.
7. The carcasses from which the breast is removed are excellent for cooking with dried peas, cabbage, and similar dishes; eat the meat along with it. Goose carcasses can be smoked and kept for later in the winter.
