## Ingredients

- 2 male pheasants
- Fresh bacon for larding
- Butter
- Flour
- Cream
- Milk
- Game stock or soup
- Salt
- Sugar
- Coloring
- Beef extract or Maggi bouillon cubes
- Bread for base
- Tinfoil and white paper for decoration
- Celery or herb salad

## Directions

1. Choose the male pheasant for its beautiful plumage
2. Hang the pheasant for several days before preparation as its meat is naturally dry
3. Pluck as other poultry, but keep the head, neck, wings, and tail to use as decoration around the roasted bird; remove these parts after plucking
4. Singe, remove innards, and dry both inside and outside with a cloth
5. Prepare as directed for poultry
6. Lard the breast and thighs with fine strips of fresh bacon
7. Brown in butter in a pot along with the giblets and heart
8. Make a sauce from browned butter and flour, diluted with cream, milk, and game stock or soup, seasoned with salt, a little sugar, and coloring, and pour over the bird so it is half covered
9. Cook gently over low heat for about 1 hour, turning the bird frequently and basting regularly
10. Strain the sauce and add a little cold butter, a few spoonfuls of raw cream, and if needed, beef extract or Maggi bouillon cubes
11. Prepare a bread base carved from rye or wheat bread with the same circumference as the bird and about 5 centimeters high; hollow slightly in the center where the bird will rest and cover with tinfoil and white paper cut into fringe around the edge
12. Insert a decorative skewer through the beak and neck and fix in the base
13. Attach the wings and tail in their respective places in the same manner
14. Slice the pheasant breast thinly and arrange on the base in its original form
15. Cover the pheasant with a spoonful of sauce and garnish with celery or herb salad
