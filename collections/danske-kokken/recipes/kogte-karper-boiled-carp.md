## Ingredients

- 1½ to 2 kg carp per portion
- Salt
- Whole peppercorns
- Bay leaves
- Parsley
- Vinegar (3¾ dl or 1½ pint per 2 to 3 carp)
- Water

## Directions

1. Clean the carp, scraping the scales lightly and leaving them on. The fish may be cooked whole or split, with bones remaining on one half.
2. After thorough rinsing, sprinkle the carp with salt and let stand for one hour.
3. Place in a casserole and pour over boiling vinegar (3¾ dl or 1½ pint per 2 to 3 carp). Turn the fish regularly and let stand for a good quarter hour.
4. Add whole peppercorns, a couple of bay leaves, a bunch of parsley, and enough water to cook the fish.
5. Bring to a rapid boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer for 3 quarters of an hour to 1 hour, depending on the size of the fish.
6. Serve with cold cream and horseradish sauce, melted butter, and boiled potatoes. Garnish with boiled crayfish, lemon slices, and parsley.
