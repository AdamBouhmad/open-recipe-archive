## Ingredients

- Rhubarb stalks
- 125 gr (1/4 lb) powdered sugar per 1 liter (1 pot) juice
- 2 small teaspoons salicylic acid
- Water

## Directions

1. This syrup is quite excellent and also the cheapest available. Since the stalks contain much acidity, they can be boiled 3 times, and yet the third batch of syrup is good and long-lasting. At the end of May or beginning of June is when the stalks are most juicy.
2. First Boiling: Cut 50 good thick stalks into 8 centimeter (1/2 quarter) long pieces and rinse well. Cover the bottom of the preserving pot with 1/2 liter (1/2 pot) of boiling water to prevent the stalks from burning. Boil them completely under regular stirring and over very low heat. Pour the mass into a stretched jelly cloth until the juice has partially run off after 1/2 hour.
3. Second Boiling: Put the rhubarb mass back in the pot together with 2 liters (2 pots) of boiling water and boil for 10 minutes, then strain in the cloth again until the juice has almost completely run off.
4. Third Boiling: This time add 1.5 liters (1.5 pots) of boiling water, and boil the mass again for 10 minutes before straining in the cloth, so that the last bit of juice can run off.
5. Now you can, if you wish, mix all 3 batches of juice and boil them together, but the first drained juice is decidedly the strongest and should be preserved separately. Only 125 gr (1/4 lb) sugar is added to each liter (pot) of juice, which is boiled and skimmed for 10 minutes.
6. Dissolve 2 small teaspoons of salicylic acid in some of the warm juice and distribute throughout the entire batch.
7. The salicylic acid must not be omitted. Pour the juice hot into bottles which are tied immediately with parchment paper.
8. Rhubarb syrup is a refreshing summer drink and can be made as a good dessert at little expense. Even after the stalks have been boiled 3 times, water can be added again and they can boil for 10 minutes before straining in the cloth and hanging until the next day. The juice that runs through can be made into rhubarb pudding and if necessary mixed with a few drops of red fruit coloring.
9. From ordinary good large stalks, about 14 bottles of good syrup can be obtained from this amount.
