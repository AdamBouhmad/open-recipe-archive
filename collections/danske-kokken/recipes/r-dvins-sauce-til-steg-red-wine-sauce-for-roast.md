## Ingredients

- Butter
- 2 tablespoons wheat flour
- Meat broth
- Skimmings from the roast
- Red wine
- Chopped onions
- Chopped anchovies
- Stirred mustard
- Pickled oyster sauce
- Pepper
- Soy sauce

## Directions

1. Brown a piece of butter in a roasting pan, but not too strongly
2. Add 2 tablespoons wheat flour and rub it out with the back of a spoon
3. Continue moving it from one spot in the pan to another so it does not rest long enough to burn
4. Keep the heat from being too strong under the pan
5. When it has acquired a nice brown color, gradually add meat broth and skimmings from the roast
6. The broth does not need to be very strong if you have skimmings
7. When this is mixed a few times but still too thick, add red wine until it becomes the desired thickness
8. Add chopped onions, chopped anchovies, a little stirred mustard, pickled oyster sauce to taste, and a little pepper
9. Cook all this well and then strain it
10. Place the sauce back in the pan to heat it and add a little soy sauce
11. If the roasting pan is only iron and not well mixed with steel, do not keep the wine in the pan, but when the sauce is mixed with broth, place it in a small tinned copper pan or glazed pot and prepare it there
12. This sauce is suitable for beef roast and can also be used for mutton roast; for goose, duck, and ham roast use more mustard
