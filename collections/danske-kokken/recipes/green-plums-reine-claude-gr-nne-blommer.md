## Ingredients

- fully developed but unripe green plums
- powdered sugar equal in weight to the plums
- half a measure of brandy per pound of original fruit weight

## Directions

1. The plums must be fully developed but not ripe.
2. After wiping the plums, cast them into boiling water but remove them as soon as they float on top and place in a sieve to drain.
3. Cook a thick sugar syrup with powdered sugar equal in weight to the plums.
4. Place the plums in the syrup, then immediately remove the pot from heat and let the plums rest in the liquid until the next day.
5. The next day, remove the plums and reduce the syrup until it becomes even, then pour over the plums and let cool.
6. Repeat this process on the third day and again on the fourth day.
7. On the fourth day, place the plums in the jar for storage and cook the syrup until it is even and thick, then set aside to cool.
8. When cold, stir half a measure of brandy per pound of the fruit's original weight into the syrup, adding it by spoonfuls with constant stirring in the same direction.
9. Pour the syrup over the plums and store in the usual manner.
