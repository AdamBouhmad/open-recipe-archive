## Ingredients

- One pork liver of 2 pounds
- 2 pounds offal (heart, etc.)
- 2 pounds panade (soaked and seasoned bread)
- 1/2 pound meat without sinews and skin
- 1 pint sweet cream
- Salt
- Nutmeg and white pepper in equal quantities, ground and mixed together

## Directions

1. After all sinews and membranes are removed, cut the liver and offal into square pieces of one inch size.
2. Melt the offal in a frying pan over gentle heat; mix salt and seasonings with the liver; stir everything together for 3 minutes over the heat and then set aside to cool.
3. Place the meat first 30 minutes in boiling water, then in cold water and cut into small cubes.
4. Pound the liver and offal, press through a steel sieve, then pound in a mortar into a fine mass together with the panade.
5. Add the cream and eggs little by little.
6. Season carefully with salt and spices; finally mix the meat cubes into the whole.
7. Fill the finished farce either into sausage casings and treat in the usual manner, after which it is eaten cold; or it can also be placed in a large, round or oblong mold that has been previously buttered and cooked evenly for 2 hours, after which the farce is set aside to cool and the mold is turned. Cut into slices and eaten cold.
