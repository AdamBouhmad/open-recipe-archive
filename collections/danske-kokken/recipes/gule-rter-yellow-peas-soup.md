## Ingredients

- Approximately 1/2 pot or 1/4 pound peas per person
- Cold water
- Peeled small onions
- 8 pounds meat or salt meat
- 5 pots water
- Celery, parsley roots, whole peeled onions
- Peeled apple pieces
- Chopped thyme (optional)
- Toasted bread cubes
- Salt

## Directions

1. The evening before cooking, remove damaged peas and soak overnight in cold water
2. Place peas in a pot with enough cold water to stand about 4 inches above them
3. Add peeled small onions but no salt
4. Cook uncovered over moderate heat until peas begin to shed their shells, skimming but being careful not to remove the onions
5. When peas are soft enough to mash, stir with a flat spoon until they become like porridge
6. Place 8 pounds meat or salt meat over heat with 5 pots water
7. Boil, skim, and strain the broth as usual
8. Thin the peas with the broth until they reach the consistency of gruel, adding boiling water if needed
9. Bring to a good boil with constant stirring to prevent scorching
10. Serve as hot as possible with the meat
11. For strained yellow peas, press through a sieve and thin with broth until smooth, then cook in a pot with chopped celery, parsley roots, whole peeled onions, and peeled apple pieces
12. Cook over gentle heat with constant stirring until roots are tender
13. Serve as hot as possible with the meat and toasted bread cubes
