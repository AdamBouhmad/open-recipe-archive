## Ingredients

- turkey
- salt
- pepper
- liver
- gizzard
- heart
- bread crumbs
- eggs
- currants
- sugar
- butter
- anchovies
- lemons
- flour
- bouillon
- cream
- potatoes

## Directions

1. Slaughter the turkey 3 to 4 days before use.
2. Pluck, singe, and cut up the turkey.
3. Remove the innards and wash thoroughly.
4. Blanch in boiling water for 2 minutes, then wash in cold water.
5. Rub inside with salt and pepper.
6. Place the cleaned liver under one wing and the cleaned, prepared gizzard under the other, unless they are to be used for stuffing.
7. Tie the legs together over the rump.
8. For bread stuffing: remove the crust from an old French bread, soak in sweet milk, squeeze out the milk, let stand for one hour, then mix with 3 eggs, 2 tablespoons washed currants, salt, pepper, sugar, half a quarter pound of softened butter, and finely chopped liver.
9. For alternative stuffing: scrape the liver, gizzard, and heart, chop 4 anchovies, 2 lemons, and a French bread soaked in water, add 1 tablespoon flour, pepper, salt, and 2 eggs.
10. Lard the turkey with 3 fine strips on the breast and a star on the thigh, or cover with white writing paper buttered during roasting.
11. Roast for 1.5 to 2 hours depending on size in light, browned butter with frequent basting, being careful not to burn it.
12. Skim the fat from the sauce and thicken with flour, thin with bouillon, strain, and stir in a little cream.
13. Arrange the turkey with the breast up after removing the strings.
14. Serve with browned potatoes.
