## Ingredients

- Pears
- 1 pound white sugar per pound of pears
- 1/2 pint water
- Cinnamon stick
- Lemon peel or finely ground and sifted ginger at 1 tablespoon per pound of pears
- Lemon juice (if pears are very sweet)
- 1/4 gill vinegar per pound of pears (if for serving with roast)

## Directions

1. If unripe, put the pears in boiling water and cook slightly until they begin to soften
2. Remove, peel, and cut into 4 pieces, removing all the core
3. Weigh the pears
4. Cook sugar into a syrup with 1/2 pint water
5. Add the pears and cook until tender
6. Pour out and cover with a cloth
7. After several days, pour the pears into a coarse sieve so the juice can drain, then place in a pot
8. Cook the juice with a piece of cinnamon and lemon peel or ginger, skim, and pour cold over the pears
9. The next day cover with paper dipped in brandy and seal
10. If pears are ripe, do not cook them first in water but peel, cut into pieces or leave whole if small, and cook longer in the syrup
11. If for serving with roast, add 1/4 gill vinegar per pound of pears
