## Ingredients

- Beef tenderloin or neck piece, not freshly slaughtered but aged several days
- Butter
- Finely chopped red onion
- Pepper
- Gravy or stock

## Directions

1. Cut the meat into appropriately sized pieces and place them on a dry, strongly heated pan.
2. Fry the pieces at brisk heat so they remain juicy inside, and as they are done, place them on the serving dish and pour over them the juice that has run from the meat.
3. When all pieces are fried, add butter to the pan and stir well with a spoon to loosen the gravy that still clings to it.
4. Add finely chopped red onion to this, cook it a little with it, then add more gravy and pepper.
5. Alternatively, when the meat is cut into appropriately thick slices, beat these, dip them in chopped red onion and a little pepper, and place them on a pan in which you have browned some butter.
6. Fry the slices over a brisk heat, turn them as soon as they begin to brown, and under no circumstances fry them so much that the meat becomes dry.
7. Stir up the sauce on the pan with gravy and pour it over the pieces.
8. Serve with boiled, peeled potatoes; sometimes with a fried egg on top.
9. Calculate 3 portions from one pound of meat.
