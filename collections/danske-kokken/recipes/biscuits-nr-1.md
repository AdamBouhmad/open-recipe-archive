## Ingredients

- 1 pound flour
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 pound fat
- 1 quart cold milk

## Directions

1. Sift 1 pound flour with 1 tablespoon baking powder and 1 teaspoon salt.
2. Rub 1/4 pound fat quickly into the flour in small pieces.
3. Dust the dough pan and table with flour.
4. Pour 1 quart cold milk into the dough and blend as quickly as possible.
5. Turn the dough onto the table, roll out 1 inch thick, and cut the small biscuits into squares and place on a buttered baking pan.
6. Bake in a hot oven. All of this must be done with the greatest haste so the biscuits can get into the oven before the gas from the baking powder and milk escapes. The dough is made light by this gas, which forces itself up through it, but if the biscuits are not baked before the gas escapes, they will be heavy.
7. When half-baked—after about 10 minutes—open the oven door and brush a little milk over them, then finish baking.
