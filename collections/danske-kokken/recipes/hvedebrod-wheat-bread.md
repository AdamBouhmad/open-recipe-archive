## Ingredients

- 7 pounds sifted flour
- 1 quart lukewarm water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 0.5 gill yeast or 2 cents compressed yeast
- additional lukewarm water as needed

## Directions

1. Put 7 pounds sifted flour in a deep vessel
2. Make a hole in the center
3. Add 1 quart lukewarm water, 1 tablespoon salt, 1 teaspoon sugar, and 0.5 gill yeast or 2 cents compressed yeast dissolved in a little lukewarm water
4. Have more lukewarm water ready and use as much as needed to make a fairly soft dough
5. Knead thoroughly with both hands; the softer the dough, the better the bread, as long as it releases from the hands and vessel
6. When even and good, sprinkle a little flour on it, lay a folded cloth over the vessel, and let it stand four or five hours in a warm place to rise
7. If the dough was stirred in the evening, it can stand overnight if the room is warm
8. When risen enough, turn out onto a floured breadboard and knead for about fifteen minutes, using enough flour so it does not stick to the board or hands
9. Put it back in the bread pan and let it rise fully again, which happens faster than the first time
10. Divide the dough into 4 breads, put in baking pans, let stand fifteen minutes, then bake in a hot oven
11. The recipe makes about 8 pounds of bread; divided into 4 pieces, bake for one hour
12. Test with a splint or straw; if it comes out clean, the bread is done
13. It should also have the proper color
