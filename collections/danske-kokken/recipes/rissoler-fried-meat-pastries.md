## Ingredients

- Butter dough
- hash or a mixture of cooked picked fish
- bread crumbs
- cold melted butter
- a small cooked onion
- pepper
- salt
- egg yolks
- water
- egg
- bread crumbs for coating
- clarified butter for frying
- fried parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. Roll out butter dough fairly thin and cut it into square pieces about 2 inches on each side. Place a teaspoon of hash or a similar amount of a mixture of cooked picked fish, one third as much grated bread crumbs, a little cold melted butter, a small cooked onion that is chopped, pepper, salt, and 2 egg yolks in the middle of each piece. When the rissole is filled, brush the edges with water. Wrap the dough around the filling, making sure the dough closes tightly at the edges. Brush the rissolers with egg, roll in crushed bread, fry in clarified butter and turn during frying until they become golden brown. Place them on paper or a sieve so most of the fat can drip off. Arrange them dry, garnished with fried parsley. Usually place a napkin on the platter under them.
