## Ingredients

- Suckling pig, at least 6 weeks old
- Lard
- Cracker
- Wheat bread or flour
- Water
- Butter
- Pepper
- Thyme
- Marjoram
- Sage
- Parsley

## Directions

1. Select a pig at least 6 weeks old and have the butcher clean it completely inside and out.
2. Slit the skin crosswise over the back from head to tail; bend the knees and sew the udders together at the tips behind the head.
3. Rub it with lard all over and place a cracker in its mouth.
4. Make a stuffing from fresh wheat bread (without the crust) or flour (salted as for bread dough) and stir into a stiff dough with boiling water; when this is cooked, break it up and add 1 pound butter, a little pepper, thyme, marjoram and sage.
5. Fill the pig with this dough and sew it shut.
6. Place it on its knees in a roasting pan with enough water to baste it continuously until done.
7. Turn it during roasting as you would a turkey.
8. Make a sauce from the hot fat in the pan (strained through a fine sieve) and thicken with flour; add boiling water if needed, and stir in a tablespoon of finely chopped parsley.
9. Roasting takes about two hours, more or less.
10. Some use finely chopped lungs, liver and heart, and a pair of eggs in the dough before making the stuffing.
