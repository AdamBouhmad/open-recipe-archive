## Ingredients

- Ripe grey pears, fig pears, bergamots, or common cooking pears
- Water
- Sugar (for inferior varieties)

## Directions

1. Peel the pears preferably with a nickel-plated fruit knife, dipping it regularly in water
2. Keep the pears whole, cutting off only the blossom end while preferably leaving the stem
3. Cook them tender in water until they can be easily pierced with a straw
4. Remove the pears from the water as soon as they are cooked and place them on a sieve to drain before placing them on the trays with the stem facing upward
5. The thermometer should show 50°R at this point
6. When the pears are so dry that they are somewhat tough on the outside and can withstand pressing without bursting, place them briefly under a press or use a pear clamp consisting of 2 boards connected with a hinge
7. After the fruit is partially pressed flat, place them back in the oven and continue drying until no more juice can be pressed from them
8. Pack the completely cold, dried pears tightly in boxes lined with parchment paper
