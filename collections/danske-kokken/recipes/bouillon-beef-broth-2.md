## Ingredients

- 1 pot water per 1 pound beef
- Beef with bones (such as shank, brisket, leg, breast, etc.)
- Salt
- Carrots
- Parsley roots
- Celery
- Onions (peeled)
- Peppercorns
- Ginger (small piece)
- Candy sugar (small piece)
- Twine for bundling herbs

## Directions

1. Rinse the beef and place it in a pot with cold water, allowing 1 pot of water per pound of beef. The water should stand slightly above the meat, and the pot should be nearly full with a tightly fitting lid.
2. Bring the broth to a gentle boil over low heat until it begins to foam. Add a little salt, which helps bring the foam to the surface more readily.
3. Keep the broth simmering under a closed lid with even heat, skimming frequently until all impurities are removed.
4. When clean, add several well-rinsed whole soup vegetables: carrots, parsley roots, and celery, as well as a bundle of herbs tied with twine.
5. Add peeled onions, a few whole peppercorns, a small piece of ginger, and a small piece of candy sugar.
6. Continue simmering the broth over even heat with gentle cooking until the vegetables are tender, then remove them. Continue cooking the meat until it is completely tender.
7. When the broth is finished cooking, strain it and let it rest quietly for a quarter hour, then skim off the fat.
8. Serve the bouillon with an egg yolk and toasted bread slices, or use it as a base for other soups and sauces.
