## Ingredients

- one good meaty pork head
- 1/2 to 1 kg (1 to 2 pounds) lean pork shoulder or tenderloin
- salt
- whole pepper
- 2 handfuls salt
- bay leaves
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Split the pork head and clean it; remove the brains, eyes, and tongue
2. Clean the ears carefully or cut them off entirely if preferred
3. Cut away the snout cartilage to better drain the blood, and clean the snout
4. Soak the head in water overnight, then rinse it several times in cold water
5. Scald it in boiling water and it is then ready for cooking
6. Place it over the fire in cold water with salt and whole pepper; skim the broth well when it boils
7. Cook 1/2 to 1 kg of lean pork shoulder or tenderloin together with the head; this will be distributed in the aspic
8. When the head is cooked so much that the meat loosens from the bones, remove it
9. Spread a cloth wrung out in boiling water in a mold or deep dish
10. Cut the rind from the head and use it to line the mold
11. Cut the remaining meat into smaller pieces, distribute the fat and lean meat, and sprinkle salt and ground pepper between layers
12. Bind the cloth tightly together with a band and place a lid with weight on top; the lid should not be larger than to fit down into the mold, as the aspic shrinks greatly during pressing
13. The next day, remove the cloth and place the aspic in a cold, mild brine made of 2 handfuls salt, some bay leaves, whole pepper and a little vinegar, and pour 2 L (2 pots) of boiling water over it
14. When serving, cut the aspic into slices and eat with vinegar, pepper, mustard and beets
15. The tongue can be boiled and added to the aspic, or else salted as a condiment
16. The head broth can be used for pea soup, kale soup, or brown cabbage soup, or boiled together with herbs and marrow bones and used in the household as economy broth
