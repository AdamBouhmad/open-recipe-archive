## Ingredients

- broth from previously prepared meat or pork
- 4 ox feet
- 1 pot water
- 3 quint ginger
- 3 quint pepper
- 3 quint allspice
- 1 pound peeled onions
- a little thyme and marjoram
- 3 pounds St. Croix sugar

## Directions

1. Use broth that has been used in winter for meat or pork. Boil it, skim it, and when clear, strain through a napkin.
2. Set 4 ox feet over heat and boil with 1 pot water, 3 quint ginger, 3 quint pepper, 3 quint allspice, 1 pound peeled onions and a little thyme and marjoram.
3. When the feet are tender, remove them, and the soup should then amount to about 1 pot.
4. Soften 3 pounds St. Croix sugar in water and boil until it becomes brown like caramel.
5. Dilute with 2 pots of the cooked broth and boil together with the soup from the ox feet for approximately 3 hours with 3 quint mace.
6. Then strain the mixture through a very fine sieve, pour into clean, dry bottles, cork them and keep for later use.
