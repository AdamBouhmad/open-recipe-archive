## Ingredients

- 2 cups good, not very salty melted butter
- 2 cups flour
- A little Cayenne pepper
- A little salt
- 1 gallon nearly boiling milk
- 1 quart fresh oysters
- Water

## Directions

1. In 2 cups of good, not very salty melted butter, stir 2 cups of flour, a little Cayenne pepper and a little salt.
2. Pour in one gallon of nearly boiling milk, a little at a time; stir well so that the flour does not lump.
3. Pour a little water over 1 quart of fresh oysters, place it over a good fire and skim clean; strain this water with the oyster liquid into the soup, then rinse the oysters in cold water to free them from all small shells.
4. The soup gets a boil and is then immediately removed from the fire so that it does not curdle, and the oysters are added to it.
5. Everything should be at hand before you begin, as you must constantly stir in the soup.
