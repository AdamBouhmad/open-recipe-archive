## Ingredients

- 5 l (5 pots) vinegar, one of which should preferably be tarragon vinegar
- 125 gr (1/4 lb) ground ginger
- 250 gr (1/2 lb) English mustard (Colman's Mustard)
- 125 gr (1/4 lb) salt
- 25 gr (5 drams) ground white pepper
- 10 gr (2 drams) ground Cayenne pepper
- A little Indian saffron to give it a beautiful yellowish color or 2 tablespoons curry
- Small carrots (scraped)
- Small cucumbers (dried)
- Sugar peas (ribboned and cut into smaller pieces)
- String beans (ribboned and cut into smaller pieces)
- Pearl beans (ribboned and cut into smaller pieces)
- Cauliflower (divided into small florets)
- Pearl onions (peeled)
- Shallots (peeled)
- Red onions (peeled)
- Celery (peeled and cut into pieces)
- Nasturtium seeds

## Directions

1. Mix all these spices into some of the vinegar before adding the remaining vinegar.
2. Cover the jar with a lid or tie it shut with parchment paper and let it stand for 3 to 4 weeks, but it must be stirred regularly in the meantime.
3. Dry the fresh vegetables in a cloth and they must be completely dry before being placed in the vinegar, gradually as they appear during the course of the summer.
4. While the pickles are being prepared, stir frequently, and only when all the vegetables have been collected and laid down, distribute them in smaller jars or glasses, which are tied shut and stored.
5. As they are stored well, the raw vegetables become sufficiently soft.
