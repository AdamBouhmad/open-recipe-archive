## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg (1 pound) scraped fish for farce
- 3 chickens
- 1/2 kg (1 pound) flour for short pastry
- Butter
- Flour
- Chicken stock
- Tomato purée
- Madeira or Sherry
- Egg

## Directions

1. The pâté must be baked in a mold, either porcelain or a common metal form with a firm base.
2. For a metal mold, use short pastry for lining, as the pâté would otherwise be exposed to too strong heat.
3. Make the short pastry from 1/2 kg (1 pound) flour (see Nr. 21) and roll it out fairly thin.
4. Line the buttered mold with the pastry; it must be ample enough to hang 2 to 3 centimeters (1 inch) over the edge of the mold.
5. Lay a layer of fish farce (see Nr. 205), approximately 2 to 3 centimeters (1 inch) thick, over the short pastry on both the bottom and sides.
6. Boil 3 chickens until tender and remove the meat from the bones, placing it in the center of the mold.
7. Make a sauce from butter and flour, thinned with the chicken stock, add tomato purée to taste and Madeira or Sherry.
8. Pour enough sauce into the mold to roughly cover the meat.
9. Place a layer of farce on top and finally a layer of short pastry.
10. Fold the pastry hanging over the mold up onto the lid so it closes well.
11. Brush the lid with egg and bake the pâté for about one hour in a well-heated oven.
12. At serving, place the mold on a dish on which a folded napkin has been laid.
13. Conceal the mold either by fastening a napkin around it or using an embroidered band.
14. Prepare the sauce for the pâté at the same time you make the interior sauce to pour over the meat; thin it and add more tomato purée, Maggi's bouillon cubes, and wine if needed.
15. Small fish balls may be added to the sauce boat.
16. Champignons may be used instead of tomatoes.
