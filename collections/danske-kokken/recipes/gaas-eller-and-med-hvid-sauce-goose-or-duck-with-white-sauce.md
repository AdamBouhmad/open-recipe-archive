## Ingredients

- Goose or duck
- Fat from the bird
- 2 to 3 spoonfuls of fat
- 2 tablespoons flour without heaping
- Mustard
- Cayenne
- Apples cut in 4 pieces
- White root vegetables
- Sliced almonds (optional)

## Directions

1. Half-boil the goose while it is whole, then remove it and let it cool slightly
2. Chop it up on a board with a sharp knife and a hammer, first removing the legs and wings
3. Split the back and breast, and chop each of these parts into suitable pieces
4. Boil the soup when the chopped meat is returned to it; when it is tender, place it in a warm dish with a lid while the sauce is strained and prepared
5. Skim the fat well, take 2 to 3 spoonfuls and place on the bottom of the cleaned pot
6. Take 2 tablespoons of flour without heaping for a goose, stir it out in the fat and then fill it with the soup
7. If sour or sweet sauce is desired, it must be prepared in a tinned copper pot or enameled dish
8. Add mustard and a little cayenne, apples cut in 4 pieces, white root vegetables, and if desired, sliced almonds
9. If making the sauce without sweet and sour, make it stronger with seasonings, and use only root vegetables and meat balls
10. For duck, cook for 1.5 to 2 hours; for a whole goose, 2 to 3 hours or slightly less if very young
11. The bird should not be covered completely as it is better to spit-roast it; salt as you would other meat
