## Ingredients

- A good, meaty, fresh ham of 5-6 kg (10-12 lb)
- 1 kg (2 lb) salt
- 60 grams (12 quintal) saltpeter
- ½ kg (1 lb) powdered sugar
- A few cloves
- Peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 small onions
- 2 carrots
- 1 lemon, peeled and cut
- ½ liter (½ pint) good broth or soup
- 1 bottle red wine
- Breadcrumbs
- Powdered sugar
- Sago flour

## Directions

1. Remove the rind from a good, meaty, fresh ham of 5-6 kg and cut off the shank.
2. Place the ham on a trough or in a basin and rub it with a mixture of 1 kg salt, 60 grams saltpeter and ½ kg powdered sugar.
3. Let it stand for a week, but rub it daily with the brine that collects gradually.
4. Before roasting, wash it well and place it on the roasting pan with a few cloves, peppercorns, 1 bay leaf, 2 small onions, 2 carrots, 1 peeled and cut lemon, and ½ liter good broth or soup.
5. A ham of this size requires 3½ hours roasting and must, after being placed in the hot oven, be basted for the first two hours with 1 bottle of red wine and then sprinkled well with a mixture of breadcrumbs and powdered sugar.
6. If it does not become sufficiently crispy, sprinkle with more sugar.
7. Strain the broth, skim it and thicken it with sago flour.
8. Serve the ham hot with various vegetables or with macaroni, chestnuts, or purees.
