## Ingredients

- 32 pounds ripe berries, preferably yellow or white and clear
- 24 pounds powdered sugar
- Cold water
- Old drawn-off gooseberry wine, white French wine (Graves), or Sherry for topping up

## Directions

1. Crush the berries in a clean kettle or new bucket
2. Add 24 pounds powdered sugar and stir well together with the mashed berries
3. Pour the juice into a barrel that is well cleaned and sulfured
4. Rinse the berries with cold water until the juice comes out of them; pour this water also into the barrel, which should not be completely full so the wine has room to ferment
5. When the juice has finished fermenting and no more sound is heard in the barrel, fill it with old drawn-off gooseberry wine, white French wine (Graves), or Sherry and seal
6. Seal the bunghole and tap with clay
7. Around New Year's time the wine will be ready for drawing off into bottles
