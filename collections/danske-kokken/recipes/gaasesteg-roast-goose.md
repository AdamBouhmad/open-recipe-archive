## Ingredients

- 1 whole uncarved goose, about 5 to 5½ kg
- 5 to 6 peeled apples
- 125 grams scalded prunes, pitted
- salt
- pepper
- butter
- flour
- water
- Maggi bouillon cubes or meat extract
- parsley

## Directions

1. Clean the goose by carefully removing all feather follicles and singeing it over a gas or spirit flame.
2. Remove the head with neck, wings, feet, giblets, and heart for soup or ragout; reserve the liver for liver pâté.
3. Make a crosswise cut at the rump, large enough only to remove the innards.
4. Remove the giblets and place in cold salted water along with any fat picked from the intestines.
5. Wash the goose thoroughly in cold water, rub the inside with pepper and salt, and fill with the 5 to 6 apples cut into 4 pieces each, along with the 125 grams of scalded pitted prunes.
6. Sew the goose closed, rub the outside with salt, place in a roasting pan, and put in a hot oven for a few minutes before pouring a little boiling salted water over it.
7. To ensure crispy skin, baste the goose only at the beginning.
8. Roast for 2½ to 3 hours.
9. Half an hour before serving, strain the pan drippings through a sieve and return the goose to the oven with very little water in the pan.
10. For the sauce, brown butter, add flour and bake it, then thin with the strained pan drippings from which all fat has been carefully skimmed.
11. Season the sauce with color, salt, and Maggi bouillon cubes or meat extract, as the goose drippings are not very strong.
12. When serving, remove the thread and garnish the goose with parsley (and with Danish flags if Christmas goose).
13. Serve with roasted or boiled potatoes, red cabbage, asparagus, preserves, and the apples and prunes that were in the goose.
