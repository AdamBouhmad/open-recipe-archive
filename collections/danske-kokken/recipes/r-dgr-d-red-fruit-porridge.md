## Ingredients

- 4 pots juice
- 1/4 to 1/2 pound sugar
- Thickening agent: 4 1/2 ounces sago flour, or 1/2 pound rice flour, or 1 pound sago grains
- Cold sweet cream or creme for serving
- Vanilla sugar
- Optional: A small handful of scalded and finely crushed almonds (some bitter)

## Directions

1. For the best Rødgrød, use 3 pots of black currant juice and 1 pot of raspberry juice, placed over heat to boil.
2. When boiling, add the thickening agent (mixed with a little of the cold juice) to the pot and cook with continuous stirring for 6 to 8 minutes; cooking is shortest with sago flour.
3. Before ladling the porridge into large flat bowls, test if it is stiff enough by placing a teaspoon on a saucer and setting it in cold water.
4. If the porridge is too thin, add a little more thickening agent; if too thick, thin it with boiling water.
5. Serve with cold sweet cream or creme and sprinkled vanilla sugar.
6. Alternatively, add a small handful of scalded and finely crushed almonds, including some bitter ones.
7. Other options for juice: exclusively black currant (from 3 pounds berries) or 2 pounds cherries and 1 pound black currant. For cooking the fruit, use 1 pot of water for each pound of fruit; from 3 pounds of berries, 4 pots of juice are obtained after straining.
