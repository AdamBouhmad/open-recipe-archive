## Ingredients

- 3 pounds powdered sugar per pound of plums
- Half gill vinegar per pound of plums
- 1 tablespoon water per pound of plums
- Cinnamon
- Cloves

## Directions

1. For each pound of plums, take 3 pounds powdered sugar, which is put on the fire with half gill vinegar and 1 tablespoon water.
2. When it is melted, pour it over the plums, which have first been dried, pricked and placed in a crock.
3. After 3 to 4 days, during which the plums are poured each day into another crock so that the syrup can reach around them, set them over the fire where they are cooked until they are not sour by the stones, about three-quarters of an hour.
4. Then place them in a coarse strainer and from there in a crock which is filled half full with them.
5. Cook the syrup with cinnamon and cloves for a quarter hour or until it is smooth. Pour it warm over the plums, which are tied up the next day.
6. Plums can be preserved in the same way with half pound sugar and half gill vinegar per pound, but then less syrup is obtained.
