## Ingredients

- 6 pounds syrup
- 2 pounds honey
- 1 pound butter
- 18 quintal potassium carbonate, dissolved in a cup of cold water
- 8 pounds flour
- spices to taste

## Directions

1. Give the syrup and honey a good boil. To this add the butter. Dissolve the potassium carbonate in a cup of cold water and measure out the flour in a dish. When the syrup has cooled enough that you can hold your hand in it, pour it onto the flour, add the dissolved potassium carbonate, and stir the whole together into a dough, which stands in a crock for 5-6 days in a cold place. Immediately before baking, a little spice can be added. The oven must be well heated. The whole is baked as one large cake of appropriate thickness, which is cut into oblong, square small cakes when it is taken from the oven. A scalded almond can be placed in the middle of each cake.
