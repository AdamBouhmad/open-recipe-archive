## Ingredients

- Beef loin, 5 to 6 kg (10 to 12 pounds)
- Cold strained beef blood, 5 liters (5 pots)
- Vinegar, 1/2 liter (1/2 pot)
- Salt, a good handful
- Parsley for larding
- Cream, thin
- Butter for paper
- Paper

## Directions

1. The loin, which should preferably have hung for a few days, is prepared like a veal or lamb loin and placed in a marinade for a couple of days.
2. Place a loin of 5 to 6 kg in a trough and cover it with 5 liters of cold, strained beef blood mixed with 1/2 liter of vinegar and a good handful of salt.
3. In this marinade, which must completely cover the loin, it can stand during winter for approximately a week.
4. When ready to use, rinse and dry the loin well.
5. Remove the membranes and lard the roast thickly and place it in a hot oven.
6. Initially, baste it with gravy, soup, or water and later with thin cream, and to prevent it from becoming too brown, cover it with paper smeared with butter.
7. Roast for 2½ hours.
8. Make the sauce as game sauce (see Nr. 772).
9. Carve the loin and place it back in position as described for veal and lamb loin, garnish with salad and browned potatoes or glazed chestnuts.
10. Red currant and lingonberry jelly, as well as apple sauce and compote, are suitable accompaniments.
