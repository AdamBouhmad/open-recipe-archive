## Ingredients

- Apples
- Sugar
- Powdered sugar (1¼ kg to ½ kg of sieved pulp)
- Optional: vanilla or orange essence, candied orange peel, blanched and cut nuts, almonds, or walnuts

## Directions

1. Apples that are less good and not suitable for other uses may be employed.
2. Quarter the apples; remove the blossom, stem, and core, and place the fruit gradually in cold water until it is put in the pot to be cooked covered over very low heat so as not to scorch.
3. Press the cooked apples through a coarse sieve, weigh them, and return them to the pot to cook with sugar with constant stirring until it becomes a fairly firm marmalade.
4. The flavor may be varied by means of different additions such as vanilla or orange essence, or it may be cooked with finely cut candied orange peel, finely sliced lemon peel, blanched and cut nuts, almonds, or walnuts.
5. Spread out, let dry, and store as the preceding recipes.
