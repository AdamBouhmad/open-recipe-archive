## Ingredients

- Wild boar head
- Red wine
- Stock
- 2 onions
- 1 celery
- 2 carrots
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 lemon
- Whole peppercorns
- Juniper berries
- Butter
- Lemon juice
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Black peppercorns
- Green salad
- Stock (for reduction)
- Red currant or raspberry jelly

## Directions

1. Cut off the head at the shoulder blades as the neck is the greatest delicacy.
2. The hairs can either be singed or scalded off; then soak it overnight in cold water to draw out the blood.
3. Remove the eyes and clean the head very thoroughly and wrap it in thin cloth.
4. Boil for at least 3 hours in a marinade of red wine and stock with 2 chopped onions, 1 celery, 2 carrots, 2 bay leaves, 1 lemon sliced, some whole peppercorns and a little juniper berries.
5. When cooked, set aside and only when cold, remove from the soup, unwrap, and garnish for serving.
6. Cut the neck meat into slices, reassemble them, and arrange the head on a platter garnished with green salad.
7. Place a whole lemon in the snout and replace the eyes with 2 slices of hard-boiled eggs with a black peppercorn in each.
8. Mix fat and butter with lemon juice and pipe through a piping bag to create decorative figures on the head, which can also be covered with stiff stock and cut vegetables or simply wrapped with flowers stuck into the head.
9. Serve Cumberland Sauce on the side: reduce some of the soup in which the head was cooked until it becomes a strong stock, add the juice of 1 or 2 lemons along with the peel of 1 orange with the white skin well removed, bring to a boil, and stir in red wine, mustard and paprika to taste, strain and thicken with dissolved tapioca flour.
