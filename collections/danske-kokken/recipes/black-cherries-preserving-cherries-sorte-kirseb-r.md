## Ingredients

- large, ripe black cherries
- vinegar
- powdered sugar equal in weight to the berries
- cinnamon sticks
- whole cloves

## Directions

1. Remove the stones from large, ripe berries and place them in a terrine, covering with enough good vinegar to cover the berries.
2. Let sit overnight.
3. The next day, pour the berries into a colander so all vinegar drains off.
4. Weigh the berries and for each pound take 1 pound powdered sugar.
5. On the bottom of a preserving jar place, for the stated quantity, 4 drams whole cinnamon and 15 drams whole cloves.
6. Over this lay a 3-fingers-thick layer of powdered sugar, then a layer of cherries, then a layer of sugar, continuing in this manner with the top layer being a 4-fingers-thick sugar layer.
7. Tie the jar immediately with a bladder and place in a cool location.
8. The drained vinegar can be bottled and used in other ways, such as for sweet soup and similar.
