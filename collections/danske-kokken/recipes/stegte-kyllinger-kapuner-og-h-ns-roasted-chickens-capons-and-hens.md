## Ingredients

- chickens or capons or hens
- parsley leaves
- butter
- salt
- bouillon or water
- cream

## Directions

1. After the birds are plucked, singed, and the innards removed and feet cut off, wash and dry them.
2. Fill with picked parsley leaves and cold butter.
3. Brown butter lightly in a casserole and place the birds breast-side down.
4. When they have roasted somewhat and are golden brown, turn them, sprinkle a little salt on them, and pour a couple of spoonfuls of warm bouillon or boiling water over them.
5. Continue roasting for approximately another half hour, total roasting time about one hour.
6. Thicken the sauce with sweet cream and strain.
7. Serve with potatoes and cucumber salad.
8. For capons and young hens: lard with bacon or cover with bacon slices and roast over high, clear heat with constant basting with butter.
9. Skim the fat from the sauce, thicken with a little flour, thin with bouillon, and strain.
10. Garnish the platter with salad leaves and curly parsley.
