## Ingredients

- apples
- butter
- rusk or coffee bread
- powdered sugar
- cinnamon
- currant or cherry juice or water

## Directions

1. Peel and slice the apples not too thin, removing the cores
2. Finely grate rusk or coffee bread
3. Melt a small piece of butter in a stone pot and turn it so butter coats all sides
4. Spread a fairly thick layer of bread in the pot, followed by small butter pieces spaced apart
5. Lay a layer of apple slices as close together as possible, sprinkle with powdered sugar according to the apples' tartness and cinnamon
6. Pour currant or cherry juice around, or water if not using juice
7. Begin again with a good layer of bread and continue alternating
8. The top layer must be bread, covered with butter all around and again drizzled with juice or water
9. Bake in a moderately warm oven until apples can be pierced without resistance
10. Can be made without juice by simply pouring water over when the pot is full, or with rye bread
