## Ingredients

- Currant juice
- 3 pounds powdered sugar per quart of juice
- Brandy

## Directions

1. When the juice is prepared and measured, set it on the fire and boil and simmer well for 8 to 10 minutes.
2. Then add 3 pounds powdered sugar per quart of juice, boil and skim for 1 quarter hour, pour out and stand uncovered until the next day.
3. If thick juice has accumulated on top of the thin, remove this, put in a crock and can be used as thick simple juice, but preferably first in the winter.
4. Pour the thin juice into bottles, into which pour brandy, then cork them.
5. One can cook this juice with 3 pounds sugar per quart of juice, but then for it to be able to keep, it must boil half an hour with the sugar, and since the juice loses some in the longer cooking, little is gained by saving sugar here.
