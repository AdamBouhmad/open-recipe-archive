## Ingredients

- The best, red crabapples
- 12 pounds sugar per pound of fruit
- 1 small cup water per pound of fruit
- Juice of 1 lemon per 3 pounds of fruit

## Directions

1. Choose the best red crabapples. With the stems on, place them in the pot and pour warm water over them, enough to cover them.
2. Bring it to a boil and boil slowly until the skin cracks. Then cool, drain and peel them, then remove the cores from the top where the flower sits with a penknife.
3. Bring water and sugar to a boil for each pound of fruit (using 12 pounds sugar and 1 small cup water), and skim it well.
4. Then add the fruit and boil evenly under a lid until it is clear, soft and red.
5. Then take up the apples with a fish slice and spread them out on platters to cool and become firm.
6. To the syrup in the pot add the juice of 1 lemon for every 3 pounds of fruit and cook until it is smooth and clear.
7. Fill the fruit into crocks or glasses with enough room above to pour the syrup over, and when it is cold, tie them up.
