## Ingredients

- Hare
- Lard
- Butter
- Boiling water
- Cream
- Wheat flour
- Milk
- Roasted potatoes
- Redcurrant jelly

## Directions

1. Hang the hare by the hind legs with head downward in a dry, cool place for several days before roasting.
2. Skin the hare when ready to use, remove all membranes, and cut off the head. Cut off the front legs but keep them for roasting. Cut away enough from the breast and ribs so the hare lies flat on the pan.
3. Wash the hare well and rinse the inside with boiling water using a teapot, holding the hare by the hind legs so the water drains immediately.
4. Lard densely with lard, including the front legs. Place on the pan, cover with butter and roast at even good heat with constant basting of the sauce, and when almost tender, also with cream.
5. Roasting takes 1 to 2 hours depending on size. When tender and removed from the pan, whisk the sauce with a little wheat flour, thin with cream, and immediately before serving stir in a piece of cold butter.
6. Serve with roasted potatoes and redcurrant jelly. If the hare has hung too long, after cleaning let it soak for 24 hours in milk.
