## Ingredients

- 12 lemons
- salt
- 2 pieces Spanish pepper
- 2 tablespoons white pepper
- a few pieces scraped ginger
- 2 pots best wine vinegar
- 12 tablespoons mustard seed
- 3-4 shallots

## Directions

1. Dry the lemons well and make a cross-cut at one end down to the middle of the lemon
2. Widen the cross-cuts and fill each one with a spoonful of salt
3. Stand the lemons in a dish with the whole end down
4. After they have stood uncovered for 8 days, turn them and let them stand the same length of time on the other end
5. Place them in an enameled pot side by side
6. Pour the drained brine over them and add 2 pieces Spanish pepper, 2 tablespoons white pepper, and a few pieces scraped ginger
7. Add 2 pots best wine vinegar to the pot and place over a good heat of coals
8. Boil the lemons for half an hour over even heat until they are tender
9. Place them in a jar and pour the hot brine over them; if the brine is not sufficient, boil some vinegar and pour it hot into the jar
10. Sew a small but fairly roomy bag of white, open linen large enough to fill the upper part of the jar
11. Put 12 tablespoons mustard seed and 3-4 shallots in the bag
12. Boil this slightly while the lemons are cooking and place it over them in the jar
13. The next day, bind the jar in the usual way
14. After 3 months have passed, you can begin to use them; they become better by standing even longer
