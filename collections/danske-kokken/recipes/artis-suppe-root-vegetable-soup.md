## Ingredients

- 4 large carrots
- 1 celery
- 2 leeks
- 1 small parsnip or parsley root
- 4 medium potatoes
- 1 parsnip
- 2 ounces butter
- Salt
- A few whole peppercorns
- 2.5 quarts water

## Directions

1. Cut all the vegetables into pieces, approximately one inch long and of the thickness of a finger.
2. After the vegetables are washed, drain the water from them on a colander.
3. Put 2 ounces of butter on the fire; when it is melted, add the vegetables and cook for a quarter hour with constant stirring, so that it does not brown.
4. Then add salt, a few whole peppercorns and 2.5 quarts of water, but only 0.5 quart at a time, and in such a way that it boils between each addition.
5. In total the soup must cook for about two hours, and to prevent it from boiling over, it must simmer evenly and with a lid on.
6. If you find that there are too many vegetables, some of them can be held back during serving.
7. It is served with toasted bread and is for 3 to 4 persons.
