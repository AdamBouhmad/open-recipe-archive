## Ingredients

- 1 lb. butter
- 1 lb. flour
- 1 pint good cognac (or water)
- cold water

## Directions

1. Wash the butter well in several changes of cold water until it becomes smooth and free of salt, then lay it in cold water.
2. Place 1 lb. flour on a pastry board and make a depression in the middle, into which put 1 pint good cognac (or water) together with a piece of the butter.
3. Work this together with a little cold water until it becomes a suitably thick dough; but it must be worked well together until eyes appear in it.
4. Roll out the dough to the thickness of a straw and take the rest of the butter out of the water and pat it in a dry cloth, place it in the middle of the dough.
5. Fold the dough into three and beat well with a rolling pin.
6. Thus roll out the dough 4 to 5 times after each time folding it 4 to 5 sections and set aside to cool for a couple of hours.
7. When the dough is to be baked, roll it out to the thickness of a two-crown coin.
