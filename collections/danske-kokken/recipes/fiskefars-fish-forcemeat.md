## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg scraped fish
- 125 grams butter
- 3 eggs
- 3 tablespoons potato flour
- about 1/2 liter sweet milk
- salt
- ground white pepper

## Directions

1. Fish forcemeat can be made from pike, cod, or pollack, or half of each of the last two kinds
2. Cut the fish along the back, clean, rinse well and dry with a fish cloth
3. Scrape, chop finely and pound with the butter and flour together with salt until it becomes a tough mass
4. Then stir with quite small amounts of milk at a time
5. Use only ground white pepper as seasoning
6. By using potato flour there is considerably more forcemeat, but you can take half wheat flour and half potato flour, though the milk must then be calculated accordingly
7. It does not tolerate being as soft for balls and meatballs as for pudding and border mold
8. If the fish is cleaned the day before, bind it, hang by the tail and keep in a cool place
9. Garfish can be made into forcemeat, but then care must be taken that the dark stripe closest to the skin does not get in
