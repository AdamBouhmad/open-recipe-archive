## Ingredients

- 5 egg yolks
- 10 tablespoons milk or cream
- salt
- egg whites, stiffly beaten
- butter
- leftover cooked or fried fish
- Palmin for heating
- tomato sauce, mushroom sauce, hollandaise sauce, lobster sauce, crab sauce, or caper sauce
- Tartar butter, Maitre d'hotel butter, tomato butter, or watercress butter
- smoked fish such as herring, hornfish, or mackerel
- salt cod

## Directions

1. Beat egg yolks well
2. Stir in 10 tablespoons milk or cream and a small amount of salt
3. Fold in the stiffly beaten egg whites
4. Place a good slice of butter on the pan and when it boils, pour in the eggs
5. With a fork or knife, lightly prick the eggs and constantly shake the pan so the omelet does not burn or form a hard crust
6. Bake the omelet only on one side
7. For cooked fish: pick the flesh from bones in small pieces and stew in tomato, mushroom, hollandaise, lobster, crab, or caper sauce, then place on the omelet
8. For fried fish: heat leftover in boiling Palmin, pick from bones and place in the center of the omelet with Tartar, Maitre d'hotel, tomato, or watercress butter
9. For smoked fish such as herring, hornfish, or mackerel: chop and place in a stripe on the omelet, then fold. Serve as smørrebrød, as hot as possible
10. For salt cod: chop finely and stir into the egg mixture before pouring on the pan. Omit salt from the omelet recipe
11. Serve immediately
