## Ingredients

- Beef from the tenderloin
- Smoked bacon, half lean and half fat, the size of a small finger
- Peeled and quartered Charlotte onions
- Ground ginger
- Ground pepper
- Cloves
- Finely chopped onions
- Salt
- Butter
- Water
- Bay leaves
- Peppercorns
- Red wine or Port wine
- Flour

## Directions

1. Beat the beef well on all sides and remove the sinews.
2. Cut the bacon into strips and finely chop the Charlotte onions.
3. Mix ground ginger, pepper, cloves, finely chopped onions, and salt together.
4. Lard the meat with the bacon strips and rub it with the spice mixture.
5. Also rub the bacon in the spice mixture before larding.
6. Lard the meat on all sides.
7. Heat butter in a pot over the fire until browned.
8. When the butter begins to brown, place the meat in it and brown slowly on both sides.
9. Add enough water to just cover the meat.
10. Add a few bay leaves, some peppercorns, an onion, a few cloves, and a glass of red wine or Port wine.
11. Boil for approximately 4 hours until the meat is tender.
12. Drain the broth and skim off the fat.
13. Reduce the broth over high heat to half its volume.
14. Add salt to taste.
15. The sauce can also be thickened with butter and flour.
