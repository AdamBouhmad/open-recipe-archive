## Ingredients

- Wild duck (1 for 2 to 3 people)
- 1¼ dl (½ pint) vinegar
- 1 l (1 pot) cold water
- Milk
- Sauce (see Pheasant, No. 963)
- Cold butter
- Browned potatoes
- Compote or jelly

## Directions

1. Use preferably the broad-billed variety. Remove the head with neck and tips of wings, cut open, clean and rinse well inside.
2. If freshly shot, lard and roast immediately. If hung for a time, soak in a mixture of 1¼ dl vinegar to 1 l cold water for 4 to 5 hours after plucking.
3. Rinse and soak in milk for a few hours.
4. Lard and brown, then braise best in a pot for about 5 quarters of an hour.
5. Prepare the sauce first and roast the duck in it (see Pheasant, No. 963).
6. If roasting a larger number in the oven, do not brown first but place breast side up and drip well with a thin, baked sauce.
7. The sauce can be given more colour at serving and thickened with a piece of cold butter.
8. At serving, remove the back and divide the breast into 4 parts, which are reassembled.
9. Garnish with browned potatoes and serve with compote or jelly.
