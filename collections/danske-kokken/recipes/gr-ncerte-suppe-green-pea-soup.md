## Ingredients

- meat stock made from beef or mutton
- dried peas
- butter
- flour
- parsley
- nutmeg
- cayenne pepper
- young carrots (optional)

## Directions

1. Cook ordinary meat stock from beef or mutton and take as many dried peas as you judge will make the soup suitably thick; it should be like yellow peas that are somewhat thin
2. Since peas cook slowly in strong stock, especially when it is smooth, it is best to strain off some stock before the meat is completely cooked, mix it with water and let the peas cook longer in it
3. When the meat is cooked, strain off the second soup; dry the pot and place it empty on the fire; melt so much butter in it that you can smooth out an almost spoonful of flour in it, and when it is smoothed out, thin it first with water, then with stock
4. When all the soup has come in and boils, add fine parsley together with the peas and their soup, nutmeg and a little cayenne
5. If you wish to have young carrots, cook them whole with the peas
6. You can make this soup thinner with peas and with less butter and add meat balls and carrots
