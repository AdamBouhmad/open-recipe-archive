## Ingredients

- Several beef rump steaks
- Bacon pieces
- Pepper
- Ginger
- Flour
- Butter
- Broth
- Whole onions
- Anchovies
- Bay leaves
- Whole ginger pieces
- Whole peppercorns
- Oysters or gravy

## Directions

1. For this several beef rump steaks are used, and since these are usually lean, many lard them with bacon pieces, done by cutting a deep slit in several places in the meat, taking small bacon pieces, dipping them in pepper and ginger and pushing them as deep in as possible.
2. If the cut gapes, it is closed together with a small skewer or with one of the bacon strips with which the roast is larded on the outside.
3. Such bacon strips must be cut lengthwise through the meat; if taken crosswise, they break apart.
4. The roast is then dusted with flour, but not more than can be well rubbed in; then it is browned on all sides in butter, and one must not take more of it than exactly necessary, for then the roast would become less brown.
5. When all sides are brown, the roast is taken up, a good spoonful of flour is browned in the butter that is in the pan; this is filled out with about 3 quarts of hot broth little by little.
6. The roast is placed back in and some whole onions, a couple of anchovies, a small handful of bay leaves, some pieces of whole ginger and whole peppercorns are added to the sauce.
7. This all boils together under a lid until the roast is tender, then the sauce is strained, thinned if it is too thick, and whatever oysters or gravy you have is added.
