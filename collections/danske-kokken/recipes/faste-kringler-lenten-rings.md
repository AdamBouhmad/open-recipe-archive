## Ingredients

- 6 eggs
- 2 large tablespoons powdered sugar
- 1 pound flour
- water
- salt

## Directions

1. Whip 6 eggs in a quarter hour with two large tablespoons powdered sugar.
2. Stir as much of 1 pound flour as it can take into it, and then knead it on a baking board with the rest, but hold back as much as is needed to dust the board with during kneading.
3. The dough must be worked well and kneaded so long that it completely releases from hands and board, then cut it into small pieces, approximately 18 to 20, which are equal in size.
4. Roll these with the hands, but without flour on the board, into sausages of a finger's thickness, shape them into rings or wreaths, place them in a pot with plenty of gently and strongly boiling water and boil until they rise, then remove them and bake at once very slowly until quite light.
5. Do not put so many rings in the water at once that they come to lie on top of one another.
6. If the rings do not quickly rise, they have settled on the bottom and must then be carefully loosened with a spoon.
