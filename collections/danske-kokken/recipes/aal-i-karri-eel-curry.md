## Ingredients

- eels
- salt
- cloves
- 1 charlotte onion
- whole peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- 1/4 pound butter
- 1 chopped onion
- 1 teaspoon curry
- 1/2 pound Carolina rice

## Directions

1. Clean the eels and cut into pieces as mentioned above, then cook them in water with salt, a few cloves, 1 charlotte onion, some whole peppercorns, and 1 bay leaf.
2. In a saucepan, cook together 1/4 pound butter, 1 chopped onion, and 1 teaspoon curry; thin the roux with the soup in which the eel was cooked.
3. Strain the sauce, add salt to taste, and shortly before serving stir in 1 tablespoon butter. If the sauce does not taste sufficiently of curry, mix a little more curry in cold water and add it.
4. Arrange the eels in the center of a large flat dish, pour the curry sauce over them, and arrange a border of cooked rice around them, prepared as follows: After 1/2 pound Carolina rice is cleaned and blanched, cook it in as much water as to cover it well. When tender but still whole, strain it, pour cold water over it, and return it to the saucepan so the remaining moisture can evaporate.
