## Ingredients

- barely ripe apricots
- 3/4 pot cold water per pound of fruit
- as many pounds of powdered sugar as the fruit weighs
- as many measures of water as sugar
- juice of one lemon
- a little brandy or spirits

## Directions

1. The fruit must be barely ripe and dried before placing in a preserving kettle with 3/4 pot cold water per pound.
2. When the apricots are tender, remove them and lay on clean linen to dry.
3. Cut them in half, remove the stones, and peel the skin if desired.
4. Cook equal parts powdered sugar and water with the sugar measure of water into a syrup, skim well, and add the juice of one lemon.
5. Cook the fruit in the syrup until clear, then remove and place in a preserving jar.
6. Pour the syrup over them and add a little brandy or spirits on top.
7. Note: Apricots and peaches tend to ferment quickly and require frequent monitoring, and if necessary must be cooked again with more sugar.
