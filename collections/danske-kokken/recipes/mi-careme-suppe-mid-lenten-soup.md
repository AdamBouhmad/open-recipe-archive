## Ingredients

- 2 pounds flounder
- 1 carrot
- 1 parsnip
- 1 onion
- Bundle of greens
- 1.25 quarts cold water
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 0.5 dozen lettuce leaves
- Slices of cold boiled beet
- 1 quart milk
- 2 ounces butter
- Flour
- White pepper
- Nutmeg

## Directions

1. Prepare the flounder as for fillet.
2. Chop up a carrot, a parsnip and an onion; prepare a bundle of greens as previously shown.
3. Place these vegetables on the fire in 1.25 quarts of cold water together with the fish and one teaspoon of salt, and bring the whole to a boil.
4. Gently remove the filet, place it between two plates to cool, and let the soup cook gently.
5. Cut half a dozen lettuce leaves into small rounds with an instrument such as a core remover, and plunge them for a minute in boiling, well-salted water, then dry them and place in cold water until needed.
6. Cut some slices of cold boiled beet in the same manner as the lettuce, and treat the filet in the same way; set all this aside until it is needed.
7. Rub the remainder of the filet through a strainer with a spoon.
8. Place 1 quart of milk on the fire to boil; place 2 ounces of butter on the fire beside it, and stir into it the same amount of flour.
9. Now thin this little by little with the boiling milk and with 1 quart of the water in which the vegetables have been cooked; season the soup with a little white pepper and grated nutmeg, and, if needed, with a little more salt.
10. Stir in the sieved fish and thicken the soup slowly with a whipped egg yolk.
11. When the soup is ready for serving, add the fish, lettuce and beets, and serve it immediately, so that the strongly contrasting colors do not lose their appearance.
