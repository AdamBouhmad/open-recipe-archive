## Ingredients

- Ripe pears
- 1 pound crushed white sugar per pound of pears
- Nearly 3 pints brandy

## Directions

1. Peel the pears, remove the blossom and trim the stem
2. If large, cut them in half and remove the core and stem
3. Weigh the pears
4. Layer them in a pot with 1 pound crushed white sugar per pound of fruit
5. Pour nearly 3 pints of brandy over
6. Place a cover or lid that fits well at the sides over the pot
7. Seal around the edge with rye flour and water made into a thick dough so no steam can escape
8. Place the pot in an oven that is not as hot as needed for roasting
9. Leave until juice begins to come out, then remove the pot and check if the seal has loosened and put it back
10. Repeat for 1 day until the pears are done
11. Keep the pot sealed until needed; later tie it well, if possible with a bladder, so the brandy does not lose its strength
12. Peaches may be preserved the same way, as well as raspberries and strawberries
