## Ingredients

- Ham
- Cloves
- Bay leaf
- Breadcrumbs
- Root vegetables (carrots and white roots)

## Directions

1. Test the ham as follows: run a thin-bladed knife close to the bone; if the smell you notice is sweet, the ham is good.
2. Soak it in cold water for twenty-four hours, changing the water once.
3. Scrape it well and remove all sinews.
4. Place it in enough cold water to cover it, with a lid in which 6 cloves and a bay leaf are stuck.
5. Boil for 4 hours.
6. Remove it, take off the skin, spread sifted breadcrumbs over it, and brown it in the oven.
7. It can be eaten either cold or warm; if to be served warm, it is often accompanied by sauce and vegetables arranged around it as follows: cut some large carrots and white roots into olive shapes and other forms.
8. Boil them in salted boiling water, then place in cold water until needed.
9. Heat them in the sauce and arrange around in small groups, pouring a little sauce over them.
