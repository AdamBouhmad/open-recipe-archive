## Ingredients

- 2 heads pointed cabbage
- 1/4 kg sausages
- yellow and white carrots, half-cooked
- salt
- pepper
- 125 gr melted butter
- brown sauce

## Directions

1. A Chartreuse can either be cooked in a pudding mold without a tap or placed in the oven in a mold with a solid bottom. If the latter, a piece of paper buttered with butter and with some small holes cut in it can be placed over it to allow steam to escape. The mold used must be well buttered with cold butter.
2. Yellow and white carrots, which are half-cooked, are cut into thin slices and cut into equal sizes. They are pressed firmly against the bottom and sides of the mold to form a pattern.
3. The cabbage, which can be savoy cabbage, pointed cabbage, or white cabbage, is cut into 4 parts and cooked nearly tender in water. The stalks are cut off, and the water is pressed well out of the cabbage, which is chopped very coarsely with salt and pepper.
4. The previously decorated mold is lined with a 3 centimeter (1 inch) thick layer of cabbage, and the sausages are placed in the middle. Over this cabbage is laid again, and finally the Chartreuse is poured over with 125 gr (1/4 pound) melted butter.
5. It is cooked or baked for about an hour, carefully turned out onto a serving dish and served with good, brown sauce.
