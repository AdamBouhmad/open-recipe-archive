## Ingredients

- 4 pounds mutton shoulder
- Onions
- Breadcrumbs
- Butter
- Egg yolks
- Salt
- Pepper
- Carrots
- Parsnips
- Butter for the sauce
- Flour
- Cream
- Sugar

## Directions

1. The shoulder should not be cracked at the butcher; wash it and lay it on the table with the inner side up.
2. Remove the shoulder blade and cook it to be completely clean.
3. With a sharp, thin-bladed knife, cut out the lower joint of the bone and half of the second, breaking off halfway between the first and second joint and being careful not to tear the meat or cut through the skin.
4. Shape the end of the bone so it resembles a duck's beak.
5. Stuff the shoulder with a forcemeat or filling made as follows: peel 6 onions, cut them into pieces and cook them in three different changes of water.
6. Soak 8 ounces of breadcrumbs in lukewarm water and wring out in a clean cloth.
7. When the onions are tender, about half an hour, chop them and mix together with the breadcrumbs, a piece of butter, 10 egg yolks and a little salt and pepper.
8. After filling the skin with this, sew it and tie it together so it resembles a duck.
9. Place it in the roasting pan on some pieces of vegetables and roast in a hot oven about 20 minutes per pound.
10. Meanwhile, prepare carrots and parsnips by cutting them into small balls, cook them tender in well-salted boiling water and place them in cold water to preserve their color.
11. When the canton is done, remove it to a warm platter and keep warm without covering while the sauce is made.
12. Make the sauce from browned butter with a little flour, strain the gravy from the roasting pan with the vegetables into the sauce, and finally stir in a spoonful of raw cream and a little sugar.
13. Warm the root vegetables in the sauce and arrange them around the meat.
14. The shoulder blade, which should be scraped after being cooked and have the broad end chopped so it resembles a feather, is placed behind the canton, which is then served.
