## Ingredients

- Calf's liver
- Bacon for larding
- Butter
- Salt
- Ground pepper
- Crushed cloves
- Broth or gravy and water
- Flour
- Cream
- White wine
- Whole onions (optional)

## Directions

1. After the membranes are removed from the liver, soak it in several changes of cold water so the blood can drain out, then lard it with bacon.
2. Brown a piece of butter and turn the liver several times in it.
3. When it is light brown, add salt, ground pepper, and a couple of crushed cloves.
4. Add enough broth or gravy and water to have sufficient sauce.
5. When the liver is tender, remove it and strain the sauce if necessary, return it to heat and thicken with a little flour mixed in gravy, cream, and a little white wine.
6. Pour the sauce over the liver.
7. It gives good flavor to roast some whole onions with the liver and serve them around it.
8. Alternatively, the liver can be cut into fairly thick pieces and fried in a pan in enough butter and frying fat or lard so it can cook in it. When the liver is cooked through and laid on a platter, pour off the fat, add some cold butter on top, and when this is brown, stir in gradually a little cold water or gravy until the sauce is smooth, then pour it over the liver.
