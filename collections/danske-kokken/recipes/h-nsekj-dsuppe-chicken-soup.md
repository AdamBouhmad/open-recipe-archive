## Ingredients

- 1 pound hen per portion
- 3 pots water
- veal shank (optional)
- salt
- soup vegetables
- dumplings or bread dumplings

## Directions

1. If you do not wish to use the cooked hen as a second course, this soup can be made with an old bird.
2. When the hen is slaughtered, soak it for one hour in cold water, then scald, pluck, clean and place it over heat with 3 pots water.
3. If you also have a veal shank to put in the pot, the soup will be much stronger.
4. When the soup boils, skim it well, add a little salt and soup vegetables, then continue cooking under even heat and under a covered lid until the vegetables are tender.
5. Remove these, cut them into pieces and place them in the tureen, while the soup continues to cook until the meat is tender.
6. Remove it, strain the soup and give it a good boil with the dumplings, and when the dumplings are tender, pour the soup into the tureen.
7. You can use small, fine dumplings or bread dumplings.
8. If the hen is old, it must preferably be chopped up before cooking, and all strength extracted from it, so the meat is not worth much.
9. Young hens (or turkeys) are instead cooked whole and can be served as a second course with cream horseradish.
10. To prevent the liver from cooking apart, place it during cooking inside the carcass with the cleaned giblets and heart.
