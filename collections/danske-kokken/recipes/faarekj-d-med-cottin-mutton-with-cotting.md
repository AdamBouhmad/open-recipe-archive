## Ingredients

- Fat mutton or lamb, cut into pieces
- Butter
- Flour
- Nutmeg
- Cayenne pepper
- Cotting (breadcrumb coating)
- Eggs, beaten
- Paper for covering

## Directions

1. Fat mutton or lamb meat can be used with cotting; cook it first, but not too much, and let it cool.
2. The sauce is made with a roux of butter and flour, slightly thinner than for fricassee, and with nutmeg and cayenne, and should also be cold.
3. Layer the pieces tightly together in a deep platter so the upper side is fairly smooth without protruding bones.
4. Pour the sauce over and spread cotting about a finger's thickness over it.
5. Make a hole in the middle so steam can escape.
6. Cover the whole with beaten egg.
7. Place paper over when the crust is golden; it must not become brown.
