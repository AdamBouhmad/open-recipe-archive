## Ingredients

- 2 pounds half-ripe grapes
- ½ pound of the same kind of grapes
- 1 pound white sugar
- 2 ounces sugar per pound (later)
- Brandy
- Paper dipped in brandy

## Directions

1. Place 2 pounds half-ripe grapes in a jar; crush ½ pound of the same kind of grapes
2. Strain the juice and mix with 1 pound white sugar and pour over the whole berries, which stand covered for 2 days
3. Then place the whole over the fire and boil until the grapes have changed color
4. Remove them and place in the jar
5. Boil the juice a little longer, skim it, and pour hot over the grapes, which are covered with a cloth and stand again for 2 days
6. Put the whole over the fire again; when it begins to boil, remove the grapes and place in the jar
7. Boil the juice well, skim it, and pour hot over it, covered with a cloth and sealed the next day
8. When they have stood for 8 days, the juice is usually thin; then boil it with an addition of 2 ounces sugar per pound and pour cold over the grapes
9. Cover with paper dipped in brandy and seal
10. They can be filled in the same way with powdered sugar, and are then good for cakes, sweet soup, and sauces
