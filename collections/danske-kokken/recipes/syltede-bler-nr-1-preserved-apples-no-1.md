## Ingredients

- Good juicy apples
- Water
- 1 quart water per pound apples
- Cinnamon sticks
- White sugar, 1 pound per pound apples
- ½ pint of the water the apples were cooked in
- Lemon juice

## Directions

1. The apples must be good and juicy
2. Peel them, cut into 4 pieces, remove the cores, and weigh them
3. Place 1 pound apples over the fire with 1 quart water and a couple of pieces of cinnamon
4. When it boils, add the apples, and when they have had 2 boilings, place them on a coarse strainer so the water can drain off
5. Boil 1 pound white sugar to syrup with ½ pint of the water in which the apples were cooked
6. Add these along with a little lemon juice, and boil very gently until they are clear, usually 5 minutes
7. Then remove the pot from the fire, let the apples stand a little in it, then remove them and place in a jar
8. If the juice is not stiff when cold, boil it a little more and pour cold over the apples
9. Seal the next day
10. These preserved apples can only keep for a couple of months
