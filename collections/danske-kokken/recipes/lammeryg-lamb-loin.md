## Ingredients

- Lamb loin from a large lamb
- Bacon or lard for larding (optional)
- Parsley for larding (optional)
- Butter, margarine, or Palmin
- Salt
- Gravy, soup, or water

## Directions

1. From a large lamb, remove the neck, front ribs, legs, and enough of the ribs so that the loin has an even and attractive shape.
2. The lamb loin can be larded with bacon and roasted like veal loin (Nr. 839) or larded with parsley and roasted like lamb roast.
3. Alternatively, it can simply be covered with small pieces of butter, margarine, or Palmin and sprinkled with salt and placed in a well-heated oven.
4. After 10 minutes, pour gravy, soup, or water over it, with which it is basted during roasting, which lasts about one hour.
5. When serving, loosen the meat along the center of the loin and cut it from the bones, then cut into smaller pieces which are reassembled on the loin.
6. Garnish with cooked vegetables and decorate with salad or parsley.
