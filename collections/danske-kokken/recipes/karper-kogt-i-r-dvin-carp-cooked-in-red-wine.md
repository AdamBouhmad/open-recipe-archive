## Ingredients

- Butter
- Flour (1 good spoonful)
- Red wine (½ bottle)
- Thin broth (enough to make sufficient sauce to cover the fish)
- Bay leaves
- Whole peppercorns
- Onions (a couple) or a small bunch of soup vegetables
- Carp (prepared as in the previous recipe)
- Roe or milk from the carp
- Parsley
- Lemon slices
- Sago flour (if needed for thickening)

## Directions

1. Brown a slice of butter and cook a good spoonful of flour in it.
2. Gradually add ½ bottle of red wine and then enough thin broth to make sufficient sauce to cover the fish.
3. Add bay leaves, whole peppercorns, and onions or a small bunch of soup vegetables for seasoning.
4. Place the prepared carp in the sauce with the scale side up, and cook gently along with the roe or milk until tender.
5. If necessary, thicken the sauce further with a little sago flour mixed with liquid.
6. Strain the sauce and serve in a sauce boat.
7. Garnish the fish with parsley and lemon slices.
