## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg (3 lb) fresh pork fat
- 1 medium liver, about 1.5 kg (3 lb)
- 125 gr (1/4 lb) margarine
- 125 gr (1/4 lb) flour
- Milk
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon ground cloves
- Salt
- 1 small onion, grated
- Egg whites (whipped)

## Directions

1. Mince the pork fat very finely.
2. Make a roux by cooking the margarine and flour together, then thin it with milk to create a thick batter or soft gruel.
3. Add the minced pork fat to the batter.
4. Place the pot over very low heat to prevent burning and let the mixture cook very gently until the pork fat is rendered.
5. While the mixture cooks, prepare the liver.
6. Select the lightest livers, which are also the fattest, with a fragile membrane that breaks easily with finger pressure.
7. Wash the liver thoroughly and remove the membrane.
8. Scrape the liver so that no veins or connective tissue are included.
9. Mince the liver finely and place on a coarse, inverted sieve.
10. Gradually press the warm mixture through the sieve with the liver.
11. In the strained liver mixture, stir in 2 egg yolks, 1 teaspoon ground black pepper, 1 teaspoon ground cloves, salt to taste, 1 grated small onion, and the whipped egg whites.
12. Fill molds (not completely full) and place on a baking sheet in a moderately warm oven.
13. Avoid high heat as it will form a hard crust on top.
14. Cover the molds with paper during baking.
15. Bake in a water bath for 1 hour.
16. If a layer of liquid fat forms on top of the hot pâté, do not pour it off as it will be reabsorbed.
17. Let rest until the next day.
18. Place the mold over a steaming kettle or pass it several times over a weak gas flame to loosen it from the sides.
19. Turn the pâté out of the mold.
20. In a gelatin mold, pour good, clear aspic mixed with isinglass.
21. When set, turn it over onto the liver pâté.
22. Decorate with parsley, pickled shallots, small onions, pickles, slices of cucumber, or other garnishes.
