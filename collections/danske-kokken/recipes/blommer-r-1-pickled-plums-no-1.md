## Ingredients

- Fully ripe plums
- Half gill vinegar per pound of plums
- Half pound powdered sugar per pound of plums
- Cinnamon stick
- Cloves
- Fresh bay leaves (optional, for serving with roasted meat)

## Directions

1. Plums must be fully ripe. Dry them, prick them with a pointed wooden stick or needle and place them in a crock, where they are covered with half a gill of vinegar per pound of plums and left until the next day.
2. Pour off the vinegar and add half pound of powdered sugar per pound of plums. Heat this on the fire, and when it is just melted, add the plums and turn them a couple of times around so that those that were at the bottom come to the top.
3. When it begins to boil, pour it into the crock, leave uncovered until the next day, pour into another crock so that the syrup can reach all the plums, and the day after set the whole thing back on the fire, but stop as soon as it begins to boil.
4. Two days later set it on the fire again, and now cook the plums until they are not sour by the stones, usually in half an hour. Then place them in a strainer so the syrup can drain off.
5. Cook the syrup a good quarter hour, or until it is smooth, with whole cinnamon and cloves. Pour it into a crock, and when it is cold, place the plums in it, but not too many, so there can be sufficient syrup around the plums.
6. The next day tie them up. If there is more syrup than can be with the plums, pour this into bottles. If one wishes to use these plums with roast, it is good to add a few fresh bay leaves.
