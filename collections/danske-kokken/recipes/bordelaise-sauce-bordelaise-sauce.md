## Ingredients

- A couple of tablespoons finely chopped shallots
- A clove of garlic
- Butter
- ¼ liter (1 pint) red wine
- ½ liter (½ pot) good brown sauce
- 125 grams (¼ pound) fresh or pickled mushrooms
- A couple of tablespoons tomato purée
- 1 glass Madeira
- Paprika
- 1 teaspoon chopped parsley
- 50 to 75 grams (10 to 15 ounces) small marrow dumplings

## Directions

1. A couple of tablespoons finely chopped shallots, among which there should preferably be a clove of garlic, are sautéed in butter and covered with a good ¼ liter red wine.
2. This boils until some of the wine has evaporated, then ½ liter good, brown sauce is added, made as usual but it must be strong.
3. In the sauce, some mushrooms (125 grams fresh or pickled) cut into thin slices are given a boil, then a couple of tablespoons tomato purée is stirred in, 1 glass Madeira, a little paprika, and 1 teaspoon chopped parsley.
4. Just before this sauce is served, 50 to 75 grams small marrow dumplings, blanched in boiling water, are placed in it.
5. The marrow can be omitted.
