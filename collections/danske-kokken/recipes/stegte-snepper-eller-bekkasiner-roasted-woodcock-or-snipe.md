## Ingredients

- Woodcock or snipe
- Alcohol for singing
- Forcemeat (fars)
- Peppercorn
- Bacon slices, thin
- Butter
- Cream
- Bread, finely ground (Tvebak)
- Pepper
- Salt
- White bread, toasted

## Directions

1. Pluck the birds and singe them over burning alcohol.
2. Remove the windpipe, but keep the head attached to the bird.
3. Flay the skin with feathers from the head.
4. Scald the feet and cut off the outermost joint with the toes.
5. Bend the feet in under the thigh and tie them with twine.
6. Fill the eye sockets with forcemeat containing a peppercorn.
7. Bend the head so the beak passes through both thighs, which are then bent well upward.
8. Wrap the birds with thin bacon slices.
9. Brown butter in a casserole and roast the birds in it with frequent basting until tender, about a quarter hour.
10. Remove them and stir the roasting sauce with cream and a piece of butter; serve with the snipe.
11. Remove the twine.
12. Mince the entrails and liver (but not the stomach) very fine and brown them with butter, a little finely ground bread, and a very little pepper and salt in a casserole for a few minutes.
13. Spread this on thinly toasted white bread, which is placed on the platter around the snipe.
