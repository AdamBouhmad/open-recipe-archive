## Ingredients

- 1/4 measure oat grits
- 4.5 pots water
- sugar
- fruit juice
- preserves

## Directions

1. Wash the oat grits in cold water. This rinsing is especially important when the soup is to be used for the sick, as grits often contain considerable dirt.
2. Boil 4.5 pots of water, and when it boils, add the grits and cook over fairly strong heat without stirring until it becomes an even soup, which will take approximately 3 quarters of an hour.
3. Strain the soup: place a hair sieve over an earthenware dish, add some of the soup to the sieve, stir the thin part through with a spoon and rinse the remaining grits with a little boiling water.
4. Empty the grits from the sieve into a dish and repeat with a new portion until all the oat soup has been sieved, ensuring the soup becomes appropriately even during sieving and dilution.
5. Return the sieved soup to the fire and bring to a good boil.
6. When ready to serve, season with sugar, juice and preserves to taste and serve with baked wheat bread cut into small cubes.
7. For sick persons, prepare the oat soup with only sugar and possibly a few drops of lemon juice or wine, but not with fruit juice or preserves.
