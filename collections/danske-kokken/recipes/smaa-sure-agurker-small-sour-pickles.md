## Ingredients

- cucumbers without black spots
- salt brine strong enough to float an egg
- soft water
- vinegar
- soda (natron)
- best quality vinegar
- small shallots
- white pepper
- horseradish, cut into squares
- small amount of sugar

## Directions

1. When choosing cucumbers, be careful that there are none with black spots among them, as these are always more or less bitter.
2. Brush the cucumbers clean, rinse them and place on a linen cloth so the water can drain from them.
3. They must then lie for 24 hours in a salt brine that is so strong it can float an egg. The water for this must be soft.
4. Place the cucumbers in the pickling pot with so much vinegar that it stands over them and cook for ¼ hour.
5. When you add a small amount of soda to the water, the color becomes stronger green without harm to health.
6. Remove the cucumbers and place in a jar.
7. Now boil best quality vinegar with some small shallots, white pepper, horseradish cut into squares and a tiny bit of sugar.
8. When it is well boiled, add the cucumbers, give them a brief boil, then place them in jars and pour the vinegar over them.
9. If you boil the cucumbers in a brass kettle, they will turn black.
