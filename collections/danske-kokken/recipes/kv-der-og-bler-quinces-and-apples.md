## Ingredients

- Quinces
- Cooking apples
- Powdered loaf sugar
- Water

## Directions

1. After the quinces are peeled, cut into pieces, and cores removed, weigh the fruit before placing it over the fire in enough boiling water to cover the fruit.
2. Cook the quinces until they begin to soften, but not until tender; then remove them on a colander.
3. Add the sugar to the water in which the fruit was cooked, and cook it to a syrup, in which the quinces are cooked until tender, but the pieces must remain whole.
4. After they are removed, some firm, peeled, cut cooking apples can be cooked tender in the liquid.
5. Both types are distributed in the jars. The apples will take on both the appearance and taste of the quinces.
6. If the liquid is not sufficiently even, it must be reduced a little more before pouring hot over the fruit.
7. Seal the jars when the preserve is finished.
