## Ingredients

- Medium-sized and white cabbage heads
- Vinegar
- Salt
- Caraway seeds
- Barberries
- Sourdough or rye flour and vinegar dough

## Directions

1. Remove outer leaves from cabbage heads and cut off the stumps, then cut the cabbage very finely
2. Line a well-cleaned wooden barrel or tub with a little firm sourdough or dough made from rye flour and vinegar on the bottom and sides
3. Layer the shredded cabbage in the barrel, sprinkling each layer with vinegar and a little salt, caraway, and barberries
4. Pack the cabbage tightly with a wooden pestle so it releases its juice
5. Place a wooden cover with a weight on top
6. Put the barrel or tub in a warm room so the cabbage ferments and becomes sour, which usually takes 1 to 2 weeks
7. The fermentation produces acid that prevents further spoilage and makes it suitable for long storage
8. If mold appears, remove it carefully
9. Place the barrel in the cold to freeze; it can then be kept all winter
