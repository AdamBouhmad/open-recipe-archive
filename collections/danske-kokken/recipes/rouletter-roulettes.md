## Ingredients

- stew mixture (as used for crust cases)
- isinglass
- breadcrumbs
- eggs
- Claret or Palmin (for frying)
- parsley

## Directions

1. The same stews used for crust cases can be used for roulettes, only the sauce must be baked up to a thick paste; if it is too thin, the roulettes will burst.
2. While the sauce is still warm, add some dissolved isinglass.
3. Pour the stew into a layer about 2 to 3 centimeters thick on a flat dish and should preferably be made the day before so the roulettes can be formed better.
4. With a spoon, take as much of the mixture as the size of a walnut, roll it in ground bread into a ball, and continue until everything is formed.
5. Then dip the roulettes in beaten eggs and again in bread.
6. The roulettes are best cooked in a larger, flat saucepan or pot.
7. If you have a frying basket, it is very easy to place them in it during cooking; otherwise, use a skimming ladle to take them out.
8. The roulettes must be cooked in plenty of Claret or Palmin, cooked until they are brown and crisp, then taken up and placed on paper so the fat can drain off.
9. Serve on a dish with a napkin laid on it; arrange them in a pyramid and decorate either with parsley boiled in the fat until it is crisp, or, as many prefer, with fresh green parsley or head lettuce.
10. Begin cooking 10 to 15 minutes before serving.
