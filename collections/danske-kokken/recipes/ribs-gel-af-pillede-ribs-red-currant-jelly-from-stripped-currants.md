## Ingredients

- 0.5 kg red currants
- 625 gr sugar
- 1.25 dl water per 0.5 kg fruit juice
- Salicylic acid as needed

## Directions

1. Pick the currants from their stems and place them in a jar, which is set in a pot of boiling water, keeping it at a boil until the berries begin to burst and release their juice well.
2. Pour into the jelly cloth and leave thus until the following day.
3. Sugar and water are cooked as No. 93. The currant juice must cook for at most 8 minutes and be well skimmed at the same time.
4. Pour into jars and can be sealed after several hours have passed.
5. The currants remaining in the cloth, along with the juice still adhering to them, can be placed over heat and cooked for a quarter hour with sugar according to taste.
6. This can be either pounded sugar or good powdered sugar.
7. After the pot is removed from heat, dissolve 1 small teaspoonful of salicylic acid in some of the warm juice for every 3 to 4 kg currants and then stir throughout the pot.
8. Seal the jar immediately.
