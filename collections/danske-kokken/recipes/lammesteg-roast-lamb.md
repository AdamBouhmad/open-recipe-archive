## Ingredients

- 3.5 kg lamb hindquarter with kidney piece
- parsley
- butter
- salt
- water
- flour
- cream

## Directions

1. The hindquarter with kidney piece is generally what is used for roast lamb, and to have the proper form, it should be cut so that 3 of the ribs follow with the roast.
2. These ribs are given a break on the inside with an axe, so the roast can lie well on the dish.
3. The shank is cut off, and the back is broken on the underside at appropriate intervals to ease carving, but not so deeply that the back comes apart.
4. With a pointed knife, holes are pierced along the back and leg, into which parsley is inserted together with a little butter; some of the parsley must be visible over the meat.
5. On the roasting pan, which is placed in the hot oven, a little meat gravy or butter is laid before the roast is placed on it.
6. The roast is sprinkled with salt and must stand thus for about 10 minutes; then boiling water is poured on the pan, and the roast is basted well.
7. Usually one counts 1.5 hours of roasting for a lamb roast of 2.5 kg.
8. For the sauce, butter is browned, which is baked with flour, thinned with the strained drippings with the fat skimmed off.
9. The sauce must not be too dark, and after it has boiled well, a little raw cream and a slice of cold butter can be added, but this is not necessary.
10. Roast lamb can be served garnished with a bouquet of parsley, salad or flowers, pierced with a skewer, which is fastened in the shank joint at the tail part.
