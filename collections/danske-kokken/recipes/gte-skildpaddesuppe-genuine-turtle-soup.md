## Ingredients

- 1 turtle
- Beef and veal meat
- 2 ounces ham
- 1 hen
- Onions
- Carrots
- Celery and parsley roots
- Seasoning extract
- Cloves
- Pepper
- Salt
- ½ pint Madeira wine
- 2 bottles white wine
- Bouillon
- 3 to 4 lemons
- 40 pounds beef and veal shank (for 60-pound turtle)
- 8 onions
- 2 carrots
- 2 celeries
- 2 leeks
- Thyme
- 3 bay leaves
- Peppercorns
- Cloves

## Directions

1. Place the turtle on a straw-covered board and pull out the neck with a rope as far as possible, then cut off the head
2. If the turtle is large, allow 10 to 12 hours for the blood to drain
3. Open it with a knife inserted between the belly and back shell
4. Cut off the flippers, neck and tail as close to the body as possible
5. Remove the intestines first, then the meat and bones, and finally the fat attached to the shell
6. Cut the belly and back shells into large squares and cook for a few minutes until the fine outer shell can be removed
7. Treat the flippers, tail and head the same way; cook everything in water until the meat can be loosened from the bones, which are set aside for serving
8. Scald the greenish fat with water and cook it for 3 quarters of an hour with wine and bouillon; the reddish meat is cooked tender in the bouillon but is not served
9. Brown approximately 40 pounds of beef and veal shank in butter or good fat in a large pot together with 8 onions, 2 carrots, 2 celeries, 2 leeks, a little thyme, 3 bay leaves, some peppercorns and cloves
10. Pour it up with the bouillon in which the turtle was cooked, with the addition of salt
11. After 5 hours of cooking, remove the meat and strain the soup through a fine sieve
12. A few hours before serving, pour about 4 quarts of turtle bouillon (calculated for 12 persons) into a casserole
13. Add a hen browned together with the giblets, 3 pounds lean veal in square pieces, ¼ pound ham, cut vegetables, seasonings and parsley
14. Bring to a boil, move to a gentler heat, add ½ pint Madeira and continue cooking for 1 hour; the soup should then be clear and pale yellow in color
15. Skim and strain through a sieve into another casserole
16. Mix it with the necessary amount of turtle meat and the flippers cut into cubes
17. When it has cooked with these for 12 to 15 minutes, add a little cayenne, a glass of seasoning extract and some of the turtle fat cut into cubes
18. Pour the soup into the tureen and serve with lemon slices
