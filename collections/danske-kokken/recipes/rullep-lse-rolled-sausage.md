## Ingredients

- Beef, veal, or lamb meat, preferably from the best cuts
- Salt
- 1 Teaspoon Saltpeter
- Ground pepper
- Allspice
- 2 finely chopped onions
- For veal sausages: fresh or salted, fatty bacon
- For lamb sausages: chopped parsley
- String for sewing

## Directions

1. Lay the outer, fatty layer of beef on a board
2. Where the meat is thickest, cut some of it away and distribute it in the sausage along with any other smaller meat pieces
3. Sprinkle salt mixed with 1 teaspoon saltpeter, ground pepper, allspice, and 2 finely chopped onions
4. Roll the sausage very tightly
5. Insert a fork through the middle of the sausage to hold it together while sewing
6. Begin sewing from one end using a sewing needle and very strong thread, sewing as tightly as possible so it holds together after cooking
7. Bind the beef rolled sausage tightly with sailcloth before cooking
8. Beef rolled sausages require up to 2 hours of cooking; test tenderness by pricking with a fork
9. From one beef cut, 3 rolled sausages can be made
10. For veal rolled sausages, add fresh or salted fatty bacon and the same seasonings as mentioned
11. Lamb cuts are usually sufficiently fatty on their own; they can be prepared like beef and veal sausages or with chopped parsley, pepper, salt, and saltpeter
12. Beef rolled sausages are salted within 10 to 12 days, while veal and lamb rolled sausages require only 5 to 6 days
13. When the sausages are cooked, remove them from the boiling broth and place them in a press between 2 bacon boards with sufficient weight on top, and leave them until the next day
