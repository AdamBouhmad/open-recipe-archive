## Ingredients

- Veal, cut into pieces as thick as a finger
- Butter, in proportion to the meat
- Salt
- Ground pepper
- A few crushed cloves
- Broth or gravy and water
- Cream
- Flour
- White wine

## Directions

1. Cut the veal into pieces as thick as a finger, beat lightly with a kitchen knife on one side and lard on the other side.
2. Brown a suitable amount of butter in a casserole or braising pan, lay the meat with the larded side down and fry until light brown.
3. Turn the meat and cook covered until tender, generally about a quarter of an hour.
4. Remove the meat and place on a platter, which is set over coals.
5. If there is fat on the sauce, skim it off, and remove from heat while stirring in a little gravy and cream, then return to heat, stirring constantly until it begins to boil and is smooth.
6. Pour the sauce over the meat, with the larded side turned upright.
7. Serve with stiff currant jelly as for beef.
