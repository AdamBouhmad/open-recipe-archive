## Ingredients

- Eggs
- Salt water

## Directions

1. Wash the eggs in salt water and place them in an egg net, which is lowered into the boiling water. When boiling eggs, account must be taken for the size of the eggs. Very large, fresh eggs easily tolerate boiling for 4 minutes; otherwise the common rule is 3 minutes. 6 to 7 minutes are needed for the white to be firm and the yolk soft for cut eggs. The eggs must not be boiled in too much water or under a cover, as it causes them to crack easily. Fresh eggs have a greater density than water and therefore sink to the bottom of the pot, whereas less good ones float on top. Soft-boiled eggs are arranged immediately either in an egg coddler or on a platter lined with a napkin, which is folded over the eggs and garnished with parsley.
