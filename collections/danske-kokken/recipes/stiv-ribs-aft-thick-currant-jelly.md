## Ingredients

- Currants
- Nearly 2 pounds whole sugar per quart of juice
- Half pint water

## Directions

1. Pick the currants and place them in a well-glazed earthenware crock, which does not need to be new if it is well cleaned.
2. Set the crock covered (so that the topmost ones do not become hard) in a hot oven where it stands, without being allowed to boil, until the currants sink, change color, and a little juice appears when you gently press on them with a spoon.
3. Then take out the crock and pour the currants together with the juice that is with them into a bag, which hangs still until the juice has run off.
4. For 1 quart of juice take nearly 2 pounds of whole sugar, which is cooked into a syrup with half pint water, but a little longer than according to directions, so that the syrup begins to become white, but is by no means thick or crystallized.
5. Lift the pot from the fire while the currant juice is stirred into it, set it again on and cook the juice evenly, not hard for 6 to 7 minutes, during which time it is skimmed, then poured into crocks or bottles, which the next day are covered with paper dipped in brandy, and tied up.
6. To get the juice lighter, one can let one-fourth of the currants be white.
