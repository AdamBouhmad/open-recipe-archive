## Ingredients

- Best quality cherries, preferably black and well-ripened
- White sugar at 1 pound per pound of berries, or 1/2 pound per pound of berries if longer cooking is desired
- Juice from the berries
- Vinegar (if for serving with meat)
- Cinnamon
- Cloves

## Directions

1. Remove the stems and pits using a quill, rinse the pits in several changes of warm water
2. Dry them in a cloth and spread in the air, in an oven, or in a pan over coals until dry
3. Gently crush so the kernels do not break, then remove the kernels
4. Weigh the berries
5. Put sugar on the fire with a little of the juice from the berries, or if for serving with meat, with a little vinegar
6. When the sugar is melted, add the berries and cook for about a quarter hour or until no longer sour, but not so long they become mushy
7. Pour onto a dish
8. Put the juice on the fire with cinnamon and cook another quarter hour or until thick
9. Add the berries with the kernels and a few cloves to be blended with the juice, but stop before they boil
10. Seal the next day
