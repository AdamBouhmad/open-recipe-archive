## Ingredients

- pork head, prepared and cooked as in recipe No. 262
- broth from cooking the head
- bay leaves
- vinegar
- whole peppercorns
- 6 to 8 isinglass sheets
- 2 to 3 beaten egg whites
- beet vinegar or fruit coloring for red coloring

## Directions

1. After the pork head is treated and cooked as described in the preceding recipe, set it aside to cool before cutting it into slices
2. Place some of the broth in which the head was cooked over the fire to reduce it
3. When the broth is strong enough for aspic, take 1 L (1 pot) of broth and add 2 bay leaves, vinegar to taste, some whole peppercorns, 6 to 8 sheets of isinglass, and 2 to 3 beaten egg whites to clarify the aspic (see recipe No. 247)
4. When clarified, color half of the aspic red with beet vinegar or fruit coloring
5. Pour a thin layer of the red aspic into the bottom of the mold where the aspic is to be placed
6. While this sets, keep the remaining aspic only liquid near the heat
7. Lay a layer of meat slices and pour the clear aspic over it, then alternate red and clear layers; but the previous layer must always be set before the next is laid on top
8. If there is more aspic, it can be finely chopped at serving time and placed on the platter around the turned-out aspic
9. Nearest to the aspic, place a wreath of parsley or salad leaves
