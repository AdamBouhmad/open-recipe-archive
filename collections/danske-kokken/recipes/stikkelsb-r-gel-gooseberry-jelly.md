## Ingredients

- 0.5 kg green unripe gooseberries
- 625 gr sugar
- 1.75 dl water per 0.5 kg fruit juice
- 2.5 dl water per 0.5 kg berries

## Directions

1. For jelly use the large green unripe berries, the so-called glass berries.
2. The berries, which do not need to be picked over, are rinsed and placed over heat with 2.5 dl water for each 0.5 kg and cooked until they burst.
3. Pour them into the jelly cloth and leave thus until the next day; then weigh the strained juice.
4. Cook the specified amount of sugar and water as described in No. 93, and only when it is cooked completely dry, pour in the juice and cook for 10 minutes, while skimming well at the same time.
5. Even if the jelly should be less firm in the first days after it is preserved, it matters nothing; seal it and it will then be firm after a short time.
6. If the gooseberries remaining in the cloth are to be used for Russian marmalade, they must be rubbed through a coarse sieve or through a food mill.
