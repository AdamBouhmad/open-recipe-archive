## Ingredients

- pheasant
- butter
- salt
- bouillon
- white wine

## Directions

1. Pluck the pheasant with great care, singe over burning spirits, carefully remove the innards, and dry inside but do not wash.
2. Do not cut off the head but leave the feathers on it as decoration and wrap it with paper during roasting.
3. Usually place the tail feathers on the platter as well.
4. Rub the pheasant with butter before roasting, sprinkle a little salt on it, baste with butter, and toward the end with a little bouillon and white wine.
5. Roast for half an hour to three quarters of an hour over a clear but not too strong fire, being careful not to overcook it.
6. Do not lard or cover with bacon as this harms the flavor.
7. The pheasant must have hung for some time before roasting to develop the proper game flavor.
8. Serve on the platter with the breast up, with the head and neck positioned using a steel wire.
