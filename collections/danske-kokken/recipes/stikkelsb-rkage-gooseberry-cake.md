## Ingredients

- 3 pots gooseberries, barely fully ripe
- powdered sugar, same weight as the berries
- a little ground Ceylon cinnamon
- a little grated lemon zest
- 1 pint French white wine
- 3/4 pound clarified butter
- 16 egg yolks
- 3/4 pound powdered sugar
- 60 quintals crushed rusks
- 16 egg whites, beaten
- paper dipped in melted butter
- powdered sugar for topping

## Directions

1. Weigh 3 pots gooseberries, barely fully ripe, and give them a brief boil, then drain in a hair sieve to let the water run off.
2. Boil powdered sugar of the same weight as the berries with a little ground Ceylon cinnamon, a little grated lemon zest and 1 pint French white wine to a very thick syrup, and add the gooseberries, which are cooked to marmalade.
3. Beat 3/4 pound clarified butter white, then beat again with 16 egg yolks, 3/4 pound powdered sugar and 60 quintals crushed rusks.
4. When this is well beaten together, pour in the gooseberries and stir carefully.
5. Finally stir in the 16 beaten egg whites.
6. Line a form with paper dipped in melted butter, then fill with the dough and bake slowly.
7. When baked through, carefully turn out onto a serving dish, remove the paper, and sprinkle with white powdered sugar.
