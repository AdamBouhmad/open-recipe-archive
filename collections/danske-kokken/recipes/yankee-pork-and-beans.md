## Ingredients

- One quart small white beans
- Water
- Soda
- Lard
- Molasses or syrup
- Salt pork
- Pepper
- Salt

## Directions

1. Soak one quart of small white beans in cold water overnight.
2. The next morning, strain them into fresh water and let them dry.
3. Place them in cold water with 3 tablespoons of soda over the fire; let them boil for approximately half an hour and drain the water.
4. Take an earthenware pot and place a slice of lard in the bottom, then the beans with two or three tablespoons of molasses.
5. Next place in the center 1 to 3 pounds of salt pork with the skin scored crosswise; add a little pepper and, if needed, a little salt.
6. Pour boiling water over until it covers the meat.
7. Let it bake 6 hours or more over an even, moderate fire, adding boiling water as needed to keep it moist as evaporation requires.
8. It cannot easily be baked too long.
9. Keep the lid on to prevent scorching on top, but remove it for one hour before serving so the pork becomes crispy.
