## Ingredients

- Walnuts, picked at same time and of same size as preserved walnuts
- Salt brine
- Vinegar
- Shallots
- Garlic
- Ginger
- Horseradish
- Nutmeg
- Cloves
- Mustard seed
- Pepper
- Mixed spices

## Directions

1. The walnuts are placed in a cold brine which is so strong that it can bear an egg, and must be fresh and well stirred. They stand in this for 6 days, are taken out and placed in a fresh and equally strong, cold brine, in which they again stand for 6 days
2. Strain them through a sieve where the brine is well drained, and place in a crock
3. Then place on the fire so much vinegar that it completely stands over the walnuts; to this for 100 walnuts add 4 shallots, a small piece of garlic, 3 pieces of ginger, 2 pieces of thin horseradish, 5 to 6 pieces of nutmeg, 10 cloves, 6 tablespoons whole mustard seed, 2 tablespoons pepper and 2 tablespoons mixed spices
4. When all this has boiled, strain it, cover with a cloth and pour cold over the walnuts, which are then tied up very securely, as air would make them soft
5. After a year they are ready to use and keep for several years. They are quite dark in color, but give a pure taste to sauce, especially in melted butter
