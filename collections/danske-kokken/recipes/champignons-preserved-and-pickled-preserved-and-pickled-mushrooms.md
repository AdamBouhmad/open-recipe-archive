## Ingredients

- Mushrooms (small, fresh)
- Butter
- Lemon juice (half or one lemon)
- Glass jars
- Bladder for sealing
- Salt water (light brine)
- Vinegar (best sort)
- Nutmeg flower

## Directions

1. Choose mushrooms carefully, discarding any that are dark or white on the underside of the cap, have thin stems, or have an onion-like formation at the base of the stem. Use small, underdeveloped genuine mushrooms. To preserve: Peel mushrooms carefully, clean them, and cut larger ones into pieces after removing the reddish membrane from the underside of the cap. Discard any with worms. Place peeled pieces immediately in cold water, rinse, and place on a colander then on a dry linen cloth to remove water. Melt butter in a pot, add the dry mushrooms, and immediately squeeze the juice of half or one lemon (as much as needed) over them to keep them white. Cook over gentle heat until the butter looks like oil and a sample from the bottom does not look grainy. Remove from heat immediately, and when cooled, fill glass jars with wide openings and seal with a moistened bladder. To pickle: As mushrooms are cleaned and prepared, place them immediately in cold water. Remove and place in a colander to drain. Whisk together a light brine, place mushrooms in it, and let stand for 2 hours. Remove and place on a colander again. When water has drained, boil in a pot without a lid for 10 minutes with best sort vinegar and a little nutmeg flower. When slightly cooled, fill glass jars, seal with butter, and bind with bladder. Handle mushrooms immediately upon receipt as they darken when left standing.
