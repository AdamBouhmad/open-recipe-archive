## Ingredients

- Walnuts, half-grown
- Cinnamon
- Cloves
- White sugar
- Water

## Directions

1. Walnuts are usually suitable for preserving in late July. They must only be half-grown and not yet have a hard shell, which can be tested by pricking with a larding needle; if there is nothing hard in them, they are good and are placed for 5 days in well water, which is renewed each day
2. Then prick them all around with a larding needle, and place them again in water for 4 days, which is likewise renewed each day
3. Then place them on the fire in cold water and cook under a lid until they are so tender that a pointed piece of cinnamon can be stuck through them, then place them on a cloth to drain and weigh them
4. Stick each walnut with 4 pieces of cinnamon and 2 cloves, and place in a crock
5. For each pound of nuts take 1 pound white sugar, which is cooked to a syrup with 3 gills water, and poured immediately over the walnuts, which then stand covered for 4 to 5 days
6. Then strain them, and the syrup is cooked with an addition of ½ pound sugar for 10 minutes, or until it is smooth, and is then poured immediately over the walnuts, which the next day are covered with paper dipped in brandy, and tied
7. If the syrup after about 3 weeks has become thin, it must be cooked again, but is then poured cold over the walnuts
