## Ingredients

- pears
- salt brine strong enough to float an egg
- best quality wine vinegar
- Spanish pepper
- black pepper
- cloves
- small shallots
- horseradish, cut into small cubes

## Directions

1. Peel the pears and hollow them out.
2. Wipe them dry with a linen cloth.
3. Leave them for 24 hours in a salt brine strong enough to float an egg, made with soft water.
4. Remove them and place on a colander.
5. Leave them for another 24 hours in fresh salt brine.
6. Put best quality wine vinegar, Spanish pepper, black pepper, cloves, some small shallots, and horseradish cut into small cubes in an enameled pot and heat over the fire and cook briefly.
7. Place the pears in the pot, but do not let them boil, and remove after a moment.
8. Place them in jars and pour the vinegar over them.
9. There must be enough vinegar to stand well over the pears.
10. If the vinegar evaporates, cook a little more and pour it cold over them.
