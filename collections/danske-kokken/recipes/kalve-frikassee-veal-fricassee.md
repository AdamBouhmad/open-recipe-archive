## Ingredients

- Veal (same cuts as used for curry)
- Butter
- Flour
- Water or stock
- Leek and celery leaves
- Whole celery and parsley roots
- Nutmeg
- Lemon juice
- Egg yolk
- White wine (optional)
- Green peas (optional)
- Oysters (optional)
- Asparagus (optional)

## Directions

1. Cut the meat into pieces in the same manner as for curry.
2. Melt a piece of butter over heat and stir in enough flour for thickening, then gradually add water or stock until it becomes a thick sauce.
3. Add the meat with salt and add water until it just covers the meat.
4. Add a bundle of leek and celery leaves, but remove it before it falls apart.
5. Add whole celery and parsley roots and simmer until the meat is tender, then remove the meat and roots, cutting the roots into pieces.
6. Strain the sauce and return it to the pot with a little nutmeg and lemon juice.
7. When simmering, bind the sauce with an egg yolk mixed with white wine if desired.
8. Pour the sauce over the meat, which should be kept warm on a platter.
9. If using green peas, omit the egg and nutmeg, adding only a little chopped parsley just before serving.
10. For oysters: remove the beards and cook them in a little stock, then strain and add to the fricassee; add the oysters to the sauce after binding with eggs, without cooking them further.
11. For asparagus: omit the roots, trim the asparagus, cut into short pieces, and cook more in the sauce.
