## Ingredients

- Flatfish
- Bread crumbs dried in the oven
- Egg
- Cold water
- Fat for frying
- Parsley

## Directions

1. Fillet is made only from flatfish.
2. Wash the flatfish, dry in a clean cloth and place flat on the table.
3. Cut off the fins with a sharp, thin-bladed knife.
4. Press the knife down in the middle of the fish until it reaches the bones, then turn it with the edge toward the fins and guide it along the bones outward so that a whole quarter of the fish is loosened from the bones.
5. In the same way loosen the other quarters and place them all with skin down on the table.
6. Set one of the fingers of the left hand firmly on the end of one of the quarters and let the knife run along the skin so that the fish piece is freed from it.
7. Skin all the fillet in this way and place it on a plate.
8. The whole is a matter of skill, and what it is about is that the fillet does not fall to pieces.
9. Sprinkle bread crumbs, which have been dried in the oven, crushed fine and sifted, on the table; beat an egg with a spoonful of cold water.
10. Place the fillet on the crumbs, press lightly, turn, press again, dip in the beaten egg and take away quickly so that the crumbs are not washed off.
11. Place it again in the crumbs and press in a neat form with the knife.
12. Heat as much fat as will be sufficient to cover three or four fillets until it begins to smoke; place the fillets in one at a time, fry them light brown, take them up with a skimming ladle and place them for a moment on brown paper so that they are freed from fat.
13. Place them neatly on a plate with a few parsley leaves and serve them.
