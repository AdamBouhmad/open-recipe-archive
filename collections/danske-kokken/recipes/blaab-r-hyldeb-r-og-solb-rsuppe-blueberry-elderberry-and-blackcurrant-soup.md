## Ingredients

- 3 pots blueberries, or 14 pounds ripe elderberries, or 12 pots blackcurrants
- 9 pots water
- a couple of sticks cinnamon
- a little lemon peel
- 2 to 3 teaspoonfuls potato flour
- a little cold water
- sugar to taste
- lemon juice or sour fruit juice
- bread balls or baked wheat bread cubes

## Directions

1. Clean the berries well and put them on to cook with the water until they are so tender that they can be mashed through a sieve. The cooking will take about an hour.
2. Strain the soup and put it back over the fire with a couple of sticks of cinnamon and a little lemon peel and cook it, then skim it.
3. Mix 2 to 3 teaspoonfuls of potato flour in a little cold water and thin it with a little of the soup.
4. Add sugar to taste, add the thickening to the soup, give it just one boil, but no more.
5. If the soup needs more sourness, add a little lemon juice or sour fruit juice.
6. Serve it with bread balls on top or with baked wheat bread cubes on the side.
7. Elderberry soup gets a good flavor when you take pieces of peeled good apples, with the cores removed, and cook them in the soup after it has been strained.
