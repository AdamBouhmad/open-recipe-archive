## Ingredients

- A good piece of leg of beef, three or four pounds
- Two ounces of lard
- Three pounds of onions
- A quarter pound of bacon in loose pieces
- A hand of veal
- Salt and seasoning
- Parsley and other herbs in a bouquet
- Carrots, turnips, and parsnips if available
- One quarter of a unit of red wine
- Half a unit of vinegar

## Directions

1. In a pot of adequate capacity, place the piece of beef shaped like a brick.
2. Pour two ounces of lard and brown the meat well.
3. Add three pounds of trimmed onions, a quarter pound of bacon in loose pieces, a hand of veal, salt and seasoning, parsley and other herbs in a bouquet, carrots, turnips, and parsnips if available.
4. The water released by the onion is all the liquid the stew should have.
5. When the onion softens and begins to paste, add one quarter of a unit of red wine and half of vinegar.
6. Cook everything over high heat for one hour until the broth reduces by half, then three more hours consecutively over very low heat with the pot hermetically sealed.
7. Serve this piece of meat as a first course with the vegetables around it and the hand of veal in small pieces alternating with them, all moistened with the little broth or liquid that remains.
8. Leftovers, kept whole for the next morning breakfast, can be served cold with fried eggs, breadcrumbs, salad, or as desired, and make an excellent second course.
