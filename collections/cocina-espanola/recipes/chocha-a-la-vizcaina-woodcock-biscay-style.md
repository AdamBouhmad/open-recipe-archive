## Ingredients

- Woodcock with its entrails
- Pork butter
- Bacon slice
- Small onions
- Broth
- Sherry
- Small turnips
- One very finely chopped onion
- Flour

## Directions

1. After removing the entrails from the woodcock (which will be kept for the sauce) and arranging it so the beak comes out between the right breast, place it in a small pot with pork butter, a thin slice of bacon, two or three small onions, two cups of broth, a measure of Sherry, and four small turnips, being careful to move the pot from time to time and adding broth if it reduces.
2. When the woodcock has cooked enough, fry separately one very finely chopped onion, and when it is half done, add the chopped entrails of the woodcock to the same pan so they release all their juice, but being careful to first remove the crop or pouch, which gives a bad taste. Add a little flour and all the sauce together.
