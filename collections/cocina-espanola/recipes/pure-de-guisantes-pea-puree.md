## Ingredients

- Dried peas
- Water
- Butter from cows
- Flour
- Broth
- Milk
- Salt
- Pepper
- Ground clove
- Egg yolk

## Directions

1. Soak dried peas overnight in cold water as you would chickpeas
2. Cook over gentle heat in a water boiler
3. Once cooked, pass through a sieve using a mortar pestle, collecting the paste on a deep platter
4. In a quick-cooking pot, reheat 60 grams of butter from cows per pound of pea paste
5. Dust with flour without letting it brown or toast
6. Moisten with broth and thicken
7. Incorporate the pea paste and stir vigorously over high heat with a spatula
8. Add half a quarter-liter of milk and season with salt, pepper, and ground clove
9. Remove from heat at the first boil
10. Away from heat, bind the puree with an egg yolk beaten in two spoonfuls of cold milk
11. Serve in a legume dish as it comes from the pot, or on a round platter shaped like a half orange and surrounded by molded croutons
