## Ingredients

- one chicken, four to five months old
- butter or pork fat, melted
- salt
- pepper
- watercress or other edible green vegetable for garnish

## Directions

1. Prepare and arrange the chicken well, threading it onto the spit of the roaster and tying it with string so that it does not swing and the legs remain fixed, making a good figure.
2. Coat the outside with melted butter or pork fat using a kitchen brush. Before tying and fixing on the spit, place inside it about the size of a walnut of the fat being used.
3. Sprinkle with ground salt and a pinch of pepper.
4. Roast over a brisk fire rapidly and with much care, because a well-roasted chicken should not be very browned and should have no part of its meat inside that is not completely white.
5. Serve the roasted chicken alone, without any juice, over a bed of watercress or other green edible vegetable served raw, but not to serve as garnish to strengthen the plate, but only to adorn it.
