## Ingredients

- one chicken, plucked, cleaned and cut into pieces
- fine oil
- one dozen small onions the size of walnuts
- one bay leaf
- half a clove of garlic
- salt and pepper to taste
- one tablespoon of flour
- chicken broth or water
- one small glass of good cognac

## Directions

1. Cut and quarter a good chicken into pieces.
2. In a wide, flat skillet, add enough fine oil to just coat the bottom.
3. Over high heat, sauté the pieces of chicken with a dozen small onions the size of walnuts, one bay leaf, half a clove of garlic, and salt and pepper to taste.
4. The onions and chicken pieces must brown very well and evenly in just under half an hour, stirring the skillet constantly without stopping.
5. After this time, remove the chicken from the skillet with a slotted spoon and set aside on a plate.
6. Pass the fat and onions from the skillet to a saucepan to brown one tablespoon of flour until it takes on a chestnut color, stirring it constantly for five minutes.
7. Moisten with chicken broth or water and add one small glass of good cognac.
8. Once the sauce is thickened, cook over high heat for another five minutes, stirring always with a spatula.
9. Remove the saucepan from the heat, incorporate the quartered chicken, cover the saucepan well and serve after ten minutes.
10. Total time: just under one hour.
