## Ingredients

- two pounds of veal breast
- butter from cows or pork
- flour
- broth or water
- about two dozen large onions the size of walnuts
- about two dozen small mushrooms or champignons
- about two dozen pitted olives
- salt and pepper
- dried parsley powder
- egg yolk
- water

## Directions

1. Cut the veal breast into pieces the size of chocolate ounces.
2. Pass the veal pieces in cow or pork butter without letting them brown. Remove them with a skimmer and set aside on a plate.
3. In the same butter, without letting it take on color either, brown two tablespoons of flour.
4. Immediately moisten with broth or water, and when the sauce is thickened and somewhat thin, incorporate the veal pieces that were set aside all at once, along with about two dozen large onions the size of walnuts, an equal number of small mushrooms or champignons, and the same quantity of pitted olives.
5. Cook everything over low heat until the sauce reduces and becomes like a paste.
6. Half an hour before serving, season with salt and pepper, and sprinkle with a good tablespoon of dried parsley powder.
7. Away from the heat, with the dish already in a covered serving bowl, bind the sauce with one egg yolk dissolved in a couple of tablespoons of water.
