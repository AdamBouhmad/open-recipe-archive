## Ingredients

- apples
- lemon juice (from one lemon)
- sugar (one pound per two pounds of apples for preservation, or half a pound if to be eaten immediately)
- cinnamon sticks

## Directions

1. Remove the seeds and seed cells from the apples and soak the pieces in very cold water acidulated with the juice of one lemon, squeezed thoroughly
2. Remove the apple pieces one by one, drain them, and cut them crosswise into very thin slices about the thickness of coins
3. Place them in a saucepan without water over medium heat until they begin to liquefy
4. Stir constantly with a spatula so they do not burn or stick to the bottom of the pan
5. When the apples are almost cooked, incorporate one pound of sugar per two pounds of apples if the marmalade is to be preserved, or only half a pound of sugar if it is to be eaten immediately
6. It is better to eat the marmalade cold
7. Before serving, add a few cinnamon sticks, which should be removed before they have imparted too much flavor to the dish
