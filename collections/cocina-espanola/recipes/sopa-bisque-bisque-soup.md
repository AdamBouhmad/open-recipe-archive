## Ingredients

- many crayfish
- 1 ounce beef butter or lard (for 1 liter of soup)
- 1 spoonful flour

## Directions

1. Cook many crayfish excessively, seasoning well.
2. Remove the feet and tails, which are set aside on a plate and crush and flatten the bodies or corselets, aiding the operation with a bit of the water in which they cooked.
3. Give everything a boil and strain through a sieve the pasty broth or purée that results, which will be more concentrated according to whether the quantity of crayfish and water respectively is greater or lesser.
4. In a pan, place to melt 1 ounce of beef butter—for a liter of soup—or lard, and sauté a spoonful of flour, allowing it to take color.
5. Moisten with the water of the crayfish until the paste curdles, and then increase in the necessary quantity, adding the crayfish purée.
6. Allow to cook five minutes on medium heat; bind as has been said and pour it into the tureen, where the cleaned tails and feet will be beforehand.
