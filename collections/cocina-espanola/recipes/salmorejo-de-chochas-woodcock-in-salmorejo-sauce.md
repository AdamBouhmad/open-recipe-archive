## Ingredients

- Woodcocks
- Tarragon vinegar
- Beef lard, size of a walnut per bird
- Bacon rind
- Beef lard for sautéing
- Clean concentrated broth
- Jamaica rum
- Fried croutons
- Salt
- Strong seasoning

## Directions

1. Remove the giblets from the woodcocks as previously described.
2. Clean them well and return them to the body.
3. Rinse the woodcocks inside with tarragon vinegar.
4. Tie the thighs, neck, and legs with twine, with the beak inserted into the flesh.
5. Insert a piece of beef lard the size of a walnut into each woodcock's body.
6. Season strongly and coat the outside of the woodcocks with fat obtained by rubbing a bacon rind over them.
7. Sauté the prepared birds in beef lard over high heat until evenly browned.
8. Moisten with clean concentrated broth and cook for forty minutes over low heat.
9. Divide each cooked woodcock into four pieces and arrange on a platter so they appear uncut, on top of as many fried croutons soaked in the juices as there are woodcocks, ensuring each bread piece is larger than each woodcock piece.
10. At the table, uncover the croutons and sprinkle them with Jamaica rum.
11. Remove all the contents from inside the bird to a soup plate, mince it, and knead with a little rum.
12. Coat all the croutons with this paste.
13. Serve each diner one crouton and one piece of woodcock.
14. Set fire to the crouton so it slightly chars the woodcock, and eat once the dish has cooled.
