## Ingredients

- Pieces of hard bread or well-toasted bread slices
- Broth rich in fat
- Additional broth as desired

## Directions

1. Over low heat and in a pot with some broth rich in fat, cook pieces of hard bread or slices of well-toasted bread.
2. When the bread has soaked well and a toasted crust (called gratin in culinary terms) has formed at the bottom of the pot, incorporate the desired broth to give the soup more or less consistency.
3. Before serving this soup, carefully remove the fat. When transferring the broth from the puchero to another dish for serving or preparation, strain it through fine linen cloth that has been previously moistened in cold water.
4. This soup, which is the same as what in French cuisine is called croute au pot, should be served very hot. Since the gratin is the most delicate part of the dish, it should be served in the pot or pan in which it was made, because when transferred to a tureen it loses all its merit.
