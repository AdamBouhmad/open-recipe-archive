## Ingredients

- Dried beans
- Water
- Onion, chopped in volume equal to the beans
- One tablespoon of fried and re-fried oil per quarter pound of beans
- One ounce of well-trimmed bacon per quarter pound of beans
- Salt
- One head of garlic, cleaned but not separated into cloves
- Bay leaf
- A pinch of paprika
- One tablespoon of strong vinegar

## Directions

1. Wash the beans very well and soak them in slightly salted water for two or three hours.
2. Cook them as instructed, according to their drying time, in plenty of boiling or cold water until the skin begins to wrinkle.
3. Remove and drain them.
4. In a pot, add the beans with chopped onion in volume equal to the beans, one tablespoon of fried and re-fried oil and one ounce of well-trimmed bacon per quarter pound of beans.
5. Season well and add a head of garlic cleaned but without separating the cloves, a bit of bay leaf and a pinch of paprika.
6. Let everything cook over excessively slow heat, and when the bean disintegrates between your fingertips, the onion has disappeared and the broth or juice presents a viscous appearance, moisten with one tablespoon of strong vinegar, give a final boil and off the heat, before serving, with the vessel very well covered, let it rest.
