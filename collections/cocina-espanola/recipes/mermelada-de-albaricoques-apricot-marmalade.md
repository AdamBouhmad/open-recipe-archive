## Ingredients

- four pounds of apricots, weighed without the pit
- two pounds of pilón sugar, broken into very small pieces

## Directions

1. Quarter four pounds of apricots, weighed without the pit. Cut the quarters into thin slices and place them in a basin or tub with two pounds of pilón sugar broken into very small pieces
2. Place the basin in a cool place, and from time to time move the mixture with a spatula. Leave the marmalade to macerate for eight hours. Then transfer it to a large pot over low heat and cook until it acquires good consistency, stirring constantly so that the marmalade does not stick
3. When it is well cooked, and if you want it to be finely made, pass it through a fine sieve very little by little, with great patience and while it is still boiling, so that the skins and fibers of the fruit remain in the sieve, which in ordinary marmalades form part of the dish, so that nothing is lost
