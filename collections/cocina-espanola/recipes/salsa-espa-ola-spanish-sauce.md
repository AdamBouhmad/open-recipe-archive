## Ingredients

- half a pound of bacon
- one pound of ham
- two pounds of veal kidney without bone and without fat
- onions
- carrots
- concentrated broth
- fine herbs in a bundle
- salt
- pepper
- some cloves
- flour for liaison

## Directions

1. In a saucepan, add the bacon and ham, all cut into small pieces or strips.
2. Place on top two pounds of veal kidney without bone and without fat, and make the meat sweat with some spoonfuls of concentrated broth, cooking slowly until the juices run.
3. Incorporate some onions and carrots during the cooking.
4. Prick the meat in different places with the point of a knife so all the juice comes out, then add abundant concentrated broth to cover the contents of the saucepan.
5. Add fine herbs in a bundle, salt, pepper, and some cloves, and let cook very slowly for about two hours.
6. Pass the sauce through a sieve and to give it consistency, bind it with flour, making the liaison separately and dark, done skillfully.
7. Mix the liaison with the sauce, place the saucepan over heat, and reduce by slow cooking to one quarter.
8. When about to use in a stew, for example, add two spoonfuls of this sauce to a small pan with a glass of white wine, Sherry, or Champagne, let reduce considerably over high heat, and incorporate pieces of truffles and mushrooms.
