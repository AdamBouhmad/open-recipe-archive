## Ingredients

- good lean ham
- a piece of veal tongue or two or three pieces of mutton tongue, cooked beforehand
- poultry breasts
- leftover rabbit or hare
- poultry livers
- a handful of mushrooms
- artichoke hearts
- a cup of pot broth
- a glass of white wine
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- a bouquet of fines herbes
- potato starch

## Directions

1. Make a brown sauce moistened with a cup of pot broth and a glass of white wine, reduced to achieve some consistency.
2. In this sauce, cook good lean ham, a piece of veal tongue or two or three pieces of mutton tongue (cooked beforehand), poultry breasts, leftover rabbit or hare, poultry livers, a handful of mushrooms, and artichoke hearts.
3. Cut all ingredients into equal size and shape, like dice.
4. Season well with salt, pepper, nutmeg, and a bouquet of fines herbes.
5. Cook slowly for half an hour until all is well soaked in the sauce.
6. Remove the bouquet and decant the sauce into another saucepan.
7. Give the sauce more consistency with a little potato starch.
8. Stir vigorously and add the salpicón when the sauce is well thickened, or better, if the salpicón has been kept very hot, place it on a serving dish and pour the sauce over it at that moment.
