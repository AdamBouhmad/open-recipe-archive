## Ingredients

- peeled, washed, and well-drained potatoes
- beef or pork lard
- fresh beef lard or strained pork lard
- a few spoonfuls of juice or Spanish sauce
- or veal, mutton, or roasted loin broth from the previous day's meal

## Directions

1. Place the peeled, washed, and well-drained potatoes in a casserole with plenty of beef or pork lard to saturate them.
2. The casserole should have a wide base so that all the potatoes can be placed in a single layer on the bottom without overlapping.
3. Place the casserole over a brisk fire, also adding heat to the lid, and take care to turn the potatoes one by one so they do not burn and brown evenly.
4. Once done, remove them from the fat in which they have been sautéed, and place them in another ordinary casserole with a little fresh beef lard or strained pork lard, and add a few spoonfuls of juice or Spanish sauce.
5. When these substances are not available, they can be substituted with veal, mutton, or roasted loin broth, set aside from the previous day's meal for this purpose.
