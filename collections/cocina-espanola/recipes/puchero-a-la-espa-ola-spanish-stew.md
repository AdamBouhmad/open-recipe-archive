## Ingredients

- One piece of meat, larger than needed for usual consumption
- Two cuartillos of water per pound of meat
- Bones from the meat, preferably shin bones
- Soaked chickpeas
- Carrots
- Parsnips
- Turnips
- Leeks
- Celery
- Parsley
- Two bay leaves
- Two large onions
- Half a clove of garlic
- Two cloves of spice
- Two ounces of bacon per pound of meat
- One quarter of a veal hand
- Sugar for coloring (optional)

## Directions

1. Take a piece of meat larger than needed for usual consumption, as the larger the piece, the fresher and more solid it will be, and it will save time and fuel
2. Do not wash the meat so as not to strip it of a large part of its juices
3. Tie or bind the meat after removing the bones so it maintains a presentable shape
4. When the water in the pot boils vigorously, introduce the meat suddenly
5. Use two cuartillos of water per pound of meat
6. After the meat has cooked for a quarter hour, add the bones that the piece had, and some more, preferably shin bones
7. Add pre-soaked chickpeas and other vegetables such as carrots, parsnips, turnips, a bundle made with leeks, celery, parsley and two bay leaves, and two large onions in which are embedded respectively half a clove of garlic and two cloves of spice
8. The proportion of vegetables in the stew, not counting the chickpeas, should be in total weight equal to that of the meat
9. After the vegetables, add the bacon, two ounces per pound of meat, and one quarter of a veal hand
10. After two hours of slow boiling that has begun since the meat was added, taste the broth and correct the seasoning made at the start
11. For the broth to be good, the stew must cook slowly and continuously for six hours
12. To diminish evaporation as much as possible without damaging the cooking, the pot should not be perfectly covered
13. When adding water, do so carefully with boiling water
14. For coloring, burn a little ground sugar in a small pan, and when the caramel has formed, introduce the pan and all into the stew until the sugar detaches
15. To remove the broth from the pot, with a skimmer and carefully, extract all the solid parts that the vessel contains: meats, vegetables, etc., to proceed afterward with their placement and arrangement on the plates to go to the table
16. Strain the broth through fine linen placed in the bottom of the strainer, which should be well soaked in cold water so that all the fat it contains stays in the linen, resulting in clean and transparent liquid with this natural clarification
