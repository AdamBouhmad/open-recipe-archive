## Ingredients

- Flour
- Beer yeast, one ounce per pound of flour amasada, or one ounce per four pounds of dough
- Water, lukewarm
- Cow butter, one good quarter-pound per pound of flour
- Three eggs per pound of flour
- Fine salt, two grams per pound of flour

## Directions

1. Begin by making a leaven: place a mound of flour on the table, about one quarter of the total flour to be used
2. Make a hole in the middle and add beer yeast
3. Pour lukewarm water over it and knead well
4. Place the dough in a trough, make two cuts in the shape of a cross, cover with a cloth and let it ferment
5. When the leaven has acquired the required quality, knead separately the rest of the flour with cow butter, eggs and fine salt
6. Join the leaven to the dough and fold the dough many times, sprinkling it with flour
7. Let it rest at least eight hours before making the rolls
