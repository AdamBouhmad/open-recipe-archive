## Ingredients

- One beef tongue
- Pepper
- Saltpeter
- Salt
- Cloves
- Thyme
- Laurel
- Casing (as for sausage making)

## Directions

1. Remove the hard, rough part of the tongue by holding it over live coals until the skin loosens.
2. After separating the skin, rub the tongue with pepper and saltpeter.
3. In a terrine, on a layer of salt, place the tongue and cover it completely with more salt mixed with cloves, thyme, and laurel.
4. After 24 hours, add more salt, and do so every day so the tongue is continuously bathed in this brine.
5. After fifteen days, remove the tongue from its marinade, drain it very well, and introduce it into a casing as if it were sausage.
6. Tie the two ends of the casing very well so it fits snugly, and hang it in the smoke for several days in a fireplace where wood is burning, but high enough so it does not become charred.
7. When ready to cook the tongue, soak it first for five hours.
8. Then place it in a pot full of water with thyme, laurel, and cloves. Cook for six hours over very low heat.
9. Let it cool in its own broth, and remove it clean of adhering fat.
10. Serve cold as a cold dish, either sliced on plates as you would serve sausage, or alternating slices with ham in aspic or turkey galantine, as a cold appetizer at a luncheon or light meal.
