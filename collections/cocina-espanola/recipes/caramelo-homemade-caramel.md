## Ingredients

- 1/2 pound powdered sugar
- 1 liter cold water

## Directions

1. Place half a pound of powdered sugar in a pastry pan.
2. Melt it by stirring with a wooden spoon.
3. When the sugar is well melted, let it boil over the fire for one hour at a very slow boil with the spoon inside so you can stir from time to time.
4. When the melted sugar has acquired a very dark brown color, add one liter of cold water.
5. Let the sugar, which should have solidified when the cold water was added, dissolve well over a slow fire, and let it boil for twenty minutes at the edge of the fire.
6. Let it cool, put it in a bottle, which should be carefully corked for use when needed.
7. The caramel should be the color of dark mahogany, which is obtained by making it over a slow fire.
