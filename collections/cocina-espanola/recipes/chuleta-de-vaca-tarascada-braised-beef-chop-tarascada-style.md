## Ingredients

- one well-trimmed and full beef chop
- lard for larding
- salt and pepper
- twine
- bacon slices
- ham slices
- two cups of good broth without fat
- six carrots
- a bouquet of fine herbs and scallions or leeks
- one bay leaf
- two cloves, minced into one onion of suitable size

## Directions

1. Choose a well-trimmed beef chop and have the butcher prepare it, reserving the bone and trimmings for broth
2. Lard it as if making braised beef and season the surfaces
3. Tie with twine so it does not lose its shape during cooking
4. In a braising pan, place bacon slices and ham slices
5. Place the chop on top, covered with thin bacon slices
6. Moisten with two cups of good broth without fat
7. Add six carrots, a bouquet of fine herbs and scallions or leeks, one bay leaf, and two cloves minced into one onion
8. Place the pan over high heat until it comes to a boil, then cover and continue cooking over low heat for three hours or more, until the meat is very tender
9. Remove the chop from heat, remove the twine, and keep it warm in some of the cooking broth
10. Separately, pass the remaining juice through a sieve and reduce over low heat until it reaches a thick consistency
11. Pour the sauce over the chop, which has been placed on a serving platter
