## Ingredients

- Duck, cleaned and prepared
- Bacon leaves, larger than the duck and one finger thick
- Two slices of very lean veal
- Two carrots
- Two onions with cloves
- Bay leaf
- Bouquet of fresh herbs
- Salt and pepper
- Two ladles of broth
- Turnips cut into small chunks, or parsnips
- Duck fat
- Olives, large and sound, pitted

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the duck, and tie its wings with twine.
2. Place the duck in a pot between two leaves of bacon.
3. Add two slices of very lean veal, two carrots, two onions with their cloves, a bay leaf, a bouquet of fresh herbs, good seasoning of salt and pepper, and two ladles of broth.
4. Cook the duck this way for half an hour and remove it from the pot.
5. Strain the broth and remove the fat.
6. Separately, sauté and brown turnips cut into small chunks or parsnips in the duck's fat.
7. Once the turnips have browned well, place them in the pot with the duck and in the defatted juice, so they cook together.
8. When cooking is finished, place the duck on a platter, the turnips around it, and pour the hot sauce over it and the turnips.
9. For duck with olives: pit the olives, which must be large and sound, and add them to the pot in which the duck cooks in its clean juice ten minutes before serving, so they only give a few boils.
10. Note: Duck with potatoes, with glazed onions, with vegetable purée, with chard and cabbage is also a tasty dish, but turnips stand out the most.
