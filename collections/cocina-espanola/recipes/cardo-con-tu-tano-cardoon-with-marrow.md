## Ingredients

- Cardoon stalks
- Salt
- Pork lard or oil
- Broth from the pot, clean and defatted
- Roux-based sauce (rubia-blanca)
- Egg yolks

## Directions

1. Kill the corners of the stalk and cut into pieces fifteen centimeters long.
2. Blanch the pieces in boiling water with a pinch of salt until the outer layer comes off easily.
3. Add cold water to the hot water to be able to put your hands in and manipulate the cardoon pieces, removing the skin and putting them in very cold water as they are peeled.
4. Remove and let drain very well to put them in a saucepan with a very loose roux-based sauce.
5. Wet with broth from the pot, clean and defatted, in quantity sufficient to cover all the cardoon, and cook over very low heat until all the sauce is consumed.
6. Place the cardoons in a serving dish, and pour over at the moment of serving a white sauce well bound with two or three egg yolks, dissolved in half a cup of water.
