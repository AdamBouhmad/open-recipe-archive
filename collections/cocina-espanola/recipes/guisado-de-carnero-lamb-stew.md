## Ingredients

- Pieces of lamb breast, the size of chocolate squares
- Onions, cut into rounds, in equal quantity to the meat
- Two or three carrots
- Several turnips
- A sprig of thyme
- A bay leaf
- Two or three ladles of clean broth from the pot
- Potatoes, cut into informal but equal pieces the size of pigeon eggs
- Lard, very hot

## Directions

1. Cut pieces of lamb breast the size of chocolate squares.
2. Put onions cut into rounds in equal quantity to the meat in the bottom of the cooking pot, and place the meat on top with two or three carrots, several turnips, a sprig of thyme, and a bay leaf.
3. Add two or three ladles of clean broth from the pot and cook everything over very low heat, but very low indeed, until the sauce is almost completely consumed.
4. Then add an equal quantity of broth or water as before, and let it cook again for two hours over heat even lower, if possible, than the first cooking.
5. One hour before serving, incorporate potatoes into the stew, cut although uneven and the size of pigeon eggs, but before putting them in the pot it is advisable to sauté and brown them separately in very hot pork lard.
6. It is not necessary to bone the pieces of meat, nor to degrease the sauce, nor to reduce it.
7. The only variation can be to put more or fewer potatoes, or none at all, and substitute them with fine turnips, because there is nothing that goes better with stewed lamb than tender turnips.
