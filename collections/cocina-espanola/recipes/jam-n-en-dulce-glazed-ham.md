## Ingredients

- ham
- water
- three bottles of superior white wine (preferably Sherry)
- thyme
- rosemary
- spearmint
- nutmeg
- tarragon leaves
- sugar

## Directions

1. Cook the ham in water over low heat for twelve hours in a quantity such that the water is consumed at the end of that time.
2. Debone the ham, trim and arrange it in the shape of a loaf or cake, and wrap and sew it very tightly in a piece of linen cloth, well washed and rewashed.
3. Cook for twenty-four hours over low heat and until the liquid is consumed, in three bottles of superior white wine—Sherry preferably—taking care that the pot or pan is deep so that the wine bathes the ham well.
4. Add a little thyme, rosemary, spearmint, nutmeg and a few leaves of tarragon.
5. Cover the pot well so that all the steam from the boiling is consumed inside.
6. After twenty-four hours, remove the ham and place it, without removing the wrapping, in a dish or mold of proportionate size.
7. Place much weight on top with small boards to press it well.
8. When it is completely cold, overturn the mold and place and unwrap the ham on a platter, arranging it for presentation according to the aesthetic preferences of the preparer.
9. If you want it to make a good appearance, dust its surface with sugar, which is burned or browned with a hot iron.
