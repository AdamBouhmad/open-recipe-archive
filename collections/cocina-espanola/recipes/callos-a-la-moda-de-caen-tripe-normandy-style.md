## Ingredients

- one beef or cow stomach (panza) with tripe and abomasum
- salt
- pepper
- clove
- nutmeg
- bacon cut into strips like paper cigarettes
- carrots, chopped
- onions, chopped
- butter
- bouquet of aromatic herbs
- garlic cloves
- lamb's feet, well blanched (about one quarter of the volume)
- veal, cooked and boned in advance
- celery heart
- bundle of whole leeks
- one bottle of white wine
- one glass of good cognac
- two liters of water
- 300 grams of beef marrow

## Directions

1. Clean the stomach very well, blanch it, and place it in fresh water for one hour
2. Cut into small pieces the size of chocolate ounces and season with salt, pepper, clove, and nutmeg
3. Add bacon cut into strips like paper cigarettes
4. In an earthenware pot, brown the chopped carrots and onions with a little butter, adding a bouquet of aromatic herbs and garlic cloves
5. Place on top about one quarter in volume of well-blanched lamb's feet and some veal that has been cooked and boned beforehand
6. Place the prepared tripe on top with a celery heart and a bundle of whole leeks to maintain moisture during cooking so the dish does not dry out
7. Moisten with one bottle of white wine, one glass of good cognac, two liters of water, and 300 grams of beef marrow
8. Cover with a well-greased sheet of paper and seal with a paste made of water and flour like a crust
9. Bring to a boil over high heat, then continue cooking very slowly for twelve hours with coals around the pot and covered with a domed lid with heat on top
10. At the moment of serving, remove the paste and paper
