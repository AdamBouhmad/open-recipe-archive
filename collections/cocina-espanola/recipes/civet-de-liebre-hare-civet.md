## Ingredients

- 125 grams butter or lard
- one good tablespoon flour
- 125 grams bacon in shavings
- hare, quartered
- one and a half quarts good red wine
- bouquet of fine herbs
- two dozen mushrooms (champignons), trimmed
- two bay leaves
- salt and pepper
- two dozen small onions the size of walnuts
- lard for reheating the onions
- triangular croutons

## Directions

1. Make a dark roux or brown sauce with 125 grams butter or lard and one tablespoon flour.
2. In this roux, sauté and brown 125 grams bacon shavings.
3. Add the quartered hare pieces to the pan and brown them over high heat to firm the meat.
4. Pour one and a half quarts of good red wine into the pan, ensuring all hare pieces are well covered.
5. Add a bouquet of fine herbs, two dozen trimmed mushrooms, two bay leaves, salt and pepper.
6. Cook over high heat so the sauce reduces violently by at least two-thirds.
7. When the hare is cooked, add two dozen small onions previously sautéed separately in lard.
8. From that point, let the civet rest by the fire without cooking, keeping it very hot for serving.
9. Minutes before serving, add triangular croutons to the dish, well soaked in the sauce, to garnish the platter.
