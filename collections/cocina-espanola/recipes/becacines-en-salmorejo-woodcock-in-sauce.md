## Ingredients

- two woodcock
- four lemons, well-fleshy
- zest of one lemon, grated to powder
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- two teaspoons of good French mustard
- half a glass of white wine
- oil

## Directions

1. Roast the two woodcock on the spit at medium doneness after cleaning them very well on the outside, since it is known that inside, like shells and snails, this animal is not emptied.
2. When the woodcock are half-roasted, divide them according to all the good rules of the art of carving, and then cut the wings, legs, stomach, and tail into two pieces, placing the pieces on a platter.
3. On the silver plate (or moirez or alfenide) in which the dissection has been made, crush and squash the livers, intestines, and all the giblets of the birds, squeezing the juice of four well-fleshy lemons and the grated zest of a single lemon over them.
4. Place the cuts of the woodcock on top, seasoning them with salt, pepper, nutmeg, and two teaspoons of good French mustard, adding half a glass of white wine.
5. Place the plate on a spirit lamp (called infierno), and stir constantly with a silver fork so that during this last point of cooking the sauce coats well and the pieces do not stick.
6. Take great care that everything cooks inside without boiling, and when you see that this moment is about to arrive, add a few drops of oil and remove the plate from the flame of the spirit lamp, and begin again until the operation is complete.
7. Eat this salmorejo very hot, with a fork and not with the hand, as with all birds, for the risk of devouring your fingers while sucking the sauce.
