## Ingredients

- river crabs
- salt
- pepper
- thyme
- bay leaf
- parsley
- onion
- water

## Directions

1. In a saucepan add salt, pepper, thyme, bay leaf, parsley, onion cut into slices and the necessary amount of water so that the crabs are well bathed.
2. When the water boils in bubbles, over vivid and sustained fire, add the crabs after being well washed and cleaned—an operation carried out by pulling the swimming fin found in the middle of the tail, and which upon coming out pulls out with it a black and very bitter intestine.—This operation is done at the moment of going to cook the crabs, so that they die in their stew.
3. After four or five minutes of the water beginning to boil, if the crabs are small, and after six or seven if they are large, remove the saucepan from the fire, and so that they do not dry out, leave them a quarter of an hour in their water and serve them cold, or as a garnish.
