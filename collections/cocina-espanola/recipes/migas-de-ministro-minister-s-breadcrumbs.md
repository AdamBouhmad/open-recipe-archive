## Ingredients

- two French rolls
- salt
- sweet paprika
- two garlic cloves
- watered milk
- one quarter pound of bacon, cut into pieces

## Directions

1. Cut two French rolls into walnut-sized pieces and place them in a bowl or mortar with salt, sweet paprika, two finely chopped garlic cloves, and watered milk so the bread becomes well moistened without becoming too soggy.
2. Invert one bowl over another (the second without an opening) and, holding both with your hands, shake them vigorously by inclining the first bowl's opening downward so any excess water can drain out.
3. When done, the salt and paprika will be well mixed with the bread.
4. In a frying pan over high heat, add one quarter pound of bacon pieces, and when it has released half of its fat, pour it over the bread.
5. Cover again with one bowl over another and shake them anew, while the remaining bacon in the pan melts and becomes golden brown.
6. Then add the bread to the pan, and without setting down the skimmer, crush and pound it while it fries and absorbs all the fat.
7. When the breadcrumbs are dry on the outside and saturated with fat on the inside, and when the pan is clean and shining without a drop of oil, remove them and arrange them in a dome shape on a napkin placed on a serving platter.
