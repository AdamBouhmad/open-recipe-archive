## Ingredients

- One quarter of roe deer cut to the beginning of the ribs
- Ground salt
- Six eggs
- Water
- Flour, two to three pounds depending on the volume of meat
- Ten to fifteen grams of salt
- Cow or pig lard for greasing
- Bread sauce or currant jelly for serving

## Directions

1. Cut a quarter of roe deer to the beginning of the ribs, taking half of the middle section
2. Beat to tenderize
3. Dust with ground salt all over and leave for a couple of days
4. Prepare a firm dough by beating six eggs with a little water
5. Add flour, varying in quantity from two to three pounds according to the volume of the meat
6. Add ten to fifteen grams of salt
7. Knead the dough very well and let it rest for one hour, wrapped in a slightly damp napkin
8. Extend the dough with a rolling pin on the work table and form a sheet the thickness of a coin
9. Completely wrap the quarter of roe deer in this dough, sealing the edges of the dough by moistening them slightly
10. Wrap it very well in a sheet of thick paper greased with cow lard and roast on a spit with gentle fire
11. After three hours of cooking, remove the paper so the dough crust takes color
12. Serve on a platter in the dry, with the dish resting on a well-pressed napkin
13. Serve separately in a sauce boat the famous bread sauce or currant jelly
