## Ingredients

- Sole fish
- Butter
- Parsley
- Thyme
- Chopped onion
- White wine
- Broth or water
- Salt
- Pepper powder
- Grated nutmeg
- Twelve oysters
- Twelve clams
- Truffle pieces or earthnut truffles
- Veal strips
- Ham or bacon strips
- Flour
- Small onion
- Carrot slices
- Bay leaves
- Mushrooms
- Lemon juice
- Bread slices

## Directions

1. After cleaning the fish, remove the skin if very large and place on butter in a fish kettle or baking dish where it fits comfortably.
2. Add parsley, thyme, chopped onion, a glass of good white wine and another of broth or water, salt, pepper powder, grated nutmeg, twelve oysters and twelve clams which have been briefly boiled to remove them from their shells and make them white, and some truffle pieces or earthnut truffles, putting it all on the fire with a little butter.
3. For the oysters and clams sauce: in another pan or small saucepan, fry lightly small strips of veal and ham or bacon without browning them.
4. Add half a spoonful of flour, stir everything very well and add a little broth, a small onion, carrot slices, pepper, nutmeg, bay leaves, and some salt.
5. When the meat is cooked, add this strained broth and sauce over the sole.
6. Place on the fire to cook, covering it with fire also on the baking dish cover.
7. After the fish is cooked, place it on the plate and add some mushrooms cooked with lemon juice, small slices of bread moistened with milk and fried in butter to serve it very hot.
