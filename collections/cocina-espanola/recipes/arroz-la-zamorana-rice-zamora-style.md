## Ingredients

- four ounces of excellent pork lard
- one pound of onions
- half pound of turnips
- parsley
- oregano
- thyme
- half a dozen garlic cloves
- a bit of sweet pepper
- pork leg, ear, and snout
- one pound of good ham
- water
- salt

## Directions

1. Melt the four ounces of excellent pork lard in a good earthenware pot or bowl.
2. In it, sauté one pound of onions and half a pound of turnips.
3. Add parsley, oregano, thyme, half a dozen garlic cloves, and a bit of sweet pepper.
4. Fry all of this well, and before the onion takes on color, fry and let it cook for four hours.
5. After incorporating water, add the pork leg, ear, and snout, all well divided into small pieces and properly deboned.
6. Add one pound of good ham and complete the seasoning.
7. When everything boils vigorously, add the rice, which will cook halfway over high heat.
8. Then remove from heat; cover the surface of the pot with very thin slices of fresh bacon.
9. Cover the vessel with an iron lid and place hot coals on top.
10. When the bacon becomes crispy, remove the lid, let the dish rest, and serve directly from the pot.
