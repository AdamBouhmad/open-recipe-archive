## Ingredients

- quarter liter glazed broth
- 1 large glass white wine
- salt
- peppercorns
- a handful of parsley root cut into thin strips
- curly leaf parsley

## Directions

1. In a quarter liter of glaze add a large glass of white wine, salt, peppercorns in grain and a small handful of parsley root cut into thin strips
2. Let cook over low heat until the sauce reduces and takes on consistency
3. Separately blanch curly leaf parsley by submerging it briefly in boiling water, cut any which way
4. Remove from water and cool in another very cold water to add it to the drunken sauce at the moment of serving
5. When it is desired that this sauce be for vigil days, use the vigil broth or better the broth that has been used to cook fish with the same procedure, especially since this sauce generally serves for fish cooked plain or in short broth
