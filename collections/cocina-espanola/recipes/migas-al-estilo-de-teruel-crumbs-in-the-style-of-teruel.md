## Ingredients

- Bread two or three days old
- Water
- Fine salt
- Oil
- Two garlic cloves

## Directions

1. Take bread that is two or three days old and divide it in half.
2. Taking either half, make vertical cuts at its angles, and then horizontal cuts, so that small pieces the size of chickpeas result. The crust is better than the crumb.
3. After cutting, place them in a napkin and pour a little water over them, stirring well so they become evenly moistened.
4. Dust them with salt, as fine as possible, in the necessary quantity.
5. This must be done at night, and wrapped in the same napkin so it does not lose moisture, keep them until the following day.
6. Half an hour before the time set for breakfast, fry oil in a pan, burning two garlic cloves.
7. When the oil is at its point, remove the garlic and add the crumbs all at once, stirring constantly with a rasera or iron skimmer for twelve or fifteen minutes, and very hot, serve them to the table.
