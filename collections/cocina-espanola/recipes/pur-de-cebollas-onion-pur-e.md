## Ingredients

- three dozen large onions, the size of eggs
- beef butter, a considerable amount
- flour
- salt and pepper
- one ladle of fresh water
- half a quarter-liter of milk
- two egg yolks
- milk for dissolving the yolks

## Directions

1. Peel three dozen large onions and chop them very finely.
2. Sauté them in a pot over high heat in considerable beef butter, without allowing the butter or onions to brown, and dust with flour until everything sets a little.
3. Season moderately with salt and pepper and moisten with one ladle of fresh water.
4. Let cook vigorously for a while, stirring constantly with a spatula, and add half a quarter-liter of milk.
5. From this point forward, cooking must be very slow and will last about fifteen minutes until the broth reduces by half.
6. Pass everything through a fine sieve, and set the paste to curdle in a bain-marie in another pot, with two egg yolks dissolved in milk at the moment of serving.
7. This is a luxury dish that is presented with success at great tables, serving as a complement, though separate, to fried ham or grilled salmon.
