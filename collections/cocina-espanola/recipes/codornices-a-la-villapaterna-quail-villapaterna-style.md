## Ingredients

- Quail
- Truffle about the size of a hazelnut
- Butter from Isigny cows with the volume of a plum
- Salt
- Pepper
- Bread crumb
- Fresh bacon leaf
- Water

## Directions

1. Pluck, eviscerate and clean the bird very well.
2. Tie and bridle its legs and small wings and introduce into the body a truffle about the size of a hazelnut, wrapped in good butter from Isigny cows and about the size of a plum.
3. Season inside with salt, pepper to the diner's taste, and close the opening with a bread crumb.
4. Cover the quail completely with a very thin fresh bacon leaf and place the bird, thus prepared, in a baking dish with a hermetically sealed lid.
5. Cook for one hour in a water bath and serve on a fried bread crouton as a lunch dish.
