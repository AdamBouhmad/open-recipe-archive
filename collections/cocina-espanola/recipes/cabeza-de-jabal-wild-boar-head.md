## Ingredients

- Head of a wild boar
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg in powder
- Parsley, finely chopped
- Chives, finely chopped
- Tongue
- Brains
- Ears
- Loin of the same wild boar
- Pork lard, melted
- Toasted and sifted breadcrumbs
- Kitchen twine
- Truffle slices (optional)
- Pistachio slices (optional)

## Directions

1. Singe and clean the head of a wild boar with great care, split it lengthwise underneath and remove all the bones without damaging the skin.
2. Divide into very neat strips all the edible parts: tongue, meat, brains; add the ears, cut in the same manner, and if this seems insufficient to fill the deboned head, complete the filling with a little loin from the same wild boar.
3. Place all these pieces in a trough, in overlapping layers, sprinkling each layer with salt, pepper, and powdered nutmeg, finely chopped parsley and chives. Leave the dish in this brine for six days in summer and at least ten days in winter.
4. Fill the head with everything that was in the trough and reassemble it, sewing closed the split from where the bones and edible parts were removed.
5. Secure the head very well with kitchen twine, taking care not to deform it or correcting any slight imperfection, and thus well tied, cook it for ten hours in plenty of water and over low heat in a spacious pot.
6. Once the head is cooked, remove it from the vessel and wrap it in a napkin to squeeze and compress it well with your hands so that all the cooking water drains out.
7. Remove the napkin and twine, place it on a platter, and with a kitchen brush give it a coat of melted pork lard, and sprinkle it with toasted, sifted, and finely ground breadcrumbs so that the grain is uniform.
8. Present the head at the table cold and cut it into vertical slices, beginning with the fattest part and ending with the snout.
9. This preparation will turn out finer if truffle slices and pistachios are added with the viands used to fill the deboned head.
