## Ingredients

- rabbit
- 30 grams butter
- 3 tablespoons oil
- 1 pinch nutmeg
- 2 pinches mixed spices
- 2 pinches salt
- 2 pinches pepper
- 25 grams flour
- 2 deciliters white wine
- 1 deciliter broth
- 20 grams small onions, chopped
- 1 tablespoon chopped parsley

## Directions

1. Clean and gut the rabbit and cut it into pieces.
2. Place in a flat sauté pan: 30 grams butter, 3 tablespoons oil, 1 pinch nutmeg, 2 pinches of mixed spices, 2 pinches salt, and 2 pinches pepper.
3. When the butter is melted, place the rabbit pieces in the sauté pan over high heat for twenty minutes.
4. After this time, remove the rabbit and place on a serving dish.
5. In the same pan add 25 grams of flour, stirring circularly over the fire for one minute.
6. Add two deciliters of white wine and one deciliter of broth.
7. Bring to a boil for five minutes, then strain the sauce through a chinois strainer.
8. Clean the sauté pan and return the sauce and rabbit to it.
9. Add 20 grams of chopped small onions, well washed, and 1 tablespoon of chopped parsley.
10. Bring to a boil and serve at the first boil in the sauce.
11. Do not cook further as boiling hardens them after the first boil.
12. If necessary, the rabbit can be sautéed in an ordinary pan over the flame, proceeding the same way but taking care to constantly sauté the rabbit so it cooks evenly.
