## Ingredients

- Half a lamb
- Thin strips of bacon
- Salt
- Pepper
- Fine herbs
- A trace of beef butter
- A ladleful of broth
- Beef butter, melted and lukewarm
- Breadcrumb coating well salted and peppered

## Directions

1. Separate the shoulder blade and debone it completely.
2. Lard it with thin strips of bacon and season with salt, pepper and fine herbs.
3. Tie and secure it well in a rounded form that bulges on one side.
4. Braise it in a pot with a trace of beef butter and a ladleful of broth.
5. Continue quartering what remains of the half lamb: the ribcage, the breast, the flank, the hind quarter, etc., and cook all of it together in the juice and seasoning of the shoulder blade, which will already be braised.
6. When cooking is finished, strain the sauce, degrease it and reduce it if it is clear.
7. Allow the ribcage and shoulder blade to cool, but compress them beforehand between two pot lids so they do not deform as they cool.
8. Once cold, pass and repass them through melted and lukewarm beef butter and coat them with finely grated breadcrumb well salted and peppered, giving them color on the grill over a strong fire.
9. Separately, sauté the small chops as described for mutton, and roast on a spit the leg and all the pieces already prepared.
10. Place the shoulder blade, removing the string, in the center of a serving dish, and arrange all the other pieces around it, making the difference of each piece of meat and its special seasoning evident to the eye.
11. Pour over the dish thus arranged the sauce from the cooking, which can be increased with meat juice, incorporating in all cases lemon juice.
12. Serve this dish very hot.
