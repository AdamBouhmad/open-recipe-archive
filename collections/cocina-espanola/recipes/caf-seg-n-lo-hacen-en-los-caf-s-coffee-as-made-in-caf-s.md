## Ingredients

- 200 grams of roasted and ground coffee of the day
- Fifteen cups of prepared water
- A few grams of fish glue
- 50 grams of chicory

## Directions

1. Fill two-thirds of a large coffeepot with water and the rest with residue from previously made coffee.
2. Heat until it boils, then remove from the fire and let the liquid settle.
3. When it clears, decant it; this water is what will be used to make the new coffee.
4. Remove and discard the old coffee residue from the coffeepot.
5. Clean the coffeepot and add 200 grams of roasted and ground coffee of the day for each fifteen cups of prepared water.
6. Dissolve a few grams of fish glue in the water and add 50 grams of chicory.
7. Stir well with a spatula and place the vessel over high heat, covered hermetically.
8. As soon as it has boiled for two or three seconds only, remove from the fire and let the coffee clear, stirring well.
9. Decant carefully by pouring in a thin stream, transferring it to an earthenware container that is kept from that moment in a bain-marie, taking care that it does not boil.
