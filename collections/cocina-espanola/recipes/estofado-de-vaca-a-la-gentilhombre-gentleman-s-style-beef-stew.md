## Ingredients

- A solid piece of beef, preferably from the top round
- Thick strips of fresh bacon for larding
- A veal hand (foreleg), deboned
- A bouquet of fine herbs
- One large onion
- Two or three cloves
- One quarter of a liter of good broth per kilogram of meat
- White wine from Rueda or Yepes, one quarter liter per kilogram of meat
- Half a dozen carrots, trimmed
- Two tablespoons of strong vinegar
- A thin, wide slice of bacon as wide as the palm of the hand

## Directions

1. Two hours before cooking begins, soak the piece of beef in white wine from Rueda or Yepes in the proportion of one quarter liter per kilogram of meat, turning the meat frequently so that the soaking is uniform
2. Season and spice the meat raw, after larding it with fairly thick strips of fresh bacon
3. Place the prepared meat in a braising pot with the deboned veal hand, a bouquet of fine herbs, and one large onion in which two or three cloves are studded
4. Pour over this a bowl of good broth and let it cook very slowly for one hour
5. After one hour, turn the meat and add the white wine that was used for soaking, along with half a dozen trimmed carrots, and continue cooking very slowly until the meat is well done
6. When one hour remains to finish cooking, add two tablespoons of strong vinegar
7. Although the meat bathes in the broth, place over it, like a cap, in the same liquid, a thin and wide slice of bacon as wide as the palm of the hand
8. The broth must bathe the meat for at least four-fifths of the cooking time
9. When serving hot, degrease the broth very well, which will then take on a gelatinous consistency
10. Place the piece of meat on a round platter, and the veal hand and vegetables, cut into equal-sized pieces, are arranged around it, pouring the broth over in small quantity, only enough to moisten and soak the dish
