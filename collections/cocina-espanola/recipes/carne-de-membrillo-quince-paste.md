## Ingredients

- very ripe and yellow quinces
- water
- sugar in proportion of five pounds sugar to six pounds fruit

## Directions

1. Choose very ripe and yellow quinces and prepare them to heat the syrup afterwards, which should be in the proportion of six pounds of fruit to five pounds of sugar; peel the quinces and put them with sufficient quantity of water in a small cauldron, which should be on the fire so that the quinces do not turn black, and add them in pieces as they are peeled, in hot water, taking care that the boiling does not stop from when it begins, so that it does not take on the color of wine and is well yellow and pleasant to the sight
2. When the water does not cover them well, always add more, well heated and boiled, in no way cold; as soon as the pieces are well cooked and soft, but still whole, remove them with a skimmer and spread them on a clean cloth, and when cold, pound them well in a marble mortar or pass them through a fairly open strainer
3. Make a thick syrup of half a cuartilla of water per pound of sugar, and well clarified, pour into it—cold or hot, it makes no difference—the prepared quince paste, put it on the fire and stir until it takes the consistency of preserve; this is also poured while hot into glasses, jars or small tin boxes, and after four days have passed; if the open boxes are placed in an oven for half an hour, a thin film forms which preserves the sweet for a long time
