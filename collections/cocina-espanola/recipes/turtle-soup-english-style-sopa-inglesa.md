## Ingredients

- 4 liters of water
- 2 kilos of beef sirloin (solomillo)
- 2 or 3 carrots
- 1 onion
- 1 celery stalk
- 1 bunch of fine herbs
- 20 grams of pepper
- 30 grams of salt
- 1 kilo of lean veal with little bone
- half a calf's head, well deboned, without brains and without tongue
- 250 grams of very lean ham
- 4 shallots
- 120 grams of beef butter
- 6 cloves
- a little nutmeg
- 1 sprig of thyme
- 3 coffee spoons of finely chopped parsley
- half a lemon in slices
- 60 grams of fine flour
- Cayenne pepper
- juice of 1 lemon
- 2 glasses of Sherry
- 2 dozen quenelles of meat or poultry breast

## Directions

1. In a pot or marmite with 4 liters of water, add 2 kilos of beef sirloin, 2 or 3 carrots, 1 onion, 1 celery stalk, 1 bunch of fine herbs, 20 grams of pepper and 30 grams of salt.
2. When the meat is well cooked, strain the broth, let it cool and put it back on the fire with 1 kilo of lean veal with very little bone. Let it cook slowly until the meat separates from the bone, being careful that the boiling does not accelerate so that the broth does not reduce too much.
3. Separately, have ready half a calf's head, well deboned, without brains and without tongue. These should have been cooked beforehand to reinforce the substance of the soup with their broth.
4. Pass the first broth through a hair sieve, letting the meat drain well. Let it cool to remove the solidified fat. Then wrap the half calf's head like a cloth, securing it with twine, and place this package and the tongue loose in a marmite, pouring the first cold broth over it and letting it cook over low heat for three quarters of an hour, or at most one hour.
5. Place the half calf's head and the tongue in a deep baking dish, pour the broth over it and let it cool. This precaution is so that the calf's head does not take on color.
6. On the other hand, cut 250 grams of very lean ham into very thin slices, which are then subdivided into small square pieces, and mince 4 shallots.
7. In a 4-liter pot, melt 120 grams of beef butter; add the ham with the shallots, half a dozen cloves, a little nutmeg, 1 sprig of thyme, 3 coffee spoons of finely chopped parsley, and half a lemon in slices.
8. Keep this mixture on the fire for one hour, being careful that the butter does not blacken and constantly sautéing the contents of the pot.
9. Then add 60 grams of fine flour gradually, and after cooking this for a few minutes, pour the defatted broth free of the sediment that forms at the bottom of the baking dish in small portions.
10. Keep everything on the fire, but without cooking, for four hours, and after that time strain the broth through a sieve, passing it to another vessel. Cut the calf's head and tongue into fine and equal pieces, and as if they were slices of bread, add them to the broth.
11. For this soup to be more delicate, cut the skin and fatty parts of the calf's head into thin leaves; when the soup is for a family meal, without guests, cut the meat of the head and glutinous parts at the same time, but it is preferable to set aside the lean meat, even if the soup remains clearer.
12. With everything prepared this way, put the soup in a baking dish and let it cook for one hour, so that the calf's head is tender. Add a touch of Cayenne pepper and a little salt if the seasoning does not seem very spiced.
13. Three minutes before serving, add the juice of 1 lemon, 2 glasses of Sherry and 2 dozen quenelles of meat or poultry breast to the turtle soup.
