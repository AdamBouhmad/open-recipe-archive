## Ingredients

- a piece of beef lard or a couple of tablespoons of pork lard
- flour in sufficient quantity

## Directions

1. Place the lard in a saucepan
2. Heat the fire considerably, and when the flour has taken on color, stir continuously with a wooden spatula
3. Moisten with broth from the pot, clear water, or the same broth for which the brown sauce is being made
4. Reheat at medium fire in a saucepan, flour in beef or pork lard until everything takes on a truly golden color
5. Without ceasing to stir, once the brown sauce has taken on this color, cover the saucepan hermetically, remove from heat and place some coals on the lid when there is no oven, so that the sauce continues cooking for half an hour without risk of burning from direct fire
6. From time to time uncover the vessel to stir the sauce
7. The flour cooked properly, without tasting burned, will give the brown sauce a good color tone and an exquisite flavor
8. Later moisten with broth, water or as stated previously, without seasoning at all, as this remains for the moment when the brown sauce is incorporated into a dish
