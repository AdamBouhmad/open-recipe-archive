## Ingredients

- two large tablespoons of fine flour
- cold water
- two ounces of beef lard
- salt
- a touch of pepper
- one egg yolk without the germ
- two tablespoons of cold water

## Directions

1. In a metal saucepan, add two large tablespoons of fine flour
2. Gradually, with cold water and a boxwood spatula, knead the mixture away from the fire on the kitchen counter
3. Once the dough is formed and without stopping stirring, continue adding water to produce a very clear and uniform liquid
4. Place the saucepan on low heat and continue stirring with the spatula very quickly until the mixture begins to boil
5. Then remove from the heat and leave in a place where it can continue cooking very slowly for ten minutes without needing further attention
6. At the moment of serving, remove the saucepan from the heat and add two ounces of beef lard, salt and a touch of pepper
7. Stir vigorously to mix everything and activate the melting of the lard, and add the liaison
8. The liaison is one egg yolk without the germ, dissolved in two tablespoons of cold water in a glass, all this without ceasing the movement with the spatula
