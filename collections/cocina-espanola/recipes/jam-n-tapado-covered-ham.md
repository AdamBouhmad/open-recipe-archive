## Ingredients

- one ounce of beef butter
- six slices of ham, large as the palm of the hand and thin as the edge of two duros together
- ham broth
- one glass of Jerez wine
- pinch of pepper
- six eggs

## Directions

1. Melt one ounce of beef butter in a dish that can go to the fire or in a skillet for six ham slices
2. When the butter is sizzling, sauté the ham slices one by one until they pass and blanch, taking care that they do not toast
3. Remove the dish from the fire and place all the ham slices in a single layer, lining the bottom of the plate
4. Put everything back on the fire and moisten with a ladle of broth and a glass of Jerez wine with a pinch of pepper
5. Drop six eggs one by one on top so they set and cover the ham, which is only discovered when serving at the table
