## Ingredients

- turbot
- sea water or very salty water
- two lemon halves
- beef butter, raw
- salt

## Directions

1. Rub the turbot on all sides with two lemon halves before placing it in the water.
2. Cook the turbot in a turbotera (a diamond-shaped cooking vessel) with short broth, using sea water or, in its absence, very salty water.
3. The fish is cooked as soon as the water reaches its first boil, and it can be served immediately, or it can remain in the water away from heat for as long as desired without the cooking point being altered.
4. Remove the turbot from the turbotera and place it on a platter over a well-pressed napkin that serves as a bed for the fish.
5. Serve melted beef butter that has been warmed in a bain-marie with a pinch of salt in a sauce boat.
6. When the turbot is served cold, each diner seasons it to taste with oil and vinegar.
7. Garnish the platter with fresh parsley branches, whether served hot or cold.
