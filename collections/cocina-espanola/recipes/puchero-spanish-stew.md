## Ingredients

- 2 liters of water
- half kilogram of beef chuck
- 1 veal hand
- 1 marrow bone
- 1 piece of pork knuckle the size of a chocolate ounce
- chickpeas (soaked in cold water and salt 6 hours before)
- salt
- 1 small piece of aged bacon
- 1 bouquet of parsley
- 2 bay leaves
- 3 or 4 leeks, cut and tied in a bundle
- 1 onion with 2 cloves studded in it
- 2 or 3 turnips
- 2 or 3 carrots
- burned sugar or caramel for coloring
- 4 potatoes the size of an egg

## Directions

1. Six hours before starting, soak the chickpeas in cold water and salt.
2. Pour 2 liters of water into a 3-liter pot and place over heat.
3. When the water boils vigorously, add half kilogram of beef chuck, then a veal hand, then a marrow bone, and a piece of pork knuckle the size of a chocolate ounce, adding them gradually and successively so the boiling does not interrupt.
4. While the water continues to boil vigorously, add the chickpeas and salt.
5. Cook everything over high heat for half an hour with great violence in an uncovered pot.
6. Then add a small piece of aged bacon, a bouquet of parsley, 2 bay leaves, 3 or 4 leeks cut and tied in a bundle, an onion with 2 cloves studded in it, 2 or 3 turnips, and 2 or 3 carrots.
7. Without stopping the boil, taste and adjust seasoning, then fill the pot completely with boiling water.
8. Skim off about 6 spoonfuls of fat, then color with a bit of burned sugar or caramel.
9. From this moment, half-cover the pot and adjust so cooking is very slow and continuous for 4 hours.
10. One hour before serving, add 4 potatoes the size of an egg.
11. To serve the broth, strain through a colander lined with fine linen cloth.
