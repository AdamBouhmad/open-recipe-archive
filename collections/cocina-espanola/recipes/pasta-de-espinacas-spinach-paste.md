## Ingredients

- Spinaches
- Water
- Coarse salt
- One quarter pound of beef or pork butter
- Flour, a couple of tablespoons
- Water from cooking the vegetables
- One egg yolk
- Water, two tablespoons
- Croutons
- Hard boiled eggs

## Directions

1. Wash the spinaches and cut them in any manner.
2. According to the volume they make, place on high heat a pot with water in quantity four or six times greater than that of the vegetable.
3. When it boils in bubbles, add the spinaches and coarse salt by handfuls—that is, a great deal of salt. After ten minutes of violent boiling of the water, and without covering the vessel, remove the spinaches and submerge them in a basin or bucket of cold water.
4. Remove them from there immediately and place in fresh cold water, leaving them for five minutes, after which add them one last time in another cold water, where they can remain ten minutes, or as long as an hour, until beginning to season them.
5. Remove the spinaches from the water, drain and discharge all the water they contain, and set aside on a platter.
6. In a pan or baking dish, melt afterwards one quarter pound of beef or pork butter, and before it is very russet add a couple of tablespoons of flour, and without letting it take color moisten it all to form a somewhat clear pap with the water in which the vegetables cooked; season well, let cook a couple of minutes and remove from the heat.
7. Once the spinaches are dry, chop them very finely on a board, and with their own water, in small quantities, pass the resolved paste through a fine sieve, collecting it in the vessel in which the pap is prepared.
8. Mix and blend well; give it a boil so that all that mass comes together, and away from the heat bind it with an egg yolk, dissolved in two tablespoons of water.
9. To serve this dish place it on a round platter, in a heap or spherical casque, studding its surface with croutons and hard boiled eggs split in half and symmetrically presented.
