## Ingredients

- Two chickens
- 125 to 250 grams of beef or pork butter, depending on the importance of the dish and its garnish
- Salt
- Pepper
- One tablespoon of flour
- Plenty of mushroom scrapings
- Rooster crests and crayfish
- Two ladles of clean broth
- Small onions, double or triple the number of pieces of poultry

## Directions

1. Quarter two chickens and place the pieces in fresh water mixed with white wine or two tablespoons of vinegar
2. Drain and rinse, and place in a pot over low heat with 125 or 250 grams of beef or pork butter
3. Season with salt, pepper, one tablespoon of flour, plenty of mushroom scrapings, rooster crests and crayfish
4. The chicken pieces should not take color in the fat, but rather firm up their meat
5. Moisten with two ladles of clean broth and cook over high heat for up to one hour
6. Half an hour before serving the dish, add very small onions to the pot in double or triple the number of pieces of poultry
7. Bind the sauce after removing the chicken and placing it in a pyramid on a platter
8. Pour the sauce over it without letting the mushrooms and onions fall, then arrange them with a spoon to garnish the fricassee with art and symmetry
