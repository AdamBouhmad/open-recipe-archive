## Ingredients

- One liter of water
- Two pounds of lamb leg, cut into very small pieces, cleaned of fat, tendons and skin
- Oregano
- One large onion the size of an egg, finely chopped
- Half a tablespoon of paprika
- One heaping tablespoon of lard
- Potatoes, cut into pieces the size of coins

## Directions

1. Put a liter of water to boil in a pot and at the first boil, add two pounds of lamb leg cut into very small pieces, cleaned of fat, tendons and skin.
2. Cook the meat over slow and steady heat for three hours, without needing to skim the broth.
3. Season and add a little oregano, one large onion the size of an egg finely chopped, half a tablespoon of paprika, and one heaping tablespoon of lard.
4. Continue cooking the broth for a quarter hour.
5. Thirty minutes before serving the dish, which with these quantities is calculated for six people, add potatoes cut into pieces the size of coins.
6. This stew-soup must be very brothy, so care should be taken during cooking, and if the broth reduces or is consumed, add water before seasoning.
7. Serve with a spoon.
