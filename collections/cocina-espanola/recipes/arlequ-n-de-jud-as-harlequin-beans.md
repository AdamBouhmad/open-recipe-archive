## Ingredients

- handful of fine long green beans like those from Gandía
- handful of fresh shelled beans like those from La Granja
- salt
- pepper
- two ounces of beef butter for each pound of the dish
- fine herbs chopped
- clove
- nutmeg grated
- half lemon

## Directions

1. Cook together a handful of each class of beans in boiling water with salt and a pinch of pepper.
2. At fifteen minutes remove them and drain them, and keeping them hot, place them in a vegetable dish where there will be a mixture of two ounces of beef butter—for each pound of the dish—finely chopped herbs, salt, pepper and a pinch of clove and grated nutmeg.
3. Cover the vegetable dish with a pot lid so as not to dirty its own cover, and holding it well with both hands, shake it in the air so that everything mixes very well with the butter that melts from the heat of the beans.
4. Then place the vegetable dish at the mouth of a pot to keep it hot, and place its lid. At the moment of serving squeeze the juice of half a lemon over the dish.
5. Sprinkle chopped parsley generously, because it gives very good appearance and facilitates digestion.
