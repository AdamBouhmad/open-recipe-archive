## Ingredients

- one whole sirloin, cleaned of fat, skin, and tendons
- 125 grams of beef butter for six regular-sized beefsteaks
- salt
- pepper
- parsley, finely chopped
- a few drops of lemon juice
- fresh butter, very fresh

## Directions

1. From a whole sirloin cleaned of fat, skin, and small tendons, cut twelve regular beefsteaks, neither very thick nor very thin, cutting the meat against the grain with a long, sharp knife blade.
2. Profile each beefsteak in a round shape by trimming the edges, and then on the cutting board, beat both sides with a heavy, long boxwood mallet.
3. Sprinkle salt on both faces of the beefsteak.
4. Melt 125 grams of beef butter over low heat in a saucepan for six regular-sized beefsteaks, and while the butter is liquid but not very hot, dip the beefsteaks in it on both sides using a fork.
5. Then place all of them together on the grill over coals that should not be too lively nor half-extinguished.
6. When the beefsteaks have been well seized by the fire and have taken on color without being burned, making the cooking give firmness to their meat, then remove them from the fire.
7. Once the beefsteaks are done, place them on a round platter in the form of a crown, and in the center place, for six beefsteaks, 125 grams of very fresh butter, kneaded with salt, pepper, and very finely chopped parsley, seasoned with a few drops of lemon juice.
8. The beefsteaks are served with the maitre d'hôtel sauce as described.
9. The platter in which the dish is served must be very hot beforehand, submerged in boiling water, so that the beef butter melts and remains fluid with the heat of the meat.
10. The beefsteaks may be served English-style with fried potatoes covering the plate literally, or with potatoes cooked in steam and cut into slices, which placed in the center will take on the seasoning of the beefsteak.
