## Ingredients

- Ham
- Carrots
- Onions
- Cloves
- Nutmeg
- Peppercorns
- Thyme
- Bay leaves
- Water
- Toasted breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Prepare the ham by removing all the exposed surface meat and trimming the edges of fat which are always yellowish.
2. Saw off the tip of the bone with a bit of the knuckle, which can be used whole or divided in the pot.
3. Desalt the ham for one, two or three days depending on how salty it is, in an iron pot or ham kettle filled with water.
4. Remove and wrap in a strong white linen cloth, tying and pressing with the four corners.
5. Place to cook in a braising pot in plenty of water so the meat is bathed.
6. Add carrots, onions, cloves, nutmeg, peppercorns, thyme and many bay leaves.
7. Cook all this for five hours over low heat.
8. Check if done by inserting a larding needle: if it enters without resistance, the ham is cooked.
9. Remove the ham from the pot and untie the cloth knots to debone it with great care.
10. Tie the cloth again but pressing tightly, and place in a deep dish until completely cooled.
11. Remove the cloth completely and lift off the ham rind, which comes off easily.
12. Sprinkle the surface that was covered by the rind with toasted breadcrumbs passed through a sieve.
13. Decorate the handle with a paper bow.
14. Serve on a well-pressed napkin in an oval dish when ready to eat.
