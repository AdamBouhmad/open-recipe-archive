## Ingredients

- Pure spring water
- Bacon
- Repollo (cabbage)
- Chard or Moorish chard
- Fresh tender beans
- Lettuce
- Cauliflower
- Cardoon
- Escarole
- Salt
- Various vegetables

## Directions

1. First put pure spring water so that the stew does not retain the smell of vegetables cooked the day before
2. When the water is very hot, or better, boiling, add the bacon
3. Three hours before eating, add the cabbage, and two hours before, the Castilian or Moorish chard; both are added while the stew is boiling and stirring from time to time with a wooden spoon
4. If using fresh tender beans, add the chard or lettuce first, and an hour later the beans; if they are already very large, add them before the vegetables, and if they are dry, soak them beforehand and remove the eye, then put them in cold water like all dried legumes; fresh ones of all kinds in boiling water
5. Also cooked vegetables, such as cauliflower, cardoon, lettuce, escarole and others, should be put in boiling water, adding salt with the vegetable when the first boil breaks
6. The most tender ones need an hour and a half of continuous boiling so they do not lose color; the hardest ones two or three hours
