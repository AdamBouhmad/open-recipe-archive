## Ingredients

- four dozen beautiful chestnuts
- water
- sugar syrup at 16 degrees
- one-quarter of a vanilla pod

## Directions

1. Remove the shells from four dozen beautiful chestnuts and place them in fresh water
2. Place them on the fire in a pot with capacity of two liters with one and a half liters of water, and blanch them; that is, remove the inner skin or peel, and place them in a pan, avoiding that some rest on top of others
3. Cover with syrup at 16 degrees, and then make the mixture tremble over the fire, just barely, for just under half an hour
4. When the chestnuts are cooked, drain them in a sieve
5. Strain the syrup through a chinois strainer and reduce it to 30 degrees
6. Arrange the chestnuts in the compote dish and water them with the syrup
7. To the chestnut compote, made as just described, add one-quarter of a vanilla pod
