## Ingredients

- Small fish such as red mullet, sea bass, tinoso, scorpion fish, pica, mácete, gilt-head bream, escamón, and barbudas
- Langostinos (Norway lobsters)
- Amasuelas (clams)
- Lapas (limpets)
- Other shellfish
- Onions
- Parsley
- Black pepper
- Red pepper paste
- Valencia olive oil
- Superior Sherry wine
- Nutmeg
- Guindilla (hot pepper)

## Directions

1. Wash and clean all fish and shellfish with sea water, ensuring the fish is very fresh
2. Cut onion into large slices and chop parsley
3. Half an hour before the scheduled meal time, place onion slices on the bottom of a wide, low pan with a tight-fitting lid
4. Layer the poorest quality fish on top, then cover with onion slices and chopped parsley
5. Sprinkle with black pepper, nutmeg, salt, red pepper paste, and guindilla, and add some of the shellfish
6. Add another layer of better quality fish and cover again with onion, parsley, pepper, guindilla, nutmeg, red pepper paste, and shellfish
7. Continue layering until the pan is full, placing the best quality fish (such as bream and sea bass) on top as a final layer
8. Pour half a bottle of olive oil and half a bottle of Sherry wine over everything
9. Cover the pan hermetically and place a weight on top
10. Prepare a wood fire with large flames and place the pan on stones over the fire
11. Heat until it comes to a boil, then remove from the flame and maintain the boil with hot coals underneath for uniform cooking
12. Cook for fifteen minutes until ready to serve
13. Serve in the same pan, with an experienced person serving to avoid breaking the fish and to ensure good distribution of sauce and shellfish among all diners
