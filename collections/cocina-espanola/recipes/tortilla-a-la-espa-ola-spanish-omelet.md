## Ingredients

- twelve eggs
- half a pound of potatoes
- lard or oil in large quantity
- salt
- pepper
- three tablespoons of fat

## Directions

1. Fry the potatoes cut like coins in lard or oil in large quantity.
2. Before they toast or become crispy, remove them from the pan and set aside on a plate, sprinkling them with a little salt.
3. Remove excess fat from the pan, leaving three tablespoons, and put the pan back over high heat.
4. At this moment, the twelve egg whites should be beaten beforehand, separately, until foamy, and the twelve yolks, also well beaten, with two tablespoons of fresh water and the seasoning of salt and pepper that the omelet is to have.
5. Mix the whites and yolks and beat them together while the pan is on the fire.
6. When the fat smokes blue, without stopping beating, pour the mixture into the pan and beat it still inside for a few seconds, during which the set-aside potatoes are incorporated.
7. With the left hand shake the pan in a circle, imprinting it with a circular movement so that the omelet, as soon as it sets, begins to turn on its base, but in the opposite direction.
8. That is the moment to turn it over to the other side, using a tinplate kitchen utensil if needed.
9. With this apparatus turn the omelet at will, and proceed in the same way so that it sets on the other side.
10. As the setting becomes firmer, increase the shaking so that the dish colors equally, according to the operator's preference.
11. The Spanish omelet should be solid, well-combined, round and of great thickness.
