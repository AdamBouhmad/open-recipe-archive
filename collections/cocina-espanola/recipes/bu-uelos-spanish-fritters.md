## Ingredients

- eggs
- flour
- sugar
- cinnamon
- frying fat (3 pounds)
- paper strips
- confectionery or similar toppings

## Directions

1. Crack one egg into a saucepan and stir with a spoon; when well mixed, add two more eggs, one after the other; mix well, and if the paste is too hard, add half an egg or a whole egg as needed.
2. To test the consistency of the paste, take a quantity the size of an egg on a spoon and let it drip. It should not spread nor remain stuck to the spoon, but should be pulled down by its own weight.
3. When the dough is finished, dust the table with flour, and place a quarter of the dough on it; dust this also with flour so it does not stick to the table or hands, and cut into pieces to form balls of two and a half centimeters, which are lined up on strips of paper four centimeters wide, which will have been lightly greased with frying fat.
4. Put three pounds of frying fat in a frying pan, which is tested by frying a small piece of bread that should barely quiver; place the pan beside the stove; dip the paper strip in the frying fat and remove when the fritters have separated; move with a skimmer and increase the heat of the frying fat by gradually moving the pan to the fire until it is completely over the stove.
5. When the fritters are golden and dry, drain them on a clean kitchen cloth, dust with sugar and arrange in a pyramid on a platter over a folded napkin.
