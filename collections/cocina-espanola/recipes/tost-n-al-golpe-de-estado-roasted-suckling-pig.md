## Ingredients

- One suckling pig, fifteen to twenty days old
- Water
- Salt
- Bacon rind
- Small wooden sticks or reeds for supports

## Directions

1. Select a young suckling pig between fifteen and twenty days of age.
2. Kill the animal and submerge it completely in a caldron of boiling water.
3. Once clean and white, split it down the belly, opening it lengthwise from the snout to the tail inclusive.
4. Empty it entirely and wash it well inside and out, drying it thoroughly with burlap.
5. Extend it as if it were tanned hide and pierce it lengthwise with the spit of the roaster, using small canes or reeds as crosspieces so that it does not close.
6. Prepare a simple brine of water and salt.
7. With a kitchen brush dipped in this brine, coat and moisten it well on all sides.
8. Place it before a fire of burning wood coals, turning it constantly.
9. At each turn, rub it with a piece of good bacon rind, then moisten it again with the brine, until the skin forms blisters and acquires a hazelnut color.
10. One and a half hours should suffice for the operation.
11. The bacon rind is preferable to butter because butter softens what is toasting, while bacon makes it crisp and friable.
