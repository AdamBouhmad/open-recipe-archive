## Ingredients

- one good hen
- one beautiful partridge
- one pound of well-cured ham, trimmed into very thin slices
- one pound of veal shoulder
- half a dozen bird feet, charred beforehand on grills
- a bundle of fine herbs
- two carrots
- two onions
- some spoonfuls of concentrated broth
- two eggs with shells
- two spoonfuls of broth
- one glass of vinegar
- one glass of good white wine

## Directions

1. Place the hen, partridge, ham, veal shoulder, charred bird feet, bundle of fine herbs, carrots, and onions in a pot with some spoonfuls of concentrated broth.
2. Cook all this over low heat, and when the juice has taken on good color, add broth in sufficient quantity to cover everything in the pot.
3. Let cook for three hours over gentle heat, after skimming and seasoning appropriately.
4. Pass the boiling juice through a moistened napkin and let cool.
5. Break two eggs in a terrine, and crushing the shells well, beat them together with the yolks and whites, two spoonfuls of broth, one glass of vinegar, and another glass of good white wine.
6. Add all this to the cooled and well-degreased major sauce and place the pot over high heat.
7. Remove the sauce from heat before it begins to boil, cover the pot with a lid upon which good coals are placed, and keep the vessel near the fire.
8. From time to time, uncover the pot to see how the clarification operation is proceeding, and as soon as it is at the right point, strain again through the moistened napkin.
