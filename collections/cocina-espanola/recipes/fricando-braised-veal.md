## Ingredients

- a piece of veal shoulder, about three pounds
- thin strips of bacon
- broth
- sorrel

## Directions

1. Debone and lard a piece of veal shoulder of three pounds with thin strips of bacon.
2. Place the meat in a braising pan with two deciliters of broth.
3. Put on the heat and let it boil until the broth is reduced, preventing the meat from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
4. When the juice of the meat takes on a yellowish tint and becomes consistent and gummy, add another six deciliters of broth.
5. Let it cook very gently for one hour and a quarter with very slow boiling, the pan half covered with its lid.
6. After this time of slow cooking, remove the lid and replace with a flat iron lid with a shovelful of coals on top.
7. Baste the veal every four minutes until a yellowish and shiny tint appears; this operation serves to concentrate.
8. Arrange the meat in the serving dish.
9. Strain the juice through a chinois strainer; decrease it and pour it over the piece of veal.
10. Place the sorrel puree in the serving dish first; the piece of veal on the sorrel and baste with the juice.
