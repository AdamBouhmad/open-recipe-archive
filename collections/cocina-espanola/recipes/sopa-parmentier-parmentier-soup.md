## Ingredients

- one and a half liters of good pot broth
- one pound of potatoes of the best class among the most floury
- half a quartillo of milk
- one tablespoon of flour
- 50 grams of beef butter
- a couple of tablespoons of chervil
- parsley
- spearmint

## Directions

1. Cook one pound of potatoes in one and a half liters of good pot broth
2. Break them up and mash with a fork
3. Pass through a food mill to form a light pap, which is then thickened over low heat
4. Add half a quartillo of milk in which one tablespoon of flour is well dissolved
5. At the moment of boiling, and away from the fire, melt 50 grams of beef butter in the soup
6. Add a couple of tablespoons of very finely chopped chervil, or in its place, one of parsley with a hint of spearmint
7. In the tureen when the soup is poured, there will be small squares of fried bread; but for the soup to have all its character, instead of bread, put some cooked potatoes cut raw into small balls with a vegetable-shaped knife
