## Ingredients

- One leg of mutton
- Fresh cow butter
- Ground salt
- Optionally: garlic clove

## Directions

1. Allow the leg of mutton to age for two days in summer or three to four days in winter, hanging in a cool pantry or larder
2. Beat the meat vigorously on both sides with a wooden mallet to tenderize it
3. Place the leg on a vertical spit roaster as shown on page 197
4. Begin with a lively fire
5. If the spit does not have mechanical movement, turn the meat frequently
6. When presenting the meat to the fire, lightly grease it with very fresh cow butter
7. Dust the entire surface with ground salt to prepare and draw out the juices
8. Continuously baste the meat with its own juices
9. Optionally, embed a garlic clove in the handle of the leg
10. Once the meat has begun to take color, moderate the fire and continue cooking slowly
11. Cook until no violet-red color remains in the interior of the meat, and when cut it is neither dry from overcooking nor bloody from undercooking
12. For a piece of five to six pounds, approximately one and a half hours will suffice
13. Serve from the spit to the table on a very hot platter with the juices in a separate sauceboat
14. Adapt a screw handle to the leg of mutton before serving
15. Decorate the bone or handle with a frilled paper rosette or wrap it elegantly with a tea napkin secured with a ribbon so it can be held with the left hand and carved without fork assistance
