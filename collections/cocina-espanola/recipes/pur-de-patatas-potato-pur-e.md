## Ingredients

- Potatoes of farinaceous variety
- Salted water, one quartillo (approximately 1.15 liters) per two liters of potatoes
- Cow's milk, cooked beforehand, up to half a quartillo
- Two ounces or 60 grams of fresh cow's butter
- One egg yolk
- Two tablespoons of milk
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Directions

1. Steam potatoes of farinaceous variety
2. If large, cut into pieces the size of walnuts
3. Using a purée press with tongs, squeeze the potatoes without great effort, passing the fine paste through the colander attached to the apparatus into a plate below
4. Put a cooking pot on strong heat with one quartillo of salted water for every two liters of potatoes
5. Incorporate the reserved potato paste
6. Without ceasing to stir quickly and vigorously with a boxwood spatula, add cooked cow's milk up to half a quartillo
7. The purée thickens and reaches its point when it boils with bursting bubbles
8. Then remove from strong heat and leave at the side of the stove to cook gently for a quarter of an hour, being careful that the dish does not stick
9. At the moment of serving, remove the pot from the heat and add two ounces or 60 grams of very fresh cow's butter
10. Stir the paste well, holding the pot by a handle, and bind with one egg yolk beaten in two tablespoons of milk in which salt and pepper to taste have been added
