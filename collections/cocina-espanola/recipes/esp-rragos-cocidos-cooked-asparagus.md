## Ingredients

- Asparagus bundles
- Salt
- Water

## Directions

1. Gather asparagus into a bundle without untying it, and cut all the stems together at once so that all the ends are equal, leaving them at a length three times that of the edible part.
2. Untie the bundle, scrape the non-edible extremities, wash very well, and arrange them neatly in a package on the removable bottom of an asparagus cooker box, securing them with a few turns of twine.
3. Place the asparagus cooker box full of water over high heat so that the asparagus submerges well.
4. Add a little salt, and when the water boils vigorously, place the asparagus inside and cook for twelve minutes.
5. Remove the asparagus, cut the twine from the removable bottom, and place them on a silver or metal tray whose flat part or bottom is covered with one or two well-pressed napkins, leaving the edges of the tray uncovered.
6. Arrange the asparagus on the tray with care and skill in two bundles, with the tips of the two bundles touching each other, so that the diner may easily serve themselves by taking them with their own left hand and with the aid of a small spatula in the right hand.
7. Serve with a sauce of choice, accompanied by a double-spouted gravy boat of the same metal, with its saucer in one piece.
