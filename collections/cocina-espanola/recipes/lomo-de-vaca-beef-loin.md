## Ingredients

- Beef loin
- Suet
- Oil
- Salt
- Laurel leaves
- Onion
- Butter
- Paper

## Directions

1. Choose a loin of the best quality and place it in a suet melter with suet in branches; when the suet is about to boil, introduce it with a cord into the pot, leaving it until it is half cooked
2. Allow it to drain and place it in a cool location so that the suet, condensed by the cold, forms a covering and in some way a layer or crust around the loin
3. When you want to roast it, place the spit in front of a very lively fire, taking care to detach the parts of suet that separate in layers; then all the suet will flow, being careful not to baste the loin with it
4. Serve it in slices when cooked, and it will yield such an abundance of juice that it is a true inundation
5. Alternatively, roast the loin by coating it with oil after removing the bone; sprinkle it with fine salt and place some laurel leaves and strips of onion on top; keep it in this condition for one, two or three days depending on the season; pierce it with the spit through the thickest part of the loin, wrap it in paper well soaked in butter, and place it to roast over strong fire, serving a sauce separately
