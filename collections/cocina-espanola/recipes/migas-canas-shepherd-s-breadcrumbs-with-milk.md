## Ingredients

- a good piece of tallow
- half the kidneys of a ewe
- two bread rolls
- half a quart of water
- salt
- milk from freshly milked goats

## Directions

1. Place an iron cauldron over the fire.
2. Add a good piece of tallow to the cauldron, along with half the kidneys of a ewe.
3. While the tallow melts, take a wooden plate that is quite deep and crumble two bread rolls in it as if for soup.
4. Once the tallow is melted and the solid residues have been removed, add one piece of bread to the cauldron and let it brown there for at least two minutes to remove the raw taste from the tallow, then remove it and discard.
5. Add the bread prepared in the wooden plate, crumbled as for soup, and press it slightly with a wooden spoon.
6. Using the wooden spoon held upside down (with the back facing up), pour about half a quart of water over it so it falls scattered, moistening all the bread.
7. Add a bit of well-ground salt immediately.
8. As soon as the cauldron begins to steam, start stirring and crumbling the bread with the spoon, and from time to time take the cauldron by its handle and give it a special shake (a mixture of force and skill), turning over the entire mass so what is below comes to the top and vice versa.
9. After ten more minutes, remove the cauldron from the fire.
10. Make a hole in the middle of the breadcrumbs with the spoon and pour in freshly milked goat's milk, which transforms the dish into migas canas (breadcrumbs with milk).
