## Ingredients

- apple marmalade (prepared as in the previous recipe)
- slices of crumb from French bread two or three days old, half a finger thick
- butter, melted and lukewarm
- apricot marmalade, quince paste, currant jelly, or any other sweet with fluidity

## Directions

1. Prepare an apple marmalade as in the previous recipe, but keep it on the heat longer so that it loses as much water as possible
2. Cut slices of bread crumb into triangular pieces rounded at the base, so that when joined together the point of all of them is the center of a circle
3. Dip the bread slices in lukewarm butter and place them on the bottom of an enameled porcelain pan, covering it completely
4. Line the interior walls of the pan with slices of the same bread, cut into rectangles two fingers wide and as long as the height of the pan, also greased with lukewarm butter
5. Pour the prepared apple marmalade into this mold, and with a spatula make a hollow in the center
6. Without removing the spatula, fill it with apricot marmalade, quince paste that is somewhat loose, currant jelly, or any other sweet that has fluidity and can adapt well there
7. Even out the surface of the dish when the pan is full, and cover it with a garnish of bread equal to that on the bottom
8. Bury the pan up to the rim in hot ashes, and cover the pan with a dome lid well loaded with heat
9. Let the charlotte cook until the bread on top has browned enough, then turn the dish over onto a platter so that the slices of bread from below, which will also be browned, are on top
