## Ingredients

- Potatoes, peeled
- Oil or lard for frying

## Directions

1. Use a quantity of fat four to ten times the volume of all the potatoes
2. Maintain a lively and consistent fire throughout the cooking time
3. Use a splendidly wide and deep pan in relation to the quantities of fat
4. Cut peeled potatoes into thin wheels the size of a coin, into small squares the length of a paper cigar and one centimeter thick, or into thin sticks using a cutting apparatus
5. For thin sticks: hold one handle of the cutting device with the left hand and place the other handle into one of the handles of a bowl positioned on the work table; with the same left hand grasp the other handle of the bowl; with the right hand cut the potatoes by passing them over the cuts of the device, pressing firmly and in one direction
6. When cut into sticks, place immediately into very hot oil and remove at once
7. For wheels or small squares, keep them cut in a bowl of water; when ready to fry, remove with hand, place in a strainer, turn them over to drain a little, and while still wet, place in the hot oil where they will sizzle but fry better
