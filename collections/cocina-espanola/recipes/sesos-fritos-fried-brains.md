## Ingredients

- calf's brains, blanched, chilled, and cut into small slices
- lard
- dried parsley powder
- salt and pepper
- eggs, beaten and seasoned with salt and pepper
- breadcrumbs
- paste for frying (as explained on page 194)
- egg white, beaten clear

## Directions

1. For the first method: Fry the well-prepared brain slices directly in lard. Dust with dried parsley powder, salt, and pepper when serving.
2. For the second method: Coat the brain pieces in beaten seasoned eggs, then bread them with breadcrumbs, dip again in egg, and fry in hot fat.
3. For the third method (fritters, which is best): Cook blanched and chilled brains in a blonde-white sauce with salt, pepper, parsley, and finely chopped fine herbs.
4. Before the brains are completely cooked, let them cool and cut into walnut-sized pieces. Dust with salt and moisten with vinegar. After a quarter hour, drain carefully and coat in frying paste.
5. Fry the breaded pieces in lard that is not too hot. The greater the amount of beaten egg white in the frying paste, the more the fritters will puff up.
6. Fry in abundant fat, serving immediately from pan to table.
7. Serve alternating the fried brain fritters with fried calf's hands prepared the same way, all garnished with fried parsley.
