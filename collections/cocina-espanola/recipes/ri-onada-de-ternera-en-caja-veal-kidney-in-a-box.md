## Ingredients

- veal kidney
- lean ham cut into very fine strips
- fresh beef lard
- parsley, finely chopped
- six shallots
- mushrooms
- salt and pepper
- one glass of Sherry wine
- white paper
- kitchen twine

## Directions

1. Lard the veal kidney with strips of very finely cut lean ham
2. Separately, cook and sauté in very fresh beef lard, taking care they do not burn, a little finely chopped parsley, six shallots and one portion of mushrooms
3. Season with salt and pepper and moisten with one glass of Sherry wine
4. In this juice, sauté the veal kidney for a few minutes, cut open in the shape of an open book as mentioned before
5. Remove the meat from the pan and also remove the fine herbs and vegetables from the juice, well drained, place them on the meat and close the kidney so it recovers the shape it had before being cut
6. Let it cool, and when it is well cold, wrap it four or six times in as many sheets of paper, like making a package, and around the edge or thickness of the meat tie it with kitchen twine
7. Thus prepared, cook the kidney in the previous juice for two hours in a pan over high heat, covered with a dome-shaped lid heavily weighted with fire
8. At the moment of serving, place the kidney on a platter and with scissors cut in a circle the part of paper that corresponds to the surface, leaving everything else, so the meat is as if inside a box
9. To cover the twine that holds and presses the paper, wrap it around with another white paper, which is ruffled or molded and secured with a pin
10. Pour the well-degreased and reduced sauce from the stew over the meat and serve this dish by cutting the meat within its own paper case
