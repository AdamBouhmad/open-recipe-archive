## Ingredients

- minimal quantity of oil possible
- garlic heads
- one dried chili pepper, emptied inside
- one or more other dried chili peppers, finely chopped
- red wine from Priorat or similar fortified wine
- fish of all kinds, blue and white, large and small
- water
- salt

## Directions

1. In an iron cauldron, fry the smallest possible quantity of oil until it is boiling hot.
2. Add the garlic heads and one dried chili pepper emptied inside, which can be removed or left at the consumer's preference.
3. Remove from heat and add the finely chopped dried chili peppers, so that the red vegetable neither burns nor darkens from overcooking.
4. Sprinkle the prepared mixture with red wine from Priorat or similar fortified wine.
5. Clean the fish of all kinds, blue and white, large and small, and cut it into equal pieces.
6. Incorporate the fish into the mixture and add water so that it barely covers the fish during cooking.
7. Cook over high heat for twenty minutes.
8. Serve in clay bowls.
