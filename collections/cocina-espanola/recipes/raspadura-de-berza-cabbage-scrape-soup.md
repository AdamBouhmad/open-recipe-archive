## Ingredients

- Cabbage
- Two or three tablespoons of pork fat
- A good piece of ham
- Broth from the stew pot
- Brown bread or rye bread
- Grated cheese of Gruyere or La Mancha (optional)

## Directions

1. Cut the cabbage finely, wash it and drain it perfectly
2. Put it in a pot with two or three tablespoons of pork fat
3. Sauté over high heat, stirring constantly with a spatula so the cabbage colors evenly without burning
4. Transfer the cabbage to another pot with a good piece of ham
5. Add broth from the stew pot and let it boil until the cabbage is well cooked
6. Drain the contents of the pot well and separate the broth
7. Line the bottom of the pot completely with slices of brown or rye bread
8. Layer with cooked cabbage the thickness of the bread, then bread, then cabbage, and so on until the pot is full, ending with a layer of cabbage
9. Place the pot back over the fire, adding the separated broth in just enough quantity so everything is soaked without oozing
10. Toast in the oven or with fire on the pot lid placed over hot coals
11. Serve in the same pot, saving the ham for another meal or as garnish for another dish
