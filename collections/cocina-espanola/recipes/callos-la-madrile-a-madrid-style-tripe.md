## Ingredients

- Five pounds of tripe for ten persons
- Garlic
- Carrots
- Onions with cloves
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg
- Bay leaf
- Thyme
- Rosemary
- Four veal hands
- A pair of chorizos
- One morcilla (blood sausage)
- Three quarters of a pound of lard
- Six onions
- Twelve cloves of garlic
- Three quarters of a pound of lean, well-cured ham, cut into dice
- Chili pepper
- Fresh or preserved pimiento strips
- Good clean broth
- White wine

## Directions

1. Three days before eating, purchase the tripe from the best tripe shop and wash, clean, and scrape the tripe thoroughly.
2. Place the tripe in a basin and for twenty-four to thirty hours keep it under running water from a fountain spout so that the water in the basin remains fresh.
3. The day before eating, cut the tripe into squares smaller than chocolate ounces and boil in plenty of water in a large pot with garlic, carrots, onions with cloves embedded in them, salt, pepper, nutmeg, bay leaf, thyme, and rosemary.
4. Add four veal hands, a pair of chorizos, and one morcilla, and ensure the cooking, without being vigorous, is animated and even for eight consecutive hours, as any interruption will ruin the dish.
5. When the chorizo and morcilla are cooked, remove and reserve on a plate, then remove the veal hands, bone them carefully, and shred the meat.
6. Once the tripe is cooked, remove from the pot, draining well, and let cool in a basin or large dish for an entire night.
7. The next day, two and a half to three hours before eating, wash the tripe in hot water to eliminate the gelatinous and slimy coating covering it.
8. In a large saucepan over high heat, in three quarters of a pound of lard, sauté six onions and twelve cloves of garlic with three quarters of a pound of lean, well-cured ham cut into dice.
9. Season and add a few points of chili pepper, then add the chorizo and morcilla in small pieces.
10. When the onion takes color, sauté the tripe and veal hands, being careful that nothing sticks to the bottom of the pan, then quickly add good clean broth that covers the dish well.
11. As soon as everything begins to boil, place strips of fresh or preserved pimientos on top and put the tripe in the oven at medium heat for two hours, or if there is no oven, over very low heat on the stove for slow cooking.
12. Serve very hot and drink plenty of white wine.
