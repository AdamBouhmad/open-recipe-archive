## Ingredients

- 600 grams chopped onion
- 1 kilogram bread, crumbled and moistened 24 hours prior
- 250 grams raw oil
- 125 grams paprika
- salt to taste
- a small handful of anise seeds
- beef tripe, one quarter in length

## Directions

1. Keep stirring so ingredients do not stick together
2. When half melted, add 600 grams chopped onion and continue stirring with a wooden spoon
3. After a quarter hour, add 1 kilogram crumbled bread that has been moistened in a basin 24 hours prior, and continue stirring
4. Pour 250 grams raw oil and 125 grams paprika over the mass, add salt to taste and a small handful of anise seeds
5. Keep on the fire, stirring constantly until the ingredients form a harmonious whole, that is, a slightly compact mass
6. While hot, stuff the mixture into beef tripe pieces, each one quarter in length
7. Hang the farinatos to air dry in the kitchen near the hearth
8. After fifteen days, take down the farinatos and flatten them on a board
9. Hang them again, and they are ready to be eaten; the longer they hang, the richer they become
10. To serve in Castile at mid-morning snack time, fry them in small pieces with pork lard
