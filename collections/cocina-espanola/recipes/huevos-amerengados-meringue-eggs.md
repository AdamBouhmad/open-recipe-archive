## Ingredients

- six eggs (separated into whites and yolks)
- salt
- thirty grams of powdered sugar
- one tablespoon of orange blossom water
- one liter of cream (or two liters of milk as substitute)
- lemon peel
- one hundred grams of sugar
- two tablespoons of orange blossom water

## Directions

1. Separate the whites from the yolks of six eggs.
2. Beat the whites to stiff peaks and add salt with 30 grams of powdered sugar, one tablespoon of orange blossom water, and continue beating so that the mixture combines well.
3. Apart, over low heat, reduce by a quarter one liter of cream, in which the peel of one lemon with one hundred grams of sugar and two tablespoons of orange blossom water will have been added.
4. If there is no cream, substitute with two liters of milk, which will be reduced by half, cooking it very slowly.
5. When the milk is boiling, the beaten whites are added successively in portions with a small ladle, so that they have a spherical form.
6. Turn the balls in the milk so they cook evenly, and as they coagulate they are removed and placed to drain on a sieve or hair sieve.
7. When all the whites are cooked, remove the milk from the fire and incorporate four yolks, stirring the milk strongly and quickly, so that it gains consistency without curdling.
8. Place the eggs in a pyramid on a platter and pour the cream or milk over them, prepared as explained above.
