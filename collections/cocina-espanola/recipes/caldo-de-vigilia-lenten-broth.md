## Ingredients

- Beans, dried
- Peas, dried
- Salt
- Parsley sprigs
- Celery
- Carrot
- Onion
- Cloves
- Butter from cows or olive oil

## Directions

1. Cook dried beans and peas in equal parts in water until they break down into a paste, with salt
2. Add a bundle of parsley and celery, a carrot, and an onion with a couple of cloves of spice embedded in it
3. Pass the broth through a sieve
4. Serve immediately as a soup base by adding a good piece of cow butter or two or three tablespoons of oil in which potatoes or another substance that does not impart flavor to the oil have been fried
5. To preserve for many days, decant carefully when cold to separate it from the sediment that forms and deposits in the vessel, without which the broth would turn sour
