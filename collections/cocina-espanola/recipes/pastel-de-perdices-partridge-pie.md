## Ingredients

- six good partridges
- two pounds of calf liver
- one pound of fresh bacon
- one pound of onions
- a pair of turnips
- a pair of carrots
- salt
- spices
- aromatic herbs, with rosemary predominating
- one hundred grams of fine fresh butter
- two tablespoons of flour
- one glass of Jerez or good common white wine
- optional truffle slices

## Directions

1. Cook the six partridges in water with the calf liver, fresh bacon, onions, turnips, and carrots with appropriate seasoning, spices, and aromatic herbs, for four hours in continuous, slow cooking.
2. Remove the partridges and debone them entirely.
3. With the remaining cooked ingredients except the aromatic herbs, make a picadillo that is converted into a paste by passing it through a food mill with the aid of the cooking broth, which should be reduced by half and thickened considerably.
4. In a pot, place one hundred grams of fine fresh butter and incorporate two tablespoons of flour to brown it lightly without toasting.
5. Moisten with one glass of Jerez or good common white wine.
6. Incorporate and mix the purée very well, ensuring it is somewhat loose, which is achieved with the auxiliary broth.
7. Fill an earthenware terrine of the kind used for pastels of this class.
8. Cook for one hour in a water bath.
9. When cold, this pastel can include truffle slices if desired, and when kept in a cool place it keeps perfectly for days.
