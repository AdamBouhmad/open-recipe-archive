## Ingredients

- Quail
- Butter
- Broth or water
- Flour
- Butter from fresh cows
- White wine
- Water

## Directions

1. Clean the quail well and remove the legs, head and wings.
2. Pierce the thighs with small sticks or reeds from one side to the other.
3. Place them in a cooking pot with butter and add broth or water.
4. Remove and split them lengthwise down the middle.
5. Remove the small bones and fry again in butter with a little hot broth or water.
6. Make the pastry dough with one and a half pounds of flour, one pound of fresh cow butter and one glass of white wine at the start, the rest water.
7. Work the dough well so it is not hard. After working it well, place the butter in the middle of the dough, extend it a little and then fold the dough over it, doing this three times.
8. Cut one round piece for the base and then one long strip; moisten it slightly with a feather in water and place it around the edge.
9. Cut another round piece for the lid and place it in a well-heated oven with heat above and below, moistening it from time to time with butter on top.
10. As it begins to cook, cut off the lid and remove the middle leaves and let it cook more. When it reaches the right point, remove it.
