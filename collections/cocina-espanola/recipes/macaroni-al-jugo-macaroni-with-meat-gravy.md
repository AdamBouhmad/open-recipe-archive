## Ingredients

- 2 pounds macaroni
- salt
- water
- grated Parmesan cheese
- 2 pounds sirloin
- 2 ounces beef fat or pork fat
- 1 large onion the size of a fist
- parsley
- 2 ounces bacon
- spices
- 1 glass water
- 3 pounds tomatoes

## Directions

1. Cook the macaroni in plenty of water over high heat, seasoning with salt.
2. After a few minutes of cooking, and taking care that they do not fall apart and remain somewhat firm, remove and drain.
3. Arrange the macaroni in a deep dish in layers interspersed with finely grated Parmesan cheese.
4. Pour the meat gravy over everything at the moment of serving.
5. To prepare the gravy for two pounds of macaroni: brown two pounds of sirloin in a saucepan with two ounces of beef fat or pork fat.
6. Add one large onion the size of a fist, parsley, two ounces of bacon, and spices, chopping everything very fine.
7. When the onion is consumed without darkening, moisten with one glass of water and increase the fire, then add three pounds of well-cleaned and crumbled tomatoes.
8. As the tomato quickly reduces, add the water calculated for the desired quantity of gravy and leave everything to cook over low heat for two hours.
9. Remove the meat, which can be eaten cold at another meal, and pour the broth or gravy over the macaroni.
