## Ingredients

- ten to twelve eggs
- two tablespoons of very fresh water
- salt
- a pinch of pepper
- two ounces of fresh unsalted beef butter
- one ounce of fresh beef butter

## Directions

1. Beat ten to twelve eggs in a bowl with a steel fork with long tines.
2. Add two tablespoons of very fresh water, which serves to make the mixture of yolks and whites more intimate.
3. Season with salt and a small pinch of pepper.
4. Melt two ounces of very fresh unsalted beef butter over high heat in the pan, and when it is shiny and about to take color, pour in the beaten eggs and stir with a spatula until it begins to set.
5. Once it sets well and always holding the pan by the handle, lift the omelet on one side with the tip of a kitchen knife, and through the opening pass more beef butter in the amount of one ounce.
6. Once it has melted, fold the omelet in half over itself, and with the pan tilted mold and straighten the edges or sides, and turn it out onto a serving platter to serve it very hot.
