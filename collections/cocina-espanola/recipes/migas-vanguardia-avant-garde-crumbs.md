## Ingredients

- One loaf or large bread
- Water
- Salt to taste
- Oil in quantity equal to the volume of bread
- Half a dozen garlic heads, whole and separated into cloves

## Directions

1. Cut slices from the loaf one finger thick and soak them in water seasoned with salt to taste.
2. Drain the water and dry the bread slices with a napkin.
3. Heat oil in a pan or skillet in quantity equal to the volume of bread.
4. When the oil is very hot, fry and burn half a dozen garlic heads, whole and with their cloves separated.
5. Remove and discard the garlic, and over high heat fry the bread slices in that oil until their surfaces are toasted.
6. Then, holding the pan firmly with one hand, crumble the bread with the other, armed with a spatula or skimmer, crumbling everything equally, without interrupting the frying.
7. Once all the bread pieces are well colored and have acquired a certain hardness and looseness, remove the crumbs and serve them, dusting with a little salt or with sugar, according to whether they are to be eaten with another dish or alone.
