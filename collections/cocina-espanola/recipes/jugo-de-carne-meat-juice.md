## Ingredients

- 250 grams of beef rump
- 250 grams of veal rump
- One veal foot or hand
- One large carrot
- One large onion
- Parsley, chervil, tarragon, bay leaf, and thyme in proper proportion
- Cloves
- Nutmeg
- Salt
- Pepper (optional)
- Water

## Directions

1. Cut the beef rump and veal rump into pieces the size of plums.
2. Place them in a saucepan with a large carrot and a large onion, cut into thin slices.
3. Make a small bundle with parsley, chervil, tarragon, bay leaf, and thyme, tied with white thread, and add this seasoning.
4. Season with clove, nutmeg, salt, and a pinch of pepper if desired.
5. Note that the seasoning should err on the side of being bland, as any strong spice would be inconvenient for sauces in which the juice will be used.
6. Cover the saucepan and sweat over high heat, moving the utensil frequently.
7. When the pieces of meat and the pieces of onion and carrot begin to brown, and when the juice they release sticks to the saucepan, add cold water in sufficient quantity so that everything is completely covered, without excess water.
8. Without stopping stirring with a spatula, scrape up the juice that is stuck to the saucepan.
9. Add the veal foot or hand, cut into three or four pieces.
