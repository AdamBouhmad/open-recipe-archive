## Ingredients

- Whole hake
- Water
- Two ounces of the best butter, from Isigny or Montpellier
- Shallots
- Parsley
- Chervil
- One clove of garlic
- One spoonful of flour
- Water
- Small pickles cut like capers
- One egg yolk

## Directions

1. Cook the whole hake in water without any seasoning, in a short broth; leave in the fish kettle away from the fire to serve in a platter, after the fish is drained, with its sauce on top
2. For the sauce: reheat two ounces of the best butter in it, sweating shallots, parsley, chervil, and one clove of garlic, all very finely chopped
3. Add one spoonful of flour toasted to medium color and some water to dilute it
4. Cut small pickles like capers and, as the French say, blanch them, then bind the sauce with an egg yolk dissolved in cold water
