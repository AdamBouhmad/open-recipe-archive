## Ingredients

- Small cucumbers (no larger than a finger)
- Salt
- Good wine vinegar
- Bay leaves
- Peppercorns
- Oil

## Directions

1. Clean the gherkins well with a brush and cut off the tip
2. Place in a tinned iron pot with two handfuls of ground salt, mixing it with them
3. Leave for twenty-four hours so they release their water
4. Remove and let drain for a few moments
5. Return to the same vessel and pour boiling good vinegar over them in sufficient quantity to cover
6. Cover the vessel and leave for twenty-four hours so they take on a yellowish color
7. Remove the vinegar and cook it alone over high heat
8. When it boils vigorously, add the gherkins and stir so they turn green again; four minutes of boiling is sufficient
9. Remove and place in fresh cold water where they are left to cool
10. Spread on dry linen to air, and when they have released the water, pack in wide-mouthed glass jars or bottles, arranging them one on top of another, placing here and there some bay leaves and some peppercorns
11. Pour white vinegar over them in quantity sufficient for the cucumbers to be completely covered, adding two ounces of salt per azumbre of vinegar
12. Finish the operation by adding oil, which remains on top like that of a small lamp, thus forming a layer that preserves the food from any spoilage
