## Ingredients

- five pounds of clams
- water
- two ounces of cow butter
- salt
- pepper
- clove
- nutmeg
- parsley
- bay leaf
- two more ounces of cow butter
- three tablespoons of corn starch
- tamized pasta
- two cups of Jerez or other white wine
- three hard-boiled egg whites
- one egg yolk
- three tablespoons of cold water

## Directions

1. Cook the clams in water without seasoning for half an hour
2. Remove clams from shells and sauté them in a saucepan with two ounces of cow butter
3. Before they take color, add half a pint of the water in which they began to cook
4. Season with salt, pepper, clove, nutmeg, parsley, and bay leaf
5. Cook slowly for one hour
6. Remove the saucepan from heat and pass the stew through a strainer, using the reserved water to help
7. Empty the saucepan and without cleaning it, melt two more ounces of cow butter over high heat
8. Incorporate three tablespoons of corn starch to create a thick paste
9. Add the first water, then add the tamized pasta and two cups of Jerez or white wine
10. Stir with a spatula for ten minutes over high heat
11. In the serving tureen, have ready three hard-boiled egg whites cut into small squares
12. Before pouring the saucepan contents into the tureen, bind the soup off the heat with an egg yolk dissolved in three tablespoons of cold water
