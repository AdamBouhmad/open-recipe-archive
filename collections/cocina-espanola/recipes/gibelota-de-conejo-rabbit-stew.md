## Ingredients

- rabbit
- 125 grams beef or pork lard
- 125 grams bacon, cut into small squares
- 1 heaping tablespoon flour
- 2 ladles of broth
- 2 ladles of white wine
- mushrooms
- bouquet of fine herbs
- 2 dozen small onions
- pork lard for the onions

## Directions

1. Prepare the rabbit and quarter it as shown on page 443.
2. Brown it well in 125 grams of beef or pork lard and equal quantity of bacon cut into small squares.
3. When the bacon is very golden, remove it from the pot, and in the same fat, brown the rabbit pieces until the meat becomes firm and toasted.
4. Set aside the browned rabbit on a plate.
5. In the same pot with the same fat, toast a heaping tablespoon of flour, and moisten with two ladles of broth and the same amount of white wine.
6. Immediately incorporate into the pot the bacon pieces and rabbit pieces that were set aside.
7. Add trimmed mushrooms and cook over high heat.
8. When the gibelota lacks only a quarter of the cooking time, season with salt, pepper, a bouquet of fine herbs, and two dozen small onions that have been previously browned in pork lard.
9. Manage the cooking so that the sauce is thick and reduced when the rabbit is cooked through.
10. Serve very hot.
