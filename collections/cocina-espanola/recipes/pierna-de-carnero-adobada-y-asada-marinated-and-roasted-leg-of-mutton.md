## Ingredients

- One rather lean leg of mutton
- Strips of bacon that have been in brine
- Vinegar
- Broth from the pot without fat
- Salt
- Pepper
- Fine herbs
- Shallots, chopped
- A pair of garlic cloves
- Fine clarified oil

## Directions

1. Choose a rather lean piece
2. Skin it and lard it with strips of bacon that have been in brine beforehand
3. Submerge the larded leg of mutton in an escabeche made of vinegar and fat-free pot broth in equal parts, salt, pepper, fine herbs, and chopped shallots with a pair of garlic cloves
4. Allow the leg of mutton to remain in this broth for three days, completely submerged
5. At the moment of roasting, remove it, drain it, and clean it with a cloth
6. Rub the entire surface with fine clarified oil
7. During cooking, continuously moisten the meat with its own juices and serve with them
8. Note: Small legs of mutton from Pamplona, the region of Burgos, and Cuenca are best for this preparation
9. If no spit roaster is available and only coal and coke are used for cooking, roasting can be done in the oven, but it is more difficult as one must exercise exquisite care and constantly turn the leg of mutton while continuously moistening it with its own juices to prevent it from becoming toasted on the outside or burned while remaining raw inside
10. Use oven trays according to the models on page 186, or better, if the kitchen is well equipped, use the roasting pan described in the Appendix
