## Ingredients

- Two veal feet
- Six pounds of beef or cow meat
- One and a half pounds of veal top
- Five pounds of mutton
- Water
- Salt
- One or two chickens or one or two partridges (optional, for more delicate broth)

## Directions

1. Place all ingredients in an earthenware pot and cook with low heat in sufficient water.
2. Skim carefully and strain the broth, pressing it well.
3. Boil a second time with fresh water and strain again.
4. Let both broths cool and remove the fat.
5. Combine the two broths and strain.
6. Evaporate in a double boiler until it reaches the consistency of paste.
7. Remove from the vessel and spread on marble stone.
8. Cut into small tablets and finish drying in a double boiler or oven until brittle.
9. Enclose in a glass bottle sealed with cork and wax, and store in a dry place.
10. To use: dissolve half an ounce or 15 grams of tablets in water, add a little salt, heat over gentle heat, and serve or use in making soups.
