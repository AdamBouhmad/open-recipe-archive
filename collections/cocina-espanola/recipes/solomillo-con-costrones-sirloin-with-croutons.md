## Ingredients

- slices of roasted sirloin from the previous day
- broth from the pot
- croutons or slices of bread
- pork lard
- beef butter
- parsley, chopped
- roasting juice
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Heat slices of roasted sirloin by soaking them in boiling broth from the pot away from the fire
2. Fry and sauté croutons or bread slices of equal form and size to the sirloin slices in pork lard, having first soaked them in broth
3. Arrange the sirloin pieces and croutons alternately in a crown on a round platter, standing them on edge
4. To the broth that was used to reheat the meat, add a couple of spoonfuls of roasting juice and reduce it over high heat
5. Add 60 grams of beef butter kneaded with considerable chopped parsley, add a thin stream of vinegar, and pour this sauce into the bowl formed by the dish arranged in a crown on the platter
6. Keep the platter warm by the fire or in an open oven
