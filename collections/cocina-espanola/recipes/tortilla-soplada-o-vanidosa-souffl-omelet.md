## Ingredients

- six eggs
- 125 grams of ground sugar
- zest of one lemon or orange, grated
- a hint of vanilla or other aroma of choice
- two ounces of butter

## Directions

1. Separate the egg yolks from the whites of six eggs.
2. Put 125 grams of ground sugar into the yolks along with the grated zest of a lemon or orange, or a hint of vanilla—any aroma you wish—and beat it all very quickly.
3. Beat the whites separately until they form stiff peaks, with nothing else added.
4. In a silver or metal oval serving dish, melt two ounces of butter.
5. Place the dish over high heat.
6. Mix the whites and yolks quickly and pour into the dish.
7. Cover with a dome cover loaded with considerable heat.
8. Five minutes, by the clock, are enough for the omelet to puff up.
9. At that moment sprinkle with sugar, an operation that must be done skillfully between the kitchen and the table so that it is presented puffed up and burning.
