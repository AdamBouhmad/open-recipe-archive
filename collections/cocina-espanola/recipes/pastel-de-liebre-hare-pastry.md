## Ingredients

- one whole hare, completely deboned
- 250 grams of raw ham
- equal quantity of pork loin
- 500 grams of beef shank
- bacon leaves
- truffles or mushrooms (optional)
- one good glass of cognac
- luten (hermetic sealing paste)

## Directions

1. Completely debone the hare and set aside the loins, cut each into three or four pieces, and the meat from the legs
2. Make a minced mixture with everything else, meat fibers, liver, etc.
3. Prepare an earthenware or porcelain pot that resists fire
4. Line the interior, bottom and walls, with thin bacon leaves
5. Place half of the minced mixture in the pot, and on top place the reserved pieces of deboned hare
6. Cover these pieces with the other half of the minced mixture, to which you can add thin slices of truffles or mushrooms
7. Moisten everything with one good glass of cognac
8. Close with bacon leaves as on the inside, ensuring the pot is completely full
9. Cover the pot with its lid and seal it with luten, a type of paste used to hermetically close vessels
10. Place in the oven at medium-strong heat and leave to cook for four hours
11. Allow to cool and serve in the pot at family tables
12. When wanting to give more solemnity to the dish, submerge the already-cold pot for two minutes in boiling water, and the hare pastry will detach easily, then place it on a platter by inverting the pot over it
