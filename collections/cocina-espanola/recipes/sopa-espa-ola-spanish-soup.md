## Ingredients

- Chicken breasts
- Butter
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg
- Bread crumb
- Broth from the pot
- Flour
- Egg yolks

## Directions

1. Cut chicken breasts into small pieces and cook slowly in butter with salt, pepper and nutmeg
2. Pound the cooked meat in a marble mortar until it becomes a paste
3. In the same pan where the breasts cooked, place a bread crumb well soaked in broth and cook slowly over very low heat, stirring often with a wooden spoon to form a thick paste
4. Let cool and incorporate into the mortar with the chicken breasts and some egg yolks according to the quantity of meatballs being prepared
5. Do this work in a very cool place, and in high-end kitchens add small pieces of ice during the pounding
6. Spread the mixture on a marble table dusted with flour and form quenelles of equal size
7. Poach them for ten minutes in pot broth in a large pan so they float freely and cook without losing shape
8. As the meatballs are removed from the broth, coat them with butter using a blunt knife
9. For Spanish soup, mold the quenelles paste into meatballs the size of hazelnuts
10. Place them in the soup tureen with some very thin slices of French bread, then pour in sufficient broth so the soup is clear
