## Ingredients

- thin, delicate slices of sirloin
- beef butter
- Spanish sauce or good concentrated broth
- roasting juice
- Sherry wine
- financier garnish
- mushrooms or truffles

## Directions

1. Cut thin and delicate slices of sirloin
2. Sauté them in beef butter over high heat, but without cooking them completely
3. Remove from the sauté pan and cook them slowly for a quarter hour in some spoonfuls of Spanish sauce, or if not available, in good concentrated broth lengthened with roasting juice and a glass of Sherry
4. Prepare a dark financier garnish separately as previously explained
5. Place the sirloin slices in a crown on a platter, laying them down and overlapping one upon another
6. Pour the garnish into the hollow, reinforced with the reduced sauce in which the meat finished cooking
7. For variation, mushrooms or truffles cooked in the sauce used for the meat can be used instead of the financier garnish
