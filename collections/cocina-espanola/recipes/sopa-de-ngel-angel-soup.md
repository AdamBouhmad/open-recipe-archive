## Ingredients

- rice - eight pounds
- milk - twenty pounds
- eggs - six dozen
- sugar - six pounds
- cinnamon - two ounces (one and a half ounces for infusion, half ounce for powder)
- meat broth - one quarter of the total liquid, without spices

## Directions

1. Infuse one and a half ounces of cinnamon in water in a small covered pot, covered with doubled paper
2. Mix half ounce of cinnamon powder with half pound of sugar
3. Clean the rice well and wash it with warm water, twice if necessary
4. Dry the rice and grind it, then pass through a sieve used for sifting flour
5. Spread the flour on cloths if the weather is hot
6. Ensure the milk is not sour by testing it in a clean pan over heat
7. Place the milk in a pot over heat and bring to a boil
8. Dissolve the rice flour in a pot with milk over heat with the sugar
9. Place the pot containing the soup and paste over heat
10. Add half the eggs with egg whites and the other half without them
11. Stir constantly with a ladle, adding little by little, until it cooks and becomes thick
12. Stop when the mixture no longer tastes of flour
13. Serve with sugar and cinnamon on top
14. Add one quarter broth of meat without spices; it should not have much fat, and the pot should not be rancid - only meat and bacon
