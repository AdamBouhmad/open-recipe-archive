## Ingredients

- Eggs
- Pork lard or oil (ten times the volume of an egg for every half dozen eggs)

## Directions

1. Fry the eggs in pork lard or oil; the fat must be prepared so that the oil is free of bad smell and acrid flavor, and the lard is entirely anhydrous, without water
2. Make the fried eggs one at a time, and serve as they are made, immediately from the pan to the plate
3. In a large pan, add lard or oil in a quantity ten times greater than the volume of an egg, calculated for frying half a dozen
4. Break the egg, put the white in one cup and the yolk in another, agitate the white lightly without beating it
5. Put the pan over high heat
6. When the liquid is hot, it will smoke with a thin vapor that takes on a brownish color, turning into the characteristic blue smoke of all fats when boiling begins
7. At that moment, remove the pan from the fire, pour the white in a stream from about a hand's breadth of height, as if decanting, and immediately, without losing time and with great speed, throw the yolk in the middle
