## Ingredients

- two raw egg yolks without the germ
- salt
- white pepper
- three to four drops of vinegar
- one and a half tablespoons of refined oil per egg yolk
- lemon juice or good vinegar

## Directions

1. In a porcelain bowl, placed inside a bowl filled with fresh water or in a very cold place (because the sauce breaks with the heat developed by manipulation), add two raw egg yolks without the germ.
2. Season well with salt and white pepper, thinning them with three or four drops of vinegar.
3. Drop by drop, in the proportion of one and a half tablespoons of very refined oil per egg yolk, incorporate the liquid, stirring constantly with a spatula in circular motions, using a stopper with a hole to dispense the oil very slowly.
4. Beat vigorously without stopping and at the same pace for a quarter of an hour.
5. When the oil and eggs have bound together and the sauce has the point of a thin cord and tends to separate from the bowl, flavor it with lemon juice, and if none is at hand, with a little good vinegar.
