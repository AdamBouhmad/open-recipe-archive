## Ingredients

- flour
- egg
- salt
- pepper
- aguardiente, cognac, or rum (one spoonful)
- milk diluted in water or water

## Directions

1. Place the flour on a plate and make a hollow in the middle. For half a pound of flour, add one egg, salt, and pepper to taste, and one spoonful of aguardiente, cognac, or rum. Mix everything very well together.
2. Add milk diluted in water or water alone gradually until the batter has only the consistency necessary to coat the dishes with a layer the thickness of a small dog's paw.
3. Mix well and beat with a metal fork, then let it rest for one hour.
4. At the moment of using this batter, add half of an egg white beaten until stiff peaks form, but only if you want the fried dish to puff up considerably.
5. Note that this puffing has the disadvantage of impregnating the dish heavily with grease.
6. This batter can also be used to fry fish, but as a general rule and for better taste, fried fish comes out better with simple flouring.
