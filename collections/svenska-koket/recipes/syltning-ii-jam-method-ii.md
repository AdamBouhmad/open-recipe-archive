## Ingredients

- 2 kg berries
- 2 kg lump sugar
- 1/2 litre water

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the berries as in the previous recipe.
2. Cut the sugar into small pieces, place in the pot, and add the water.
3. When the sugar is almost melted, cook it while skimming to a clear syrup that is ready when it threads between the fingers.
4. Lift the pot from the heat, pour in the berries and shake them down well in the syrup, then return the pot to the heat and let the jam simmer slowly, shaking the pot often and skimming the jam until it is clear, or approximately 1/2 hour.
5. Treat it thereafter as in the previous recipe.
6. This method is used for all kinds of berries. For cherries and mahaleb cherries, remove the pits, crush some of them, and place the kernels in the jam when it is almost cooked.
7. Mixed jams, for example of wild strawberries and blueberries, raspberries and blueberries, gooseberries and raspberries are advantageously made this way.
