## Ingredients

- 2 kg. lamb roast (chilled)
- 1 1/2 hg. lard
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.)
- 3/4 lit. boiling broth or water

## Directions

1. The roast, which should have hung so that it is tender, is dried, beaten, larded (see page 232), and the shank is cut off.
2. It is larded most on the outer thigh and back, broken to facilitate carving, after which the roast is rubbed with a mixture of the salt and pepper.
3. A roasting pan is heated, the butter is browned therein together with some of the lard, the roast is placed in with the inner thigh up and browned for approximately 1/2 hour in a warm oven, during which it is frequently basted with the fat in the pan.
4. It is diluted with the boiling broth or water and is allowed to roast until tender, or approximately 1 1/2—2 hours; during which it is basted every 10 minutes.
5. Placed on a warm serving platter and carved at the table.
6. Served with white beans and vegetables or vegetable pastries as well as the strained, skimmed meat gravy or with reduced sauce (see no. 429).
