## Ingredients

- 1 kg rye sifted flour
- 75 gr wheat flour
- 1 liter skimmed milk
- 50 gr pressed yeast
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr)
- 50 gr butter, margarine or lard
- 3 dcl syrup
- 2 tbsp crushed fennel
- 3 tbsp finely chopped orange peel

## Directions

1. From the rye flour, a portion of the wheat flour, the lukewarm milk and the yeast mixed with salt, make a dough as described on page 462, and let the dough rise to double its size.
2. Warm the syrup somewhat in a saucepan together with the fat and spices and work it into the dough along with enough wheat flour that the dough becomes quite firm.
3. Let the dough rise again, then work it well with the rest of the wheat flour on the baking board and divide into 6-8 parts.
4. Work each part until it is smooth and without cracks, then roll it into an oblong bun, which is placed on a warm, soft baking cloth, on which is placed a baking cloth that is floured.
5. Place the buns next to each other with the baking cloth folded into a large pleat between each bun and cover them.
6. When the buns have risen, turn them out onto a bread peel, place on buttered, lukewarm baking sheets, score lengthwise with a larding needle, brush with cold milk and bake in a not too hot oven for about 1/2 hour.
7. When the buns are light, remove them, brush with hot water and place between cloths so the crust becomes soft.
8. The dough can be mixed with cold liquid the evening before it is to be baked.
