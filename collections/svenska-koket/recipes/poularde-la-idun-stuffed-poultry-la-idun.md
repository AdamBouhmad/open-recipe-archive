## Ingredients

- 1 poularde about 3 kg
- 1/2 lemon
- 1/2 tablespoon salt (8 gr.)
- 1 hectogram lard, in 1 slice
- For filling: 1 1/4 deciliter rice grains (125 gr.)
- 1 liter broth
- 1 small can goose liver
- 2–3 truffles
- 1 teaspoon salt, 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 tablespoon Madeira (optional)
- For cooking: 1 liter clear chicken or veal broth

## Directions

1. Prepare the bird in the usual way, remove the bones and rub the breast and thighs with lemon.
2. Rinse the rice grains, scald them in hot water, place them in boiling broth and let them cook until half soft, then drain and let them cool.
3. Cut the goose liver and truffles into cubes and mix them into the rice together with salt and pepper, and the wine if desired.
4. Stuff the bird with the rice and sew it well shut, then rub it with salt, place it in position and cover it with the lard slice, which is fastened with fine twine.
5. Place it in a pot that should be no larger than to accommodate the bird snugly, pour on the cold broth, cover with the lid, place the pot over the fire and heat the broth until it begins to simmer gently, then skim it well.
6. Do not heat the broth beyond 90 degrees and at this temperature let the chicken lie until it is tender, or about 2 1/2–3 hours.
7. If you prick with a larding needle in the thick part of the thigh next to the bone and the liquid that seeps out is white, the bird is done.
8. Carve the bird and arrange it on the platter so that it looks whole, garnish with the stuffing, salad and parsley and serve with a light mushroom sauce, made from the cooking liquid and added with thick cream and 40 gr. fine butter.
9. If desired, the bird can be larded with 25 gr. truffle before it is stuffed and prepared.
