## Ingredients

- 1 kg boneless entre-côte
- 1 hg smoked ham
- 3 tbsp butter (60 gr.)
- 1 tsp salt (5 gr.)
- a little white pepper
- 5 dcl dark broth
- 1 1/2–2 dcl Madeira

## Directions

1. The meat, which should have hung until properly tender, is prepared and larded with ham (see page 232), after which it is rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper and tied so that it gets a nice shape.
2. The butter is browned in an iron pot that should not be larger than that the meat fits snugly, the roast is browned on all sides until a nice brown crust is formed, then the boiling broth and half of the wine are added.
3. The meat is then allowed to cook with the lid tightly closed until tender, or approximately 1 1/2–2 hours, during which it is turned a few times.
4. Toward the end of cooking the rest of the wine is added.
5. The roast is sliced into thin slices, placed on a warm dish, covered with a few tablespoons of sauce, garnished with salad or parsley and small browned potatoes or potato patties.
6. Served with brown sauce (see no. 430).
