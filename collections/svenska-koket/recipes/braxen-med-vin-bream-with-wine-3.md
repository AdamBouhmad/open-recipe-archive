## Ingredients

- 1 bream weighing 2 kg
- 1 msk salt (15 gr.)
- 3 msk butter (60 gr.)
- 4 msk flour (40 gr.)
- 4 dcl white or red wine
- broth
- 5 white peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- 3 parsley sprigs
- 2-3 egg yolks
- sugar

## Directions

1. Scale and clean the fish as described on page 171; the head may remain but remove the gills
2. Rub with salt and let rest 1 hour, then dry again
3. Brown the butter lightly and fry with the flour for 2 minutes
4. Add wine and enough broth so the sauce is properly simmering
5. Place the fish in a smaller fish kettle without a strainer along with the roe or milt, pour the sauce over and it should reach the level of the fish
6. Add the pepper, bay leaf and parsley and let the fish simmer gently until the meat is tender, about 20 minutes
7. Shake the kettle often so the sauce does not burn on the bottom
8. Place the fish on a warm dish and garnish with parsley and sliced lemon
9. Strain the sauce, finish with egg yolks, let simmer, season with salt and sugar and serve in a separate sauce bowl
