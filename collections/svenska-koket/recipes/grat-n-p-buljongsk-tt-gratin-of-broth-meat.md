## Ingredients

- 400 g boneless broth meat
- 1–2 red onions
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 3 deciliters meat stock or broth
- 1 liter potatoes
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 g)
- 1½–2 deciliters boiling milk
- 2 eggs
- salt, sugar
- 1 tablespoon finely crushed rusks
- 1–2 tablespoons grated cheese
- 2 tablespoons melted butter (20 g)
- 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 g) for the dish

## Directions

1. Chop the meat finely (do not grind it).
2. Peel and chop the onion, brown it in butter, then add the meat and brown well.
3. Sprinkle with spices and dilute with the hot meat stock or broth.
4. Peel the potatoes raw, cut into pieces, boil quickly, drain well, and press through a potato ricer.
5. Place the mash in a pot, add the cold butter immediately, and work the mash with a fork.
6. Dilute with the boiling milk, add the beaten egg yolks, and stir until white and fluffy.
7. Season with salt and sugar, then fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
8. Butter a fireproof dish or pie form, lay the meat in it, spread the potato mash over the meat.
9. Sprinkle with rusks mixed with cheese, then pour the melted butter over all.
10. Place in a hot oven and gratinée for 20–30 minutes.
11. Serve with pan sauce or melted butter.
