## Ingredients

- 3 sole, approximately 5 hg. each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 1 1/2 tablespoon butter (30 gr.) for cooking
- 5 dcl. fish stock
- 2 tablespoon butter (40 gr.) for sauce
- 4 tablespoon flour (40 gr.)
- 5 dcl. fish stock
- 2–3 egg yolks
- 1 tablespoon fine white wine
- salt
- white pepper
- 1/2 liter good potato purée for garnish
- 3 tablespoon grated cheese
- 2 tablespoon melted butter (40 gr.)

## Directions

1. Clean and rinse the sole well and dry them; cut off the fins.
2. Make a deep cut with a pointed knife along both sides of the backbone and carefully cut the fillets from the bone so they do not break.
3. Place them skin-side down on a cutting board and cut the flesh from the skin.
4. Cut off the gills and rinse the head, bone, and white skin well, then cook stock from these parts.
5. Rub the fillets with salt and let them lie on top of each other for 1 hour, then dry again with a fish towel and cook 5–8 minutes in the fish stock.
6. Remove with a slotted spoon and let drain on cloth.
7. Measure the fish stock, reduce if it yields more than 5 dcl., and use for the sauce.
8. Butter a fireproof dish or preferably a silver one with 1/2 tablespoon butter, place the fillets on it, and pour the sauce over.
9. Pipe the potato purée around the edge and sprinkle the grated cheese over the arrangement, then pour the clarified butter over.
10. Place the dish in a water bath with coarse salt, put in a warm oven, and glaze quickly for 10–15 minutes.
11. For the sauce: fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the stock and let the sauce boil for 5 minutes; add the whisked egg yolks with the wine and let the sauce simmer while whisking and adjust seasoning.
