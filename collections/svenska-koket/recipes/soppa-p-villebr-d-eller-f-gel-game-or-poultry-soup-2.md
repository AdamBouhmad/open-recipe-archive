## Ingredients

- Remains and bones of hare or carcass and remains of poultry
- 2 carrots
- 1/2 celeriac
- 1 small red onion
- 4 parsley stems
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 2 liters broth without meat
- 4 white peppercorns
- 2 cloves
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 grams)
- salt, cayenne, sugar
- 1/2 deciliter sherry or Madeira
- 10 grams fine butter

## Directions

1. If raw bones and carcass are used, the soup will have a finer flavor. The best meat should then be cut away and used for forcemeat or meatballs, which are served in the soup.
2. Chop the remains and bones into small pieces and brown them in 2 tablespoons of the butter together with the cleaned and chopped vegetables and parsley.
3. Add the cold broth, add the whole spices, and let the soup cook with the lid tightly closed for about 2 hours.
4. Separate all the meat well from the bones, which should cook further in the broth for 1/2 hour.
5. Grind the meat 4 times through a meat grinder, mix with 1 deciliter of broth and pass through a sieve.
6. Strain and skim the broth and measure it - it should be 1 1/2 liters.
7. Fry the rest of the butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the broth and the passed meat and let the soup cook for 5 minutes.
8. Season with the spices, wine, and butter and serve with meatballs of game or cheese croutons.
