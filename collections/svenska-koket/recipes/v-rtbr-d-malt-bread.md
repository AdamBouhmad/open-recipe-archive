## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 litres malt (concentrated from 5 litres of malt)
- 1 1/2 kg wheat flour
- 1/2 litre unskimmed milk
- 125 gr pressed yeast
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr)
- rye sieve flour
- 1 hectogram butter or good lard
- 1/2 litre syrup
- 3 tablespoons crushed fennel
- 4 tablespoons finely chopped orange peel

## Directions

1. The day before the bread is to be baked, concentrate the malt, which takes about 6 hours
2. In the evening, sift about 3/4 kg of the wheat flour into a large bowl or trough
3. Stir the cold milk and the yeast mixed with salt into the flour, work the dough quickly, cover with a cloth and let it stand in a warm place overnight
4. Early the next morning, warm the malt to about 40° and add it to the yeast dough together with so much rye sieve flour that it becomes a firm dough, cover with a warm cloth and let it rise to double its size
5. Mix the butter, syrup and spices in a saucepan, bring to boil and let cool
6. Work the spice mixture into the dough together with more flour and let it rise again
7. Work the dough on the baking board with the rest of the wheat flour and divide into 12 parts
8. Work each part until smooth and without cracks, then roll it into an oblong bun, which is placed to rise on a soft, warmed baking cloth covered with a floured cloth
9. Place the buns close together with the cloth folded up in a large pleat between each bun, and cover
10. When the buns have risen, tip them onto a bread peel, place on buttered, lukewarm baking sheets and let stand about 10 minutes
11. Notch them lengthwise with a pastry needle, brush with cold malt or egg yolk and water, and bake about 3/4 hour in a not too hot oven
12. When half-baked, brush again with warm malt, and again when fully baked
13. Let them dry a moment in the oven before removing and placing between blankets so the crust becomes soft
14. Instead of malt, porter, health beer or Christmas ale mixed with sugar can be used
15. The malt bread is best if baked in a baking oven, which is heated as soon as the dough is made and swept as soon as the bread is shaped
16. The oven should then be kept completely closed until the bread is ready to bake
