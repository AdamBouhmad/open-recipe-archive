## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg. middle brisket (or 1 1/2 kg. middle chuck)
- 2 1/2 liters water
- 2 tbsp. salt (30 gr.)

## Directions

1. Dry the brisket with a cloth wrung out in hot water, crack it well, roll it together and bind it, so that it will take less room and be easier to turn.
2. Place it in boiling water, which is skimmed well when it comes to a boil, add the salt and let the brisket cook slowly with a tight lid until it is tender and the bones easily come loose, or approximately 2 1/2-3 hours.
3. Take it immediately out of the broth, cut into beautiful pieces, garnish with parsley and serve with boiled potatoes and horseradish-, caper-, or olive sauce.
4. The broth is used for purée or simpler soups.
5. The brisket can also, after it is cooked, be placed in a long pan with the meat side up, a little of the broth is added and the brisket is browned in the oven until it has taken on a beautiful brown color. It is then cut and served with potato purée and the broth in the pan, which is strained and skimmed. If the meat is desired to be fatter, use the front brisket.
