## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg beef brisket or flank steak
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in flat slices
- 1/4 teaspoon ginger
- For the marinade: 2 red onions
- 2 carrots
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 4 parsley stems
- 1 bay leaf
- 6 deciliters red or white wine
- 50 g fresh scalded pork jelly
- For roasting: 1 1/2 hectograms fat, 1 liter brown broth

## Directions

1. Lightly pound the meat with a damp mallet, dry it with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and rub it with the spices.
2. Blanch the onion and carrots, cut them into slices, brown them in butter, and place half of them together with the parsley in a deep dish. Place the meat on top and place the rest of the roots, parsley, bay leaf and pork jelly on top of the meat.
3. Pour the wine over and let the roast marinate for about 6 hours, turning it 3-4 times. Remove it, let it drain, dry it well with a cloth, cover it with lard slices and tie it so it gets a nice shape.
4. Heat the fat in an iron pot, brown the roast on all sides until a nice brown crust forms, then remove it. Remove the fat and wipe the pot well with white paper.
5. Pour the marinade into the pot, place the meat on top, put the lid on and let the wine reduce rapidly until it has the consistency of syrup. Then add the boiling broth, which should be level with the meat (the pot should be no larger than the roast fits snugly), put the lid on and let the roast roast slowly for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, turning it a few times but do not add liquid.
6. Place on a warm platter, garnish with parsley or kale and cut at the table. Serve with vegetables, browned potatoes and salads or potato patties.
7. Serve the sauce as jus or reduced (see no. 429).
