## Ingredients

- 1 kg rye sifted flour
- 75 gr wheat flour
- 1 litre skimmed milk
- 50 gr pressed yeast
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr)
- 50 gr butter, margarine or lard
- 3 dcl syrup
- 2 tbsp crushed fennel
- 3 tbsp finely chopped orange peel

## Directions

1. From the rye flour, a portion of the wheat flour, the warm milk and the yeast mixed with salt, make a dough as described on page 462, and let the dough rise to double its size.
2. Heat the syrup somewhat in a saucepan together with the fat and spices and work into the dough along with so much wheat flour that the dough becomes quite stiff.
3. Let the dough rise again, then work it well with the rest of the wheat flour on the baking board and divide into 6-8 portions.
4. Work each portion until it is smooth and without cracks, then roll it into an oblong roll, which is placed on a warm, soft baking cloth, on which is placed a floured baking cloth.
5. Place the rolls next to each other with the baking cloth folded in a large fold between each roll and cover.
6. When the rolls have risen, turn them out onto a bread peel, place on buttered, warm baking sheets, score lengthwise with a larding needle, brush with cold milk and bake in a not too warm oven for about 1/2 hour.
7. When the rolls are light, remove them, brush with warm water and place between cloths so the crust becomes soft.
8. The dough can be prepared with cold liquid the evening before it is to be baked.
