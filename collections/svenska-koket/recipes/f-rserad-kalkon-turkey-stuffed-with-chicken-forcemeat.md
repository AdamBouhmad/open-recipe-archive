## Ingredients

- 1 turkey cock of about 5 kg
- 4-5 truffles
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in 1 slice
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 1 liter good broth
- 2 chickens of about 450 g scraped meat
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 g)
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 tablespoons flour (15 g)
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 6 deciliters thick cream
- 3-4 truffles

## Directions

1. Pluck the turkey preferably while still warm, singe it, remove the giblets, rinse well and dry (see page 299 et seq.), then carefully remove the breastbone from within.
2. Lard the breast and legs thickly with finely cut truffle strips and let the turkey hang 2-6 days, depending on the season.
3. The day before the turkey is to be roasted, prepare the chicken forcemeat.
4. Pluck the chickens, singe them, remove the giblets (see page 299), rinse very well, dry with a cloth and skin them.
5. Cut all meat loose from the bones, scrape free of sinews and membranes, mix with seasonings and drive 5 times through the meat grinder.
6. Pound it thereafter together with the flour with a wooden pestle in a stone mortar until fine.
7. Whisk the eggs and add to the forcemeat, little by little, under vigorous stirring, then let stand in a cool place until the next day.
8. Place the forcemeat in a saucepan set in a pan of ice (otherwise it will break when the cream is worked in) and add the cream teaspoon by teaspoon under even stirring, after which add the truffle cut in fine strips or cubes and taste the forcemeat carefully.
9. Dry the turkey with a damp cloth, rub inside and outside with seasonings, fill with the chicken forcemeat, sew it well with a poultry needle, truss it and cover with the lard (see page 300).
10. Roast it thereafter as in no. 606, carve it beautifully and arrange on a platter so that it appears whole, garnish and serve as in no. 605.
11. The turkey can also be boiled as in no. 600 and served with truffle sauce.
