## Ingredients

- 2 cod fish, approximately 7 hectograms each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 grams)
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 grams)
- 1 small red onion
- 3 deciliters strong meat stock
- 4 deciliters fish broth
- 1/2 deciliter white wine
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 grams) for sauce
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 grams)
- 4 1/2 deciliters of the broth in which the fish cooked
- 1/2 deciliter white wine for sauce
- cayenne

## Directions

1. Clean the cod, rinse well, dry, and cut into fillets.
2. Cut away the backbone and remove the meat from the skin.
3. Rub the fillets with salt, let lie 1 hour, and dry again.
4. Cook broth on the head, bones, and skin.
5. Melt the butter in a low saucepan or small long pan, fry the peeled and sliced onion in it, place the fish fillets in, add the meat stock, broth, and wine, and cook the fish 10-15 minutes as in no. 328.
6. Place it up with a slotted spoon on a warm serving platter and pour the sauce over.
7. Garnish the arrangement with parsley and serve with small boiled potatoes.
8. For the sauce: fry butter and flour 2 minutes, add the strained fish broth, let the sauce cook 5 minutes and taste with wine and cayenne.
