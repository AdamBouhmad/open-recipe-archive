## Ingredients

- 2.5 hg. boneless beef
- 2.5 hg. fresh lean pork
- 50 gr. smoked ham
- 0.25 Portuguese onion
- 25 gr. butter
- 1 French loaf
- 2 tbsp. good milk
- 2 tsp. salt
- 0.5 tsp. white pepper
- For breading: 1 egg, 2 tbsp. crushed rusks, 2 tbsp. grated strong cheese
- For frying: 1 tbsp. butter, 1 liter stock or water

## Directions

1. Wash the meat with a cloth dipped in hot water, cut together with the pork into cubes and pass three times through a meat grinder.
2. Peel the onion, chop it finely and brown it in the butter, then mix it into the forcemeat.
3. Work in the egg, the French bread softened in milk, and the seasonings.
4. If the mass is too loose, add a little crushed rusks.
5. Shape the forcemeat into an oblong roll, flatten it from the sides so that it resembles a hare's back, brush it with egg and sprinkle with the crushed rusks mixed with the grated cheese.
6. Place the forcemeat on a butter-coated long pan and put it in the oven to get a beautiful brown color; then thin it with the boiling liquid and baste it during frying about every tenth minute.
7. The frying takes about 0.75 hour.
8. If desired, reduce the sauce according to general rules for sauces.
9. False hare can advantageously be used cold on the smörgåsbord.
