## Ingredients

- 1 liter fresh or 1/2 liter bottled bilberries
- 1 1/2–2 dcl. crushed sugar
- 8 dcl. water
- 1 tbsp. mondamin flour (15 gr.)
- 1 tbsp. potato starch (15 gr.)

## Directions

1. Clean the bilberries, rinse them in a sieve and let them drain.
2. Cook a thin, clear syrup from the sugar and water.
3. Add the bilberries and let them cook until they begin to fall apart.
4. Mix both types of flour with 1 dcl. cold water and slowly stir the mixture into the cream, which should not boil as it will become lumpy.
5. Let the cream cook for 5 minutes while stirring.
6. Pour into a compote dish rinsed with lukewarm water and sprinkle with sugar to prevent a skin from forming.
7. Serve with milk or cream.
8. If desired, the berries can be strained out and only the juice used. In this case, 1 tsp. good butter may advantageously be added, which prevents the cream from becoming stringy.
9. If bottled bilberries are used, they need only one boil in the sugar syrup.
