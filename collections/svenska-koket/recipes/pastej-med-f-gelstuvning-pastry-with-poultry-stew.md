## Ingredients

- 225 gr. flour
- 1 hg. butter
- 5 gr. salt
- 10 gr. sugar
- 1 dcl. water
- 1 egg yolk
- Remains of grouse or other poultry (3-4 hg.)
- 1 liter water
- 1 tsp. meat extract
- 1 tsp. salt
- 1 bouquet
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 gr.)
- 5 tbsp. flour (50 gr.)
- 6 dcl. poultry stock
- 1 tbsp. tomato puree
- salt, white pepper
- 1 egg yolk
- 1-2 tbsp. Madeira
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 gr.) for the form

## Directions

1. Prepare the shortcrust dough as in recipe no. 35 and let it rest 1 hour.
2. Cut the poultry remains into slices. Break the carcass and set it to boil in 1 liter of water, added with the meat extract, salt and 1 bouquet (1 leek, 3 parsley stems, 1 celery top). Let the stock boil approximately 2 hours, or until it has a good flavor, then strain and skim it.
3. Fry the butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the stock and let the sauce boil for 5 minutes. Season it with the tomato puree and spices, finish with the beaten egg yolk and finally stir in the wine.
4. Roll out the dough to approximately 1/2 cm. thickness and with it line a well-buttered, smooth pastry form. The dough should hang 2 cm. over the edge of the form.
5. Place the poultry slices in and pour in enough sauce so that it nearly covers the meat. (The remaining sauce is thinned with more stock and served with the pastry.)
6. Fold over the edge that hangs over the form over the stew and place a lid of dough on top. Garnish with figures of dough and brush with beaten egg.
7. Bake the pastry in a warm oven for approximately 1 hour. Place the form on a plate with tart paper and wrap it with a broken napkin.
8. Serve as a middle course.
