## Ingredients

- Giblets of 1 goose
- 1/3 liter goose or pork blood
- 6 tablespoons flour (60 grams)
- 1/2 liter broth in which the giblets were cooked
- 2 liters broth
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 teaspoon ginger
- 1 teaspoon crushed cloves
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 grams)
- 8 tablespoons sugar (120 grams)
- 8 tablespoons good vinegar
- 2 hectograms prunes
- 1 liter fresh apples
- 3-4 deciliters Madeira or sherry

## Directions

1. The giblets consist of head, neck, wing tips, feet, heart, liver, and stomach.
2. Remove the goose and carefully separate the innards from each other. (The fat is carefully saved, allowed to stand in water, strained, and used as butter or in cooking delicacies.)
3. Scald the head in hot water, split it lengthwise, rinse well, remove the eyes, and scrape the beak.
4. Scald and skin the feet, cut the heart in two, open the stomach, scrape out the contents, and remove the thick yellow membrane.
5. Carefully loosen the gallbladder from the liver, which is used for goose liver sausage.
6. Remove the neck skin and use it as sausage casing.
7. Break the neck, scald the wing tips, and split them.
8. Rinse the giblets well and preferably let them soak in water for a few hours.
9. Place them in 1 liter of cold water, skim well, add 1/2 tablespoon of salt and 6 white peppercorns and 4 cloves, and let everything cook with a tightly closed lid for about 3 hours.
10. Rinse the prunes well in lukewarm water, place them in 3 deciliters of cold water with 1 tablespoon of sugar and let them cook until tender.
11. Ten minutes before they are done, add the apples, peeled and cut into wedges.
12. When the meat is tender, take it out, cut it into pieces, and place it in the soup bowl.
13. Strain the broth, skim it, and let it cook down to 1/2 liter.
14. Strain the blood and whisk it with the flour and 1/2 liter of the cold broth; this preparation should stand and swell for 1 hour.
