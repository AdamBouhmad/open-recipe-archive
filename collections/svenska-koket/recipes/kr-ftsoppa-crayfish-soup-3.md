## Ingredients

- 3 dozen large or 4 dozen small crayfish
- 6 liters boiling water
- 1 hectogram coarse salt
- 3 dill bunches
- 150 grams butter
- 3.5 liters fish stock or clear stock
- 90 grams flour
- salt
- sugar
- cayenne
- 0.5 to 1 deciliter Madeira or sherry
- 2 to 3 egg yolks
- 1 deciliter thick cream
- 1 can green asparagus tips (0.25 liters)

## Directions

1. Rinse the crayfish, place in water with salt added, and boil for 10-15 minutes depending on size, preferably letting them cool in the cooking liquid.
2. Remove the meat from the tails, remove the intestine, and cut the tails lengthwise to be served in the finished soup.
3. Remove the stomach from the shells and pound these very finely in a mortar with the butter, then place the mixture in a pot and let it fry over the fire while stirring for 10 minutes, then add the cold stock.
4. Let the soup thereafter cook slowly with lid for about 0.5 hour. Pass through a sieve and let stand a quarter hour so that the crayfish butter has time to float to the surface. Skim off the butter.
5. Fry the butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the stock little by little, and let the soup boil for 5 minutes, then season with spices and wine.
6. Whisk the egg yolks and cream in the soup bowl and pour the soup in while whisking.
7. Add the crayfish tails together with the asparagus tips, which have been heated in a water bath, and serve the soup very hot.
