## Ingredients

- 1 kg calf's liver
- 50 g lard
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 0.5 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 caul
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 6 deciliters boiling milk
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 g)
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 g)
- 1–2 deciliters thick cream
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper
- 0.5 tablespoon cold butter (10 g)

## Directions

1. The liver, which should be light and tender, should soak in water for about 0.5 hour, then be well dried, cleaned and larded with fine strips of lard rolled in a mixture of salt and pepper.
2. Season the liver with the remaining spices and wrap it in the caul (which usually comes with the purchase), which is sewn or fastened together with kitchen twine.
3. Brown the butter in an iron pot, place the liver in it, brown on all sides and add the milk gradually. Let it cook slowly with the lid tightly closed for about 1.5 hours, basting it frequently.
4. Remove the caul, slice the liver thinly, place on a warm serving dish, pour a little of the sauce over it and serve the rest of the sauce in a sauce boat.
5. The sauce is made as in recipe no. 429 and, if desired, mixed with grated mesost cheese.
6. Serve with boiled potatoes and lingonberry jam.
