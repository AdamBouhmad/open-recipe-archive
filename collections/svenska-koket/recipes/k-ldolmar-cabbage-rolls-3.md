## Ingredients

- 1 cabbage head
- water, salt
- 1 1/2 deciliters rice grains (125 g)
- 1/2 tbsp. butter, 6-7 deciliters milk
- 3 hectograms boneless beef
- 1 hectogram boneless veal or pork
- 50 grams lard
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 1-2 deciliters cream
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 g) for browning
- 8 deciliters broth

## Directions

1. Carefully loosen the cabbage leaves from the core, place them in boiling salted water and blanch for about 5 minutes.
2. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on a cloth, then trim away the thickest part of the central vein.
3. Rinse the rice, scald it in hot water, drain in a colander and let it cool.
4. Melt the butter in a pot, add the milk, bring to a boil, add the rice and cook while stirring until it is almost tender, then set aside to cool.
5. Prepare the meat forcemeat as in recipe no. 637.
6. Mix the forcemeat and the cooled rice well and season carefully. (If the forcemeat is too firm, thin it with a little more cream.)
7. Place one tbsp. of the mixture on each cabbage leaf, roll it up and tie with fine twine previously dipped in boiling water.
8. Brown the butter in a braising pot, add the rolls and brown them nicely, thin with a little of the boiling broth and simmer slowly with the lid tightly closed for about 2 hours.
9. During cooking, add more broth as needed.
10. Remove them, cut off the twine and place the rolls on a carrot.
11. Strain the sauce, clarify it and pour it over them.
12. The rice grains can be omitted, but then more forcemeat should be calculated.
