## Ingredients

- 1 pike, 1 1/2 kg
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 4 parsley stems
- 5 white peppercorns
- 1/2 hectogram lard
- 3 decilitres fish stock
- 2 decilitres white wine
- 1 bay leaf
- browned sugar (1/2 tablespoon caster sugar, 1 tablespoon water)

## Directions

1. Remove the pike, rinse well, and dry
2. Skin the pike with the scales on; the head may remain but cut away the gills and fins
3. Rub the pike with 2 teaspoons of salt and let rest 1 hour, then dry again and lard it in tight rows with 1/2 hectogram of lard cut into even thin strips, dipped in the remaining salt
4. Place the remaining lard, cut into flat slices, on the bottom of a small fish kettle, place the pike on top, and add the stock, wine, parsley, spices, and browned sugar
5. Let the fish simmer gently with the lid tightly closed for approximately 20 minutes, then place on a warm serving platter and garnish with parsley and small boiled potatoes
6. Strain the cooking liquid, skim it, and if desired, deglaze with a little wheat flour, bring to a boil for 5 minutes, taste, and pour over the fish
