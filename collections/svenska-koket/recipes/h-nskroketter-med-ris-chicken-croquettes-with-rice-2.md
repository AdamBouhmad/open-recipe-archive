## Ingredients

- 1 young hen or remnants of cooked chicken
- 1/2 lemon
- 1 litre water
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 cloves
- 1 decilitre rice grains
- For the sauce: 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 grams), 3 tablespoons flour (30 grams), 4 decilitres chicken broth, 2 egg yolks, salt, white pepper
- For breading: 4 tablespoons flour, 2 eggs, 2 teaspoons cooking oil, salt, white pepper, 1 cup crushed rusks
- For cooking: 1 kilogram lard

## Directions

1. Pluck and singe the hen; cut off wings and legs.
2. Cut it up, remove the insides, rinse well and let it soak in water for a few hours.
3. Dry the hen with a cloth, rub it inside and outside with lemon and place in boiling water; skim the stock well when it boils up, then add the spices.
4. When the meat is tender after 1-2 hours, remove it and cut into fine cubes.
5. Place the carcass back in the broth, which should cook until it tastes properly strong, then strain and skim it.
6. Rinse the rice grains in warm water, place in 1 litre boiling salted water and let them cook until they are soft but do not fall apart.
7. Drain them in a colander, rinse with cold water and let them drain.
