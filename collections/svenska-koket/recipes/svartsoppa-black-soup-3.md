## Ingredients

- Giblets from 1 goose (neck, wing tips, heart, stomach, liver)
- 1 liter goose or pork blood
- 3 tablespoons flour
- 2.5 liters broth (including the broth in which the giblets cooked)
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 teaspoon ginger
- 10 cloves (pounded)
- 2-3 teaspoons salt
- 6-8 tablespoons sugar
- 5-6 tablespoons vinegar
- 1 liter fresh apples or 0.5 hectogram dried apples
- 3 kilograms prunes
- 2 tablespoons port wine or madeira

## Directions

1. Blanch the goose giblets, rinse well and let them soak in cold water overnight. Remove the neck skin and liver (to be used for goose liver sausage). Place the remaining giblets in cold water and cook with the lid closed. When the meat is tender, remove it, cut into pieces and place in the soup tureen.
2. Strain the cooking liquid and skim off all fat carefully. In another pot, cook the peeled apples cut into pieces and the rinsed prunes. Strain the blood and whip vigorously; add the flour and 0.5 liter of broth. Bring the remaining broth to a boil, add the blood with vigorous whisking, which continues until the soup has cooked for 10 minutes.
3. Add the cooked fruit with its cooking liquid and season the soup.
