## Ingredients

- Fish
- Salt (2 teaspoons/10 grams per kilogram of fish)
- Cold water
- Coarse salt (7 grams per liter of water)
- Parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. For large whole fish, rub with salt (2 tsp/10 grams per kg fish)
2. Place fish on a sieve in a fish kettle and add enough cold water so it covers the fish evenly
3. If no fish kettle is available, invert a bowl on the bottom of another kettle, spread a coarse towel over it, and place the fish on the towel so it can be easily lifted out when done without breaking
4. Measure the water before adding to the kettle and add 7 grams of coarse salt per liter of water
5. Cover the kettle, bring water to a rapid boil and skim well
6. Move the kettle to the side of the stove and finish cooking the fish while the water only simmers gently, allowing 15-20 minutes per kilogram of fish
7. For small fish or fish cut into slices, place in boiling salted water, skim, then move kettle to the side and finish cooking as described above
8. Once cooked, immediately transfer fish to a platter with a sieve or on a napkin, garnish with parsley, and serve with sauce made from the cooking liquid or another fine sauce
