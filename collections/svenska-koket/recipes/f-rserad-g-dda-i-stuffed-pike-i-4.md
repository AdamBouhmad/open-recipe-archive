## Ingredients

- 1 pike weighing 1.5 kg
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 150 g scraped fish meat
- 70 g butter
- 1 egg
- 1 tablespoon flour (10 g)
- 1.5 dcl cream
- 1 teaspoon salt (5 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon sugar
- white pepper
- 1/2 egg
- 2 tablespoons crushed rusks
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 3 dcl fish stock

## Directions

1. Clean the pike preferably the day before use.
2. Scale it, clean it, rinse very thoroughly, dry with a fish cloth.
3. Rub inside and outside with salt and hang in a cool place until the next day.
4. Using a sharp knife, cut out the backbone while keeping the head and tail attached.
5. (Cook the backbone to make stock for basting the fish.)
6. Scrape 150 g of fish meat, carefully removing all small bones.
7. Prepare the forcemeat as in the previous recipe, but do not whisk the egg white to stiff peaks; instead mix it with the yolk in the cream.
8. Fill the pike with forcemeat up to 3/4 capacity.
9. Close the belly with a wound closure stitch.
10. Brush the fish with the beaten egg.
11. Sprinkle with crushed rusks.
12. Place in a long pan greased with half the butter.
13. Arrange the remaining butter in flakes on top of the pike.
14. Fry in good oven heat for approximately 1/2 hour.
15. Baste with fish stock and baste frequently.
16. Place on a hot dish.
17. Remove the thread.
18. Garnish the fish with parsley, crayfish tails, or stewed mushrooms.
19. Serve with crayfish or mushroom sauce.
