## Ingredients

- 5 kg. rostbiff or dubbelbiff
- 2 tbsp. salt (30 gr.)
- water

## Directions

1. Rostbiff should hang 1-2 weeks depending on the season and be dried daily with a dry towel.
2. Pound on the skin side with a damp meat mallet, dry with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and rub in the salt.
3. If no special rostbiff pan is available, place the meat with the fillet up on a rack in a long pan and insert into a warm oven.
4. The oven should not be too hot at first, as the fat should partially melt before the roast browns.
5. Pour a little boiling water into the pan and gradually increase the heat.
6. When the roast is brown, baste it every 10 minutes with the gravy, which should be diluted with boiling water if needed. (Note: Baste the roast first and dilute afterwards, not the other way around.)
7. Calculate cooking time at 20-25 minutes per kg.
8. If the meat becomes too brown, place a sheet over it or cover with buttered paper.
9. Place the rostbiff on a warm platter and carve at the table.
10. Strain the gravy, skim well, reduce, taste and adjust seasoning, and serve without thickening.
11. Serve with French potatoes, potato croquettes or vegetables, along with glazed onions and if desired with horseradish shavings.
12. Horseradish should be scraped, rinsed, dried and cut with a vegetable slicer.
