## Ingredients

- 2 pike at 750 gr. each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 1/2 egg
- 3 tablespoons crushed hardtack
- For roasting: 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 3 decilitres fish stock (made from head and backbone)
- 1 teaspoon salt (5 gr.)
- 2 decilitres thick cream

## Directions

1. Do not scale the pike; clean them as described on page 171, cut into fillets, and remove the backbone and all small bones
2. Place the fillets with the skin side against the cutting board and cut the flesh carefully from the skin with a sharp knife
3. Rub the fillets with salt and let rest 1 hour, then dry again
4. Brush on both sides with the beaten egg and sprinkle with crushed hardtack
5. Grease an oblong fish mold with lid with half the cold butter, place the fillets in with the thin side against the thick, so they lie evenly, distribute the remaining butter in flakes on top, add the stock and cream
6. Put the lid on the mold and roast the fish in a warm oven for approximately 20 minutes
7. Serve with mashed potatoes. If no fish mold is available, the pike can be roasted as in recipe no. 339, but omit the parsley. The fillets can be cut into 3 cm. wide pieces crosswise, dipped in beaten egg, rolled in crushed hardtack, fried in butter on the stovetop, topped with a little browned butter, and served with stewed spinach or on a smorgasbord
