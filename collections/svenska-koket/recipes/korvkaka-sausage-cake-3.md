## Ingredients

- 2 deciliter rice grains (2 hg.)
- 1 1/2 liter milk
- 3 hg. calf liver
- 2 hg. fresh pork
- 30 raisins (30 gr.)
- 3 tablespoon syrup
- 1 1/2 tablespoon salt (23 gr.)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper (or 1/2 teaspoon marjoram)
- 1/2 tablespoon butter for the mold (10 gr.)
- 2 tablespoon crushed rusks

## Directions

1. Rinse the rice grains, scald them in hot water, add them to the boiling milk and let cook, stirring, until almost tender, then pour out and let cool.
2. Rinse the liver well, dry it, cut it into pieces, grind it 3 times through a meat grinder, and pass it through a fine sieve.
3. Grind the pork 2 times through the grinder.
4. Rinse the raisins in lukewarm water, scald them in hot water, cut them in half, remove the seeds, and mix them into the rice grains along with the liver, pork, syrup, and spices.
5. If desired, add a little marjoram. Mix the mixture well and fill it into a buttered and breadcrumb-strewn mold or baking pan and bake in regular oven heat for about 3/4 hour.
6. Serve with clarified butter and lingonberry jam.
