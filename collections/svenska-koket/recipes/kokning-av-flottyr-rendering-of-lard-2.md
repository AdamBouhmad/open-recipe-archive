## Ingredients

- 2 kg kidney suet
- 1 kg pork lard
- 1/2 liter water
- 1 raw potato

## Directions

1. Cut the suet and lard into cubes; carefully remove all glands and coarser membranes.
2. Place the cubes in plenty of water and let them sit for 2 days, changing the water once during this time.
3. Press the fat well and place it in a high iron pot along with 1/2 liter of cold water to cook. During the first hour, keep the lid on the pot.
4. Peel and halve the potato and let it cook with the lard, which will thereby become clearer.
5. The lard is done cooking when the membranes ('the counts'), which surrounded the fat, sink to the bottom and it gives off a thin smoke with a characteristic smell of lard.
6. Remove the pot from heat and after 10 minutes, strain the lard through a sieve or coarse cheesecloth into a stone jar and store in a cold place. From this batch you will get approximately 2 kg.
