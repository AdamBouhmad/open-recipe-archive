## Ingredients

- 150 gr. boneless chicken meat
- 1 tsk. salt (5 gr.)
- 1/4 tsk. white pepper
- 1 egg
- 1/2 msk. flour (5 gr.)
- 1 1/2–2 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. The meat is scraped free from sinews and membranes, mixed with the seasonings, ground 4 times through a meat grinder and pounded with a wooden pestle in a stone mortar to a fine mass.
2. The egg is whisked up and added little by little together with the flour, while the forcemeat is stirred vigorously.
3. It is passed through a hair sieve, placed in a small saucepan, which is set in a bowl with ice (it will break otherwise when the cream is worked in) and the cream is added teaspoonful by teaspoonful with steady stirring, after which the forcemeat is tasted carefully.
4. A long pan is brushed with butter and allowed to cool.
5. The forcemeat is placed in a piping bag with a smooth or ridged tip and piped directly into the pan in the form of small balls or oblong shapes, which are placed very close together.
6. When all the quenelles are formed, the pan is placed at the side of the stove and boiling salted water is added.
