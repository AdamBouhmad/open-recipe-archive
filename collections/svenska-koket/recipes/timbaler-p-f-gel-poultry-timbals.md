## Ingredients

- 2 partridges or 1 hazel grouse (about 225 grams scraped meat) or 1 large chicken (about 225 grams scraped meat)
- salt, white pepper
- 1 hectogram panade
- 1 hectogram butter
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 1/2 decilitres water
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 30 grams butter
- 75 grams sifted flour
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 grams) for the molds
- 6 truffles

## Directions

1. Prepare the panade: boil the water with salt and butter, then add the flour.
2. Stir the mixture with a spoon over direct heat until it releases from the pan and spoon, then place it on a buttered platter and brush lightly with butter so it does not dry.
3. Prepare the forcemeats separately. Pluck the poultry, clean the insides, rinse very well, dry with a cloth and skin.
4. Cut all meat loose from the bones and scrape clean of sinews and membranes.
5. Pound the meat with seasonings in a stone mortar until it becomes a fine forcemeat.
6. Take it up and pound the panade, which has cooled, with the butter to a smooth dough, add the forcemeat, and pound the mixture very well.
7. Place it in a bowl, add the egg with yolks under continuous vigorous stirring.
8. Pass the forcemeat through a sieve and stir again about 1/2 hour.
9. Test by cooking a little of it in salted water.
10. If the forcemeat is too loose, add a little egg white; if too stiff, an egg yolk or a little softened butter.
11. Butter small timbal molds and garnish with truffle cut in decorative figures.
12. Chop the remaining truffle finely and mix into the forcemeat.
13. Fill the molds 3/4 full with forcemeat, bind with butter-brushed butcher paper and bake in a water bath in the oven about 45 minutes.
14. The timbals are used as garnish around larded beef fillet, smoked pork loin or tongue.
15. The forcemeats can also be placed separately in one large decorated timbal mold and baked in a water bath in the oven about 1 1/2 hours.
16. Turn out, garnish with salad, parsley and tomatoes and serve as a course with brown sauce made from the browned poultry bones, or with mushroom sauce.
