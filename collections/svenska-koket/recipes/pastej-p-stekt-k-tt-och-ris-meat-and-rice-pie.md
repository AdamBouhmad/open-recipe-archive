## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 parts thick cream (about 180 gr.)
- 1/2 weight butter to the dough (about 165 gr.)
- flour
- 1/2 msk. butter (10 gr.) for the mold
- 1 sheet of butter paper
- 2 lit. peas
- 1/2 dcl. rice (150 gr.)
- 1–1 1/4 lit. milk
- 3 eggs
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 5 hg. boneless, cooked meat
- 1 medium-sized salted cucumber or 12 pickled onions
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 3 dcl. roasting sauce or 2 dcl. broth

## Directions

1. Whip the cream to stiff peaks and work in enough flour to make a reasonably firm dough.
2. Weigh the dough and incorporate half the weight of butter to the dough as for regular butter dough.
3. When the dough has rested, roll it out to a cake about 1 cm. thick and line a fluted or smooth baking pan (buttered with the butter) holding about 2 lit.; cut out a lid for the pan.
4. Cover the butter dough with thin butter paper and fill the pan with peas, place in a warm oven and bake until half done.
5. Pour out the peas and remove the paper.
6. Rinse the rice, scald it in hot water, add it to the boiling milk and let it cook until tender, then pour out and let it cool, stirring often.
7. Mix with the egg yolks and salt; last, fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
8. Cut the meat into thin, even slices or into fine dice.
9. Chop the cucumber or onion finely.
10. Layer the meat and rice in the pan alternately, sprinkling a little of the chopped cucumber or onion and spices on each layer of meat, and pour a little sauce or broth.
11. Place the butter dough lid on top, press down around the edges and bake the pudding in a warm oven for about 3/4 hour, or until the dough is completely tender, which is tested with a whisk.
12. Turn the pudding out onto a round platter, garnish around with small squares of butter dough and serve with brown gravy sauce if desired.
