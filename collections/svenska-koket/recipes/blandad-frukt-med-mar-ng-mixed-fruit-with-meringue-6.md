## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 hg. fine prunes (approximately 14 pieces)
- 1 hg. dried apricots (approximately 12 pieces)
- 1 dcl. caster sugar (85 gr.)
- 4 dcl. water
- 1 1/2 dcl. crushed sugar (143 gr.)
- 1/2 kg. large, beautiful apples
- 3 egg whites
- 105 gr. icing sugar

## Directions

1. Rinse the dried fruit well in several lukewarm waters, let drain on cloth and layer alternately with caster sugar in a pot, add the water, and let the fruit stand for approximately 12 hours.
2. Pour off the water and cook it with the crushed sugar into a clear syrup, which is allowed to cool.
3. Rinse the apples, dry, peel, cut into 4-6 wedges and remove the cores, place in the syrup and cook until they are soft, but must not fall apart.
4. Remove them with a slotted spoon and let cool, after which the syrup is cooked until it is thick as jelly.
5. On a silver platter, layer the apples, prunes and apricots alternately, between each layer place a little of the reduced syrup.
6. Prepare the meringue as in the previous recipe, pipe through a pastry tube in beautiful ridges over the fruit and place in a gentle oven heat.
7. When the meringue is half-dry and has a light yellow color, remove the dish and let cool.
8. Serve with whipped cream or vanilla sauce.
