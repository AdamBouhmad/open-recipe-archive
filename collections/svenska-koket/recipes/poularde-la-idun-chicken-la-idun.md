## Ingredients

- 1 poularde (hen) about 3 kg
- 1/2 lemon
- 1/2 tablespoon salt (8 gr.)
- 1 hectogram lard, in 1 slice
- 1 1/4 deciliter rice grains (125 gr.)
- 1 liter broth
- 1 small can goose liver
- 2–3 truffles
- 1 teaspoon salt, 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 tablespoon Madeira (optional)
- 1 liter clear chicken or veal broth
- thick cream
- 40 gr. fine butter
- 25 gr. truffle (optional, for larding before stuffing)

## Directions

1. Prepare the bird in the usual way, remove the bones and rub the breast and thighs with lemon.
2. Rinse the rice grains, scald in hot water, add to boiling broth and let cook until half soft, then drain and let cool.
3. Cut the goose liver and truffle into cubes and mix into the rice with salt, pepper and wine if desired.
4. Stuff the bird with the rice and sew well closed, then rub with salt, truss and place a lard slice on top, fastening it with fine twine.
5. Place in a pot just large enough to fit the bird, add the cold broth, cover with lid, place over heat and bring the broth to a simmer, then skim well.
6. Keep the broth at no more than 90° and let the bird rest at this temperature until tender, about 2 1/2–3 hours.
7. Test doneness by pricking a larding needle into the thick part of the thigh near the bone; if the liquid that runs out is white, the bird is done.
8. Cut the bird and arrange on a platter so it appears whole, garnish with the stuffing, salad and parsley and serve with light mushroom sauce prepared from the broth and mixed with thick cream and 40 gr. fine butter.
9. If desired, lard the bird with 25 gr. truffle before stuffing and trussing.
