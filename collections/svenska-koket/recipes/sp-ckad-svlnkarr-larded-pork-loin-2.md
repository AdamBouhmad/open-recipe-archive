## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg pork loin with bone
- 1/2 lemon
- 2 tsp. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1/4 tsp. ginger
- 1 hg. prunes
- 3 dcl. water
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 gr.)
- 1/2 liter boiling water
- 1/2 tsp. solid meat extract (4 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp. salt (3 gr.)

## Directions

1. Cut away most of the fat from the loin carefully, and loosen the bone halfway with a pointed knife.
2. Dry with a cloth wrung out in hot water, beat lightly with a damp mallet and rub well with the lemon.
3. Rub the loin with a mixture of salt, white pepper and ginger.
4. Rinse the prunes well in lukewarm water, blanch for 3 minutes in 3 dcl. water, and use the broth to thin the roast.
5. Pit the prunes, cut them lengthwise in two halves, and lard the loin with them in tight, even rows, then tie it with fine twine previously dipped in boiling water.
6. Brown the butter in a roasting pan, place the loin in it with the meat side down and brown well on all sides.
7. Thin it first with the hot prune broth and then with the water mixed with meat extract and salt.
8. Let the loin roast slowly with a tightly closed lid until tender, about 2.5 hours, basting about every 10 minutes.
9. Carve and arrange on the bone so it looks whole, place on a warm plate and garnish with 2 hg. cooked prunes and parsley or salad.
10. Serve with browned potatoes and Russian peas and with the strained and skimmed meat gravy mixed with soy and spices, or with a reduced sauce (see recipe no. 429).
