## Ingredients

- 2 kg marrow bone, beef neck or rib
- 4 liters water
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 2 carrots
- 1 parsnip
- 1 leek
- 1 piece celery root
- 1/2 rutabaga
- 4 white peppercorns
- 1 hectogram lean boneless meat (for clarification)
- 2 egg whites (for clarification)
- 1/2 carrot (for clarification)
- 1/2 leek (for clarification)
- 1 small piece rutabaga (for clarification)
- 1 small piece celery (for clarification)
- a few sprigs of parsley (for clarification)
- or 2 whole eggs (for clarification)

## Directions

1. The meat, preferably freshly slaughtered, is washed with a cloth dipped in cold water, cut into small pieces, and placed in cold water in a tall pot of copper or enameled iron with a tight-fitting lid.
2. Salt is added at once, after which the kettle is left at the side of the fire for 1/2 hour.
3. The whole is then brought slowly to a boil, skimmed very well when it comes to a boil, and is then allowed to simmer slowly for 5-6 hours.
4. During the first hour the broth is boiling, all the scum that floats to the surface is carefully skimmed. (One can add a little cold water, so that the boiling stops for a moment, and when the broth boils again, it is skimmed at once.)
5. After the broth has been boiling for a couple of hours, the roasted and rinsed vegetables are added along with the peppercorns.
6. When the broth has cooked its full time, it is strained and left to stand until the fat has risen to the top, after which this is very well skimmed off.
7. It is best if the broth is cooked the day before it will be used.
8. For clarification, the ground meat is mixed with the egg whites and the chopped vegetables, and the cold broth is stirred into this little by little.
9. The broth is then allowed to boil for 15 minutes and is then strained through a cloth or woolen cloth wrung out in hot water.
10. Broth can also be clarified with whole eggs. It will have a beautiful color from the yolk and will be clear.
11. If the broth is cooked the day before it will be used, clarifying agents may be unnecessary.
12. If brown broth is desired, the meat is browned before cooking.
