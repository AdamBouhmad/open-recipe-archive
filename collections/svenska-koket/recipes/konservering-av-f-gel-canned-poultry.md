## Ingredients

- Poultry
- Butter
- Broth or water

## Directions

1. Brown the poultry in butter and then let it roast until it is almost tender. During roasting, moisten it, preferably with broth, otherwise with water (never with milk or cream). When it is roasted, remove it and divide it into pieces. First cut off the thighs and wings, then separate the breast bone with attached meat from the rest of the carcass, which is not used in the preservation. Place the divided poultry in a well-cleaned tin canning jar, arranging the pieces as close together as possible. Strain the meat gravy (but do not skim off the fat) and pour it over the meat so that it is completely covered. When the fat solidifies, it forms a seal impervious to air. Screw or seal the canning jars shut and place them in a pot of boiling salted water. The water should stand well above the jars. If it bubbles at the lid, this is a sign that the jar is not tight. Remove it and screw it tighter or seal it again. Continue cooking for 0.5 to 1 hour, depending on the size of the jar. Remove the jars and cool them quickly. Store them in a cold place.
