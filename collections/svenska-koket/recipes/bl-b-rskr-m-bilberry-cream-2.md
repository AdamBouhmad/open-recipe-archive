## Ingredients

- 1 liter fresh or 1/2 liter preserved bilberries
- 1 1/2–2 dcl. crushed sugar
- 8 dcl. water
- 1 tbsp. mondamin flour (15 gr.)
- 1 tbsp. potato flour (15 gr.)

## Directions

1. Clean and rinse the bilberries in a sieve and drain.
2. Boil a thin, clear syrup from the sugar and water.
3. Add the bilberries and let them boil until they begin to fall apart.
4. Mix both flour types with 1 dcl. cold water and slowly stir into the cream, which should not boil as it may become lumpy.
5. Let the cream boil for 5 minutes while stirring.
6. Pour into a compote bowl rinsed with lukewarm water and sprinkle with sugar to prevent a skin from forming.
7. Serve with milk or cream. If desired, the berries may be strained out and only the juice used; in this case, add 1 tsp. good butter to prevent the cream from becoming tough.
8. If using preserved bilberries, they only need one boil in the sugar syrup.
