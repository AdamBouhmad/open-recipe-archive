## Ingredients

- Peas and beans
- Lightly salted water (1 tablespoon salt per liter of water)
- Bicarbonate or alum
- Parsley
- Sugar peas, fresh beans, spinach, and parsley

## Directions

1. Drying of vegetables can be done with or without prior blanching.
2. It is best to blanch them as follows.
3. Sort peas and beans and blanch them for 3-4 minutes in boiling lightly salted water (1 tablespoon salt per liter of water).
4. To preserve the color, add a knife-tip of bicarbonate or alum and 1 bunch of parsley to the cooking water.
5. Rinse sugar peas, fresh beans, spinach, and parsley well, let them drain, and blanch for a few minutes.
6. Rinse the vegetables with cold water, spread them out on clean linen, preferably on drying racks, otherwise on baking sheets, and place in an oven at 35-40°C heat (preferably a baking oven).
7. If the vegetables are not to be blanched, rinse them well, let them drain, and dry in the oven for 2-3 days.
8. You can also dry them simply in the open air.
9. Nettles are best dried this way and should not be rinsed.
10. The vegetables should be stored in tin cans but should not be stored until they are completely dry.
11. Store in a dry place.
