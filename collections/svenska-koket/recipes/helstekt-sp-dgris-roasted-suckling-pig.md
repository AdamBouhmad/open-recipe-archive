## Ingredients

- 1 suckling pig of approximately 4 kg.
- 1 1/2 tablespoons salt (23 gr.)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon ginger
- small raw, sour apples
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.)
- 3/4 litre broth (1 hectogram lard)

## Directions

1. Clean the pig thoroughly in the belly cavity and mouth, brush the teeth well, and rinse and dry the pig.
2. Rub it internally with a mixture of salt, white pepper and ginger.
3. Rinse the apples, dry them, remove stem and blossom, and fill the pig with the apples so that it retains its shape, then sew it up with a running seam.
4. Bend the knees, and place the pig on its knees in a long pan, brushed with half of the butter.
5. Place the pan in a warm oven, and pour on the boiling broth.
6. Let the pig roast until it is tender, or about 2 hours. During the first hour, baste it frequently with the broth, but afterwards rub only the skin every 5 minutes with the lard or with the remaining butter, wrapped in a linen cloth, or with a linen cloth dipped in cooking oil, so that the skin becomes crisp and golden and does not burst.
7. Place it on a large dish, cut into slices, which are again put together so that the pig appears whole.
8. Place an apple in the mouth, and wrap the ears with curled paper.
