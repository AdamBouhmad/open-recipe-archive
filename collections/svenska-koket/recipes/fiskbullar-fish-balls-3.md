## Ingredients

- 7 hg. saithe
- 250 gr. scraped fish meat
- 80 gr. butter
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 2 eggs
- 1 1/2 tablespoon potato flour (23 gr.)
- 4-5 dcl. good milk
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 2 teaspoons sugar (10 gr.)
- mace (optional)
- 1 1/2 liters fish stock for cooking

## Directions

1. Clean the saithe, rinse it well and dry it. Cut off the head, remove the gills; fillet the fish and remove the backbone.
2. Scrape all meat from the bones and finely scrape the remaining fish meat. Weigh the meat and it should amount to 250 gr.
3. Make stock from the head, bones and skin, in which the balls will later be cooked.
4. Mix the fish meat with the butter and pass it 5 times through a meat grinder. Pound the mixture in a mortar with a wooden pestle together with the salt.
5. Mix in the beaten eggs and flour and pound the mixture again until it becomes fine and cohesive.
6. Work the forcemeat with a spoon for 3/4 hour, adding the milk one tablespoon at a time.
7. Taste and season with salt, sugar and, if desired, a pinch of mace. Form with 2 tablespoons, dipped in lukewarm water, into small round balls.
8. Cook the balls in the fish stock for 3-4 minutes. Test the forcemeat first by cooking a small sample; if it is too firm, add a little more milk, otherwise add 1 egg yolk.
9. Serve the fish balls in crayfish sauce or in ordinary fish sauce, made from butter, flour, fish stock and 1-2 egg yolks.
