## Ingredients

- Sockerkaka (Sugar cake): 2 eggs
- 120 gr. sugar
- 60 gr. wheat flour
- 4 bitter almonds
- 0.5 tablespoon butter (10 gr.) for the pan
- 2 tablespoons crushed biscuits
- Äppelkompott (Apple compote): 2 hectograms sugar
- 4 deciliters water
- juice and peel of 1/4 lemon
- 1.5 liters apples
- Marängmassa (Meringue): 3 egg whites
- 105 gr. icing sugar
- For garnish: 10 gr. pistachio almonds (approximately 22 pieces)
- 40 gr. sweet almond (approximately 40 pieces)

## Directions

1. Butter a form or pan with the same width as the dish on which the meringue will be served, and sprinkle with crushed biscuits.
2. Beat the eggs and sugar for 0.5 hour, then add the flour and the blanched and grated almonds.
3. Fill the batter into the buttered pan and bake in regular oven heat for approximately 20 minutes.
4. Turn out onto a wire rack, let cool, and place on a round dish, preferably silver.
5. Prepare the apple compote as in recipe no. 820. If desired, cook the sugar syrup with the lemon.
6. When all the apples are cooked, cook down the syrup until it becomes syrupy.
7. When the apples are cold, arrange them in a pyramid on the cake together with the syrup, which should be like jelly.
8. Whip the egg whites until stiff, sift the icing sugar and fold it little by little with a silver fork into the egg white foam.
9. Spread a portion of the meringue immediately over the apples and cake.
10. Pipe the remaining meringue through a piping bag in small peaks around the edge of the cake.
11. Blanch the pistachio almonds, chop finely and sprinkle evenly over the meringue.
12. Blanch the sweet almonds and cut lengthwise into thin slices; insert these densely into the meringue and one slice in each peak.
13. Place the arrangement in low oven heat and let stand until the meringue is half-dry and has a light yellow color.
14. Serve cold with vanilla sauce or whipped cream.
