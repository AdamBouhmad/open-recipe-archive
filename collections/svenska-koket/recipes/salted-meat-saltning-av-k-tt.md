## Ingredients

- 10 kg meat
- 708 gr finely pounded coarse salt
- 6 gr finely pounded potassium nitrate
- 6 gr granulated sugar
- For the brine: 10 liters cold water
- 1.7 kg coarse salt
- 50 gr saltpeter
- 50 gr granulated sugar

## Directions

1. Cut the meat into suitable pieces (see butchering plans), remove all glands carefully.
2. Rub the meat pieces lightly with a mixture of 708 gr finely pounded coarse salt, 6 gr finely pounded potassium nitrate and 6 gr granulated sugar.
3. Place them in a stone or earthenware vessel that has been scoured with hot water and sprinkled with salt.
4. Prepare the brine by dissolving 1.7 kg coarse salt, 50 gr saltpeter and 50 gr granulated sugar in 10 liters cold water. Do not boil the brine, only dissolve the spices in the water, then skim it and it is ready.
5. Pour the brine immediately over the meat.
6. Add a bottom and a light weight, and pour in enough brine so that it stands well over the meat.
7. After 10-12 days, transfer the meat to another vessel treated in the same way, and skim the same brine in which the meat lay well and pour it over. Repeat this every week until the meat or pork is completely salted.
