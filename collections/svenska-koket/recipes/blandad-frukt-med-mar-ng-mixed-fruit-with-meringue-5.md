## Ingredients

- 1.5 hectograms fine prunes (about 14 pieces)
- 1 hectogram dried apricots (about 12 pieces)
- 1 deciliter granulated sugar (85 g)
- 4 deciliters water
- 1.5 deciliters lump sugar (143 g)
- 0.5 kg large, fine apples
- For the meringue: 3 egg whites
- 105 g powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Rinse the dried fruit thoroughly in several lukewarm waters, let it drain on cloth and layer it with granulated sugar in a pot.
2. Add the water and let the fruit stand for about 12 hours.
3. Pour off the water and cook it with the lump sugar into a clear syrup and let it cool.
4. Wash, dry, peel the apples, cut them into 4-6 wedges and remove the cores.
5. Place them in the sugar syrup and let them cook until they are soft but do not fall apart.
6. Remove them with a slotted spoon and let them cool, then let the syrup cook until it is thick as jelly.
7. On a silver dish, layer the apples, prunes and apricots alternately, placing a little of the reduced syrup between each layer.
8. Prepare the meringue as in the previous recipe, pipe it through a piping bag in beautiful lines over the fruit and place in gentle oven heat.
9. When the meringue is half dry and has a light yellow color, remove the dish and let it cool.
10. Serve with whipped cream or vanilla sauce.
