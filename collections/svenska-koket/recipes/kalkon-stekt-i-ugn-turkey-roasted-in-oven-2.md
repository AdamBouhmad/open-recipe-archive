## Ingredients

- 1 turkey cock, about 5 kg.
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 1 1/2 hg. lard, in 1 slice
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 1 lit. broth

## Directions

1. Pluck the turkey immediately after it is slaughtered and let it hang for several days thereafter, so that the meat becomes tender.
2. Draw it, rinse (see page 300), dry with a cloth, rub inside and outside with a mixture of salt and pepper, truss it, and cover the breast with a large slice of lard, which is fastened with fine twine.
3. Grease a roasting pan with butter over as large an area as the bird occupies.
4. Place the bird on it with the breast up, add the giblets (see page 300) and pour on half of the broth.
5. Place the turkey in a warm oven and let it roast until tender, or about 2—3 hours, during which time baste it about every 10 minutes and add more broth as needed.
6. If the bird becomes too brown, cover it with butter-brushed paper.
7. Strain the meat gravy, skim it, cook it down, season it and serve as gravy, or thicken it as in no. 429.
8. Carve the turkey neatly, arrange on a warm dish so that it looks whole, garnish with salad, parsley and browned potatoes.
9. Serve with green salad and sour salads.
