## Ingredients

- 6 large pork chops
- 1/2 lemon
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 tablespoon butter (30 g)
- 2 deciliters finely crushed breadcrumbs
- 2 tablespoon finely chopped parsley
- 2 1/2 tablespoon butter for frying (50 g)
- 2 deciliters broth

## Directions

1. If the chops are small, calculate 2 per person for a meal.
2. Dry the chops with a cloth wrung out in hot water, chop them so that a bone follows with each chop, then lightly pound, trim, and shape them.
3. Pour the lemon juice over the chops, rub them with the seasonings, and let them lie on top of each other for 1/4 hour.
4. Brush them with butter and turn them in a mixture of the crushed breadcrumbs and parsley.
5. Brown the butter in a frying pan, place the chops in and let them fry slowly without a lid for about 7 minutes on each side.
6. Place on a warm platter, whisk the pan with the boiling broth, and pour the sauce onto the platter.
7. Garnish with parsley or salad and serve with vegetables, boiled or fried potatoes, or potato puree.
8. Alternatively, the chops can be fried without breading, but should then be browned on both sides, diluted with broth or cream, and thereafter cooked through with a lid, otherwise they will dry out.
