## Ingredients

- 4 partridges
- 1 chicken
- 1 hg. boneless veal
- 80 gr. butter
- 2 eggs
- 3 dcl. thick cream
- 1 tbsp. flour (10 gr.)
- salt, white pepper
- 2–3 truffles
- 3 hg. lard, in flat slices
- 1 1/2 lit. broth for cooking
- 1 cup rice grains
- 1 lit. water
- 2 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. French mustard
- 1/2 lit. strong, clear, brown broth
- 4–5 sheets gelatin
- 1 lettuce head
- parsley
- 2 truffles
- 2 tomatoes

## Directions

1. Pluck and singe the partridges; chop off the head, wings, and feet carefully, but leave the bones. Slit them up the back, remove the entrails, rinse the birds well and dry them. Loosen the meat from the carcass and skin as close to the skin as possible, without breaking it. Remove the chicken, rinse well and pluck it. Loosen all the meat from the carcass and scrape together with the partridge and veal meat, free from sinews and membranes.
2. Beat the meat well with the butter and eggs, and work the forcemeat for 1/2 hour, during which add the cream little by little, together with the seasonings and flour. Pass the forcemeat through a fine sieve and mix with the truffles cut into strips, then place on ice to set.
3. When the forcemeat is set, fill it into the bird skins, which are shaped like partridges. Sew the skin together, lay lard slices on the birds, tie a scallop shell around each one. Place them in a flat pan breast-side up and cook in gentle broth for about 40 minutes.
4. Rinse the rice grains, put them in boiling salted water and cook until soft, then drain in a colander and let drain well. Mix with mustard and pack into a water-rinsed, flat, round tin.
5. For the aspic, rinse the gelatin in cold water and dissolve it in the hot broth, which is then poured onto a plate to set.
6. Turn the rice base out onto a round serving platter and garnish with chopped aspic and parsley; place the lettuce head in the middle. Free the birds from the lard and string with which they were sewn together and cut into beautiful slices, but so that they retain their shape. Arrange them in hollows around the salad and garnish the dish with the cut tomatoes and the remaining aspic, cut into triangles with a small truffle slice on each triangle. Serve the partridges as a middle course with brown gravy made from the browned bird bones, or with bird sauce reduced with cream.
