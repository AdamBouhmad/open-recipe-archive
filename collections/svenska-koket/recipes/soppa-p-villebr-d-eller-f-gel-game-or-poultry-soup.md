## Ingredients

- Remnants and bones of hare or scraps and remnants of poultry
- 2 carrots
- 1/2 celeriac
- 1 small red onion
- 4 parsley stems
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.)
- 2 liters broth without meat
- 4 white peppercorns
- 2 cloves
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 gr.)
- salt, cayenne
- sugar
- 1/2 deciliter sherry or Madeira
- 10 gr. fine butter

## Directions

1. If raw bones and scraps are used, the soup will be finer in taste.
2. Cut away the best meat and use it for forcemeat or meatballs, which are served in the soup.
3. Cut the remnants and bones into small pieces and brown in 2 tablespoons of the butter together with the cleaned and cut vegetables and parsley.
4. Add the cold broth, place in the whole spices and let the soup cook with a tightly closed lid about 2 hours.
5. Separate all meat well from the bones, which should cook further in the broth for 1/2 hour.
6. Grind the meat 4 times through a meat grinder, mix with 1 deciliter broth and pass through a sieve.
7. Strain and skim the broth and measure it—should be 1.5 liters.
8. Fry the remaining butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the broth and the passed meat and let the soup cook for 5 minutes.
9. Season with the spices, wine and butter and serve with meatballs of game or cheese croutons.
