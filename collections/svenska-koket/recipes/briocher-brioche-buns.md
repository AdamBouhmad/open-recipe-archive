## Ingredients

- 500 g premium wheat flour
- 15 g fresh yeast
- 1.5 dl lukewarm milk
- 5 g salt
- 45 g sugar
- 3-4 cardamom pods (optional)
- 3 eggs
- 300 g washed butter
- 1 egg for brushing

## Directions

1. Sift the flour. Crumble the yeast into one third of the flour, then add the milk and work the dough, which should be loose, quickly and roll it into a ball on the work surface, making a cross on top.
2. Place the dough in a covered bowl and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour.
3. Work in the salt, sugar, cardamom pods if desired, and the eggs one at a time. Between each egg addition, work the dough for 5 minutes and add more flour so the dough becomes appropriately firm.
4. Roll out the dough and work in the butter as described in the previous recipe, then place it in a bowl, cover it, and let it stand in a moderately warm room for 6-8 hours.
5. After 3 hours, stop the rising by placing the dough on a floured work surface and slapping it with your hand. Return it to the bowl and let it rise for another 4-5 hours.
6. Place the dough in brioche molds, brush with egg, let it rise, and bake in a warm oven. Alternatively, it can be shaped into small round buns with a small indentation in the middle, filled with a small ball the size of a small nut. Treat the same as above.
7. Serve with coffee and tea.
