## Ingredients

- 1 kg. peeled potato, about 1 1/2 liters unpeeled
- 30 gr. butter
- 1 tsp. salt, 2 tsp. sugar
- a little white pepper (a little nutmeg)
- 3 egg yolks
- For filling: 60 gr. butter
- 25 gr. grated cheese
- 1 egg yolk (1/4 tsp. sugar)
- For brushing: 1 egg

## Directions

1. Treat the potato as described on page 337 et seq., peel thinly, cut into pieces, weigh and cook quickly in salted water until soft but not falling apart.
2. Empty them at once into a colander and let stand in the front of the oven a few minutes to dry.
3. Pass the potato through a hair sieve.
4. Place the purée in a saucepan, add the cold butter and spices and stir with a fork over the heat until thick.
5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and add the egg yolks one at a time, stirring vigorously with a fork, then taste carefully and let cool.
6. Pipe onto a buttered sheet in ring upon ring to fairly tall crusts with a base. Small lids for the crusts are piped separately, brushed with beaten egg and baked separately.
7. For the filling, stir the butter until white and fluffy, mix with the cheese and the beaten egg yolk and sugar, if desired.
8. Fill the crusts with the mixture, brush with beaten egg and bake in a warm oven until they get a beautiful golden-yellow color, or about 10 minutes.
9. Remove them, place the lids on top and serve the crusts as a garnish around fried meat.
10. The crusts may also be filled with carrots, cut into fine dice and stewed, or with fine preserved peas, cooked in a little butter.
