## Ingredients

- 1 calf's head
- 2.5 liters water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 6 white peppercorns
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 1 slice smoked pork (20 gr.)
- 2 red onions
- 3 carrots
- 1 leek
- 1/2 celery
- 1 tablespoon tomato purée
- 6 tablespoons flour (60 gr.)
- 2 liters brown broth
- sugar soy sauce (2 teaspoons burnt sugar, 2 tablespoons hot water)
- salt
- cayenne
- 1 deciliter Madeira wine
- hard-boiled egg yolks
- fish quenelles

## Directions

1. The calf's head should be scalded and shaved with the skin not removed.
2. Split it; remove the tongue, brain (to be used for croquettes and stewing) and eyes.
3. Rinse the head and let it soak in plenty of cold water for about 12 hours so the blood is drawn out.
4. Clean and blanch for 10 minutes, place in cold water with the salt, skim carefully and let it cook with the peppercorns until the meat loosens from the bones.
5. Remove the head, cut the meat loose, place on a flat dish under light pressure, strain the broth and skim.
6. Brown the pork slice and the prepared, cut-up vegetables well in 2 tablespoons of the butter.
7. Add the broth and tomato purée and let everything cook for about 2 hours.
8. Strain the broth and skim again, measure it and it should make 1 liter.
9. Fry the remaining butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the broth and bouillon, place the meat cut into small, even pieces in it and let the soup cook while stirring for 5 minutes.
10. Color it with sugar soy sauce (2 teaspoons burnt sugar, 2 tablespoons hot water), season with the spices and wine, and serve with hard-boiled egg yolks and fish quenelles.
