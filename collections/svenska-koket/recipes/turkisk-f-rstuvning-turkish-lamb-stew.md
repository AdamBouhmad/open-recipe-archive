## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg lamb brisket
- 0.5 tablespoon salt (8 gr.)
- 0.5 teaspoon white pepper
- 6 tablespoons butter (120 gr.)
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped red onion
- 3–4 tablespoons tomato paste
- 4 deciliters brown sauce
- 3 deciliters broth
- 1 small sprig thyme
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 small leek
- 2 carrots
- 2–3 sugar beets
- 1 celery
- 1 Portuguese onion
- 2 deciliters rice grains (2 hg.)
- 2 teaspoons finely chopped red onion
- 1 gr. finely ground saffron
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 8 deciliters boiling broth
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)
- 0.25 teaspoon white pepper
- 2 tablespoons cold butter (40 gr.)

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water and cut into even, beautiful pieces, which are rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper and browned in half the butter together with the onion.
2. Dilute the tomato paste and brown sauce and add to the meat, which is allowed to slowly boil for approximately 45 minutes.
3. Transfer to another pot, strain the sauce, skim it, dilute with broth and add.
4. Tie together the thyme, bay leaf, and leek and add.
5. Prepare the carrots, beets, and celery, cut into beautiful pieces and brown in the remaining butter together with the chopped Portuguese onion.
6. Add the vegetables to the meat, which is then allowed to boil with a lid for approximately 30 minutes in the oven.
7. Test if the meat is tender.
8. Rinse the rice grains first with cold and then with boiling water and allow to drain well.
9. Then sauté them in the butter together with onion and saffron in an iron pot.
10. Dilute the broth, place a butter-brushed paper over it and allow the grains to boil in the oven for approximately 20 minutes.
11. Mix well with the cold butter and spices and arrange in a ring on a warm serving dish.
12. Place the meat in the middle of the dish and pour the sauce over it.
13. Garnish the dish with parsley and serve very hot.
14. Curry can be used instead of saffron.
