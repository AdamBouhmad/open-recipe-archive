## Ingredients

- 2 cod of approximately 7 hg each
- 1 tbsp salt (15 g)
- 1 1/2 tbsp butter (30 g)
- 1 small red onion
- 3 dcl strong meat stock
- 4 dcl fish broth
- 1/2 dcl white wine
- 1 1/2 tbsp butter (30 g)
- 4 tbsp flour (40 g)
- 4 1/2 dcl of the broth in which the fish cooked
- 1/2 dcl white wine
- cayenne

## Directions

1. Clean the cod, rinse well, dry and cut into fillets.
2. Cut away the backbone and cut the meat from the skin.
3. Rub the fillets with salt, let them lie 1 hour and dry again.
4. Cook broth on the head, bones and skin.
5. Melt the butter in a low saucepan or small long pan, fry the peeled and sliced onion in it, place the fish fillets in, add the meat stock, broth and wine, and cook the fish 10-15 minutes as in recipe no. 328.
6. Place it on a warm serving platter with a slotted spoon and pour the sauce over.
7. For the sauce, fry butter and flour 2 minutes, add the strained fish broth, let the sauce boil 5 minutes and season with wine and cayenne.
8. Garnish the platter with parsley and serve with small boiled potatoes.
