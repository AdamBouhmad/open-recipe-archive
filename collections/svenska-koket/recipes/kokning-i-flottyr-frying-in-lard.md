## Ingredients

- Lard (for frying)
- Food items to be fried

## Directions

1. Use an iron or copper pot, preferably round in shape and sufficiently deep so that items placed in it have good room to come into contact with the lard, even if the pot is only half-filled with it.
2. Heat the lard until a thin smoke rises from its surface, at which point it is around 160 degrees C. You can also test if the lard is suitably hot by placing a hard piece of bread in it, around which small bubbles will form immediately.
3. Place what is to be fried in it, not too much at once so the lard does not cool, and fry until it becomes golden brown, then place it on gray paper so the lard drains well.
4. Leftover lard can be used several times if it is reheated in water or milk. When it has solidified, gently scrape off any sediment from the bottom.
