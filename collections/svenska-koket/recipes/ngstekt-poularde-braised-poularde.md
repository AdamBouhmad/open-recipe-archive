## Ingredients

- 1 poularde or 1 capon, approximately 3 kg
- 0.5 lemon
- 25 g truffle, ham or tongue
- 0.5 tablespoon salt (8 g)
- 0.25 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 hectogram fat, in 1 slice
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 2 carrots
- 1 red onion
- 1 celery stalk
- 1 tablespoon raw, finely chopped ham
- 0.75 liters clear veal or chicken broth
- 1 deciliter mushroom puree, Madeira or white wine
- 3 deciliters dark broth

## Directions

1. Prepare the bird in the usual manner (see page 299 onwards), rub with lemon on breast and legs, then dip these for a few moments in boiling light broth or water
2. Lard it with strips of truffle, ham or tongue, rub inside and outside with a mixture of salt and pepper, truss it and lay the fat slice on top, which is fastened with fine twine
3. In a pot, not larger than that the bird just fits, brown the butter, place the bird in it and brown it nicely on all sides, then remove it
4. Trim the carrots and finely chop the red parts, likewise the red onion and the inner part of the celery stalk
5. Place the vegetables in the pot together with the ham; place the bird on top and dilute with one-third of the broth
6. Cover and let the broth reduce over low heat until thick and simmering
7. Then add the rest of the broth, place the pot without cover in the oven and roast the bird until tender, basting every 10 minutes
8. Ten minutes before the bird is done, add the mushroom puree or wine
9. Place the bird in a deep dish and cover to keep warm
10. Strain the vegetables (which should not be burnt) with the dark broth and let cook 10 minutes
11. Strain through a cloth wrung out in hot water, then carefully remove all fat from the sauce, bring to a boil and taste
12. Cut the bird (see page 300), garnish with small sausages, vegetables and browned potatoes
13. Serve the sauce in a sauce bowl
