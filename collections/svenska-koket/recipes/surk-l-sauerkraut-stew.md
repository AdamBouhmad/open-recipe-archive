## Ingredients

- 1.25 liters fresh sauerkraut
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 500 g fresh pork
- 1 carrot
- 1 small red onion
- 1 clove (20 g juniper berries)
- 50 g lard
- 4 parsley stems
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 liters secondary broth

## Directions

1. Fresh sauerkraut need not be soaked. If the sauerkraut is old, it should be soaked about 12 hours, then drained well on a sieve before preparation.
2. Mix the cabbage with salt and pepper.
3. Cut the pork into slices and use them to line the bottom and sides of an iron pot.
4. Add the cabbage along with the peeled and quartered carrot, the peeled onion with the clove inserted into it, the juniper berries (tied in muslin) if desired, the lard, and the parsley stems tied together with the bay leaf.
5. Lay the remaining pork slices on top, add the broth so it reaches just above the cabbage.
6. Let the cabbage come to a boil, then place the pot in a warm oven.
