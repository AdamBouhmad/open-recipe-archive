## Ingredients

- Leftovers of cooked chicken (3 hg.) or 1 young chicken
- 12 dcl. water
- 1 msk. salt
- 4 white peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 salad heads
- 1/4 tsk. salt
- 1/4 tsk. paprika
- 1 msk. oil
- 1 msk. vinegar
- 2 egg yolks
- 1/2 tsk. salt, cayenne
- 1/4 tsk. French mustard
- 1 1/2 dcl. fine oil
- 1 msk. tarragon vinegar
- 1/2 msk. lemon juice
- 1 msk. boiling water
- 2 small salad heads
- 2 hard-boiled eggs, in wedges
- 2 msk. capers
- 4 pitted, preserved olives

## Directions

1. If using leftovers of cooked chicken, cut the meat into even pieces.
2. If using a whole chicken, pluck it and singe it with denatured alcohol or brandy.
3. Chop off wings and legs.
4. Cut it up, remove the meat, rinse very well and let it soak in water for about 6 hours.
5. Dry the chicken with a kitchen towel and place in boiling water, which should be well skimmed when it comes to a boil, then add the spices.
6. When the meat is tender, after 1–2 hours, remove it and pull off the skin.
7. Let the meat cool and cut from the bones into small, beautiful, even pieces.
8. Prepare the green salad as in recipe no. 176 and place on a round plate, preferably of silver.
9. Prepare the mayonnaise as in recipe no. 10.
10. Arrange the meat and mayonnaise in alternating layers in pyramid form on top of the salad.
11. Garnish the arrangement with the innermost part of small salad heads, egg wedges, capers and slices of olives.
12. Serve as a side course.
