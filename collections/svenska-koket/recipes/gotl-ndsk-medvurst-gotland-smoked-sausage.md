## Ingredients

- 1 kg beef
- 1 kg pork
- 1 kg lard
- 1 kg cooked, peeled potato
- 2 liters unskim med milk
- 75-100 grams butter salt (Luneburg)
- 1 grated nutmeg
- 1½ teaspoons ground cloves
- 2 teaspoons ground white pepper
- 2 teaspoons grated ginger
- 2 level teaspoons ground potassium nitrate
- 6 meters straight casings
- For rubbing in: 3 tablespoons salt
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 teaspoon ground potassium nitrate

## Directions

1. Wash the meat with a cloth dipped in cold water and cut into cubes.
2. Remove sinews and membranes and pass the meat through the grinder: the beef 7 times and the pork 5 times.
3. The cooked, cooled potato is grated or passed through a purée press.
4. Cut the lard into fine cubes.
5. Boil the milk, preferably the day before, and when it is properly cooled, whip it with its cream.
6. Work all the ingredients well together with your hands for 1 hour.
7. Stuff the forcemeat as tightly as possible into straight, prepared ox casings with the tallow side turned out.
8. Prick the sausages with a needle so that all air goes out and tie them up well.
9. Rinse with cold water and then rub with a mixture of salt, sugar, and potassium nitrate.
10. Cold smoke the smoked sausages for 6 days, not too heavily.
