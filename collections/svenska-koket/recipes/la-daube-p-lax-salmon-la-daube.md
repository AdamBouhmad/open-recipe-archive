## Ingredients

- 1 piece of salmon weighing 1 1/2 kg.
- 3 tbsp. salt
- 1 1/2 liters water
- 6 allspice berries
- 1 bay leaf
- 5 dill stems
- 1 1/2 liters of fish stock
- 10-12 sheets gelatin
- 3 egg whites
- 1 tbsp. cold water
- 4 tbsp. good white wine
- 5 dill stems
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- dill

## Directions

1. Scale, clean and wash the salmon piece well with a fish towel.
2. Cut it into slices and rub these with salt, let them rest for a while.
3. Place the fish in cold water with the remaining salt, spices and dill and let it cook slowly until tender, about 15 minutes.
4. Let it cool in the stock and then place it on a dish with a slotted spoon.
5. Taste the fish stock and prepare the gelée as in recipe no. 181.
6. When cold, add the wine.
7. Pour a little of the clear gelée on the bottom of a beautiful, water-rinsed à-la-daube mold and place on ice to set.
8. Garnish the bottom with the hard-boiled egg, cut into beautiful shapes, and dill.
9. Pour a few drops of gelée over the garnish and let it set so the garnish does not move.
10. Fill the mold with the boned fish, pour the remaining gelée carefully over and let the dish set in a cold place.
11. Turn out as in the previous recipe, garnish with chopped gelée and dill and serve at the smörgåsbord or as a course with real mayonnaise.
