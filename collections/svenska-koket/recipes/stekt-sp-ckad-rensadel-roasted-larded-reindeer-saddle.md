## Ingredients

- 2 kg short-cut saddle
- 3 litres skimmed milk or drinking milk
- 1½ hectograms lard
- ½ tablespoon salt (8 grams)
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 3 decilitres broth
- 3 decilitres cream or good milk

## Directions

1. Thaw the saddle as the meat in the previous recipe.
2. Dry it well and carefully remove all membranes, then lard the saddle thickly with finely cut lard strips dipped in salt.
3. Brown the butter in a roasting pan, place the saddle in with the meat side down and brown it beautifully, then turn it and add liquid, first with the boiling broth, then with the boiling cream or milk, and let it roast with a tightly closed lid until tender, about 2½ hours, basting about every 10 minutes.
4. Place the saddle on a warm platter, loosen the meat neatly on both sides of the backbone, place on a cutting board and slice across into beautiful slices, which are then reassembled on the saddle so it looks whole.
5. Garnish with browned potatoes, salad or parsley and serve with lingonberry jelly and reduced sauce (see no. 429).
