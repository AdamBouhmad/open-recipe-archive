## Ingredients

- 150 gr. flour
- 1 dcl. water (scant)
- 250 gr. fine butter
- 1 egg white
- 10 gr. dried morels
- 1 can mushrooms (1/2 litre)
- 1 can chanterelles (1/4 litre)
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 5 tbsp. flour (50 gr.)
- 2 dcl. mushroom stock
- 4 dcl. cream
- salt, sugar
- white pepper
- 1 egg yolk

## Directions

1. Prepare the puff pastry as in recipe 33, roll out to 2 bottoms, one 3 cm. thick and the other 5 cm. thick.
2. Take a measure from the dish on which the vol-au-vent is to be served. This is done most easily by cutting a pattern of paper, and then cutting out the dough with a pastry wheel.
3. The dough shrinks during baking and should therefore be 4 cm. wider in circumference than the model.
4. Place the thinnest bottom on a baking sheet and brush with egg white, but none of this should come down over the edge as it prevents the puff pastry from rising.
5. The other bottom is cut into a 5 cm. wide ring, which is placed on top of the bottom and brushed carefully with egg white.
6. Bake the vol-au-vent in a hot oven; it should often be turned around so that it is evenly baked and acquires a golden yellow color.
7. The remaining dough is cut into beautiful figures or squares, which are baked and used as garnish on top of the stew.
8. Prepare the stew as in recipe 29. Stir in the beaten egg yolk, let the stew simmer and then fill it into the vol-au-vent.
