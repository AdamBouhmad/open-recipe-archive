## Ingredients

- 1 calf's head
- 1.5–2 liters water
- 1.5 tablespoons salt (23 g)
- 10 white peppercorns
- 1 carrot
- 0.5 lemon, sliced
- 1 red onion
- 5 parsley stems
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 1 tin mushrooms (0.25 liter)
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g)
- 4 deciliters of broth
- 0.5 deciliter mushroom broth
- 1–2 egg yolks
- salt
- white pepper
- 1 tablespoon chopped parsley

## Directions

1. Split the calf's head (remove the tongue and brain; the tongue is cooked with the head, the brain is used for a stew, see no. 107), rinse and soak in water overnight.
2. Clean it well, place it along with the tongue in boiling water and skim; add the spices, the blanched carrot, the lemon slices (freed from seeds), the peeled onion and parsley, then let the meat cook until tender.
3. Remove the head and let it cool; separate the meat from the bones and cut it into even pieces. Strain and skim the broth.
4. Melt the butter, slice the mushrooms and fry them in the butter until clear, then remove them with a spoon and fry the flour with the butter for 2 minutes, add the meat broth and mushroom broth and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes.
5. Finish with egg yolk, add the cut meat and mushrooms, season with spices and a little wine if desired.
6. Place on a warm platter, sprinkle with parsley and serve with boiled potatoes.
