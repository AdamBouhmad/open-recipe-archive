## Ingredients

- For thin pancakes: 3 eggs
- 6 dcl. milk
- 90–100 gr. flour
- 1 tsk. salt (5 gr.)
- For the pan: 2 msk. butter (40 gr.)
- For the stew: 3 hg. boneless, fried meat
- 1 can mushrooms (1/4 lit.)
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.)
- 4 msk. flour (40 gr.)
- 4 dcl. broth
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 dcl. thick cream
- salt
- white pepper
- For frying: 2 dcl. finely crushed rusks
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.)

## Directions

1. For the pancake batter, whisk the ingredients well with a steel whisk, then let the batter rest for 1 hour to swell.
2. Cut the meat into fine cubes.
3. Cut the mushrooms likewise into cubes and fry them in butter until clear, then press them out with a spoon.
4. Fry the flour with the butter for 2 minutes, add the broth and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes.
5. Whisk the egg yolk with the cream and add it to the sauce, then let it simmer.
6. Add the meat and mushroom and season the stew with spices.
7. Heat a pancake pan slowly, melt the butter in a water bath and brush the pan with it.
8. Make thin pancakes from the batter, browning them on one side only.
9. Lay them on a cutting board, sprinkled with finely crushed rusks, with the fried side up.
10. Cut the pancakes in half, place 1 msk. of the stew on each half and fold the pancake well so it takes the form of an oblong croquette.
11. Carefully turn the rolls in crushed rusks and fry them a nice brown in butter.
12. Place them on a hot platter, garnish with parsley and serve with brown mushroom sauce or clarified butter.
13. Pancakes can be filled and fried in the same way and served for the smorgasbord.
