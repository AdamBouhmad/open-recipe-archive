## Ingredients

- 4 kg. veal saddle
- 1 1/2 hg. lard
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g.)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 5 tablespoons butter (100 g.)
- 1 liter broth

## Directions

1. By saddle is meant the two connected rows of cutlets. The saddle is trimmed and should hang for a couple of days so the meat becomes tender. The membrane is carefully removed and the saddle is larded in dense rows with fine strips of lard, which are rolled in a mixture of salt and pepper. The butter is browned in a long pan, the saddle is placed in with the larded side up, placed in a hot oven and browned while frequently basted with the butter in the pan. It is sprinkled with the rest of the seasonings, diluted with the boiling broth, and allowed to roast until it is tender, or about 1 1/2 hours, during which it is basted every 10 minutes. If it becomes too brown, it is covered with a butter-brushed paper. The saddle is removed, the meat is carefully loosened on both sides of the backbone, placed on a cutting board and cut across into beautiful slices, which are laid together on the saddle so that it looks whole. It is placed on a warm serving platter and garnished with cooked vegetables, such as peas, small fresh beans, cauliflower, asparagus, carrots, as well as French potatoes and green salad or parsley. Served with strained sauce (see no. 429), mixed with thick cream, sherry or Madeira and a little lemon juice.
2. Veal saddle can also be larded with olives and otherwise prepared the same way as above.
