## Ingredients

- 1 kg rye sifted flour
- 75 gr wheat flour
- 1 liter skimmed milk
- 50 gr fresh yeast
- 2 tsk salt (10 gr)
- 50 gr butter, margarine or lard
- 3 dcl syrup
- 2 tbsp crushed fennel
- 3 tbsp finely chopped bitter orange peel

## Directions

1. From the rye flour, a portion of the wheat flour, the lukewarm milk, and the yeast mixed with salt, make a dough as described on page 462, and let the dough rise to double its size.
2. Warm the syrup somewhat in a pot together with the fat and spices and work into the dough along with enough wheat flour that the dough becomes quite firm.
3. Let the dough rise again, then work it well with the rest of the wheat flour on the work surface and divide into 6-8 parts.
4. Work each part until it is smooth and without cracks, and then roll it into an oblong roll, which is placed on warm, soft baker's cloth, on which is placed a baking cloth that is floured.
5. Place the rolls next to each other with the baking cloth drawn up in a large fold between each roll and cover.
6. When the rolls are risen, tip them onto a bread peel, place on buttered, lukewarm baking sheets, prick along the length with a landing needle, brush with cold milk and bake in not too hot an oven for about 1/2 hour.
7. When the rolls are light, take them out, brush with hot water and place between cloths so that the crust becomes soft.
8. The dough can be mixed with cold liquid the evening before it is to be baked.
