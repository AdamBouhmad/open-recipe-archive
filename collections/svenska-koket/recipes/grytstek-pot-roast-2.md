## Ingredients

- 2 kg. boneless sirloin
- 2 hg. fatback
- 2 hg. drippings
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g)
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 3 tbsp. flour (30 g)
- 1 1/2 liter boiling broth
- 1 bay leaf
- 10 whole allspice berries
- 2 red onions

## Directions

1. The meat is prepared and larded (see page 232), after which it is tied, not too tightly, with kitchen twine, previously dipped in hot water, so that it gets a beautiful shape.
2. The drippings are heated in an iron pot (not too large), the meat is browned in it on all sides until a beautiful brown crust is formed, then it is removed and sprinkled with salt.
3. The drippings are discarded and the pot is cleaned well with white paper. The roast is returned, sprinkled with flour and broth, and the spices and peeled onions are added.
4. It is then allowed to roast slowly with a tight lid for 3 1/2—4 hours and is turned several times during this time but is not moistened.
5. The roast is sliced, laid on a warm platter, garnished with green salad or parsley and served with vegetables, potato croquettes or stewed macaroni.
6. The sauce is strained, skimmed well, allowed to boil down if it is too thin, or diluted if it is too thick, and a little cream is added.
