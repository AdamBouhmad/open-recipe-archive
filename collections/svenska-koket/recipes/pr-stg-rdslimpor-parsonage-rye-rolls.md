## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg coarsely ground rye flour
- 1/2 litre good drink or Swedish ale
- 1 litre skimmed milk
- 2 1/2 kg wheat flour
- 80 gr pressed yeast
- 1 tbsp salt (15 gr)
- 75 gr butter, margarine or lard
- 5 dcl syrup
- 2 tbsp crushed fennel, 1 tbsp anise
- 3 tbsp finely chopped orange peel

## Directions

1. Mix the rye flour in a bowl and stir with the drink, which should be heated to about 80°, stir the mixture well, cover with a baking cloth and let it stand in a warm place overnight.
2. Early the next morning add 1 kg of the wheat flour, the warm milk and the yeast mixed with salt, and work the dough by hand until it becomes even and smooth, and add more flour so that the dough becomes quite stiff, then cover and let it rise to double its size.
3. Mix the fat, syrup and spices in a saucepan, bring to a boil, let cool and work into the risen dough together with wheat flour, then place the dough in a warm place and let it rise again.
4. Treat it as in the previous recipe, shape and bake accordingly.
