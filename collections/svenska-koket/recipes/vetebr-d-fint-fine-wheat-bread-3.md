## Ingredients

- 850 gr. prime wheat flour
- 1/2 liter milk
- 30 gr. pressed yeast
- 1 tsp. salt
- 1 hg. butter
- 125 gr. sugar
- 20 gr. sweet almonds (20 pieces)
- 5 bitter almonds
- 1 egg
- 6-8 cardamom pods

## Directions

1. Sift the flour into a bowl, reserving a little for shaping. Heat the milk to about 40° and mix it into the flour along with the yeast, dissolved with salt and 1 tsp. of sugar, then work the dough until it is smooth and glossy.
2. Cover and let it rise to double its size.
3. Beat the butter with the sugar until fluffy, blend with the blanched and ground almonds and the egg, and add to the dough along with the finely crushed cardamom if desired.
4. Work the dough well and add more flour if the dough is too loose, then let it rise again.
5. Turn out onto a floured work surface, work well, and divide into 5 equal parts, which are shaped into wreaths or braids and placed on buttered baking sheets to rise.
6. Brush with beaten egg, sprinkle with blanched and ground almonds mixed with pearl sugar, and bake in a hot oven until light and golden.
7. Alternatively, each part can be rolled out into a 1 cm thick square, brushed with melted butter, sprinkled with a mixture of blanched and ground sweet almonds, sanding sugar, and finely chopped candied citrus peel if desired, rolled tightly, and shaped as a roll or wreath with seam-side down on a buttered sheet.
8. Score the breads nicely, let them rise, and treat and bake as above.
9. Instead of baking the dough rolls whole, each can be cut into 12 pieces, placed seam-side down on buttered sheets, or pressed in the middle with the handle of a table knife, turned over, and placed on buttered sheets to rise, then treated and baked as above.
