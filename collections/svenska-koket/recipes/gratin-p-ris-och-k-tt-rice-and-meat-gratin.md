## Ingredients

- 150 g rice
- 2 liters water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- approximately 3 hectograms cooked or fried, white meat (leftovers)
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 2 deciliters milk
- 2 deciliters bouillon
- 1 egg yolk
- salt, white pepper
- For the mold: 1 tablespoon butter (20 g), 3 tablespoons crushed rusks, 1 tablespoon grated Parmesan cheese

## Directions

1. Rinse the rice, put it in boiling salted water and let it cook vigorously for 15–20 minutes.
2. Pour it into a colander, let it drain and mix it, while it is still warm, with 30 g of the butter.
3. Cut the meat into fine dice.
4. Fry the rest of the butter with the flour for 2 minutes, thin with the milk and bouillon and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes, after which it is bound with the egg yolk and seasoned with the spices.
5. Butter a fireproof mold and sprinkle it with crushed rusks, layer the rice, meat and sauce in it in alternating layers. The top layer should be sauce, which is sprinkled with the grated cheese and the rest of the crushed rusks.
6. Put the dish in a warm oven and gratinee as in recipe no. 112.
7. Serve as a breakfast dish.
