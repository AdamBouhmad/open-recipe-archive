## Ingredients

- 3 partridges
- 1 hg. lard, in 3 slices
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 2 tsp. salt
- 3 dcl. bouillon
- 4 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. Recognize young partridges by their yellow legs, whereas older ones have a reddish color. Old partridges should hang longer than young ones and require longer cooking time, but the meat is equally juicy and good as from young birds.
2. If the partridges are frozen, thaw them overnight in a warm kitchen, clean them (see page 299) and let them lie in lukewarm milk for a few hours.
3. Remove, let drain, dry well, truss and cover with thin lard slices.
4. Brown in butter together with the giblets, salt, and dilute first with the boiling bouillon, then with the cream, and let them stew slowly with a tight lid until tender, 1—1 1/4 hours; baste frequently during this time.
5. If the partridges are young and unfrozen, they should not lie in milk and should not be covered with lard. They are roasted in plenty of butter as previously mentioned.
6. If desired, they can be diluted with bouillon or cream alone.
7. Carve, garnish (see page 300) and serve with the reduced sauce or with poured sauce (see no. 429). Mushrooms can be fried in butter and added to the sauce.
