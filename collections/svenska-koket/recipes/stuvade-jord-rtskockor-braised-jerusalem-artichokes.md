## Ingredients

- 1.25 liters Jerusalem artichokes
- 1 liter water
- 1 teaspoon salt for blanching
- 4 deciliters boiling skim milk
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 1 teaspoon sugar (5 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1–2 egg yolks

## Directions

1. Treat the Jerusalem artichokes as in the previous recipe, blanch in boiling salted water for 3–4 minutes, drain in a colander, and allow to drip dry.
2. Place in boiling milk and simmer until tender, testing with a whisk.
3. Drain again in a colander and reserve the cooking liquid.
4. Melt butter in a pot, add the Jerusalem artichokes, sprinkle with flour, and sauté well while shaking the pot.
5. Add enough of the reserved liquid to make a simmering sauce and let it simmer about 5 minutes.
6. Season with spices, finish with the beaten egg yolk, and let it just simmer.
7. Serve with roasted meat. The Jerusalem artichokes may also be cooked in broth instead of milk and prepared the same way; season the stew with a little lemon juice.
