## Ingredients

- 2 large Portuguese onions
- 1 1/2 liters water
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 3 hg boneless veal
- 50 gr butter
- 1 tablespoon flour (10 gr)
- 1 egg
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 2 dcl cream
- For browning: 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr), 2 dcl broth

## Directions

1. Peel the onion, place in boiling salted water and let simmer gently without a lid for about 7 minutes.
2. Remove the onion, carefully separate the leaves and place on linen to drain.
3. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into pieces and grind 3 times through a meat grinder.
4. Pound thoroughly in a mortar together with the butter and wheat flour, then add the beaten egg and seasonings.
5. Work the forcemeat for about 1/2 hour, adding the thin cream little by little, as the forcemeat can easily break otherwise.
6. If too stiff, dilute with a little more cream.
7. Place a teaspoon of forcemeat on each onion leaf, roll up and tie with cotton string previously dipped in hot water.
8. Brown the butter in an iron pot, add the dolmas and brown nicely, then put on the lid and let braise gently for about 40 minutes.
9. Toward the end of cooking add the broth.
10. Place on a warm platter, cut off the string and pour the sauce over them. Serve with the smörgåsbord.
