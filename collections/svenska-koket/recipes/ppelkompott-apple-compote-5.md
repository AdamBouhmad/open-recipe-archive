## Ingredients

- 2 hectograms caster sugar
- 4 deciliters water
- 1 1/2 liters good, tart apples (about 8 hectograms)
- juice of 1/2 lemon or 2 tablespoons vinegar

## Directions

1. Cook the sugar and water to make a clear syrup and let it cool.
2. Rinse the apples, dry them, peel thinly, cut into 4-6 pieces depending on size, and remove the cores.
3. As the apples are cut, place them in 1 1/2 liters of water, mixed with the lemon juice or vinegar.
4. Take only as many apple pieces as fit on the surface of the sugar syrup at a time, let them drain on cloth, place in the sugar syrup, and let them cook gently until soft, which is tested with a whisk.
5. Remove with a slotted spoon to a flat dish and let cool.
6. Add more sugar and water to the syrup if needed.
7. When all apple pieces are cooked, boil the syrup together if it is thin.
8. Place the apples in a compote bowl, strain the syrup and pour over, and garnish if desired with apple, redcurrant, or gooseberry jelly.
9. Serve cold with whipped cream.
