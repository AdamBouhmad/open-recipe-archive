## Ingredients

- 5 hg. lean pork shoulder
- 1 hg. lard
- 1/4 tsp. ginger
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 3-4 cooked, grated potatoes
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 2-3 dcl. cream or milk
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 gr.) for frying
- 5 dcl. broth

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, remove sinews and membranes, and cut into cubes.
2. Scrape the lard and cut likewise into cubes.
3. Grind the meat and lard 6 times through a meat grinder, the last time together with the potatoes.
4. Work the forcemeat for 1 hour together with the seasonings, the beaten egg and egg yolk, and the milk, adding the latter by the spoonful so the forcemeat does not separate.
5. Test the forcemeat as in the previous recipe.
6. Grease a long pan with butter, add the forcemeat in the form of an oblong ball, smooth it with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water.
7. Fry the meat loaf as in recipe no. 632, place on a hot platter, cut into slices, garnish with parsley and serve with potato purée, stewed Jerusalem artichokes or stewed cauliflower and with the strained, skimmed and seasoned meat gravy, or with strained sauce (see recipe no. 429).
