## Ingredients

- 1 wood grouse or 2 ptarmigans
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 1 1/2 liters good broth
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 6 white peppercorns
- 8 deciliters of broth
- 8 leaves gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 1 tablespoon good Madeira wine
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 2 truffles
- parsley

## Directions

1. Pluck the bird, singe it, and clean it; chop off the wings and legs
2. Rinse it very well and dry it with a meat towel
3. Truss it (see bird preparation) and brown it in the butter
4. Pour in the boiling broth; add the spices
5. Simmer until tender, about 1 1/2 hours
6. Let it cool and remove the meat from the bones, cutting it into attractive pieces
7. Place the carcass in the broth and simmer until it reduces to barely 1 liter
8. Strain and skim the broth
9. Prepare the gelatin as in recipe no. 181
10. Garnish the bottom of the mold with hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into attractive pieces, and parsley
11. Pour a few drops of gelatin over the garnish and let set so the garnish is not disturbed when the meat is added
12. Place the most attractive game pieces at the bottom and on the sides
13. Fill the mold with the remaining meat and pour the remaining gelatin carefully over
14. Place the aspic in a cold place to set
15. Unmold as in recipe no. 185 and garnish with chopped gelatin, truffle, and parsley
16. Serve with mayonnaise as a course
