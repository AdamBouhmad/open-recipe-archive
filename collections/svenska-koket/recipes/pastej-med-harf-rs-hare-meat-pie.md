## Ingredients

- 250 gr. flour
- 2 dcl. water (scant)
- 3 hg. butter
- 1 young hare
- 50 gr. butter
- 8 dcl. thick cream
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 egg white
- 2 tsp. salt
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 6–7 truffles

## Directions

1. Prepare the pastry dough as in no. 34.
2. Skin the hare and remove the organs; cut away the head, paws and flanks, then rinse the hare well and dry it. Remove all membranes and cut the hare into pieces. Cut the meat from the bones and scrape it. Pass it 8 times through a meat grinder, the last 3 times together with the butter.
3. Place the forcemeat in a bowl and stir for about 1 hour, gradually adding the cream by the tablespoon. Pass the forcemeat through a fine sieve, then stir in the egg yolks, egg white and the truffle cut into strips, as well as a little of the truffle juice.
4. Season the forcemeat with the spices and cook a small test portion in salted water. If the forcemeat is too firm, add a little more cream; if too soft, add a beaten egg.
5. Roll out the pastry dough to about 1/2 cm. thickness and line a tart form with removable ring. The dough should hang 2 cm. over the edge of the form. Place the forcemeat inside, fold the dough over and brush the edge with egg white. Place a lid of dough over the forcemeat. Brush the lid with egg white and garnish with the remaining dough cut into beautiful figures. Around the edge place a strip of dough, neatly crimped.
6. Bake the pie in good oven heat for about 1 1/2 hours. After 10 minutes remove the tart ring. Serve the pie as a side dish, warm or cold, with a sharp sauce made from the hare bones.
