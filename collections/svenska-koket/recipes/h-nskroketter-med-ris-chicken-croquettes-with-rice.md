## Ingredients

- 1 young hen or leftovers of cooked chicken
- 1/2 lemon
- 1 liter water
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 cloves
- 1 deciliter rice grains
- For the sauce: 1 1/2 tablespoon butter (30 gr.)
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 gr.)
- 4 deciliters chicken broth
- 2 egg yolks
- salt, white pepper
- For breading: 4 tablespoons flour
- 2 eggs
- 2 teaspoons cooking oil, salt, white pepper
- 1 cup crushed rusks
- For frying: 1 kg. lard

## Directions

1. Pluck and singe the hen; cut off wings and legs.
2. Cut it up, remove the innards, rinse well and let it lie in water for a few hours.
3. Dry the hen with a cloth, rub it inside and outside with lemon and place it in boiling water; skim the stock well when it comes to a boil, then add the spices.
4. When the meat is tender after 1-2 hours, take it out and cut it into fine cubes.
5. Place the carcass back in the broth, which is allowed to cook until it tastes properly strong, then strain and skim it.
6. Rinse the rice grains in warm water, place them in 1 liter boiling, salted water and let them cook until they are soft but do not fall apart.
7. Pour them into a colander, rinse with cold water and let them drain.
