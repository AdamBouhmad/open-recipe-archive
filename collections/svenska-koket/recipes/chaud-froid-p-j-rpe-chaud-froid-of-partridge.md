## Ingredients

- 6 partridges
- 2 hg. lard in flat slices
- 1 hg. butter
- 3 tsk. salt
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 1 lit. strong brown broth
- For the sauce: 2 msk. butter (40 gr.)
- 6 msk. flour (60 gr.)
- 8 dcl. meat broth
- 1 dcl. thick cream
- 1–2 msk. Madeira
- 1/2 tsk. salt, cayenne
- 1 tsk. beef steak sauce
- 3–4 leaves gelatin
- For garnish: 2 hard-boiled eggs
- 4–5 large truffles
- For aspic: 1 lit. dark broth
- 8–10 leaves gelatin
- 2 egg whites and rinsed shells
- 1 msk. cold water

## Directions

1. Prepare the partridges as in the previous recipe. Truss (see poultry) so the breast is high and oval, bind over with a slice of lard each and season. Brown the butter in a roasting pan, place the birds in and roast in a warm oven for about 1/2 hour, basting frequently and adding broth. Remove, pull off the skin and let the birds cool. Strain and skim the meat broth. Cut off the bones, trim away the back; split the birds and carefully remove the breastbone.
2. For the sauce, fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the meat broth and cream and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes. Add the wine, spices and beef steak sauce, taste the sauce, let it come to a boil and skim. Rinse the gelatin, dissolve in 1 msk. hot water and stir in. Keep the sauce in a bowl and stir slowly until it is thick enough to coat the spoon. Assemble the bird breasts with a little of the sauce so they appear whole, glaze with the remaining sauce, place on a platter and garnish with stars of egg white and truffle. Prepare the aspic as in recipe no. [reference incomplete in source].
