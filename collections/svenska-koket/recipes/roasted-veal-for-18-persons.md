## Ingredients

- 4½ kg. grain-fed veal roast
- 3 hectograms lard
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 grams)
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 grams)
- 2 liters broth or water

## Directions

1. The leg with the thin skin is used. The shank is cut off and cooked to broth to dilute the roast with. The meat from it can be used for herring salad or gratin. The meat, which should have hung for 3–10 days depending on the season, is well pounded, dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water, all sinewy membranes are cleaned away. The roast is larded on the outer thigh (see page 232) and rubbed with a mixture of the salt and pepper. A roasting pan is heated, the butter is melted in it, the roast is placed in with the inner thigh up, placed in a hot oven to brown and is often basted with the butter. When it is browned, it is diluted with the boiling broth, but not more than it goes about ½ cm. high in the pan; the meat is then roasted until it is tender, or about 3 hours, during which it is basted every 10 minutes and diluted with more broth when needed. If the roast becomes too brown, it is covered with a paper brushed with butter. It is served with strained sauce (see no. 429) and mixed vegetables as well as small potatoes, cucumbers, and salads.
