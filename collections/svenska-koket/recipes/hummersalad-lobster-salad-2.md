## Ingredients

- 3 lobsters (400 g each)
- 5 liters water
- 2 hectograms coarse salt
- 2 carrots
- 1 parsnip
- 1 celery
- 1 cauliflower head
- 1 can green beans
- 1 can fine peas
- 3-4 truffles
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 6 tablespoons flour (60 g)
- 4 tablespoons fine oil
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- 2 tablespoons vinegar
- 1/4 teaspoon English mustard
- 5 deciliters water
- 4 egg yolks
- 2 deciliters thick cream
- 3 teaspoons salt
- 1 teaspoon sugar, white pepper
- 2 heads salad
- parsley
- 3 tomatoes in wedges
- 3 hard-boiled eggs in wedges

## Directions

1. Cook the lobster as in recipe 170, let it cool, split it and cut into small fine pieces.
2. Prepare the root vegetables in the usual way, cut into small even cubes, cook tender in lightly salted water, then drain in a colander so the water runs off well.
3. Clean the cauliflower, cook in salted water, let cool and divide into small pieces.
4. Pour the peas into a colander, cut the beans diagonally into three parts and cut the truffle into strips.
5. For the mayonnaise, fry butter, flour and mustard for 2 minutes, add water a little at a time, and let the sauce boil while stirring for 10 minutes.
6. Pour it out and stir until it has cooled, then stir in the egg yolks one at a time and work the sauce with a spoon for about 1/2 hour while adding the oil, lemon juice and vinegar alternately and drop by drop.
7. Stir in the cream beaten to stiff peaks and taste the sauce with the seasonings.
8. Fold the vegetables and truffle into the sauce together with the lobster, then mix the salad very carefully so the lobster does not fall apart.
9. Arrange the salad high on a round silver platter and garnish with green salad, parsley, tomato and egg wedges.
10. Serve as a course between meals.
11. Instead of lobster, cold fish or fowl cut into cubes can be used.
