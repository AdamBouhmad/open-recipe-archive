## Ingredients

- 2 liters fresh (or 1 1/2 hectograms dried) apples
- 1 tablespoon vinegar
- 2 liters water
- 1 1/2 cups crushed sugar
- 1/2 lemon (rind and juice)
- 1 1/2 - 2 tablespoons potato flour

## Directions

1. Peel the apples, remove the cores, and cut into wedges. (To keep them from turning dark, place the peeled apples in water mixed with 1 tablespoon vinegar.)
2. Place them in boiling water together with sugar, lemon rind and juice and let cook until the apple wedges are soft.
3. Thicken the soup with the potato flour mixed with a little cold water, bring to a good boil, taste and adjust seasoning, and serve with rusks.
4. If using dried apples, rinse them and soak overnight in the water in which they are to cook. In this case, more water should be used.
