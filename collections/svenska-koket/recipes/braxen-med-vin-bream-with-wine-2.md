## Ingredients

- 1 bream weighing 2 kg
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g)
- 4 deciliters white or red wine
- Broth
- 5 white peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- 3 parsley stems
- 2–3 egg yolks
- Sugar

## Directions

1. Scale and clean the fish (see page 171); the head may remain but must be freed from gills.
2. Rub it with salt and let it rest for 1 hour, then dry it again.
3. Brown the butter lightly and fry it with the flour for 2 minutes; add the wine and as much broth as needed to make the sauce properly thin.
4. Place the fish in a small fish kettle without a sieve together with the roe or milk; pour the sauce over it and it should stand level with the fish.
5. Add the pepper, bay leaf and parsley and let the fish simmer slowly until the meat is tender, about 20 minutes.
6. During this time shake the kettle often so that the sauce does not burn.
7. Place the fish on a warm platter and garnish with parsley and fluted lemon slices.
8. Strain the sauce, bind it with the egg yolks, let it simmer, season with salt and sugar and serve in a separate sauce bowl.
