## Ingredients

- 2 1/2 kg. salted fat
- 5 kg. boneless beef inner loin
- 2 1/2 kg. fresh, lean pork
- 150 gr. salt
- 1 hg. sugar
- 32 gr. ground strong and white pepper
- 4 ctl. roe
- approximately 8 m. fat strips

## Directions

1. Cut the fat and meat the day before the sausage is to be made.
2. Cut the fat into 1 1/2 mm. large dice.
3. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut it into pieces, free it from membranes and sinews, mix with 75 gr. of the salt and let it stand in a cold place until the next day.
4. Grind the meat then 6 times through a meat grinder together with the meat juice, but after each time it is driven through the grinder, spread out the forcemeat and let it cool.
5. Work the forcemeat thereafter vigorously together with spices and roe for approximately 45 minutes, then add the fat and work the forcemeat further for approximately 1/2 hour.
6. Stuff the forcemeat tightly into straight beef casings, and let the sausages hang in a cold place for 4–6 days depending on the season.
7. Then let them hang in ordinary room temperature—in summer 5–6 hours, in winter 24–30 hours—to dry and become uniformly red.
8. Cold smoke thereafter for approximately 14 days.
