## Ingredients

- 3 kg from the hindquarters
- 2 hg lard
- 1 tbsp salt (15 g)
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 4 tbsp butter (80 g)
- 3 dcl broth
- 5 dcl broth
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 3 dcl broth
- 3 dcl cream or good milk

## Directions

1. The venison should be young, not weighing more than 15-20 kg before it is skinned, in order to be juicy and tender.
2. The meat should hang for 4-5 days before it is prepared.
3. When the venison is to be skinned, loosen the skin around the knee joint, hang it by the hind legs, and slit the skin with a sharp knife from the inside of the hindquarters through the belly and up to the head.
4. Skin the animal, cut off the head, and remove the entrails.
5. Divide it as sheep, but with a saddle instead of two cutlet rows.
6. Pound the roast with a damp mallet, remove the membranes, lard it, rub with the remaining spices, roast in a pot, and add the boiling broth and cream, ready after 2-2 1/2 hours.
7. Cut the roast, place on a warm dish, pour over a couple of tbsp of sauce, garnish with browned potatoes, salad, and parsley, and serve with red currant jelly, apple sauce, and reduced sauce (see recipe no. 429), with 1 tbsp red currant jelly added.
