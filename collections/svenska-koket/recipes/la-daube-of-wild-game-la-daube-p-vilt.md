## Ingredients

- 1 grouse or 2 partridges
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 1 1/2 liters good broth
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 6 white peppercorns
- 8 deciliters of the broth
- 8 sheets gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 1 tablespoon good Madeira
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 2 truffles
- parsley

## Directions

1. Pluck the bird, singe it, and remove the insides; chop off the wings and legs.
2. Rinse it very well and dry it with a kitchen towel, truss it (see poultry) and brown it in butter.
3. Add the hot broth, add the seasonings, and let the bird cook until tender or about 1 1/2 hours.
4. Let it cool and cut the meat loose from the bones and cut it into nice pieces.
5. Put the carcass in the broth, let it cook down until it is about 1 liter.
6. Strain the broth and skim it.
7. Prepare the aspic as in recipe 181.
8. Garnish the bottom of the mold with the hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into nice pieces, and parsley.
9. Pour a few drops of aspic over the garnish and let it set so that the garnish does not shift when the meat is added.
10. Lay the nicest bird pieces on the bottom and at the sides.
11. Fill the mold with the remaining meat and pour the rest of the aspic carefully over it.
12. Set the à-la-daube on a cold place to set.
13. Turn out as in recipe 185 and garnish with chopped aspic, truffle, and parsley.
14. Serve with mayonnaise as a middle course.
