## Ingredients

- 1/2 litre blood
- 4 dcl milk or weak broth
- 2–3 msk syrup
- approximately 210 g ground rye flour
- 3 msk finely chopped kidney fat, butter or cream
- 1 1/2 msk salt (23 g)
- 1/2 tsk white pepper
- 1/2 tsk ginger
- 1/2 tsk finely ground marjoram
- 1 finely chopped red onion
- 1 msk butter for the onion
- 1/2 msk butter (10 g) for the mould
- 2 msk crushed rusks

## Directions

1. Strain the blood, whisk well and mix with the milk or broth and syrup.
2. Whisk in the flour, add the kidney fat, spices and the onion fried in butter, and test the batter to see if it is properly seasoned and of proper thickness by frying a little of it in a frying pan.
3. Grease a pudding mould with a lid with butter and sprinkle with crushed rusks.
4. Fill the mould two-thirds full with the batter, put on the lid, and boil the pudding in a water bath on top of the stove for approximately 2 1/2–3 hours.
5. Test with a whisk to see if the pudding is cooked through.
6. Serve with melted butter and lingonberry preserves.
