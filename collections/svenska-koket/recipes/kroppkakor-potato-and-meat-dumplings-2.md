## Ingredients

- 2 litres potatoes
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 225–250 g flour
- 2 tsk salt (10 g)
- 2 hg salt-cured side pork
- 150 g smoked ham
- 1 small finely chopped red onion
- 1/4 tsk white or strong pepper
- 4 litres water for cooking
- 3 msk salt (45 g) for cooking

## Directions

1. Rinse and brush the potatoes clean, peel raw and boil until just tender.
2. Drain the water well, and let the potatoes stand for about 10 minutes with the lid slightly ajar so the steam escapes, then press through a potato ricer and let cool.
3. Whisk the egg and egg yolks and add together with 2 hg of the flour and salt.
4. Work the dough slightly on a pastry board with the remaining flour and roll into a long roll, which is cut into pieces; these are then rolled like rusks.
5. Fry the pork and ham together with the onion if desired, and add the spices.
6. Make a depression in each dumpling, which is filled with pork pieces, then press the dumpling closed well.
7. Put the dumplings in boiling salted water and let simmer gently with the lid on for about 10 minutes.
8. Test with a whisk to see if they are done.
9. Lift out with a slotted spoon and serve warm with melted butter.
10. It is best to test-cook one dumpling before the others are finished, to determine if the dough is of proper firmness.
