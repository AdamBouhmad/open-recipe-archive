## Ingredients

- 3 sole fish, approximately 5 hg each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- Fish forcemeat: 1/2 kg pike = 210 g scraped fish meat
- 125 g good butter
- 15 g flour
- 2 dcl cream
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1 teaspoon sugar (5 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- For garnish: 4 truffles or 48 shrimp tails
- For cooking: 1 1/2 tablespoon butter (30 g)
- fish broth
- parsley
- hollandaise sauce, crayfish sauce, or lobster sauce
- small boiled potatoes

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the sole as in recipe 371
2. Pound the fillets with a damp meat mallet, rub with salt and let them lie on top of each other for a couple of hours
3. Prepare the fish forcemeat as in recipe 356
4. Dry the fillets again, cut each fillet in half and trim the pieces so they become oblong and round, or cut them pear-shaped
5. Cover them with a 1 1/2 cm thick layer of fish forcemeat, spread evenly with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water, and brush over with lightly beaten egg white
6. Garnish the forcemeat with truffle cut into beautiful figures or with shrimp tails or with both
7. Clean a long pan (so it does not darken) and grease it with cold butter, place the fillets in it and add enough fish broth (cooked from head, bones and skin) so that it reaches up to the lower edge of the forcemeat
8. Place a lid or sheet over it, put the pan in regular oven heat and let the fillets simmer 15-20 minutes
9. Test with a skewer to see if they are done
10. Place on a warm dish with a slotted spoon, garnish with parsley and serve with hollandaise, crayfish or lobster sauce and small boiled potatoes
