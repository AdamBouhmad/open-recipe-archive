## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg. boneless veal
- 80 gr. butter
- 2 eggs
- 4 dcl. thick cream
- 3 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 2 tbsp. flour (20 gr.)
- 2 carrots
- 1/2 can haricots verts
- 1 hg. macaroni
- 2 tbsp. butter for the mold (40 gr.)

## Directions

1. Butter a timbal mold evenly with cold butter and garnish it.
2. Wash the carrots, cook them tender in salted water, peel them, and cut them into slices, which are cut out with a vegetable cutter into flowers or beautiful figures.
3. Cut the beans diagonally into 3 parts.
4. Parboil the macaroni in salted water and cut into 1 cm. long pieces.
5. Garnish the buttered mold with flowers of carrots and beans and between them double rings of macaroni.
6. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into slices and scrape very well.
7. Pound it thereafter in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle, first alone until it is fine, thereafter with the butter and the whole eggs, one at a time.
8. Add half of the cream little by little with even stirring, and pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve.
9. Whip the rest of the cream to stiff peaks and stir it into the forcemeat tablespoonwise together with the seasonings and flour.
10. Fill the forcemeat into the garnished mold and cook in a water bath in the oven for about 1 hour.
11. Turn it out, garnish with mushroom stew or fine vegetables and serve as a course between meals.
12. The timbal can also be garnished with only macaroni, which are then placed close together so that it resembles a honeycomb.
