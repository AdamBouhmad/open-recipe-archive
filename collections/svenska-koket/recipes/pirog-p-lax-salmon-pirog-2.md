## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 dcl. rice grains (150 gr.)
- 1/2 lit. water
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 3-4 dcl. thick cream
- 3 1/2 hg. salted salmon
- 1 msk. finely chopped red onion
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.)
- 3 hard-boiled eggs
- 150 gr. flour for puff pastry
- 1 dcl. water (scant) for puff pastry
- 2 hg. butter for puff pastry
- 1 egg white for brushing

## Directions

1. Rinse the rice grains, scald in hot water, bring to a boil in salted water, dilute with the thick cream and allow to cook until soft, then drain and allow to cool.
2. Rinse the salmon, cut into slices and allow to lie in 1 lit. skim milk for about 3 hours, remove, wrap in cloth or white paper and cut into cubes.
3. Fry the onion in the butter. Chop the eggs finely.
4. Prepare the puff pastry as in recipe no. 33 and roll out to a 1 1/4 cm. thick cake.
5. Using a fireproof dish on which the pirog is to be baked, take a measure. This is done most easily by cutting a pattern of paper and then cutting out the dough with a pastry wheel.
6. Lay the pastry bottom on the dish and a 3 cm. wide strip of dough is laid in a ring on top of the bottom and fastened with egg white.
7. Mix the rice grains with the salmon, onion and eggs and place them inside the ring.
8. Cover the pirog with a puff pastry lid of the same size as the bottom, and fasten the lid with the base by means of egg white.
9. Garnish the lid in squares with narrow strips of puff pastry and in each square make a small cut with a fork so that the dough rises and the dish gets a neat appearance.
10. Brush the pirog with egg white, which must not come down over the sides, as this prevents the puff pastry from rising.
11. Bake in a warm oven.
