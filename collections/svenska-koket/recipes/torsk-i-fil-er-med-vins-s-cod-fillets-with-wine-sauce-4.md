## Ingredients

- 2 cods of about 7 hg. each
- 1 tbsp salt (15 gr.)
- 1 1/2 tbsp butter (30 gr.)
- 1 small red onion
- 3 dcl. strong meat stock
- 4 dcl. fish stock
- 1/2 dcl. white wine
- 1 1/2 tbsp butter (30 gr.)
- 4 tbsp flour (40 gr.)
- 4 1/2 dcl. of the stock in which the fish cooked
- 1/2 dcl. white wine
- cayenne

## Directions

1. Clean the cod, rinse well, dry and cut into fillets.
2. Cut away the backbone and cut the meat from the skin.
3. Rub the fillets with salt, let them lie for 1 hour and dry again.
4. Make stock from the head, bones and skin.
5. Melt the butter in a low saucepan or small baking pan, fry the peeled and sliced onion in it, place the fish fillets in, dilute with meat stock, fish stock and wine, and cook the fish for 10-15 minutes as in no. 328.
6. Remove it with a slotted spoon onto a warm serving platter and pour the sauce over.
7. The dish is garnished with parsley and served with small boiled potatoes.
8. For the sauce: fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, dilute with the strained fish stock, let the sauce cook for 5 minutes and season with wine and cayenne.
