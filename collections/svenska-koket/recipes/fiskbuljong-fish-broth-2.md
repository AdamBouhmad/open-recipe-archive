## Ingredients

- 8 hectograms sole, cod, or whiting, or raw remnants and bones thereof
- 1/2 hectogram red onion
- 30 grams parsley (stalks or roots)
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 gr.)
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1 1/2 deciliters white wine
- 2 liters cold water
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 1 gram saffron
- 1 gram curry
- 1 hectogram fresh or preserved caviar (for clarifying)

## Directions

1. For fish broth, use only saltwater fish; sole, cod, and whiting are best, but if necessary, half the weight may be replaced with flounder.
2. Clean, wash, and dry the fish, then cut it into pieces. If using remnants and bones, wash them well and let them drain.
3. Peel the onion, blanch it (place in boiling salted water and cook 5 minutes without a lid), and cut into slices.
4. Clean and wash the parsley and wrap it in cloth.
5. Butter a heavy-bottomed pot; place the onion, parsley, and fish in it.
6. Add the lemon juice, cover the pot, place it over low heat, and let it stand for about 15 minutes, shaking it often.
7. Add the wine and let the liquid reduce by half without a lid, then pour in the cold water.
8. Skim the broth well, add the spices, and let it simmer gently with a tight lid for about 20 minutes.
9. Strain through a sieve and let cool, then clarify.
10. Pound the caviar finely in a mortar with a wooden pestle and mix with the cold broth; pour the mixture into a pot and place over fresh heat.
11. Stir the broth until it reaches boiling point, then move it to the side of the stove and let it simmer gently with a lid for 20 minutes, then strain carefully.
