## Ingredients

- 2 kg lamb roast (from cold storage)
- 1.5 hg lard
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr.)
- 0.5 tsp white pepper
- 3 tbsp butter (60 gr.)
- 0.75 litre boiling broth or water

## Directions

1. The roast, which should have hung so that it is tender, is dried, beaten, larded (see page 232), and the leg is cut off. It is larded most on the outer leg and back, broken to facilitate carving, after which the roast is rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper.
2. A long roasting pan is heated, the butter is browned in it along with some of the lard, the roast is placed in with the inner leg up and browned for about 0.5 hour in a warm oven, during which it is basted frequently with the fat in the pan.
3. It is extended with the boiling broth or water and is allowed to roast until tender, or about 1.5–2 hours; during which it is basted every 10 minutes.
4. Placed on a warm serving platter and carved at table. Served with white beans and vegetables or vegetable pasties as well as the strained, skimmed gravy or with prepared sauce (see no. 429). For small households, the French cut is best for roasting.
