## Ingredients

- Butter dough: 150 gr. flour
- 1 dcl. water (scant)
- 250 gr. fine butter
- 1 egg white
- Stew: 10 gr. dried morels
- 1 can mushrooms (1/2 liter)
- 1 can chanterelles (1/4 liter)
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 5 tbsp. flour (50 gr.)
- 2 dcl. mushroom stock
- 4 dcl. cream
- salt, sugar
- white pepper
- 1 egg yolk

## Directions

1. Prepare the butter dough as in recipe no. 33, roll out to 2 bottoms, one 3 cm. thick and the other 5 cm. thick.
2. From the dish on which the vol-au-vent is to be served, take a measure. This is done most easily by cutting a pattern from paper and then cutting out the dough with a pastry wheel.
3. The dough shrinks during baking and should therefore be 4 cm. wider in circumference than the model.
4. Place the thinnest bottom on a baking sheet and brush with egg white, but none of it should come down over the edge as it prevents the butter dough from rising.
5. The other bottom is cut into a 5 cm. wide ring, which is placed on top of the bottom and brushed carefully with egg white.
6. Bake the vol-au-vent in a warm oven, it should be turned frequently so that it bakes evenly and gets a golden yellow color.
7. The leftover dough is cut into beautiful figures or squares, which are baked and used as garnish on top of the stew.
8. Prepare the stew as in recipe no. 29. The whipped egg yolk is stirred in, the stew simmers and is then filled into the vol-au-vent.
