## Ingredients

- 6-8 fresh herrings
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 g)
- 2 two-öre almond breads
- 1 cup cream milk
- 4 anchovies
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 g)
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped parsley
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 egg
- 50 g melted butter
- 40 g flour
- 60 g cold butter

## Directions

1. Skin the herrings, clean them, remove the head, backbone and all small bones without splitting the fish
2. Rinse them very well, dry them, rub with salt and let them rest for 2 hours
3. Soak the almond breads in the cream milk, chop the anchovies finely and pound them in a stone mortar with the bread, egg yolks and butter
4. Pass the forcemeat through a sieve and mix with the parsley and pepper; if it is too loose, add a little pounded rusks, otherwise a little cream
5. Dry the herrings again, fill with the forcemeat and fold together
6. Brush with the beaten egg, dip in the melted butter and turn in flour
7. Grease a frying pan with half of the cold butter, lay the herrings in and place the rest of the butter in flakes on top of the fish
8. Fry brown in a hot oven, basting often
9. Serve with brown butter mixed with chopped parsley and potato puree
