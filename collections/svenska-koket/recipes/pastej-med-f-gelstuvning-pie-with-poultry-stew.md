## Ingredients

- 225 gr. flour
- 1 hg. butter
- 5 gr. salt
- 10 gr. sugar
- 1 dcl. water
- 1 egg yolk
- Remains of grouse or other poultry (3-4 hg.)
- 1 liter water
- 1 tsp. meat extract
- 1 tsp. salt
- 1 bouquet
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 gr.)
- 5 tbsp. flour (50 gr.)
- 6 dcl. poultry broth
- 1 tbsp. tomato puree
- salt, white pepper
- 1 egg yolk
- 1-2 tbsp. madeira
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 gr.) for the form

## Directions

1. Prepare the shortcrust dough as in no. 35 and let it rest 1 hour.
2. Cut the poultry remains into slices. Break up the carcass and set it to boil in 1 liter of water, added with the meat extract, salt and 1 bouquet (1 leek, 3 parsley stalks, 1 celery top). Let the broth boil for about 2 hours, or until it has good flavor, then strain and skim it.
3. Fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the broth and let the sauce boil 5 minutes. Season it with the tomato puree and spices, bind with the whisked egg yolk and finally stir in the wine.
4. Roll out the dough to about 1/2 cm thickness and line a well-buttered, smooth pie form with it. The dough should hang 2 cm over the edge of the form.
5. Lay the poultry slices in and pour in enough sauce to nearly cover the meat. (The rest of the sauce is diluted with more broth and served with the pie.) Fold the overhanging edge over the stew and place a lid of dough on top. Garnish with dough figures and brush with whisked egg.
6. Bake the pie in a warm oven for about 1 hour. Place the form on a dish with baking paper and wrap with a broken napkin.
7. Serve as a course between courses.
