## Ingredients

- 1 kg halibut
- 5 tablespoons salt (75 g)
- 3 litres water
- 1/2 lemon
- 1/2 egg
- 2 tablespoons crushed hardtack
- 100 g butter

## Directions

1. Clean the fish piece, remove the dark skin and treat the fish thereafter as in the previous recipe
2. Once the fish piece has been rubbed with lemon, brush it with the beaten egg on the side from which the skin has been removed, and sprinkle with crushed hardtack
3. Butter a long pan with half of the butter over a space as large as the fish piece occupies, place it in with the breaded side up, cover with the remaining butter distributed in flakes, and fry in a moderately warm oven for approximately 40 minutes, during which time pour the butter in the pan over it frequently
4. If desired, the butter can be diluted with 2 decilitres of boiling water
5. Garnish as previously, serve with lemon or mushroom sauce and boiled potatoes
6. Alternatively, after the fish piece has been rubbed with lemon juice, cut it across into 1 1/2 cm thick slices, which are beaten with a damp meat mallet
7. Dip the fish slices in beaten egg, turn in crushed hardtack, fry in butter on the stovetop and serve with lemon sauce, stewed vegetables or potato purée
