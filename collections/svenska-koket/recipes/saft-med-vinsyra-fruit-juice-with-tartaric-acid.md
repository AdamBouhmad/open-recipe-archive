## Ingredients

- 2 kg. strawberries (approximately 4 liters) or 2 kg. raspberries (approximately 3.8 liters)
- 40 gr. tartaric acid
- 1 deciliter boiling water
- 1.3 liters fresh water
- 1 kg. granulated sugar per kilogram of juice

## Directions

1. Clean the berries, rinse them if necessary, and place them in a pot.
2. Dissolve the tartaric acid in 1 deciliter boiling water, mix it with the cold water and pour it over the berries, which should then stand covered for 24 hours.
3. Strain the berries in cheesecloth and let them drip.
4. Measure the juice and add the sugar, then stir slowly with an enameled spoon until the sugar is melted, or approximately 1 hour.
5. Skim the juice and pour it hot into dry bottles, which are closed with clean cotton dipped in salicylic acid powder, and then bound with parchment paper (see page 505).
