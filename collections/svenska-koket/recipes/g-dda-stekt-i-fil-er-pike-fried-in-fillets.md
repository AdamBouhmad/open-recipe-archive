## Ingredients

- 2 pike at 750 gr. each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 1/2 egg
- 3 tablespoons crushed rusks
- For frying: 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 3 decilitres fish broth (made from head and backbone)
- 1 teaspoon salt (5 gr.)
- 2 decilitres thick cream

## Directions

1. Do not scale the pike, clean as described on page 171, cut into fillets and remove the backbone along with all small bones. Place the fillets with the skin side against the cutting board and cut the flesh from the skin with a pointed knife.
2. Rub the fillets with salt and let rest for 1 hour, then dry again, brush on both sides with the beaten egg and sprinkle with crushed rusks.
3. Grease an oblong fish form with lid with half of the cold butter, place the fillets in with the thin side against the thick, so they lie evenly, place the remaining butter in flakes on top, add the broth and cream. Place the lid on the form and roast the fish for about 20 minutes in a hot oven.
4. Serve with mashed potatoes. If a fish form is not available, the pike can be roasted as in no. 339, but omit the parsley. The fillets can be cut into 3 cm. wide pieces crosswise, dipped in beaten egg, rolled in crushed rusks, fried in butter on top of the stove, covered with a little browned butter and served with braised spinach or for a smörgåsbord.
