## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg. middle brisket (or 1 1/2 kg. middle rib)
- 2 1/2 liters water
- 2 tbsp. salt (30 gr.)

## Directions

1. Dry the brisket with a cloth wrung out in hot water, break well, roll up and bind, so that it takes up less room and is easier to turn.
2. Place it in boiling water, which is well skimmed when it boils up, add the salt and let the brisket simmer slowly with a tight lid until it is tender and the bones come loose easily, or approximately 2 1/2-3 hours.
3. Remove immediately from the broth, cut into beautiful pieces, garnish with parsley and serve with boiled potatoes and horseradish-, caper-, or olive sauce.
4. The broth can be used for purée or simpler soups.
5. The brisket can also, after it is boiled, be placed in a long pan, with the meat side up, a little of the broth added and the brisket browned in the oven until it has taken on a beautiful brown color. It is then cut and served with potato purée and the broth from the pan, which is strained and skimmed. If a fatter meat is desired, use the fore-brisket.
