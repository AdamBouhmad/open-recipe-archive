## Ingredients

- 2 young chickens, approximately 3 kg each
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in 2 slices
- 1 1/2 liters chicken or veal broth
- 1 small wedge Portuguese onion
- 4 parsley stems
- 2 cloves
- 5 white peppercorns

## Directions

1. The chickens are plucked, singed, and cleaned (see page 299 onwards), rinsed thoroughly, and left to soak in water for 6 hours.
2. They are removed, dried inside and out with a cloth, rubbed with salt, trussed (see page 300), covered with lard, which is fastened with fine sail thread previously dipped in boiling water.
3. The chickens are placed in a pot that should not be wider than to just fit them, the cold broth is poured in, brought to a boil, and skimmed well, after which the onion, parsley, and spices are added.
4. The chickens should then not boil but only lie in the broth, which should be kept at approximately 90°, until tender, or 2-3 hours, depending on the age of the chickens.
5. They are removed and kept warm in a water bath.
6. The broth is reduced and from it is prepared a light mushroom, truffle, tomato, or oyster sauce.
7. The chickens are cut and arranged on the platter so that they appear whole, garnished with salad, parsley, and, if desired, with slices of truffle or mushroom caps, sautéed in butter.
8. Served with rice or boiled potatoes. The sauce is served in a sauce boat.
