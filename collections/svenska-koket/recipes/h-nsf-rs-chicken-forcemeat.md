## Ingredients

- Meat from one chicken
- 200 g. veal
- 100 g. butter
- 3 eggs
- 2 portions cream
- Salt, white pepper
- 75 g. salt-boiled tongue
- 0.5 can truffle (at kr. 1:25)
- For panade: 1.5 twelve-öre wheat bread rolls, 3 portions boiling milk

## Directions

1. Scrape the chicken meat well from the bones, cut it together with the veal into cubes and pass three times through the meat grinder
2. Free the bread well from brown crusts, cut it into pieces, add the milk, and work the panade over the heat with a wooden spoon until it forms a loose and smooth dough
3. Let it cool and pass it thereafter through a hair sieve
4. Pound the meat and butter well together in a mortar and pass it thereafter through a hair sieve
5. Gradually mix the whisked egg yolks, seasonings and cream into the forcemeat, which is worked for 1 hour
6. Cut the tongue and truffle into cubes and add them
7. If the forcemeat is too stiff, add a little more cream; if too soft, add an egg yolk
8. Whisk the egg whites until stiff and fold them in carefully
9. Immediately beat the forcemeat into a nice mold (preferably a pipe-shaped or à-la-daube form with lid)
10. Bind it over with buttered paper if the form lacks a lid, and place it in a water bath to cook about 1 hour
11. Test the forcemeat with a whisk, turn it out onto a hot platter and serve with light or dark sauce, made from good stock that has been boiled on chicken bones
