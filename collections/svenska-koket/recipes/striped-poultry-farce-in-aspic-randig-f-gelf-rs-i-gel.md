## Ingredients

- Farce from game: 2 partridges or 1 hazel hen approximately 225 grams scraped meat, salt, white pepper, 100 grams panada, 100 grams butter, 1 egg, 2 egg yolks, 2 truffles
- Farce from chicken: 1 large chicken approximately 225 grams scraped meat, salt, white pepper, 100 grams panada, 100 grams butter, 1 egg, 2 egg yolks, 2 truffles
- Panada: 1.5 deciliters water, 1/4 teaspoon salt, 30 grams butter, 75 grams sifted flour
- For the mold: 1 tablespoon butter (20 grams)
- Aspic: 1.5 liters dark broth, 12-15 sheets gelatin, 2 egg whites, 1 tablespoon cold water, 2 tablespoons good Madeira

## Directions

1. Prepare the farces as in recipe 67, adding the truffle cut into fine strips mixed in
2. Layer them alternately in an oblong, buttered bread mold holding approximately 1.5 liters
3. Cover the mold with buttered paper and bake the farce in a water bath in the oven for approximately 1 hour
4. Test with a whisk, turn out onto an oblong platter and let cool
5. Prepare the aspic as in recipe 181
6. Slice the farce into approximately 7 millimeter thick slices and lay them on large platters with a little space between them
7. Pour the clear aspic over and let set
8. Cut out the slices with a pointed knife so a border of aspic goes beyond the edge of each slice
9. Arrange them in a circle on a large round platter, preferably silver
10. Place vegetable salad in the center and garnish around with chopped aspic, light lettuce leaves, and tomato wedges
11. Serve as a middle course
