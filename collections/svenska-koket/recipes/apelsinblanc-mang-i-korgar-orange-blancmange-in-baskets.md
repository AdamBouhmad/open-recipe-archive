## Ingredients

- 9 oranges
- Peel of 1 orange
- 1.5 dcl. juice from the oranges
- 5 leaves gelatin
- 4 tablespoons sugar (60 gr.)
- 4 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. Wash and dry large, beautiful oranges.
2. Remove the peel from two-thirds of each orange, leaving a 1 cm wide strip to form a handle.
3. Carefully remove the fruit with a silver spoon and scrape away most of the white from the peel.
4. Trim the basket edges and handle into attractive points, then rinse well and soak in cold water for about 30 minutes.
5. Drain on a cloth.
6. Grate the orange peel from the sides next to the handle.
7. Press out the juice, measure it, and heat it.
8. Rinse the gelatin in cold water, cut into pieces, and dissolve in the orange juice, then mix with 3 tablespoons of the sugar.
9. Whip the cream with the remaining sugar until stiff peaks form.
10. Gradually pour the orange juice into the whipped cream while whisking.
11. Immediately fill the baskets with the blancmange and let them set on ice for 2-3 hours.
12. Pour the remaining cream into a tall, narrow, water-rinsed and sugar-sprinkled tin mold and let it set on ice for 2-3 hours.
13. When serving, loosen the cream from the mold edge and unmold onto a flat glass dish with cake paper.
14. Garnish the baskets with whipped cream and top with a small star of candied orange peel.
15. Place one basket in the center of the pudding with a wreath of orange stars around it.
16. Arrange the remaining baskets around the pudding and between them place almond mounds garnished with finely chopped pistachios.
