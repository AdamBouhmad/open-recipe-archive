## Ingredients

- 3 lobsters (400 g each)
- 5 liters water
- 200 g coarse salt
- 1 liter good fish stock or light meat stock
- 10 sheets gelatin
- 2 egg whites and rinsed shells
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 3 tablespoons good white wine
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 1 truffle
- parsley
- salad

## Directions

1. Cook the lobster as in recipe no. 170. Cut the meat into beautiful pieces.
2. Prepare the gelée as follows: pour the stock into a pot together with the rinsed gelatin and the egg whites lightly beaten with water, mixed with the crushed eggshells.
3. Bring the mixture to a good boil while whisking, place the lid on the pot, remove from heat and let it stand beside the stove for approximately 15 minutes.
4. Strain the stock through a cheesecloth or napkin wrung out in hot water. If the first liquid that runs through is not clear, pour it back into the cheesecloth. Repeat this until the gelée is completely clear, and when it is cold, add the wine.
5. Pour a little of the clear gelée onto the bottom of a rinsed, beautiful à-la-daube mold and place on ice to set.
6. Garnish the bottom with the hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into beautiful figures, and parsley.
7. Pour a few drops of gelée over the garnish and let it set so that the garnish is not disturbed when the lobster is added.
8. Place the most beautiful pieces of lobster on the bottom and sides, fill the mold with the remaining lobster meat.
9. Pour the remaining gelée over and place the dish on ice to set.
10. When ready to serve, quickly dip the mold into a pot of hot water, dry it and turn out onto a round plate.
11. Garnish the à-la-daube with chopped gelée, salad and parsley and serve to the smörgåsbord or as a course with genuine mayonnaise.
