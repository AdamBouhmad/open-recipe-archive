## Ingredients

- 15 egg yolks
- 1 1/2 egg white
- 250 gr. powdered sugar
- 2 1/2 dcl. extra thick cream
- 1/2 wine glass cognac
- 750 gr. wheat flour
- 1 1/2 kg. melted lard for frying
- 1 tbsp. brandy for frying

## Directions

1. Stir the eggs and sugar in the same direction for 1 hour, then add the cream beaten to stiff peaks, the cognac, and finally the flour.
2. Work the dough very well so that it becomes elastic.
3. Cover it and let it rest overnight, otherwise it is very difficult to roll out.
4. Roll out the dough to 2 mm thickness and cut with a pastry cutter into squares, which are folded at the corners, strips that are twisted, or into oblong cakes, in the middle of which a slit is made, and one end of the cake is pulled through it so that a knot forms in the middle.
5. Heat the lard together with 1 tbsp. brandy, then add a raw potato slice (when the potato slice becomes dark brown, add a new one).
6. Add the klenäter—not too many at once—and fry light golden, place on brown paper to drain and cool with a wing or a fan if you have one.
