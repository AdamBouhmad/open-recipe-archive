## Ingredients

- 6-8 fresh herrings
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 g)
- 2 two-ore almond breads
- 1 cup sour milk
- 4 anchovies
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 g)
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped parsley
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 egg
- 50 g melted butter
- 40 g flour
- 60 g cold butter

## Directions

1. Skin the herrings, clean them, and remove the head, backbone, and all small bones without splitting the fish.
2. Rinse very well, dry, rub with salt and let rest for 2 hours.
3. Soak the almond breads in the milk, finely chop the anchovies and pound them in a mortar with the bread, egg yolks, and butter.
4. Pass the farce through a sieve and mix with parsley and pepper; if too loose, add finely crushed breadcrumbs, otherwise add a little cream.
5. Dry the herrings again, fill with the farce and fold closed.
6. Brush with beaten egg, dip in melted butter and turn in flour.
7. Grease a frying pan with half the cold butter, place the herrings in it and place remaining butter in flakes on top of the fish.
8. Fry brown in a hot oven, basting frequently.
9. Serve with brown butter mixed with chopped parsley and potato puree.
