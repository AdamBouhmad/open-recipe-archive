## Ingredients

- 6 large tomatoes
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 3 tbsp flour (60 g)
- interior of the tomatoes
- 3 tbsp cream
- 3 tbsp mushroom paste
- 1/2 tsp finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 1 hg calf sweetbread
- 1 can mushrooms (1/4 liter)
- 1/2 tbsp Madeira
- salt
- white pepper
- 2 tbsp butter (40 g) for the mold
- 2 tbsp crushed rusks for the mold

## Directions

1. Rinse and dry the tomatoes and cut out a lid at the stem. Hollow out the tomatoes with a teaspoon.
2. Melt half the butter with the flour, add the interior of the tomatoes, cream and mushroom paste together with the finely chopped onion, and let the sauce, which should be quite thick, cook for 5 minutes, then pass through a strainer.
3. Blanch the calf sweetbread, place in boiling salted water and let cook for 15 minutes or until white and firm. When cooled, skin and cut into small cubes.
4. Cut the mushrooms likewise into cubes and sauté in the remaining butter together with the calf sweetbread.
5. Mix the mushrooms and sweetbread with the strained sauce and season with wine and spices.
6. Fill the tomatoes with the mixture and replace the lids. Place them in a buttered and breadcrumbed shallow baking dish and scatter small flakes of butter over them.
7. Finally sprinkle the remaining crushed rusks over and bake the tomatoes in the oven for about 1/2 hour.
8. Serve with the smorgasbord or as an entree.
