## Ingredients

- 1 piece of salmon weighing 1 1/2 kg
- 3 tbsp salt
- 1 1/2 liters water
- 6 allspice berries
- 1 bay leaf
- 5 dill stalks
- 1 1/2 liters fish stock
- 10-12 leaves gelatin
- 3 egg whites
- 1 tbsp cold water
- 4 tbsp good white wine
- 5 dill stalks
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- dill

## Directions

1. Clean the salmon piece, remove scales and rinse well with a fish cloth.
2. Cut into slices and rub these with salt and let them rest for a while.
3. Place the fish in cold water with the remaining salt, seasonings (allspice and bay leaf) and dill and let it simmer slowly until tender, about 15 minutes.
4. Let it cool in the stock and then place on a serving dish with a slotted spoon.
5. Taste the fish stock and prepare the aspic as in recipe no. 181.
6. When cold, add the wine.
7. Pour a little of the clear aspic into the bottom of a beautiful, water-rinsed à-la-daube mold and place on ice to set.
8. Garnish the bottom with the hard-boiled egg, cut into beautiful shapes, and dill.
9. Pour a few drops of aspic over the garnish and let it set so the garnish does not shift.
10. Fill the mold with the boned fish, carefully pour the remaining aspic over and let it set in a cold place.
11. Turn out as in the previous recipe, garnish with chopped aspic and dill and serve for the smorgasbord or as a side course with real mayonnaise.
