## Ingredients

- 2 kg boneless veal loin
- 1.5 hg lard
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 1/2 tbsp salt (8 g)
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 1 large carrot
- 1 shallot
- 5 parsley stems
- 1 liter broth
- 5 hg of the meat
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 2 tbsp flour (20 g)
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 1/2 tbsp salt (8 g)
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- 2-2.5 dl cream
- 1 can truffle (1/8 liter)
- 2 olives
- 25 g salt ox tongue
- 3 hg cheap rice grains
- 1.5 liters water
- 3 carrots
- 1 can peas (1 liter)
- 1 can haricots verts (1/2 liter)
- 1 can asparagus (1/2 liter)
- 2 cauliflower heads
- 4 tbsp clarified butter (80 g)
- French potatoes
- green salad, parsley

## Directions

1. Beat the meat, prepare and lard it all around (see page 232), then cut it from one short side so it forms a large pocket.
2. Carefully cut out so much meat that 5 hg remains, and from this prepare forcemeat as in recipe no. 325.
3. Cut the truffle into strips, the olives into small triangles and the tongue into fine cubes and add last to the forcemeat, which should be quite firm and carefully seasoned.
4. Fill the meat with the forcemeat and sew the opening well closed.
5. Brown it in the butter together with the scraped carrot cut into slices, the peeled onion and parsley and sprinkle with a mixture of salt and pepper.
6. Pour on the boiling broth and let the meat braise slowly with a tightly closed lid until tender, approximately 3 hours, basting often and turning several times.
7. Cut the roast into thin slices, which are laid together so it looks whole, then placed on a bed of rice grains.
8. Rinse the rice grains, blanch in hot water, place in cold water and let boil until soft, approximately 20 minutes, then drain in a colander and let drip well.
9. Form the rice on the serving dish into a 3-4 cm high, even base, which should stand for a few hours so it becomes firm.
10. Around the meat garnish with the cooked carrots, beautifully cut with a vegetable knife, the peas, beans and asparagus, heated in a water bath, the cooked cauliflower, French potatoes, green salad and parsley.
11. Pour the clarified butter over the vegetables.
12. Serve the sauce strained, mixed with thick cream (see recipe no. 429).
