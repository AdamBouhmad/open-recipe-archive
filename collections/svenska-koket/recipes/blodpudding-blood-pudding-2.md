## Ingredients

- 1/2 litre blood
- 4 dcl milk or weak beer
- 2–3 tbsp syrup
- approximately 210 g ground rye flour
- 3 tbsp finely chopped kidney fat, butter or lard
- 1 1/2 tbsp salt (23 g)
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 1/2 tsp ginger
- 1/2 tsk finely ground marjoram
- 1 finely chopped red onion
- 1 tbsp butter for the onion
- 1/2 tbsp butter (10 g) for the mould
- 2 tbsp crushed rusks

## Directions

1. Strain the blood, whisk well and mix with the milk or beer and syrup.
2. Whisk in the flour, add the kidney fat, spices and the onion fried in butter, and test the batter to see if it is properly seasoned and properly thick by frying a little of it in a frying pan.
3. Butter a pudding mould with a lid and sprinkle with crushed rusks.
4. Fill the mould two-thirds full with the batter, cover with the lid, and cook the pudding in a water bath on the stove for approximately 2 1/2–3 hours.
5. Test with a whisk to see if the pudding is cooked through.
6. Serve with melted butter and lingonberry jam.
