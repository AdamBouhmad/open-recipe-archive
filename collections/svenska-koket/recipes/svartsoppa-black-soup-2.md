## Ingredients

- Giblets of 1 goose
- 1/3 liter goose or pork blood
- 6 tablespoons flour (60 gr.)
- 1/2 liter broth in which the giblets were cooked
- 2 liters broth
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 teaspoon ginger
- 1 teaspoon ground cloves
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 gr.)
- 8 tablespoons sugar (120 gr.)
- 8 tablespoons good vinegar
- 2 hectograms prunes
- 1 liter fresh apples
- 3-4 deciliters Madeira or sherry

## Directions

1. The giblets consist of head, neck, wing tips, feet, heart, liver and stomach.
2. Remove the goose and carefully separate the innards from each other.
3. Scald the head in hot water, split lengthwise, rinse well, remove the eyes and scrape the beak.
4. Scald and flay the feet, cut the heart in two, cut open the stomach, scrape out the contents and remove the thick yellow membrane.
5. Carefully loosen the gallbladder from the liver, which is used for goose liver sausage.
6. Remove the neck skin and use as sausage casing.
7. Break the neck, scald the wing tips and split them.
8. Rinse the giblets well and preferably let them soak in water for a few hours.
9. Place them in 1 liter of cold water, skim well, add 1/2 tablespoon of the salt and 6 white peppercorns and 4 cloves and let everything cook with a tightly closed lid about 3 hours.
10. Rinse the prunes well in lukewarm water, place in 3 deciliters of cold water together with 1 tablespoon of the sugar and cook until soft.
11. Ten minutes before they are done, add the apples, peeled and cut into quarters.
12. When the meat is tender, take it out, cut into pieces and place in the soup bowl.
13. Strain the broth, skim it and let it boil down to 1/2 liter.
14. Strain the blood and whisk with the flour and 1/2 liter of the cold broth; this mixture should stand and swell for 1 hour.
15. Add the blood mixture to the hot broth, stirring constantly, then add the remaining sugar, vinegar and the cooked prunes with apples and let the soup cook a few minutes.
16. Season with white pepper and sherry and serve with fried goose liver.
