## Ingredients

- 2 cod fish, approximately 7 hg each
- 1 msk salt (15 gr.)
- 1 1/2 msk butter (30 gr.)
- 1 small red onion
- 3 dcl strong meat stock
- 4 dcl fish broth
- 1/2 dcl white wine
- 1 1/2 msk butter (30 gr.) for sauce
- 4 msk flour (40 gr.)
- 4 1/2 dcl of the broth in which the fish cooked
- 1/2 dcl white wine
- cayenne

## Directions

1. Clean the cod, rinse well, dry and cut into fillets.
2. Cut away the backbone and remove the meat from the skin.
3. Rub the fillets with salt, let lie 1 hour and dry again.
4. Cook broth on the head, bones and skin.
5. Melt the butter in a low saucepan or small long pan, sauté the peeled onion cut into slices in it, place the fish fillets in, add the meat stock, broth and wine, and cook the fish 10-15 minutes as in no. 328.
6. Place it on a warm serving dish with a slotted spoon and pour the sauce over.
7. Garnish the dish with parsley and serve with small cooked potatoes.
8. For the sauce: sauté butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the strained fish broth, let the sauce cook 5 minutes and season with wine and cayenne.
