## Ingredients

- 1 chicken
- 1 pork tenderloin
- 1 kg veal knuckle
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 5 liters brown broth
- 1 can of preserved turtle (1/4 liter)
- 2 carrots, 1/2 celery
- 1 leek
- 6 parsley stems
- 1 red onion
- 1 clove
- a little thyme and marjoram
- salt, cayenne
- 1-2 deciliters aged Madeira
- 1-2 tablespoons fine cognac

## Directions

1. Trim the chicken in the usual manner and cut into pieces (the breast meat is reserved for quenelles). Cut the pork tenderloin into slices.
2. Rinse the veal knuckle, dry it and chop it up. Brown the meat in butter, place in a pot and add the cold, clear broth.
3. Bring the broth to a boil, skim, and add the trimmed vegetables and spices together with the stock from the turtle can.
4. Let everything simmer with a tightly closed lid for 4-5 hours. Remove the meat with a slotted spoon, strain the broth through a cloth, skim well and measure—should be 3 liters, if necessary let it boil down with the lid on.
5. Cut the turtle into beautiful pieces, place in the broth, which is brought to a boil again and seasoned with spices and wine. Serve with hard-boiled egg yolks and quenelles of chicken forcemeat.
6. If a thick soup is desired, thicken it with 20 g cornstarch mixed in 1 cup cold water; let it simmer for 5 minutes afterwards.
