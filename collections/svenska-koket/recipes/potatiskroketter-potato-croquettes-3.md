## Ingredients

- 1 kg peeled potatoes
- 30 gr butter
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tsp sugar
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- a little nutmeg
- 3 egg yolks
- 1 1/2 tbsp butter (30 gr) for the dish
- 2 tbsp flour (20 gr) for coating
- 1 egg for coating
- 1 tsp cooking oil
- 1/2 tsp salt (3 gr) for coating
- 1 1/2 dcl crushed rusks for coating
- 1 kg lard for frying

## Directions

1. Peel the potatoes thinly, cut into pieces, weigh them and cook quickly in salted water until soft but not falling apart.
2. Immediately drain in a colander and place in the front of the oven for a few minutes to dry.
3. Pass the potatoes through a fine sieve.
4. Place the puree in a saucepan, add the cold butter and seasonings and stir with a fork over the heat until firm.
5. Remove from heat and add the egg yolks one at a time, stirring constantly with a fork, then taste and adjust seasonings.
6. Butter a flat dish and spread the mixture thinly so it cools quickly.
7. Brush the surface with butter so it does not dry out.
8. Divide the mixture with a spoon into smaller pieces—each piece should weigh about 35 gr—and roll on a floured work surface in the form of corks, pears, buns or round patties.
9. Dip in the stiffly beaten eggs mixed with cooking oil and seasoned with the spices, then roll in the finely crushed rusks and let sit 1 hour to harden.
10. Fry golden brown in hot lard and place on absorbent paper to drain.
11. Serve with roasted meat. If the croquettes are formed as pears, use parsley sprigs or spinach leaves as stems.
