## Ingredients

- 1 young goose, approximately 6 kg
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 gr.)
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 5-6 apples
- 125 gr. prunes
- 1 1/2 liters broth or water

## Directions

1. Regarding purchase of goose, see page 299. Singe the goose over a spirit or gas flame, then carefully pluck all feathers and wash the goose well with lukewarm water and a soft brush, then dry it.
2. The head with neck, wings and feet are cut off and used together with heart, liver and gizzard for black soup (see recipe 255).
3. Cut open and remove the organs from the goose (see page 300) — remove the fat and place it in cold water along with the fat that is picked from the intestines — rinse well in cold water, dry and rub inside and out with the seasonings.
4. Fill the bird with the apples, peeled and cut into large wedges, and with the blanched and pitted prunes, sew it closed, truss it (see page 300) and place in a roasting pan.
5. Place in a warm oven and baste frequently with the fat that forms in the pan; when beautifully browned, pour in the boiling broth and let the goose roast until tender, basting every 10 minutes. Allow 2 1/2 to 3 hours for roasting.
6. If crispy skin is desired, baste the goose only the first hour, then let it roast with the oven door slightly ajar.
7. Half an hour before it is done, strain off the gravy and return the goose to the oven with 1/2 liter of boiling water in the pan.
8. Skim the gravy well, reduce it, season with meat extract and soy sauce and thicken with browned thickening (see page 210).
9. Place the goose on a platter, remove the trussing strings, cut it and arrange it again so that it appears whole, garnish with browned potatoes, kale or parsley and the fruit with which it was filled.
10. Serve with red cabbage and pickled cucumber.
