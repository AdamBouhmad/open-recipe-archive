## Ingredients

- 50 gr. butter
- 60 gr. mondamin (a flour prepared from corn)
- 1/2 litre boiling milk
- 4–5 eggs
- 40–50 gr. grated cheese
- 1/2 tsp. salt
- 1/6 tsp. white pepper
- 1/6 tsp. mustard
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 gr.) for the mould
- 2 tbsp. crushed rusks for the mould

## Directions

1. The butter is melted in a saucepan, the mondamin is added and stirred until it is clear.
2. The milk is added gradually and the sauce is allowed to boil 5 minutes.
3. The pan is removed from heat, and when most of the heat has subsided the egg yolks are added, one at a time, then the grated cheese and the seasonings.
4. Last the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks are folded in.
5. The mixture is poured into a buttered and breadcrumbed fireproof mould and baked in ordinary oven heat for about 45 minutes.
6. The soufflé is served immediately as a course with browned butter or to the smorgasbord.
7. The mixture can also be baked in shells or small moulds and served instead of cheese and crackers.
