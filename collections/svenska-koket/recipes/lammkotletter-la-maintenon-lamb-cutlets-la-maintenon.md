## Ingredients

- 6 large lamb cutlets
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 hectogram cooked veal or chicken
- 30 gr. salt ox tongue
- 2 truffles
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 gr.)
- 1 teaspoon chopped shallot onion
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 gr.)
- 2 deciliters bouillon
- 1 egg yolk
- salt, white pepper
- 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
- 1 egg yolk for brushing
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 1 1/2 deciliters meat gravy or bouillon

## Directions

1. The lamb cutlets should be chopped with long bones and be approximately 2 cm. thick. Dry them with a cloth wrung out in hot water, trim them, beat on both sides and rub in with the spice mixture. The meat, tongue and truffle are cut into small, even cubes. The butter and onion are sautéed with the flour 2 minutes, the bouillon is added and the sauce is allowed to cook 5 minutes. The beaten egg yolk is stirred in along with the meat and mushroom, the stuffing is seasoned with spices and parsley and allowed to cool. The cutlets are fried 4 minutes on one side in half of the butter. They are then placed on a cutting board with the fried side up. The stuffing is divided into 6 equal parts and spread out on the fried side of the cutlet and brushed with the beaten egg yolk. The rest of the butter is sautéed in the frying pan, the cutlets are placed in with the stuffing side up and gratinéed in the oven approximately 8 minutes. They are placed on a hot serving dish, the pan is whisked out with the meat gravy, the sauce is brought to a boil, seasoned with 1 teaspoon cold butter and poured over the cutlets. Paper frills are slipped on the bones. Garnish with peas or French potatoes and parsley and serve as a breakfast or intermediate course.
