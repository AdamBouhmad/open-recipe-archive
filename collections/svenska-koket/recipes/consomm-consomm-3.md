## Ingredients

- 2 1/2 kg. beef topside with bone
- 1 kg. veal neck
- 1 old hen
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 6 liters water
- 1 carrot
- 1 parsnip
- 1/4 celeriac
- 1 small red onion
- 1 clove
- 10 gr. salt
- 1/2 kg. lean, boneless beef (for clarification)
- 2 egg whites and rinsed eggshells (for clarification)

## Directions

1. Rinse the meat quickly and dry with a kitchen towel. Cut from the bones into small pieces and crack the bones well. Pluck the hen, singe it, remove, rinse and let it soak in water for about 6 hours.
2. Place the bones in cold water, which is brought to a rapid boil and skimmed well. Add the prepared vegetables and the onion, in which the clove has been stuck, and let everything cook for 6 hours with the lid tightly closed.
3. Strain the broth through a sieve, let it cool and pour it back into the well-cleaned broth pot. Dry the hen well with a kitchen towel and brown it in the butter until it has taken on a beautiful golden-brown color, then place it in the broth along with the ground meat and salt.
4. Let the consommé cook slowly for 4 hours, strain and let it stand until the next day, when it is clarified as in recipe no. 223. Measure it, bring to a boil, season and serve as clear broth.
