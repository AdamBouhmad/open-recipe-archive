## Ingredients

- 2 pikes weighing about 700 g each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1 1/2 hectograms butter
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 small celery
- 1 small red onion
- 3 tablespoons chopped parsley
- 3 decilitres fish stock
- 1 decilitre thick cream
- 2 egg yolks

## Directions

1. Treat the pikes as in recipe 352
2. Rub the fillets with salt and let them rest 1 hour
3. Dry them well with a cloth
4. Cut them into 4 cm wide pieces across the width and dredge in flour
5. Butter a pot with half of the butter and lay the fish pieces in it
6. Between each layer of fish place some pepper, the peeled celery cut into small cubes, the peeled onion sliced into rounds, the parsley, and the remaining butter distributed in flakes
7. Dilute the fish stock (made from head, bones, and skin) and cream and pour over
8. Let the fish simmer gently with the lid tightly closed for about 20 minutes
9. Transfer to a warm dish
10. If needed, thin the sauce
11. Add the beaten egg yolks and let the sauce simmer while whisking
12. Pour the sauce over the fish
13. Serve with boiled potatoes
