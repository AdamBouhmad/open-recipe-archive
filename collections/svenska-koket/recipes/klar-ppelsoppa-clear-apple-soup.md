## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 liters tart apples
- 1 1/2 liters water
- Zest of 1/4 lemon
- 1–1 1/2 coffee spoons crushed sugar
- 1/2 deciliter French wine or sherry
- 2 tablespoons potato flour (80 gr.)
- 2 tablespoons red currant jelly

## Directions

1. Rinse and dry the apples. Do not peel 1 liter of them; cut them into wedges and remove the flower, stem, and core.
2. Put them in cold water and let them cook until soft but not falling apart or mushing.
3. Pour them into cheesecloth and let the liquid drain. (The pulp can be cooked again and the liquid used for cream.)
4. Peel the remaining apples, cut them into 6 wedges, and cook them with the lemon zest, sugar, and wine in the apple liquid until soft.
5. Thicken the soup with the potato flour mixed in 1/2 coffee spoon cold water and let it cook 2 minutes.
6. Taste it, let it cool, and add small dollops of currant jelly.
7. Serve with rusks or biscuits.
