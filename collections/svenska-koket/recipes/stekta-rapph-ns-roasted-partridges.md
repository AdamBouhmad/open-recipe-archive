## Ingredients

- 3 partridges
- 1 hectogram lard, in 3 slices
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 3 decilitres bouillon
- 4 decilitres thick cream

## Directions

1. Young partridges are recognized by their yellow legs, whereas older ones have a reddish color. Old partridges should hang longer than young ones and require longer roasting time, but the meat is equally juicy and good as from young birds.
2. If the partridges are frozen, thaw them overnight in a warm kitchen, clean them (see page 299) and let them lie in lukewarm milk for a few hours.
3. Remove, let them drain, dry well, truss and cover with thin lard slices.
4. Brown them in the butter together with the giblets, salt and add first the boiling bouillon, then the cream, and let them slowly roast with a tight lid until tender, or 1—1¼ hours; baste them frequently.
5. If the partridges are young and not frozen, they should not lie in milk and should not be covered with lard. They are roasted in plenty of butter as previously stated.
6. If desired, they can be diluted with only bouillon or cream.
7. Cut, garnish (see page 300) and serve with the reduced sauce or with strained sauce (see recipe 429). Mushrooms can be sautéed in butter and added to the sauce.
