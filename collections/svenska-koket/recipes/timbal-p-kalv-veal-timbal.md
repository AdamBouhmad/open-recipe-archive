## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg. boneless veal
- 80 gr. butter
- 2 eggs
- 4 dcl. thick cream
- 3 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 2 tbsp. flour (20 gr.)
- 2 carrots
- 1/2 can green beans (haricots-verts)
- 1 hg. macaroni
- 2 tbsp. butter for the mold (40 gr.)

## Directions

1. Butter a timbal mold evenly with cold butter and garnish it. Rinse the carrots, cook them soft in salted water, peel them, and cut them into slices, which are cut out with a vegetable cutter into flowers or beautiful figures. Cut the beans diagonally into 3 parts. Pre-cook the macaroni in salted water and cut into 1 cm. long pieces. Garnish the buttered mold with carrot flowers and beans with double rings of macaroni between them.
2. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into slices and scrape very thoroughly. Pound it thereafter in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle, first alone until it is fine, then with the butter and the whole eggs, one at a time. Half of the cream is added little by little with even stirring, and the forcemeat is passed through a hair sieve. The rest of the cream is whipped to stiff foam and stirred into the forcemeat by spoonfuls along with the seasonings and flour.
3. Fill the forcemeat into the garnished mold and cook in a water bath in the oven for about 1 hour. Turn it out, garnish with mushroom stew or fine vegetables and serve as a middle course. The timbal can also be garnished with macaroni alone, arranged tightly next to each other so that it resembles a honeycomb.
