## Ingredients

- 750 gr. beef tenderloin
- 1 hg. beef marrow
- 2 tbsp. chopped shallots
- 1/2 tbsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 gr.)
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 gr.) for sauce
- 2 tsp. chopped shallots for sauce
- 1/2 dcl. meat jus
- 1 1/2 dcl. good broth
- 1 tbsp. white wine
- paprika
- 1 tbsp. finely chopped parsley
- 1 tbsp. beef marrow in fine dice for sauce

## Directions

1. First cook the sauce: brown the butter, add the onion and brown nicely, add the meat jus, broth and wine, bring to a good boil and strain through a sieve. Let the sauce boil, season with a little paprika, and lastly stir in the parsley and the diced marrow.
2. Dry the tenderloin with a cloth wrung out in hot water. Carefully remove all outer sinews and membranes, then cut the tenderloin into 6 slices, which are lightly flattened. Make an incision from the side in each slice so that a small opening or pocket is formed. The marrow, which should have soaked in water for 1 hour to draw out the blood, is blanched, i.e. placed in cold salted water and allowed to boil 2-3 minutes without a lid, then drained on a sieve and cooled. Cut into 7 slices (one of which is cut into fine dice and placed in the sauce), brush with melted butter and sprinkle with the chopped onion fried in a little butter. Place the slices into the opening made in the steak, which is pressed shut. Rub the steaks on both sides with the spice mixture and brush with a little butter. Braise or fry in butter 6-8 minutes, taking care that the openings face the pan handle and this is lifted slightly during frying so that the marrow does not flow out. Place on a warm dish, pour the sauce over and serve the dish with small fried potatoes, sprinkled with finely chopped parsley.
