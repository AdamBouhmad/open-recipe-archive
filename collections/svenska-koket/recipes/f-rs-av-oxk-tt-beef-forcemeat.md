## Ingredients

- 5 hg. boneless beef
- 50 g. lard or cleaned kidney fat
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 2 tsp. salt (10 g.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 2 hg. bread panade or 2 tbsp. flour (20 g.)
- 2-3 dcl. cream or milk
- 1/2 tbsp. finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 g.) for the onion
- 1 egg white for brushing
- 2 tbsp. crushed rusks for coating
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 g.) for browning
- 1/2 liter boiling broth or water, mixed with 1/2 tsp. solid meat extract

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and cut into cubes, removing all sinews and membranes carefully.
2. Scrape and cut the lard into pieces; if using kidney fat, chop it finely.
3. Mix the meat with the lard or kidney fat and the seasonings, and grind 6 times through a meat grinder.
4. Pound the forcemeat in a stone mortar together with the panade (see recipe no. 626) or flour, until it is smooth and supple, then place it in a bowl and add the beaten egg and egg yolk under vigorous stirring.
5. Continue stirring the forcemeat for about 3/4 hour while gradually adding the cream or milk.
6. Test it (see recipe no. 628); if it is too firm, add a little more cream; if too soft, add an egg white.
7. If desired, add lastly the onion that has been fried in butter.
8. Grease a long baking pan with cold butter, place the forcemeat in it and shape it as an oblong roll.
9. Brush it with the egg white and sprinkle with crushed rusks.
10. Put the forcemeat in the oven and brown it, basting it frequently with the butter in the pan.
11. When browned, dilute it with the boiling broth or water mixed with meat extract, and let it finish cooking, which takes about 1/2 hour.
12. During cooking, baste it about every 10 minutes.
13. Place the forcemeat on a hot platter, cut into thin slices, garnish with parsley and serve with the beaten, strained meat gravy or with a prepared sauce (see recipe no. 429).
14. If desired, use 3 hg. beef and 2 hg. veal, with the same amount of lard or kidney fat.
15. The forcemeat may be diluted with broth or water instead of cream or milk.
16. If the forcemeat is cooked in a water bath, dilute it with somewhat more cream.
