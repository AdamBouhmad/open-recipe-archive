## Ingredients

- 3-4 woodcocks
- 1 hg. bacon, in 3-4 slices
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 5 msk. butter (1 hg.)
- For the farced bread: giblets from the birds
- 20 gr. bacon
- 1/2 msk. finely chopped parsley
- 1 1/2 msk. butter (30 gr.)
- 1-2 egg yolks
- 1/2 msk. red wine
- salt
- white pepper
- 9 bread slices
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.) for the bread

## Directions

1. Woodcock is among the most expensive game we have and is considered a great delicacy.
2. The birds should hang 3-4 days and be plucked the day they are to be served.
3. The head and neck are left on, but the skin on the head is removed and the eyes taken out.
4. The birds are gutted carefully, the giblets saved and used for the farce.
5. The claws are clipped, the feet are blanched in hot water and the skin removed with a cloth.
6. The birds are rinsed quickly, dried well inside and out, and trussed so that the legs are pressed tightly against the body, the head bent back, and the beak inserted through the leg, body, and out through the other leg.
7. The breast is covered with thin bacon slices, which are tied with fine string.
8. The birds are then roasted in butter like chicken (see no. 602) and require the same roasting time.
9. The giblets are cleaned well, rinsed, wrapped in cloth, finely chopped together with the bacon, pounded in a mortar, and strained.
10. The farce is mixed with the parsley, quickly fried in butter and allowed to cool, after which the egg yolk, wine, and seasonings are added.
11. The bread slices are formed into neat oblongs, fried in butter, covered with the farce, placed on a baking sheet and put in a hot oven so the farce is quickly browned.
12. The woodcocks are halved lengthwise, placed on croutons (see p. 300) with the heads facing out; peppercorns are placed in the eye sockets.
13. Garnished with the farced bread slices and salad or parsley.
14. Served with browned potatoes, green salad, and lingonberry jelly.
