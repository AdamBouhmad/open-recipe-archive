## Ingredients

- 6 dcl. sugar syrup (made from 1 hg. to 5 hg. sugar per 3 1/2 dcl. water)
- 1 liter fruit purée or juice
- lemon juice to taste

## Directions

1. Cook a clear syrup from the sugar and water. The proportions of sugar and water are calculated according to how sweet and juicy the fruit is to be used, and can vary as stated above.
2. Crush and strain the fruit or let it run naturally and mix with the sugar syrup.
3. If a saccharometer is available, measure the mixture. If it reads above 22 degrees, dilute it with a little cooled boiled water; if it reads below, add a little more dissolved sugar.
4. Freeze the fruit ice as cream ice (see no. 929), but it must stand approximately 1 hour longer in the mold with the salt mixture packed tightly around it before unmolding.
5. It melts quickly and should therefore be served just as it is to be consumed.
