## Ingredients

- 1 fresh ox tongue
- water
- 2 tablespoons salt
- 5 white peppercorns
- 1-2 eggs
- 1 part white French wine
- 1 liter of cooking liquid
- 8 leaves gelatin

## Directions

1. Let the ox tongue soak in cold water for 1 hour, then scald in hot water and scrape well.
2. Place in boiling water, skim carefully when it comes to a boil, and add the spices.
3. Let the tongue simmer slowly for 2-3 hours, then remove and peel off the skin.
4. Strain and skim the cooking liquid, then return it to the heat with the rinsed gelatin dissolved in a little hot water, the wine and the beaten eggs.
5. Let the mixture come to a good boil while whisking, then strain through a cloth or fine sieve.
6. Pour the clear cooking liquid into the bottom of a rinsed à-la-daube mold and set aside to set.
7. Cut the tongue into thin slices, which are arranged spirally in the mold until full.
8. Carefully pour the remaining aspic over and set the à-la-daube aside to set.
