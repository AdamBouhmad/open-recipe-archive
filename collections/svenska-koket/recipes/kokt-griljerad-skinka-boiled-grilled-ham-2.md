## Ingredients

- 1 lightly salted ham, about 5 kg
- 1 egg white
- 1 teaspoon dry mustard
- 2 teaspoons sugar
- 2 tablespoons finely crushed rusks

## Directions

1. The ham should have been in salt brine (see Curing of Meat) for 3-4 weeks. Rinse it and let it soak in plenty of cold water for about 12 hours to draw out the salt.
2. Place it in cold water with the skin side up, with enough water to cover it well.
3. Bring the water rapidly to a boil, skim it and boil for 15 minutes at 100°.
4. Cool the broth to 82°-83°, and at this temperature let the ham rest for about 3½ hours, or until tender, which is tested with a whisk (see page 233).
5. Place the ham in a deep dish, pour the broth over, and let it cool in it. (In summer, the ham should be cooled in cold water, because the broth easily sours then.)
6. Remove the skin; press a towel against the lard to remove all dissolved fat.
7. Beat the egg white with a fork to a light foam, blend with mustard and sugar, and coat the ham's lard side with this.
8. Sprinkle the crushed rusks over it, place the ham in a roasting pan and put in a hot oven to get a beautiful brown color, which takes about ½ hour.
9. Place on a large platter, garnish beautifully with greens and serve warm or cold with vegetables, prunes and stuffed apple halves. Garnish the bone of the ham with curled paper.
