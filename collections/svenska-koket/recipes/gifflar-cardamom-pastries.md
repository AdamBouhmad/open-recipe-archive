## Ingredients

- 2 dl cream
- 100 g butter
- flour, as needed for a firm dough
- 1 whole egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 40 g caster sugar
- 30 g fresh yeast
- 1 egg for brushing
- 50 g sweet almonds

## Directions

1. Warm the cream and melt the butter in it.
2. Sift 300 g flour into a bowl, add the warm cream together with the whisked egg and egg yolk, as well as the sugar and the yeast dissolved in 1 teaspoon sugar, then work the dough until it is smooth and shiny.
3. Work in more flour so the dough becomes quite firm, then let it rise to double its size.
4. Place the dough on a floured work surface, work it quickly, and divide it into 6 parts.
5. Roll out each part into a 1 cm thick cake and cut it with a pastry wheel into 4 pieces. Roll these up toward the point into the shape of gifflar, place them on buttered baking sheets, brush with whisked egg, sprinkle with finely chopped almonds, and let them rise to double their size, preferably over steam.
6. Bake in a very hot oven and serve with coffee and tea. Before cutting the cakes with the pastry wheel, they can be brushed with melted butter and sprinkled with sugar and almonds.
