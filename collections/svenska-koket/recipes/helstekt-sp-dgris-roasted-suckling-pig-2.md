## Ingredients

- 1 suckling pig of approximately 4 kg
- 1 1/2 tablespoon salt (23 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon ginger
- Small raw, tart apples
- 3 tablespoon butter (60 g)
- 3/4 liter broth (1 hectogram lard)

## Directions

1. Clean the pig thoroughly in the belly cavity and mouth, brush the teeth well, and rinse and dry the pig.
2. Rub it internally with a mixture of salt, white pepper, and ginger.
3. Rinse the apples, dry them, remove the stem and blossom, and fill the pig with the apples so that it retains its shape, then sew it together.
4. Bend the legs, and place the pig on its knees in a long pan, coated with half of the butter.
5. Place the pan in a warm oven and pour in the boiling broth.
6. Let the pig roast until it is tender, or about 2 hours.
7. During the first hour, baste it frequently with the broth, but thereafter rub only the skin every 5 minutes with the lard or with the remaining butter, wrapped in a linen cloth, or with a linen cloth dipped in cooking oil, so that the skin becomes crispy and golden and does not burst.
8. Place it on a large platter and cut it into slices, which are then put back together so that the pig appears whole.
9. Place an apple in its mouth and wrap the ears with curled paper.
