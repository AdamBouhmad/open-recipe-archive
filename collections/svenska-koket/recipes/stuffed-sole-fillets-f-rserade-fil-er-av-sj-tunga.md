## Ingredients

- 3 sole, approximately 500 g each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- Fish forcemeat: 1/2 kg pike = 210 g scraped fish meat
- 125 g good butter
- 15 g flour
- 2 decilitres cream
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1 teaspoon sugar (5 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- For garnishing: 4 truffles or 48 shrimp tails
- For cooking: 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 g)
- fish stock

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the sole as in recipe 371
2. Flatten the fillets with a moistened meat mallet, rub with salt and let them lie on top of each other for a couple of hours
3. Prepare the fish forcemeat as in recipe 356
4. Dry the fillets again, cut each fillet in half and trim the pieces so they become oblong and rounded, or cut them pear-shaped
5. Cover them with a 1 1/2 cm thick layer of fish forcemeat, spread evenly with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water, and brush over with lightly beaten egg white
6. Garnish the forcemeat with truffle cut into beautiful figures or with shrimp tails or with both
7. Scour a long pan clean (so it does not blacken) and grease it with cold butter, lay the fillets in it and add enough fish stock (cooked from head, bones and skin) so that it reaches up to the lower edge of the forcemeat
8. Cover with a lid or sheet, place the pan in ordinary oven heat and let the fillets cook for 15-20 minutes
9. Test with a skewer to see if they are done
10. Place on a warm dish with a slotted spoon, garnish with parsley and serve with hollandaise, crayfish or lobster sauce and small boiled potatoes
