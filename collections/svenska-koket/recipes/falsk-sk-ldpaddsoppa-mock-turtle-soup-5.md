## Ingredients

- 1 calf's head
- 2.5 liters water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 6 white peppercorns
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 1 slice smoked pork (20 g)
- 2 red onions
- 3 carrots
- 1 leek
- 1/2 celery
- 1 tablespoon tomato purée
- 6 tablespoons flour (60 g)
- 2 liters brown broth
- sugar soy sauce, salt, cayenne
- 1 deciliter Madeira wine
- hard-boiled egg yolks
- fish quenelles

## Directions

1. The calf's head should be scalded and shaved with the skin not removed. It is split; the tongue, brain (used for croquettes and stewed dishes) and eyes are removed, the head is rinsed and left in plenty of cold water for about 12 hours so that the blood is drawn out.
2. It is cleaned and blanched for 10 minutes, placed in cold water with the salt added, skimmed carefully and allowed to boil with the pepper until the meat loosens from the bones.
3. The head is then removed, the meat is cut loose, placed on a flat plate under light pressure, the broth is strained and skimmed.
4. The pork slice and the prepared, cut up vegetables are browned well in 2 tablespoons of the butter. The broth and tomato purée are added and everything is allowed to boil for about 2 hours.
5. The broth is strained and skimmed again, measured and should make 1 liter.
6. The remaining butter is fried with the flour for 2 minutes, the broth and stock are diluted, the meat cut into small, even pieces is added and the soup is allowed to boil while stirring for 5 minutes.
7. It is colored with sugar soy sauce (2 teaspoons burnt sugar, 2 tablespoons hot water), seasoned with spices and wine and served with hard-boiled egg yolks and fish quenelles.
