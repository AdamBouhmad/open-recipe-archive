## Ingredients

- 1½ liters tart apples
- 1½ liters water
- zest of 1/4 lemon
- 1-1½ teaspoons crushed sugar
- ½ deciliter French wine or sherry
- 2 tablespoons potato flour (80 g)
- 2 tablespoons red currant jelly

## Directions

1. Rinse and dry the apples. Do not peel one liter of them; cut them into wedges, removing the flower, stem, and core. Place them in cold water and let cook until soft but not falling apart or becoming mushy.
2. Pour them through cheesecloth and let the juice drain off. (The pulp can be cooked again and the juice used for cream.)
3. Peel the remaining apples, cut into 6 wedges and cook with the lemon zest, sugar, and wine in the apple juice until soft.
4. Thicken the soup with the potato flour, stirred into ½ cup cold water, and let cook 2 minutes.
5. Taste for seasoning, let cool, and add small pieces of the currant jelly.
6. Serve with rusks or biscuits.
