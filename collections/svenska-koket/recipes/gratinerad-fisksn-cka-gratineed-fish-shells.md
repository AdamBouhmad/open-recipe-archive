## Ingredients

- Leftovers of cooked fish, approximately 3 dcl.
- 1 can of lobster (1/4 liter)
- 6-8 mushrooms
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 grams)
- 4 tbsp. flour (40 grams)
- 1/2 liter boiling milk
- salt, white pepper
- 1.5 tbsp. butter (30 grams) for the shells
- 2 tbsp. crushed hardtack or breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Free the fish from all bones and cut into fine cubes.
2. Cut the lobster into small pieces and the mushrooms into thin slices.
3. Fry the mushrooms in the butter until clear, then squeeze them out of the butter with a spoon.
4. Fry the flour with the butter for 2 minutes, add the milk and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes and season with spices.
5. Grease 6 shell-shaped dishes well with butter and place fish, lobster and mushroom inside.
6. Pour the sauce over the contents in the shells, sprinkle with crushed hardtack and drizzle with a little clarified butter over.
7. Gratinize the stew and serve as in the previous recipe.
