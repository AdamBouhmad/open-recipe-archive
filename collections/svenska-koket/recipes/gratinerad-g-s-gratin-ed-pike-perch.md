## Ingredients

- 2 pike perch weighing 600 g each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1.5 tablespoons butter (30 g)
- Fish stock
- For the fish forcemeat: 600 g pike = 220 g scraped fish meat
- 125 g butter
- 1.5 tablespoons flour (15 g)
- 2.5-3 decilitres cream
- 2 eggs
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 teaspoon sugar (5 g)
- For the sauce: 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 5 tablespoons flour (50 g)
- 8 decilitres fish stock
- Salt
- White pepper
- 3 egg yolks
- 50 g cold butter
- 3 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese

## Directions

1. Treat the pike perch as in recipe no. 365, but keep the fillets whole.
2. Rub them with salt and let them rest on top of each other for 1 hour.
3. Cook stock on the head, bones, and skin.
4. Prepare the fish forcemeat as in recipe no. 356.
5. Butter an oblong ovenproof dish, or preferably a silver one, with cold butter, spread the forcemeat on it but it should be shaped 2 cm smaller than the bottom of the dish.
6. Place the dish in a roasting pan with coarse salt and let the fish forcemeat bake in the oven for approximately 15 minutes.
7. Cook the fillets as in recipe no. 328 and let them drain on a cloth.
8. Cook the cooking liquid together and use it for the sauce.
9. Place two of the fillets on top of the fish forcemeat, pour half of the sauce over them, place the remaining fillets on top and pour the rest of the sauce over.
10. Sprinkle the dish with the grated cheese, place the dish back in the roasting pan, and gratinée the fish in a warm oven for 10-15 minutes.
11. Serve with small boiled potatoes.
12. Note: The sauce is prepared as in recipe no. 365.
