## Ingredients

- 4 hg boned beef
- 1 hg boned veal or pork
- 50 gr lard
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr)
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 dcl breadcrumbs
- 3–4 dcl cream, milk or broth
- 1 msk finely chopped Portuguese onion (optional)
- 1/2 msk butter (10 gr) for the onion (optional)
- 4 msk butter (80 gr) for frying
- 4 dcl broth

## Directions

1. If desired, beef alone may be used.
2. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, remove sinews and membranes, cut into cubes, mix with lard and spices and pass 6 times through a meat grinder.
3. Mix the forcemeat with the whisked egg, egg yolk and breadcrumbs and work for 1 hour, adding the cream tablespoon by tablespoon.
4. If desired, add at the end the finely chopped onion, fried in butter.
5. Test the forcemeat by frying a small ball to verify it is sufficiently seasoned and has the right consistency.
6. Heat a frying pan slowly and brown the butter in it.
7. Form the forcemeat with two wooden spoons, dipped in warm water, into small balls, which are browned nicely in the butter and placed in a pot.
8. Whisk the frying pan with the broth and pour the sauce over the meatballs, which then cook covered 10–15 minutes.
9. Place on warm carrot, whisk up the sauce, taste and adjust seasoning, and pour over the balls.
10. The sauce may also be finished as in recipe n:o 429.
