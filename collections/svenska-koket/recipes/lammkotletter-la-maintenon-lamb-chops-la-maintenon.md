## Ingredients

- 6 large lamb chops
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 hg (approximately 50 g) cooked veal or chicken
- 30 g salted ox tongue
- 2 truffles
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 g)
- 1 teaspoon chopped shallot
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 g)
- 2 deciliters stock
- 1 egg yolk
- salt, white pepper
- 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
- 1 egg yolk for brushing
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g) for frying
- 1 1/2 deciliters meat jus or stock

## Directions

1. Lamb chops should be cut with long bones and be approximately 2 cm thick.
2. Dry them with a cloth wrung out in hot water, trim, pound on both sides and rub in with the spice mixture.
3. Cut the meat, tongue and truffle into small, even dice.
4. Fry the butter and onion with the flour for 2 minutes, add the stock and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes.
5. Stir in the beaten egg yolk together with the meat and mushroom, season the stuffing with the spices and parsley and let it cool.
6. Fry the chops 4 minutes on one side in half of the butter.
7. Lay them on a cutting board with the fried side up.
8. Divide the stuffing into 6 equal parts and spread it out on the fried side of the chop and brush with the beaten egg yolk.
9. Fry the remaining butter in the frying pan, place the chops in with the stuffing side up and gratinee in the oven for approximately 8 minutes.
10. Place on a hot serving dish, whisk the pan with the meat jus, let the sauce come to a boil, season with 1 teaspoon cold butter and pour over the chops.
11. Slip paper frills on the bones.
12. Garnish with peas or French potatoes and parsley and serve as a breakfast or side dish.
