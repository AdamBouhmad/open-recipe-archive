## Ingredients

- 7 hg. saithe (coalfish)
- 250 gr. scraped fish meat
- 80 gr. butter
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 2 eggs
- 1 1/2 tbsp. potato flour (23 gr.)
- 4-5 dcl. good milk
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 2 tsp. sugar (10 gr.)
- mace (optional)
- 1 1/2 lit. fish broth for cooking

## Directions

1. Clean the saithe well and dry it. Cut off the head, remove the gills; fillet the fish and remove the backbone.
2. Scrape all meat from the bone and scrape the remaining fish meat finely. Weigh the meat, which should amount to 250 gr.
3. Make broth from the head, bones and skin, in which the balls will later be cooked.
4. Mix the fish meat with the butter and pass 5 times through the meat grinder. Work the mixture in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle together with the salt.
5. Mix in the beaten eggs and flour and work the mixture again until it becomes fine and cohesive.
6. Work the forcemeat with a spoon for 3/4 hour, gradually adding the milk in tablespoonful amounts.
7. Season with salt, sugar, and if desired a little mace. Form with 2 tablespoons dipped in lukewarm water into small round balls.
8. Cook the balls in the fish broth for 3-4 minutes. Test the forcemeat first by cooking a small sample; if it is too firm, add a little more milk, otherwise add 1 egg yolk.
9. Serve the fish balls in crayfish sauce or in regular fish sauce made from butter, flour, fish stock and 1-2 egg yolks.
