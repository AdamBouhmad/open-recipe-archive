## Ingredients

- 2 kg. lamb roast
- 1 bunch parsley
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.)
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/4 tsk. white pepper
- 3 msk. butter for roasting (60 gr.)
- 1/2 lit. broth
- 2 dcl. cream

## Directions

1. The roast is treated as in no. 531 and the shank is cut off.
2. The parsley is cleaned, rinsed, wrung in a cloth, chopped coarsely and worked into the butter, which is then allowed to harden in a cold place.
3. With a larding pin or sharp knife, holes are made in the roast at regular intervals, and small pieces of the parsley butter are inserted therein.
4. A roasting pot is heated, the butter is browned therein; the roast is placed in and browned well on all sides, sprinkled with the seasonings, diluted with the boiling broth and cream and is allowed to roast slowly with a tightly closed lid until tender, or approximately 2 1/2—3 hours, during which it is frequently basted and turned 3—4 times.
5. Served with vegetables or with boiled potatoes, cucumber and salads as well as reduced sauce (see no. 429).
6. The meat can also be roasted in the oven (see no. 531), but then it is only diluted with broth.
