## Ingredients

- 2 cock pheasants
- 2 hg. lard
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 gr.)
- For sauce: the butter in which the birds were browned
- 4 tbsp. flour (40 gr.)
- 5 dcl. meat broth or stock
- 5 dcl. thick cream
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper

## Directions

1. The cock pheasant's meat is considered finer than hen and is therefore preferred for roasting. The plumage is magnificent and used by many for decoration. The bird should hang for a few days before preparation so that the meat becomes juicy and obtains the fine flavor of game which is lacking in the freshly slaughtered bird. When it is to be cleaned, first save the head with the neck, wings and tail. The bird is then plucked and treated as stated in the introduction to poultry, larded on the breast and legs with finely cut strips of lard and tied. The birds are then browned in the butter together with the giblets in a roasting pan which must not be so large that they do not just fit. When the birds are well browned, they are removed together with the giblets. The butter in the pan is fried with the flour for 2 minutes, the broth and cream are added and the sauce is allowed to boil for 5 minutes, after which it is seasoned with the spices. The birds are placed in with the breast up and allowed to roast slowly until tender, or 1 to 1 1/2 hours.
