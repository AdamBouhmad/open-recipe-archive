## Ingredients

- 5 hg. boneless veal loin
- 1 hg. dry, cleaned suet from beef or 1 hg. lard
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 1/2 tbsp. flour (15 gr.)
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 5-6 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, free it from sinews and membranes, cut it into dice and grind it 3 times through a meat grinder.
2. Chop the suet finely with a knife on a cutting board, mix it together with the seasonings with the veal forcemeat, which is ground an additional 3 times through the grinder.
3. Pound the forcemeat thereafter together with the flour in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until it becomes even and smooth, then add the egg and egg yolks, one at a time, with continued vigorous pounding.
4. Pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve and let it stand in a cold place or on ice for several hours, or preferably until the following day.
5. Then work it vigorously with a spoon while the cream is added a little at a time, test it as in recipe 628, and add, if needed, a little egg white or cream.
6. The forcemeat can, if desired, be flavored with a little grated onion, finely chopped mushrooms or truffle, and the same amount of butter can be used instead of suet or lard.
7. Veal forcemeat can also be prepared as in recipes 628, 629 and 630.
8. Cook in a water bath or braise in an oven.
9. Forcemeat of pork can be prepared in the same way.
