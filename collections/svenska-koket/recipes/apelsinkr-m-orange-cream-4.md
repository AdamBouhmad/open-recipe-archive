## Ingredients

- juice and peel of 2 oranges
- 150 grams crushed sugar
- 1 egg, 3 egg yolks
- 1 deciliter milk
- 1 tablespoon sherry
- 2 grams citric acid
- 3 deciliters thick cream
- 5 leaves gelatin, 3 tablespoons water
- For garnish: 1 orange, 1/2 hectogram biscuits

## Directions

1. Wash the oranges well, dry them, and scrape the peel against the sugar, which is placed in a well-scoured saucepan.
2. Press out the orange juice and strain it over the sugar; add the egg, egg yolks and milk, and whisk the mixture until the sugar is melted, then let it simmer over the fire with vigorous whisking until the cream is thick and fluffy.
3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and continue whisking until the cream is cold, then add the sherry and the crushed citric acid.
4. Rinse the gelatin, cut into pieces and dissolve in 3 tablespoons warm water.
5. Whip the cream until stiff peaks form.
6. Add the gelatin to the cream while whisking and then the cream, very carefully.
7. Rinse a nice tin form with cold water and sprinkle with granulated sugar, pour in the mixture and let it stand on ice for 2-3 hours.
8. When the cream is to be served, loosen it from the edge with a pointed knife, turn it out onto a flat glass dish with paper and garnish with candied orange slices and biscuits.
