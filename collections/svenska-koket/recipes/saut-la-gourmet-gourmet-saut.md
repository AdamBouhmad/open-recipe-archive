## Ingredients

- 1 calf kidney
- 1 liter water for blanching
- 1 teaspoon salt for blanching
- 1 pork fillet
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 1/2 liter raw potato
- 2 deciliters brown broth
- 2 tablespoons Madeira
- 1 hectogram butter for the form
- 2 tablespoons crushed rusks for the form
- 2 egg yolks for mayonnaise
- 1/2 teaspoon salt for mayonnaise
- cayenne for mayonnaise
- 1/4 teaspoon French mustard
- 2 deciliters fine cooking oil
- 1 tablespoon tarragon vinegar
- 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon hot water

## Directions

1. Blanch the kidney as in the previous recipe. Cut it into slices and season.
2. Dry the pork fillet with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into thin slices, flatten them out and rub with the spices.
3. Peel and cut the potatoes into slices. Peel and chop the onion finely.
4. Butter a fireproof form with some of the butter. On the bottom of the form lay a layer of potatoes, sprinkle a little salt over it, then a layer of fillet and kidney slices. Continue until there are 3 layers of potatoes and 2 layers of meat.
5. Sprinkle the onion over each meat layer, and place the remaining butter in flakes on top.
6. Add the broth and sprinkle the crushed rusks over it.
7. Place the dish in ordinary oven heat to cook for about 1 1/4 hours. A quarter of an hour before it is done, pour in the wine.
8. Serve the sauté as a breakfast dish.
