## Ingredients

- 750 gr. beef tenderloin or boneless sirloin
- 2 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 1 dcl. boiling broth or water
- 1 small bunch parsley for garnish
- 1 1/2 tbsp. butter (30 gr.) for garnish

## Directions

1. The meat, which should have hung for 2-6 days depending on the season, is dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into approximately 1 cm. thick slices, which are lightly pounded on both sides and sprinkled with the spices.
2. Heat a frying pan until it becomes smoking hot, brown the butter, add the steaks and fry them quickly on both sides.
3. Place them on a warm serving dish, whisk out the pan with the boiling broth or water and pour the sauce over the dish.
4. Garnish the steaks with parsley that has been cleaned, rinsed and well browned in butter.
5. Serve immediately with newly boiled or fried potatoes as a breakfast course.
