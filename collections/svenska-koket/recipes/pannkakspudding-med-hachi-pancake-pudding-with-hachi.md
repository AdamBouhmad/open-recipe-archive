## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon butter for hachi
- 1 1/2 tablespoons flour for hachi
- 4 cups broth for hachi
- white pepper for hachi
- 4 cups cooked or fried minced meat for hachi
- 1 egg yolk for hachi
- 2 1/2 cups flour for pancake batter
- 1/2 teaspoon salt for pancake batter
- 2 eggs for pancake batter
- 6 cups unskimmed milk for pancake batter
- 2 tablespoons butter for pancake batter
- salt for pancake batter
- 2 eggs for egg custard
- 2 cups milk for egg custard
- salt for egg custard
- sugar for egg custard

## Directions

1. Melt butter and flour together, thin with broth, and let the sauce cook for a few minutes. Add the meat, bring the stew to a boil and season. Lastly stir in the egg yolk.
2. Prepare the pancake batter in the usual way, and make thin pancakes fried on only one side. Place them with the unfried side up on a deep plate or in a fireproof mold.
3. Spread a moderately thick layer of hachi on each pancake; place the next pancake on top with the fried side up. Continue until the mold is full.
4. Pour the egg custard over the pancakes, and place the pudding in the oven to set and turn a beautiful light golden color.
5. If desired, the egg custard can be omitted. Serve the pudding with meat gravy or clarified butter.
