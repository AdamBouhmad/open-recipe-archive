## Ingredients

- 2 chickens, approximately 3 kg each
- 1 large bunch parsley
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in 2 slices
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g) for roasting
- 3/4 liter broth
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)

## Directions

1. The chickens, which should have hung 2-6 days depending on the season, are plucked, singed, and cleaned (see page 299 onwards), left to soak in water for 6 hours, and dried thoroughly.
2. The parsley is cleaned, rinsed, wrapped in cloth, and worked into the butter.
3. The birds are filled with the parsley butter and sewn shut, after which they are covered with lard slices, which are fastened with fine sail thread.
4. The butter is browned in a roasting pan, the chickens are placed in breast-side up, browned well on all sides, salted, diluted with the boiling broth, and roasted with the lid tightly closed until tender, approximately 2-3 hours, during which time they are basted every 10 minutes.
5. Each chicken is cut into 6 pieces, arranged on the platter so that they appear whole, garnished with salad and parsley, and served with browned potatoes, salads, and strained sauce with added cream (see recipe 429).
6. If desired, the birds may be diluted with equal parts cream and broth.
