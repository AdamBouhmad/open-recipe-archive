## Ingredients

- 1 kg beef outside round
- 1 1/2 kg beef ribs
- 3 1/2 liters water
- 10 grams salt
- 1 carrot
- 1 parsnip
- 1/4 celeriac
- 1 small leek
- 1 small red onion
- 1 clove
- 3 hectograms lean, boneless beef
- 1 egg white
- 1 rinsed eggshell

## Directions

1. The broth should preferably be cooked the day before it is to be used.
2. Prepare the meat and cook the broth as in the preceding recipe.
3. If desired, add the peeled red onion with a clove inserted into it at the same time as the vegetables.
4. The meat used for clarification should be dried with a cloth, wrung out in cold water, cut into small dice and pounded together with the egg white and rinsed eggshell in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until a fine mass is obtained, which is placed in a soup pot with a thick bottom.
5. Carefully remove the fat cake from the broth, then pour it into the pot, but the sediment at the bottom must not come along.
6. Place the pot over the fire and let the broth come to a boil and boil for 5 minutes with frequent stirring at the bottom.
7. Then set the pot to the side of the stove, cover it and let the broth stand for 1 hour without boiling, but keep it hot.
8. Strain it through a flannel sieve wrung out in hot water and measure it.
9. It can be brought to a boil; if it is more than 1 1/2 liters, the broth can be boiled down until the desired amount is obtained.
10. Season the broth with salt and cayenne and serve with various kinds of garnish, such as strips of tongue, truffle and mushrooms, asparagus tips, vegetables, egg royal, quenelles of fish, veal or chicken forcemeat, cheese fritters, cheese sticks, pastries, croutons, vermicelli, dumplings, etc.
11. If the broth is to be used for reduced soups or purées, 2 liters of water can be taken per 1 kg of meat; the broth should then not be boiled down.
