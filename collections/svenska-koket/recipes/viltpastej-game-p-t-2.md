## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg. leftover, scraped poultry
- 100 g lard
- 3 eggs
- 1 tbsp. flour (10 g)
- 3-4 dcl. thick cream
- salt, sugar, white pepper
- 1 1/2 tbsp. butter
- 2 tbsp. finely chopped truffle

## Directions

1. Grind the scraped poultry together with the lard 5 times through a meat grinder and then pound with a wooden pestle in a stone mortar until a smooth and pliable forcemeat is achieved.
2. Whisk the egg yolks with the flour and cream and add this mixture spoonful by spoonful to the forcemeat while working it for about 1 hour.
3. Season the forcemeat carefully with the spices and lastly fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
4. Grease a timbale mold with butter and sprinkle with finely chopped truffle, pour in the forcemeat and cook slowly in a water bath for about 2 hours.
5. Turn it out onto a round platter, garnish with peas and browned potatoes and serve with game or truffle sauce as well as lingonberry jelly.
