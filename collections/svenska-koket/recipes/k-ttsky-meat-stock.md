## Ingredients

- cheap cuts of beef such as neck and legs
- raw or roasted remnants of veal, lamb, pork, chicken and game
- bones and sinews, all well chopped
- butter or lard for browning
- 2 peeled and cut onions
- 2 carrots, cleaned and cut into slices
- 1 celery, cleaned and cut into slices
- several white peppercorns
- meat broth or water to cover the meat

## Directions

1. In an iron pot, brown a generous amount of butter or lard
2. Add the meat and bones and brown them well together with the onions, carrots, celery, and white peppercorns
3. Pour in enough meat broth or water so that it is level with the meat
4. Skim well and simmer slowly for 5-6 hours
5. Strain the stock through a fine sieve or cloth and boil down further if desired
6. Let it stand until the following day so that the fat solidifies
7. When the stock is to be used, remove the solidified fat cake
8. If not all stock is used at once, the remainder should be boiled up with the fat, which may solidify again
9. If the fat cake lies undisturbed over the stock, it can be kept several weeks without spoiling
