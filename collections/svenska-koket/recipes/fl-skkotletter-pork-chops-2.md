## Ingredients

- 6 large pork chops
- 1/2 lemon
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 gr.)
- 2 decilitres finely crushed rusks
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped parsley
- 2 1/2 tablespoons butter for frying (50 gr.)
- 2 decilitres broth

## Directions

1. If the chops are small, allow 2 per person for a meal.
2. Dry the chop rack with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut so that a bone follows with each chop, which is then lightly pounded, trimmed and shaped.
3. Sprinkle the chops with lemon juice, rub in the seasonings and let them lie on top of each other for 1/4 hour.
4. Brush with butter and turn in a mixture of the crushed rusks and parsley.
5. Brown the butter in a frying pan, place the chops in and let them fry slowly without a lid for about 7 minutes on each side.
6. Place on a warm dish, deglaze the pan with boiling broth, and pour the sauce onto the dish.
7. Garnish with parsley or salad and serve with vegetables, boiled or fried potatoes or mashed potatoes.
8. The chops can also be fried without breading, but should then be browned on both sides, thinned with broth or cream and subsequently cooked through with a lid, otherwise they will become dry.
