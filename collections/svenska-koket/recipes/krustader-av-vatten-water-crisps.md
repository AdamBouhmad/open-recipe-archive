## Ingredients

- 2 hg flour
- 3 dcl water
- 1-2 egg yolks
- 1/2 tsk salt
- 1/2 tsk sugar
- 2 tsk cooking oil
- 3/4 litre cooking oil or 3/4 kg lard for frying

## Directions

1. Sift the flour into a bowl, add water, egg yolk and seasonings while whisking with a steel whisk.
2. When the batter is even and smooth, pour in the cooking oil.
3. The pan in which the crisps are to be cooked should be deep but not wider than a teacup. At the same time as the oil is poured into the pan, place the iron mold in it to heat; otherwise the batter will not stick to the iron.
4. Pour the batter into as many spoonfuls as there are forms on the iron. When the oil is hot, dip the iron into the batter, but not all the way to the edge, and hold it first for a minute over the heat so that the crisps set slightly, otherwise they will be blistery.
5. Hold the iron thereafter in the hot oil until the crisps have turned golden brown, loosen them with a larding needle and lay them upside down on gray paper to drain.
6. Fill them immediately with warm stew and serve as a side dish or as garnish for fish and meat dishes.
7. The crisps can be stored 2-3 weeks if placed in a tin with a tightly closed lid, but must then be heated in the oven before the filling is added.
8. Crisps can be filled with stewed poultry and mushrooms, calf sweetbreads and truffles, asparagus, crayfish, lobster, shrimp and fish, oysters, and with vegetables and purées.
