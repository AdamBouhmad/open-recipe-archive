## Ingredients

- 2 kg. short-chopped saddle
- 1 1/2 hg. lard
- 1/2 tbsp. salt (8 gr.)
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 3 dcl. broth
- 3 dcl. cream

## Directions

1. With a sharp knife, remove all membranes on the back without any meat being included.
2. Lard the saddle with finely shredded lard strips, dipped in a mixture of salt and pepper.
3. Place in a long pan, cover with butter distributed in flakes, set in a warm oven and let stand 15 minutes.
4. Then add the boiling broth and cream and let roast until tender, or about 1 1/2 hours, basting every 10 minutes, otherwise it will dry out.
5. Cut the saddle as mutton saddle, garnish with browned potatoes, small tarts filled with lingonberry jelly, or with vegetable salad in small shells of raw cabbage leaves.
6. Serve with reduced sauce (see no. 429), added with 1 tbsp. lingonberry jelly.
7. From the saddle, cutlets can be cut out, which are fried as veal cutlets, with or without breading.
8. Serve with stewed mushrooms or champignons, potato croquettes or chestnut purée.
