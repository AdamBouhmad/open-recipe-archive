## Ingredients

- 3 Dover soles, approximately 4 hg. each
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g.)
- Fish farce: 4 hg. pike, 150 g. scraped fish meat, 75 g. butter, 10 g. flour, 1½–2 dcl. cream, 1 egg, 2 tsp. salt, ½ tsp. sugar, ¼ tsp. white pepper, 1 truffle
- For cooking: 1½ tbsp. butter (30 g.), fish stock
- For garnish: hollandaise or crayfish sauce, 2 truffles, parsley, 12 crayfish or shrimp tails

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the Dover soles as in recipe no. 371.
2. Pound the fillets with a moistened meat mallet, rub with salt and let lie on top of each other for a couple of hours.
3. Prepare the fish farce as in recipe no. 356 and mix in the truffles cut into fine strips.
4. Dry the fillets again with a fish towel, trim them and cover on the inside with a thin layer of fish farce.
5. Roll them up with the farce side inward and place them tightly next to each other in a low saucepan with a flat bottom, buttered with the cold butter. (The saucepan should not be wider than to have the roulades completely fill it, otherwise they will lose their shape during cooking.)
6. Smooth the farce that protrudes at the upper end with a spoon dipped in warm water.
7. Add enough fish stock (made from heads, bones and skin) so it just reaches over the roulades, place the lid on the saucepan and set it in a regular oven temperature, and let the roulades cook until done for approximately 20–25 minutes.
8. Test with a skewer to see if they are cooked through.
9. Lift out with a slotted spoon onto a warm plate and let them drain.
10. Place the sauce on a round plate, preferably of silver, and arrange the roulades in a ring on it.
11. Garnish with truffles cut into thin slices or strips, parsley and crayfish or shrimp tails.
12. Serve with small boiled potatoes.
