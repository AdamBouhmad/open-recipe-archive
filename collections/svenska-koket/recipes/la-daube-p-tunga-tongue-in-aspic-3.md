## Ingredients

- 1 large calf tongue
- 1 1/4 liters water
- 2 tsp meat extract
- 1 1/2 tbsp salt
- 5 white peppercorns
- 3 allspice berries
- 6 pitted preserved olives
- 8 dcl stock
- 8 leaves gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tbsp cold water
- 2 tbsp good Madeira wine
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 1 truffle
- parsley

## Directions

1. Soak the tongue in water for 12 hours, then blanch in hot water and scrape well.
2. Place it in cold water with the dissolved meat extract and skim well when it comes to a boil; add salt and pepper.
3. Let the tongue cook until tender, about 1 1/2 hours, which is tested by piercing through it with a fine whisk.
4. Remove it, peel off the skin immediately and let the tongue cool.
5. Strain the stock, remove fat, taste and prepare the aspic as in recipe no. 181; when cold add the wine.
6. Pour a little of the clear aspic into the bottom of a water-rinsed à-la-daube mold and place on ice to set.
7. Garnish the bottom with the hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into beautiful shapes, and parsley.
8. Pour a few drops of aspic over the garnish and let it set so that the garnish does not shift when the tongue is placed in.
9. Cut the olives into slices; the tongue likewise.
10. Place an olive slice on each slice of tongue, roll these up and layer in the mold with the remaining aspic, then set in a cold place.
