## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 dcl. rice groats (150 gr.)
- 1/2 lit. water
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 3-4 dcl. thick cream
- 3 1/2 hg. salted salmon
- 1 msk. finely chopped red onion
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.)
- 3 hard-boiled eggs
- 150 gr. flour
- 1 dcl. water (scant)
- 2 hg. butter
- 1 egg white for brushing

## Directions

1. Rinse the rice groats and blanch in hot water, then place in boiling salted water, thin with the thick cream and allow to cook until soft, then pour out and let cool.
2. Rinse the salmon, cut into slices and let soak in 1 lit. skimmed milk for approximately 3 hours, take out, wrap in a cloth or white paper and cut into cubes.
3. Fry the onion in the butter. Chop the eggs finely.
4. Prepare the pastry dough as in no. 33 and roll out to a 1 1/4 cm. thick cake.
5. From an ovenproof dish on which the pirog shall be baked, take a measure. This is done most easily by cutting a pattern of paper and thereafter cutting out the dough with a pastry wheel.
6. Place the dough base on the dish and a 3 cm. wide strip of dough is placed in a ring on top of the base and fastened with egg white.
7. Mix the rice groats with the salmon, onion and eggs and place inside the ring.
8. Cover the pirog with a pastry dough lid of the same size as the base, and fasten the lid to it with egg white.
9. Garnish the lid in squares with narrow pastry dough strips and in each square make a small cut with a fork so that the dough rises and the dish gets a neat appearance.
10. Brush the pirog with egg white, which must not drip down the sides, because it prevents the pastry dough from rising.
11. Bake in a warm oven.
