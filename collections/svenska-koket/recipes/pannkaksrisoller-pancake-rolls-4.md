## Ingredients

- For thin pancakes: 3 eggs
- 6 dcl. milk
- 90-100 g. flour
- 1 tsp. salt (5 g.)
- For the pan: 2 tbsp. butter (40 g.)
- For the stew: 3 hg. boneless, cooked meat
- 1 can mushrooms (1/4 liter)
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 g.)
- 4 tbsp. flour (40 g.)
- 4 dcl. broth
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 dcl. thick cream
- salt
- white pepper
- For frying: 2 dcl. finely crushed hardtack
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 g.)

## Directions

1. For the pancake batter, whisk the ingredients well with a steel whisk, then let the batter rest 1 hour to swell.
2. Cut the meat into fine cubes. Cut the mushrooms likewise into cubes and sauté in butter until clear, then press them up with a spoon.
3. Sauté the flour with the butter 2 minutes, add the broth and let the sauce simmer 5 minutes.
4. Whisk the egg yolk with the cream and add it to the sauce, then let it simmer.
5. Add the meat and mushroom and season the stew with spices.
6. Heat a pancake pan slowly, melt the butter in a water bath and brush the pan with it.
7. Make thin pancakes from the batter that are only browned on one side.
8. Lay them on a cutting board; sprinkled with finely crushed hardtack, with the browned side up.
9. Cut the pancakes in half, place 1 tbsp. of the stew on each half and fold the pancake well so it takes the form of an oblong croquette.
10. Turn the risolles carefully in crushed hardtack and fry them nicely brown in butter.
11. Lay them on a hot plate, garnish with parsley and serve with brown mushroom sauce or clarified butter. Small pancakes can be filled and fried the same way and served at the smorgasbord.
