## Ingredients

- 2 hg. smoked ham
- 3 hg. boneless veal
- 1/4 tsk. white pepper
- 2 hg. bread crumbs
- 2 whole eggs, 2 egg yolks
- 3-4 dcl. thick cream
- 1/2 msk. finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 1/2 msk. butter (10 gr.) for the onion
- 1 egg white for brushing
- 2 msk. crushed rusks for brushing
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.) for frying
- 1/2 liter boiling broth or water with 1/2 tsk. solid meat extract

## Directions

1. Cut the ham and meat into cubes, pass 6 times through a meat grinder and pound together with the pepper using a wooden pestle in a stone mortar
2. Add the bread crumbs (see no. 626) and pound the forcemeat further until it becomes even and smooth
3. Place the forcemeat in a bowl, add the beaten eggs and egg yolks little by little while stirring constantly, then pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve, place on ice and stir vigorously for about 1/2 hour while adding the cream by spoonfuls
4. Test the forcemeat (see Färs, page 316); if it is too firm, add a little more cream, otherwise a little egg white
5. If desired, add last the onion fried in butter
6. Grease a long pan with cold butter, place the forcemeat in it and cook in a water bath for about 2 hours or bake in the oven and serve warm or cold with vegetables
7. It can also be layered in a mold alternately with veal, chicken, game, or poultry forcemeat, cooked in a water bath and served cold in aspic as a more refined side dish
