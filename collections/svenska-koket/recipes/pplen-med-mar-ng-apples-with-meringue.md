## Ingredients

- 10-12 loose, tart apples
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 grams)
- 1 1/2-2 decilitres caster sugar (128-170 grams)
- 1/2 tablespoon butter for the mold (10 grams)
- 3 egg whites
- 105 grams icing sugar

## Directions

1. The apples are washed, dried, peeled, cut into thin slices or dice and freed from the cores.
2. The butter is melted in a saucepan, the apple slices are added together with the sugar and shaken over heat until they are completely soft.
3. They are then placed in a buttered, ovenproof mold and allowed to cool.
4. For the meringue mixture, the egg whites are whipped until stiff peaks form, the icing sugar, which should be sifted, is folded in a little at a time into the egg white foam, and half of the mixture is spread over the apples.
5. The rest of the meringue mixture is piped through a piping bag in diagonal squares over the meringue.
6. The dish is baked in gentle oven heat until the meringue is half dry and has a pale yellow colour. It is then removed, allowed to cool and garnished in each square with apple or red currant jelly.
7. Serve with whipped cream.
