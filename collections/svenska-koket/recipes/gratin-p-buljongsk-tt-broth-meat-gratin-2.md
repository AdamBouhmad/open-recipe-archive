## Ingredients

- 400 g boneless broth meat
- 1–2 red onions
- 4 msk butter (80 g)
- 2 tsk salt (10 g)
- 1/4 tsk white pepper
- 3 dcl meat sauce or broth
- 1 liter potatoes
- 1 msk butter (20 g)
- 1 1/2–2 dcl boiling milk
- 2 eggs
- salt, sugar
- 1 msk finely crushed rusks
- 1–2 msk grated cheese
- 2 msk melted butter (20 g)
- 1/2 msk butter for the dish (10 g)

## Directions

1. Chop the meat finely (do not grind).
2. Peel, chop, and fry the onion in butter; add the meat and fry well, sprinkle with the seasonings and thin with the boiling meat sauce or broth.
3. Peel the potatoes raw, cut into pieces, boil quickly, drain well, and press through a purée press.
4. Put the purée in a saucepan, add the cold butter immediately and work the purée with a fork; thin with the boiling milk, add the beaten egg yolks, and stir until white and fluffy.
5. Season with salt and sugar and lastly fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
6. Butter a fireproof dish or pie form, spread the meat in it, spread the potato purée over the meat, sprinkle with the rusks mixed with cheese, and finally pour over the melted butter.
7. Place the dish in a hot oven and gratinee for 20–30 minutes.
8. Serve with meat gravy or melted butter. If desired, the meat may be fried in cream instead of butter; in the potato purée the butter and eggs may be omitted.
