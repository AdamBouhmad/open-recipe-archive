## Ingredients

- 1 lightly salted ham, approximately 5 kg
- 1 tablespoon egg white
- 1 teaspoon dry mustard
- 2 teaspoons sugar
- 2 tablespoons finely crushed rusks

## Directions

1. The ham, which should have lain in brine (see Salting of Meat) for 3-4 weeks, is rinsed and left to soak in plenty of cold water for about 12 hours, so that the salt is drawn out.
2. Place it with rind side up in enough cold water to cover it well.
3. Let the water come to a boil quickly, skim and let it boil for 15 minutes at 100°.
4. Cool the liquid thereafter to 82°-83°, and at this temperature let the ham lie for about 3 1/2 hours, or until it is tender, which is tested with a whisk (see page 233).
5. Place the ham in a deep dish, pour the liquid over, and let the ham cool in it. (In summer the ham should be cooled in cold water, as the liquid then easily becomes sour.)
6. Remove the rind; press a towel against the lard to remove all dissolved fat.
7. Beat the egg white with a fork into a light foam, mix with mustard and sugar, and with this brush the ham's lard side.
8. Sprinkle the crushed rusks over, place the ham in a roasting pan and put in a warm oven to get a beautiful brown color, which takes about 1/2 hour.
9. Place on a large platter, garnish beautifully with greens and serve warm or cold with vegetables, prunes and filled apple halves. The ham bone is garnished with curled paper.
