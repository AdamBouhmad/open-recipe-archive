## Ingredients

- 1 kg boneless beef sirloin
- 100 g lard
- 1 tbsp finely chopped onion
- 1 tbsp finely chopped parsley
- 2 tsp anchovy sauce
- 2 tbsp vinegar
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 tbsp white wine or Madeira
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- 1 tsp anchovy sauce
- 3 tbsp butter
- 2 tbsp flour
- 6 dl stock or water
- 2 tbsp red or white wine

## Directions

1. Prepare the meat as in the previous recipe, cut into 12 slices, beat them well, place on a platter, pour the marinade over them and let them rest 1 hour, turning them once.
2. Mix all ingredients for the marinade and add, if desired, 1/2 tbsp finely chopped onion.
3. Cut the lard into 12 strips, which are rolled in a mixture of the onion, parsley and anchovy sauce.
4. Wrap the meat slices in cloth, and on each one place a strip of lard, then roll the meat slices up tightly and bind with fine sailcloth yarn or fasten together with toothpicks.
5. Heat a frying pan, brown the butter in it, add the rolls and brown them nicely all around, then place them in a warm iron pot.
6. Fry the flour with the butter in the frying pan, add the boiling stock or water together with the wine, and let the sauce come to a good boil and pour it over the rolls.
7. Let them thereafter slowly cook with a tightly closed lid until tender, or about 2-2 1/2 hours.
8. Place them on warm carrots, taste the sauce and pour it over.
9. Serve with pressed potatoes or potato puree.
