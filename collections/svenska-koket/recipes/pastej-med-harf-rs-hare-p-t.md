## Ingredients

- 250 gr. flour
- 2 dcl. water (scarcely)
- 3 hg. butter
- 1 young hare
- 50 gr. butter
- 8 dcl. thick cream
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 egg white
- 2 tsp. salt
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 6–7 truffles

## Directions

1. Prepare the puff pastry as in no. 34.
2. Skin and gut the hare; remove the head, paws and side ribs, then rinse the hare well and dry it. Remove all membranes and cut the hare into pieces. Cut the meat from the bones and scrape it. Pass it 8 times through the meat grinder, the last 3 times together with the butter.
3. Place the force meat in a bowl and stir for approximately 1 hour, during which time add the cream bit by bit. Pass the force meat through a hair sieve, then stir in the egg yolks, egg white and truffle cut into strips, as well as a little of the truffle liquid.
4. Season the force meat with the spices and cook a small test portion in salted water. If the force meat is too firm, add a little more cream, otherwise add a beaten egg.
5. Roll out the puff pastry to approximately 1/2 cm. thickness and line a tart form with a removable ring. The pastry should hang 2 cm. over the edge of the form. Place the force meat inside, fold the pastry over and brush the edge with egg white. Place a lid of pastry over the force meat. Brush the lid with egg white and garnish with the rest of the pastry cut into beautiful figures. Around the edge place a strip of pastry which is fluted neatly.
6. Bake the pâté in good oven heat for approximately 1 1/2 hours. After 10 minutes remove the tart ring and serve the pâté as a course, warm or cold, with gravy made from the hare bones.
