## Ingredients

- 750 gr. beef filet
- 1 hg. beef marrow
- 2 tbsp. chopped shallot onion
- 1/2 tbsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 gr.)
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 gr.) for sauce
- 2 tsp. chopped shallot onion for sauce
- 1/2 dcl. meat gravy
- 1 1/2 dcl. good beef broth
- 1 tbsp. white wine
- paprika
- 1 tbsp. finely chopped parsley
- 1 tbsp. beef marrow in fine dice

## Directions

1. Prepare the sauce first: brown the butter, add the onion and brown nicely, add the meat gravy, broth and wine, bring to a good boil and pass through a sieve. Let the sauce boil again, season with a little paprika, and finally stir in the parsley and beef marrow.
2. Dry the filet with a cloth wrung out in hot water. Remove all outer sinews and membranes carefully, then cut the filet into 6 slices, which are flattened slightly.
3. Make an incision from the side in each slice, so that a small opening or pocket is formed.
4. The marrow, which should have been lying in water for 1 hour to draw out the blood, is blanched, i.e. placed in cold, salted water and boiled 2-3 min. without a lid and allowed to drain on a sieve and cool.
5. Cut it into 7 slices (one of them is cut into fine dice and placed in the sauce), which are brushed with melted butter and sprinkled with the chopped onion fried in a little butter.
6. Place the slices into the opening made in the patty, which is pressed shut.
7. Rub the patties on both sides with the spice mixture and brush with a little butter.
8. Braise or fry in butter 6-8 min., taking care that the openings are placed against the pan handle and this is lifted slightly during frying, so that the marrow does not flow out.
9. Place on a warm plate, pour sauce over and serve the dish with small fried potatoes, sprinkled with finely chopped parsley.
