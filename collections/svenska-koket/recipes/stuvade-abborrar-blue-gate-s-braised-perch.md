## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg beautiful, even perch
- 1 tbsp salt (15 g)
- 180 g butter
- 4 tbsp coarsely chopped parsley
- 1 tbsp chopped fresh dill
- 2 tbsp flour (20 g)
- 1 dcl water

## Directions

1. Scale the perch, gut them (save the roe), rinse very thoroughly, dry with fish towel and clip off the fins.
2. Rub them inside and outside with salt and let them lie for 1 hour, then dry them again.
3. Line a pot with some of the butter, place the perch in it tightly next to each other with the backs up and between each layer place the remaining butter, distributed in flakes, the parsley, the dill and the flour.
4. Add the water and let the fish simmer slowly with the lid tightly closed for 15-20 minutes; during this time shake the pot now and then so that the fish does not burn.
5. Place on a warm serving dish with all heads to one side, whip up the sauce, taste it and pour over the fish, which is garnished with the roe, which has been cooked in salted water and cut into slices.
6. Serve with boiled potatoes. — The perch can also be cut into fillets and the skin removed (then cook stock on the head, bones and skin, which is used for the braising), otherwise arrange and serve the same way.
