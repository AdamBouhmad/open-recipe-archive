## Ingredients

- 6 hectograms raw fish, or 5 deciliters finely chopped fish
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 grams)
- 3 deciliters fish stock
- 2 tablespoons salt
- 1 teaspoon vinegar
- 1 liter water
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 grams)
- 2 deciliters boiling milk
- salt, white pepper
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter for shells
- 2 tablespoons crushed rusks
- 1 tablespoon grated cheese

## Directions

1. Clean and rinse the fish well and dry with a fish towel
2. Rub with some of the salt and vinegar and let sit for a while
3. Place in hot water with the remaining salt and simmer slowly until done, about 10-15 minutes
4. Remove immediately from the stock, which is saved
5. Remove the skin, remove all bones and cut the fish meat into fine cubes
6. Fry the butter and flour for 2 minutes
7. Add the fish stock and milk and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes
8. Season with spices and lemon juice and bind with the egg yolk
9. Gently blend the fish into the sauce
10. Place the mixture in 6 buttered shells
11. Sprinkle with a mixture of rusks and cheese and drizzle with a little clarified butter
12. Place the shells in a baking pan with coarse salt
13. Set in a warm oven and gratinée for 10-15 minutes
14. Serve as a breakfast or intermediate course
