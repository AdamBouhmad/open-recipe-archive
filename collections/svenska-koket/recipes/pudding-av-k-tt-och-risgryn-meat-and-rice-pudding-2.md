## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg. boneless fried or cooked meat
- 1–2 red onions
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 g.)
- 2 tsp. salt (10 g.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 3–4 dcl. meat gravy or good broth
- 1 1/2 dcl. rice (150 g.)
- 1 1/4 lit. milk
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 g.)
- 2 eggs
- 1 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 g.) for the mold
- 2 tbsp. crushed breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Cut the meat into thin slices or chop it finely (do not grind). Peel the onion, chop it and brown it in the butter in a frying pan; add the meat and brown well, season and mix with the boiling gravy or broth.
2. Rinse the rice with cold water, scald it in hot water, add it to the boiling milk and let it cook until soft; then pour it out, immediately mix it with the cold butter and let it cool.
3. Add the beaten egg yolks along with the salt, and finally fold in the stiffly beaten egg whites.
4. Butter a pie mold and sprinkle it with crushed breadcrumbs. Layer the meat and rice alternately in the mold, with the top layer being rice sprinkled with crushed breadcrumbs.
5. Bake the pudding in the oven for approximately 30–40 minutes.
6. Serve with meat gravy or clarified butter.
7. Instead of onion, finely chopped pickled or preserved cucumber can be used. The pudding can be made simpler by using cream instead of butter and omitting the eggs in the rice.
