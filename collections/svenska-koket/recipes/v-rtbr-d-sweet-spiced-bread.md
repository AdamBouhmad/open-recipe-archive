## Ingredients

- 6 liters good spiced broth (vört)
- 1/2 liter unskimmed milk
- 120 grams pressed yeast
- 2 parts syrup
- 5 teaspoons crushed fennel
- 2 tablespoons anise
- 6 tablespoons finely cut orange peel
- 1/2 kg wheat flour
- sifted rye flour

## Directions

1. The day before the bread is to be baked, boil the spiced broth down to 1 1/2 liters.
2. In the evening, make a loose dough from the slightly warm milk, wheat flour, and dissolved yeast.
3. Early the following day, warm the spiced broth and mix it into the previously made dough along with enough rye flour to make a moderately stiff dough.
4. When this has risen, add the boiled and cooled syrup and the spices.
5. If necessary, work in more rye flour, then let the dough rise again.
6. Shape with wheat flour, and let the buns rise on soft, warm baking cloths with baking cloths laid between them.
7. When the buns have risen, place them on warm baking sheets, brush with cold water, and set in a good oven heat.
8. When they are half-baked, brush them with warm water, and again when they are taken from the oven.
9. (To make the buns glossier and more beautiful, they can be brushed before placing in the oven with spiced broth or Christmas ale.)
10. When the buns are taken from the oven, place them between soft cloths to prevent the crust from becoming hard.
11. It is best to bake spiced bread in a bakehouse oven. This should be heated as soon as the dough is added, and swept at the same time as the bread is shaped. It should then be left completely closed until the bread is ready to bake.
