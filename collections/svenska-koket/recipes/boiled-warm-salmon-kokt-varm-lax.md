## Ingredients

- 1 salmon, 2 kg
- For the broth: 3.5 liters water
- 1.5 dcl vinegar
- 40 g coarse salt
- 1 carrot
- 1 red onion
- 1 bay leaf
- 4 parsley stalks
- 7 white peppercorns
- 1 lemon
- Parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. In a pot, add the water and vinegar. Add the salt, the scraped carrot cut into small pieces, the peeled onion, bay leaf, and parsley, and let the broth boil for 1 hour.
2. Ten minutes before it is done, add the peppercorns.
3. Strain the broth through a fine sieve, taste, and let cool.
4. Scale the salmon, clean without cutting too much, and wash well with a cloth dipped in cold water, then dry.
5. The salmon can be boiled whole, split lengthwise into two halves (the bone is removed but cooked with the fish), or cut into 3–4 cm thick slices; cook in the broth according to the instructions in recipe 327.
6. Serve with hollandaise, mousseline, crayfish, or shrimp sauce and small boiled potatoes.
7. Leftover boiled salmon can be used for mayonnaise (recipe 176), salad (recipe 177), or gratin (see sole gratin recipe 377).
