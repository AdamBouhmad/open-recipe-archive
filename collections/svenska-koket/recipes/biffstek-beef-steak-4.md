## Ingredients

- 1 kg beef (ox loin, beef loin, boneless inner loin, or filet)
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr)
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 4 tbsp butter (80 gr)
- 1–2 dcl broth
- 3–4 red onions or 1 large Portuguese onion

## Directions

1. The meat, which should have hung until properly tender, is dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water and cut across the muscle fibers into approximately 1.5 cm thick slices, which are beaten with a damp meat mallet or meat tenderizer until thin, but must not be beaten to pieces. If filet is used, the meat slices are only gently tapped with a knife.
2. The steaks are sprinkled with the seasonings.
3. The onion is peeled, cut into slices, and fried first in some of the butter, then placed in the middle of a hot serving plate.
4. The frying pan is rinsed out with hot water and heated over a brisk fire; enough butter is added so that it covers the bottom of the pan when melted.
5. When the butter is brown, the steaks are added and fried approximately 2 minutes on each side, or until nicely browned.
6. They are arranged in a ring around the onion and covered either with browned butter or the frying pan is rinsed out with the broth and the sauce is poured over the steaks.
7. Served with fried potatoes sprinkled with finely chopped parsley. If the steaks are desired rare inside, they should be somewhat thicker and not fried more than 1 minute on each side.
