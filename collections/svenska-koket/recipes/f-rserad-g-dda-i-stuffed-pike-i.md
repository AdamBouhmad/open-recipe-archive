## Ingredients

- 1 pike weighing 1 1/2 kg.
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)
- 150 gr. scraped fish meat
- 70 gr. butter
- 1 egg
- 1 tablespoon flour (10 gr.)
- 1 1/2 decilitres cream
- 1 teaspoon salt (5 gr.)
- 1/2 teaspoon sugar
- white pepper
- 1/2 egg
- 2 tablespoons crushed rusks
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.)
- 3 decilitres fish stock

## Directions

1. Clean the pike preferably the day before it is to be used.
2. Scale it, gut it, rinse very well, dry with a fish towel, rub it inside and outside with salt and let it hang in a cool place until the next day.
3. Then remove the backbone with a sharp knife, but leave the head and tail intact. (The backbone is cooked to make stock for basting the fish.)
4. Scrape 150 gr. of fish meat, carefully removing all small bones, and prepare the farce as in the previous recipe, but the egg white should not be whisked to peaks but mixed with the yolk in the cream.
5. Fill the pike with the farce up to 3/4 and sew the belly closed with a surgical stitch.
6. Brush the fish with the beaten egg, sprinkle with crushed rusks, place it in a long pan greased with half of the butter, and lay the remaining butter in flakes on top of the pike.
7. Fry it in good oven heat for about 1/2 hour, baste with the fish stock and baste often; place on a warm plate, remove the thread and garnish the fish with parsley, crayfish tails or sautéed mushrooms and serve with crayfish or mushroom sauce.
