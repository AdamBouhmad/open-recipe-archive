## Ingredients

- 2 ducks
- 1 hg. lard, in 2 slices
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- For sauce: The butter in which the birds are browned, 2 tbsp. flour, 3 dcl. meat stock or bouillon, 3 dcl. thick cream, salt, sugar, white pepper

## Directions

1. Duck should be prepared immediately after it is shot.
2. The bird is young if the webbing is thin and can easily be torn.
3. The broad-billed mallard is best. Those with pointed bills are usually so rank that they are nearly inedible.
4. Clean the birds, rinse well, dry inside and out, truss and cover with thin slices of lard, which are fastened with fine sailing twine.
5. Roast in a pot like pheasant and are tender after 1 1/4-1 1/2 hours.
6. Cut and arrange again on the platter so that they appear whole, garnish with browned potatoes, parsley and olives.
7. Serve with jelly or compote.
