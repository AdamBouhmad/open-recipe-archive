## Ingredients

- 2 large chickens, approximately 450 gr. scraped meat
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 2 hg. bread panade
- 2 hg. butter
- 2 whole eggs
- 4 egg yolks

## Directions

1. The chickens are plucked, cleaned out, rinsed very well, dried with a cloth and flayed.
2. All meat is cut loose from the carcasses and scraped free from tendons and membranes.
3. The meat is then ground 6 times through a meat grinder and pounded together with the spices with a wooden pestle in a stone mortar, until the forcemeat becomes fine and smooth.
4. It is then removed and the panade (see no. 626) is pounded together with the butter into an even dough, the forcemeat is added and the mass is pounded very well.
5. It is placed in a dish, the beaten eggs together with the yolks are added under continuous vigorous stirring.
6. The forcemeat is passed through a hair sieve and stirred again for approximately 1/2 hour.
7. It is then tested by cooking a little of it in salted water. If the forcemeat is too hard, 1-2 egg yolks or a little softened butter are added, otherwise 1 egg white.
8. Last, if desired, chopped truffle is added to taste.
9. The forcemeat is filled in a buttered mold with a lid, sprinkled with finely chopped truffle or crushed rusks, and cooked in a water bath in an oven or on a stove for approximately 1 hour.
10. It is tested with a skewer to see if it is done.
11. It is preferably turned out onto a rice bed and served with truffle sauce.
12. If the forcemeat is served together with tongue, ham etc. as a mixed meat dish, this batch serves 12 persons.
