## Ingredients

- 1/2 deciliter rice grains
- 3 1/2 deciliters milk
- 1 goose liver
- 30 gr. sultana raisins
- 1 teaspoon salt (5 gr.)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon marjoram
- 2 teaspoons finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 10 gr. butter, for the onion
- 1 small egg

## Directions

1. The rice grains are rinsed, scalded in hot water, rinsed again, allowed to drain, put on in the boiling milk and allowed to cook until soft, then the porridge is removed and allowed to cool
2. The liver is pounded, passed through a hair sieve and mixed in together with the cleaned and pre-boiled raisins, spices and the lightly browned onion
3. Last the egg is stirred in and the forcemeat is tasted and filled into the well-cleaned and at one end sewn-shut neck skin of a goose
4. The sausage is not filled more than 3/4, otherwise it breaks easily, is sewn shut and allowed to cook slowly in salted water, without a lid, for approximately 1/2 hour
5. It is allowed to cool, cut into slices and served with black soup
