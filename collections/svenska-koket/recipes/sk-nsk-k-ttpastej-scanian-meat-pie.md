## Ingredients

- 1.3 kg. boneless beef
- 212 gr. lean pork
- 650 gr. pork cubes
- 1 large red onion
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 gr.) for the onion
- 3 eggs
- salt and white pepper to taste
- 1/2 liter good, clear broth
- 1 wine glass fine vinegar
- 1 medium salt or vinegar cucumber
- 6 olives
- 750 gr. coarse rye flour
- 1/2 liter water

## Directions

1. Dry the beef and pork with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into pieces and pass through the meat grinder 6-8 times.
2. Peel the onion, chop finely and fry in the butter.
3. Work the meat together with the pork cubes, onion, the beaten eggs and seasonings for about 1 hour, adding the broth and vinegar little by little.
4. Pit the olives, chop coarsely together with the cucumber and mix well with the forcemeat.
5. Make a dough from the rye flour and water, and line a large, crimped baking pan with it.
6. Place the forcemeat in, cover with a lid of dough and bake in the oven for about 3 hours.
7. Let cool in the pan, turn out, cut into beautiful slices and garnish with green salad, hard-boiled eggs and beets.
8. Serve as a middle course with mayonnaise.
9. The pie can be stored 2 months in winter in its pan.
