## Ingredients

- Lamb meat
- Butter salt
- Sugar
- Potassium saltpeter (kalisalpeter)
- Coarse salt
- Juniper twigs

## Directions

1. If salting only a smaller supply of meat, Höganäs jars (glazed inside and outside) can be used advantageously.
2. For salting larger supplies, wooden vessels are used.
3. Oak or ash vessels are best, but pine vessels can also be used.
4. Before use, the wooden vessels should be boiled out with juniper brine.
5. The brine is made from water and juniper twigs and is poured into the vessel, which should be filled to the brim.
6. Let the brine stand until it has cooled; then pour it out and dry the vessel well.
7. Rub the bottom and sides with salt, and additionally place a fairly thick layer of coarse salt on the bottom.
8. Now cut the meat into pieces.
9. First cut off steaks and shoulder pieces, then cut the remaining meat into appropriately sized pieces.
10. Remove all glands, and rub the meat well, especially near the bones, with a mixture of salt, sugar, and saltpeter.
11. For 2 kg of meat calculate: 6 tablespoons butter salt, 3 tablespoons sugar, 1 1/2 teaspoons potassium saltpeter.
12. The saltpeter prevents the red meat color from being lost during salting.
13. Although the saltpeter makes the meat tough, this is counteracted by the sugar.
14. Layer the meat in the prepared vessel alternating with salt.
15. Use coarse salt here, as it contains more salt than fine salt.
16. Place the skin sides down and the meat sides facing each other.
17. At the bottom, place steaks and shoulder pieces with the edges inward and as closely together as possible.
18. Fill all small cavities with salt, and place salt between the meat pieces so that they do not directly touch each other.
19. When all the meat is layered, spread a thick layer of salt on top.
