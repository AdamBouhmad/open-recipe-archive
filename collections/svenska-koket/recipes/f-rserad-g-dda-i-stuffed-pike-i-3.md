## Ingredients

- 1 pike weighing 1 1/2 kg
- 2 tsp. salt (10 gr.)
- 150 gr. scraped fish meat
- 70 gr. butter
- 1 egg
- 1 tbsp. flour (10 gr.)
- 1 1/2 dcl. cream
- 1 tsp. salt (5 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp. sugar
- a little white pepper
- 1/2 egg
- 2 tbsp. crushed rusks
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 3 dcl. fish broth

## Directions

1. Clean the pike preferably the day before use. Scale it, remove the innards, rinse very thoroughly, dry with a fish towel, rub inside and outside with salt and let it hang in a cool place until the next day.
2. Using a sharp knife, cut out the backbone while keeping the head and tail intact. (Boil the bone to make broth for basting the fish.)
3. Scrape 150 gr. of fish meat, carefully removing all small bones, and prepare the stuffing as in the previous recipe, but the egg white should not be beaten to a foam but mixed with the yolk in the cream.
4. Fill the pike with the stuffing to 3/4 capacity, then sew the belly shut with a surgical seam.
5. Brush the fish with the beaten egg, sprinkle with crushed rusks, place it in a long baking pan greased with half the butter, and distribute the remaining butter in flakes on top of the pike.
6. Bake in good oven heat for approximately 1/2 hour, baste with fish broth and baste frequently.
7. Place on a warm platter, remove the thread, and garnish the fish with parsley, crayfish tails or sautéed mushrooms and serve with crayfish or mushroom sauce.
