## Ingredients

- 1 large cabbage head
- water, salt
- 3 hectograms boneless beef
- 2 hectograms boneless veal or pork
- 50 grams lard
- 2 tsp. salt (10 g)
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 deciliter crushed rusks
- 4-5 deciliters cream or milk
- 1 hectogram lard for browning
- 1 hectogram lard, in one large slice
- 1 liter broth

## Directions

1. Place the cabbage head in boiling salted water and cook until tender.
2. Remove it immediately and drain on a cloth.
3. Remove the outer green leaves and hollow out the head from above.
4. Prepare the forcemeat as in recipe no. 637 and it should be fairly loose.
5. Fill the cabbage head with the forcemeat (but not completely, as the forcemeat rises during cooking), cover with a few large cabbage leaves and tie with fine twine.
6. Brown well in hot lard.
7. Place it in a pot on the lard slice, along with the cabbage that was cut from the head, add the boiling broth and simmer slowly with the lid tightly closed for about 2 hours, or until the forcemeat is done, which is tested with a whisk.
8. Place the cabbage head on a round dish, garnish with the cut-out cabbage and with small meatballs made from the forcemeat that did not fit in the head.
9. Serve with strained sauce (see recipe no. 429), made from the pan drippings.
