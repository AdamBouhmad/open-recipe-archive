## Ingredients

- 1 small cabbage head
- 1/5 cup rice grains
- 4 cups milk
- 200 g boneless beef
- 100 g boneless veal
- 100 g lard bacon
- 1 egg
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1/2 liter bouillon
- 1 part cream

## Directions

1. Remove the outer leaves of the cabbage head and carefully loosen the remaining leaves from the core so they remain whole.
2. Lay them in boiling water to blanch for a few minutes, then lay them on a cloth to drain.
3. Rinse the rice grains in cold water and scald them in boiling water; add 3 cups milk and let the grains cook until soft.
4. When done, pour them out to cool.
5. Treat the meat and bacon as in the preparation of meat forcemeat.
6. Mix the egg, 1 cup milk, the spices and the cooled grains together.
7. Cut away the thickest part of the main vein from the cabbage leaves.
8. Place one tablespoon of forcemeat on each cabbage leaf, roll it up and tie it with fine twine previously dipped in hot water.
9. Brown the rolls in butter, add the bouillon and let them simmer slowly with the lid on for at least 1 hour, shaking them occasionally.
10. Remove them, take off the twine, skim the sauce, add the cream and bring to a boil while whisking.
11. Place the cabbage rolls on a carrot and pour the sauce over them.
