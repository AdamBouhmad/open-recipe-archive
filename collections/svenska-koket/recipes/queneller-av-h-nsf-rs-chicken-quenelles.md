## Ingredients

- 150 gr. boneless chicken meat
- 1 tsk. salt (5 gr.)
- 1/4 tsk. white pepper
- 1 egg
- 1/2 msk. flour (5 gr.)
- 1 1/2—2 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. The meat is scraped free from tendons and membranes, mixed with the seasonings, ground 4 times through a meat grinder and pounded with a wooden pestle in a stone mortar to a fine paste.
2. The egg is whisked up and added little by little along with the flour, while the forcemeat is stirred vigorously.
3. It is passed through a fine sieve, placed in a small saucepan which is set in a bowl with ice (it will separate otherwise when the cream is worked in) and the cream is added teaspoon by teaspoon with steady stirring, after which the forcemeat is tasted carefully.
4. A long baking pan is brushed with butter and allowed to cool.
5. The forcemeat is placed in a pastry bag with a smooth or fluted nozzle and piped directly into the pan in the form of small balls or oblong shapes, placed close together.
6. Once all the quenelles are formed, the pan is placed at the side of the stove and boiling salted water is added.
