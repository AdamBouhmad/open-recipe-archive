## Ingredients

- 50 small white or green cucumbers
- 26 gr. tartar
- 20 gr. alum
- 375 gr. coarse salt
- 5 1/2 liters water
- 6 dcl. spirit vinegar
- cherry and currant leaves
- dill flowers

## Directions

1. Brush the cucumbers clean in cold water and let them lie in fresh water for one day.
2. Crush the tartar, alum and salt, place them in the water and let stand for one day.
3. Remove the cucumbers, let them drain well and place them on doubled linen to dry for about 3 hours.
4. Layer them thereafter in a large crock alternately with the leaves and dill flowers.
5. The cucumbers must not touch each other or the side of the vessel, as they will become spotted.
6. Carefully pour the salt brine, mixed with vinegar, over them and it should stand well above the cucumbers.
7. Place a bottom and a light weight on the crock, bind it with parchment paper and store in the cellar.
8. After 3-4 weeks the cucumbers are ready.
9. Check often that the brine is sufficient, and that no mold forms on the surface; if so, add more vinegar and dissolved salt.
