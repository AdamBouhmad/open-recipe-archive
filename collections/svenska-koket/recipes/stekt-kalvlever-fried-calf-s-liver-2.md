## Ingredients

- 1 kg calf's liver
- 50 g lard
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 0.5 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 caul
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 6 deciliters boiling milk
- 1 tablespoon soy
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 g)
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 g)
- liver sauce
- 1-2 deciliters thick cream
- salt, sugar
- white pepper
- 0.5 tablespoon cold butter (10 g)
- 1 tablespoon grated mesost cheese

## Directions

1. The liver, which should be light and tender, is soaked in water for about 0.5 hour, dried well, cleaned and larded with fine strips of lard, rolled in a mixture of salt and pepper.
2. The liver is rubbed with the remaining seasonings and wrapped in the caul (which usually comes with the purchase), which is sewn or tied together with kitchen twine.
3. The butter is browned in an iron pot, the liver is placed in it, browned on all sides and diluted with the milk, a little at a time.
4. It is allowed to fry slowly with a tightly closed lid for about 1.5 hours, during which it is basted frequently.
5. The caul is removed, the liver is cut into thin slices, placed on a warm serving dish, a little of the sauce is poured over it and the rest of the sauce is served in a sauce boat.
6. The sauce is prepared as in recipe no. 429 and, if desired, the grated mesost cheese is added.
7. Served with boiled potatoes and lingonberry jam.
