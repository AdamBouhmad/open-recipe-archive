## Ingredients

- 3 kg beef chuck or rib
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 2 hectograms smoked ham, in slices
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 2 carrots
- 3 shallots
- 1/2 liter boiling broth
- 1/2 liter Madeira wine

## Directions

1. Chop the meat briefly before weighing it. Remove the rib bones, but leave the backbone. Trim most of the fat from the outside, rub the meat with salt and lard or cover it with ham slices, which are fastened with twine dipped in boiling water.
2. Grease a long pan with cold butter, large enough to hold the meat. Place the blanched carrots cut into thick slices and the peeled shallots cut into wedges in the pan, and place the meat on top with the outside up.
3. Set in a hot oven and baste often with the butter until it is beautifully browned (the vegetables must not burn), then gradually add the broth and wine.
4. Baste the meat every 10 minutes and let it roast until tender, which is tested with a whisk (see p. 233); remove it and cut off the strings.
5. Cut the roast, place on a warm platter, garnish with vegetable patties or potato croquettes and kale or parsley. Serve with brown sauce (see no. 430).
6. Rib roast can also be roasted in a pot on top of the stove.
