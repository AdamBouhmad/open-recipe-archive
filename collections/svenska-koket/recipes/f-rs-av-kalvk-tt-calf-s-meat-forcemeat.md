## Ingredients

- 500 g boneless veal loin
- 100 g dry, cleaned beef kidney fat or 100 g lard
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 tablespoon flour (15 g)
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 5-6 decilitres thick cream

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, free it from sinews and membranes, cut it into small cubes and grind 3 times through a meat grinder.
2. Finely chop the kidney fat with a knife on a cutting board, mix it along with the seasonings into the veal forcemeat, and grind it 3 more times through the grinder.
3. Pound the forcemeat together with the flour in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until it becomes even and smooth, then add the egg and egg yolks, one at a time, under continued vigorous pounding.
4. Pass the forcemeat through a fine sieve and let it stand in a cold place or on ice for a few hours, or preferably until the next day.
5. Work it vigorously with a spoon while adding the cream gradually, taste it (as in recipe 628), and add, if needed, a little egg white or cream.
6. If desired, flavor with a little grated onion, finely chopped mushrooms or truffle, and the same weight of butter can be used instead of kidney fat or lard.
7. Veal forcemeat can also be prepared as in recipes 628, 629 and 630.
8. Cook in a water bath or bake in the oven.
9. Ham forcemeat is prepared in the same way.
