## Ingredients

- 2 chickens of approximately 3 kg each
- 1 large bunch of parsley
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 1.5 hectograms of lard, in 2 slices
- 3 tablespoons butter for roasting (60 g)
- 3/4 liter of broth
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)

## Directions

1. Clean the chickens by plucking carefully in the direction the feathers grow so the skin is not damaged
2. Singe the chickens over a spirit, gas, or straw flame to remove remaining down and feathers
3. Remove the wings and legs
4. Pull up the neck skin so the neck can be drawn out and cut off at the breast while leaving the skin intact
5. Remove the crop and windpipe and loosen the esophagus
6. Make a small incision in the back part (the rump) and carefully remove the organs so the intestines do not break
7. Reserve the liver, heart, and gizzard to cook with the chicken, removing the bile from the liver, splitting the heart, and cleaning the gizzard
8. Rinse the chicken thoroughly and dry it well
9. Season the chicken inside with spices if desired and fill with fruit, rice, chestnuts, or forcemeat
10. Position the chicken so the breast is high and it has a neat appearance, laying it breast up with wings crossed over the back and neck skin pulled down toward the wings
11. Thread a trussing needle with fine twine through one wing, the neck skin and backbone, and out through the other wing, then through the thighs which are pressed tightly to the body, tighten the cord and tie the ends together
12. Quickly dip the breast and thighs in boiling broth or water to firm the meat
13. Arrange lard slices on the breast and thighs, fastening them with fine twine that has been dipped in boiling water
14. Roast with 3 tablespoons butter and 3/4 liter of broth, seasoning with 2 teaspoons salt
15. Before serving, gently remove the trussing cord
