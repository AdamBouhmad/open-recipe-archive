## Ingredients

- 1 pike-perch weighing 1 1/2 kg
- 1 msk salt (15 gr.)
- 1 hg butter
- 2 msk chopped parsley
- 2 msk chopped mushrooms
- 2 tomatoes cut into cubes
- 1/2 egg
- 2 msk crushed crackling bread

## Directions

1. Scale the pike-perch, clean as described on page 171, keep the head on but cut off the gills and fins.
2. Make cuts 2 cm apart, rub with salt and let rest 1 hour, then dry again.
3. Stir 2 msk (40 gr.) of the butter until it becomes white and fluffy, and mix with the parsley, mushrooms and tomatoes; stuff the pike-perch with this mixture, then sew it up.
4. Brush with egg and sprinkle with crushed crackling bread.
5. Butter a long pan with some of the butter over a spot as large as the fish takes up, place the pike-perch in it with back up, place the rest of the butter in flakes on top.
6. Place in a warm oven and let roast for about 1/2 hour, basting it often with the butter.
7. Place pike-perch on a warm plate, remove the thread, pour the butter from the pan over the fish and garnish with parsley and fluted lemon slices.
8. Serve with mushroom or tomato sauce.
