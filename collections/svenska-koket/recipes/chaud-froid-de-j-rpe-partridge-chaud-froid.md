## Ingredients

- 6 partridges
- 2 hg. lard in flat slices
- 1 hg. butter
- 3 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 liter strong brown broth
- For the sauce: 2 tbsp. butter (40 gr.)
- 6 tbsp. flour (60 gr.)
- 8 dcl. meat stock
- 1 dcl. thick cream
- 1-2 tbsp. Madeira
- 1/2 tsp. salt, cayenne
- 1 tsp. beef steak sauce
- 3-4 sheets gelatin
- For garnish: 2 hard-boiled eggs
- 4-5 large truffles
- For the aspic: 1 liter dark broth
- 8-10 sheets gelatin
- 2 egg whites and rinsed shells
- 1 tbsp. cold water

## Directions

1. Prepare the partridges as in the previous recipe.
2. Truss them (see poultry) so the breast is high and oval, bind each with a slice of lard and season.
3. Brown the butter in a roasting pan, place the partridges in and roast in a warm oven for about 1/2 hour, basting frequently and adding broth.
4. Remove, pull off the skin and let the partridges cool.
5. Strain and skim the meat stock.
6. Cut off the bones, trim away the back; split the partridges and carefully remove the breastbone.
7. For the sauce, fry the butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the stock and cream and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes.
8. Add the wine, seasonings and beef steak sauce, taste the sauce, let it come to a boil and skim.
9. Rinse the gelatin, dissolve it in 1 tbsp. hot water and stir in.
10. Keep the sauce in a bowl and stir slowly until it is thick enough to coat the spoon.
11. Reconstruct the chicken breasts with a little of the sauce so they appear whole, glaze them with the remaining sauce, place on a platter and garnish with stars of egg white and truffle.
12. Prepare the aspic as in recipe no. [reference incomplete in source].
