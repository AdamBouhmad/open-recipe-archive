## Ingredients

- 3 kg from the leg
- 2 hectograms lard
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 3 deciliters broth
- 5 deciliters broth
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 3 deciliters broth
- 3 deciliters cream or good milk

## Directions

1. The roebuck should be young, not weighing more than 15-20 kg before it is skinned, in order to be juicy and tender.
2. The meat should hang for 4-5 days before it is prepared.
3. When the roebuck is to be skinned, the skin is loosened around the knee joint, it is hung by the hind legs and the skin is ripped open with a sharp knife from the inside of the leg through the belly and up to the head.
4. The skin is pulled off, the head is cut off and the entrails are removed.
5. It is then cut up like mutton, but with a saddle instead of two rows of chops.
6. The roast is beaten with a damp club, the membranes are pulled off, it is larded, rubbed with the rest of the spices and roasted in a pot and extended with the boiling broth and cream and is ready after 2-2 1/2 hours.
7. The roast is cut, laid on a warm plate, poured over with a couple of tablespoons of sauce, garnished with browned potatoes, salad and parsley and served with redcurrant jelly, apple sauce and reduced sauce, supplemented with 1 tablespoon redcurrant jelly.
