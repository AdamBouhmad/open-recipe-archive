## Ingredients

- 3 hg. calf thymus
- 1 1/2 lit. water for blanching
- 1/2 tbsp. salt for blanching
- 2 tsp. salt
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 1 egg
- 1 tbsp. water
- 1 cup crushed rusks
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 gr.)
- 1 can peas (1/2 lit.)
- 1 1/2 tbsp. butter (30 gr.)
- parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. Let the calf thymus soak in water, changing it often so that all blood is drawn out.
2. Blanch it as in recipe no. 47, for 10 minutes and place in a bowl with fresh water.
3. When the thymus is cold, clean it well, that is, remove cartilage and membranes, and place in a cloth under light pressure for about 1 hour.
4. Cut into 8 mm thick slices, which are sprinkled with the seasonings, dipped in the egg whisked with water, and turned in the crushed rusks.
5. Fry them a beautiful golden brown in the butter and arrange in a ring on a round platter.
6. Heat the peas with a spoon in a pot, let them come to a boil and place with a serving spoon in the middle of the platter.
7. Place cold butter in flakes on top of the peas.
8. Garnish with parsley and serve as a breakfast dish.
