## Ingredients

- 1 kg boneless beef chuck
- 100 g lard
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped onion
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped parsley
- 2 teaspoons anchovy sauce
- 2 tablespoons vinegar
- 2 tablespoons cooking oil
- 1 tablespoon white wine or Madeira
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 teaspoon anchovy sauce
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 6 decilitres broth or water
- 2 tablespoons red or white wine

## Directions

1. Prepare the meat as in the previous recipe and cut into 12 slices, which are beaten well, placed on a plate, covered with the marinade and left for 1 hour, during which they are turned once.
2. Mix all marinade ingredients well and add, if desired, 1/2 tablespoon finely chopped onion.
3. Cut the lard into 12 strips, which are rolled in a mixture of the onion, parsley and anchovy sauce.
4. Wrap the meat slices in cloth, and on each one place a lard strip, then roll the meat slices up tightly and bind with fine twine or fasten with toothpicks.
5. Heat a frying pan, brown the butter in it, add the rolls and brown nicely all around, then place them in a warm iron pot.
6. Fry the flour with the butter in the frying pan, add the boiling broth or water together with the wine, and let the sauce come to a good boil and pour over the rolls.
7. Let them simmer slowly with a tight-fitting lid until tender, or about 2-2 1/2 hours.
8. Place them on warm carrots, taste the sauce and pour over.
9. Serve with pressed potatoes or potato puree.
