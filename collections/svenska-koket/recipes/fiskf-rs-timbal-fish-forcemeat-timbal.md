## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg pike
- 2 hectograms scraped fish meat
- 1 hectogram butter
- 20 grams flour
- 2 decilitres cream
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 3 hectograms shrimp or 1 can lobster (1/2 litre)
- 6 pre-boiled oysters
- 8 small mushrooms
- 3 truffles
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 grams)
- 3 balls crayfish butter (30 grams)
- 5 tablespoons flour (50 grams)
- 4 decilitres cream
- 2 egg yolks
- salt, sugar, white pepper

## Directions

1. Clean and rinse the fish well, cut into fillets and remove the skin.
2. Pound the fillets with a damp meat mallet until even, rub with salt and vinegar and let rest 1 hour.
3. Take the pike for the forcemeat, rinse well and dry with a fish towel.
4. Fillet the fish, scrape the meat finely ensuring no scales or bones are included.
5. Mix the fish meat with butter and pass through a meat grinder 3 times.
6. Pound the mixture in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until fine and cohesive.
7. Mix flour, cream, eggs and seasonings in a bowl.
8. Work this mixture into the forcemeat by the tablespoon, stirring for about 1/2 hour.
9. Butter a smooth timbal mold holding about 1 1/2 litres well.
10. Wrap the fish fillets in a fish towel and line the mold internally with them, laying them side by side with their tails around the bottom and opposite ends protruding above the rim.
11. Press well so they take the mold's shape.
12. Cover the fillets with a 2 cm thick layer of fish forcemeat.
13. Place the mold in regular oven heat and let stand 10-15 minutes until the forcemeat sets to the fillets.
14. Cut off the protruding fillet pieces and fill the timbal with the stew to about 3 cm from the top edge.
15. Lay the cut fish pieces on top of the stew and cover the timbal with a layer of fish forcemeat.
16. Bake about 45 minutes in a water bath in regular oven heat.
17. After removing the timbal from the oven, let it rest 10 minutes, then turn out onto a round platter, remove the mold and garnish the top with shrimp tails and truffle slices and around it with parsley or salad.
18. Serve with crayfish sauce as a course.
19. For the stew: Clean the shrimp; if using lobster, cut into pieces.
20. Cut mushrooms and truffles into slices and sauté in butter.
21. Sauté the crayfish butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the cream and let the sauce simmer 5 minutes.
22. Add the shrimp, oysters and mushrooms and bring to a boil, add the beaten egg yolks and season the stew with seasonings.
