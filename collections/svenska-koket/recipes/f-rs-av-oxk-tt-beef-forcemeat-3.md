## Ingredients

- 500 g boneless beef
- 50 g lard or cleaned kidney fat
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 200 g bread panade or 2 tablespoons flour (20 g)
- 2-3 decilitres cream or milk
- 1/2 tablespoon finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 g) for the onion
- 1 egg white for brushing
- 2 tablespoons crushed rusks for coating
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g) for frying
- 1/2 litre boiling broth or water with 1/2 teaspoon solid meat extract

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water and cut it into cubes, removing all sinews and membranes carefully.
2. Scrape the lard and cut it into pieces; if using kidney fat, chop it finely.
3. Mix the meat with the lard or kidney fat and seasonings and grind 6 times through the meat grinder.
4. Pound the forcemeat in a stone mortar together with the panade (see recipe 626) or flour until it is even and smooth, then place it in a bowl and add the beaten egg and egg yolk under vigorous stirring.
5. Stir the forcemeat for approximately 3/4 hour while gradually adding the cream or milk.
6. Taste it (see recipe 628); if too stiff, add a little more cream, if too soft, add an egg white.
7. If desired, add last the onion fried in butter.
8. Grease a long pan with cold butter, place the forcemeat in it and shape it like an oblong roll.
9. Brush it with egg white and sprinkle with crushed rusks.
10. Place the forcemeat in the oven and brown it, basting frequently with the butter in the pan.
11. When browned, add the boiling broth or water mixed with meat extract and let it finish baking, which takes about 1/2 hour.
12. During cooking, baste it about every 10 minutes.
13. Place the forcemeat on a warm platter, slice it thinly, garnish with parsley and serve with the beaten, strained meat gravy or with a reduced sauce (see recipe 429).
14. If desired, use 300 g beef and 200 g veal with the same amount of lard or kidney fat.
15. The forcemeat can be diluted with broth or water instead of cream or milk.
16. If the forcemeat is cooked in a water bath, dilute it with somewhat more cream.
