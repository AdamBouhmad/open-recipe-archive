## Ingredients

- 3–4 woodcock
- 1 hg. (100 g) bacon, in 3–4 slices
- 2 tsk. (10 g) salt
- 5 msk. (100 g) butter
- Farced Bread: Giblets from the birds
- 20 gr. (20 g) bacon
- 1/2 msk. finely chopped parsley
- 1 1/2 msk. (30 g) butter
- 1–2 egg yolks
- 1/2 msk. red wine
- Salt
- White pepper
- 9 wheat slices
- 2 msk. (40 g) butter for the bread

## Directions

1. Woodcock is among the most expensive game we have and is considered a great delicacy. The birds should hang for 3–4 days and be plucked the same day they are to be served.
2. The head and neck are left intact, but the skin on the head is removed and the eyes taken out.
3. The birds are drawn neatly, the giblets are saved and used for forcemeat.
4. The claws are clipped, the feet are scalded in hot water and the skin is removed with a cloth.
5. The birds are rinsed quickly and dried well inside and out, and are trussed so that the thighs are pressed tightly against the body, the head is bent back, whereupon the beak is inserted through one thigh, the body and out through the other thigh.
6. The breast is covered with thin bacon slices, which are fastened with fine twine.
7. The birds are then roasted in butter like chicken and require the same roasting time.
8. The giblets are cleaned well, rinsed, wrapped in a cloth, finely chopped together with the bacon, pounded in a mortar and passed through a sieve.
9. The forcemeat is mixed with the parsley, sautéed quickly in butter and allowed to cool, after which the egg yolk, wine and seasonings are added.
10. The bread slices are formed into neat elongated shapes, fried in butter, covered with the forcemeat, placed on a baking sheet and put in a hot oven so that the forcemeat is quickly browned.
11. The woodcock are split lengthwise, arranged on croutons with the heads pointing outward; peppercorns are placed in the eye sockets.
12. Garnish with the farced bread slices and salad or parsley.
13. Serve with browned potatoes, green salad and lingonberry jelly.
