## Ingredients

- 1 skinned hare weighing 3 1/2 kg
- 2 hectograms lard
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 grams)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 grams)
- 2 deciliters broth
- 5 deciliters cream or milk

## Directions

1. Hares should not weigh more than stated above, as much larger ones are usually old, coarse and tough in meat. The hare should hang for 3-6 days, depending on the season, to become tender. Skin it (see roe deer, no. 584), remove the head, hind legs and shoulders, then lay the hare on its back and cut off the ribs down to the fillets. Rinse it very thoroughly and dry it, then loosen all membranes with a sharp knife and remove them without damaging the meat. If desired, place the hare in milk afterwards and let it stand until the next day, when it is taken out and wrapped well in cloth. Break the backbone and hind legs on the inside to facilitate carving. Lard the hare on the back, hind legs and shoulders with fine strips of lard dipped in a mixture of salt and white pepper. Brown the butter in a roasting pot, place the back, hind legs and shoulders with the meaty sides down, brown beautifully on both sides, then add first the boiling broth, then the cream. Let it braise slowly with a tightly closed lid until tender, or about 1 1/2 hours. Cut the hare into attractive pieces, arrange on a hot platter, garnish with browned potatoes, salad or parsley, serve with strained sauce (see no. 429) mixed with 1 tablespoon lingonberry jelly, and with green salad and acidic salads.
