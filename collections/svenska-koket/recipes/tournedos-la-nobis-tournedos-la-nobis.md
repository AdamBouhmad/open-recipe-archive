## Ingredients

- 300 grams beef fillet
- salt and white pepper
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 9 asparagus tips
- 3 slices truffle
- 1/2 teaspoon chopped shallot
- 30 grams chopped tarragon stalks
- 30 grams chervil
- 2 grams salt
- some white pepper
- 2 tablespoons vinegar
- 3 egg yolks
- 80 grams melted butter
- cayenne

## Directions

1. Cut slices 1 1/2 centimeters thick from the beef fillet, lightly pound them flat, form them round, season and fry in butter or braise them.
2. Place the steaks in small fireproof, round molds, garnish with the asparagus tips, fried in some of the butter.
3. Pour the Béarnaise sauce over and place a slice of truffle on each steak.
4. Serve as a breakfast dish with fried potatoes, sprinkled with finely chopped parsley.
5. For the sauce, place the seasonings and vinegar in a sauce pan. Reduce the vinegar until one-third remains, then lift the pan from the heat and let the contents cool slightly, then whisk in the egg yolks, one at a time.
6. Return the pan to the heat and let the sauce simmer, then add the butter little by little while whisking. Strain the sauce through a cloth and blend with a hint of cayenne and finely chopped parsley.
7. Heat it in a water bath until lukewarm and serve immediately. If heated too much it will break.
