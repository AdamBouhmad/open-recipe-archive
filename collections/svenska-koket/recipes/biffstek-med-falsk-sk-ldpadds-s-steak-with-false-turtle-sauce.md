## Ingredients

- 750 gr. boneless pork loin
- 1/2 msk. salt
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.)
- 2 msk. butter
- 1 slice smoked bacon (30 gr.)
- 2 small red onions
- 2 small carrots
- 1 small leek
- 1 piece celery
- 8 dcl. strong veal broth (made from the bones)
- 1 msk. tomato purée
- 1 tsk. soy
- 1 msk. butter (20 gr.)
- 2 msk. flour (20 gr.)
- 1/2 msk. Madeira
- 1/2 msk. cognac
- cayenne

## Directions

1. For the sauce, brown the butter with the bacon, cut into cubes, and the cleaned and chopped vegetables.
2. When the vegetables are well browned, add the broth, tomato purée and soy, then let everything cook for 1 1/2 hours.
3. Dry the pork loin with a cloth, wring out in hot water, and cut into slices, which are pounded on both sides and sprinkled with salt and pepper.
4. Let the steaks rest while the sauce finishes cooking.
5. Strain and skim the broth. Fry the butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the broth gradually, and let the sauce cook under stirring for 5 minutes.
6. Season with the wine, cognac and cayenne.
7. Fry the steaks quickly in butter, place on a warm serving dish and pour the sauce over.
8. Serve with potatoes or hard-boiled eggs as a breakfast dish.
