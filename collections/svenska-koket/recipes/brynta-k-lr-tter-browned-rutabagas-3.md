## Ingredients

- 2 large rutabagas
- 1½ liters water
- 2 tsp. salt (10 gr.) for blanching
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 gr.)
- 2 tbsp. sugar (30 gr.)
- 1 tsp. salt (5 gr.)
- 4 dcl. boiling broth
- ¼ tsp. white pepper

## Directions

1. Peel the rutabagas, cut them into fairly large cubes or oblong rectangles and blanch them 3-4 minutes with the lid open
2. Drain them in a colander and let them drip dry on a cloth
3. Brown the butter in an iron saucepan, add the roots, sprinkle with sugar and salt and brown them, stirring often, until they get a nice color
4. Pour on the broth and let the roots simmer slowly until they are completely tender, about 1½ hours
5. Season with a little pepper if desired and serve with salted meat
