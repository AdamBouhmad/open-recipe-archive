## Ingredients

- 1 kg beef bottom round
- 1.5 kg beef ribs
- 3.5 liters water
- 10 grams salt
- 1 carrot
- 1 parsnip
- 1/4 celery root
- 1 small leek (or 1 small red onion)
- 1 clove (optional)
- 3 hectograms lean boneless beef (for clarification)
- 1 egg white and rinsed eggshell (for clarification)

## Directions

1. The broth should preferably be cooked the day before it will be used.
2. Prepare the meat and cook the broth as in the previous recipe. If desired, add the peeled red onion with a clove stuck into it at the same time as the vegetables.
3. The meat used for clarification should be dried with a cloth, wrung out in cold water, cut into small cubes and pounded together with the egg white and rinsed eggshell in a stone mortar (with wooden pestle) into a fine mass, which is placed in a soup pot with a thick bottom.
4. Carefully remove the fat layer from the broth, then pour the broth into the pot with the clarification mixture, but do not let the sediment from the bottom follow with it.
5. Place the pot over the fire and let the broth boil up under frequent stirring at the bottom and boil for 5 minutes. Then place the pot at the side of the stove, cover it with a lid and let the broth stand for 1 hour without boiling, but keep it hot.
6. Strain it through flannel cloth wrung out in hot water and measure it. Let it boil up; if there is more than 1.5 liters, the broth can be reduced until you have the desired amount.
7. Season the broth with salt and cayenne and serve with various garnishes, such as strips of tongue, truffle and mushrooms, asparagus tips, vegetables, egg royal, quenelles of fish, veal or chicken forcemeat, cheese fritters, cheese sticks, pastries, croutons, vermicelli, dumplings, etc.
8. If the broth is to be used for finished soups or purées, 2 liters of water can be used per 1 kg of meat; the broth should then not be reduced.
