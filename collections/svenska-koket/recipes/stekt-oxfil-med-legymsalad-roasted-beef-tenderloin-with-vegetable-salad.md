## Ingredients

- 1 kg. beef tenderloin
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 1 tsp. salt
- a little white pepper
- 1/2 liter brown broth
- 1 dcl. madeira
- 1 12-ore shaped white bread
- 1 small white cabbage head
- vegetable salad
- parsley

## Directions

1. The tenderloin, which should have hung until properly tender, is cleaned (sinews and membranes removed), dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and tied so that it takes a nice form.
2. Brown the butter in an iron pot, which should not be larger than to just fit the meat; the tenderloin is placed in it and browned well on all sides and sprinkled with a mixture of salt and pepper.
3. Place it in a hot oven, dilute with the boiling broth and wine, and let it roast 30–40 minutes, basting frequently.
4. Remove the roast, cut away the string, and slice the tenderloin into thin slices, which are again laid together so that it retains its form, and placed on a 4 cm. high base, cut from the white bread. The base should have the same length as the tenderloin and can, if desired, be cooked golden brown in aspic or covered with tinfoil.
5. Around the base, garnish with small shells cut from raw white cabbage leaves, which are filled alternately with vegetable salad and browned potatoes.
6. Serve with meat gravy, strained and skimmed, or with truffle sauce (see no. 433).
