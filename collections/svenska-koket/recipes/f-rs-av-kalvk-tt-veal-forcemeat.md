## Ingredients

- 5 hg. boneless veal loin
- 1 hg. dry, cleaned beef kidney fat or 1 hg. lard
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 1/2 tbsp. flour (15 g.)
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 5-6 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, free it from sinews and membranes, cut into cubes and grind 3 times through a meat grinder.
2. Finely chop the kidney fat with a knife on a cutting board, mix with the seasonings and the veal forcemeat, which is ground an additional 3 times through the grinder.
3. Pound the forcemeat together with the flour in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until it becomes smooth and supple, then add the egg and egg yolks, one at a time, under continued vigorous pounding.
4. Pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve and let it rest in a cold place or on ice for several hours, or preferably until the following day.
5. Work it vigorously with a spoon while adding the cream gradually, little by little. Test as in recipe no. 628, and add, if needed, a little egg white or cream.
6. The forcemeat may, if desired, be flavored with a little grated onion, finely chopped mushrooms or truffle, and the same amount of butter may be used instead of kidney fat or lard.
7. Cook in a water bath or bake in an oven.
8. Forcemeat of pork may be prepared in the same way.
