## Ingredients

- Eel, alive
- Salt
- Pepper
- Lemon or horseradish
- Melted butter
- Boiled potatoes
- Wheat flour
- Egg
- Breadcrumbs
- Parsley
- Dill
- Mayonnaise

## Directions

1. Buy eel alive and slaughter by cutting the backbone near the gills and letting blood drain
2. Sprinkle with salt and let lie 1/2 hour before skinning
3. Loosen skin around the head and hang the eel on a nail by a rope tied around its neck
4. With a fish towel, grip the loosened skin and pull it off with a quick motion
5. Cut off the head and clip off fins with scissors
6. Remove the innards, ensuring the gall is not broken, rinse thoroughly, dry, cut into 5-6 cm long pieces, and salt them
7. For boiled eel: cook in water with salt and pepper, serve with lemon or horseradish, melted butter, and boiled potatoes
8. For eel in gelée: see recipe 184
9. For oven-roasted eel: do not cut into pieces but into two fillets with backbone removed, dip in wheat flour, then beaten egg, coat with breadcrumbs, fry in butter, garnish with fluted lemon slices and parsley, serve with browned butter mixed with lemon juice and potato purée
10. For cold oven-roasted eel: serve with mayonnaise mixed with chopped dill
11. For eel fried in pan on stove: cut fillets into smaller pieces, treat as described above, serve warm or cold for smörgåsbord
12. For eel as main course, allow 1 1/2 kg for 6 persons
