## Ingredients

- 10 liters green, unripe gooseberries
- 5 liters water
- 0.5 kg sugar per kg juice

## Directions

1. Rinse the gooseberries, trim them and place them in a stone crock.
2. The water is boiled and poured over the berries in the stone crock, which is then covered with a towel and a lid.
3. The next day the juice is poured off, boiled and poured over the berries again. This is repeated for three days in a row.
4. On the fourth day the juice is cooked with sugar. It is cooked with skimming until it becomes sticky and has gained a beautiful red colour. It is bottled warm or cold, the bottles are corked and sealed with resin.
