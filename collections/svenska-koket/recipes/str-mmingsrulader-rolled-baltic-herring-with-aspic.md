## Ingredients

- 24 Baltic herrings
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon sugar
- 1 cup vinegar
- 1 cup water
- 5 allspice berries
- 5 white peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 4 dill stems
- The broth in which the herrings cooked
- 3 gelatin sheets
- 1 tablespoon hot water
- 1 egg white
- dill for garnish

## Directions

1. Clean the herrings, remove the backbone, scrape away the dark skin, rinse well, wrap in a fish towel and rub with salt and sugar.
2. Bring the vinegar, water and other spices to a boil.
3. Roll the herrings tightly and place them close together in a pot with dill stems between each layer and pour the broth over them, then let the herrings simmer slowly for about 1/2 hour.
4. Remove carefully and place in a water-rinsed aspic mold with beautiful dill stems on the bottom and around the edges.
5. Beat the egg white to a light foam and whisk into the broth along with the rinsed gelatin dissolved in 1 tablespoon hot water.
6. Bring the broth to a good boil, let it stand covered for 10 minutes, strain through a cloth and pour over the herrings.
7. Place the mold in a cold place. Turn out when the aspic is set and serve at the smorgasbord or as a breakfast dish.
