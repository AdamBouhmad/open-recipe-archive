## Ingredients

- 1 hg. flour
- approximately 3/4 dcl. water
- 175 gr. fine butter
- 1 egg white
- 2 hg. crayfish tails
- 1 ball crayfish butter (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 gr.)
- 2 tbsp. flour (20 gr.)
- 2 1/2 dcl. cream
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper
- 1 egg yolk

## Directions

1. Prepare the puff pastry as in recipe no. 33, roll out to 3/4 cm thickness, and cut small round cakes with a drinking glass. Place half of these on a baking sheet; hollow out the middle of the other half with a wine glass so they form rings. Brush the cakes with egg white, then place the rings on top. Place the cases on a baking sheet along with the small lids and brush both parts on top with egg white, which must not run down the sides as it prevents the puff pastry from rising. Bake light brown in a warm oven. Carefully remove the puff pastry that has risen in the center of each case during baking to make room for the filling.
2. Soak the crayfish tails in milk for several hours before use. Drain them well in a colander. Fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the cream and let the sauce boil for 5 minutes, then add the crayfish tails and bring the stew to a boil and taste for seasoning. If desired, add a whisked egg yolk and let the stew simmer.
3. Fill the cases while hot with the stew and place the lids on if desired. Instead of lids, the cases can be garnished with a crayfish tail, shrimp tail, mushroom, or oyster if filled with stewed fish. The same fillings used for croustades can also be used in these cases.
4. Arrange on parchment paper or a folded napkin and serve as a middle course.
