## Ingredients

- 60 gr. good macaroni
- 1/2 tablespoon salt (8 gr.)
- 1 liter water
- 3 tablespoon butter (60 gr.)
- 3 tablespoon flour (30 gr.)
- 3 deciliters cream
- 1 egg yolk
- 1/2 teaspoon salt (3 gr.)
- a little white pepper
- 1/2 tablespoon grated cheese (optional)
- 2 tablespoon flour (20 gr.) for coating
- 1 egg
- 1/2 teaspoon salt (3 gr.)
- 1 teaspoon cooking oil
- 1 1/2 deciliters breadcrumbs
- 1 kg. lard for frying

## Directions

1. Break the macaroni into pieces, place in boiling salted water and cook until soft, about 20 minutes.
2. Strain through a colander, rinse with cold water, drain on cloth and cut into 2 mm. thick rings.
3. Fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the cream and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes.
4. Add the macaroni, finish the stewing with the egg yolk and season with spices and cheese if desired.
5. Spread the stewing on a flat dish, grease with butter, brush with a little butter to prevent drying and let cool.
6. Divide the mass into smaller portions—each portion should weigh about 35 gr.—which are rolled into oblong shapes on a floured board.
7. Dip the croquettes in the stiffly beaten eggs mixed with cooking oil and seasoned with spices, then roll in the breadcrumbs and let rest for 1 hour to harden.
8. Fry golden-brown in hot lard and place on absorbent paper to drain.
9. Serve with roasted meat.
