## Ingredients

- 1 hg. fresh apples
- 1 hg. malaga raisins
- 1 hg. sultana raisins
- 1 hg. currants
- 60 gr. preserved orange peel
- 3 dcl. maraschino liqueur
- 225 gr. kidney fat
- 225 gr. bread crumbs
- 120 gr. sugar
- juice and zest of 1/2 orange
- juice and zest of 1/4 lemon
- 10 gr. ground ginger
- 1/2 tsk. ground cinnamon
- 1/4 tsk. ground cardamom
- 1/4 tsk. ground cloves
- 2 eggs
- 2 dcl. milk
- 1 hg. flour
- 60 gr. sweet almond
- 8 cl. cognac or rum
- 1 dcl. porter
- 1 msk. butter (20 gr.) for the mold
- 2 msk. wheat flour (20 gr.) for the mold

## Directions

1. Peel and core the apples and chop finely. Clean, rinse well, split and pit the malaga raisins. Clean and rinse well the sultana raisins and currants. Cut the orange peel into fine dice.
2. If desired, soak all the fruit in the liqueur two days before the pudding is to be prepared.
3. Rinse the kidney fat, dry in cloth, clean and chop finely together with the wheat flour. Weigh bread crumbs, soak in milk and squeeze out. Finely grate the zest of orange and lemon, and press out the juice.
4. Work the kidney fat well with the bread crumbs, then add the fruit and all the other ingredients, and stir the mixture well.
5. Butter a large fireproof mold and dust with wheat flour, pack the mixture into it, and bind the mold with a buttered and floured napkin, which is tied in a knot on top of the pudding.
6. Cook the pudding thereafter for about 4 hours in water or steam.
7. Serve warm or cold with wine sauce or flaming sauce (see no. 457 and 465).
