## Ingredients

- 750 gr. veal filet or boneless veal loin
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.) for frying
- 1 dcl. broth
- 9 wheat slices
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.) for garnish
- 1 can asparagus tips (1/4 lit.)
- 1 tsk. chopped shallot onion
- 1/2 gr. white pepper
- 2 gr. salt
- 4 msk. good vinegar
- 5 egg yolks
- 180 gr. melted butter
- 20 gr. chopped tarragon stems
- cayenne
- 3 msk. good meat gravy or broth

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into 9 slices, which are flattened to half thickness and rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper.
2. Cut the edges from the bread slices, which should be 1 cm. thick, then fry them a nice golden brown in butter and place on a round plate, preferably of silver.
3. Warm the asparagus in a water bath.
4. Heat a frying pan, brown the butter therein, place the filets in and fry a nice brown or about 3 minutes on each side. Place them on the bread slices (croutons).
5. Deglaze the pan with broth, which is used for the béarnaise sauce or poured on the serving plate.
6. Pour the béarnaise sauce (see recipe 436) over the filets, garnish the arrangement with asparagus and parsley and serve very hot as a main or lunch course.
