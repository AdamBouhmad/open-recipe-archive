## Ingredients

- 1 fresh ox tongue
- water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 5 white peppercorns
- 1–2 eggs
- 1 part white French wine
- 1 liter of cooking liquid
- 8 sheets gelatin

## Directions

1. Let the ox tongue lie in cold water for 1 hour, then blanch in hot water and scrape well.
2. Place it in boiling water, skim carefully when it comes to a boil, and add the spices.
3. Let the tongue cook slowly for 2–3 hours, then remove and peel off the skin.
4. Strain the cooking liquid, skim it, and return it to the heat together with the rinsed gelatin dissolved in a little cold water, the wine, and the beaten eggs.
5. Bring the mixture to a good boil while stirring and strain through a napkin or cheesecloth.
6. Pour the clear aspic onto the bottom of a rinsed aspic mold and set out to set.
7. Cut the tongue into thin slices, which are arranged in a spiral in the mold until it is full.
8. Pour the remaining aspic carefully over, and set the aspic out to set.
