## Ingredients

- 50 small white or green cucumbers
- 26 grams cream of tartar
- 20 grams alum
- 375 grams coarse salt
- 5 1/2 liters water
- 6 deciliters spirit vinegar
- Cherry and currant leaves
- Dill heads

## Directions

1. Brush the cucumbers clean in cold water and let them soak in fresh water for a day.
2. Grind the cream of tartar, alum and salt, place them in the water and let it stand for a day.
3. Remove the cucumbers, drain them well and lay them on double linen to dry for about 3 hours.
4. Layer them in a large crock alternately with the leaves and dill heads. The cucumbers must not touch each other or the jar's edge, as they will then become spotted.
5. Pour the brine, mixed with vinegar, carefully over them; it should stand well above the cucumbers.
6. Place a weight on the crock, cover it with parchment paper and store it in a cellar.
7. After 3-4 weeks the cucumbers are ready.
8. Check frequently that the brine is sufficient and that no mold forms on the surface; if so, add more vinegar and dissolved salt.
