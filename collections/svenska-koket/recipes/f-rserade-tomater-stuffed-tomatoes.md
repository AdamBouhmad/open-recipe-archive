## Ingredients

- 6 large tomatoes
- 3 tbsp butter (60 gr.)
- 3 tbsp flour (60 gr.)
- the insides of the tomatoes
- 3 tbsp cream
- 3 tbsp mushroom paste
- 1/2 tsp finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 1 hg. calf sweetbread
- 1 can mushrooms (1/4 liter)
- 1/2 tbsp Madeira
- salt
- white pepper
- 2 tbsp butter (40 gr.) for the form
- 2 tbsp crushed hard biscuits for the form

## Directions

1. Rinse the tomatoes, dry them and cut out a lid at the stem. Hollow out the tomatoes with a teaspoon.
2. Fry half the butter with the flour, add the insides of the tomatoes, cream and mushroom paste together with the finely chopped onion, and let the sauce, which should be quite thick, boil for 5 minutes, then strain it through a sieve.
3. Blanch the calf sweetbread (see no. 47), place it in boiling salted water and let it boil for 15 minutes or until it is white and firm. When it has cooled, mince it and cut it into small cubes.
4. Cut the mushrooms likewise into cubes and fry them in the remaining butter together with the calf sweetbread.
5. Mix the mushrooms and sweetbread with the strained sauce and season with the wine and spices.
6. Fill the tomatoes with this mixture and place the lids on top. Put them in a buttered and breadcrumbed low pie form and top with small pieces of butter.
7. Finally sprinkle the rest of the crushed hard biscuits over and bake the tomatoes in the oven for about 1/2 hour.
8. Serve with the sandwich board or as a side dish.
