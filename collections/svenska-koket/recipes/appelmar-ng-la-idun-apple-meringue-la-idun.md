## Ingredients

- For the sponge cake: 2 eggs
- 120 g sugar
- 60 g wheat flour
- 4 bitter almonds
- For the pan: 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 g)
- 2 tablespoons crushed rusks
- For the apple compote: 2 hectograms sugar
- 4 deciliters water
- juice and peel of 1/4 lemon
- 1.5 liters apples
- For the meringue: 3 egg whites
- 105 g powdered sugar
- For garnish: 10 g pistachio almonds (about 22 pieces)
- 40 g sweet almonds (about 40 pieces)

## Directions

1. Butter a pan or skillet with the same width as the dish on which the meringue will be served and sprinkle it with crushed rusks.
2. Beat the eggs and sugar for half an hour, then add the flour and the blanched and grated almonds.
3. Fill the mixture into the buttered pan and bake the cake in regular oven heat for about 20 minutes.
4. Turn it out onto a cooling rack, let it cool, and place it afterwards on a round dish, preferably silver.
5. Prepare the apple compote as in recipe 820. If desired, cook the sugar syrup with the lemon.
6. When all the apples are cooked, boil down the syrup until it is clear as jelly.
7. When the apples are cold, arrange them in a pyramid on the cake together with the syrup, which should be like jelly.
8. Whip the egg whites until stiff peaks form. The powdered sugar, which should be sifted, is stirred in little by little with a silver fork into the egg white foam.
9. Spread a portion of the meringue immediately over the apples and cake.
10. Pipe the remaining meringue through a piping bag in small peaks around the edge of the cake.
11. Blanch the pistachio almonds, chop them finely and sprinkle evenly over the meringue.
12. Blanch the sweet almonds and cut them lengthwise into thin slices; press these thickly into the meringue and one slice in each peak.
13. Place the dish in gentle oven heat and let it stand until the meringue is half dry and has a light yellow color.
14. Serve cold with vanilla sauce or whipped cream.
