## Ingredients

- 1 1/4 liters fresh sauerkraut
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 kg. fresh pork
- 1 carrot
- 1 small red onion
- 1 clove (20 gr. juniper berries)
- 50 gr. lard
- 4 parsley stems
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 liters stock

## Directions

1. Fresh sauerkraut should not be soaked in water. If the sauerkraut is old, it should be soaked for about 12 hours, and thereafter it should drain well in a sieve before it is prepared.
2. Mix the cabbage with salt and pepper.
3. Cut the pork into slices, and line the bottom and sides of an iron pot with it.
4. Add the cabbage together with the cleaned carrot cut into four pieces, the peeled onion with the clove inserted into it, juniper berries (wrapped in muslin), if desired, the lard and the parsley stems, tied together with the bay leaf.
5. Lay the remaining pork slices over top, add the stock so that it stands above the cabbage.
6. Let the cabbage come to a boil, then place the pot in a warm oven, and let the preparation simmer covered for about 5 hours.
7. Remove the carrot, onion, juniper berries and parsley bundle.
8. Taste and adjust seasoning, arrange on a warm platter, garnish with boiled frankfurter sausage and smoked ham or boiled pork.
