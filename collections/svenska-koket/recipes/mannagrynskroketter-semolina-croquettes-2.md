## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 dcl. semolina (114 gr.)
- 1 msk. butter (20 gr.)
- 7 dcl. milk
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 1 tsk. salt (5 gr.)
- 1 tsk. sugar (5 gr.)
- 1-2 tsk. grated cheese
- 1 msk. melted butter for breading (20 gr.)
- 1 msk. crushed rusks for breading (1/2 msk. grated cheese)

## Directions

1. Sift the semolina, rinse with cold water and let drain. Grease a saucepan with 1/2 msk. of the butter, pour in the milk and bring to a boil. Whisk in the semolina, then stir the porridge with a spoon until it thickens. Move it to a weaker heat and let it simmer slowly for about 20 minutes, stirring it often. Pour out the porridge, blend immediately with the rest of the butter and let it cool. Then add the whisked eggs, seasonings and grated cheese. Grease a flat dish with butter, spread the mixture on it about 2 cm. thick and let it stand 2-3 hours on ice to cool. With a round measure, cut out small round cakes, place them on a buttered baking sheet, brush with butter, sprinkle with a mixture of the crushed rusks and grated cheese and bake a beautiful golden brown in a warm oven. Serve with roasted meat, e.g. venison.
2. Divide the cooled porridge mass into smaller parts—each part should weigh about 35 gr.—which are rolled oblong on a floured work surface. Dip the croquettes in the stiffly whisked eggs, mixed with oil and seasoned with spices, then roll them in the crushed and sifted rusks and let them stand 1 hour to harden. Fry them golden brown in hot fat and place on absorbent paper to drain. Serve with roasted meat.
