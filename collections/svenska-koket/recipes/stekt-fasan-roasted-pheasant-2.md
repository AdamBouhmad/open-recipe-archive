## Ingredients

- 2 cock pheasants
- 2 hg. lard
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 gr.)
- 4 tbsp. flour (40 gr.) for sauce
- 5 dcl. meat broth or stock
- 5 dcl. thick cream
- Salt
- Sugar
- White pepper

## Directions

1. The meat of the cock pheasant is considered finer than that of the hen and is therefore preferred for roasting. The plumage is magnificent and is used by many for decoration. The bird should hang a few days before it is prepared, so that the meat becomes juicy and develops the fine flavor of game that is lacking in the freshly slaughtered bird. When it is to be cleaned, the head with neck, wings and tail are first saved. The bird is then plucked and treated as described in the poultry introduction, larded on the breast and thighs with finely cut lard strips and trussed. The birds are then browned in the butter together with the giblets in a roasting pan, which should not be larger than just enough to hold them. When the birds are well browned, they are removed together with the giblets. The butter in the pan is fried with the flour for 2 minutes, the broth and cream are thinned in and the sauce is allowed to boil for 5 minutes, after which it is seasoned with the spices. The birds are placed in with the breast up and allowed to roast slowly until tender, for 1 to 1 1/2 hours.
