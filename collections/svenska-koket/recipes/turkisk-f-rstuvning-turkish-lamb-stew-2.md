## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg lamb breast
- 0.5 tablespoon salt (8 gr.)
- 0.5 teaspoon white pepper
- 6 tablespoons butter (120 gr.)
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped red onion
- 3-4 tablespoons tomato purée
- 4 deciliters brown sauce
- 3 deciliters broth
- 1 small sprig thyme
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 small leek
- 2 carrots
- 2-3 sugar beets
- 1 celery root
- 1 Portuguese onion
- Rice: 2 deciliters rice grains (200 gr.)
- 2 teaspoons finely chopped red onion
- 1 gram powdered saffron
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 8 deciliters boiling broth
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)
- 0.25 teaspoon white pepper
- 2 tablespoons cold butter (40 gr.)

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into even, beautiful pieces, which are rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper and browned in half of the butter together with the onion.
2. The tomato purée and brown sauce are diluted and the meat is left to simmer slowly about 45 minutes.
3. Transfer to another pot, strain the sauce, skim, dilute with broth and add.
4. Tie together the thyme, bay leaf and leek and add.
5. Trim the carrots, beets and celery, cut into beautiful pieces and brown in the remaining butter together with the chopped Portuguese onion.
6. Add the vegetables to the meat, which then simmers covered about 30 minutes in the oven.
7. Test if the meat is tender.
8. Rinse the rice grains first with cold and then with boiling water and let drain well.
9. They are then sautéed in butter together with onion and saffron in an iron pot.
10. Dilute the broth, place a butter-brushed paper over and let the grains cook in the oven about 20 minutes.
11. Mix well with the cold butter and spices and arrange in a ring on a warm serving dish.
12. Place the meat in the middle of the dish and pour the sauce over.
13. Garnish with parsley and serve very hot.
14. Instead of saffron, curry can be used.
