## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg. sirloin or flank
- 2 tsp. salt (10 g)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 1/2 hg. fatback, in flat slices
- 1/4 tsp. ginger
- Marinade: 2 red onions
- 2 carrots
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 g)
- 4 parsley stems
- 1 bay leaf
- 6 dcl. red or white wine
- 50 g. fresh, blanched pork rinds
- For roasting: 1 1/2 hg. drippings
- 1 liter brown broth

## Directions

1. The meat is lightly pounded with a damp mallet, dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and rubbed with the spices.
2. The onion and carrots are roasted, sliced, which are browned in butter, and half of them together with the parsley are laid in a deep dish, the meat is laid on top and the rest of the roots, the parsley, bay leaf and pork rinds are laid on top of the meat.
3. The wine is added and the roast is allowed to lie in this marinade for about 6 hours, during which time it is turned 3—4 times.
4. It is taken out, allowed to drip, dried well with a cloth, covered with fatback slices and tied so that it gets a beautiful shape.
5. The drippings are heated in an iron pot, the roast is browned in it on all sides until a beautiful brown crust is formed, then it is removed.
6. The drippings are discarded and the pot is cleaned well with white paper. The marinade is poured into the pot, the meat is laid on top, the lid is placed and the wine is allowed to boil rapidly until it gets the consistency of syrup.
7. Then the boiling broth is added, which should be level with the meat (the pot should not be larger than that the roast just fits neatly), the lid is placed on and the roast is allowed to roast slowly for 2 1/2—3 hours, during which time it is turned several times but is not moistened.
8. It is laid on a warm platter, garnished with parsley or kale and sliced at table. Served with vegetables, browned potatoes and salads or potato pastries.
9. The sauce is served as gravy or reduced (see no. 429).
