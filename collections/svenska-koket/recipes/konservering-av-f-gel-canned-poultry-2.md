## Ingredients

- Poultry
- Butter
- Bouillon or water
- Salt

## Directions

1. Brown the poultry in butter and then roast it until it is almost tender.
2. During roasting, add liquid, preferably bouillon, otherwise water (never milk or cream).
3. When roasted, remove it and divide it into pieces.
4. First cut off the thighs and wings, then separate the breastbone with attached meat from the rest of the carcass, which is not used in the preservation.
5. Place the divided poultry in a well-cleaned tin canning jar, arranging the pieces as closely together as possible.
6. Strain the meat gravy (but do not skim off the fat) and pour it over the meat so that it is completely covered.
7. When the fat solidifies, it forms an airtight protective layer.
8. Screw or solder the canning jars shut and place them in a pot of boiling salted water.
9. The water should stand well above the jars.
10. If it bubbles at the lid, this is a sign that the jar is not airtight; remove it and screw it tighter or solder it again.
11. Continue cooking for 1/2 to 1 hour, depending on the size of the jar.
12. Remove the jars and cool them quickly.
13. Store in a cold place.
