## Ingredients

- 2 kg short-cut saddle
- 1.5 hectograms lard
- 1/2 tablespoon salt (8 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 3 deciliters broth
- 3 deciliters cream

## Directions

1. With a sharp knife, remove all membranes on the back without any meat being carried along.
2. The saddle is then larded with finely cut strips of lard, dipped in a mixture of salt and pepper, placed in a long pan, covered with butter, distributed in flakes, put in a warm oven and left for 15 minutes.
3. It is then extended with the boiling broth and cream and allowed to roast until it is tender, or about 1 1/2 hours, during which it is basted every 10 minutes, otherwise it becomes dry.
4. The saddle is cut like mutton saddle, garnished with browned potatoes, small tartlets filled with redcurrant jelly, or with vegetable salad in small shells of raw white cabbage leaves.
5. Served with reduced sauce, supplemented with 1 tablespoon redcurrant jelly.
6. Chops can be cut from the saddle, which are roasted like veal chops, with or without breading.
7. Served with stewed mushrooms or champignons, potato croquettes or chestnut puree.
