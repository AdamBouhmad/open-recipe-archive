## Ingredients

- Fish
- Salt (2 tsp. (10 gr.) per kg. fish)
- Cold water
- Coarse salt (7 gr. per liter of water)
- Parsley for garnish
- Sauce made from the cooking liquid or other fine sauce

## Directions

1. If the fish is large and will be cooked whole, treat it as previously described and rub it with salt (2 tsp. (10 gr.) per kg. fish), then place it on a sieve in a fish kettle and add enough cold water so that it just covers the fish.
2. If a fish kettle is not available, turn a tub upside down on the bottom of another kettle and spread a coarse towel over it, in which the fish is placed; it can then be easily lifted up with the towel when it is done, without breaking.
3. Measure the water before pouring it into the kettle and add 7 gr. coarse salt for each liter of water. Place the lid on the kettle, let the water come to a rapid boil and skim well, then move the kettle to the side of the stove and finish cooking the fish while the water only simmers gently, 15-20 minutes per kg. of fish.
4. Fish varieties with loose flesh tolerate even shorter cooking time and require great attention during cooking. If the fish is small or cut into slices, place it in boiling, salted water and skim, then move the kettle to the side and finish cooking the fish as mentioned above.
5. As soon as the fish is done, arrange it on a platter with a sieve or on a napkin, garnish with parsley and serve with sauce made from the cooking liquid, or with some finer sauce as indicated in each recipe.
