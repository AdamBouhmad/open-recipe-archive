## Ingredients

- Calf forcemeat: 3 hg. boneless veal
- 50 gr. butter
- 1 egg
- 2 1/2 dcl. thick cream
- 2 tsp. salt
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 1 1/2 tbsp. flour (15 gr.)
- Chicken ragout: 1 young chicken or chicken leftovers
- 1 liter water
- 1/2 tbsp. salt
- 1 1/2 tbsp. butter (30 gr.)
- 3 tbsp. flour (30 gr.)
- 3 dcl. chicken broth
- salt
- white pepper
- 1 tbsp. madeira
- 2 tsp. tomato purée
- For the mold: 1 1/2 tbsp. butter (30 gr.)
- 30 gr. truffle
- 50 gr. salted ox tongue

## Directions

1. Butter a timbal mold well with cold butter and garnish it with the tongue and truffle, cut into beautiful figures.
2. Prepare the calf forcemeat as in recipe no. 68 and line the garnished mold with forcemeat.
3. Press the forcemeat against the tongue with a spoon dipped in hot water.
4. Reserve a little of the forcemeat for a lid.
5. Pluck the chicken, singe it, remove the innards, cut off the neck, feet and wing tips and rinse it very well.
6. Put it in boiling water with the salt, skim it and let it cook until it is tender.
7. Loosen the meat from the bones and cut it into beautiful slices.
8. Strain and skim the broth.
9. Fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, add 3 dcl. of the broth, let the sauce cook 5 minutes, then season it with the seasonings, wine and tomato purée.
10. When the sauce is cold, blend in the meat, and fill the ragout into the lined mold.
11. Cover it with the rest of the forcemeat and cook the timbal in a water bath in the oven for about 1 hour.
12. If the timbal browns on top, cover it with a butter-brushed paper.
13. Turn the timbal out, garnish with salad and tomatoes and serve as a course between meals with tomato sauce, prepared from the rest of the chicken broth.
