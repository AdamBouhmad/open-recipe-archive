## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg pike = 200 gr scraped fish meat
- 100 gr butter
- 20 gr flour
- 2 dcl cream
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 1/2 tbsp salt, 1 tsp sugar
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- 300 gr shrimp or 1 can lobster (1/2 liter)
- 6 blanched oysters
- 8 small mushrooms
- 3 truffles
- 1 tbsp butter (20 gr)
- 3 balls crayfish butter (30 gr)
- 5 tbsp flour (50 gr)
- 4 dcl cream
- 2 egg yolks
- salt, sugar, white pepper

## Directions

1. Clean the fish, rinse well, cut into fillets and remove the skin.
2. Beat the fillets with a damp meat mallet so they become even, rub with salt and vinegar and let rest 1 hour.
3. For the forcemeat, take the pike, rinse well and dry with a fish towel. Fillet it, scrape the fish meat finely, no scales or bones must be included.
4. Mix the fish meat with butter and pass 3 times through a meat grinder.
5. Pound the mixture in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until it is fine and cohesive.
6. In a bowl, mix flour, cream, eggs and seasonings. Work this mixture into the forcemeat by spoonfuls, stirring for about 1/2 hour.
7. Butter a smooth timbale mold holding about 1 1/2 liters well. Wrap the fish fillets in a fish towel and line the mold with them, placing them side by side with their tails around the bottom and opposite ends protruding above the edge.
8. Press them well so they take the shape of the mold. Cover the fillets with a layer of forcemeat about 2 cm thick.
9. Place the mold in regular oven heat and let stand 10-15 minutes or until the forcemeat has set to the fillets.
10. Cut off the protruding fillet pieces and fill the timbale with the stew to about 3 cm from the upper edge.
11. Place the cut fish pieces on top of the stew and cover the timbale with a layer of forcemeat.
12. Bake about 45 minutes in a water bath in regular oven heat.
13. After the timbale is removed from the oven, let it rest 10 minutes, then turn out onto a round dish, remove the mold and garnish the top with shrimp tails and truffle slices and around with parsley or salad.
14. For the stew: clean the shrimp; if using lobster, cut it into pieces. Cut mushrooms and truffles into slices and sauté in butter. Sauté the crayfish butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the cream and let the sauce simmer 5 minutes.
15. Add the shrimp, oysters and mushrooms and bring the stew to a boil, add the whisked egg yolks and season with seasonings.
16. Serve with crayfish sauce as an entrée.
