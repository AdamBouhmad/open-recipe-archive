## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg inner loin or chuck steak
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 1/4 tsk ginger
- 1 1/2 hg lard, in flat slices
- 2 red onions
- 2 carrots
- 2 tbsp butter (40 gr.)
- 4 parsley stems
- 1 bay leaf
- 6 dcl red or white wine
- 50 gr fresh, blanched pork broth
- 1 1/2 hg lard for frying
- 1 liter brown broth

## Directions

1. Lightly pound the meat with a damp mallet, dry it with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and rub it with the spices.
2. Peel and slice the onion and carrots, brown them in butter, and place half of them along with the parsley in a deep dish.
3. Place the meat on top and arrange the remaining vegetables, parsley, bay leaf, and pork broth on top of the meat.
4. Pour the wine over and let the roast marinate for about 6 hours, turning it 3-4 times.
5. Remove the meat, let it drain, dry it well with a cloth, cover it with lard slices and tie it so it has a nice shape.
6. Heat the lard in a cast iron pot, brown the roast on all sides until a nice brown crust forms, then remove it.
7. Pour out the lard and wipe the pot well with white paper.
8. Pour the marinade into the pot, place the meat on top, cover, and let the wine reduce rapidly until it has the consistency of syrup.
9. Add the boiling broth, which should reach the level of the meat (the pot should not be larger than necessary to fit the roast), cover and let the roast braise slowly for 2 1/2-3 hours, turning it several times but do not add liquid.
10. Place on a warm platter, garnish with parsley or kale, and slice at the table.
11. Serve with vegetables, browned potatoes, salads or potato pastries.
12. Strain the sauce, skim well, reduce if too thin or dilute if too thick, and add a little cream.
