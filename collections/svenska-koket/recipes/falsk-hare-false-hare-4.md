## Ingredients

- 3 hg. boneless beef
- 2 hg. fresh pork belly
- 1 dcl. crushed rusks
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 2-3 dcl. cream or milk
- 50 g. lard
- 1 egg white for brushing
- 2 tbsp. crushed rusks for coating
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 g) for frying
- 5 dcl. broth for frying

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung in hot water, cut into dice and run 6 times through the meat grinder.
2. Blend the ground meat with the crushed rusks, spices, beaten egg and egg yolk and stir vigorously for 1 hour, adding the cream tablespoon by tablespoon.
3. Test the forcemeat by frying a small ball of it to check if it is sufficiently seasoned and of proper firmness.
4. Scrape and cut the lard into fine strips.
5. Grease a long pan with cold butter, add the forcemeat and shape with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water into a ball, flattening it on both sides so that it resembles a hare's back, and lard it densely with the lard strips.
6. Brush the hare with beaten egg white, sprinkle with crushed rusks and roast in the oven as in recipe no. 632.
7. Cut into slices, garnish with parsley and fried potatoes.
8. Serve with brown sauce and aspic or preserved rowan berries.
