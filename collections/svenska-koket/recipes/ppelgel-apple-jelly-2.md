## Ingredients

- 5 litres apples
- 2 litres water
- 3/4–1 kg. granulated sugar

## Directions

1. The apples should be firm and juicy; dry them well with a cloth; remove the stems and blossom ends, but do not peel them.
2. Cut them into sections and cook with the water on a very low heat until they begin to fall apart.
3. Stir occasionally during cooking.
4. Pour the apple pulp into cheesecloth and let it drip naturally as long as any juice falls from the cloth.
5. Weigh the juice, return it to the pot, and add the sugar.
6. Bring the juice slowly to a boil and then let it cook slowly until the jelly becomes so thick that a drop on a plate does not run out.
7. Skim the jelly well during cooking and immediately pour into warm jars rinsed with brandy.
