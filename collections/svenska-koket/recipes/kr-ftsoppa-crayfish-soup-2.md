## Ingredients

- 3 dozen large or 4 dozen small crayfish
- 6 liters boiling water
- 1 hectogram coarse salt
- 3 dill stalks
- 150 grams butter
- 3.5 liters fish stock or clear stock
- 90 grams flour
- salt
- sugar
- cayenne
- 0.5–1 deciliter Madeira or sherry
- 2–3 egg yolks
- 1 deciliter thick cream
- 1 can green asparagus tips (0.25 liter)

## Directions

1. Rinse the crayfish, place them in the water with salt added, and let them cook 10–15 minutes, depending on size, and should preferably cool in the cooking liquid.
2. Remove the meat from the tails, remove the intestine and cut the tails in half lengthwise, then serve them in the finished soup.
3. Remove the stomach from the shells and pound these very finely in a mortar together with the butter, then place the mixture in a pot and let it fry over the heat while stirring for 10 minutes, then add the cold stock.
4. Let the soup cook slowly with a lid for about half an hour. Pass it through a sieve and let it stand for a quarter hour so that the crayfish butter has time to float to the surface. Skim off the butter and fry it with the flour for 2 minutes, add the stock little by little, and let the soup cook for 5 minutes, then season it with the spices and wine.
5. Whisk the egg yolks and cream in the soup bowl and pour the soup in while whisking. Add the crayfish tails along with the asparagus tips, which have been warmed in a water bath, and serve the soup very hot.
