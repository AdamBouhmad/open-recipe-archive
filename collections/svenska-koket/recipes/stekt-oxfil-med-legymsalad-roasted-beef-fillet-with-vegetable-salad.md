## Ingredients

- 1 kg. beef fillet
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 1 tsp. salt
- a little white pepper
- 1/2 liter brown broth
- 1 dcl. madeira
- 1 12-öre shaped bread
- 1 small head white cabbage
- vegetable salad
- parsley

## Directions

1. The fillet, which should have hung until properly tender, is trimmed (sinews and membranes removed), dried with a cloth wrung in hot water, and tied so it takes a nice form.
2. Brown the butter in an iron pot not larger than needed to fit the meat, place the fillet in and brown well on all sides, sprinkle with a mixture of salt and pepper.
3. Place in a hot oven, dilute with the boiling broth and wine, and roast 30–40 minutes, basting frequently.
4. Remove the roast, cut the string, and slice the fillet into thin slices, which are laid back together so it retains its form, and placed on a 4 cm. high base cut from the wheat bread.
5. The base should have the same length as the fillet and can, if desired, be browned in aspic or covered with tinfoil.
6. Around the base garnish with small shells cut from raw cabbage leaves, filled alternately with vegetable salad and browned potatoes.
7. Serve with meat gravy, strained and skimmed, or with truffle sauce (see no. 433).
