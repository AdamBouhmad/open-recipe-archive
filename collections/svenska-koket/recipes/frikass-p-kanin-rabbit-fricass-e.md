## Ingredients

- 1 unflayed rabbit, approximately 3 kg
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 2 teaspoons finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped parsley
- 1 can mushrooms (1/4 litre)
- 5 decilitres broth or water
- 1/2 decilitre white wine

## Directions

1. The rabbit, which has been hung so the meat is tender, is skinned and treated as hare, chopped into smaller pieces, which are rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper.
2. Cut the lard into slices and place some of it in the bottom of a pot.
3. Place the meat in it; between each layer of meat sprinkle the flour, onion, parsley and the mushrooms cut into slices.
4. Place the rest of the lard on top, then add the broth and wine and let the meat simmer slowly with the lid on until it is tender, approximately 1 1/4 hours.
5. Place it on a warm platter, whisk up the sauce, strain it, skim it, taste it and pour over the meat.
6. Serve with boiled potatoes.
