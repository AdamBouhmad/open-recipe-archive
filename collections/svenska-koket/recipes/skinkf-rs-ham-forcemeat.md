## Ingredients

- 2 hg. smoked ham
- 3 hg. boneless veal
- 1/4 tsk. white pepper
- 2 hg. bread panada
- 2 whole eggs, 2 egg yolks
- 3-4 dcl. thick cream
- 1/2 msk. finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 1/2 msk. butter (10 gr.) for the onion
- 1 egg white for brushing
- 2 msk. crushed rusks for brushing
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.) for frying
- 1/2 liter boiling broth or water with 1/2 tsk. solid meat extract

## Directions

1. Dry the ham and meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into cubes, removing all sinews and membranes carefully.
2. Scrape the fat and cut into pieces; if using kidney fat, chop it finely.
3. Mix the meat with the fat or kidney fat and the spices and pass 6 times through a meat grinder.
4. Pound the forcemeat in a stone mortar together with the panada or flour until smooth and supple, then place in a bowl and add the whipped egg and egg yolk under vigorous stirring.
5. Stir the forcemeat for about 3/4 hour while gradually adding the cream or milk.
6. Test the forcemeat; if too stiff, add a little more cream, otherwise add an egg white.
7. If desired, add last the onion fried in butter.
8. Grease a long pan with cold butter, place the forcemeat in it and shape as an oblong roll.
9. Brush with egg white and sprinkle with crushed rusks.
10. Place in the oven and brown, frequently basting with the butter in the pan.
11. When browned, add the boiling broth or water with meat extract, and let finish cooking, which takes about 1/2 hour.
12. During cooking, baste every 10 minutes.
13. Place on a warm platter, cut into thin slices, garnish with parsley and serve with the whipped, strained meat gravy or with reduced sauce.
