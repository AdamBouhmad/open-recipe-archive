## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon butter for hachi
- 1.5 tablespoons flour for hachi
- 4 cups bouillon for hachi
- white pepper for hachi
- 4 cups cooked or fried chopped meat for hachi
- 1 egg yolk for hachi
- 2.5 cups flour for pancake batter
- 1/2 teaspoon salt for pancake batter
- 2 eggs for pancake batter
- 6 cups unskinned milk for pancake batter
- 2 tablespoons butter for pancake batter
- salt for pancake batter
- 2 eggs for egg custard
- 2 cups milk for egg custard
- salt and sugar for egg custard

## Directions

1. Melt butter and flour together, dilute with bouillon, and let the sauce cook for a few minutes. Add the meat, bring the stew to a boil and season. Lastly stir in the egg yolk.
2. Prepare the pancake batter in the usual way, and make thin pancakes from it, which are cooked only on one side. Lay them with the uncooked side up on a deep dish or in a fireproof mold.
3. Spread an appropriate thick layer of hachi on each pancake; place the next pancake on top, but with the cooked side up. Continue until the mold is full.
4. Pour the egg custard over the pancakes, and place the pudding in the oven to set and achieve a beautiful light golden color.
5. If desired, the egg custard may be omitted. Serve with meat gravy or clarified butter.
