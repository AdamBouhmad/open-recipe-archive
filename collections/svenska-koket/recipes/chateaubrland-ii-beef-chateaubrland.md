## Ingredients

- 6 hg. beef tenderloin
- 3 tsk. salt
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.)
- 1 small bunch parsley
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.) for parsley butter

## Directions

1. Dry the tenderloin with a cloth wrung out in hot water. Carefully remove all outer tendons and membranes, then cut the tenderloin into 6 slices, which are lightly beaten with a damp mallet and rubbed on both sides with the spice mixture.
2. Melt 1 msk. of the butter and brush the steaks with it, then let them rest for 15 minutes (fine cooking oil can be used instead of butter).
3. For the parsley butter: rinse the parsley, wrap it in a cloth, clean it, chop it finely, wring it hard in a cloth and work it well into the butter. Form this in a damp cloth into a roll of a finger's thickness and let it lie in the cloth on ice or in a cold place until the butter is hard.
4. Heat a frying pan slowly, brown the remaining butter in it, place the steaks in it, let them fry 3-4 minutes on each side and place them on a hot plate, and pour the butter from the pan over them.
5. Place a slice of parsley butter on each steak. Serve immediately as a breakfast dish with fried potatoes.
