## Ingredients

- 1.2 kg pike
- 5 hectograms scraped fish meat
- 250 g butter
- 4 eggs
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 4 decilitres thick cream
- 2 decilitres thin cream
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 2 teaspoons sugar (10 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 tablespoon butter or 1 ball crayfish butter for the mold (10 g)
- 2 tablespoons finely crushed breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Do not scale the pike, clean it as described on page 171, rinse well, dry with a fish towel, fillet it and remove the backbone
2. Scrape the fish meat finely, ensuring no scales or bones are included, weigh it, mix with cold butter and pass through a meat grinder 4-5 times
3. Beat the mass in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until it is fine and cohesive
4. In a bowl, whip the egg yolks, flour, cream and seasonings together
5. Work this mixture into the forcemeat a tablespoon at a time, stirring the mass for about 1 hour
6. Then pass the forcemeat through a strainer
7. Finally fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks and taste the forcemeat carefully
8. Fill it into a well-buttered and breadcrumb-lined fish forcemeat mold holding about 2 litres
9. Cover with the lid and cook the forcemeat in a water bath in the oven or on top of the stove
