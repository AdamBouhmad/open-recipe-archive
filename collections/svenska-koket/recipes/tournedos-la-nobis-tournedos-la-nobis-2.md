## Ingredients

- 3 hg. beef tenderloin
- salt
- white pepper
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.)
- 9 asparagus tips
- 3 slices truffle
- 1/2 tsk. chopped shallot onion
- 30 gr. chopped tarragon stems
- 30 gr. chervil
- 2 gr. salt
- some white pepper
- 2 msk. vinegar
- 3 egg yolks
- 80 gr. melted butter
- cayenne

## Directions

1. Cut the beef tenderloin into 1 1/2 cm. thick slices, which are lightly flattened, formed round, seasoned and fried in butter or braised.
2. Place the steaks in small fireproof, round molds, garnish with the asparagus tips, braised in some of the butter.
3. Pour the Bearnaise sauce over and place a slice of truffle on each steak. Serve as a breakfast dish with fried potatoes, sprinkled with finely chopped parsley.
4. For the sauce: place the seasonings and vinegar in a sauce pan. Boil the vinegar until one third remains, then lift the pan from the heat and let the contents cool slightly, then whisk in the egg yolks, one at a time.
5. Place the pan back over the heat and let the sauce simmer, then add the butter little by little with whisking. Strain the sauce through a cloth and mix with a hint of cayenne and finely chopped parsley. Heat it in a water bath until it is lukewarm and serve immediately. If it is heated too much it will break.
