## Ingredients

- 1 kg rye sifted flour
- 75 gr wheat flour
- 1 liter skimmed milk
- 50 gr pressed yeast
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr)
- 50 gr butter, margarine or lard
- 3 dcl syrup
- 2 tablespoons crushed fennel
- 3 tablespoons finely chopped bitter orange peel

## Directions

1. Make a dough from the rye flour, some of the wheat flour, the lukewarm milk, and the yeast mixed with salt, as described on page 462, and let the dough rise to double its size.
2. Warm the syrup slightly in a pot together with the fat and spices and work into the dough along with enough wheat flour to make the dough quite stiff.
3. Let the dough rise again, then work it well with the rest of the wheat flour on the baking board and divide into 6-8 parts.
4. Work each part until it is smooth and without cracks, then roll it into an oblong roll, which is placed on a warm, soft baking cloth with a baking cloth laid on it which is floured.
5. Place the rolls next to each other with the baking cloth drawn up in a large fold between each roll and cover.
6. When the rolls are risen, turn them out onto a bread peel, place on greased, lukewarm baking sheets, nick along the length with a skewer, brush with cold milk and bake in a not too hot oven for about 1/2 hour.
7. When the rolls are light, remove them, brush with hot water and place between cloths so the crust becomes soft.
8. The dough can be mixed with cold liquid the evening before it is to be baked.
