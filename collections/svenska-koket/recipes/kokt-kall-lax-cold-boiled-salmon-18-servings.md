## Ingredients

- Broth: 5 liters water
- 2.5 deciliters vinegar
- 70 grams coarse salt
- 2 carrots
- 1 red onion
- 2 bay leaves
- 6 dill sprigs
- 12 white peppercorns
- 1 salmon 3 kg
- 23 grams fine salt
- White aspic: 1 liter fish broth
- 10 sheets white gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 1 tablespoon white wine
- Red aspic: 4 deciliters fish broth
- 4 sheets red gelatin
- 1 egg white
- 1 tablespoon white wine
- For garnish: mayonnaise, capers or truffle, beets, tomatoes, lettuce, parsley, hard-boiled eggs

## Directions

1. Cook the broth and prepare the salmon as in the previous recipe.
2. If the salmon is to be served on two platters, split it lengthwise into two halves and carefully remove the bone. Place the two halves with the bone in the cold broth and cook as in recipe 327.
3. Let the salmon cool in the broth.
4. From the broth prepare white and red aspic as in recipe 181.
5. Let a little of the white fish aspic set on the bottom of the platters where the salmon will be served. Place the salmon on top.
6. Remove the skin in 3 cm wide strips across the fish, leaving equal-width strips of skin in between.
7. Where the skin has been removed, spread mayonnaise mixed with a little white fish aspic and let it set. Garnish the mayonnaise with capers or truffle in attractive figures, such as fleurs-de-lis or royal crowns.
8. Garnish the remaining strips with white and red fish aspic piped through a fine decorating tube, and small slices of beets or tomatoes.
9. Garnish the platter with lettuce, parsley, hollowed-out tomatoes filled with vegetable salad, egg halves with decorated edges and aspic.
10. Serve with mayonnaise or remoulade sauce.
