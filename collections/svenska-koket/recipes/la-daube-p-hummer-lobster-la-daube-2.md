## Ingredients

- 3 lobsters (400 g each)
- 5 liters water
- 200 g coarse salt
- 1 liter good fish stock or light meat stock
- 10 gelatin leaves
- 2 egg whites and rinsed shells
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 3 tablespoons good white wine
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 1 truffle
- parsley
- salad

## Directions

1. Cook the lobsters as in recipe no. 170. Cut the meat into beautiful pieces.
2. Prepare the aspic as follows: pour the stock into a pot together with the rinsed gelatin and the egg whites lightly beaten with water, mixed with the crushed eggshells.
3. Whip the mixture to a good boil, cover the pot, remove from heat and let stand beside the stove for approximately 15 minutes.
4. Strain the stock through a cloth or napkin wrung out in hot water. If the first liquid that runs through is not clear, pour it back into the cloth. Repeat this until the aspic is completely clear.
5. When cool, add the wine.
6. Pour a little of the clear aspic onto the bottom of a rinsed, beautiful à-la-daube mold and place on ice to set.
7. Garnish the bottom with the hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into beautiful shapes, and parsley.
8. Sprinkle the garnish with a few drops of aspic, let set so the garnish is not disturbed when the lobster is added.
9. Arrange the most beautiful lobster pieces on the bottom and sides, fill the mold with the remaining lobster meat.
10. Pour the remaining aspic over and place on ice to set.
11. When ready to serve, dip the mold quickly in a pot of hot water, dry it and turn out onto a round platter.
12. Garnish the à-la-daube with chopped aspic, salad and parsley and serve at the smörgåsbord or as a course with real mayonnaise.
