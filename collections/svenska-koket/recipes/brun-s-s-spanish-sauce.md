## Ingredients

- 2 shallot onions
- 1 carrot
- 5 parsley stems
- 50 g lean smoked ham
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 1 liter previously prepared meat stock
- 1 dcl white wine
- 5 tbsp flour (50 g)
- 6 dcl good meat stock from the roast
- Salt and white pepper
- 1/2-1 dcl Madeira wine

## Directions

1. Clean and cut the onions and carrot into pieces, clean the parsley stems, rinse them, wring them in a cloth, cut the ham into cubes and brown everything lightly in 1 tbsp of the butter
2. Add the previously prepared meat stock and white wine and let simmer slowly with a lid for about 2 hours
3. Strain the liquid through a fine sieve, skim and measure—it should yield 8 dcl
4. Fry the remaining butter with the flour for 2 minutes, gradually dilute with the stock cooked with vegetables and the skimmed meat stock from the roast
5. Season with salt, white pepper and Madeira wine and let simmer while stirring for 15 minutes
6. Season with more Madeira if desired
7. If this sauce is to be used with game, omit the white wine and season with 1-2 tbsp black or red currant jelly
