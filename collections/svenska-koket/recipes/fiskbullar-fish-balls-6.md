## Ingredients

- 7 hg. saithe (or 250 gr. scraped fish meat)
- 80 gr. butter
- 1 msk. salt (15 gr.)
- 2 eggs
- 1 1/2 msk. potato flour (23 gr.)
- 4-5 dcl. good milk
- 1/4 tsk. white pepper
- 2 tsk. sugar (10 gr.)
- mace (optional)
- 1 1/2 lit. fish stock for cooking

## Directions

1. Gut the fish, rinse well and dry it.
2. Cut off the head, remove the gills; fillet the fish and remove the backbone.
3. Scrape all meat from the bones and scrape the remaining fish meat finely.
4. Weigh the meat, which should be 250 gr.
5. Make stock from the head, bones and skin, in which the balls will subsequently be cooked.
6. Mix the fish meat with butter and pass 5 times through a meat grinder.
7. Pound the mixture in a mortar with a wooden pestle together with the salt.
8. Fold in the beaten eggs and flour and pound again until the mixture becomes fine and cohesive.
9. Work the forcemeat with a spoon for 3/4 hour, while gradually adding the milk in tablespoon portions.
10. Season with salt, sugar and, if desired, a pinch of mace.
11. Form with 2 tablespoons dipped in lukewarm water into small round balls.
12. Cook the balls in the fish stock for 3-4 minutes.
13. Test the forcemeat first by cooking a small sample; if it is too firm, add a little more milk.
