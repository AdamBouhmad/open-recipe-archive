## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 parts thick cream, approximately 180 g flour
- 1/2 weight of butter to the dough (approximately 165 g) for the butter dough
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 g) for the mold
- 1 sheet of parchment paper
- 2 liters peas
- 1/2 dcl. rice grains (150 g)
- 1-1 1/4 liters milk
- 3 eggs
- 2 tsp. salt (10 g)
- 5 hg. boneless, roasted meat
- 1 medium pickled cucumber or 12 pickled onions
- 2 tsp. salt (10 g)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 3 dcl. roasting sauce or 2 dcl. broth

## Directions

1. Whip the cream to stiff peaks and work in enough flour to make a fairly firm dough.
2. Weigh the dough and incorporate half the weight of butter as in regular butter dough.
3. After the dough has rested, roll it out to approximately 1 cm thickness and line a fluted or smooth baking tin (buttered) holding approximately 2 liters, and cut out a lid.
4. Cover the butter dough with thin parchment paper and fill the mold with peas.
5. Place in a hot oven and bake until halfway done.
6. Remove the peas and parchment paper.
7. Rinse the rice grains, scald in hot water, add to the boiling milk and cook until tender.
8. Cool while stirring frequently.
9. Mix with egg yolks and salt, then fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
10. Cut the meat into thin, even slices or fine cubes.
11. Finely chop the cucumber or onion.
12. Lay the meat and rice alternately in the mold, sprinkling a little chopped cucumber or onion and spices on each layer, and pour a little sauce or broth.
13. Place the butter dough lid on top, press down around the edges and bake in a hot oven for approximately 3/4 hour, or until the dough is completely tender, which is tested with a whisk.
14. Turn out onto a round plate, garnish around with small squares of butter dough and serve with brown sauce if desired.
