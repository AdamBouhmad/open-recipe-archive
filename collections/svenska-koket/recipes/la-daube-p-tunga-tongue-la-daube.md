## Ingredients

- 1 large calf's tongue
- 1 1/4 liters water
- 2 tsp. meat extract
- 1 1/2 tbsp. salt
- 5 white peppercorns
- 3 allspice berries
- 6 pitted preserved olives
- 8 dcl. of stock
- 8 sheets gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tbsp. cold water
- 2 tbsp. good Madeira
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 1 truffle
- parsley

## Directions

1. Let the tongue soak in water for 12 hours, then blanch it in hot water and scrape well.
2. Place it in cold water with the dissolved meat extract, skim well when it comes to a boil; add salt and pepper.
3. Let the tongue cook until tender, about 1 1/2 hours, which is tested by piercing it with a fine skewer.
4. Remove it, peel off the skin immediately and let the tongue cool.
5. Strain the stock, remove the fat, taste it and prepare the gelée as in recipe no. 181; when cold add the wine.
6. Pour a little of the clear gelée on the bottom of a water-rinsed à-la-daube mold and place on ice to set.
7. Garnish the bottom with the hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into beautiful shapes, and parsley.
8. Pour a few drops of gelée over the garnish and let it set so the garnish does not move when the tongue is placed in.
9. Cut the olives into slices; likewise the tongue.
10. Place an olive slice on each slice of tongue, roll these up and arrange in the mold with the remaining gelée and let it set.
