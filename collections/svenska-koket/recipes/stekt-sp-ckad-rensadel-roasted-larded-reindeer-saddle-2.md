## Ingredients

- 2 kg short-chopped saddle
- 3 liters skim milk or drinking liquid
- 1 1/2 hg lard
- 1/2 msk salt (8 gr.)
- 3 msk butter (60 gr.)
- 3 dcl broth
- 3 dcl cream or good milk

## Directions

1. Thaw the saddle as the roast in the previous recipe. Dry it well and remove all membranes carefully, then lard it thickly with finely cut lard strips dipped in salt.
2. Brown the butter in a roasting pan, place the saddle in it with the meat side down and brown beautifully, then turn it and dilute first with the boiling broth, then with the boiling cream or milk, and let it roast with a tightly closed lid until tender, about 2 1/2 hours, basting every 10 minutes or so.
3. Place the saddle on a warm platter, loosen the meat neatly on both sides of the backbone, place on a cutting board and cut across in beautiful slices, which are then laid back together on the saddle so that it looks whole.
4. Garnish with browned potatoes, salad or parsley and serve with lingonberry jelly and reduced sauce (see no. 429).
