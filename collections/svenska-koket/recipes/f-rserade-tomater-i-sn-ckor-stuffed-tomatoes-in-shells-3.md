## Ingredients

- 6 large tomatoes
- 20 g dried morels
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 3 tbsp flour (30 g)
- 3 dcl cream
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp finely chopped parsley
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper
- 1 tbsp butter (20 g) for the shells
- 2 tbsp crushed rusks for the shells

## Directions

1. Dip the tomatoes for 1 minute in boiling water and remove the skin. Open the fruit at the stem and carefully remove the seeds with a teaspoon handle.
2. Let the morels soak in water for several hours, place in boiling lightly salted water and let cook for about 1/2 hour, then drain in a colander and let drip dry.
3. Cut them into fine cubes and sauté in half the butter until clear.
4. Melt the remaining butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the cream and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes. Add the beaten egg yolk and let the sauce simmer.
5. Add the morels and parsley, and season the stewing with spices.
6. Fill the tomatoes with the stewing. Grease scallop shells or small ovenproof molds with butter and place a tomato in each.
7. Sprinkle with crushed rusks and pour over clarified butter. Place the shells in a long pan with coarse salt, put in a warm oven and gratinée for 10-15 minutes.
8. Serve as an entree or with roasted meat.
