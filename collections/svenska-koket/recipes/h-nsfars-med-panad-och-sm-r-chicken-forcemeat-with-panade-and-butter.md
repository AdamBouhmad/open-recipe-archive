## Ingredients

- 2 large chickens, about 450 gr. scraped meat
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 2 hg. bread panade
- 2 hg. butter
- 2 whole eggs
- 4 egg yolks

## Directions

1. Pluck the chickens, clean them, rinse very well, dry with a cloth and skin them.
2. Cut all the meat loose from the carcasses and scrape it completely free from sinews and membranes.
3. Grind the meat 6 times through a meat grinder and pound it together with the spices using a wooden pestle in a stone mortar until the forcemeat becomes fine and smooth.
4. Remove it and pound the panade (see no. 626) with the butter to form an even dough, add the forcemeat and pound the mixture very well.
5. Place it in a dish and add the beaten eggs together with the yolks under continuous vigorous stirring.
6. Pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve and stir again for about 1/2 hour.
7. Test it by cooking a little of it in salted water. If the forcemeat is too stiff, add 1-2 egg yolks or a little softened butter; if too soft, add 1 egg white.
8. Finally, if desired, add chopped truffle to taste.
9. Fill the forcemeat into a greased mold with lid, strewn with finely chopped truffle or crushed rusks, and cook in a water bath in the oven or on the stove for about 1 hour.
10. Test with a skewer to see if it is done.
11. Turn out preferably onto a rice base and serve with truffle sauce.
12. If the forcemeat is served together with tongue, ham etc. as a mixed meat dish, this recipe serves 12 persons.
