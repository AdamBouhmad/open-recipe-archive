## Ingredients

- 3—4 hg. cooked, boneless meat of poultry or game
- 8 dcl. bouillon or water
- 2 cloves
- salt
- 1 1/2 msk. butter (30 gr.)
- 4 msk. flour (40 gr.)
- 5 dcl. of the bouillon
- 1/2 msk. currant jelly
- 1 tsk. red wine

## Directions

1. Ragu on game is usually used as filling in vol-au-vents, puff pastry cases, pastries, risoles and for croquettes etc.
2. The meat is separated from the bones and cut into small cubes or oblong pieces, the bones are crushed and cooked in the bouillon (or water, with 4 gr. solid meat extract and salt) with the cloves, until the bouillon has a good game flavor, then it is strained.
3. Butter and flour are fried for 2 minutes; the bouillon is thinned in and the sauce is allowed to boil for 5 minutes.
4. The meat is added and warmed in the sauce but must not boil, as it will then become tough.
5. The preparation is seasoned with currant jelly and red wine and spices, if needed.
