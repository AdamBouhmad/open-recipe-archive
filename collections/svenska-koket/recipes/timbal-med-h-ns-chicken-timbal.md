## Ingredients

- 3 hg. boneless veal
- 50 gr. butter
- 1 egg
- 2 1/2 dcl. thick cream
- 2 tsp. salt
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 1 1/2 tbsp. flour (15 gr.)
- 1 young chicken or chicken leftovers
- 1 liter water
- 1/2 tbsp. salt
- 1 1/2 tbsp. butter (30 gr.)
- 3 tbsp. flour (30 gr.)
- 3 dcl. chicken broth
- salt, white pepper
- 1 tbsp. Madeira
- 2 tsp. tomato purée
- 1 1/2 tbsp. butter for the mold (30 gr.)
- 30 gr. truffles
- 50 gr. salt ox tongue

## Directions

1. Butter a timbal mold well with cold butter and garnish it with the tongue and truffles, cut into beautiful figures. Prepare the veal forcemeat as in recipe 68 and line the garnished mold with forcemeat. Press the forcemeat against the tongue with a spoon dipped in hot water. Reserve some of the forcemeat for a lid.
2. Pluck the chicken, singe it, remove the insides, cut off the neck, feet and wing tips and rinse it very thoroughly. Place it in boiling water with salt added, skim it and let it cook until tender. Remove the meat from the bones and cut into beautiful slices. Strain and skim the broth. Fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, add 3 dcl. of the broth, let the sauce cook 5 minutes, then season it with the seasonings, wine and tomato purée. When the sauce is cold, mix in the meat, and fill the ragout into the lined mold.
3. Cover with the remaining forcemeat and cook the timbal in a water bath in the oven for about 1 hour. If the timbal becomes brown on top, cover it with a butter-brushed paper. Turn it out, garnish with salad and tomatoes and serve as a middle course with tomato sauce, prepared from the remainder of the chicken broth.
