## Ingredients

- 1 ham, approximately 5 kg
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 g)
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 150 g lard, in flat slices
- 3/4 liter good meat gravy or broth
- 5 parsley stems
- 1 bay leaf

## Directions

1. Dry the ham with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and score the rind 1 cm deep in diagonal squares.
2. Rub the underside and in the scores with a mixture of the salt and pepper.
3. Spread the lard slices in a roasting pan, place the ham on top with rind side up, put in a warm oven and let it roast until tender, or about 2 1/2 hours.
4. When the rind pieces turn a beautiful golden color, harden and loosen, remove them and set aside for garnishing.
5. When the ham has roasted about 1 1/2 hours, add the boiling meat gravy mixed with the parsley and bay leaf, well tied together, and baste over about every 10 minutes.
6. Place on a warm serving platter, cut, and arrange the slices so that the ham appears whole, garnish with the rind pieces, salad and parsley as well as fluted, cooked potatoes.
7. Serve with apple sauce, pickled cucumbers, preserved rowan berries and the like. Strain the gravy, skim, boil down, season with meat extract and spices and serve in a sauce bowl.
