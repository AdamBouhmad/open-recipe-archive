## Ingredients

- 3 hg. boneless, fried meat
- 1 hg. butter
- 2 tsk. finely chopped onion
- 1 hg. flour
- 5–6 dcl. boiling milk
- 5 eggs
- salt
- white pepper
- For the mold: 1/2 msk. butter (10 gr.)
- 2 msk. crushed rusks

## Directions

1. Scrape and chop the meat finely.
2. Fry the butter with the onion and flour for 2 minutes, add the boiling milk and let the sauce simmer under stirring for 5 minutes; pour out and stir until cooled.
3. Then add the egg yolks, one at a time, and stir the mass for 10 minutes, after which the meat is added and the stew is seasoned with spices.
4. Finally, fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks, and pour the mass into a buttered and breadcrumbed pudding mold with lid and let it cook in a water bath on top of the stove for approximately 2 hours.
5. Turn out onto a platter, garnish with slices of pickled cucumber or tomatoes and parsley.
6. Serve with brown gravy sauce (see p. 218) or with reduced sauce (see no. 429), added with finely chopped pickled onion, pickles, or pickled cucumber.
