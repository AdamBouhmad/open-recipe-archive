## Ingredients

- 2 hens of about 3 kg each
- 1 large bunch parsley
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in 2 slices
- 3 tablespoons butter for roasting (60 gr.)
- 3/4 liter broth
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)

## Directions

1. The hens, which should have hung 2–6 days depending on the season, are plucked, singed, and drawn (see p. 299 et seq.), left to lie in water for 6 hours, and dried thoroughly.
2. The parsley is cleaned, rinsed, wrapped in a cloth, and worked into the butter.
3. The birds are filled with the parsley butter and sewn shut, after which they are covered with lard slices, which are fastened with fine sail thread.
4. The butter is browned in a roasting pan, the hens are put in with breasts up, browned well on all sides, salted, diluted with the boiling broth, and allowed to roast with a tightly closed lid until tender, or about 2–3 hours, during which they are basted every 10 minutes.
5. The hens are cut each into 6 pieces, arranged on the platter so that they look whole, garnished with salad and parsley, and served with browned potatoes, salads, and strained sauce mixed with cream (see no. 429).
6. If desired, the birds can be diluted with equal parts cream and broth.
