## Ingredients

- 3 kg of leg
- 3 litres skimmed milk or drinking milk
- 3 hectograms lard
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 grams)
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 grams)
- 1/2 litre broth
- 1/2 litre cream or good milk

## Directions

1. If the meat is frozen, it should lie in milk or drinking milk until just thawed.
2. If unfrozen, wash it very thoroughly with a cloth dipped in hot water and beat with a damp meat mallet.
3. Dry the meat well and remove all membranes, then lard it and rub with the remaining salt and tie it so it takes a beautiful shape.
4. Brown the butter in a roasting pan, place the meat in and brown it well on all sides, then add liquid, first with the boiling broth, then with the boiling cream or milk, and let it roast with a tightly closed lid until tender, about 3½ hours, turning it a few times and basting every 10 minutes.
5. Slice the meat into thin slices, place on a warm platter and pour over a couple of tablespoons of sauce.
6. Garnish with browned potatoes, salad or parsley and serve with reduced sauce (see no. 429), mixed with thick cream, as well as green salad and sour salads or lingonberry jelly.
