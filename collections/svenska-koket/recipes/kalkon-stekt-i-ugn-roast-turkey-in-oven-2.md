## Ingredients

- 1 turkey cock of approximately 5 kg
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in 1 slice
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 1 liter broth

## Directions

1. The turkey is plucked immediately after slaughter and thereafter allowed to hang for a few days so that the meat becomes tender.
2. It is drawn, rinsed, dried with a cloth, rubbed inside and outside with a mixture of salt and pepper, trussed, and the breast is covered with a large slice of lard, which is fastened with fine sailcloth twine.
3. A roasting pan is buttered over a space large enough for the bird, the turkey is placed on it with the breast up, the giblets are added and half of the broth is poured in.
4. The turkey is placed in a hot oven and roasted until tender, or approximately 2-3 hours, during which it is basted approximately every 10 minutes and more broth is added as needed.
5. If the bird becomes too brown, it is covered with buttered paper.
6. The meat gravy is strained, skimmed, boiled together, tasted and served as gravy, or it is made as in no. 429.
7. The turkey is carved neatly, placed on a hot dish so that it appears whole, garnished with salad, parsley and browned potatoes.
8. Served with green salad and acidic salads.
