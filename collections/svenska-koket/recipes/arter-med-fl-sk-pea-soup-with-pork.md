## Ingredients

- 6 deciliters yellow peas
- 1.5 liters water
- 750 grams salt-cured pork loin or brisket

## Directions

1. The peas are cleaned and rinsed well, and if they are considered hard to cook, they are placed the evening before in the water in which they are to be cooked
2. If the pork is heavily salted, it should lie in cold water for several hours, otherwise it is only rinsed, placed in cold water, skimmed and allowed to cook through for about 2 hours
3. Peas are always added in cold water; if the water is hard, it is boiled up before use and allowed to cool
4. The peas are allowed to boil up slowly, but then cook quickly for the first hour, as the skins loosen more easily
5. They are thinned with cold water and when the skins float up they are immediately skimmed off
6. The skins should be carefully removed, as this makes the soup less coarse and tastes better
7. After 1.5 to 2 hours, the broth from the pork is added, which should first be tasted, and the peas are allowed to cook until they begin to fall apart
8. They are seasoned with salt and, if desired, with a little marjoram or ginger
9. The pork is cut into slices and served separately
10. Leftover pea soup can be made into purée, but then 2 deciliters more peas should be taken when the soup is cooked
