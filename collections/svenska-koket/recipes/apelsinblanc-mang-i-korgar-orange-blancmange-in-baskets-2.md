## Ingredients

- 9 oranges
- Peel of 1 orange
- 1 1/2 dcl. juice from the oranges
- 5 sheets gelatin
- 4 tablespoons sugar (60 gr.)
- 4 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. Wash the large, beautiful oranges well and dry them.
2. Remove the peel from two-thirds of each orange except for 1 cm. wide strip to form a handle.
3. Carefully remove the fruit flesh with a silver teaspoon and scrape away most of the white from the peel.
4. Trim the edges of the baskets and handles into attractive points, then rinse the baskets well and let them soak in cold water for about 1/2 hour.
5. Let them drain on cloth.
6. For the blancmange, use the juice from the removed fruit and the peel from the sides of the handles.
7. Grate the orange peel neatly.
8. Press out the juice, measure it, and heat it.
9. Rinse the gelatin well in cold water, cut into pieces, and dissolve in the orange juice, then mix with 3 tablespoons of the sugar.
10. Whip the cream with the remaining sugar until stiff peaks form.
11. Gradually pour the orange juice into the cream while whipping.
12. Immediately fill the blancmange into the baskets and let them set on ice for 2-3 hours.
13. Pour the remaining cream into a tall, narrow tin mold rinsed with cold water and sprinkled with granulated sugar, and let it also set on ice for 2-3 hours.
14. When ready to serve, loosen the cream from the edge of the mold and turn out onto a flat glass plate with parchment paper.
15. Garnish the baskets with whipped cream, and on top of the cream place a small star of candied orange peel.
16. Place one basket in the center of the pudding with a wreath of orange stars around it.
17. Arrange the other baskets around the pudding and between them place almond mountains garnished with finely chopped pistachio nuts.
