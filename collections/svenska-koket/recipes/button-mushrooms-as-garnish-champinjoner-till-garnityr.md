## Ingredients

- 450 g. small, fresh button mushrooms
- 1/2 liter water
- 1 tsp. salt (5 g.)
- 30 g. butter
- juice of 1/2 lemon

## Directions

1. The button mushrooms are cleaned as soon as they are picked: the gills are removed gently, the outer skin is not removed from tender mushrooms, the stems are cut level with the caps, then the mushroom is quickly rinsed and dried on a cloth.
2. The caps are scored beautifully with a pointed knife, immediately placed in boiling water, mixed with salt, butter and lemon juice and allowed to boil 5–10 min.
3. Remove with a slotted spoon and use as garnish around finer dishes.
4. The mushrooms can be kept 2–3 days in a cold place if they are covered with butter-brushed, so-called butter paper.
5. The broth is used for sauces and stews. Small preserved French button mushrooms can be scored in a similar way, placed in their broth, mixed with 1 tbsp. butter, boiled about 2 min. and used as before.
