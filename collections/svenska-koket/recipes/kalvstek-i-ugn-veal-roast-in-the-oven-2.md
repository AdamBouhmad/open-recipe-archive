## Ingredients

- 4½ kg veal roast
- 3 hg lard
- 2 msk salt (30 gr.)
- 1 tsk white pepper
- 4 msk butter (80 gr.)
- 2 liters broth or water

## Directions

1. The shank is used with the French cut. The leg bone is hacked off and cooked to make broth for basting the roast. The meat, which should have hung for 3–10 days depending on season, is beaten well, dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and all sinewy membranes are removed. Lard the roast on the outer leg (see p. 232) and rub with a mixture of salt and pepper. Heat a long roasting pan, brown the butter in it, place the roast in skin-side up, put it in a hot oven to brown, and baste often with the butter. When browned, add boiling broth, but only enough so it rises about ½ cm. in the pan. Let the meat roast until tender, about 3 hours, basting every 10 minutes and adding more broth as needed. If the roast becomes too brown, cover it with buttered paper. Serve with reduced sauce (see no. 429) and mixed vegetables, small potatoes, cucumbers, and salads.
