## Ingredients

- 10-12 loose, tart apples
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 g)
- 1 1/2-2 dcl. granulated sugar (128-170 g)
- 1/2 tbsp butter for the mold (10 g)
- 3 egg whites
- 105 g. powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Wash the apples, dry them, peel them, cut them into thin slices or cubes and remove the cores.
2. Melt the butter in a saucepan, add the apple slices together with the sugar and shake over the heat until they are completely soft.
3. Place them in a buttered, ovenproof mold and let cool.
4. For the meringue, whip the egg whites until stiff foam, gradually fold in the sifted powdered sugar, and spread half of the mixture over the apples.
5. Pipe the remaining meringue through a pastry bag in diagonal squares over the meringue.
6. Bake in gentle oven heat until the meringue is half-dry and has a light yellow color.
7. Remove and let cool, then garnish each square with apple or red currant jelly.
8. Serve with whipped cream.
