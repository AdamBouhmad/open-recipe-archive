## Ingredients

- 12 large potatoes (preferably Dutch)
- 30 gr. butter
- 1 egg yolk
- 2–3 tablespoons thick cream
- 1 teaspoon finely sliced tongue
- 1 teaspoon sliced truffle
- 1 teaspoon sliced mushrooms
- salt
- white pepper
- 40 gr. melted butter for glazing

## Directions

1. Rinse the potatoes, brush well, peel thinly and shape them with a knife like eggs.
2. Cut them off slightly at one end so they can stand upright, place on a well-buttered baking sheet and fry in a hot oven until soft.
3. When the potatoes have cooled somewhat, cut them like eggs and hollow out carefully with a teaspoon, reserving the lids.
4. Work the inside with a fork together with the cold butter, the beaten egg yolk and cream until the puree is white and fluffy.
5. Finally add the tongue, truffle and mushrooms, fried in 1 teaspoon butter.
6. Season the mixture with salt and a little pepper and fill into the potatoes.
7. Place the lids on, put the potatoes on a buttered baking sheet and let them stand in a warm oven for about 10 minutes.
8. Place on a warm dish, glaze with the melted butter and serve with the smorgasbord or as a garnish around fried meat.
