## Ingredients

- 6 dcl. yellow peas
- 1 1/2 lit. water
- 750 gr. salted pork loin or brisket

## Directions

1. Clean and rinse the peas well. If they are considered hard to cook, soak them the night before in the water they are to be cooked in.
2. If the pork is heavily salted, let it soak in cold water for several hours, otherwise just rinse it. Place it in cold water, skim, and let it cook about 2 hours until done.
3. Always add peas to cold water. If the water is hard, boil it first and let it cool.
4. Let the peas come to a boil slowly, then boil rapidly for the first hour so the skins loosen more easily. Add cold water and when the skins float up, skim them away immediately. Remove the skins carefully as this makes the soup less grainy and better tasting.
5. After 1 1/2-2 hours, add the pork broth that has been tasted beforehand, and let the peas cook until they begin to fall apart. Season with salt and, if desired, with a little marjoram or ginger.
6. Slice the pork and serve separately.
