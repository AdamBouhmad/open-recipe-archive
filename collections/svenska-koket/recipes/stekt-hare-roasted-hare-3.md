## Ingredients

- 1 unflayed hare of 3 1/2 kg. (2 liters buttermilk)
- 2 hg. lard
- 2 tsp. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 gr.)
- 2 dcl. broth
- 5 dcl. cream or milk

## Directions

1. Hares should not weigh more than stated above, as very large ones are usually old, coarse and tough in meat.
2. The hare should hang 3-6 days, depending on the season, to become tender.
3. Skin it (see venison, no. 584), cut off the head, legs and shoulders, then place the hare on its back and cut off the ribs all the way down to the filets.
4. Rinse it thoroughly and dry it, loosen all membranes with a sharp knife and remove them without damaging the meat.
5. If desired, place the hare in milk and let it stand until the next day, then remove it and wrap it well in a cloth.
6. Break the spine and legs on the inner side to facilitate carving.
7. Lard the hare on the back, legs and shoulders.
