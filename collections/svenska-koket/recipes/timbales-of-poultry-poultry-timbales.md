## Ingredients

- For game forcemeat: 2 grouse or 1 female capercaillie = about 225 gr scraped meat, salt, white pepper, 100 gr panada, 100 gr butter, 1 egg, 2 egg yolks
- For chicken forcemeat: 1 large chicken = about 225 gr scraped meat, salt, white pepper, 100 gr panada, 100 gr butter, 1 egg, 2 egg yolks
- For panada: 1 1/2 dcl water, 1/4 tsp salt, 30 gr butter, 75 gr sifted flour
- For molds: 2 tbsp butter (40 gr), 6 truffles

## Directions

1. Prepare the panada: bring the water to a boil with salt and butter, then add the flour. Stir the mixture with a spoon over direct heat until it releases from the pan and spoon, then spread it on a buttered dish and brush lightly with butter on top so it does not dry out.
2. Prepare each forcemeat separately. Pluck the birds, remove innards, rinse very well, dry with a cloth and fillet them. Cut all meat loose from the bones and scrape it free from sinews and membranes.
3. Pound the meat together with seasonings in a stone mortar until it becomes a fine forcemeat. Remove it and pound the cooled panada with the butter to an even dough, add the forcemeat, and pound the mixture very well.
4. Place it in a bowl, add the egg together with the yolks under continuous, vigorous stirring. Pass the forcemeat through a sieve and stir again for about 1/2 hour.
5. Test it by cooking a small amount in salted water. If the forcemeat is too loose, add a little egg white; otherwise add an egg yolk or a little softened butter.
6. Butter small timbale molds and garnish with truffle cut into beautiful figures. Finely chop the remaining truffle and mix into the forcemeat.
7. Fill the molds 3/4 with forcemeat, bind over with butter-spread parchment paper and bake in a water bath in the oven about 45 minutes.
8. The timbales are used as garnish around larded beef fillet, smoked pork loin or tongue.
9. The forcemeats can also be placed separately in one large garnished timbale mold and baked in a water bath in the oven about 1 1/2 hours. Turn out, garnish with salad, parsley and tomatoes and serve as an entrée with brown gravy made from the roasted bird bones, or with mushroom sauce.
