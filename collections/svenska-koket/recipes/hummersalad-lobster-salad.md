## Ingredients

- 3 lobsters (400g each)
- 5 liters water
- 2 hectograms coarse salt
- 2 carrots
- 1 parsnip
- 1 celery
- 1 cauliflower head
- 1 can green beans
- 1 can fine peas
- 3-4 truffles
- 3 tablespoons butter (60g)
- 6 tablespoons flour (60g)
- 4 tablespoons fine cooking oil
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- 2 tablespoons vinegar
- 1/4 teaspoon English mustard
- 5 deciliters water
- 4 egg yolks
- 2 deciliters thick cream
- 3 teaspoons salt
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- white pepper
- 2 lettuce heads
- curly parsley
- 3 tomatoes in wedges
- 3 hard-boiled eggs in wedges

## Directions

1. Cook the lobster as in recipe no. 170, let it cool, split it and cut it into small beautiful pieces.
2. Prepare the root vegetables in the usual way, cut them into small, even cubes, which are cooked soft in lightly salted water, then drained in a colander so the water drains well.
3. Clean the cauliflower, cook it in salted water, let it cool and divide it into small pieces.
4. Pour off the peas in a colander, cut the beans diagonally into three parts and cut the truffle into strips.
5. For the mayonnaise, sauté butter, flour and mustard for 2 minutes; thin the water a little at a time, and let the sauce cook while stirring for 10 minutes.
6. Drain it and stir until it has cooled, then mix in the egg yolks one at a time and work the sauce with a spoon for about 1/2 hour while the oil, lemon juice and vinegar are added, alternating and drop by drop.
7. Stir in the whipped cream and season the sauce with the spices.
8. Stir the vegetables and truffle into the sauce together with the lobster, then mix the salad very carefully so the lobster does not break apart.
9. Arrange the salad high on a round silver platter and garnish with lettuce, parsley, tomato and egg wedges.
10. Serve as a course between meals.
