## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg pike
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 2–3 chopped pickled onions (optional)
- 1 part boiling water
- 1/2 lemon
- 5 tablespoons butter (100 g)
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g)
- 2 parts cream
- 1/2–1 deciliter good white wine
- 1 teaspoon sugar (5 g)

## Directions

1. When the pike is slaughtered, sprinkle it with salt and let it rest for a while.
2. Skin it as you would an eel, cut off the fins, and carefully remove the pike, saving the roe and liver.
3. Rinse it very well, dry it, rub it with salt and let it rest for 1 hour, then dry it again and cut it into 5 cm wide pieces crosswise, pouring over it the juice of 1/2 lemon.
4. Line a saucepan with half the butter, lay the fish pieces close together, sprinkle flour over them, add the onion if desired, and lay the remaining butter in flakes on top.
5. Add the water and cream carefully and let the fish cook slowly with a tightly closed lid for 15–20 minutes.
6. Place it on warm carrot, whisk up the sauce, season with wine and sugar and pour over the fish.
7. Garnish the dish with the roe and liver, which have been rinsed, cooked in salted water and cut into pieces, and with fluted lemon slices.
