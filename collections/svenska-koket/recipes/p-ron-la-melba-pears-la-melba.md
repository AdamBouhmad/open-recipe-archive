## Ingredients

- 1 hectogram sugar
- 2 decilitres water
- 2 decilitres white wine
- 6 French or other good, loose pears
- 3 egg yolks
- 1 hectogram powdered sugar
- 1 tablespoon water
- 4 decilitres thin cream
- 1/2 vanilla bean
- 150 grams dried apricots
- 4 decilitres water
- 40-50 grams sugar
- carmine
- 1 teaspoon potato flour (5 grams)
- spun sugar or 50 grams roasted sweet almond

## Directions

1. Make a clear syrup from the sugar, water, and wine, and let it cool.
2. Peel the pears, carefully remove the bloom at the end, scrape the stem, and place the pears directly in the sugar syrup and cook until soft, but they must not mash in the least.
3. Remove them with a slotted spoon and let them cool.
4. Pack the vanilla glace (see Vanilla Glace) in the bottom of a beautiful compote dish or silver bowl.
5. Place the pears on the glace, pour cold red apricot sauce over them and garnish on top with plenty of spun sugar (see spun sugar) or with almond that has been blanched, cut into strips and roasted in the oven together with 1 tablespoon sugar.
6. Prepare the apricot sauce as in recipe 462, but colour it with carmine so that it becomes beautifully red, before thickening it with potato flour.
7. Instead of apricot sauce, a sauce made from strawberry juice can be used.
