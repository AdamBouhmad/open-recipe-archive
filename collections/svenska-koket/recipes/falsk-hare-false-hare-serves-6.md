## Ingredients

- 250 g. beef without bone
- 225 g. fresh lean pork
- 50 g. smoked ham
- 0.25 Portuguese onion
- 25 g. butter
- 1 French bread roll
- 2 tablespoons good milk
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 0.5 teaspoon white pepper
- For breading: 1 egg
- 2 tablespoons crushed breadcrumbs
- 2 tablespoons grated strong cheese
- For frying: 1 tablespoon butter, 1 liter stock or water

## Directions

1. Wash the meat with a cloth dipped in hot water, cut it together with the fat into cubes and pass through the meat grinder three times
2. Peel and finely chop the onion and brown it in the butter, then mix it into the forcemeat
3. Work in the egg, the French bread softened in milk, and the seasonings
4. If the mixture is too loose, add a little crushed breadcrumbs
5. Shape the forcemeat into an oblong roll, flatten it from the sides so that it resembles a hare's back, brush with egg and sprinkle with the crushed breadcrumbs mixed with the grated cheese
6. Place the forcemeat on a butter-greased long pan and put it in the oven to get a nice brown color; then add the boiling liquid and baste it during cooking about every ten minutes
7. The cooking takes about 45 minutes
8. If desired, reduce the sauce according to general rules for sauces
9. False hare can be advantageously used cold on the smorgasbord
