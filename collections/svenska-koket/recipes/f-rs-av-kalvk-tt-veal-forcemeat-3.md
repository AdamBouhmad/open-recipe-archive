## Ingredients

- 5 hg. boneless veal loin
- 1 hg. dry, cleaned kidney suet from beef or 1 hg. pork fat
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 1/2 tbsp. flour (15 gr.)
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 5-6 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, free it from tendons and membranes, cut it into dice and grind 3 times through the meat grinder.
2. Finely chop the kidney suet with a knife on the cutting board, mix it with the spices into the veal forcemeat, and pass it through the grinder an additional 3 times.
3. Pound the forcemeat together with the flour in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until it becomes even and smooth, then add the egg and egg yolks, one at a time, with continued vigorous pounding.
4. Pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve and let it stand in a cold place or on ice for several hours, or preferably until the next day.
5. Work it vigorously with a spoon while adding the cream a little at a time, test as in recipe 628, and add, if needed, a little egg white or cream.
6. The forcemeat can, if desired, be flavored with a little grated onion, finely chopped mushrooms or truffle, and an equal amount of butter can be used instead of kidney suet or pork fat.
7. Cook in a water bath or fry in the oven.
