## Ingredients

- 2 medium celeriac roots
- 2 liters water
- 15 grams salt
- 60 grams butter
- 70 grams flour
- 5 deciliters boiling milk
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 5 eggs
- For the form: 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 grams), 2 tablespoons crushed breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Peel the celeriac, rinse, place in boiling salted water and boil until tender, which is tested with a fine whisk.
2. Remove, let cool and cut into cubes.
3. Fry the butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the boiling milk and let the mixture boil, while stirring, for 5 minutes.
4. Pour it into a dish and stir in the seasonings.
5. When most of the heat has dissipated, stir in the egg yolks, one at a time, and stir the mixture for 15 minutes.
6. Finally, carefully fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
7. Butter a baking form and coat with crushed breadcrumbs.
8. Layer the egg mixture and celery cubes alternately, with the first and last layer being egg mixture.
9. The form should not be filled more than 3/4 full, as the egg mixture will rise.
10. Bake the pudding in a water bath in the oven for about 1 1/2 hours, adding boiling water to the bath as needed, as the pudding requires even heat in order to rise.
11. Test with a whisk and serve with melted butter immediately after removing from the oven.
12. Note: Gratin of mixed vegetables (asparagus, tender French beans or wax beans, and cauliflower) is prepared in the same way.
