## Ingredients

- Outer or inner loin of beef, salted
- Cold water

## Directions

1. If the meat has been salted for only 1½–2 weeks, it need not be soaked before cooking. Rinse the meat and place it in enough cold water to cover it well. Bring it rapidly to a boil, skim thoroughly, and let it boil for 15 minutes at 100°. Cool the broth to 82°–83° and keep the meat at this temperature for 3½ hours, or until tender. If serving cold, place the meat in a stone crock, pour the broth over it, and let it cool in the crock. The meat can be stored in the broth for several days. If the meat is heavily salted, soak it for 12–24 hours before cooking.
