## Ingredients

- 3 liters half-cooked potatoes (2 kg)
- 1 kg boneless beef
- 3 kg lean pork
- 6 hg fatback
- 5 tablespoons salt (75 g)
- 2 teaspoons white pepper (6 g)
- 1 small tablespoon sugar (15 g)
- 1/3 teaspoon saltpeter (1 g)
- 1 1/2 liters cold water
- approximately 6 meters blue or curved intestines
- 4 tablespoons salt for rubbing
- 1 tablespoon sugar for rubbing
- 1/4 teaspoon saltpeter for rubbing

## Directions

1. Cook the potatoes the day before use, but do not peel them at that time
2. Peel the potatoes and pass them once through the meat grinder
3. Dry the beef and pork with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into pieces and pass 3 times through the meat grinder, the last time together with the ground potatoes
4. Scrape the fatback and cut half of it into fine cubes
5. Work the forcemeat vigorously for 3/4 hour together with the seasonings, while adding the water little by little, after which the pork cubes are added and the mixture is worked again for 15 minutes
6. Taste the forcemeat by frying a small sample and add more seasoning if desired
7. Fill the well-cleaned intestines, not too tightly
8. Rinse the sausages with cold water, rub with the salt mixture and place them the next day in salt brine with a weight on top
9. This batch yields approximately 7 kg of sausage. Cold cooked milk can be used instead of water.
