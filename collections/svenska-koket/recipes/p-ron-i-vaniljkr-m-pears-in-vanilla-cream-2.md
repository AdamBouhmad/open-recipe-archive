## Ingredients

- 3 large pears
- 1/4 coffee cup sugar
- 3 parts water
- 6 parts thin cream
- 75 grams granulated sugar
- 1/4 vanilla bean
- 3 teaspoons potato flour
- 4–5 egg yolks
- 1 hectogram dried apricots
- 3 parts water
- 1 hectogram granulated sugar
- pitted cherries for garnish

## Directions

1. Heat the cream with the vanilla bean and sugar until it has sufficient vanilla flavor. Make a roux with the potato flour mixed in a little cream, add to the cream, bring to a short boil and remove from heat. Whisk the egg yolks together in a bowl and fold the cream in with vigorous whisking. Return to heat and let simmer but not boil. When thickened, pour onto a round platter. Peel and halve the pears. Make a clear syrup from the sugar and water; cook the pears in this until soft, then arrange in a ring on the cream. Soak the apricots in lukewarm water for a few hours, then cook in the same water. When soft, strain and season the sauce with sugar, then pour over the pears. Place a pitted cherry on each pear. Set the platter in a low oven for about 10 minutes, then serve.
