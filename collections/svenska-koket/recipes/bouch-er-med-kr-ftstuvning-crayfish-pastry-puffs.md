## Ingredients

- 1 hg. flour
- approximately 3/4 dcl. water
- 175 gr. fine butter
- 1 egg white
- 2 hg. crayfish tails
- 1 ball crayfish butter (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 gr.)
- 2 tbsp. flour (20 gr.)
- 2 1/2 dcl. cream
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper
- 1 egg yolk

## Directions

1. Prepare the puff pastry as in no. 33, roll out to 3/4 cm. thickness, and cut small round cakes with a drinking glass.
2. Place half of these on a baking sheet; hollow out the centers of the other half with a wine glass so they become rings.
3. Brush the cakes with egg white, then place the rings on top.
4. Lay the bouchées on a baking sheet along with the small lids and brush both parts on top with egg white, which must not run down the sides as it prevents the puff pastry from rising.
5. Bake until light brown in a hot oven.
6. Carefully remove the puff pastry that has risen in the center of the bouchées during baking, making room for the filling.
7. Place the crayfish tails in milk several hours before use.
8. Strain them well in a colander.
9. Fry the butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the cream and let the sauce boil for 5 minutes, then add the crayfish tails and bring the stew to a boil and taste it.
10. If desired, add a whisked egg yolk and let the stew simmer.
11. Fill the bouchées while warm with the stew and place the lids on if desired.
12. Arrange the bouchées on pastry paper or on a broken napkin and serve as a middle course.
