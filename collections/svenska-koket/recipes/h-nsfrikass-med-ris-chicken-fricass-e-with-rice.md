## Ingredients

- 2 young chickens
- 1 tablespoon vinegar
- 1½ liters boiling water
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 cloves
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 part thick cream
- 1½ cups Carolina rice
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2½ liters water
- 1 bunch parsley

## Directions

1. Pluck and singe the chickens, remove wings and legs. Open them, clean out, rinse thoroughly and dry, then rub inside and outside with vinegar diluted with a little water.
2. Place the chickens in the boiling water, skim well once it comes to a boil, then add the spices.
3. When the chickens are tender, remove them, cut into neat pieces and keep warm in a water bath. Strain and skim the broth.
4. Brown the butter and flour together, add the broth, and let the sauce simmer for 10 minutes. Finish it with the egg yolks and cream.
5. Rinse the rice in lukewarm water, place in boiling salted water and cook until tender. Stir occasionally with a fork.
6. Place the cooked rice on a serving platter and garnish with fresh parsley. Lay the cut chicken on top and pour some sauce over. Serve the remaining sauce in a separate sauceboat.
