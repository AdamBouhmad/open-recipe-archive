## Ingredients

- 2 pike weighing approximately 700 g each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 150 g butter
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 small celery root
- 1 small red onion
- 3 tablespoons chopped parsley
- 3 dcl fish stock
- 1 dcl thick cream
- 2 egg yolks

## Directions

1. Fillet the pike. Rub the fillets with salt and let them rest for 1 hour.
2. Dry the fillets well with a cloth and cut them into 4 cm wide pieces crosswise.
3. Dredge the fish pieces in flour.
4. Butter a pot with half the butter and arrange the fish pieces in it.
5. Between each layer of fish, add pepper, peeled celery root cut into small cubes, peeled red onion sliced thinly, parsley, and the remaining butter distributed in flakes.
6. Add the fish stock (made from head, bones, and skin) and cream.
7. Simmer slowly with a tight-fitting lid for approximately 20 minutes.
8. Place on a warm plate. Thin the sauce if necessary.
9. Add the beaten egg yolks and let the sauce simmer while whisking, then pour over the fish.
10. Serve with boiled potatoes.
