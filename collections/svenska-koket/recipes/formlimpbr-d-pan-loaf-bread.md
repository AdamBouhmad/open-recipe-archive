## Ingredients

- 8 hectograms coarse rye flour
- 4 hectograms wheat flour
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2 tablespoons crushed fennel
- 1 liter cold milk
- 2 tablespoons syrup
- 80 grams pressed yeast

## Directions

1. Dissolve the yeast in a little cold milk.
2. Sift the rye flour and wheat flour and mix together with the spices in a large bowl.
3. Stir the cold milk into the flour, and work the dough until it is smooth and glossy.
4. Then add the syrup and dissolved yeast, and work everything together well.
5. Sprinkle a little flour over the dough, cover it with a cloth, and place in normal room temperature to rise for about 24 hours.
6. When well risen, work the dough again and place it in buttered and floured bread forms.
7. Fill the forms to just under halfway and place in a warm room to rise.
8. When the dough has risen to the edge of the form, set the forms in a good oven heat, and let the breads bake for 2 1/2 to 3 hours.
9. After the breads have been in the oven for 1 hour, maintain the fire with only one piece of wood at a time.
10. Test the breads with a whisk and turn them out when they have cooled slightly.
