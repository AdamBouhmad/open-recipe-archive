## Ingredients

- 1 skinned hare weighing 3 1/2 kg (2 liters skim milk)
- 2 hg lard
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr)
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- 4 tbsp butter (80 gr)
- 2 dcl broth
- 5 dcl cream or milk

## Directions

1. Hares should not weigh more than mentioned above, as very large ones are usually old, coarse and tough in the meat. The hare should hang 3-6 days, depending on the season, to become tender. Skin it (see recipe no. 584), cut off the head, hindquarters and shoulders, then lay the hare on its back and cut off the ribs down to the fillets. Rinse it very thoroughly and dry it, loosen all membranes with a sharp knife and remove them without damaging the meat.
2. If desired, place the hare in milk and let it stand until the next day, then remove it and wrap it well in cloth.
3. Break the backbone and hindquarters on the inside to facilitate carving. Lard the hare on the back, hindquarters and shoulders with fine lard strips dipped in a mixture of salt and white pepper.
4. Brown the butter in a roasting pan, lay in the back, hindquarters and shoulders with the meaty sides down, brown nicely on both sides. Thin with the hot broth first, then with the cream.
5. Let braise slowly with the lid tightly closed until tender, about 1 1/2 hours.
6. Cut the hare into beautiful pieces, place on a warm platter, garnish with browned potatoes, salad or parsley. Serve with reduced sauce (see no. 429) mixed with 1 tbsp currant jelly, as well as green salad and tart salads.
