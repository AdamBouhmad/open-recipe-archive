## Ingredients

- 1 large calf's tongue
- 1 1/4 liters water
- 2 teaspoons meat extract
- 1 1/2 tablespoons salt
- 5 white peppercorns
- 3 allspice berries
- 6 pitted preserved olives
- 8 deciliters of broth
- 8 leaves gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 2 tablespoons good Madeira wine
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 1 truffle
- parsley

## Directions

1. Soak the tongue in water for 12 hours
2. Blanch it in hot water and scrape it well
3. Place it in cold water with dissolved meat extract
4. Skim well when it comes to a boil; add salt and pepper
5. Simmer until tender, about 1 1/2 hours, tested by piercing with a thin whisk
6. Remove the tongue, immediately peel off the skin, and let it cool
7. Strain the broth and remove all fat; taste and season
8. Prepare the gelatin as in recipe no. 181; when cold, add the wine
9. Pour a little of the clear gelatin onto the bottom of a rinsed aspic mold and place on ice to set
10. Garnish the bottom with hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into attractive figures, and parsley
11. Pour a few drops of gelatin over the garnish and let set so the garnish is not disturbed when the tongue is added
12. Slice the olives; slice the tongue as well
13. Place one olive slice on each tongue slice, roll them up, and place them in the garnished mold
14. Pour the remaining gelatin carefully over and let the aspic set
15. Chop the leftover gelatin and use it as garnish
16. To unmold, dip the mold briefly in hot water, dry it, and turn it upside down onto a plate that has been dampened with water so the aspic can be slid onto the plate if necessary
17. Serve as a sandwich buffet dish or as a course with mayonnaise
