## Ingredients

- 1 small white cabbage head
- 3 liters water, 1 tablespoon salt (15 g) for blanching
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 2 tablespoons sugar (30 g)
- 2 teaspoons soy sauce
- 3 deciliters boiling broth
- 1–2 teaspoons salt

## Directions

1. Remove the outer green leaves from the cabbage head; loosen the remaining leaves from the core and remove the thickest part of the main nerve.
2. Blanch the cabbage (place in boiling salted water and let cook 8–10 minutes without a lid); lift out with a slotted spoon, let drain on cloth, and cut into cubes or strips.
3. Brown the butter and sugar in a saucepan; add the cabbage, pour the soy sauce over it, and brown with frequent stirring until it takes on a nice color.
4. Add the broth and let the cabbage cook slowly with a lid until completely soft, about 3 hours.
5. Season with salt and more sugar if desired.
6. Serve with boiled or fried pork and boiled sausage.
