## Ingredients

- 1 hectogram flour
- ¾ deciliter water
- 150 g butter
- leftovers of cooked or fried chicken (approximately 3 cups finely chopped meat)
- ½ hectogram tongue in cubes
- 8 mushrooms
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g)
- 4 deciliters chicken stock
- 1 deciliter cream
- 1 tablespoon white wine
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper
- 6 poached eggs
- parsley

## Directions

1. Prepare the butter pastry as in recipe no. 34, roll it out to approximately 3 mm thickness.
2. Turn ordinary small scallop shells upside down.
3. Lay the dough over the shells, cut it off all around with a fluting tool, press it against the shell, and trim the edge.
4. Place the shells on a baking sheet and bake until a beautiful golden color in a warm oven.
5. Lift the butter pastry shells off the scallop shells and keep them warm.
6. Cut the mushroom into fine cubes and fry it in butter until it becomes clear.
7. Remove the mushroom from the butter with a spoon.
8. Fry the flour with the butter for 2 minutes, add the stock and cream, and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes.
9. Set aside one-third of the sauce in another pan and season with a little curry and spices, and thin with a little more cream if desired.
10. In the rest of the sauce, stir in the chopped meat, tongue, and mushroom; bring the mixture to a boil and season with wine and spices.
11. Fill the shells with the mixture, place a poached egg on top with the curry sauce in a ring around it.
12. Serve as a breakfast or light dish; in the latter case, omit the eggs.
