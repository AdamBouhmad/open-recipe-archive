## Ingredients

- 2 kg. kidney fat
- 1 kg. pork fat
- 1/2 liter water
- 1 raw potato

## Directions

1. Cut the fat and lard into cubes; remove all glands and coarser membranes carefully.
2. Place the cubes in plenty of water and let them sit for 2 days, changing the water once during this time.
3. Squeeze out the fat well and place it together with 1/2 liter cold water to cook in a high iron pot. The lid should remain on the pot for the first hour.
4. Peel the potato, cut it in half, and let it cook with the lard, which becomes clearer thereby.
5. The lard is done cooking when the membranes ("the countesses"), which enclosed the fat, sink to the bottom and it gives off a thin smoke with a characteristic smell of lard.
6. Remove the pot from heat and after 10 minutes, strain the lard through a sieve or coarse cheesecloth into a stone crock and store in a cool place. This amount yields approximately 2 kg.
