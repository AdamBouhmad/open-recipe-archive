## Ingredients

- 2 turbot fish of approximately 900 g each
- 1/2 lemon
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 g)

## Directions

1. Remove the insides from the fish, rinse them thoroughly in several changes of water and dry them with a fish cloth.
2. Keep the head on and trim off the gills.
3. Clean the fins, but do not cut them off.
4. Make a deep cut along the backbone on the dark side of the fish so that the white skin on the other side does not crack.
5. Rub the fish inside and out with the lemon juice and salt and let rest 1 hour, then dry them again.
6. Place them on a fish kettle with the white side up and cook as in no. 327.
7. The fish will be whiter if you cook it in 2/3 water and 1/3 milk with 7 g coarse salt to each liter of liquid.
8. Place the turbot on a napkin, garnish with parsley and beautifully cut lemon wedges and serve with hollandaise, crayfish or lobster sauce.
