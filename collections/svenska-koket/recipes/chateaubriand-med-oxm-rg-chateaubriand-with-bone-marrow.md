## Ingredients

- 1½ lb beef tenderloin
- 3½ oz beef marrow
- 1 Portuguese onion
- ½ tbsp salt
- ¼ tsp white pepper
- 5 tbsp butter
- 2 parts bouillon or water
- 1 bunch parsley
- juice of ½ lemon

## Directions

1. Wash the meat with a cloth dipped in hot water. Remove the outer skin of the tenderloin and cut it into approximately 3 cm thick slices, which are beaten lightly. The slices are split horizontally, but without the parts separating, only so that a small opening or pocket is formed.
2. The marrow, which should have been soaked in water for a while to draw out the blood, is blanched, drained and cooled, cut into slices, brushed with melted butter and sprinkled with the chopped onion fried in a little butter. A marrow slice is placed in each steak; the steak is pressed together and seasoned on both sides.
3. The parsley is rinsed, cleaned and chopped finely and worked together with the lemon juice into 1 tbsp softened butter.
4. The remaining butter is heated in a frying pan, and the steaks are placed in. Care is taken that the openings face the pan's handle, and this is lifted slightly during frying, so that the marrow does not flow out. The steaks are fried quickly on both sides and placed on a hot plate.
5. The frying pan is whisked out with the boiling bouillon, and the sauce is poured over the steaks. A dollop of the parsley butter is placed on each steak.
6. Serve very hot.
