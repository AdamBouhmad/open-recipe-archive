## Ingredients

- finely crushed ice
- coarse salt
- saltpeter (12 kg. ice, 1 1/2 kg. coarse salt, 250 gr. saltpeter)

## Directions

1. The ice is chopped into pieces, placed in a sack and crushed with a wooden club.
2. The container is placed in the middle of the tub, ensuring that the turning pin in the middle of the container fits into the corresponding sleeve at the bottom of the wooden tub.
3. The stirrer is inserted, the lid is placed, the cross beam with the handle is screwed fast, and the ice mixture is then packed around the container with a wooden stick or wooden shovel, but no more than to two-thirds of the container's height, otherwise the salted ice can easily enter the container while the glace freezes.
4. The container should be frozen down if possible at latest 10 minutes before the glace cream is poured in.
5. The cold glace mixture is poured in, but must not fill the container to more than two-thirds.
6. The lid is placed, the handle is screwed fast, and the glace is then turned continuously for 15-30 minutes, depending on whether it is cream glace or fruit glace that is to be frozen.
7. After the glace is frozen in the aforementioned manner, it can be directly served with a spoon in glasses or in a pyramid on a dish with napkin or cake paper, but it gains in goodness if it is allowed to stand 1-2 hours to "ripen".
8. The glace mixture can also be filled in the mold and frozen directly in it, such as parfait, frozen pudding, bombe and others.
9. When the glace is to be served, the mold is taken out of the ice, rinsed thoroughly to remove all salt, dipped for a moment in lukewarm water so that the glace loosens easily from the mold, the mold is dried, and the glace is turned out onto a dish or platter with napkin or cake paper.
