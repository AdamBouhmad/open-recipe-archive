## Ingredients

- Broth: 3.5 liters water
- 1.5 deciliters vinegar
- 40 grams coarse salt
- 1 carrot
- 1 red onion
- 1 bay leaf
- 4 parsley stems
- 7 white peppercorns
- 1 salmon 2 kg
- 1 lemon
- Parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. Pour the water and vinegar into a pot. Add the salt, scraped carrot cut into small pieces, peeled onion, bay leaf and parsley, and let the broth cook for 1 hour. Ten minutes before it is done, add the peppercorns.
2. Strain the broth through a fine sieve, taste and let cool.
3. Scale and clean the salmon without cutting too much, and wash well with a cloth dipped in cold water, then dry.
4. The salmon can be boiled whole, split lengthwise into two halves (the bone is removed but cooked with the fish) or cut into 3-4 cm thick slices.
5. Cook in the broth according to instructions in recipe 327.
6. Serve with hollandaise, mousseline, crayfish or shrimp sauce and small boiled potatoes.
7. Leftovers of boiled salmon can be used for mayonnaise (recipe 176), salad (recipe 177) or gratin (see sole gratin, recipe 377).
