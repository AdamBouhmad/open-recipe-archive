## Ingredients

- 1 hazel grouse or 2 ptarmigans
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 1 1/2 liters good broth
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 6 white peppercorns
- 8 deciliters of the broth
- 8 sheets gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 1 tablespoon good Madeira
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 2 truffles
- parsley

## Directions

1. Clean the bird, singe it, draw it, and chop off the wings and legs.
2. Rinse it very well and dry it with a meat cloth, truss it (see fowl) and brown it in the butter.
3. Pour on the boiling broth, add the seasonings, and let the bird cook until tender, approximately 1 1/2 hours.
4. Let it cool and cut the meat loose from the bones and cut into beautiful pieces.
5. Place the carcass in the broth, which should cook down until it becomes barely 1 liter.
6. Strain and skim the broth.
7. Prepare the gelatin as in no. 181.
8. Garnish the bottom of the mold with the hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into beautiful pieces, and parsley.
9. Coat the garnish with a few drops of gelatin, which should set, so that the garnish is not disturbed when the meat is laid in.
10. Lay the most beautiful bird pieces in the bottom and at the sides.
11. Fill the mold with the remaining meat and pour the rest of the gelatin carefully over it.
12. Place the à-la-daube in a cold place to set.
13. Turn out as in no. 185 and garnish with chopped gelatin, truffle and parsley.
14. Serve with mayonnaise as a course.
