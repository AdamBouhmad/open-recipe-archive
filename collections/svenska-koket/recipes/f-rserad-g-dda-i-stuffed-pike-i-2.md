## Ingredients

- 1 pike weighing 1.5 kg
- 2 tsp salt (10 g)
- 150 g scraped fish meat
- 70 g butter
- 1 egg
- 1 tbsp flour (10 g)
- 1.5 dcl cream
- 1 tsp salt (5 g)
- 1/2 tsp sugar
- white pepper
- 1/2 egg
- 2 tbsp crushed breadcrumbs
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 3 dcl fish stock

## Directions

1. Clean the pike preferably the day before use. Scale it, gut it, rinse very thoroughly, dry with a fish towel, rub inside and outside with salt and let it hang in a cool place until the next day.
2. Cut out the backbone with a sharp knife, but keep the head and tail. (Cook stock on the bone to dilute with the fish.)
3. Scrape 150 g of fish meat, carefully removing all small bones, and prepare the stuffing as in the previous recipe, but the egg white should not be whipped into foam but mixed with the yolk in the cream.
4. Fill the pike with the stuffing to 3/4 capacity, then sew the belly shut with surgical thread.
5. Brush the fish with the whipped egg, sprinkle with crushed breadcrumbs, place in a greased baking pan with half the butter, and place the remaining butter in flakes on top of the pike.
6. Bake in good oven heat for approximately 1/2 hour, baste with fish stock and baste often.
7. Place on a warm serving dish, remove the thread and garnish the fish with parsley, crayfish tails or sautéed mushrooms and serve with crayfish or mushroom sauce.
