## Ingredients

- 20 gr. gelatin (approximately 12 sheets)
- 2 egg whites
- 6 dcl. warm water
- 2 1/2 dcl. French wine
- 1/2 lemon
- 2 1/2 dcl. Rhine wine or sherry
- 250 gr. loaf sugar

## Directions

1. Rinse the gelatin well in cold water and dissolve it in the warm water, add the lemon zest, from which all the white has been removed, lemon juice and sugar, and let the mixture come to a boil and stand 10 minutes off the heat.
2. Whip the egg whites in a well-scoured saucepan together with the French wine, and add the gelatin mixture under vigorous whisking. Move the saucepan over the fire and whisk the jelly vigorously until it boils, then put the lid on and let the saucepan stand on the side of the stove for 10 minutes without boiling.
3. Pour the jelly into a stretched cheesecloth, previously wrung out in hot water, and let it drain by itself. As long as the jelly runs cloudy through, pour it back into the cheesecloth. When the jelly is clear, add the Rhine wine or sherry.
4. Pour into a jelly mold or large or several small molds, previously rinsed with cold water and sprinkled with granulated sugar. When the jelly is to be served, loosen it from the edge with a sharp knife, turn it out onto a dish and garnish with a little chopped jelly or preserved fruit. Serve with almond shavings or almond brandy.
