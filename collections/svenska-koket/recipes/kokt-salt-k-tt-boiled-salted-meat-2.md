## Ingredients

- Outer or inner leg of meat suitable for salting
- Cold water

## Directions

1. If the meat has been in salt for only 1½–2 weeks, it does not need to be soaked before cooking. Rinse the meat and place it in enough cold water to cover it well. Bring rapidly to a boil, skim thoroughly, and boil for 15 minutes at 100°. Cool the broth to 82°–83° and let the meat rest in this temperature for 3½ hours or until tender. If serving cold, place the meat in a stone crock, pour the broth over it, and let it cool in the broth. The meat can be kept in the broth for several days. If the meat is heavily salted, soak it for 12–24 hours before cooking.
