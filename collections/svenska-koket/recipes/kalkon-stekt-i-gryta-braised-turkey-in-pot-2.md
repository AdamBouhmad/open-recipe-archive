## Ingredients

- 1 turkey cock of approximately 5 kg
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 6 almond biscuits (2-öres)
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 3 deciliters milk
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in 1 slice
- 4 tablespoons butter for roasting (80 g)
- 1 1/2 liters broth

## Directions

1. The turkey is treated as in the preceding recipe and rubbed with the spices.
2. The almond biscuits are split, brushed with butter, dipped in milk and stuffed into the bird, which is thereafter sewn with a purse stitch, trussed and covered with the slice of lard, which is fastened with fine sailcloth twine.
3. Half of the butter is rendered in a roasting pot, the turkey is placed in it with the breast up, the giblets are likewise placed in the pot, the broth is poured in and allowed to simmer slowly with the lid closed for 1 1/2-2 hours, or until the turkey is almost done.
4. When nearly ready, the broth is poured off and the lard is removed.
5. The rest of the butter is melted and the bird is basted with it.
6. Half of the broth is added, the pot is placed in a hot oven and the turkey is finished roasting, while being basted frequently, but is only allowed to become light brown.
7. It is carved as in the preceding recipe, garnished with browned potatoes, small fine peas, stewed mushrooms and salad and parsley.
8. Served with reduced sauce mixed with cream (see no. 429), and salads.
