## Ingredients

- 800 g sole, cod or whiting, or raw scraps and bones thereof
- 50 g red onion
- 30 g parsley (stalks or roots)
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 g)
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1 1/2 deciliters white wine
- 2 liters cold water
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 1 g saffron, curry
- 100 g fresh or preserved caviar (clarifying agent)

## Directions

1. Only saltwater fish should be used for fish broth. Sole, cod and whiting are best, but if not available, half the weight can be replaced with flatfish.
2. Clean the fish, rinse, dry and cut into pieces. If using scraps and bones, rinse them well and let them drain.
3. Peel the onion, blanch it (place in boiling salted water and cook for 5 minutes without a lid) and cut into slices.
4. Clean the parsley, rinse and wrap it in a cloth.
5. Butter a pot with a thick bottom; add the onion, parsley and fish.
6. Add the lemon juice, place the lid on the pot and set over gentle heat for about 15 minutes, shaking it often.
7. Add the wine and let the liquid reduce without a lid to about half, then pour in the cold water.
8. Skim the broth well and add the spices, then let it simmer slowly with a tight lid for about 20 minutes.
9. Strain through a sieve and let cool, then clarify.
10. Pound the caviar finely in a mortar with a pestle and mix with the cold broth, which is poured into the pot and set over fresh heat.
11. Stir the broth until it reaches boiling point, then move it to the side of the stove and let it simmer with a lid for 20 minutes, then strain carefully.
