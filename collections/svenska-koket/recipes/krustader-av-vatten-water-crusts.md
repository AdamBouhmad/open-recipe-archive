## Ingredients

- 2 hg. flour
- 3 dcl. water
- 1—2 egg yolks
- 1/2 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. sugar
- 2 tsp. cooking oil
- 3/4 liter cooking oil or 3/4 kg. lard for frying

## Directions

1. Sift the flour into a bowl, add the water, egg yolk and seasonings while whisking with a steel whisk.
2. When the batter is even and smooth, pour in the cooking oil.
3. The pan in which the crusts are to be cooked should be deep, but no wider than a tea cup.
4. When the oil is poured into the pan, place the iron form in it to warm, otherwise the batter will not stick to the iron.
5. Pour the batter into as many cups as there are forms on the iron.
6. When the oil is hot, dip the iron in the batter, but not all the way to the rim, and hold it first a minute over the heat so that the crusts can stiffen a little, otherwise they will become blistery.
7. Hold the iron thereafter in the hot oil until the crusts have turned golden brown, loosen them with a larding needle and place them upside down on gray paper to drain.
8. Fill immediately with warm stew and serve as a side dish or as a garnish to fish and meat dishes.
9. The crusts can be stored 2—3 weeks if placed in a tin with a tightly sealed lid, but must then be heated in the oven before the filling is added.
10. Crusts can be filled with stew of poultry and mushrooms, calf's sweetbreads and truffle, asparagus, crayfish, lobster, shrimp and fish, oysters as well as with vegetables and purées.
