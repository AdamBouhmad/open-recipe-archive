## Ingredients

- 1 unflayed hare weighing 3 1/2 kg. (2 liters buttermilk)
- 2 hg. lard
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/4 tsk. white pepper
- 4 msk. butter (80 gr.)
- 2 dcl. broth
- 5 dcl. cream or milk

## Directions

1. Hares should not weigh more than as mentioned above, as very large ones are usually old, coarse and tough in the meat.
2. The hare should hang 3-6 days, depending on the season, to become tender.
3. Skin it (see roebuck, no. 584), cut off head, legs and shoulders, then place the hare on its back and cut off the ribs right down to the fillets.
4. Rinse very thoroughly and dry, loosen all membranes with a sharp knife and remove without damaging the meat.
5. If desired, place the hare in milk and let it stand until the next day, then remove and wrap well in cloth.
6. Break the backbone and legs on the inside to make carving easier.
7. Lard the hare on the back, legs and shoulders.
