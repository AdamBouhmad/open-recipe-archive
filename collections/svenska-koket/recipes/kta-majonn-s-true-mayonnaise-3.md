## Ingredients

- 2 egg yolks
- 1 teaspoon salt (5 gr.)
- a few grains cayenne (1/4 teaspoon French mustard)
- 1 tablespoon tarragon vinegar or vinegar
- 2 deciliters fine olive oil
- 1/2—1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon boiling water

## Directions

1. Place the egg yolks in a large mixing bowl together with the spices (and mustard, if desired) and stir with a wooden spoon for 10 minutes
2. Gradually pour the vinegar over the yolks while stirring vigorously
3. Once the vinegar is blended with the yolks, add the oil in a thin stream while stirring constantly
4. Finish the sauce with lemon juice and the boiling water—the latter to prevent it from curdling
5. Let the mayonnaise stand in a cool (not too cold) place for 4—5 hours to thicken
6. It can be stored for 3—4 days without damage if well covered and not placed on ice
7. If the sauce curdles, it can be remedied by gradually stirring it into 1 egg yolk and 1/2 tablespoon vinegar
8. The sauce becomes lighter if mustard is omitted and only lemon juice is used instead of vinegar
