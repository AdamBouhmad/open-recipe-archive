## Ingredients

- 2 hens of approximately 3 kg each
- 1 large bunch of parsley
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 1.5 hectograms of lard, in 2 slices
- 3 tablespoons butter for roasting (60 g)
- 3/4 liter of broth
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)

## Directions

1. Clean the hens by plucking them carefully in the direction the feathers grow so the skin is not damaged, then singe them over a spirit, gas, or straw flame
2. Remove the wings and legs
3. Pull up the skin on the neck so the neck can be drawn out and cut at the breast while keeping the skin attached
4. Remove the crop and windpipe and loosen the esophagus
5. Make a small incision in the back of the bird where the giblets are located and carefully remove the innards without breaking the intestines
6. Set aside the liver, heart, and gizzard to cook with the bird, removing the bile from the liver and splitting the heart and gizzard
7. Rinse the bird thoroughly and dry it
8. Truss the bird by placing it breast up with wings crossed over the back and neck skin pulled down toward the wings, then thread a fine needle and string through the wing, neck skin, and breastbone and out through the other wing, then through the thighs, pulling tight and tying the ends together
9. Quickly dip the breast and thighs in boiling broth or water to firm the meat
10. Attach lard slices with fine string that has been dipped in boiling water
11. Place in a roasting pan with 3 tablespoons butter and 3/4 liter broth
12. Roast until the hens are cooked through
13. Serve hot
