## Ingredients

- 2 medium bunches green or blue kale
- 3 liters water for blanching
- 1 tablespoon salt for blanching
- 2 liters stock
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g)
- salt
- white pepper
- sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon finely ground fennel
- kale stems
- fish quenelles

## Directions

1. Place the kale, which should be frozen, in water to draw out the ice, shred and blanch for 15 minutes as in recipe 257
2. Place on a sieve, rinse with cold water and drain well, wrap in cloth, chop finely, pass through a sieve and cook in stock for 1 hour
3. Sauté butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the soup and let simmer while stirring for 5 minutes
4. Season with spices and serve with kale stems cooked in butter and fish quenelles
5. The thickest kale stems should be blanched, peeled, wrapped in cloth, cut into 5 cm pieces, rolled in flour, dipped in beaten egg and then in breadcrumbs, and cooked golden brown in hot butter
6. Place the stems in the soup bowl with the quenelles, pour in the soup and serve immediately
7. Canned kale can be used with advantage
