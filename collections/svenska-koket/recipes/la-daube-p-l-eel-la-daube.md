## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg. eel
- 2 tbsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- dill
- 3/4 liter water
- 1 tbsp. vinegar
- 6 allspice berries
- 3/4 liter of stock
- 6 sheets gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tbsp. cold water
- 2 tbsp. good white wine

## Directions

1. Skin, clean, rinse well and dry the eel.
2. Cut off the fins and carefully remove the backbone, but do not cut the fish in two at the back.
3. Rub it with half the salt and pepper and cover with dill stems.
4. Roll the eel up from the tail and tie the roll with coarse thread previously dipped in hot water.
5. Place it in cold water along with the backbone, vinegar, remaining salt and pepper and let it cook slowly until tender, about 40 minutes. (If the eel is large, it should be cut across and two rolls made from it.)
6. Remove the roll, let it cool and cut it into 1 1/2 cm thick slices.
7. Taste the stock and prepare the gelée as in recipe no. 181; when cold, add the wine.
8. Prepare the à-la-daube as in the previous recipe, but garnish the mold only with dill.
9. Serve at the smörgåsbord.
10. Some of the gelée can also be poured on a dish and allowed to set. Place the eel slices on it and cover with the remaining gelée. When set, remove the slices with a round measure, place on a dish and garnish with dill.
