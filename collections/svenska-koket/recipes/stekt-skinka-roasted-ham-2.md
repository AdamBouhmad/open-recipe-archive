## Ingredients

- 1 ham, about 5 kg
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 g)
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 1½ hectograms lard, in flat slices
- ¾ liter good meat stock or broth
- 5 parsley stems
- 1 bay leaf

## Directions

1. Dry the ham with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and score the skin 1 cm deep in diagonal squares.
2. Rub the underside and in the scores with a mixture of salt and pepper.
3. Spread the lard slices in a roasting pan, place the ham on top with the skin side up, put in a hot oven and roast until tender, about 2½ hours.
4. When the skin pieces get a beautiful golden color, harden and loosen, remove them and set aside for garnishing.
5. After the ham has roasted about 1½ hours, add the boiling meat stock with the parsley and bay leaf, well bundled together, and baste every 10 minutes.
6. Place on a warm serving platter, carve, and arrange the slices so that the ham appears whole. Garnish with the skin pieces, salad and parsley, and fluted boiled potatoes.
7. Serve with apple sauce, pickled cucumbers, preserved rowan berries and the like. Strain the stock, skim it, reduce it, season with meat extract and spices, and serve in a sauce bowl.
