## Ingredients

- 2 kg. lamb saddle
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 1/2 liter broth
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.) for roasting
- 1/2 liter broth for roasting
- 2 deciliters cream

## Directions

1. The saddle is treated as in recipe no. 531 and the flaps are cut off. The parsley is cleaned, rinsed, wrung in a cloth, chopped coarsely and worked into the butter, which is then left in a cold place to harden.
2. Make holes in the saddle with a larding pin or sharp knife at even intervals, and insert small flakes of parsley butter into them.
3. Heat a roasting pan, brown the butter in it; place the meat in and brown well on all sides, sprinkle with the seasonings, thin with the boiling broth and cream and let it stew slowly with a tightly closed lid until tender, or about 2 1/2–3 hours, during which it is basted frequently and turned 3–4 times.
4. Serve with vegetables or with boiled potatoes, cucumber and salads and reduced sauce (see recipe no. 429).
5. The meat may also be roasted in the oven (see recipe no. 531), but then it is only thinned with broth.
