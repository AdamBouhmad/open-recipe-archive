## Ingredients

- 3 hard-boiled eggs
- 12 shrimp
- parsley
- 2 fresh eggs for mayonnaise
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- cayenne
- 1/4 teaspoon French mustard
- 1-1/2 parts fine salad oil
- 1 tablespoon tarragon vinegar
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon boiling water
- 1 leaf gelatin for mayonnaise
- 1/2 liter veal stock
- 5 leaves gelatin for aspic
- 1 teaspoon vinegar
- 2 egg whites and rinsed shells
- 1 tablespoon cold water

## Directions

1. The mayonnaise should preferably be prepared the day before it is to be used to become sufficiently firm.
2. Separate the egg yolks from the whites (the whites and shells will be used for the aspic), place in a large bowl together with the spices and mustard and stir with a wooden spoon for 15 minutes.
3. Add the oil thereafter drop by drop, alternating with the vinegar and lemon juice; finally stir in the boiling water.
4. Taste the mayonnaise and let it stand in a cool place for a few hours.
5. One hour before it is to be used, add the rinsed gelatin dissolved in 1/2 tablespoon hot water.
6. Shell the eggs, which should be cold, cut off the whites at the tips and cut each egg into 4 slices, which are placed on a deep plate.
7. In the middle of each slice place a shelled shrimp tail and a small parsley sprig.
8. Pipe the mayonnaise through a fine fluted pastry tube in a beautiful ring around the egg yolk.
9. Pour the stock into a pot together with the rinsed gelatin, vinegar and the egg whites lightly beaten with water, mixed with the crushed eggshells.
10. Bring the mixture to a good boil while whisking, place the lid on the pot, remove from heat and let it stand at the side of the stove for about 15 minutes.
11. Strain the stock through a cloth or napkin wrung out in hot water.
12. Let the clear aspic cool and pour it carefully over the garnished eggs.
13. When the dish has set, remove the egg slices with a round cutter so that a border of aspic extends beyond the egg edge.
14. Place them on an oval plate, garnish with parsley or salad and serve at the sandwich table.
