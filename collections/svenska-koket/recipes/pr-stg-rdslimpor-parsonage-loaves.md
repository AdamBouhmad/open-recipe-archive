## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg sifted rye flour
- 1/2 liter good drink or 'Swedish ale'
- 1 liter unskimmed milk
- 2 1/2 kg wheat flour
- 80 gr pressed yeast
- 1 tbsp salt (15 gr)
- 75 gr butter, margarine or lard
- 5 dcl syrup
- 2 tbsp crushed fennel, 1 tbsp anise
- 3 tbsp finely chopped orange peel

## Directions

1. Pour the rye flour into a bowl and stir it with the drink, which should be heated to about 80°, stir the mass well, cover with a baking cloth and let it stand in a warm place overnight.
2. Early the next morning add 1 kg of the wheat flour, the lukewarm milk and the yeast mixed with salt, and work the dough with the hand until it becomes even and smooth, and add more flour so that the dough becomes quite firm, after which it is covered and allowed to rise to double its size.
3. The fat, syrup and spices are mixed in a saucepan, brought to a boil, allowed to cool and worked into the risen dough together with wheat flour, after which the dough is placed in a warm place and allowed to rise again.
4. It is then treated, shaped and baked as in the previous recipe.
