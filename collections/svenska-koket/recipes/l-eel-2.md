## Ingredients

- Eel
- Salt
- Pepper
- Lemon or horseradish
- Melted butter
- Boiled potatoes
- Wheat flour
- Egg
- Crushed bread crumbs
- Butter for frying

## Directions

1. Always purchase the eel alive
2. Kill the fish by cutting the backbone up at the gills and let the blood drain
3. Sprinkle with salt and let lie for 1/2 hour before skinning
4. Loosen the skin around the head and hang the eel on a nail with a cord tied firmly around its neck
5. Using a fish towel, grip the loosened skin and pull it off with a quick motion
6. Cut off the head and clip off the fins with scissors
7. Clean the eel carefully so the gall is not broken, rinse very well, dry, and cut into 5-6 cm long pieces
8. Rub the pieces with salt
9. Cook in water with salt and pepper, serve with lemon or horseradish, melted butter, and boiled potatoes, or cook and serve in aspic
10. If frying in the oven, do not cut into pieces but into two fillets with the backbone removed, dip in wheat flour, then in beaten egg, and coat with crushed bread crumbs
11. Fry the fillets in butter, garnish with fluted lemon slices and parsley, and serve with browned butter mixed with lemon juice and potato puree
12. When served cold from the oven, serve with mayonnaise mixed with chopped dill
13. If frying in a pan on the stove, cut the fillets into smaller pieces, treat as described, and serve warm or cold for the smörgåsbord
14. When serving eel as a main course, allow 1 1/2 kg for 6 persons
