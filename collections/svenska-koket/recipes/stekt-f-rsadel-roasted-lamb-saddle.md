## Ingredients

- 2½ kg lamb saddle
- 1 kg lard
- 1 tbsp salt
- 1 tbsp butter
- 1 liter broth or water

## Directions

1. The lamb saddle, which should have hung 8-14 days, is beaten well, then washed with a cloth dipped in hot water.
2. Remove the fat on the back and kidneys and some of the ribs so the thin sides can be folded up.
3. Cut the lard into fine strips, roll in salt, and lard the saddle with them.
4. Bind it and place on a long pan brushed with butter, put in the oven and roast at good heat for 2-3 hours.
5. Add broth and often baste with its drippings. If the roast becomes too brown, cover with buttered paper at the end of roasting.
6. Place on a platter and garnish with potato croquettes, glazed carrots, and green beans.
7. Strain the sauce, skim, and serve as gravy.
