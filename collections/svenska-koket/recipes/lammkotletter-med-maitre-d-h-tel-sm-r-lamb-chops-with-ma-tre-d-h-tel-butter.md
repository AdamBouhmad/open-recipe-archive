## Ingredients

- 12 lamb chops
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 1 small bunch parsley
- 50 g butter
- 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper

## Directions

1. Lamb chops should be cut with long bones and be approximately 2 cm thick.
2. Dry them with a cloth wrung out in hot water, trim, pound on both sides and rub in with the spice mixture.
3. Fry them in the browned butter 6-8 minutes on each side, arrange in a circle on a round serving dish, and slip paper frills on the bones.
4. On each chop place a small slice of maître d'hôtel butter, which is prepared as follows: rinse the parsley, wrap it in a cloth, clean it, chop it finely and wring it hard in a cloth, after which it is mixed well with the butter, lemon juice and pepper.
5. Form the butter in a damp cloth into a roll of finger thickness and let it lie in the cloth, which is placed on ice or in a cold place, until it is hard.
6. Serve the chops with French or fried potatoes as a breakfast dish.
