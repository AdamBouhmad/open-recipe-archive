## Ingredients

- 3 hg. boneless beef
- 2 hg. boneless veal
- 1 hg. fat bacon
- 2 eggs
- 3-4 tbsp. cream milk
- 0.5 tbsp. crushed rusks
- 2 tbsp. chopped Portuguese onion
- 0.5 tbsp. butter
- 1 tbsp. salt
- 0.5 tsp. white pepper
- For breading: 1 egg (or 1 egg white), 2 tbsp. crushed rusks
- For frying: 1.5 tbsp. butter, 0.5 liter boiling stock or water

## Directions

1. Wash the meat with a cloth dipped in hot water, cut together with the bacon into cubes and pass three times through a meat grinder.
2. Pound it in a stone mortar until it becomes fine and smooth.
3. Whisk the eggs and milk, add the crushed rusks and let them soak.
4. Peel the onion, chop it finely and braise it in 0.5 tbsp. butter.
5. Mix the soaked rusks together with the seasonings into the forcemeat; work it until it becomes cohesive like dough.
6. Butter a long pan with cold butter, place the forcemeat in it and shape it like an oblong loaf.
7. Brush it with egg (or egg white) and sprinkle with finely crushed rusks.
8. Place the forcemeat in the oven, brown it and then thin it with a little boiling stock or water, baste around every tenth minute and let it fry for about 1 hour.
9. Serve the sauce either as a gravy or reduced according to general rules for sauces.
