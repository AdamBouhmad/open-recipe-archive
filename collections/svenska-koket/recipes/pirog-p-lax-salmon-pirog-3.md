## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 dcl. rice grains (150 gr.)
- 1/2 lit. water
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 3-4 dcl. thick cream
- 3 1/2 hg. salted salmon
- 1 msk. finely chopped red onion
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.)
- 3 hard-boiled eggs
- 150 gr. flour for the butter dough
- 1 dcl. water (scant) for the butter dough
- 2 hg. butter for the butter dough
- 1 egg white for brushing

## Directions

1. Rinse the rice grains, scald in hot water, place in boiling salted water, thin with the thick cream and allow to cook until soft, then pour out and allow to cool.
2. Rinse the salmon, cut into slices and allow to lie in 1 lit. skim milk for approximately 3 hours, remove, wrap in cloth or white paper and cut into cubes.
3. Fry the onion in the butter.
4. Chop the eggs finely.
5. Prepare the butter dough as in recipe no. 33 and roll out to a 1 1/4 cm. thick cake.
6. From an ovenproof dish on which the pirog is to be baked, take a measure. This is done most easily by cutting a pattern of paper and then cutting out the dough with a pastry wheel.
7. Place the dough bottom on the dish and a 3 cm. wide strip of dough is placed in a ring on top of the bottom and fastened with egg white.
8. Mix the rice grains with the salmon, onion and eggs and place inside the ring.
9. Cover the pirog with a butter dough lid of the same size as the bottom and fasten it to it by means of egg white.
10. Garnish the lid in squares with thin butter dough strips and in each square make a small cut with a fork, so that the dough rises and the dish gets a neat appearance.
11. Brush the pirog with egg white, which must not come down over the sides, as this prevents the butter dough from rising.
12. Bake in a warm oven.
