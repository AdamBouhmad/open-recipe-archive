## Ingredients

- 1 wood grouse or 2 ptarmigans
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 1 1/2 liters good broth
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 6 white peppercorns
- 8 deciliters of broth
- 8 leaves gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 1 tablespoon good Madeira wine
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 2 truffles
- parsley

## Directions

1. Pluck the bird, singe it, clean it, and cut off wings and legs.
2. Rinse very well and dry with a meat towel, truss the bird and brown it in the butter.
3. Add the boiling broth, add the spices, and let the bird cook until tender, about 1 1/2 hours.
4. Let cool and carefully separate the meat from the bones, cutting it into attractive pieces.
5. Place the carcass in the broth and let it simmer until it reduces to just under 1 liter.
6. Strain and skim the broth.
7. Prepare the aspic as in recipe no. 181.
8. Garnish the bottom of the mold with the hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into attractive pieces, and parsley.
9. Pour a few drops of aspic over the garnish and let set so the garnish does not move when the meat is added.
10. Arrange the most attractive pieces of game on the bottom and sides.
11. Fill the mold with the remaining meat and pour the remaining aspic carefully over.
12. Place the aspic in a cold place to set.
13. Unmold as in recipe no. 185 and garnish with chopped aspic, truffle, and parsley.
14. Serve with mayonnaise as a course.
