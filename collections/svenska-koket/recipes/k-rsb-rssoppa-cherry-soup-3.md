## Ingredients

- 1 liter fresh or 5 deciliters dried cherries
- 2 liters water
- zest of 1/4 lemon
- 1 piece cinnamon (5 cm)
- 1-1½ teaspoons crushed sugar
- 2 tablespoons potato flour (30 g)

## Directions

1. Rinse the berries in a sieve and let drain; crush half of them in a mortar until the pits are crushed.
2. Place the crushed berries in 1½ liters of water together with the lemon zest and let cook for ¾ hour.
3. Strain the berries from the liquid, add the sugar to the juice, let it come to a boil and thicken with the potato flour, stirred into ½ cup cold water, then let the soup cook for 2 minutes.
4. Remove the pits from the remaining cherries, cook the berries in the rest of the water with 2 tablespoons sugar and add to the finished soup.
5. Serve preferably cold with whipped cream and rusks or biscuits. If using dried cherries, rinse these in lukewarm water, crush so that the pits are crushed, and let them soak for a few hours in the water in which they are to be cooked.
