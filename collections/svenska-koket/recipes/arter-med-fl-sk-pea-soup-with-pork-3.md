## Ingredients

- 6 dcl. yellow peas
- 1 1/2 lit. water
- 750 gr. salt-cured pork loin or shoulder

## Directions

1. Clean and rinse the peas well; if they are hard to cook, soak them the night before in the water they will be cooked in.
2. If the pork is heavily salted, soak it in cold water for a few hours; otherwise just rinse it, place in cold water, skim, and let cook for about 2 hours.
3. Always add peas in cold water; if the water is hard, boil it first and let it cool.
4. Let the peas come to a boil slowly, then boil rapidly for the first hour so the skins loosen more easily. Dilute with cold water and when the skins float to the surface, skim them away immediately. Remove the skins carefully as this makes the soup less coarse and better tasting.
5. After 1 1/2–2 hours, add the pork broth which should be tasted first, and let the peas cook until they begin to break apart.
6. Season with salt and, if desired, with a little marjoram or ginger. Slice the pork and serve separately.
7. Leftover pea soup can be made into purée, but add 2 dcl. more peas when cooking the soup.
