## Ingredients

- 750 gr. veal filet or boneless veal loin
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.) for frying
- 1 dcl. broth
- 9 wheat bread slices
- 3 msk. butter (60 gr.) for garnish
- 1 can asparagus tips (1/4 lit.)
- 1 tsk. chopped shallot onion
- 1/2 gr. white pepper for béarnaise
- 2 gr. salt for béarnaise
- 4 msk. good vinegar
- 5 egg yolks
- 180 gr. melted butter
- 20 gr. chopped tarragon stems
- cayenne
- 3 msk. good meat stock or broth

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water and cut into 9 slices, which are flattened to half thickness and rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper.
2. Cut the edges from the bread slices, which should be 1 cm. thick, then fry them beautifully golden brown in butter and place on a round dish, preferably of silver.
3. Warm the asparagus in a water bath.
4. Heat a frying pan, brown the butter in it, place the slices in and fry them beautifully brown or about 3 minutes on each side.
5. Place them on the bread slices (croutons), whisk the pan with broth, which is used for the béarnaise sauce or poured onto the serving dish.
6. Pour the béarnaise sauce (see no. 436) over the slices, garnish the dish with asparagus and parsley and serve very hot as a dinner or lunch dish.
