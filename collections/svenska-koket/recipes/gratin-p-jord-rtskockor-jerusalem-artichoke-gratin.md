## Ingredients

- 1 liter Jerusalem artichokes
- 3/4 liter boiling water
- 1 teaspoon salt (5 g)
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 g)
- 1 teaspoon sugar (5 g)
- a little white pepper
- 1.5 tablespoons butter (30 g) for the sauce
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g)
- 3 deciliters Jerusalem artichoke cooking liquid
- 2 deciliters cream
- 1 teaspoon salt (20 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1–2 egg yolks
- 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 g) for the dish
- 4 tablespoons grated cheese
- 2 tablespoons finely crushed rusks
- 1/2 tablespoon melted butter (30 g)

## Directions

1. Prepare and cook the Jerusalem artichokes as in recipe no. 696, drain in a colander, cool on a cloth, and slice.
2. Sauté well in butter together with sugar and pepper.
3. For the sauce: sauté butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the cooking liquid and cream, and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes.
4. Season with spices, finish with the beaten egg yolk, and let it just simmer.
5. Butter an ovenproof dish (preferably silver), sprinkle with cheese, and pour a little of the white sauce over it.
6. Layer the Jerusalem artichokes, a little grated cheese, and sauce, continuing until there are two layers of Jerusalem artichokes and three layers of sauce.
7. The top layer should be sauce, sprinkled with remaining cheese and crushed rusks, then drizzled with melted butter.
8. Serve for lunch or as a side dish.
