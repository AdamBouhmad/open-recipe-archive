## Ingredients

- 1 grouse
- water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 5 white peppercorns
- 1 liter of broth
- 8-10 leaves gelatin
- 2 teaspoons soy sauce
- 2 teaspoons meat extract
- 2 eggs
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- parsley

## Directions

1. Pluck the bird, singe it, clean it and cook in just enough water to cover it.
2. When the liquid comes to a boil, skim it carefully and add the spices.
3. Let the bird simmer until fully tender.
4. Remove the meat from the bones and cut into beautiful pieces.
5. Heat the broth; add the soy sauce, meat extract and dissolved gelatin.
6. When the mixture is warm, whisk in the eggs and let the broth come to a good boil while whisking.
7. Strain through a cloth wrung out in hot water.
8. Pour the clear aspic into the bottom of a rinsed à-la-daube mold and set aside to set.
9. Garnish the mold with hard-boiled egg and parsley.
10. Place the most beautiful pieces on the bottom and on the sides.
11. Fill the mold with the remaining meat, and carefully pour the rest of the aspic over.
12. Set the à-la-daube on a cold place to set.
