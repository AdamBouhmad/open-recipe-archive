## Ingredients

- 2 wood grouse or 4 partridges, about 450 gr. scraped meat
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 2 hg. flour panade
- 2 eggs
- 2 egg yolks
- 3-4 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. Prepare the birds and treat and pound the meat as in the previous recipe.
2. Pound the forcemeat with the panade until the mixture becomes even and smooth, then place it in a dish and add the beaten eggs and egg yolks, a little at a time, under continuous vigorous stirring.
3. Pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve, place it in a small bowl or saucepan which is set in a dish with ice (it will break otherwise when the cream is worked in) and let it stand for 1 hour or until completely cooled.
4. Stir it, let it remain on the ice and add 2 dcl. of the cream, a little at a time, under even and vigorous stirring.
5. Finally stir in the rest of the cream, whipped to stiff peaks.
6. Test it as in the previous recipe; if it is too loose, add a little egg white; if too stiff, add a little cream and more spices if needed.
7. Finally blend with chopped truffle if desired.
8. Cook as in the previous recipe and serve hot or cold with truffle sauce or brown mushroom sauce, or cold, in aspic, with vegetable salad.
