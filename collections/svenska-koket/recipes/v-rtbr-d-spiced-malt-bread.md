## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 liters malt beverage (reduced from 5 liters)
- 1 1/2 kg wheat flour
- 1/2 liter unskimmed milk
- 125 grams pressed yeast
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 grams)
- 1 hectogram rye sieve flour
- 1 hectogram butter or good lard
- 1/2 liter syrup
- 3 tablespoons ground fennel
- 4 tablespoons finely chopped orange peel

## Directions

1. The day before baking, prepare the malt beverage, which takes approximately 6 hours.
2. In the evening, sift approximately 3/4 kg of the wheat flour into a large bowl or trough.
3. Mix the cold milk and yeast (prepared with salt) into the flour and work the dough quickly, cover with a cloth and let it stand in a warm place overnight.
4. Early the following morning, warm the malt beverage to approximately 40 degrees and add it to the yeast dough along with enough rye sieve flour to make a firm dough, cover with a warm cloth and let it rise to double in size.
5. Heat the fat, syrup and spices in a saucepan, bring to a boil and then cool.
6. Work the spice mixture into the dough along with more flour and let it rise again.
7. Work the dough on the work surface with the remaining wheat flour and divide into 12 pieces.
8. Work each piece until it is smooth and without cracks, then roll into an oblong bun and place to rise on a soft, warmed baking cloth with a floured cloth laid on top.
9. Place the buns next to each other with the cloth drawn up in a large fold between each bun and cover.
10. When the buns are risen, turn them out onto a bread paddle, place on buttered, lukewarm baking sheets and let stand approximately 10 minutes.
11. Score lengthwise with a larding needle, brush with cold malt beverage or egg yolk and water and bake approximately 3/4 hour in a not too hot oven.
12. When half-baked, brush with warm malt beverage, and again when fully baked.
13. Afterwards let them dry a moment in the oven before removing and placing between cloths so the crust becomes soft.
