## Ingredients

- 1 kg lamb breast
- 2 liters secondary broth
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.)
- 1 leek
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 gr.)
- 2 carrots
- 1 parsnip
- 1/2 celery root
- 1/4 liter preserved peas (or 2 tablespoons fresh peas)
- 12 small Jerusalem artichokes
- 1 small cauliflower head
- salt
- white pepper

## Directions

1. Dry the lamb breast with a cloth wrung out in hot water, break it well and place it to boil in the broth.
2. When the meat is half-cooked, remove it and cut it into small, even pieces.
3. Strain the broth, skim carefully and pour it back into the pot.
4. Brown the meat in 2 tablespoons of the butter together with the leek, cut into fine strips, sprinkle the flour over and stir the meat well.
5. Place it in the broth, which should cook slowly for about 1 hour.
6. Prepare the root vegetables, cut into fine strips, brown in the remaining butter and add together with the peas to the soup 1/2 hour before it is ready.
7. Clean the Jerusalem artichokes and cauliflower in the usual way, place them in 1/2 liter of boiling water with 1/2 teaspoon salt and let them cook until tender.
8. Place them in the soup bowl and boil down the broth until it becomes about 3 deciliters, then pour it over the vegetables.
9. Skim the soup, season with the spices, pour it into the soup bowl and serve very hot.
