## Ingredients

- 3 sole, approximately 400 g each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- Fish forcemeat: 400 g pike
- 150 g scraped fish meat
- 75 g butter
- 10 g flour
- 1 1/2–2 decilitres cream
- 1 egg
- 2 teaspoons salt, 1/2 teaspoon sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 truffle
- For cooking: 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 g)
- Fish broth
- For garnish: hollandaise or crayfish sauce
- 2 truffles, parsley
- 12 crayfish or shrimp tails

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the sole as in recipe 371
2. Flatten the fillets with a damp meat mallet, rub with salt and let them rest on top of each other for a couple of hours
3. Prepare the fish forcemeat as in recipe 356 and mix in the truffle cut into fine strips
4. Dry the fillets again with a fish cloth, trim them, and cover the inside with a thin layer of fish forcemeat
5. Roll them up with the forcemeat on the inside and place them tightly next to each other in a low, flat-bottomed saucepan, buttered with cold butter
6. Smooth the forcemeat that protrudes at the top with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water
7. Add enough fish broth (made from head, bones, and skin) so it comes just over the rolls, cover the saucepan and place it in a regular oven
8. Cook the rolls until done, approximately 20–25 minutes
9. Test with a skewer to see if they are cooked through
10. Lift them out with a slotted spoon onto a warm platter and let them drain
11. Pour the sauce onto a round platter, preferably silver, and arrange the rolls in a ring on top
12. Garnish with truffle cut into thin slices or strips, parsley, and crayfish or shrimp tails
13. Serve with small boiled potatoes
