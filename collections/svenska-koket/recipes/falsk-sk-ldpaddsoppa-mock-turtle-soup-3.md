## Ingredients

- 1 calf's head
- 2 1/2 liters water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 6 white peppercorns
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 1 slice smoked bacon (20 g)
- 2 red onions
- 3 carrots
- 1 leek
- 1/2 celeriac
- 1 tablespoon tomato puree
- 6 tablespoons flour (60 g)
- 2 liters brown beef broth
- soy sauce, salt, cayenne
- 1 deciliter Madeira

## Directions

1. The calf's head should be scalded and shaved with the skin not removed. Split it; remove the tongue, brain (to be used for croquettes and stewing) and eyes, rinse the head and let it soak in plenty of cold water for approximately 12 hours so that the blood is drawn out.
2. Clean and blanch it for 10 minutes, place in cold water with the salt, skim carefully and let it cook with the pepper until the meat loosens from the bones.
3. Remove the head, cut the meat loose, place on a flat dish under slight pressure, strain and skim the broth.
4. Brown the bacon slice and the trimmed, cut vegetables well in 2 tablespoons of the butter. Add the broth and tomato puree and let everything cook for approximately 2 hours.
5. Strain and skim the broth again, measure and it should be 1 liter.
6. Brown the remaining butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the broth and the beef broth, add the meat cut into small, even pieces and let the soup cook under stirring for 5 minutes.
7. Color with soy sauce (2 teaspoons burnt sugar, 2 tablespoons hot water), season with the spices and wine and serve with hard-boiled egg yolks.
