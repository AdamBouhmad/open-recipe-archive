## Ingredients

- 2 kg lamb roast
- 1 bunch parsley
- 3 tbsp butter (60 gr.)
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr.)
- 0.25 tsp white pepper
- 3 tbsp butter for roasting (60 gr.)
- 0.5 litre broth
- 2 dcl cream

## Directions

1. The roast is treated as in no. 531 and the leg is cut off. The parsley is cleaned, rinsed, wrung in a cloth, chopped coarsely and worked into the butter, which is then allowed to harden in a cold place.
2. Holes are made in the roast with a larding needle or sharp knife at regular intervals, and small flakes of parsley butter are inserted therein.
3. A roasting pot is heated, the butter is browned in it; the roast is placed in and browned well on all sides, sprinkled with the spices, extended with the boiling broth and cream and is allowed to roast slowly with a tightly closed lid until tender, or about 2.5–3 hours, during which it is basted frequently and turned 3–4 times.
4. Served with vegetables or with boiled potatoes, cucumber and salads as well as prepared sauce (see no. 429). The meat can also be roasted in the oven (see no. 531), but then it is only extended with broth.
