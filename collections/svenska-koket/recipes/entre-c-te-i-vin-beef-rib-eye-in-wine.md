## Ingredients

- 1 kg boneless beef rib-eye
- 1 hg smoked ham
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 1 tsp salt (5 g)
- a little white pepper
- 5 dcl dark stock
- 1 1/2-2 dcl Madeira wine

## Directions

1. The meat, which should have hung until properly tender, is prepared and larded with ham (see page 232), after which it is rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper and tied so that it has a nice shape.
2. The butter is browned in an iron pot that is not larger than the meat fits snugly in, the meat is browned on all sides until a nice brown crust is formed, then the boiling stock and half of the wine are added.
3. The meat is then braised with a tightly closed lid until tender, or approximately 1 1/2-2 hours, during which it is turned a few times.
4. Near the end of braising, the rest of the wine is added.
5. The meat is sliced thinly, placed on a warm dish, covered with a couple of tablespoons of sauce, garnished with salad or parsley and small browned potatoes or potato patties.
6. Served with brown sauce (see no. 430).
