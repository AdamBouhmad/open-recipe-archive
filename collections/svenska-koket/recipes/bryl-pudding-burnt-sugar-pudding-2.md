## Ingredients

- 6 egg yolks
- 40 g sugar
- 1/2 liter cream milk
- 1/2 vanilla pod
- 3 egg whites
- 3 deciliters sugar for the mold (255 g)
- 1/2 deciliter water for the mold

## Directions

1. Brylé pudding is best if it is prepared the day before it is to be served.
2. Cook the sugar with the water until it is dry, then stir it until it becomes liquid again and has a nice light brown color.
3. Pour some of the sugar mixture into a pie mold, which is turned around so that the mixture covers both the bottom and the sides.
4. The remaining sugar mixture is diluted with water and served as sauce with the pudding.
5. Stir the egg yolks with the sugar for 20 minutes.
6. Heat the cream milk (2 deciliters cream, 3 deciliters milk) with the vanilla pod for about 5 minutes and add to the stirred egg yolks with vigorous beating; continue beating until the cream is cold, then remove the vanilla pod and fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
7. Pour the cream into the coated mold, and let the pudding boil in a water bath in ordinary oven heat for about 1 1/2 hours.
8. Let it stand in the mold until the next day, or until it is completely cold. Turn it out and serve with the remaining brylé sauce.
