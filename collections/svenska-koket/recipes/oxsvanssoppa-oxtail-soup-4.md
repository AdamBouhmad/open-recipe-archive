## Ingredients

- 1 oxtail
- 1/2 kg beef neck
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 1-2 red onions
- 2 carrots
- 1 parsnip
- 1/4 celeriac
- 5 parsley sprigs
- 1 bay leaf
- 3 liters stock or water
- 15 g cornstarch flour
- salt
- sugar
- cayenne
- 1/2 deciliter dry sherry (1 tablespoon tomato puree)

## Directions

1. Rinse the oxtail in cold water, blanch in boiling water, dry with a cloth and cut into pieces along the joints. Split the largest pieces crosswise.
2. Rinse the meat quickly, dry and cut into smaller pieces.
3. Peel the onion, prepare the vegetables and cut them up, rinse the parsley and tie it together with the bay leaf.
4. Brown the butter in an iron pot, add the meat and vegetables and allow to brown over gentle heat for about 1 hour.
5. Then add 1/4 liter of the boiling stock, which is allowed to boil down to a glaze with constant stirring.
6. When the glaze has turned a nice dark brown without burning, add the rest of the stock and allow the soup to cook slowly with a tightly closed lid for about 3 hours or until the meat is completely tender.
7. Remove with a slotted spoon, trim the tail pieces, cut the meat into small pieces and place in the soup bowl.
8. Strain the soup, skim well, bring to a boil, thicken with the cornstarch flour mixed in 1/2 cup cold water, and allow to boil while stirring for 5 minutes.
9. Season with the spices, wine and tomato puree and pour over the meat in the soup bowl.
10. Serve very hot. If desired, a cooked carrot can be cut into attractive shapes and served as a garnish in the soup.
