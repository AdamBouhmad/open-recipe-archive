## Ingredients

- 1 liter fresh cherries or 5 deciliters dried cherries
- 2 liters water
- Zest of 1/4 lemon
- 1 piece cinnamon (5 cm.)
- 1–1 1/2 coffee spoons crushed sugar
- 2 tablespoons potato flour (30 gr.)
- 2 tablespoons sugar

## Directions

1. Rinse the cherries in a colander and let them drain; pound half of them in a stone mortar until the stones are crushed.
2. Put the crushed cherries in 1 1/2 liters of water with the lemon zest and let them cook 3/4 hour.
3. Strain the berries out, add sugar to the liquid, bring it to a boil, and thicken with the potato flour mixed in 1/2 coffee spoon cold water, then let the soup cook 2 minutes.
4. Remove the stones from the remaining cherries, cook the berries in the rest of the water with 2 tablespoons sugar, and add them to the finished soup.
5. Serve preferably cold with whipped cream and rusks or biscuits.
6. If using dried cherries, rinse them in lukewarm water, pound them so the stones are crushed, and let them soak a few hours in the water in which they will be cooked.
