## Ingredients

- 20 gr. gelatin (approximately 12 sheets)
- 2 egg whites
- 6 dcl. hot water
- 2 1/2 dcl. French wine
- 1/2 lemon
- 2 1/2 dcl. Rhine wine or sherry
- 250 gr. loaf sugar

## Directions

1. Rinse the gelatin well in cold water and dissolve it in the hot water, add the lemon zest (from which all the white has been removed), lemon juice and sugar, and bring the mixture to a boil and let it stand 10 minutes beside the stove.
2. Whip the egg whites in a well-scoured saucepan together with the French wine, and add the gelatin mixture under vigorous whipping. Move the saucepan over the heat and whip the jelly vigorously until it comes to a boil, then cover and let the saucepan stand on the stovetop for 10 minutes without boiling.
3. Pour the jelly into a stretched cheesecloth, previously wrung out in hot water, and let it drip through. As long as the jelly runs turbid through, pour it back into the cheesecloth. When the jelly is clear, add the Rhine wine or sherry.
4. Treat the jelly as described above. Serve with almond shavings or almond sprits. If desired, 200 gr. fresh grapes can be placed in the mold. Pour a little jelly on the bottom and let it set, then place some grapes, then jelly again to set, and grapes over, until the mold is full. While the mold is being filled, the remaining jelly must be kept warm.
