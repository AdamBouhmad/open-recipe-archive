## Ingredients

- 1 hectogram flour
- 3/4 deciliter water
- 150 grams butter
- leftovers of cooked or fried chicken (about 3 cups finely cut meat)
- 1/2 hectogram tongue cut into dice
- 8 mushrooms
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g)
- 4 deciliters chicken stock
- 1 deciliter cream
- 1 tablespoon white wine
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper
- 6 poached eggs
- parsley
- curry

## Directions

1. Prepare the puff pastry as in recipe 34, roll it out to about 3 mm thickness.
2. Turn ordinary small shells upside down, place the dough over them, cut it off around with a fluted wheel, press it against the shell and trim the edge.
3. Place the shells on a baking tray and bake to a beautiful golden colour in a warm oven.
4. Lift the puff pastry shells off the shell moulds and keep them warm.
5. Cut the mushrooms into fine dice and fry in butter until it becomes clear, then remove it from the butter with a spoon.
6. Fry the flour with the butter for 2 minutes, add the stock and cream and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes.
7. Set aside one third of the sauce in another pot and season with a little curry and spices, and thin with a little more cream if desired.
8. In the remaining sauce stir in the cut meat, tongue and mushrooms, let the stewing come to a boil and season with the wine and spices.
9. Fill the shells with the stewing, place a poached egg on top with the curry sauce in a ring around it.
10. Serve as a breakfast or light course; in the latter case omit the eggs.
