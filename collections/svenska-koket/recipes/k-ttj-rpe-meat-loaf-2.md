## Ingredients

- 5 hg. lean pork belly
- 1 hg. lard
- 1/4 tsk. ginger
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper

## Directions

1. Cut the meat into cubes, pass 6 times through a meat grinder and pound together with the pepper using a wooden pestle in a stone mortar
2. Add the bread crumbs (see no. 626) and pound the forcemeat further until it becomes even and smooth
3. Place the forcemeat in a bowl, add the beaten eggs and egg yolks little by little while stirring constantly, then pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve, place on ice and stir vigorously for about 1/2 hour while adding the cream by spoonfuls
4. Test the forcemeat (see Färs, page 316); if it is too firm, add a little more cream, otherwise a little egg white
5. Cook the forcemeat in a water bath for about 2 hours or bake in the oven and serve warm or cold with vegetables
6. It can also be layered in a mold alternately with veal, chicken, game, or poultry forcemeat, cooked in a water bath and served cold in aspic as a more refined side dish
