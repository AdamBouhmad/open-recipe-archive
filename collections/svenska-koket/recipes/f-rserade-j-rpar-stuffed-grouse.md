## Ingredients

- 4 grouse
- 1 chicken
- 100g boneless veal
- 80g butter
- 2 eggs
- 300ml thick cream
- 1 tablespoon flour (10g)
- salt, white pepper
- 2–3 truffles
- 300g lard, in flat slices
- 1.5 liters broth for cooking
- 100g rice grains
- 1 liter water
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 1/2 teaspoon French mustard
- 500ml strong, clear, brown broth
- 4–5 leaves gelatin
- 1 head of lettuce
- parsley
- 2 truffles
- 2 tomatoes

## Directions

1. Pluck and singe the grouse, carefully remove the head, wings and feet, but leave the bones. Cut them open along the back, remove the innards, rinse the birds well and dry them. Cut the meat loose from the breastbone as close to the skin as possible, without tearing it. Remove and rinse the chicken well and pluck it. Remove all meat from the bones and scrape it together with the grouse and veal meat, free of sinews and membranes.
2. Beat the meat well with the butter and eggs, and work the forcemeat for 1/2 hour, adding the cream little by little, together with the seasonings and flour. Pass the forcemeat through a fine sieve and mix with the truffles cut into strips, then place on ice to set.
3. When the forcemeat is set, fill it into the bird skins, which are shaped like grouse. Sew the skin together, cover the birds with lard slices, tie a mussel shell around each one. Place them in a flat pot with the breast up and cook in gentle broth for about 40 minutes.
4. Rinse the rice grains, add to boiling salted water and cook until tender, then drain in a colander and let drain well. Mix with the mustard and pack into a water-rinsed, smooth, round baking form.
5. For the gelatin, rinse it in cold water and dissolve in the hot broth, which is then poured onto a dish to set. Turn the rice bottom out onto a round serving dish and garnish with chopped gelatin, parsley, and place the lettuce head in the middle. Free the birds from the lard and thread with which they were sewn together and cut into beautiful slices, but so that they retain their form. Place them in indentations around the salad and garnish the arrangement with the cut tomatoes and the rest of the gelatin, cut into triangles with a small truffle slice on each triangle.
6. Serve the grouse as an entrée with brown gravy prepared from the roasted bird bones or with bird sauce, thinned with cream.
