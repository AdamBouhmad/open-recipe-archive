## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 parts Carolina rice groats (150 gr.)
- 1 liter water
- 1 1/2 liters fresh apples or 1 1/2 hg. dried apples or 1 1/2 liters preserved gooseberries
- 1 1/2–2 parts crushed sugar (1–2 tbsp. punch)

## Directions

1. Rinse the rice groats, scald them in hot water, let them drain and add to boiling water.
2. Peel the apples, cut them into fine sections and add along with the sugar when the porridge has cooked about 15 minutes.
3. Let the porridge then cook slowly until the groats and apples are completely soft, or about 1 1/2 hours, stirring often so that it does not burn.
4. Season the porridge with more sugar if needed, and add the punch if desired.
5. Serve with cream or milk.
6. If dried apples are used, rinse them and soak in 2 parts water for about 12 hours before cooking. Preserved gooseberries can be used instead of apples and the porridge is otherwise cooked in the same way.
7. Coarse groats can also be used for this porridge.
