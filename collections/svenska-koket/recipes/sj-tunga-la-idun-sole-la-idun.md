## Ingredients

- 3 sole fillets of approximately 5 hg. each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g.)
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 g.) for cooking
- 5 dcl. fish stock
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g.) for sauce
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g.)
- 5 dcl. fish stock
- 2-3 egg yolks
- 1 tablespoon fine white wine
- salt
- white pepper
- 1/2 liter good potato purée for garnish
- 3 tablespoons grated cheese
- 2 tablespoons melted butter (40 g.)

## Directions

1. Clean the sole, rinse well and dry; trim the fins. Make a deep cut with a pointed knife along both sides of the backbone and carefully cut the fillets from the bone so they do not break. Lay them skin side down on a cutting board and scrape the flesh from the skin. Cut away the gills and rinse the head, bone and white skin well, then cook stock from them.
2. Rub the fillets with salt and let them lie on top of each other for 1 hour, then dry again with a fish cloth and cook 5-8 minutes in fish stock. Remove with a slotted spoon and let drain on cloth. Measure the fish stock, boil it down if it amounts to more than 5 dcl., and use it for the sauce.
3. Butter a fireproof dish or preferably a silver one with 1/2 tablespoon butter, lay the fillets on it, pour the sauce over, pipe the potato purée around the edge and sprinkle the dish with grated cheese, finally pour the melted butter over it. Set the dish in a roasting pan with coarse salt, place in a warm oven and glaze the dish quickly for 10-15 minutes.
4. For the sauce: fry butter and flour for 2 minutes, dilute with the stock and let the sauce cook 5 minutes; add the beaten egg yolks along with the wine and let the sauce simmer while whisking and taste for seasoning.
