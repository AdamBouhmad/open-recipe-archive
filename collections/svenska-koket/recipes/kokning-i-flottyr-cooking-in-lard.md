## Ingredients

- Iron or copper pot
- Lard (rendered fat), heated to approximately 160 degrees C.

## Directions

1. Use an iron or copper pot for cooking in lard, preferably round in shape and sufficiently deep so that items placed in it have good space to come into contact with the lard, even if the pot is only half filled with it.
2. Heat the lard until a thin smoke rises from its surface, which indicates it is approximately 160 degrees C. You can also test if the lard is hot enough by placing a hard piece of bread in it, around which a mass of small bubbles will immediately form.
3. Place what is to be cooked in the lard, not too much at once so that the lard does not cool down, and cook until it becomes golden brown. Place it on gray paper so that the lard drains off well.
4. Leftover lard can be used several times if it is reheated in water or milk. When it has solidified, scrape off all sediment from the bottom lightly.
