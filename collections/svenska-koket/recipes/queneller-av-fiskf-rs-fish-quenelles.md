## Ingredients

- 4 hg. pike = 150 gr. scraped fish meat
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/4 tsk. white pepper
- 1 egg
- 1/2 msk flour (5 gr.)
- 1 1/2 dcl. thick cream

## Directions

1. Clean the pike (do not scale), rinse well and dry with a fish towel. Fillet it, loosen the backbone and scrape the fish meat finely; no scales or bones must be included.
2. Mix with the seasonings, pass 3 times through a meat grinder and pound with a wooden pestle in a stone mortar until a fine mass.
3. Whisk the egg and add it little by little along with the flour, while stirring the forcemeat vigorously.
4. Pass through a hair sieve, place in a small saucepan set in a bowl of ice (otherwise it will break when the cream is worked in) and add the cream by teaspoonful while stirring evenly, then taste the forcemeat carefully.
5. Brush a long pan with butter and let it cool. Place the forcemeat in a piping bag with smooth or fluted tip and pipe directly into the pan in the form of small balls or oblong shapes, placed very close together.
6. When all the quenelles are formed, place the pan to the side of the stove and add boiling salted water carefully until it stands level over them. Let the water come to a boil while shaking the pan slowly, then let it cook until the quenelles have swelled and feel firm, about 2-3 minutes.
7. Remove immediately with a slotted spoon and place in a soup bowl. Pour the hot consommé or broth over just before serving.
