## Ingredients

- 1 fresh or lightly smoked ham, approximately 5 kg
- 50 g salt
- 1 tsp white pepper
- 1 tsp ginger
- 25 g sugar
- 2 bottles champagne or Rhine wine
- 1½ bottles sherry
- 50 g truffle
- 1 tbsp egg white
- 1 tsp dry mustard
- 2 tsp sugar
- 2 tbsp finely crushed rusks
- Coarse rye flour and water for sealing

## Directions

1. If using fresh ham, rub it with a mixture of salt, sugar, white pepper and ginger and let it rest 1 day. If using smoked ham, soak it in plenty of cold water for approximately 24 hours.
2. Dry the ham and place it in a kettle that is not wider than necessary for the ham to fit snugly. Add the wine so it reaches the same level as the ham; if needed, add more Rhine wine to reach this level.
3. Place the lid on the kettle and seal it well with a ring of dough made from coarse rye flour and water.
4. Cook the ham as in the previous recipe but turn it when it has cooked 2 hours, and shake the kettle often so the ham does not stick to the bottom.
5. Place it in a deep dish, pour the broth over it, and let the ham cool in the broth.
6. Remove the skin, press a towel against the fat to remove all dissolved fat, then lard the ham densely with beautifully cut truffle strips.
7. Beat the egg white with a fork to light foam, mix with mustard and sugar, and coat the ham's fat side with this mixture.
8. Sprinkle the crushed rusks over it, place the ham in a roasting pan and put it in a warm oven to achieve a beautiful brown color, which will take approximately ½ hour.
9. Place it on a warm dish, slice it, and arrange the slices back together so the ham appears whole.
10. Garnish with stewed mushrooms, tender peas, green beans, asparagus and salad or parsley.
11. Strain the broth, skim it well, reduce it, taste it, add finely chopped truffle and serve in a sauce bowl.
