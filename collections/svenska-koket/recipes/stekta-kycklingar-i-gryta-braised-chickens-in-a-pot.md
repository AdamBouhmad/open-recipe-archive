## Ingredients

- 3 chickens, approximately 1/2 kg each
- 1 bunch parsley
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 1/2 tablespoon salt (8 g)
- 3 tablespoons butter for roasting (60 g)
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 g)
- 4 deciliters thick cream
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper

## Directions

1. Clean the chickens, singe them, draw them (see page 299 onwards), rinse well and dry.
2. Clean the parsley, rinse it, wrap it in a cloth, chop it finely and work it into the butter.
3. Fill the chickens with the parsley butter, sew them closed, rub them with salt and truss them.
4. Brown the butter in an iron pot, add the birds together with the giblets (necks, livers and hearts) and brown well on all sides over gentle heat.
5. Then let them roast with breasts up with a tight lid until tender, approximately 1/2 hour. During this time baste them often with the butter in the pot.
6. Place the birds in a deep dish and cover over so they do not cool.
7. Take out the giblets and fry the flour 2 minutes with the butter in the pot, thin the cream and let the sauce boil 5 minutes, then season with the spices; finally add 10 g cold butter.
8. Cut the chickens in half, arrange on fried bread croutons; garnish with browned potatoes, salad and parsley.
9. Serve with green salad and redcurrant jelly. Strain the sauce and serve in a sauce bowl.
