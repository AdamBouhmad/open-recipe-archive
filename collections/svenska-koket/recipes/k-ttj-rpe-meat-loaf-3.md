## Ingredients

- 5 hg. lean pork belly
- 1 hg. lard
- 1/4 tsp. ginger
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 3-4 cooked, grated potatoes
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g)
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 2-3 dcl. cream or milk
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 g) for frying
- 5 dcl. broth for frying

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung in hot water, remove sinews and membranes and cut into dice.
2. Scrape the lard and cut likewise into dice.
3. Grind the meat and lard 6 times through the meat grinder, the last time together with the potato.
4. Work the forcemeat for 1 hour together with the spices, beaten egg and egg yolk and the milk, adding it tablespoon by tablespoon so that the forcemeat does not break.
5. Test the forcemeat as in the previous recipe.
6. Grease a long pan with butter, add the forcemeat in the form of an oblong ball, smooth it with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water.
7. Fry the meat loaf as in recipe no. 632, transfer to a warm serving dish, cut into slices, garnish with parsley and serve with potato purée, braised artichokes or braised cauliflower and with the strained, skimmed and seasoned meat gravy, or with brown sauce (see recipe no. 429).
