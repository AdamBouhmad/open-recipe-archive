## Ingredients

- 2 eggs
- 1 egg yolk
- 70 gr. granulated sugar
- 125 gr. flour
- 1 1/2 dcl. thick cream
- 1 kg. lard for frying

## Directions

1. Whisk the eggs and sugar with a steel whisk for 20 minutes, then add the cream and the flour, which should be sifted, and stir the batter well.
2. Heat the lard in a small iron saucepan, not larger than a saucer.
3. At the same time as the lard is placed in the saucepan, place the struvjäm (puff iron) in it to heat, otherwise the batter will not stick to the iron.
4. When the lard is hot, dip the iron in the batter and lower it into the hot lard until the puff gets a beautiful golden brown color, then loosen it with a larding pin and place it upside down on absorbent paper to drain.
5. Serve warm and sprinkled with sugar, if desired, with coffee or tea or as a dessert with jam or compote.
6. If no struvjäm is available, pour 2 msk. of the batter into a funnel and squeeze it back and forth into the lard so that it forms a broken cake according to the size of the saucepan.
7. When the puff gets a beautiful golden brown color on the bottom side, turn it with a lard spoon, and when it is even in color, place it on absorbent paper, over a stick if desired, so that the puff becomes curved.
