## Ingredients

- 850 grams beef
- 850 grams pork meat
- 850 grams lard
- 850 grams cooked, cooled potato
- 1.5 liters milk
- 4 tablespoons salt
- 2 teaspoons ground allspice
- 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
- 5 meters hooked casings
- 1 teaspoon potassium nitrate
- 2 tablespoons salt for rubbing
- 1 tablespoon powdered sugar for rubbing
- 1 teaspoon potassium nitrate for rubbing

## Directions

1. Wash the meat with a cloth dipped in cold water.
2. Cut it into pieces, remove sinews and membranes, and grind the meat 3 times through a meat grinder.
3. Pound it well.
4. Cut the lard into fine cubes.
5. Grind the potato (which should not be overcooked) through the grinder.
6. Heat the milk and let it cool.
7. Work all the ingredients well together for 1 hour and then stuff them into well-cleaned hooked casings, not too tightly.
8. Rinse the sausages with cold water and rub them with a mixture of salt, sugar, and saltpeter.
9. The meat sausage can then either be placed in salt brine or smoked for 2 days. In the latter case, use a smaller amount of milk for thinning and stuff it somewhat more tightly.
