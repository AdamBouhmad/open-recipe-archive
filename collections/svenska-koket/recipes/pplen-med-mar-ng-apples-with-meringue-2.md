## Ingredients

- 10-12 loose, tart apples
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 gr.)
- 1.5-2 deciliters caster sugar (128-170 gr.)
- 3 egg whites
- 105 gr. icing sugar
- 0.5 tablespoon butter (10 gr.) for the pan

## Directions

1. Wash, dry, peel, and cut the apples into thin slices or dice, removing the cores.
2. Melt the butter in a saucepan, add the apple slices together with the sugar and shake over heat until they are completely soft.
3. Place them in a buttered, heat-resistant form and let cool.
4. For the meringue: whip the egg whites until stiff, sift the icing sugar and fold it little by little into the egg white foam.
5. Spread half of the meringue over the apples.
6. Pipe the remaining meringue through a piping bag in diagonal squares over the meringue.
7. Bake in low oven heat until the meringue is half-dry and has a light yellow color.
8. Remove, let cool, and garnish each square with apple or red currant jelly.
9. Serve with whipped cream.
