## Ingredients

- 1 cabbage head
- water, salt
- 1 1/2 dcl. rice grains (125 g)
- 1/2 tbsp. butter, 6-7 dcl. milk
- 300 g boneless beef
- 100 g boneless veal or pork
- 50 g lard
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 1-2 dcl. cream
- 4 tbsp. butter (80 g)
- 8 dcl. broth

## Directions

1. Carefully loosen the cabbage leaves from the stem, place them in boiling salted water and blanch for about 5 minutes.
2. Remove them with a slotted spoon and let them drain on a cloth, then cut off the coarsest part of the main vein.
3. Rinse the rice grains, scald in hot water, pour into a colander and let them drain.
4. Melt the butter in a pot, pour in the milk, let it come to a boil, add the grains and let them cook with stirring until they are almost soft, then set aside to cool.
5. Prepare the ground meat as in recipe no. 637.
6. Mix the ground meat and the cold grains well and season carefully. (If the meat is too hard, thin it with a little more cream.)
7. Place one tbsp. of the meat mixture on each cabbage leaf, roll it up and tie with fine twine that has been dipped in boiling water.
8. Brown the butter in a frying pot, add the rolls and brown nicely, thin with a little of the boiling broth and let them cook slowly with the lid tightly closed for about 2 hours.
9. During this time, add more broth as needed.
10. Remove them, cut off the twine and place the rolls on a platter.
11. Strain the sauce, skim off fat and pour it over them.
12. The rice grains can be omitted, but then calculate more meat.
