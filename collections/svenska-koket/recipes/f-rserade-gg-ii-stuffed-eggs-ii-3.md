## Ingredients

- 3 eggs
- 1 hg. boneless beef
- 1/2 hg. boneless veal
- 30 gr. lard
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp. crushed rusks
- 1 tsp. salt, white pepper
- 1 tsp. finely chopped pickled onion
- 4 tbsp. cream
- 1 egg for breading
- 1 cup crushed rusks for breading
- 1/2 kg. frying fat

## Directions

1. Cook the eggs for 5 minutes, cool in cold water and peel.
2. Dry the meat with a cloth, wring out in hot water, cut together with the lard into pieces and pass 5 times through a meat grinder.
3. Beat with a wooden spoon until the forcemeat becomes even and smooth.
4. Whisk the egg yolks well with the cream and work in the forcemeat tablespoon by tablespoon, together with the rusks, seasonings and pickled onion.
5. Taste the forcemeat carefully.
6. Coat the peeled eggs with the meat forcemeat, roll in crushed rusks, then in the beaten egg and again in crushed rusks.
7. Flatten the forcemeat at both ends and let it rest for 1 hour to harden.
8. Heat the frying fat and cook the eggs in it until they are nicely brown.
9. Arrange on brown paper so that the fat drains off.
10. Cut them in half crosswise and insert a parsley sprig as a handle.
11. Garnish with salad and serve with green salad in sharp sauce or with mayonnaise as a breakfast dish.
