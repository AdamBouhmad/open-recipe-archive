## Ingredients

- 6 large tomatoes
- 20 gr. dried mushrooms
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 3 tbsp. flour (30 gr.)
- 3 dcl. cream
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp. finely chopped parsley
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 gr.) for the shells
- 2 tbsp. crushed rusks for the shells

## Directions

1. Dip the tomatoes for 1 minute in boiling water and peel off the skin. Open the tomatoes at the stem and carefully remove the seeds with a teaspoon handle.
2. Let the dried mushrooms soak in water for several hours, then place them in boiling, lightly salted water and let them cook for approximately 1/2 hour. Drain in a colander and let them drain well. Cut them into fine cubes and fry in half of the butter until clear.
3. Fry the remaining butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the cream and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes. Add the whisked egg yolk and let the sauce simmer. Add the mushrooms and parsley, and season the stew with the spices.
4. Fill the tomatoes with the stew. Grease scallop shells or small ovenproof molds with butter and place one tomato in each. Sprinkle with crushed rusks and pour clarified butter over them. Place the shells in a long pan with coarse salt.
5. Place in a hot oven and gratinée for 10-15 minutes. Serve as a middle course or with roasted meat.
