## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg eel
- 2 tbsp salt
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- dill
- 3/4 liter water
- 1 tbsp vinegar
- 6 allspice berries
- 3/4 liter stock
- 6 leaves gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tbsp cold water
- 2 tbsp good white wine

## Directions

1. Skin the eel, clean, rinse well and dry.
2. Clip off the fins and carefully remove the backbone, but do not cut the fish through the back.
3. Rub it with half the salt and pepper and lay dill stalks on it.
4. Roll the eel up from the tail and bind the roll with thick thread that has been dipped in hot water.
5. Place it in cold water together with the backbone, vinegar, remaining salt and pepper and let it simmer slowly until tender, about 40 minutes. (If the eel is large, it should be cut in half crosswise and two rolls made from it.)
6. Remove the roll, let it cool and cut into 1 1/2 cm thick slices.
7. Taste the stock and prepare the aspic as in recipe no. 181; when cold, add the wine.
8. Prepare the à-la-daube as in the previous recipe, but garnish the mold only with dill.
9. Serve for the smorgasbord.
10. Some of the aspic can also be poured onto a platter and set. Lay the eel slices on it and cover with the remaining aspic. When set, remove the slices with a round mold, place on a platter and garnish with dill.
