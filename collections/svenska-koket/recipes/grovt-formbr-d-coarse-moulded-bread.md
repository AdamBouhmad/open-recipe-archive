## Ingredients

- 6 decilitres sour milk or malt drink
- 70 gr pressed yeast
- 525 gr ground rye flour
- 6 hectograms wheat flour
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr)
- 2 decilitres syrup
- 1 tablespoon crushed fennel
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped orange peel
- 1 tablespoon butter for the moulds (20 gr)

## Directions

1. Sift the flour into a bowl
2. Whisk the sour milk, skimmed milk or malt drink and stir it into the flour
3. Mix the yeast with the salt and add it together with the syrup and spices, and work the dough until it is smooth and glossy, then place it in two buttered bread moulds and let it rise overnight
4. Put the moulds in a not too hot oven and bake the breads for about 1 hour, towards the end in somewhat milder heat
5. Test with a wooden fork and brush with hot water when done, turn out of the moulds and place between cloths until cold
6. If the dough is made with warm liquid and lukewarm syrup, it rises in about 5 hours
