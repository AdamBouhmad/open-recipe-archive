## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg. beef rib or marrow pipe
- 1 kg. veal neck
- 1 carrot
- 1 parsnip
- 1/4 celeriac
- 1 red onion
- 80 gr. butter
- 4 liters water
- 3 parsley stems
- 1 clove
- 10 gr. salt
- 3 hectograms lean, boneless beef for clarification
- 1 egg white and the rinsed eggshells for clarification

## Directions

1. Prepare the meat as in recipe no. 222 and cut it from the bones into small pieces.
2. Crush the bones as finely as possible and brown them well in half of the butter in a frying pan together with the prepared and chopped vegetables and the onion.
3. Place the browned bones in the broth pot and deglaze the frying pan with a little boiling water, which is then poured into the pot.
4. Add the cold water and parsley, and set the broth over fresh fire to boil rapidly.
5. When the boiling point is reached, skim it well, wipe the edge, place the lid on and let the broth cook slowly for 4 hours.
6. During this time, brown the meat in the remaining butter; when the bones have cooked 4 hours, add the meat and let everything cook for an additional 3 hours.
7. Treat the broth as in the previous recipe and serve with the same kinds of garnish.
8. The dark broth is used advantageously for à-la-daube of game, poultry in aspic, chaud-froid, etc., but not for prepared soups and purées.
