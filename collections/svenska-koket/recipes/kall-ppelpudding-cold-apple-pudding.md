## Ingredients

- 190 grams sugar
- 2 deciliters white wine
- 2 deciliters water
- 1 1/2 liters tart apples (approximately 800 grams)
- peel of 1/2 lemon
- juice of 1 lemon
- 6 leaves red gelatin
- 1 deciliter water, for the gelatin

## Directions

1. Make a clear, simmering liquid by boiling the sugar, wine and water together.
2. Wash, dry and peel the apples, cut them into wedges, remove the cores and place directly in the sugar liquid.
3. Add the lemon peel and cook the apples until they become a puree.
4. Pass the apple puree through a hair sieve and flavor with lemon juice and more sugar if desired.
5. Rinse the gelatin in cold water, cut it into pieces, dissolve it in hot water and mix well with the apple puree.
6. Immediately fill the mixture into a water-rinsed and sugar-sprinkled à-la-daube mold of porcelain and let stand on ice for approximately 5 hours.
7. When the pudding is to be served, carefully loosen it around the edge with a pointed knife and turn it out onto a tart plate.
8. Garnish with biscuits and serve with vanilla sauce or whipped cream. It is best to cook the apple puree the day before the pudding is to be made.
