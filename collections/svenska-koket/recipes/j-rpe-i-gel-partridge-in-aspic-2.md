## Ingredients

- 6 partridges
- 1 hg. butter
- 3 tsk. salt
- 1/2 lit. boiling broth
- Meat bones
- 2 lit. good broth
- 12–15 leaves gelatin
- 3 egg whites and rinsed eggshells
- 1 msk. cold water
- 1/2 dcl. good Madeira or sherry
- 6–8 truffles
- Aspic croutons
- 2 heads of lettuce

## Directions

1. Clean the partridges, singe them, remove the entrails and cut off wings and bones. Rinse very well, dry with a meat cloth and truss (see poultry). Brown the butter in a roasting pan, place the birds with breasts up and roast in a warm oven for about 1/2 hour, basting frequently and adding broth. Remove and let cool, then carefully loosen the breasts from the carcasses; cut each breast half into 3 slices. Return the carcasses to the roasting pan, add broth and let simmer with the meat bones until about 1/2 lit. remains. Strain the broth, let cool and skim off all fat carefully.
2. Prepare the aspic as in recipe no. 181; when cold, add the wine. Pour a little of the clear aspic onto the bottom of 12 rinsed partridge molds and place on ice to set. Garnish the molds with truffles cut into attractive shapes. Coat the garnish with a few drops of aspic and let set so the garnish does not move when the meat is added. Place half a partridge breast in each mold so it appears whole with the outer side toward the garnish, then fill the molds with aspic. Place on a cold place to set. Pour the remaining aspic onto a platter and let set.
3. Cut a cone-shaped crouton from a day-old wheat bread, hollow out the top, wrap with tinfoil and place in the center of a large round silver or porcelain platter. Place flowers and greens in the top of the crouton. Turn the partridge breasts out of the molds and arrange against the crouton. Between them garnish with piped aspic and around the platter with small triangular aspic croutons decorated with chopped truffle. Garnish the arrangement with the inner pale lettuce leaves and serve as an intermediate course with mayonnaise.
