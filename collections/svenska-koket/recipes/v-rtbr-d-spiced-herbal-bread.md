## Ingredients

- 6 liters good herbal wort
- 1/2 liter skim milk
- 120 grams pressed yeast
- 2 parts syrup
- 5 teaspoons salt
- 4 tablespoons crushed fennel
- 2 tablespoons anise
- 6 tablespoons finely cut orange peel
- 1/4 kg wheat flour
- rye flour, sifted through steel sieve

## Directions

1. The day before the bread is to be baked, boil the wort down to 1 1/2 liters.
2. In the evening, make a loose dough from the slightly warm milk, wheat flour, and dissolved yeast.
3. Early the following day, warm the wort and mix it into the previously made dough together with enough rye flour to make a suitably firm dough.
4. When this has risen, add the boiled and cooled syrup and the spices.
5. If needed, work in more rye flour, after which let the dough rise again.
6. Shape out with wheat flour, and let the buns rise on soft, warm baking cloths with baking cloths stretched between them.
7. When the buns have risen, place them on warm baking sheets, brush with cold water and set into a good oven heat.
8. When they are half-baked, brush them with warm water, likewise when they are taken out of the oven.
9. Place the buns between soft cloths to prevent the crust from becoming hard.
10. Best is to bake spiced herbal bread in a baker's oven. This should be heated as soon as the dough is ready, and swept at the same time the bread is shaped. It should then be shut completely tight until the bread is ready to bake.
