## Ingredients

- 1 ox tongue
- water
- 3-4 tablespoons salt
- 10 white peppercorns
- 2 cloves
- 1 carrot

## Directions

1. Clean the tongue and let it soak in water for a couple of hours.
2. Place it in boiling water, which should just cover it, and skim well.
3. Add the spices and the peeled carrot.
4. Let the tongue simmer slowly for 2½-3 hours, or until the skin can be easily pulled off.
5. After the skin is removed, place it back in the broth to keep it warm until serving.
6. Slice the tongue diagonally in ½ cm thick slices, arrange them so that the tongue appears whole on the plate when served.
7. Garnish with parsley or salad and serve with boiled or stewed vegetables or with a sauce, such as brown mushroom, morel, or olive sauce, and potatoes.
8. Leftover boiled tongue can be sliced, dipped in egg and breadcrumbs, and fried in butter.
9. It can also be served in brown caper sauce made from 4 dcl meat stock, 1-2 teaspoons cornstarch, 1 tablespoon finely chopped capers, ½ tablespoon Madeira, salt, and sugar. When the sauce has cooked 5 minutes, add the tongue slices and cook 5 more minutes. Serve with browned potatoes as a breakfast dish.
