## Ingredients

- 1 chicken
- 1 pork tenderloin
- 1 kg veal shank
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 5 liters brown beef broth
- 1 can of turtle (1/4 liter)
- 2 carrots
- 1/2 celeriac
- 1 leek
- 6 parsley stems
- 1 red onion
- 1 clove
- a little thyme and marjoram
- salt, cayenne
- 1-2 deciliters aged Madeira
- 1-2 tablespoons fine cognac

## Directions

1. Prepare the chicken in the usual way and cut into pieces (reserve the breast meat for quenelles). Cut the pork tenderloin into slices.
2. Rinse the veal shank, dry and cut into pieces.
3. Brown the meat in the butter, place in a pot and add the cold, clear broth. Bring the broth to a boil, skim, and add the trimmed vegetables and spices together with the broth from the turtle can.
4. Let everything cook with the lid tightly closed for 4-5 hours.
5. Remove the meat with a slotted spoon, strain the broth through a cloth, skim well and measure—should be 3 liters, or let it boil down with the lid on if necessary.
6. Place the turtle cut into beautiful pieces into the broth, bring to a boil again and season with the spices and wine. Serve with hard-boiled egg yolks and quenelles of chicken forcemeat.
7. If a thicker soup is desired, thicken with 20 g cornstarch mixed with 1 cup cold water; then let it cook for 5 minutes.
