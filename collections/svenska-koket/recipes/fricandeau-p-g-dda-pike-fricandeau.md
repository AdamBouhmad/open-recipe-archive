## Ingredients

- 1 pike, 1 1/2 kg
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 4 parsley stems
- 5 white peppercorns
- 1/2 hectogram lard
- 3 decilitres fish broth
- 2 decilitres white wine
- 1 bay leaf
- Browned sugar (1/2 tablespoon caster sugar, 1 tablespoon water)

## Directions

1. Gut the pike, rinse well and dry. Remove the skin with the scales; the head may remain but cut away the gills and fins. The fish can also be cut into fillets, placing the skin side against the cutting board and carefully cutting the flesh from the skin.
2. Rub the pike with 2 teaspoons of the salt and let rest for 1 hour, then dry again and lard in close rows with 1/2 hectogram of lard, cut into fine even strips, which are dipped in the remaining salt.
3. Place the remaining lard, cut into flat slices, on the bottom of a small fish kettle, place the pike on top and add the broth, wine, parsley, spices and browned sugar.
4. Let the fish simmer slowly with a tightly closed lid for about 20 minutes, then place on a hot serving dish and garnish with parsley and small boiled potatoes. Strain the cooking liquid, skim, thicken if desired with a little wheat flour, let boil for 5 minutes, taste and pour over the fish.
