## Ingredients

- 5 litres apples (preferably Gravensteins)
- 2 litres water
- sugar equal in weight to the juice

## Directions

1. Rinse the apples, dry them, cut them into pieces, removing all dark, spoiled and worm-eaten parts.
2. Cook the apple pieces soft in the water but do not let them fall apart.
3. Pour into cheesecloth and let it drip until the following day.
4. Weigh the juice and dissolve the sugar in the cold juice, then let it come to a boil and boil rapidly for 15-20 minutes under careful skimming.
5. The jelly is ready when a drop of it, poured onto a plate, quickly sets.
