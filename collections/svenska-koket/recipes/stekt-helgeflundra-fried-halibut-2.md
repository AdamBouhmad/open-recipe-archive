## Ingredients

- 1 kg halibut
- 5 tbsp salt (75 g)
- 3 liters water
- 1/2 lemon
- 1/2 egg
- 2 tbsp crushed hardtack
- 1 hg butter

## Directions

1. Clean the fish piece, remove the dark skin and treat the fish as in the previous recipe.
2. Once the fish piece is rubbed with lemon juice, brush it with the beaten egg on the side from which the skin has been removed, and sprinkle with crushed hardtack.
3. Butter a long pan with half of the butter on a spot as large as the fish piece occupies, place it there with the breaded side up, cover with the remaining butter distributed in flakes, and fry in a moderately warm oven for about 40 minutes, during which it should often be basted with the butter in the pan.
4. If desired, the butter can be thinned with 2 dcl of boiling water.
5. Garnish as above and serve with lemon or mushroom sauce and boiled potatoes.
6. Alternatively, after rubbing the fish piece with lemon juice, it can be cut across into 1.5 cm thick slices, which are beaten with a damp meat mallet. The fish slices are dipped in beaten egg, turned in crushed hardtack, fried in butter on the stovetop and served with lemon sauce, stewed vegetables or mashed potatoes.
