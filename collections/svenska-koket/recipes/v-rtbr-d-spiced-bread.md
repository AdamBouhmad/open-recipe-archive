## Ingredients

- 1.5 liters wort (concentrated from 5 liters wort)
- 1.5 kg wheat flour
- 0.5 liters unskimmed milk
- 125 grams pressed yeast
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 grams)
- 100 grams rye sifted flour
- 100 grams butter or good lard
- 0.5 liters syrup
- 3 tablespoons ground fennel
- 4 tablespoons finely chopped orange peel

## Directions

1. The day before baking, prepare the wort, which takes about 6 hours.
2. In the evening, sift about 0.75 kg of the wheat flour (reserve the rest for shaping) into a large bowl or trough.
3. Mix the cold milk and yeast (dissolved with salt) into the flour and work the dough quickly.
4. Cover with a cloth and let it rest in a warm place overnight.
5. Early the next morning, warm the wort to about 40 degrees and add it to the yeast dough along with enough rye flour to make a firm dough.
6. Cover with a warm cloth and let it rise to double its size.
7. In a saucepan, mix the fat, syrup and spices, bring to a boil and cool.
8. Work the spice mixture into the dough along with more flour and let it rise again.
9. Work the dough on the baking board with the remaining wheat flour and divide into 12 parts.
10. Work each part until smooth and without cracks, then roll into an oblong roll and place on a soft, warmed baking cloth dusted with flour.
11. Place the rolls next to each other with the cloth drawn up in a large fold between each roll and cover.
12. When risen, turn the rolls onto a bread paddle, place on buttered, lukewarm baking sheets and let stand about 10 minutes.
13. Prick lengthwise with a larding needle, brush with cold wort or egg yolk and water, and bake about 45 minutes in a not-too-hot oven.
14. When half-baked, brush with hot wort, and again when fully baked.
15. Let dry briefly in the oven before removing and placing between cloths so the crust becomes soft.
16. Instead of wort, porter, health ale or Christmas ale with added sugar may be used.
17. The bread bakes best in a baking oven, which should be heated as soon as the dough is ready and swept clean as soon as the bread is shaped. The oven should remain completely closed until the bread is ready to bake.
