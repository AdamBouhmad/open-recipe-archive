## Ingredients

- 1 veal tongue
- 2 liters water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- white pepper
- 3 cups good beef broth
- 1 teaspoon soy sauce
- 4 deciliters meat stock
- 1 teaspoon mondamin flour
- 1 tablespoon Madeira wine
- 2 tablespoons finely cut, browned mushrooms

## Directions

1. Brush the tongue well, rinse it, place it in the boiling water with salt added, and let it cook until the skin can easily be pulled off.
2. Brown the butter in an iron pot, place the tongue in and brown it nicely on all sides, salt it and dilute with the broth, with soy sauce added.
3. Let it cook slowly until tender, or about 1 hour, and baste it every ten minutes so it does not become dry.
4. Cut it, arrange it on a warm serving platter and garnish with small bread croutons.
5. Thicken the meat stock with the mondamin flour, dissolved in the wine, and let the sauce cook about 5 minutes.
6. Add the mushrooms and season the sauce with salt and pepper.
7. Pour a little of the sauce over the tongue and pour the rest into a sauce bowl.
8. Serve as a breakfast course with browned potatoes.
