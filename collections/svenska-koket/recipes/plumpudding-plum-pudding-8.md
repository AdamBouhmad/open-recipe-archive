## Ingredients

- 1 hg. fresh apples
- 1 hg. Malaga raisins
- 1 hg. sultana raisins
- 1 hg. currants
- 60 gr. preserved orange peel
- 225 gr. beef kidney suet
- 225 gr. breadcrumbs
- 120 gr. sugar
- juice and zest of 1/2 orange
- juice and zest of 1/4 lemon
- 10 gr. ground ginger
- 1/2 tsk. ground cinnamon
- 1/4 tsk. ground cardamom
- 1/4 tsk. ground cloves
- 2 eggs
- 2 dcl. milk
- 1 hg. flour
- 60 gr. sweet almonds
- 8 cl. cognac or rum
- 1 dcl. porter
- 1 msk. butter for the mold (20 gr.)
- 2 msk. wheat flour for the mold (20 gr.)

## Directions

1. Peel the apples, remove cores and chop finely.
2. Clean the Malaga raisins, rinse well, split and remove pits.
3. Clean and rinse the sultana raisins and currants well.
4. Cut the orange peel into fine dice.
5. If desired, soak all the fruit in the liqueur for two days before the pudding is to be prepared.
6. Rinse the kidney suet, dry in a cloth, clean and chop finely together with the wheat flour.
7. Weigh breadcrumbs, soak in milk and squeeze out.
8. Grate the orange and lemon zest finely and press out the juice.
9. Work the kidney suet well with the breadcrumbs, then add the fruit and all other ingredients, and stir the mixture well.
10. Butter a large ovenproof mold and dust with wheat flour, pack the mixture into it, and bind the mold with a buttered and floured cloth, tying it in a knot on top of the pudding.
11. Boil the pudding for about 4 hours in water or steam.
12. Serve warm or cold with wine sauce or flaming sauce (see no. 457 and 465).
