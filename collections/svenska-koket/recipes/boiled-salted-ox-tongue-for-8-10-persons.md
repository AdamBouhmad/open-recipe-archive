## Ingredients

- 1 salted ox tongue
- water

## Directions

1. The tongue, which should be lightly salted (see salting of beef), should lie for a couple of hours in chilled water, is placed in cold water, skimmed, and boils for 15 minutes at 100°. The broth is then cooled to 82°–83° and at this temperature the tongue lies for 3½ hours, or until the skin can easily be drawn off. It is then placed in a stone pot with a flat bottom, the broth is poured over and the tongue is allowed to cool in it — it becomes juicier if allowed to cool in the broth. If it is to be served warm, it should be warmed in the broth after the skin has been removed. If the tongue is to be served with mixed meat dishes, it is sliced as in no. 493. For cold sliced tongue, thinner slices are cut, and then it is most economical to begin slicing from the tip of the tongue. If the tongue is heavily salted, it should lie in water for about 12 hours.
