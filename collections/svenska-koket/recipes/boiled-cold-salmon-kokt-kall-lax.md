## Ingredients

- 1 salmon, 3 kg
- For the broth: 5 liters water
- 2.5 dcl vinegar
- 70 g coarse salt
- 2 carrots
- 1 red onion
- 2 bay leaves
- 6 dill sprigs
- 12 white peppercorns
- 23 g fine salt
- For white aspic: 1 liter fish broth
- 10 sheets white gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tbsp cold water
- 1 tbsp white wine
- For red aspic: 4 dcl fish broth
- 4 sheets red gelatin
- 1 egg white
- 1 tbsp white wine
- For garnish: mayonnaise, capers or truffle, beets, tomatoes, green salad, parsley, hard-boiled eggs

## Directions

1. Prepare the broth and clean the salmon as in the previous recipe.
2. If the salmon is to be served on two platters, split it lengthwise into two halves and carefully remove the bone.
3. Place both halves with the bone in the cold broth and cook as in recipe 327.
4. Let the salmon cool in the broth.
5. From the broth, prepare white and red aspic as in recipe 181.
6. Let a little of the white fish aspic set on the bottom of the platters where the salmon will be served.
7. Place the salmon on top.
8. Remove the skin in 3 cm wide strips running crosswise, leaving the skin in equally wide strips between.
9. Where the skin has been removed, spread mayonnaise mixed with a little white fish aspic and let set.
10. Garnish the mayonnaise with capers or truffle in beautiful figures, such as French fleurs-de-lis or royal crowns.
11. Garnish the remaining strips with white and red fish aspic, piped through a fine pastry bag, and small slices of beets or tomatoes.
12. Garnish the platter with green salad, parsley, hollowed-out tomatoes filled with vegetable salad, egg halves with fluted edges, and aspic.
13. Serve with mayonnaise or remoulade sauce.
