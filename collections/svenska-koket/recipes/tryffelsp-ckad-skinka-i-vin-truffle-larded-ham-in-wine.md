## Ingredients

- 1 fresh or lightly smoked ham, approximately 5 kg
- 50 g salt
- 1 tsp white pepper
- 1 tsp ginger
- 25 g sugar
- 2 bottles champagne or Rhine wine
- 1.5 bottles sherry
- 50 g truffle
- 1 tbsp egg white
- 1 tsp dry mustard
- 2 tsp sugar
- 2 tbsp finely crushed rusks

## Directions

1. If using fresh ham, rub it with a mixture of salt, sugar, white pepper and ginger and let it rest for 1 day. If using smoked ham, soak it in plenty of cold water for approximately 24 hours.
2. Dry the ham and place it in a pot that is not too large, just large enough to fit the ham snugly. Add wine so that it reaches the same level as the ham; if needed, add more Rhine wine.
3. Place the lid on the pot and seal it well with a ring of dough made from coarse rye flour and water.
4. Cook the ham as in the previous recipe but turn it after it has cooked for 2 hours. Shake the pot frequently so that the ham does not stick to the bottom.
5. Place the ham in a deep dish, pour the cooking liquid over it, and let it cool in the liquid.
6. Remove the skin, press a towel against the fat to remove all dissolved fat, then lard the ham densely with beautifully cut truffle strips.
7. Beat the egg white with a fork into a light foam, mix with mustard and sugar, and brush this mixture on the ham's fat side.
8. Sprinkle the crushed rusks over the top. Place the ham in a roasting pan and set it in a warm oven to achieve a beautiful brown color, which takes approximately 1/2 hour.
9. Place the ham on a warm plate, slice it, and arrange the slices back together so that the ham appears whole.
10. Garnish with stewed mushrooms, tender peas, green beans, asparagus and salad or parsley.
11. Strain the cooking liquid, skim it well, reduce it, taste it, add finely chopped truffle, and serve in a sauce bowl.
