## Ingredients

- 1 kg lamb breast
- 2 liters secondary broth
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 g)
- 1 leek
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 2 carrots
- 1 parsnip
- 1/2 celery root
- 1/4 liter preserved (or 2 tablespoons fresh) peas
- 12 small Jerusalem artichokes
- 1 small cauliflower head
- salt
- white pepper

## Directions

1. The lamb breast is dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water, broken well and set to boil in the broth.
2. When the meat is half-cooked it is removed and cut into small, even pieces. The broth is strained, skimmed carefully and poured back into the pot.
3. The meat is browned in 2 tablespoons of the butter together with the leek, cut into fine strips, the flour is sprinkled over and the meat is stirred well.
4. It is placed in the broth, which is allowed to cook slowly for about 1 hour.
5. The roots are prepared, cut into fine strips, browned in the remaining butter and added together with the peas to the soup 1/2 hour before it is done.
6. The Jerusalem artichokes and cauliflower are cleaned in the usual way, placed in 1/2 liter of boiling water with 1/2 teaspoon salt added, and allowed to cook until tender.
7. They are placed in the soup bowl and the broth is boiled down until it becomes about 3 deciliters, when it is poured over the vegetables.
8. The soup is skimmed, seasoned with spices, poured into the soup bowl and served very hot.
