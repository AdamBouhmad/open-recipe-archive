## Ingredients

- 2 kg half-ripe medlars
- 3 liters water
- 1 part salt
- 2 tablespoons vinegar
- 2 kg lump sugar
- 5 parts water

## Directions

1. Rinse the fruit and prick with a silver fork (the skin will not burst in cooking). Cut off the stems somewhat.
2. Mix the water and salt cold together, place the fruit in the brine and let it sit for two days.
3. Remove it and let it sit in fresh water for one day.
4. Place the fruit on the heat in new, fresh water. As the berries float up, remove them with a slotted spoon and place them in vinegar-mixed water.
5. When they are completely cold, place them on linen to drain.
6. From the sugar and water, boil a clear, thick syrup. Let the syrup cool and then pour it over the berries.
7. Sprinkle the top layer with sugar (otherwise the berries will be dark).
8. The next day, pour off the syrup, boil it up, let it cool and pour it over the fruit.
9. Repeat the following day. Now let the syrup stand over the berries for 3-4 days.
10. Pour off the syrup once more, let it come to a boil, then carefully place the berries back in.
11. Bring the jam to a light boil, remove the berries with a slotted spoon and place them in jars.
12. Let the syrup boil down further until it is thick.
13. Cool it and pour it over the fruit.
14. Tie the jars and store in a cool place.
