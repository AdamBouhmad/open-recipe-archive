## Ingredients

- 1.5 hectograms fine prune plums (approximately 14 pieces)
- 1 hectogram dried apricots (approximately 12 pieces)
- 1 deciliter caster sugar (85 gr.)
- 4 deciliters water
- 1.5 deciliters crushed sugar (143 gr.)
- 0.5 kg large, beautiful apples
- 3 egg whites
- 105 gr. icing sugar

## Directions

1. Rinse the dried fruit well in several lukewarm waters, let drain on cloth and layer alternately with caster sugar in a pot.
2. Add water and let the fruit stand for approximately 12 hours.
3. Pour off the water and cook it with the crushed sugar to a clear syrup, then let cool.
4. Wash, dry, peel, and cut the apples into 4-6 wedges, removing the cores.
5. Place them in the sugar syrup and cook until they are soft, but do not let them fall apart.
6. Remove with a slotted spoon and let cool, then cook the syrup until it is as thick as jelly.
7. On a silver dish, arrange the apples, plums, and apricots alternately, placing some of the reduced syrup between each layer.
8. Prepare the meringue as in the preceding recipe and pipe through a piping bag in beautiful streaks over the fruit.
9. Place in low oven heat.
10. When the meringue is half-dry and has a light yellow color, remove the arrangement and let cool.
11. Serve with whipped cream or vanilla sauce.
