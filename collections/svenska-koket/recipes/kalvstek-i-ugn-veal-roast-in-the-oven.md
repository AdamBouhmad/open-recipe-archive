## Ingredients

- 4½ kg. veal roast
- 3 hg. lard
- 2 tsk. salt (30 gr.)
- 1 tsk. white pepper
- 4 msk. butter (80 gr.)
- 2 lit. broth or water

## Directions

1. The shank with the knuckle is used. Cut off the leg and cook it for broth to baste the roast with; the meat from it can be used for herring salad or gratin. The meat, which should have hung for 3–10 days depending on the season, is beaten well, dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and all sinewy membranes are trimmed away. The roast is larded on the outer thigh and rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper. A long pan is heated, the butter is fried in it, the roast is placed in with the inner thigh up, set in a hot oven to brown, and is often basted with the butter. When it is browned, it is diluted with boiling broth, but no more than about ½ cm. high in the pan; the meat is then roasted until tender, or about 3 hours, during which it is basted every 10 minutes and more broth is added as needed. If the roast becomes too brown, it is covered with a buttered paper. Served with reduced sauce and mixed vegetables, small potatoes, cucumbers, and salads.
