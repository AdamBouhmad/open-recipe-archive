## Ingredients

- 2 hg. fine sugar
- 4 dcl. water
- 1 1/2 liters good, tart apples (about 8 hg.)
- juice of 1/2 lemon or 2 tbsp. vinegar

## Directions

1. Cook a clear syrup from the sugar and water and let it cool.
2. Rinse the apples, dry them, peel them thinly, cut them into 4–6 pieces according to size, and remove the cores.
3. Place the apples in 1 1/2 liters of water with the lemon juice or vinegar as they are cut.
4. Take only as many apple pieces as will fit on the surface of the sugar syrup at a time, let them drain on cloth, place them in the sugar syrup, and let them boil just until soft, which is tested with a whisk.
5. Place them on a flat dish with a slotted spoon and let them cool.
6. Add more sugar and water to the syrup if needed.
7. When all the apple pieces are cooked, boil down the syrup if it is thin.
8. Place the apples in a compote bowl, strain the syrup and pour it over, and garnish the compote, if desired, with apple, currant, or gooseberry jelly.
9. Serve cold with whipped cream.
