## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg fat whitefish
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 3 parsley stems
- 1/2 tablespoon finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 3 tablespoons crushed breadcrumbs
- 1/2 egg
- 80 g butter
- 1 1/2 dcl French wine
- 1 1/2 dcl water
- 1 dcl broth

## Directions

1. Scale and clean the fish, see page 171, and rub with salt.
2. Clean the parsley, rinse it, wring it in a cloth, chop it finely and mix with the onion and crushed breadcrumbs and, if desired, with 1 tablespoon blanched, finely chopped mushrooms.
3. Brush the fish with egg and sprinkle on both sides with the breadcrumb mixture.
4. Grease a long pan with half of the cold butter over an area as large as the fish occupies.
5. Place the fish in with the back up and sides spread out and place the rest of the butter in flakes on top; add half of the wine and water and place the pan in a warm oven.
6. Let the fish fry for about 20 minutes, basting it often and adding the rest of the wine and water.
7. Place it on a warm platter, bring the sauce to a boil with the broth and thin it, if necessary, with a little wheat flour, strain and pour over the fish.
8. Garnish with parsley and fluted lemon slices and serve with mashed potatoes.
