## Ingredients

- 7 dcl. water
- 1 dcl. syrup
- 8 hg. graham flour
- 50 gr. pressed yeast
- 2 tsp. salt (10 gr.)
- wheat flour (approx. 4 hg.)
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 gr.) for the pans

## Directions

1. Boil the water with the syrup and mix with the graham flour.
2. Work the dough quickly, cover well, and let it stand overnight.
3. Early the following morning, add the yeast, dissolved with the salt and 2 tbsp. lukewarm water, then work the dough well and let it rise covered in a warm place until it begins to crack.
4. Spread some of the wheat flour out on the baking board, turn the dough out into it, and work it very well until it is smooth and reasonably firm.
5. Form into two oblong loaves, which are placed in buttered bread pans and allowed to rise well, then baked in a warm oven for about 1 hour.
6. Brush with hot water, turn out, wrap in baking cloth and felt, and let rest until the next day.
7. The dough can also be made with milk on the same day it is to be baked. The milk is warmed and the dough prepared as described on page 462. The syrup is then omitted.
