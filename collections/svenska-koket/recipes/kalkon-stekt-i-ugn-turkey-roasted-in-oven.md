## Ingredients

- 1 turkey cock, about 5 kg.
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 1 1/2 hg. lard, in 1 slice
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- 1 liter broth

## Directions

1. Pluck the turkey as soon as it is slaughtered and let it hang for a few days so that the meat becomes tender.
2. Draw it, rinse (see p. 300), dry with a cloth, rub inside and outside with a mixture of salt and pepper, truss it, and cover the breast with a large slice of lard, which is tied on with fine twine.
3. Butter a roasting pan on an area as large as the bird occupies, place the bird on it with breast up, add the giblets (see p. 300) and pour on half the broth.
4. Place the turkey in a warm oven and let it roast until tender, or about 2-3 hours, during which baste it about every 10 minutes and add more broth as needed.
5. If the bird becomes too brown, cover it with buttered paper.
6. Strain the meat gravy, skim it, reduce it, season it and serve as gravy, or prepare it as in no. 429.
7. Carve the turkey neatly, place on a warm platter so that it looks whole, garnish with salad lettuce, parsley and browned potatoes.
8. Serve with green salad and sour salads.
