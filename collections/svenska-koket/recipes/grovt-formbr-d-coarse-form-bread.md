## Ingredients

- 6 decilitres sour milk or ale
- 70 gr pressed yeast
- 525 gr ground rye flour
- 6 hectograms wheat flour
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr)
- 2 decilitres syrup
- 1 tablespoon crushed fennel
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped orange peel
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 gr) for the pans

## Directions

1. Sift the flour into a bowl.
2. Whip the sour milk, skim milk or ale into the flour.
3. Mix the yeast with salt and add it along with the syrup and spices.
4. Work the dough until it is smooth and glossy, then place it in two buttered bread pans.
5. Let it rise overnight.
6. Place the pans in a not-too-hot oven and bake for about 1 hour, toward the end in slightly gentler heat.
7. Test with a whisk and brush with hot water when done.
8. Turn out of pans and lay between cloths until cold.
9. If the dough is made with warm liquid and lukewarm syrup, it will rise in about 5 hours.
