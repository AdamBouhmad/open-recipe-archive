## Ingredients

- 1 turkey cock about 5 kg
- 4-5 truffles
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in 1 slice
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.)
- 1 liter good broth
- 2 hens about 450 gr. scraped meat
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 gr.)
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 1/2 tablespoons flour (15 gr.)
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 6 deciliters thick cream
- 3-4 truffles

## Directions

1. Pluck the turkey preferably while it is warm, singe, draw, rinse well and dry (see page 299 et seq.), after which the breastbone is carefully cut out from inside.
2. Lard the breast and thighs thickly with finely cut truffle strips and let the turkey hang 2-6 days depending on the season.
3. The day before the turkey is to be roasted, prepare the chicken forcemeat. Pluck the hens, singe, draw (see page 299), rinse very well, dry with a cloth and skin them.
4. Cut all meat loose from the carcass, scrape free from sinews and membranes, mix with seasonings and pass 5 times through a meat grinder.
5. Pound it together with flour, with a wooden pestle, in a stone mortar until a fine mass.
6. Whip the eggs and add to the forcemeat, little by little, under vigorous stirring, after which it is left in a cool place until the next day.
7. Place the forcemeat in a saucepan set in a bowl with ice (it will separate otherwise when the cream is worked in) and add the cream teaspoon by teaspoon under even stirring, after which the truffles cut into fine strips or dice are added and the forcemeat is carefully tasted.
8. Dry the turkey with a damp cloth, rub inside and outside with seasonings, stuff with the chicken forcemeat, sew well with a surgical stitch, truss and cover with lard (see page 300).
9. Roast thereafter as in no. 606, slice beautifully and arrange on a platter so that it appears whole, garnish and serve as in no. 605.
10. The turkey can also be boiled as in no. 600 and served with truffle sauce.
