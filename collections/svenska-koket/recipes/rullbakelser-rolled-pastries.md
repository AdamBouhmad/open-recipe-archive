## Ingredients

- 110 gr. butter
- 110 gr. powdered sugar
- 2 eggs (approx. 110 gr.)
- 110 gr. wheat flour
- 1 tsp. baking powder (3 gr.)
- 1 1/2 portions jam or currant jelly

## Directions

1. Wash the butter and stir until white and fluffy, then add the sugar together with the eggs, one at a time, and stir the mixture for 10 minutes.
2. Lastly stir in the flour, which should be sifted and well mixed with the baking powder.
3. Line a small baking sheet with low edges with white, buttered paper (15 gr. butter).
4. Spread the batter onto it to a cake of approx. 1/2 cm. thickness and bake in a warm oven until it has a beautiful golden-brown color.
5. Turn the cake out immediately onto a paper sprinkled with granulated sugar, brush with warm jam or stirred currant jelly and roll up quickly.
6. Let the cake cool and then cut across into 1 1/2 cm. thick slices.
7. Alternatively, the cake can be cut in half, spread a cream made of well-washed butter, sugar and arrack or punch or vanilla cream or jelly on one half, and place the other half on top.
8. The cake is then cut out with small measures or cut into small square, diagonal or oblong pieces, which are glazed in different colors and garnished with finely chopped orange peel, sweet almond or pistachio, or with walnuts or hazelnuts.
9. These cakes are served with glace, together with small rings of butter dough or small canapés.
