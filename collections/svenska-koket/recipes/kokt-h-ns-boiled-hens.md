## Ingredients

- 2 young hens of about 3 kg each
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)
- 1 1/2 hectograms lard, in 2 slices
- 1 1/2 liters hen or veal broth
- 1 small piece Portuguese onion
- 4 parsley stems
- 2 cloves
- 5 white peppercorns

## Directions

1. The hens are plucked, singed, and drawn (see p. 299 et seq.), rinsed thoroughly, and left to lie in water for 6 hours.
2. They are taken out, dried inside and out with a cloth, rubbed with salt, trussed (see p. 300), covered with lard, which is fastened with fine sail thread previously dipped in boiling water.
3. The hens are placed in a pot that should not be wider than that they fit snugly, the cold broth is poured in, allowed to come to a boil, well skimmed, after which the onion, parsley, and spices are added.
4. The hens are then not allowed to boil but only to lie in the broth, which should be kept at about 90°, until tender, or 2–3 hours, depending on the hens' age.
5. They are taken out and kept warm in a water bath. The broth is reduced and a light mushroom, truffle, tomato, or oyster sauce is made from it.
6. The hens are cut and arranged on a platter so that they look whole, garnished with salad, parsley, and if desired with slices of truffle or mushroom caps sautéed in butter.
7. Served with rice or boiled potatoes. The sauce is served in a sauce boat.
