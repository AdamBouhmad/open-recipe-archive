## Ingredients

- 1.2 kg pike
- 500 g scraped fish meat
- 250 g butter
- 4 eggs
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 4 dcl thick cream
- 2 dcl thin cream
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 2 teaspoons sugar (10 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 g) or 1 ball crayfish butter (10 g) for the mold
- 2 tablespoons finely crushed breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Do not scale the pike. Remove the intestines (see page 171), rinse well, and dry with a fish cloth.
2. Fillet the pike and remove the backbone.
3. Scrape the fish meat finely, ensuring no scales or bones are included. Weigh it.
4. Mix the fish meat with cold butter and grind 4-5 times through a meat grinder.
5. Pound the mixture in a mortar with a wooden pestle until it is fine and cohesive.
6. In a bowl, whisk the egg yolks, flour, cream, and seasonings together.
7. Work this mixture gradually into the forcemeat, stirring for approximately 1 hour.
8. Pass the forcemeat through a sieve.
9. Fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
10. Taste and adjust seasonings carefully.
11. Fill the mixture into a well-buttered and breadcrumb-coated fish forcemeat mold with a capacity of approximately 2 liters.
12. Place the lid on the mold and poach the forcemeat in a water bath either in the oven or on the stovetop.
