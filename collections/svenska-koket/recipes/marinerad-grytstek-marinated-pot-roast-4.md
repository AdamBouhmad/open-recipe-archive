## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg beef chuck or brisket
- 2 tsp salt (10 g)
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 1.5 hg lard, in flat slices
- 1/4 tsp ginger
- 2 red onions
- 2 carrots
- 2 tbsp butter (40 g)
- 4 parsley stems
- 1 bay leaf
- 6 dcl red or white wine
- 50 g fresh scalded pork stock
- 1.5 hg lard for browning
- 1 liter brown broth

## Directions

1. Pound the meat lightly with a damp mallet, dry with a cloth wrung out in hot water, and rub with the spices.
2. Peel and chop the onions and carrots into slices, brown them in butter, and lay half of them along with the parsley in a deep pan.
3. Place the meat on top and lay the rest of the roots, parsley, bay leaf, and pork stock on top of the meat.
4. Add the wine and let the roast marinate for about 6 hours, turning it 3-4 times.
5. Remove the meat, let it drain, dry well with a cloth, lay the lard slices on top and tie it so it has a beautiful shape.
6. Heat the lard in an iron pot, brown the roast on all sides until a beautiful brown crust forms, then remove it.
7. Pour out the lard and scrub the pot well with white paper.
8. Pour the marinade into the pot, place the meat on top, cover, and let the wine reduce rapidly until it has the consistency of syrup.
9. Then add the boiling broth, which should be level with the meat (the pot should be only just large enough for the roast), cover and let the meat braise slowly for 2.5-3 hours, turning it a few times but do not add liquid.
10. Place the roast on a warm platter, garnish with parsley or kale and carve at the table.
11. Serve with vegetables, browned potatoes and salads or potato pastries.
12. Serve the sauce as gravy or reduced (see no. 429).
