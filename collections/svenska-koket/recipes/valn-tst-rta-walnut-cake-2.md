## Ingredients

- 100 gr. walnuts
- 225 gr. fine sugar
- 6 eggs
- 2 tablespoons water
- 50 gr. wheat flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder (3 gr.)
- 40 gr. dried potato flour
- 15 gr. butter for the pan
- 3 tablespoons crushed rusks for the pan
- 125 gr. walnuts for filling and garnish
- 6 deciliters extra thick cream
- 2 tablespoons sugar (30 gr.)

## Directions

1. Crack and grind the walnuts. Sift the fine sugar and stir it with the egg yolks and water for about 3/4 hour, then add the walnuts, the wheat flour sifted with the baking powder and the potato flour. Last, carefully fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks and fill the batter into a buttered and breadcrumb-strewn form or baking pan. Bake the cake in a not too hot oven for about 40 minutes. Crack the remaining walnuts, save the most whole ones for garnishing, chop the others fairly finely. Whip the cream to stiff peaks and add the sugar. The thickest cream foam is used to garnish the cake, the rest (about 1/3) is mixed with the chopped nuts and used for filling. Turn the cake out and cut it, when cold, in half, between which the filling is placed. Spread some of the cream over the cake and with the rest garnish it in diagonal squares through a fluted garnishing tube. Place the whole walnuts in the squares. The cake can also be garnished with fruit and jelly. Serve as a dessert or with coffee and tea.
