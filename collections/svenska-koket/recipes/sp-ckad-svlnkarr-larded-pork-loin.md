## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg. loin with bone
- 1/2 lemon
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 1/4 tsk. ginger
- 1 hg. prunes
- 3 dcl. water
- For frying: 2 msk. butter (40 gr.)
- 1/2 lit. boiling water
- 1/2 tsk. solid meat extract (4 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. salt (3 gr.)

## Directions

1. Carefully trim most of the fat from the loin and loosen the bone halfway with a pointed knife.
2. Dry it with a cloth wrung out in hot water, beat lightly with a damp mallet, and rub well with lemon.
3. Rub the loin with a mixture of salt, white pepper, and ginger.
4. Rinse the prunes well in lukewarm water, blanch 3 minutes in 3 dcl. water, and save the liquid for the gravy.
5. Pit the prunes and split them lengthwise in two halves, then lard the loin in tight, even rows, after which tie it with fine twine previously dipped in boiling water.
6. Brown the butter in a roasting pan, place the loin in it with the meat side down and brown well on all sides.
7. First add the hot prune liquid and then the water mixed with meat extract and salt.
8. Let the loin roast slowly with the lid tightly closed until tender, about 2 1/2 hours, basting about every 10 minutes.
9. Carve and arrange on the bone so it looks whole, place on a warm platter and garnish with 2 hg. cooked prunes and parsley or salad.
10. Serve with browned potatoes and Russian peas and with the strained and skimmed meat gravy, mixed with soy and spices, or with a thickened sauce (see no. 429).
11. Alternatively, the loin can be larded with 1 hg. pork fat instead of prunes and moistened toward the end of roasting with 2 dcl. cream mixed with 2 tsk. soy.
