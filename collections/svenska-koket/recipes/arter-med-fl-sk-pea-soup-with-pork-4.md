## Ingredients

- 6 deciliters yellow peas
- 1.5 liters water
- 750 g salt-cured pork loin or shoulder

## Directions

1. The peas are cleaned and rinsed well, and if they are considered hard-cooking they are placed the evening before in the water in which they are to be cooked.
2. If the pork is heavily salted, it should lie in cold water for a few hours, otherwise it is only rinsed, placed in cold water, skimmed and cooked until done for about 2 hours.
3. Peas are always started in cold water; if the water is hard, it is boiled before use and allowed to cool.
4. The peas are allowed to come to a boil slowly, but then boil rapidly for the first hour, when the skins loosen more easily.
5. They are thinned with cold water and when the skins float up they are immediately skimmed off.
6. The skins should be carefully removed, as this makes the soup less gritty and tastes better.
7. After 1.5-2 hours the pork broth is added, which should previously be seasoned, and the peas are allowed to cook until they begin to break apart.
8. They are seasoned with salt and, if desired, with a little marjoram or ginger.
9. The pork is sliced and served separately.
10. From leftover pea soup, puree can be made, but one should then take 2 deciliters more peas when the soup is cooked.
