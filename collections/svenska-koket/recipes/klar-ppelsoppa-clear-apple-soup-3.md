## Ingredients

- 1.5 liters tart apples
- 1.5 liters water
- Zest of 1/4 lemon
- 1 to 1.5 cups crushed sugar
- 1/2 deciliter French wine or sherry
- 2 tablespoons potato flour (80 grams)
- 2 tablespoons red currant jelly

## Directions

1. Rinse and dry the apples. Do not peel one liter of them; cut them into quarters, removing the blossom, stem, and core
2. Place them in cold water and let them cook until soft but not falling apart or mushy
3. Pour them into a cloth and let the juice drip off. (The pulp can be cooked again and the juice used for cream)
4. Peel the remaining apples, cut into 6 quarters, and cook with the lemon zest, sugar, and wine in the apple juice until soft
5. Thicken the soup with the potato flour, stirred in 1/2 cup cold water, and let it cook for 2 minutes
6. Taste and let it cool; add small spoonfuls of currant jelly
7. Serve with rusks or biscuits
