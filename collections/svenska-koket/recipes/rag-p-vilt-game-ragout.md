## Ingredients

- 3—4 hg. cooked, boneless meat of poultry or game
- 8 dcl. bouillon or water
- 2 cloves (salt)
- 1 1/2 msk. butter (30 gr.)
- 4 msk. flour (40 gr.)
- 1/2 msk. lingonberry jelly
- 1 tsk. red wine

## Directions

1. Ragout of game is usually used as filling in vol-au-vents, pastries, risoles and for croquettes, etc.
2. The meat is loosened from the bones and cut into small cubes or oblong pieces, the bones are crushed and cooked in the bouillon (or water, with 4 gr. meat extract added and salt) with the cloves, until the bouillon obtains a good game flavor, when it is strained.
3. Butter and flour are fried for 2 minutes; the bouillon is thinned and the sauce is allowed to boil for 5 minutes.
4. The meat is added and heated in the sauce but must not boil, as it then becomes tough.
5. The dish is seasoned with lingonberry jelly and red wine and spices, if needed.
