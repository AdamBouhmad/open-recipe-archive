## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg. boneless beef tenderloin
- 150 gr. lard
- 2 tsp. salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1/2 tsp. sugar
- 1 dcl. finely crushed breadcrumbs
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp. flour (10 gr.)
- 2 1/2-3 dcl. cream or milk
- 3/4 kg. pork jelly for browning
- 1 1/2 liters secondary broth for cooking

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, remove sinews and membranes, cut into cubes and pass 7-8 times through a meat grinder.
2. Scrape the lard and cut into fine cubes.
3. Mix the forcemeat with the seasonings and lard and work vigorously for 1/2 hour.
4. Add the breadcrumbs, the beaten egg, egg yolk and flour, and work the forcemeat for another 1/2 hour; add the cream by the spoonful during this time.
5. Test the forcemeat to see if it is sufficiently seasoned and has proper consistency.
6. Shape into 2 oblong round balls, which are cooked in the hot pork jelly until they have taken on a nice brown color.
7. Transfer the forcemeat balls to the boiling broth and let them cook with a closed lid for 1 hour.
8. Remove, cut into slices, garnish with parsley and serve with vegetables.
9. Leftover meat grotta is cut into thin slices and served at the sandwich table.
