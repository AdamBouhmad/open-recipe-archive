## Ingredients

- 850 grams beef
- 850 grams pork
- 850 grams lard
- 850 grams cooked, cooled potato
- 1¼ liters milk
- 4 tablespoons salt
- 2 teaspoons ground allspice
- 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
- 5 meters curved casings
- For rubbing in: 2 tablespoons salt
- 1 tablespoon powdered sugar
- 1 teaspoon potassium nitrate

## Directions

1. Wash the meat with a cloth dipped in cold water.
2. Cut it into pieces, remove sinews and membranes, and pass the meat 3 times through the meat grinder.
3. Then beat it well.
4. Cut the lard into fine cubes.
5. Pass the potato (which should not be too thoroughly cooked) through the grinder.
6. Boil the milk and let it cool.
7. Work all the ingredients well together for 1 hour and then stuff into well-cleaned curved casings, not too tightly.
8. Rinse the sausages with cold water and rub them in with a mixture of salt, sugar, and potassium nitrate.
9. The meat sausage can then either be laid down in saltwater or smoked for 2 days. In the latter case, a smaller amount of milk is used for thinning and it is stuffed somewhat tighter.
