## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg. boneless beef inside round
- 150 g. lard
- 2 tsp. salt (10 g)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1/2 tsp. sugar
- 1 dcl. finely crushed rusks
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp. flour (10 g)
- 2 1/2-3 dcl. cream or milk
- 3/4 kg. pork fat for browning
- 1 1/2 liters secondary broth for cooking

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung in hot water, remove sinews and membranes, cut into dice and run 7-8 times through the meat grinder.
2. Scrape the lard and cut into fine dice.
3. Blend the forcemeat with the spices and lard and work vigorously for 1/2 hour, then add the rusks, beaten egg, egg yolk and flour and work the forcemeat another 1/2 hour; meanwhile add the cream tablespoon by tablespoon.
4. Test the forcemeat (see Forcemeat, page 316) to check if it is sufficiently seasoned and of proper firmness.
5. Shape the forcemeat into 2 oblong round balls, which are cooked in the hot pork fat until they take on a nice brown color.
6. Transfer the forcemeats to boiling broth and simmer with the lid closed for 1 hour.
7. Remove them, cut into slices, garnish with parsley and serve with vegetables.
8. Leftovers of meat cake are cut into thin slices and served on the smorgasbord.
