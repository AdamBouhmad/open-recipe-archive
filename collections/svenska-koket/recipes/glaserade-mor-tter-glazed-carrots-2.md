## Ingredients

- 1 litre small carrots (carrots)
- 60 gr. butter
- 1 teaspoon salt (5 gr.)
- 4 teaspoons sugar (20 gr.)
- 4 parts boiling water
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped parsley

## Directions

1. Small pear-shaped carrots, so-called carrots, are best suited for this preparation method.
2. Clean the carrots, rinse, brush well, scrape neatly, rinse again, dry in a cloth and cut in half lengthwise or in quarters, depending on the size.
3. Melt the butter in a saucepan, add the carrots, sprinkle with salt and sugar and fry well in the butter.
4. Add water and let it boil with a lid until the carrots are almost soft, then remove the lid and let the sauce boil down so that it becomes thick as syrup.
5. Shake the carrots gently in the sauce until they are evenly glazed.
6. If desired, add the finely chopped parsley last.
7. Serve as a garnish around fried meat or together with cooked vegetables.
8. If the carrots are large and old they should be shaped like long olives, placed in cold salted water, let boil for 5 minutes, drained in a colander and prepared as above.
