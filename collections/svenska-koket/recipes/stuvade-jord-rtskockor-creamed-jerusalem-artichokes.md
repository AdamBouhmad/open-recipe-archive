## Ingredients

- 1.25 liters Jerusalem artichokes
- 1 liter water
- 1 teaspoon salt for pre-cooking
- 4 deciliters boiling skim milk
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 gr.)
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 gr.)
- 1 teaspoon sugar (5 gr.)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1-2 egg yolks

## Directions

1. Treat the Jerusalem artichokes as in the previous recipe, pre-cook in boiling salted water 3-4 minutes, drain in a colander and let drip.
2. Place in the boiling milk and cook until soft, tested with a whisk. Drain again in a colander and reserve the broth.
3. Melt the butter in a pot, add the Jerusalem artichokes, sprinkle with flour and fry well while shaking the pot.
4. Add enough of the broth to create a simmering sauce, which should cook slowly about 5 minutes.
5. Season the stew with spices, blend with the beaten egg yolk and let simmer.
6. Serve with fried meat. The Jerusalem artichokes can also be cooked in broth instead of milk and otherwise prepared the same way, seasoning the stew with a little lemon juice.
