## Ingredients

- 2 shallot onions
- 1 carrot
- 5 parsley stems
- 50 gr. lean, smoked ham
- 3 tbsp butter (60 gr.)
- 1 liter previously prepared meat stock
- 1 dcl. white wine
- 5 tbsp flour (50 gr.)
- 6 dcl. good meat stock from the roast
- salt, white pepper
- 1/2–1 dcl. Madeira

## Directions

1. Peel and chop the onions and carrot into pieces, clean the parsley stems, rinse them, wring them in a cloth, and dice the ham. Brown everything lightly in 1 tbsp of butter.
2. Add the previously prepared meat stock and white wine and let simmer slowly with a lid for approximately 2 hours, then strain the liquid through a fine sieve, skim, and measure it—it should yield 8 dcl.
3. Fry the remaining butter with the flour for 2 minutes, gradually add the meat stock cooked with vegetables and the skimmed meat stock from the roast, then season with spices and Madeira and let cook while stirring for 15 minutes.
4. Season with more Madeira if desired. When using this sauce for game, omit the wine and season the sauce with 1–2 tbsp black or red currant jelly.
