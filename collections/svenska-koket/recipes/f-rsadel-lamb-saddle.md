## Ingredients

- 2 kg lamb saddle
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 1/2 liter broth
- 3 tablespoons butter for roasting (60 gr.)
- 1/2 liter broth
- 2 deciliters cream

## Directions

1. The roast is treated as in recipe n:o 531 and the flaps are cut off. The parsley is cleaned, rinsed, wrung in a cloth, chopped coarsely and worked into the butter, which is then left in a cool place to harden.
2. With a larding needle or sharp knife, make holes in the roast at even intervals, and insert small pieces of parsley butter into them.
3. Heat a roasting pot, brown the butter in it; place the roast in and brown it well on all sides, sprinkle with seasonings, dilute with the boiling broth and cream and let it stew slowly with a tightly closed lid until it is tender, or about 2 1/2—3 hours, during which it is basted frequently and turned 3—4 times.
4. Serve with vegetables or with boiled potatoes, cucumber and salads, and with a finished sauce (see recipe n:o 429).
5. The meat can also be roasted in the oven (see recipe n:o 531), but then it is diluted only with broth.
