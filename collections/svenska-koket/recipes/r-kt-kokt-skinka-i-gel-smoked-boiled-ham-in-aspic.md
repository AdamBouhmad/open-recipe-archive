## Ingredients

- 1 lightly smoked ham, approximately 4 kg
- 6 liters water
- White aspic: 1/2 liter light broth
- 5 sheets white gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 1 tablespoon good Madeira wine
- Red aspic: 3 deciliters light broth
- 4 sheets red gelatin
- 1 egg white
- 1/2 tablespoon cold water
- For garnish: 1/4 can green beans
- 1 head of lettuce

## Directions

1. Soak the ham in plenty of cold water overnight to remove salt.
2. Place the ham skin-side up in cold water and simmer slowly until tender, approximately 3 1/2 hours. It is done when the small bone on the ham hock loosens easily.
3. Let the ham cool in its cooking liquid, then transfer it to an elongated platter and remove the skin.
4. Prepare the white and red aspic as in recipe no. 181. If the red aspic is too light, color it with a little carmine.
5. Slice the ham into even, attractive slices and lay them back on the bone so the ham appears whole.
6. Gradually pour the white aspic over the slices so they hold together and the ham becomes beautifully glazed.
7. When the white aspic has set completely, garnish the ham with the red aspic, piped through a fluted paper cone to form flowers and swags.
8. Cut the beans into stems and leaves.
9. Garnish the bone of the ham with crinkled paper and the platter with chopped aspic, aspic croutons, and greens.
10. Serve as a course dish with vegetable salad or stuffed tomatoes.
