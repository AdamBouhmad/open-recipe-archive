## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg lamb breast
- 1 1/2 liters water
- 1 1/2 tablespoons salt (23 grams)
- 4 parsley sprigs
- 1 teaspoon grated red onion
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 grams)
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 grams)
- 5 deciliters of the stock
- curry powder to taste
- 1 tablespoon water
- 1/2 teaspoon sugar
- 2 deciliters rice grains (2 hectograms)
- 2 liters water
- 1 1/2 tablespoons salt (23 grams)
- 1/2-1 teaspoon curry powder

## Directions

1. Treat and cook the breast as in the previous recipe. Cut it into pieces, which are covered over to keep them warm. Strain the stock and skim it well.
2. For the sauce, fry the onion in the butter, add the flour and fry with the butter and onion for 2 minutes, add the stock gradually, little by little.
3. Let the sauce cook for 5 minutes, then season it with curry powder (approximately 1 teaspoon) mixed with 1 tablespoon warm water, and the sugar.
4. Rinse the rice grains in cold water, scald them in hot water, place them in boiling salted water and let them cook until soft — 15-20 minutes — then drain them in a colander, immediately rinse with cold water, place in a warm oven and stir often with a fork until they are separated and all water has evaporated.
5. Arrange the rice in a ring on a round serving platter and sprinkle with a little dry curry. Arrange the meat in the center of the platter and pour the sauce over it. Garnish with parsley.
