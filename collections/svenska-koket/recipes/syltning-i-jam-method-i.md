## Ingredients

- 2 kg berries
- 2 kg granulated sugar

## Directions

1. Clean the berries, rinse quickly in a colander and let drain well.
2. Layer the berries and sugar alternately in an enameled jam pot, cover and let stand for one day, until the sugar is nearly dissolved in the fruit juice.
3. Place the pot approximately 10 minutes beside the fire and then move it over low heat.
4. When the jam has come to a boil, lift the pot from the heat and shake gently so that the foam can be removed, then return the pot to the heat and let the jam simmer slowly until it is clear (approximately 20 minutes).
5. During this time shake the pot often and skim the jam well.
6. Pour the jam immediately into warmed jars rinsed with brandy, which should stand in a cool and airy room until the jam is cold, then cover with a sheet of parchment paper dipped in brandy.
7. Bind the jars thereafter with parchment paper as previously described.
8. This method is used for strawberries, raspberries, cloudberries, cloudberries, red and black currants and mixed jam of raspberries, currants and blueberries.
