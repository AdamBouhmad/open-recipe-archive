## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 litres wort (concentrated from 5 litres wort)
- 1 1/2 kg wheat flour
- 1/2 litre unskim milk
- 125 gr pressed yeast
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr)
- rye sifted flour
- 1 hectogram butter or good lard
- 1/2 litre syrup
- 3 tablespoons crushed fennel
- 4 tablespoons finely chopped orange peel

## Directions

1. The day before baking, boil down the wort, which takes about 6 hours.
2. In the evening, sift about 3/4 kg of the wheat flour into a large bowl or trough.
3. Mix in the cold milk and the yeast mixed with salt and work the dough quickly.
4. Cover with cloth and let stand on a warm place overnight.
5. Early the next morning, warm the wort to about 40° and add it to the yeast dough along with enough rye flour to make a stiff dough.
6. Cover with warm cloth and let rise to double size.
7. Mix the butter, syrup and spices in a pot, bring to boil and cool.
8. Work this mixture into the dough along with more flour and let it rise again.
9. Work the dough on the board with the remaining wheat flour and divide into 12 parts.
10. Work each piece until smooth and without cracks, then roll into an oblong bun.
11. Place on a soft, warmed baking cloth dusted with flour, with cloth gathered up in a fold between each bun and cover.
12. When risen, turn onto a bread peel, place on buttered, lukewarm baking sheets and let stand about 10 minutes.
13. Score along the length with a larding needle, brush with cold wort or egg yolk and water, and bake about 3/4 hour in a not-too-hot oven.
14. When half-baked, brush with warm wort, likewise when fully baked.
15. Let dry briefly in the oven before removing and laying between blankets so the crust stays soft.
16. Porter, health ale or Christmas ale mixed with sugar can be used instead of wort.
17. Wort bread is best baked in a bake oven, which is fired as soon as the dough is made and swept as soon as the bread is shaped. The oven is then kept completely closed until the bread is ready to bake.
