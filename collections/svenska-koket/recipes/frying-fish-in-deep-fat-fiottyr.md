## Ingredients

- Small whole fish, fish slices, or small fillets
- Salted milk
- Flour
- Eggs
- Breadcrumbs
- Parsley
- Lemon
- Deep fat (fiottyr)

## Directions

1. Only small fish, fish slices, or smaller fillets should be prepared this way, as the high temperature of hot deep fat would scorch and dry larger fish on the surface before they are cooked through.
2. If the fish is thick, after cleaning, make a few deep crosswise cuts before breading to facilitate cooking through.
3. For flounder, partially loosen the two lower fillets from the backbone instead of cutting them.
4. Before frying, dip the fish first in salted milk and roll in flour, or bread them, that is, turn them in flour, dip in beaten egg and then roll in dried breadcrumbs.
5. Pat them with a knife blade so the breading adheres well, and score them crosswise with the back of a knife if desired so they look neater when fried.
6. Heat the deep fat in a frying or long pan, lay the fish in and fry to a beautiful golden brown, then place on absorbent paper so the fat drains off.
7. Lay it up on broken napkin or parchment paper and garnish with parsley fried in deep fat, and beautifully cut lemon halves or grooved lemon slices.
