## Ingredients

- 1 young goose, approximately 6 kg
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 grams)
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 5-6 apples
- 125 grams prunes
- 1 1/2 liters broth or water

## Directions

1. Singe the goose over a spirit or gas flame, then carefully pluck out all feathers and wash the goose thoroughly with lukewarm water and a soft brush, then dry it.
2. Remove the head with neck, wings and feet and use them together with heart, liver and gizzard for black soup (see recipe 255).
3. Cut open and gut the goose (see page 300) — remove the fat and place it in cold water together with the fat picked from the intestines —, rinse well in cold water, dry and rub inside and outside with the seasonings.
4. Fill the bird with the apples, peeled and cut into large wedges, and with the blanched and pitted prunes, sew it up, truss it (see page 300) and place it in a roasting pan.
5. Insert into a warm oven and baste frequently with the fat that forms in the pan; when nicely browned, add the boiling broth and let the goose roast until tender, basting every 10 minutes. Allow 2 1/2 - 3 hours for roasting.
6. If crispy skin is desired, baste the goose only during the first hour, then let it roast with the oven door slightly ajar.
7. Half an hour before it is done, strain off the gravy, and put the goose back in the oven with 1/2 liter boiling water in the pan.
8. Skim the gravy well, reduce it, season with meat extract and soy and thicken with browned thickening agent (see page 210).
9. Place the goose on a platter, remove the trussing strings, carve it and reassemble it so that it looks whole, garnish with browned potatoes, kale or parsley and the fruit with which it was filled.
10. Serve with red cabbage and pickled cucumber.
11. The fat should lie in water for 12 hours, changing the water once, then melt it together with 1 apple and 1 small red onion, strain it, let it cool and use it as butter. If the fat is desired firmer, melt it together with 3 hectograms of kidney tallow.
12. Goose can also be filled with chestnuts (see recipe 607) and otherwise roasted as above.
