## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 dcl. risgryn (150 gr.)
- 1/2 lit. vatten
- 2 tsk. salt (10 gr.)
- 3-4 dcl. tjock grädde
- 3 1/2 hg. salt lax
- 1 msk. finhackad rödlök
- 2 msk. smör (40 gr.)
- 3 hårdkokta ägg
- 150 gr. mjöl
- 1 dcl. vatten (knappt)
- 2 hg. smör
- 1 äggvita, till pensling

## Directions

1. Rinse the rice grains and scald them in hot water, then place them in boiling salted water, add the thick cream and allow them to cook until soft, then pour them out and let them cool.
2. Rinse the salmon, cut it into slices and let it lie in 1 lit. skimmed milk for approximately 3 hours, take it up, wrap it in cloth or white paper and cut it into cubes.
3. Fry the onion in the butter. Chop the eggs finely.
4. Prepare the pastry dough as in recipe no. 33 and roll it out to a thickness of 1 1/4 cm. Take a measure of the fireproof dish on which the pirog is to be baked. This is done most easily by cutting a paper pattern and then cutting out the dough with a pastry wheel. Place the dough bottom on the dish and a 3 cm. wide strip of dough is placed in a ring on top of the bottom and fastened with egg white.
5. Mix the rice grains with the salmon, onion and eggs and place them inside the ring. Cover the pirog with a pastry dough lid, of the same size as the bottom, and fasten the lid to it by means of egg white. Garnish the lid in squares with narrow strips of pastry dough and in each square make a small cut with a fork, so that the dough rises and the dish gets a neat appearance.
6. Brush the pirog with egg white, which must not come down over the sides, as it prevents the pastry dough from rising. Bake in a warm oven.
