## Ingredients

- 2.5 kg lamb saddle
- 1 kg larding fat
- 1 tbsp salt
- 1 tbsp butter
- 1 liter broth or water

## Directions

1. The lamb saddle, which should have hung 8-14 days, is beaten well, then washed with a cloth dipped in hot water. Remove the fat on the back and around the kidneys and some of the ribs so the thin sides can be folded up. Cut the larding fat into fine strips, roll them in salt, and use them to lard the saddle. Bind it and place on a baking pan brushed with butter, put in the oven and roast over good heat for 2-3 hours. Baste with broth and frequently with its juices. If the roast becomes too brown, cover it with buttered paper toward the end of roasting.
2. Place on a platter and garnish with potato croquettes, glazed carrots, and green beans.
3. Strain the sauce, skim, and serve as gravy.
