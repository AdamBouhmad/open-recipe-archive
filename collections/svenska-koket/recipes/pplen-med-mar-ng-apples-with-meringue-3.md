## Ingredients

- 10-12 loose, tart apples
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 g)
- 1.5-2 deciliters granulated sugar (128-170 g)
- For the pan: 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 g)
- For the meringue: 3 egg whites
- 105 g powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Wash, dry, peel the apples, cut them into thin slices or cubes and remove the cores.
2. Melt the butter in a pot, add the apple slices together with the sugar and shake over heat until they are completely soft.
3. Place them in a buttered, ovenproof dish and let them cool.
4. For the meringue, whip the egg whites until stiff peaks form. The powdered sugar, which should be sifted, is stirred in little by little into the egg white foam.
5. Spread half of the meringue mixture over the apples.
6. Pipe the remaining meringue mixture through a piping bag in diagonal squares over the meringue.
7. Bake the dish in gentle oven heat until the meringue is half dry and has a light yellow color.
8. Remove it, let it cool, and garnish each square with apple or red currant jelly.
9. Serve with whipped cream.
