## Ingredients

- 3/4 kg wheat flour
- 1 part water
- 1/4 part brandy
- 1 egg
- 1 kg butter (equal weight to the remaining dough)

## Directions

1. Make a smooth dough from the flour, water, brandy, and egg, and roll it out in a square to approximately 15 mm thickness.
2. Place the well-washed butter in the middle.
3. Fold all four corners of the dough over the butter, and gently clap it out with a rolling pin to a square of approximately 10-15 mm thickness.
4. Fold this square so that 1/4 is folded inward from the left and 1/4 is folded inward from the right.
5. The edges of these two folds meet in the middle of the square.
6. Fold one half of the dough over the other lengthwise.
7. The dough now has four layers on top of each other.
8. Fold the long, narrow dough into three parts on top of each other (so you get nine layers).
9. Work the dough until all the butter is well incorporated, then place it on ice to rest for 20 minutes.
10. Roll out the dough again to a square of approximately 5-7 mm thickness, fold it together in the same way as before and let it rest on ice for 20 minutes.
11. Roll it out again and fold it as before.
12. Then place the dough with the triple-folded side toward you and roll it out in one direction from bottom to top, but fold it together only in three parts.
13. Do this twice more with 20 minutes rest between each time.
14. The butter dough is now ready, but should preferably rest a while longer before it is baked out.
