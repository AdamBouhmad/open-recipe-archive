## Ingredients

- 1 1/4 liters fresh sauerkraut
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 gr.)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 kg. fresh pork
- 1 carrot
- 1 small red onion
- 1 clove
- 20 gr. juniper berries
- 50 gr. pork fat
- 4 parsley sprigs
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 liters stock

## Directions

1. Fresh sauerkraut should not be soaked in water. If the sauerkraut is old, it should be soaked for about 12 hours, and then drain well on a sieve before preparing.
2. Mix the cabbage with salt and pepper. Cut the pork into slices, and line the bottom and sides of an iron pot with them.
3. Add the cabbage together with the scraped carrot cut into four pieces, the peeled onion with the clove inserted in it, juniper berries (tied in muslin) if desired, the pork fat and the parsley sprigs tied together with the bay leaf.
4. Lay the remaining pork slices on top, add the stock and it should stand above the cabbage. Bring the cabbage to boil, then place the pot in a warm oven, and let the preparation simmer covered for about 5 hours.
5. Remove the carrot, onion, juniper berries and parsley bundle. Taste the cabbage, place it on a warm platter, and garnish with boiled frankfurter sausage and smoked ham or boiled pork.
