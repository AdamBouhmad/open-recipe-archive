## Ingredients

- 3 hg. boneless beef
- 2 hg. fresh pork shoulder
- 1 dcl. crushed breadcrumbs
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 2-3 dcl. cream or milk
- 50 gr. lard
- 1 egg white for brushing
- 2 tbsp. crushed breadcrumbs for coating
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 gr.) for frying
- 5 dcl. broth

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into cubes and pass 6 times through a meat grinder.
2. Mix the forcemeat with the crushed breadcrumbs, seasonings, the beaten egg and egg yolk, and stir vigorously for 1 hour, adding the cream by the spoonful.
3. Test the forcemeat by frying a small ball to check if it is sufficiently seasoned and has proper consistency.
4. Scrape the lard and cut into fine strips.
5. Grease a long pan with cold butter, add the forcemeat and shape it with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water into a ball, flatten it on both sides so it resembles a hare's back, and lard it densely with the lard strips.
6. Brush the hare with the beaten egg white, sprinkle with crushed breadcrumbs and roast in the oven as in recipe no. 632.
7. Cut into slices, garnish with parsley and fried potatoes.
8. Serve with strained sauce and jelly or preserved rowan berries.
