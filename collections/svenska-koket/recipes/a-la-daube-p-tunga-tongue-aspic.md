## Ingredients

- 1 large calf's tongue
- 1 1/4 liters water
- 2 teaspoons meat extract
- 1 1/2 tablespoons salt
- 5 white peppercorns
- 3 allspice berries
- 6 pitted preserved olives
- 8 deciliters of broth
- 8 leaves gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 2 tablespoons good Madeira wine
- 1 hard-boiled egg
- 1 truffle
- parsley

## Directions

1. Let the tongue soak in water for 12 hours, then blanch in hot water and scrape well.
2. Place in cold water mixed with dissolved meat extract, skim well when it comes to a boil; add salt and pepper.
3. Let the tongue cook until tender, about 1 1/2 hours, testing by piercing through with a thin whisk.
4. Remove the tongue, peel off the skin immediately, and let cool.
5. Strain the broth, remove fat, taste and adjust seasoning, then prepare the aspic as in recipe no. 181; when cold, add the wine.
6. Pour a little of the clear aspic onto the bottom of a rinsed aspic mold and place on ice to set.
7. Garnish the bottom with the hard-boiled egg and truffle, cut into attractive shapes, and parsley.
8. Pour a few drops of aspic over the garnish and let set so the garnish does not move when the tongue is added.
9. Cut the olives into slices; slice the tongue likewise.
10. Place one olive slice on each tongue slice, roll them up, and place in the garnished mold.
11. Pour the remaining aspic carefully over and let the aspic set.
12. Chop any leftover aspic and use as garnish.
13. To unmold, dip the mold quickly in hot water, dry it, and turn it upside down onto a plate moistened with water so the aspic can be moved into place if needed.
14. Serve on the smorgasbord or as a course with mayonnaise.
