## Ingredients

- 12 oranges
- 1 litre orange pips sugar
- sugar equal in weight to the mass

## Directions

1. Wash the oranges well, dry them, place them whole in plenty of cold water and let them cook until they are so soft that they can be pierced with a fine whisk.
2. When they have cooled, press out the fruit flesh from the peel with a silver spoon.
3. Remove the pips carefully, and cut the peels into very fine strips.
4. Scrape the fruit flesh somewhat, removing the coarsest membranes.
5. Mix together the peel, fruit flesh and 1 litre of the liquid in which the oranges were cooked, weigh it, and take an equal weight of fine sugar.
6. Let the mass cook for 1 hour before adding the sugar, and let the marmalade cook thereafter about 45 minutes, or until it is clear and of proper consistency.
7. Pour it into small warm glass jars, previously rinsed with brandy.
8. Bind the jars when the marmalade is cold.
9. If a less bitter marmalade is desired, remove the peels from 3 oranges.
