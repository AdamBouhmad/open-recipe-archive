## Ingredients

- 1 old hen
- 2 liters water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 3 white peppercorns
- 1 clove
- 1 1/2 liters light broth
- 65 grams rice grains
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 grams)
- white pepper
- 2-3 egg yolks

## Directions

1. Prepare the hen in the usual manner and let it soak in plenty of water for 12 hours.
2. Dry it, cut into 4 pieces, place in cold water with salt, skim well, and let it cook with the spices and 1 liter of the broth until the meat is tender, about 4 hours.
3. Rinse the rice grains in hot water and let them cook tender in the rest of the broth.
4. When the meat is tender, separate it from the bones, which should cook again in the broth until it amounts to 2 liters.
5. Cut the meat into pieces, run it 4 times through a meat grinder, and pound it in a stone mortar together with 2 tablespoons of the butter and the cooked rice grains.
6. Pass the forcemeat through a hair sieve.
7. Strain and skim the broth.
8. Fry the rest of the butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the broth little by little, add the forcemeat and let the purée cook with stirring for 5 minutes, then season with the spices.
9. Whisk the egg yolks in a soup bowl and pour in the purée with vigorous whisking.
10. Serve with cheese or puff pastry sticks.
