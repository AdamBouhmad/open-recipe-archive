## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg. beautiful, even perch
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 180 gr. butter
- 4 tbsp. coarsely chopped parsley
- 1 tbsp. chopped, fresh dill
- 2 tbsp. flour (20 gr.)
- 1 dcl. water

## Directions

1. Scale the perch, remove the innards (save the roe), rinse very thoroughly, dry with a fish towel and trim off the fins.
2. Rub them inside and outside with salt and let them lie for 1 hour, then dry them again.
3. Line a pot with some of the butter, place the perch tightly against each other with their backs up and between each layer distribute the rest of the butter in flakes, the parsley, dill and flour.
4. Add the water and let the fish simmer slowly with a tightly closed lid for 15–20 minutes; during this time shake the pot now and then so the fish does not burn.
5. Place on a warm serving dish with all heads to one side, whisk up the sauce, taste it and pour over the fish, which is garnished with the roe, which has been cooked in salted water and cut into slices.
6. Serve with boiled potatoes. — The perch can also be cut into fillets and the skin removed (boil broth from the head, bones and skin to use for the stewing), otherwise arrange and serve the same way.
