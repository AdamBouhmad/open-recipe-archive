## Ingredients

- Peas
- Beans
- Sugar peas
- Spinach
- Parsley
- Lightly salted water (1 tablespoon salt per liter of water)
- Bicarbonate of soda or alum
- Nettles

## Directions

1. Drying of vegetables can be done either with or without prior blanching. It is best to blanch them. Peas and beans are shelled and blanched for 3-4 minutes in boiling lightly salted water (1 tablespoon salt per liter of water). To preserve the color, add a knife's point of bicarbonate of soda or alum and 1 bunch of parsley to the cooking water. Sugar peas, fresh beans, spinach, and parsley are rinsed well, drained, and blanched for a few minutes. Rinse the vegetables with cold water, then spread them out on clean linen, preferably on drying racks, otherwise on trays, and place them in an oven with 35-40° heat (preferably a baking oven).
2. If the vegetables are not to be blanched, rinse them well, drain them, and dry them in the oven for 2-3 days. You can also dry them simply in the open air. Nettles are best dried in this way. They should not be rinsed.
3. The vegetables should be stored in tin cans but should not be put away until they are completely dry. They are stored in a dry place.
