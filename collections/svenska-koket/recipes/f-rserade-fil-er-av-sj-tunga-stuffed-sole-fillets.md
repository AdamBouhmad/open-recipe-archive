## Ingredients

- 3 sole, approximately 500 g each
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- Fish forcemeat: 500 g pike
- 210 g scraped fish meat
- 125 g good butter
- 15 g flour
- 2 decilitres cream
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 2 teaspoons salt (10 g)
- 1 teaspoon sugar (5 g)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- For garnish: 4 truffles or 48 shrimp tails
- For cooking: 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 g)
- Fish broth

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the sole as in recipe 371
2. Flatten the fillets with a damp meat mallet, rub with salt and let them rest on top of each other for a couple of hours
3. Prepare the fish forcemeat as in recipe 356
4. Dry the fillets again, cut each fillet in half and trim the pieces so they become oblong and rounded, or cut them into pear shapes
5. Cover them with a 1 1/2 cm thick layer of fish forcemeat, smoothed evenly with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water, and brush over with lightly beaten egg white
6. Garnish the forcemeat with truffle cut into beautiful shapes or with shrimp tails or with both
7. Clean a long pan (so it does not darken) and butter it with cold butter, lay the fillets in it and add enough fish broth (made from head, bones, and skin) so it comes up to the lower edge of the forcemeat
8. Cover with a lid or sheet, place the pan in a regular oven and cook the fillets 15–20 minutes
9. Test with a skewer to see if they are done
10. Lift them out with a slotted spoon onto a warm platter, garnish with parsley and serve with hollandaise, crayfish or lobster sauce and small boiled potatoes
