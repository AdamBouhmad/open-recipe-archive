## Ingredients

- 10 liters lingonberries
- 5 liters water
- 50 grams tartaric acid
- 1 deciliter hot water
- 2 1/4 kilograms granulated sugar

## Directions

1. Clean the berries, crush them with a wooden pestle and place in a crock.
2. Dissolve the tartaric acid in the hot water and add together with the cold water.
3. Stir the mixture well, cover it and let it stand for 2 days, stirring occasionally.
4. Strain the berries in cheesecloth and let them drain naturally as long as any juice drips from the cloth, or approximately 1 1/2 days.
5. Add the sugar to the juice, which is then stirred slowly for 1 hour.
6. Treat the juice as in the previous recipe. Dilute with water to taste when serving.
7. The remaining pulp can be made into a simpler juice by adding 1 1/2 liters water, 15 grams citric acid and 6 hectograms sugar, which is used first.
