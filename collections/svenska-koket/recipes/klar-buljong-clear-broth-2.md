## Ingredients

- 2 kg marrow bone, beef neck or rib
- 4 liters water
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 2 carrots
- 1 parsnip
- 1 leek
- 1 piece celery root
- 1/2 celeriac
- 4 white peppercorns
- 1 hectogram boneless, lean meat
- 2 egg whites
- 1/2 carrot
- 1/2 leek
- 1 small piece celeriac
- 1 small piece celery
- a few sprigs of parsley (or 2 whole eggs)

## Directions

1. Wash the meat, preferably freshly slaughtered, with a cloth dipped in cold water, cut into small pieces, and place in a tall copper or enameled iron pot with a tightly fitting lid in cold water
2. Add salt immediately, then let the pot stand at the side of the fire for 1/2 hour
3. Bring slowly to a boil, skim very well as it comes to a boil, and then let it simmer slowly for 5-6 hours
4. During the first hour of boiling, carefully skim all sediment that floats to the surface (you can add a little cold water so that boiling stops for a moment, and when the broth boils again, skim it immediately)
5. After the broth has boiled for a couple of hours, add the cleaned and rinsed vegetables and peppercorns
6. When the broth has cooked for its full time, strain it and let it stand until the fat floats to the top, then skim off the fat very well
7. Preferably the broth should be cooked the day before it is to be used
8. To clarify, mix the ground meat with the egg whites and the chopped vegetables, and stir the cold broth into this mixture, a little at a time
9. Let the broth boil for 15 minutes and then strain through a cloth or wool cloth wrung out in hot water
10. Broth can also be clarified with whole eggs, which gives it a beautiful color and makes it rich
