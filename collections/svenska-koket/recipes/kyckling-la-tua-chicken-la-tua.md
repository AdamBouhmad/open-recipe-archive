## Ingredients

- 3 chickens, 4-5 hg. each
- 1 hg. lard, in 3 slices
- 1 1/4 dcl. rice (125 gr.)
- 3/4 liter broth
- 50 gr. goose liver
- 2 truffles
- 1 tbsp. sherry
- 1 tsp. salt
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 1 liter clear veal or chicken broth
- 6 canned artichoke bottoms
- 6 slices truffle
- 6 mushroom caps
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 gr.)
- salad lettuce
- parsley
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 gr.)
- 2 tbsp. flour (20 gr.)
- 4 dcl. chicken broth
- 1 dcl. thick cream
- 3-4 egg yolks
- 2-3 tsp. lemon juice
- salt, white pepper
- 2 tbsp. cold butter (40 gr.)

## Directions

1. Pluck the chickens, singe them, draw them (see p. 299 f.), rinse very well and dry, then remove the breastbone carefully.
2. Rinse the rice, blanch it in hot water, add it to the boiling broth and let it cook until almost tender, then remove and let it cool.
3. Cut the goose liver and truffle into small cubes and mix into the rice together with the wine and seasonings.
4. Stuff the chickens with the rice, sew them shut, truss them (see p. 300) and cover with the lard, which is tied on with fine twine.
5. Place them in a pot, no wider than they just fit, add the boiling broth, bring to a boil, skim, and then keep at a temperature of 90°.
6. At this temperature let the birds lie until they are tender, or about 1 hour.
7. Halve them, place on a warm platter, cover with the sauce and garnish with the artichoke bottoms, warmed in a water bath, on each one 1 slice of truffle and on top of the truffle 1 mushroom cap, sautéed in butter, as well as salad lettuce and parsley.
8. For the sauce, brown the butter and flour for 2 minutes, add 4 dcl. of the broth in which the birds cooked, and let the sauce cook 5 minutes.
9. Whip the cream with the egg yolks and add it; then let the sauce simmer and season with the lemon juice and seasonings.
10. Finally add the cold butter.
