## Ingredients

- Forcemeat of game: 2 partridges or 1 grouse, approximately 225 grams scraped meat
- Salt, white pepper
- 1 hectogram panada
- 1 hectogram butter
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 2 truffles
- Forcemeat of poultry: 1 large hen, approximately 225 grams scraped meat
- Salt, white pepper
- 1 hectogram panada
- 1 hectogram butter
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 2 truffles
- Panada: 1 1/2 deciliters water
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 30 grams butter
- 75 grams sifted flour
- For the mold: 1 tablespoon butter (20 grams)
- Aspic: 1 1/2 liters dark broth
- 12-15 sheets gelatin
- 2 egg whites
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 2 tablespoons good Madeira wine

## Directions

1. Prepare the forcemeats as in recipe no. 67, and stir in the truffles cut into fine strips.
2. Layer the forcemeats alternately in an elongated, buttered bread mold that holds approximately 1 1/2 liters.
3. Bind the mold over with buttered paper and bake the forcemeat in a water bath in the oven for approximately 1 hour.
4. Test with a whisk and turn out onto an elongated platter to cool.
5. Prepare the aspic as in recipe no. 181.
6. Slice the forcemeat into approximately 7 mm thick slices and arrange them on large platters with small spaces between.
7. Pour the clear aspic over and let it set.
8. Cut out the slices with a pointed knife so that a border of aspic extends beyond the edge of each slice.
9. Arrange the slices in a wreath on a large round platter, preferably silver.
10. Place vegetable salad in the center and garnish around with chopped aspic, light lettuce leaves, and tomato wedges.
11. Serve as a course dish.
12. The truffle may be omitted from the forcemeat and instead cut into attractive shapes to garnish the slices before the aspic is poured over.
