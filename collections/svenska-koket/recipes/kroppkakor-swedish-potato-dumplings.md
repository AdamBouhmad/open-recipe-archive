## Ingredients

- 2 litres potatoes
- 1 egg
- 2 egg yolks
- 225–250 g flour
- 2 tsp salt (10 g)
- 2 hg salt-cured side pork
- 150 g smoked ham
- 1 small finely chopped red onion
- 1/4 tsp white or strong pepper
- 4 litres water for cooking
- 3 tbsp salt (45 g) for cooking

## Directions

1. Rinse and brush the potatoes clean, peel raw and cook until just tender.
2. Drain the water well, and let the potatoes stand approximately 10 minutes with the lid slightly ajar so the steam escapes, then press through a potato ricer and let cool.
3. Whisk the egg and egg yolks and add along with 2 hg of the flour and salt.
4. Work the dough very lightly on the baking board with the remaining flour and roll out into a long roll, which is cut into pieces; these are then rolled like rusks.
5. Brown the pork and ham together with the onion, if desired, and add the spices.
6. Make an indentation in each dumpling, which is filled with pieces of pork, then press the dumpling closed well.
7. Place the dumplings in boiling salted water and let simmer gently with a lid for approximately 10 minutes.
8. Test with a whisk to see if they are done.
9. Lift out with a slotted spoon and serve hot with melted butter.
10. It is best to test-cook one dumpling before finishing the rest, to determine if the dough is properly firm.
