## Ingredients

- 75 grams veal sweetbread
- Water for blanching: 1/2 liter
- 1 tsp. salt
- Leftovers of roast or poultry (approximately 250 grams meat)
- 1 can mushrooms (1/4 liter)
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 grams)
- 3 tbsp. flour (30 grams)
- 1 dcl. mushroom broth
- 1 dcl. broth
- 2 dcl. cream
- salt, white pepper
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 grams) for the shells
- For the egg mixture: 2 eggs, 1 tsp. flour, 2 dcl. milk, salt, sugar
- For garnish: 1 can mushrooms (1/4 liter), 1.5 tbsp. butter (30 grams)

## Directions

1. Cut the meat into fine cubes.
2. Let the veal sweetbread soak in water, changing the water until all blood is drawn out.
3. Blanch it (see recipe no. 47) for 10 minutes, rinse with cold water and let it drain on a sieve.
4. Remove cartilage and membranes carefully and cut the sweetbread into small cubes.
5. Cut the mushrooms likewise into cubes and save the broth.
6. Fry the sweetbread and mushroom cubes in the butter until clear, then squeeze them out with a spoon and fry the flour with the butter for 2 minutes.
7. Add the various liquids and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes.
8. Add the meat, mushroom and sweetbread and season the stew with spices.
9. Grease 6-8 shell-shaped dishes with butter and place the meat stew inside.
10. For the egg mixture: whisk the eggs well with the flour, milk and spices.
11. Heat a griddle and grease it with butter.
12. Fry small omelets from the egg mixture and place one on each shell.
