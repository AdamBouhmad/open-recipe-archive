## Ingredients

- 2 young chickens
- 1 tablespoon vinegar
- 1.5 liters boiling water
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 cloves
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 1 part thick cream
- 2 egg yolks
- 1.5 cups Carolina rice
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2.5 liters water
- 1 bunch parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. Pluck and singe the chickens, cut off wings and legs. Open them, remove organs, rinse very well and dry. Rub inside and outside with vinegar diluted with a little water.
2. Place the chickens in the boiling water, skim well when it comes to a boil, then add the spices. When the chickens are tender, remove them, cut into nice pieces and keep warm in a water bath.
3. Strain and skim the broth.
4. Mix butter and flour together, add the broth, and let the sauce simmer for 10 minutes. Finish the sauce with egg yolks and cream.
5. Rinse the rice grains in warm water, place in boiling salted water and cook until tender. Stir occasionally with a fork.
6. Arrange the cooked rice on the serving platter and garnish with fresh parsley. Place the chicken pieces on top and pour some sauce over. Serve the remaining sauce separately.
