## Ingredients

- 8 kg salmon
- 200 grams coarse salt
- 200 grams butter salt
- 8 tablespoons sugar
- 4 tablespoons butter salt
- 1 tablespoon potassium nitrate

## Directions

1. Brush and rinse the salmon well.
2. Cut it open carefully so that the gall is not broken, clean and wash well.
3. Split it into two halves, leaving some of the flesh on both sides of the backbone attached to the spine.
4. Remove the side fins.
5. The head, backbone and fins can be salted separately.
6. Cut the fillets into smaller pieces.
7. Rub these with a mixture of salt, sugar and nitrate.
8. An alternative method is to dissolve the nitrate in a little water, wash the fish pieces with this and then rub them with sugar and salt.
9. Line a glazed stone crock or wooden tub with salt.
10. Place the salmon with skin side down and sprinkle with a mixture of coarse and fine salt.
11. Layer the salmon pieces with flesh side to flesh side, skin side to skin side and with salt between each layer.
12. Sprinkle salt on top and place a lid with weight on it.
13. For fish, no special brine is cooked, but it can remain in the brine formed during salting, or in a brine made from cold water and salt.
14. The salmon must therefore be checked often and the pieces turned, placing the top pieces at the bottom and vice versa.
