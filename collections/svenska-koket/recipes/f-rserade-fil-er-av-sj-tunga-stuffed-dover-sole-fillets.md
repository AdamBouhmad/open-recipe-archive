## Ingredients

- 3 Dover soles, approximately 5 hg. each
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g.)
- Fish farce: ½ kg. pike = 210 g. scraped fish meat, 125 g. good butter, 15 g. flour, 2 dcl. cream, 1 egg, 1 egg yolk, 2 tsp. salt (10 g.), 1 tsp. sugar (5 g.), ¼ tsp. white pepper
- For garnish: 4 truffles or 48 shrimp tails
- For cooking: 1½ tbsp. butter (30 g.), fish stock

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the Dover soles as in recipe no. 371.
2. Pound the fillets with a moistened meat mallet, rub with salt and let lie on top of each other for a couple of hours.
3. Prepare the fish farce as in recipe no. 356.
4. Dry the fillets again, cut each fillet in half and trim the pieces so they become oblong round, or cut them into pear shapes.
5. Cover them with a 1½ cm. thick layer of fish farce, smoothed evenly with a spoon dipped in warm water, and brush over with lightly beaten egg white.
6. Garnish the farce with truffles cut into beautiful shapes or with shrimp tails or with both parts.
7. Clean a long pan (so it does not darken) and butter it with the cold butter, place the fillets in it and add enough fish stock (made from heads, bones and skin) so it reaches up to the lower edge of the farce, place a lid or sheet over it, set the pan in a regular oven temperature and let the fillets cook for 15–20 minutes.
8. Test with a skewer to see if they are done.
9. Lift them out with a slotted spoon onto a warm plate, garnish with parsley and serve with hollandaise, crayfish or lobster sauce and small boiled potatoes.
