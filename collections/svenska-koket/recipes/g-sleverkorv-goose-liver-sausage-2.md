## Ingredients

- 1/2 dcl. rice grains
- 3 1/2 dcl. milk
- 1 goose liver
- 30 gr. sultana raisins
- 1 tsp. salt (5 gr.)
- 1/4 tsp. white pepper
- 1/4 tsp. marjoram
- 2 tsp. finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 10 gr. butter, for the onion
- 1 small egg

## Directions

1. Rinse the rice grains, scald them in hot water, rinse again, let them drain, place them in the boiling milk and allow them to cook until soft, then pour out the porridge and let it cool.
2. Pound the liver, pass it through a hair sieve and mix it together with the cleaned and blanched raisins, the spices and the lightly browned onion.
3. Finally stir in the egg and taste the forcemeat, then fill it into the well-cleaned neck skin of a goose, sewn closed at one end.
4. The sausage must not be filled more than 3/4 full, otherwise it will burst easily. Sew it shut and allow it to cook slowly in salted water, without a lid, for about 1/2 hour.
5. Allow it to cool, cut it into slices and serve with black soup.
