## Ingredients

- 2 tjog crayfish
- 4 lit. water
- 90 gr. coarse salt
- butter, equal weight to the crayfish shells

## Directions

1. Boil the crayfish as in no. 171. Clean them; tails, claws and all fat are used for stews or sauces, the remainder is weighed and an equal weight of butter is taken.
2. Pound the shells and butter in a stone mortar into a fine mass, which is allowed to melt in a water bath with stirring and strain through fine cheesecloth into a bowl with cold water.
3. When the butter is half set, form it into balls, which should weigh 10 gr. each.
4. Store them well covered in brine and use for sauces and stews.
5. Alternatively, all fat from the crayfish together with the shells can be pounded with the butter into a fine mass, which is passed through a hair sieve and pressed through muslin. This butter has an exquisite fine taste but should be used within 1-2 days, as it becomes rancid quickly.
6. Lobster butter is prepared from lobster shells in the same way.
7. Butter can be colored green with spinach coloring or with pistachios. The pistachios, 120 gr., are blanched, ground through an almond mill, pounded in a stone mortar with 30 gr. butter and passed through a hair sieve.
