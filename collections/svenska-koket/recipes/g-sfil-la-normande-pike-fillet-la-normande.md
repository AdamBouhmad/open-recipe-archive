## Ingredients

- 2 pike on 5 hg. each
- 2 tsk. salt
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 gr.)
- fish stock
- 1 can mushrooms (1/4 liter)
- 2 tbsp. butter (40 gr.)
- 3 tbsp. flour (30 gr.)
- 4 dcl. fish stock
- 1 hg. shrimp
- 1 can mussels (1/4 liter)
- salt
- white pepper
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 1/2 tbsp. butter (30 gr.)
- 4 tbsp. flour (40 gr.)
- 4 dcl. fish stock
- 2 tbsp. mushroom liquid
- 2 tbsp. mussel liquid
- 2 tbsp. thick cream
- 2 egg yolks
- 30 gr. butter
- salt
- cayenne
- 6 slices truffle
- 12 crayfish or shrimp tails
- 6 mushroom caps
- 6–8 small crescents of puff pastry
- parsley sprigs

## Directions

1. Scale off the pike, clean according to page 171, cut into fillets and remove the backbone and all small bones.
2. Place the fillets skin-side down on a cutting board and cut the meat from the skin with a pointed knife.
3. Cut the fillets in half lengthwise and then cut in half crosswise.
4. Beat them out lightly with a damp mallet so they resemble sole fillets, rub with salt and let them lie on top of each other for 1 hour.
5. Cook good fish stock on the head, bones and skin and poach the fillets in it, after they have been dried again, as in recipe 328.
6. Arrange them with a slotted spoon in a wreath on an oblong dish, preferably of silver, place the dish for a few minutes in the front of the oven and carefully pour off the liquid that collects underneath.
7. The fish stock is reduced and used for the stew and sauce.
8. For the stew, cut the mushrooms into smaller pieces, after 6 caps have been removed for garnish.
9. Sauté the cut mushrooms in butter until it is clear, then press them up with a spoon and sauté the flour with the butter for 2 minutes, dilute the fish stock and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes.
10. Add the cleaned shrimp and mussels, let the stew come to a boil, season with spices, finish with the whipped egg yolk and let it simmer.
11. For the sauce, sauté butter and flour for 2 minutes, dilute with fish stock, mushroom liquid, mussel liquid and cream and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes.
12. Add the whipped egg yolks and cold butter, let the sauce simmer while whisking and season with spices.
13. Place the stew in the middle of the dish, pour the sauce over it and garnish beautifully with the truffle, crayfish tails, mushrooms sautéed in butter, puff pastry crescents and parsley.
14. Serve with small round boiled potatoes, cut out with a melon baller.
