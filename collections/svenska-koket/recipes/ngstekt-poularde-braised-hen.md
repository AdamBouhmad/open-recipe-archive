## Ingredients

- 1 poularde or 1 capon, approximately 3 kg
- 1/2 lemon
- 25 gr. truffle, ham, or tongue
- 1/2 tablespoon salt (8 gr.)
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 hectogram lard, in 1 slice
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.)
- 2 carrots
- 1 red onion
- 1 celery stalk
- 1 tablespoon raw, finely chopped ham
- 3/4 liter clear veal or chicken broth
- 1 deciliter mushroom puree, Madeira, or white wine
- 3 deciliters dark broth

## Directions

1. Prepare the bird in the usual way, rub it with lemon on the breast and legs, then dip these briefly in boiling light broth or water.
2. Lard it with strips of truffle, ham, or tongue.
3. Rub inside and outside with a mixture of salt and pepper, truss it, and cover it with the slice of lard, fastened with fine string.
4. In a pot just large enough to contain the bird, brown the butter, place the bird in it and brown it nicely on all sides, then remove.
5. Peel the carrots and finely chop the red part; also chop the red onion and the inside of the celery stalk.
6. Place the vegetables in the pot together with the ham; place the bird on top and add one-third of the broth.
7. Cover and let the broth reduce over gentle heat until thick and syrupy.
8. Add the rest of the broth, place the pot uncovered in the oven, and roast the bird until tender, basting it every 10 minutes.
9. Ten minutes before the bird is done, add the mushroom puree or wine.
10. Place the bird in a deep dish and cover to keep warm.
11. Strain the vegetables (which should not have browned) with the dark broth and let simmer 10 minutes.
12. Strain through a cloth wrung out in hot water, carefully remove all fat from the sauce, bring to a boil, and taste for seasoning.
13. Cut the bird, garnish with small sausages, vegetables, and browned potatoes.
14. Serve the sauce in a sauce boat.
