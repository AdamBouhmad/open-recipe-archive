## Ingredients

- 1 calf's head
- 2 1/2 liters water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 6 white peppercorns
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 g)
- 1 slice smoked bacon (20 g)
- 2 red onions
- 3 carrots
- 1 leek
- 1/2 celery
- 1 tablespoon tomato puree
- 6 tablespoons flour (60 g)
- 2 liters brown broth
- soy sauce, salt
- cayenne
- 1 deciliter Madeira

## Directions

1. The calf's head should be scalded and scraped and the skin not removed. Halve it; remove the tongue, brain (to be used for croquettes and stewing) and eyes, rinse the head and let it soak in plenty of cold water for about 12 hours so that the blood is drawn out.
2. Clean and blanch for 10 minutes (see no. 47), place in cold water with the salt added, skim carefully and simmer with the peppercorns until the meat loosens from the bones.
3. Remove the head, cut the meat loose, place on a flat dish under light pressure, strain and skim the stock.
4. Brown the bacon slice and the trimmed, cut-up vegetables well in 2 tablespoons of butter. Add the stock and tomato puree and let everything simmer for about 2 hours.
5. Strain and skim the stock again, measure and should yield 1 liter. Fry the remaining butter with the flour for 2 minutes, add the stock and broth, add the meat cut into small, even pieces and let the soup simmer while stirring for 5 minutes.
6. Color with soy sauce (2 teaspoons caramelized sugar, 2 tablespoons hot water), season with spices and wine and serve with hard-boiled egg yolks.
