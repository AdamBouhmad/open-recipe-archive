## Ingredients

- 24 fermented Baltic herrings
- 1 bottle beer
- 1 liter cooked potatoes
- 2 red onions
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 1 tablespoon crushed hardtack
- 2 gingerbread cookies
- 1 tablespoon butter (20 grams) for the pan
- 1 tablespoon crushed hardtack for the pan

## Directions

1. Clean the herrings, remove the bones, rinse them and let them soak in beer for 2 hours
2. Wrap them in white paper
3. Peel the potatoes and cut them into slices; peel the onions and chop them finely
4. Grease a heat-resistant pan with butter and sprinkle with crushed hardtack
5. On the bottom of the pan, layer potatoes, then herring, then onion and flakes of butter
6. Continue layering until you have 5 layers of potatoes and 4 layers of herring
7. On top, distribute the remaining butter in flakes, then sprinkle the crushed hardtack mixed with the crumbled gingerbread cookies
8. Fry the herring in a hot oven for approximately 1/2 hour
9. Serve as a breakfast or supper dish
10. Note: The potatoes can be omitted and the herring fried in the same manner; it can also be eaten directly from the barrel 'au naturel', in which case the herring is only rinsed and served with raw chopped onion and freshly cooked potatoes
