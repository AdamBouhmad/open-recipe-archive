## Ingredients

- 1.2 kg pike
- 5 hg scraped fish meat
- 250 g butter
- 4 eggs
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 g)
- 4 dcl thick cream
- 2 dcl thin cream
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 g)
- 2 teaspoons sugar (10 g)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 g) or 1 ball crayfish butter (10 g) for the mold
- 2 tablespoons finely crushed rusks

## Directions

1. Do not scale the pike; clean it as described, rinse well, dry with a fish cloth, fillet and remove the backbone.
2. Scrape the fish meat finely, ensuring no scales or bones are included; weigh it.
3. Mix with cold butter and pass through a meat grinder 4-5 times.
4. Pound the mass in a mortar with a wooden pestle until fine and cohesive.
5. In a bowl, whisk the egg yolks, flour, cream, and seasonings.
6. Work this mixture into the forcemeat by tablespoon, stirring for approximately 1 hour.
7. Pass the mixture through a sieve.
8. Finally, fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks.
9. Season carefully.
10. Fill into a well-buttered and breadcrumb-coated fish forcemeat mold of approximately 2 liter capacity.
11. Place the lid on and cook the forcemeat in a water bath in the oven or on top of the stove.
12. If cooked in the oven, it is done after 1 hour.
13. If cooked on top of the stove, the pot in which the mold sits should be deep enough for the lid to fit snugly and the water should simmer gently for approximately 1.5 hours.
14. Top up with boiling water as needed.
15. Turn the forcemeat out onto a hot dish.
16. Garnish with parsley and crayfish tails.
17. Serve with crayfish or lobster sauce and small boiled potatoes.
