## Ingredients

- 1 kg beef sirloin, ox loin, boneless inner loin or fillet
- 2 tsp salt (10 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 4 tbsp butter (80 gr.)
- 1–2 dcl bouillon
- 3–4 red onions or 1 large Portuguese onion

## Directions

1. The meat, which should have hung until properly tender, is dried with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut across the muscle fibers in approximately 1.5 cm thick slices, which are beaten with a moist meat mallet or meat tenderizer until thin, but must not be beaten to pieces. If fillet is used, the meat slices are only lightly tapped with a knife.
2. The steaks are sprinkled with the seasonings.
3. The onion is peeled, cut into slices and fried first in part of the butter and placed in the center of a warm serving dish.
4. The frying pan is rinsed out with hot water and heated over a brisk flame; enough butter is added so that it covers the bottom of the pan when melted.
5. When the butter is brown, the steaks are added and fried approximately 2 minutes on each side, or until they are nicely brown.
6. They are arranged in a ring around the onion and covered either with browned butter or the frying pan is rinsed out with the bouillon and the sauce is poured over the steaks.
7. Served with fried potatoes, sprinkled with finely chopped parsley.
8. If the steaks are desired rare inside, they should be somewhat thicker and fried only 1 minute on each side.
