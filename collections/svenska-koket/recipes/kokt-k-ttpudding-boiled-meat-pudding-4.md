## Ingredients

- 4 hg. boneless cooked or fried meat
- 6 dcl. boiling milk
- 1 1/2 dcl. finely crushed breadcrumbs
- 3–4 eggs
- 1 hg. butter (melted)
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 g.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 1 tbsp. finely chopped pickled cucumber
- 1/2 tbsp. finely chopped Portuguese onion
- 10 g. butter for the onion
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 g.) for the mold
- 2 tbsp. crushed breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Chop the meat very finely (do not grind) and mix it with the milk, breadcrumbs and the beaten egg yolks; let the forcemeat rest for 1 hour to swell; if it is then too firm, add a little more milk.
2. Add the butter, seasonings, cucumber and onion (sautéed in butter), and taste the forcemeat carefully.
3. Finally fold in the stiffly beaten egg whites.
4. Pour the mixture into a buttered and breadcrumb-lined baking pan and bake the pudding in a water bath in the oven for approximately 1 1/2 hours.
5. Turn it out onto a plate and garnish with slices of tomatoes or cucumber and parsley.
6. Serve with meat gravy, meat sauce or melted butter.
7. If desired, the cucumber and onion can be omitted.
