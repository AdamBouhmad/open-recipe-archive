## Ingredients

- 8 kg. lax (salmon)
- 200 gr. grovt salt (coarse salt)
- 200 gr. smörsalt (butter salt)
- 8 msk. strösocker (powdered sugar) for rubbing
- 4 msk. smörsalt (butter salt)
- 1 msk. kalisalpeter (potassium nitrate)

## Directions

1. Brush and rinse the salmon well.
2. Cut it open carefully, so that the gall is not burst, clean and wash well.
3. Split it into two halves, allowing some of the meat on both sides of the backbone to remain with the backbone.
4. Cut off the side fins. Head, backbone and fins can be salted separately.
5. Cut the fillets into smaller pieces. Rub these with a mixture of salt, sugar and saltpeter.
6. Another method is to dissolve the saltpeter in a little water, wash the fish pieces with it, and then rub them with sugar and salt.
7. Coat a glazed stone crock or wooden tub with salt, place the salmon with skin side down and sprinkle with coarse and fine salt mixed together.
8. Layer the salmon pieces with meat side to meat side, skin side to skin side, with salt between each layer.
9. Sprinkle salt on top and place a cover with weight on top.
10. Fish never has a special brine made for it, but should lie in the brine formed during salting, or in a brine made from cold water and salt.
11. The salmon must therefore be checked often and the pieces turned over, placing the topmost pieces at the bottom and vice versa.
