## Ingredients

- 4 kg. veal loin
- 1 1/2 hg. lard
- 1 tbsp. salt (15 gr.)
- 1/2 tsp. white pepper
- 5 tbsp. butter (1 g.)
- 1 liter broth

## Directions

1. By saddle is meant the two connected chop rows. The saddle is trimmed and should hang for a couple of days so that the meat becomes tender. The membrane is carefully removed and the saddle is larded in dense rows with fine lard strips, which are rolled in a mixture of salt and pepper.
2. The butter is browned in a long pan, the saddle is placed in it with the larded side up, put in a warm oven and browned while frequently basted with the butter in the pan. It is sprinkled with the rest of the seasonings, diluted with the boiling broth and allowed to cook until tender, or about 1 1/2 hours, during which it is basted every 10 minutes. If it becomes too brown, cover it with a butter-brushed paper.
3. The saddle is removed, the meat is loosened neatly on both sides of the backbone, placed on a cutting board and cut across into beautiful slices, which are arranged on the saddle so that it looks whole. It is placed on a warm serving dish and garnished with cooked vegetables such as peas, small fresh beans, cauliflower, asparagus, carrots, as well as French potatoes and green salad or parsley. Served with strained sauce (see no. 429), mixed with thick cream, sherry or Madeira and a little lemon juice.
