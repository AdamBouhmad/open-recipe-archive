## Ingredients

- 1 1/4 kg boneless beef (leg or flank)
- 2 cups crushed hardtack
- 2 tbsp finely chopped kidney fat
- 2 tbsp finely chopped parsley
- 2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 2 eggs
- 2 tbsp butter
- 2 large carrots
- 1 onion
- 1/2 Portuguese onion
- 3–4 cups broth or water

## Directions

1. Wash the meat with a cloth dipped in hot water and cut into two large slices, about 1 cm thick.
2. Mix the crushed hardtack, kidney fat, parsley and seasonings with the beaten eggs and work into a smooth dough.
3. Divide the mixture in half and spread on each meat slice.
4. Roll up the slices and bind well with string.
5. Heat the butter in a roasting pan, brown the rolls, and dilute with boiling broth or water.
6. Rinse the carrots, scrape them and cut into thick slices. Peel the onions and cut into wedges, and add everything to the meat.
7. Let the stew simmer slowly with a tightly closed lid for about 1 1/2–2 hours.
8. Remove the rolls, take off the string, and cut the meat into nice slices, which are placed in the center of a plate and garnished with the roots and onion.
9. Strain the sauce, skim it and pour over the meat.
10. If desired, peeled potatoes can be added to the stew 1/2 hour before the dish is ready. Naturally, more liquid must then be used.
