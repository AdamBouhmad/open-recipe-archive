## Ingredients

- 2 egg yolks
- 1 tsp salt (5 gr.)
- a few grains cayenne (1/4 tsp. French mustard)
- 1 tbsp tarragon vinegar or vinegar
- 2 dcl fine oil
- 1/2—1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tbsp boiling water

## Directions

1. Place egg yolks in a large mixing bowl together with the spices (and mustard, if desired) and stir with a wooden spoon for 10 minutes.
2. Pour the vinegar gradually over the yolks with vigorous stirring.
3. When the vinegar is blended with the yolks, add the oil in a thin stream with constant stirring.
4. Finish the sauce with lemon juice and boiling water — the latter to prevent it from breaking.
5. Mayonnaise prepared this way is quite liquid and should therefore stand in a cool (not too cold) place for 4—5 hours to thicken.
6. It can be stored for 3—4 days without damage, as long as it is well covered and does not stand on ice.
7. If the sauce breaks, this can be remedied by gradually stirring it into 1 egg yolk and 1/2 tbsp vinegar.
8. The sauce becomes lighter if the mustard is omitted and only lemon juice is used instead of vinegar.
