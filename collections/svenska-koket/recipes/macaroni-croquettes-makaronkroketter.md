## Ingredients

- 60 gr. good macaroni
- 1/2 tablespoon salt (8 gr.)
- 1 liter water
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.)
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 gr.)
- 3 deciliters cream
- 1 egg yolk
- 1/2 teaspoon salt (3 gr.)
- a little white pepper
- grated cheese (optional)
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 gr.)
- 1 egg
- 1/2 teaspoon salt (3 gr.)
- 1 teaspoon table oil
- 1 1/2 deciliters crushed rusks
- 1 kg. lard for frying

## Directions

1. Break the macaroni into pieces, place in boiling salted water and cook until soft, about 20 minutes. Drain in a colander, rinse with cold water, drain on cloth and cut into 2 mm. thick rings.
2. Fry the butter and flour for 2 minutes, add the cream, and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes. Add the macaroni, stir in the egg yolk and season with the seasonings and cheese, if desired.
3. Spread the mixture on a flat dish buttered with butter, brush with a little butter so it does not dry, and let cool. Divide the mass into smaller portions—each portion should weigh about 35 gr.—which are rolled into elongated shapes on a floured board.
4. Dip the croquettes in the stiffly whipped eggs mixed with the table oil and seasoned with the seasonings, then roll in the crushed and sifted rusks and let sit for 1 hour to harden.
5. Fry golden-brown in hot lard and place on absorbent paper to drain. Serve with roasted meat.
