## Ingredients

- 1 lightly smoked ham, approximately 4 kg
- 6 liters water
- White aspic: 1/2 liter light broth, 5 sheets white gelatin, 2 egg whites, 1 tablespoon cold water, 1 tablespoon good Madeira
- Red aspic: 3 deciliters light broth, 4 sheets red gelatin, 1 egg white, 1/2 tablespoon cold water
- For garnish: 1/4 can green beans, 1 head of lettuce

## Directions

1. Soak the ham in plenty of cold water overnight to remove salt
2. Place it in cold water, skin side up, and simmer gently until tender, approximately 3.5 hours. It is done when the small bone on the ham hock loosens easily
3. Let the ham cool in its cooking liquid, then place it on an oblong platter and remove the skin
4. Prepare the white and red aspics as in recipe 181. If the red aspic is too light, color it with a little carmine
5. Slice the ham into even, attractive slices and lay them back on the bone so the ham appears whole
6. Gradually pour the white aspic over the slices so they hold together and the ham is beautifully glazed
7. When the white aspic is completely set, garnish the ham with the red aspic piped through a fluted paper cone into flowers and scrolls
8. Cut the beans into stalks and leaves
9. Garnish the ham bone with crinkled paper and the platter with chopped aspic, aspic croutons, and salad
10. Serve as a middle course with vegetable salad or stuffed tomatoes
