## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 kg lamb breast
- 1 1/2 liters water
- 1 1/2 tablespoons salt (23 g)
- 4 parsley sprigs
- For the sauce: 1 teaspoon grated red onion
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 g)
- 4 tablespoons flour (40 g)
- 5 deciliters of the broth
- curry powder to taste
- 1 tablespoon water
- 1/2 teaspoon sugar
- For the rice: 2 deciliters rice grains (2 hg)
- 2 liters water
- 1 1/2 tablespoons salt (23 g)
- 1/2-1 teaspoon curry powder

## Directions

1. Treat and cook the breast as in the previous recipe. Cut it into pieces, which are covered to keep them warm. Strain the broth and skim it well.
2. For the sauce, sauté the onion in the butter, add the flour and sauté it with the butter and onion for 2 minutes, add the broth a little at a time, and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes, then season it with curry powder (about 1 teaspoon), stirred with 1 tablespoon warm water, and the sugar.
3. Rinse the rice grains in cold water, scald in hot water, place in boiling, salted water and let them cook until soft — 15-20 minutes — then strain them through a colander, immediately rinse with cold water, place in a warm oven and stir often with a fork, until they are separated and all water has evaporated.
4. Arrange the rice in a ring on a round serving platter and sprinkle with a little dry curry. Arrange the meat in the center of the platter and pour the sauce over. Garnish with parsley.
