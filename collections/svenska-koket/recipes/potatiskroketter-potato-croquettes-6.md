## Ingredients

- 1 kg peeled potatoes (approx. 1 1/2 liters unpeeled)
- 30 gr butter
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 teaspoons sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper (small amount nutmeg)
- 3 egg yolks
- 1 1/2 tablespoons butter (30 gr) for the dish
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 gr) for breading
- 1 egg for breading
- 1 teaspoon cooking oil
- 1/2 teaspoon salt (3 gr) for breading
- 1 1/2 deciliters crushed rusks for breading
- 1 kg lard for frying

## Directions

1. Peel the potatoes thinly, cut into pieces, weigh them, and cook quickly in salted water until soft but not falling apart.
2. Immediately drain in a colander and let stand in the front of the oven for a few minutes to dry.
3. Pass the potatoes through a hair sieve.
4. Place the puree in a saucepan, add the cold butter and spices, and stir with a fork over the heat until firm.
5. Remove from heat and add the egg yolks one at a time, stirring constantly with a fork.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning with spices.
7. Butter a flat dish and spread the mixture thinly so it cools quickly.
8. Oil the surface so it does not dry.
9. Divide the mixture with a spoon into smaller portions (each about 35 gr), which are rolled on a floured work surface in the shape of corks, pears, buns, or round discs.
10. Dip these in the stiffly beaten eggs mixed with cooking oil and seasoned with spices, then roll in the finely crushed rusks and let sit 1 hour to harden.
11. Fry golden brown in hot lard and place on absorbent paper to drain.
12. Serve with roasted meat. If formed as pears, use parsley sprigs or spinach leaves as stems.
