## Ingredients

- 3 chickens, approximately 1/2 kg each
- 1 bunch parsley
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr.)
- 1/2 tablespoon salt (8 gr.)
- 3 tablespoons butter for frying (60 gr.)
- 2 tablespoons flour (20 gr.)
- 4 deciliters thick cream
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper

## Directions

1. Clean, singe, and eviscerate the chickens (see page 299 and following), rinse well and dry.
2. Clean the parsley, rinse it, wrap it in a cloth, chop finely and work it into the butter.
3. Fill the chickens with the parsley butter, sew them closed, rub with salt and truss them.
4. Brown the butter in an iron casserole, add the chickens along with the giblets (necks, livers and hearts) and brown well on all sides over gentle heat, then let them roast with the breast up with a tight lid, until tender, or approximately 1/2 hour. During this time baste them frequently with the butter in the casserole.
5. Place the chickens in a deep dish and cover so they do not cool.
6. Remove the giblets and fry the flour for 2 minutes with the butter in the casserole, add the cream and let the sauce boil for 5 minutes, then season with the spices; finally add 10 gr. cold butter.
7. Halve the chickens, arrange on fried bread croutons; garnish with browned potatoes, salad and parsley.
8. Serve with green salad and currant jelly. Strain the sauce and serve in a sauce bowl.
