## Ingredients

- 12 lamb cutlets
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 teaspoon white pepper
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 1 small bunch parsley
- 50 gr. butter
- 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper

## Directions

1. Lamb cutlets should be chopped with long bones and be approximately 2 cm. thick. Dry them with a cloth wrung out in hot water, trim them, beat on both sides and rub in with the spice mixture. Fry them in the browned butter 6–8 minutes on each side, arrange in a ring on a round serving dish, and slip paper frills on the bones. On each cutlet place a small slice of maitre d'hôtel butter, which is prepared as follows: rinse the parsley, wrap it in a cloth, clean it, chop it finely and wring it hard in a cloth, after which blend it well with the butter, lemon juice and pepper. Shape the butter in a damp cloth into a roll of a finger's thickness and let it lie in the cloth, which is placed on ice or in a cold place, until it is hard. The cutlets are served with French or fried potatoes as a breakfast dish.
