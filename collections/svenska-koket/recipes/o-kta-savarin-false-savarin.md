## Ingredients

- 2 eggs
- 150 gr. sugar
- 30 gr. melted butter
- 130 gr. wheat flour
- 1 1/2 tsk. baking powder
- 1/2 kkp buttermilk
- For the mold: 1/2 msk. butter (10 gr.)
- 2 msk. crushed rusks
- Sugar syrup: 250 gr. sugar
- 1 dcl. wine or 1 1/2 msk. arrack
- 2 1/2 dcl. water

## Directions

1. The eggs and sugar are stirred vigorously for 20 minutes, thereafter the melted but cooled butter is added, the wheat flour, which should be well sifted with the baking powder, and the buttermilk.
2. The batter is stirred well, is poured immediately into a buttered and breadcrumb-strewn ring mold and is baked in ordinary oven temperature for approximately 1/2 hour, or until the cake, when tested with a whisk, feels dry.
3. A clear, thin syrup is boiled from the sugar and water, to which wine or arrack is added.
4. The cake is turned out, the mold is filled halfway with sugar syrup, after which the cake is immediately placed in and is allowed to stand until it has absorbed all the syrup.
5. The savarin is turned out of the mold and placed on a flat glass plate with cake paper.
6. Garnish and serve as in the previous recipe.
