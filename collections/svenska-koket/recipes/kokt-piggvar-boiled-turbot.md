## Ingredients

- 2 fish of about 900 g each
- 1/2 lemon
- 2 tablespoons salt (30 g)

## Directions

1. Clean the fish, rinse them well in several waters and dry them with a fish towel.
2. Leave the head on and cut off the gills.
3. Polish the fins but do not cut them off.
4. Make a deep incision along the backbone on the dark side of the fish so that the white skin on the other side does not crack.
5. Rub the fish inside and out with the lemon juice and salt and let them rest for 1 hour, then dry them again.
6. Place them on a fish steamer with the white side up and cook as in no. 327.
7. The fish will be whiter if you cook it in 2/3 water and 1/3 milk with 7 g coarse salt to each liter of liquid.
8. Place the turbot on a napkin, garnish with parsley and beautifully cut lemon wedges and serve with hollandaise, crayfish, or lobster sauce.
