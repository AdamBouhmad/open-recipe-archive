## Ingredients

- meat from one hen
- 2 hg. veal
- 100 gr. butter
- 3 eggs
- 2 parts cream
- salt, white pepper
- 75 gr. salt boiled tongue
- 0.5 tin truffle (at 1:25 kr.)
- 1.5 twelve-öre wheat bread for panading
- 3 parts boiling milk

## Directions

1. Scrape the chicken meat well from the bones, cut together with the veal into cubes and pass three times through a meat grinder.
2. Free the bread well from brown edges, cut into pieces, add the milk, and work the panade over the fire with a wooden spoon until it forms a loose and smooth dough.
3. Let it cool and pass it through a fine sieve.
4. Pound the meat and butter well together in a stone mortar and pass through a fine sieve.
5. Gradually blend the whisked egg yolks, seasonings and cream into the forcemeat, which is worked for 1 hour.
6. Cut the tongue and truffle into cubes and add them.
7. If the forcemeat is too stiff, add a little more cream, otherwise an egg yolk.
8. Whisk the egg whites until stiff and blend them in gently.
9. Immediately shape the forcemeat in a fine metal mold (preferably a pipe mold with lid).
10. Bind it over with butter paper, if the mold lacks a lid, and place it in a water bath to poach for about 1 hour.
11. Test the forcemeat with a whisk, turn it out onto a hot plate and serve with light or dark sauce, made from good stock cooked on the chicken bones.
