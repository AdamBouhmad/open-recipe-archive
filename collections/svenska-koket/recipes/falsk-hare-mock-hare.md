## Ingredients

- 3 hg. boneless beef
- 2 hg. fresh pork belly
- 1 dcl. crushed rusks
- 1 msk. salt (15 gr.)
- 1/2 tsk. white pepper
- 1 egg, 1 egg yolk
- 2-3 dcl. cream or milk
- 50 gr. lard
- 1 egg white for brushing
- 2 msk. crushed rusks for brushing
- 2 msk. butter (40 gr.) for frying
- 5 dcl. broth

## Directions

1. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into cubes and pass 6 times through a meat grinder
2. Mix the forcemeat with the crushed rusks, spices, the beaten egg and egg yolk and stir vigorously for 1 hour while adding the cream by spoonfuls
3. Test the forcemeat by frying a small ball of it to check if it is sufficiently seasoned and of the right consistency
4. Scrape the lard and cut it into fine strips
5. Grease a long pan with cold butter, place the forcemeat in it and shape it with a spoon dipped in lukewarm water into a ball, which is flattened on both sides so that it resembles a hare's back, and lard it thickly with the lard strips
6. Brush the hare with the beaten egg white, sprinkle with crushed rusks and bake in the oven as in no. 632
7. Cut, garnish with parsley and fried potatoes
8. Serve with reduced sauce and aspic or preserved rowan berries
