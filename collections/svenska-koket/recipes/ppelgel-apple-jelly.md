## Ingredients

- 5 liters apples
- 2 liters water
- 3/4 to 1 kg sugar

## Directions

1. The apples should be firm and juicy; dry them well with a cloth.
2. Remove the stem and blossom end, but do not peel the apples.
3. Cut them into quarters and cook with the water on very low heat until they begin to fall apart.
4. Stir occasionally during cooking.
5. Pour the apple pulp into a cheesecloth and allow it to drip as long as juice falls from the cloth.
6. Weigh the juice, pour it back into the pot and add the sugar.
7. Bring slowly to a boil and allow to cook slowly until the jelly is so thick that a drop on a plate does not run out.
8. Skim the jelly well during cooking and immediately pour into warmed jars rinsed with cognac.
