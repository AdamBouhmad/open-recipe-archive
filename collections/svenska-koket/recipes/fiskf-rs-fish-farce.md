## Ingredients

- 1.2 kg. pike
- 5 hectograms scraped fish meat
- 250 gr. butter
- 4 eggs
- 3 tablespoons flour (30 gr.)
- 4 decilitres thick cream
- 2 decilitres thin cream
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 2 teaspoons sugar (10 gr.)
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 1/2 tablespoon butter (10 gr.) or 1 ball crayfish butter (10 gr.)
- 2 tablespoons finely crushed rusks

## Directions

1. Clean the pike without scaling, as described on page 171, rinse well, dry with a fish towel, fillet and remove the backbone.
2. Scrape the fish meat finely, ensuring no scales or bones are included, weigh it, mix with the cold butter and pass through a meat grinder 4-5 times.
3. Pound the mass in a stone mortar with a wooden pestle until it is fine and cohesive.
4. In a bowl, whisk the egg yolks, flour, cream and seasonings; work this mixture into the farce tablespoon by tablespoon, stirring for about 1 hour, then pass it through a sieve.
5. Lastly fold in the egg whites beaten to stiff peaks and season the farce carefully.
6. Fill it into a well-buttered and rusk-breadcrumbed fish farce mould holding about 2 litres.
7. Place the lid on and cook the farce in a water bath in the oven or on the stovetop.
8. If cooked in the oven, it is ready after 1 hour; if cooked on the stovetop, the saucepan in which the mould stands should be deep enough that the lid fits tightly on the saucepan and the water should cook slowly for about 1 1/2 hours.
9. Add boiling water as needed.
10. Turn the farce out onto a warm plate, garnish with parsley and crayfish tails and serve with crayfish or lobster sauce and small boiled potatoes.
