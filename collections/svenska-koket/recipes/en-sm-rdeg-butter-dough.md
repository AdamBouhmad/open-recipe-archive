## Ingredients

- 150 grams flour
- 1 decilitre water (scant)
- 250 grams fine butter (equal weight to the dough)

## Directions

1. Sift the flour and mix it with enough water to make a suitably firm dough, which is worked quickly, weighed, and left to rest for 20 minutes.
2. Wash the butter and squeeze out the water well, then beat it out into a round cake.
3. Roll the dough out into a round cake, somewhat thicker in the middle. Place the butter in the centre of the dough and fold the edges of the dough over the butter so that it is completely covered. Let the dough rest for 10 minutes.
4. Now beat the dough lightly out with a rolling pin into a square of approximately 1/2 cm thickness. This square is then folded together so that the edges of the dough meet in the middle of the square. Then one half of the dough is folded over the other, and the dough lies in four layers on top of each other.
5. Roll the dough out again into an oblong square. This is repeated 4 times in succession, after which the dough is left to rest for 15 minutes.
6. Roll it out again 4 times and let it rest for 15 minutes. This is done two more times with the dough, after which it rests for 1 hour before being rolled out into vol-au-vents, bouchées, pastries, butter dough spirals, cakes, etc.
7. Butter dough is baked in a warm oven.
