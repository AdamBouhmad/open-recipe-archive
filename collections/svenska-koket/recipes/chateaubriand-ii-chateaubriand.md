## Ingredients

- 600 grams beef fillet
- 3 teaspoons salt
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams)
- 1 small bunch parsley
- 2 tablespoons butter (40 grams)

## Directions

1. Dry the fillet with a cloth wrung out in hot water. Remove all outer sinews and membranes carefully, then cut the fillet into 6 slices, which are lightly pounded with a damp mallet and rubbed on both sides with the spice mixture.
2. Melt 1 tablespoon of the butter and brush the steaks with it, then let them rest 15 minutes (fine olive oil can be used instead of butter).
3. For the parsley butter, rinse the parsley, wrap it in a cloth, clean it, chop it finely, wring it hard in a cloth and work it well into the butter. Form this in a damp cloth into a roll of finger thickness and let it lie in the cloth, which is placed on ice or a cold place, until the butter is hard.
4. Heat a frying pan slowly, brown the remaining butter in it, place the steaks in, let them fry 3-4 minutes on each side and place on a warm dish, and pour the butter from the pan over them.
5. Place a slice of the parsley butter on each steak. Serve immediately as a breakfast dish with fried potatoes.
