## Ingredients

- 2 carrots
- 1 tbsp. butter (20 gr.)
- 2 dcl. fine preserved peas
- For the potato purée: 750 gr. peeled potato, about 1 1/4 liters unpeeled
- 30 gr. butter
- 1–1 1/2 dcl. cooked cream
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tsp. salt, 1 tsp. sugar (a little grated nutmeg)

## Directions

1. Rinse the carrots well, place in cold salted water and let cook almost soft, then the skin is immediately removed and the carrots are cut into fine strips.
2. Brush small pastry molds well with cold butter, line the bottom of the molds thickly with peas and garnish the sides thickly, on the slant, with carrot strips.
3. Treat the potato as described on page 337 et seq., peel thinly, cut into pieces and cook quickly.
4. Drain the water well and let the potato stand about 5 minutes with the lid ajar so the steam escapes.
5. Pass the potato through a purée press or stir with a fork into a purée.
6. Add the cold butter at once and work the purée with a fork until it becomes white and fluffy, then add the cooked cream together with the beaten egg yolks.
7. Last, cut in the egg white beaten to stiff peaks and taste the purée carefully with the spices.
8. Fill the molds halfway with the purée, place a layer of peas on top, then fill the molds with purée.
9. Cook the pastries at once in a water bath in the oven about 15 minutes, turn out and serve as a garnish around fried meat.
