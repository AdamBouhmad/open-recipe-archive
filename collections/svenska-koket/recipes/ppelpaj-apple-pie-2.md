## Ingredients

- 2 1/2 dcl. flour (125 gr.)
- 1 tbsp. sugar (15 gr.)
- 125 gr. butter
- 3-4 tbsp. cold water
- 1 1/2 liters good, loose apples
- 2 tbsp. water
- 2 tbsp. white wine
- 1 1/4 dl. sugar (108 gr.)
- 1/2 tbsp. butter (10 gr.) for the pan

## Directions

1. Sift the flour and sugar into a bowl, distribute the butter, which should be firm, in flakes into the flour and work it in with the fingertips until the mixture is even in color and without lumps.
2. Add the water and work the mixture quickly with a wooden spoon into an even dough, which should rest in a cold place for about 1 hour.
3. Rinse the apples, dry them, peel them, free them from the cores with a pastry tube and cut them into thin slices.
4. Grease a pie form with butter, place a 'pie tube' or, if lacking, an inverted, greased coffee cup in the middle.
5. Pack the apple slices around it, alternating with the sugar, so that the form becomes ridged, then add the water and wine.
6. Roll out the dough to 1 cm thickness, and cut out a lid from it, which should be somewhat larger than the form.
7. Brush the edge of the pie form with water and cover it with a strip of dough, which is pressed on the inside of the form.
8. Brush the dough strip with water, place the lid on it and press it on in the same way as the strip.
9. Trim the dough with a sharp knife and notch the edge with the knife point.
10. If desired, garnish the pie lid with the rest of the dough, cut in the form of flowers and leaves or hearts, and brush with water or beaten egg.
11. Bake the pie in a warm oven for about 40 minutes, or until the apples are soft; this is tested with a whisk.
12. Serve warm with whipped cream or vanilla sauce.
