## Ingredients

- 2 ducks
- 1 hg. bacon, in 2 slices
- 3 tbsp. butter (60 gr.)
- For sauce: The butter in which the birds are browned
- 2 tbsp. flour
- 3 dcl. meat broth or bouillon
- 3 dcl. thick cream
- Salt
- Sugar
- White pepper

## Directions

1. Duck should be dressed immediately after it is shot.
2. The bird is young if the webbed skin is thin and can be easily torn.
3. The broad-billed mallard is best.
4. Those with pointed beaks are usually so fishy that they are almost inedible.
5. The birds are cleaned, rinsed well, dried inside and out, trussed and covered with thin bacon slices, which are tied down with fine sailing yarn.
6. Roast in a pot like pheasant and are tender after 1 1/4-1 1/2 hours.
7. Cut and arranged again on the platter so that they look whole, garnished with browned potatoes, parsley and olives.
8. Served with jelly or compote.
