## Ingredients

- 1 kg boned entre-côte
- 1 hg smoked ham
- 3 tbsp butter (60 g)
- 1 tsp salt (5 g)
- a little white pepper
- 5 dcl dark broth
- 1 1/2–2 dcl Madeira

## Directions

1. The meat, which should have hung until properly tender, is prepared and larded with ham (see page 232), then rubbed with a mixture of salt and pepper and tied so it takes on a beautiful shape.
2. The butter is browned in an iron pot, which should not be larger than to just fit the meat snugly, the roast is browned on all sides until a beautiful brown crust has formed, then the boiling broth and half the wine are added.
3. The meat is thereafter allowed to braise with a tightly closed lid until tender, or approximately 1 1/2–2 hours, during which it is turned a few times.
4. Toward the end of braising the remainder of the wine is added.
5. The roast is sliced thin, laid on a warm platter, covered with a couple of tablespoons of sauce, garnished with salad or parsley and small browned potatoes or potato patties.
6. Served with brown sauce (see recipe 430).
