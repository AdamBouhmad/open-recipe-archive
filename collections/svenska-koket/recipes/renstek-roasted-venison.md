## Ingredients

- 3 kg. of the hind quarter
- 3 liters skim milk or drink
- 3 hg. lard
- 1 tablespoon salt (15 gr.)
- 4 tablespoons butter (80 gr.)
- 1/2 liter broth
- 1/2 liter cream or good milk

## Directions

1. If the meat is frozen, it should lie in milk or drink until just thawed.
2. If unfrozen, it is washed very carefully with a cloth dipped in hot water and beaten with a damp meat mallet.
3. The meat is dried well and all membranes are trimmed away, after which it is larded (see p. 232) and rubbed with the rest of the salt and tied so that it has a nice shape.
4. The butter is browned in a roasting pan, the meat is placed in and browned well on all sides, after which it is moistened first with the boiling broth, then with the boiling cream or milk, and is roasted with a tightly closed lid until tender, or about 3 1/2 hours, during which it is turned several times and basted every 10 minutes.
5. The meat is sliced thinly, laid on a hot platter and covered with a couple of tablespoons of sauce.
6. Garnished with browned potatoes, salad or parsley and served with reduced sauce (see no. 429) mixed with thick cream, along with green salad and sour salads or currant jelly.
