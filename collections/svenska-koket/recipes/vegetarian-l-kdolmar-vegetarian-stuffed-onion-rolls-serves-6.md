## Ingredients

- 2 large Portuguese onions
- 1 1/2 liters water
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 2 cups cold rice porridge (leftover)
- 2 eggs
- 2 tablespoons crushed rusks
- 40 gr butter, clarified
- salt
- sugar
- white pepper
- For browning: 3 tablespoons butter (60 gr), 2 cups vegetable broth or potato water

## Directions

1. Peel the onions, place in boiling salted water and let simmer gently without a lid for about 7 minutes.
2. Remove the onions, carefully separate the leaves and place on linen to drain.
3. For the forcemeat use cold rice porridge.
4. Beat the eggs and mix into the porridge together with the rusks and clarified butter, then season well with salt, sugar and white pepper.
5. Place a teaspoon of forcemeat on each onion leaf, roll up and tie with cotton string previously dipped in hot water.
6. Brown the butter in an iron pot, add the dolmas and brown nicely, then dilute with the hot vegetable broth or potato water.
7. Put on the lid and let braise gently for about 30 minutes.
8. Place on a warm platter, cut off the string and pour the sauce over it. Serve as a breakfast dish with potato pudding or fried potatoes.
