## Ingredients

- 2 young chickens
- 1 tablespoon vinegar
- 1½ liters water
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 8 parts chicken broth
- 80 grams sweet almonds
- 2 parts buttermilk

## Directions

1. Pluck and singe the chickens, remove wings and legs. Open them, clean out, rinse thoroughly and dry, then rub inside and outside with vinegar diluted with a little water.
2. Place the chickens in 1½ liters of boiling water or light broth. When the liquid comes to a boil, skim it carefully and add the salt.
3. When the chickens are tender, remove them and cut into neat pieces. Strain the broth and skim off the fat.
4. Blanch the almonds, grind them through an almond mill, pound very fine in a mortar and cook in the buttermilk.
5. Brown the butter and flour together, add the broth and the buttermilk with its almonds, and let the sauce simmer for 10 minutes. Finally add the meat.
