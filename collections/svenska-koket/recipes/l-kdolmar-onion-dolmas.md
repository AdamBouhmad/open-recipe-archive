## Ingredients

- 2 large Portuguese onions
- 1 1/2 liters water
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 3 hectograms boneless veal
- 50 grams butter
- 1 tablespoon flour (10 grams)
- 1 egg
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 2 deciliters cream
- 3 tablespoons butter (60 grams) for frying
- 2 deciliters broth

## Directions

1. Peel the onion, place in boiling salted water and let cook slowly without a lid about 7 minutes.
2. Remove and carefully loosen the layers and place on linen to drain.
3. Dry the meat with a cloth wrung out in hot water, cut into pieces and grind 3 times through a meat grinder.
4. Pound well in a mortar together with the butter and flour, then add the whipped egg and seasonings.
5. Work the forcemeat about 1/2 hour, adding the thin cream gradually, as the forcemeat easily breaks if done too quickly.
6. If it is too stiff, thin it with a little more cream.
7. Place a teaspoon of forcemeat on each onion layer, roll up and tie with cotton twine, first dipped in hot water.
8. Brown the butter in an iron pot, add the dolmas and brown nicely, then place the lid on and let braise slowly about 40 minutes.
9. Toward the end of cooking, add the broth.
10. Place on a warm platter, cut off the twine and pour the sauce over. Serve at the smörgåsbord.
