## Ingredients

- Crab apples
- Water
- Sugar

## Directions

1. Cut sour, unripe crab apples into pieces, but do not peel them. Neither do you remove the kernels. These last give a special fine aroma to the jelly. Put the fruit in a stone pot, which is set in a pot of hot water, and let it cook 8 to 9 hours. If the apples are very dry, take a cup of water for every 6 pounds of fruit. When it is cooked let it stand in the pot half the night. Next morning press out all the juice, and prepare the jelly in the usual manner, giving 1 pound of sugar for each pint of juice. Crab-apple jelly is considered one of the finest, both in appearance and in taste.
