## Ingredients

- Peaches that are nearly ripe
- For each pound of peaches: 1 pound sugar
- For each pound of sugar: 1 pint white wine
- Water as needed

## Directions

1. Blanch the peaches and remove the pits.
2. Dip the sugar in water and boil into a syrup.
3. When the syrup is boiled, add to each pound of sugar 1 pint white wine.
4. Add the peaches, but only a few at a time, bring to a boil and carefully remove with a slotted spoon, placing on a cloth to drain.
5. When the syrup has drained, put it back in the pan to boil again.
6. Skim well, then add the kernels after cracking the stones and cutting them into pieces.
7. Place the peaches in jars and pour the syrup over them.
8. If the syrup becomes watery after a few days, boil again and pour cold over the peaches.
