## Ingredients

- Butter
- Milk
- Salt

## Directions

1. Heat butter on a griddle for the first pancakes
2. The griddle must be hot before you begin baking and butter is added to the griddle for the first pancakes, later not. The dough is held thin on the griddle and when the underside is baked enough, the pancake is turned. This is best done by loosening it around the edge with a knife, taking it at two spots at the edge and turning it quickly, after which it is baked finished on the other side
3. For the pancakes to become hard, they must be baked slowly over the griddle, not burning, as this gives too harsh heat, from which the pancakes become dark and not good; these should not be laid warm on top of one another. If you wish the pancakes soft, little butter is added to the griddle for each pancake, they are baked faster and laid warm on top of one another
4. If the dough becomes too thick during baking, it can be thinned with a little milk
5. The first pancakes are seldom whole, but when several are baked on the griddle, it goes better
