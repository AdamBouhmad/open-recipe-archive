## Ingredients

- Young fresh game bird
- Paper
- Water
- Soda
- Salt
- Pepper
- Bread stuffing or egg stuffing

## Directions

1. A fresh game bird is young and fresh, which is the main condition for it to become well-cooked.
2. Pluck the feathers, remove the down and hairs by rubbing them well with a piece of paper so that nothing dark is left on them.
3. Cut the birds in the following manner: cut a slit along the backbone and pull it out boneless, and large enough that a hand can pass through it, and remove the contents.
4. Remove the crop, which lies at the bird's gullet boundary.
5. The entire cleaning is done in lukewarm water and must be very careful; in the next water add a piece of soda; let cold water run through the bird many times, and when you are completely certain of the cleaning, dry it well inside with a clean cloth.
6. The head and bones below the ankle are cut off and not used.
7. The gizzard, heart and liver, when cleaned, can be used in various ways; either minced and added to the filling or in the sauce, or one can simply put them inside the whole bird.
8. On hens and chickens the breastbone can be cut inside to give them a round shape, but on game birds it must be done from the outside.
9. One can have a bread dough and stick it between the loose skin and the meat; it gives the breast a beautiful golden appearance.
10. To prevent the bones from sticking out when the bird is cooked, you can cut a hole fairly high up on each side of the cut skin, push the hairs back so that the tendons can be pulled into the hole and tied around them.
11. On ducks and geese the breastbone is not cut.
12. One should never use a fork to turn the birds—because precious juice runs out—but use a clean wooden spoon.
13. One counts generally 15 minutes of roasting for every pound the bird weighs.
14. Wash the game bird in cold water.
15. If you prefer, you can singe it.
16. To fill it around where the crop has been; for this use is allowed a piece of a few ounces of white bread that is soaked in milk and then stands covered for an hour.
17. A piece of cold butter is stirred until smooth, then the bread is stirred, after being moistened into pieces with a spoon, salt, a little sweet ginger, a pair of cloves and 3 eggs.
18. When it is well mixed, one can add the gizzard, liver and heart, which are finely minced, fill it in the skin and sew it, but the filling must not push down into the game bird, so a piece of white bread must be placed in the internal hole below the neck.
19. The oven must be well heated before the game bird is put in, and it must have an even warmth, but not too strong, as it would otherwise become too dark brown.
20. It cannot be roasted in a pot when it is filled.
21. It is placed in with water and a little salt, by which it is gradually basted; later butter is laid on it, by which it is then basted.
22. The best filling for a duck is apples and good prunes, it gives the most pleasant flavor; but others allow a dough of eggs, bread and milk and fill with it.
23. One can also just rub it inside with salt and pepper and then place a piece of juniper wood inside; these are completely consumed during roasting, but they give the duck a fresh flavor.
24. It is placed in the oven with a little water on it, which soon becomes fat, by which the duck is often basted.
25. As sauce sharp red wine sauce can be used, but generally only sauce made in the usual way is used.
26. Fat should be well skimmed off before the sauce is made.
