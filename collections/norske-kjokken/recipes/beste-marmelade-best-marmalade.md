## Ingredients

- Quinces
- Water
- Sugar (scant 1 pound sugar per 1 pound fruit)
- Apple juice (juice of 2 apples per 3 pounds fruit, optional)

## Directions

1. Peel and slice the quinces into pieces with the stones removed; cook the peelings, stones and cores in scant water under a lid; when they have cooked long enough to draw out all the juice, strain the water through a thick cloth.
2. When this water is approximately cold, put the quinces in a clay pot and pour the water over them, and it cooks, stirring and mashing the fruit with a spoon, gradually as it becomes soft.
3. This gives a pleasant flavor if you add the juice of 2 apples to every 3 pounds of fruit.
4. When it is stirred to a soft pulp, stir in scant 1 pound of sugar for every pound of fruit; cook for another 10 minutes, stirring.
5. Then remove it, and when cold, put it in small jars or glasses with brandy paper over.
