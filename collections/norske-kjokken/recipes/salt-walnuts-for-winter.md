## Ingredients

- Walnuts
- Vinegar
- Shallots
- Onion
- Ginger
- Young peppercorn
- Mace
- Allspice berries
- Mustard seed
- Pepper
- Allspice

## Directions

1. The walnuts must be picked at the same time and be of the same size as the others. They are placed in a cold brine, which is so strong that it can bear an egg, and which must be boiled and skimmed well. They stay in it for 6 days, are taken up and placed in a fresh and equally strong, cold brine, in which they stay again for 6 days.
2. They are drained in a sieve, where the brine is well pressed out, and placed in a crock. Then as much vinegar as is needed is put over the fire so that it can stand completely over the walnuts; into this is put for 100 walnuts: 4 shallots, a little piece of an onion, 3/4 pieces of ginger, 2 pieces of young peppercorn, 5 to 6 pieces of mace, 10 allspice berries, 6 spoonfuls of mustard seed, 2 spoonfuls of pepper and 2 spoonfuls of allspice.
3. When all this has cooked, it is poured off, covered with a cloth and poured cold over the walnuts, which are then bound up very well, as air would make them soft. After the passage of a year they are ready for use and keep themselves for several years. They are quite black in color, but give a good taste in sauce, especially in butter sauce for fish.
