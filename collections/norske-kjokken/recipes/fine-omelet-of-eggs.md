## Ingredients

- Eggs (6, 8 or 10)
- Half an onion or veal stock
- Parsley
- Milk
- Cornstarch
- White pepper
- Salt
- Lemon juice
- Parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. A white sauce is prepared as follows: half an onion or veal stock is cut in strips and added together with some parsley in a quart of cold water, boiled and skimmed until the meat is cooked in pieces and half the soup has boiled away. Then it is carefully strained and returned to the pot with half a cup of milk. When it boils, thicken it with a tablespoon of cornstarch, stirred in a little cold milk, season with white pepper, salt, and the juice of half a lemon. Set aside in the oven and keep warm. Six, eight or ten eggs are boiled hard, the yolks carefully removed and the whites cut into thin strips. The yolks are now piled up in the middle of a deep dish, and the strips are arranged around it so it takes the form of a bird's nest; stir in the sauce and pour it carefully over the eggs. The sauce should go halfway up the nest. Garnish with parsley.
