## Ingredients

- butter
- flour
- beef broth
- sugar
- currants
- butter
- flour
- bone broth
- currants
- redcurrant jelly

## Directions

1. The ordinary sauce is the one that forms itself in the earthenware when one rubs the roast well before it goes into the brown butter, and adds whole pieces of juniper, whole peppercorns and whole onions which are strained off. But some prefer a brown and cold sauce, and then less wine is put on the roast. One makes a little browned roux as for Madeira sauce, thins it with broth and adds sugar and currants. Then either peppercorns or mushrooms are used, only a little juniper. One also uses butter and flour as for brown sauce and thins with bone broth. When the flour is well browned and one has had soup in it once, take a couple of tablespoons of sugar which is dissolved in the rest. Then add more broth and finally redcurrant jelly. This sauce is used for grilled meat.
