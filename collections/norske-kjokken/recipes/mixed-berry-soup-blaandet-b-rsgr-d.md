## Ingredients

- various berries
- currants
- strawberries
- water
- white sugar
- cornstarch

## Directions

1. Can be made of all kinds of berries, but becomes most palatable made of currants and strawberries together. Currants must be hulled to avoid getting the flavor of the seeds.
2. The most common way is to make it like strawberry soup, but use more cornstarch.
3. The finest version is made this way: Press the berries raw through a fine cloth; when all the juice is extracted, place it over heat with an equal amount of water as juice.
4. When it is warm, stir in sufficient white sugar.
5. Cornstarch is dissolved in lukewarm water, and when the juice is near boiling, stir this in; it need only boil a couple of minutes to thicken, but stir gently so it becomes smooth.
6. Thereafter pour it either into molds or small forms from which it is turned out the next day.
7. Blanched and skinned split almonds look nice sprinkled on fruit pudding.
