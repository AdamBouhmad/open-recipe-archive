## Ingredients

- Cider (1 kettle)
- Apples (various kinds, or better if sour)
- Sugar (scant 1 pound per 1 pound apples)
- Nutmeg and cinnamon (optional, for flavoring)

## Directions

1. A kettle full of cider is boiled until one-third of it is boiled away.
2. Various kinds of apples can be used as long as they are juicy, but it does not matter as much if they are sour; peel them and cut into small pieces, removing the cores, and put as many in the kettle as the cider can stand over.
3. It cooks slowly while you stir often with a flat spoon or wooden utensil, and when the apples are quite tender, remove them with a perforated skimming spoon or sieve, taking care not to get juice with them.
4. Now put fresh apples in the cider, as many as it can hold, and treat them as before.
5. Then remove the pot from the stove, and the pulp is poured into a large jar where it is allowed to stand until the next day, under a closed lid.
6. Then it is put back in the kettle and cooked, constantly stirring, until it is like a soft paste and quite brown.
7. It can be flavored according to taste with nutmeg and cinnamon.
8. It keeps all winter in stone jars, in a dry, cold place.
