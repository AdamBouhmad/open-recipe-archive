## Ingredients

- Small, red crab apples
- Sugar
- Water
- Lemon juice

## Directions

1. Select small, red crab apples for this. The stems remain on, and they are placed in preserving liquid with enough distilled water to cover them.
2. Bring to boil and cook slowly until the skin cracks; then strain, cool and peel them; with a small knife pit them through the end where the blossom sits.
3. Weigh them; for one pound of fruit take 1.5 pounds sugar and a small cup of water.
4. Water and sugar are cooked and properly skimmed; then add the fruit and cook gently under cover until the apples are clear, red and tender.
5. Then take them out with a fork and spread on dishes to cool and become firm.
6. To the syrup add the juice of one lemon for every three pounds of fruit, and cook until it is thick and clear.
7. Fill the glasses or crocks three-quarters full with fruit, pour syrup over, and tie when cold.
