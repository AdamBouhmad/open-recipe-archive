## Ingredients

- Goose or duck
- Water
- Salt
- Butter
- Bread or flour

## Directions

1. The meat becomes more flavorful when the sauce is cooked whole, but the sauce improves by being chopped up; it is also easier to serve.
2. To enjoy both advantages, one can half-cook the goose while it is whole, take it up when it is slightly cooled and then chop it up on a board with a sharp knife and a hammer.
3. Legs and wings are cut off first, breast and thigh are separated, and each of these parts is chopped into suitable pieces.
4. These become more tender than when the goose is chopped up raw; because then the bones often stick out of the meat when it shrinks during cooking, and it retains its strength better.
5. If one has time, then everything that belongs to goose soup can be taken with it for white goose, except the head; it is placed under the other on the board.
6. The soup must boil when the chopped meat is added to it again; when it is tender, it is placed in a warm dish with the sauce.
