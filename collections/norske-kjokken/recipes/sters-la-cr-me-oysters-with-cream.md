## Ingredients

- Oysters
- Butter
- Water
- Salt
- Pepper
- Cream or milk
- Corn starch

## Directions

1. Drain the liquid from two quarts of good oysters into a saucepan and cook until the milk begins to separate out
2. Strain the oysters; put the liquid over heat, and when it is near boiling, bind it with corn starch stirred into a little cold milk
3. Add the oysters and cook for five minutes
4. Serve hot with toast
5. The sauce is made from two quarts of good oyster liquid, blended with a little hot water, a little salt and pepper and poured over the side
6. When it is near boiling, add a large cup of cream or good milk
7. When it comes to a boil, add the oysters and they boil for about five minutes
8. When they shrivel, add two spoonfuls of butter and when it is melted, remove the pan from the heat
9. Arrange quickly with oyster crackers
10. Oysters become flavorless if they are cooked too long or if too long after being cooked
