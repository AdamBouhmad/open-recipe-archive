## Ingredients

- small cucumbers
- salt
- cold water
- grape leaves
- alum (a piece the size of a pea per 2 gallons)
- vinegar (cider vinegar)
- sugar
- black pepper
- nutmeg

## Directions

1. Select small cucumbers no larger than a small finger. Pack them in a crock or stone jar with generous salt between the layers. Cover the top completely with salt and pour enough cold water over to cover the cucumbers. Place a plate over them and weight it down with a clean cloth to keep them submerged. They can stand in the brine for a whole month, stirring from the bottom every other day. After 48 hours, the cucumbers can be removed if desired.
2. To pickle: drain the brine and remove any soft cucumbers. Place the remaining cucumbers in fresh water for 24 hours, then change the water and let stand another 24 hours. Line the bottom of the pickling vessel with grape leaves and arrange the cucumbers on them, sprinkling powdered alum between the layers. Fill with cold water and cover with more grape leaves. Heat gently without boiling for 4-5 hours.
3. For the vinegar: to 1 gallon of cider vinegar add 1 cup sugar, 3 dozen black peppercorns, the same number of nutmegs, half as much allspice, and a little mace. Boil for 5 minutes. Pour the hot vinegar over the cucumbers in a stone crock. Cover with a plate. Two days later, reheat the vinegar and pour it over again. Repeat this process three times at intervals of 2, 4, and 6 days. Place a plate that fits inside the crock over the cucumbers and tie a cloth over the top. They can be eaten after 6 weeks.
