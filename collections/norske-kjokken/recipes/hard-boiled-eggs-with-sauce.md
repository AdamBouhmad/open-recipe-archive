## Ingredients

- Eggs (6, 8 or 10)
- Tongue or game bird
- Parsley
- Melted butter
- Mustard
- Sauce from game or veal stock
- Parsley
- Salt
- Pepper
- Cream

## Directions

1. Hard-boiled eggs (cooked 10 minutes) are cut in half and the yolk is removed; it is finely mixed with a little chopped tongue or game bird, a little parsley, a little melted butter and quite a little mustard. This is stirred well together and with it the egg whites are filled, which are placed close together in a deep dish with sauce, the open ends facing up. Now some sauce from game stock or veal stock is prepared; this is brought just to boiling with half a tablespoon of chopped parsley, salt, pepper and finally three spoonfuls of cream. When it boils up, it is poured over the eggs, the dish is covered, it stands for five minutes, and is then sent to the table. As soon as the hard-boiled eggs are ready, place them in cold water, so the yolk does not become dark.
