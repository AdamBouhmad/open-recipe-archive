## Ingredients

- Peaches, peeled and stoned
- For each pound of fruit: 1 pound sugar
- Kernels from a third of the stones
- Water for cooking the kernels

## Directions

1. Peel, slice and remove the stones from the fruit, using 1 pound sugar for each pound of fruit.
2. Crack a third of the stones, remove the kernels, cut into pieces and cook in barely boiling water until soft, then set aside on the stove covered.
3. In the bottom of a stone jar, place a layer of sugar, then a layer of fruit, continuing alternately until all is used, placing this on top of the pot so it will warm slowly until the sugar is melted and the fruit heated through.
4. Drain the water from the kernels and pour into the jar.
5. Let it cook gently until the fruit is tender and clear.
6. Carefully remove with a slotted spade and spread on flat plates, arranging so the pieces lie as little as possible on each other.
7. Boil the syrup until it is thick and even, and skim carefully.
8. Fill stone jars two-thirds full of fruit, and fill the remaining space with the boiled syrup.
9. Spread a paper over until it is cold, then cover with double paper moistened with brandy, then a cloth and finally with white paper.
