## Ingredients

- 1 pound carrots (dark red)
- 4 lemons
- 1 pound sugar
- vinegar (from cooking the carrots)
- additional sugar for syrup
- lemon juice

## Directions

1. Select dark red carrots best found in August. Scrape clean, cut into slices thin as straw, then cut these into equally thin strips and weigh them. For 1 pound carrots, take 4 lemons, peel very thinly so no white pith remains, and cut the peel into fine, thin strips. Place both carrots and lemon peel in cold water on the fire and bring to a boil with lid on for about half an hour, or until carrots are tender (but must not fall apart). Drain through a sieve.
2. For 1 pound carrots, take 1 pound sugar and boil it to a thin syrup with the water in which the carrots were cooked. Add the carrots with the juice of 2 lemons and boil for approximately 10 minutes. Place in a crock and let stand a couple of days, then drain so the syrup can drip off. Boil this syrup for 8-10 minutes with 3 ounces sugar and more lemon juice to taste. Return the carrots (but do not boil them further). Pack with parchment paper and tie up.
