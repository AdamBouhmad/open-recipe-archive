## Ingredients

- Crowberries
- Water
- Cornstarch
- Sugar
- Cinnamon
- Butter

## Directions

1. If the berries are unripe and very sour, let them stand an hour and draw in warm water, which is then poured off and put on the fire in fresh water.
2. For 2 quarts of berries, take 1/2 quart water.
3. Pick the berries and boil them out well and stir often from the bottom.
4. Then add the porridge thickened with sugar and cinnamon.
5. For crowberry porridge, coarse sugar can be used, but white sugar gives finer taste and more beautiful appearance.
6. One can also boil the berries without letting them be extracted first; use more water for the same portion of berries, and when the resulting porridge becomes too thin, thicken it with a couple spoonfuls of cornstarch stirred in cold water.
7. A third method is to take more berries and not let them boil quite to porridge, but to a gruel.
8. The pot is then put back on the fire, thickened with sugar and cinnamon, and finished with cornstarch.
9. Preserved crowberries in glasses can also be used for porridge, but always need thickening with cornstarch, as they lose their binding quality when preserved.
