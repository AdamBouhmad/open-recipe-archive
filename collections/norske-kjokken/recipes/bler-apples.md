## Ingredients

- 1 pound butter
- 1 quart water
- 1 pair stick cinnamon
- 1 pound white sugar
- 1 pint of the water in which apples are cooked
- A little lemon juice

## Directions

1. The apples must be good and juicy. Scrape, cut into 4 pieces, remove the core and weigh them.
2. Place 1 pound butter on fire with 1 quart water and 1 pair stick cinnamon; when it boils, add the apples, and when they have had 2 boils place them on a sieve so the water can drain.
3. Cook 1 pound white sugar into syrup with 1 pint of the water in which the apples are cooked; add these along with a little lemon juice, and cook very gently until they are clear, usually in 5 minutes; then remove from fire; the apples remain a little in it, whereupon they are taken out and placed in the crock.
4. If the juice is not thick when it is cold, it must be cooked a little more and poured cold over the apples, which are tied the next day.
5. These preserved apples can only keep a couple of months.
