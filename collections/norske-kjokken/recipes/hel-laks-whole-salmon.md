## Ingredients

- Salmon
- Water
- Salt
- Parsley
- Small shallots (optional)
- Celery (optional)
- Small roasted pepper (optional)

## Directions

1. Clean the salmon in 3 changes of water and rub lightly with a coarse cloth to remove the salt.
2. Afterwards, soak the salmon in lukewarm water.
3. Change the water in the morning with cold water, and the fish lies in it for 3 hours.
4. Then take it up, dry it and cut it into strips as in the previous method.
5. Place these in a pot with boiling water, which is poured beside a saucepan with stock prepared as for fresh salmon.
6. Beside these have the airtight glass jars with the lids lying ready, and when the salmon has cooked properly for five minutes, take the pieces up with a fork, and pass them quickly into the glass jars.
7. Fill them with the boiling stock from the other pot and seal tightly.
8. Two days after, the salmon can be taken into use and is almost as good as fresh pickled salmon.
9. It has the advantage that it can always be obtained and is relatively cheap.
