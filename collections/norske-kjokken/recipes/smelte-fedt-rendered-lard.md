## Ingredients

- All fat trimmed from the rind
- Water
- Salt

## Directions

1. Cut all the fat from the rind and cut it into small pieces afterwards.
2. Fill a pot with it and add a cupful of water to prevent the skin from burning.
3. Melt it very slowly over low heat, not too strong, stirring occasionally with small movements.
4. This continues until the meat is reduced to quite small, dry pieces.
5. These are then taken up with a spoon; a little salt is now added.
6. When the fat is clear, strain it either through a sieve or a large cloth.
7. If the latter is thickened by the fat, dip it in hot water.
8. As long as the clear fat runs through, the cloth must not be pressed, and when this is necessary, it is best to press this fat into another pot for simpler use.
9. What you mainly have to be careful about is not to burn the fat.
10. During the simmering stir constantly, and after the salt has been added, put the pot back where it is less hot.
11. It is best to bind cloths over the handles; otherwise you can use paper, and over it a cloth dipped in melted fat.
