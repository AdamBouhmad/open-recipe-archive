## Ingredients

- corn on the cob
- salt
- melted fat (not lard)

## Directions

1. Remove the leaves and boil the corn until no milky juice comes out when you prick the kernels.
2. Cut the kernels off and pack them in stone crocks in the following order: 3 pounds, one bag salt, one half gallon of dill, then one bag corn, two gallons of dill, and so on until the crock is nearly full.
3. The topmost layer should be one gallon of salt, and over the whole pour melted fat (not lard).
4. Press a bag of clean white paper that fits the crock on top, and bind the crock with paper or better with a pig's bladder.
5. Store in a cold place.
6. Soak overnight before use.
7. If the top layer should become moldy, which may happen, not all the corn is affected, and you only need to remove this layer.
