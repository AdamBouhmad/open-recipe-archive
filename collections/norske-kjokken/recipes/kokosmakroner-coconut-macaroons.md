## Ingredients

- 4 tablespoons syrup
- 2 tablespoons rubber sugar
- 1 pound butter
- 2 ounces prepared potash dissolved in a gill of rose water
- 6 pounds ice beaten quickly
- 1 ounce grated nutmeg
- 1/2 ounce ground cinnamon
- 4 ounces finely chopped, split apple pits
- 1/2 pound ground almonds

## Directions

1. Heat 4 tablespoons syrup, 2 tablespoons rubber sugar, and 1 pound butter (which is clarified) over the fire
2. When it boils, add 2 ounces prepared potash dissolved in a gill of rose water
3. When it stops foaming, lift the pot from the fire but let it stand on the back of the stove until it no longer foams; then pour it into the pan one uses for bread
4. Beat 6 pounds ice by handfuls and quickly, likewise with 1 ounce grated nutmeg, 1/2 ounce ground cinnamon, 4 ounces finely chopped, split apple pits, and 1/2 pound ground almonds (the last two parts can be omitted)
5. Knead until it releases the hands and set it in a warm place where it can stand for several days
6. From this the dough becomes stiff; when you want to bake, pieces of dough are cut, placed on a baking sheet, spread out like small cakes, and shaped, half with a pastry bag in whatever forms you like, topped with chopped almonds and baked in a hot oven for half an hour
