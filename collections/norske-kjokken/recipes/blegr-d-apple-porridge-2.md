## Ingredients

- Whole apples
- Water
- Sugar

## Directions

1. Apples can be made from whole apples that are first peeled.
2. They are boiled in water or steamed; as the apples are peeled, they are placed in water to prevent browning.
3. This water is discarded.
4. When the apples are tender, rub them through a coarse sieve so that the core remains behind.
5. Place them back on the fire to be thickened with sugar.
6. Alternatively, cut the apples into small pieces after they are peeled and have soaked in cold water, and place them on the fire with little water according to how liquid the apples are, and let them boil quite gently; they should boil under a covered lid, and you should not stir until they are fully cooked.
7. Then stir very gently, and white sugar is added according to the apples' sweetness.
8. Stir quickly during the last cooking the porridge now gets, and it is kept to be eaten cold with sugar and cream.
9. It is not correct to use any kind of thickening for apple porridge, but should you happen to make it too thin, there is nothing for it but to take a couple spoonfuls of cornstarch stirred in a little cold water, which is then added after the sugar; it should only boil a couple minutes.
10. As you do not willingly stir in this porridge before it is fully cooked, it is somewhat liable to scorch; this is avoided best when, while the porridge is clean and dry, you smear it inside with soft butter and cover it with cold water.
11. Of course you should not place it over the fire.
