## Ingredients

- Apples
- stale bread or rusk bread
- butter
- sugar
- cinnamon
- cherry or other fruit juice or water

## Directions

1. Peel and slice the apples into not too thin pieces; remove the inner cores, and it does not matter if the slices are not uniform.
2. Grate stale bread or rusk bread finely; put a little butter in a bowl, which should be a stoneware vessel, place it on the stove until the butter is melted and spread the bowl around so the butter goes all over.
3. First spread a fairly thick layer of bread in the bowl and over it lay small pieces of butter at regular intervals; over this lay a layer of apples, as close together as possible, and over this sprinkle powdered sugar according to the apples' sourness and a little cinnamon; when this is done, pour cherry or other fruit juice around; then begin again with a good layer of bread and proceed thus; the top layer must be bread and then spread well with butter all around and finally pour the juice over it again.
4. Bake it in a moderately warm oven; when the apples can be pierced through without resistance, the cake is done.
5. It can become excellent without juice; then nothing is put inside the bowl instead of juice, but when the bowl is full, a little water is poured over it.
