## Ingredients

- Large cucumbers or small melons, still whole and green
- Loaf sugar
- Ginger

## Directions

1. Large cucumbers or whole small melons, both still quite green, are cut into large pieces, about an inch in width and half as thick, are rinsed, placed on a clean cloth to dry. The outer skin should remain on, where it is not on the cucumber too thick and hard.
2. Finely pounded loaf sugar is mixed with as much vinegar as it can dissolve; it is cooked until it is skimmed and clear, with 1 teaspoon of pounded ginger for each 2 pounds. Now the pieces get a good boil in the sugar, after which they are taken up, placed in a crock, and the sauce is poured lukewarm over. Should it not cover, then more must be cooked.
3. As the pieces tend to float up, they are pressed down with a spoon or a small round object, which is placed in the crock with something heavy on it. After 3 days they are taken up, to place the top pieces at the bottom. When they have stayed thus so that one can taste that they are thoroughly penetrated by the sugar, they are taken up and placed in a dry crock or a thick wood, and sprinkled between each day with dry loaf sugar.
4. This also becomes syrup, and both this and the first can be used to fill other cucumbers. The conserve pieces are now placed in layers in a press with paper above and below. When they are pressed, a string is drawn through them and they are allowed to hang in an airy place to dry, but not in the sun. Finally they are stored in a crock with soft white sugar between each layer. The fruit must not be allowed to dry too much.
