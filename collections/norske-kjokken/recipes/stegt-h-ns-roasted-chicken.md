## Ingredients

- Chicken
- Water
- Bread crumbs
- Finely minced onion
- Powdered sage
- Melted butter
- Pepper
- Grated nutmeg
- Salt
- Eggs
- Mel (flour)
- Parsley
- Celery
- Lemon or plum
- Butter
- Flour
- Water
- Cherry liqueur

## Directions

1. As soon as the chicken is slaughtered and cold, have a pot full of boiling water ready.
2. Put the chicken in cold water for half an hour; then take hold of one hind leg and rotate it carefully around the boiling water, as long as until the hairs can be pulled off in handfuls.
3. Then take it up, dry it, and with a brush or a very coarse cloth, brush it from head to tail until the skin is quite clean.
4. Then cut it open, take out the entrails, first rinse with lukewarm water, then with thin soda water, and finally with plenty of cold salted water.
5. Dry it inside afterwards.
6. Now wrap it in a damp cloth and keep it damp until you are ready to fill it.
7. For filling, take a cupful of bread crumbs, half a cup of finely minced onion, two tablespoons of powdered sage, three tablespoons of melted butter, half a tablespoon of pepper, a quarter tablespoon of grated nutmeg, half a teaspoon of salt and two well-beaten eggs.
8. Everything except the eggs is mixed well together; then the eggs are stirred in, and the whole is filled into the chicken so it gets a natural shape.
9. Sew it up, bend the forelegs backwards, the hind legs forwards, under the body and close to it; they can be held in place with string.
10. Dry it well now and brush it with flour.
11. Put it in the oven with a small amount of fat and water, but salt it first later.
12. Three times drip it with butter and water, later with broth.
13. If you want the skin soft and tender, rub it every fifth minute or so with a cloth dipped in melted butter, after it has begun to steam.
14. An old hen will usually roast for two hours or so on that, if the oven is good and hot.
15. To serve: On the platter lay a thick bed of parsley, upon which the chicken kneels; here and there in the bed arrange stalks of the freshest celery that rise above the rest, a wreath of parsley around the neck, and in the mouth give it either a lemon or a piece of pineapple; use two sauceboats for the sauce.
16. For the sauce, brown a lump of butter; in it brown two tablespoons of flour, the sauce from the pan is stirred in after first being carefully skimmed, boiling water is added if it is too thick, and when it is taken off the heat, add the juice of half a lemon and half a small glass of cherry liqueur.
17. When serving, cut the breast off first; then slice the thighs, skin and legs are removed and the ribs are loosened.
18. The filling is taken on a small dish and served around the table.
19. With this, mushrooms or whatever else matches.
