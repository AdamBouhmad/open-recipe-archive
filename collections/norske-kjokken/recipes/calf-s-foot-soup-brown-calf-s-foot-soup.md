## Ingredients

- 8 ounces butter
- 2 large carrots
- 1 parsnip
- 3 onions
- A small bunch of parsley and celery
- A little thyme
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 tablespoon whole pepper
- 1 pint water
- 1 stop (measure) of wine
- 5 pints water (for dilution)
- Salt
- Good beef stock (for finishing)
- 1 pint wine or rum (optional)
- Beef balls (meatballs)

## Directions

1. Cut the cleaned calf's feet into pieces.
2. Melt 8 ounces of butter over the fire in a pot until well browned.
3. Add 2 large carrots, 1 parsnip, 3 onions, a small bunch of parsley and celery, a little thyme, and 3 bay leaves, all finely chopped, along with 1 tablespoon of whole pepper.
4. Brown the pepper carefully in the butter without burning it.
5. Simmer for 5 hours, then add 1 pint of water.
6. After this has cooked a while, dilute gradually with 5 pints of water.
7. Add the calf's foot pieces with salt and simmer under cover with regular stirring until the meat is so tender it can easily be removed from the bones with a spoon.
8. Remove the pieces and pour them warm into the tureen while the soup is strained and finished with good stock and optionally 1 pint of wine or rum.
9. If the upper parts of the feet are too large to be in the tureen, cut the meat from the bones and add it to the tureen.
