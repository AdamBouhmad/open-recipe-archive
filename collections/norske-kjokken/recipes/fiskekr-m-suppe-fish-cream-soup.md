## Ingredients

- 2 fillets
- 1 carrot
- 1 parsnip
- 1 onion
- Fish broth
- 1 tablespoon salt
- Half a head of endive salad leaves
- Several slices of cold cooked beets
- 1 quart milk
- 2 ounces butter
- Flour
- White pepper
- Nutmeg

## Directions

1. Prepare fillets as for filet (see that recipe), cut a carrot, a parsnip and an onion; make a fish broth
2. Place these vegetables over the stove in 1.5 quarts cold water together with the fish and 1 tablespoon salt, and bring the whole to a boil
3. Then remove the fillet carefully, lay it between two kitchen towels to cool, and let the soup cook gently
4. Cut half a head of endive salad leaves into small pieces with an instrument, such as a knife or vegetable cutter, and briefly place in boiling, well salted water, then dry and lay in cold water until needed
5. Some slices of cold cooked beets are formed like the salad and handled in the same way; keep all of this aside until it is needed
6. Rub the rest of the fillet through a strainer with a pestle
7. Place 1 quart milk over the stove to boil; place 2 ounces butter over the stove at the side and stir in an equal amount of flour
8. Gradually add this mixture with the boiling milk and with a quart of the water in which the vegetables were cooked; season the soup with a little white pepper and some nutmeg, and if needed with a little more salt; stir in the ground fish and season the soup slowly with a whipped egg
9. When the soup is ready to serve, add fish, salad and beets, and serve at once so that the contrasting colors do not fade
