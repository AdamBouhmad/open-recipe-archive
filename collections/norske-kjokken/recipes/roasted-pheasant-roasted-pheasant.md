## Ingredients

- Pheasant
- Salt
- Salted or smoked bacon
- Thin gray paper
- Fat
- Hot water
- Butter
- Flour

## Directions

1. Pheasant requires at least 25 minutes roasting time per pound and must not be red inside
2. After plucking, rub the roast very strongly with salt, which helps the skin become crisp, and cut it so the entire skin is in small strips
3. The oven must be very hot when the roast is placed in so the skin becomes crisp
4. The skin can also be cut off together with a piece of hot fat if the skin is very fat
5. Place the roast in to become warm and take it out again while it is sprinkled with heated breadcrumbs mixed with roasted chestnuts, peppers, and salt, then place it back in the oven and roast it
6. It is always better for safety to put some hot water on the roast when it is placed in
7. First wash the roast well in lukewarm water, then stuff it with salt or smoked bacon
8. Rub a strip of thin gray paper rubbed in fat and place it over
9. Place the roast on the roasting pan with a little hot fat water over
10. The fire must be very good and steady
11. 20 minutes per pound is never too much for pheasant
12. Half an hour before you think it is done, remove the paper and for most it will be necessary to test the roast
13. If a meat fork with certainty goes through to the bone at the thickest spot, the heat is applied in all the ways you can and the roast is basted very diligently
14. In the last half hour it becomes beautifully brown
15. An excellent sauce is made as follows: from the raw roast cut off all irregular or unnecessary pieces to give the roast a more rounded appearance
16. It is good if you can get the meat this way in a little hot water with 1/2 nutmeg, a few cloves, a little salt, and a small teaspoon of cayenne pepper
17. It boils slowly until it has reduced to half
18. You must skim it as soon as foam appears
19. It is then strained and when the sauce from the water is skimmed, it is blended with it
20. 1 tablespoon butter is browned in a pan, with which 1/2 tablespoon flour is blended, and all the sauce is now stirred with it
21. If it should be too thick, it can be thinned with boiling water
22. You can also make it using only butter, flour, and the sauce from the roasting pan, plus boiling water if necessary
23. Lingonberry jelly is always served with pheasant
