## Ingredients

- sago
- water
- fruit juice
- sugar

## Directions

1. The best large sago grains are particularly suited to sago pudding, in which no thickening is needed.
2. If you get the best sago that swells up, you can reckon 1 pound sago to 4-5 quarts water.
3. Juice and sugar are added according to taste.
4. The sago should cook soft in the water before juice and sugar are added, and thereafter the whole gets a good boiling.
5. Pour a spoonful onto a plate to see if it is the right thickness.
6. If it is too thick, it can be thinned with a little boiling water, but if it is too thin, there is nothing to do but smooth it with a spoonful of cornstarch stirred in cold water.
7. The best pearl sago can cook out to a clear whole, and it becomes best with juice; but if you prefer it, it can also be made with stewed fruit and lemon juice.
8. If the berries are unripe and very sour, they should stand a while and draw themselves in warm water, which is then drained off and they are placed over heat in fresh water.
9. To 2 quarts berries take ½ pint water. Hull the berries and cook them well, stirring often from the bottom, after which the pudding is finished with sugar and cinnamon.
10. For gooseberry pudding, brown sugar can be used, but white sugar gives finer taste and more beautiful appearance.
