## Ingredients

- smoked pork
- lard
- carrot
- onion
- dry flour
- bouillon or water
- ground pork
- salt
- pepper
- bay leaf
- wine

## Directions

1. A pound of smoked pork sliced into small pieces the size of a thumb is fried in one pound of lard. As soon as it is brown, add two pounds of carrot sliced in layers and as much onion, likewise sliced in layers. Two pounds of dry flour are stirred in and browned. After that a quart of bouillon is added. If you do not have that, then one quart of water and one pound of finely chopped meat. It is seasoned with a tablespoon of salt, a little pepper and a bay leaf. It is simmered slowly for one hour and skimmed properly. After that it is strained and a glass of wine can be added. This sauce can keep over a week in a cold place and is used for all kinds of dark meat, game or light fish.
