## Ingredients

- butter
- flour
- beef broth
- meat drippings
- chopped onion
- chopped anchovies
- mustard
- sauce of stewed oysters
- pepper

## Directions

1. Brown a piece of butter in a roasting pan, but not too much. Add two tablespoons of flour and rub it out with the back of a spoon. Be careful to move it from one place to another to prevent it from burning on the stove. The butter must not burn too strongly during the work. When it has acquired a nice brown color, gradually add beef broth and drippings from the roast. When this has been mixed a few times but is still too thick, add stock until it becomes the right consistency. Add chopped onion, chopped anchovies, a little undiluted mustard, sauce of stewed oysters as desired and pepper. Boil everything well together and strain it. Put the sauce back in the pan to warm it and add a little soda. If the roasting pan is only iron and not well lined with steel, do not keep the wine in the pan, but when the sauce has been made with broth, it is put into a small tinned copper pan or glazed earthenware and finished there. This sauce is good for beef roast and can also be used for goose. For goose, duck and pork more mustard is used.
