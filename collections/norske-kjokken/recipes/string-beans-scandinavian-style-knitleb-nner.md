## Ingredients

- string beans
- butter
- wheat flour
- cream or milk
- minced parsley
- sugar
- nutmeg
- salt

## Directions

1. With the edge of a knife, take hold of the stem and you can then draw a long thread from it right up to the tip of the bean. This is called stringing the beans. Afterwards cut them diagonally, very fine; cook them tender in water without salt, then drain them dry in a sieve. They should not cook more than just tender, because they must not break. Melt butter, stir in wheat flour and thin it with cream or milk and let it boil to a smooth sauce. Afterwards the string beans get a seasoning with a little minced parsley, a piece of sugar, a little nutmeg and finally salt. About one good spoonful of butter, half a teaspoon of flour and a little pint of milk is approximately suitable for a bowl full of well-drained beans.
