## Ingredients

- Fully ripe plums
- Vinegar (half a gill per pound of plums)
- Sugar (3 ounces per pound of plums)
- Cinnamon stick
- Cloves

## Directions

1. The plums must be fully ripe
2. Dry them, prick with a pointed toothpick or needle and place in a crock
3. Pour over them half a gill vinegar per pound of plums and let stand until the next day
4. Pour off the vinegar onto the plums with 3 ounces sugar per pound, place on the fire, and when it is just melted, add the plums and turn them a couple of times around so those that were at the bottom come to the top
5. When it begins to boil, pour it into the crock, leave uncovered until the next day, pour it into another crock so the juice can come to all the plums, and the next day put the whole thing back on the fire, but remove it as soon as it begins to boil
6. Two days later set it back on the fire, and now cook the plums until they are not sour by the stones, usually in 1.5 hours; then place them in a sieve so the juice can drain off
7. Cook the juice a good quarter hour, or until it becomes thick, with whole cinnamon and cloves
8. Pour it into a crock and when it is cold, place the plums in it, though not too many, so there can be sufficient juice around them
9. Seal them the next day
10. If there is more juice than can be with the plums, pour this into bottles
11. If one wishes to use these plums for roasts, it is good to add some fresh currant leaves
