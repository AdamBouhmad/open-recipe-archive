## Ingredients

- Quinces (ripe, 2 pounds)
- Water (1 pint)
- Vinegar (1 pint)
- Sugar (1.5 pounds, good, crushed)
- Additional water if needed

## Directions

1. The quinces should be very ripe, peel and cut into 4 to 6 pieces according to their size; cut away the stone, weigh them and scatter them with small pieces of butter and scraped horseradish.
2. For 2 pounds of quinces take 1 pint of water, 1 pint of vinegar and 1.5 pounds of good crushed sugar.
3. If the quinces are not completely covered, take out more of this mixture.
4. Put it together on the stove until the quinces are tender, then remove them and put in a tureen.
5. Cook the sauce until it is perfectly smooth, and pour it over the quinces which are tied up the next day.
