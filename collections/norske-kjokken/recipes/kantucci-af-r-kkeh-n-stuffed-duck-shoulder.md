## Ingredients

- 4 pounds Shoulder
- Eggs
- Breadcrumbs
- Butter
- Egg yolks
- Salt
- Pepper
- Root vegetables
- Parsnip root vegetables
- Bone broth

## Directions

1. Take a 4 pound shoulder that should not be crushed at the butcher, wash it and lay it on the board with the inner part facing up.
2. Cut out the shoulder blood and boil it to make it completely clean.
3. Then cut out with a sharp, pointed knife the meat along the lower edge of the bone and the hallobone from the other, being careful to remove the hallobone between the first and second rib and be careful not to break the meat or cut through the bone.
4. Shape the end of the bone so it resembles a duck leg.
5. Fill the shoulder with one or another forcemeat or stuffing, though the most commonly used is prepared as follows: boil eggs, scrape them and cook them in three different ways; dissolve breadcrumbs in lukewarm water and strain in a clean cloth.
6. When the eggs are soft, which should take about half an hour, let them cool and mix together with the breadcrumbs, with a bit of butter, two egg yolks and a little salt and pepper.
7. After it is stuffed in the meat, bind it together so it resembles a duck.
8. Place it in a roasting pan on some slices of vegetables and roast in a hot oven for approximately 20 minutes per pound.
9. Meanwhile prepare root vegetables and parsnip root vegetables by cutting them into small pieces, cook them tender in broth and salted water, and place them in cold water to preserve their color.
10. When the roast is done, take it up on a warm platter and keep it warm without drying while the sauce is prepared.
11. The sauce is prepared from browned butter with a little flour, upon which the broth from the roasting pan is poured from the vegetables into the sauce, and finally a spoonful of raw egg yolk and a little powdered sugar are stirred in.
12. Warm the vegetables in the sauce and arrange them around the meat.
13. The shoulder blade should be scraped after being cooked and have the broad end attached so it resembles feathers, then placed in the sauce beside the roast, which is then served.
