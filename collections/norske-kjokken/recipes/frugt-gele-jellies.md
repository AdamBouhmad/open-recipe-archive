## Ingredients

- Fruit
- Sugar

## Directions

1. Put the fruit in a stone pot; set this in a pot with lukewarm water and set a cover over it. Let it cook with a closely fitting lid until the fruit is soft. If you do not have a fruit press, which is the easiest, press the fruit through a coarse sieve very hard, only putting in a few handfuls at a time. For each pint of juice take 1 pound of sugar. The juice alone is set over heat and while it heats the sugar is divided into several parts and added gradually. Place a pot in the oven, which should not be too hot; from time to time open the door and stir the sugar to prevent it from burning. When the juice has come to a boil let it cook for 20 minutes and skim if necessary. The sugar should be so hot that you cannot put your hand in it. Should it melt around the edges it is no harm, though it is better to avoid; the melted parts will only form into lumps in the syrup and can easily be removed. Once the sugar is added to the boiling syrup, while stirring very quickly; it will melt quickly. As soon as you are quite sure the sugar is melted, remove the spoon. Then let the juice just come to a boil and immediately take the pot from the fire. Roll the glasses in hot water and fill with boiling jelly. Tie it up the next day.
