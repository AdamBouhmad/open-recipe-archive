## Ingredients

- Plums (for each pound, take 0.75 pound sugar)
- Vinegar (0.5 gill per pound)
- Water (1 tablespoon per pound)
- Cinnamon stick
- Cloves

## Directions

1. For each pound of plums take 0.75 pound sugar, which is cooked on the fire with 0.5 gill vinegar and 1 tablespoon water; when it is melted, pour it over the plums, which are first dried, pricked and placed in a crock
2. After 3-4 days, during which the plums are poured into another crock each day so the juice can come around them, place them over the fire where they cook until they are no longer sour by the stones, about 1.5 hours; then place them in a coarse sieve and from there in a crock, which is filled halfway with them
3. Cook the juice with cinnamon and cloves in a quarter hour or until it is thick
4. Pour it warm over the plums, which are sealed the next day
5. The plums can also be preserved in the same way with 0.75 pound sugar and 0.5 gill vinegar per pound, but then they have less juice
