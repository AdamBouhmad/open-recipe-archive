## Ingredients

- Best quality dark ripe cherries
- Sugar, 3 ounces per pound of berries
- Cinnamon
- Vinegar, if to be used for table

## Directions

1. Remove stems and stones using a cannelle spoon, rinse clean in several changes of warm water
2. Dry in cloths and spread in air or in an oven over coals until soft
3. Then crush gently so the kernels do not break, and remove the kernels
4. Weigh the berries and take 3 ounces sugar per pound
5. Heat sugar and a little of the juice from the berries, and if for table use, a little vinegar
6. When sugar is melted, add the berries and simmer gently until no longer tart, but not so long they become mushy
7. Pour onto a sieve
8. Heat the juice with cinnamon and simmer another quarter hour until even, then add the berries with the crushed kernels and a little orange peel to blend with the juice, but pour into jars and let cool the next day before sealing
9. Can use 0.5 pound sugar per pound berries, but must cook longer to keep and yields less juice
