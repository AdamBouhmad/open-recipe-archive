## Ingredients

- 1 pound semi-ripe grapes
- half pound of the same sort of grapes
- 1 pound brown sugar

## Directions

1. Lay the semi-ripe grapes in a crock; crush half a pound of the same sort of grapes; strain the juice and mix with 1 pound brown sugar and pour over the whole berries, which stand covered for 2 days, then place the whole over fire and cook until the grapes have changed color, whereupon they are taken out and placed in the crock.
2. Cook the juice a little longer, skim and pour hot over the grapes, which are covered with a cloth and left for another 2 days.
3. Then place the whole over fire again; when it begins to cook, take out the grapes and place in the crock; cook the juice well, skim and pour hot over the grapes, which are covered with a cloth and tied the next day.
4. After they have stood for 8 days, the juice may have become thin; it is then cooked with an addition of 2 ounces sugar per pound and poured cold over the grapes, which are covered with paper dipped in brandy and tied.
5. They can also be preserved in the same way with powdered sugar, and are then good for cakes, sweet soup and sauces.
