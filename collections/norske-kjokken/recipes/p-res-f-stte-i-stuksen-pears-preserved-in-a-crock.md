## Ingredients

- Ripe pears of all kinds
- White sugar (1 pound sugar per pound of pears)
- Water (nearly 1 pint brandy)
- Cinnamon stick
- Lemon or lemon juice
- Ground and pounded ginger
- Paper soaked in brandy
- Paper or cloth that fits well to the sides
- Flour and water

## Directions

1. Peel the pears, remove the blossom and a piece of the stem which is scraped off; if the pears are large, cut them over
2. Remove the pear's core and stem, weigh them, and for each pound of pears take 1 pound white sugar, with which layer them in a crock
3. When the crock is filled, pour nearly 1 pint brandy on top
4. Place a board or cloth that fits well to the sides over it, and seal around the edges with a paste of flour and water, stirred to a thick paste, so the steam cannot escape
5. Place the crock in an oven that is not too warm, as needed for roasting a roast, and leave it standing until a little juice comes out, then remove it, check if the seal is broken and set it back in
6. Repeat this for 1 day until the pears are ready
7. The crock remains sealed until use; later seal it well, if possible with a bladder, so the brandy does not lose strength
8. Such preserved fruit keeps very well
