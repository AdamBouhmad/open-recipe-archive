## Ingredients

- Quinces
- Sugar (1 pound sugar per 1 pound quinces)
- Water (1 pint)
- Brandy for paper wrapping

## Directions

1. Put the quinces in boiling water and cook until they begin to soften.
2. Remove them, peel them, and cut them according to their size into 4 or 8 pieces.
3. Remove the core and all the stone around them, and weigh them.
4. For 1 pound of quinces take 1 pound of sugar, which is put on the stove with 1 pint of water; when the first scum is removed, put in the quinces, stir well, cook evenly for half an hour or longer, if needed, so that they can become perfectly tender and the syrup even.
5. Store them, cover a cloth over and the next day they are covered with paper, dipped in brandy, and tied.
