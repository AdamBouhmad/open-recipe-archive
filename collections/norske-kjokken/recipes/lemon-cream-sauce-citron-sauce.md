## Ingredients

- cream
- salt
- butter
- flour
- bouillon
- lemon

## Directions

1. One pint of cream is boiled very gently with a little salt, kept warm and the juice of half a lemon. When it has boiled a little, a roux is made of two to eight ounces of butter (depending on how thick the cream is) mixed with a tablespoon of flour. This is stirred slowly until it becomes thick and then finished with bouillon until the sauce has acquired the right consistency. When it is dished up, the juice of half a lemon is pressed into it. Good milk can be used instead of cream but then more butter is used. Instead of lemon juice and juice, there can be so much strong egg yolk or good soda cooked into the sauce that it gets a reddish color.
