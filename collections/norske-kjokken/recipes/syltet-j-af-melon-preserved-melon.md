## Ingredients

- A melon that is not fully ripe
- For each pound of melon: 1 pound white sugar
- A third pint water

## Directions

1. Peel the melon, remove the seeds, and cut into finger-length pieces, approximately an inch wide and half as thick.
2. Weigh the pieces, place in a barrel that is so tight it can hold an egg floating in it, and let stand for 4 days.
3. Remove the pieces and place in cold water for 3 days, renewing the water each day.
4. Then place them on the stove in cold water and cook covered until so tender that a straw can be pierced through them.
5. Remove them and dry well in a cloth.
6. For each pound of melon, take 1 pound white sugar, which is boiled into a syrup in a third pint of water.
7. Add the melon pieces, boil for 5 to 6 minutes, pour the whole into a jar and let stand covered for 4 days.
8. Pour off the syrup, boil it for a few minutes and pour it warm over the melon, which stands for three more days.
9. Drain into a sieve, letting it stand some time for the juice to run off, then place on a plate.
10. Boil the syrup again until it is thick enough to pour over the melon.
11. Turn the melon around in it and let stand a few days in an airy place to dry.
12. Store in a dry place.
