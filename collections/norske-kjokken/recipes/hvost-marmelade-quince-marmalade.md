## Ingredients

- Quince peelings (1 pound)
- Water (1 pint)
- Quinces (1 pound)
- Sugar (1 pound sugar per 1 pound quinces)
- Currant juice (optional, for color)

## Directions

1. Dry the quinces, cut the peelings and weigh them.
2. For 1 pound of peelings take 1 pint of water, which they cook very gently for one hour, after which they are strained.
3. Slice the quinces in 4 to 8 pieces, remove the core and all the stone, weigh them.
4. For 1 pound of quinces take 1 pound of sugar, which is put in the water that has been strained from the peelings; when it is melted, put the quinces in and cook with the lid on, over a good and even fire, only not quickly, and with constant stirring, until they are tender.
5. This can take a couple of hours.
6. During this slow cooking, under a closed lid, the quinces often become red; if not, you can add currant juice.
7. When reduced to a soft pulp, stir in almost 1 pound of sugar for each pound of fruit; cook for another 10 minutes, stirring.
8. Remove from heat, and when cold, put into small jars or glasses with brandy paper over.
