## Ingredients

- 4 or 5 scrags of mutton and shank bones
- 1 teaspoonful of salt
- 1 dozen peppercorns
- 1 onion
- 1 carrot
- rice or pearl barley

## Directions

1. Carefully trim the scrags of mutton, remove the pith from the bones, and wipe with a damp cloth
2. Break these and the shank bones into very small pieces
3. Put them into an enamelled saucepan, well covered with cold water
4. Add a teaspoonful of salt
5. Stand on the stove, and when it boils up remove the scum very carefully
6. Add 1 dozen peppercorns, and an onion and carrot, if vegetables are allowed the patient
7. Boil steadily for eight or nine hours; the liquor should then be reduced to one quart
8. Strain off, and, if possible, let it stand till quite cold; it should then be in a jelly, and can be made hot as required
9. When serving this to a convalescent a spoonful of rice or pearl barley well washed in cold water and boiled in either stock or milk may be added
