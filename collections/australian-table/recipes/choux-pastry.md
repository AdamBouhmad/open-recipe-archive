## Ingredients

- a pound of fresh butter
- two ounces of sugar
- a little salt
- the peel of a lemon
- very dry flour
- five or six eggs

## Directions

1. Let the butter, sugar, salt, and lemon peel boil until the whole is properly melted
2. Take some very dry flour and pass through a sieve
3. Take the lemon-peel out with a ladle, and throw a handful of flour into the preparation while boiling, taking care not to put more flour than the liquor can soak up
4. Stir with a wooden spoon until the paste can be easily detached from the stewpan, and then take it off the fire
5. Break an egg into this paste, and mix it well; then break a second, which also mix
6. Do not put more eggs than the paste can absorb, but be careful not to make this preparation too liquid
7. Form them en choux, in the shape of a ball, an inch in circumference
8. Dress the choux on a baking-sheet, at an inch distance from each other
9. Brush them over with egg-wash to which has been added a little milk
10. Put them into an oven moderately hot, but do not open the oven till they are quite baked
11. Dry them, and sometimes you may glaze them, or not
12. To detach them from the baking-sheet, apply the sharp end of a knife, and take them off gently
13. Make a small opening in the side of the choux, into which put with a tea-spoon such sweetmeats as you think proper, and serve them dished en buisson
