## Ingredients

- two parts of fine flour of pale barley malt
- one part of wheat flour
- fifty pounds of the mixture
- one hundred quarts of cold water
- one quart of good, fresh beer-yeast

## Directions

1. Mix two parts of fine flour of pale barley malt with one part of wheat flour
2. Stir fifty pounds of this mixture gradually into one hundred quarts of cold water, with a wooden spatula, till it forms a smooth pap
3. Put this pap into a copper over a slow fire
4. Stir it well till the temperature rise to fully 150° to 160°, when a partial formation of sugar will take place, but this sweetening must not be pushed too far
5. Turn out the paste into a flat cooler, and stir it from time to time
6. As soon as the wort has fallen to 59° Fahr., transfer it to a tub, and add for every fifty quarts of it one quart of good, fresh beer-yeast, which will throw the wort into brisk fermentation in the course of twelve hours
7. Occasionally stir the preparation
