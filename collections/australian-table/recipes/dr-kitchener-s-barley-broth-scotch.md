## Ingredients

- three-quarters of a pound of Scotch barley
- a shin or leg of beef of about eight pounds' weight, or the same weight of mutton
- cold water
- two onions
- two heads of celery
- a large turnip
- pepper and salt
- a glass of port wine

## Directions

1. Wash three-quarters of a pound of Scotch barley in a little cold water.
2. Put it into a soup-pot, or digester, with a shin or leg of beef of about eight pounds' weight, or the same weight of mutton, sawn in pieces.
3. Cover it well with cold water.
4. Set it on the fire; when it boils, skim it, and put in two onions.
5. Let it simmer for about a couple of hours.
6. Put in two heads of celery, and a large turnip cut into pieces.
7. Let it simmer a short time longer, and take out the meat to serve separate with the broth.
8. When the meat is taken out, season with pepper and salt, and a glass of port wine may be added or not.
9. The meat can be served with caper sauce or minced gherkins or walnuts.
