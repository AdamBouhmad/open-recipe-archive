## Ingredients

- Twelve bushels of malt to the hogshead for beer, and eight for ale
- Three-quarters of a pound of hops to a bushel for strong beer, or half a pound for ale
- Two quarts of yeast
- A pound and a half of hops dried before the fire

## Directions

1. Pour the whole quantity hot, but not boiling, on at once, and let it infuse three hours, being covered
2. Mash it in the first half-hour, and let it stand the remainder of the time
3. Run it on the hops, previously infused in water
4. Boil them with the wort two hours from the time it begins to boil
5. Cool a pailful to add two quarts of yeast to, which will prepare it for putting to the rest when ready next day; but if possible, put them together the same night
6. Tun as usual
7. Cover the bung-hole with paper when the beer has done working
8. When it is to be stopped, have ready a pound and a half of hops dried before the fire, put them into the bung-hole, and fasten it up
9. After the beer or ale has run from the grains, you can make table-beer of a similar quantity
