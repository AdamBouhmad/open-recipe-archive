## Ingredients

- Potatoes
- Eighty quarts of water per scheffel of potatoes
- Crushed malt, twelve scheffels
- Ninety gallons of cold water (English)
- Six barrels of cold water (English)
- Hops, due proportion

## Directions

1. Wash the potatoes well
2. Rub down the potatoes by a grating cylinder into a hopper
3. Crush and grind them into pulp
4. Put eighty quarts of water with each scheffel of potatoes into a copper and make to boil
5. Work twelve scheffels of crushed malt in the mash-tub with ninety gallons of cold water to a thick pap
6. Suddenly introduce six barrels of cold water with constant stirring and let stand an hour at rest
7. When the potatoes have been boiled to a fine starch paste, speedily ladle the whole malt mash, thin and thick, into the copper
8. Stir the mixture for an hour, keeping the temperature at from 144° to 156°F. all the time, so that the diastase of the malt may convert the starch into sugar and dextrine
9. When the white pasty liquid becomes transparent and thin, raise the fire to make the mash boil and keep it at its heat for ten minutes
10. Withdraw the fire and transfer the contents of the copper into the mash, work well, and let settle for half an hour
11. During this time, wash out the copper and quickly charge it once more with boiling water
12. Draw off the clear wort from the tun as usual and boil as soon as possible with the due proportion of hops
13. Add boiling water in any desired quantity to the drained mash for the second mashing
