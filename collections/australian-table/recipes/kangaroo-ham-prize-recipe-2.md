## Ingredients

- quarter of a pound of fine Liverpool salt
- three ounces of coarse brown sugar
- one ounce of pounded allspice
- cold water
- mace
- salt
- pepper
- forcemeat balls
- smoked bacon
- boiled pork or ham

## Directions

1. Mix salt, brown sugar, and allspice well together
2. Rub the whole well on and down the leg bone
3. Let them lay in the pickle for a fortnight, rubbing them every other day
4. If not required for immediate use they may be hung up to dry
5. When required for the table, chop off the bone close to the meaty part
6. Cut slightly the sinew over the knee-joint, and bind it round
7. Steep in cold water for twelve hours or more before using
8. Place it in a saucepan of cold water on a slow fire
9. Let it boil only ten minutes, drawing it aside until the water is nearly cool
10. When dished, pour over a rich brown gravy, flavoured with mace, salt, and pepper
11. Garnish with forcemeat balls, adding to the usual ingredients a little smoked bacon very finely chopped
12. A slice of boiled pork or ham is an agreeable addition
