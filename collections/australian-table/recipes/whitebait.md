## Ingredients

- whitebait
- flour
- hot lard
- lemon-juice
- cayenne pepper

## Directions

1. The fish should be dressed within an hour after being caught, or they are apt to cling together.
2. They are kept in water, from which, they are taken by a skimmer as required.
3. They are then thrown upon a layer of flour contained in a large napkin, in which they are shaken until completely enveloped in flour.
4. They are then put into a colander, and all the superfluous flour is removed by sifting.
5. The fish are next thrown into hot lard, contained in a copper cauldron, or stewpan, placed over a charcoal fire.
6. In about two minutes they are removed by a tin skimmer, thrown into a colander to drain, and served up instantly, by placing them on a fish-drainer, in a dish.
7. At table, lemon-juice is squeezed on them, and they are seasoned with cayenne pepper.
