## Ingredients

- hind-quarters of kangaroo
- tail
- legs
- small onion
- peppercorns
- bay leaves
- grated bread
- shred suet
- thyme
- parsley
- onion
- sausage-meat or ham or bacon
- pepper
- salt
- eggs
- caul of sweet mutton suet
- mushroom ketchup
- flour
- butter
- currant jelly
- ham or bacon

## Directions

1. Take the hind-quarters, above the kidney.
2. Cut off the tail, also the legs, at the first joint from the back.
3. Discard the feet and cut up the remainder of the legs with the tail to stew for gravy.
4. Add to the gravy meat a small onion sliced, a few peppercorns, and one or two bay leaves.
5. Let it stew till all goodness is out of the meat, then strain.
6. Make a good stuffing as for turkey, with a large cupful of grated bread, half as much shred suet, some thyme, parsley, and a small portion of onion, chopped very fine.
7. Add either half a pound of sausage-meat, or a little finely-shred ham or bacon.
8. Season with pepper and salt, and mix well with one or two eggs.
9. Put as much stuffing as it will hold into the aperture of the body and make the rest into balls about the size of pullets' eggs.
10. You may lard the kangaroo, but it is very good without, if covered with a caul of sweet mutton suet while baking.
11. Bake with long cooking, letting it stay in the oven a full half hour after you think it done.
12. Serve with some of the gravy having added to it some mushroom ketchup, and a little flour and butter.
13. Have currant jelly to eat with it or some persons prefer ham or bacon instead.
14. Hashed with the remaining gravy, the kangaroo is often nicer than it was the first day.
