## Ingredients

- hind-quarters of kangaroo
- bones, skin, tail, or part of fore-quarter
- water
- small onion
- pound of nice fat bacon
- mushroom ketchup
- flour
- salt
- pepper
- sippets of toasted bread
- forcemeat

## Directions

1. Cut the meat off the hind-quarters and take away the skin.
2. Mince, pound, or grind the meat in a sausage mill, not set finely.
3. Set it aside until next day.
4. Prepare the gravy by slowly simmering the bones, skin, tail, or part of the fore-quarter, well bruised and hacked up, with three pints of water and a small onion till reduced to little more than one pint.
5. Do this the day before you want to serve the steamer and strain off the gravy to be cold.
6. Next day take a pound (or more, if liked) of nice fat bacon, free from rust, cut it into dice.
7. Add it to the minced meat and stir all well into the cold gravy.
8. Let the whole warm gradually and simmer slowly until cooked.
9. Season to your palate, being careful not to add much salt, if any, as the bacon salts it.
10. A spoonful or two of mushroom ketchup and a dredge of flour will improve the flavour and consistency.
11. Serve with sippets of toasted bread and if you would have a steamer superlative, garnish round with nicely-fried balls of forcemeat.
