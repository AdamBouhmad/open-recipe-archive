## Ingredients

- 2 eggs (yolks only)
- a salt-spoonful of ordinary dry mustard
- about a tablespoonful of good vinegar
- quite a teacupful of olive oil
- pepper
- salt

## Directions

1. Break each egg by knocking its side midway between the two ends against the rim of the bowl.
2. Allow the greater part of the white of the egg to escape into a small vessel, as it is not required for the mayonnaise.
3. With the yolk in one half of the shell, pour away all the white remaining in the other half.
4. Dextrously turn the yolk into the latter emptied shell and then get rid of the white left in the half previously occupied by the yolk.
5. Slip the two yolks into the bowl and break up with a few stirs of the egg-whisk.
6. Place within the bowl about a salt-spoonful of the ordinary dry mustard and beat well up in a second or two.
7. Add about a tablespoonful of good vinegar, the whisk going vigorously to work, and thus blend well together egg yolk, dry mustard, and vinegar.
8. With the left hand, tilt up the salad bowl and hold a bottle of salad oil at the same time.
9. Keep the bottle inverted over the contents of the bowl in such a way as to allow only a drop or so of the oil to escape at a time.
10. As the oil drops, keep unceasingly plying the mixture with the whisk.
11. Continue adding oil, faster and faster, until the amber-looking sauce is ready.
12. After stirring in a sufficient quantity of pepper and salt, the mayonnaise is complete.
