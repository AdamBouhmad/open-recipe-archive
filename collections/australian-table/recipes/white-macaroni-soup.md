## Ingredients

- 1½ oz. Macaroni
- 1 pint Milk
- 1 oz. Butter
- 3 pints Bone Stock
- Vegetables
- Flour
- 1 tablespoonful Cornflour
- Salt
- White Pepper
- Lemon Juice

## Directions

1. The stock made from veal or mutton bones is the best for this soup, as it must be white.
2. If not plenty of vegetables have been boiled in it, boil an onion or leek, a slice of turnip, and a small piece of celery stalk in the stock for twenty minutes, and strain them out.
3. Put the butter into a stewpan, and when it is melted stir in a tablespoonful of cornflour, pour over the milk and stock, and stir until it boils.
4. Boil the macaroni in salt and water for twenty minutes, strain off the water, and cut it into pieces about 1 inch long; put these into the soup, and simmer for ten minutes.
5. Just before serving, flavour with salt, a dust of white pepper, and a few drops of lemon juice.
