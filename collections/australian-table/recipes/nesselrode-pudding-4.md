## Ingredients

- about forty chestnuts
- a little syrup
- a pint of syrup made from a pound of sugar clarified
- vanilla
- a pint of cream
- the yolks of a dozen eggs
- a glass of maraschino
- an ounce of citron
- two ounces of currants
- two ounces of stewed raisins

## Directions

1. Blanch about forty chestnuts in boiling water, remove the husks and pound them in a mortar, adding a little syrup
2. rub them through a fine sieve, and mix in a basin, with a pint of syrup made from a pound of sugar clarified, and flavoured with vanilla, a pint of cream, and the yolks of a dozen eggs
3. Set this mixture over the fire, and keep stirring it, and just as it begins to boil take it off and pass it through a tamis
4. When it is cold put it into a freezing-pot, adding a glass of maraschino, and let the mixture set
5. then add an ounce of citron, two ounces of currants, and two ounces of stewed raisins
