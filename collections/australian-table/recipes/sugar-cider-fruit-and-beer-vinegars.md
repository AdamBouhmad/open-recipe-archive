## Ingredients

- one pound and a quarter of sugar
- every gallon of water
- a quarter of a pint of good yeast
- one ounce of cream of tartar
- one ounce of crushed raisins
- juices of currants, gooseberries, and other indigenous fruit

## Directions

1. Add to a syrup consisting of one pound and a quarter of sugar for every gallon of water, a quarter of a pint of good yeast
2. Maintain the liquor at a heat of from 75° to 80° F.
3. Allow acidification to proceed for two or three days
4. Rack off from the sediment into the ripening cask
5. Mix with one ounce of cream of tartar and one ounce of crushed raisins
6. When completely freed from the sweet taste, draw off into bottles and closely cork up
7. The juices of currants, gooseberries, and many other indigenous fruit may be acetified either alone or in combination with syrup
