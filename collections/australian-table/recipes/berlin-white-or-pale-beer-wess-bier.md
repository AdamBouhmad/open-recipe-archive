## Ingredients

- One part barley malt
- Five parts wheat malt
- Water at 95°F., thirty quarts per scheffel of malt
- Seventy quarts of boiling water
- Half a pound of Altmark hops per bushel of malt

## Directions

1. Mingle the barley malt and wheat malt together, then moisten and crush between rollers
2. Work up the mixture with water at 95°F., in the proportion of thirty quarts per scheffel of malt
3. Add seventy quarts of boiling water to the pasty mixture
4. Mash the whole in the tun
5. Leave it a little to settle
6. Draw off a portion of the thin liquor by the tap and transfer to the copper
7. Add to it a decoction of half a pound of Altmark hops, separately prepared, for each bushel of malt
8. After half an hour's boiling, turn the hopped wort back with the hops into the mash-tun, of which the temperature should now be 162°F., but not more
9. In half an hour draw the wort from off the grains and pump into the cooler
10. Afterwards mash the grains with from forty to fifty quarts of boiling water per scheffel of malt
11. Draw off this infusion and add to the former malt
12. Set the whole mixture at 66°F. with a due proportion of top yeast or ordinary barm
13. Ferment very moderately
