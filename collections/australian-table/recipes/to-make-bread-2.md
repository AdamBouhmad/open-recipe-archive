## Ingredients

- one bushel of flour
- one pint of yeast
- milk-warm water
- six ounces of salt
- milk-warm water or milk

## Directions

1. Put a bushel of flour into a kneading trough
2. Mix a pint of yeast thoroughly with as much milk-warm water
3. Make a deep hole in the middle of the flour, and pour the yeast and water into it
4. Take a spoon and work it round the edges of this body of moisture, so as to bring into it by degrees flour enough to make a thin batter
5. Stir well for a minute or two
6. Throw a handful of flour over the surface of this batter, and cover the whole with a cloth thickly folded to keep it warm
7. Set it by the fire, regulating the distance by the state of the weather and the season of the year
8. When the batter has risen enough to make cracks in the flour, form the whole mass into dough by strewing six ounces of salt over the heap
9. Beginning round the hole containing the batter, work the flour into it, pouring in milk-warm water or milk as it is wanted
10. When the whole mass is moistened, knead it well
11. Mould the loaves, let them rise for twenty minutes
12. Put them into an oven that has been previously heated
13. Bake for about an hour for a four pound loaf, fifty minutes for a three pound, and so on in proportion
