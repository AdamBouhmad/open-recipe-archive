## Ingredients

- one ounce of lean bacon or ham
- a pound and a half of lean gravy beef
- a cow-heel
- the inner rind of a carrot
- a sprig of lemon-thyme
- winter-savory
- three times the quantity of parsley
- a few green leaves of sweet basil
- two shallots
- a large onion
- four cloves
- eighteen corns of allspice
- eighteen corns of black pepper
- a quarter of a pint of cold water
- boiling water
- two or three table-spoonfuls of flour
- the juice of a lemon
- a table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup
- a tea-spoonful of salt
- half a tea-spoonful of ground black pepper
- a little nutmeg
- a glass of sherry or Madeira

## Directions

1. Line the bottom of a stew pan that will hold five pints with an ounce of lean bacon or ham, a pound and a half of lean gravy beef, a cow-heel, the inner rind of a carrot, a sprig of lemon-thyme, winter-savory, three times the quantity of parsley, a few green leaves of sweet basil, and two shallots
2. Put in a large onion with four cloves stuck in it, eighteen corns of allspice, the same of black pepper
3. Pour on these a quarter of a pint of cold water, cover the stewpan, and set it on a slow fire to boil gently for a quarter of an hour
4. Take off the cover, and watch it; and when it has got a good brown colour, fill up the stewpan with boiling water, and let it simmer gently for two hours
5. If you wish to have the full benefit of the meat, only stew it till it is just tender, cut it into mouthfuls, and put it into the soup
6. Pour to two or three table-spoonfuls of flour a ladleful of the gravy, and stir it quickly till it is well mixed
7. Pour it back into the stewpan where the gravy is, and let it simmer gently for half an hour longer
8. Skim it, and then strain it through a tamis into the stewpan
9. Cut the cow-heel into pieces about an inch square
10. Squeeze through a sieve the juice of a lemon, a table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup, a tea-spoonful of salt, half a tea-spoonful of ground black pepper, a little nutmeg, and a glass of sherry or Madeira
11. Let it simmer for five minutes
12. Forcemeat or egg balls may be added
