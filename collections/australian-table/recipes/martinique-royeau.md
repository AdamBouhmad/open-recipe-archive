## Ingredients

- Preserved quarns and their syrup, or jelly of that fruit
- Oil of sweet almonds
- Sweet almonds, beaten fine
- Bitter almonds
- Preserved ginger and its syrup
- Cinnamon and cloves, bruised
- Nutmeg and pimento
- Jamaica ginger
- Candied lemon and citron, of each
- White sugar-candy, powdered
- Proof spirit of wine
- Orange-flower water
- Brandy

## Directions

1. Put into a stone jar the preserved quarns and their syrup, oil of sweet almonds, sweet almonds beaten fine, bitter almonds, preserved ginger and its syrup, cinnamon and cloves bruised, nutmeg and pimento, Jamaica ginger, candied lemon and citron, white sugar-candy powdered, and proof spirit of wine
2. Boil the oil with a little brandy, and mix it with the almonds, when beaten to a paste with orange-flower water
3. Stop up the jar securely, and let it remain in a warm room, or the sun, shaking it often, for a fortnight
4. Keep it in the tun for twelve months
5. Strain it, and filter repeatedly until it is as clear as spring water
6. Rinse phials or half-pint bottles with any white wine, drain them and fill
7. Cork and seal well
8. In six months it will be fit for use, but will improve greatly by age
