## Ingredients

- tender part of kangaroo
- smoked bacon (fat)
- marjoram
- pepper
- salt
- white of egg
- mace
- rich brown gravy
- forcemeat balls

## Directions

1. Take the most tender part of the kangaroo, being careful to remove all the sinews.
2. Chop it very fine, about the same quantity of smoked bacon (fat).
3. Season with finely-powdered marjoram, pepper, and a very little salt.
4. Let it steam or stew for two hours.
5. Pack or press tight in open-mouthed glass bottles.
6. Seal the bung down and wash the outside of the bottles well with white of egg, beaten.
7. Preserved in this way it will keep good for twelve months or more.
8. When needed for use, put the vessel containing the preserve into a saucepan of cold water and allow to boil for fifteen minutes (if a large bottle).
9. When dished, pour a little rich brown gravy over it, flavoured with mace, salt, and pepper.
10. Garnish with forcemeat balls.
11. If required for immediate use, half an hour will cook it sufficiently and no gravy will be necessary.
12. Forcemeat balls without bacon will be found a great improvement.
