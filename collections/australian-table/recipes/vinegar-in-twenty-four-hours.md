## Ingredients

- cider, beer, or wine
- beech shavings
- vinegar
- cotton wicking

## Directions

1. Take a clean flour barrel, and bore auger holes all around the sides and in the bottom
2. Set it over a tub or open cask, and fill it lightly with beech shavings which have been soaked in vinegar
3. On the top of this barrel, which is open, lay two strips of wood, and rest on these a pail filled with cider, beer, or the like
4. Procure fifteen lengths of cotton wicking, about thirty inches long, which, after dipping in the liquid, arrange round the side of the pail at regular intervals, so that one end of each will be hanging in the cider and the other hanging down outside, and below the bottom of the pail
5. By means of these wicks the pail will be gradually emptied of its contents, which, trickling over the shavings, will be exposed to the air, absorb oxygen, and finally be received in the tub beneath
6. By returning the liquor into the pail above, and suffering this trickling process to be repeated two or three times, a splendid vinegar will be obtained
7. Perform the process in a room heated to 80° or higher
8. Cover the whole apparatus with a tight hogshead, without a head, turned upside down
