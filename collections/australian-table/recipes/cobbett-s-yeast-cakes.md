## Ingredients

- seven pounds of Indian corn meal
- three and a half pounds of rye flour
- three ounces of hops
- one gallon of boiling water

## Directions

1. Separate the hops by the hand, strew and boil them in the water for half an hour
2. Strain the liquor into an earthen vessel
3. Put in the rye while hot, stirring quickly as the fermentation commences
4. Next day, when it is working, put in the Indian meal, stirring it well
5. Knead it well, and roll it out as you would a pie-crust, to the thickness of the third of an inch
6. Cut it into cakes with a tumbler
7. Place these cakes in the sun to dry
8. Turn them every day until they are hard
9. Keep them in a dry place
10. When you bake, take a couple of these cakes, break them and put them into warm water over-night
11. Let the vessel containing them stand near the fire; they will dissolve
12. Use the liquid for setting your sponge, just as you would the yeast of beer
