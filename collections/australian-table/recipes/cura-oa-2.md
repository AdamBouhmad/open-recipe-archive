## Ingredients

- six ounces of thin-cut Seville oranges
- a pint of genuine whisky
- clarified syrup
- a drachm of powdered alum
- a drachm of carbonate of potash

## Directions

1. Put six ounces of thin-cut Seville oranges into a quart bottle, with a pint of genuine whisky
2. Cork it tightly, and let the rind infuse for ten or twelve days
3. Take out the peel, and fill up the bottle with clarified syrup
4. Shake it well, and let it remain for three days
5. Pour a tea-spoonful of the liqueur into a mortar, and mix with it a drachm of powdered alum, and the same quantity of carbonate of potash
6. Pour this into the bottle, shake it well, and the curaçoa will be bright and well-flavoured
