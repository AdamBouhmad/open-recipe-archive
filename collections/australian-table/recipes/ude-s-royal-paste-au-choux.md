## Ingredients

- half a pint of water
- a quarter of a pound of fresh butter
- two ounces of sugar
- a little salt
- the peel of a lemon
- very dry flour
- eggs

## Directions

1. Take a stewpan large enough to hold four quarts of water
2. Pour half a pint of water in it, with a quarter of a pound of fresh butter, two ounces of sugar, a little salt, and the peel of a lemon
3. Let them boil until the whole is properly melted
4. Take some very dry flour and pass through a sieve
5. Take the lemon-peel out with a ladle
6. Throw a handful of flour into the preparation while boiling, taking care not to put more flour than the liquor can soak up
7. Stir with a wooden spoon until the paste can be easily detached from the stewpan
8. Take it off the fire
9. Break an egg into this paste and mix it well
10. Break a second egg and mix it in
11. Do not put more eggs than the paste can absorb
