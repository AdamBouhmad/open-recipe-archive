## Ingredients

- One pound lean pork
- A dessertspoonful of lard, olive oil, butter or peanut oil
- One small cupful sliced onions
- One cupful hot chicken stock or water
- Six Chinese mushrooms, soaked or steamed with stems removed
- Six Chinese potatoes, peeled and sliced thin
- Two cupfuls celery, sliced crosswise
- One tablespoon Seasoning Sauce
- Two tablespoons Flavoring Sauce
- A heaping teaspoon rice flour or cornstarch
- Four drops Sesamun Oil

## Directions

1. Cut one pound of lean pork in small pieces and braise in the kettle with a dessertspoonful of lard, olive oil, butter or peanut oil, the latter being preferred.
2. Cover tightly, removing the cover and stirring occasionally till cooked through and lightly browned.
3. Watch carefully to prevent burning.
4. Add one small cupful sliced onions and continue to cook for three or four minutes.
5. Pour a cupful of hot chicken stock or water, carefully around the sides of the kettle, taking great care not to pour over the top of the food.
6. Cut the Chinese mushrooms fine and the Chinese potatoes into thin slices.
7. Add the mushrooms, potatoes, and celery immediately to the pork and onions.
8. Put in one tablespoon of the Seasoning Sauce and mix well with the ingredients and cover the kettle tightly.
9. Let cook for twelve or fifteen minutes slowly, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking to bottom of kettle and burning.
10. Add water or stock, as necessary, to prevent the mixture from becoming dry.
11. Mix two tablespoons of the Flavoring Sauce with a heaping teaspoon of rice flour or cornstarch and add to the ingredients in the kettle, allowing the gravy to thicken slowly.
12. Add four drops of Sesamun Oil, stir carefully and remove from the fire.
13. Serve with rice in separate bowls and with tea.
