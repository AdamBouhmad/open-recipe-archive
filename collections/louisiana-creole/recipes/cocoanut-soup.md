## Ingredients

- 8 calves' feet
- 6 or 7 blades of mace
- yellow rind of a lemon
- 1 gallon of water
- half a pound of grated cocoanut
- 1 pint of cream or rich milk
- 3 ounces of fresh butter
- arrowroot or rice flour
- 1 small nutmeg

## Directions

1. Put the calves' feet into a soup kettle with mace and grated lemon rind
2. Pour on a gallon of water, cover the kettle and let it boil very slowly, skimming it well till the flesh is reduced to rags and has dropped entirely from the bones
3. Strain it into a broad white-ware pan and set it away to get cool
4. When it has congealed, scrape off the fat and sediment, cut up the cake of jelly and put it into a clean porcelain kettle
5. Mix the grated cocoanut with a pint of cream or rich milk and three ounces of butter divided into three parts, each bit rolled in arrowroot or rice flour
6. Mix it gradually with the cocoanut and add it to the calves' feet stock in the kettle, seasoned with a small nutmeg grated
7. Set it over the fire and boil it slowly about a quarter of an hour, stirring it well
8. Put it into a tureen and serve it up with French rolls or light milk biscuits and powdered sugar
