## Ingredients

- one pound and a half flour
- half ounce yeast
- warm water
- milk or tepid water
- half pound reserved flour
- salt
- six eggs
- sugar
- butter
- beaten egg for glazing
- dragees and caramels for decoration

## Directions

1. Put flour in a wooden bread trough
2. Make a hole in the center of the flour and put in half ounce of yeast dissolved in a little warm water
3. Add milk or tepid water to make the dough, using milk if you want it very rich and delicate, and water if you have not the milk
4. Knead and mix the flour with one hand while adding the milk or water with the other
5. Make a dough that is neither too stiff nor too soft
6. When perfectly smooth set the dough to rise in a moderately warm place, covering with a cloth
7. Remember that if you use milk to make the dough it must be scalded, that is, must be heated to the boiling point, and then allowed to grow tepid
8. Let the dough rise for five or six hours, and when increased to twice its bulk, take it and add the reserved half pound of flour into which you will have sifted the salt
9. Add six eggs beaten very light with the sugar and butter, and mix all well together, kneading lightly with your hands, and adding more eggs if the dough is a little stiff
10. Knead the dough by turning it over on itself three times, and set to rise again for an hour or three-quarters of an hour
11. Cover with a cloth
12. At the end of this time take it up and work again lightly
13. Form into a great ring, leaving a hole in the center
14. Pat gently and flatten a little
15. Have ready a baking pan with a buttered sheet of paper in it and set the central roll in the middle
16. Cover the pan with a clean stiff cloth and set the cake to rise for an hour longer
17. When well risen, set in an oven a few degrees cooler than that used for baking bread
18. Let bake for an hour and a half; if medium, one hour, and if very small, a half hour
19. Glace the Brioche lightly with a beaten egg spread lightly over the top before placing in the oven
20. Decorate with dragees, caramels, etc.
