## Ingredients

- Fruit
- Water
- White sugar
- Beaten white of an egg

## Directions

1. Wash and drain the fruit, put it in a stone jar, and put the jar into a kettle of water over the fire
2. Let it boil, but see that none of the water gets into the fruit
3. When the fruit is tender, it will begin to break; pour it now into a flannel bag, but do not squeeze it
4. To each pint of juice strained, add one pound, or one pound and a quarter of white sugar, and the half of the beaten white of an egg
5. Boil this rapidly, skim, but do not stir the syrup, as stirring breaks its continuity and prevents its jellying
6. Boil it twenty minutes, and try a little in some cold water, to find out if it jellies; if it does not, boil it a little longer
