## Ingredients

- 4 Fine Louisiana Oranges
- 1 Pint of the Best Claret or Any Fine Red Wine
- 1 Pint of Old Port Wine
- 1 Pint of Water
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Cloves
- 1 Pint of Gelatine or the Whites of 10 Eggs, Beaten to a Froth
- 2 Pounds of White Sugar
- 3 Tablespoonfuls of Raspberry Juice or a Few Drops of Cochineal

## Directions

1. Select fine Louisiana oranges, when in season, otherwise fine, ripe oranges
2. Set them on a plate in the oven, and let them bake till very tender, without letting the juice exude
3. Make a boiling syrup of the sugar, water and the cloves
4. Drop the roasted oranges into the syrup, add a pint of the best Claret or any good red wine, and set the mixture to cool, letting the oranges steep in the wine and syrup
5. When it grows cold, cut the oranges and press out all the juice, mashing them into the steeped syrup, and then strain all through a fine sieve into the ice cream freezer
6. Add the gelatine (Calf's Foot Jelly) or the whites of ten eggs, beaten to a stiff froth
7. Stir well and add the raspberry juice, if raspberries are in season, otherwise color with a few drops of Cochineal, so that the punch will be of a bright cardinal color
8. Cover immediately and set to freeze as hard as possible for three or four minutes
9. Take a wooden spoon and detach the frozen punch from the bottom, mix in with the inner liquid mixture, and cover and freeze again for three or four minutes
10. Repeat this four or five times
11. Serve in punch glasses
