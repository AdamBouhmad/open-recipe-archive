## Ingredients

- 1 Pound of Beef or Mutton Suet
- 1 Pounds of Apples, Pared and Chopped
- 2 Pounds of Raisins, Seeded and Chopped
- 1 Pound of Sultana Raisins, Picked and Washed
- 2 Pounds of Currants, Picked and Washed
- 1 Pound of Citron, Cut Very Fine
- 3/4 Pound of Lemon Peel, Grated Very Fine
- 2 Pounds of Brown Sugar
- Pound of Candied Lemon Peel, if desired
- 2 Grated Nutmegs
- 1 Tablespoontul Each Of Ground Cloves and Allspice
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Ground Mace
- 1 Tablespoontul of Fine Salt
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Ground Cinnamon
- 1 Quart of Good Sherry or Madeira
- 1 Quart of the Best Brandy
- The Juice of Two Lemons
- The Juice of 2 Oranges, and the Rind, Grated Fine

## Directions

1. The meat should be good and lean, and should be boiled the day before needed, or early in the morning, and allowed to cool
2. In boiling the meat, plunge it into boiling (not cold) water, for the boiling water enables it to retain its juices
3. Boil steadily till tender, then stand away to cool
4. Chop the meat very fine, and clear away all gristle and skin
5. Shred the suet, and chop, or rather mince it very fine
6. Pare and core the apples, and chop very fine
7. Stone the raisins, and cut the citron fine
8. Grate the orange and lemon peel very fine
9. Mix the meat and suet together, chopping again, and add all the ingredients, mixing well together
10. Add the juices of the lemon and orange, and the grated rinds, and then mix all well again, and pack in a stone jar
11. Pour over this the quart of brandy, and the wine, and cover very tightly, and set in a very cool place
12. When ready to use the meat, dish out the quantity you desire, and thin to the desired consistency with good Sherry or Madeira wine
