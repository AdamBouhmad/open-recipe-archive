## Ingredients

- 23 Pounds of Pork
- Coarse Salt Sufficient to make a Brine
- 1 Ounce of Saltpetre
- 12 Bay Leaves
- 2 Dozen Onions
- 12 Cloves
- 6 Allspice

## Directions

1. Pork should be pickled about twenty hours after killing.
2. To twenty-five pounds of pork, allow one ounce of saltpetre.
3. Pulverize saltpetre thoroughly and mix with a sufficient quantity of salt to thoroughly salt the pork.
4. Cut the pork into pieces of about two pounds, and slash each piece through the skin, and then rub thoroughly with the salt and saltpetre mixture till the meat is thoroughly penetrated through and through.
5. Mash the cloves very fine and ground the allspice.
6. Chop the onions.
7. Take a small barrel and place at the bottom a layer of salt, then a layer of coarsely chopped onions, and sprinkle over this a layer of the spices and minced bay leaves.
8. Place on this a layer of the pork; pack tightly; then place above this a layer of the salt and seasonings and continue with alternate layers of pork and seasonings till all the pork is used up.
9. Conclude with a layer of the minced herbs and spices and have a layer of salt on top.
10. Cover the preparation with a board on which a heavy weight must be placed to press down the meat.
11. It will be ready for use in about ten or twelve days.
