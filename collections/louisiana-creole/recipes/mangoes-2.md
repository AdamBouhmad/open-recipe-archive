## Ingredients

- mangoes
- salt
- water
- vinegar
- alum
- cabbage leaves
- 1 lb. ginger
- 1 lb. grated horse radish
- 1/2 lb. garlic
- 2 ounces black mustard seed
- 2 ounces white mustard seed
- 2 ounces celery seed
- 2 ounces ground mace
- 2 ounces ground cloves
- small cucumber
- long white pepper
- olive oil
- worsted string
- whole red pepper
- brown sugar
- mustard

## Directions

1. Put the mangoes into salt and water and let them remain for nine days
2. Split them and take out the insides
3. Put them in a kettle and cover them with vinegar and water
4. Put in a lump of alum the size of a hickory nut
5. Put cabbage leaves around them
6. Put them on the stove where they will keep warm, but not boil, and keep them there until green
7. Take 1 lb. of ginger, crack it but don't powder it fine, 1 lb. of grated horse radish, 1/2 lb. of garlic, 2 ounces of black mustard seed, 2 ounces of white mustard seed, 2 ounces of celery seed, 2 ounces of ground mace, 2 ounces of ground cloves
8. In each mango put a small cucumber and a long white pepper
9. Mix the stuffing with olive oil
10. Fill the mangoes and tie them with a worsted string
11. Pour boiling vinegar over them two or three times
12. Put a whole red pepper in each jar
13. Fill the spaces with cucumbers
14. Put a little unmixed mustard and brown sugar in the top of each jar
15. Seal them up and put them away for a year or more
