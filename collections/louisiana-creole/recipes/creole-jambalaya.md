## Ingredients

- 1 and a half cups of rice
- 1 pound of fresh pork
- 1 slice of ham
- 1 dozen fine chaurice (pork sausage)
- 2 onions
- 1 tablespoonful of butter
- 2 cloves of garlic
- 2 sprigs of thyme
- 2 sprigs of parsley
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 cloves ground very fine
- 3 quarts of beef broth or hot water
- 1/2 spoonful of chili pepper
- salt, pepper and cayenne to taste

## Directions

1. Cut the pork very fine, lean and fat, into pieces of about half an inch square
2. Chop the onions very fine, and mince the garlic and fine herbs
3. Grind the cloves
4. Put a tablespoonful of butter into the saucepan, and add the onions and pork, and let them brown slowly
5. Stir frequently, and let them continue browning slightly
6. When slightly brown, add the ham, chopped very fine, and the cloves of garlic
7. Add the minced herbs, thyme, bay leaf and parsley and cloves
8. Let all brown for five minutes longer
9. Add a dozen fine chaurice, cut apart, and let all cook five minutes longer
10. Add the three quarts of water or broth
11. Let it all cook for ten minutes
12. When it comes to a boil add the rice, which has been carefully washed
13. Add a half teaspoonful of chili pepper, and salt and cayenne to taste
14. Let all boil for a half hour longer, or until the rice is firm
15. Serve hot
16. Stir often, to mix all well
