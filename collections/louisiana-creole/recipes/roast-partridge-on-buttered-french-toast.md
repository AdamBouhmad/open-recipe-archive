## Ingredients

- Partridges
- Salt and pepper
- Melted butter
- Truffles
- Thyme
- Parsley
- Bay leaf
- Thin strips of bacon
- Buttered French toast
- Water

## Directions

1. Clean, singe and draw and wipe the birds neatly
2. Rub each bird well with salt and pepper, and then with melted butter
3. Stuff the bird with truffles, placing one or two in each bird
4. Place within the bird a pinch of salt and pepper, a lump of butter about the size of a peanut, and a pinch of chopped thyme, parsley and bay leaf, all minced very fine
5. Bind the birds with thin strips of bacon, and fasten each strip with a skewer
6. Put the Partridges in a baking pan in a brisk oven, and add two tablespoonfuls of butter and one of water
7. Let them bake from twenty-five to thirty minutes, according to size, basting occasionally with their own juice
8. When done, have ready the buttered French toast; place the birds upon the toast
9. Prepare gravy by adding two tablespoonfuls of water to the Partridge juice after removing the birds
10. Let it cook for two minutes, then strain and let it cook two minutes more
11. Pour the gravy upon the breast of the birds, so that it will soak into the toast
12. Garnish the dish nicely with sprigs of parsley or watercress and serve hot
