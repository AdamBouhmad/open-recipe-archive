## Ingredients

- 8 Dozen Large, Fine Oysters
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 6 Yolks of Eggs
- Spices, Thyme, Bay Leaf, Parsley
- 1 Slice of Grated Bread
- Pie Crust
- Flour
- Cayenne
- Rich, hot cream or boiled milk

## Directions

1. Take an earthen dish which will hold about three and a half pints and line the sides with rich old crust. Set in the stove and let it bake a few minutes.
2. In the meantime, take about one pint and a half of the oyster liquor and put on the fire, after having drained well.
3. Add the seasonings of chopped herbs and Cayenne.
4. Rub a tablespoonful of flour into the butter and add to the liquor, stirring constantly.
5. Mash the grated crumbs; add to this, and mix well.
6. Chop the hard-boiled eggs fine.
7. Then pour the oysters into the pan of pie crust, sprinkle some of the chopped eggs and grated bread crumbs over, and put a teaspoonful of butter in small bits here and there over this.
8. Then roll out very fine and thin a layer of the pie crust.
9. Place this over the preparation and ornament here and there, all around, with neat notches or designs, which can be easily formed with the end of a spoon or the prongs of a fork.
10. Cut a hole in the center in the shape of the letter X.
11. Set in a moderately quick oven and bake till brown.
12. In the meantime, melt one tablespoonful of butter, add the remaining oyster liquor and season with pepper and salt.
13. When it is about to come to a boil, add one-half cup of rich, hot cream or boiled milk, and when the pie is nearly brown, put a funnel into the opening in the center and pour in as much of the liquor as the pie will hold.
14. Place a delicate garnish of pastry leaves over the whole and bake a minute or so longer.
