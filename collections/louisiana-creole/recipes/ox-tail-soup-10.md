## Ingredients

- 1 Ox Tail
- A Bunch of Soup Herbs
- 1 Head of Celery
- 4 Quarts of Boiling Water
- 1 Large Onion
- 2 Carrots
- 3 Cloves
- A Sprig of Parsley
- A Small Slice of Lean Ham or Beef
- Salt and Pepper
- A tablespoonful of lard
- Thyme
- Bay leaf
- 1 clove of garlic
- 1 tablespoonful of flour
- 5 Quarts of Consomme or boiling water
- 3 tablespoonfuls of barley
- Cayenne
- 2 tablespoonfuls of sherry wine

## Directions

1. Cut the tail in pieces from the joint, and then cut again into the size of a peanut, or one inch and a half in length
2. Chop the onions very fine
3. Put the onion and a tablespoonful of lard into a frying pan and add the ox tail
4. Cook slowly until it begins to brown
5. Add the carrot, cut in pieces about the size of a green pea, and about a square inch of ham, chopped very fine
6. Let this brown
7. When it begins to brown nicely, add the thyme, bay leaf, three cloves, one clove of garlic, all chopped very fine
8. Let this continue to brown, being careful not to burn
9. Add one tablespoonful of flour, dredged in lightly and stirred
10. When all is nicely browned, add about five quarts of Consomme, if you have it; if not, five quarts of boiling water and three tablespoonfuls of barley
11. Let it cook together about four hours, simmering gently
12. Season with salt, pepper and cayenne to taste
13. When ready to serve, add two tablespoonfuls of sherry wine
14. Serve one joint to each person
