## Ingredients

- 1 Pint of Red Beans
- 2 Quarts of Cold Water
- 1 Minced Onion
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Bay Leaf
- Bunch of Parsley
- Sprig of Thyme
- Salt and Pepper

## Directions

1. Wash the beans and soak them overnight in lukewarm water.
2. Drain and put them in a saucepan with the cold water.
3. Allow them to come gradually to a boil; then set them back, and let them simmer gently for about two hours.
4. Melt the butter in a saucepan and fry in it the onion, parsley, thyme and bay leaf until brown.
5. Add these to the beans and boil about an hour and a half longer.
6. If the beans are perfectly tender at this point, press the whole through a colander.
7. Return to the fire, and add the seasonings.
8. Let them boil up once and serve with the Croqutons.
9. Some think that the flavor is enhanced by beating up an egg in the tureen and pouring the boiling soup gradually over it, stirring constantly.
