## Ingredients

- fine, large, ripe oranges with thin deep colored skins
- 1 lb. of loaf sugar to each lb. of oranges
- cold water
- beaten white of egg, allowing 1 white of egg to every 2 lbs. of sugar
- 1/4 pint of clear water to each pound of sugar
- brandy paper

## Directions

1. Weigh oranges and allow 1 lb. of loaf sugar to each lb. of oranges
2. Pare off the yellow outside of the rind from half of the oranges as thin as possible
3. Put the rind into a pan with plenty of cold water
4. Cover it closely to keep in the steam and boil it slowly until it is so soft that the head of a pin will pierce it
5. Grate the rind from the remaining oranges
6. Take out all the pulp and juice, removing the seeds and core
7. Put the sugar into a preserving kettle with 1/4 pint of clear water to each pound
8. Mix it with some beaten white of egg, allowing 1 white of egg to every 2 lbs. of sugar
9. When the sugar is all dissolved, put it on the fire and boil and skim it till it is quite clear and thick
10. Take the boiled parings and pound them to a paste in a mortar
11. Put this paste into the sugar and boil and stir it 10 minutes
12. Put it into the pulp and juice of the oranges and the grated rind
13. Boil all together for 1/4 hour till it is a transparent mass
14. When cold, put it up in glass jars with brandy paper on the top
