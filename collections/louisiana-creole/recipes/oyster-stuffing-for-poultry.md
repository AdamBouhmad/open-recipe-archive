## Ingredients

- 3 Dozen Oysters
- 1 Quart of Stale Bread, Wet and Squeezed
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Tablespoonful of Parsley
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 3 Tablespoonfuls of Sage
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- Liver and Gizzard of the Fowl
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- Chopped Onions

## Directions

1. Drain the oysters
2. Wet the stale bread with hot water, squeezing thoroughly
3. Chop fine the liver and gizzard of the fowl, and put a tablespoonful of lard into the frying pan
4. Mix in the chopped onions and add the chopped liver and gizzard
5. As it begins to brown, throw in the chopped herbs, and then add the bread which has been mixed well and seasoned with the chopped sage
6. Mix well
7. Add to this one tablespoonful of butter and stir, blending all thoroughly
8. Add the pint or so of oyster water, and as it is reduced mix in the oysters
9. Stir for three or four minutes and take off and dress the fowl
