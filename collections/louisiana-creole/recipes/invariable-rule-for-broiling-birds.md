## Ingredients

- Birds
- Salt and Pepper
- Melted Butter
- Strip of Very Finely-Sliced Bacon
- Buttered French Toasts
- Chopped Parsley
- Lemon Juice
- Sliced Lemon

## Directions

1. Prepare the birds by hand picking.
2. If of the very small variety, such as grassets, reed birds, robins, etc., do not pick out the entrails.
3. Rub the bird well with salt and pepper, and then with melted butter.
4. Tie a strip of very finely-sliced bacon around the body of the bird, joining with a skewer.
5. Place on a broiler over a slow fire, and let it cook for ten, fifteen or twenty or even thirty minutes, according to the size of the bird.
6. Turn frequently, so that it may cook well without burning.
7. When done, take off the broiler.
8. Place the birds upon buttered French toasts, allowing a slice of toast for each bird.
9. Trim away the rough edges of the toast.
10. Pour over the bird a little of the juice that has run from it in broiling, and let it soak down into the toast.
11. Pour over a little melted butter and chopped parsley, and lemon juice if you like.
12. Garnish with sliced lemon and parsley sprigs, and bring to the table hot.
