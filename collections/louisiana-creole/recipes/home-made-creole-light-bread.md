## Ingredients

- 2 Quarts of Flour
- ½ Cake of Compressed Yeast
- 1½ Pints of Milk or Water
- 1 Teaspoonful of Salt
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard

## Directions

1. Sift the flour and put into a wooden bread trough or a pan, and make a hole in the center of the flour
2. Add warm water or milk sufficient to make a nice, smooth, rather stiff batter
3. Mix the yeast with a little tepid water till well-dissolved, and add
4. Set to rise over night in a warm place
5. In the morning take up and again make a hole in the center of the dough, and add half a tablespoonful of melted lard or butter, one and one-half teaspoonfuls of salt, and one and one-half pints of warm milk or water
6. Add the flour and knead well about ten times
7. When it forms a smooth dough, form into a cake, and set to rise again
8. When risen to about twice its size, take it up and knead again about five times, that is, work one piece of dough over the other about five times, and set to rise again
9. Always, when setting to rise, sprinkle the bottom of the pan with a little flour
10. After an hour, take up the bread and knead for about ten minutes, till you have a nice dough, that can be worked without sprinkling the biscuit board with flour
11. Form the dough into two loaves and place them side by side in the baking pan
12. Bake in a moderate oven for about three-quarters of an hour
13. Serve hot or cold
