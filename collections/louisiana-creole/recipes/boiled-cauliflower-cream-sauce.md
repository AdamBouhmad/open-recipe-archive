## Ingredients

- 2 Medium-Sized Cauliflowers
- 1 Teaspoonful of Salt
- A Cream Sauce
- 2 Lemons Cut in Quarters

## Directions

1. Pick off the outer leaves of the cauliflower, leaving only the one delicate row near the bottom of the flowerets.
2. Cut the stem close to the flowerets.
3. Wash the cauliflower well in cold fresh water, and then soak, with the head downwards, about twenty minutes, to drain off all possible insects.
4. Have ready a pot of boiling water.
5. Take a nice, clean piece of cheesecloth, and tie the cauliflower in it, to prevent breaking while boiling.
6. Put the cauliflower in the kettle of boiling water, with the stem downwards.
7. Add a teaspoonful of salt, and cover the kettle.
8. Let the cauliflower boil from thirty to forty minutes, according to size, or until the vegetable is tender.
9. When cooked, lift it gently out by the cheesecloth, untie and set it in a dish, stem downward.
10. Pour over it a Cream Sauce and serve hot.
11. Place on each plate, when serving, a quarter of a lemon, nicely cut.
