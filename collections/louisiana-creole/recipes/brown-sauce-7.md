## Ingredients

- 1 Pound of Neck or Brisket of Veal
- Bones of Beef
- 1 Gallon of Water
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Lard
- 1/2 Can Mushrooms or 1/2 Can Truffles
- 2 Carrots
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 2 Cloves Garlic
- 1 Herb Bouquet
- 1 Wineglass of Sherry

## Directions

1. Take a good quantity of bones, place in a gallon of boiling water, and make a strong consommé, seasoning well with salt and pepper.
2. Take a piece of the brisket or neck of the beef, and roast rare, so that the blood spurts out when pricked with a needle.
3. After roasting cut it in pieces of about one inch square.
4. Take two tablespoonfuls of lard and three of flour, and brown slightly, stirring all the time.
5. After browning, add the water of the consommé, which has been reduced to about half a gallon, pouring it in slowly and stirring constantly.
6. Then add all the pieces of the roast beef which you have cut.
7. Add three carrots, two cloves of garlic, one onion, an herb bouquet (tied together of thyme, parsley and bay leaf), and let the whole boil well two hours, stirring every five minutes until reduced to the consistency of starch.
8. Then strain well through a strainer or sieve, season to taste, and set back on the stove to cook a few minutes longer.
9. Taste, and if sufficiently seasoned take it off and allow it to get cool.
10. This sauce is then used as a foundation sauce, and will keep for at least one month in our climate of New Orleans, if put in a cool place in winter or the ice box in summer.
