## Ingredients

- 1 Pound of Raisins
- 1 Pound of Currants
- 1/4 Pound of Citron
- 3/4 Pound of Butter
- 3/4 Pound of Suet
- 3/4 Pound of Sugar
- 3/4 Pound of Flour
- 6 Eggs
- 3/4 Pint of Milk
- 3/4 Cup of Brandy
- 1 Grated Nutmeg
- 1/2 Ounce Each of Ground Cloves, Mace, Cinnamon and Allspice
- The Grated Zest of 1 Lemon
- The Grated Zest of Half an Orange

## Directions

1. Seed the raisins and chop them fine.
2. Seed the currants, after washing and picking over very carefully.
3. Mince the citron, and grate the zest of the orange and lemon.
4. Beat the butter and sugar to a cream, and then beat in the yolks of the eggs, which have been previously beaten to a perfect degree of lightness.
5. Add the flour, beating in by degrees till very light, and the whites of the eggs, which have been beaten to a stiff froth.
6. Add the spices and beat in well.
7. Mince the suet and the fruits very fine and add, and lastly add the brandy, which you will beat thoroughly into the pudding.
8. When all is well-mixed, have ready the pudding cloth; wet with hot water, and wrung perfectly dry.
9. Dredge it slightly with flour, and wrap the pudding in it.
10. Sew it up, and put into a kettle of boiling water, and let it boil for six hours.
11. Serve with a Hard or Brandy Sauce.
