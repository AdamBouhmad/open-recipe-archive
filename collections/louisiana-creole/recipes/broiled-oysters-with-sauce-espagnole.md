## Ingredients

- 1 Pint of Oyster Liquor to Every 2 Dozen Oysters
- 1 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Sifted Flour
- Salt and Cayenne to Taste
- Chopped Parsley
- 6 or 8 Squares of Buttered Toast

## Directions

1. Drain the oysters and allow about one pint of the oyster liquor to every two dozen oysters.
2. Have ready a porcelain-lined saucepan and put the liquor on to boil.
3. As the scum rises skim it carefully.
4. Put one tablespoonfuls of butter into a frying pan, and when it begins to heat, add gradually two tablespoonfuls of sifted flour.
5. Mix well and brown.
6. Pour over this the oyster liquor and stir constantly till it begins to boil, seasoning with salt and pepper (Cayenne) and parsley chopped very fine.
7. Stand the sauce in a vessel in hot water (bain-marie) until wanted.
8. Proceed to broil the oysters in the same manner as in the recipe first given.
9. Place squares of buttered toast in a dish, lay the oysters on top, pour over the sauce, and serve immediately.
