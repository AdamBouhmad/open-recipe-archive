## Ingredients

- 1 Large Tender Chicken
- 2 Large Slices or ½ Pound Lean Ham
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Butter or 1 of Lard
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 3 Sprigs of Parsley
- 3 Dozen Oysters
- 1 Large Onion
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- 2 Quarts of Oyster Water
- 2 Quarts of Boiling Water
- 1 Half Pod of Red Pepper, Without the Seeds
- Salt and Pepper and Cayenne to Taste
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of File

## Directions

1. Clean and cut up the chicken as for a fricassee
2. Dredge with salt and black pepper, judging according to taste
3. Cut the ham into dice shapes and chop the onion, parsley and thyme very fine
4. Put the lard or butter into the soup kettle or deep stewing pot, and when hot, put in the ham and chicken
5. Cover closely and fry for about five or ten minutes
6. Then add the onion and parsley and thyme, stirring occasionally to prevent burning
7. When nicely browned add the boiling water and throw in the oyster stock, which has been thoroughly heated
8. Add the bay leaf chopped very fine, and the pepper pod, cut in two, and set the gumbo back to simmer for about an hour longer
9. When nearly ready to serve dinner, and while the Gumbo is boiling, add the fresh oysters
10. Let the gumbo remain on the stove for about three minutes longer, and then remove the pot from the fire
11. Have ready the tureens, set in a bain-marie or hot water bath
12. Take two tablespoonfuls of the File and drop gradually into the pot of boiling hot Gumbo, stirring slowly to mix thoroughly
13. Pour into the tureen or tureens and serve with boiled rice, about two spoonfuls of rice to one plate of gumbo
