## Ingredients

- Salt meat
- Large onions
- Turnips
- Carrots
- Clove of garlic
- Tablespoonful of lard
- Glass of sherry, Madeira or claret
- Salt, pepper and cayenne
- Bay leaves
- Sprigs of thyme and parsley
- Rump of beef

## Directions

1. Cut the fat of the salt meat into thin shreds
2. Chop the onion and bay leaf very fine, as also the garlic, thyme and cloves
3. Rub the shreds well with salt and pepper
4. Take the rump of beef and lard thickly by making incisions about three or four inches in length and inserting the pieces of salt fat and spices, onion and thyme and garlic, mixed thoroughly
5. Take two large onions and cut into quarters and put in a saucepan with one tablespoonful of lard
6. Let the slices brown and then lay on top the rump of beef, well larded
7. Cover closely and let it simmer very slowly till well browned
8. Add the chopped bay leaf and parsley
9. When brown add five carrots cut into squares of an inch, and two turnips, cut in the same manner, and two large onions, chopped fine
10. Let the whole brown, keeping well covered, and cooking slowly over a slow but regular fire
11. Keep the cover very tight on the pot
12. When it has simmered about ten minutes, turn the daube on the other side, cover closely and let it simmer ten minutes more
13. Cover with sufficient boiling water to cover the daube; or use boiling consommé or pot-au-feu, and if possible, a glass of sherry or Madeira wine; or if you have neither, a glass of good claret
14. Season according to taste with salt, cayenne and black pepper
15. Cover the pot tight and set it back on the stove, letting it smother slowly for about three hours, or until tender
16. Serve hot or cold
