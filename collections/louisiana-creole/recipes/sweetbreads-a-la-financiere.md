## Ingredients

- 3 Pairs of Sweetbreads
- 1/2 Pound of Butter
- 3 Carrots
- 1 Onion
- 2 Sprigs of Thyme
- 3 Bay Leaves
- 1 Pint of Beef Consomme or Water
- 1 Pint of Rich Chicken Broth or Water
- 2 Truffles
- 12 Mushrooms
- 18 Stoned Olives
- 12 Godiveau Quenelles
- 2 Blanched Chicken Livers
- 1/2 Pint of Madeira or Sherry Wine
- A Dash of Cayenne
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- Croutons Fried in Butter to Garnish

## Directions

1. Select fine, fresh Sweetbreads and prepare as in the recipe for Sweetbreads Larded With Mushrooms.
2. Parboil for twenty minutes; then drain of all water; press them into shape, lay on a clean napkin and cover with a plank and place a weight upon them to press and make solid.
3. Take a piece of fine salt pork, and cut into little thin strips like matches and lard the Sweetbreads with this, using a very fine larding needle, larding each Sweetbread eight times.
4. Take a shallow saucepan and put within a half pound of butter.
5. When the butter melts, lay in the Sweetbreads, one by one.
6. Season with salt and pepper very lightly, and add the three carrots, sliced fine, and the onion, sliced very fine.
7. Add the finely minced thyme and bay leaves.
8. Butter a piece of brown paper and cover the saucepan; then set in the oven and let the Sweetbreads cook slowly till they are of a bright golden brown.
9. From time to time uncover the saucepan and turn the Sweetbreads, so that all portions of them may be evenly colored.
10. When they have reached this beautiful color add one pint of good beef broth and let them simmer for a half or three-quarters of an hour.
11. When nearly ready to serve, prepare a Sauce a la Financiere: Take two tablespoonfuls of butter, melt and remove from the fire, and add gradually a tablespoonful of flour.
12. Blend well with a wooden spoon till very smooth, and moisten with one pint of rich chicken broth and set on the fire.
13. Add the truffles, nicely sliced; a dozen and a half stoned olives; the blanched chicken livers, cut in pieces; the mushrooms, nicely chopped; a half pint of Madeira or Sherry Wine, salt and pepper to taste, and a dash of Cayenne or Tabasco.
14. Let the sauce cook for twenty minutes.
15. Place the sauce in a round dish, lay the Sweetbreads over it, garnish with the Godiveau Quenelles and Croutons fried in butter and send to the table hot.
