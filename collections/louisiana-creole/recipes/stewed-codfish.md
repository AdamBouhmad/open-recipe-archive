## Ingredients

- 1 Dozen Small Potatoes, or Left-Over Salt Cod, Enough for Six Persons
- 1 Quart of Milk
- 1 Dozen White Onions
- 3 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour Blended With Two of Milk

## Directions

1. If the potatoes are not left-over from the day before, wash and peel, and also the onion.
2. Boil the potatoes till tender.
3. Soak the fish overnight, and boil for twenty minutes, or until tender.
4. Cut the fish into pieces of about two or three inches in length.
5. Put a tablespoonful of lard in the stewpan and lay in the potatoes and then the onions, and the Codfish on top.
6. Add enough cold water to cover the whole, and let it simmer until the fish is well cooked.
7. Take out the fish, and allow the potatoes and onions to simmer on.
8. Remove every piece of bone from the fish, and trim edges nicely.
9. Dissolve in another stewpan the butter and flour as directed; let this simmer gently without browning.
10. Put in the potatoes, onions and fish in the order given.
11. Pour over this the quart of cream or milk.
12. Let it simmer for about ten minutes more till the milk comes to a boil, and serve hot.
