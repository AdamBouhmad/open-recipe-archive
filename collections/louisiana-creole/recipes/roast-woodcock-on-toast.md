## Ingredients

- 6 Fine Woodcock
- Slices of Buttered Toast
- 6 Strips of Bacon
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 1 Sprig Each of Thyme and Parsley
- 1 Bay Leaf
- Salt and Pepper
- Truffle for stuffing
- Four spices
- Sliced Lemon and Sprigs of Parsley or Watercress to Garnish

## Directions

1. Prepare the birds as in the Invariable Rule for Roasting Birds.
2. Rub with salt and pepper and melted butter.
3. If you can afford it, put a truffle in each bird for stuffing.
4. In all put a little lump of butter and a little salt and pepper, a pinch of chopped thyme, parsley and bay leaf, and a small pinch of the four spices.
5. Truss neatly and bind with strips of bacon.
6. Place in a baking pan with a tablespoonful of butter.
7. Let them bake or roast thirty minutes or less, according to size.
8. Remove from the oven and place on buttered French toast on a hot dish, cover and set over a pot of boiling water to keep warm.
9. Prepare gravy by adding a tablespoonful of water to the gravy made when cooking the birds.
10. Let it cook for two minutes, then strain.
11. Let it cook for two minutes more and pour upon the breast of the bird so that it will soak down into the toast.
12. Garnish nicely with sprigs of parsley and lemon, and serve hot.
