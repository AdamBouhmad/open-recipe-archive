## Ingredients

- three pints of warm water
- half a gill of baker's yeast or an inch square of turnpike cake
- a heaping teaspoonful of salt
- half a small teaspoonful of saleratus
- buckwheat flour
- a large handful of cornmeal optional
- an egg optional
- a cup of milk optional

## Directions

1. Put three pints of warm water into a stone jar
2. Add half a gill of baker's yeast or an inch square of turnpike cake dissolved in a little warm water
3. Add a heaping teaspoonful of salt and half a small teaspoonful of saleratus
4. Gradually stir in enough buckwheat flour to make a nice batter
5. Beat it perfectly smooth
6. Cover it and set it in a moderately warm place until morning
7. A large handful of cornmeal may be put with the flour if desired, and if the meal is added it will require an egg and a cup of milk
