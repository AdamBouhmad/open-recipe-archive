## Ingredients

- ½ Dozen Large Spanish Onions
- A Stuffing of Chaurice
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 3 Chaurice
- 1 Cup of Bread, wet and squeezed
- 1 Inch of Ham, finely chopped
- 1 Sprig each of Thyme and Parsley and Sweet Marjoram
- 1 Bay Leaf
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- Powdered Bread Crumbs

## Directions

1. Take a half dozen fine, large Spanish onions, and put them in hot ashes to roast.
2. When they are sufficiently cooked, which will be in about half an hour, take them out of the ashes, dust off, and peel well.
3. Then open the interiors and fill with a stuffing made as follows: Take a tablespoonful of butter and put in a frying pan, and add three chaurice, which you will have taken out of the casings and chopped finely again.
4. Add a cup of bread, which has been wet and squeezed, and mix well.
5. Then add an inch of ham, finely chopped, and a sprig each of thyme and parsley and sweet marjoram, and one bay leaf, all finely minced.
6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
7. Fry about fifteen minutes, and then stuff the onions as far down in the center as possible, and between the folds.
8. Sprinkle the top with powdered bread crumbs and put a little dot of butter on top of each.
9. Set in the oven and let them bake for about thirty or forty minutes.
10. Serve with roast beef.
