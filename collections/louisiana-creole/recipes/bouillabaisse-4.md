## Ingredients

- 6 Slices of Red Snapper
- 6 Slices of Redfish
- 1/2 Bottle of White Wine
- 1/2 Lemon
- 6 Large Fresh Tomatoes, or 1/2 Can
- 3 Onions
- 1 Herb Bouquet
- 3 Cloves of Garlic
- 3 Bay Leaves
- 3 Sprigs of Thyme
- 3 Sprigs of Parsley
- 6 Allspices
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Olive Oil
- 1 Good Strong Pinch of Saffron
- Salt, Pepper and Cayenne to Taste

## Directions

1. Cut off the head of the Red Snapper and boil it in about one and a half quarts of water to make a fish stock.
2. Put one sliced onion and a herb bouquet consisting of thyme, bay leaf and parsley into the water.
3. When reduced to one pint, take out the head of the fish and the herb bouquet and strain the water and set it aside for use later on.
4. Take six slices of Redfish and six slices of Red Snapper of equal sizes and rub well with salt and pepper.
5. Mince three sprigs of thyme, three sprigs of parsley, three bay leaves and three cloves of garlic very fine, and take six allspice and grind them very fine, and mix thoroughly with the minced herbs and garlic.
6. Rub each slice of fish well with this mixture till every portion is permeated by the herbs, spice and garlic.
7. Take two tablespoonfuls of fine olive oil and put into a very large pan, so large that each slice of the fish may be put in without one piece overlapping the other.
8. Chop two onions very fine and add them to the heating oil.
9. Lay the fish slice by slice in the pan and cover, and let them "etouffer," or smother for about ten minutes, turning once over so that each side may cook partly.
10. Take the fish out of the pan and set the slices in a dish.
11. Pour a half bottle of white wine into the pan and stir well.
12. Add a half can of tomatoes, or six large fresh tomatoes sliced fine, and let them boil well.
13. Add half a lemon, cut in very thin slices, and pour over a pint of the liquor in which the head of the snapper was boiled.
14. Season well to taste with salt, pepper and a dash of Cayenne.
15. Let it boil until very strong and till reduced almost one half.
16. Lay the fish slice by slice, apart one from the other, in the pan, and let boil five minutes.
17. In the meantime prepare one good pinch of saffron, chopped very fine, and add a little of the sauce in which the fish is boiling to dissolve well.
18. When well melted, and when the fish has been just five minutes in the pan, spread the saffron over the top of the fish.
19. Take out of the pan and lay each slice on toast which has been fried in butter.
