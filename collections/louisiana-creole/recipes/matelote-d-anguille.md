## Ingredients

- 2 pounds of eel, or a 2-pound fish
- 1 large onion
- 1 pint of button onions
- 1 clove of garlic
- 1 tablespoonful of lard
- 2 tablespoonfuls of flour
- 1 glass of good Claret
- 1 can of mushrooms
- 1 pint of oyster water
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 sprig of thyme
- Croutons to garnish
- Parsley
- Cayenne and salt to taste

## Directions

1. Clean and skin the eel, using about two pounds.
2. Cut in one inch or two-inch pieces, as desired.
3. Take one large onion and a half pint of button onions, or a clove of garlic, and chop fine.
4. Make a roux with one tablespoonful of lard, adding, when hot, the chopped onions and parsley, and gradually adding, when these begin to brown, the two tablespoonfuls of flour.
5. Pour in one good glass of Claret and a can of mushrooms.
6. Add a pint of oyster stock or oyster water, and let cook a while.
7. Season to taste with Cayenne and salt, adding bay leaf and thyme.
8. When it boils, add the eels that have been thoroughly cleansed and placed in cold water and allowed to stand about fifteen minutes.
9. When these are added to the matelote, let it simmer about an hour.
10. Serve with fancy garnish of croutons.
