## Ingredients

- 1 Dozen Soft-Shell Crabs
- 1 Quart of Milk
- Bread Crumbs
- Salt, Pepper and Cayenne to Taste

## Directions

1. Wash carefully, removing all sand, but do not scald or blanch them, as this destroys the fine flavor completely
2. Remove the spongy, feathery substances under the side points called the "man-eaters"
3. Remove also the sand bag or "sand pouch" from under the shells just between the eyes
4. Remove the "tablier" or "apron"
5. Wash well in cold water and dry with a clean towel
6. Take a pint of milk and season well with pepper and salt; season the crabs and crack them in the milk, rubbing thoroughly, so that the milk may thoroughly impregnate them
7. Take out and roll in a little sifted flour
8. Pat lightly with the hand, shake off superfluous flour and fry in boiling grease, being always careful to have sufficient grease in the pan for the crabs to swim in it
9. When a delicate golden brown, take out of the grease with a skimmer
10. Drain on a piece of heated brown paper, and serve on a bed of fried parsley, with garnishes of sliced lemon
11. Serve with Sauce à la Tartare
