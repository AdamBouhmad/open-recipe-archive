## Ingredients

- 4 pounds of lean, fresh pork
- 2 pounds of fat fresh pork
- 2 large onions, minced very fine
- 1 clove of garlic, minced very fine
- 1 teaspoonful of cayenne pepper and chili pepper (very hot)
- 1 teaspoonful of red pepper
- 3 teaspoonfuls of salt
- 2 teaspoonfuls finely ground black pepper
- 1 sprig of thyme, well minced
- 3 sprigs of parsley, finely minced
- 2 bay leaves, chopped or minced very fine
- 1/8 teaspoonful of allspice, very fine
- Sausage casings

## Directions

1. Hash the pork as fine as possible — fat and lean — and mix together.
2. Season highly with the salt and black pepper and cayenne, chili and red pepper (pimento).
3. Mince the onion and garlic as fine as possible, then add to the chaurice.
4. Mince the herbs as fine as possible, and add, and then mix the finely ground spices thoroughly with the chaurice.
5. Hash all together, and when well mixed, take the casings (that have been well cleaned by the butcher), scald them and wash thoroughly again.
6. Dry them and fill with the mixture, tying them in the lengths you desire.
7. Fry in boiling lard for breakfast, always having sufficient to have the sausage swim in it, and serve, after draining of all grease, on a hot dish with minced parsley thrown over as a garnish.
