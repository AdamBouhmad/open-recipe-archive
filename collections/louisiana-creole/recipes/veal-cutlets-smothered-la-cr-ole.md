## Ingredients

- 6 Veal Cutlets
- 6 Onions, Sliced Fine
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Vinegar
- Pepper and Salt to Taste
- Chopped Parsley
- Thyme
- Bay Leaf
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 1 Cup of Water or 1 Pint

## Directions

1. Cut off the outer skin of the cutlets, and season well with salt and pepper.
2. Dredge with flour.
3. Place the tablespoonful of lard in a deep frying pan and let it melt.
4. Lay in the sliced onions, and over these lay the cutlets.
5. Cover closely.
6. Let them simmer over a hot fire for a few minutes and then turn the cutlets on the other side.
7. After three minutes, add two tablespoonfuls of vinegar, chopped parsley, thyme and bay leaf and a clove of garlic.
8. Turn the veal, letting the flour brown well, and keep the pot closely covered.
9. When brown, pour over one cup of water, or a pint, which will be sufficient to cover the meat.
10. Bring this to a brisk boil and set the pot back, where it can simmer gently for about two hours.
11. Serve with a nice garnish of parsley or radishes.
