## Ingredients

- 1 Pint of Flour
- 1 Pound of Good Butter, or Half Lard and Half Butter
- 1 Teaspoonful of Salt
- 1 Cup of Ice Water
- 1 Egg

## Directions

1. Have the flour dry and fresh and the butter of the freshest.
2. Wash the butter well, till it becomes clear and elastic to the touch, and cut it into four equal parts, and place it in a bowl, which you will have cooled with ice water.
3. Set it away to cool and harden.
4. Have your flour sifted and ready, and the egg beaten and at hand.
5. Put the flour into a bowl and add to it a lump of butter; work it with your hand, handling always as lightly as possible.
6. Add the egg, then salt, and, if the paste is intended for cakes, a teaspoonful of sugar.
7. Work all well, but very lightly, with the fingers and thumb, and then gradually add the ice water, working it well and lightly into the flour.
8. When all is worked in, knead lightly for about five minutes, just as though you were making bread.
9. Cut the dough into halves; sprinkle the board lightly with flour.
10. Take the rolling pin and roll the paste out very thin, rolling each half into a half sheet.
11. Break the butter into bits; take about one-quarter of the remainder, quickly spread this over the paste.
12. Dredge it very lightly with flour and lay over it the other sheet.
13. Dredge this with flour lightly and pound with the rolling pin lightly.
14. Roll this from you into a long thin sheet.
15. Spread a thin coating of butter over this, and then fold the paste over from the sides to the center, and then back again, till you have three folds.
16. Turn and roll this again till quite thin; add a second quarter of butter broken into bits.
17. Dredge lightly with flour and fold and roll as before, always in the same direction — from you.
18. Repeat this process, folding and rolling, till all the butter is used.
19. Cut the paste into three or four parts, according to quantity, and set in the ice box in tin plates for twenty minutes, or until you are ready to use.
20. Bake in a very hot oven, being careful not to set the crust too near the inner fire part of the oven as it will bake unevenly.
