## Ingredients

- citron
- a pound and a quarter of sugar per pound of rind
- grapevine leaves
- pulverized alum
- water

## Directions

1. Pare off the green skin and all the soft part of the rind
2. cut the firm part in strips, or any shape you fancy
3. Allow a pound and a quarter of sugar to each pound of rind
4. line your porcelain kettle with grapevine leaves and fill with the rind
5. scatter a little pulverized alum over each layer
6. Cover with vine-leaves three thick
7. pour on water enough to reach and wet these and cover with a close lid
8. Let them heat together for three hours, but the water must not actually boil
9. Take out the rind, which will be well greened by this process
10. throw at once into very cold water
11. Let it soak for four hours, changing the water for fresh every hour
12. Then make a syrup, allowing two cups of water to every pound and a quarter of syrup
13. Boil and skim until no more scum comes up
14. put in the rind and simmer gently nearly an hour
15. Take it out and spread on dishes in the sun until firm
