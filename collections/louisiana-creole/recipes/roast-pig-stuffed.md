## Ingredients

- 1 Pig, Four or Five Weeks Old
- 3 Large Onions
- 2 Cups of Bread Crumbs
- 3 Sprigs of Chopped Parsley
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- 2 Ounces of Butter
- 2 Teaspoonfuls of Powdered Sage
- 3 Hard-Boiled Eggs
- 1 Herb Bouquet

## Directions

1. Wash the young pig well, cleaning again, and scraping thoroughly and taking out all remaining hair from the ears and nostrils
2. Wash again thoroughly in cold water, inside and out, shaking the pig vigorously, head downward
3. Turn upwards and pour cold water over it
4. Wipe dry inside and out with a coarse towel
5. Rub well inside with salt and pepper and minced parsley, thyme and bay leaf
6. Wet the bread crumbs and squeeze thoroughly
7. Add the sprigs of minced parsley and hard-boiled eggs and the powdered sage
8. Mix well and season all highly with black pepper and salt using about a teaspoonful of salt and a half teaspoonful of black pepper
9. Place two ounces of butter in a frying pan on the stove, and when it melts, add the minced onions
10. Let them brown, and then add the dressing, stirring well, and letting it fry for five minutes
11. Stuff the pig and sew up the opening
12. Truss the fore legs forward and the hind feet forward, and close under the body
13. Wipe the pig carefully with a damp towel, and place a corn cob in its mouth to keep it open
14. Rub the pig all over the outside with butter, dredging lightly with salt and pepper
15. Place in a moderate oven, and bake steadily for two and a half or three hours, according to size and age
16. Baste frequently, and when half done, rub again with butter until the pan is saturated
17. Continue basting at intervals
18. When done, take out of the oven and place on a hot dish
19. Garnish the dish with parsley
20. Take the corncob out of the mouth and place instead a nice, rosy apple
21. Serve very hot, with Apple Sauce
