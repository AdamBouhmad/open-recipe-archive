## Ingredients

- cucumbers
- brine
- vine leaves
- pulverized alum
- vinegar
- ginger tea
- one quart of strong vinegar
- one pint of water
- three pounds of sugar
- four pounds of cucumbers
- one ounce of cinnamon
- cloves
- mace
- white ginger

## Directions

1. Put your cucumbers in brine for eight days
2. Slice them without soaking; let the slices be an inch thick
3. When cut, soak them until the salt is nearly out, changing the water very often
4. Put them in a kettle with vine leaves laid between the layers
5. Cover them well with leaves and sprinkle pulverized alum all through them to harden and green them
6. Cover with vinegar and set them on the back of the stove until they become green
7. Take the cucumbers out and boil them a little in ginger tea (half an hour will be enough)
8. Make a syrup of one quart of strong vinegar and one pint of water, three pounds of sugar to four pounds of cucumbers, with one ounce of cinnamon, cloves, mace and white ginger to every ten pounds of fruit
9. Make this syrup hot and put in the cucumbers and boil them until clear
10. When they are clear take them out and boil the syrup until it is thick enough to keep
11. Pour it over the cucumbers which should have been placed in jars ready for the syrup
