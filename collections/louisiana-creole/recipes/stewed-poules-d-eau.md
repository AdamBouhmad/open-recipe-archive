## Ingredients

- 1 Pair of Poules d'Eau
- 1 Square Inch of Ham
- 2 Onions
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 1 Herb Bouquet, Chopped Very Fine
- 1 Glass of Claret
- 1 Cup of Water
- 1 Can of Mushrooms
- Salt and Pepper

## Directions

1. Clean and pick the Poules d'Eau nicely
2. Cut into joints or stew whole, as desired
3. Rub well with salt and pepper
4. Chop two onions very fine and put them into the stewpan with a tablespoonful of melted butter, and let them brown slightly
5. Add the well-seasoned ducks and let these brown well
6. Add the one square inch of finely minced ham
7. Add the clove of garlic and two sprigs each of thyme, parsley and one bay leaf, minced very fine
8. Let this brown with the Poules d'Eau, stirring frequently, and then pour over one good glass of claret
9. Let this simmer for ten minutes, stirring constantly so that it will not burn, and add one cup of boiling water
10. Season well to taste, and let them simmer well for about an hour
11. Serve hot with Croûtons for a garnish
