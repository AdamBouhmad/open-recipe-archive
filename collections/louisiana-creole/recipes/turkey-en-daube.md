## Ingredients

- 1 Large Turkey
- 1 Bunch Each of Parsley, Thyme and Small Celery Leaves
- Large Slice of Salt Pork
- 2 Onions and 2 Carrots, Sliced
- 10 Cloves
- ⅛ Calfs Foot
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- Bouquet of Sweet Herbs
- 1½ Pints of Broth or Boiling Water
- 2 Spoonfuls of Brandy
- 1 Pint of White Wine

## Directions

1. Clean and prepare the turkey, then stuff either with egg dressing or oyster stuffing.
2. Rub well with salt and pepper.
3. Place at the bottom of a deep pot slender strips of salt pork and half of a calf's foot, well prepared.
4. Place on top of this the slices of onions, carrots, fine herbs, minced nicely; garlic, minced, celery, parsley, etc., and lay the turkey on this bed.
5. Pour over it one pint of white wine and two tablespoonfuls of brandy, and one pint and a half of good broth or boiling water.
6. Season well to taste and cover tightly.
7. Set on the stove to simmer very slowly for at least five hours if the turkey is old.
8. Turn it once very carefully when half done cooking.
9. After five hours, lift the turkey out of the sauce, place on a hot dish.
10. Strain the sauce through a sieve, and if the turkey is served at once, serve hot in a separate dish.
11. If not, pour it over the turkey and set it away to cool.
12. It will become quite jellied and makes an excellent luncheon dish.
