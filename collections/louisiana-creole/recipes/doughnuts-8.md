## Ingredients

- 3/4 cup sugar
- 1 pint of milk
- 3 eggs
- 2 tablespoonfuls of butter
- 3/4 cake of compressed yeast
- 1 teaspoonful of salt
- flour in sufficient quantity to make a dough

## Directions

1. Scald the milk, and then add the butter, and let it stand and cool.
2. When it has cooled, add the yeast and the sugar, and beat in the flour gradually.
3. Beat well, and then cover, and set the mixture in a warm place over night, with only moderate warmth.
4. In the morning beat the eggs till very light, and stir them into the butter.
5. Add more flour, sufficient to make a dough, which must be soft.
6. Knead this dough lightly, and stand it away to rise.
7. When it has risen well, take one-half of the dough and roll it on the biscuit board, and then cut into doughnuts, using a large cake or biscuit cutter.
8. Take a small cutter and make a small hole in the center of each doughnut.
9. Spread a clean towel over the table, and dust it lightly with flour, and let the doughnuts stand upon it for half an hour, being well covered.
10. Let the frying kettle be ready with boiling fat or lard, deep enough that the doughnuts can swim in it.
11. Put the doughnuts into the boiling lard, and let them fry to a golden brown.
12. When a beautiful dark golden brown, drain out of the pan with a skimmer, place on a hot dish, sprinkle with powdered sugar, and serve hot.
