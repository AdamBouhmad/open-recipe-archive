## Ingredients

- 1 Dozen Oysters
- 2 Quarts of Oyster Liquor
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard or Butter
- 1 Quart of Hot Water
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 1 Large White Onion
- Parsley
- Thyme
- Bay Leaf
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Filé

## Directions

1. Put the lard into a kettle, and when hot add the flour, making a brown roux.
2. When quite brown without burning, add the chopped onions and parsley.
3. Fry these, and when brown, add the chopped bay leaf.
4. Pour in the hot oyster liquor and then add the hot water.
5. When it comes to a good boil, just before serving, add the oysters which have been well drained, without pouring water over them.
6. Cook for about three minutes longer and take off the stove and stir gradually two tablespoonfuls of Filé into the boiling hot gumbo.
7. Have the tureen ready in a bain-marie, or hot-water bath, and pour in the gumbo and cover.
8. Bring to the table immediately and serve with boiled rice, allowing about six or eight oysters to each person.
