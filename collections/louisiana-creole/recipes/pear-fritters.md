## Ingredients

- 3 Fresh Pears or 4 Canned Pears
- Brandy or Rum (If desired)
- Grated Lemon Peel
- Powdered Sugar
- Batter a la Creole
- Brown paper

## Directions

1. Peel and core the pears, which will be all the nicer if they are a little tart.
2. Take out the seeds and core.
3. Cut them into slices, more or less thick or thin, according to taste.
4. Soak them in brandy or good whisky or rum for the space of two hours, sprinkling with the zest of a lemon and sugar, according to judgment.
5. Make a batter a la Creole, and have ready a deep saucepan of boiling lard.
6. Drain the pears.
7. Dip the slices, one at a time, into the batter.
8. Lift out with a large kitchen spoon, drop into the boiling lard, and fry to a golden brown.
9. Then lift out with a skimmer, and set on brown paper, in the mouth of the oven, and drain.
10. Sift powdered white sugar over them, and serve hot, piling high in pyramidal shape, and sprinkling again with powdered white sugar.
11. Serve as an entremet or as a dessert.
