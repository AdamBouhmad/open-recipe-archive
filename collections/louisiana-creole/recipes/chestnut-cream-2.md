## Ingredients

- 2 Dozen Chestnuts
- Yolks of 2 Eggs
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Pint of Cream
- 1 Pint of Milk
- 4 Ounces of Powdered Sugar

## Directions

1. Shell the chestnuts and blanch them, and pound sufficient quantity to make two ounces reduced to flour.
2. Or roast two dozen chestnuts and peel them, and pound them till very fine, and blend with a little milk till you have a thick paste.
3. Add the yolks of two eggs, well beaten, and two tablespoonfuls of the best butter.
4. Beat well, and add four ounces of powdered white sugar.
5. Beat all this very light, and put into one pint of boiling milk, or half milk and half cream.
6. Let it boil for about ten minutes, and then take off.
7. Strain, decorate in a glass dish, and serve cold.
