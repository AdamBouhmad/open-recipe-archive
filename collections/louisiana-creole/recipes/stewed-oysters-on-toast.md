## Ingredients

- the oysters from their liquor
- whole pepper
- two blades of mace
- three cloves
- a piece of butter as large as a hen’s egg for two dozen oysters
- crackers
- dressed celery

## Directions

1. Take the oysters from their liquor, let it settle
2. Then strain and add some whole pepper, two blades of mace, and three cloves
3. Put over a moderate fire in a block-tin covered sauce-pan
4. Mix a little flour with a piece of butter, as large as a hen’s egg for two dozen oysters
5. And stir in the boiling liquor
6. Remove any scum which may rise
7. Then put in the oysters and let them cook for five minutes
8. Line a hot oyster dish with toasted, well-buttered bread
9. And pour over it the boiling oysters
10. Only rich juicy oysters will stew to advantage
11. Milk is always an improvement, but in this recipe it can be dispensed with
12. If, however, it is convenient pour in a half a pint just as the oysters are put in to boil
13. As earlier it might curdle
