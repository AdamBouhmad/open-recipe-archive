## Ingredients

- lean beef
- bones
- ham
- cold water
- salt
- onions
- carrots
- celery
- cayenne pepper
- vermicelli
- maccaroni
- pates
- egg whites

## Directions

1. Have 5 or 6 pounds of lean beef from above the knee cut into small pieces and the bones chopped
2. Add a slice of ham cut fine
3. Put this on in about 6 quarts of cold water
4. Let the soup come to a boil and skim it well
5. Throw in a tablespoonful of salt and a half tea cup of cold water to help the soup throw up the scum better
6. Carefully take off all scum until it ceases to rise
7. Add one large or two medium sized onions, two carrots cut fine, and one or two pieces of celery
8. Let this boil slowly from 6 to 8 hours, or until the broth is reduced to one-half the quantity
9. Your meat should be cooked to pieces
10. Pass all through a cullender and strain all through a coarse cloth
11. Put this in a cold place until the next day, when it will be jellied
12. Skim all the grease carefully from the top
13. Take the jelly, or soup as it may be, and pour it carefully into another vessel without disturbing the bottom
14. Heat it well
15. Have a clean flannel cloth, or bag, well wet, which dip in ice water
16. Pass a small quantity of the broth at a time through the bag, constantly shaking the dregs from the flannel and wetting it with ice water
17. Season highly with cayenne pepper and salt to the taste
18. Heat it now to serve, adding vermicelli, maccaroni or pates, as you like
19. If the soup is cloudy before you strain it, beat the whites of three eggs stiff and pour them into the soup and let all come to a good boil
20. Let it set about five minutes before you strain
