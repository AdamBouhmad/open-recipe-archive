## Ingredients

- 1 Quart Milk
- ½ Split Vanilla Stick
- 6 Ounces of Ground Rice
- ¼ Pound of Sugar
- 2 Ounces Butter
- Yolks of 8 Eggs
- 1 Ounce of Pineapple
- 2 Ounces of Apricots
- 3 Ounces of Cherries
- 1 Ounce of Orange Peel
- A Cream Sauce

## Directions

1. Boil a quart of milk, and add to it while boiling the split vanilla stick.
2. Cut all the fruit and orange peel into small dice, throwing away the seeds.
3. Drop the ground rice like a shower of rain into the boiling milk, stirring it continuously with a whisk of the hand.
4. When it begins to soften, set it back, and let it cook for fifteen minutes longer.
5. Then mix in the cut fruits, and add the butter, which you will have melted.
6. Add the eggs, which have been beaten very light in the sugar.
7. Mix thoroughly, and add the orange peel cut into quarter dice.
8. When the mixture is very light, set it to cool by spreading it out on a baking sheet, covered with white paper.
9. When cold, divide it into small balls, roll these in powdered Macaroons, dip in beaten egg, and then roll in white bread crumbs.
10. Fry in boiling lard.
11. Drain in a heated colander or on a piece of brown paper.
12. Then dress the croquettes nicely on a dish, sprinkle with vanilla sugar, and serve each separately with a Cream Sauce.
