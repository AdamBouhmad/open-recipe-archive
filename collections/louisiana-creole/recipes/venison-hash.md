## Ingredients

- 3 Cups of Left Over Venison
- 6 Potatoes
- 1 Herb Bouquet
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard or Butter

## Directions

1. After having taken off all the rough edges of the roast and cut out the gristle and hard membrane, hash the venison into pieces of about one-inch in size.
2. Take six left-over tomatoes, or freshly boiled, and cut into quarters.
3. Chop fine one herb bouquet.
4. Place a tablespoonful of butter or a half tablespoonful of lard into the stewpot, and as it melts add the venison, seasoned well, and a few minutes later the fine herbs.
5. Mince the clove of garlic if the flavor is liked and add.
6. Stir constantly without browning much, and add a tablespoonful of flour.
7. Let this brown very slightly, and then add the tomatoes.
8. Cover and let all simmer for about twenty minutes, and then pour over a pint of boiling water.
9. Season again to taste and set back on the stove and let it simmer gently for about three-quarters of an hour.
10. Cut some Croutons and fry them in butter; place on a dish and serve with the hash.
