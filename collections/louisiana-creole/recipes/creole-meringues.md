## Ingredients

- 1 Pint of Sifted White Sugar
- The Whites of 12 Eggs
- 1 Tablespoonful of Vanilla

## Directions

1. Beat the whites to a stiff, snowy froth, and then gradually add the sugar and vanilla, beating until the mixture is stiff enough to cut with a knife.
2. Cut stout sheets of foolscap paper into strips measuring at least two inches in width, or place the sheet of paper, slightly buttered, on the table.
3. Take a tablespoon and gather up the meringue mixture as nearly as possible in the shape of an egg, and drop slopingly from the spoon upon the strips of paper at a distance of at least an inch apart.
4. Draw the edge of the spoon very sharply around the edge of the meringue, to give it a smooth, round shape, resembling an egg.
5. Sprinkle sifted sugar over them, and let them remain in this state for about three minutes.
6. Take hold of the strips at either end, and shake lightly, to remove all excess of sugar.
7. Place the strips in rows about one inch apart on the wet board or baking tin, and as soon as the sugar begins to dissolve slightly on their surface, push them into the oven, which must be at a very moderate heat, and bake them a very light fawn color.
8. When the meringues are done, remove each one very carefully and separately from the paper.
9. If you desire to fill the meringues, scoop out the soft white part, and smooth the interior of the meringues.
10. Place them with the rounded side downward on the baking tin in the oven to dry for a few minutes.
11. When thoroughly crisp, without browning, place them between sheets of paper in a dry place.
12. When using meringues for desserts, garnish the interior with whipped cream, beaten to a stiff froth, and flavored with vanilla, orange, lemon, orange flower water, or any kind of fancy liqueur, stick the two halves together lightly and serve.
