## Ingredients

- 4 Dozen Bayou Cook Oysters
- The Oyster Liquor
- 1 Large Onion
- 4 Sprigs Parsley
- 1 Quart Boiling Water
- 1 Tablespoonful Lard
- 2 Tablespoonfuls Sifted Flour
- 1 Tablespoonful Butter

## Directions

1. Put the tablespoonful of lard into the soup kettle
2. Have ready one onion and some parsley, chopped very fine
3. When the lard is hot, stir in two tablespoonfuls of sifted flour and make a Brown Roux, stirring constantly to prevent burning
4. When the Roux is of a light brown color, add the chopped onions and parsley, continuing to stir, being exceedingly careful to avoid burning
5. Strain the oyster juice of about four dozen oysters into the Roux, mixing thoroughly to avoid bits of shell
6. Mix with about a quart of boiling water and pour
7. When it shows signs of coming to a boil, add the oysters and a teaspoonful of butter
8. At the boiling point remove from the stove and serve with oyster soda crackers or dry toast
