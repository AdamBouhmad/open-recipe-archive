## Ingredients

- 6 or 8 Oysters to Each Person
- Salt and Cayenne to Taste
- Melted Butter and Chopped Parsley
- Sliced Lemon and Sprigs of Parsley to Garnish

## Directions

1. Allow six or eight oysters for each person. The oysters must be large and fat, else they will shrivel to nothing in cooking.
2. Drain the oysters through a colander, lay them on a dish and wipe with a dry, clean towel.
3. Melt butter and dip in the oysters, seasoned well with salt and Cayenne on both sides.
4. Have ready the gridiron (use always the double wire broiler) and test the heat by dropping a little water on it.
5. If the water hisses, the broiler is quite ready.
6. Place the broiler in a warm place — just over the oven will do.
7. Butter and place the oysters on it and return to moderate coals.
8. As soon as the oysters are browned on one side, turn on the other and brown.
9. Have ready a heated dish and serve the oysters, pouring over melted butter and chopped parsley (chopped very fine).
10. Garnish with sprigs of parsley and sliced lemon and serve immediately.
