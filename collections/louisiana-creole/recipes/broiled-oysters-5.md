## Ingredients

- 3 dozen fine oysters
- 1 tablespoonful of parsley
- 1 bay leaf
- Sprigs of thyme
- 1 sprig of sweet basil
- 2 shallots
- 1 tablespoonful of butter
- Grated bread crumbs
- Asparagus tips
- Sliced lemon
- 1 dozen well washed oyster shells

## Directions

1. Blanch three dozen fine oysters in their own water and drain.
2. Chop a tablespoonful of fine parsley, bay leaf and thyme, using a sprig each of the latter and a sprig of sweet basil.
3. Cut up two nice shallots very fine and add.
4. Place a tablespoonful of butter in a saucepan; add a little oyster juice and sauté, or shake the oysters without making a bouillon.
5. When two minutes have passed, take off the stove.
6. Place the oysters in the well washed shells.
7. Sprinkle over a little bread crumbs, and put on top of each a pinch of butter.
8. Have ready the gridiron or broiler very hot; place the shells between the double broiler, set over the fire to broil for about four or five minutes.
9. Serve with delicate garnishes of asparagus tips and sliced lemon.
