## Ingredients

- Six Fine Slices of Redfish or Red Snapper (equal to 3 Pounds)
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 12 Well-Mashed Allspice
- 3 Sprigs of Thyme
- 3 Sprigs of Parsley
- 3 Sprigs of Sweet Marjoram
- 3 Bay Leaves
- 1 Large Onion
- 1 clove of Garlic
- Large Fresh Tomatoes or a Half Can
- 1 Quart of Water
- 1 Glass of Claret
- The Juice of 1 Lemon
- Salt and Cayenne to Taste

## Directions

1. Slice the Redfish in fine, clear-cut pieces, after having thoroughly washed and cleaned it.
2. Make a Roux by putting one tablespoonful of lard in a deep can or kettle.
3. When hot add gradually two tablespoonfuls of flour, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
4. Throw in about ten or twelve well-mashed allspice, and three sprigs each of chopped thyme, parsley, bay leaf and sweet marjoram, one clove of garlic and one large onion, chopped very fine.
5. Add six fresh, large tomatoes, chopped fine, or one-half can of tomatoes.
6. Pour in one glass of good claret.
7. Add about one quart of water, and let it boil well.
8. Add salt and Cayenne to taste.
9. When this has boiled about five minutes add the fish, putting in slice by slice.
10. Add the juice of a lemon.
11. Let all boil about ten minutes.
12. Serve with French fried potatoes, mashed potatoes or potato croquettes.
