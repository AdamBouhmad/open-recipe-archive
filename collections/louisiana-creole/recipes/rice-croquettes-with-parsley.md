## Ingredients

- 1 Cup of Rice
- 1 Quart of Milk
- 1 Tablespoonful of Chopped Parsley
- The Yolks of 4 Eggs
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Wash the rice well, and put it on to boil in a farina boiler with the milk, or use cold boiled rice, and set it to boil with the milk, after pressing through a sieve.
2. If the rice has not been cooked, let it boil about an hour. If it has already been cooked, twenty minutes will suffice.
3. When very thick take from the fire and beat until very smooth, mashing all the grains.
4. Then add the well-beaten yolks of the eggs, and cook for about eight or ten minutes longer.
5. Add the parsley and seasoning, using the white pepper.
6. Take from the fire and mix well, and turn out on a plate, and let it cool.
7. When cool, form it into pretty cylinders of about three inches in length and one and a half in thickness.
8. Roll these in a beaten egg, to bind, and then in bread crumbs, and fry in boiling lard.
9. Drain and serve with any daube, or with any meat cooked with gravy.
