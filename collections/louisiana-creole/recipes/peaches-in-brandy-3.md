## Ingredients

- Fine peaches
- Three quarters of a pound of white sugar to every pound of fruit
- Water
- A pint of brandy to a pint of the sugar syrup from the peaches
- Rosin

## Directions

1. Soak fine peaches in lye until you can remove the fuzzy outside
2. Wipe them, and turn them into cold water
3. Weigh them, and to every pound of fruit, put three quarters of a pound of white sugar
4. Make a syrup like that for preserves, only using less water
5. Boil the peaches in the syrup until they are tender
6. Take them out of the kettle, and place them in jars
7. Fill up the jars with a brandy syrup, made of a pint of brandy, to a pint of the sugar syrup from the peaches
8. Cook them very carefully, and dip the mouths of the jars in rosin melted
9. Keep them in a cool dark place
