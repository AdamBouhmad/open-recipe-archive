## Ingredients

- A Haunch or Saddle of Venison
- Melted Butter
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- Watercress to Garnish
- Currant Jelly

## Directions

1. Prepare the haunch or saddle of Venison in the same manner as you would the roast beef.
2. Pour a cup of water over the venison when putting in the oven, for it is a dry meat, and requires a little moistening if roasted.
3. Bake in a quick oven, allowing ten minutes to the pound.
4. To prevent the hoof and hair just above changing color in cooking always bind this with a coarse piece of muslin, in four or five pieces of thickness, covering the hoof and hair.
5. Wet with cold water, and bind a buttered paper tightly around and over it.
6. Baste every ten minutes, with melted butter first, and then with the drippings of the Venison.
7. When half cooked, turn the venison over, so that the other side may cook.
8. Unbind the hoofs and garnish them with quilled paper.
9. Place the venison on a dish garnished with Watercress.
10. Serve with Currant Jelly.
