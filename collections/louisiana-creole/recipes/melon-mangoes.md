## Ingredients

- late small smooth green melons
- salt
- water
- sliced horseradish
- very small cucumbers
- nasturtiums
- small white onions
- mustard seed
- whole pepper
- cloves
- allspice
- vinegar
- coarse needle and thread

## Directions

1. Get late small smooth green melons not larger than a teacup
2. Cut out a piece from the stem end large enough to allow you to take the seeds from the inside
3. Scrape out all the soft part and cover with the piece cut out
4. Lay them in rows in a stone or wooden vessel
5. Make a strong brine of salt and water and pour it over the melons
6. Let them remain in the brine twenty-four hours
7. Scald the pickles and cull them
8. Rinse the melons in cold water and wipe each one dry
9. Fill each melon with a cucumber, one or two small onions, sliced horseradish and mustard seed
10. Put on the piece belonging to it and sew it with a coarse needle and thread
11. Lay them in a stone pot or wooden vessel with the cut side up
12. Strew cloves and pepper over them
13. Make vinegar enough to cover them boiling hot and put it over them
14. Cover with a folded towel and let them stand one night
15. Drain off the vinegar, make it hot again and pour it on
16. Repeat this scalding four or five times until the mangoes are a fine green
17. If you wish to keep them till the next summer, choose the most firm, put in a jar and cover with cold fresh vinegar
18. Tie thick paper over them
