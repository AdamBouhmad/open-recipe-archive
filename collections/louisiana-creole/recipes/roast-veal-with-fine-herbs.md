## Ingredients

- 4-Pound Filet of Veal
- 1 Cup of Broth or Water
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 1 Onion
- 3 Bay Leaves
- 2 Sprigs of Sweet Marjoram
- 3 Sprigs of Parsley
- 3 Sprigs of Thyme
- Juice of 1 Lemon
- 1/4 Teaspoonful Each of Ground Cloves, Mace and Allspice
- Salt and Cayenne to taste
- Bread crumbs
- 1 Egg

## Directions

1. Have the butcher cut the filet of veal square, wipe with a damp towel and then take one tablespoonful of lard and dredge the roast nicely.
2. Season well with salt and Cayenne; mince the onion, bay leaf, marjoram, parsley and thyme, and mix these with the ground spices; add the juice of a lemon, and pour all over the meat.
3. Place in a quick oven for about fifteen minutes.
4. After this, lessen the heat, place on top of the veal a buttered piece of brown paper, and let it roast slowly, allowing twenty minutes for each pound of veal.
5. When almost done, take off the paper and let the roast brown nicely for twenty minutes longer.
6. Then take the roast out of the gravy and place in a hot dish in the oven.
7. Take the gravy, stir well, mixing all the herbs that have run out of the beef. Add one cup of broth or water and the juice of one lemon, and mix this thoroughly.
8. Grate some bread crumbs, beat well in one egg, and pour this over the veal, let it brown nicely and serve with the sauce.
