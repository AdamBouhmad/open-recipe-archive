## Ingredients

- 24 pepper mangoes or young melon mangoes or muskmelons
- a piece of alum the size of a pecan
- 1/2 pound of ginger
- 1/4 ounce each of black pepper, mace, allspice, turmeric
- 1/4 pound of garlic
- 1/2 pint of grated horseradish
- 1/2 pint each of black and white mustard seed
- 1/2 teacupful of the best olives
- 1/2 head of fine young cabbage
- 4 green tomatoes
- brine
- pure water
- green grape leaves
- vinegar
- cider vinegar
- 1 pound and 1/2 of brown sugar

## Directions

1. Take two dozen pepper mangoes or young muskmelons that are no larger than an orange
2. Let them lie for two weeks in a brine strong enough to float an egg
3. Soak them in pure water for two days, changing the water every day
4. Remove the seed by cutting a slit down the side of the mangoes, but do not take the meat out
5. Rinse in cold water
6. Put a layer of green grape leaves in a kettle, and lay on this a layer of mangoes, and then a layer of leaves, and so continue till all are used up
7. Let the top layer be of grape leaves
8. Add a piece of alum about the size of a pecan, and pour vinegar over the mixture, and let all boil for fifteen or twenty minutes
9. Take out the leaves, and let the mangoes remain in this vinegar for five or six days
10. Prepare a stuffing of half a pound of ginger which has been soaked in brine for two days and cut into small dice; one-half ounce each of black pepper, mace, allspice, turmeric; one-quarter of a pound of garlic which has been soaked in brine for twenty-four hours and dried; a half pint of grated horseradish, and a half pint each of black and white mustard seed
11. Bruise all the spices well till fine, and mix them with a half teacupful of the best olive oil
12. Take a half head of fine solid cabbage, and chop fine; add a half pint of finely-chopped onions, a few small cucumbers, and a few green tomatoes which have lain in a brine overnight
13. Drain all well and then select any imperfectly shaped mangoes, or any that have become too soft to stuff, and chop these fine with the spices
14. Mix all this stuffing well together, and stuff the mangoes
15. Tie them with pieces of twine, or sew them up, put in a stone jar, and pour over them the very best cider vinegar
16. Set them in a dry place and cover well
17. At the end of a month add a pound and a half of brown sugar, and bottle for use
