## Ingredients

- 1 Pound of Brown Sugar
- 1 Pound of Butter
- 10 Eggs
- 2 Pounds of Seeded Raisins
- 2 Pounds of Currants
- 1 Pound of Citron
- 1/4 Pound of Almonds
- 1 Nutmeg
- 1 Teaspoonful Each of Ground Allspice and Cinnamon
- 1/8 Teaspoonful Each of Ground Mace and Cloves
- 3/4 Pound of Sifted Flour
- The Zest Each of an Orange and Lemon
- 1/4 Teaspoonful of Salt
- The Juice of 1 Orange and 1 Lemon
- 2 Teaspoonfuls of Baking Powder
- 1 Wineglassful of Good French Brandy
- 1 Wineglassful of Sherry Wine or Jamaica Rum

## Directions

1. Prepare the fruit by seeding the raisins, washing and drying the currants, cutting the citron into shreds and dice, and mashing the almonds.
2. Beat the butter and sugar to a cream and add the eggs, which you will first beat, without separating whites and yolks, till very light.
3. Beat these into the sugar and butter till very light and then add the flour gradually and the spices.
4. Sift the flour with the baking powder and salt, and then mix the fruit into it before adding to the cake.
5. Beat vigorously.
6. Then add the grated zest of the orange and lemon, and the juices of both.
7. Stir well and add the brandy and wine or rum.
8. Mix well.
9. Divide the batter into two pans, which you will have lined with greased paper, and bake in a steadily moderate oven for four hours.
10. Use the broom wisp test, and if it comes out clean and dry the cake is done.
11. If not, let the cake bake from a quarter to a half hour longer, watching carefully.
12. When done, put the cake into an earthenware bowl, with a flat bottom.
13. Pour over a pint of the best brandy and half a pint of Sherry Wine.
14. Make the vessel air tight by tying around a linen cloth, and put on the cover.
15. Set the cake away for about three weeks, and then open and turn.
16. Let it remain three weeks longer, and the cake is ready to cut.
17. If you wish to use it sooner, do not add the liquor after baking, but set the cake away to cool in the pan in which it was baked.
18. Then place in a cool place after taking out and removing the paper, and wait eight days before cutting.
