## Ingredients

- Figs
- Sugar
- Water
- Lemons
- Ginger

## Directions

1. Take the weight in sugar of the quantity of figs to be preserved; the figs must be ripe.
2. Cover the figs with cold water for twelve hours.
3. Simmer in water enough to cover them until tender, and spread out upon a sieve to cool and harden.
4. Make a syrup of the sugar, a cup of cold water being allowed for every pound.
5. Boil until clear of scum, put in the figs, and simmer for ten minutes.
6. Take them out and spread upon the dishes in the sun.
7. Add the lemons and the ginger; boil the syrup thick, give the figs another boil of fifteen minutes and fill the jars three-quarters of the way up to the top.
8. Then, fill up with boiling syrup, cover, and, when cold, seal up.
