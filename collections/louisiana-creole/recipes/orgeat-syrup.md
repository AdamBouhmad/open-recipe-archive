## Ingredients

- 1 Pound of Sweet Almonds
- 4 Ounces of Bitter Almonds
- 2 Pounds of Sugar
- 1 Quart of Water
- 2 Ounces of Orange Flower Water
- The Zest of a Lemon

## Directions

1. Throw the almonds into cold water after shelling them until the peeling or skin comes off easily
2. Mash them and pound them in a mortar till they are reduced to a fine powder, adding from time to time a little water and the well-grated zest of the lemon
3. Moisten the paste with one-half of the water, and then squeeze it hard through a linen cloth
4. Return the paste to the mortar, throw over the rest of the water, mix thoroughly, pounding well, and then squeeze again through the towel
5. Put the sugar into a farina boiler, and let it boil to the degree of Le Petit Cassé, or crack, that is, till it forms a thick syrup that will not cling to the teeth in tasting, or instantly snaps asunder between the fingers after testing in cold water
6. Take the syrup off the fire and add the milk of almonds, stirring well
7. Return it to the fire, and let it simmer gently till it begins to boil
8. Let it boil for several minutes
9. Take it off the fire and let it cool
10. When cold, add the orange flower water and mix well
11. Drain all through a cloth or bag, and fill and seal the bottles
12. Shake the bottles daily to preserve the exact mingling of the mixture
