## Ingredients

- two pounds of veal
- half as much beef or lamb
- one small chicken cut up
- three quarts of water
- two sliced aleeks or onions
- grated large carrot or two small ones
- two tablespoonfuls of salt
- one of pepper
- a spoonful of butter worked in flour

## Directions

1. Take two pounds of veal, half as much beef or lamb, and one small chicken cut up; boil them in three quarts of water, skim off all the scum as it rises; slice aleek or two onions, grate a large carrot or two small ones; put all these to the soup; add two tablespoonfuls of salt and one of pepper. Let it boil gently for two hours, then add a spoonful of butter worked in flour; cover this for fifteen minutes, and serve in a tureen. Take the chicken into a deep dish, put over it butter, pepper, and sprigs of parsley; or you may chop the chicken up, season with pepper, salt, butter, and an egg; form into balls, roll them in flour, and drop them in a few minutes before serving.
