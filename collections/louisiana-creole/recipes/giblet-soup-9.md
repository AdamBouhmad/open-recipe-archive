## Ingredients

- 1 Hard-Boiled Yolk for Each Person
- 2 1/2 Cups of Chicken Broth
- 3 Quarts of Boiling Water or Broth
- 1 Onion, Carrot and 1/2 Turnip, chopped
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Port or Madeira Wine
- Parsley
- Juice of 1 Lemon
- 1 Leaf each of Sage and Bay
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour and 1 of Butter
- The Giblets, heart, liver, etc., of Two Turkeys or Four Chickens
- Pepper
- Salt
- Cayenne pepper

## Directions

1. Chop the onion fine and put it into the stewpan with the butter; let it brown, and then add the chopped vegetables, whole giblets, etc.
2. Fry until nicely browned; but do not let it burn.
3. Slit the giblets with a knife, that the juices may run out in boiling, and put all into the soup kettle, with pepper, salt, sage, parsley, and the three quarts of consomme or boiling water.
4. Add bones or lean meat, cooked or raw, that are left, preferably the meat of the chicken, and let all simmer for five hours.
5. Then strain.
6. Mash one liver fine and add it to the broth; season with Cayenne pepper, lemon juice to taste, and two tablespoonfuls of Madeira or Port wine.
7. Boil three minutes, and have in the tureen one hard-boiled yolk of an egg for each person.
8. Pour the soup over it and serve hot.
