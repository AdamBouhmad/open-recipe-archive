## Ingredients

- a quart of nice fresh milk
- the yolks of eight eggs
- a pound of fine white sugar
- extract, either of lemon, orange, peach or vanilla
- one quart of pure cream
- ice, pounded fine
- coarse dairy salt

## Directions

1. Put on the fire a stew-pan containing a quart of nice fresh milk.
2. While it is coming to the boil beat the yolks of eight eggs and a pound of fine white sugar.
3. When these are well beaten, take off the boiling milk, let it stand to cool five minutes, and pour it very hot over the eggs and sugar.
4. Strain this mixture, and add for flavoring any favorite extract, either of lemon, orange, peach or vanilla.
5. Let it stand to get cool, and pour it into the freezer and surround it with layers of ice, pounded fine, and coarse dairy salt, well beaten down.
6. Fill up till within a few inches of the top of the freezer.
7. Pour in one quart of pure cream, and beat it with a wooden spoon into the mixture in the freezer.
8. Turn the crank of your freezer briskly if you have a five minute freezer; if not, turn the can with your hand for fifteen minutes.
9. Pack round again with ice and salt.
10. Draw off the melted ice and salt water, and fill up again and set away to harden before serving.
