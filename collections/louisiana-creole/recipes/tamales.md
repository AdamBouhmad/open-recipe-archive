## Ingredients

- 1 Tender Young Chicken
- 1 Cup of Boiled Irish Potatoes
- 1 Large Onion
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 2 Sprigs of Parsley
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- 1 Spoon of Butter
- 1 Cup of Cracker Crumbs
- Salt and Pepper, to Taste
- A Good Dash of Cayenne
- Corn-shuck leaves

## Directions

1. Boil a young chicken until the meat can be picked off the bones
2. Chop up very fine
3. Mince the onion, clove of garlic, thyme, bay leaf, and parsley, very fine
4. Put the onion and butter into a saucepan and let brown slightly
5. Add the minced garlic, thyme, parsley and bay leaf
6. Add the chicken immediately and one cup of mashed potatoes and grated cracker crumbs
7. Stir well, and season to taste with salt and Chili pepper
8. Add a good dash of Cayenne
9. Mix all well by stirring and let cook for five minutes
10. Have ready a pot of boiling water and about a dozen nice, clean corn-shuck leaves
11. Cut the leaves into nice oblong shapes, and divide the chicken mixture into equal parts
12. Roll each of these parts into a corn leaf and tie, and immerse in boiling water for five minutes
13. Drain off the water, arrange the husks on a dish and send to the table hot
