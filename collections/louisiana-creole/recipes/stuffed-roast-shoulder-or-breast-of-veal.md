## Ingredients

- 1 Shoulder of Veal
- 1/4 Pound of Ham
- 1 Herb Bouquet
- 1 Hard-Boiled Egg
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Spoon of Flour
- 1 Cup of stale bread
- Salt pork or ham, preferably ham
- 1 Carrot
- 1 Onion
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- 1 Sprig of Parsley
- Hot pepper to taste
- 1/4 Glass of white wine or 2 Tablespoonfuls
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of beef broth
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- Salt and Pepper
- Lemon juice
- Prepared mustard (optional)

## Directions

1. If the shoulder of the veal is used, have the butcher remove the bone.
2. When ready to cook, wipe well with a damp towel, and then dredge with salt and pepper, seasoning highly.
3. Make a dressing by taking one cup of stale bread, wet and squeezed, one-quarter pound of sliced ham or salt pork, preferably the ham, and chop very fine.
4. Mix this with the bread and season highly with hot pepper, according to taste; add 1 carrot, 1 onion, 1 bay leaf, 1 hard-boiled egg, 1 sprig of thyme, 1 of parsley, all minced very fine.
5. Add one tablespoonful of butter, and place in a frying pan; let it fry for about ten minutes and then add, if you have it, a quarter of a glass of white wine or two tablespoonfuls, and two tablespoonfuls of beef broth; stir well and cook for five minutes longer.
6. Then stuff the shoulder well and skewer the filet to prevent the dressing from falling out in cooking. It is well to tie the veal at either end with a piece of twine.
7. Take one tablespoonful of lard and dredge roast according to preceding recipes.
8. If the breast of the veal is used, make long gashes between the ribs and fill with a dressing prepared as above, place in the baking pan and roast slowly, according to directions.
9. When finished cooking, remove the shoulder or breast, and stir the gravy well, adding a cup of broth or water and the juice of one lemon and a teaspoonful of butter, seasoning to taste.
10. Serve with the roast. Some like the addition of a teaspoonful of prepared mustard, but that is a matter of taste.
