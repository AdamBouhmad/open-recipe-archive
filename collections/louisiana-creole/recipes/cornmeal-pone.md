## Ingredients

- 1 Quart of the Finest Indian Meal
- 1 Teaspoonful of Salt
- A Heaping Teaspoonful of Lard or Butter
- 1 Pint of Sour Milk or Buttermilk
- 3 Eggs

## Directions

1. Pour just enough boiling water over the meal to scald it through and through, without making a dough or batter.
2. Stir it well, and let it grow tepid.
3. It will be then quite dry.
4. Beat two eggs very light, and add to the meal, and add a melted tablespoonful of lard or butter.
5. Mix this well, and add the milk or buttermilk, and beat till the batter is very smooth.
6. Dissolve one teaspoonful of soda in a little boiling water, and add to the mixture, and grease a baking pan.
7. Turn the mixture in and bake in a quick oven for about thirty-five minutes, till a golden brown.
8. Take the pone from the pan whole, and place on a hot platter, and serve, cutting it at the table into slices about three inches long and two wide, and serving with butter.
