## Ingredients

- 12 Mutton Feet
- ½ Cup of Wet Bread
- 1 Hard-Boiled Egg
- 1 Spoon of Butter
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 3 Thin Slices of Veal
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- 3 Carrots
- 2 Onions
- The Juice of 1 Lemon
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Clean the mutton feet well, according to directions given, and boil and take out all the bones.
2. Take a half cup of wet bread and squeeze well.
3. Season well with salt and pepper and fry in a little butter and add a chopped egg.
4. Stuff the feet with this, splitting down the length and sewing up to prevent the dressing escaping.
5. Take a saucepan and put in one tablespoonful of lard and lay over it thin slices of veal, well seasoned.
6. Add one bay leaf, one sprig of thyme and geranium (minced very fine), three chopped carrots, and two onions (chopped very fine).
7. Pour over this the juice of a lemon and let it simmer gently for about a half hour, turning the veal that it may cook well and be thoroughly penetrated by the juices.
8. Put the stuffed mutton feet on top, cover closely, and let all simmer for a half hour longer.
9. Unsew the mutton feet, lay them on the slices of veal, garnish nicely and serve with a Sauce à l'Espagnole, Sauce aux Tomates, a Sauce aux Oignons, or Sauce à la Provençale.
