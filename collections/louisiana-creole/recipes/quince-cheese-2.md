## Ingredients

- fine ripe quinces
- brown sugar (1/2 lb. per lb. of cut quinces)
- water

## Directions

1. Take fine ripe quinces, pare and core them
2. Cut them into pieces and weigh them
3. To each lb. of the cut quinces allow 1/2 lb. of the best brown sugar
4. Put the cores and parings into a kettle with water enough to cover them, keeping the lid of the kettle closed
5. When they are all boiled to pieces and quite soft, strain off the water over the sugar
6. When it is entirely dissolved, put it over the fire and boil it to a thick syrup, skimming it well
7. When no more scum rises, put in the quinces
8. Cover them closely, and boil them all day over a slow fire, stirring them and mashing them down with a spoon till they are a thick smooth paste
9. Take it out and put it into buttered deep pans or dishes
10. Let it set to get cold
11. It will then turn out so firm that you may cut it into slices like cheese
12. Keep it in a dry place in broad stone pots
