## Ingredients

- 2 Fine Spring Chickens
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 6 Large Fresh Tomatoes, or 1/2 Can
- 6 Fresh, Sweet Green Peppers
- 2 Cloves of Garlic
- 3 Large Onions
- 3 Sprigs Each of Thyme and Parsley
- 2 Bay Leaves
- 1 Pint of Consomme or Boiling Water
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Take two spring chickens and clean nicely and cut into pieces at the joints.
2. Season well with salt and pepper.
3. Put two tablespoonfuls of butter into a stewpan, and when it melts, add the chicken.
4. Let this brown slowly for a good five minutes.
5. Add three large onions sliced and let them brown. Every inch must be nicely browned, but not in the slightest degree burned.
6. Add two tablespoonfuls of flour; let this brown.
7. Add a half dozen large, fresh tomatoes nicely sliced, or a half can of tomatoes, and let these brown.
8. Cook very slowly, allowing the mixture to simply simmer.
9. Add chopped parsley, thyme and bay leaf and two cloves of garlic finely minced.
10. Let all brown without burning.
11. Cover and let it smother over a slow but steady fire.
12. If you have sweet green peppers, add a half dozen, taking the seeds out before adding and slicing the peppers very fine.
13. Stir well.
14. Let all smother steadily for twenty minutes at least, keeping well covered and stirring occasionally.
15. Add one cup of Consomme, if you have it; if not, one cup of boiling water.
16. Let it cook again for a full half hour, very, very slowly over a very steady fire, and season again to taste.
17. Cook ten minutes more, and serve hot.
