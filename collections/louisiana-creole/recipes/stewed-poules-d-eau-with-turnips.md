## Ingredients

- 1 Pair of Poules d'Eau
- 6 Turnips
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 2 Onions Chopped Fine
- 1 Square Inch of Ham, Minced Very Fine
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 3 Sprigs Each of Thyme and Parsley
- Water

## Directions

1. Clean the Poules d'Eau and cut into pieces at the joints.
2. Put a tablespoonful of butter into the pot, and as it melts add the onions, chopped fine.
3. Let this brown and then add the pieces of Poules d'Eau.
4. Let them brown, and add the minced ham. (Omit the ham on fast days.)
5. Immediately after add the turnips, sliced or cut in quarters, and a tablespoonful of sifted flour.
6. Stir well, let it brown slightly, and add the minced thyme, parsley and bay leaf, and one clove of garlic, minced very fine.
7. Stir well again, and let it smother for about fifteen minutes, stirring frequently, so that it will not burn.
8. Then add water almost sufficient to cover the Poules d'Eau, and stir well.
9. Cover tight and let the mixture smother for a half hour longer.
