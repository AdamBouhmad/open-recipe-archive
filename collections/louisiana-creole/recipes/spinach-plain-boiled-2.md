## Ingredients

- ½ Peck of Fresh Young Spinach
- 1 Cupful of Water
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Select young and tender spinach and carefully pick it over, rejecting all wilted leaves and coarse fibers and nerves.
2. Cut off the roots.
3. Wash and drain it well in cold water, and press out all the water.
4. Put the spinach in a kettle, and add one cupful of water.
5. Place on a moderate fire and let it simmer for ten minutes.
6. As soon as it appears to wilt, take off the fire, for that is all the cooking that is needed.
7. Then drain the spinach of all water through a colander, and then press through a very fine sieve, to drain off the remaining water from the vegetable.
8. Chop the spinach very fine.
9. Then put a tablespoonful of butter in a saucepan, and add the spinach to it.
10. Stir well.
11. Add a few spoonfuls of water, just sufficient to thin, if a little thick, and let it heat thoroughly.
12. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
