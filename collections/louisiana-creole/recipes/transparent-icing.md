## Ingredients

- 1/2 Pound of Pine White Sugar
- Half of the White of 1 Egg
- The Juice of Half a Lemon
- 1 Tablespoonful of Any Fancy Liqueur

## Directions

1. Take a half pound of the finest white clarified sugar, and clarify again by boiling it with half of the white of an egg, beaten to a stiff froth
2. Clear off the scum as it rises, and continue skimming till the sugar is perfectly clear
3. Then add to the sugar half a gill of clarified sugar, boiled to a syrup, and to which you will have added the juice of a quarter or half a lemon
4. Strain, and add to the sugar enough Liqueur, Curaçoa, Noyau, Cedrati or Maraschino to flavor the icing to taste, and beat or whip it to the proper consistency
5. Then take a silver tablespoon and stir the sugar well together, rubbing up and against the sides of the pan till it acquires an opaline appearance
6. Then take a silver fork, lift one of the cakes intended to be iced, dip in the sugar, or else hold it on a fork and with a spoon pour the icing over
7. Take another fork, lift off the cake and place it on the baking sheet, and so on till all the small cakes are iced
8. Then dry in the sun or in the oven for about ten minutes
