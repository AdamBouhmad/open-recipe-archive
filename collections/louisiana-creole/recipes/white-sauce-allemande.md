## Ingredients

- 4 Pounds of Raw Veal
- The Bones of a Chicken
- 1 Gallon of Water
- 1 Carrot
- 1 Turnip
- Celery Tops
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 2 Spoonfuls of Lard
- 1 Herb Bouquet of Thyme, Parsley, Bay Leaf
- 1 Stalk of Celery
- 2 Long Carrots
- 1 Wineglassful of Madeira or Sherry Wine

## Directions

1. Take the veal and the bones of the chicken and put into a pot with a gallon of water
2. Add the herb bouquet, tied together, and one chopped carrot, one turnip chopped, celery tops, and other ingredients of a good pot-au-feu
3. Let all boil slowly for three hours until it is reduced one-half
4. Salt and pepper to taste
5. Take off the fire and strain the broth into a jar
6. Take two tablespoonfuls of butter and three of flour, and put into a saucepan together, letting the butter and flour blend, without browning
7. Add all the broth to this, stirring slowly while on the fire
8. Add a good, strong bouquet of herbs, thyme, parsley and bay leaf, all tied whole together
9. Add two large carrots, and let it boil till reduced to one-half again
10. Season to taste
11. When it has reached the consistency of starch take off the fire and strain and let it get cool
12. This sauce can be used for all white meats and fish
