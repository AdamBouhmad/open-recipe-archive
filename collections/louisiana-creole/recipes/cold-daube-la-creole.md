## Ingredients

- Three pounds of the rump or round of beef
- Two pounds of veal rump
- Two pigs' feet
- Half pound of salt fat meat
- Five large onions
- Two turnips
- Five carrots
- Two cloves of garlic
- Three bay leaves
- Tablespoonful of lard
- Glass of sherry
- Three sprigs of thyme and parsley
- Salt, pepper and cayenne
- Three cloves
- Six allspice

## Directions

1. Cut the salt meat into shreds, roll well in cayenne and black pepper
2. Chop finely several sprigs of thyme and three bay leaves, one clove of garlic, three sprigs of parsley
3. Mash well three cloves and six allspice
4. Roll the strips of salt meat, which must be about three inches in length and one-half inch thick, in this mixture
5. Make incision into the rump of meat and force in the strips of fat meat and the spices
6. Rub the whole well with salt and pepper, judging according to taste
7. Proceed to cook according to the recipe for Beef à la Mode
8. Let the daube cook about four hours when you intend to serve it cold
9. In another pot, place a veal steak of about two pounds, and two pigs' feet
10. Season well with salt and pepper and cayenne, and cover well with four quarts of water
11. Let them boil and add one bay leaf, one sprig of thyme, one-half clove of garlic and one onion, all minced very fine
12. Add two cloves mashed into almost a jelly, and one glass of sherry or Madeira wine
13. Let these boil well with the veal and pigs' feet
14. When the veal and pigs' feet are cooked very tender, take them out of the pot and mince the meat of each very fine
15. Return to the sauce, and again season highly, according to taste
16. After the daube has cooked four or five hours, take off the stove and pour over the sauce and set all in a cool place
17. Serve the next day cold, cutting into thin slices
