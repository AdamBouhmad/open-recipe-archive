## Ingredients

- 1 Cup of Rice
- 3 Dozen Crawfish
- 2 Ounces of Butter
- 1 Onion
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 2 Sprigs of Thyme
- 2 Sprigs of Parsley
- 1 Bay Leaf
- Juice of Half a Lemon
- 1 Tablespoonful of Curry Powder
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- 1 Quart of Water
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- A Dash of Cayenne

## Directions

1. Boil the crawfish according to recipe.
2. Clean and pick the crawfish the same as for a fricassee.
3. Put two ounces of butter in the frying pan.
4. Cut one onion in slices, add it to the butter, letting it brown nicely.
5. Then add the well-seasoned crawfish, and fry them to a golden brown.
6. Add one clove of garlic, finely minced, and minced thyme, parsley and bay leaf.
7. Let this brown.
8. After five minutes, add a quart of boiling water.
9. Stir well.
10. Season to taste with salt, pepper and a dash of Cayenne.
11. Simmer gently until the crawfish are very tender.
12. When done, add the juice of half a lemon, and mix one even tablespoonful of Curry Powder and one of flour with a little water.
13. Bring it to a smooth paste by rubbing well, and add it to the crawfish.
14. Stir constantly, and let it boil five minutes longer.
15. Serve with a border of boiled rice heaped around it.
