## Ingredients

- A Half Dozen Picayune Frogs
- 3 Eggs
- A Cup of Sifted Bread Crumbs
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- 2 Gills of Milk or Fresh Cream
- 1/2 Teaspoonful of Salt
- 1/4 of an Onion Chopped Very Fine
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- Lemon Juice
- Lard for frying
- Parsley

## Directions

1. Take a half dozen Picayune Frogs, the more delicate the better
2. Cut off the hind legs and skin
3. Scald the legs about four minutes in boiling water, adding salt and lemon juice to the water
4. Take out and dry with a clean towel
5. Beat the yolks and whites of two or three eggs, and mix in bread crumbs sifted
6. Have ready a pot of heated lard
7. Rub the Frog legs well with pepper and salt, and put into the pot of hot lard
8. Let them fry to a nice golden brown
9. Make a sauce: Put one tablespoonful of butter in a saucepan over the fire
10. As soon as it melts, add the flour, which has been rubbed smoothly
11. Stir in gradually, and when blended, add by degrees the boiling milk, stirring constantly to prevent burning
12. Take a teaspoonful of chopped parsley and a half of a chopped onion that has been well grated
13. Have ready two fresh eggs, beaten in a bowl
14. Warm the Frogs' legs in the sauce, and when it begins to simmer stir the sauce briskly into the eggs
15. Return to the back of the stove a minute or two, being careful not to let the sauce boil after the eggs have been added, else they will curdle the sauce
16. Serve hot
