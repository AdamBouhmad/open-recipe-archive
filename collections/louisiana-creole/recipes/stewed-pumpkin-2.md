## Ingredients

- 2 pints of pumpkin or cashaw
- 4 tablespoonfuls of butter
- 1 teaspoonful of salt
- 1 teaspoonful of ground mace
- 1 teaspoonful of ground cinnamon
- 1 teaspoonful of ground cloves
- 2 gills of good whisky or brandy
- Sugar to taste

## Directions

1. Cut the pumpkin into halves and then into quarters, and pare.
2. Cut it into pieces of about one inch square.
3. Place them all in a deep pot, and add sufficient water to cover.
4. Let the pumpkin stew slowly for about an hour, stirring frequently to prevent burning.
5. Take out of the pot, press through a colander, and set back on the stove.
6. Add, for every pint of pumpkin, two large tablespoonfuls of butter, and a half teaspoonful of salt, one of ground mace, one of ground cinnamon and one of ground allspice.
7. Add a gill of good whiskey or brandy.
8. Mix all thoroughly, and add sugar to taste.
9. Let it simmer slowly for half an hour longer, and serve hot with daubes, Grillades a la Sauce, etc.
