## Ingredients

- 6 Fine Snipe
- 6 Slices of Buttered French Toast
- 6 Strips of Bacon
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 1 Sprig Each of Thyme and Parsley
- 1 Bay Leaf
- Sliced Lemon and Sprigs of Parsley or Watercress to Garnish
- Salt and Pepper
- Truffle (optional)
- Four Spices (pinch)

## Directions

1. Prepare the birds as in the Invariable Rule for Roasting Birds
2. Rub with salt and pepper and melted butter
3. If desired, put a truffle into each bird for stuffing, and in all put a little lump of butter and a little salt and pepper, a pinch of chopped thyme, parsley and bay leaf, and a small pinch of the four spices
4. Bind with strips of bacon, and place in a baking pan with a tablespoonful of butter
5. Let them roast thirty minutes or less, according to size
6. Remove and place always on buttered French toast on a hot dish, and cover and set over a pot of boiling water to keep warm and juicy
7. Meanwhile prepare a gravy by adding a tablespoonful of water to the gravy made in cooking the birds
8. Let it cook for two minutes, then strain and let it cook for two minutes more
9. Pour a little upon the breast of each bird so that it will soak down into the toast
10. Garnish nicely with sprigs of parsley or watercress and slices of lemon, and serve hot
