## Ingredients

- The Hind Quarter of a Lamb
- 3 Sprigs Each of Thyme, Parsley and Bay Leaf
- 6 Minced Shallots
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Cup of Grated Bread Crumbs
- The Juice of 1 Lemon
- Parsley and Sliced Lemon to Garnish

## Directions

1. Select a fine, white hind-quarter of the lamb; lard it in the same manner as given for larding 'Roast Filet of Beef.' (See recipe.)
2. Rub well with butter on top and sprinkle over thickly with the soft of bread crumbs, minced parsley, thyme, bay leaf, salt and pepper and minced shallots.
3. Set in the stove and cover with a buttered brown paper.
4. Let it roast; allowing eighteen or twenty minutes to the pound, in a quick oven, and when done, take off the paper.
5. Sprinkle again lightly with grated bread crumbs; let it brown and set in a hot dish.
6. Sprinkle over with lemon juice; garnish the dish with sprigs of parsley and sliced lemon, and serve, carving in slices and placing a quarter of a lemon on each plate.
