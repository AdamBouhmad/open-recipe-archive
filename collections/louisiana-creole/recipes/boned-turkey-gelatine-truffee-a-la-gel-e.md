## Ingredients

- 1 Young Turkey Hen
- 2 Pounds of Young Veal
- 1 Pound of Fat Fresh Pork
- 1 Pound of Lean Fresh Pork
- 1/2 Pound of Cooper's Gelatine
- 1/2 Can of Truffles
- 1 Tablespoonful Each of Minced Parsley and Thyme
- 3 Minced Bay Leaves
- 1 Lemon
- 3 Sprigs Each of Thyme and Bay Leaf
- 1 Glass of Brandy
- 1 Wineglass of Sherry
- 2 Carrots
- 1 Turnip
- 1 Stalk of Celery
- 2 Gallons of Water
- 1/2 Teaspoonful Each of Grated Cinnamon and Allspice
- 1/2 Nutmeg
- Salt and Black Pepper
- 2 Raw Eggs
- 1 Onion

## Directions

1. Select a young hen turkey that has been hand-picked and not scalded
2. Clean it thoroughly and place on table with breast down
3. Cut the turkey open from the neck to the rump down the backbone with a sharp knife
4. Run the knife between the bones and flesh to the wings, unjointing and separating bones from body without breaking flesh
5. Remove each bone as you reach the joint, leaving the small bones in the wing tips
6. Carefully slit out the bones of the leg and run the knife between bones and flesh to the breast bone
7. Skillfully separate the flesh from the bone and remove the carcass
8. Spread out the turkey and wipe inside and out with a damp towel
9. Rub well with salt and pepper inside and out, then set aside in a cool place
10. Take two pounds of young veal, one pound of young fat fresh pork, and one pound of lean fresh pork and mince as fine as possible
11. Season the minced meat with one-half grated nutmeg, one tablespoonful minced parsley, one tablespoonful minced thyme, three minced bay leaves, one teaspoonful salt, one teaspoonful black pepper, one teaspoonful grated cinnamon, one-quarter teaspoonful grated allspice, and the juice of one onion
12. Mix thoroughly and add two raw beaten eggs, one wineglass of Sherry, and one of Brandy, stirring well
13. Add one-quarter of a box of chopped truffles and mix well
14. Lay the turkey open and carefully cut a layer of meat in nice slices from the inner part
15. Put in a thick layer of stuffing, then a layer of the meat using the whole liver sliced in strips as alternate layers
16. Put in the rest of the stuffing as a layer and bring the turkey together
17. Sew up the turkey so it retains its original shape
18. Roll the turkey in a clean towel and tie it securely at both ends and around the middle
19. Take all the bones of the turkey, skinned feet, cleaned head, and place in a large pot
20. Add two pounds of veal cut in pieces and two calves' feet
21. Put in two carrots, one turnip, several sprigs of thyme and parsley, three bay leaves, and a large piece of celery
22. Add two gallons of water and let boil very hard for an hour
23. Add the turkey tied in the towel and let it boil for two hours
24. Remove the turkey from the towel and roll the towel out very smoothly again
25. Place the turkey back in it while hot and roll carefully again, tying at both ends and across the middle
26. Place on a table with a board on top and a fifteen-pound weight over it
27. Leave in a cool place but not in the ice box to cool gradually and naturally
28. After five or six hours you may put it in the ice box
29. Let the pot with water and bones continue boiling for two hours longer
30. Strain the whole through a strainer into another pan, then strain through a towel to remove cinnamon, herbs, and dregs
31. Skim off all grease from the top and put on fire again to simmer
32. Add one lemon cut in four or five pieces and season with salt to taste
33. Put one-quarter pound of Cooper's gelatine in a bowl and add one pint of water in which the turkey was boiled
34. Stir well and let the gelatine melt
35. Pour the melted gelatine into the boiling mixture and beat as you would a cake, mixing thoroughly
36. Break three raw eggs in another bowl and mash the shells and add
37. Add one small wineglass of water and beat well again as you would a cake
38. Prepare a flannel bag of size sufficient to hold a half gallon
39. Pour the eggs into the broth beating very thoroughly and rapidly through and through for ten minutes while it remains on the fire
40. When the mixture begins to boil and curdle, take off the fire and strain in the flannel bag
41. Let this fall into tin cups or cans and set on ice
42. The next morning it will be hard
43. Unroll the turkey and put it in a dish
44. Cut the jelly from the can into fancy shapes and ornament the turkey with it, placing a fancy border around the dish
