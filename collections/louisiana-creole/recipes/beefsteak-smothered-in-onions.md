## Ingredients

- 3 Pounds of Round Steak
- 6 Onions, sliced fine
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Vinegar
- 2 Sprigs each of Thyme and Bay Leaf
- 4 Sprigs of Parsley
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 1 Pint of Water
- Pepper and Salt to Taste

## Directions

1. Beat the round steak well with the rolling pin or steak hammer; cut off the outer skin and press the meat back into shape
2. Place the tablespoonful of lard in the deep frying pan and let it melt
3. Lay in the sliced onions, and over these the beefsteak, which has been well seasoned with salt and pepper and dredged with the flour
4. Cover closely and let it simmer over a hot fire for a few minutes
5. Turn the steak on the other side and after three minutes, add two tablespoonfuls of vinegar, chopped parsley, thyme and bay leaf and a clove of garlic
6. Turn the steak, letting the flour brown well, and keep the pot closely covered
7. When brown pour over one cup of water, or a pint, which will be sufficient to cover the meat
8. Bring this to a brisk boil and set the pot back where it can simmer gently for about two hours
