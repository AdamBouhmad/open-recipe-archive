## Ingredients

- 3 Fresh Apples
- 1 Gill of Brandy or Rum (if desired)
- Grated Peel of 1/2 Lemon
- Powdered Sugar
- Fritter Batter a la Creole

## Directions

1. Peel and core the apples, which will be all the nicer if they are a little tart.
2. Cut them into slices, more or less thick or thin, according to taste.
3. Soak them in brandy or good whisky, or rum, for the space of two hours, sprinkling with the grated zest of a lemon and sugar, according to judgment.
4. Make a batter a la Creole and have ready a deep saucepan of boiling lard.
5. Drain the apples.
6. Dip the slices, one at a time, into the batter, lift out with a large kitchen spoon, drop into the boiling lard, and fry to a golden brown.
7. Lift out with a skimmer, and set on brown paper, in the mouth of the oven, and drain.
8. Sift powdered white sugar over them, and serve hot, piling high in pyramidal shape, and sprinkling again with powdered white sugar.
