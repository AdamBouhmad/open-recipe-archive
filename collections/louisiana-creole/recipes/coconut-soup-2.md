## Ingredients

- 6 or 8 Calves' Feet
- 1/2 Pound of Grated Coconut
- 1 Gallon of Water
- 1 Pint of Cream or Rich Milk
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour or Arrowroot
- 3 Ounces of Fresh Butter
- Blades of Mace
- The Grated Rind of 1 Lemon
- Nutmeg
- French rolls or milk biscuit

## Directions

1. Scald the calves' feet, and scrape thoroughly without skinning.
2. Put them into the soup kettle with a gallon of cold water, and cover the kettle well.
3. Let the feet come to a slow boil and skim carefully.
4. Add the blades of mace and let the soup boil slowly till the meat is reduced to rags and has fallen from the bones.
5. Strain into a white porcelain dish or pan, and set it away to cool.
6. After it has congealed, scrape off fat and sediment, and a beautiful jelly will remain.
7. Cut up this cake of jelly and put it into a thoroughly cleansed, white porcelain soup kettle.
8. Grate the coconut very fine, till about a half pound is on hand.
9. Mix this with the pint of rich cream or milk, and add the butter which has been rolled smoothly in the arrowroot or flour.
10. Mix this carefully and gradually with the calves' feet stock or soup, and season with grated nutmeg.
11. Set back on the fire and allow to boil slowly for about fifteen minutes, stirring almost constantly.
12. Pour into the tureen and serve with French rolls, or milk biscuit, made very light and thin.
