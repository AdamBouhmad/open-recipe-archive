## Ingredients

- 1 pair chickens
- salt
- boiling water
- blades of mace
- pepper corns
- celery
- onion
- milk
- 2 teaspoons flour
- 1 lb. fresh butter
- 1 pint boiling milk
- madeira or sherry
- nutmeg
- 4 tablespoons rich cream

## Directions

1. Cut a pair of chickens and wash the pieces through 2 or 3 waters
2. Lay them in a large pan and sprinkle slightly with salt
3. Fill the pan with boiling water, cover and let the chicken stand in it half an hour
4. Put them into a stewpan and add a few blades of mace and pepper corns (whole)
5. Add a handful of celery split thin and chopped fine and a small onion sliced
6. Pour on cold water and milk in equal quantities to cover the chicken
7. Cover the pan and let them stew until quite done and tender
8. Prepare gravy in a small saucepan by mixing 2 teaspoons of flour with enough cold water to make a batter, stir it quite smooth
9. Add gradually 1 pint boiling milk and 1 lb. fresh butter cut in pieces
10. Set on the fire until it comes to a boil and the butter is perfectly mixed
11. Take off and while hot, stir in a glass of madeira or sherry, a little nutmeg, 4 tablespoons of rich cream
12. Take chickens out of saucepan and pour off the liquor
13. Return the chickens and pour the gravy over them
14. Cover the pan closely and set it over a kettle of boiling water for 10 minutes
15. Serve very hot
