## Ingredients

- 2 quarts of oysters
- water
- 1 handful of chopped ham
- a few blades of mace
- one-fourth of a large onion
- salt
- pepper
- 1 tablespoonful of flour
- 1 quarter pound of butter
- 1 pint of milk
- 1 pint of cream

## Directions

1. Strain and drain two quarts of oysters; if you have less than a quart of liquor, make up that amount with water
2. Add a handful of chopped ham, fat and lean, a few blades of mace, one-fourth of a large onion, salt and pepper to taste
3. Boil one hour, then strain
4. Make a tablespoonful of flour and a quarter pound of butter into a soft paste and set aside
5. Put oysters into liquor and give them 8 or 10 minutes to plump up
6. Stir in the paste and leave until dinner is ready
7. Put the soup on again until it comes to a boil
8. Add a pint each of milk and cream
9. Stir all the time until it boils again, then dish and serve immediately
