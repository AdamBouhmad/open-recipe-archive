## Ingredients

- 2 Pounds of Eel
- 1 Cup of Hot Vinegar
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Can of Mushrooms
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 1 Glass of White Wine
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- 1 Sprig of Sweet Basil
- 2 Sprigs of Parsley
- 1 Onion Minced Very Fine
- The Yolks of 3 Eggs
- Juice of 2 Lemons
- A Dash of Cayenne
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- Croutons and Sliced Lemon to Garnish

## Directions

1. Clean and skin the eels and put in a pot of boiling water.
2. To be sure that they disgorge thoroughly add a cup of hot vinegar.
3. After boiling fifteen minutes, take them out and cut into three-inch pieces.
4. Take one tablespoonful of butter, put into a frying pan, and add the eels and one can of mushrooms poured over.
5. Add two tablespoonfuls of flour rubbed smoothly in butter as the eels begin to fry.
6. Pour over, when this begins to brown, a glassful of white wine.
7. Add parsley, thyme, bay leaf, sweet basil, and an onion minced like the herbs, very fine.
8. As the grease rises, floating, skim it off.
9. Add a little Cayenne, and salt to taste, if necessary.
10. When quite done, take the yolks of three eggs and mix thoroughly with the juice of two lemons.
11. Take the eels off the fire and add the lemon juice and eggs and be careful not to set back on the fire when once these are added, or the eggs will curdle.
12. Place on a dish and garnish with Croutons and slices of lemon and serve hot.
