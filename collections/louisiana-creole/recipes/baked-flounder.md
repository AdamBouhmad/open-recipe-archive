## Ingredients

- A 3-Pound Flounder
- 1 Large Onion
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- 3 Sprigs of Parsley
- 1/2 Bottle of White Wine
- 6 Fresh Tomatoes
- 2 Dozen Lake Shrimp
- 1 Dozen Oysters
- 1 Can of Mushrooms
- 2 Crackers
- Dry Toast Cut in Dice Shape
- Salt and Black Pepper

## Directions

1. Clean and wash the fish; place on a platter.
2. Chop one large onion fine.
3. Rub the fish first with salt and black pepper.
4. Take a large and deep kitchen pan, place within a tablespoonful of butter, the chopped onion, bay leaf and thyme.
5. Place the fish on top of this and pour over a half bottle of white wine.
6. Cover with another close pan and put the whole on top of the oven (not on the inside).
7. Bake from the bottom.
8. When it begins to boil from below, turn the fish over carefully without breaking, and let it bake on the other side.
9. Take a saucepan and put within a tablespoonful of butter and mix thoroughly with two tablespoonfuls of flour.
10. Let it brown without burning, and then add six fresh tomatoes, skinned and chopped fine, or a half can.
11. Add two dozen Lake Shrimp that have been cleaned well and scalded, a half can of mushrooms, salt and pepper to taste.
12. Let all cook for about five minutes and then water with the gravy in which the fish is cooking.
13. Mix well and cover the fish with it.
14. Parboil one dozen oysters, and when the fish is cooked, set it in the dish in which it is to be served and place the oysters all around it on small slices of dry toast.
15. Cover the fish alternately with the shrimp and oysters, as a garnish over and around it.
16. Mash two crackers into crumbs and sprinkle over it; also some finely-chopped parsley.
17. Place small bits of butter alternately over the fish.
18. Set the dish in the oven, and bake with a quick fire until brown.
19. Serve immediately.
