## Ingredients

- 6 Fine Fat Squab
- A Half Can of Mushrooms
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 2 Sprigs Each of Thyme and Parsley
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 1 Onion
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of White Wine
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 3 Cloves ground very fine
- 3 Allspice ground very fine
- Salt and Pepper
- 3/4 Cup of Consomme
- Thin Strips of Bacon
- Toasted Croflton

## Directions

1. Clean the squabs nicely; singe, draw and truss, with their legs inside.
2. Rub well with salt and pepper and three cloves, ground very fine, and three allspice, also ground very fine.
3. Take a tablespoonful of butter and melt in a saucepan.
4. Add the sliced onion, and as it browns add the sliced carrot.
5. Let this simmer gently for three or four minutes, and then add a minced sprig of thyme and parsley, and one bay leaf, and the clove of garlic, minced very fine.
6. Let all this brown, and then place on top the pigeons, which you will have bound in thin strips of bacon tied around the body.
7. Add two tablespoonfuls of white wine and cover well.
8. Let this simmer for about fifteen minutes, till the pigeons are nicely browned.
9. Add a half cup of consomme if you have it, if not, a half cup of boiling water.
10. After ten minutes add a half can of mushrooms.
11. Let all simmer gently for an hour longer, being careful not to let the pigeons go to pieces.
12. Place each pigeon on a slice of toasted Croflton, and garnish with the mushrooms.
13. Pour over the gravy, and serve hot.
