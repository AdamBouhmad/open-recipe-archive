## Ingredients

- 1 Pint of Lima Beans
- 3 Pints of Water
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1/2 Pint of Cream
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Sort the beans as you shell them, and save the very large ones for a purge.
2. Soak the small ones over night.
3. Drain the water when about to cook, and put in a porcelain or agate saucepan, and cover with three pints of boiling water.
4. Boil them until very tender, which will require at least two hours.
5. After they have boiled one hour, add a teaspoonful of salt, or salt to taste.
6. When done, drain the beans and return to the saucepan.
7. Add a half pint of cream or milk, a tablespoonful of flour, blended well with butter, salt and pepper to taste, a sprig each of thyme and parsley and bay leaf minced very fine.
8. Let all simmer for ten minutes, and then serve hot.
