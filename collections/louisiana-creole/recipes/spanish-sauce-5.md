## Ingredients

- 1/2 Pound of Brisket or Veal
- Bones of Beef
- 1 Quart of Water
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 1/2 Can Mushrooms or 1/4 Can Truffles
- 1 Carrot
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 2 Sprigs Each of Thyme and Parsley
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 1 Wineglass of Sherry
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Take a good quantity of bones, place in a quart of boiling water, and make a strong consommé, seasoning well with salt and pepper
2. Take a piece of the brisket or neck of the beef, and roast rare, so that the blood spurts out when pricked with a needle
3. Cut it in pieces of about one inch square
4. Take two tablespoonfuls of lard and three of flour, and brown slightly, stirring all the time
5. Add the water of the consommé, which has been reduced to about half a pint, pouring it in slowly and stirring constantly
6. Add all the pieces of the roast beef which you have cut
7. Add three carrots, two cloves of garlic, one onion, a herb bouquet, tied together, of thyme, parsley and bay leaf
8. Let the whole boil well two hours, stirring every five minutes, until reduced to the consistency of starch
9. Strain well through a strainer or sieve
10. Season to taste, and set back on the stove to cook a few minutes longer
11. Add one wineglass or two tablespoonfuls of Sherry to dissolve, and a half pint of broth
12. Set it to boil again, and add a half can of mushrooms or truffles, as desired
13. Serve hot with meats, fish and fowl
