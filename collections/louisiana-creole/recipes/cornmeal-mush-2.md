## Ingredients

- 1½ Cups of Cornmeal
- 2 Quarts of Boiling Water
- 1¼ Teaspoonfuls of Salt

## Directions

1. Set the water to boil in a porcelain-lined or agate stewpot; add the salt.
2. When the light scum comes on top, skim it off.
3. Add the fresh, sweet white cornmeal, putting a handful at a time into the water, and stirring with a spoon, or a pudding stick, round and round, as the meal falls lightly from the hand.
4. When one handful is exhausted, refill it, and continue stirring and letting the meal fall by degrees, until the pudding stick will stand in it.
5. Continue stirring, and when sufficiently cooked, which will be in a half hour, as the bubbles begin to puff up, turn into a bowl.
6. Bring to the table either hot or cold and eat with milk, butter, sugar, syrup or with meat and gravy.
