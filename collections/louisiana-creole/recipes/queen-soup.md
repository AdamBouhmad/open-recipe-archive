## Ingredients

- 1 Chicken
- 1/4 Pound of Rice
- 1/2 Pint of Cream
- 1/4 Blade of Mace
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- 4 Sprigs of Parsley
- 2 Quarts of White Veal Broth
- Salt and Pepper
- Chopped onion
- Bay leaf
- 1 Carrot
- Small bunch of celery
- 1 Cup of rice
- 1 Tablespoonful of butter
- 1 Tablespoonful of flour
- 1 Glass of milk
- 1/4 Grated nutmeg
- Croutons of bread fried in butter

## Directions

1. Take a fine large chicken, clean it nicely and put it whole into a pot containing about five quarts of water.
2. Add chopped onion, thyme, bay leaf, one carrot, a small bunch of celery, and one cup of rice.
3. Let the chicken simmer well, for about four hours, and, when well cooked, take out the chicken from the broth.
4. Cut off the white meat and cut it into pieces about the size of dice.
5. Then strain the broth, mashing the rice well.
6. Make a puree by taking another saucepan, putting in one tablespoonful of butter and one of flour, letting it melt together without browning.
7. Moisten this well with the soup and a glass of milk, and season with salt and pepper, and one-quarter of a grated nutmeg and add to the broth.
8. Then add the chicken, which has been cut up.
9. Put in the tureen little dice of croutons of bread fried in butter.
10. Pour the soup over and serve hot.
