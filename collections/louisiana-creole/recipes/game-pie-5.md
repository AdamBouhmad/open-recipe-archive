## Ingredients

- 1 Dozen Small Birds
- 1 Dozen Eggs
- A Rich Pie Crust
- 1 Dozen Hard-Boiled Eggs
- 2 Cups of Egg Dressing
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- Water

## Directions

1. Take one dozen small birds. Snipe, Quail, Woodcock, etc., and clean well, inside and out.
2. Stuff each one with a dressing the same as for turkey, using either egg or oysters as desired.
3. Loosen the joints with a knife but do not separate them.
4. Put them in a stewpan, with water enough to cover them, and let them cook till nearly tender.
5. Season with salt and pepper again and two tablespoonfuls of butter.
6. Thicken the gravy with one tablespoonful of flour, let cook for ten minutes more and then remove and set to cool.
7. Butter a pudding dish and line the sides with a rich pie crust.
8. Have ready the hard-boiled eggs, cut in slices.
9. Put in a layer of the eggs and a layer of the birds until the dish is full.
10. Pour over the gravy and then cover the pie with a crust and bake to a light brown.
