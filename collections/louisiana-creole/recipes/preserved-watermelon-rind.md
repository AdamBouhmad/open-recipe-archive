## Ingredients

- Watermelon rind
- Lime
- Cold water
- Alum water
- Grape-leaves
- Ginger tea
- Sugar
- Water

## Directions

1. In a bucket of cold water, put a handful of lime, stir it in, and when it settles clear, pour it over the watermelon rind you intend preserving
2. Let it stay in the weak lime-water one day
3. Soak it a few hours, and get the taste of the lime from the rind
4. Put it in alum water and scald for ten minutes
5. Put grape-leaves in with the alum water while scalding; they will make the rind green
6. Take the rind from the alum, and put it in cold water for a few hours
7. When cold, boil it in strong ginger tea until it is soft, and tastes of the ginger
8. Make the syrup of one and a half pounds of sugar to each pound of rind, and a half pint of water to each pound of sugar
9. Let it cook slowly, skim it, and when it looks clear, put in the rind, and let it cook slowly until clear and transparent
10. The rind should be cut into beautiful shapes, and preserved with care
