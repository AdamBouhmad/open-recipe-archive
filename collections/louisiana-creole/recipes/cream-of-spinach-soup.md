## Ingredients

- Half a Peck or Four Pints of Spinach
- 2 Ounces of Fresh Butter
- Two Quarts of Oyster Water
- 1 Teaspoonful of Salt
- 1 Teaspoonful of Granulated Sugar
- 1/4 of a Grated Nutmeg
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- Croutons

## Directions

1. Wash and boil one-half peck, or four pints of Spinach; this quantity will measure about one pint when cooked, chopped and pounded into a fine paste.
2. Put it into a stew pan with four ounces of fresh butter, the grated nutmeg and a teaspoonful of salt.
3. Let it cook for ten minutes, stirring constantly.
4. Add two quarts of oyster juice (or other than fast days consommé may be used, or good bouillon).
5. Let all boil up, and then press through a strainer.
6. Set it over the fire again and just at the boiling point mix with it a tablespoonful of butter and a teaspoonful of granulated sugar.
7. Serve hot with Croutons.
