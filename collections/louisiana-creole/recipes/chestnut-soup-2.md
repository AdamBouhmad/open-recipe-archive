## Ingredients

- 3 Quarts of Oyster Water, or A Good Round of Beef or Veal
- 1 Quart of Chestnuts
- 1 Herb Bouquet
- Cayenne Pepper
- Salt
- Butter
- Flour

## Directions

1. Make a good broth of the veal or beef; season with the Cayenne pepper and salt. Follow the rule given for making soups, by allowing a pound of meat to each quart of water.
2. Skim and boil till the meat falls into rags; then strain and put in a clean porcelain pot.
3. In the meantime, shell the chestnuts and throw them into boiling water until the skin comes off easily.
4. Put them into a saucepan with some of the soup water, and boil about thirty minutes, till quite soft.
5. Press through a colander; add butter, pepper and salt.
6. Then add to the soup.
7. Make dumplings the size of a marble with fresh butter rolled in flour, and add.
8. Boil the soup about fifteen minutes longer and serve.
9. On fast days use the oyster water instead of the beef broth, following the recipe in all other particulars, and adding a half tablespoonful of butter to the purée before pressing through the colander.
