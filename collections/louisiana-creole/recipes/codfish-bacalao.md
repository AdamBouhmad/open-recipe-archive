## Ingredients

- 1 pound of salted codfish
- 1 large onion, chopped fine
- 1 pint of rich tomato sauce
- 2 cloves of garlic, chopped fine
- 1 red pepper
- 2 tablespoonfuls of olive oil
- Pepper to taste
- 6 tomatoes
- Thyme
- Bay leaf
- Parsley
- Croutons
- 1 heaping tablespoonful of butter
- 4 sprigs of parsley
- 1 tablespoonful of butter
- 2 tablespoonfuls of flour
- Cayenne
- Salt
- 1 gill of olive oil or 2 tablespoonfuls of butter

## Directions

1. Soak the codfish well overnight, and in the morning boil for about forty-five minutes, until very tender, beginning with cold water.
2. After it has boiled, scald again thoroughly, and pick out all the bones and set away to cool.
3. Take six large fresh tomatoes, or a half can of tomatoes, and add a heaping tablespoonful of butter, four sprigs of parsley, thyme and two bay leaves, all chopped very fine.
4. Add two chopped onions and a clove of garlic, chopped fine, and which has been fried in a little butter.
5. Set the saucepan, with the sauce, into boiling water, and add pepper, Cayenne and a pinch of salt to taste.
6. Stew very gently for about two hours or longer, if necessary.
7. Strain the sauce and make a roux with one tablespoonful of butter and two of flour; stir and let it brown lightly, and stir in the sauce.
8. Boil about four minutes longer until rich and thick.
9. Fry the remaining large onion and clove of garlic, chopped very fine, in a gill of olive oil, or two tablespoonfuls of butter if the taste of the oil is disliked, and when it browns add this to the sauce and a red sweet pepper, finely chopped.
10. Cut about a dozen croutons in dice or diamond shapes, from the soft part of the bread, and fry in boiling lard.
11. Heat a dish, put the codfish into it, pour over the tomato sauce, border the dish with the fried croutons, and set in the oven, allowing it to bake ten or fifteen minutes longer.
