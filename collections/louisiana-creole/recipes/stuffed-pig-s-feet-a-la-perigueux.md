## Ingredients

- 3 Pigs' Feet
- 2 Minced truffles
- 3/8 Glass of Madeira wine
- 1 Pound of boned turkey forcemeat
- 6 Pieces of crepinette
- 1 Egg
- 2 Ounces of butter
- 1/2 Pint of hot Perigueux sauce

## Directions

1. Boil the Pig's Feet, and then split them in two.
2. Take out the bones, lay the flesh on a dry, clean cloth and wipe well.
3. Make a forcemeat of boned turkey.
4. Add the truffles, which have been finely minced, and a half glass of Madeira or Sherry wine.
5. Mix this well together.
6. Take six pieces of crepinette, cut them the size of a man's hand and lay on a clean biscuit board.
7. Place on each piece of skin a portion of the forcemeat about the size of a hen's egg and flatten out well.
8. Place one-half of a pig's foot on top of this farcie, and cover with another layer of the stuffing.
9. On either side lay three thin slices of truffles.
10. Wrap the crepinettes up in some fanciful shape, such as an envelope or card case.
11. Dip them separately in a well-beaten egg, and then in grated bread-crumbs.
12. Put two tablespoonfuls of butter in a saucepan or deep frying pan, cover closely.
13. Let the feet cook on a slow fire for twenty-five minutes, allowing twelve minutes and a half to each side.
14. Serve with a pint of hot Perigueux sauce.
15. Place the sauce in a dish, lay the feet neatly over it and send to the table hot.
