## Ingredients

- A Rump of Veal of Two Pounds Weight
- 1 Pint of Broth
- 2 Onions
- 2 Carrots
- ½ Dozen Slices of Bacon
- ½ Can of Green Peas or Mushrooms
- Thyme
- Parsley
- Bay Leaf
- Salt and Pepper
- Lard
- ½ Glass of White Wine

## Directions

1. Take one whole piece of the rump of the veal, cut about two inches in thickness, and about the size of a large saucepan or frying pan in length and width.
2. Lard the veal well with larding needles, and rub well with salt and pepper.
3. Slice two onions, two carrots, two sprigs of thyme and two bay leaves, and a half dozen slices of thinly-sliced fat bacon, two inches long and about the thickness of a dollar.
4. Place the bacon in strips in the bottom of the saucepan, and lay over this a layer of the sliced carrots and onions.
5. Put about a dozen little dots of lard over this at intervals, and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
6. Lay the veal on top of this, and then cover up with a layer of the sliced onions and carrots, and lay strips of bacon on top.
7. Cut a piece of paper the size of the saucepan, and cover it up.
8. Place in a good oven, and let it bake three-quarters of an hour longer, slowly.
9. Watch carefully.
10. When done, take out the meat and place it in a dish.
11. Take the sauce and add, if possible, one-half glass of white wine, and let it boil with the vegetables a moment.
12. Then add one pint of broth or water, and let it cook well.
13. Strain after it boils fifteen minutes.
14. Add, if desired, a half can of mushrooms, or a half can of green peas, and let it boil for ten minutes longer.
15. Then add the Fricandeau of Veal and let it warm well for about ten minutes and it is ready for the table.
