## Ingredients

- 3 Quarts of Corn
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard to Each Quart of Corn
- salt

## Directions

1. Boil the corn on the cob until no milk will exude if the grains are pricked with a needle
2. cut the corn off the cob, and pack into cans or stone jars, putting first a layer of salt half an inch deep, and then a layer of corn two inches deep, and continuing with these alternate layers until the jar is nearly filled
3. The top layer must be of salt, and about an inch thick
4. Pour over all this melted lard in proportion of about a tablespoonful to a quart of the corn
5. Set on the fire, in a bain-marie, and let all the bubbles come to the surface
6. Take off, stick a silver fork down into the jar, to see that all bubbles have ceased to form
7. Seal hermetically, and then set in a bain-marie, at a temperature of from 80 to 100 degrees, and let it cool
8. Keep in a cool place, and use as wanted
