## Ingredients

- 3 fine heads of celery
- 2 dozen slices of beef's marrow
- 1 pint of Madeira sauce
- Salt

## Directions

1. Cut off the green leaves of the celery and pare nicely.
2. Wash well and drain.
3. Tie each head near the end where the green portion has been taken away.
4. Put them into boiling salted water and let them blanch for ten minutes.
5. Take out of the water and drain through a colander.
6. Make one pint of Madeira sauce, add the celery to this and let it cook for a quarter of an hour.
7. Take the celery, place on a dish and untie.
8. Add to the sauce in the saucepan about two dozen slices of beef marrow cut half an inch thick; cook for two minutes; do not allow the marrow to break.
9. Put in the dish with the celery, pour the sauce over and serve hot.
