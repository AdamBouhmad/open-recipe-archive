## Ingredients

- 1 Filet of Beef
- Lard sufficient to lard thoroughly
- 1 Small Onion
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 4 Cloves, if desired
- 1½ Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- 1 Tablespoonful of Glace
- 1 Glass of Madeira or Sherry Wine (or Water)
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Trim the filet nicely, removing the outer muscular skin.
2. Lard the filet well, using larding needles. The lard must be very thin, like a shoestring.
3. Fill the needles with the lard and push them through the filet as far as they will go.
4. Repeat this process all down the center and along the sides of the filet, about an inch apart, having the rows neat and even.
5. When well larded, dredge well with salt and pepper, rubbing this thoroughly into the beef.
6. Cut up one small onion, one bay leaf, and mash four cloves, and place in the bottom of the baking pan.
7. Lay the larded filet on this bed, the larded side being uppermost.
8. Put small bits of butter equal to a half tablespoonful on top, and bake in a quick oven thirty minutes.
9. To ascertain if sufficiently done, stick a fork into the filet; if the blood bubbles out, it is ready to serve.
10. Prepare the following brown sauce: Take one tablespoonful of butter and one of Glace, and three of water; rub smoothly and melt in a saucepan, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
11. When brown, add one glass of Madeira or Sherry wine and add a half cup of water.
12. Season well with salt and pepper.
13. Pour over the filet, which must be placed in a hot dish, and serve.
