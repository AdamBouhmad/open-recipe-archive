## Ingredients

- half a package of gelatine
- water
- eggshells
- corn starch
- vanilla
- sugar
- lemon rind

## Directions

1. Using a little more than half the quantity of water given in the recipe for making jelly, prepare the gelatine; in all other respects use the same proportions.
2. When ready to strain put it into a large oval dish (a meat dish is nice); fill it nearly to the edge.
3. Set it away to harden.
4. Take some eggshells that you have broken just the end off in getting out the egg.
5. Make a blanc-mange of corn starch; flavor it with vanilla, and sweeten.
6. Put this into the shells before it cools and hardens at all.
7. Set the eggs on end in a vegetable-dish so that they will stand top up, being careful not to let the blanc-mange run out.
8. Cut some very thin yellow parings off the lemon rind, stew them in a little sugar and water.
9. When cold lay each piece separately in a circle on the jelly, making two or three nests.
10. Break open the eggshells, take out the blanc-mange, and lay it in groups like eggs inside the nest.
11. Ivy sprays or myrtle wound around the edge of the dish improves the appearance.
