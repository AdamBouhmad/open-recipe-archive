## Ingredients

- 4 Dozen Large Fresh Oysters
- 1 Quart Rich Milk
- The Oyster Liquor
- 1 Tablespoonful Butter
- 3 Sprigs Parsley, Chopped Fine
- 1 Dozen Pepper Corns
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. In purchasing the oysters, be careful to make the vendor add the oyster juice when intended for soup
2. Drain the oysters through a colander and set them over the ice box to keep fresh and cold
3. Strain the liquor and put it into a soup kettle, adding the chopped parsley and pepper-corns
4. Let it come to a boil
5. Boil the milk separately in a saucepan, as boiling the milk and oyster juice together is likely to curdle the milk
6. When the milk comes to a boil, add to the oyster juice and put in the tablespoonful of butter
7. Stir the soup constantly, throwing in the oysters and continuing to stir until it comes to a boil again
8. Under no circumstances allow the oysters to boil, as that destroys their flavor and makes them tough
9. Watch for the ruffling of the edges, which indicates the right condition
10. At this point the soup must be served immediately
11. Serve with sliced lemon and oyster or wafer crackers
