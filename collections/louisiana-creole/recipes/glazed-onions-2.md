## Ingredients

- 1/2 Dozen Small Onions of Uniform Size
- 1 Tablespoonful Sugar
- 1 Cup Water
- 1 Large Teaspoonful Butter
- Salt to Taste
- 1 Tablespoonful Flour

## Directions

1. Select small Creole onions of uniform size, top the heads and the stems, and remove the skins, but not too closely, lest they should break up when boiling.
2. Take a frying pan, large enough for the onions to lie in it, side by side.
3. Put the butter in it first, and when melted add the onions.
4. Sprinkle with the sugar and water, and season with salt to taste.
5. Set on the back of the Stove, where they can simmer gently for an hour.
6. When nearly done, and tender all through, add a tablespoonful of flour, mixed in water, blended well.
7. Set in the oven, with a paper on top.
8. Let them stand for about half an hour, and use as a garnish for beef, veal, etc.
