## Ingredients

- 3 Pounds of Sugar to every Gallon of juice
- 0.33 of a Gallon of Water
- The White of 1 Egg
- 1 Cake of Compressed Yeast

## Directions

1. Take fine Louisiana oranges, and wash them well
2. Dry with a cloth, and peel off the yellow outer rind very thin
3. Strain all the juice through a hair sieve
4. Put the sugar, the crushed eggshell and white of the egg, beaten to a froth, in a porcelain-lined saucepan, and add the water
5. Boil to a syrup, stirring constantly to prevent the egg from hardening
6. Let the syrup boil till it looks clear when seen through the froth of the egg
7. Strain the syrup, and pour it over the grated orange zest
8. Let it stand for twenty-four hours
9. Add the orange juice, and strain
10. Put it into a cask with a small cake of compressed yeast
11. Close tightly, but leave the bung out of the cask until the wine ceases to ferment
12. When fermentation ceases, add a glass of good brandy for each gallon of wine
13. Close the cask, drive in the bung, and let the wine stand for at least nine months
14. Bottle it, and about four months after it has been bottled it will be ready for use
