## Ingredients

- 3 Dozen Oysters
- 2 Eggs
- 1 Cup of Milk
- 2 Cups of Flour
- 1 Teaspoonful of Salt
- 1/2 Teaspoonful of Good Baking Powder
- Parsley or Asparagus Tips to Garnish

## Directions

1. Take two dozen large oysters, drain in a colander and remove any pieces of shell or grit that may adhere
2. Chop the oysters fine
3. Take two eggs and beat until very light
4. Add a cup of milk and rub in smoothly two cupfuls of flour and one teaspoonful of salt
5. Beat until perfectly smooth
6. Add one-half teaspoonful of good baking powder
7. Mix well and then drop in the oysters which must be dry
8. Drop into boiling lard or oil
9. When browned on one side, turn on the other, being careful not to use a fork or to pierce them
10. Use a skimmer in removing from the pot, and drain on brown paper
11. Serve on a dish in which you have placed a folded napkin and garnish with sprigs of parsley or asparagus tips
