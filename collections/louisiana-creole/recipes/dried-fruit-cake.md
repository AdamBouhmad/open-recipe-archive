## Ingredients

- 3 Cups of Dried Apples, Peaches or any Kind of Dried Fruit
- 2 Cups of Louisiana Molasses
- 1 Cup of Milk
- 2 Eggs
- 1/4 Cup of Butter
- 1 Cup of Sugar
- 1 Teaspoonful of Ground Cinnamon
- 3/4 Teaspoonful Each of Ground Cloves and Mace
- 3/4 Pound of Stoned Raisins
- 3/4 Grated Nutmeg
- 1/4 Teaspoonful Salt
- The Juice of a Lemon
- 1 1/2 Teaspoonfuls of Baking Powder or 1 Teaspoonful of Soda

## Directions

1. Soak the fruit over night.
2. Chop very fine, and simmer in a saucepan with the molasses for two hours and a half.
3. Let it cool.
4. Seed the raisins.
5. Beat the butter and sugar to a cream, until very light.
6. Add the yolks of the eggs, and beat till light.
7. Add the milk and then the fruit and molasses, stirring carefully.
8. Beat hard for five minutes.
9. Add the flour, which you will have sifted with the baking powder, and beat vigorously for five minutes.
10. If you use soda, dissolve it at this point into a tablespoonful of boiling water, and add, mixing well.
11. Add the raisins, and beat vigorously, and the spices and salt.
12. Add the juice of the lemon, and finally the whites of the eggs, beaten to a stiff froth.
13. Bake in a moderate oven for two hours.
14. Use the broom wisp to test; if it comes out clean and dry, then the cake is done.
