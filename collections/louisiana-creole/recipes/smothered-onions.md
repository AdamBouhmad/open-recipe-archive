## Ingredients

- 1 Dozen Small Onions
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Quart of Broth
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Put the onions into cold water, and remove their skins.
2. Then put them into a saucepan, and cover with a soup stock (pot-au-feu or bouillon), or otherwise use water.
3. Let them stew slowly for an hour and a half, till they are almost falling to pieces.
4. Then drain the onions through a colander, and save the stock.
5. Put a tablespoonful of butter into a frying pan, and add a tablespoonful of flour, and make a Brown Roux.
6. When brown, add a half pint of the broth in which the onions were boiled.
7. Season well with salt and pepper and a dash of Cayenne.
8. Put the onions into this and let them simmer gently for about twenty minutes longer.
9. Serve hot.
