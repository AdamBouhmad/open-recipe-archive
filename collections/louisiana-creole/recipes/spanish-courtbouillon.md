## Ingredients

- 6 Slices of Redfish or Red Snapper (equal to 3 pounds)
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 1 Large Onion
- 6 Tomatoes
- 3 Sprigs of Parsley
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 3 Sprigs of Thyme
- 1 Sprig of Sweet Basil
- 2 Bay Leaves
- 1 Cup of White Wine
- 2 Pints of Water
- Salt and Pepper
- Vinegar or White Wine

## Directions

1. Buy in the French Market four or six slices of Redfish.
2. Make a Roux by putting one tablespoonful of lard into the stew pan, and when it is hot, stir in gradually two tablespoonfuls of flour.
3. Add one large chopped onion, six tomatoes (chopped), the chopped parsley, clove of garlic, sweet basil and thyme, all chopped very fine.
4. Add two bay leaves whole.
5. When it browns nicely without burning, pour in about two pints of water and let it come to a boil.
6. Rub the fish well with salt and pepper, and pour over it a cup of boiling vinegar, if you have no white wine.
7. Put the fish slice by slice into the pot and let it simmer for about a half hour, or until the flesh begins to be soft.
8. Remove from the fire, take out of the pot, and lay the slices in a dish.
9. Take the bay leaves out of the gravy, pour the gravy over the fish, and serve with garnishes of sliced lemon.
