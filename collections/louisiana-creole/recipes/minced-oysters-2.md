## Ingredients

- 4 Dozen Oysters
- 1 Gill of Oyster Juice
- A Half Can of Mushrooms
- 1 Gill of White Wine
- 3 Yolks of Eggs
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Gill of Rich Cream
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- Parsley and Chives and Thyme, chopped fine

## Directions

1. Scald the oysters in their own water.
2. Drain and mince, but not too fine.
3. Put into the saucepan a tablespoonful of butter, and, when melted, add the parsley (chopped fine), the herbs and the mushrooms.
4. Then add the flour, which has been rubbed smoothly in a gill of oyster juice, and, after it stews about five minutes, add the white wine; if this is not obtainable, add another gill of oyster juice.
5. Mix thoroughly, and then add the minced oysters, and stew gently until the sauce is absorbed and the mince forms a thick batter.
6. Be very careful not to scorch.
7. Remove from the fire and add in the yolks of the eggs, which have been beaten smoothly, in the cream.
8. Set it back on the fire and let it remain about one minute, and serve on large toasted and buttered Croutons, with garnish of lemon and parsley and olives.
