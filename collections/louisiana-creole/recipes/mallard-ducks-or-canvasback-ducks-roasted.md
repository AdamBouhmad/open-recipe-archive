## Ingredients

- 1 Pair of Wild Ducks
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Clean the ducks as you would a chicken, without scalding, however, rinse out the inside and wipe well inside and out with a wet towel.
2. Do not wash the duck unless you have broken the gall bladder, as the washing destroys their flavor.
3. Rub the inside well with salt and pepper, and rub outside as thoroughly.
4. Place a three-inch lump of butter on the inside.
5. Truss nicely and place the ducks in a baking pan, and brush the tops with melted butter.
6. Pour over two tablespoonfuls of water, and set in a very hot oven, and allow them to bake twenty minutes, if they are not very large, and thirty minutes, if larger than the ordinary size of Canvasback ducks.
7. A wild duck is never cooked dry. It must reach the point where the blood will not run if the flesh is pierced with the fork in carving.
8. When done, place the ducks in a very hot dish, and serve with their own gravy poured over them.
9. Garnish nicely with parsley or water cress.
10. Serve with Currant Jelly.
11. Always have the plates very hot in which you serve the ducks at table.
