## Ingredients

- 1 onion of medium size
- butter
- a little more than a teaspoonful of curry powder
- 1 breakfast cupful of gravy or good stock
- 1 teaspoonful of flour
- a small piece of butter
- 1 clove
- Harvey or Worcestershire sauce
- lemon peel (chopped fine)
- lemon juice (about a teaspoonful)
- about a teaspoonful of chutney
- ½ teaspoonful of anchovy sauce
- a pinch of ground mace
- salt
- about a dessertspoonful of milk or cream
- meat, chicken, or vegetable (cooked and cut into pieces)
- rice (boiled)

## Directions

1. Peel and slice an onion of medium size into a saucepan in which some butter is boiling (just sufficient to fry the onion)
2. Let the onion fry till it is of a nice light brown color
3. Add a little more than a teaspoonful of curry powder and let this fry with the onions about half a minute, stirring all the time
4. Have a breakfast cupful of gravy or good stock and mix upon a plate about a teaspoonful of flour with a small piece of butter
5. When quite smooth, incorporate this with the gravy or stock and add to the onion and curry powder, stirring all the time
6. Add one clove, a little Harvey or Worcestershire sauce, a little lemon peel (chopped fine) and juice, about a teaspoonful of chutney, ½ teaspoonful of anchovy sauce, a pinch of ground mace, and salt to taste
7. Let this sauce simmer on the fire for about two hours or longer
8. Add about a dessertspoonful of milk or cream (after this is added it must not boil, or it will curdle)
9. Have the meat, chicken, or vegetable that is to be curried, ready cooked and cut into convenient pieces, put it into the curry sauce for about five minutes, heat thoroughly, but do not boil
10. Serve with rice, boiled, in a separate dish, and handed around before the curry
