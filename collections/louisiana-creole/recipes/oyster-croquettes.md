## Ingredients

- 3 Dozen Oysters
- 3 Gill of Rich Cream
- 1 Gill of Oyster Liquor
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- The Yolks of 2 Eggs
- 3 Sprigs of Parsley
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- A Dash of Cayenne
- A Well Beaten Egg
- Grated Bread Crumbs
- Boiling Lard

## Directions

1. Take two dozen oysters and boil them in their own liquor
2. Stir constantly and boil for about five minutes
3. Remove from the fire
4. Take out the oysters and chop very fine
5. Put them into a saucepan with about one gill each of rich cream and oyster liquor
6. Rub together two tablespoonfuls of flour and one of butter
7. Add this and the oysters to the boiling milk and cream
8. Stir until it thickens and boils
9. Add the yolk of two eggs
10. Stir this over the fire for about one minute, and then take off and add parsley, chopped fine, salt and Cayenne
11. Mix well and place in a dish to cool
12. Roll in a beaten egg to bind and form into cylinders of about a finger in length
13. Roll in bread crumbs mashed fine, and fry in boiling lard or oil
