## Ingredients

- 2 Pounds of Turtle Meat, or a 2-Pound Turtle
- 2 Fine Large Onions
- 1 Square inch of Ham
- 2 Cloves of Garlic
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Flour
- 1/2 of a Small Lemon
- 1 Glass of Sherry Wine
- 6 Cloves
- 4 Allspice
- Parsley
- Thyme
- Bay Leaf
- 2 Hard-Boiled Eggs
- Salt, Pepper and Cayenne to Taste

## Directions

1. Select two pounds of fine Green Turtle meat. If the turtle is bought whole, first cut off the head by hanging the turtle with the head downwards and using a very sharp knife to cut off the head as close as possible.
2. To remove the shells, first separate the upper from the lower shell, being exceedingly careful to avoid touching the gall bladder.
3. Clean the turtle and the entrails by cutting open and washing thoroughly in cold water.
4. Put the meat and entrails into a saucepan and parboil about ten minutes. Save this stock of water.
5. Chop an onion very fine, and the ham into very fine pieces.
6. Cut the turtle meat into one-inch pieces, mash the cloves and the allspice very fine and chop the thyme and bay leaf.
7. Brown the onions in a tablespoonful of butter or lard, and add immediately the turtle meat.
8. Brown together slightly and after a few minutes add the chopped ham.
9. Add two cloves of garlic chopped fine, and the thyme, bay leaf minced fine, cloves and allspice ground, all mixed together, and lay on the turtle.
10. Stir almost constantly to prevent burning, and add two tablespoonfuls of flour that has been well rubbed, stirring constantly all the time.
11. Dissolve the meat with the water in which the turtle was parboiled, adding gradually until a certain consistency is reached. About three quarts of water will be the required amount.
12. Season with salt, black pepper and Cayenne to taste, and boil slowly for fully an hour, stirring almost constantly.
13. After cooking one hour taste, and if not seasoned sufficiently season again and taste.
14. Chop one-quarter of a small lemon and put it in the soup.
15. When well done—that is when no blood exudes from the turtle after sticking it with a fork—pour into the tureen.
16. Add the whites and yolks of two hard-boiled eggs chopped fine, and one good glass of Sherry wine, and the soup is ready to serve.
