## Ingredients

- 1/2 Cup of Rice
- 3 Cups Water (boiling)
- 3 Eggs
- 1/2 Cup of Sugar
- 1/4 Cake of Compressed Yeast
- 1/2 Teaspoonful of Grated Nutmeg
- Powdered White Sugar
- Boiling Lard
- 3 Tablespoonfuls of Flour

## Directions

1. Put three cups of water in a saucepan and let it boil hard.
2. Wash half a cup of rice thoroughly, and drain and put in the boiling water.
3. Let it boil till very soft and mushy.
4. Take it out and set it to cool.
5. When cold, mash well and mix with the yeast, which you will have dissolved in a half cup of hot water.
6. Set the rice to rise overnight.
7. In the morning beat three eggs thoroughly, and add to the rice, mixing and beating well.
8. Add a half cup of sugar and three tablespoonfuls of flour, to make the rice adhere.
9. Mix well and beat thoroughly, bringing it to a thick batter.
10. Set to rise for fifteen minutes longer.
11. Then add about a half teaspoonful of grated nutmeg, and mix well.
12. Have ready a frying pan, in which there is sufficient quantity of lard boiling for the rice cakes to swim in it.
13. Test by dropping in a small piece of bread. If it becomes a golden brown, the lard is ready, but if it burns or browns instantly it is too hot.
14. Take a large deep spoon, and drop a spoonful at a time of the preparation into the boiling lard, remembering always that the cake must not touch the bottom of the pan.
15. Let fry to a nice brown.
16. Take the Galas out of the frying pan and drain off the lard by laying in a colander or on heated pieces of brown paper.
17. Place in a hot dish, and sprinkle over with powdered white sugar, and eat hot with Cafe au Lait.
