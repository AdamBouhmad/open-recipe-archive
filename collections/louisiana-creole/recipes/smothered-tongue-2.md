## Ingredients

- 1 fresh tongue of beef
- 1 pint of liquor in which the tongue was boiled
- 2 onions, minced very fine
- 3 sprigs each of thyme and parsley
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 cloves
- 1 tablespoonful of butter
- 1 glass of white wine (if desired)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 square inch of ham, well chopped
- 2 carrots sliced

## Directions

1. Parboil the tongue for about ten minutes.
2. Skin and clean well.
3. Chop an onion very fine, and brown this in a tablespoonful of butter.
4. When brown, add the tongue, which you will have arranged by fastening the thick part to the tip with a skewer, as for roast beef.
5. Let it cook, smothering slowly, for fifteen minutes, and then add another onion sliced nicely.
6. Let this brown, and add one square inch of ham, well chopped, two carrots sliced, and a bay leaf, and two sprigs of thyme, minced fine.
7. Brown again, and add a pint of broth.
8. Season well, and add a glass of white wine, and then let it smother for one hour and a half longer, turning every quarter, so that every part may cook thoroughly.
9. Serve with the sauce in which it was cooked, or with a Sauce Piquante.
