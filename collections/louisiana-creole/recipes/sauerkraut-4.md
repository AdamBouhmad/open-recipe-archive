## Ingredients

- 1 Fine Head of Cabbage
- 1 Pint of Vinegar
- 1 Bottle of White Wine
- 1 Glass of Brandy or Whisky
- Salt in Sufficient Quantity to Allow 3 Ounces for each Layer of Cabbage

## Directions

1. Take a large head of cabbage, and take off the green leaves.
2. Shred the cabbage into fine pieces, of about five inches long and one wide.
3. Get an earthern vessel or a keg, and line the bottom and sides with the green leaves of the cabbage.
4. Put in a layer of salt, of about three ounces, and lay over this a layer of cabbage leaves of about three inches in thickness.
5. Cover again with a layer of salt, and pound down well, and so continue until you have used up the cabbage.
6. Pour over this sufficient vinegar to cover, and also, if possible, a bottle of White Wine and a glass of brandy or whisky.
7. Take some boards or the cover of the keg and line them with cabbage leaves, and cover the keg closely.
8. Put the cover on the keg, or the board over the bowl, with a fifteen-pound weight on top.
9. Set it in a place of even moderate temperature.
10. Bore a hole in the bottom of the keg, and insert a piece of wood.
11. When the cabbage begins to ferment, take the piece of wood out, and let the liquor from the fermentation flow through this canal. This will be in about four or five days.
12. After this first operation open the keg and renew the vinegar and wine, skimming the fermentation from the top, and so continue until the cabbage is clear and without odor.
13. Place the Chou-Croute in a cool place.
14. When ready to use, take it out and let it soak for two or three hours in cool fresh water, and when quite fresh-looking put it into a saucepan and cook as you would cabbage, with salt meat, pork, sausage or corned beef.
