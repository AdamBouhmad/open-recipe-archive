## Ingredients

- 4 Pounds of the Brisket of Mutton
- 3 Large Onions
- 1 Bay Leaf
- 1/2 Clove of Garlic
- 1/4 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 2 Quarts of Water
- 1 Square Inch of Ham, Chopped Very Fine
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- Potatoes

## Directions

1. Cut the mutton into pieces of about an Inch square and season well with salt and pepper.
2. Put one-quarter of a tablespoonful of lard into the stew-pot, and when it melts add the thinly sliced onions.
3. Let these brown for a few minutes and then add the mutton and the ham, chopped very fine.
4. Let this continue browning, and when slightly browned, add one tablespoonful of finely sifted flour and stir well.
5. Then add the finely-minced bay leaf and a half clove of garlic, minced fine.
6. Brown lightly, for a mutton stew must never be dark.
7. After twenty minutes, add two quarts of boiling water and let it boil for about ten minutes longer, seasoning to taste.
8. Then add the potatoes, cut into halves, and let the mixture cook for three-quarters of an hour longer, making one hour and a half in all.
9. Let it simmer gently all the time, so that the meat may be perfectly tender.
