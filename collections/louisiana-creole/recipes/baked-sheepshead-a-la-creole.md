## Ingredients

- 3-Pound Sheepshead
- 1 Cup of Stale Bread Crumbs
- 1 Onion
- 1 Tablespoonful of Chopped Parsley
- 1 Tablespoonful of Melted Butter
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 1 Pint of White Wine or Boiling Water
- Salt and Pepper and Flour to Dredge
- Hollandaise, Bechamel or Tomato Sauce
- Lemon slices
- Fried potato balls
- Optional: One dozen oysters or shrimp or the yolks of two hard-boiled eggs

## Directions

1. Clean the fish thoroughly, inside and out; wash well and dry thoroughly with a clean towel.
2. Rub it well with salt and pepper.
3. Make a dressing by taking one cup of stale bread crumbs, wet and squeezed thoroughly of all water, one tablespoonful of melted butter, one tablespoonful of chopped parsley, a half teaspoonful of salt and a little black pepper.
4. Mix well and fry in a little lard, optionally adding one dozen oysters or shrimp or the yolks of two hard-boiled eggs.
5. Stuff the body of the fish and sew up with soft thread or soft yarn.
6. Score or slit the fish on either side, making the scores about an inch apart and lard it either by putting a strip of salt fat in each gash or filling with lard.
7. Grease the bottom of a baking pan and put the fish in it.
8. Dredge thickly with salt and flour and a little Cayenne.
9. Pour over a half bottle of white wine, or simply cover the bottom of the pan with the grease and add a little boiling water.
10. Put the fish in a hot oven.
11. Baste every ten minutes or so by taking a spoon and pouring the gravy over the fish.
12. Allow about fifteen minutes of baking to every pound of fish.
13. When brown on one side, turn on the other.
14. When done carefully, slide the fish into the center of the flat dish in which it is to be served.
15. Garnish nicely with slices of lemon, fried potato balls and chopped parsley, placing sprigs of parsley between the fried potato balls and laying on the slices of lemon.
16. Serve with Hollandaise Sauce or Bechamel Sauce.
