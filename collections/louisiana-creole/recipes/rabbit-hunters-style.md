## Ingredients

- A Pair of Rabbits
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1 Onion
- 1 Slice of Ham
- 1 Tablespoonful of Flour
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- 2 Sprigs of Thyme
- 2 Bay Leaves
- ½ Box of Mushrooms
- The Zest of a Lemon
- ½ Bottle of Claret Wine
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- Croutons to Garnish
- 1 Quart of Consommé or Water

## Directions

1. Prepare the rabbit; clean and draw, and cut into pieces at the joints.
2. Rub well with salt and pepper; put a tablespoonful of butter into the saucepan with the rabbit and let it brown slowly.
3. When nearly brown, add the onion, chopped fine, and let this brown slightly.
4. Then add the ham, minced very fine, and the clove and garlic and bay leaves and thyme, minced very fine.
5. Stir with the rabbit, and let these brown for about two minutes; then add a tablespoonful of flour and brown for a few minutes.
6. Add a half bottle of Claret wine and let all simmer for five minutes; then add a quart of consommé or water, and let all cook for about one hour.
7. Season according to taste.
8. Add a half can of mushrooms, chopped fine, and the zest of a lemon and again season to taste.
9. Let all cook for a half hour longer and serve on a hot dish, with croutons fried in butter.
