## Ingredients

- 1 Dozen Large Live Crabs
- 1 Tablespoonful of Butter or Lard
- 1 Stalk Chopped Celery
- 1 Dozen Fresh Tomatoes or 1 Can
- Thyme
- Parsley
- Bay Leaf
- 1 Clove
- Garlic
- Salt and Cayenne to Taste
- 1 Large Onion

## Directions

1. Boil a dozen fine large crabs about five minutes in order to kill them.
2. Take off the fire and place in a dish.
3. When sufficiently cooled, cut off the claws and crack, separating the joints.
4. Remove the "apron" or "tablier" of the crab and the "dead man's fingers," and take off the spongy substance.
5. Remove the shell, cut the body of the crab into four parts, cutting down the center and across.
6. Chop a large onion very fine and brown with butter or lard, using a tablespoonful of either.
7. Add a dozen fine, large, fresh tomatoes, chopped fine, in their liquor, and brown nicely.
8. Stir in chopped celery, thyme, parsley, one bay leaf, chopped fine; pepper and salt to taste, and a dash of Cayenne pepper.
9. Add one clove of garlic, chopped fine.
10. Taste and add more seasoning if necessary.
11. Let the mixture cook ten minutes, then add the crabs and let them cook ten minutes longer.
12. Never add water to this sauce, as the liquor of the tomatoes is sufficient and makes an excellent sauce.
13. Serve with boiled rice or potatoes boiled whole.
