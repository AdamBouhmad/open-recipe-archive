## Ingredients

- 4 Pounds of Veal Rump
- 2 Pig's Feet
- ½ Pound of Salt Fat Meat
- 5 Large Onions
- 2 Turnips
- 5 Carrots
- 2 Cloves of Garlic
- 3 Bay Leaves
- 1 Tablespoonful of Lard
- 1 Glass of Sherry
- 3 Sprigs of Thyme and Parsley
- Salt, Pepper and Cayenne to Taste
- 2 Pounds Veal Steak
- 4 Quarts of Water
- 2 Cloves
- 1 Sprig of Thyme
- Madeira Wine

## Directions

1. Cut the salt meat into shreds, roll well in Cayenne and black pepper.
2. Chop finely several sprigs of thyme and three bay leaves, one clove of garlic, three sprigs of parsley, and mash well three cloves and six allspice.
3. Roll the strips of salt meat, which must be about three inches in length and one-half inch thick, in this.
4. Make incisions into the rump of meat and force in the strips of fat meat and the spices.
5. Rub the whole well with salt and pepper, judging according to taste, and proceed to cook according to the recipe for Beef à la Mode.
6. Let the daube cook about four hours when you intend to serve it cold.
7. In the meantime, in another pot, place a veal steak of about two pounds and two pigs' feet.
8. Season well with salt and pepper and Cayenne, and cover well with four quarts of water, and let them boil.
9. Add one bay leaf, one sprig of thyme, one-half clove of garlic and one onion, all minced very fine, and two cloves mashed into almost a jelly, and one glass of Sherry or Madeira Wine.
10. Let these boil well with the veal and pigs' feet.
11. When the veal and pigs' feet are cooked very tender, take them out of the pot and mince the meat of each very fine.
12. Return to the sauce, and again season highly, according to taste.
13. After the daube has cooked four or five hours, take off the stove and pour over the sauce and set all in a cool place.
14. Serve the next day—cold, cutting into thin slices.
