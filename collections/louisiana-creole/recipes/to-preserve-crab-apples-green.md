## Ingredients

- crab apples
- water
- vine leaves
- sugar

## Directions

1. Wash the apples and boil them in a very little water, cover them with vine leaves, while on the fire simmering, and they will then be very yellow.
2. Take them out and spread them on a large dish to cool.
3. Pare and core them, put them back in the kettle, with fresh leaves to cover them.
4. Hang them over the fire, or on the stove in a preserving kettle until they are green.
5. Take them out of the pot, let them cool, weigh them, and allow a pound of sugar to a pound of fruit.
6. Put only water sufficient to dissolve the sugar, as the fruit, having been already boiled, will require very little water—a small cupful to each pound being quite enough.
7. Boil this syrup, skim it, and put in your green apples, and boil them until they are clear and tender.
8. Put the apples in jars, turn the juice on to them, and when cold tie them up, or rather seal them.
