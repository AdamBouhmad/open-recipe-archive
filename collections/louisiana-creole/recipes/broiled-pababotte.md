## Ingredients

- 6 Pababottes
- 6 Fine Strips of Bacon
- 6 Slices of Buttered French Toast
- 3 Tablespoonfuls of Melted Butter
- Juice of 1 Lemon
- 1 Tablespoonful of Chopped Parsley
- Sliced Lemon and Parsley Sprigs to Garnish
- Salt and Pepper to Taste

## Directions

1. Clean the Pababotte well, removing the entrails, and be particularly careful to throw away the gizzard.
2. Rub the birds with salt and pepper and then with melted butter.
3. Tie a strip of very finely-sliced bacon around the body of each bird, joining the bacon with a skewer.
4. Place the birds on a broiler over a slow fire and let them cook fifteen, twenty or even thirty minutes, according to the size of the birds.
5. Turn frequently, so that they may broil without burning.
6. When done, take off the broiler; have ready the slices of buttered French toast, and place a bird upon each slice.
7. Trim away the rough edges of the toast.
8. Pour over the birds a little of the juice that has run from them in broiling, and let this soak down into the toast.
9. Pour over a little melted butter and chopped parsley, and add a little lemon juice, if desired.
10. Garnish with slices of lemon and parsley sprigs and bring to the table hot.
