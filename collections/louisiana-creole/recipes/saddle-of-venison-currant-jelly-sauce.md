## Ingredients

- A Saddle of Venison Weighing About 5 Pounds
- 1 Onion
- 1 Carrot
- 1/2 Tablespoonful of Butter
- 1/2 Glass of Madeira Wine
- 1 Gill of Consomme
- Currant Jelly Sauce

## Directions

1. Skin the Venison neatly and remove all the sinews from the surface.
2. Take fine larding needles and lard closely.
3. Tie the saddle around four times.
4. Slice the carrot and onion and put in the resting pan.
5. Place the Saddle of Venison on top of these sprinkle lightly with a pinch of salt, and spread a half tablespoonful of butter over.
6. Set in a brisk oven and roast for forty minutes, frequently basting the venison with its own gravy.
7. Before taking it from the pan, remove the cord which binds it and place the saddle in a hot dish.
8. Pour the Madeira wine and a gill of veal consommé into the pan, set on the stove and let it come to a boil.
9. Skim the gravy of all fat and strain over the venison.
10. For the sauce: Take a half pint of Currant Jelly and stir till it is thoroughly dissolved.
11. Put in a saucepan a wineglassful of good old Port wine, and set on the stove and let it come gradually to a boil.
12. Add the currant jelly and mix till thoroughly dissolved.
13. Add a tablespoonful of Sauce Espagnole and let it again come to a boil.
14. Serve with the venison, sending each to the table separately.
