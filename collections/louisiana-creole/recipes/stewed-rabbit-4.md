## Ingredients

- 1 Quart of Water or Consomme
- Salt and Pepper to Taste
- A Dash of Cayenne
- Rabbit
- Lard or Butter
- Two tablespoonfuls of Flour
- Ten or twelve Allspice
- Three sprigs each of chopped Thyme, Parsley, Bay Leaf and Sweet Marjoram
- One clove of Garlic
- One large Onion
- Six fresh large Tomatoes or one-half can of Tomatoes
- One glass of good Claret
- Juice of a Lemon

## Directions

1. Skin, clean, wash and cut the rabbit into pieces at the joints
2. Put the lard or butter into a deep stewpan or kettle
3. When hot, add gradually two tablespoonfuls of flour, stirring constantly to prevent burning
4. Throw in about ten or twelve well-mashed allspice, and three sprigs each of chopped thyme, parsley, bay leaf and sweet marjoram, one clove of garlic, and one large onion, chopped very fine
5. Add six fresh large tomatoes, chopped fine, or one-half can of tomatoes
6. Pour in one glass of good claret, add about one quart of water, and let it boil well
7. Then add salt and Cayenne to taste, and when this has boiled about five minutes, add the rabbit, putting in piece by piece
8. Add the Juice of a lemon, and let all boil about ten minutes
9. Serve with French Fried Potatoes, Mashed Potatoes, or Potato Croquettes
