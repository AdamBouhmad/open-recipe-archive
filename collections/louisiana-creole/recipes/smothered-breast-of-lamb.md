## Ingredients

- Shoulder and Breast of a Lamb
- 2 Sprigs of Parsley
- 1 Carrot
- 2 Onions
- 1 Clove of Garlic
- ½ Can of Tomatoes
- A Sprig Each of Thyme and Bay Leaf
- Bread Crumbs
- 2 Tablespoonfuls of Butter
- Pepper and Salt to Taste
- 1 Pint of Water

## Directions

1. Select nice, fresh breast and shoulder of lamb. Have the butcher remove all the bones; wash or wipe carefully with a damp towel.
2. Take one cup of bread crumbs, which have been wet and squeezed, and season well with one grated onion and clove of garlic, and chopped parsley, thyme and bay leaf, and spices to taste, put in a frying pan with one tablespoonful of Butter, and fry about five minutes.
3. Place this dressing into the open side of the lamb, and roll it up in its own meat, and tie it securely with thin strips of twine that the dressing may not escape in baking.
4. Slice the carrot, onion and turnip very fine, and fry in a tablespoonful of butter in a deep pan.
5. When brown, add the lamb and cover and let it simmer for about fifteen minutes.
6. Then add the tomatoes and let them brown; then add just enough boiling water to cover the meat (one pint).
7. Set the pot back on the stove and let it simmer gently and steadily for about three hours, or according to the size of the roll.
8. Serve with the vegetables dished around and with its own gravy.
