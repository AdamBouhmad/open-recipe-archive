## Ingredients

- 2 pounds of fresh fish, preferably redfish or sheepshead
- 3 medium-sized potatoes
- 1 onion
- 1 clove of garlic
- 3 sprigs each of thyme, parsley and bay leaf
- 1/4 can of tomatoes
- 1/4 cup of milk
- 1 quart of boiling water
- 2 ounces of salt pork or ham chopped very fine
- Grated oyster crackers
- Salt, Cayenne and black pepper to taste

## Directions

1. Cut two pounds of fresh fish of any kind, preferably the redfish or sheepshead.
2. Take three medium-sized potatoes and one onion and cut into slices.
3. Take two ounces of salt pork, wash well and chop very fine.
4. Put the pork into a frying pan, and when it is hot add the sliced onion.
5. Smother slightly, and add chopped thyme, parsley, bay leaf, one clove of garlic very fine, and Cayenne and black pepper to taste.
6. Let this simmer for about ten minutes longer.
7. Pour over this one quart of boiling water, and add fish and half a can of tomatoes and the potatoes.
8. Season to taste, and cover the pan and let the contents simmer for half an hour.
9. A half cup of milk may be added, if desired.
10. Take oyster crackers, place in a bowl and pour the chowder over and serve hot.
