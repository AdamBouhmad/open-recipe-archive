## Ingredients

- a pint cup of fig-leaves
- a quart of cold water
- Irish potatoes enough to fill a pint-cup
- a tablespoonful of brown sugar
- flour
- corn-meal
- a cup of cold water
- a spoonful of brown sugar
- two quarts of flour
- lard

## Directions

1. Take a pint cup of the leaves, put them to a quart of cold water, and boil them until a strong tea or decoction is made
2. put away to cool
3. pour off the tea carefully, leaving the dregs and leaves
4. Now boil and wash Irish potatoes enough to fill a pint-cup
5. put them to the tea of fig-leaves
6. beat them up with a tablespoonful of brown sugar and flour, to make a stiff batter
7. put it in a covered vessel to rise
8. When this yeast is light and frothing, thicken it immediately with corn-meal, until it is thick enough to be rolled out like biscuit
9. Roll it out, cut and dry the cake, turning them very often until dry
10. When you wish to make bread, take one of the cakes in the morning, put it in a covered mug or pitcher
11. put on it a cup of cold water, and when it is dissolved, put to it a spoonful of brown sugar, and make a batter of the water and yeast cake
12. Make this batter as stiff as pound-cake batter
13. when it rises well, mix with two quarts of flour, and the bread will be most excellent
14. Use lard as usual in making the bread up for baking
