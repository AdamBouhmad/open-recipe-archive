## Ingredients

- 3 Pounds of the Best Flour
- ⅛ Ounce of Yeast
- 1 Cup of Sugar
- ⅛ Ounce of Salt
- 1 Pound of the Best Butter
- 12 or 14 Eggs
- ½ Pound of Seeded Raisins
- ¼ Pound of Currants
- ¼ Glass of Sweetened Water
- A Tinge of Saffron

## Directions

1. The dough for the Baba is exactly the same as that for the Brioche.
2. Proceed in exactly the same manner, only at the moment of adding the reserved flour to the leavened dough, add a half pound of raisins seeded, and a half pound of currants, washed, picked and dried.
3. Add also a half glass of sweetened, tepid water, in which you will have put a little saffron.
4. Keep this dough much softer than that for the Brioche, adding more eggs, if necessary, to insure the proper degree of consistency.
5. The dough must rise at least six or seven hours.
6. When it has increased to twice its volume, after the third rising, cook as you would the Brioche, in an oven a few degrees cooler than that used in baking Bread.
7. The Baba is formed into a round shape with the hands.
8. After baking let cool and sprinkle with powdered white sugar.
