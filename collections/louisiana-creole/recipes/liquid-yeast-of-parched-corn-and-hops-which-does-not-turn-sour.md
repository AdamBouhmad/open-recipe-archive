## Ingredients

- corn
- hops
- water
- potatoes
- vinegar
- sugar
- salt
- good yeast
- boiled potatoes
- flour

## Directions

1. Take two teacupfuls of corn and parch it thoroughly, being careful not to burn it
2. Add a good handful of hops
3. Boil in water enough to cover well for an hour and a half
4. Pare six good-sized potatoes and boil them for half an hour with the corn and hops
5. Sift the potatoes through a colander when done and strain the liquor through a cloth onto the potatoes
6. Add a tablespoonful of vinegar, one cup of sugar, and a half cup of salt
7. Put in cold water enough to make up a gallon
8. Add a teacupful of good yeast to raise it
9. Put the whole in a jug and set it without corking in a warm place till it begins to work
10. Cork it and put it in the cellar; the longer it stands the better it becomes
11. At noon take five or six boiled potatoes and mash them very fine
12. Stir in a teacupful of flour and pour on a quart of boiling water
13. Put in a cup nearly full of yeast and set the ferment in a warm place till night
14. Set a soft sponge with warm water and flour, adding the ferment
15. It will be ready to mould up hard the first thing in the morning
16. Let it rise till quite light, then mould it out in loaves
17. Rise again and bake in the usual way
