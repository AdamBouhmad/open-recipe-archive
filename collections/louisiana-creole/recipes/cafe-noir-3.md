## Ingredients

- coffee, ground
- freshly boiled water

## Directions

1. Have the water heated to a good boil. Set the coffee pot in front of the stove; never on top, as the coffee will boil, and then the taste is destroyed.
2. Allow one cup, or the ordinary mill, of coffee to make four cups of the liquid, ground and put in the strainer, being careful to keep both the strainer and the spout of the coffee-pot covered; to prevent the flavor from escaping.
3. Pour, first, about two tablespoonfuls of the boiling water on the coffee grounds, or, according to the quantity of coffee used, just sufficient to settle the grounds.
4. Wait about five minutes, then pour a little more water, and allow it to drip slowly through; but never pour water the second time until the grounds have ceased to puff or bubble, as this is an indication that the grounds have settled.
5. Keep pouring slowly, at intervals, a little boiling water at a time, until the delightful aroma of the coffee begins to escape from the closed spout of the coffee pot.
6. When you have produced a rich, fragrant concoction, serve in fine china cups, using no preference than sugar for sweetening.
