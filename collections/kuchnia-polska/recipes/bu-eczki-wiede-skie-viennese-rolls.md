## Ingredients

- two cups of milk
- one tablet of yeast
- half a cup of warm water
- three cups of sifted flour
- one well-beaten egg
- quarter cup of melted lard or butter
- two tablespoons of sugar
- one teaspoon of salt
- three more cups of sifted flour

## Directions

1. Heat two cups of milk and cool to lukewarm temperature, then add one tablet of yeast previously dissolved in half a cup of warm water and add three cups of sifted flour
2. Beat with a wooden spoon for about ten minutes until the mixture is uniform and smooth, then cover and set in a warm place
3. When well risen, add one well-beaten egg, quarter cup of melted lard or butter, two tablespoons of sugar and one teaspoon of salt
4. Gradually add three more cups of sifted flour while mixing, turn out onto a work surface and knead for at least fifteen minutes
5. Cover again and set to allow the dough to rise; when risen to double volume, shape into rolls or croissants of any desired form and set to rise again
6. Bake in a hot oven for about fifteen minutes; before removing from the oven, brush the top with egg white
