## Ingredients

- 1 young duck
- salt
- 1/2 quart grated white bread, preferably stale
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon pepper
- 1/2 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
- 1 tablespoon chopped onion
- 1/2 teaspoon powdered sage
- 1/3 cup butter
- flour
- water
- lemon juice
- red currant jelly
- butter
- flour
- water
- salt
- pepper

## Directions

1. Pluck, clean, and wipe the duck inside with salt
2. Prepare the stuffing: take 1/2 quart of grated white bread, preferably stale; add 1/2 teaspoon salt, 1/4 teaspoon pepper, 1/2 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley, 1 tablespoon chopped onion, 1/2 teaspoon powdered sage, and 1/3 cup butter; mix everything together well
3. Stuff the duck with this mixture
4. Wipe the top with salt and then with butter, and finally sprinkle with flour so that a coating forms from the butter and flour
5. Place in a very hot oven for a few minutes so the flour browns slightly
6. Then pour some water into the roasting pan and roast for one hour, frequently basting and adding fresh water as it evaporates, but add it gradually
7. When one side is well browned, turn to the other side
8. Before serving, sprinkle with lemon juice, place on a platter, and pour the sauce from the roasting pan over it
9. Separately serve red currant jelly and giblet sauce prepared as follows: chop the giblets finely and cook them covered on very low heat with 1 tablespoon of butter mixed with flour and a very small amount of water, salt, and pepper
