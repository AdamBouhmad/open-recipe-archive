## Ingredients

- a large quarter of veal weighing ten to fifteen pounds
- one pound of salt
- half ounce of powdered saltpeter

## Directions

1. Salt the large quarter of veal with one pound of salt and half ounce of powdered saltpeter and place it in a barrel, pressing it down with a stone.
2. It may be placed together with other meat that is being salted.
3. Every day the meat must be turned over and basted with brine so that it does not dry out on any side.
4. Leave the salted veal to lie for two weeks, then smoke it in a smokehouse like any other cured meat.
5. After smoking, it is best to roast it in a very hot oven, having first covered it with rye dough; it bakes for two to three hours, depending on size.
6. Instead of roasting, it may also be boiled like ordinary ham.
