## Ingredients

- tart apples, one quart when cut into hazelnut-sized pieces
- three cups flour
- two teaspoons baking powder
- two tablespoons lard or butter
- salt
- cold milk or water
- nutmeg or cloves, ground
- sour cream or sauce

## Directions

1. Peel and cut tart apples into pieces the size of a hazelnut until you have one quart.
2. Sift three cups of flour with two teaspoons of baking powder and mix with two tablespoons of lard or butter, add a pinch of salt and enough cold milk or water to make a soft dough.
3. Roll out on a work surface into a long, fairly thick cake, arrange apples on one end, sprinkle with a pinch of ground nutmeg or cloves, and wrap the apples in the pastry.
4. Wrap in a napkin or piece of cloth wrung out in very hot water and steam for at least one and a half hours.
5. Serve with sour cream or with sauce made by pouring one tablespoon of hot water into half a cup of butter to soften it, then adding slightly more than half a cup of brown sugar and beating strongly for several minutes, then adding for flavor a pinch of nutmeg and a few drops of vanilla extract.
