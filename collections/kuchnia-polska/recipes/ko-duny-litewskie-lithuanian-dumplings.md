## Ingredients

- 1 pound sirloin, finely chopped but not minced
- 1 pound kidney fat, minced
- salt
- pepper
- 2 large onions, minced
- broth
- meat scraps
- 1 quart flour
- 1 egg
- water

## Directions

1. Remove sinews from the sirloin and chop very finely without mincing.
2. Mince the kidney fat, add salt and pepper to taste, and mix with the meat.
3. Mince the onions, place in a pan with a little broth, the meat scraps, and braise covered until the onion is tender.
4. Pound in a mortar and pass through a sieve.
5. Add to the prepared meat and mix well.
6. Take one quart of flour, one egg, and enough water to make a fairly soft dough.
7. Knead and roll out as thin as possible.
8. Place mounds of filling the size of a walnut on the dough and seal the edges well.
9. Wrap the dough loosely around the filling, as otherwise the dumplings may burst during cooking.
10. Bring water to a boil in a wide pan and drop in the dumplings.
11. When they float to the surface, they are done; do not cook longer as the meat will harden.
12. Remove with a slotted spoon and serve immediately.
13. Some make these dumplings with fatty lamb, proceeding in exactly the same way as with beef dumplings.
