## Ingredients

- pike or sandacz weighing at least eight to ten pounds
- various vegetables
- salt
- gelatin
- egg whites
- vinegar
- burnt sugar
- red gelatin
- oil (one and one-half to two pounds for a ten-pound fish)
- egg yolks
- white pepper
- mushrooms
- cornichons
- cooked vegetables (carrots, peas, potatoes)
- lettuce
- capers

## Directions

1. Use a pike or sandacz weighing no less than eight to ten pounds.
2. Clean the fish and carefully split it through the belly with a sharp knife.
3. Carefully remove the backbone without damaging the skin.
4. Fold the fish back together so it looks whole.
5. Prepare a court-bouillon (smak) from various vegetables, heavily salted, and cool it.
6. Place the fish in a long pan so it fits completely.
7. Pour the cold vegetable court-bouillon over it.
8. Cook on a low fire until tender.
9. Remove from heat and place in a cool location where it must stay until the next day.
10. On the next day, remove the fish and place it on another platter.
11. To the remaining court-bouillon add gelatin and bring to a boil.
12. Beat two egg whites with a spoon of water, add vinegar and slowly pour this into the gelatin mixture, continuously beating with a whisk.
13. Strain the gelatin and court-bouillon mixture, then place on fire and boil until it clears.
14. Remove from heat and strain through dense cloth into a deep platter and cool to make an aspic for coating the fish.
15. This aspic can be made in two or more colors, using burnt sugar for yellow coloring and red gelatin for pink color.
16. For a ten-pound fish, take one and one-half to two pounds of oil.
17. Beat two egg yolks in a bowl, mix them well.
18. Add salt and a pinch of white pepper.
19. Slowly pour the oil while stirring vigorously.
20. When half a pound of oil has been incorporated, add another beaten egg yolk, a few spoons of oil, and then a fourth egg yolk.
21. Continue stirring and pouring the oil in drops until all is used.
22. Do this in a cool place.
23. When the mayonnaise is thick, add strong vinegar to taste and stir in one teaspoon of fine sugar.
24. Cover the entire fish arranged on a platter with this mayonnaise.
25. Around it arrange aspic, neatly sliced mushrooms, cornichons and cooked vegetables such as carrots, green peas, potatoes etc., which should be cut into cubes, stars and other neat shapes.
26. These vegetables are best cut raw and cooked in heavily salted water.
27. Arrange lettuce leaves around the platter.
28. Cut the aspic with a serrated knife into triangles.
29. Sprinkle capers over the mayonnaise on top.
