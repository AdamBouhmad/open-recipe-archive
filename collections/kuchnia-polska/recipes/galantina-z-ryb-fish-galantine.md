## Ingredients

- pike, 2 fish (one large, one small)
- salt
- onion, parboiled
- butter
- bread roll, soaked
- pepper
- nutmeg
- hard-boiled eggs
- napkin, butter-smeared
- twine
- vegetables miscellaneous
- gelatin

## Directions

1. Pike is best for galantine, though sandacz may also be used.
2. Take two pike, one large and one small; clean the large one, fillet lengthwise, remove tail and head, and remove all bones, salt and leave for one hour.
3. Clean the small pike, remove skin and pick all the meat, which should be finely chopped, with the addition of parboiled onion, butter, soaked bread roll, salt and pepper to taste and a pinch of nutmeg.
4. With this forcemeat, after proper mixing, fill the filleted pike, interspersing with hard-boiled eggs cut into quarters.
5. Fold both halves together, wrap tightly in a butter-smeared napkin and tie around with twine.
6. Place the fish prepared in this way in an oblong pan, cover with broth made from the head, bones and miscellaneous vegetables and cook for at most one hour over low heat.
7. After cooking, remove from heat and let rest in a cool place until it cools.
8. After complete cooling, remove the fish, unwrap from the napkin and arrange on a platter.
9. Cook the remaining broth, clarify with egg whites, add gelatin and chill into aspic, which should then be cut into neat pieces with a fluted knife and used to decorate the fish arranged on the platter.
10. Separately cook miscellaneous vegetables, previously cut into nice cubes and stars, and use them to decorate the platter.
