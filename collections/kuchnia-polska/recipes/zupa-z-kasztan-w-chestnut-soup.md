## Ingredients

- one pound chestnuts
- one-quarter pound sugar
- two tablespoons water
- one-quarter pound fresh butter
- one glass light white wine
- light veal broth
- about ten whole cooked and peeled chestnuts
- a handful of finely chopped croutons fried in butter

## Directions

1. Cook one pound of chestnuts, drain the water, peel them while hot, covering with a napkin so they do not cool.
2. Immediately after peeling, crush them in a mortar, then pass through a fine sieve, pouring water at the same time so they pass through more easily.
3. Lightly brown one-quarter pound of sugar with two tablespoons of water over high heat.
4. Into this sugar place the mashed chestnuts, add one-quarter pound of fresh butter, mix well, then pour in one glass of light white wine.
5. Prepare separately a light veal broth, from which all the fat should first be carefully removed.
6. Take one-quarter of this broth and thin the chestnuts with wine, then pour into a dish into which first place about ten whole cooked and peeled chestnuts, as well as a handful of finely chopped croutons fried in butter.
7. If the soup is to be meatless, water can be used instead of broth.
