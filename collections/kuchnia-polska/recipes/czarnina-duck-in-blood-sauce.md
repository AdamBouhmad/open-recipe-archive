## Ingredients

- duck, goose, piglet, or hare front quarters
- vinegar
- salt
- blood from the animal
- vegetables
- mushrooms
- onion
- fresh pork fat with skin
- flour
- sugar
- dried pears and plums, or fresh pears and apples
- flour dumplings

## Directions

1. Boil some vinegar, salt it, cool it, and catch the blood in it during slaughter of the duck or piglet.
2. Clean the animal, scald it, singe the hair over fire, and place on broth.
3. For goose, typically use only the offal, as the goose itself is roasted as a roast stuffed with apples, since a stew from it is not good; instead cook a few pounds of beef with the offal.
4. After the broth comes to a boil and is salted, add vegetables, a few mushrooms, onion, and a piece of fresh pork fat with skin.
5. When everything is cooked tender, strain the broth, remove the duck or piglet to a platter and cover so it does not cool quickly, from which a stew with sauce is made, for which some clean broth should be reserved.
6. When the broth is cooked, strain it and season with the blood prepared with vinegar and flour.
7. Add vinegar to taste, a few tablespoons of sugar, cut the pork fat into cubes, add to the soup and boil everything together, being careful it does not curdle.
8. Separately cook dried pears and plums, or fresh pears and apples in a clay pot, and these together with the sauce in which they cooked, of which there should be very little, pour into the serving bowl.
9. Separately cook sliced dumplings in water and place in the bowl along with the offal.
