## Ingredients

- young and fat turkey, ten to twelve pounds
- baking soda
- salt
- two dozen chestnuts
- turkey liver
- parsley, finely chopped
- onion
- pepper
- two egg yolks
- five small pork sausages
- butter
- grated bread or white bread
- smoked bacon, thinly sliced
- flour

## Directions

1. Select a young and fat turkey weighing ten to twelve pounds
2. After plucking, singe over a sheet of lit paper, holding the turkey carefully to avoid tearing the innards
3. Wash carefully in four waters; add one teaspoon of baking soda to the third water
4. Dry thoroughly both inside and outside, then rub the inside with a little salt
5. Roast two dozen chestnuts and peel them
6. Pound eight of these chestnuts and the turkey liver in a mortar into a paste, add finely chopped parsley, onion, salt and pepper, and two egg yolks, mix into a paste and stuff the cavity and sew it shut
7. Fry five small pork sausages in butter until half cooked; add one cup of grated bread or white bread, one large spoon of butter, salt and pepper to taste and the remaining whole chestnuts, stuff the turkey body with this filling and sew it shut with strong twine
8. Tie the legs and wings to the body; brush with butter and wipe with salt and pepper, then coat with flour
9. Place in a roasting pan and cover with thin slices of smoked bacon; place in a moderately hot oven
10. Roast for twenty minutes per pound of turkey, so for a twelve-pound turkey roast for four hours
11. During roasting, frequently baste and turn so that it browns evenly on all sides
12. Cook the giblets separately in salted water, then chop very finely and add to the sauce remaining in the pan after removing the turkey
