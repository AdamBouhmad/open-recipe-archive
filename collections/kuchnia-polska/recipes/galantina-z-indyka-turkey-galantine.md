## Ingredients

- turkey
- one pound young pork fat
- one pound veal
- veal liver
- turkey liver
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- two soaked and squeezed bread rolls
- three eggs
- young cooked pork fat, sliced
- turkey meat, sliced
- ham or tongue, in strips
- olives, optional
- salted water
- mixed vegetables
- four calf's feet
- two egg whites
- lemon juice

## Directions

1. After cleaning the turkey, discard the giblets and carefully butterfly it along the backbone, remove the bones, cut away meat remaining on the bones
2. Season with salt and spread a layer of the following filling on the flattened turkey: Take one pound of young pork fat, cut into pieces and sauté without browning; take one pound of veal, veal liver and turkey liver, braise all in the fat, then pass through a meat grinder, then strain through a sieve, add salt and pepper to taste, a pinch of nutmeg, two soaked and squeezed bread rolls and three eggs; mix everything properly and spread a one-inch thick layer on the flattened turkey
3. Over this spread a layer of slices of young cooked pork fat alternating with meat cut from the turkey and strips of ham or tongue, then again a layer of filling; if desired, add several olives to the filling
4. Roll the prepared turkey carefully, sew it shut, wrap in a napkin, shaping it into an oblong form, tie with twine and cook in salted water with mixed vegetables and four calf's feet for three hours
5. After cooking, remove from heat and let rest for one hour, then remove the turkey, unwrap the napkin and rinse in clean hot water, soak in cold water, wrap in the napkin again and press down with a weight so all sauce drains
6. From the remaining sauce, remove all fat, clarify with two egg whites beaten with water, remove from heat, add juice of half a lemon, then strain through a napkin, and place in a cold place to set as aspic
7. When the turkey has completely cooled, slice it, arrange on a platter and coat with aspic
8. Galantine can be made in the same way with chicken
