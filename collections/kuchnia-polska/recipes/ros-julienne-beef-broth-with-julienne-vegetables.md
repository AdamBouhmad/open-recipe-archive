## Ingredients

- three-quarters pound beef, preferably from the cross-rib, per person
- some veal, in small pieces
- some chicken giblets
- beef fat, a few spoonfuls
- onions, roasted
- carrots, one or two
- water
- cooked green peas, one spoonful
- spinach leaves cut into strips, or asparagus, cauliflower, or brussels sprouts

## Directions

1. Chop finely together the veal, giblets, and small pieces of beef, about half a pound total. Add several spoonfuls of beef fat and place in a pot over heat with just enough water so it does not scorch. When well browned, add a couple of roasted onions and one or two finely chopped carrots, allowing them to brown together. Keep moistening with water to prevent scorching. Cook the meat separately as usual for broth. Once the pieces with onion and carrot are well browned, add them with their juices to the broth and cook everything together. Separately, chop various vegetables finely into thin strips, place in another pot, and braise over very low heat, moistening gradually with broth. When both the meat and vegetables are completely cooked, add one spoonful of previously cooked green peas or strips of spinach leaves; alternatively, use asparagus, cauliflower, or brussels sprouts. Serve without any other additions.
