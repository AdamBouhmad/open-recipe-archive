## Ingredients

- one piglet
- salt
- butter (for the first stuffing method)
- pork lungs
- young pork fat
- raw liver
- three beaten eggs
- salt and pepper
- one large baked and grated onion
- one-half pound grated breadcrumbs
- a pinch of marjoram
- currants (optional)
- buckwheat groats (for second stuffing method)
- water
- pork fat (for second stuffing method)
- salt and pepper
- marjoram
- lard or pork fat (for basting)

## Directions

1. Clean the piglet and soak in cold water for one hour
2. Remove from water and rub with salt on the surface
3. Prepare the first stuffing: boil the piglet's lungs with a piece of young pork fat and mince as finely as possible together with raw liver
4. Add two tablespoons of melted butter, three beaten eggs, salt and pepper to taste, one large baked and grated onion, one-half pound of grated breadcrumbs, and a pinch of marjoram
5. Mix everything well; add a handful of small currants if desired
6. Alternative second stuffing: for a small piglet, take one-half quart of buckwheat groats, place in a clay pot and cover with the same amount of boiling water
7. Add one-half pound of pork fat cut into small cubes and fried; salt and mix well
8. Place on hot heat until the groats begin to thicken, then place the pot in a moderately hot oven for one-half hour
9. Cook the lungs until tender and the liver only briefly, then mince both together very finely
10. Add salt, pepper and a pinch of grated marjoram, mix with the groats
11. Stuff the piglet with either mixture and sew
12. Place in the oven on a baking pan, baste with lard and turn so it browns evenly on all sides
