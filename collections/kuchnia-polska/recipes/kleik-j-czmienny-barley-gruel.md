## Ingredients

- half a quart pearl or regular barley
- soft water, one quart
- fresh butter, one tablespoon
- salt

## Directions

1. One of the most important conditions when making gruel is to cook it in a stone or clay pot, because when cooked in iron, even if glazed, the barley will always be dark or at least dark.
2. Rinse half a quart of barley on a sieve with water and rinse lightly once with the husks, which are still found there.
3. Pour into a pot, cover with one quart of soft water and cook on low heat without salt, having put in one tablespoon of fresh butter, so that the barley separates better; stir frequently so as not to burn it.
4. White glue will constantly settle on top, similar to scum, and it should be constantly collected with a spoon, as this is the best gruel for the sick.
5. One must be careful so that this glue does not cook into the barley, or it will not be as good.
6. When the glue stops appearing, pass the barley through warm water or broth, then add the glue collected from the top.
7. Before serving at table, only then salt it, add some butter and mix it well with the gruel.
8. Barley passed through broth makes an excellent white soup, very nourishing and healthy.
