## Ingredients

- meat cut one inch thick
- butter or fat
- salt

## Directions

1. The best beefsteak is fried on an iron grill over which fire burns and flame reaches almost the meat
2. If such a grill is not available, fry on a skillet on very high heat
3. Commonly used in America are the following cuts: tenderloin, porterhouse, short porterhouse, and sirloin; porterhouse is considered the best
4. A good beefsteak must be cut one inch thick, and generally the larger the slice, the better the beefsteak
5. Throw such a slice of meat onto a heavy, strongly heated skillet on high heat with a small amount of butter or fat
6. Since the quality of beefsteak depends largely on high heat during frying, when frying on a skillet it is probably best to use fat rather than butter, as the temperature of hot fat is higher than butter, but after frying on fat, smear the beefsteak with butter
7. It is important that the meat not be beaten or salted at all—salt it only after frying
8. Always fry the entire slice of meat, never cut it into pieces
9. Serve only with the meat juice remaining on the skillet
10. Fry briefly, at most ten to fifteen minutes, and after frying the meat should be red inside
11. Immediately after frying, smear the beefsteak with butter and serve
12. Serve with potatoes as a vegetable, preferably fried French style
