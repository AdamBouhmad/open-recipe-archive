## Ingredients

- one tablespoon butter
- one tablespoon flour
- strong broth or bouillon
- a few pieces of sugar
- a handful of large seedless raisins
- half lemon
- or vinegar instead
- a few slices of lemon

## Directions

1. Brown one tablespoon butter with one tablespoon flour, dilute with strong broth or bouillon and bring to a boil.
2. Soak a few pieces of sugar in water and brown in a pan and pour into the sauce, also add a handful of large seedless raisins.
3. Squeeze juice from half a lemon, or instead pour in vinegar and bring to a boil.
4. Just before serving, add a few slices of lemon, from which the seeds should be removed as they give a bitter taste.
5. This sauce is used for tongue, veal feet or fish.
6. If it is to be used for fish, instead of broth dilute it with stock cooked from fish.
