## Ingredients

- ripe sour apples
- sugar in the proportion of three quarters of a quart per quart of juice
- geranium leaf (optional)

## Directions

1. Take ripe sour apples, chop them and pour a very small amount of water over them, then boil until they break down
2. Pour all the mass into a triangular bag made of thin cloth and hang it over a placed dish
3. When all the juice has dripped out, add three quarters of a quart of sugar for each quart of juice
4. Place on the fire and boil for fifteen minutes, counting from the time it boils
5. Pour into glasses and seal tightly
6. To make good jelly, never boil more than a few glasses at a time
7. A different and very pleasant aroma can be given to this jelly by placing a geranium leaf in a glass before pouring the jelly into it; after some time this leaf will turn yellow and float to the surface, then discard it
