## Ingredients

- fish weighing four to six pounds
- salt
- pepper
- bacon
- butter
- water
- parsley

## Directions

1. Take any fish weighing four to six pounds.
2. Clean it, wipe dry, salt inside and outside, stuff and sew.
3. With a sharp knife, make diagonal cuts two inches apart, arranged in a zigzag pattern.
4. Place the fish on a roasting pan.
5. Insert sliced bacon into the cuts.
6. Sprinkle with salt and pepper and place in the oven.
7. When it begins to brown, baste with butter and water every ten minutes.
8. When roasted (taking about an hour), remove the bacon and place on a platter.
9. Garnish with parsley sprigs.
10. To prevent the fish from sticking to the pan, you can place a couple of strips of clean cloth under it and lift it with the cloth after roasting.
11. Prepare the stuffing using one of three methods below: Method 1 - Soak one cup of grated bread or white bread in cold water then squeeze out the water; sauté three spoons of chopped onion in white fat; add one teaspoon of chopped parsley, one teaspoon of capers, one teaspoon of chopped cornichons, salt and pepper to taste and mix well. Method 2 - Chop very finely half a pound of lean veal and two ounces of smoked bacon; add one quarter cup of soaked and then squeezed out bread, one teaspoon of chopped onion, one teaspoon of chopped parsley, salt and pepper to taste and mix. Method 3 - Sauté one spoon of very finely chopped onion in two spoons of butter in white fat; add one cup of soaked and then squeezed out bread, one teaspoon of chopped green parsley, one beaten egg, salt and pepper to taste and mix carefully.
