## Ingredients

- a large but thin slice of round steak, about two pounds
- cooked ham, finely chopped, one cup
- two whole eggs
- one teaspoon mustard
- red pepper
- three tablespoons broth or water
- flour
- lard
- two onions, finely chopped
- three cups water
- salt
- pepper
- two cloves
- fresh parsley, chopped

## Directions

1. Cut a large but thin slice of round steak, about two pounds
2. Finely chop a piece of cooked ham and take one cup of this mass
3. Beat in two whole eggs, add one teaspoon of mustard, a pinch of red pepper and three tablespoons of broth or water
4. Mix it all into a smooth mass and spread it on the meat, then roll it tightly and tie it with twine in several places, but loosely enough that the twine does not cut the meat during cooking
5. Dust the rolled meat with flour and brown it in lard on fairly strong heat, then remove it
6. To the remaining lard in a pot, add two finely chopped onions, fry a little, add two tablespoons of flour, mix well and fry for about three minutes, then pour in three cups of water and bring to a boil
7. Place the meat in a pot of such size that it just fits and cover with the above sauce, add salt and pepper and two cloves, then cover and braise on very low heat for three hours, turning the meat once during this time
8. When serving, strain the sauce and pour it over the meat on a platter, and sprinkle chopped fresh parsley on top
