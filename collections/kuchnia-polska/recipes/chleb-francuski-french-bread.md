## Ingredients

- one quarter cup of boiled and cooled water
- one tablet of pressed yeast
- three quarters cup of sifted wheat flour
- half quart of boiled water cooled to lukewarm, about 82 to 84 degrees Fahrenheit
- one teaspoon of salt
- five cups of sifted flour

## Directions

1. Place one quarter cup of boiled and cooled water in a bowl and dissolve one tablet of pressed yeast, mixing well
2. Add about three quarters cup of sifted wheat flour to form a stiff dough
3. Knead well and form into a ball
4. Make cuts across the top of the ball with a knife on both sides
5. Drop into half quart of boiled water cooled to lukewarm, about 82 to 84 degrees Fahrenheit
6. Initially the dough will sink, but in fifteen or twenty minutes it will float to the surface
7. Pour the water with the dough into a bowl and add one teaspoon of salt and five cups of sifted flour
8. Mix thoroughly; the dough should be stiff enough for kneading; if too soft, add more flour
9. Turn onto a floured surface and knead for at least fifteen minutes
10. Cover in a bowl and place in a warm location for the dough to rise
11. When risen to double volume, carefully turn onto the surface without flipping, and cut in half
12. Form two long loaves and place in special long narrow forms (French bread pans)
13. Make a groove down the center of each loaf with a round stick or finger
14. Cover and let rise again
15. Bake in a moderately hot oven for half an hour
16. Just before baking is complete, remove the loaves and brush the top with egg white beaten with one tablespoon of cold water, then return to the oven for a smooth, hard crust; or brush with cornstarch mixed with cold water and then cooked, for a soft crust
