## Ingredients

- lamb bones, preferably from the shoulder
- one small veal bone
- three quarts cold water
- four tablespoons pearl barley
- chopped onion, some amount
- several peppercorns
- nutmeg, some amount
- one clove
- one or two celery stalks
- one quart good milk
- one tablespoon butter
- one tablespoon flour
- salt to taste
- small croutons from white bread

## Directions

1. Take lamb bones (preferably from the shoulder) and a small veal bone, cover with three quarts of cold water, add four tablespoons of pearl barley, some chopped onion, several peppercorns, a bit of nutmeg, one clove and one or two celery stalks, then cook on very low heat for four to five hours.
2. Then strain.
3. Separately bring one quart of good milk to a boil with one tablespoon of butter and one tablespoon of flour.
4. Pour the milk into the strained broth, salt to taste and pour into a dish lined with small croutons from white bread.
