## Ingredients

- Turkey
- Lard, sliced thin
- Salt
- Butter (quarter pound)
- Turkey liver, boiled and finely chopped
- One bun, soaked and squeezed dry
- One roasted grated onion
- Two eggs
- Cooked diced lard
- Pepper
- Nutmeg
- Sugar (one teaspoon)
- Raisins
- Fat or butter for basting

## Directions

1. All poultry for roasting should be slaughtered several days before roasting in winter, and one day in summer
2. Immediately after slaughtering pluck the turkey on the breasts, back and thighs, leaving the feathers on the wings
3. Before use take all the giblets for broth, and the turkey, carefully cleaned, lard in thin strips of sliced lard on the breasts and thighs, rub with salt inside and outside, stuff, sew and place in the oven on a roasting pan
4. Roast at first in a very hot oven to brown, then complete roasting in a moderately hot oven, basting with fat or butter
5. The best stuffing is made in the following way: Cream one quarter pound of butter in a bowl, add the boiled, finely chopped and then sieved turkey liver, one bun soaked and squeezed dry, one roasted grated onion, two whole eggs, some cooked diced lard, salt and pepper to taste, a little nutmeg, one teaspoon of sugar, a handful of raisins; mix everything well and stuff the turkey with this stuffing and sew it up
6. During roasting cover the stuffing with paper so it does not cook too much, and prick the skin with a fork so it does not burst
7. To remove the fishy taste from the turkey, which is sometimes detected, rub it inside with powdered ginger
