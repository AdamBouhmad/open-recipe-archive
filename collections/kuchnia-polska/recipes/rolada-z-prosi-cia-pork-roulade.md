## Ingredients

- dressed and cleaned piglet
- pork meat
- one pound of finely chopped veal
- liver of the piglet
- salt and pepper to taste
- nutmeg flower
- young pork fat
- two small bread rolls
- three whole eggs
- cooked ham with fat
- cooked young pork fat
- hard-boiled eggs
- water
- salt
- vinegar
- mixed vegetables

## Directions

1. Cut the prepared and cleaned piglet lengthwise along the belly, discard the head and legs.
2. Remove from the bones and lay the skin on the table, skin side down.
3. Scrape all meat from the bones and head, chop finely, and mix with one pound of finely chopped veal.
4. Add the chopped liver of the piglet.
5. Season with salt, pepper, and a small amount of nutmeg flower.
6. Fry this mixture for half an hour in young pork fat on low heat, being careful not to brown the fat.
7. Remove from heat and cool slightly.
8. Add two small bread rolls (moistened and squeezed of water), three well-beaten whole eggs, and mix everything thoroughly.
9. Pass through a fine sieve.
10. Spread this farce on the laid out piglet.
11. Layer with slices of cooked ham with fat, or cooked young pork fat.
12. Layer with hard-boiled eggs, sliced.
13. Add another layer of farce.
14. Roll up, sew closed, wrap in a cloth, and cook in salted water with vinegar and mixed vegetables for two hours.
15. Remove from heat and cool slightly, then remove and press down with a board or stone to flatten, and cool completely.
16. When completely cold, slice and serve with vinegar and oil.
17. Note: If storing the roulade for longer during summer, do not add eggs or ham.
18. The remaining sauce in the pot should be clarified with egg whites, reheated, strained through cloth and cooled; this aspic is used to coat the sliced roulade.
