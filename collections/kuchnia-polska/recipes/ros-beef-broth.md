## Ingredients

- half pound beef per person, or one pound per person for a stronger broth
- water
- parsley with roots
- leeks
- carrots
- onion
- beef fat

## Directions

1. Do not rinse the meat, as this removes significant nutrients. Instead, immediately pour water over it in a pot, preferably soft water, and simmer without skimming for three and a half to four hours. Cook over low heat, pouring in enough water at the start so that you do not need to add more during cooking, as this would diminish the quality of the broth. When cooking on very low heat, half a quart of water per person is sufficient. After the broth has cooked for about an hour and the foam stops appearing, pour in a spoonful of cold water, which will cause fat to rise to the surface. Skim this fat with a spoon. Then add whatever vegetables are available: parsley with roots, leeks, carrots, and onion. The onion can first be roasted on a sheet in the oven, which enhances the flavor of the broth. Before serving, let it rest off the heat for ten minutes, skim off the remaining fat, then strain through a sieve into a serving bowl, in which a little finely chopped parsley is placed.
