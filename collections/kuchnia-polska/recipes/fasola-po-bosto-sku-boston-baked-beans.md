## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 quarts white beans
- cold water
- 1/2 pound salt pork
- 1/2 pound salt pork for top
- 1 teaspoon dry mustard
- 1/2 cup molasses
- boiling water

## Directions

1. Pour 1 1/2 quarts white beans in a bowl, cover with cold water and let soak overnight.
2. Next morning, drain the water, pour fresh water and place on heat.
3. When the beans are half cooked (just beginning to soften), drain the water.
4. Place the beans in a clay pot with a cover, having placed 1/2 pound salt pork on the bottom of the pot.
5. On top of the beans place another piece of salt pork of the same size, with skin on top.
6. Score the skin with a sharp knife in a crosshatch pattern.
7. Mix 1 teaspoon dry mustard powder with 1/2 cup molasses and pour on top.
8. Then pour boiling water until it covers the pork and place in a moderately hot oven, where it should bake slowly for eight to ten hours.
9. As water evaporates during baking, add fresh boiling water, watching carefully so the beans on top do not dry out.
