## Ingredients

- young chicken weighing three or four pounds, not fatty
- several stalks of celery
- one large onion
- three cups of grated bread or bread
- one tablespoon of chopped parsley
- one teaspoon of salt
- half teaspoon of white pepper
- two pinches of red pepper
- one beaten egg
- one tablespoon of butter
- water

## Directions

1. Select a young chicken, not fatty. Cut off the head and then pull the skin from the neck as far as possible and cut the neck close to the body without damaging the skin.
2. Remove the crop through the neck without damaging the skin on the breast.
3. Clean and dry the inside.
4. Chop several stalks of celery, one large onion and add three cups of grated bread or bread, one tablespoon of chopped parsley, one teaspoon of salt, half teaspoon of white pepper, two pinches of red pepper, one beaten egg and one tablespoon of butter.
5. Mix everything into a smooth mass and fill the chicken inside and also the breast, putting the stuffing through the skin to the neck, which should then be wrapped on the back and sewn or fastened.
6. Put the rest of the stuffing inside the chicken, then sew it up, tie the legs to the body and steam in a double pan for two hours or longer until the meat is completely tender, keeping only a small amount of water in the lower part of the pan.
7. Place on a deep platter and pour over the following sauce: Brown two tablespoons of butter with two tablespoons of flour; add half teaspoon of salt, quarter teaspoon of white pepper, a pinch of red pepper; mix, then slowly pour in half a quart of water over which the chicken was steamed, stirring constantly.
8. Separately cook half a quart of finely chopped celery in a very small amount of water; just before serving pour the celery into the sauce, mix and pour over the chicken.
