## Ingredients

- two pounds lean beef without bones, cut for steak
- water to barely cover the meat
- one teaspoon salt
- pepper to taste
- a pinch of nutmeg
- one large tablespoon tomato sauce (catsup)
- one beaten egg
- butter for frying
- parsley for garnish
- one tablespoon butter for sauce
- one cup milk
- two tablespoons water
- one teaspoon flour
- salt and pepper to taste for sauce

## Directions

1. Place the beef in a flat pan
2. Add enough water to barely cover the meat
3. Braise covered, basting with hot water as it evaporates, until the meat is very tender
4. Chop the cooked meat as finely as possible, almost into a paste
5. Add one teaspoon salt, pepper, a pinch of nutmeg, and one large tablespoon tomato sauce
6. Add one beaten egg
7. Mix everything well
8. Form thin patties about three inches long and wide
9. Fry in butter until browned
10. Sprinkle hot platter with parsley
11. Arrange the fried meat on the platter
12. For the sauce: add one tablespoon butter, one cup milk, two tablespoons water, one teaspoon flour, salt and pepper to the sauce left from frying the meat
13. Mix and bring to a boil
14. Pour the sauce over the meat
15. Mushroom sauce also works well
