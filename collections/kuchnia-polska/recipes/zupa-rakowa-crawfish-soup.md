## Ingredients

- thirty small crawfish
- salt
- dill
- good butter
- flour
- beef broth, cooked previously
- sour cream
- bread for farce
- dill
- nutmeg
- egg yolks
- meat from crawfish tails and shells

## Directions

1. For four persons, cook thirty small crawfish with salt and dill, being careful not to overcook or they will be difficult to shell.
2. Remove the tails and extract the meat from the tails and soft mass under the shell on the body, using this for stuffing the shells.
3. Leave a dozen whole shells and place all the rest, as is, in a mortar and crush to a paste.
4. Take a spoon of good butter and crush again in the mortar together with the crawfish.
5. Place this mass in a pot and braise over low heat for at least an hour, pouring in previously cooked ordinary broth, and red crawfish butter will begin to appear on the surface, which should be collected with a spoon, strained through a sieve and poured into a pot in which a spoon of butter has been browned with a spoon of flour, mixing well together.
6. When the crawfish butter stops appearing, press the crawfish through fine cloth and allow the obtained liquid to settle; pour off the clear liquid from the top and discard the sediment at the bottom.
7. Pour the above liquid into the browned butter, mix well and dilute with sufficient broth, add some chopped dill and stuffed crawfish shells, bring to a boil, lighten with two spoons of sour cream, then put the removed tails into a bowl and pour the soup over them.
8. For the stuffing of the shells, proceed as follows: chop very finely the previously selected meat from the tails and shells, add grated bread crumbs, chopped dill, butter, nutmeg, salt and one egg yolk, mix everything well into a thick paste and fill the empty crawfish shells, well cleaned and washed.
