## Ingredients

- hind quarter of veal
- cold water
- young bacon
- salt
- butter

## Directions

1. The best roast and generally every dish made from veal is from a calf aged five to nine weeks.
2. For the roast, take the hind quarter, rinse in cold water, remove the membrane and cut off the bone found underneath near the tail.
3. Lard densely with young bacon, salt it and let it rest for two hours.
4. Place in a roasting pan and roast in a hot oven for a while until it browns well, then finish roasting in a moderately hot oven.
5. It is advised to never pour water over veal roast, but only fat, which should be poured over frequently during roasting.
6. A medium-sized roast cooks for about an hour and a half.
7. When serving, pour over with browned butter. The best roast is naturally made on a spit.
