## Ingredients

- one quart of fresh or preserved tomatoes
- one tablespoon of butter
- a few tablespoons of broth
- one tablespoon of flour
- one cup of sour cream
- salt
- pepper
- rice
- white bread
- croutons

## Directions

1. Take one quart of fresh or preserved tomatoes, place in a pot with one tablespoon of butter, add a few tablespoons of broth and stew for half an hour.
2. When the tomatoes are completely soft, press through a fine sieve or colander so that all the seeds and skins remain on it, and then dissolve with as much broth as there is liquid from the cooked tomatoes.
3. Beat one tablespoon of flour with one cup of sour cream and season the soup with this, pouring the cream slowly and constantly stirring.
4. This soup is usually served with rice cooked thick with butter, or small croutons made from white bread fried in butter.
5. Instead of cream, the soup may be seasoned with egg yolks: for four people, beat two egg yolks well in a bowl and only then pour the soup over it and mix well.
