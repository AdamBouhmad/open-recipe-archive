## Ingredients

- 1 cleaned hare
- salted water
- 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
- 3-4 thin slices salted bacon
- 1 cup rice
- 3 cups water
- 1 can preserved tomatoes or 1 cup fresh tomato sauce
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 1 tablespoon sour cream
- salt to taste
- 1/4 teaspoon curry powder
- 1 cup milk
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 1 teaspoon curry powder
- 1 tablespoon flour
- cold milk
- 1/2 lemon, juice

## Directions

1. Clean the hare, disassemble into parts, and soak in salted water for half an hour; remove from water and place in a pot, also add the onion sliced into thin slices and three or four thin slices of salted bacon, and braise covered until the meat is soft, which will take about one hour
2. Separately, place 1 cup of rice in a pot with three cups of water; when the rice is almost soft, pour into it the sauce from one can of preserved tomatoes, about 1 cup, or the same amount of sauce from cooked fresh tomatoes; continue cooking until the rice absorbs all this sauce
3. When the meat is soft, add to the rice 1 tablespoon butter, 1 tablespoon sour cream, salt to taste, and 1/4 teaspoon curry powder, bring to a boil, and place the rice on a platter in such a way that in the center there is only a thin layer and around the edges a high rim
4. In this prepared platter, arrange the hare and the slices of bacon cooked with it, and on top pour the following sauce: To the remaining sauce in the pot in which the hare cooked, add 1 cup milk, 1 tablespoon butter, and 1 teaspoon curry powder, bring to a boil, and then thicken with 1 tablespoon flour mixed in a small amount of cold milk, then squeeze in the juice from half a lemon
