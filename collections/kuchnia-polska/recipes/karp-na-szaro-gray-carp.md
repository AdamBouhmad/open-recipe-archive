## Ingredients

- carp
- vinegar
- salt
- mixed vegetables (whatever is available)
- pepper
- English herbs
- rye bread crust
- lemon peel
- butter
- flour
- white wine
- sugar
- burnt sugar for color
- currants without seeds

## Directions

1. If the carp is alive, stun it and drain the blood into boiled and salted vinegar, as the blood is very good for the sauce.
2. Cut the cleaned carp into pieces and salt it; do not discard the soft roe, but salt it together.
3. Prepare a broth from various vegetables with the addition of pepper and English herbs, then strain and cool it.
4. Arrange the cut fish in a pot, pour cold broth from the vegetables over it, add a little rye bread crust and lemon peel, and cook until the fish is tender.
5. In a separate pot, brown a spoonful of butter with a spoonful of flour until golden, dilute with the broth in which the fish was cooked, add a glass of white wine, a few pieces of sugar and a teaspoon of burnt sugar for color.
6. Carefully wash a handful of currants without seeds and cook until the sauce thickens and becomes sufficiently thick.
7. Pour this sauce over the fish arranged on a serving dish and garnish with lemon slices.
