## Ingredients

- young chickens
- bread
- butter
- chopped parsley
- salt
- flour
- one egg
- grated and sifted bread
- milk

## Directions

1. After slaughtering, pluck the chickens immediately, clean them and hang them in a cool place for at least twelve hours so they become firm.
2. Do not scald them, but pluck them dry, as scalded ones have a completely different and worse taste.
3. After preparing, make a stuffing from bread, butter and chopped parsley, stuff the chickens, salt them and sprinkle generously with flour.
4. Beat one egg on a plate, dip the chickens in it and immediately sprinkle with grated and sifted bread so thickly that the meat is covered everywhere.
5. Place the prepared chickens in a pan, put a piece of butter on each one, and put in a hot oven for forty or forty-five minutes.
6. Alternatively instead of bread with butter and parsley, you can make a stuffing by mixing with egg yolk some bread previously soaked in milk; then add butter and parsley.
