## Ingredients

- beef for strong broth
- chicken or hen meat, deboned and raw
- butter, preferably crayfish butter
- soaked bread roll
- salt and pepper
- nutmeg, a pinch
- egg yolks, two per half pound of mixture
- cooked green peas, a few spoonfuls
- cooked rice, fried in butter, a few spoonfuls

## Directions

1. Prepare a strong beef broth as usual. Separately, make the following stuffing from poultry, preferably from a hen: finely chop the raw deboned poultry. Heat butter (preferably crayfish butter) and add the chopped meat to it, allowing it to braise for about half an hour. Then add as much soaked bread roll as you have meat, salt and pepper to taste, a pinch of nutmeg, and finally for every half pound of this mixture, beat in two egg yolks and mix everything well. On a work surface, form two dozen oblong dumplings from this mixture. Drop them into boiling water and after they are cooked, remove them with a spoon and place in a serving bowl. In addition, place a few spoonfuls of cooked green peas and the same amount of cooked rice that has been fried in butter in the bowl, then pour the broth over everything.
