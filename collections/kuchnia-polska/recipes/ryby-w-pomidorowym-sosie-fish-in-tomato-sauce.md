## Ingredients

- fish fillets
- salt
- pepper
- lard
- flour
- onion
- tomatoes from a can
- water
- cloves
- bay leaf
- garlic
- white bread
- butter
- Worcestershire sauce

## Directions

1. Fry four large fish steaks about half an inch thick in a pan until golden brown.
2. In another deep pan, brown half a spoonful of lard with the same amount of flour and one finely chopped onion; when the onion browns well, add a cup of tomatoes pressed through a sieve from a can.
3. Place on heat and cook for seven minutes from the moment it boils.
4. Place the fried fish in the tomatoes, add half a cup of boiling water, add salt and pepper to taste, a few cloves, one bay leaf and, if desired, a clove of garlic.
5. Braise on very low heat for several minutes, stirring carefully.
6. Toast four slices of white bread for croutons, spread with butter and arrange on a serving dish.
7. Place a piece of fish on each crouton, pour the sauce over it and serve.
8. This sauce will be more delicious if, just before serving, a spoonful of Worcestershire sauce is added to it, which is sold in all grocery stores.
