## Ingredients

- Squabs
- Hot water
- Almonds, blanched and chopped
- Tomato
- Onion, chopped
- 1 tablespoon of vinegar
- 1 tablespoon of dry sherry
- Capers
- Olives, chopped
- 1 teaspoon of white bread powder ground with cinnamon, pepper and clove
- Butter
- Salt
- String
- 1 glass of vinegar
- 1 glass of sherry
- 1 to 2 tablespoons of butter
- Bay leaves
- 1 sprig of thyme

## Directions

1. Scald the squabs with hot water; if they are very tender, simply pluck them without wetting them
2. Bone them by opening them along the backbone, removing the meat carefully so as not to tear it
3. Cut off the wings leaving the legs, removing the center bone with great care
4. Chop all the giblets of the squab with blanched and chopped almonds
5. Partially fry this with tomato and chopped onion
6. Add a tablespoon of vinegar, a tablespoon of dry sherry, capers and chopped olives, a teaspoon of white bread powder ground with cinnamon, pepper and clove
7. Stuff the squabs with this mixture after having rubbed them inside and out with butter and salt
8. Sew them with string not too thick
9. Place them in a dish with a glass of vinegar, a glass of sherry, 1 or 2 tablespoons of butter, chopped onion, bay leaves and a sprig of thyme
10. Increase the preparation according to the quantity of squabs
11. These squabs are properly for banquets to avoid the bad appearance presented by squabs with bones
12. Alternatively, prepare a filling with very finely chopped vegetables, parboiled with salt, fry peeled and chopped tomato with onion and a clove of garlic, add the vegetables with pieces of ham and sausage cut lengthwise, add bread powder with spices as before, cook and serve the same as the others
