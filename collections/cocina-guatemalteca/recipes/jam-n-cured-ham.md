## Ingredients

- 1 ham leg, as hot as possible after removal
- 1 ounce saltpeter
- 1 pound salt
- 2 to 3 pounds salt for the pan
- 8 days of smoke exposure
- water
- 2 hours boiling water
- 1 bottle red wine
- water to cover the leg
- 6 bay leaves
- sprigs of thyme
- 1 bunch parsley
- 1 bunch leeks
- 1 ounce Castilian pepper

## Directions

1. While the leg is still very hot (just removed), prick it with a fork and flatten it very well with a mallet
2. Mix 1 ounce of saltpeter with 1 pound of salt and rub the leg very hard with this mixture
3. Place it in a pan on 2 to 3 pounds of salt
4. The next day, rub it with the same salt and continue rubbing it daily with the same salt for twenty days
5. After twenty days, expose it to smoke for eight days
6. After this time, place it in a pan, cover with water and bring to a boil for two hours
7. Remove that water and place it back on the fire with 1 bottle of red wine and water until the leg is covered
8. Add about 6 bay leaves, sprigs of thyme, 1 bunch of parsley, 1 bunch of leeks, and 1 ounce of Castilian pepper
9. Let it boil for two hours, turning it over after one hour of boiling
10. Serve cold
