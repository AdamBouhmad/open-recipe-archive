## Ingredients

- salpor or rice
- water with lime
- 1 liter of boiled milk
- 1 ounce of salt
- half an eighth of saffron
- half liter of boiling water
- 1 pound of lard
- 6 ounces of peeled almonds
- 1 pound of white sugar
- 4 ounces of pepitoria
- 4 ounces of sesame
- 15 cents of egg bread
- clove
- Castilian pepper
- quarter canela
- banana peel
- 2 roasted dried chiles or 2 guaque chiles
- 2 ounces of raisins
- 2 ounces of lard for frying
- 2 glasses of water
- pieces of turkey and pork
- 1 small teaspoon of salt
- half a pound of sugar
- prune
- raisin
- almond
- soaked and split fibers for tying

## Directions

1. If using salpor, cook with lime water, wash, grind, and sieve, everything very clean
2. If using rice, soak the night before
3. The next day wash very well and dissolve with 1 liter of boiled milk and 1 ounce of salt
4. The night before, extinguish half an eighth of saffron with half a liter of boiling water
5. Add to the dough 1 pound of lard
6. Put on fire without stopping stirring so it does not stick
7. Add 6 ounces of peeled almonds ground dry, not very fine, and 1 pound of white sugar
8. If too thick, add more milk
9. For the sauce, toast 4 ounces of pepitoria, 4 ounces of sesame, 15 cents of the best egg bread
10. When the bread is well toasted, grind dry as fine as possible
11. Then grind the pepitoria with one eighth clove, one eighth Castilian pepper, a quarter canela, the well-burnt peel of a plantain, 2 dried chiles roasted in lard; if unavailable, grind 2 guaque chiles and 2 ounces of raisins
12. Dissolve this with a little water
13. Put a pan on the fire with 2 ounces of lard and fry the bread powder
14. Then add the sauce and 2 more glasses of water, the pieces of turkey and pork (both kinds are used), 1 small teaspoon of salt and half a pound of sugar
15. Let boil until the water is consumed
16. Remove and let cool
17. Put a large spoonful on the leaves, in the middle the pepián without staining the tamal
18. Put a prune, a raisin and half an almond
19. Wrap and cook as the previous ones, tying them with soaked fibers split in four
20. The way the tamal is wrapped is what gives it its appeal
