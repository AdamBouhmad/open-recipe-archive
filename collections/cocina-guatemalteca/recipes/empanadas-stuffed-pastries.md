## Ingredients

- two pounds of bread flour
- one pound of lard
- pound and a half of melted lard in which fine achiote is dissolved
- four ounces of sugar
- water
- six chayotes
- two pounds of potatoes
- ten or twelve large carrots
- green beans, chopped
- peas
- a little baking soda
- one pound of good dried fish or cod
- two ounces of lard
- six large tomatoes
- four onions
- salt
- four ounces of sugar
- six tablespoons of sweet bread powder ground with four cloves
- six Castilian peppers
- a small piece of cinnamon

## Directions

1. Moisten two pounds of bread flour and one of lard with pound and a half of melted lard in which fine achiote is dissolved, four ounces of sugar and a little water
2. Knead for half an hour so the dough remains quite soft
3. After being well kneaded, let it rest for three hours; if possible it is better to make it from the night before
4. Chop six chayotes, two pounds of potatoes, ten or twelve large carrots
5. Cook everything separately, with salt
6. Cook chopped green beans and peas, all that is green with a little baking soda, being careful they do not fall apart
7. Soak one pound of good dried fish or cod
8. Put a pan on the fire with two ounces of lard; chop six large tomatoes and four onions
9. Once this is fried, add it to the well-drained vegetables, salt, four ounces of sugar, six tablespoons of sweet bread powder ground with four cloves, six Castilian peppers and a small piece of cinnamon, add the fish well broken into small pieces
10. Once everything is well mixed, remove from the fire
11. From the wet dough make thirty parts, according to the size desired for the empanadas, stretch with the wooden pin and fill with the vegetables, fold, crimp the edge and place on baking sheets in the oven
