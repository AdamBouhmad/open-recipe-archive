## Ingredients

- One pound of cooked pork meat, chopped
- One tablespoon of lard
- Peeled tomato
- Onion
- One teaspoon of vinegar
- One teaspoon of sherry
- Raisins
- Peeled and chopped almonds
- Masa dough, well ground with salt
- One quarter ounce of pepitoria seeds
- One quarter ounce of sesame seeds
- One cup of roasted tomatillo
- Two dried pasilla chiles, seeded and fried in lard
- Six cloves of spice
- Six Castilian peppercorns
- A small piece of cinnamon
- One French bread, fried in lard
- Water
- Grated hard cheese
- Finely chopped lettuce
- Chopped onion
- Lemon juice

## Directions

1. Chop one pound of cooked pork meat and fry in one tablespoon of lard with peeled tomato and onion.
2. Add one teaspoon of vinegar, one teaspoon of sherry, raisins, and peeled chopped almonds.
3. Prepare masa dough ground well with salt and form tortillas to desired size.
4. Fill with the meat mixture.
5. Fold in half, ensuring edges are well sealed so they don't open.
6. Fry in hot lard until golden, then remove.
7. Grind one quarter ounce of pepitoria seeds, one quarter ounce of sesame seeds, one cup of roasted tomatillo, two fried dried pasilla chiles, six cloves of spice, six Castilian peppercorns, a small piece of cinnamon, and one French bread fried in lard.
8. Mix this with a little water.
9. Fry and coat the chalupas in this sauce.
10. Arrange on a platter and finish coating with the remaining sauce.
11. Sprinkle with grated hard cheese, finely chopped lettuce, and chopped onion dressed with lemon juice.
