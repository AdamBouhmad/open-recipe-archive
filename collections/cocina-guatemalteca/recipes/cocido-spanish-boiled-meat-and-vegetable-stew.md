## Ingredients

- four liters of water
- two pounds of very good beef brisket
- two pounds of very good bone
- one tablespoon of salt
- four to six leeks
- one head of garlic
- half an ounce of well-washed rice
- two ounces of chickpeas (soaked the night before)
- ten crayfish, well washed with hot water (optional)
- one sheep's head (optional)
- one chicken or half of a fat hen (optional)
- one chayote squash
- one piece of cabbage
- two tender corn ears
- two carrots
- two white turnip roots
- one piece of tender güicoy squash
- some small potatoes (if available), about four large potatoes
- tender green beans
- ichíntalo
- plantain slices (steamed)
- pieces of black ayote squash
- fried seasoning paste of tomato, onion, and minced garlic
- one tablespoon of flour fried in lard or butter
- minced parsley

## Directions

1. Put four liters of water in a pot and add two pounds of very good beef brisket and two pounds of very good bone
2. Add one tablespoon of salt, four to six leeks, one head of garlic, half an ounce of well-washed rice, and two ounces of chickpeas that have been soaked the night before
3. When it begins to boil, remove all the foam
4. From a medium height, let one liter of cold water fall into it
5. Let it boil for two and a half hours
6. If desired, add ten crayfish well washed with hot water, lifting their little caps and cutting the tips off their pincers, one sheep's head, one chicken or half of a fat hen
7. After all of this has boiled for two and a half hours, add one chayote squash, one piece of cabbage, two tender corn ears, two carrots, two white turnip roots, and one piece of tender güicoy squash
8. Add tender green beans, ichíntalo, some small potatoes if available (about four large potatoes)
9. When everything is cooked, serve the cooked or stewed meat on one platter
10. On another platter serve all the vegetables with slices of plantain steamed and pieces of black ayote squash, not forgetting to add tender green beans and ichíntalo to the cocido
11. The broth is used for all kinds of hearty soups by simply adding fried seasoning paste of tomato, onion, and minced garlic, one tablespoon of flour fried in lard or butter, and minced parsley
