## Ingredients

- rice or corn kernels (salpor)
- two ounces pepitoria
- two ounces sesame seeds (ajonjolí)
- one cup miltomate
- two dried chile peppers (chiles de pasa), fried in lard
- four French peppers, well fried
- half ochavo of Castilian pepper
- half ochavo of clove
- half ochavo of cinnamon
- a piece of burned plantain peel
- three ounces lard
- a small piece of achiote
- one teaspoon salt
- six ounces brown sugar
- raisins and almonds

## Directions

1. Cook the rice or corn kernels as in previous recipes and grind them.
2. Parboil the masa with water, saffron, sugar, and salt in the same quantities as before.
3. For the recado (sauce), toast two ounces pepitoria and two ounces sesame seeds, one cup miltomate, and two dried chile peppers fried in lard, and four well-fried French peppers.
4. Grind these with half ochavo of Castilian pepper, half ochavo of clove, and half ochavo of cinnamon, and a piece of burned plantain peel.
5. Fry this mixture in three ounces of lard in which a small piece of achiote has been dissolved.
6. Add one teaspoon salt and six ounces of brown sugar.
7. When cool, wrap as before, adding only raisins and almonds.
8. Cook the same way as the other tamales.
