## Ingredients

- 2 pounds of rice
- 1 liter of water
- 1 ounce of salt
- half an eighth of saffron
- hot water
- 1 pound of lard
- 2 ounces of pepitoria
- 2 ounces of sesame
- 2 cooked guaque chiles
- a small piece of fine achiote
- half an eighth of Castilian pepper
- half an eighth of clove
- 6 French beans soaked
- pieces of turkey or pork
- a small teaspoon of salt
- tamale leaves

## Directions

1. Soak 2 pounds of rice overnight
2. The next day let it half dry and grind it very fine
3. Put it in a pan or dish with 1 liter of water and 1 ounce of salt
4. The day before, extinguish half an eighth of saffron with hot water and add it to the well-broken dough
5. Put on the fire with 1 pound of lard, stirring continuously so it does not become lumpy
6. After half an hour of boiling on medium heat, lower and let cool
7. Toast 2 ounces of pepitoria and 2 ounces of sesame until golden but not burnt
8. Grind with 2 cooked guaque chiles, a small piece of fine achiote, half an eighth of Castilian pepper and half an eighth of clove
9. Grind 6 cold soaked French beans
10. Dissolve this with a little water and fry in 2 ounces of lard
11. Add the pieces of turkey or pork with a small teaspoon of salt
12. Let the sauce and meat boil for one hour
13. Remove from fire and let cool
14. Prepare the leaves well cleaned, remove a piece from the bottom, put the leaf upside down with the small piece right side up
15. Put a spoonful of white dough, make a hole in the middle with the same spoon or skimmer
16. Put the meat with half a spoonful of dough, trying not to stain the tamal
17. Place to cook in a clay or pewter pot with cleaned leaf sticks or folders on the bottom
18. Arrange the tamales on top, leaving a hollow in the middle where a small jug containing 3 liters of water is placed
19. The tamales should boil for 3 hours on medium heat
20. If the water runs out during this time, add another liter and a half
