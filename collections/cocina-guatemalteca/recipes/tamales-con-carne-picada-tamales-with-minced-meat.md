## Ingredients

- rice or two pounds corn kernels (salpor) cooked with lime
- one liter water
- one ounce salt
- half liter saffron water infused the night before
- one and a half pounds lard
- ten cooked tomatoes
- two guaque chiles
- a small piece of achiote
- Castilian peppercorns
- a small piece of cinnamon
- two ounces lard
- three pounds raw pork meat, minced
- olives
- capers
- hard-boiled egg (optional)

## Directions

1. Soak the rice; the next day wash it and while still half-damp, grind it very fine.
2. If using corn kernels, cook two pounds with lime, wash, grind while dry, and sieve as before.
3. Dissolve the masa with one liter of water, one ounce salt, and half liter of saffron water that was infused the night before.
4. Add one and a half pounds lard.
5. After boiling for half an hour over moderate heat, remove and let cool.
6. Grind ten cooked tomatoes with two guaque chiles, a small piece of achiote, some Castilian peppercorns, and a small piece of cinnamon.
7. Fry this in two ounces of lard.
8. Add three pounds of minced raw pork meat to the seasoned sauce and let it boil for half an hour.
9. When everything is cool, place one tablespoon of white masa in the center of a corn husk, then one tablespoon of meat with sauce, an olive, about four capers, and if desired, a slice of hard-boiled egg.
10. Wrap and cook as before.
