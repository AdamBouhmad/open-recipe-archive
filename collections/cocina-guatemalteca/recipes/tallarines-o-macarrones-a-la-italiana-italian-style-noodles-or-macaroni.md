## Ingredients

- 1 pound flour
- 4 eggs
- 1/2 tablespoon salt
- 2 teaspoons milk or water
- 1 fat hen
- 2 pounds soft and fatty meat
- lard and butter
- onion
- green chile
- ripe tomato
- 2 cups water
- powdered pepper
- salt
- Parmesan cheese

## Directions

1. For macaroni, boil as previously described; for noodles, mix one pound of flour with four eggs (white and yolk), half a tablespoon of salt and two teaspoons of milk or water
2. Knead until the dough is very fine, then roll it out with a rolling pin until very thin
3. Cut with a special cutter if available, otherwise with a knife, into long forms or as desired
4. Dust the work surface with flour and place the strips on a cloth also dusted with flour
5. Prepare a large pot or dish with boiling water and drop the noodles in, making sure they do not stick together
6. Add a tablespoon of salt to the water, let boil for a quarter hour then remove so they do not retain a flour taste
7. Drop them in cold water
8. Prepare the sauce: clean a fat hen and cut it into very small pieces
9. Fry in lard and butter with two pounds of soft and fatty meat
10. When it has taken a golden color (without burning), add chopped onion and, if desired, finely chopped green chile
11. Have ready peeled and chopped ripe tomato; to improve the taste of the sauce, strain it to remove all seeds
12. Fry this together with the meat for a quarter hour
13. Add two cups of water with powdered pepper very well seasoned with salt
14. Let boil for two or three hours and if it becomes too thick add a little water so it has a consistency that is neither thin nor thick
15. Remove all the meat and in a platter layer sauce, noodles, Parmesan cheese powder and so on until finished, making sure the last layer is cheese
16. Serve with the sauce as hot as possible; if desired, bake for a quarter hour in the oven
