## Ingredients

- Parmesan cheese, 4 ounces
- Butter, 4 ounces
- Heavy cream, 3 1/2 ounces
- Flour, 2 ounces
- Gruyere cheese, 1 ounce
- Salt, 1 ochavo ounce
- Pepper powder, 1/2 ochavo ounce
- Onion, 1 head
- Carrot, 1 head
- Celery, 1 branch
- Milk, 1 cup
- Thyme, 1 sprig
- Bay leaf
- Hierbabuena (mint)
- Eggs, according to the number of people
- Bread for canapés or sandwiches
- Vinegar
- Nutmeg
- Optional: bacon or goose liver

## Directions

1. Brown in 2 1/2 ounces of butter: the onion, celery and carrot, for five minutes.
2. Wet everything with one cup of milk, add a sprig of thyme, a bay leaf, onion, hierbabuena, salt and pepper powder; let it boil ten minutes, pass the sauce through a strainer, add the cream making sure it is sufficiently thick, add the grated Parmesan cheese.
3. Prepare square canapés of bread, sandwiches or cold bread from a long mold; brown them in a little butter.
4. Break the eggs in a little boiling water with a little vinegar; place one egg on each slice of bread.
5. Spread a dish with a little lard, put a layer of sauce, a layer of bread slices with the egg (when breaking the egg, add salt, pepper powder and nutmeg), a layer of bread and grated Gruyere cheese.
6. Put on the fire to boil five minutes. If desired, spread the fried bread before putting the egg on it with bacon or goose liver.
