## Ingredients

- three pounds corn dough
- one cup saffron water
- salt and sugar to taste
- one pound melted lard
- one pound cooked pork meat (chopped)
- one spoonful lard (for frying the meat)
- sugar and salt to taste
- pepper powder
- cloves
- cinnamon
- wide, fine dobladores (wrappers)
- gragea (small candies)
- leaves of maxán (optional)

## Directions

1. Cook three pounds of corn dough as for the others
2. Sift and grind dry
3. Moisten with one cup of saffron water, salt, sugar to taste, and one pound of melted lard
4. Beat for half an hour
5. Have ready one pound of cooked pork meat, chopped
6. Fry it in one spoonful of lard
7. Add sugar and salt to taste, pepper powder, cloves, and cinnamon
8. When well fried, remove from heat and let cool
9. Have clean, wide, fine wrappers ready
10. Place one spoonful of dough in each wrapper, in the middle put a teaspoonful of the chopped meat
11. Tie with a thin strip of wrapper, very tightly so the tamales come out very round
12. Cook the same as the others; they can also be wrapped in maxán leaves, putting a little gragea before adding the dough
