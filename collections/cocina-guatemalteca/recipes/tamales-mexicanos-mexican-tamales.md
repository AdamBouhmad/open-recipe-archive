## Ingredients

- two pounds corn kernels (salpor)
- salt
- one glass cold water
- one pound melted lard
- half pound additional lard
- six dried chile peppers (chiles de pasa), seeded and soaked
- four roasted tomatoes
- two ounces lard
- raw chicken pieces
- corn husks (dobladores)
- olives
- capers
- almonds
- raisins

## Directions

1. Cook two pounds of corn kernels as before and grind the masa well.
2. Season with salt and add one glass of cold water and one pound of melted lard.
3. Knead for half an hour, beating it very hard.
4. Add another half pound of lard and knead for another quarter hour.
5. If too thick, add a little more water until the masa is not hard but can be wrapped.
6. Grind six seeded and soaked dried chile peppers with four roasted tomatoes.
7. Fry this in two ounces of lard and season with salt.
8. Add raw chicken pieces to this sauce and let them boil for half an hour, then remove from heat and let cool.
9. Wash large corn husks with hot water, wring them out, and shake until all water is removed.
10. Place a little white masa on the corn husk, in the middle put a piece of chicken with its sauce, an olive, two capers, half an almond, and two raisins.
11. Cover with another small amount of white masa, wrap, and tie on both sides like small cambray tamales, cutting the tip of the corn husk with scissors.
12. Cook in steam; they will take about one hour over moderate heat, adding a little water, and when it is consumed add more, ensuring the pot has a good base so they do not burn.
