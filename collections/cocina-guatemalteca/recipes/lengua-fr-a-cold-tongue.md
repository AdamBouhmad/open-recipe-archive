## Ingredients

- Cleaned tongue
- Half ounce of salt
- One quarter ounce of saltpeter
- Castilian black pepper
- Bay leaves
- Thyme
- Nutmeg
- Ginger
- Onion
- Garlic
- Pork meat
- Brown bell peppers
- Linen cloth
- Water
- Puerros (leeks)
- Orange leaves
- Mustard sauce with oil, vinegar, and olives
- Dried chile and roasted tomato sauce

## Directions

1. Clean the tongue well and split it lengthwise as if to stuff it
2. Rub with half ounce of salt and one quarter ounce of saltpeter
3. Grind together a little Castilian pepper, two bay leaves, a sprig of thyme, a bit of nutmeg, and a bit of ginger
4. Rub the tongue again with this mixture
5. Place in a dish with very thinly sliced onion and three or four garlic cloves sliced lengthwise
6. Leave for three days in this mixture
7. Add thin slices of pork meat and strips of brown bell peppers to the same sauce and use to fill the tongue
8. Sew with thick thread, wrap well in linen, and cook with water, onions, garlic, leeks, two bay leaves, two orange leaves, and one tablespoon of salt for six hours over steady heat
9. Press when hot and serve the next day with a mustard sauce made with plenty of oil, vinegar, and olives, or with a dried chile and roasted tomato sauce
