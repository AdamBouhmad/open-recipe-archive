## Ingredients

- güisquiles
- salt
- one pound pork, partially cooked
- one tablespoon lard
- peeled tomato
- onion
- garlic clove
- one tablespoon vinegar
- one tablespoon sherry
- pepper powder
- clove or cinnamon
- three eggs
- bread flour
- flour
- lard
- peeled tomato
- chopped onion
- one teaspoon ground bread powder
- achiote
- Castilian peppercorns

## Directions

1. Peel the güisquiles and make slices neither thick nor thin
2. Cook with salt taking care they do not fall apart
3. Remove and let drain
4. For two güisquiles, chop one pound of partially cooked pork and fry in one tablespoon of lard with peeled tomato, onion and one chopped garlic clove
5. Add one tablespoon of vinegar, if available one tablespoon of sherry, salt, pepper powder, clove or cinnamon, a touch of sweetness to taste if desired
6. When fried, place one tablespoon of this on one slice of güisquil, cover with another slice
7. Beat three eggs, first the whites and then the yolks, add a little salt and bread flour
8. Dust the güisquil with flour so it does not peel, wrap the güisquiles in the egg and fry in hot lard
9. If desired serve alone, if not, with a sauce made of peeled tomato and chopped onion fried in a little lard, add one teaspoon of ground bread powder with a little achiote and some Castilian peppercorns
