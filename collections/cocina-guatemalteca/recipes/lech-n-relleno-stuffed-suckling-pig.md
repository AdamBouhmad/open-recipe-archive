## Ingredients

- Suckling pig no more than one month old
- milk
- salt
- corn
- water from rapadura
- potatoes
- soap
- lemon
- fork
- bay leaf powder
- black pepper powder
- melted butter
- stick
- vinegar
- cup of dry sherry
- four pounds of pork loin
- lard
- peeled tomato
- onion
- garlic cloves
- bread powder
- cinnamon
- clove
- black pepper
- chayote
- carrots
- green peas
- olives
- capers
- peeled almonds
- raisins
- lettuce
- Russian salad

## Directions

1. Choose a suckling pig no more than one month old and feed it for forty days only with sweetened milk, corn cooked with salt, water from rapadura and boiled potatoes
2. After this time, kill the piglet by cutting its throat, cover it with cloth and having a vessel with boiling water prepared beforehand, plunge the piglet in it and scrape with a dull knife or a piece of tin until it is very white
3. When well cleaned, wash with soap, open it down the middle and remove everything that is liver and tripe and wash with soap and lemon
4. Prick it with a fork and rub with salt, bay leaf powder, black pepper powder and melted butter inside and out
5. Put a stick in its mouth so it does not close and leave it in this brine overnight, adding a cup of vinegar and one of dry sherry
6. The next day, partially cook four pounds of pork loin
7. Once cooked, remove and chop very well
8. In a pan put a little lard, peeled tomato, onion and two well-peeled garlic cloves, add the meat with half a cup of good vinegar, a glass of dry sherry, bread powder ground with cinnamon, clove and black pepper
9. When half-fried, drain and add chayote, potatoes, carrots and green peas, chopped and well cooked with salt, olives, capers, peeled almonds and raisins
10. Well mixed, stuff the piglet with this, sew with thick thread and in a platter with the same brine put it in the oven, taking care to baste it with its juice every ten minutes
11. When well cooked, after about two hours, decorate with lettuce and serve with Russian salad
