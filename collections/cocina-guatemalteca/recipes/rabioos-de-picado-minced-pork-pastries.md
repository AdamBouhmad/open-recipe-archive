## Ingredients

- 1.5 pounds bread flour
- 2 tablespoons lard
- 2 eggs
- 1 ochavo of salt
- 1 teaspoon sherry (if dough is too stiff)
- 0.5 pound pork, partially cooked
- 1 tablespoon lard for frying the filling
- peeled and chopped tomato
- onion
- 1 clove garlic
- powder of pepper
- clove
- cinnamon
- small piece of white bread
- 1 tablespoon dry sherry
- lard for frying
- cooked tomato sauce
- 1 dried chile pepper, seeded and soaked
- 1 teaspoon oil for frying the sauce

## Directions

1. Knead the flour, lard, eggs, and salt very well for half an hour; if too stiff, add a teaspoon of sherry
2. Flatten the dough with a rolling pin and cut into rounds like empanadas
3. Chop half a pound of partially cooked pork and fry in one tablespoon of lard with peeled and chopped tomato, onion, and garlic
4. Season the filling with powder of pepper, clove, cinnamon, a small piece of white bread, salt, and one tablespoon of dry sherry
5. Fill the dough rounds and fold edges like empanadas
6. Fry in very hot lard
7. Prepare sauce by frying the seeded and soaked dried chile pepper in one teaspoon of oil with the cooked tomato
8. Serve the pastries hot covered with the sauce
