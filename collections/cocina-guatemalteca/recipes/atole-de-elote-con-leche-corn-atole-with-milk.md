## Ingredients

- six to eight white corn ears
- water
- one small teaspoon of salt
- half a pound of sugar
- cooked milk
- tender green beans
- cinnamon powder
- or alternatively: one dry chile, a small piece of burnt tamale, a small piece of cinnamon, two Chiapa peppercorns, salt, water

## Directions

1. Shuck about six to eight white corn ears
2. Grind them very well
3. Dissolve this with half a liter of water and strain through loose cloth, adding small amounts of cold water
4. Place on the fire without stopping stirring, with one small teaspoon of salt and half a pound of sugar
5. When it thickens, gradually add cooked milk little by little, as otherwise it will curdle
6. To test doneness, drop a small amount in a bowl of cold water; if it does not rise, it is ready
7. Cook tender green beans with salt and serve a little atole, then a spoonful of cooked corn kernels and sprinkle cinnamon powder on top
8. Alternatively, toast one dry chile, grind it with a small piece of burnt tamale, a small piece of cinnamon and two Chiapa peppercorns, dissolve with a little water and a few grains of salt, and sprinkle over the atole with a small cloth
