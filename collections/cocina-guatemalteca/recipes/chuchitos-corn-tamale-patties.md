## Ingredients

- two pounds corn dough (ground fine)
- salt
- one cup saffron water (or made without saffron, plain white)
- miltomate (ground)
- tomato
- dried guaque chile (cooked)
- lorocos (optional)
- half pound melted lard
- two dried chiles (cooked)
- a small piece of cinnamon
- three roasted tomatoes
- a small piece of achiote
- broth from cooked pork
- corn husks or dobladores (wrappers)
- pork meat with sauce (for filling)
- achiote, chile, and a little cumin (alternative filling seasonings)

## Directions

1. Grind two pounds of corn dough fine
2. Add salt and one cup saffron water, or make it plain white without saffron
3. To the white version, grind plenty of miltomate, tomato, and cooked dried guaque chile; lorocos may be added if desired; add half pound of melted lard
4. Knead for half an hour; if it sticks to the hand, add four more ounces of lard
5. Grind two cooked chiles with a small piece of cinnamon, three roasted tomatoes, and a small piece of achiote
6. Mix this with a little of the broth in which the pork meat was partially cooked
7. Form tortillas of the desired size from the white dough
8. Place a piece of meat with its sauce on each tortilla
9. Close the dough and wrap in corn husks or dobladores
10. Cook the same way as the others; if desired, grind only achiote, chile, and a little cumin for the sauce
