## Directions

1. Chop chayote, cabbage, carrot, green beans, and buxnayes; cook each vegetable separately with salt
2. Add a little baking soda to the peas; very tender pacaya shoots are cooked without chopping
3. Do all this the day before
4. Two days before, put peeled small onions in vinegar, cut in a cross and blanched with boiling water
5. If desired, color some with saffron and others with a piece of cooked beet
6. Cook one or two chickens with salt according to the amount of fiambre
7. Cook one or two cured tongues in the same broth; add twenty or thirty sausages, two pounds of lean dried beef, about ten blood sausages, ten eggs
8. When all is well cooked, let cool
9. Then chop, making sure everything comes out very whole; slice the tongue, slice the egg or lengthwise, cut the chicken in small pieces or chunks of desired size
10. Strain the chicken broth well, cover and let cool
11. Cut ham from the leg, whether York or local variety, well cooked
12. Soak two pounds of very good fish, or if not, cod, in water overnight
13. The following day cook and chop it, removing all the bones
14. Peel with boiling water a pound of the largest shrimp available and cook with the fish
15. Prepare in a large platter a bottle of very good vinegar; it is better to use acetic acid or wine vinegar
16. Prepare a bottle of very clean cold water, the chicken broth, an ounce of salt, a bottle of the finest olive oil, half an ounce of pepper powder, two small jars of English mustard, a bundle of parsley and one of creole mustard with four or five ground onions, ten or twelve peeled tomatoes
17. Mix everything well, add all the vegetables stirring them with the chopped meats, setting aside separately the slices of black and red chorizo, egg and blood sausage
18. Arrange on a platter putting on top slices of salami, slices of chorizo, egg, pieces lengthwise of dry cheese, small pieces of fresh cheese, strips of red peppers, olives and capers, the onions in vinegar, its pieces of salmon, chives, pickles in mustard, canned lobsters and green chiles
19. Decorate the platter with tender lettuce leaves, pacaya shoots and designs of cooked beets
