## Ingredients

- Water (two glasses)
- Lard (four ounces)
- Salt
- Flour (half a pound, well sifted)
- Eggs (eight to ten, depending on size)
- Flour (four ounces)
- Sugar (four ounces)
- Eggs (three)
- Cooked milk (half a liter)
- Caramel-point honey

## Directions

1. Put a saucepan on the heat with two glasses of water, four ounces of lard, and a pinch of salt.
2. When boiling, add half a pound of well-sifted flour, stir well and put back on the heat.
3. When it no longer sticks to the hand, remove it.
4. When cool, add eggs two by two; if they are large, eight eggs, if small, ten eggs; mix everything well and put it in a cloth bag with its respective tube.
5. On a buttered pan, place small pieces of the desired size; the éclairs are long and the cream puffs are round.
6. Put them in a very hot oven.
7. When cooked, remove them, split them down the middle with scissors and fill with a cream made as follows: put four ounces of flour, four ounces of sugar, three eggs (white and yolk) in a container; mix everything very well with half a liter of cooked milk; when everything is well mixed without lumps, put it on the heat to boil for ten minutes; coat with this cream and have caramel-point honey separate and glaze with this.
