## Ingredients

- two pounds of bread flour, starch, or salpor flour, well sifted
- one liter of water
- one-eighth ounce of anise
- twelve eggs
- lard for frying
- half water and half white honey, or one pound of clarified sugar at high stage
- small roses and a sprig of fennel

## Directions

1. Place two pounds of well-sifted flour in a strong pot
2. Add one liter of water and a small amount of water in which one-eighth ounce of anise has been boiled
3. Place over fire, stirring vigorously with a wooden paddle
4. When the dough is cooked (does not stick to the hand when touched), remove from heat and place in a wrung-out damp cloth to prevent a crust from forming
5. The next day, place in a trough, add one egg and knead for ten minutes
6. Add another egg and knead for another ten minutes
7. Continue adding one egg every ten minutes until all twelve eggs are incorporated
8. Have a clay earthenware pan prepared with sufficient lard over a high flame
9. With the point of a knife, take small amounts of dough and drop into the lard, turning them on all sides with a pointed stick
10. Remove and coat with a mixture of half water and half white honey, or with one pound of clarified sugar at high stage
11. Decorate with small roses and a sprig of fennel
