## Ingredients

- Wild boar head
- Toucinho fino (fine bacon)
- Sal (salt)
- Pimenta (pepper)
- Adubos (seasonings)
- Hervas finas (fine herbs)
- Lombos de vacca (beef loins)
- Lombos de porco (pork loins)
- Línguas (tongues)
- Orelhas de porco manso (domestic pig ears)
- Pedaços de barriga (belly pieces)
- Toucinho (bacon)
- Presunto (ham)
- Salitre (saltpeter)
- Agua (water)
- Vinho branco (white wine)
- Cravos (cloves)
- Cebola (onion)
- Raizes (roots)
- Alhos (garlic)
- Tomilho (thyme)
- Louro (bay leaf)
- Manjerição (marjoram)
- Falsa (parsley)

## Directions

1. Singe the head, clean it, and cut it open from the bottom
2. Remove all bones except those of the snout tip and jaw points so that the teeth are visible after cooking
3. Soak in water to remove blood, then drain
4. Lard all the meat of the neck with thick bacon seasoned with salt, pepper, seasonings, and fine herbs
5. Cut beef loins, pork loins, tongues, domestic pig ears, belly pieces, bacon, and ham into fillets and season as the head with common salt, saltpeter, pepper, seasonings, and chopped fine herbs
6. Place the seasoned meat in brine for three or four days
7. Remove and place on cloth with ears facing down
8. Fill the interior with loins interspersed with all remaining ingredients, keeping it full and tight
9. Sew closed with needle and thread and wrap in strong cloth
10. Secure and tie well with cord
11. Cook in an adobo (broth) as described for domestic pig head for six hours in water, white wine, salt, whole pepper, cloves, onion rings, roots, garlic, thyme, bay leaf, and marjoram
12. After cooking and cooling, drain briefly, press in a press or under a great weight until well compressed
13. Trim and serve on a napkin garnished with parsley and ham slices
