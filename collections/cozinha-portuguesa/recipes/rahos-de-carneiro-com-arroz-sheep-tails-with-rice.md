## Ingredients

- rice
- hot water
- ordinary broth
- broth from the half-broth preparation, somewhat fatty
- sheep tails cooked in broth
- eggs
- butter

## Directions

1. Take the quantity of rice needed and wash it very well two or three times with hot water
2. Cook it in a small pan with ordinary broth, adding a spoonful of the fatty broth from the half-broth preparation
3. When almost cooked and half cooled, cover the bottom of the plate with a little rice
4. Arrange the cooked sheep tails on top
5. Cover with the rest of the rice, preserving each tail's shape
6. Brush with beaten eggs and when the rice is colored, drain off the fat
7. Clean the edge of the plate and serve hot as an entree
8. They can also be served à la Parmesan with a little broth and grated cheese at the bottom of the plate, and when arranged, pour the rest of the sauce thickened with a piece of washed butter over them, cover well with grated cheese, place in the oven to brown, and serve, adding a little broth
