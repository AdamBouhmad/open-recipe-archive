## Ingredients

- one fresh leg of beef
- two legs of veal by weight
- eight measures of mutton
- four old turkeys
- twelve hens
- six livers and capons
- two dozen partridges
- four or five measures of clean ham without bones and fat
- two dozen cloves of the Indies
- two or three ounces of whole pepper and ginger
- seven or eight measures of shredded venison
- a bundle of carrots
- bundles of parsnips
- turnips
- leeks
- celery
- whole onions
- eggs beaten
- salt to taste

## Directions

1. Clean and empty all the birds, remove feathers, soak them well along with the meat from the legs of beef and veal made in large pieces free of fat and bones
2. Wash and scald everything very well
3. Put in a large pot or kettle full of water and bring to boil slowly
4. Season with salt
5. Add two dozen cloves of the Indies, two or three ounces of whole pepper and ginger, all tied in a cloth
6. After two hours of boiling, add a decoction of seven or eight measures of shredded venison, a bundle of carrots, bundles of parsnips, turnips, leeks, celery, and whole onions, all blanched in boiling water and tied with twine
7. When all is cooked, pass the broth through a cloth with pressure
8. When almost cold and without fat, pour into a basin and place over a furnace
9. Clean it with beaten eggs and when it comes to a boil, remove from heat
10. Pass again through a cloth
11. When clear, transfer to a large pot and bring to boil slowly again, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon until the broth is reduced
12. When thick and almost at the consistency of a glaze, pour onto earthenware plates or pans, but no more than a finger's thickness of fat
13. Let cool, then cut into equal pieces each weighing about an ounce
14. Place on trays or in sieves with paper leaves underneath and set to dry in a stove or expose to north wind for several days
15. When completely dry, wrap each piece in paper and store in wide-mouthed jars very well sealed
16. To use, dissolve one or one and a half ounces in a quarter measure of boiling water depending on desired strength
17. In case of illness, give the sick one broth of these every four hours
