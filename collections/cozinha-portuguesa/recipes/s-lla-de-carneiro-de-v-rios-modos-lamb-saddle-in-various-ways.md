## Ingredients

- Lamb saddle
- Pork belly lard
- Paper
- Butter
- Shallots
- Lemon juice
- Braising liquid
- Breadcrumbs
- Egg yolks
- Vegetables

## Directions

1. The saddle is that part of the lamb that extends from the middle of the thighs to the middle of the ribs, and ordinarily serves as a middle course or to reveal the soups
2. Once cut in the aforesaid manner and aged for a few days to be more tender, trimmed of all its skin, lard it with fine pork belly, cover it with leaves of buttered paper, place it in a roasting pan, and when roasted and of good color, serve it with shallot sauce and lemon juice
3. It can also be cooked as a fricandó, and after being browned with its glaze, serve it with its vegetable ragout or with whatever sauce is preferred
4. The same lamb saddle can be put to cook in braising liquid as beef brisket, and when cooked and well trimmed, brush it with clarified butter and thickened with egg yolk, well covered with breadcrumbs, place it in the oven to brown, and serve it with sauce or chopped parsley or as preferred
