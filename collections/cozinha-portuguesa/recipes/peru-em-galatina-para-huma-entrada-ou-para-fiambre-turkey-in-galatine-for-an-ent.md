## Ingredients

- Two or three turkeys
- Chicken breast
- Best of the thighs
- Cured ham
- Bacon
- Pistachios
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Veal meat
- Bacon
- Cured ham
- Salt
- Pepper
- Spices
- Fine herbs
- Sauce
- Shallot
- Egg yolks
- String
- Bacon slabs
- Linen cloth
- Broth
- White wine
- Condiments
- Fine herbs
- Ham essence
- Spanish sauce

## Directions

1. Take two or three turkeys according to the number of galatines needed, singe and clean them, open them along the backbone without tearing the skin
2. Remove the breasts and best parts of the thighs, cutting them into fillets
3. Also cut cured ham, bacon, pistachios, and hard-boiled eggs into fillets
4. From the remaining meat mixed with veal, a piece of bacon and another of cured ham, make a raw hash seasoned with salt, pepper, spices, fine herbs, sauce, and shallot
5. After finely chopping, pound in a stone mortar and bind with egg yolks
6. Take the turkey skins, spread them on cloth, and cover with the hash about a finger's thickness
7. Arrange on top the fillets of turkey meat, cured ham, and the others each in its order, lengthwise, as if the turkeys were whole
8. Sew the skins closed with thread, cover on top with bacon slabs and a piece of linen cloth on top of these
9. Place to cook in a broth of broth, white wine, condiments, and fine herbs
10. When cooked, let cool in the same broth
11. When cold, drain very well, trim neatly, and serve as a cold meat dish for entrees
12. They may be cut into rounds or served whole with ham essence sauce, or with Spanish sauce of good flavor
