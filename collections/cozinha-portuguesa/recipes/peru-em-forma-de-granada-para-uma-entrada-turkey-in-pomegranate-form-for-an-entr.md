## Ingredients

- Turkey (prepared and deboned)
- Bacon plank (prancha de toucinho)
- Paper leaves (folhas de papel)
- Twine (barbante)
- Pork fat or melted bacon (gordura de porco ou toucinho derretido)
- Fine bacon (toucinho fino)
- Turkey breast (peito de peru)
- Partridge breasts (peitos perdizes)
- Chicken breasts (peitos de galinhas)
- Ham (presunto)
- Truffles (trufas)
- Veal gizzards (molejas de vitella)
- Salt
- Pepper
- Fine herbs (hervas finas)
- Scraped bacon (toucinho raspado)
- Bacon planks (pranchas de toucinho)
- Veal pieces (bocados de vitella)
- Ham (presunto)
- Bundle of fine herbs (capella de hervas finas)
- Onion studded with cloves (cebola cravejada)

## Directions

1. After the turkey is prepared and deboned, spread it on the table on top of a cloth, insert inside a plank of bacon, and finish filling with paper leaves.
2. After filling, sew it all around with twine, prepare it in a casserole in pork fat or melted bacon, and after cooling, lard it like a fricandeau.
3. After finishing larding, remove the paper and bacon from inside, and fill it with a raw stuffing made of turkey breast, partridge breasts, chicken breasts, ham, truffles, veal gizzards, all cut into cubes, and seasoned with salt, pepper and fine herbs, mixed with scraped bacon.
4. Once stuffed, sew it all around again, and put it to cook in broth with bacon planks, pieces of veal, ham, a bundle of fine herbs, and onion studded with cloves, letting it simmer slowly.
5. Once cooked, pass the broth through a sieve, reduce it in caramel and give it good color, make the glaze stick like that of fricandeau, and serve it as a ragout or sauce that seems best.
