## Ingredients

- ash
- water (two, or three to five almudes)
- green olives (two or three alqueires)
- cold water
- salt
- bay leaves
- fennel branches
- oregano branches
- rosewood

## Directions

1. Take the quantity of ash that is judged necessary, and boil it in two, or three to five almudes of water, so that after boiling a strong lye results
2. Let it rest and cool; strain through a cloth
3. Take two or three alqueires of green olives, place them in the lye, and let them sit until the lye penetrates to the pit, which will happen in the space of twenty-four hours
4. Pour off the lye, add cold water, and changing it three or four times until it comes out clear, let them remain in it for eight days, changing the water in the morning and in the afternoon
5. When the olives are a clear green color and have lost their bitterness, place them in a clay pot or in glazed jars
6. Cover them with brine, seal them, place them in a cool place, and they can be used after fifteen days, or when needed
7. Note that the brine shall be made only with water and the necessary salt, and it should be poured hot with some oregano branches, some fennel branches, and some bay leaves, rosewood, and after boiling with all this, let it rest and pour it over the olives hot, as stated
