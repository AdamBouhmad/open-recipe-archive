## Ingredients

- Two or three young chickens
- Paper
- Bacon sheet
- String
- Melted bacon fat
- Fine bacon
- Forcemeat
- Fine ground mixture
- Good ragú
- Bacon sheets
- Veal
- Ham
- Demi-glace
- Studded onion
- Broth

## Directions

1. Take two or three young chickens, trim and clean, open by the back, remove all bones, and after deboning, fill with paper covered with a bacon sheet and tie with string around so they remain round
2. Sear in a pot with melted bacon fat, then let cool, dry and lard with fine bacon
3. Once larded, remove the paper and interior bacon, and fill with a raw forcemeat or a fine ground mixture and a good ragú in the middle, bound and well-flavored
4. Place to cook in a pot with bacon sheets, veal, and ham, a demi-glace, studded onion, and a spoon of broth
5. Once cooked, pass the broth through a sieve, reduce, color the chickens on the bacon side like fricandeau, and serve with a ragú or whatever sauce seems best
