## Ingredients

- one or two loins of veal
- salt
- pepper
- parsley
- chives
- one clove of garlic
- fine herbs
- cucumbers
- truffles
- half a quarter-liter of good oil
- lemon juice
- raw minced meat, well bound
- bacon slices
- veal slices
- ham
- onion rounds
- substance broth
- cullis

## Directions

1. Take one or two loins of veal, prepare them and cut them as the above, marinate them in a saucepan with salt, pepper, parsley, chives, one clove of garlic, fine herbs, cucumbers and truffles, all chopped fine, half a quarter-liter of good oil and lemon juice.
2. After marinating for some time, place on top a little raw minced meat, well bound, and in such form that it remains well closed.
3. After rolling the slices, tie them with twine.
4. Line a saucepan with bacon slices, veal, ham, and onion rounds; arrange the roulades on top and cover them in the same manner as you prepared the base below.
5. Put them to cook over low heat: when cooked, transfer them to another saucepan, and pour into the saucepan in which they cooked a little substance and a little cullis, and put it to simmer.
6. Once the sauce is reduced and of good taste, strain it through a sieve and pour it over the roulades, and keep them well heated until serving.
7. They can also be served with truffle sauce, capers, and sorrel, all very finely chopped, or with Italian sauce.
