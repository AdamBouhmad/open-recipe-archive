## Ingredients

- leg of mutton or beef
- bread
- bacon strips
- salt
- pepper
- water
- white wine or red wine
- black pepper
- cloves
- lemon juice
- bread slices or toasted bread

## Directions

1. Take a leg of mutton or the quantity desired and pound it with bread from one side and the other so it becomes very white
2. Make lardons, which are strips of bacon, adding salt and pepper to the bacon, and using a larding needle, insert the bacon into the leg of mutton
3. Take a pot with a little water that covers the leg, and let it cook until more than half the water is spent
4. Add a little chopped bacon and let the water continue to be spent until it is all gone
5. Once the water is gone, the meat will fry in the substance left, turning it from one side and the other until it is well browned
6. Then add half a canada of white wine (if you want it clear, and if thickened, red wine) and let it reduce very well over low heat with its pepper and black spices, which are pepper and cloves
7. Place on bread slices or toasted bread, and pour the sauce with the meat over it, its lemon juice, and lemon slices on top, and send to the table
