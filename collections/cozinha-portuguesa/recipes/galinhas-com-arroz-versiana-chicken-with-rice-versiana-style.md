## Ingredients

- two young chickens
- one quarter of rice
- a little broth
- a little beef marrow melted
- grated nutmeg
- pepper
- a little saffron dissolved in a drop of broth
- two partridge breasts
- thin slices of ham cut in cubes
- ham
- melted bacon

## Directions

1. Bone two young chickens, and after cleaning them well, remove all the bones from the neck area
2. At the same time, put a quarter of rice to cook in a little broth
3. When the rice is half open, remove it from the fire and add a little melted beef marrow, grated nutmeg, pepper, and a little saffron dissolved in a drop of broth
4. Take two partridge breasts, cut them into thin slices, cut some slices of ham into cubes, pass them quickly through the fire with melted bacon
5. Being thus browned, mix them with the rice, and when cool, stuff the chickens with this
6. After stuffing, sew them with thread or twine, truss them, and then put them to cook in broth with a piece of ham and another of bacon
7. When half cooked, add half an arratel of well-washed rice, let it boil little by little
8. When the chickens are finished cooking and the rice is dry, arrange them on the plate and serve them garnished with ham, taking care that the rice grains remain whole
9. Other times grated cheese can be added on top; and browned in the oven, serve hot as a first course
