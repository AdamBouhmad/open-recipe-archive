## Ingredients

- Old turkey
- Bacon (toucinho)
- Salt
- Pepper
- Saffron (salsa)
- Garlic (alho)
- Shallot (echalota)
- Bacon (toucinho)
- Twine (barbante)
- Broth (caldo)
- White wine (vinho branco)
- Roots (raizes)
- Onions studded with cloves (cebolas cravejadas)
- Bundle of fine herbs (capella de hervas finas)

## Directions

1. Old turkeys ordinarily serve for cold meat dishes after being cooked in an adobo sauce.
2. They serve for pies and also for different entrees after being cooked in a braze.
3. To be served cooked in adobo, after the turkey is dead and mortified three or four days, empty it skillfully, tuck the legs inside, and singe it over coals.
4. After being well cleaned, lard it with thick bacon seasoned with salt, pepper, saffron, garlic, and shallot, all finely chopped. Cover it with bacon planks, tie it with twine, and put it to cook in a pot or braising pan of its size, with broth, white wine, roots, onions studded with cloves, a bundle of fine herbs, seasoned with salt and whole pepper.
5. Let it simmer five or six hours. Once cooked, let it cool in the same adobo, and after cooling, remove it and put inside the turkey's body the broth in which it was cooked, which should be jellied, and cover the turkey with pork lard with various decorative patterns, place it on its plate with a folded napkin underneath, and serve it.
