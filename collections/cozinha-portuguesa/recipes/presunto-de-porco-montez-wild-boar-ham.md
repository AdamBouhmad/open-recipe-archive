## Ingredients

- wild boar hams
- salt
- saltpeter
- aromatic herbs (thyme, bay leaf, marjoram, oregano)
- juniper berries, crushed
- water
- wine dregs
- vinegar

## Directions

1. After cutting the legs for hams, singe and clean them well
2. Make a sufficiently strong brine with salt, saltpeter, all manner of aromatic herbs such as thyme, bay leaf, marjoram, oregano, juniper berries crushed, moistened with as much water as wine dregs
3. After infusing for twenty-four hours, clarify it, then place the hams in the brine and leave them there for fifteen or twenty days
4. After this time, press them in a press for some days, remove them, place in a well-sealed room, and perfume them with smoke from aromatic herbs and some crushed juniper berries
5. When dry, keep them in a cool and fresh place to be used when desired: this is for sliced ham, pies, entries, and for the spit, prepared in the same manner as domesticated pork hams are served
6. Alternatively, after rubbing with crushed salt and well dried in the oven, arrange them in a salting box, salt mixed with saltpeter on the meat side, and cover with fine salt, seasonings, fine herbs, and crushed juniper berries, and thus they will cure: the salting box filled and covered with cloth or its lid, placed in a cool location, leave them for three or four weeks; remove them then, place between two boards with sufficient weight on top or pressed in a press for three or four days, perfume them, and keep them as stated
