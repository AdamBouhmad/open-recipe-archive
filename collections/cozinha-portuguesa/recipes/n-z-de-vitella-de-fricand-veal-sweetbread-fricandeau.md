## Ingredients

- one or two veal sweetbreads, well mortified and of the whitest available
- lard made of fine bacon
- a piece of ham
- onion studded with cloves
- sprig of parsley
- veal stock without salt
- bacon lard
- cullis
- juice of lemon

## Directions

1. Take one or two veal sweetbreads that are well mortified and of the whitest available, trim them of all their skins
2. Place them between two cloths and beat them with the flat of a knife so that they become flat
3. Lard them afterwards with fine bacon
4. Soak them for a short time in cold water, then blanch them in another water
5. Once blanched, place them in a casserole with a piece of ham, an onion studded with cloves, and a sprig of parsley
6. Cover with strips of bacon and moisten with veal stock without salt, and cook
7. When cooked, remove it from the pot, strain the stock through a sieve, and remove the fat
8. Place the stock on the fire, and once reduced to caramel and of good color, place the veal sweetbread with the larded side down
9. Place on gentle heat until the glaze sticks to the bacon
10. Once finished, remove from the fire, scrape off what remains stuck to the bottom of the casserole with a little stock, add a little cullis, strain through a sieve, and serve hot with lemon juice
