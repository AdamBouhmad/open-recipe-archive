## Ingredients

- one veal leg
- bacon slices
- fine bacon for larding
- minced forcemeat made from the veal meat removed
- small pigeons
- truffles
- artichoke hearts in ragout
- ham essence
- Spanish sauce
- veal glaze

## Directions

1. Remove the bones from a veal leg by turning it inside out
2. Remove a good portion of meat and lard the rest with fine bacon all around from bottom to top
3. Take a round copper mold in the shape of a timbale and line it with bacon slices inside
4. Place the larded veal leg in the center on top of the slices
5. Line it with forcemeat at the bottom and all around, made from the meat removed
6. Arrange very well, then place in the middle half a dozen small pigeons, truffles, and artichoke hearts in ragout
7. Being very well arranged, cover everything with the rest of the forcemeat, well smoothed
8. Garnish with bacon slices and cook in the oven or over moderate heat below and above
9. When cooked, turn out onto a platter
10. Remove the bacon slices and, if it has good color, place gracefully on the serving dish and serve hot with ham essence or with Spanish sauce
11. If it does not have good color, it can be glazed with veal glaze
