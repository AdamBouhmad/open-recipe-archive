## Ingredients

- a dozen sheep tails
- one cabbage in quarters
- one arratel of entremeared bacon cut into pieces without separating from the rind
- water
- salt
- whole pepper
- a bundle of parsley
- chives
- two cloves
- one head of garlic
- one sprig of fennel
- broth without salt
- breadcrumb slices
- butter
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Put all the tails, cabbage and bacon to boil in plain water for the space of half an hour
2. Remove to cold water and squeeze the cabbage very well
3. Tie it with twine and arrange the tails and cabbage in a small pan with the bacon, six whole onions on top
4. Season with little salt, whole pepper, a bundle of parsley, chives, two cloves, one head of garlic, and one sprig of fennel
5. Moisten all with broth without salt and cook slowly until tender
6. Cut slices of breadcrumb interior in round form the size of new crusados in silver
7. Pass these through melted butter until they turn golden color
8. Then put half a handful of flour in the butter that remains in the casserole, return to gentle heat, stirring always until the flour is colored
9. Moisten it with the same broth in which the cabbages were cooked, with a little vinegar
10. Cook for half an hour to cook the flour
11. Remove the tails and cabbages from the pan, drain them of fat, and arrange them on the plate
