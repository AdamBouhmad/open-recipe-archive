## Ingredients

- one loin of veal, well aged and pale
- minced meat the size of a five-reis coin
- eggs, beaten
- bacon slices
- fresh butter
- fine bread crumbs
- Spanish sauce or essence with lemon juice

## Directions

1. Take one loin of veal well mortified and very pale, and clean it well of its skins as if for fricassee.
2. Cut lengthwise into very thin slices, which you will flatten well with a knife.
3. Once thus prepared and well equal, place on top of each a little minced meat the size of a five-reis coin, which you will unite well with the flat of the knife wetted in beaten eggs.
4. Roll these slices very tightly and well closed, and wrap them in bacon slices of the same size, and fasten them with skewers so they cook on the spit covered with paper.
5. Once cooked, remove the paper and bacon, brush them with fresh butter corrected and prepared with fine bread crumbs, give them good color.
6. Serve them on a plate with Spanish sauce underneath or an essence with lemon juice, and serve hot as an appetizer.
