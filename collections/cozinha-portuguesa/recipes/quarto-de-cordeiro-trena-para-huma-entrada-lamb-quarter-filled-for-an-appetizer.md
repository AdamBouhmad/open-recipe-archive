## Ingredients

- lamb quarter, roasted and cold
- bacon, chilled
- veal udders, half-cooked
- beef fat
- parsley
- green onions
- shallots
- truffles
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg, grated
- coriander seeds, dried
- bread crumb soaked in milk with butter
- four egg yolks
- some beaten egg whites
- bacon slices for lining
- clear essence sauce
- lemon juice
- veal sweetbreads
- fat livers
- small pigeon ragout

## Directions

1. Once the lamb quarter is roasted and cold, remove all the meat from the thigh and fillet and make a forcemeat with it
2. Add chilled bacon, half-cooked veal udders, a little beef fat, parsley, green onions, shallots, and truffles, all finely chopped and seasoned with salt, pepper, grated nutmeg, and a dozen dried coriander seeds
3. Pound everything in a mortar into which you will add a piece of bread crumb cooked in milk with butter, four egg yolks, and some beaten egg whites
4. Line a pie dish with bacon slices and place the lamb quarter on top, cover it with the forcemeat giving it the same shape
5. Cover with breadcrumbs and put it to cook in the oven
6. When cooked and of good color, serve with a sauce of good essence and lemon juice
7. It can also be served with a garnish in the middle made of veal sweetbreads, fat livers, and truffles, or with a small pigeon ragout, observing that it be well bound, of good taste, and cold
