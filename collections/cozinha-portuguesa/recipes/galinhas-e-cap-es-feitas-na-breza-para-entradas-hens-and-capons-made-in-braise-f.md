## Ingredients

- Hens and capons
- Thick bacon for larding
- Salt and pepper
- Bundle of parsley
- Fine herbs
- Carrots
- Onion studded with cloves
- Bacon slices
- Veal or beef slices
- Paper leaves
- Broth
- Wine
- Vegetable ragú or other sauce

## Directions

1. Prepare the hens and capons and cook them as is customary, lard them with thick bacon seasoned with salt and pepper.
2. After finishing larding, line the bottom of a casserole with bacon and veal or beef slices, and place the hens on these with the breast down, season with salt and pepper, one bundle of parsley, fine herbs, carrots, onion studded with cloves.
3. Cover with other slices of bacon and veal, and paper leaves, and place to cook between coals for a little while.
4. Moisten with half a spoonful of broth and half a quart of wine, and let simmer gently.
5. When properly cooked, remove and serve with any vegetable ragú or any other sauce that seems better.
6. These same hens and capons, if they are young, can be cooked in the same braise without browning.
