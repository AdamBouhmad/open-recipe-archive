## Ingredients

- Lamb leg
- Broth
- Carrots
- Turnips
- Onions
- Celery
- Pork belly
- Parsley
- Salt
- Cloves
- Water

## Directions

1. Take an aged lamb leg, trim it of fat and bone ends, firm it in hot water, tie it with twine, and place it to cook in a pot with broth
2. Add a dozen pieces of tournéed carrots, a dozen pieces of turnips, six whole onions, three or four celery stalks, all firmed in water and well squeezed, tied with their twine, seasoned with little salt and some cloves, a piece of lard, and a bunch of parsley
3. When everything is cooked, strain the broth through a sieve and reduce it in a casserole
4. Once reduced, place the leg in to brown, and when the glaze adheres to it, serve it garnished with all its vegetables and a sauce passed through the casserole to deglaze what sticks
5. It can also be larded with thick pork belly and cooked in plain water with a bunch of parsley, an onion studded with cloves, and a piece of ham; and when cooked and well browned with its glaze, serve it with its chicory ragout, sorrel, or any other sauce, observing to deglaze with the same sauce what remains stuck to the bottom of the casserole
6. Also after trimming, lard it with fine pork belly and prepare and serve it as a fricandó
