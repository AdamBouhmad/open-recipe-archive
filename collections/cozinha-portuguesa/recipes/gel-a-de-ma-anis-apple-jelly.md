## Ingredients

- a dozen apples
- sugar clarified and reduced to high point

## Directions

1. Take a dozen apples, cut them into quarters, and cook them in water
2. Once cooked, place them in a cloth, squeeze them very well over an earthenware vessel
3. Once well squeezed, pour this juice into clarified sugar reduced to high point
4. Place it to boil again until, placing the skimming spoon in it and raising it in the air and tilting it to one side, the sugar falls in the form of pearls
5. When it reaches this point, remove from heat, pour into small pots, allow to cool so it keeps better, cover with paper soaked in brandy and another paper on top, and keep in a cool place
6. In the same manner are made jelly of quinces and of pears
