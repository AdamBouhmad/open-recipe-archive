## Ingredients

- one or two loins of veal
- fine bacon for larding
- oil or melted bacon
- salt
- pepper
- onions
- lemon slices
- bay leaves
- chicken or turkey breasts
- veal meatballs
- thin slices of ham
- cullis

## Directions

1. Take one or two loins of veal well trimmed so they remain round, cut them across their entire width into three or four pieces, which you will flatten very well between two cloths with the flat of a knife.
2. Lard them afterward with fine bacon, and once larded, marinate them in a little oil, or in place of this, melted bacon, with salt, pepper, onions, lemon slices, and bay leaves.
3. At the same time marinate the pieces that are trimmed from around the loins with some chicken or turkey breasts, veal meatballs, and thin slices of ham.
4. After marinating everything for some time, place the veal on a washed cloth with the larding facing down, and arrange on top the slices, meatballs, breasts, etc., and cook everything around as a ball, and thus closed put them to cook in a saucepan with bacon slices, veal, ham, onion rounds, and thus covered above and below, cook them over low heat.
5. Once cooked and of good color, remove the balls, and adding a spoonful of cullis to what remained at the bottom of the saucepan, put it to simmer, and reduced and of good taste, remove the fat, and serve them hot with lemon juice.
6. If they do not have good color, they can be glazed afterward, as we have already said.
