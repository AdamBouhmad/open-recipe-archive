## Ingredients

- Five or six breasts of capons or young hens
- Fine bacon for larding
- Water
- Veal pieces
- Ham slice
- Bundle of parsley
- Onion studded with cloves
- Bacon slices
- Broth
- Glaze
- Cucumber ragú or chicory fillets in béchamel sauce or other sauce

## Directions

1. Take five or six breasts of capons or young hens, remove the skins and breast bones, leaving only the wing tips, lard them with fine bacon, and cook them in water after larding.
2. When larded, place them to cook in a casserole with some pieces of veal, a slice of ham, one bundle of parsley, one onion studded with cloves.
3. Cover with bacon slices, add a little broth, and place to cook simmering gently.
4. When cooked, pass the broth through a sieve, reduce it and thicken it to the consistency of glaze.
5. Place the breasts on top with the larding underneath, and when the glaze is set on them and of good color, serve hot with cucumber ragú or chicory fillets in béchamel sauce or any other sauce that seems better.
