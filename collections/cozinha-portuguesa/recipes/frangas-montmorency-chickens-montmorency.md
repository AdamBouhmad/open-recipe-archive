## Ingredients

- Two or three chickens
- Paper
- Bacon slice
- Pork lard melted
- Fine bacon for larding
- Minced filling
- Ragú
- Veal sweetbreads or truffas
- Fat livers
- Kidneys
- Mushrooms
- Bacon slices
- Veal slices
- Ham slices
- Onion studded with cloves
- Bundle of parsley
- Herbs
- Good broth
- Clear essence
- Lemon juice

## Directions

1. Flambe and singe two or three chickens, open them by the sides, empty them, and remove the breast bones without damaging the meat.
2. Fill them with paper covered with a bacon slice, tie as if they were whole, and place to cook in melted pork lard.
3. After cooking and cooling, lard them with fine bacon; after finishing larding, remove the bacon and paper, and fill with a minced filling and a well-bound ragú in the middle, either of veal sweetbreads or truffas, fat livers, kidneys, mushrooms, or finally whatever is most convenient.
4. After filling, sew with needle and thread, and place to cook with bacon slices, veal slices, and ham slices, onion studded with cloves, one bundle of parsley, fine herbs, and moisten with one spoonful of good broth.
5. After cooking, pass the broth through a sieve, remove the fat, and reduce until it becomes like a fricandeau, and brown the chickens in it with the bacon underneath.
6. When browned, remove the thread and serve with clear essence, strained through cheesecloth, which stuck to the bottom of the casserole, with a squeeze of lemon.
