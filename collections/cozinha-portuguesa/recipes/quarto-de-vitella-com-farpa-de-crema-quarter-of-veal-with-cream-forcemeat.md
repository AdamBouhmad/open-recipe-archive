## Ingredients

- one thumb-sized piece, loin, or quarter of veal
- veal meat from the top part
- bacon
- veal udders
- beef fat
- salt
- pepper
- parsley
- shallots
- bread crumb soaked in cow's milk
- eight or ten egg yolks
- nutmeg
- egg whites
- chicken breasts
- sweetbreads
- truffles
- cooked ham
- clear cullis sauce
- lemon juice
- béchamel sauce

## Directions

1. Roast a thumb-sized piece, loin, or quarter of veal and let it cool
2. Remove all the meat from the top part
3. Make a minced forcemeat with this meat, bacon, veal udders, and all half-cooked ingredients, and beef fat
4. Season with salt, pepper, parsley, and shallots, and pound everything in a stone mortar
5. Add a bread crumb soaked in cow's milk and well-squeezed in cloth, eight or ten egg yolks, grated nutmeg, and some beaten egg whites
6. Arrange the piece of veal on a platter with bacon slices underneath
7. Place part of the forcemeat on top with a ragout in the middle made of chicken breasts, sweetbreads, truffles, and cooked ham, all cut in dice and well-bound
8. Cover with the rest of the forcemeat
9. Smooth it with egg whites and the flat of a knife so it appears whole
10. Cover with grated bread and place in the oven to cook and brown
11. Serve with a clear cullis sauce of good flavor and lemon juice, or with the same ragout made with béchamel, served hot, or with any other sauce as desired
