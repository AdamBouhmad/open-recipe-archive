## Ingredients

- venison haunch
- twine
- bacon planks
- water
- white wine
- salt
- whole pepper
- cloves
- thyme
- bay leaf
- marjoram
- onion slices
- green lemon
- melted butter
- egg yolks
- bread
- lime peel
- sugar
- powdered cinnamon
- salt
- sweet sauce or peverada sauce

## Directions

1. Cut the haunch of a venison from the middle of the thighs to the ribs so that the hind quarters form the shape of a saddle, and when thus arranged, trimmed, and well washed, tie it with twine
2. Put it in a braising pan of its length, covered with bacon planks, moistened with water and white wine, and seasoned with salt, whole pepper, cloves, thyme, bay leaf, marjoram, some onion slices, and a piece of green lemon
3. Let it simmer for two or three hours; when cooked and almost cold, remove it and after drying, place it on a serving dish
4. Baste it two or three times with melted butter thickened with egg yolks, and cover it with bread and lime peel, both grated, sugar, powdered cinnamon, a little salt, so that all this forms a crust of the thickness of half a finger
5. Put it in the oven to brown; when it has taken on good colour, remove it and serve it with sweet sauce or bound peverada sauce
