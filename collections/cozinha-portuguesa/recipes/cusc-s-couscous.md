## Ingredients

- One quarter of ground grain flour
- Wheat grain
- Water

## Directions

1. Take one quarter of ground grain flour sifted through a fine sieve.
2. Put it in a mortar and sprinkle it with a limited amount of water, twisting it in your hands with great force in only one direction so that it becomes granular.
3. When all the flour is made this way, pour it into a winnowing basket and winnow it, what stays on top is the couscous and what falls below is ground again with the same fine sprinkler or with a sprig of myrtle or other clean thing that is not bitter.
4. The water used to sprinkle the flour should have a bit of wheat grain in it.
5. Once the flour is finished this way, place the couscous in a couscous maker lined very well with paper inside.
6. Place a pot of iron or clay on a trivet with water over the heat and when it boils well place the couscous maker over the mouth of the pot.
7. When the couscous is cooked, which will taste like cooked bread, remove it and return it to the mortar to break it up again with great force in only one direction and when broken up, place it in the sun to dry and harden.
8. This is how couscous is made.
9. When you want to eat it, the broth is made separately in a bowl.
