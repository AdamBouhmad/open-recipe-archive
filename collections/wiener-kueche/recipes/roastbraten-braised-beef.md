## Ingredients

- A well-aged beef roast of about 3 kilos
- Salt and pepper
- 4 tablespoons lard or skimmed fat
- 4 pieces finely cut onion
- Root vegetables
- 4 bunches marjoram
- 5 crushed juniper berries
- 5 grains of mixed spices of every kind
- 3 pieces dried figs
- 1 liter white wine
- 1/2 liter soup
- 16 pieces black bread cut into pieces
- Optional: vinegar and soup to taste

## Directions

1. Beat the beef with the flat of the meat cleaver, then wipe clean with a cloth and rub with salt and pepper
2. In a casserole or pan, place 4 tablespoons lard, 4 pieces finely cut onion, root vegetables, 4 bunches marjoram, 5 crushed juniper berries, 5 grains of mixed spices and 3 pieces dried figs
3. Place the meat on top, sprinkle finely cut onion over it, pour 4 tablespoons lard over it and cover with paper brushed with lard
4. Let the meat stand in a cool place for about 2 hours if time permits, then place in the oven and braise covered for 1 hour, occasionally basting
5. Remove the fat, add 1 liter white wine, 1/2 liter soup and 16 pieces of black bread cut into pieces, and let the meat braise for another about 2 hours
6. Remove from the pan, strain the well-defatted sauce into a casserole and add vinegar and soup if needed to adjust the taste, bring to a boil and let stand warm
7. The meat must roast uncovered in the oven for about 1/2 hour more to develop a crust
8. When serving, slice as desired and garnish with rice, macaroni or similar; serve the sauce in a sauceboat
