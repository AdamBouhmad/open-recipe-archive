## Ingredients

- 1 cleaned and trussed turkey
- Salt
- Pepper
- Lard (pencil-thick and half-finger-long)
- Lard
- Root vegetables
- Onion
- 1 bay leaf
- 3 sprigs thyme
- 1/4 liter white wine
- Broth

## Directions

1. Rub the inside and outside of the turkey with salt and pepper.
2. Truss it like pheasant (recipe 735) and lard the breast with pencil-thick, half-finger-long strips of lard.
3. Line a saucepan with lard, root vegetables, onion, 1 bay leaf, and 3 sprigs thyme.
4. Place the turkey with the larded side up on top and braise it covered.
5. After approximately 1/4 hour, pour 1/4 liter white wine and some broth over it.
6. Cover the turkey with double-layered, buttered paper and braise it slowly for approximately 2 hours.
7. After this time, set the turkey aside, let it rest in the braising liquid for a while, then remove it, remove the lard and twine, cut it into desired pieces, place them in a bowl, and serve with the strained, defatted, and strained braising liquid.
8. The turkey can be served with any salted starches.
