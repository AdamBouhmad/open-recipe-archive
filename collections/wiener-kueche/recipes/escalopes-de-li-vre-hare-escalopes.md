## Ingredients

- One large field hare
- Butter
- Onion
- Crushed juniper berries
- Spices, 5 grains of each type
- 1 bay leaf
- 5 sprigs thyme
- 1 piece black bread, finely cut
- 1.25 liters red wine
- 0.33 liter vinegar
- 0.33 liter water
- Salt and pepper
- Puff pastry for garnish
- Polenta, noodles, or similar optional

## Directions

1. Choose a large, beautiful field hare
2. After the hide is removed, carefully remove the thin skins
3. Carefully remove all the hare meat from the bones
4. Place the tenderest legs and hare trimmings in a casserole with some onion, 5 crushed juniper berries, 5 grains of each spice, 1 bay leaf, 5 sprigs thyme, 1 piece finely cut black bread, 1.25 liters red wine, 0.33 liter vinegar and 0.33 liter water
5. Let this braise covered for approximately 2 hours
6. Meanwhile, cut the meat from the hare back into pieces 2 finger-widths broad, 1 finger-width long, and knife-back thick
7. Cut similar pieces from the thigh meat
8. Gently flatten all pieces with a flat knife and sprinkle with salt and pepper
9. Lay these hare pieces in a shallow casserole buttered and lined with butter-brushed paper
10. Let this stand covered in a cool place until serving
11. Strain the contents of the first casserole through a sieve over a deep bowl
12. Pass the strained liquid into a casserole and let it dissolve; if too thick, add 2 tablespoons Madeira wine
13. About 8 minutes before serving, place the casserole over strong heat and brown the escalopes about 2 minutes on each side
14. Arrange the prettier pieces in a circle on a platter and place the less beautiful escalopes in the center
15. If the sauce is too thin, stir 1 tablespoon flour into the butter remaining in the casserole and pour this with the sauce over the escalopes
16. Garnish with puff pastry, or serve with polenta, sauce, noodles, or similar
