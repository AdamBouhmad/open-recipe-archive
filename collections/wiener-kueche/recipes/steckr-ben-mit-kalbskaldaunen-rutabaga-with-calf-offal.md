## Ingredients

- rutabagas
- beef broth
- flour
- rendered fat or butter
- sugar
- salt
- calf offal (tripe)
- wine vinegar
- water
- breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Peel and slice the rutabagas as usual and cook them in beef broth.
2. In rendered fat or butter, fry a little flour, dissolve it with some of the broth from the rutabagas, add a little sugar and salt, and let them cook down so that only a small amount of sauce remains.
3. Meanwhile, cut the calf offal into small pieces, remove all the fatty tissue, rub with salt thoroughly, and wash in water until all slime is gone.
4. Make a light roux with a few drops of wine vinegar in a pan, add the offal, cook it in water, skim it properly, and let it become very tender.
5. Drain it well on a colander, add it to the rutabagas, and toss together.
6. Alternatively: turn the cooked offal in butter and breadcrumbs, fry it in paper capsules on a grate, and use it to garnish the arranged rutabagas.
