## Ingredients

- asparagus
- flour or bran
- salt
- fat
- pitch

## Directions

1. During the best asparagus season, up to the end of it, select the finest asparagus spears, cut them, wash them clean, and dry them with a cloth.
2. Mix flour or bran, which must be very well dried, with some roasted salt, place a layer of this mixture in a well-bound barrel, treated on the outside with melted pitch to make it airtight (probably much higher than wide).
3. Layer asparagus over it, then spread half an inch of the mixture again, continuing in this manner by laying asparagus and spreading the mixture over it until the barrel has only 2 inches of space remaining.
4. The topmost layer must consist of the mixture; after it is firmly pressed down, pour melted fat over it and set the barrel in a dry place.
5. In large households, many small barrels of this kind are made, so that eight dishes for twelve persons can be prepared from one of them.
6. The asparagus keeps throughout the winter, and one can see for oneself that the bran or flour and fat are not lost.
