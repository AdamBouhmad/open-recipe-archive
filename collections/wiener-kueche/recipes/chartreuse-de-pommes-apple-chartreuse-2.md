## Ingredients

- About 12 beautiful apples
- Three quarters pound sugar
- Lemon juice
- Butter
- Apple marmalade
- Apricot marmalade
- Various preserved fruits: apricots, currants, etc.

## Directions

1. Peel about 12 beautiful apples, give them form with a pastry cutter, and lay them immediately in water with lemon juice so they remain white.
2. Clarify three quarters pound sugar and mix with lemon juice.
3. Lay the cut-out apples, which must always be in an oblong round form, in the clarified sugar, and bring them to a boil a couple of times.
4. Remove them and lay them on a hair sieve.
5. Cook the apple scraps remaining from the cutting into a marmalade and let them cool.
6. Butter a casserole, layer the oblong-cut apples symmetrically in a circle around the bottom.
7. Spread apple marmalade thickly on top, fill the middle of the casserole with apricot marmalade, then continue with apple marmalade until the casserole is full.
8. Place it for a moment in boiling water and turn it out onto a platter.
9. Cook a good syrup from the remaining juice and pour it over.
10. Garnish these apples with various preserved fruits, apricots, currants, etc.
