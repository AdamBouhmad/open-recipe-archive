## Ingredients

- Medium-sized green cucumbers
- horseradish leaves
- bay leaves
- cucumber herb
- salt
- cloves
- allspice
- bay leaves
- mustard seeds
- vinegar
- sweet and bitter mustard seeds
- cold salted water

## Directions

1. Take medium-sized green cucumbers, cut off both ends (capers), and make a small cut in the middle of each with a knife, then place in cold water.
2. Cover the bottom of a barrel, crock, or glass with horseradish leaves, grape leaves, and cucumber herb.
3. Sprinkle with salt, cloves, allspice berries, bay leaves, and some mustard seeds.
4. Add one or two layers of cucumbers and cover with leaves.
5. Continue this process until the vessel is nearly full.
6. Mix sweet and bitter mustard seeds and scatter over the cucumbers.
7. Fill the spaces with cold, salted water that should not be too sour.
8. Cover the cucumbers and weight down with a wooden plate so water covers them.
9. Store initially at a moderately warm place until fermentation begins.
10. To eat the cucumbers sooner, add some sourdough or a piece of black bread tied in a cloth.
11. When they smell sour, move them to a cool place and shake them occasionally.
12. If liquid evaporates, refill with water.
13. If cucumbers are not sour enough, add vinegar as desired.
14. Always ensure remaining cucumbers are covered with liquid when removing some.
15. Mustard seeds are added because they aid digestibility of the cucumbers.
