## Ingredients

- potatoes
- butter
- flour
- meat broth
- salt
- beef from lung roast
- 3 onions of medium size
- a strong handful of fresh parsley
- half a teaspoon of ground pepper
- fat

## Directions

1. Make a purée or pressed dish of potatoes as follows: cook the potatoes, peel them, and when they have cooled, grate them on a grater
2. In a casserole, warm a piece of butter, add one cooking spoon of flour, which should brown slightly but not become dark
3. Add the grated potatoes and pour good meat broth over it in proportion, stir it until it looks like a cook's work, salt it and let it cook well
4. From a lung roast, which must first be washed and beaten well, cut thin round slices, salt them, mix together 3 onions of medium size, a strong handful of finely chopped fresh parsley, and half a teaspoon of ground pepper
5. Put some spoonfuls of fat in a flat pan, place the meat slices with the cut ingredients in it, and let it stew until the slices are cooked through; the onion and parsley inside should only become light yellow
6. When serving, the potato purée, which is mixed with the strained fat from the slices, comes first on the dish, then the slices thickly coated with the stewed onions and parsley are laid in order on top, and brought to table
