## Ingredients

- strong beef broth
- celery heads
- parsnip or parsley root
- yellow turnip roots
- thyme
- marjoram
- beef cut into 2-inch thick slices
- veal knuckles
- 2-pound carp
- 1-pound pike
- 1/2 pound veal meat
- 2 hard-cooked eggs
- butter
- salt
- flour
- 1 egg yolk
- cooked veal foot
- pepper
- Burgundy wine

## Directions

1. Make a good broth and add cleaned celery heads, parsnip or parsley root, yellow turnip roots with some thyme and marjoram.
2. Fill with broth and cook until the beef sliced 2 inches thick and the veal knuckles are half-cooked.
3. Take a 2-pound carp, wash it clean, cut it into pieces, and add it to the broth.
4. When the meat is completely tender, strain the soup through a fine hair sieve and keep it hot.
5. Make a force from 1 pound of pike and separately from 1/2 pound of veal meat.
6. Take the yolk from 2 hard-cooked eggs, stir it smooth in a casserole with a proportional piece of butter, add some salt, a little flour, and 1 egg yolk.
7. From a cooked veal foot, remove all bones and cartilage and cut each piece into fine thin strips, placing them immediately into the soup.
8. Alternately form small round dumplings with a spoon from the pike force and the veal meat force, and poach these along with the egg yolk force in boiling broth.
9. Make the soup very hot, season with pepper, and add 1 quarter liter of Burgundy wine.
10. Let it stand at the fire to become hot again, but do not let it come to a boil; serve when properly warmed in the soup pot.
11. Drain all the various dumplings in a sieve and add them to the soup.
