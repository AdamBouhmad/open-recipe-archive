## Ingredients

- 30 pieces of the finest, ripe but not overripe, fairly hard peaches
- 5 pounds white sugar
- half a pound of spring water
- 5 to 6 minutes later
- 3 pounds strong spirits of wine
- 1 pound spirits of wine (added after 2 months)

## Directions

1. Wipe the peaches clean and pierce them with a strong needle
2. Dissolve 5 pounds white sugar with half a pound of spring water, skim in a pan, and cook gently until the sugar reaches the feather stage (when a small sample thrown into the air with a spatula forms feather-like flakes)
3. Remove the pan from the fire, wait 5 to 6 minutes, then bring the fruits in with a wooden spoon and stir them, simmer over gentle fire, stirring the peaches until they are uniformly warmed, then remove them individually and place on a sieve to be returned to the syrup from the pan removed from fire, adding what dripped from the sieve
4. Let everything stand cold for 24 hours, then repeat the process; the pan stands cold for 48 hours, and after it is boiled again, let the mixture cool
5. Place the peaches on the sieve, pour 3 pounds of strong spirits of wine onto the syrup, stir it well together, strain it through, and bring the strained liquid into a suitable container; let one fruit after another fall in, close the container tightly, place it in a cool location, shake it every 3 days until all fruits have sunk to the bottom
6. After 2 months add another pound of spirits of wine
7. Fruits prepared this way should retain color, smell, and taste for over a year
