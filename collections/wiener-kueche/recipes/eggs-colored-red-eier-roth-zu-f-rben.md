## Ingredients

- 24 decagrams logwood shavings
- 1 piece alum
- 3 liters water
- eggs

## Directions

1. One day before, soak 24 decagrams of logwood shavings with 1 piece of alum in 3 liters of water in an earthen dish
2. Boil this for 3-4 hours, then set it aside
3. Boil it again the next day for the same length of time and repeat this for 3-4 days or even longer
4. If the liquid has reduced too much, add water as needed
5. Once the decoction has achieved a beautiful red color, place the eggs to be dyed into it (they must not have been washed beforehand) and boil them for 20 minutes
6. Then place them in cold water, remove them, lay them on a cloth or board, or on 2 wooden spoons so the liquid drains off
7. Once they are completely dry, coat them with dissolved gum or with a greased fine linen cloth
8. If one wishes to dye the eggs red with chemical dye, they are hard-boiled without being washed beforehand and then placed in fresh water
9. Pour cold water into a deep bowl, add 1 tablespoon of chemical dye, place the eggs in it and let them lie there until they have achieved a beautiful red color, light or dark as desired
10. Then let them dry as before and may coat them with gum if desired, though not with fat
11. The red dye used for this purpose can be obtained from any drugstore under the name 'Red dye for coloring eggs'
12. By this method, eggs can be dyed not only red, but any color desired
