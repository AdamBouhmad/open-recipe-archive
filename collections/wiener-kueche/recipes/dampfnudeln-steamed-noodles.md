## Ingredients

- approximately 2 pounds fine flour
- 2 tablespoons good thick beer yeast
- some lukewarm milk
- 1 piece butter the size of an egg
- 1 egg
- salt
- 1 piece butter for the pan
- 1 inch milk
- a couple pieces of sugar
- cream for the sauce
- egg yolks
- sugar
- ground vanilla

## Directions

1. Take approximately 2 pounds fine flour in a bowl, make a hole in the middle of the flour, and work in 2 tablespoons good thick beer yeast and some lukewarm milk to make a dough, let it stand for a while until it has risen well
2. Take a piece of butter the size of an egg, let it melt in the warm milk, beat in 1 egg, salt it a little, and make the dough neither too soft nor too stiff, but it must be beaten well
3. Cover it with a cloth and let it rise
4. Then make noodles from it in desired size and let them rise again on the board
5. In a large pan melt a piece of butter, pour in 1 inch of milk, and add a couple pieces of sugar
6. Lay the steamed noodles in the pan, but not too crowded together, cover with a well-fitting lid, and heat from below until they begin to cook down
7. Then pull the heat out a little, lift the finished noodles out with a noodle scraper somewhat later, and serve them either plain or with a sauce prepared from cream, egg yolks, sugar, and ground vanilla
