## Ingredients

- goose
- salt and spices
- white mineral pitch (colophonium)
- onion and root vegetables
- bacon slices
- raw turnips or roasted chestnuts, or beans boiled in salt water and drained, or macaroni, braised sturgeon or rice (optional fillings)
- melted fat
- broth
- white wine

## Directions

1. To remove fine down from the goose, after plucking, rub it with finely powdered white mineral pitch (colophonium), then place in a deep dish, pour warm water from the side, turn the geese in it several times quickly, brush off the pitch and wash it off.
2. Open the goose, clean it well, rub inside and out with salt and marjoram as desired, truss it and roast slowly on a spit for 1-2 hours.
3. Alternatively, place a trussed goose in a pan, pour melted fat over it and roast in the oven slowly, basting frequently with its own juices.
4. Before trussing, if desired, the goose can be filled with raw turnips, roasted chestnuts, beans boiled in salt water and drained, macaroni, braised sturgeon or rice. The minced liver is always added to the filling in the goose; sew it closed and truss as directed in recipe 735.
