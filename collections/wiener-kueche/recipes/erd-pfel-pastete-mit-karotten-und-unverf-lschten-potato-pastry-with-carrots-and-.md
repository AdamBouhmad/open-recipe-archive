## Ingredients

- 16 decagrams butter
- 4 egg yolks
- 24 decagrams boiled, strained potatoes
- 8 decagrams debarked rolls, softened in milk
- Salt to taste
- Egg white, beaten stiff
- Puff pastry, finger-thick
- Hard-boiled eggs, sliced leaflike
- Truffles, cleaned and cut into fine dice
- Crayfish tails, dissolved
- Crayfish shells
- 10 decagrams crayfish butter
- 10 decagrams flour
- 1/2 pint milk cream
- Salt and lemon juice to taste
- Beaten eggs

## Directions

1. Cream 16 decagrams of butter until fluffy, add 4 egg yolks one after another and mix well with 24 decagrams of boiled, strained potatoes, 8 decagrams of debarked rolls softened in milk and squeezed out well, and the necessary salt; lastly fold in the beaten egg white lightly.
2. Line a buttered form with flour, dusted with puff pastry finger-thick on the bottom.
3. Spread the bottom with half of the forcemeat, then layer with hard-boiled eggs cut into leaflike slices, cleaned truffles cut into fine dice, and dissolved crayfish tails, which are covered with the second half of the forcemeat and this covered with a finger-thick puff pastry plate fitted to the size of the form.
4. Make several cuts with a knife in this pastry cover, brush with beaten eggs, and bake the pastry at fairly strong heat in the oven.
5. During this time prepare 10 decagrams of crayfish butter from crayfish shells, add it to a saucepan, let it melt, roast 10 decagrams of flour in it, which you pour 1/2 pint of milk cream over, season with necessary salt and some lemon juice, and let cook thoroughly while stirring constantly.
6. When the pastry is baked, remove it from the oven, turn it out onto a dish, carefully cut the cover all around and remove it, pour the sauce described above into the pastry, cover again with the lid, and serve.
7. The sauce can also be served separately in a sauce boat if desired.
