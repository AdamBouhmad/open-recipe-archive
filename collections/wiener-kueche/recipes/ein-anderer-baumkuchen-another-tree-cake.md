## Ingredients

- 1.5 pounds sugar
- 1.5 pounds butter
- 3 pounds fine flour
- 1.5 seidelan sweet cream
- 20 eggs
- half a lot cinnamon and cloves

## Directions

1. Stir the sugar and butter together as in the previous mixture
2. Add the flour gradually
3. Add the cream
4. Add the eggs and spices
5. Wrap a conical piece of beech wood (called the tree) 1.5 feet long in two sheets of white paper, wrapping it tightly with twine
6. Attach the wrapped wood to a spit and wedge it firmly with wood at the fire
7. Make the fire from small wood so it can be controlled
8. Have someone turn the spit
9. When the paper around the tree is golden yellow, pour some of the batter onto a lid or platter onto the tree, keeping it turning
10. Place a roasting pan underneath to catch dripping batter and return it to the mixture
11. Apply the first layer the thickness of a small finger; if it sets well, it will form small points that grow larger
12. When golden yellow over charcoal fire with constant turning, apply the second layer the same way as the first
13. Continue until all the batter is used, always turning not too quickly
14. Finally let the cake dry for a while over a dying fire
15. When the fire has completely died, let it stand on the wood for another half hour to dry completely
16. Trim it even with the tree at both ends and remove it
17. Tap the spit against the wall and the cake will release from the tree with twine and paper together
18. Note: the tree must be firmly wedged to the spit so it does not slip; hold a napkin gently underneath with both hands when tapping to catch the cake, but do not be fearful
19. If it sticks anywhere, reach in from the top, loosen the twine and pull it out completely; the paper will come off with it
20. Such a cake keeps well and does not become stale quickly
