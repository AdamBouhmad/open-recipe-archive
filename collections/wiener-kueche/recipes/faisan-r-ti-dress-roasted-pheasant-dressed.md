## Ingredients

- 1 pheasant
- White paper
- Salt
- Twine (spagat)

## Directions

1. Using the eye of a trussing needle, draw out the entrails of the pheasant and replace them with a piece of white paper
2. Hang the pheasant by its feet in an airy place for 8-10 days
3. Pluck the pheasant, singe it over a flame or hot charcoal, remove any remaining pinfeathers, and wipe with a cloth
4. Lay the pheasant head toward you, pull the neck skin tight with the left hand, cut the skin from the back to the head with a knife, and separate it completely from the head
5. Carefully loosen and draw up the gullet and crop from this skin
6. Reach into the pheasant's interior and loosen the mucous membranes all around with your index finger
7. Turn the pheasant head away from you, pinch the skin under the belly with thumb and index finger, and cut it fingernail-width downward
8. Make an equally long cut upward toward the belly and carefully remove the heart, liver, and stomach
9. Wipe the pheasant inside and outside with a cloth without putting it in water
10. Cut off the head at the neck, close to the back, but leave the neck skin attached to the pheasant
11. If desired, scrape off the claws from the feet
12. Salt the pheasant inside and outside, then truss it as follows: Lay the pheasant on its back, place your left hand on the pheasant's breast, press the thighs toward the back with thumb and middle finger to make the breast protrude, and insert a trussing needle threaded with twine just under the wing joint, pulling it out on the other side at the same spot, pulling the twine through so that 3 fingers' width protrudes
13. Turn the pheasant over (lay it on its breast), at the same side where you last came out at the thigh, pull out the wing's inside joint, insert the needle a thumb's width away, pull the twine through the neck skin and back, come out at the same spot on the other side's wing, insert the needle a thumb's width below, pull the twine out, tie it to the twine protruding from the thigh, and cut it half a finger's length long
14. Twist the fingers back behind the neck
15. After this is done, insert the needle under the leg's wing joint, pull the twine out on the opposite side at the same spot, holding the twine over the foot, pull the needle through just under the breastbone, and tie the twine with the protruding end together over the opposite foot
