## Ingredients

- cabbage heads
- salt
- caraway seeds

## Directions

1. Let the cabbage heads lie for 8 days or longer to ferment
2. Remove the first two leaves from each head and twist out the stem with a knife or corer
3. Slice the cabbage with a cabbage plane or knife onto a clean cloth
4. Line the bottom of the barrel or tub with cabbage leaves
5. Sprinkle with salt mixed with caraway seeds
6. Add hand-height pieces of cabbage, then salt and caraway again, and continue this process
7. When the barrel is half full, stamp down the cabbage very firmly with a wooden pestle or by foot
8. Ensure the cabbage forms an even surface without any indentations
9. Continue filling and stamping in this manner
10. When the barrel is full and salt and caraway are sprinkled on top, stamp again
11. Cover with cabbage leaves and place a fitting lid on top, weighting it down with stones
12. If using a special barrel, drill two holes one finger width below the rim and insert a tube so excess water can drain off
13. If using a screw barrel, the screw must be tightened daily
14. After three weeks, when the cabbage is already edible, skim off the foam and water, rinse the cabbage well, cover and weight it, and pour fresh water over so it stands hand-high above the cabbage
15. Each time cabbage is removed, take it evenly so no indentations form, then cover, weight, and pour fresh water over
16. Wash the cabbage, lid, and stones every 8 days
17. Cabbage can be stored 1-2 years
