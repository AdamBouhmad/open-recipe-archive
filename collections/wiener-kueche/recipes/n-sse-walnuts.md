## Ingredients

- walnuts picked before St. John's (end of June)
- fresh water
- 1 litre water
- 60 decilitres sugar
- nutmeg
- vanilla
- cloves
- mace

## Directions

1. Pick walnuts before St. John's (end of June) from the tree
2. Pierce each piece five times with a skewer at the stem
3. Place in fresh water and soak for 12 days, changing the water daily, until they become completely black
4. Place in a casserole with hot water and scald for ½ hour
5. Drain and place in cold water and then in an earthen pot
6. Boil 1 litre water, 60 decilitres sugar and, wrapped in a cloth bundle, some nutmeg, vanilla, cloves and mace for ½ hour
7. Pour this liquid hot over the walnuts and let them stand overnight
8. The next day pour off the liquid, add 12 decilitres sugar, let it boil, pour it cold over the walnuts and repeat this for 3 days
9. On the 5th day cook the walnuts with the sugar once, thoroughly finished, fill in preserving jars where the liquid must completely cover the walnuts, bind and cook as the Muscatel pears (Nr. 1842) for 3-4 hours in steam
