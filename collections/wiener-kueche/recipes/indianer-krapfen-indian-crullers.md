## Ingredients

- 8 egg whites
- 32 deka fine powdered sugar
- unsalted lard for the baking sheet
- fine powdered sugar for sprinkling
- 18 deka flour
- whipped cream
- vanilla sugar
- chocolate or preserve glaze

## Directions

1. Mix 8 egg whites lightly with 32 deka fine powdered sugar
2. Using a piping bag, pipe the mixture onto a baking sheet lined with unsalted lard in elongated heaps of desired size
3. Sprinkle with fine powdered sugar and dry in a moderately heated oven
4. When a biscuit made from 18 deka flour and baked knife-back thick has cooled completely, cut it into round finger-thick slices using a grapefruit corer
5. From the completely dry and cold Spanish biscuit, cut out the center of the base and hollow it out nearly to the tip with a small knife, being careful as it breaks easily
6. Once all are hollowed out, fill them with whipped cream mixed with vanilla sugar, place them on the aforementioned biscuit slices
7. Glaze with chocolate or preserve glaze by placing a spoonful on a wooden spoon and drizzling it spoonwise with hot chocolate glaze over a saucepan so the drippings can be caught and reused
8. Serve the crullers as soon as they are dry
