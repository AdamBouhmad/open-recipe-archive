## Ingredients

- 1 pound marzipan dough
- juice of 4 lemons
- quarter pound raspberry jelly
- cochineal
- ground sugar
- handful of extracted flour
- quarter seidel raspberry juice
- granulated sugar (a la gréle)

## Directions

1. Take 1 pound marzipan dough, juice of 4 lemons, quarter pound raspberry jelly, and some cochineal
2. Stir everything well together with a wooden spatula
3. Since the mass becomes somewhat thin from the addition of these liquids, thicken it through addition of ground sugar and a handful of extracted flour
4. Form raspberry-like balls from this dough
5. Let them dry well on paper for 8 to 10 days
6. When this is done, pour a quarter seidel raspberry juice into a deep earthen vessel
7. Add some powdered sugar and some cochineal
8. Throw a handful of marzipan raspberries in and color them in it
9. Have ready a vessel with granulated sugar (a la gréle) - this is made by separating the very fine sugar dust from ground sugar through a silk sieve, then sieving what remains through a hair sieve, what stays in this is called in technical language granulated sugar
10. Roll the marzipan fruits moistened with raspberry juice in this granulated sugar so the grains adhere firmly to them
11. Lay them carefully to dry
