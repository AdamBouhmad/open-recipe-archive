## Ingredients

- beef, cooked as usual
- six to eight bacon slices
- a whole onion
- yellow turnips, sliced in leaves
- good broth
- young kohlrabi
- large yellow turnips
- butter
- sugar
- potatoes

## Directions

1. Once the beef, cooked as usual, begins to become tender, lay six to eight bacon slices, a whole onion, and yellow turnips sliced in leaves in a braising pan, and place the beef on top.
2. Cover and braise slowly; from time to time pour good broth over it so it does not burn, turning it on all sides so it browns beautifully.
3. Take some young kohlrabi and large yellow turnips, and using a hollow iron as customarily used for kohlrabi, form small balls.
4. In a casserole, put a piece of butter, let it become hot, add a piece of sugar, and once it has melted, add the turnips and kohlrabi and let them braise until soft.
5. Give broth to them often so they do not brown too much.
6. Before serving, remove the small sauce from the fat, strain it through a sieve, arrange the braised kohlrabi and turnips like a ring around the plate, place the meat in the middle, and pour the sauce over it.
7. If serving with potatoes, treat the meat the same way, but you can also add a few pieces of ham cut in leaves, cook small potatoes, peel and braise them, then use them to garnish.
