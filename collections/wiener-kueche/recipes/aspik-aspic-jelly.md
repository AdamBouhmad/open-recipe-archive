## Ingredients

- ½ calf's head
- 1 calf's foot
- 2 pork feet
- 3/4 kg pork rind
- 25 dkg hind beef shank
- 8 dkg beef liver
- 8 Heller cleaned, washed vegetables (leafy cut)
- 1 large onion
- 2 bay leaves
- 10 peppercorns and spice berries
- thyme
- 1 spoon salt
- 6 spoons vinegar
- 4 liters water

## Directions

1. Clean and wash all the meat and ingredients well several times.
2. Bring to a boil with cleaned, washed, leafy-cut vegetables, onion, bay leaves, peppercorns, spice berries, thyme, salt, and vinegar in an enameled pot with 4 liters of water.
3. Skim off foam.
4. Cook slowly for 3 hours, skimming frequently.
5. Season again to taste with salt and acid.
6. Cook slowly for another hour.
7. Strain through a sieve into a vessel.
8. Let stand until the next day.
9. Remove the fat from the top with a spoon the next day.
10. Place the vessel on the heat until the aspic is melted.
11. Pour into a larger pot; season with a little wine and lemon juice.
12. Add 6 firm snow and whisk the mass until very hot, almost boiling.
13. Let the pot stand on the edge of the stove until the aspic settles.
14. Place an inverted stool on a chair, tie a hot-water-dipped, well-wrung linen cloth or napkin to the 4 feet; place the chair near the stove with a bowl underneath the cloth.
15. Remove the cloudy, dirty snow from the top of the aspic; pour the aspic spoonful by spoonful onto the cloth.
16. Do not stir the aspic.
17. If it is not clear, place another bowl under the cloth and let the aspic run through again.
18. It must be clear, golden yellow, and slightly sour.
19. Fill the still-warm aspic into molds rinsed with water.
20. Let it set in the molds.
21. To unmold, hold the form in hot water for a moment, or remove with a silver spoon and serve broken into pieces.
22. Garnish cold fish (such as pike-perch or pike) and goose liver with it; also give to the sick.
23. For red aspic, add a few drops of beet juice to the molds before filling.
24. It can be kept for weeks by pouring it into preserving jars, covering them well with parchment paper, letting it set in steam for 1 hour, and storing in a cool place. Otherwise it keeps only 2–3 days.
