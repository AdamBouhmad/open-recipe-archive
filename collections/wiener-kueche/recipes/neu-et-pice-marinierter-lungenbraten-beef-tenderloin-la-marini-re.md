## Ingredients

- beef tenderloin
- 1/2 liter water
- 1/2 liter vinegar
- 1 piece onion
- 1 finger-length piece yellow turnip and parsley
- 1 small piece celery
- spice grains
- 10 crushed juniper berries
- 1 bay leaf
- 5 sprigs thyme
- fat
- white paper

## Directions

1. After removing the veins, fat and skin from a beef tenderloin, spit it unsalted
2. Prepare a marinade: 1/2 liter water, 1/2 liter vinegar, 1 piece onion, 1 finger-length piece yellow turnip and parsley, 1 small piece celery, all cut into thin slices, 5 grains of each type of spice, 10 crushed juniper berries, 1 bay leaf, 5 sprigs thyme
3. Boil this for approximately 1/2 hour
4. Place the tenderloin with the removed fat and detached skin in an earthen or wooden dish and pour the marinade, which must be completely cooled, over it so the liquid stands above the meat
5. Brush a sheet of white paper well with fat and cover the tenderloin with it, then also place a lid on top
6. Set the meat in a cool place and turn it the next day, but on the third day remove it from the marinade, boil the marinade and pour it cold over the tenderloin again
