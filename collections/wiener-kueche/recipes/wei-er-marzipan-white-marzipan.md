## Ingredients

- Marzipan dough
- Sugar
- Orange blossom water
- Fine, narrow, thread-like cut candied orange peel
- Diamond-shaped citron leaves
- Colored, sugar-preserved fruits (walnuts, cherries, mirabelles, apricots)
- Nonpareille

## Directions

1. Work the marzipan dough well with sugar.
2. Stretch it to the thickness of a knife back and cut various figures with a pastry wheel, such as hearts, stars, rosettes, etc.
3. Surround these figures with a finger-width marzipan border and crimp it with a crimping iron so it appears frilly.
4. Let the marzipan dry well for 8 days.
5. Bake in a gently heated oven until light yellow.
6. Cook some sugar in orange blossom water to the large pearl stage and brush the borders of the marzipan with it.
7. Cover the base of the marzipan with the icing described above.
8. Have ready fine, narrow, thread-like cut candied orange peels and diamond-shaped citron leaves.
9. Arrange the orange peels branch- or stem-like on the still moist marzipan base.
10. Add the citron as leaves.
11. Finally arrange various colored, sugar-preserved fruits on top, such as walnuts, cherries, mirabelles, apricots, etc.
12. Let the icing dry and become glossy in the oven.
13. Brush the upper side of the borders with any sugar juice and sprinkle with nonpareille.
