## Ingredients

- three-quarters pound sifted flour
- salt
- one tablespoon ground sugar
- half a seidel lukewarm cream
- five egg yolks
- three loth melted good beef lard
- two tablespoons yeast

## Directions

1. Place the flour near a heated oven one day before use to make it dry and lukewarm
2. Put three-quarters pound sifted flour in a bowl, salt it, and add one tablespoon ground sugar
3. Take half a seidel lukewarm cream, five egg yolks, three loth melted beef lard, and two tablespoons yeast, and mix everything well together
4. Take as much of the flour as needed to make the fritters, taking care not to make the dough too stiff - it is better to take a bit more flour out than to have it too firm; you can always add more flour later if needed
5. Sieve the mixture through a sieve into the bowl, stir it with the flour until the dough releases from the spoon, and then beat it well for a few moments
6. Flour the board well, place the dough on it, and roll it out two knife-backs thick
7. Dip the fritter cutter well in flour and cut out the fritters with it
8. Lay them on a cloth dusted with flour, place them by the warm oven, and let them rise well - they should become twice as high as they were when cut out
9. Do not make the dough firmer than prescribed, otherwise they will not be so light, will have too many bubbles, and will be neither as good nor as beautiful as these fritters should be
10. When frying, the lard must not be too hot, and the pan in which you fry them must be covered immediately after the fritters are laid in and must remain covered until they are golden brown on one side, but then no longer
11. Once they are equally baked on both sides, remove them, lay them on blotting paper in a sieve so the fat drains completely, keep them warm, and serve them dusted with fine sugar
12. These fritters should be beautifully light and have a white coating
13. If desired, you can also serve stewed fruit warm in a bowl alongside them
