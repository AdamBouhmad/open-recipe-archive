## Ingredients

- pike
- salt
- butter
- lemon peel
- nutmeg
- artichokes
- morels
- crayfish tails
- bread crumbs
- broth
- hard baked dough
- eggs
- butter pastry
- egg yolks
- vinegar
- flour
- onion
- parsley water

## Directions

1. Scale and clean the pike, cut it into small pieces, salt them lightly and dry them thoroughly
2. Heat butter on charcoal, add lemon peel and nutmeg, sauté the pike in it as if making a fricassee
3. Cut artichokes into small pieces and add them with morels (previously soaked and washed) and crayfish tails to the pike
4. Let them sweat a little, sprinkle bread crumbs in, pour a couple spoonfuls of broth and let everything cook a little
5. Butter a tin platter, arrange the ragout neatly in it, pour the little broth over it
6. Add a little nutmeg and finely cut lemon peel
7. Make a sheet from hard baked dough, cover the platter with it as far as the pike lies, puff it up a little so it becomes round
8. Brush it with eggs, draw a good butter pastry over it, form and cut the pastry as well as you can and let it bake in the oven
9. When half baked, cut a small hole so it does not burst
10. Meanwhile make the following broth: whisk three to four egg yolks in a casserole, add a few drops of vinegar, a little fine flour, stir it, add a piece of butter and a whole onion, pour in broth or parsley water and stir it over the coals until it begins to thicken
