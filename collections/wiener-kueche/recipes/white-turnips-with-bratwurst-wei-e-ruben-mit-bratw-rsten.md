## Ingredients

- White turnips
- Water
- Flour
- Butter
- Beef broth
- Bread
- Pepper
- Bratwurst or veal, lamb and pork cutlets
- Anis
- Sugar
- Caraway
- Salt

## Directions

1. Peel and cut the turnips, let them boil up in hot water, then place them on a strainer, let them drain, put them in a casserole, brown flour in butter, fill with beef broth, and let the turnips cook down with it. Since young turnips are commonly bitter, place a slice of bread in which draws out the bitterness.
2. Alternatively: place the cut turnips in fresh water, let butter become hot, add the turnips after they have drained, and let them sweat well in it, pour them into a pot, pour boiling meat broth over them, add grated bread or roux and plenty of pepper, and let it simmer until it becomes a thick broth. However, they are better with the bread than with the roux. You can garnish them with bratwurst or cutlets of veal, lamb, and pork.
3. Or alternatively: when the turnips are peeled and cut, steam them brown in butter, then place one layer in a dish, sprinkle anis and sugar over it, and cover with butter slices; then place another layer of turnips, and so on until all are arranged. Finally pour meat broth over them and let the turnips steam slowly and well covered.
4. Or alternatively: cut a couple of medium onions into dice, add a fair handful of fresh parsley, steam both with the cut turnips in a fair amount of butter, after an hour add a spoonful of flour, a little caraway, water and salt, gradually pour meat broth over them so they have some but not much broth, stir them frequently, and let them become tender.
