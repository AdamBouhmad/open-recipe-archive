## Ingredients

- 1 strong Viennese Metzen of hawthorn sloes
- 1 bucket of strong French wine
- optional: 1-several pounds of sugar

## Directions

1. Take 1 strong Viennese Metzen of hawthorn sloes per bucket of wine and crush them somewhat with a wooden stamper. The best sloes are those that have already shriveled from several night frosts. If using dried sloes, these are also good and you can use somewhat less, crushing them likewise a little.
2. Roll the wine with the sloes in the cellar daily once or several times for several months so that all the strength and color of the sloes are drawn out.
3. Then bring the barrel to storage and allow the wine to clarify completely.
4. You now have a wine that is exceedingly pleasant to eye and taste, very beneficial to health, and particularly good for the stomach and intestines.
5. If you wish to add one or several pounds of sugar to the sloes, the wine will certainly become even more noble.
