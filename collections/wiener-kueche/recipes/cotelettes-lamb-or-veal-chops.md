## Ingredients

- lamb or veal ribs with backbone
- butter
- salt
- breadcrumbs
- pepper (for lamb)
- egg (optional)
- white paper (optional)

## Directions

1. Take the ribs where the backbone is still attached, carefully remove the backbone, and cut one rib after another through
2. The ribs should protrude about two inches, remove the skin well, and scrape the fine meat to the back meat, which must remain two thumbs wide at the rib; scrape the rest of the rib completely white and clean
3. Pound the back meat broad with the flat blade of a knife, and then hack it cleanly with the back of a thick knife, after which try to give the cotelettes a good form
4. Dip the cotelettes in melted butter, salt them, sprinkle with finely grated breadcrumbs, and fry quickly on a greased grill, turning them often
5. When both sides are golden-brown, they are done frying
6. Alternatively, instead of just sprinkling with breadcrumbs, dip them in egg and sprinkle with grated breadcrumbs, then brown butter in a flat pan and fry them in it very quickly
7. For lamb cotelettes, add some ground pepper as well
8. These cotelettes can also be wrapped in white round-cut paper brushed with butter and fried on a grill not brushed with butter, but they must be turned often; when the paper turns brown, they are done
