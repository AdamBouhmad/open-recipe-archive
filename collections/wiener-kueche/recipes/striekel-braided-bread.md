## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 liter flour
- 3 deca compressed yeast dissolved in 1/8 liter lukewarm milk
- 1 whole egg
- 3/8 liter milk
- 9 to 12 deca melted butter
- 6 deca sugar
- 1 teaspoon full of salt
- raisins (optional, 1/8 deca)

## Directions

1. Put 1 1/2 liter flour in a bowl, make a well in the middle, pour in 3 deca compressed yeast dissolved in 1/8 liter lukewarm milk
2. Stir it a little, dust with some flour and let it rise covered in a warm place
3. When this so-called sponge has risen, add 1 whole egg, 3/8 liter milk with 9 to 12 deca melted butter, 6 deca sugar and 1 teaspoon of salt
4. Stir it into a dough and knead it with the hands until it pulls away from them
5. Cover it in the bowl with a cloth and place in a warm location
6. After the dough has risen to double height, shape it into a striekel by placing the dough on a flour-dusted board and dividing it into 4 unequal pieces
7. Cut the largest piece into 4 pieces, shape these into 2 finger-thick ropes, which are then braided into a four-strand braid and gently stretched lengthwise
8. From the two next smaller pieces, by cutting each into 3 parts, make two 3-strand braids in the same way and stretch them slightly
9. Twist the remaining fourth piece into two ropes and twist them together lightly
10. Line a baking sheet with white writing paper, arrange the 4 braids in correct order (the four-strand at the bottom, the two-strand at the top) into a striekel shape and cover it with a cloth, let it rise 1/2 hour in a warm place
11. Brush the striekel with beaten egg and bake slowly for 1 hour in the oven
12. Alternatively, the dough can be shaped into a loaf placed in a floured breadmaking basket covered and placed in a warm location until it has risen to double height, then placed on a buttered baking sheet and treated as with the striekel
13. If desired, 1/8 deca raisins can be added when combining the dough for either the striekel or the loaf
