## Ingredients

- prepared orange jelly mass
- 10 to 12 oranges
- optional: alkermes juice or cochineal for coloring

## Directions

1. Cut a lid at the stem end of the oranges and hollow them out with a spoon, leaving only a thin upper shell that must not tear.
2. Place all hollowed oranges immediately in cold water.
3. Once the necessary number of oranges (10 to 12) are hollowed, remove them from water and let them drain on cloth.
4. Place them on ice and fill with the prepared jelly mass, put the lid on top and let them set.
5. Arrange on a platter, alternating whole oranges with orange halves and placing green leaves between them.
6. Alternatively, color portions of the finished jelly mass with alkermes juice or cochineal, then fill the oranges alternately with white and red jelly spoonwise, allowing each application to set before adding another color.
7. Once filled, cut the oranges into halves.
8. Keep the jelly masses in deep bowls at a lukewarm location so they do not set, warming gently if needed to dissolve if they do set.
