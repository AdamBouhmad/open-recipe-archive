## Ingredients

- suckling pig
- powdered white pitch (colophonium)
- boiling water
- salt
- caraway
- marjoram (optional)
- writing paper
- speck or pork fat
- beer
- peeled almonds
- butter
- butcher's needle
- twine

## Directions

1. Hold the suckling pig so it cannot move, then push a long, narrow, pointed knife through the ribs just below the neck downward so it penetrates the heart
2. Hold the suckling pig with its head downward after removing the knife so the blood runs out well
3. Rub the suckling pig with powdered white pitch and scald with boiling water
4. Strip off the bristles and clean in cold water
5. Cut open the belly and remove the entrails
6. Place in cold water and clean carefully, then place in fresh water and let sit for 5 minutes
7. Remove, dry with a cloth, and rub inside and outside with salt, inside also with caraway and marjoram if desired
8. Insert several sheets of thin writing paper into the opening made in the belly and sew it closed
9. Using a trussing needle and twine, truss the front legs backward and the hind legs forward so the suckling pig rests on its feet
10. Insert a spit or, if unavailable, a piece of wood from the anus to the snout and cut the neck of the suckling pig so the speck is well scored
11. Remove the eyes and replace with peeled almonds
12. Wrap the ears and tail with paper brushed with butter and bind with twine
13. Place the suckling pig on cooking spoons or wooden pieces in a pan with speck or pork fat
14. Roast under frequent basting with speck at moderate heat for 1-1 1/2 hours in the oven
15. If drops of juice emerge from inside the suckling pig during roasting, wipe them away each time with a clean cloth to prevent blistering
16. Once the suckling pig takes on a yellow color, dip a piece of speck on a long fork in beer, brush the pig with it, and repeat often
17. Once the pig has a beautiful yellow and crispy crust, remove the paper from the ears and tail, carefully pull out the twine without injuring the meat, remove the paper in the belly, arrange the suckling pig neatly on a platter, and insert a roasted apple or 1/2 lemon in the snout as the spit or wood is removed
