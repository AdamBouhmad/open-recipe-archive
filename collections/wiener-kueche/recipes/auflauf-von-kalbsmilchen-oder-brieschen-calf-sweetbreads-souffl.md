## Ingredients

- Half pound white bread
- Water
- One-quarter pound butter
- 12 Loth butter
- 12 egg yolks
- Nutmeg
- Salt
- Egg whites from 8 eggs
- 6 to 8 calf sweetbreads
- Salt
- Butter
- Finely chopped shallots
- Parsley
- Lemon peel
- Fine flour
- Good bouillon or stock
- One glass wine
- Lemon juice
- Butter for greasing
- Roll crumbs

## Directions

1. Soak half pound white bread in water, press it dry, and cook it over fire with one-quarter pound butter.
2. Beat 12 Loth butter with 12 egg yolks to foam, add the bread with some nutmeg and salt, and stir vigorously for a quarter hour, then fold in the snow from 8 eggs.
3. Blanch 6 to 8 calf sweetbreads in salted water, peel them clean, and cut into small dice.
4. Cook them gently with a piece of butter, finely chopped shallots, parsley and lemon peel, dust with a spoonful of fine flour, and fill with a little good bouillon or stock and one glass wine, cooking it down briefly.
5. Add a squeeze of lemon juice and set aside to cool.
6. Coat a form with butter, sprinkle with roll crumbs, fill in half the mass, place the ragout on top, cover with the remaining half, and bake the soufflé slowly.
