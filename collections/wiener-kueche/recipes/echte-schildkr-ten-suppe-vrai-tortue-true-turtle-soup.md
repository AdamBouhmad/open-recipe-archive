## Ingredients

- 2 medium-sized tortoises
- 1 hour in lukewarm water
- 20 decagrams butter
- 1 piece finely sliced Spanish onion
- sliced champignons
- 1 yellow turnip
- 1 celery root
- 1 parsley root
- 2 1/2 liters water or pea water
- 8 decagrams rice

## Directions

1. Place the tortoises on a table and hold a small, glowing coal to the back of each to make them extend their heads and feet
2. For large animals, quickly cut off the feet with a hatchet; for small ones, use a saber or tongs
3. Place the tortoises in lukewarm water for 1 hour to release the blood and impurities
4. Place them in a casserole with cold water and cook until the green color loosens from the shoulders (approximately 3/4 hour)
5. Meanwhile, in another casserole with 20 decagrams of melted butter, arrange the finely sliced Spanish onion, champignons, yellow turnip, celery, and parsley root
6. Add the tortoises with their shells and braise covered and slowly for approximately 1/3 hour until brown
7. Pour on 2 1/2 liters of water or pea water and add 8 decagrams of rice
8. Cook slowly for approximately 1 hour
9. Place the tortoises on a board, remove their shells, and cut them into desired sized pieces
10. Pass the soup through a sieve and remove the fat
11. Season with salt and spices if desired
12. Bring to a boil with the tortoise meat for approximately 10 minutes
13. Serve with fried noodles, crayfish quenelles, or fried bread slices
14. Optionally enhance with wine
