## Ingredients

- fresh trout
- vinegar
- salt
- water
- butter
- parsley
- Hollandaise sauce or egg yolk sauce (alternatively vinegar and oil, or mayonnaise)
- fish stock

## Directions

1. Have 1/4 litre of strong vinegar and some boiling water ready before beginning
2. Take one trout after another from its container, strike it on the head with the back of a knife and quickly place it back in the container
3. Prepare a board with cold water, take the trout one by one and cut the belly as quickly as possible from the snout toward the head, place immediately in cold water
4. With the right thumb, press hard on the gills from snout toward head, in this manner removing blood and entrails, and when you reach the head remove the entrails and heart with the thumb and finger
5. This removal of entrails must be done while holding the fish in water, where it remains until all trout are cleaned, which must be done as quickly as possible
6. Be careful not to disturb or wipe off the outer skin of the trout
7. Place the trout in a fish pan or shallow pan, laying them belly down so closely together that the head of one lies beside the tail of another
8. If they do not completely fill the space of the pan when lying so closely side by side, fill the remaining space with wrapping paper or a suitable piece of wood so they cannot move and let them stand 1 1/4 hours so the trout become completely stiff
9. Pour boiling vinegar over them so each one is thoroughly covered, then carefully pour hot salt water (for 1 kilo of trout use 2 strong tablespoons of salt) from the side along the edge so the liquid stands a strong finger's breadth above the fish, but the fish are not directly hit by the water as it is poured
10. Cover the trout with a sheet of blotting paper brushed with butter, place a small wooden spoon on this to hold the paper down slightly, and let them stand covered at that place on the stove where their broth can be kept near the boiling point but never comes to a boil (the broth should only pass through)
11. Let the trout stand for approximately 3/4 hours; as a sign of doneness the eyes will protrude like pearls
12. If serving them warm, remove them carefully from their broth just before serving, arrange them as they lay in the pan but not so crowded together, place some parsley between every 2 trout and garnish the platter with this as well
13. Serve with Hollandaise sauce, or blend 4 egg yolks with 4 tablespoons of cream, the juice of 1 lemon, 1/3 litre of fish broth and some salt and serve in a sauce boat, also serve vinegar and oil
14. If serving the trout cold, let them cool in their broth at a cool place, then carefully lift them onto the platter shortly before serving, place aspic or just green parsley between the trout and garnish the platter with this
15. Serve vinegar and oil or mayonnaise with them
