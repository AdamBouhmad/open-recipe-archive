## Ingredients

- 16 pounds white sugar
- 8 pounds raisins
- 4 good lemons, cut into slices
- good white French wine

## Directions

1. Fill an upright standing barrel with the white sugar, raisins, and sliced lemons, and fill with good white French wine to within a few finger-widths of the top
2. Once the sugar has dissolved, stop the tap hole in the upper bottom, then roll the barrel vigorously for half an hour
3. Place the barrel upright in a warmth of 14 degrees Reaumur
4. Pierce the cork of the tap hole and insert a feather tube that is open at both top and bottom
5. When no strong pungent vapor comes from this any longer, fermentation is finished and the opening is sealed airtight
6. Place the barrel in the coolest cellar possible and let it stand for twelve weeks
7. Tap onto bottles by means of a pipe attached at the bottom of the barrel; the bottles must be as strong as Champagne bottles, wound around the stopper with wire, sealed with pitch, laid down, and drunk after 8 weeks
8. If desired, a large piece of sugar can be placed in each bottle, which will save many bottles that otherwise tend to burst frequently
