## Ingredients

- black rye bread
- beef broth, about 2 liters
- eggs
- salt and pepper to taste
- braised bratwurst or cooked frankfurter sausages (optional)

## Directions

1. Cut a piece of black rye bread into thin slices and toast it on a baking sheet in the oven.
2. Place the toasted bread in a casserole, add approximately 2 liters of beef broth, and let it simmer slowly for about 1/4 hour.
3. Pour it into the soup pot, stir in lard, and season with pepper to taste.
4. If desired, add braised bratwurst or cooked frankfurter sausages cut into pieces.
5. Place 3 finger-widths of risen beef broth in a shallow casserole; gradually beat in as many eggs as there are servings into the simmering soup so that when the egg white has set in the simmering soup, fold it together using a slotted spoon so that the egg yolk is completely covered.
6. After 4-5 minutes, add the eggs very carefully into a bowl and pour the simmering soup to the side of it.
7. Serve the eggs with the broth soup. The eggs do not always need to be served separately; they can also be added to the soup pot.
