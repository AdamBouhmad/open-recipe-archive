## Ingredients

- Young beans
- Butter
- Cream
- Egg yolks
- Cooked mutton meat
- Fine-cut shallots
- Pepper
- Salt
- Meat broth
- Nutmeg
- Ginger
- Bread crumbs
- Vinegar
- Melted butter

## Directions

1. Cut young beans after removing stems and any fibers into small diagonal pieces and wash
2. Place butter in a casserole, let the beans sweat for a while, then pour cream over them and let them cook soft
3. Combine the cream with a pair of egg yolks from the beans and pour back over them, but do not boil further
4. At serving, sprinkle a little butter over them and top with cooked mutton meat
5. Alternatively: boil the cut beans in salt water, braise them in butter when drained, add finely cut shallots, pepper, salt, and pile in the center of the platter, then garnish with mutton cutlets, feet, sausages, or only with bread fried in butter
6. Or: cook the cut and rinsed beans in sufficiently salted meat broth, place in a casserole, pour some of the broth over them, add a little butter, nutmeg and ginger, and let cook; then add roux or bread crumbs and let the broth become thick, and garnish as desired
7. Or: without thickening, beat three egg yolks in a pot, add some vinegar, whisk thoroughly, add a little butter, pour the bean broth underneath, and whisk constantly so it does not curdle; finally pour the broth over the beans and let it soak through without boiling further
8. When serving, sprinkle with melted butter
