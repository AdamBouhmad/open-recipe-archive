## Ingredients

- 25-30 dkg flour
- 8 dkg butter or beef lard
- salt
- 2 spoons sugar
- 1 egg
- 1 egg yolk
- yeast starter (Dampfl)
- lukewarm milk as needed
- jam, powidl (plum paste), quark filling, or poppy seed filling

## Directions

1. Warm the flour in a mixing bowl with salt and 2 spoons sugar.
2. Rub in 8 dkg butter or beef lard in pieces, add 1 egg, 1 egg yolk, the risen yeast starter, and lukewarm milk as needed.
3. Beat the dough until it detaches from the spoon, cover it, and let it rise near the stove.
4. When it has doubled in size, use a floured spoon to place small pieces of dough on a floured board, stretch them out with floured fingers, fill them with jam, powidl, quark filling, or poppy seed filling, close the dough over the filling, place them with the filled side down in a well-greased pan close together, brush each one on top and on the sides with melted fat, and fill the pan this way.
5. If the pan is too large, place a stick wrapped in paper against the last rolls.
6. Let them rise for another 15 minutes near the stove and bake them at moderate heat until light brown.
7. Turn them out, sprinkle with sugar while still hot, separate them, and serve cooled.
