## Ingredients

- 24 decagrams butter
- 32 decagrams sugar
- 16 egg yolks
- 32 decagrams roasted or cooked and sifted chestnuts
- 1 knife-tip of ground coffee
- 12 egg whites
- 4 tablespoons sifted chestnuts
- 8 decagrams sugar
- 2 egg whites
- 2 tablespoons rum
- 4 egg whites
- 16 decagrams finely sifted sugar
- 2 decagrams peeled pistachios
- equal amount of pignoli nuts
- finely sifted sugar
- 2 decagrams chopped pistachios and pignoli nuts

## Directions

1. Beat 24 decagrams butter until fluffy, then stir in 32 decagrams sugar and 16 egg yolks one after another.
2. Mix well with 32 decagrams roasted or cooked and sifted chestnuts and 1 knife-tip of grated coffee, then lightly fold in the beaten snow of 12 egg yolks.
3. Divide this mixture in two parts and fill into two buttered cake forms dusted with flour, baking at low heat for 1 1/2 to 3/4 hours.
4. Remove from the forms and place the baked plates on a sieve to cool completely.
5. Stir together 4 tablespoons sifted chestnuts, 8 decagrams sugar, 2 egg whites, and 2 tablespoons rum until well combined.
6. Spread this filling on one baked plate, cover with the second plate, and place on a baking sheet.
7. Beat 4 egg whites until stiff and fold in 16 decagrams finely sifted sugar, 2 decagrams peeled pistachios, and an equal amount of pignoli nuts.
8. Distribute this mixture evenly over the top of the cake, sprinkle with finely sifted sugar, and decorate with 2 decagrams chopped pistachios and pignoli nuts.
9. Dry in the open oven for 10 to 15 minutes, then remove and decorate the sides with meringue piping using a pastry bag, forming hollow ridges that should resemble cannon barrels.
