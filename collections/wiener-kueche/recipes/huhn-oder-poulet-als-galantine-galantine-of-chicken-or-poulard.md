## Ingredients

- Chicken or pork forcemeat
- Veal
- Lard
- Bread soaked in milk
- Butter
- Eggs
- Egg yolk
- Salt
- Nutmeg
- Pepper
- Flammeed poulard or old hen
- Cooked tongue
- Lard
- Small pickled gherkins
- Goose liver
- Breast meat

## Directions

1. Finely mince chicken or pork forcemeat, veal, and lard with a chef's knife
2. Place in mortar and finely pound together with bread soaked in milk and pressed dry, butter, whole eggs, and egg yolk
3. Pass through sieve and mix thoroughly with salt, nutmeg, and pepper
4. From one flammeed poulard or old hen of large size, cut off wings at the uppermost joint and remove legs
5. Cut skin from back to head, loosen the neck and gullet from the neck skin and remove both
6. Remove and discard the head, but keep the neck skin attached to the bird
7. Dampen thumb and forefinger with cold water and gently slide skin from the bones, using a sharp knife where necessary
8. Spread the unbroken skin on a dampened board
9. Cut cooked tongue into 6 strips thumb-thick and 2 fingers long, cut lard into similar strips, cut 6 small pickled gherkins, 1 small goose liver, and the removed breast meats into strips finger-wide and 2 fingers long
10. Spread the skin 2 fingers thick with the forcemeat mixture and lay the tongue, lard and other strips lengthwise with colors alternating and thumb-width of forcemeat between each strip
11. When first layer is filled, spread again 2 fingers thick with forcemeat and repeat the layer as before
12. Cover again with forcemeat, then draw up the skin on both sides, lifting slightly so filling distributes well and close the opening
13. Tie closed with strong thread the openings left from removing legs and wings so forcemeat does not escape, and sew shut the neck hole
14. Butter half a cloth, place the filled chicken on it and roll up like a sausage, very carefully so filling does not shift
15. Bind the cloth with twine at both ends of the chicken and in the middle over the chicken
16. In a large casserole, arrange vegetables and herbs cut into strips with about 5 grains of various spices and add about 4 liters of water and bring to a good boil
17. Place the bound chicken in this boiling water and cook covered for one hour
18. After this remove carefully with two slotted spoons, place on a board with cloth, cover with a second board and weight lightly
19. The next day remove weight and board, cut the twine, unroll the cloth, wipe off the galantine and brush with dissolved aspic glaze as desired
20. Once completely dry, cut the galantine into thick knife-back slices, arrange in a platter and serve
