## Ingredients

- Veal carbonades
- Salt
- Pepper
- Butter
- Bacon
- Beef marrow
- Shallots
- Onion
- Parsley
- Bread
- Mushrooms
- Eggs
- Oil or butter
- Bouillon glace or Tartar sauce

## Directions

1. Veal carbonades, after the meat has been scraped from the backbone, are beaten flat, sprinkled with salt and pepper, and placed in a shallow casserole with a little very hot butter, to lightly fry them on each side for 1 to 2 minutes so they take on a white color.
2. Meanwhile, cut finely together 6 decagrams bacon, 6 decagrams beef marrow if desired, 1 piece shallot, onion, green parsley, 4 decagrams bread soaked in water and well pressed, and 2 mushrooms. Salt and season this mixture, add 2 egg yolks and stir well together.
3. The carbonades, after they are removed from the butter and cooled, are spread on both sides very thinly with the prepared forcemeat and filled on both sides with thin bacon slices.
4. A doubly folded paper is cut around as wide as a thumb larger than a cutlet is long, so that when the paper is unfolded it forms a heart shape. This paper is soaked in oil or spread thickly with butter, the prepared cutlet is placed on one side, the other half is folded over it, and beginning from above, namely from the opposite side of the rib bone, the paper is folded at the edges so that one fold holds down the other.
5. When all cutlets are wrapped in this way, they are placed on a sheet of paper soaked in oil or butter which lies on a roasting pan, allowed to fry slowly on both sides, then removed from the paper and arranged in a circle on a serving dish. In the center place dissolved bouillon glace or Tartar sauce.
