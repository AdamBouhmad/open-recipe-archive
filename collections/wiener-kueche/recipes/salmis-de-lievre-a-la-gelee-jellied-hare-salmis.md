## Ingredients

- 1 skinned hare back with both skinned haunches
- 16 decagrams finely cut bacon
- 1 small onion
- some red wine
- broth
- 5 juniper berries
- 5 pieces each of various spices
- 1 cleaned goose liver
- butter
- aspic

## Directions

1. Place the hare back and haunches in a casserole with finely cut bacon and onion
2. Roast, pour over with some red wine and broth
3. Cook covered until tender
4. Remove the back and haunches, place on a board, and carefully bone the meat
5. Place the meat on a dish and cover with buttered paper so it forms a crust
6. Put the hare forelegs with the bones from back and haunches into the broth
7. Add 5 juniper berries and 5 pieces each of various spices
8. Cook covered until meat is tender
9. Add 1 cleaned goose liver and cook together covered for ½ hour
10. Strain the remaining broth
11. Pound the remainder finely in mortar and pass through sieve
12. Strain and stir well together
13. If the purée is thinner than needed, dilute with some of the broth
14. Place this purée (called Salmis) in a dish and set on ice
15. When serving, place finger-high finely chopped aspic in a dish
16. Add a portion of Salmis on top
17. Arrange pieces of back and haunches on this
18. Continue layering Salmis and meat until all is used
19. When arranged in a raised dome with Salmis as the final layer, smooth with a knife dipped in hot water and cover the surface with a piece of aspic down to the dish edge
