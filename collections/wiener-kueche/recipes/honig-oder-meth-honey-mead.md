## Ingredients

- 1 Maß honey
- 8 Maß spring water
- Hops (0.5 pound per 100 pounds honey)
- optional: Mace, Cardamom
- optional: egg white
- pure yeast

## Directions

1. Best made on warm summer days.
2. Bring 8 Maß of spring water per 1 Maß of honey into a kettle and hang a sachet with hops (0.5 pound per 100 pounds honey) and, if desired, also with mace, cardamom and the like inside.
3. Simmer slowly over charcoal fire and skim diligently. If you add egg white before cooking, the impurities separate more easily.
4. Once all is skimmed, remove the fire from under the kettle and allow the mass to cool to the point that no egg can any longer be hard-boiled in it. The cooking should not last too long overall.
5. Test with a freshly laid egg: if it sinks so deep that only the tip is visible, the mead is correct; if it sinks completely under, there is still too much water, which must be evaporated; if it swims on top, there is too much honey and more water must be added.
6. When cooled, fill into a barrel with an addition of purest yeast, though some space must remain on top.
7. Once fermented, it remains for 3 months and is then drawn off and treated like wine.
8. Note: it is preferable to use the water left over from wax pressing instead of common water; one can also bring the honey cooked and skimmed with 4 to 8 parts water over gentle fire directly to barrels and let it ferment without addition, but add yeasts or some other fermentation agent to weak mead; and a well-prepared mead is said to equal the best and most delightful Spanish wines.
9. If it is sent far from its homeland and has not sufficiently fermented, it will enter into new fermentation in warmth and not rarely bursts the bottles as violently as Champagne wine.
