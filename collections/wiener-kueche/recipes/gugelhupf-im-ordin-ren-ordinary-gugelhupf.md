## Ingredients

- 14 deca lard or drippings or 10 deca butter
- 1/8 liter milk
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
- 3 egg yolks
- 1 whole egg
- 3 deca fresh yeast dissolved in 3/4 liter lukewarm milk
- 1 liter flour
- 8 deca raisins
- 8 deca currants
- butter for greasing
- flour or breadcrumbs for lining the mold

## Directions

1. Beat 14 deca lard or drippings or 10 deca butter with 1/8 liter milk until fluffy
2. Add 1 tablespoon sugar, 1 1/2 teaspoons salt, 3 egg yolks and 1 whole egg one after another, stirring
3. Mix in 3 deca fresh yeast dissolved in 3/4 liter lukewarm milk and 1 liter flour
4. Beat the dough until it separates from the spoon
5. Mix in 8 deca raisins and 8 deca currants
6. Warm a gugelhupf mold with a hole, coat heavily with butter and line with flour or breadcrumbs
7. Fill the mold with dough, distribute evenly, cover and place in a warm location
8. When the dough has risen to double its height, place the mold in the oven and bake initially at strong heat, later at moderate heat for 1 hour
9. Remove the mold from the oven, turn out onto a sieve and sprinkle with powdered sugar after cooling completely
10. To check if it is baked through, insert a pastry needle; if it comes out dry without dough, it is done
