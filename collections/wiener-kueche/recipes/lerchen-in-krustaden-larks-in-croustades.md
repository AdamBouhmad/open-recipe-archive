## Ingredients

- Larks
- Cold water
- Salt
- Veal or calves' liver pâté
- Lard
- 1/4 litre broth
- Madeira or white wine
- Glaze
- Demi-glace sauce
- Lemon juice
- Truffles
- Butter pastry or risotto rice

## Directions

1. Take the number of larks according to the number of persons to be served, usually taking some extra pieces. After the larks are plucked and flamed, they are cut lengthwise along the entire back and the bones are carefully removed, though head and neck are left attached. Lay them all on a board that has been previously moistened with cold water and dust them with a little salt. Then place in each bird 1 teaspoon of veal or calves' liver pâté, sew the skin of the bird back together, filling the eye sockets with pâté and placing the beak in the breast. Put some lard in a casserole, place the birds with heads upward, closely packed together, and if necessary, fill any excess space in the casserole with bread pieces. Cover the larks again with lard strips, add 1/4 litre broth and some Madeira or white wine, and let them stew covered for 1/3 hour. Serve them in small croustades, placing one lark with head upward in each croustade instead of pâté, and lightly coat the larks with dissolved glaze. Strain the cooking liquid, skim off fat, mix with some demi-glace sauce, lemon juice and thinly sliced truffles, and serve alongside. Alternatively, the larks can be arranged upright in a butter pastry shell with pâté in the middle so that the heads point upward; serve with the mentioned sauce with some demi-glace. The larks can also be served with steamed rice, namely place the rice raised on a round, deep bowl, press with a spoon that has been dipped in hot broth in the middle of the rice, while pushing rice from the edge of the bowl toward the middle with a tablespoon so that the rice forms a nest shape, upon which place the larks in the middle. With each of these serving styles the larks are lightly coated with glaze and the mentioned sauce is served. They can also be served in a polenta nest.
