## Ingredients

- grapes
- wax or pitch
- chaff or millet
- wheat bran
- twine

## Directions

1. Cut grapes on dry, clear days at midday
2. Dip the stem ends in melted wax or pitch
3. Remove any false or damaged berries with scissors
4. Hang grapes on an airy floor to dry completely
5. Layer them in well-sealed boxes between chaff, millet, or completely dry wheat bran so that no grape touches another
6. If desired, cut whole branches with multiple grape bunches and hang them; they will keep longer
7. Alternatively, for fleshier varieties cut before ripeness with stems attached, dip stem ends in melted pitch (better than wax), and hang in cool rooms by wrapping the stems with twine from a cord
8. Avoid creating dust in such rooms
