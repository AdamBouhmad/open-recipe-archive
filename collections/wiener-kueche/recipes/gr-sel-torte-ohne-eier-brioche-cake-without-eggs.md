## Ingredients

- 30 deca beaten butter
- 16 deca cinnamon sugar
- juice of 1 lemon
- 40 deca almonds, peeled and finely pounded
- juice of a second lemon
- finely cut lemon peel
- 30 deca flour
- egg white, beaten
- preserved fruit filling of choice
- vanilla sugar

## Directions

1. Beat 30 deca beaten butter with 16 deca cinnamon sugar and the juice of 1 lemon.
2. Gradually mix in 40 deca peeled and finely pounded almonds (mixed with the juice of a second lemon), some finely cut lemon peel, and 30 deca flour.
3. Roll out the dough finger-thick on a flour-dusted board.
4. Cut two round discs and place them in a buttered cake form dusted with flour.
5. Fill between the two discs with preserved fruit filling.
6. Brush the upper disc with beaten egg white and lay strips of dough (rolled into little fingers' thickness) across it in a lattice pattern.
7. Brush the lattice also with beaten egg white and bake at low heat for approximately 1 hour.
8. After this time, place the cake on a sieve and after it has cooled completely, fill the four corners of the lattice with preserved filling of choice.
9. Sprinkle with vanilla sugar and serve.
10. This cake is hard and can easily crack when cutting.
