## Ingredients

- ½ kilo butter dough
- Butter
- Spices
- Thin bacon slices
- Arbitrary forcemeat
- Raw meat (pork, veal, game or poultry)
- Beaten eggs

## Directions

1. Prepare ½ kilo butter dough and let it rest.
2. Butter a small cassole and line it with spices, then line it with thinly rolled and neatly cut-out butter dough in any desired pattern.
3. Cut a 2 knife-back-thick dough plate to fit the size of the cassole bottom, place it on the decoration brushed with egg, and press lightly.
4. Cut a strip of butter dough rolled out 2 knife-back-thick, as wide and long as the height and circumference of the cassole require.
5. Brush the diagonally cut ends of the strip with beaten egg and position the strip at the side wall of the cassole so that the edge of the strip and the bottom plate edge touch, and the both egg-brushed ends of the strip overlap well.
6. To give the connection of the side wall with the bottom plate more security, brush a strongly knife-back-thick and 2 finger-wide butter dough strip with beaten eggs and lay this brushed side at the joint of the dough so that one half of the strip's width adheres firmly to the bottom and the other half to the side wall.
7. Line the bottom and side walls with thin bacon slices, brush 2 fingers high with arbitrary forcemeat, and place arbitrary raw meat (pork, veal, game, or poultry) on top.
8. Brush the remaining empty space and the forcemeat with forcemeat, place 1 bacon slice over it, and cover this with a dough plate fitted to the size of the cassole, whose edge must be pressed together with that of the side wall.
9. Score the lid several times with a knife, brush with beaten eggs, and bake the pâté for 2 hours in the oven.
