## Ingredients

- beef suitable for stewing, cut into hand-wide, lengthwise pieces, no thicker than a knife back
- salt and pepper
- one brain, cooked in salt water
- green parsley
- small diced onion
- handful of breadcrumbs
- one spoonful of broth
- small piece of bacon, diced
- bacon
- onion
- yellow turnips
- a few spoonfuls of flour
- broth
- vinegar
- lemon peel, cut
- two spoonfuls of cream milk
- lemon peel, cut lengthwise

## Directions

1. Take a piece of beef suitable for stewing and cut it into hand-wide, lengthwise pieces, but no thicker than a knife back
2. Beat and salt them
3. Cook a brain in salt water, cleanly remove the skin, chop it fine, and braise it in butter with some green parsley
4. Add some finely chopped onion, salt, pepper, and a handful of breadcrumbs, also one spoonful of broth, let it come to a boil and cool
5. Chop a small piece of bacon finely, mix it with the brain, coat the meat with it, and tie it together with twine
6. Put bacon, onion, and yellow turnips in a casserole, lay the rolls in it and braise them until they have braised their sauce into themselves and become brownish
7. Take them out, put a couple spoonfuls of flour in the casserole, let it brown, then fill it with broth
8. Add some vinegar and lemon peel, let the sauce cook well, strain it, lay the rolls in it again, add two spoonfuls of cream milk and let it braise a bit more
9. Arrange the rolls in a row on the dish, strain sauce over them and sprinkle with lengthwise-cut lemon peel
