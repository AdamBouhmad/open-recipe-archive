## Ingredients

- 1 small turkey
- 1 1/2 kilo truffles
- 1 kilo truffles (cut into strips)
- 1/4 kilo truffles
- 1/4 kilo lard
- Fine herbs
- Shallots
- Thyme
- Salt
- Paper
- Lard plates

## Directions

1. For 1 small turkey, take 1 1/2 kilo truffles.
2. Clean them and cut them finely; cut 1 kilo of the truffles into strips.
3. Finely chop the remaining 1/4 kilo truffles and 1/4 kilo lard, place in a saucepan, add finely cut herbs, shallots, some thyme, and necessary salt.
4. Pluck a young, fat turkey immediately after bleeding, carefully remove the entrails, and fold the breast bone on both sides.
5. Truss the turkey and clean it inside and outside with a cloth.
6. Cut the turkey liver finely, add it to the truffles in the saucepan, and roast for 10-15 minutes.
7. Place the roasted mixture in a deep bowl, mix in the truffle strips cut into pieces, and let cool.
8. When cooled, fill the still warm turkey with this mixture, especially the crop, to give it a more beautiful appearance.
9. Sew the opening shut and wrap the turkey in lard plates and paper.
10. Let it rest for 5-6, or at most 8 days in a cool place, or it can be roasted the same day with the truffles, but the turkey must be at a cooler temperature.
11. After this time, remove it, take off paper and lard, salt the turkey, truss it, bind it with inserted lard, and roast on the spit or in the oven for approximately 2 hours.
12. If you want the turkey roasted white, wrap it in paper during roasting; if you want it golden, remove the paper 1/3 of an hour before finishing and roast it at a brighter fire.
13. Turkey with truffles is usually prepared in winter.
