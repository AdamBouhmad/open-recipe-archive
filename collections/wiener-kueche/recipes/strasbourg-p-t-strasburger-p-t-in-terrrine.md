## Ingredients

- 4 duck or goose livers
- 1/2 kilo diced bacon
- 1 handful of finely cut yellow roasted onions
- 2 tablespoons rum
- pâté seasoning
- 1 duck liver
- clarified lard with ham

## Directions

1. Cut out the hearts, veins, and bloody fibers from 4 duck livers.
2. Round them by cutting the edges, place the livers in cold water, remove after 10 minutes, and lay on a cloth to dry.
3. Make 3 cuts on the curved side of each liver lengthwise.
4. Rub with pâté seasoning and place finely cut truffles into the cuts.
5. Pound the scraps from the livers with 1/2 kilo diced bacon in a mortar and pass with 1 handful of finely cut yellow roasted onions through a sieve into a bowl.
6. Mix well with 2 tablespoons rum and pâté seasoning.
7. Line a pâté terrine with 1 duck liver, add a spoonful of the prepared forcemeat, alternate livers and forcemeat until the terrine is full.
8. Cover with a fitting lid and seal.
9. Cook in a bain-marie for 1 hour for smaller terrines or up to 3.5 hours for larger ones.
10. Remove from heat, take off the lid, and let the pâté cool in the bain-marie.
11. Press the cooked mass firmly, turn out the terrine so all liquid drains well, and smooth it.
12. Pour cold clarified lard with ham over the pâté in the terrine high enough so the fat reaches to the outer edge.
13. When set, seal with plaster or tin foil for storage.
