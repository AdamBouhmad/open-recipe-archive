## Ingredients

- hail sugar
- alkermes juice or dissolved cochineal dye

## Directions

1. Place the necessary hail sugar on a plate, moisten it with a few drops of alkermes juice or dissolved cochineal dye (both are obtainable at the pharmacy), and mix this with a metal spoon back and forth until the sugar has taken on an evenly beautiful red color and is completely dry. If one wants it darker, one must color it again after drying. The moistening must, however, always be done only in small measure, because too much moisture could dissolve the sugar; if time is pressing, one can also let the colored sugar dry in the oven, though it is better to do this in the air. Not only hail sugar but also the very fine sugar is colored in this way, only one must, after it has dried, pound and sieve it again. When the sugar is completely dry, it is stored in a glass jar.
