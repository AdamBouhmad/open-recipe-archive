## Ingredients

- 8 deca butter
- 1 piece onion
- several leaves parsley root
- 2 pieces finely sliced mushrooms
- 1 cleaned and split fish collar or broken pike head
- 3/4 liter pea water or clear water
- 5 bunches combined parsley sprigs
- 12 deca butter
- equal amount flour
- juice of 1 lemon
- salt
- pike cut into pieces
- salt water for cooking pike
- optional: cooked and stewed mushrooms
- optional: crayfish tails
- optional: green peas
- optional: 1/4 liter white wine
- optional: 1/8 liter Madeira wine

## Directions

1. Put butter and diced onion, parsley root leaves, mushroom pieces, and cleaned and split fish collar or broken pike head into a casserole
2. Let brown covered
3. Pour on 3/4 liter pea water or clear water and add 5 bunches of tied parsley sprigs
4. Let cook slowly covered for 1/2 hour
5. Make a very light roux from 12 deca butter and equal amount flour, pour it onto the stock and let cook well while stirring constantly
6. Strain the sauce, let it come to a boil again, skim off fat, and add juice of 1 lemon and necessary salt
7. Arrange pike pieces cut into pieces and cooked in salt water elevated on a platter and garnish with breaded, pea, or harlequin dumplings
8. Stir the sauce and pour over the meat and dumplings
9. Serve remaining sauce in a sauceboat
10. Optional additions: cooked and stewed mushrooms, crayfish tails, and green peas may be added with the prepared sauce
11. Optional: 1/4 liter white wine may be added with the pea water and let cook together, or sauce may be mixed shortly before serving with 1/8 liter Madeira wine
