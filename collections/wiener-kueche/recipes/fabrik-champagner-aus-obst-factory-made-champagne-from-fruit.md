## Ingredients

- selected pears that are very juice-rich and sweet for the finest sort
- good sweet apples for the second sort
- ordinary good pears for the third sort
- substantial addition of sugar for the first two sorts
- sugar added only to the bottles for the third sort
- sugar dissolved in water
- cream of tartar dissolved in warm must
- bottles with carbonated gas

## Directions

1. Let the fruit ripen very much and use it as soon as possible
2. For the finest sort, rub the fruit on machines without question on account of the larger quantity of wine being made; the rubbed fruit falls into a tub
3. For the other two sorts, press the fruit in the ordinary way
4. Clean the fruit carefully beforehand; peel only the finest sort
5. Put the crushed fruit into linen sacks under the press and press only carefully and gradually
6. After pressing for some time, turn the sacks and stir the mass and press again moderately; for each new pressing take new sacks and everything newly used must be cleaned first
7. For the better sorts, let the juice settle first in tubs before filtering the clear juice through linen cloth into the barrel
8. For the third sort, the juice goes directly from the press into the barrel
9. The barrels must have had white wine in them previously
10. Dissolve sugar in water and skim it over fire
11. Roll the barrels into the cellar and add some cream of tartar dissolved in warm must and several bottles with carbonated gas
12. After several days fermentation begins, and then drill the tap holes in the bottom and drive the pipes
