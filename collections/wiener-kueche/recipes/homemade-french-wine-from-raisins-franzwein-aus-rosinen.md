## Ingredients

- Raisins of the largest and best sort
- One pound of raisins per pitcher of soft water
- A small addition of sugar
- Tartar (Weinstein)

## Directions

1. Take raisins of the largest and best sort and tear them into small pieces, which is better than cutting them
2. For every pitcher of soft water, use one pound of such raisins
3. Place the raisins in a wine barrel and allow them to ferment on the ground
4. A small addition of sugar and tartar does no harm
5. Before fermentation is fully underway and even at its beginning, roll the barrel several times throughout the day or stir the mass
6. Once fermentation is finished, bring the barrel to the cellar
7. After filling it up, let it rest quietly until the wine is clear and bright
8. Then draw the wine into bottles and it can be drunk
9. This yields a spirited and lovely wine that is healthy and delightful, and cheaper, and indeed usually also better, than purchased French wine
