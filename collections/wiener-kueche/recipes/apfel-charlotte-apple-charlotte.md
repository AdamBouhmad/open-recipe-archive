## Ingredients

- 28 Deka flour
- approximately 24 cooking apples
- sugar as needed
- marmalade as desired
- eggs

## Directions

1. Prepare a butter dough of finest quality from 28 Deka flour (No. 1035) and let it rest covered in a cool place
2. Meanwhile prepare the filling: peel about 24 cooking apples, remove the core casings, cut the apples into wedges and put them in a casserole, sweeten as desired and steam covered without water or other liquid until no liquid remains
3. Add some spoonfuls of desired marmalade, stir well together and let it come to a boil once, then set aside and let cool
4. After the butter dough has rested for 1 hour, roll it out on a flour-dusted board to the thickness of a knife back, cut into two equal parts
5. Roll one part onto a strudel roller and unroll it without damage onto a sheet dampened with water
6. Brush the dough with the cold apple marmalade leaving a thumb-width border free on all 4 sides, brush this border with beaten egg
7. Roll the second part of the rolled out butter dough onto the strudel roller and unroll it over the apples so that the edges lie evenly together and the side that was on the board comes on top
8. Lightly press the edges together, make shallow cuts in the upper dough layer with a knife, brush the dough with beaten egg but leave the edges knife-back-width dry
9. Place these prepared apple charlottes in the fairly hot oven and bake for 1.5 to 3.5 hours
10. When the dough is half baked, remove the sheet, sprinkle the dough heavily with finely ground sugar and finish baking
11. When the apple charlotte has a brown color and the sugar looks glassy, loosen it from the sheet with a very thin knife, cut into desired pieces, arrange on a plate and serve warm or cold as desired
