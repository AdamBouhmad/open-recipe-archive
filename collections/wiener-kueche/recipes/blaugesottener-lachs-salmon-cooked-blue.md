## Ingredients

- Salmon
- Vinegar (1 liter, boiling)
- Salt water (boiling)
- Butter
- Lemon slices
- Parsley
- Butter for basting

## Directions

1. Cut the salmon under the head
2. Paint, remove, and wash it
3. Place the salmon on the insert of the fish kettle and bind lightly from head to tail with twine
4. Place the insert with the fish in the fish kettle, having put a piece of wood in the fish's belly
5. Pour 1 liter of boiling vinegar over the salmon
6. Pour boiling salt water from the side until the liquid stands finger-high above the fish
7. Cover the fish kettle with a sheet of buttered paper and a suitable lid
8. Let it stand on the stove for 1 to 1.5 hours, during which time the broth must always be simmering but not boiling
9. Remove the salmon with the insert, unbind it, and carefully place it on a platter garnished with lemon slices and parsley
10. Brush the fish with butter
11. For cold serving, let it cool in its broth, then remove and arrange with aspic and serve Mayonnaise
12. Place a lemon slice or sprig of parsley in the fish's belly in place of wood
