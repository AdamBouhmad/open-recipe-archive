## Ingredients

- butter dough
- eggs

## Directions

1. Prepare butter dough and roll it out, after it has rested for 1 hour, on a flour-dusted board 2 knife-back-widths thick.
2. From one half of the dough, round pieces are cut out with a pastry cutter, which are placed at finger-width distance on a water-moistened baking sheet, brushed with beaten eggs so far that a strongly knife-back-width margin remains all around, which must never be brushed with eggs so that the dough of the pâté becomes flaky.
3. From the other half of the rolled-out dough, somewhat smaller pieces are cut out, which are then, to form rings, cut out once more in the middle.
4. These are now placed on the round plates so that the side which lay on the board does not lie on the brushed plates, but comes to lie upward.
5. These rings are then brushed in the middle with beaten eggs so far that both outside and inside a knife-back-width margin remains.
6. They are then placed in the oven and baked yellow at fairly strong heat.
7. Then remove them with a knife from the sheet, fill the middle empty space with any desired farce, and arrange them elevated in a bowl covered with a napkin.
8. It is particularly noteworthy that the margin of the pâté must never be brushed or dripped with eggs or water, as in this case the butter dough could not puff up.
