## Ingredients

- flour
- butter (equal amount to flour)
- pinch of salt
- 1 egg
- 2 spoonfuls vinegar or wine or lemon juice
- 2 spoonfuls cold water

## Directions

1. Place the flour on a pastry board. Knead one third of it with the butter and form it into a brick shape, place on ice.
2. With the remaining flour, salt, 1 egg, 2 spoonfuls vinegar (or wine or lemon juice) and 2 spoonfuls cold water, make a strudel dough, work it very well until tender, and let it rest under a cold pot for 15 minutes.
3. Roll it out into a rectangle, place the butter brick in the middle, fold the dough from top to bottom and vice versa, also from right to left over it.
4. Carefully tap with the rolling pin first in the middle, then at top and bottom so the butter distributes.
5. Lightly roll it out so that a long but not wide strip (approximately 45 cm long, 15 cm wide) is formed, fold it into thirds, turn the dough so the open sides are at top and bottom and roll it out again to a strip.
6. Fold together once more and roll out. After folding and rolling out a third time, fold the dough a third time, wrap in paper, place on ice or in a cold place.
7. Let it rest at least 20 minutes. After that, roll out and fold together three more times, place on ice until this has been repeated a third time.
8. Before using, it must rest for a full hour to become stiff. Then it can be rolled out as thick or thin as desired.
9. It is best to make the dough in the evening and use it the next day. Leftover pieces are placed together, tapped with the rolling pin and rolled out again. They should not be kneaded together by hand as the dough would become too warm and not work.
10. The dough is always placed on an ungreased sheet and needs a hot oven. The butter pastries (vol au vent) can be made earlier and reheated, as they are better warm, or they are baked and filled in the last moment. The dough bakes for approximately 8-15 minutes.
