## Ingredients

- 0.5-1 kilogram carp (male)
- 6 decagrams root vegetables
- Some fish blood
- 8-10 dried prunes
- 0.5 liter beer
- 10 decagrams elderberry sauce
- 2 decagrams gingerbread
- 1 onion
- 6 peppercorns and spice berries
- 4 cloves
- A piece of ginger
- Lemon peel
- 1 piece of garlic
- Grated gingerbread
- Salt
- Vinegar
- 2 bay leaves
- Pinch of thyme
- Elderberry sauce
- Water
- Sugar for browning
- 2 decagrams fat or butter
- Flour
- Raisins
- 6 swelled, sliced almonds
- Several half nuts

## Directions

1. Clean, wash, and slice the root vegetables into leaves
2. Cook the vegetables with 1 onion, fish blood, 6 peppercorns and spice berries, 4 cloves, a piece of ginger, lemon peel, 1 piece of garlic, grated gingerbread, salt, vinegar, beer, 2 bay leaves, a pinch of thyme, and elderberry sauce with enough water to cover for about 1 hour
3. Cut the fish into two-finger-width pieces
4. Place the fish pieces in the broth and let them simmer gently for 9-10 minutes
5. Remove the fish and keep it warm
6. Strain the broth through a sieve
7. In a casserole, brown 1 spoonful of sugar in 2 decagrams hot fat or butter
8. Add a very small amount of flour, stir it, and let it cool
9. Pour the broth over it
10. Add the prunes, some raisins, 6 swelled sliced almonds, and several half nuts
11. Season with salt, acid, and sugar to taste
12. Let it cook for 15-20 minutes
13. Pour the sauce over the fish pieces and serve with Bohemian dumplings
