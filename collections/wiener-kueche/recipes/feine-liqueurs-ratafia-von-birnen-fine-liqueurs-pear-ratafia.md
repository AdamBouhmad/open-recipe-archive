## Ingredients

- 25 pounds or 12 measures of very good must
- 6 measures of must
- 6 measures of good, rectified spirits
- 2 quenchen of anise
- 2 quenchen of cloves
- 1 quenchen of cinnamon powder
- 13 pieces of crushed apricot and peach kernels, or in their absence, plum kernels

## Directions

1. Simmer 25 pounds or 12 measures of very good must in the kettle until reduced to half, keeping it at low heat and skimming it
2. Pour the remaining 6 measures of must into a bottle and add the same amount of good, rectified spirits
3. Add 2 quenchen of anise and 2 quenchen of cloves, 1 quenchen of cinnamon powder, and 13 pieces of crushed apricot and peach kernels, or plum kernels in their absence
4. Leave the well-stopped vessel at a warm stove for two days
5. Strain the liquid, return it to the bottle, and after some months, filter it and fill into bottles
