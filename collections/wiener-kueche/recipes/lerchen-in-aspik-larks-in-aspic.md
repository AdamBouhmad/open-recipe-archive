## Ingredients

- Larks
- Aspic jelly
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Goose liver or truffles

## Directions

1. These are prepared like the larks in croustades. When the larks in the casserole in which they were stewed have cooled completely, they are carefully removed, laid on a cloth and each lark is well dried. Pour into a deep mold that was put on ice 1/2 hour earlier, strong finger-high layer of melted aspic. While this sets, cut from 1 hard-boiled egg strips 2 fingers wide, which you cut out with a round cutter. This is then cut out again with a three numbers smaller cutter, creating a ring. Similar rings are cut out from knife-back thick pieces of tongue. Lay these pieces alternating in color on the now set aspic and place the cut-out tongue pieces in the white rings, and in the red ones the egg pieces. You can use some pieces of goose liver or truffles for this decoration. Pour finger-high aspic again on top and when completely set, lay the stewed larks on top so that the heads rest downward on the aspic and the breasts are turned toward the outer wall. So that the birds remain securely in place, support them with some chopped aspic and you can also place a piece of stewed goose liver next to each lark. When all larks are placed in the mold, carefully pour melted aspic around the edge so that the birds are completely covered. After this has set firmly, remove the mold from the ice and proceed further as with the goose liver cake in aspic. Shortly before serving, place herb mayonnaise in the center of the dish, neatly arranged. All lark dishes are recommended as side dishes, main courses, for supper or tea, or also for breakfast.
