## Ingredients

- Quince apples or pears
- Lemon juice
- Lemon water
- 80 Deca sugar from heavy syrup
- 4 Deca sugar

## Directions

1. Cut the quince apples or pears into arbitrary pieces and remove the seed cavities
2. Peel the quinces and rub with lemon juice
3. Place in cold, then boiling lemon water and cook until moderately soft
4. Strain and place in an earthen vessel
5. Pour 80 Deca of heavy-cooked sugar (No. 1859) over the quinces and let them stand overnight in a warm place
6. Strain the juice and add 4 Deca sugar, let it boil again while removing the foam, then pour over the fruit
7. Repeat this process for 6 days
8. On the last day, cook the quinces with the juice together, place some of the juice in the jars, then quickly add the quinces and the juice which must cover them two fingers high
9. Place the filled jars unclosed in a breezy location and after several days bind them with writing paper, which you pierce with a needle to let air in
10. If there is too much juice on the second day, remove the excess clear juice, strain it through cloth, fill into jars, bind them and cook like Muscatel pears (No. 1842) for 3/4 hours in steam
11. Leave enough juice for the quinces to be completely covered
12. If you wish the quince slices to be red, add dissolved cochineal color when cooking for the last time
