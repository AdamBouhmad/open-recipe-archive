## Ingredients

- 60 deca flour
- 4 deca pressed yeast dissolved in 1/4 liter milk
- 4 deca melted butter
- 6 tablespoons milk
- 5 egg yolks
- 1 whole egg
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1.5 teaspoons salt
- 26 deca beaten butter
- 16 deca Demerara sugar
- 5 egg yolks
- 1 whole egg
- 5 egg whites beaten to peaks
- raisins
- currants
- finely chopped citron
- elongated cut dates

## Directions

1. Place flour in a deep bowl, make a well in the middle, pour in yeast dissolved in milk, stir briefly and cover to rest
2. Meanwhile, whisk together melted butter, milk, egg yolks, whole egg, sugar, and salt
3. Add this mixture to the flour, mix well and beat the dough until it lifts from the spoon
4. Cover and let rise in a warm place
5. Prepare the filling: beat butter with Demerara sugar, egg yolks, and whole egg, then fold in beaten egg whites
6. Once the dough has doubled in height, roll out on a flour-dusted board to the thickness of a small finger
7. Cut into 2 pieces and spread each with a third of the filling
8. Fold dough over filling, brush the fold with filling, sprinkle with raisins, currants, citron, and dates
9. Roll up the dough, place on a butter-greased baking sheet, press the ends together and fold them slightly upward
10. Cover and let rise in a warm place
11. Brush with melted butter and bake at moderate heat for 1/2 hour
12. Cut into pieces as desired, arrange on a platter and serve with custard milk or cream, or serve cold with tea or coffee
