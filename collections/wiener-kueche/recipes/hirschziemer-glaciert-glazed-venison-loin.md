## Ingredients

- Venison loin (upper part of the haunch)
- Salt
- Spices
- 16 spoonfuls bacon or other fat
- Root vegetables
- 1 onion, cut into thin leaves
- Thyme
- 5 grains each type of spice
- 5 crushed juniper berries
- 1 piece black bread, cut into thin slices
- 1 liter red wine
- 1/4 liter vinegar
- Water
- Butter
- Bread crumbs roasted in butter
- Sugar
- 1 spice clove
- Cinnamon
- Orange and lemon slices
- Parsley
- Lingonberry or rosehip sauce

## Directions

1. Rub the venison loin with salt and spices and let it rest for 2 hours.
2. In a casserole, place bacon or other fat, root vegetables, onion cut into thin leaves, thyme, 5 grains each type of spice, 5 crushed juniper berries, and a piece of black bread cut into thin slices.
3. Lay the loin on top.
4. Pour 1 liter red wine, 1/4 liter vinegar, and some water over it, using enough liquid so that it covers the loin.
5. Cover with a sheet of parchment paper brushed with butter and braise gently for approximately 2 to 3 hours until tender.
6. Once tender, remove to a board and cut into desired pieces.
7. Arrange the pieces on a platter closely together so that everything appears as a whole.
8. If desired, sprinkle roasted bread crumbs on top.
9. Sprinkle sugar mixed with 1 clove and some cinnamon that has been ground together, then sprinkle plain sugar on top, which is then glazed (caramelized) with a hot shovel.
10. Garnish the platter with orange and lemon slices and parsley, and serve with lingonberry or rosehip sauce.
11. If desired, skim the fat from the sauce and serve it with the meat.
12. If there are leftovers, preserve them in the sauce.
