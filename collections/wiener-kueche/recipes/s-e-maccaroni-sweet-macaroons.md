## Ingredients

- One pound sweet almonds
- Egg whites
- 2 pounds finest pounded sugar
- Zest of 2 lemons
- 12 to 18 eggs typically required

## Directions

1. Blanch 1 pound sweet almonds in boiling water, then plunge into cold water for a few minutes
2. Remove the skins and wash them, then let them dry for 24 hours under a napkin
3. Take a handful of almonds, pound them in a mortar or on a grater until fine but not too fine so they do not become oily
4. Pour the white of one egg onto the crushed mass, stir and mix well until it becomes a fine dough
5. Empty the mortar and proceed with the remaining almonds in the same way, using additional egg whites as needed
6. Once all the almond mass is processed, return it to the mortar and add 2 pounds of finest pounded sugar and the zest of 2 lemons
7. Stir all well together and add enough egg white so the dough achieves the necessary consistency
8. Take a two-finger-wide wooden ruler with a handle, place some dough on it, hold the instrument with your left hand, and use a clean knife to set oblong-round heaps about finger-thick on a sheet of paper, spacing them one and a half inches apart
9. Push the macaroons on a sheet into a moderately hot oven and bake them slowly for three quarters of an hour
10. Ensure the almonds are very dry before putting them in the mortar
11. Be careful when beating the eggs so that no yolk mixes with the white, as this would ruin the entire dough mass and the macaroons would collapse in the oven instead of rising
12. Keep the oven only moderately heated as almonds and sugar burn very easily
13. To be completely safe, put a couple into the oven as a test and bake for three quarters of an hour; if they are not burned and have a beautiful yellow color, the oven has the right temperature
14. If they are black underneath, it shows the lower heat is stronger than the upper, and in this case lay 2 or 3 sheets of paper on the baking sheet to moderate the effect
15. Only after testing the oven heat in this way should the macaroon dough be placed on paper, and only when completely cooled should the macaroons be removed from it
