## Ingredients

- Three-quarters pound sago
- One measure milk
- One Seidel cream
- Some cinnamon
- One-quarter pound fresh butter
- Equal amount sugar
- 12 egg yolks
- Egg whites from 12 eggs
- Sugar for garnish

## Directions

1. Rinse the sago well, boil it several times with water, and cool it on a hair sieve with fresh water.
2. Bring one measure milk and one Seidel cream to a boil with some cinnamon, and add the sago to it so that it swells well and cooks; watch it carefully as it tends to stick to the bottom.
3. When the sago is well swollen and short, stir in one-quarter pound fresh butter and an equal amount of sugar, and let it cool.
4. In the same manner, stir in 12 egg yolks, beat the egg whites to snow, and fold them in slowly.
5. Bake in a rimmed dish.
6. If the soufflés are sprinkled with sugar beforehand, they will get a beautiful glaze.
