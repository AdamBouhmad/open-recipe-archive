## Ingredients

- 1.5 liters milk
- 5 decigrams butter
- 22 decigrams flour
- 5 whole eggs
- 4 decigrams butter or lard for filling
- calf's brain
- parsley, finely chopped
- salt and pepper to taste

## Directions

1. In a casserole, bring 1.5 liters milk and 5 decigrams butter to a boil.
2. Stir in 22 decigrams flour and stir continuously over the fire until the dough is dry and pulls away from the spoon and casserole.
3. Transfer the dough to a deep bowl and let it cool with occasional stirring.
4. Beat in 5 whole eggs.
5. Form walnut-sized balls with a spoon, fry them brown in hot lard with continuous shaking, and place them on blotting paper.
6. For the filling: In a casserole, place 4 decigrams butter or lard with a teaspoon of finely chopped parsley, let it brown somewhat.
7. Add calf's brain (freed of membranes), some pepper, and the necessary salt, stir well together, and let it roast over the fire.
8. Cut the fried balls halfway through the middle and insert a teaspoon of the prepared filling, or fill them instead with any desired forcemeat, egg dish, or braised vegetables.
9. Arrange them elevated on a dish covered with a napkin and serve with soup.
