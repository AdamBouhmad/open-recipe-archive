## Ingredients

- 2 kilos beef (preferably the round)
- Salt
- Bacon pieces, half a finger long and pencil thick
- Salted pork rinds or salted tongue
- 1 cassole
- Onion
- Root vegetables
- 1 bay leaf
- Thyme
- Several sprigs of fresh parsley
- Bacon
- Ham
- Some broth
- ½ liter wine
- Butter
- Paper
- ½ kilo calf forcemeat (cold pâté forcemeat)
- Butter dough
- Thin bacon slices
- Eggs

## Directions

1. Remove the skin from the 2 kilos of beef, salt it, and lard it alternately with half-finger-long and pencil-thick pieces of bacon and salted pork rinds or salted tongue, then shape it nicely round.
2. Line a cassole with onion, root vegetables, 1 bay leaf, thyme, several sprigs of fresh parsley, bacon and ham, place the meat on top, pour some broth and ½ liter wine over it, cover with paper brushed with butter, seal with a fitting lid, and braise the meat slowly.
3. When the meat is completely tender, set the cassole aside and let the meat cool in its juices.
4. Remove the meat, strain the sauce and set it aside.
5. Finely chop the cooked ham or bacon that was in the pot and mix it with ½ kilo calf forcemeat (cold pâté forcemeat).
6. Prepare a pâté from butter dough as indicated for the Goose Liver Pâté, line it with thin bacon slices, and brush the bottom with 2 fingers high of the forcemeat prepared above.
7. Place the meat on top, brush it with the remaining forcemeat, cover this with thin bacon slices and cover them with a small-finger-thick dough plate, which is scored several times with a knife and then brushed with beaten eggs.
8. Bake the pâté for 1½ to 2 hours at moderate heat in the oven.
9. After this time, loosen and remove the lid, pour the strained sauce inside, put the lid back on, and set the pâté to cool.
