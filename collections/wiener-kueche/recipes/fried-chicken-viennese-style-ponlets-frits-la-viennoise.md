## Ingredients

- Young, tender chicken
- Flour
- Eggs, beaten
- Breadcrumbs
- Fat for frying (pork fat and beef marrow)
- Salt
- Green parsley, fried
- Salad or compote

## Directions

1. Choose a young, tender bird. Dip the chickens, which have been freshly killed, into boiling water, lay them on a board, flatten them with the flat of the hand and quickly remove the feathers.
2. When this is done, remove the entrails and clean the chickens in cold water. Poke out the eyes, break off the beak but leave the head attached.
3. After cutting off the legs to the knee, split the chickens lengthwise and divide each half sharply at the thighbone.
4. Twist the wings backward toward the neck, cut into the joints of the thighbones and tuck the thighbones inward.
5. Salt the pieces, dip them in flour, dunk them in beaten eggs, roll them in breadcrumbs and just before serving fry them in a casserole in finger-high hot fat (one part pork fat and one part beef marrow) until golden on both sides.
6. Remove the chickens with a skimming ladle, place them on blotting paper in a serving dish and put them in the open oven.
7. Meanwhile, dip the chicken wings, necks and feet in flour, eggs and breadcrumbs, fry them in the hot fat, then remove them with the skimming ladle, place them in a serving dish and add the fried chicken.
8. Sprinkle them with fried green parsley and serve with salad or compote of choice.
