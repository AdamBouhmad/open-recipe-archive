## Ingredients

- beef tongue
- 4 liters water
- 1 handful salt
- 8 tablespoons butter
- 1 small head parsley root
- 1 finger-length piece yellow turnip
- parsley
- celery
- whole spices
- 16 tablespoons veal meat from the knuckle (shoulder)
- 1/2 hour
- 1/3 liter stock
- 1/4 liter soup
- 8 tablespoons butter
- equal amount flour
- juice of 1 lemon
- salt
- 4 tablespoons Madeira wine
- approximately 2 tablespoons sugar
- truffles

## Directions

1. Wash the beef tongue thoroughly, then cook it covered in 4 liters of water with 1 handful of salt for approximately 2 hours
2. Remove the coarse skin, which will peel off easily
3. Return the tongue to the broth and cook until completely tender, approximately 4 hours total cooking time
4. Cut the tongue lengthwise down the middle and arrange the sliced pieces in approximately 1 finger tall and 2 fingers wide portions on a platter, arranged so they form a whole tongue again
5. For the Madeira sauce: in a saucepan, put 8 tablespoons butter, 1 small head parsley root, 1 finger-length piece yellow turnip, parsley and celery, all cut into thin slices, and some whole spices, then place 16 tablespoons of diced veal meat from the knuckle on top
6. Brown this covered for 1/2 hour, then pour in 1/3 liter stock and 1/4 liter soup
7. Let cook covered
8. Make a roux from 8 tablespoons butter and equal amount flour, pour in the prepared broth, let it thicken well, then add juice of 1 lemon, adjust seasoning with salt if needed
9. Add 4 tablespoons Madeira wine and approximately 2 tablespoons sugar; if the sauce is too thick, add some soup
10. After removing from heat, pour the sauce over the cut tongue
11. When serving, finely sliced truffels may be added if desired
