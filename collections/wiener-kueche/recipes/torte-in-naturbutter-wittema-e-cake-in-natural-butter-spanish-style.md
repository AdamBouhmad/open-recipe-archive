## Ingredients

- writing paper
- 8 sheets of writing paper
- unsalted bacon
- 8 egg whites, stiffly beaten
- 32 deca vanilla sugar
- powdered sugar
- desired fruit foam or frozen dessert

## Directions

1. Cut approximately 8 round discs from white writing paper.
2. In the middle of each, cut out circular openings so that each disc forms a ring; each successive ring becomes somewhat narrower, with the eighth ring being approximately one finger-width broad.
3. Line all paper strips with unsalted bacon and place them side by side on a baking sheet with the lined side facing up.
4. Beat 8 egg whites stiffly and lightly mix with 32 deca vanilla sugar.
5. Using a paper cornet or syringe, pipe this mixture onto the paper rings without interruption and at even height (approximately finger-high).
6. Sprinkle with finely pounded sugar.
7. Additionally, cut a round disc the same size as the broadest ring, brush it with unsalted bacon, and likewise cover it finger-high with the snow mixture and sprinkle with finely pounded sugar.
8. Dry all of these in the oven at high heat. As these rings take up considerable space on the baking sheet and it is usually not possible to dry them all simultaneously, they can be made in batches, though the snow mixture should always be prepared only shortly before piping and placing in the oven.
9. Once completely dry, carefully remove the rings from the paper with a sharp, thin knife.
10. Place the large round disc on a cake plate, arrange the broadest ring on top and secure with egg glaze or white marmalade; arrange all remaining rings, each progressively smaller, as well as the bottom ring on top.
11. Fill the middle empty space with desired fruit foam or frozen dessert and serve the cake immediately on an appropriate platter.
