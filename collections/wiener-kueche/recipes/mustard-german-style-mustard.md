## Ingredients

- 2 liters of clear wine must
- 8 Deca of sugar
- 16 Deca sweet mustard flour
- 16 Deca bitter mustard flour
- 7 juniper berries
- 5 peppercorns
- 5 grains of Allspice
- 1 clove
- 1 small piece of nutmeg

## Directions

1. In a very clean dish where no iron has ever been, reduce 2 liters of clear wine must to about half its volume, removing the foam that forms
2. Before the must is sufficiently reduced, add 8 Deca of sugar
3. Sift 16 Deca sweet mustard flour and 16 Deca bitter mustard flour through a sieve into a bowl
4. Crush together in a mortar 7 juniper berries, 5 peppercorns, 5 grains of Allspice, 1 clove, and 1 small piece of nutmeg
5. Sift this through the sieve as well and mix well with the mustard flour
6. Make a well in the mixture and slowly pour the hot must while stirring constantly for about half an hour until the mustard becomes thick
7. The mustard should be thin enough to flow easily from a spoon; if not, add more hot must
8. Allow it to cool slightly covered with a cloth in the bowl at a cool, dust-free location
9. On the second day, fill the mustard into jars
10. On the third day, cover with dampened, well-wrung paper covers, bind them, and store in a cool place
