## Ingredients

- Maschen-Äpfel apples
- Flour for dredging
- Eggs for coating
- Breadcrumbs for rolling
- Lard for frying
- Powdered sugar for serving
- Preserves or jam for filling

## Directions

1. Cut a round disk from the side of the apple stem, leaving the stem on the disk.
2. Press a small cutter into the center of each apple, turn it slightly and pull it out with the core house - the apple should not be pierced completely but a knife-back-thick base must remain.
3. After the apples and round disks are peeled, dip them in flour, dip in beaten eggs, roll in breadcrumbs and fry them on both sides until golden yellow in very hot lard.
4. Remove the apples with a skimming spoon, fill the empty space with any desired preserve, cover with the round disks on which the stems are located, sprinkle with finely powdered sugar and serve hot.
5. Usually the apples are served mixed with cream fritters.
