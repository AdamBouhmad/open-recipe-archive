## Ingredients

- 2 parts white vinegar
- 1 part saffron
- spices
- some mixed spices
- green beans
- young small yellow turnips
- small onions
- small cauliflower pieces
- shallots
- rocambole
- artichoke hearts
- small cucumber pieces
- mushrooms
- asparagus
- salt
- salt water
- fresh picked cucumbers
- dill leaves
- chervil leaves
- green pepperoni (paprika)
- 5 cloves
- 5 bitter peppercorns

## Directions

1. Mix 2 parts white vinegar with 1 part saffron, spices, and some mixed spices and simmer for approximately 1/2 hour (skim off the foam that forms)
2. Let it cool in an earthen, glass, or porcelain vessel
3. Cook each type of vegetable - green beans, young small yellow turnips, small onions, small cauliflower pieces, shallots, rocambole, artichoke hearts, small cucumber pieces, mushrooms, and asparagus - separately in salt water until half-cooked
4. Place each in cold water and lay on a sieve or cloth to drain
5. For the cucumbers: place the smallest variety that have been picked in dry weather on a cloth, sprinkle with salt, and rub with the cloth until they become moist, then place on another cloth or sieve to drain slightly
6. Complete this process for the cucumbers within 1 hour
7. Line the bottom of prepared glasses with some dill leaves, chervil leaves, and green pepperoni (paprika)
8. Arrange the cucumbers and then the mixed vegetables in attractive layers, alternating with cucumbers, filling the glasses almost full
9. For each 1/2 liter glass, add 5 cloves and 5 bitter peppercorns
10. Pour the prepared cold vinegar over, ensuring the glass is not completely full
11. For ordinary household use, tie with twine; for storage of glasses, cork and seal them
12. For storage, keep at a cool, airy location
13. If the vinegar decreases over time, add water-diluted cold vinegar
14. If the vinegar becomes cloudy or creamy, strain it off, boil it, let it cool, and pour it back over the vegetables, keeping them covered and weighted at a cool, airy place
