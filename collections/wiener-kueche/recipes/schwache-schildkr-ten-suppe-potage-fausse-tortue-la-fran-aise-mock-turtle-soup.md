## Ingredients

- 1 cleaned and brushed calf's head with tongue
- broth or salt water
- 3/4 liter beef broth
- 1 1/4 liters Madeira wine
- 8 decagrams finely sliced truffles (optional)
- 14 decagrams butter
- equal amount of flour
- 1 finely sliced champignon
- green parsley
- 2 liters beef broth
- cooked pear
- salt and pepper

## Directions

1. Simmer the cleaned calf's head with tongue in broth or salt water until tender
2. Remove from broth and dry with a cloth
3. Cut round pieces with a cutter or cut into desired pieces
4. Place these pieces in a casserole with 3/4 liter of beef broth and 1 1/4 liters of Madeira wine, and optionally 8 decagrams of finely sliced truffles
5. Cook covered for approximately 1 1/2 hours
6. Meanwhile, make a brown roux from 14 decagrams of butter and equal amount of flour
7. Roast a finely sliced champignon and some green parsley in it
8. Pour on 2 liters of beef broth in which the calf's head was cooked and let it cook well
9. Cut the skinned, salt-water-cooked pear into pieces and add to the soup
10. Pass the brown sauce into the casserole with the calf's head, broth, and wine
11. Add salt and pepper if desired and bring to a boil
12. Serve with pasta, crayfish quenelles, or roasted bread slices
