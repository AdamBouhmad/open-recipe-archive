## Ingredients

- About 24 oysters
- Own oyster liquid
- A few tablespoons full of sea water or white wine
- Butter
- Flour
- Butter dough, finger-thick
- 2 deca shredded butter
- Lemon juice
- Butter dough, finger-thick
- Beaten eggs
- Bread crumbs
- A small piece butter

## Directions

1. Remove about 24 oysters from the shell, place them with their own liquid and a few tablespoons full of sea water or white wine in a casserole and let warm covered.
2. When this is done, set them aside and let them cool in their juice.
3. Line any mold brushed with butter and dusted with flour with finger-thick butter dough, add the oysters, scatter with 2 deca shredded butter, sprinkle with lemon juice, cover with a finger-thick butter dough lid matching the mold, which after being cut several times with a knife is brushed with beaten eggs, then bake the pastry at quite strong heat in the oven.
4. Meanwhile, to the oyster liquid remaining in the casserole add a little bread crumbs, a small piece of butter and lemon juice, let it boil well and pour it in after the pastry is finished baking and the lid has been removed as was indicated for the potato pastry, and serve the pastry immediately.
