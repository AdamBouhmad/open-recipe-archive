## Ingredients

- good bacon, not too heavily smoked, two finger-widths and one knife-back thickness in slices
- onions, sliced similarly
- lean beef, cut into 2-inch thick slices
- veal shanks (Jarrets)
- scraps of carbonade or similar
- water or bouillon, as much as needed
- celery heads, cleaned
- parsley
- yellow root vegetables
- savoy cabbage heads
- mace leaves

## Directions

1. Cut the bacon into slices two finger-widths and one knife-back thickness, and lay them side by side on the bottom of a flat copper casserole in which you wish to cook the schü
2. Cut onions into similar slices and cover the bacon with them
3. Cut lean beef into 2-inch thick slices and lay these over the onions, along with some veal shanks and any scraps of carbonade you may have
4. Set the casserole covered over a gentle fire and let the contents braise slowly until the meat juices settle on the bottom in a light brown color
5. Add as much water or better yet bouillon as you wish to have schü, or as the meat requires to be strong and good
6. Remove the foam that rises to the surface and let the schü draw slowly from the side rather than boil, so that it becomes clear
7. Finally add some cleaned celery heads, parsley, yellow root vegetables, some savoy cabbage heads, and a few mace leaves, and let everything cook together until the meat is quite tender
8. Pour the schü through a napkin or fine hair sieve into an earthen vessel and preserve it for use
