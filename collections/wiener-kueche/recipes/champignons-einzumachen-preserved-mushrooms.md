## Ingredients

- mushrooms
- salt
- water
- wine vinegar
- mace
- cloves
- pepper
- bay leaves
- mutton fat

## Directions

1. When the mushrooms are cleaned and the stems cut off, cut the large ones into pieces; the smaller ones remain whole and are washed in cold water.
2. Let water with some salt come to a boil, place the mushrooms in it so they cook a little, then remove them again and drain the water in a colander.
3. While they cool, bring wine vinegar to a boil with some whole mace, cloves, pepper, and bay leaves; when the vinegar boils, add the mushrooms and let them cook only briefly.
4. Place them with the vinegar and spices in preserving jars.
5. On the third day, pour melted fresh mutton fat over them and place the jars in a cool location.
6. When you wish to use some, remove the fat from the top.
7. Alternatively, after the mushrooms are cooked and drained, you may mix them with the spices, placing them in a preserving jar in layers: first a layer of fresh green bay leaves, some crushed pepper, whole mace, then a layer of mushrooms, then again green bay leaves and so forth; let the wine vinegar come to a boil and cool again, then pour it over them and cover them with mutton fat.
