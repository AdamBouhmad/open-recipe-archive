## Ingredients

- 8 decilitres goose fat or butter
- 1 head of finely chopped onion
- crushed garlic if desired
- the cleaned soft roe from the fish
- 2-finger wide and 1-finger long carp pieces
- the fish head, cut into small pieces
- some caraway
- 1 small piece onion
- root vegetables and thyme
- approximately 1 1/2 litres water
- 1/4 litre wine or 3/4 litre water and 1/8 litre vinegar if desired
- 8 decilitres blanched and finely pounded almonds
- white pepper
- salt to taste
- a small piece of sugar if desired

## Directions

1. In a casserole, place 8 decilitres goose fat or butter and a head of finely chopped onion, with crushed garlic and cleaned soft roe from the fish if desired
2. Let this roast a bit, then add the carp pieces cut into 2-finger wide and 1-finger long pieces
3. Let this simmer covered
4. Meanwhile, break the fish head into small pieces and let it cook with some caraway, 1 small piece onion, root vegetables and thyme, and approximately 1 1/2 litres water, and 1/4 litre wine or 3/4 litre water and 1/8 litre vinegar if desired
5. When this is well cooked, pour the broth over the steamed fish and let it come to a boil with 8 decilitres blanched and finely pounded almonds
6. Carefully place the fish pieces on a platter and keep them in a warm place until serving
7. Meanwhile, stir and cook the sauce well, strain it through a sieve into a platter, place it in a casserole and add white pepper, the necessary salt, and a small piece of sugar if desired
8. The sauce must be thick-liquid and piquant
