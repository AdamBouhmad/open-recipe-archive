## Ingredients

- Old quince apples
- Cold water mixed with lemon juice
- Sugar (same weight as the quince pulp)

## Directions

1. Aged quince apples are peeled, cut into 4 parts, freed of the core and immediately put into a cassole in which cold water mixed with lemon juice is found
2. When the apples have been cooked soft in this water, they are taken out with a slotted spoon and passed through a sieve
3. Now put as much finely pounded sugar as the quince pulp weighs into a saucepan and stir it with the passed apples with a wooden spoon on the fire until everything has cooked down into a very thick mass, which is then set aside
4. Arbitrary forms which are brushed with oil and dusted with finely sifted sugar are filled with the solid quince mass, which is pressed in firmly with a spoon dipped in warm water and dried in a cool, open oven for 4 hours
