## Ingredients

- beef tenderloin
- bacon
- salt
- pepper
- shallots
- green parsley
- beef lung pieces
- bacon strips or fat
- ham slices
- 2 small heads of cabbage
- onions
- yellow turnip
- parsley root
- celery
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 clove
- 1 liter white wine
- 1/2 liter broth

## Directions

1. Free the beef tenderloin of veins and skin as with a naturally roasted tenderloin
2. Thread it alternately with bacon strips and beef lung pieces that have been rolled with salt, pepper, crushed shallots, and finely cut green parsley
3. In a casserole, lay bacon strips or fat, optionally some ham slices, 2 small finely cut onions, some yellow turnip, parsley root and celery (one small piece of each), 1 bay leaf, 1 clove and some pepper
4. Place the meat on top and braise covered for approximately 1/2 hour
5. Once it has reduced, add 1 liter white wine and 1/2 liter broth and braise the tenderloin until completely tender
6. Remove to a board, cut into desired pieces, lay on the platter and can be garnished with prepared vegetables
7. Strain the defatted sauce, add broth or wine if necessary according to taste, let it boil somewhat and serve in a sauce boat
