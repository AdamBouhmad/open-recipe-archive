## Ingredients

- Young kohlrabi
- Salt water
- Cooked or stewed veal
- Half a roll soaked in milk
- Green parsley
- Onion, size of a hazelnut
- Scooped out kohlrabi
- Crawfish tails or green peas, as desired
- 2 spoons of cream
- 1 whole egg
- 2 egg yolks
- Salt
- Ground pepper
- A little nutmeg
- 2 spoons of flour
- Warm butter
- Good broth
- 2 spoons of sweet cream

## Directions

1. Cook young kohlrabi in salt water until half soft
2. Cut off the top and hollow them out well
3. For the filling: finely chop a piece of cooked or stewed veal, half a roll soaked in milk, some green parsley, an onion the size of a hazelnut, and some of the scooped out kohlrabi
4. Add crawfish tails or green peas if desired
5. Mix all in a bowl with 2 spoons of cream, 1 whole egg, 2 egg yolks, salt, ground pepper and a little nutmeg
6. Fill the kohlrabi and replace the caps
7. For the butter sauce: let 2 spoons of flour turn light brown in a piece of warm butter, fill with good broth, add ground nutmeg and 2 spoons of sweet cream
8. Place the filled kohlrabi in this sauce, cover with white paper and then an iron lid
9. Cook slowly so they remain white with heat applied above and below
10. Or for brown stewed kohlrabi: heat the butter, add roughly crushed sugar, and when brown add the kohlrabi (previously tied together with string), browning them on all sides
11. Remove them, add yellow turnips and onion pieces to the pan, dust with flour, and let cook well with some broth
12. Place kohlrabi in another pan, strain the sauce over it, place on heat, and when cooked through, arrange on the dish and serve with fried chicken, etc.
