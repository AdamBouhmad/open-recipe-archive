## Ingredients

- A beef brisket of about 4 pounds
- Lard cut into small pieces
- Tongue and anchovies
- Bacon strips
- Bacon sheets for lining
- Onion
- Root vegetables
- A few grains of mixed spices
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 liter white wine
- 1/8 liter soup
- Optional: black bread with 10 crushed beef marrow bones and 3 dried figs
- Optional: 4 shallots
- Optional: 1/4 liter vinegar mixed with 1/8 liter soup instead of wine

## Directions

1. Beat and fold a beef brisket of about 4 pounds
2. Make diagonal cuts with a small knife into which you insert half-finger-length pieces of lard while drawing the knife out of the meat, then alternately insert tongue and anchovies
3. Line the bottom of a casserole with bacon sheets, onion, root vegetables, a few grains of mixed spices and 1 bay leaf
4. Place the meat on top and let it steam covered for about 1 hour while occasionally adding some soup
5. After the liquid has evaporated, add 1 liter of white wine and 1/8 liter of soup and braise for 3 to 4 hours covered
6. Transfer the meat to another casserole, pour the strained sauce over it after removing the fat, and let it stand in a warm place
7. Serve with fine vegetables or macaroni, with the sauce in a sauceboat
8. Alternatively, place the meat whole in the casserole or add black bread with crushed beef marrow bones and dried figs to thicken the sauce, or add 4 shallots from the beginning
9. You may also substitute 1/4 liter vinegar mixed with 1/8 liter soup for the wine
