## Ingredients

- currants (quantity not specified)
- powdered sugar equal in weight to the currant juice

## Directions

1. Pass any quantity of currants through a very clean sieve without first cutting them, working quickly.
2. Place the empty sieve on the scale, weigh it, then fill with the juice from the passed currants and add powdered sugar by weight equal to the juice (for 1 kilogram juice, 1 kilogram powdered sugar).
3. Place this mixture immediately on heat and cook while stirring constantly for 15 minutes.
4. While still hot, fill into jars the same way as the Apricot Preserves.
5. Be very careful that the beautiful red color is not lost through overcooking.
6. If you want the preserves to be even more beautiful and fine, add 1.25 kilograms of sugar for 1 kilogram of juice.
7. Note that the preserves do not thicken while cooking but only gain consistency when they cool in the jars.
8. If white currant preserves have also been made, you can fill the jars with both types, red and white currants, in alternating layers, but you must always let one layer set before adding the next.
