## Ingredients

- large dried peas
- a good piece of bacon
- salt
- fatty meat broth
- fresh bacon for serving
- breadcrumbs
- tender cooked pork from the breast

## Directions

1. Choose large dried peas and fill a pot only half full with them.
2. Add fresh water, a good piece of bacon, and salt.
3. Keep the peas well covered so the husks can be removed.
4. When they are boiled down, add fatty meat broth.
5. When they begin to soften, let them cook completely down.
6. Do not stir them with a spoon so they do not get crushed; instead, shake the pot frequently and finally make a strong fire around it so they dry well.
7. When serving, tip them onto a dish, take a piece of fresh bacon, cut it small, let it turn golden in a pan, sprinkle the peas with breadcrumbs, pour the hot bacon over them.
8. Make small thumb-sized pieces from tender cooked pork breast and arrange them on top of the peas after plating.
9. Butter may be used instead of bacon for rendering. Make holes in the peas here and there so the fat can penetrate better.
