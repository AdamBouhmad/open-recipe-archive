## Ingredients

- Paper
- Writing paper
- Water
- Butter dough, 2 knife-back thickness
- Eggs
- Strong butter dough, knife-back thickness

## Directions

1. Form a ball from paper the size of a small head and cover it with writing paper, then place it on a water-wetted baking sheet.
2. Cut a square of butter dough 2 knife-back thickness in size to match the ball, fold the 4 corners slightly over each other in the center, lay the dough on the paper ball, spread the 4 corners apart at the top and fold them downward onto the baking sheet, then push them together into folds to create a dome-like form which is brushed with beaten eggs.
3. Cut 2 finger-wide and 7.5 cm long ribbon-like strips from strong butter dough, roll one strip together so that one diagonally cut end of the strip protrudes somewhat and the second end is fastened with egg white.
4. Carefully pull up the protruding end slightly and place the rolled band on the midpoint of the dome so that the bands lie on the dome and the protruding end stands upright like a peak.
5. Lay the remaining dough bands on the dome after cutting one end diagonally so that the diagonally cut points always stand free, and lay the bands in small folds down to the edge of the dome, directing them upward and fastening each fold with beaten eggs.
6. Decorate the entire dome in this way with the bands closing at the top of the dome but running apart at the bottom edge at 3 finger-widths distance.
7. Cut the pastry evenly around the edge, brush with beaten eggs, and bake at quite strong heat for 1/2 to 3/4 hours.
8. After half the time, the baking sheet on which the pastry sits must be turned so it gets an even color.
9. When this is done, remove the pastry from the oven, hold it up with the help of two knives while carefully pulling out the paper inside.
10. Place this pastry on a platter on which any meat, poultry, or salmi has been prepared beforehand and serve immediately.
11. Note: before beginning preparation of the pastry, always first check the height of the oven and form the pastry accordingly.
12. If the pastry is prepared for game, serve the sauce in a sauce boat.
