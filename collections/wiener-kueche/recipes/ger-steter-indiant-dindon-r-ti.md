## Ingredients

- Young turkey, plucked, scalded, and cleaned
- Speck (bacon) slices
- Butter
- Watercress
- Salt

## Directions

1. Remove the wings, neck, and feet from the young turkey.
2. Season the turkey inside and out, let it rest for 1 hour, then dress it as described for pheasant (No. 735).
3. Cover it with sliced speck, place on a spit, wrap it in several sheets of paper brushed with butter, and roast over moderate heat for approximately 1 1/2 hours.
4. After this time, remove the paper and roast the turkey over bright heat until it becomes a nice brown color.
5. Alternatively, place the dressed turkey covered with speck in a pan containing fat and roast in the oven.
6. Remove the turkey, cut into pieces, reassemble them, arrange on a platter, and garnish with watercress which is sprinkled with salt and sprinkled with vinegar.
7. A turkey of medium age should, if it is to be roasted, lie in the feathers for several days after the entrails have been removed so that the meat becomes tender.
8. An older turkey can no longer be roasted, but should be braised instead.
