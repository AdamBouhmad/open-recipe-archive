## Ingredients

- goose liver
- bacon
- bread crumbs
- juniper berries
- mushrooms
- egg yolks
- whole egg
- salt
- pepper
- truffles
- smoked tongue
- calf's sweetbread
- aspic
- parsley

## Directions

1. Finely chop 32 decagrams of goose liver and 16 decagrams of bacon.
2. In a mortar, combine with 12 decagrams of bread crumbs softened in milk and well pressed out, 5 juniper berries, 3 pieces of thinly sliced mushrooms roasted in 4 decagrams of butter, 4 egg yolks, and 1 whole egg, some salt and pepper.
3. Pound together and pass through a sieve into a deep bowl.
4. Add 12 decagrams of ground truffles, 12 decagrams of smoked, softly cooked tongue, and if available, 12 decagrams of softly cooked calf's sweetbread.
5. All must be finely diced and well mixed.
6. Fill into a form brushed with butter and dusted with flour.
7. Place a thin layer of bacon on top with a cut made in the middle and cook 2 hours covered in a water bath.
8. Turn out onto a funnel plate and let the form rest over it for another 1.5 hours.
9. Remove the form, clean it, and place in ice.
10. Pour a finger-high layer of cooled aspic into it and let it set.
11. Decorate with finely cut-out truffles, nicely arranged tongue or hard-boiled eggs and some lettuce leaves or parsley sprigs.
12. Pour another finger-high layer of aspic and let cool well.
13. Turn out the cake (which has been trimmed in the meantime) onto it in the same position as it was in the form.
14. Fill the empty space in the middle with aspic, which should also completely cover the cake above.
15. After this has stood 1 hour in ice, hold briefly in warm water, wipe the form, and quickly turn the cake onto a flat platter.
16. Garnish with neatly cut aspic or decorate with colored butter.
17. The cake can also be cut into pieces and these laid in aspic.
