## Ingredients

- fresh green peas that are beginning to thicken
- water
- 2 stalks of leek
- onions
- 1/2 pound butter or beef fat
- flour
- 1 pound lean ham cut into cubes
- beef broth
- handful of fresh spinach
- white bread cut into small cubes
- butter for frying

## Directions

1. Put an appropriate amount of fresh green peas into a casserole, cover with water, add 2 stalks of leek and some onions, and cook until done.
2. When all the water has cooked down, pound the peas in a mortar.
3. Melt 1/2 pound butter or beef fat, stir in one spoon of flour until it foams.
4. Add 1 pound of lean ham cut into cubes, stir the pounded peas through it, and add enough broth so the peas become properly thick.
5. Let this cook together for half an hour.
6. Pound a handful of fresh washed spinach finely in a mortar.
7. When the soup is properly thick and cooked down, strain it through a hair cloth along with the pounded spinach, then pass it through a sieve into a suitable pot.
8. Add necessary salt and keep it hot until serving.
9. Fry white bread cut into small cubes in butter until brown and hard, remove with a skimming spoon, and lay on paper to drain the fat.
10. Serve the soup over the fried bread cubes.
