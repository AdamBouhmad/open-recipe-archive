## Ingredients

- large yellow carrots
- parsnips
- meat broth
- butter or fat
- flour
- French forcemeat made from pork and bacon, or bratwurst
- smoked tongue or ham

## Directions

1. Take large, fine yellow carrots and an equal amount of parsnips, scrape them clean, and cut each kind lengthwise into thin slices.
2. Put them in good meat broth and cook until soft.
3. Strain off the broth and cut more pieces of yellow carrot into small, long pieces like trimmed noodles.
4. Braise these in butter or fat until very soft, dust with a little flour, and add good meat broth or some of the broth in which these roots were cooked.
5. Make a French forcemeat, preferably from pork and bacon. If you don't have this, take good bratwurst, squeeze it out, make it softer with good broth.
6. Take a casserole dish as large as needed, grease it well with fat, line the inside and bottom with the cooked roots, arranging them so that a yellow carrot alternates with a parsnip root, so that a narrow and a wide part meet each other each time.
7. Then it is coated around with this forcemeat finger-thick in layers: each time a layer of the small braised yellow carrots, and again a layer of finely cut smoked tongue or ham, then again a forcemeat, and continue this way until the casserole is full.
8. Cover it again with these roots, and let it bake in a heated oven for one hour.
9. When serving, turn it out onto a platter so it comes out nicely. Strain off the fat and serve it to the table.
