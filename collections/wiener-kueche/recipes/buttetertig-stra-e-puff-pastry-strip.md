## Ingredients

- Puff pastry (No. 1045)
- Beaten eggs

## Directions

1. After the puff pastry (No. 1045) has rested for 1/4 hour, roll it out on a flour-dusted board to the thickness of a small finger and, as desired, cut it round, square, or oval, which should always match the shape of the designated dish.
2. Cut out the center of this pastry sheet so that only a 3-finger wide border remains.
3. Place the cut-out puff pastry on a board moistened with water and brush the pastry with beaten eggs in such a way that a knife-back-wide border remains.
4. The remaining scraps of puff pastry are also rolled out, cut or stamped as desired, and laid in any pattern on the puff pastry ring.
5. Brush these also with beaten eggs, but not the edges.
6. Place the puff pastry ring in the oven and bake it initially at strong heat, later at moderate heat.
7. So that the puff pastry does not become too dark, it can be covered with paper after it turns yellow.
8. The puff pastry is then removed from the oven, placed in the designated dish, and served with a ragout or served two rings stacked on top of each other, make a knife-back-thick cover from the same dough, bake it at the same time next to the 2 rings, arrange the ragout or fricassee in the center, place the baked cover on top, and serve a sauce in a sauce boat.
