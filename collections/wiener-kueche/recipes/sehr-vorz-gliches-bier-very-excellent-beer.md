## Ingredients

- good lager beer brewed in winter
- strong wine spirit
- sugar
- strong sweet wine (2 to 3 bottles per 40 Wiener Maß optional)

## Directions

1. Use a good lager beer that has aged in the cellar until it is free of yeast sediment and is malt-rich and strong enough
2. Fill it into the strongest glass bottles, more slowly than wine and so carefully that foaming is avoided as much as possible
3. Do not fill the bottles completely at once, but leave one fifth space in them
4. When finished with the last bottle, fill the first, then the second and so forth just until three inches of space remains, then stopper them
5. Shortly before filling, rinse the bottles thoroughly with strong wine spirit (pouring the spirit from the first bottle into the second, from the second into the third, etc.) and immediately stopper them firmly
6. Now fill them, but put a large piece of sugar in each bottle
7. The beer becomes even more excellent if you add a strong sweet wine to it - about 2 to 3 bottles per 40 Wiener Maß
8. The stoppers or corks must be particularly good and somewhat larger than the mouth of the bottle
9. Before use, you can tap them moderately with a wooden mallet like coopers use, which makes them soft and allows them to be driven in more easily and deeply
10. Fasten the stoppers around the neck of the bottle with binding twine crossed over if not with wire as with champagne wine
11. Then, so that no air can enter, dip them deep and strongly into a mixture of melted pitch, tallow and rosin
12. Set the bottles in a good cellar in sand on their heads
13. In summer, water the sand daily
14. After some time, fermentation begins in the bottles and when the beer begins to mousse, it can be drunk
15. It will foam and prickle like champagne
