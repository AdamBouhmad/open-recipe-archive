## Ingredients

- bacon or marrow
- onion
- root vegetables
- juniper berries, crushed
- spices (5 grains of each type)
- thyme sprigs
- bay leaf
- sliced black bread (8 ounces)
- red wine (7/8 liter)
- vinegar (7/8 liter)
- broth
- cream (1/4 liter)
- butter (8 ounces)
- flour (8 ounces)
- sugar (2 ounces)
- salt and spices to taste
- puff pastry
- parsley

## Directions

1. Put some bacon or marrow, onion and root vegetables, all finely sliced, into a casserole dish along with 5 crushed juniper berries, 5 grains each of various spices, sprigs of thyme, and 1 bay leaf.
2. Place the prepared duck on top, cover it with bacon slices and roast uncovered.
3. After this, add 8 ounces of finely sliced black bread along with 7/8 liter red wine, 7/8 liter vinegar and broth, so that the liquid barely covers the duck.
4. Let it braise covered until the duck is tender - a young duck will need about 1 hour, an old duck about 2 hours.
5. Remove the meat and place it with the duck into a shallow casserole dish and set it in the oven.
6. After a few minutes, pour 1/4 liter of cream over the duck and let it reduce well.
7. In the meantime, make a fairly dark butter sauce from 8 ounces butter and 8 ounces flour, pour this over the reduced broth, add 2 ounces sugar and necessary seasoning, stir well, bring to a boil again, pass through a sieve and remove the fat.
8. Arrange the carved duck on a platter, pour some sauce over it and serve the rest in a sauce boat.
9. Garnish the duck with puff pastry cut into desired shapes and fresh parsley.
