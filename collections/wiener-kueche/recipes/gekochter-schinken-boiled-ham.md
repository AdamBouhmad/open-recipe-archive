## Ingredients

- 1 ham
- 5 juniper berries, crushed
- 1 bay leaf
- sprigs of thyme
- water

## Directions

1. Scrape the skin off the ham, leaving the ham bone slightly protruding
2. Place the ham in a large casserole with the skin side down
3. Add 5 crushed juniper berries, 1 bay leaf, and some sprigs of thyme
4. Fill the casserole with boiling water and cook the ham slowly and covered
5. If the broth tastes strongly of salt, drain it and replace with boiling water during cooking
6. Once the ham is tender, which can be tested by inserting a fork, remove it and place on a board with the skin side up
7. Cover with another board, weight it down with a heavy weight, and let it cool
8. The next day, remove the weight and board, loosen the skin slightly, replace it, and cut thin slices as needed
9. Arrange on a plate and garnish with chopped aspic, and serve with mustard and horseradish
