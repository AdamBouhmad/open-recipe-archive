## Ingredients

- Old partridges
- Ham slices (12 deciliters)
- Thyme
- Yellow turnips
- Parsley
- Bay leaf
- Salt
- Pepper
- Lard, finely cut
- Soup
- White wine
- Black bread, cut into leaves
- Cream

## Directions

1. Pluck and flame old partridges that have been hung for several days in the game larder
2. Open, salt, dress, and lard them with finely cut lard
3. Line a casserole with ham slices, thyme, yellow turnips and parsley, all cut into leaves, and a bay leaf with some thyme, salt and pepper
4. Place the larded partridges in the casserole
5. Pour in soup and white wine
6. Let them braise slowly covered
7. When the partridges are half-cooked, add finely cut black bread and let it braise together
8. Remove the fat from the sauce and add cream if desired, which must be well incorporated
9. Remove the partridges, cut the binding string, draw it out carefully, divide the partridges lengthwise in the middle, arrange on a platter, cover with the lard slices and pour the defatted strained sauce over them
10. Serve with lentil puree, braised rice, macaroni, polenta or cabbage
