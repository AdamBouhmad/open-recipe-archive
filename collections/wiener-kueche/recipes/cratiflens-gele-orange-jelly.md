## Ingredients

- 4 oranges
- 3 lemons
- 1/4 liter measure
- wine to fill the remaining measure
- 7 stiff isinglass dissolved in 1 liter water
- 40 decagrams refined sugar (approximately 1/2 liter)
- a finger-length piece of orange peel

## Directions

1. Press the juice from 4 oranges and 3 lemons through a cloth and collect the completely clear juice in a 1/4 liter measure, filling the rest with wine.
2. Prepare 7 stiff isinglass by cooking in 1 liter water, then strain and cook together with the juice until reduced to 1/4 liter of liquid.
3. Heat 40 decagrams of refined sugar to which a finger-length piece of orange peel has been added.
4. Cook the isinglass and sugar together for approximately 10 minutes and strain through fine, filtered linen cloth (straining cloth) into a deep bowl.
5. Add the prepared mixture of orange juice, lemon juice and wine (totaling 1/4 liter) to the hot liquid, stir together, add clear lemon juice if necessary.
6. Pour the jelly mass (not exceeding 1 liter) into a mold that has been buried in crushed ice, ensuring it stands straight and level.
7. Cover the filled mold with a lid, place ice over it, cover well with cloth and set in a cold place for 2 to 3 hours.
8. When serving, remove the mold from ice, dip briefly in hot water, dry quickly, place a platter over it, invert the mold and slowly lift it off.
9. The jelly should be clear, transparent and so delicate that the slightest movement causes it to tremble. It should have a pleasant sweet-sour taste and melt in the mouth. Serve immediately to preserve its beauty.
