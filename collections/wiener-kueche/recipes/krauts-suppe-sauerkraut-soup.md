## Ingredients

- Sauerkraut
- 10 tablespoons lard
- 1/8 liter of beef broth
- onion, finely cut
- flour
- 1 liter good beef broth
- approximately 1 liter good broth
- salt and spices
- wine or vinegar as needed
- fried bratwurst
- smoked sausages
- fried goose liver
- eggs (one per person)
- optionally smoked, cooked tongue

## Directions

1. Cook a portion of sauerkraut in water until soft.
2. In a casserole, heat 10 tablespoons of lard; add 1/8 liter of beef broth and finely cut onion, and toast the flour with the onion.
3. Pour 1 liter of good beef broth over this, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
4. Strain the cooked sauerkraut and pass the broth through a sieve.
5. Pour in approximately 1 liter of good broth, stir very well together and let simmer.
6. Add necessary salt and spices.
7. If the sauerkraut is not sufficiently sour, add wine or vinegar as needed so the soup obtains the correct flavor.
8. Once everything is well cooked, add some pieces of fried bratwurst, cut into pieces, some smoked sausages and one small cut, fried goose liver to the soup pot.
9. For as many persons as there are, beat that many eggs: hold a ladle with some broth in it over the simmering soup, beat the eggs into it and let them stay in until they set.
10. Add these to the soup pot containing the sausages and sauerkraut soup.
11. Alternatively, smoked, cooked tongue cut into small pieces can be added to the soup.
12. The sauerkraut soup must be hearty, spicy and flavorful.
13. Serve the groats on a separate dish without garnish with the sauerkraut soup.
