## Ingredients

- Pike or perch flesh, about 1 kilo, cleaned and deboned
- 12 Deca of peeled, leafy-cut rolls, grated in milk
- 14 Deca butter
- 8 egg yolks
- 1 whole egg
- Salt
- A knife-point of white pepper
- A knife-point of nutmeg
- Flour, 1 teaspoon
- Potato flour (if available)

## Directions

1. Take pike or perch flesh, skin it, remove the meat from the bones and cut it finely
2. Cut the peeled, leafy-cut rolls into thin pieces and grate them in milk, then stir them on the fire into a paste
3. Allow this paste to cool, then pound it together with the fish flesh in a mortar
4. Add the butter, egg yolks, and one whole egg, along with salt, then beat it well together
5. Pass it through a hair sieve into a deep bowl
6. Add flour (or potato flour if available), then if desired, a knife-point of white pepper and the same amount of nutmeg
7. Stir it well together and use as needed
8. Can be used on fasting days instead of chicken forcemeat, or on meat days instead of veal forcemeat
