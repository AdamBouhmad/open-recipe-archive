## Ingredients

- 120 pounds apple must
- 3 pounds raw finely crushed cream of tartar
- 15 pounds ripe sloes or 10 pounds dried sloes
- sugar if the must is not sweet enough

## Directions

1. Dissolve 3 pounds raw finely crushed cream of tartar in some must and add this solution to 120 pounds of apple must in a barrel
2. Once fermentation has begun, press 15 pounds of ripe sloes or crush and add 10 pounds of dried sloes to the barrel
3. After fermentation is complete, rack the wine into a clean barrel
4. The cream of tartar should prevent the cider-like taste, and the sloes should give it a similarity to sweet wine
5. If the must is not sweet enough, add sugar
6. The apples should be fresh, without rot and not overripe, nor taken unripe from the tree; they are better if they have lain in heaps in the open air for several days and nights
7. If desired, some quinces may be pressed along with the apples, and a portion of pitted plums may be added
8. Once fermentation is complete, treat the apple must with topping up and racking as with grape must
