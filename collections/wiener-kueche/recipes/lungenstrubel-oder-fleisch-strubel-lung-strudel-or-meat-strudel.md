## Ingredients

- 8 deca lard, butter, or pork fat
- 1/2 teaspoon chopped parsley
- 1 pinch marjoram
- Optional: 1/2 teaspoon finely chopped onion
- 8 deca breadcrumbs
- 62 deca cooked, finely chopped calf or lamb lung
- Salt and spices to taste
- Optional: pepper
- 2 whole eggs
- 1/2 liter flour for the dough
- 1 whole egg for the dough
- Lukewarm water as needed

## Directions

1. Place 8 deca of lard, butter, or pork fat in a casserole, add 1/2 teaspoon of chopped parsley, 1 pinch of marjoram (and if desired, 1/2 teaspoon of finely chopped onion), and roast.
2. Add 8 deca of breadcrumbs.
3. Mix this well together until hot, then place it in a bowl.
4. Add the necessary salt, spices, and if desired, a pinch of pepper.
5. Beat in 2 whole eggs and stir everything together well.
6. For the strudel dough, place 1/2 liter of flour on a board, make a depression in the middle, add 1 pinch of salt, 1 whole egg, and add lukewarm water gradually, working it with the right hand or a broad knife into the flour while supporting with the back of a knife held in the left hand.
7. The dough should stretch easily.
8. Knead it on the board until it separates from your hand.
9. Dust the board with flour, place the dough on it, work it with the palm and fingers until blisters appear on the dough.
10. Place the dough on a flour-dusted board so that the worked side is on the bottom and the smooth, pointed side is on top.
11. Cover it with a deep, warm bowl and let it rest for 1/3 hour.
12. Cover a table with a cloth, dust it well with flour, place the dough in the middle, dust it with flour, and stretch it somewhat with a rolling pin (Strudel roller).
13. Then stretch it out fully by holding both hands so that the thumbs point inward and the dough rests on both index fingers, stretching the dough over the cloth to a certain length.
14. Then lay it on the cloth and stretch it completely thin with your fingertips.
15. Cut away the edges, which never become completely thin, then brush the dough with beaten eggs using a brush and distribute the cold filling evenly along the edge of the dough.
16. Fold the dough over on one side 3 fingers wide, sprinkle this fold with the remaining filling, and roll up the dough together by holding the cloth lightly raised.
17. Dust the strudel lengthwise with flour, divide it with a wooden spoon dipped in water into pieces of desired size (approximately 2-3 fingers wide) so that when pressed up, the filling is pushed back on both sides of the piece and the dough closes.
18. Press it together on both sides with two fingers and the thumb and place it on a flour-dusted board.
19. Place the strudel pieces in simmering salted water and cook covered for 10-12 minutes.
20. Then remove them with a skimmer and place them in the hot soup, which should come to the table immediately covered.
21. Note that the lung strudel, like all cooked strudels that follow, must be served immediately after being removed from the simmering water, as they become firm with longer standing.
