## Ingredients

- Old chicken blood
- Vinegar
- Butter
- Onion (cut into thin leaves)
- Bay leaf
- Thyme (bunches)
- Lemon peel
- Black bread (cut into thin leaves)
- Wine
- Soup broth

## Directions

1. The blood obtained when slaughtering the chicken is mixed with some vinegar and placed in a suitable dish.
2. To make the meat of an old chicken tender, after the entrails are removed and disposed of, let it lie in the feathers for 1-2 days.
3. After this time, pluck it, clean it, and cut it into neat pieces.
4. Into a casserole, which you have previously put fat, 1 tablespoon of thinly sliced onion, 1 bay leaf, some bunches of thyme and lemon peel, lay the cut and salted chicken and let it braise covered.
5. When all the liquid has evaporated, add thinly sliced black bread, pour over the meat with as much wine, vinegar, and broth that the liquid just covers it, and let it braise covered until tender.
6. When this is done, add the blood of the chicken, stir it well together, and let it boil a little.
7. Then remove the meat, place it in a bowl, and pour the strained, defatted sauce over it.
