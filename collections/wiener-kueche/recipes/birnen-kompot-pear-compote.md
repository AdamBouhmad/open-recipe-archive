## Ingredients

- Steifen- or Meusel-Cateller pears
- Spice cloves
- Sugar syrup (made from 60 decigrams of sugar per liter of water, boiled 10-15 minutes and cooled)
- Lemon water (cold)

## Directions

1. Cut the stems of the pears slightly with scissors
2. Wash the pears and prick them five times near the stem with a larding needle, so the point comes out at the blossom end
3. Fill the preserving jars as full as possible with the unpicked pears, placing 1 clove in each jar
4. Pour the cooled cooked sugar syrup over the pears so it completely covers them, but leaving a small finger's breadth of space at the top of the jar
5. Bind the jars with pig's bladders that have been cut dry, washed three times in fresh cold water, and dried, laying the bladder smooth side up on the jar and tying very tightly with thin twine
6. Place all filled jars in a water bath, with each jar either packed in hay or wrapped in cloth so they do not break during cooking
7. Pour enough cold water so the jars stand halfway in it, cover and place on high heat
8. Remove the lid as soon as the water begins to boil so the bladders do not burst
9. Keep the jars in the boiling water for 3/4 hour
10. Remove the water bath from the heat, spread a cloth over it, place a lid on top, and let stand until the next day
11. Remove the jars, wash and dry them, and store in an airy place
