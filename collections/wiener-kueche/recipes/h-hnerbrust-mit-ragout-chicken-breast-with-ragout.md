## Ingredients

- Chicken
- Butter
- Truffles
- Prepared tongue
- Mushrooms (Champignons)
- Cock's combs
- Cock's kidneys
- Marrow knuckles
- Calf's sweetbread (larded and cut into neat pieces)
- Calf's brain
- Calf's head (white and soft-boiled, pieces cut out decoratively)
- Calf or veal bones
- Olives (dissolved from jars)

## Directions

1. Cut the chicken up to the breast, separate the throat and crop with a cloth and pull it out.
2. Cut the skin over the breast, carefully remove it, bend the thighs apart, and loosen the meat of the breast from the breastbone and skeleton with a sharp small knife.
3. From each chicken, two breasts are obtained, each of which has an underbreast attached by a strong tendon.
4. Carefully pull out this tendon, smooth the small breasts with a knife, and place them on a plate.
5. Pound the large breasts with a flat knife and scrape off the fine skin with a sharp knife.
6. Butter a casserole heavily and lay the large and underbreasts next to one another.
7. Cut prepared truffles and cooked tongue into small pieces, cut them out round, fix these with some glaze on the wider side of the breast, and cover with a sheet of paper brushed with butter and fry briefly on one side only over medium heat for a few minutes.
8. When the breasts have a white color, remove them and arrange them in a bowl, the large ones alternately with the small ones, running round.
9. In the middle of the bowl place the following ragout: prepared mushrooms, cock's combs, cock's kidneys, marrow knuckles, calf's sweetbread (larded and cut into neat pieces), truffles, crayfish tails, calf's brain, and decoratively cut-out pieces from a white and soft-boiled calf's head.
10. Mix all this well, place it arranged beautifully in the middle of the bowl, and push the better-looking pieces to the outside.
11. If desired, sprinkle the sauce with pitted olives dissolved from jars.
12. The remaining chicken meat is finely pounded together with the bones and a gravy sauce is prepared from it, some of which is poured over the arranged bowl and the rest is served in a sauceboat.
