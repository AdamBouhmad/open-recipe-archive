## Ingredients

- 3 kilo rye flour
- 1/2 liter sourdough starter
- 1/2 liter lukewarm water
- salt
- caraway, anise, fennel, or coriander seeds (whole or crushed)
- lukewarm water as needed

## Directions

1. First, the so-called sponge must be set: Put 3 kilo rye flour in a large suitable vessel (such as a large bowl, wooden trough or small bread trough) and place it in a warm location several hours earlier
2. Dilute 1/2 liter sourdough starter with 1/2 liter lukewarm water and pour this into the flour in the middle of which a well has been made
3. Mix in enough flour so that this sponge becomes thick, dust it with some flour, cover it and let it stand overnight after setting it up in the evening
4. The next day, mix the necessary salt with caraway or anise, fennel or also coriander, whole or crushed, into the sponge
5. Work this with the flour and the required lukewarm water into a stiff dough for as long as it is fine, that is, knead the dough with both hands until it pulls away from them
6. Once this is achieved, place it covered near the warm stove and let it ferment
