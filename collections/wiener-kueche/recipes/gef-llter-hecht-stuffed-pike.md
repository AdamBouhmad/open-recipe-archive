## Ingredients

- 1 pike
- salt
- water
- 200 grams pike flesh
- 10 decagrams breadcrumbs soaked in milk and squeezed dry
- 6 cleaned and washed crayfish tails
- 1 mushroom
- 5 bunches finely chopped parsley
- 200 grams butter
- 3 whole eggs
- 3 egg yolks
- 140 grams butter
- pepper
- cream (optional)

## Directions

1. Lay the pike on a board. Cut the skin on the back from tail toward head and separate it 2 finger-widths from the head on each side.
2. Carefully pull the skin from the flesh and spread it completely on the board.
3. Remove all flesh from the skeleton using a small knife, taking care not to damage the head and skin.
4. Leave the skeleton covered with spread skin, sprinkle lightly with salt and cover with a cloth dampened in cold water.
5. Make the forcemeat: Finely chop 200 grams pike flesh with 10 decagrams breadcrumbs that have been soaked in milk and pressed dry, 6 cleaned and washed crayfish tails, 1 mushroom that has been roasted with 5 bunches finely chopped parsley in 200 grams butter.
6. Pound this finely in a mortar, then add 3 whole eggs and 3 egg yolks and mix well.
7. Add 140 grams butter and pound together.
8. Press through a sieve, season with salt and pepper, and stir well.
9. Spread this forcemeat on the skeleton, coat well, and draw the skin together on the back.
10. Insert a piece of wood from the mouth to the tail, which must protrude during roasting.
11. Hold the fish by the head and tail and place it in a pan containing 200 grams butter.
12. Brush with melted butter, cover with buttered paper, and roast in a moderate oven, frequently basting with its own juices for approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour.
13. If roasting with cream, after 15 minutes pour 500 milliliters cream over it and continue basting frequently.
14. Garnish with fresh parsley, lemon slices, and serve with potatoes, or arrange on a platter and garnish with forcemeat quenelles, crayfish, crayfish tails, truffles if desired, and fried smelt.
15. Serve with Hollandaise or mustard sauce or butter sauce with lemon juice.
