## Ingredients

- one carp of any desired size with flawless scales
- 2 liters water
- 1.5 liters vinegar
- 1 onion pierced with 1 clove
- 5 sprigs fresh parsley
- pepper
- 2 tablespoons salt
- 0.5 liters boiling vinegar
- green parsley for garnish
- Hollandaise sauce or beaten mustard sauce
- horseradish
- boiled potatoes

## Directions

1. To kill the fish, place it on a table, hold it firmly by the head fins with the left hand and strike a strong blow on the fish's head with the meat hammer in the right hand
2. Cut open the belly, make an incision lengthwise at the head and a deeper incision lengthwise at the tail
3. Clean and wash it internally and leave it unscaled
4. In a fish pan place 2 liters water, 1.5 liters vinegar, an onion pierced with 1 clove, 5 sprigs fresh parsley, some pepper and 2 tablespoons salt
5. Let this cook for approximately 1/4 hour
6. Place the carp on the insert of the fish pan such that the head and tail are straight and the belly is spread out, loosely bind the head and tail and place a suitable piece of wood or a soup spoon in the mouth so it stays open, with a piece protruding so it can be removed when serving
7. Set the carp in the fish pan on the stove and pour 0.5 liters boiling vinegar over it, then carefully add the strained broth along the edge so it does not flow directly onto the carp
8. Let it stand covered for approximately 1 hour, during which time the liquid must always be very hot but must not boil
9. Shortly before serving, remove the insert, untie the twine and carefully transfer the fish to a flat dish or a board lined with parchment paper
10. Garnish the carp with green parsley and parsley can also be placed in the fish's cavity instead of the wood which is removed
11. Serve with Hollandaise sauce or beaten mustard sauce, horseradish and boiled potatoes
