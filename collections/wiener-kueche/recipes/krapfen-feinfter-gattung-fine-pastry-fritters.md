## Ingredients

- 60 Decaliter dried, warm, sifted, fine flour
- 4 Decaliter sugar
- 5 grams salt
- 1 liter milk, divided into 3 equal parts
- 3 Decaliter fresh yeast
- 14 Decaliter melted butter
- 10 egg yolks
- Marmalade or jam for filling
- 6-8 Decaliter beef lard
- 7/8 Decaliter pork lard of better quality
- Vanilla sugar for garnish

## Directions

1. Heat a deep bowl and add 60 Decaliter dried, warm, sifted, fine flour and 4 Decaliter sugar with 5 grams salt.
2. Divide 1 liter of milk into 3 equal parts.
3. In one saucepan, add 1/8 liter lukewarm milk and dissolve 3 Decaliter fresh yeast in it.
4. In a second saucepan, add 1/8 liter lukewarm milk and stir in 14 Decaliter melted butter.
5. In a third saucepan, add 1/8 liter milk and sprinkle 10 egg yolks into it.
6. Make a well in the flour and gradually stir in the yeast dissolved in milk, then sift the milk with eggs through a sieve and add it, finally pour in the milk with butter.
7. Stir constantly while adding the liquids.
8. Beat the mixture into a light, fine dough by stirring everything well together, using a wooden spoon to work the dough mass from the sides, holding the spoon vertically against the bottom and pressing some dough with the back of the spoon against the hands, moving from right to left and from left to right.
9. Continue beating quickly and without interruption until the dough is fine, smooth, and shiny and releases from the spoon.
10. Place half the dough on a flour-dusted board, dust lightly with flour on top, and roll out very lightly with a rolling pin until the dough is about finger-thickness.
11. Mark the Krapfen with a cutter by pressing lightly without cutting through.
12. Place half a teaspoon of marmalade in the center of each marked circle.
13. Cut out round discs with a smaller cutter, place them marked-side up over the marmalade, and press down lightly with fingertips around the filling.
14. Using a slightly smaller cutter, cut out both parts together to form the fritter.
15. Place on a warm, flour-dusted board with the side that was previously on the board now facing up.
16. Cover finished fritters with a warm cloth.
17. Add remaining dough pieces back to the bowl and work in approximately 3 tablespoons of lukewarm milk, then proceed as before.
18. Place fritters three fingers apart on a board, cover with a warm cloth, and let rise in a warm place until doubled in size.
19. Heat 1 kilo beef lard and 7/8 kilo pork lard in a saucepan until very hot.
20. Place the first risen fritters in the hot lard with the lower side facing up, cover the saucepan, and after 2-4 minutes lift the lid.
21. When the lower side turns brown, turn the fritters with a wooden stick, leave the saucepan open, shake it slightly, and blow away the foam that forms so it does not stick to the fritters.
22. The fritters should be light enough to sink only halfway into the lard, which must be maintained at an even frying temperature.
23. Once both sides are baked, which takes 6-8 minutes total, remove with a slotted spoon and place on paper with the last-fried side down.
24. After a short time, transfer to another piece of paper.
25. Just before serving, dust lightly with vanilla sugar and serve warm and heaped on a napkin-lined dish.
26. Dissolved marmalade may be served separately in a sauceboat if desired.
27. This recipe yields approximately 40-50 fritters.
