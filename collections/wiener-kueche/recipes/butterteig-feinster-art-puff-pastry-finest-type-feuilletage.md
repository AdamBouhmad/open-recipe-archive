## Ingredients

- 56 drachm flour
- 56 drachm very cold and firm butter
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 4 egg yolks
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1/4 liter cold water

## Directions

1. Place flour on a pastry board and cut very cold and firm butter into small pieces into one-third of the flour using a knife
2. With a pastry roller, lightly strike until the flour has absorbed the butter and the butter appears flaky
3. Form the butter in an oblong shape, place on a board dusted with flour, cover with a cloth, and set in a cool place or on ice
4. Make a well in the remaining flour on the board, add 1/2 teaspoon salt, 4 egg yolks, and the juice of 1/2 lemon
5. Stir while gradually adding 1/4 liter cold water with the left hand, using the right hand to form a dough
6. Work the dough as you would strudel dough with both hands until blisters appear
7. If the dough contains too much moisture and sticks to the board, dust both hands and board with a little flour, but do not work in too much flour or the dough will become too stiff
8. Once blisters appear, place the dough on a flour-dusted board, dust with flour, and roll out with a pastry roller while the butter is still cold
9. Place the cold butter on top and fold the dough together with the butter on both sides
10. Turn the dough so the open sides are on the right and left, lightly tap the middle of the dough with the pastry roller, and push the butter through by rolling until it reaches the edges
11. Once the dough is rolled out lengthwise into a strip, fold again from right to left so it lies in 3 equal layers on top of each other
12. Place on a flour-dusted sheet pan and let rest covered in a cool place
13. After approximately 1/4 hour, place the dough again on a flour-dusted board
