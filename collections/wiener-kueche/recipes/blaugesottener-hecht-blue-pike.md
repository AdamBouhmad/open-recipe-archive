## Ingredients

- 1 liter water
- 1/2 liter white wine
- 1 Spanish onion studded with 1 clove
- 1 bay leaf
- 5 sprigs thyme
- some rocambole
- 2 tablespoons salt
- pepper

## Directions

1. Prepare a broth from 1 liter water, 1/2 liter white wine, a Spanish onion studded with a clove, 1 bay leaf, 5 sprigs thyme, some rocambole, 2 tablespoons salt and pepper, simmering for 1/4 hour
2. Pour this broth around the edge of the pike as with blue-boiled carp and let stand approximately 1 hour
3. When sufficiently soaked through, carefully transfer the pike to the prepared serving dish
4. Strain 1 1/4 liters of the broth through cloth into a saucepan and pour the remainder over the pike
5. If the pike is not completely covered, dip a piece of linen or white writing paper in the broth and cover the pike with it, leaving a piece of wood in the pike's opening until serving
6. Add 2 ounces of isinglass or 1 1/2 dissolved gelatin to the 1 3/4 liters of broth and cook for 1 hour
7. If the liquid is not clear enough, clarify it with 2 egg whites in the same manner as with aspic
8. When set, pour the broth from the platter on which the pike rests and garnish it beautifully with the prepared, pearled, diced and boiled pike, a part of which can also be colored
9. Place a sprig of green parsley in the pike's opening after removing the wood
10. Any type of fish can be prepared in this manner
