## Ingredients

- Puff pastry (No. 1045)
- Peeled apples, cut into thin slices
- Bread crumbs
- Raisins, pitted
- Powdered sugar, finely ground
- Beaten eggs
- Water

## Directions

1. Roll out the puff pastry (No. 1045), both 'finest' and 'other' kind, on a flour-dusted board knife-back-thick.
2. Cut it into strips which are 3 hand-widths wide, as required by the size of the board on which it will be baked.
3. Lay peeled, thinly sliced apples in the center of this pastry strip, one hand-width wide, along its length.
4. Sprinkle with bread crumbs and pitted raisins and dust heavily with finely ground powdered sugar.
5. Brush the edges of both narrow sides with beaten eggs and fold the empty pastry on both sides over the apples so that the pastry is one finger-width apart.
6. Grasp the strudel at both narrow sides with your hands and lift it as quickly as possible onto a board moistened with water.
7. Make several cuts with a knife along the length on both sides of the pastry, brush with beaten eggs, and if desired lay small pastry strips on top which are also brushed.
8. If you have more than one strudel, place them side by side at three-finger distance apart if the board width permits, lay a moistened wooden spoon or moistened paper between them, and bake at fairly strong heat in the oven for 3/4 to 1 hour.
9. After this time, loosen the baked strudel with a very thin knife, cut it on the board into desired pieces, arrange these elevated on a dish, and serve hot or cold as desired.
