## Ingredients

- 2 cleaned, flamed and prepared chickens
- 5 juniper berries
- stripped thyme
- salt
- whole spices
- 1/2 bay leaf
- lard
- onions
- root vegetables
- 1/4 liter raw wine
- 1/8 liter soup
- 4 deciliter black bread, cut into flakes
- 1/2 liter cream
- lemon juice

## Directions

1. Crush 5 juniper berries, stripped thyme, salt, whole spices and 1/2 bay leaf in a mortar
2. Rub 2 cleaned chickens inside and out with this mixture, dress them, spit them and let rest 2-3 hours
3. Line a casserole with lard, onions, root vegetables, 1/2 bay leaf, the necks and giblets of the chickens
4. When lightly roasted, pour 1/4 liter raw wine and 1/8 liter soup over
5. Place 2 chickens with 4 deciliter black bread cut into flakes inside and braise covered slowly until tender
6. Remove the lard from the liquid, place it in a shallow casserole, lay the chickens on top
7. Pour 1/2 liter cream over them and braise in the oven approximately 10 minutes
8. Remove the chickens, cut into desired pieces and arrange on a platter
9. Defatten and strain the braising liquid, season to taste with salt, add lemon juice, stir well and serve in a sauce boat
10. Garnish the chickens with liver forcemeat (false woodcock forcemeat) made as follows: In 4 deciliter butter or lard, add a knife-tip of finely cut onions, 2 crushed juniper berries, 1 finely cut mushroom, 1 strong pinch of parsley
11. Let this roast lightly, then add the chicken livers cleaned from the gallbladder and a knife-tip of stripped thyme, stir well together and roast
12. Put the roasted mixture in a mortar and mix well with 2 deciliter trimmed bread rolls moistened in water and well pressed out, or black bread
13. Strain through a sieve into a bowl, stir with an egg yolk and season to taste with salt and spices
14. Spread on 2-finger wide and 1-finger long bread slices, place on a sheet for several minutes in the oven until the forcemeat dries slightly
15. If desired, the bread slices may be roasted in butter before spreading with forcemeat
