## Ingredients

- Well-aged, skinned tenderloin roast
- Salt and pepper
- Oil or butter
- Flour
- Fat for frying
- Herb butter, maitre d'hotel butter, or mustard butter
- Lemon slices
- Fried eggs (optional)
- Onions
- Salt and vinegar cucumbers
- Braised cabbage

## Directions

1. Cut two-finger-thick slices from a well-aged, skinned tenderloin roast.
2. Beat these only lightly, press them back together so they have the proper thickness and rounding.
3. Sprinkle them with salt and pepper, drizzle some drops of oil or butter on them, and let them rest in this state for about an hour.
4. Heat fat in a pan until very hot.
5. Dip the beefsteaks very lightly in flour on both sides and fry them on each side 3 minutes, thus 6 minutes total at high heat.
6. They must still be red and bloody when cut.
7. Arrange on a dish and place some herb butter, anchovy butter, or maitre d'hotel butter in the middle of each piece, and add lemon slices.
8. Beefsteaks can be covered with fried eggs when serving; furthermore garnish them with onions, from which you strip the skin with a knife and scrape the onion with it, also with salt and vinegar cucumbers, braised cabbage and the like.
9. Note particularly that the fat for the beefsteaks must be very clean and as hot as possible.
10. You can also fry the beefsteaks without dipping them in flour, but by doing so they will be less juicy, since the flour forms a thick crust which prevents the juice from escaping quickly from the meat.
11. Once the beefsteaks are arranged on the dish, skim the fat from the sauce, add some soup or dissolved aspic to it, and pour this boiled into a sauce boat.
