## Ingredients

- Bacon
- Onions
- Celery root
- Various vegetables, 5 grains each
- 5 crushed juniper berries
- 40 grams pork cutlets
- 1 large or 2 small goose livers
- 1/4 liter white wine
- Equal amount of broth
- Lard
- Salt
- Pepper
- 2 egg yolks
- 10 grams oil
- 1/2 liter melted aspic
- 1 tablespoon French mustard
- Lemon juice or vinegar
- Tongue
- Hard-boiled egg white
- Salad leaves
- Capers

## Directions

1. Roast 3 pieces onion, 1 piece yellow turnip, parsley and celery, all cut into leaves, with cleaned and plucked stomach, crop and liver of the capercaillie, cut into pieces
2. Add 1/2 quart wine, equal amount vinegar and water, 2 bay leaves, 3 sprigs thyme, 7 juniper berries, 5 grains of each spice
3. Boil for approximately 3/4 hours, cool and pour over the dressed capercaillie
4. Cover with bacon strips and buttered paper and let stand 3-4 days in a cool place, turning daily
5. On the second day, remove, cool, place in a casserole with the cooking liquid, cover with bacon strips and braise covered in the oven
6. After some time, add thinly sliced black bread and 5 nutmeg kernels, also some Madeira wine and truffle scraps if available
7. Braise covered until tender, requiring 2-3 hours total
8. Transfer to a platter, remove string, carve neatly and arrange
9. Skim fat from sauce, strain and reduce, add thinly sliced truffles and serve
10. Serve with a Palais-Spöbel, puff pastry or desired compotes
