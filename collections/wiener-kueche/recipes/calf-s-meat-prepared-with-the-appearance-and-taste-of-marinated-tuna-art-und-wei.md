## Ingredients

- White fresh calf's leg
- One quarter pound very dry finely grated salt
- Onions
- Whole parsley
- Thyme
- Bay leaf
- Ginger
- Garlic
- Cranberries
- Pepper
- Three washed finely pounded anchovies
- Good white wine
- Fine oil

## Directions

1. Take a white fresh calf's leg and cut out the inner part (the fricandeaux) as large as possible.
2. Let it stand for a couple of days in a cool place to tenderize; in summer no longer than one day.
3. Remove the skin and sinews and divide the piece into four parts.
4. Have ready a quarter pound of very dry finely grated salt and rub the calf pieces thoroughly with it as in the preparation of salted beef.
5. Place it in a stone pot, add sliced onions, whole parsley, thyme, a bay leaf, ginger, garlic, cranberries, pepper, and three washed finely pounded anchovies.
6. Mix everything well together and bind with a cloth.
7. After four days, turn it over and let it stand bound for another four days.
8. Then pour off half the liquid and put the other half with the calf pieces and other ingredients into a casserole.
9. Add a bottle of good white wine and let it cook slowly over gentle heat.
10. When it is tender, set it aside, place it in the same terrine or pot it was in before, and put it in a cool place to marinate well.
11. The next day, place the meat on a hair sieve to drain, then into a clean terrine or glass, and pour in enough fine oil to cover it completely.
12. Finally bind it well with a bladder.
