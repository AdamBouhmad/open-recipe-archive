## Ingredients

- Fifteen large water chestnuts
- three quarters pound of sweet potatoes
- three tablespoonfuls of vinegar
- three quarters pound of sugar
- one half pint of mirin sauce
- salt

## Directions

1. Peel the skin from fifteen large water chestnuts and slice them very thin
2. Boil in lukewarm water for about fifteen minutes
3. Peel the sweet potatoes, cut in slices, and dip in ice-cold water three or four times, changing the water with each dipping
4. Boil about one quart in an enameled saucepan, add the vinegar, then throw in the sweet potatoes
5. Cover, and boil for about twenty minutes
6. If tender, drain off all the water and add the sugar, with five tablespoonfuls of warm water and also the mirin sauce
7. Add a pinch of salt
8. Place over the fire and add the above prepared water chestnuts
9. When the water chestnuts have thoroughly absorbed the juice, add the strained sweet potatoes, stir with wooden spoon, taking care not to burn it, and boil until it is firm
10. Serve hot or cold
