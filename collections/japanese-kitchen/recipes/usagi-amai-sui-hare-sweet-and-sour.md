## Ingredients

- One good-sized rabbit or hare
- one cupful of vinegar
- one cupful of syou sauce
- one tablespoonful of salt
- three tablespoonfuls of sugar
- one half cupful of mirin sauce
- one tablespoonful of mixed spices
- one half pound of fat fresh pork
- one dozen red plums
- one small lime or lemon

## Directions

1. Take a small hare or large rabbit that has been hanging for at least two days, and after it has been skinned, and the insides removed, wash well in cold water.
2. Cut in quarters and put in a deep dish, covering it well with the vinegar, sugar, salt, and so on, mixed with water.
3. Add the spices also, and a quarter of a cupful of mirin sauce.
4. Cover up well, and let it pickle in a cool place for two or three days.
5. Remove it from the liquid and cut in small pieces.
6. Have ready the fat pork cut in small pieces, and fry.
7. Toss in the hare, and brown.
8. Have ready a covered China cook dish or casserole, and place in it the hare and pork, adding the syou sauce, a pinch of salt, pinch of the spices, and the quarter cupful of mirin sauce.
9. Cover up tightly, and cook in a slow oven for one hour.
10. Then prepare a dozen large red plums, removing the stones, and add them.
11. Dissolve one teaspoonful of Kanton and mix with the gravy, taking care not to break the plums.
12. Cook for another five minutes, then place on a hot platter, decorated with slices of lemon or limes, with a plum placed on top of each slice.
13. This is good served either with white-bean cakes or rice.
