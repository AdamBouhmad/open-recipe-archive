## Ingredients

- One good pheasant
- one pound of small Japanese almonds
- one and one half cupfuls of rice paste
- three cupfuls of mirin sauce
- one half cupful of cooking sauce
- one tablespoonful of sugar
- salt, pepper, and mixed spices
- oil

## Directions

1. Wash and singe the bird, then rub well with salt.
2. Place in deep dish and cover with three cupfuls of mirin sauce; more, if that quantity does not cover the bird.
3. Lay away in a cool place over night, then drain off all liquid.
4. Have ready the almonds, blanched and chopped very fine.
5. Add them to the rice paste with the sugar, a little salt, quarter of a cup of cooking sauce, and a pinch of mixed spices.
6. Stir all together, and stuff the pheasant with this mixture.
7. Sew up, and truss.
8. Have ready boiling hot olive oil or goma-seed oil, pour it over the pheasant, and place in a hot oven.
9. Roast until tender (about one hour), adding little by little the rest of the cooking sauce, and basting frequently.
10. Cook for a half-hour longer, then place on hot platter and garnish with flower petals and water cress.
