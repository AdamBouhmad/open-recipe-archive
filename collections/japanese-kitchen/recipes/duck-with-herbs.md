## Ingredients

- Three to four pound duck
- One quarter pound of pork fat
- One onion
- Two stalks of celery
- One lemon peeling
- One quarter clove of garlic
- Two tablespoonfuls of syou
- One tablespoonful of mixed spices
- One cup of stock
- One dozen mushrooms
- One teaspoonful of salt
- One tablespoonful of parsley

## Directions

1. Drain and singe the duck.
2. Wipe, then thoroughly dry, inside and out, with a clean, damp cloth.
3. Line a pan with small pieces of pork (fat).
4. Sprinkle the bottom with minced onion, celery, grated lemon peel, and the garlic, grated fine.
5. Lay the duck in and cover with a tablespoonful of mixed spices, two tablespoonfuls of syou, and a cup of rich stock.
6. Set over the fire and simmer for two hours, basting often.
7. Wash the mushrooms and turn them in, and cook all together for ten minutes.
8. Take up, and thicken the gravy with cornstarch or rice flour.
9. Lastly, toss in the chopped parsley.
10. Cut up the duck in small pieces (not too small), arrange on dish, pour the gravy with the herbs around the duck, and serve hot with rice.
