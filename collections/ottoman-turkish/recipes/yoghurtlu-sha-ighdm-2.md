## Ingredients

- four or five turnips
- a little salt
- flour
- fresh butter
- half a pint of broth (No. 1)
- curd
- a pinch of pepper

## Directions

1. Peel four or five turnips, cut them in slices about a quarter or a half an inch thick
2. Boil them in water with a little salt, partly tender, and let them drain
3. When free from water, dip them in flour, and fry them a nice brown in fresh butter
4. Lay them in a baking-pan or a pie-dish that will bear fire
5. Pour half a pint of broth (No. 1) over the turnips
6. Set the pan on a moderate charcoal fire, and let it simmer for eight or ten minutes
7. Take it off, and lay some curd over the turnips, over which sprinkle a little hot fresh butter, and a pinch of pepper, and serve hot
