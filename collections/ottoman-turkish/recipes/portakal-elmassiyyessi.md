## Ingredients

- six nice oranges
- five or six ounces of powdered sugar
- half a pint of water
- a small teacup of water
- an ounce of isinglass
- a few drops of prepared cochineal

## Directions

1. Get six nice oranges, take off the rind of one of them, and remove the pith, place them in a basin, and squeeze the juice of the oranges in it
2. Put five or six ounces of powdered sugar in a saucepan with half a pint of water, and boil it until it becomes a syrup
3. Take it off, and add the juice and the rind of the oranges; put the saucepan again on the fire with the cover over
4. As soon as it begins to boil, skim it well, and add gradually a small teacup of water; let it boil one or two minutes longer
5. Add an ounce of isinglass, dissolved as directed; stir well, and pass it through a jelly-bag till clear
6. Add a few drops of prepared cochineal, and pour it in a mould; place it on ice until firm
7. Turn it out as before, and serve
