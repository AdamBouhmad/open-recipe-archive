## Ingredients

- white of forty eggs
- three-quarters of a pound of ground rice
- a few drops of the essence of rose, or any other essence
- five or six pints of milk
- a tablespoonful of the mixture
- sugar and water for syrup

## Directions

1. Put in a basin the white of forty eggs, and beat them up until they are formed into a firm froth
2. Add by degrees three-quarters of a pound of ground rice, with a few drops of the essence of rose, or any other essence you fancy
3. Beat it up again for some time
4. Put into a saucepan five or six pints of milk, and set it on a charcoal fire
5. When boiling, throw in a tablespoonful of the mixture at a time, which will be done in a few minutes
6. Take it out with a hand-strainer, and throw it into boiling syrup, which you have previously prepared, of sugar and water
7. Take it out again with the hand-strainer after a few seconds, and place it in a dish
8. Continue in this manner until the whole is done, and serve
