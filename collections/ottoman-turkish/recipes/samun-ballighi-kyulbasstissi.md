## Ingredients

- salmon
- salt
- bay-leaves
- lemon
- nuts or almonds
- garlic
- bread-crumb
- olive oil
- wine vinegar
- water

## Directions

1. Cut from the tail end of a small salmon as many thin slices as are wanted, and rub them with a little salt
2. Cover a gridiron with some bay-leaves, lay the slices of fish over, set it on a moderate charcoal fire
3. When nicely browned on both sides, dish it up
4. For sauce: crack four or five dozen of nuts or almonds, skin them, then pound them well in a mortar with one or two cloves of garlic peeled
5. Add a piece of bread-crumb the size of a large egg, previously soaked in water, a little salt, two or three teaspoonfuls of olive oil
6. Pound them altogether till they form a paste, then moisten it with some lemon-juice or wine vinegar, and some water, to form a smooth sauce, but not too thick
7. Pour it over the fish and serve hot
