## Ingredients

- flour
- salt
- eggs
- fresh butter
- minced meat or cheese

## Directions

1. Put on the dresser, or in a basin, sufficient quantity of flour, with a little salt
2. Mix it up with as many eggs as it will take, and form it a softish flexible paste
3. Cut it in twelve or fourteen pieces, and roll them out the size of the baking-tin intended for laying them in
4. Fill a saucepan or iron pot with water, put in sufficient salt, and set it on the fire
5. When boiling, scald the pieces of paste one by one, except two
6. When done, drain them, then dry them with a piece of clean cloth, and spread out the wrinkled parts carefully
7. Put some fresh butter in a saucepan, and set it on the fire
8. When the butter is at proper heat, take it off, and when rather cold, butter the baking-tin with it
9. In the baking-tin lay one of the two unscalded pieces of paste, sprinkle a tablespoonful of the butter over
10. Lay half of the scalded pieces, sprinkling between each one a spoonful of butter
11. Lay minced meat or cheese all over, half an inch thick
12. Cover it with the remaining pieces of paste that are scalded, sprinkling some butter between each one as before
13. Lay the other unscalded piece over, well butter the top, and place it in a hot oven
14. When nicely browned, cut it in any shape you choose, dish up, and serve hot
