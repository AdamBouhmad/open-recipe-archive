## Ingredients

- two or three lambs' or sheeps' heads
- water
- sufficient salt
- a quarter of a pound of fresh butter
- a little pepper
- ground cinnamon
- half a dozen pieces of gum-mastic
- a pound and a half of rice, well washed

## Directions

1. Get two or three lambs' or sheeps' heads, wash well, and place them in a stewpan nearly full of water, add sufficient salt, and put the pan on the fire.
2. When boiling, skim and simmer until the meat comes off the heads.
3. Cut them open, take out the brains, put them in a basin, and beat them up with a little broth of the heads to the thickness of a thick cream.
4. Pass the broth through a sieve, measure three pints of it into a saucepan, add a quarter of a pound of fresh butter if it is not rich, a little pepper, ground cinnamon, and half a dozen pieces of gum-mastic, and set the pan on the fire.
5. As soon as boiling commences, add a pound and a half of rice well washed, and let it simmer until the liquor is absorbed.
6. Take it off, and place it on the hob for ten minutes or so, then dish it up in the shape of a dome, spread the brain all over, then brown it nicely in the oven, and serve.
