## Ingredients

- rump-steak
- string
- garlic
- fresh butter
- potatoes
- ripe tomatoes
- water
- salt
- pepper

## Directions

1. Cut five or six pounds of rump-steak into rather long slices, half or three-quarters of an inch thick
2. Tie them together one over the other with a piece of string, as if one piece of meat
3. With the point of a knife, make about fifteen holes here and there, and stick in each a clove of garlic peeled
4. Place it in a stewpan with about three-quarters of a pound of fresh butter, set it on a moderate charcoal fire with the cover over for about three hours, turning the meat every half hour
5. Add two or three pounds of sliced potatoes partly fried in butter, and the juice of two or three pounds of ripe tomatoes with a little water, and sufficient salt and pepper
6. Put the cover over the pan and let it simmer until the meat and the potatoes are well done
7. Cut the string off the meat, dish up tastefully, and serve
