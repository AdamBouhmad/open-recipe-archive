## Ingredients

- leg or any other part of lamb
- curd
- fresh butter
- onions
- salt
- pepper
- water
- lamb liver
- lamb heart
- pistachios
- currants
- mixed spice
- broth
- rice

## Directions

1. Cut as much as is wanted of a leg or any other part of lamb in pieces the size of kidneys
2. Dip each piece in curd, and fry them a nice brown in fresh butter
3. Place them in a stewpan with two or three sliced onions fried in butter, sufficient salt and pepper
4. Cover them with hot or cold water, set it on a charcoal fire, and let it simmer gently until it forms a nice thick gravy at the bottom of the stewpan
5. While the meat is cooking, chop up the liver and the heart of a lamb together, then fry it in fresh butter with a little salt
6. When nicely done, add a handful of pistachios skinned, a tablespoonful of currants, and a little mixed spice
7. Stir it with a wooden spoon for five minutes or so
8. Place it in a saucepan, with some of the gravy of the lamb, and two pints of broth
9. Set it on a charcoal fire with the cover over
10. When boiling, add a pound of rice well washed, stir it, put the cover over, and let it boil until each grain is rather soft, but separate
11. Take it off, wrap round the lid a wet cloth, to prevent the steam from escaping, and put it by the side of the fire for twenty minutes or so
12. Dish it up in the form of a dome, arrange the pieces of lamb over tastefully, and serve hot
