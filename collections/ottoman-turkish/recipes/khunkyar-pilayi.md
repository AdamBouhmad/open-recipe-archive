## Ingredients

- two pounds of the best rice
- two pounds and a half of choice mutton
- four pints of water
- salt
- half a pound of fresh butter
- three pints of liquor
- three or four tablespoonfuls of pistachios
- one or two tablespoonfuls of minced raw mutton
- one or two tablespoonfuls of currants
- half a teaspoonful of mixed spice

## Directions

1. Wash two pounds of the best rice, put it in a clean cloth, tie it, and hang it up
2. Cut two pounds and a half of the choice part of mutton in pieces about the size of walnuts
3. Wash and place them in a stewpan with four pints of water, and put it on a charcoal fire
4. When boiling, skim it well, add sufficient salt, and let it simmer for twenty or thirty minutes
5. Drain the liquor into a basin, put half a pound of fresh butter over the pieces of meat, and fry them a nice brown
6. Add three pints of the liquor, with a little salt, three or four tablespoonfuls of pistachios, the same of minced raw mutton previously fried a nice colour in fresh butter, one or two of currants, and half a teaspoonful of mixed spice
7. As soon as it boils, add the rice, and boil it for two or three minutes longer
8. Reduce the heat of the fire by removing the burning coals, and leaving only the ashes and the sparks, put on the pan with the cover over for about an hour
9. Dish up tastefully, and serve
