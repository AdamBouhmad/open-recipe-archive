## Ingredients

- half a dozen rather thin and long egg-plants
- olive oil or fresh butter
- four or five ounces
- three or four onions
- half a pound of well-washed rice
- half a teacup of hot water
- half a white plain biscuit
- two ounces of skinned pistachios
- one ounce of currants
- salt
- pepper
- mixed spice
- rib-bones of mutton

## Directions

1. Peel egg-plants very thin in spots here and there (imitating the fish scales)
2. Cut off the tops, as large as a florin, and take out the whole of the inside, leaving only sufficient to bear stuffing
3. Throw them in salt water for ten or fifteen minutes
4. Put four or five ounces of nice olive oil or fresh butter in a saucepan, and set it on the fire
5. When hot, add three or four onions chopped fine, and begin stirring it with a wooden spoon
6. When on the point of browning, add half a pound of well-washed rice, and keep stirring
7. As soon as the rice begins to brown, pour in half a teacup of hot water, and continue stirring
8. When the water is absorbed, add another half a teacup of water, and keep stirring
9. When the rice is partly soft, add the half of a white plain biscuit, well powdered, two ounces of skinned pistachios, one of currants, sufficient salt, pepper, and a little mixed spice; stir well; then take it off
10. When partly cold, stuff the egg-plants with it, and cover them with the tops contrariwise
11. Cover the bottom of a saucepan with some rib-bones of mutton, and place the egg-plants over
12. Cover them with water, set the pan on the fire with the lid over, and let it simmer till the egg-plants are partly soft
13. Take them out and fry them a nice colour in hot olive oil or fresh butter
14. Dish them up tastefully, and serve
