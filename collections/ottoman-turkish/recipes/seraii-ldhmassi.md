## Ingredients

- half a pound of plain white biscuit
- whites of twenty eggs
- fresh butter
- a pound and a quarter of loaf sugar
- water
- prepared cochineal
- essence of orange-flower or rose

## Directions

1. Pound half a pound of plain white biscuit in a mortar and sift it through a fine sieve
2. Beat up the whites of twenty eggs in a basin
3. Add by degrees the powdered biscuit and beat it up together until it forms a smooth consistency
4. Take it up a dessertspoonful at a time and pour it into boiling fresh butter, one after the other
5. Fry them a nice delicate brown until they are round and swell up to the size of a peach
6. When done, drain and place them in a stewpan half full of boiling water
7. Let them boil for ten minutes
8. Take it off and strain the water from them
9. Dissolve a pound and a quarter of loaf sugar in as much water as will cover them
10. Add a few drops of prepared cochineal and a few drops of the essence of orange-flower or rose
11. Stir it well and pour it over them
12. Set the pan on a charcoal fire with the cover over and let it boil until the syrup is nearly absorbed
13. Dish up and serve hot or cold
