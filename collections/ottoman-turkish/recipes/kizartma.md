## Ingredients

- leg or sirloin of mutton
- salt
- water
- curd
- fresh butter
- onions

## Directions

1. Cut as much as is required of a leg or sirloin of mutton in pieces the size of large eggs
2. Place them in a saucepan with sufficient salt, and cover them with water
3. Set it on the fire, and let it boil for twenty or thirty minutes
4. Skim it well
5. Take out the pieces of meat, and rub them all over with curd
6. Fry them a nice brown in fresh butter
7. Place them in a saucepan with sufficient onions, sliced thin and fried a nice brown in fresh butter
8. Add the liquor in which the pieces of mutton were boiled
9. Put the cover over the pan, set it on a moderate charcoal fire until it forms a nice thick gravy at the bottom, and the meat is tender
10. Dish it up, and serve
