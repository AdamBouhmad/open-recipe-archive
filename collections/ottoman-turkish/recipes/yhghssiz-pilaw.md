## Ingredients

- one or two fat fowls
- water
- sufficient salt
- two measures of the liquor to one of well-washed rice
- two or three pieces of cinnamon
- a few pieces of gum-mastic

## Directions

1. Put one or two fat fowls in a saucepan nearly full of water, and sufficient salt, and put it on the fire.
2. When boiling, skim and simmer until the meat separates from the bones.
3. Take it off, and pass the whole through a clean cloth or sieve by pressing and squeezing it with your hand, and cast the residue away.
4. Put in the saucepan two measures of the liquor to one of well-washed rice, add two or three pieces of cinnamon, and a few pieces of gum-mastic.
5. Set it on a charcoal fire till the liquor is absorbed.
6. Take it off, and place it on the hob, stir it once round with a fork, and let it remain covered for fifteen minutes or so.
7. Take out the pieces of cinnamon, dish up, and serve hot.
