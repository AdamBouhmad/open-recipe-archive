## Ingredients

- about an ounce of isinglass
- water
- five lemons
- half a pound of loaf sugar
- a pint of water
- three wine-glasses of water
- two tablespoonfuls of rose-water

## Directions

1. Dissolve about an ounce of isinglass in a small tumbler of water, boil and reduce it to half, then pass it through a cloth into a basin
2. Cut in two five lemons, take off the rind of one of them and remove the pith, place them in a basin, and squeeze the juice of the lemons into it
3. Put half a pound of loaf sugar in a stewpan with a pint of water, set it to boil until it becomes a syrup, then take it off and add the juice and the rind of the lemons
4. Put the stewpan again on the fire with the cover over
5. As soon as it begins to boil, skim it well, and add three wine-glasses of water by degrees, which will assist its clarification
6. Let it boil one or two minutes longer, then add the isinglass, pass it through a jelly-bag if fancied, add two tablespoonfuls of rose-water, and then pour it into a mould, and place it on ice
7. When ready, dip the mould in warm water and turn it out
