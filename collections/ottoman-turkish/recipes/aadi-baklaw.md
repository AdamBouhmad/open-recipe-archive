## Ingredients

- paste as No. 86
- wheat starch
- fresh butter
- pounded almonds, pistachios, or filberts
- half a pound or a little more of scalded fresh butter
- a pound of nice honey or loaf-sugar
- two pints of water
- powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Make sufficient paste as No. 86
2. Divide it into fifty pieces the size of walnuts, sprinkle a little wheat starch under and over, and roll them out with a rolling-pin as thin as tissue-paper, quite round, and the size of the baking-tin you intend to lay them in
3. Butter the tin, in which lay five of the pieces of paste one over the other
4. Sprinkle a little fresh butter over the top one, and lay five more pieces over
5. Sprinkle either pounded almonds, pistachios, or filberts, all over, and lay five more pieces of paste over
6. Butter again, and lay five more pieces of paste
7. Sprinkle either of the above ingredients, and lay five more pieces of paste over, butter again, and so forth, until you have laid all the pieces of paste
8. Cut it up in diamonds, pour about half a pound or a little more of scalded fresh butter all over, and bake it a nice colour in a hot oven
9. While it is baking, put a pound of nice honey or loaf-sugar into a saucepan, with two pints of water, and set it to boil until it becomes a syrup
10. When the paste is nicely browned, pour it gently all over, and let it remain for five minutes or so
11. Dish up in pyramid, sprinkle some powdered sugar over, and serve hot or cold
