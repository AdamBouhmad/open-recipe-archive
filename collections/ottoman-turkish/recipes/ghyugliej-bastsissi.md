## Ingredients

- three or four pounds of loin of mutton
- one or two egg-plants
- salt
- six ounces of French beans trimmed
- about sixty okras
- one or two vegetable marrows, peeled
- four or five green tomatos
- five or six onions
- pepper
- juice of one or two lemons
- juice of a pound and a half of tomatos
- a quarter of a pound of fresh butter, melted
- flour paste
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Cut three or four pounds of loin of mutton in pieces the size of walnuts; wash and place them in an earthen stewpan, of which the lid must closely fit
2. Cut one or two egg-plants in four crossways, and cut them in slices an inch thick; sprinkle some salt over, and when the bitter juice is drawn from them, lay them over the meat
3. Add six ounces of French beans trimmed, about sixty okras, the stalks and the ends cut off and stringed, one or two vegetable marrows, peeled and cut in slices as the egg-plants, four or five green tomatos cut in four crossways, five or six onions peeled and cut as the tomatos, sufficient salt and pepper, the juice of one or two lemons, and the juice of a pound and a half of tomatos
4. If the meat is not sufficiently fat, add a quarter of a pound of fresh butter, melted
5. Put the lid over the pan, and cement round the edges with flour paste, so as to prevent the steam from escaping
6. Put it in a moderately hot oven for about three hours
7. When done, shake the pan gently, that the gravy may mix with the vegetables, and form a nice, thick sauce, then dish up, sprinkle a little cinnamon over, and serve
