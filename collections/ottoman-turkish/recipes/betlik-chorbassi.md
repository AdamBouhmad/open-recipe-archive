## Ingredients

- three pounds of very fresh grey mullet
- salt
- six ounces of the best olive oil
- one pound and four ounces of finely-chopped onions
- handful of washed parsley
- handful of mint
- one pint of the best wine vinegar
- two or three pints of hot or cold water
- yolks of three or four eggs
- juice of one or two lemons
- half a teacup of water
- about two drachms of saffron
- cinnamon
- cayenne pepper

## Directions

1. Procure three pounds of very fresh grey mullet, clean it well, and sprinkle a little salt over it
2. Put six ounces of the best olive oil in a stewpan, with one pound and four ounces of finely-chopped onions, and sufficient salt
3. Put the pan on the fire till the onions are partly browned
4. Add a handful of washed parsley, and the same of mint, both chopped fine, and fry them a little together with the onions
5. Add one pint of the best wine vinegar, and two or three pints of hot or cold water, and boil it for twenty or thirty minutes
6. Pass the liquor through a cloth or sieve into another stewpan, and place the fish in it
7. Put it on the fire and let it simmer till the fish is done
8. Take it out and extract all the bones, cut the fish in pieces and put them into the tureen
9. Pass the liquor again through a cloth into a stewpan
10. Put in a basin the yolks of three or four eggs, the juice of one or two lemons, and half a teacup of water previously coloured with about two drachms of saffron
11. Beat up all together, and add it gradually to the liquor
12. Set it on a moderate fire for ten or fifteen minutes, stirring it all the time with a wooden spoon, and without permitting it to boil
13. Pour it over the fish, sprinkle a little cinnamon and a pinch of cayenne pepper over, and serve
