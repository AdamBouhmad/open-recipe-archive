## Ingredients

- one pound and a quarter of fresh butter
- six eggs
- two pounds and a half of the best honey or powdered sugar
- about two pounds and a half of nice sago
- five or six ounces of loaf-sugar
- about three pints of water
- half a teacup of rose-water
- powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Prepare a pound and a quarter of fresh butter, break six eggs over, and beat up well together
2. Add gradually two pounds and a half of the best honey or powdered sugar, previously made into a syrup, and beat them up well
3. Add by degrees about two pounds and a half of nice sago, bringing it to the consistency of a thick batter
4. Pour it in a rather deep baking-tin, previously buttered
5. Bake it a nice brown in a hot oven
6. Dissolve five or six ounces of loaf-sugar in about three pints of water, with half a teacup of rose-water, and keep it lukewarm
7. When the pudding is nicely done, pour it all over, and put it again in the oven
8. When the whole of the liquid is absorbed, take it out, cut it up in any shape you fancy, dish up, and serve with powdered sugar over
