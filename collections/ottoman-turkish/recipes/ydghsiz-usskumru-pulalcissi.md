## Ingredients

- seven or eight mackerel
- picked parsley
- powdered gum-mastic
- salt
- pepper
- three-quarters of a pint of water
- half or whole of a lemon

## Directions

1. Take out the insides and the gills of seven or eight mackerel, wash them, and cut off the points of the heads and the tails
2. Lay some picked parsley on the bottom of a stewpan, place half of the fish over
3. Sprinkle a little powdered gum-mastic over, some parsley again
4. Lay the other half of the fish over, gum-mastic again, then parsley on the top
5. Add sufficient salt and pepper, and three-quarters of a pint of water
6. Put the pan on a charcoal fire, and let simmer gently for twenty minutes or so
7. Dish it up, squeeze half or the whole of a lemon over, and serve
