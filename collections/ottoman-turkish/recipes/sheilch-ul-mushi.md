## Ingredients

- six or eight egg-plants, not seeded
- two or three ounces of fresh butter
- one or two pounds of minced raw mutton
- three or four onions, finely chopped
- two or three tablespoonfuls of parsley, chopped fine
- two or three tablespoonfuls of mint
- salt and pepper
- parsley stalks
- peeled parsley roots
- mutton broth or broth (No. 1)
- curd, flavoured with a little garlic

## Directions

1. Cut in four crossways, without separating from the stalk, six or eight egg-plants, not seeded
2. Cut out part of the inside of each one, and throw it away, and put the egg-plants in brine
3. Put in a fryingpan two or three ounces of fresh butter, one or two pounds of minced raw mutton, three or four finely chopped onions, and fry them a nice brown
4. Add two or three tablespoonfuls of parsley, chopped fine, the same of mint, sufficient salt and pepper, and mix them well together
5. Stuff the egg-plants with this, and tie them up separately with the stalks of parsley
6. Cover the bottom of a stewpan with some peeled parsley roots, in which lay the stuffed egg-plants, and cover them with mutton broth, or with the broth (No. 1)
7. Set the pan on the fire with the cover over
8. When boiling, let it simmer until a nice thick gravy is formed at the bottom
9. Dish them up tastefully one over the other, and serve, or dress them with some curd, flavoured with a little garlic, if approved of, then serve
