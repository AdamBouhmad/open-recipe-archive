## Ingredients

- five or six pounds of salmon or swordfish
- salt
- olive-oil
- three pints of wine vinegar
- half a teacup of saffron-water
- one or two tablespoonfuls of powdered sugar
- eight or ten cloves of garlic
- bay-leaves
- myrtle-leaves
- mixed spice
- washed peppercorns
- skinned pistachios

## Directions

1. Cut fish in slices three-quarters of an inch thick
2. Wash and dry them well in a cloth
3. Sprinkle them with sufficient salt
4. Fry them a nice brown in olive-oil
5. Pour three pints of wine vinegar in a stewpan with half a teacup of saffron-water and one or two tablespoonfuls of powdered sugar
6. Stir it round and partly boil it
7. Peel eight or ten cloves of garlic and cut them in pieces the size of pistachios
8. Lay some bay-leaves and myrtle-leaves on the bottom of a stone jar
9. Place some of the slices of fish over
10. Sprinkle some mixed spice over them with some pieces of garlic, washed peppercorns, and skinned pistachios
11. Lay bay-leaves and myrtle-leaves, then fish, then spice, garlic, pistachios, and peppercorns until the jar is full with bay-leaves and myrtle-leaves on the top
12. Pour the boiled vinegar until the fish is covered
13. Place a piece of clean board or a plate over with an iron weight on top to press it
14. Cover the jar and let it remain for three days before using
