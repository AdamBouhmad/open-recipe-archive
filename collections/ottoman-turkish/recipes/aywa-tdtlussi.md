## Ingredients

- Five or six quinces
- A pint of syrup
- Prepared cochineal
- Cloves

## Directions

1. Peel five or six quinces
2. Remove the cores with a scoop
3. Ornament the exterior with a sharp instrument
4. As you finish them put them in cold water
5. When all is ready, scald them
6. Take them out and arrange them in another stewpan
7. Pour over a pint of syrup
8. Put the cores with the pips in a small saucepan with part of the water in which the quinces are boiled
9. Put it on the fire until reduced to a pulp
10. Pass it through a sieve and add it to the fruit with a few drops of prepared cochineal and a few cloves
11. Put the pan on the fire and let it simmer gently until the fruit is tender
12. Take it off
13. When cold, dish it up with the syrup and serve
