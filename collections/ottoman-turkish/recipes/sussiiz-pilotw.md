## Ingredients

- two pounds of mutton or lamb
- sufficient salt and pepper
- two pounds and a half of rice
- half a pound of hot fresh butter
- half a dozen pieces of gum-mastic
- two or three pieces of cinnamon

## Directions

1. Cut two pounds of mutton or lamb into pieces the size of walnuts, wash them, sprinkle them with sufficient salt and pepper, and lay them on the bottom of a stewpan with a tight-fitting cover.
2. Well wash two pounds and a half of rice, and place it over the meat.
3. Pour half a pound of hot fresh butter over, add half a dozen pieces of gum-mastic, two or three pieces of cinnamon.
4. Put the cover over the pan contrariwise, wrap a wet cloth round it, and fill the cover with water.
5. Set the pan on a slow charcoal fire, and let it simmer for about four hours.
6. Take off the cover, and turn the contents carefully on to a hot dish, and serve.
