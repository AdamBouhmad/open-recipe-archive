## Ingredients

- ten or twelve sheep's trotters
- water
- three or four cloves of garlic
- salt
- half a teacup of wine vinegar or the juice of three or four lemons
- thin slices of toast
- yolks of two or three eggs
- juice of a lemon or two
- pepper
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Clean the sheep's trotters and break the long bones
2. Put them in a stewpan nearly full of water
3. Place it on the fire or in a very hot oven with the cover over it until the meat comes off the bones
4. Take out the large bones and cut the meat in small pieces
5. Pound three or four cloves of garlic in a mortar with a little salt
6. Add half a teacup of wine vinegar or the juice of three or four lemons and stir it well with a wooden spoon
7. Pour it over the trotters with a little salt and stir it once
8. Set it on the fire and let it boil again for two or three minutes
9. Lay thin slices of toast on the bottom of a dish, over which place the pieces of trotters with some of the liquor
10. Put it on a very slow charcoal fire until the liquor is nearly absorbed
11. Beat up the yolks of two or three eggs with the juice of a lemon or two and a little of the liquor in which the trotters are cooked
12. Place them on a moderate fire and begin stirring with a wooden spoon (taking it from the fire now and then when getting too hot) until it thickens
13. Pour it over the trotters, sprinkle a little pepper and cinnamon over, and serve very hot
