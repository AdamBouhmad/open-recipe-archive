## Ingredients

- two or three chickens
- five pints and a half of nice milk
- three-quarters of a pound of rice flour
- water
- three-quarters of a pound of powdered sugar
- one or two tablespoonfuls of rose-water
- almond oil
- powdered cinnamon

## Directions

1. Boil two or three chickens.
2. When done, cut off the breasts, remove the bones and the skins from them, and pound the meat in a stone mortar, or pull it in bits like fine threads.
3. Pour in a stewpan five pints and a half of nice milk, put it on the fire.
4. While boiling, put in a basin three-quarters of a pound of rice flour, which mix with some of the boiling milk and some water, to form a batter.
5. Add this to the milk and boil it until it becomes a smooth substance, but not too thick.
6. Add the pounded breasts of chickens with three-quarters of a pound of powdered sugar, and one or two tablespoonfuls of rose-water.
7. Keep stirring, and at the same time beating it up on a slow fire until it forms a rather thick glutinous substance.
8. Remove it from the fire, stir it for a few minutes longer.
9. Fill a mould previously oiled with almond oil, and place it in cool place.
10. When ready, dip the mould into warm water, and turn it out on to a dish.
11. Sprinkle a little powdered cinnamon over, and serve.
12. Rinse the pounded breasts of the chickens once or twice in cold water before you add them to the milk and ground rice.
