## Ingredients

- one well-cleaned fowl
- two or three pounds of the middle of a neck of mutton
- water
- salt
- fresh butter
- six or eight onions
- a pint and a quarter of chick-peas
- two or three tablespoonfuls of wine vinegar
- pepper

## Directions

1. Cut a well-cleaned fowl in two lengthways
2. Place it in a stewpan with two or three pounds of the middle of a neck of mutton
3. Cover them with water, put in a little salt
4. Set it on the fire and let it boil till the fowl is half cooked, and skim it well
5. Take the fowl out and fry it a nice colour in fresh butter
6. Place it in a stewpan with six or eight sliced onions fried in butter, a pint and a quarter of chick-peas previously boiled a little soft and skinned, two or three tablespoonfuls of wine vinegar, and the liquor in which the fowl is boiled
7. Add sufficient salt and pepper
8. Put it on a slow charcoal fire and let it simmer until it forms a nice thick gravy at the bottom of the pan, and the peas are tender
9. Dish it up tastefully and serve
