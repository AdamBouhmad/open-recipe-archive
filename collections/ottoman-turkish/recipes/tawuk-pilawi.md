## Ingredients

- one or two nice chickens
- water
- a little salt
- three pints of chicken broth
- a pound and a half of rice, well washed
- a quarter of a pound of fresh butter
- half a dozen pieces of gum-mastic
- two or three pieces of cinnamon
- half a teaspoonful of salt

## Directions

1. Place one or two nice chickens in a saucepan, and nearly fill it with water, add a little salt, and set it on the fire.
2. When boiling, skim and simmer until the chickens are tender.
3. Take them out, place them in a dish, and keep them hot.
4. Measure three pints of the broth of the chickens, and pour it in a pan, to which add a pound and a half of rice, well washed, a quarter of a pound of fresh butter, half a dozen pieces of gum-mastic, two or three pieces of cinnamon, and half a teaspoonful of salt.
5. Put the pan on the fire with the cover over, and let it boil gently until the liquor is absorbed.
6. Take it off, stir it once round with a fork, and keep it on the hob with the cover over for fifteen or twenty minutes.
7. Take out the pieces of cinnamon.
8. Dish up the rice, in the centre of which place the chickens, cut in pieces, and serve very hot.
