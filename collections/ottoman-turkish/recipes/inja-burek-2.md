## Ingredients

- a pound and a half of flour
- a teaspoonful of salt
- four eggs
- sufficient water
- melted fresh butter
- wheat-starch
- fresh butter
- minced mutton or cheese or mushrooms
- onions

## Directions

1. Put a pound and a half of flour on a slab, make a hole in the centre, in which put a teaspoonful of salt and four eggs
2. Mix all together, then add sufficient water, and form it a softish flexible paste
3. Dip your hands into melted fresh butter, and work it for a few minutes longer
4. Divide it into thirty pieces the size of walnuts, sprinkle a little wheat-starch both under and over them, and roll them out one by one with a rolling-pin as thin as a wafer, or thinner than that if you can, and the size of the baking-tin you intend to lay them in
5. Butter the baking-tin, in which lay five of the pieces of paste one over the other, sprinkle a little fresh butter all over, and lay five more pieces over, butter again, and over which lay five more pieces, butter again
6. Lay all over either minced mutton, mixed with mushrooms and onions, both chopped very fine, and previously stewed a nice brown in fresh butter, together with the meat, or cheese, or any other ingredients you may fancy
7. Cover it with the remaining pieces of paste in the same way as before
8. Cut it in four crossways, pour a little hot fresh butter over, and bake it a nice brown in a moderate oven
9. When done, dish up and serve
