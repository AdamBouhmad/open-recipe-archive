## Ingredients

- six or seven egg-plants
- one or two pounds of raw mutton
- a handful of well-washed rice
- two onions
- salt
- pepper
- five or six eggs
- fresh butter
- rib-bones of mutton
- broth or water
- juice of one or two lemons

## Directions

1. Procure six or seven egg-plants and remove the stalks
2. Cut off the tops as large as a two-shilling piece
3. Take out the insides without making a hole at the other end and throw them in salt water for ten or fifteen minutes
4. Mince fine one or two pounds of raw mutton
5. Add a handful of well-washed rice, two onions chopped very fine, sufficient salt and pepper, and mix them together with your hands
6. Stuff the egg-plants with these ingredients and cover them with the tops contrariwise
7. Beat up five or six eggs in a basin and dip the egg-plants in them
8. Fry them in hot fresh butter
9. Prick them here and there with a fork and place them in a stewpan, the bottom of which you have previously covered with rib-bones of mutton
10. Cover them either with broth or water and add the juice of one or two lemons
11. Set it on a charcoal fire and let it simmer till the liquor is nearly absorbed or until it forms a nice thick gravy at the bottom
12. Dish them up carefully and serve hot, or beat up two or three eggs with the gravy and the juice of half a lemon in a saucepan, place it on a very slow fire and keep stirring, and when it begins to thicken, pour over the egg-plants and serve
