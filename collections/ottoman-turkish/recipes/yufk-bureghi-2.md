## Ingredients

- two or three pounds of flour
- salt
- water
- two or three eggs
- teacup of broth
- fresh butter
- fried minced meat

## Directions

1. Place two or three pounds of flour on the table-dresser, or in a pan, make a hole in the centre, put in a little salt, and sufficient water, and stir it well up with a wooden spoon.
2. Throw a little flour over, and knead well with the hands until it forms a nice paste.
3. Divide it into small pieces (say eight or ten), and roll them out thin, about the thickness of a sixpence, and the size of a baking-tin intended for laying them in.
4. Bake them soft, one by one, on a hot stove (such as crumpets are baked on), or a baking-sheet, which is placed over a trevet on a wood or charcoal fire.
5. When done nicely on both sides, beat up two or three eggs in a basin, with a teacup of broth (No. 1 or No. 2).
6. Grease the baking-tin with fresh butter, and lay in half the pieces of paste one over the other, sprinkling some of the beaten-up egg, and fresh butter between each one with a quill or a paste-brush.
7. Lay some fried minced meat all over, half an inch thick, which is prepared from raw mutton or beef.
8. Cover it with the remaining pieces of paste, as above with egg and butter over.
9. Place it in the oven until nicely browned on both sides.
10. When done, cut it in diamonds, or any other shape, dish up, and serve hot.
