## Ingredients

- one or two finely-chopped onions
- fresh butter
- a pint of tomato juice
- a pint of broth
- sufficient salt
- a pound of the best rice, well washed
- a quarter of a pound of fresh butter
- pepper

## Directions

1. Fry a nice brown one or two finely-chopped onions in fresh butter.
2. Put them in a saucepan with a pint of tomato juice which you have previously passed through a cloth or a sieve, the same quantity of broth, and sufficient salt, and put the pan on the fire.
3. When boiling, add a pound of the best rice, well washed, and let it boil gently till the liquor is absorbed.
4. Put on the fire a saucepan with a quarter of a pound of fresh butter.
5. When very hot, pour it over the rice, stir it once round with a fork.
6. Put the cover over the pan, and let it remain on the hob for fifteen or twenty minutes.
7. Dish it up, sprinkle a pinch of pepper over, and serve.
