## Ingredients

- about fourteen pounds of quinces
- the white of an egg
- three pounds of powdered sugar
- six pints of water
- a piece of alum the size of half a pea
- a few drops of prepared cochineal

## Directions

1. Procure about fourteen pounds of quinces, peel them, cut each one in quarters, and remove the cores.
2. Beat up the white of an egg in a stewpan and add three pounds of powdered sugar and six pints of water.
3. Put it on the fire.
4. When boiling, skim it.
5. Add the fruit and boil it till reduced to a pulp.
6. Take it off and pass it through a clean cloth into another stewpan.
7. Add a piece of alum the size of half a pea.
8. Set it again on the fire and let it boil gently for a minute or two.
9. Skim and pass it again through a close muslin into the pan.
10. Add a few drops of prepared cochineal.
11. Put it again on the fire and let it boil until it becomes a thick syrup.
12. Take it off and fill the preserve jars or glass bowls.
13. When cold, cover them and keep them in a cool place.
