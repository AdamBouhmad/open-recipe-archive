## Ingredients

- small salmon
- salt
- bay-leaves
- lemon
- nuts or almonds
- garlic
- bread-crumb
- water
- olive oil
- wine vinegar

## Directions

1. Cut from the tail end of a small salmon as many thin slices as are wanted
2. Rub them with a little salt
3. Cover a gridiron with some bay-leaves
4. Lay the slices of fish over
5. Set it on a moderate charcoal fire
6. When nicely browned on both sides, dish it up
7. Squeeze a lemon over, or prepare the following sauce: crack four or five dozen of nuts or almonds, skin them, then pound them well in a mortar with one or two cloves of garlic peeled
8. Add a piece of bread-crumb the size of a large egg, previously soaked in water, a little salt, two or three teaspoonfuls of olive oil
9. Pound them altogether till they form a paste
10. Moisten it with some lemon-juice or wine vinegar, and some water, to form a smooth sauce, but not too thick
11. Pour it over the fish instead of lemon and serve hot
