## Ingredients

- the whites of twenty eggs
- about a pound of wheat starch
- water
- rose-water
- milk
- clotted cream
- powdered sugar
- a pound of loaf sugar
- two pints of water

## Directions

1. Put in a basin the whites of twenty eggs, and whip them up well
2. Add about a pound of wheat starch by degrees, stir it well, and mix it well with water, to form a very thin batter
3. Put over a charcoal fire a circular and slightly-domed sheet-iron, the under side in the centre of which you have previously cemented with ashes half an inch thick, to prevent it from getting fiercely hot
4. When moderately hot, pour a portion of the batter over the centre, with a convenient spoon, sufficient to form a round cake, the size of a large dinner-plate, or larger, and which will be done in less than a minute
5. Prepare fifty or sixty pieces in this manner
6. Lay half of them in a suitable baking-tin, one over the other, sprinkling between each piece some rose-water and milk
7. Lay some clotted cream over, a quarter of an inch thick, and shake some powdered sugar over
8. Cover it with the other half of the cakes, sprinkling rose-water and milk between each one
9. Put in a stewpan a pound of loaf sugar, with two pints of water, and set it to boil until it becomes a syrup
10. Take it off and pour it over the cakes, till covered, and let it remain covered until cold
11. Cut it up in diamonds, dish up and serve
