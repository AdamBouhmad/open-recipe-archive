## Ingredients

- three or four pounds of mutton
- salt
- water
- five or six sliced onions
- fresh butter
- one or two hundred okras
- broth
- juice of one or two lemons
- pepper
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Cut three or four pounds of mutton into pieces about the size of walnuts
2. Wash and place them in a saucepan with sufficient salt
3. Cover them with water, and set the pan on the fire
4. When boiling, skim and simmer till the meat is half done
5. Fry partly brown five or six sliced onions in fresh butter
6. Peel the stalky parts, and cut off the ends of one or two hundred okras
7. Place the pieces of meat and the okras, together with the onions in layers
8. Add the liquor in which the meat was boiled; if not enough, add some of broth and a little salt
9. Put the cover over the pan, and set it on a charcoal fire until the meat and the okras are tender, and a nice gravy is formed at the bottom
10. Just five minutes before removing it from the fire, add the juice of one or two lemons
11. When done, dish up, sprinkle a little pepper and cinnamon over, and serve
