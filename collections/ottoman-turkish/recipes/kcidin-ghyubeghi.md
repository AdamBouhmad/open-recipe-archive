## Ingredients

- three-quarters of a pound of fresh butter
- about two pounds and three-quarters of the best flour
- one teaspoonful of salt
- water
- one teaspoonful of carbonate of soda
- syrup (No. 135)

## Directions

1. Put in a stewpan three-quarters of a pound of fresh butter, and set it on the fire
2. When melted, mix in by degrees about two pounds and three-quarters of the best flour, and keep stirring until the flour is partly browned
3. Take it out, and place it on a pastry slab, make a hole in the centre, in which put a teaspoonful of salt
4. Mix it with water (in which a teaspoonful of carbonate of soda is dissolved previously) into a softish paste with your hands
5. Form it into small cakes, and make a dent in the centre of each one with the point of your finger
6. Place them in a buttered tin, and bake them a nice colour
7. Have ready some syrup as in No. 135
8. When the cakes are nicely done, pour it over
9. Put it again in the oven, or on a slow charcoal fire until the syrup is absorbed
10. Dish up tastefully, and serve
