## Ingredients

- flour
- salt
- eggs
- lemons
- olive oil or butter
- syrup
- clotted cream
- milk
- rose-water
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Put in a basin or on a slab one pound of flour
2. Make a hole in the centre, in which put a small teaspoonful of salt
3. Add the white of five or six eggs, and the juice of two or three lemons strained
4. Mix all together
5. Dip your hands in olive oil or butter
6. Work it until it forms a softish flexible paste
7. Divide it in pieces the size of walnuts
8. Roll them out to the thickness of a sixpence with a rolling-pin
9. Fold each piece even
10. Fry them a nice brown in scalded fresh butter
11. Put them in a colander
12. When free from fat, throw them in boiling syrup, which you have previously made of loaf sugar and water as in No. 149
13. Take them out and lay half of them in a baking-tin, one over the other
14. Lay all over some clotted cream, about a quarter of an inch thick
15. Cover it with the other half of the paste
16. Add half a pint of milk, or little more
17. Put it on a charcoal fire
18. Let it boil until quite soft
19. Sprinkle some rose-water over, with a little powdered cinnamon, and serve
