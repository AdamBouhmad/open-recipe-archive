## Ingredients

- flour
- salt
- eggs
- lemons
- olive oil or butter
- syrup
- loaf sugar
- water
- clotted cream
- milk
- rose-water
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Put in a basin or on a slab one pound of flour
2. Make a hole in the centre, in which put a small teaspoonful of salt, the white of five or six eggs, and the juice of two or three lemons strained
3. Mix all together
4. Dip your hands in olive oil or butter
5. Work it until it forms a softish flexible paste
6. Divide it in pieces the size of walnuts
7. Roll them out to the thickness of a sixpence with a rolling-pin
8. Fold each piece even
9. Fry them a nice brown in scalded fresh butter and put them in a colander
10. When free from fat, throw them in boiling syrup, which you have previously made of loaf sugar and water as in No. 149
11. Take them out and lay half of them in a baking-tin, one over the other
12. Lay all over some clotted cream, about a quarter of an inch thick
13. Cover it with the other half of the paste
14. Add half a pint of milk, or little more
15. Put it on a charcoal fire and let it boil until quite soft
16. Sprinkle some rose-water over, with a little powdered cinnamon, and serve
