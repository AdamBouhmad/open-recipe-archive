## Ingredients

- a pound and a quarter of the best flour
- a teaspoonful of salt
- four or five ounces of fresh butter, melted
- half a pint of water
- carbonate of soda
- boiling fresh butter
- syrup prepared of honey, or loaf sugar and water

## Directions

1. Put a pound and a quarter of the best flour on the pastry-slab, make a hole in the centre
2. In the hole put a teaspoonful of salt, four or five ounces of fresh butter, melted
3. Mix all together, then add half a pint of water, or a little more, mixed with a little carbonate of soda, and form it a softish flexible paste
4. Roll it with hands as long as it will extend, a little thicker than your thumb, and cut it up in pieces the size of a date
5. Roll them one by one upon a basket-lid, or a table-mat made of fine twigs
6. Fry them a delicate colour in boiling fresh butter
7. When done, let them drain, then throw them in boiling syrup, which you have previously prepared of honey, or loaf sugar and water
8. Let them remain for a few minutes, then dish them up tastefully, and serve
