## Ingredients

- one or two pounds of lamb
- fresh butter
- salt
- water
- six or seven dozens of young okras
- three or four finely-sliced onions
- juice of one or two lemons
- three cups of mutton or beef broth
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Cut one or two pounds of lamb in pieces about the size of walnuts
2. Place them in a stewpan with an ounce of fresh butter, a small teaspoonful of salt, and half a teacup of water
3. Put on the fire
4. When boiling, stir round with a wooden spoon for about twelve minutes, or until it forms a whitish thick gravy at the bottom
5. Lay the pieces of meat on the bottom of a rather deep and circular baking-pan
6. Over which lay regularly six or seven dozens of young okras, together with three or four finely-sliced onions, fried partly brown in fresh butter
7. Add the gravy, with the juice of one or two lemons, three cups of either mutton or beef broth, and a little salt
8. Bake it in a very hot oven until the okras are quite soft and nicely brown
9. Turn it gently on to a dish, without spoiling the shape, sprinkle a little cinnamon over, and serve
