## Ingredients

- flour
- water
- three-quarters of a pound of scalded fresh butter
- a pound of white sugar
- two pints of water

## Directions

1. Procure a tin pint pot, the shape of a tumbler, insert in the bottom eight or ten fine tubes
2. Put some flour in a basin, which mix with water to form a smooth batter
3. Put a portion of the batter in the pot, let it run through the tubes, by passing the pot up and down on a very hot crumpet-stove, which is done in a few minutes in long threads like vermicelli
4. Take them up, and spread them on a tray, and continue in this way till you have made about three pounds
5. Lay the vermicelli even in a buttered baking-tin, pour three-quarters of a pound of scalded fresh butter over, and bake it a nice brown on both sides in the oven, or on a charcoal fire
6. Put a pound of white sugar in a stewpan, with two pints of water, and set it to boil until it becomes a syrup
7. Skim, and keep it hot
8. When the vermicelli is done, pour the syrup all over
9. If you wish it to be crisp, leave it uncovered; if soft, cover it the moment you have poured the syrup over, and let it remain for fifteen minutes or so
10. Cut it in four crossways, or any other shape you fancy, dish up
11. If any syrup is left in the baking tin, pour it over, and serve hot or cold
