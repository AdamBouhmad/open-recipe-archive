## Ingredients

- two pounds of mutton or lamb
- salt
- water
- three or four sliced onions
- fresh butter
- four or five vegetable marrows
- juice of one or two lemons
- chopped mint
- pepper or cinnamon

## Directions

1. Cut two pounds of mutton or lamb in pieces about the size of walnuts
2. Put them in a stewpan with a little salt, and cover them with water
3. Set it on a charcoal fire
4. When beginning to scum, skim it well, and let it simmer till the meat is nearly tender
5. Put three or four sliced onions in a fryingpan with sufficient fresh butter, and fry them partly brown
6. Peel four or five vegetable marrows, cut each one in four crossways, take out the insides, if seeded, and slice them three-quarters of an inch thick
7. Lay some of the pieces of meat on the bottom of the stewpan; then add some slices of vegetable marrow, with part of the fried onions, then meat, then pieces of vegetable marrow and onions, then meat, &c., until the whole of them are laid
8. Add the liquor in which the pieces of mutton were boiled, and sufficient salt; if the liquor is not enough, add some of the broth
9. Set the stewpan on the fire, and let it simmer until the vegetable marrow is tender
10. Add the juice of one or two lemons, with a little chopped mint
11. Let it simmer for two minutes longer, and then take it off
12. Dish it up, sprinkle a little pepper or cinnamon over, and serve hot
