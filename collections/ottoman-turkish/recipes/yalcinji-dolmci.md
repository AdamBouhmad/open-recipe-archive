## Ingredients

- seven or eight dozens of young vine leaves
- three-quarters of a pint of olive oil
- two pounds and a half of finely chopped onions
- two pounds and a half of the best rice
- two teacupfuls of water
- one or two teaspoonfuls of powdered mint
- salt
- pepper
- cinnamon
- rib-bones of mutton
- eight or ten sour plums

## Directions

1. Procure seven or eight dozens of young vine leaves, and scald them
2. Let them drain in a colander
3. Pour in a saucepan three-quarters of a pint of olive oil; place it on the fire
4. When hot, put in two pounds and a half of finely chopped onions, and stir until they begin to brown
5. Add two pounds and a half of the best rice, well cleaned, and keep stirring
6. When the rice is on the point of browning, add two teacupfuls of water, and continue stirring gently till the water is absorbed
7. Take it off, add one or two teaspoonfuls of powdered mint, a little salt, pepper, and cinnamon, and well stir it
8. Spread the vine leaves out, and put half a tablespoonful of the prepared rice in each one, fold it about the length of your thumb, but a little thicker, and squeeze it gently in your hand, so that it may not open while cooking
9. Cover the bottom of a saucepan with rib-bones of mutton, and over which place the stuffed leaves one by one, arranging, at the same time, between them eight or ten sour plums
10. When the whole is laid, press them with a plate, then nearly cover them with water, and set the pan on the fire
11. When boiling, let it simmer until there is no liquid remaining
12. Take one out and try if the rice is soft; if not, add some more water
13. When soft, and each grain is separate, dish them up tastefully, and serve
