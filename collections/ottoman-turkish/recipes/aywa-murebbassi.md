## Ingredients

- 14 pounds quinces
- white of 1 egg
- 3 pounds powdered sugar
- 6 pints of water
- alum the size of half a pea
- prepared cochineal

## Directions

1. Peel the quinces and cut each one in quarters
2. Remove the cores
3. Beat up the white of an egg in a stewpan
4. Add 3 pounds of powdered sugar and 6 pints of water
5. Put it on the fire
6. When boiling, skim it
7. Add the fruit and boil it till reduced to a pulp
8. Take it off and pass it through a clean cloth into another stewpan
9. Add a piece of alum the size of half a pea
10. Set it again on the fire and let it boil gently for a minute or two
11. Skim and pass it again through a close muslin into the pan
12. Add a few drops of prepared cochineal
13. Put it again on the fire and let it boil until it becomes a thick syrup
14. Take it off and fill the preserve jars or glass bowls
15. When cold, cover them and keep them in a cool place
