## Ingredients

- 1 pound rose-leaves
- three-quarters of a pint of water
- 3 pounds and a half white sifted sugar

## Directions

1. When the roses are in full bloom, gather about a pound of the leaves
2. Pick out the large ones and cut the white ends off with a pair of scissors
3. Put the small ones and the white ends in a very clean stewpan with three-quarters of a pint of water and scald them
4. Pass the liquor through a piece of muslin or a sieve and squeeze well the leaves
5. Throw the leaves away
6. Put the liquor in the pan with 3 pounds and a half of white sifted sugar
7. Stir with a wooden spoon till the sugar is dissolved
8. Add the large leaves that you have picked out
9. Stir and boil till rather thick
10. Try a little on a plate to see if it sets
11. When cold, fill the preserve jars and cover them air-tight
12. Keep them in a rather cool place
