## Ingredients

- half a dozen mackerel
- water
- three or four onions
- olive oil
- a handful of picked parsley
- a little fennel
- half a teacup of wine vinegar
- two or three teacups of the water in which the fish is boiled
- salt
- pepper
- cinnamon
- one or two tomatoes
- a tablespoonful of preserved tomato sauce

## Directions

1. Take out the inside of half a dozen mackerel and wash them
2. Boil them in water for one or two minutes
3. When cold, remove the bones
4. Cut three or four onions in slices and fry them partly brown in olive oil
5. Lay half on the bottom of a stewpan with a handful of picked parsley mixed with a little fennel and pour in what oil is left in the fryingpan
6. Place the pieces of mackerel over and then the other half of the onions with the same quantity of parsley and fennel on the top
7. Add half a teacup of wine vinegar, two or three teacups of the water in which the fish is boiled, sufficient salt and pepper
8. Put the pan on a moderate charcoal fire with the lid on and let it simmer gently until the fish is nicely done, which will take about fifteen or twenty minutes
9. Dish it up, sprinkle a little cinnamon over, and serve
