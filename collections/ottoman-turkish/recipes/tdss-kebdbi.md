## Ingredients

- leg or loin of mutton
- onions
- salt
- pepper
- mixed spice
- broth
- rice

## Directions

1. Cut sufficient quantity of a leg or loin of mutton in pieces the size of small walnuts
2. Wash them with cold water, and dry them in a cloth
3. Sprinkle two or three finely-chopped onions over, with sufficient salt, pepper, and mixed spice
4. Rub them well in the meat, and let it remain covered for about two hours
5. Put the pieces of meat in a convenient basin, and place it upside downwards in the centre of a clean baking-tin an inch and a half deep, and the bottom of which must be strong enough to bear the heat of the fire
6. Place a trevet on a charcoal fire, and over which set the baking-tin for about an hour and a half
7. When the gravy has run out and is absorbed, pour in the baking-tin a pint and a half of broth No. 1 or No. 2, and add three-quarters of a pound of the best rice, well washed
8. Spread it even around the basin
9. When the broth is absorbed, and the rice is hard, sprinkle some more broth over
10. Each grain should be soft, and well separated
11. When done, take the baking-tin off the fire, and just at the time it is wanted, remove the basin from the centre of it, and serve as it is, with a napkin pinned round the baking-tin
