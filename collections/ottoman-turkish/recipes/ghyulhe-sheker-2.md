## Ingredients

- about one pound of rose leaves
- three-quarters of a pint of water
- three pounds and a half of white sifted sugar

## Directions

1. When the roses are in full bloom, gather about a pound of the leaves.
2. Pick out the large ones and cut the white ends off with a pair of scissors.
3. Put the small ones and the white ends in a very clean stewpan with three-quarters of a pint of water and scald them.
4. Pass the liquor through a piece of muslin or a sieve and squeeze well the leaves and throw them away.
5. Put the liquor in the pan with three pounds and a half of white sifted sugar.
6. Stir them with a wooden spoon till the sugar is dissolved.
7. Add the large leaves that you have picked out.
8. Stir and boil till rather thick.
9. Try a little on a plate.
10. If it sets, remove it from the fire.
11. When cold, fill the preserve jars and cover them air-tight.
12. Keep them in a rather cool place.
