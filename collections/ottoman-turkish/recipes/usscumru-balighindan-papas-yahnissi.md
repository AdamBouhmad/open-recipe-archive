## Ingredients

- half a dozen mackerel
- water
- three or four onions
- olive oil
- a handful of picked parsley
- a little fennel
- half a teacup of wine vinegar
- two or three teacups of water
- salt
- pepper
- cinnamon
- one or two tomatoes
- a tablespoonful of preserved tomato sauce

## Directions

1. Take out the inside of half a dozen mackerel and wash them
2. Boil them in water for one or two minutes
3. When cold, remove the bones
4. Cut three or four onions in slices and fry them partly brown in olive oil
5. Lay the half on the bottom of a stewpan with a handful of picked parsley mixed with a little fennel
6. Pour in what oil is left in the frying-pan
7. Place the pieces of mackerel over
8. Put the other half of the onions with the same quantity of parsley and fennel on the top
9. Add half a teacup of wine vinegar and two or three teacups of the water in which the fish is boiled
10. Add sufficient salt and pepper
11. Put the pan on a moderate charcoal fire with the lid on and let it simmer gently for about fifteen or twenty minutes until the fish is nicely done
12. Dish it up, sprinkle a little cinnamon over, and serve
