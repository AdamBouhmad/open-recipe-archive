## Ingredients

- beautiful, large, not completely ripe gooseberries
- sugar, freshly cooked and later boiled

## Directions

1. Select beautiful, large, but not completely ripe gooseberries.
2. Clean them and carefully remove the seeds with a stick.
3. Place in fresh water and press well until they soften.
4. Heat the water with the gooseberries, but do not boil; when the gooseberries rise to the top, remove from heat and cover the container so they soften away from the stove.
5. When cooled, place in cold water and then into cleaned boiled sugar.
6. Cover again and let sit quietly.
7. On the second day, drain the sugar, cook it separately, and when well reduced, add the gooseberries and continue cooking.
