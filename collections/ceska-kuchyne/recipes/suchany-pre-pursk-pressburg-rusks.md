## Ingredients

- two cups warm milk
- one piece of compressed yeast
- fine flour
- eight egg yolks
- ten tablespoons sugar
- zest of half a lemon, grated
- a little nutmeg
- butter for greasing
- egg white icing (meringue)
- finely chopped almonds or peanuts
- grated bread
- sugar for topping

## Directions

1. Mix the warm milk with compressed yeast and add fine flour until the dough is about the consistency of dumpling dough; let it rise. If compressed yeast is not available and dried yeast is used instead, prepare the dough in the evening and let it rise overnight.
2. Once the dough has risen, mix in the eight egg yolks one by one, stirring well after each addition.
3. Add ten tablespoons sugar, the grated zest from half a lemon, and a little nutmeg; work everything together well.
4. Do not add much more flour, only dust the surface lightly with a bit of flour.
5. Grease a pan of ordinary size (about 8 by 14 inches), place the dough in it, and when the pan is three-quarters full, bake over moderate heat.
6. Once baked, let it cool and cut into slices about half an inch thick.
7. On both cut sides, spread with egg white icing (meringue made from egg whites and sugar).
8. Sprinkle well with finely chopped almonds or peanuts mixed with grated bread and sugar.
9. Place again on a pan and let dry in a not too hot oven until brown.
10. Store in a dry place. If well dried and stored in a dry place, they will keep for a year—if they are not eaten—but more likely they will be gone within a week.
