## Ingredients

- eel
- butter
- white wine
- salt
- pepper
- crayfish tails
- carrot
- onion
- parsley root
- celery root
- thyme
- bay leaf
- clove
- whole pepper
- dill
- lobster
- water
- cream
- egg yolks

## Directions

1. Clean and skin the eel, trim the meat from the backbone and cut into pieces the size of oysters
2. Place in a pan with two tablespoons of butter, two glasses of white wine, salt and pepper and let it braise for a quarter hour
3. Remove the eel and place in a soup bowl with three dozen cooked and shelled crayfish tails
4. In a pot put two sliced carrots, two onions, parsley and celery root, a sprig of thyme, bay leaf, three cloves, a handful of whole pepper, two pieces of dill and four ounces of butter
5. Mix and let it steam
6. Add meat from crayfish, crushed meat from one small lobster, heads, bones and trimmings from the eels, a little salt, three quarts of water, a pint of white wine and eel sauce and let it all simmer slowly for about an hour
7. Skim the foam and fat, strain first through a sieve then through cloth
8. Thicken with a pale roux, let it simmer another quarter hour, skim the foam
9. Bind with four egg yolks mixed with a cup of cream and two tablespoons of butter and pour into the bowl over the pieces of eel and crayfish tails
10. If you do not have crayfish tails, you can use sliced lobster tail
