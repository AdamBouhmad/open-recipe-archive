## Ingredients

- warm freshly milked milk
- cream (abundant)
- rennet
- salt

## Directions

1. This cheese is also called Emmental or Gruyère.
2. Emmental is prepared at +28-30° R., whereas Gruyère is made at +24-26° R. and curdled.
3. Crumbled Gruyère cheese is heated to +55° R., Emmental cheese then to +45° R.
4. Both cheeses are prepared from warm, freshly milked milk, to which plenty of cream is added, which is heated together and left to curdle; the curdled cheese is broken and crumbled and everything is heated again to +45-48° R., with constant stirring.
5. The cheese is removed, placed in cloth, arranged into a hoop mold, covered with a board, and pressed; every two hours the cheese is turned, wrapped in fresh cloth, and pressed again more firmly.
6. The pressed loaf is salted on all sides in a warm room; in three weeks the cheese ferments, which lasts about two months; the cheese is flipped, turned, washed with water, and dried.
7. After fermentation the cheese gets holes; the cheese is ripe only in 6 to 9 months.
8. From the remaining whey, dry cheese can be obtained.
