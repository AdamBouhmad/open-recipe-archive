## Ingredients

- juice from two dozen large oysters
- butter
- beef or fish broth
- pepper
- nutmeg
- oysters
- flour
- egg yolks or whole egg
- lemon juice
- fried breadcrumb cutlets of various shapes or small rice croquettes
- hot water

## Directions

1. Let the juice from two dozen large oysters simmer in a pot with a spoonful of butter, some beef or fish broth and a pinch of pepper and nutmeg
2. Place the oysters in it, let them simmer for about two minutes, then remove them and strain the juice through a sieve
3. Make a light roux from a spoonful of butter and a spoonful of flour in a pan
4. Add enough of the strained juice to make a thickened sauce
5. Salt, pepper and let simmer for about ten minutes
6. Beat two egg yolks or a whole egg with a spoonful of butter and lemon juice
7. Mix this into the sauce, strain it through a sieve, and keeping it constantly stirred over the fire, let it heat through—but it must not boil
8. Pour the sauce over the oysters placed in a deep heated dish and surrounded by fried breadcrumb cutlets of various shapes or small rice croquettes
9. The dish should be heated in the oven and the oysters kept warm by placing them in a covered pot in a dish of hot water
