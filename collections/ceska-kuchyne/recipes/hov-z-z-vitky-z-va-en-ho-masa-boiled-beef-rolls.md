## Ingredients

- one and a half pounds of boiled beef
- butter
- flour
- beef broth
- three egg yolks
- parsley leaves, cleaned and finely chopped
- ground pepper
- salt
- stale bread, finely grated or bread crumbs or crackers
- three egg whites
- one teaspoon of warmed butter
- one teaspoon water
- lard
- parsley

## Directions

1. Remove all glands and fat from one and a half pounds of boiled beef and chop the meat finely.
2. Make a pale roux from butter and flour, then pour enough beef broth into it to make a thick sauce, then let it, stirring constantly, boil for a quarter hour.
3. Then mix in a cup a piece of butter with three egg yolks, add this mixture to the sauce, which you then stir well.
4. To this add the mentioned chopped meat, one spoon of cleaned and finely chopped parsley leaves, a little ground pepper, salt it and mix well with a spoon.
5. Then let it cool completely, sprinkle generously with grated stale bread, bread crumbs or crackers; then divide the prepared meat into 16 portions, and place each portion on a layer of this grated bread spread on a board, then again sprinkle generously with grated bread crumbs, from this make equally large and oblong rolls.
6. Then beat 3 egg whites, add to them one teaspoon of warmed butter, one teaspoon of water and mix well.
7. Dip each roll in this and coat it in grated bread crumbs; then about 20 minutes before you want to put the rolls on the table, melt lard in a pot, place the rolls on a wire basket (frying basket) and hang this in the pot in the heated lard and let them fry.
8. Then turn them with a spoon and let them fry nicely to golden brown, then remove, sprinkle with salt, arrange on a plate, surround with fried parsley and bring them to the table.
