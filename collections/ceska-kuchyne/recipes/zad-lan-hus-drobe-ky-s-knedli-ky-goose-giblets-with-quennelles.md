## Ingredients

- Goose giblets
- Vegetables
- One large tablespoon butter
- Three eggs
- Lemon peel, finely chopped
- Goose liver, grated or chopped
- Salt
- Cream
- Grated bread or crackers
- Garlic and marjoram (optional)

## Directions

1. Salt and cook the goose giblets with vegetables like beef meat until tender.
2. Stir one large tablespoon of butter, beat in three eggs, add a small piece of finely chopped lemon peel, grated or chopped goose liver (if using chopped liver, remove the veins), salt lightly and mix well.
3. Add several tablespoons of good cream and enough grated bread or crackers to make quennelles.
4. If desired, add a small amount of chopped garlic with marjoram to the quenelle mixture and set aside.
5. Put a piece of fresh butter in a small pot and make a light roux, mix it with half of the strained broth from the giblets, and pour the other half of the broth also into the pot and let it boil.
6. Shape the mixture into round quennelles and cook them in the boiling broth.
7. When the quennelles are sufficiently cooked, add the cooked goose giblets to them, pour the reduced broth over, season lightly with nutmeg, and let simmer a little longer.
8. Remove the giblets, place them on a serving dish, arrange the quennelles around them, pour the sauce over and bring to the table.
