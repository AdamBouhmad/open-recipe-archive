## Ingredients

- One rabbit
- Sour cream
- Vinegar
- Water
- One large onion
- Thyme
- Bay leaves
- Whole pepper
- Whole ginger
- Allspice
- Cloves
- Butter
- Two fresh onions
- Lard for larding

## Directions

1. After skinning the rabbit, separate the back from the front, trim all the skin from the back, wash it and salt it, place on a baking pan.
2. Into a cup of vinegar and the same amount of water, put one large sliced onion, a little thyme, bay leaves, whole pepper, whole ginger, allspice and several cloves.
3. Bring this to a boil and pour the cooled mixture over the rabbit.
4. The rabbit may rest in this mixture for 3-4 days, or even 8 days in winter, turning it daily.
5. When ready to roast, remove the rabbit to a board, lard it nicely with lard, put a piece of butter on a baking pan with two fresh sliced onions, place the rabbit on it with the lard side down, add some of the liquid in which it was resting.
6. During roasting, turn the rabbit frequently, basting it each time with thick sour cream, and place pieces of butter on top until the rabbit browns nicely on the surface and the gravy thickens.
7. If the gravy browns too much, add a little of the strained liquid and some sour cream to it.
8. Place the rabbit on a platter and pour the strained gravy over it.
