## Ingredients

- large, selected, not completely ripe redcurrants
- sugar, dissolved and later boiled to varying thicknesses

## Directions

1. Take a large, selected, not completely ripe bunch of redcurrants with several small bunches tied together.
2. Place on a dish and pour boiling water over them, covering with a lid; repeat this several times until they soften completely.
3. Remove them and place on a sieve to dry.
4. Place on a dish and pour melted sugar over them.
5. Cover with paper and let sit undisturbed.
6. On the second day, drain the sugar, boil it thicker, add the redcurrants, and heat until it begins to boil.
7. Repeat the same process on the third day.
8. Adding the redcurrants to the sugar must be done very carefully so the berries do not bruise.
9. On the fourth day, drain the sugar again, boil it thick, add the redcurrants, and let it come to a gentle boil.
10. Skim off the foam and place the preserve in containers; when cooled, seal with a bladder.
