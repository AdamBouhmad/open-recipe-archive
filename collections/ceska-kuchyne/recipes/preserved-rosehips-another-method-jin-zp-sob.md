## Ingredients

- red, ripe rosehips, not yet soft
- sugar, half a kilogram per half kilogram of rosehips
- water, one quarter liter per half kilogram of sugar
- lemon juice, from two lemons
- cinnamon, optional

## Directions

1. Wipe red, ripe rosehips that are not yet soft with a cloth.
2. Trim the stem and flower remnants with a small stick without cutting open the rosehips.
3. Remove the seeds, wash them, drop them into boiling water, and cook until half-done.
4. Cool in cold water and place on a sieve to drain.
5. For each half kilogram of rosehips, use half a kilogram of sugar cooked in one quarter liter of water.
6. When the foam is skimmed off, pour the hot sugar over the rosehips.
7. On the second day, cook the sugar again and cook the rosehips with it once; repeat this on the third and fourth days.
8. On the last day, add juice from two lemons, pour thick sugar over the rosehips, and when cooled in jars, seal them.
9. Sometimes cinnamon is added to the sugar, which must be removed before sealing.
