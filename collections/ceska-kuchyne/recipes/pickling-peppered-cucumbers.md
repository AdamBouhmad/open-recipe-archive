## Ingredients

- Healthy, firm, preferably small cucumbers
- Best quality clean vinegar
- Soft water
- Salt
- Cherry or grape leaves
- Dill greens
- Green paprika
- Plum brandy (optional)
- Fat for sealing (optional)

## Directions

1. Select healthy, firm, rather small cucumbers and the best quality clean vinegar; dilute the vinegar with soft water and add salt; to three liters of vinegar add one liter of water and six tablespoons of salt
2. Pickling cucumbers must be gathered dry; early cucumbers are better than late ones
3. Wash the cucumbers in cold water, clean and dry them, then place in glass jars or barrels
4. The barrels must be completely clean and without any smell; place cherry or grape leaves and dill greens at the bottom, then arrange the cucumbers well, and always add some green paprika with each layer
5. For each layer of cucumbers, add a layer of leaves and dill
6. Pour the boiled and cooled vinegar over everything, continuing layer by layer until the container is full, then seal completely so no air can enter
7. If the vinegar dries out, refill with new vinegar prepared the same way
8. Some pour a little plum brandy over the cucumbers in jars to keep them fresher and longer
9. It is also possible to seal them on top with fat so air cannot easily enter
10. For quick sour cucumbers, cut them at both ends and keep them for two or three days in a warm room, then store in a cold place
