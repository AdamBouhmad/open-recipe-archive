## Ingredients

- ripe but not overripe tomatoes
- good vinegar

## Directions

1. Select ripe but not overripe tomatoes, clean them thoroughly, and place on a large plate so the water drains
2. Take a stone jar of about 3 gallons capacity, clean and dry it, and arrange the tomatoes in it without crushing them
3. Boil good vinegar and pour it hot over the tomatoes
4. Cover with a plate so it cools as slowly as possible
5. When the steam escapes, seal well and store in the cellar
6. This way they can be kept until fresh with minimal effort and are more reliable than in cans
7. In winter they are good for sauces, and if not too sour vinegar is used, also for the table, though they have a sour taste
8. The skin comes off easily
