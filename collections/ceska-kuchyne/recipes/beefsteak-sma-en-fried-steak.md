## Ingredients

- 2 pounds beef steak
- 2 tablespoons butter or rendered fat from the meat
- Salt and pepper
- ½ onion, finely chopped
- A few tablespoons of boiling water

## Directions

1. Pound the meat well. If it is not a porterhouse cut, pound it thoroughly to tenderize it.
2. Do not wash the meat; if desired, wipe it with a clean dry cloth.
3. Heat the butter or fat in a pan until very hot.
4. Place the steak in the hot fat, season with salt and pepper on top, and fry on high heat without covering for about two minutes until it browns.
5. The fat must be hot so the meat does not release its juices and becomes tender.
6. Turn the steak and add the finely chopped onion, season the other side with salt and pepper.
7. When the second side is browned, remove the steak.
8. Pour a few tablespoons of boiling water into the pan to make a gravy, then pour this over the steak or serve it separately in a dish.
