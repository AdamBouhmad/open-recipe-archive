## Ingredients

- pork head, sectioned
- pork liver (half cooked raw)
- pork lungs and other offal
- bread (2 parts for every 1 part meat)
- pepper
- allspice
- clove (very little)
- marjoram
- lemon zest
- garlic (a small piece)
- broth

## Directions

1. First, boil the sectioned head; if it is a large piece, cut away the fat from under the jaw, as liver smisage should not be too fatty
2. Cook half the liver raw; boil the lungs and remaining offal together with the head
3. Chop all the cooked meat finely with a knife, as meat chopped with a machine becomes like mud
4. For every one part of meat, use two parts of bread, preferably baked two days prior as the harder it is, the better
5. Season with pepper, allspice, very little clove, marjoram, lemon zest, and a small piece of garlic
6. Mix the chopped meat with squeezed bread and seasonings, along with a little broth
7. Fill one intestine and boil it immediately so you can taste it and add any missing seasonings
8. Do not boil liver smisage in a large vessel or boil many at once, as they often burst; instead boil them in two pots with only enough to allow them to move freely
9. Use broth from the cooked meat, skimmed of fat beforehand, filling the pots halfway
10. When it reaches a boil, add the intestines and when they rise to the surface, remove them and place in clean cold water
11. Lay them on a board covered with paper
