## Ingredients

- quart of sifted flour
- coffee cup of lard
- pinch of salt
- cold water
- half teaspoon of baking powder (optional)
- quart of flour
- coffee cup of butter
- coffee cup of lard
- two egg yolks
- pinch of salt
- cold water
- two coffee cups of flour
- level teaspoon of baking powder
- sweet cream

## Directions

1. To a quart of sifted flour, add a coffee cup of lard and a pinch of salt, mix well with a spoon
2. Add a little cold water and knead the dough firmly together
3. Place on a flour-dusted board and roll out in one direction with a roller
4. If you add half a teaspoon of baking powder to the flour, the dough will be fluffy and crisp
5. This amount is sufficient for two covered or four uncovered pies
6. For better dough, mix a quart of flour with a coffee cup of butter and a coffee cup of lard, add two egg yolks, a pinch of salt and a little cold water, and proceed as above; this is sufficient for four pies
7. Another dough for two pies can be made from 2 coffee cups of flour, a level teaspoon of baking powder and enough sweet cream to moisten the flour
8. The best pies however are made from butter dough
