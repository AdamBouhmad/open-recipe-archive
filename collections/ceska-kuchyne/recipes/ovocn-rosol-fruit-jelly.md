## Ingredients

- ripe fruit
- water
- sugar

## Directions

1. Fruit jelly is juice from ripe, but not overripe fruit, cooked with sugar
2. First cook the fruit with a little water so it releases its juice
3. Strain the juice either through a thick linen strainer or a bag; if you first strain through a strainer and then through a bag, the jelly will be clearer
4. The jelly must be cooked on a strong fire and since it rises high during cooking, do not put much in the pot; when it finishes cooking, it does not rise as much and you must watch that it does not overcook; as soon as it thickens, it must be put immediately into jars, otherwise it will jell in the pot
5. After cooling, first place on top a piece of paper soaked in rum and close the jars
6. During cooking, stir constantly and carefully collect the foam, otherwise the jelly would become cloudy, would thicken on the jarring place, and would not keep long
