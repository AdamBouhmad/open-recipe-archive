## Ingredients

- Meat
- Salt
- Saltpeter
- Pepper
- Bay leaves
- Juniper
- Spices

## Directions

1. Hang the meat in a chimney into which smoke from incompletely burned wood flows.
2. Meat intended for smoking is usually cured, which helps it last longer and gain better flavor.
3. The flavor of meat depends entirely on the curing process.
4. Usually the meat is salted and rubbed with saltpeter, which makes it red.
5. Along the bone, make a cut and insert crushed pepper, and between individual layers place bay leaves, juniper, and spices.
6. Polinate the meat daily with the liquid that forms, or fill a container with salt solution, then no pouring is necessary.
7. Besides pork, eels, geese, and tongues are smoked.
