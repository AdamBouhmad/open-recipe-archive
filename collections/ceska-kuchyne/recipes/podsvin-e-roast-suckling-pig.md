## Ingredients

- suckling pig
- salt
- bread or breadcrumbs
- caraway seeds
- butter
- beer
- lemon

## Directions

1. Place the not too young suckling pig in cold water, then sprinkle with coarse salt and place in hot water, turning it so it is evenly scalded.
2. Clean it, especially the eyes, ears, and tongue which are rubbed with salt.
3. Cut under the neck and pull out the entire windpipe to avoid unpleasant odor and difficulty with subsequent cleaning.
4. Discard the intestines; save the lungs, liver, and heart; carefully remove the bile.
5. Rinse the pig in several waters, dry it, salt and season with caraway.
6. Cut the trotters to the lower joints.
7. Stuff with bread or breadcrumbs, then sew closed.
8. Place cross-sticks on a baking pan, dissolve fresh butter in a pot, coat the pig well with it, and roast slowly in the oven, basting frequently.
9. To prevent the tail and ears from burning, they may be wrapped in paper.
10. When nearly done, coat it with beer instead of butter to give it shine.
11. Before serving, remove the stuffing; place lemon or something green in its mouth.
