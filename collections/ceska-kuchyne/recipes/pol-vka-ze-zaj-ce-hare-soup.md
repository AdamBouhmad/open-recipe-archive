## Ingredients

- hind quarters from two fresh hares
- one quarter pound diced bacon
- one quarter pound butter
- carrots sliced into rounds
- two onions
- celery root
- two ounces sifted flour
- two quarts veal or weak beef broth
- two quarts water
- one pint catawba wine
- a bundle of fresh parsley
- two ounces butter

## Directions

1. Remove bones from hare hind quarters and cut meat into small pieces
2. Place bacon, butter, carrots, onions, and celery root on a casserole and let brown to golden color
3. Add hare and let braise for a while, dust with two ounces of sifted flour and mix well
4. Add two quarts of veal or weak beef broth, two quarts of water, one pint of catawba wine, and a bundle of fresh parsley
5. Let cook for about one hour, then remove the meat and place the best pieces in a soup bowl
6. Strain the broth, pound the vegetables in a mortar, return them to the broth, mix well and strain through a sieve
7. Place near the fire (but it must not boil anymore), add two ounces of butter and pour into a serving bowl with the meat
