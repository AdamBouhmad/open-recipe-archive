## Ingredients

- Hare front quarter
- butter or lard
- thyme
- onion
- lemon zest
- bay leaf
- water
- vinegar
- brown flour
- not quite a full small cup of brown grated gingerbread
- sugar

## Directions

1. Cut the hare front quarter into pieces and place on a clay pot.
2. Add under it a piece of butter or lard, a little thyme, one onion cut into slices, a piece of lemon zest and a bay leaf.
3. Pour over enough water to come about half a finger's height above the hare.
4. Salt it, cover the pot and let it cook.
5. When the hare is almost cooked through, remove it to a plate.
6. Strain the sauce and add a little vinegar to it so the sauce is slightly sour.
7. Dust it lightly with brown flour and add to it not quite a full small cup of brown grated gingerbread.
8. Put a piece of sugar in a small pot, place on high heat and when it browns, watching carefully so it does not burn, dilute it with a little broth.
9. Add this now to the sauce, place the cooked hare in it, let it simmer a little in the sauce, and serve.
