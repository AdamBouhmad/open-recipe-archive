## Ingredients

- four pounds of nice beef from the hindquarters
- salt
- all-spice
- flour
- bacon, thinly sliced
- fresh butter
- hot water

## Directions

1. Pound four pounds of nice beef from the hindquarters with a wooden mallet and slice it into cutlets, about a finger thick, pound again.
2. Sprinkle them then with salt, a little all-spice and a little flour, lay bacon thinly sliced on the cutlets, roll up each cutlet, fasten with a skewer and coat in flour.
3. Melt a piece of fresh butter in a pot, dip the rolled cutlets in it, lay them next to each other in the pot, cover and let the cutlets fry about ten minutes on moderate heat, during which time you must turn them once.
4. Then add to the cutlets from the side as much boiling water until they are submerged halfway, but watch that they do not get wet on the surface.
5. Then cover the pot so that the lid fits completely and let the beef rolls simmer on moderate heat for an hour.
6. Then put the rolls on a plate, remove the skewers from them, arrange fried potatoes around them and add the sauce.
