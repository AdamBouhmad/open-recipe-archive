## Ingredients

- One young rabbit
- Fresh bacon
- Salt
- Three onions
- Thyme
- One bay leaf
- About ten grains of allspice
- Eight grains of pepper
- Two cloves
- Garlic
- Bacon scraps
- Rabbit scraps
- Wine vinegar
- Water
- Butter
- Sour cream
- Flour
- Potatoes for frying

## Directions

1. Skin a beautiful young rabbit, separate the front from the back, wash it clean and remove the skin.
2. Lard it densely with fresh bacon and salt it.
3. Put three onions sliced into rounds on a baking pan, add a pinch of thyme, one bay leaf, about ten grains of allspice, eight grains of pepper, two cloves, a piece of garlic, bacon scraps and rabbit scraps.
4. Place the rabbit on the pan, add a little wine vinegar underneath, some water, and a piece of butter.
5. Roast the rabbit briskly; when it is nearly done, turn it and finish roasting, basting it very frequently with good sour cream in which you have dissolved a piece of fresh butter.
6. When the rabbit is tender enough, cut it into neat pieces and arrange them on a platter.
7. Dust the gravy with a little flour, stir it, press it through a sieve, add a little more sour cream, let it simmer for a few minutes longer and pour into a small bowl.
8. Serve fried potatoes alongside the rabbit. Instead of sour cream, you may also baste the rabbit with butter and pour over the roasting juices.
