## Ingredients

- Nearly ripe but still firm peaches
- Sugar
- Fresh water
- Apple pulp

## Directions

1. Select nearly ripe but still firm peaches, halve them, remove the pit, peel the flesh and cook slowly.
2. As soon as the peaches begin to cook, place them in fresh water to cool.
3. Then cook them several times in dissolved sugar, cover in a container and let sit until the next day.
4. On the second day, drain the sugar, boil it thicker, add the peaches to it, and cook them together several times.
5. On the third day, boil the sugar again thicker with apple pulp until the sixth degree, cook the peaches together with the sugar, skim off the foam, and when cooled, place in containers.
6. It is understood that preserved fruit must always be completely covered with sugar so it does not spoil and lasts longer.
