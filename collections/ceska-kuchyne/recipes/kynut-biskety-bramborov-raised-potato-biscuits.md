## Ingredients

- about 6 medium potatoes
- 2 cups hot milk
- 2 heaping tablespoons butter or lard
- 1 small teaspoon salt
- 1 small teaspoon sugar
- ½ cup flour
- 1 cup liquid yeast or 1 piece of compressed yeast dissolved in 1 tablespoon lukewarm water
- additional flour as needed

## Directions

1. Peel about 6 medium potatoes, cook them, drain the water and mash them well with a wooden spoon.
2. Add to them 2 cups of hot milk, 2 heaping tablespoons of butter or lard, 1 small teaspoon of salt and 1 small teaspoon of sugar; add ½ cup of flour, mix everything well and set aside.
3. When it cools to lukewarm, add 1 cup of liquid yeast or 1 piece of compressed yeast dissolved in 1 tablespoon of lukewarm water, and enough flour to make a thick dough.
4. Beat this mixture for about 5 minutes until well beaten and it doesn't stick to the bowl or hands.
5. Cover, set in a warm place and let it rise for about 3 hours until well leavened.
6. Add more flour so that it becomes a soft dough, roll it quickly but lightly for 15 minutes, then roll out to about finger thickness and cut out biscuits.
7. Place them on a baking sheet greased with lard, cover and set in a warm place to rise.
8. When risen, bake in a hot oven and serve hot.
