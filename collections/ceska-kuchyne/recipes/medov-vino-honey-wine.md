## Ingredients

- 5 liters of honey
- 20 liters of water
- approximately 1/4 kilogram of hops
- Several dekagrams of yeast (to start fermentation)
- Optional: ginger, nutmeg flower, cinnamon

## Directions

1. Mix 5 liters of honey with 20 liters of water, to which add approximately 1/4 kilogram of hops.
2. Let this mixture boil for about a quarter hour, carefully skimming and removing the foam that develops.
3. Add several dekagrams of yeast to the cooled liquid to begin alcoholic fermentation.
4. After fermentation, sugar, ginger, nutmeg flower, cinnamon and similar may be added as desired.
5. To hasten the clarification of the wine, strain through coarse cloth.
6. When the wine is well sealed in a barrel with a stopper, has rested for about a month, has reached its maturity, it can be siphoned into bottles.
7. Such honey wine or strong mead, reminiscent of malmsey wine taste, remains, if bottles were properly corked, unspoiled for years and becomes better the older it is.
