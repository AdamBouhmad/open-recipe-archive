## Ingredients

- 1 beefsteak from the round
- 1 tablespoon lard
- 1 large onion, halved
- Salt
- Boiling water
- 1 cup sour cream
- Flour
- 1 tablespoon ketchup
- Vinegar (optional)

## Directions

1. Remove all skin and fat from the steak and pound it well.
2. Heat a tablespoon of lard in a pan and brown the steak on both sides on a good high heat.
3. Pour boiling water over it, enough to cover the meat, season with salt, add the halved onion, cover, and let simmer gently.
4. If from young meat, it will be tender in an hour.
5. Remove the meat to a dish and strain the liquid.
6. If too much liquid has boiled away, add boiling water to the desired amount of gravy; for 1½ pounds of meat, aim for about one cup of liquid.
7. Add one cup of good sour cream to the gravy.
8. Thicken slightly with flour, stirring well to avoid lumps.
9. Add one tablespoon of ketchup, then taste and adjust for salt or sourness, adding vinegar if needed.
10. Cut the meat into small pieces and return to the gravy to simmer briefly.
