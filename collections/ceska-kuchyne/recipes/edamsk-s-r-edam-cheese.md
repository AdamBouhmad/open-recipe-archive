## Ingredients

- uncollected, freshly milked milk
- rennet
- salt

## Directions

1. Uncollected, freshly milked milk is strained into a vessel, kept warm, and curdled with the help of rennet.
2. The cheese is broken several times in succession, then the vessel is covered and allowed to stand quietly for about a quarter hour, then the cheese is removed and the whey is poured off.
3. The cheese is allowed to settle, is mixed, and is pressed into curds; when it hardens, it is removed and crumbled again.
4. Cheese molds are soaked in whey, cheese is firmly pressed into them to expel the whey.
5. The cheese removed from the molds is crumbled and crumbled again and placed into molds again, which is repeated several times until the cheese hardens completely.
6. In the molds the cheese is often flipped, pressed, then placed into smaller molds, salted, flipped, and turned.
7. In a few weeks the cheese matures and is very tasty.
