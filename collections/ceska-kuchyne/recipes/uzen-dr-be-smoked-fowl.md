## Ingredients

- Young fat geese
- Salt
- Saltpeter (salpeter)
- Finely chopped lemon zest
- Pepper
- Cloves
- Juniper berries
- Nutmeg

## Directions

1. Kill, pluck and wash young fat geese, then cut off the neck and wings close to the body.
2. Split the geese lengthwise along the back, carefully remove the innards and bones.
3. Rub the inside and outside thoroughly with salt mixed with saltpeter and finely chopped lemon zest.
4. Fold both halves together so the inner meat adheres, making it appear whole.
5. Place on a platter, cover with a board and tie tightly.
6. Let rest for eight days, turning daily and basting with the liquid that runs out.
7. Remove, dry somewhat, sprinkle inside with pepper and cloves, fold the halves together again, wrap in paper and smoke for 8 to 10 days over gentle heat.
8. Similarly prepare ducks, capons, poulardes, young hens, partridges and pheasants: pluck well, split lengthwise along the back, remove innards and carefully separate all bones from meat, cut off head and wings.
9. Rub with salt and let rest for 2 days.
10. Dry and rub coarsely with ground pepper, juniper berries, cloves and a little nutmeg.
11. Sew up the rear, sew the prepared fowl into coarse linen and let rest under weight for several hours.
12. Smoke for 8 days over juniper wood smoke.
