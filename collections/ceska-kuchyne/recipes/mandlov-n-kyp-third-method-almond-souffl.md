## Ingredients

- half a kilo almonds
- 24 egg yolks
- quarter kilo (half pound) sugar
- lemon peel
- 7 deciliters (2 tablespoons) wine
- 8 egg yolks
- piece of sugar

## Directions

1. Scald half a kilo almonds, peel them, grate them on a grater. The leftovers that could not be grated are crushed in a mortar and set aside.
2. Add 24 egg yolks one by one to a bowl and constantly beat until they foam and thicken.
3. Crush quarter kilo sugar with which the peel of one lemon was rubbed, and slowly add it to the beaten egg yolks, stirring until it foams again.
4. Add the almonds to the mixture.
5. Pour the mixture into a buttered mold that has been sprinkled with grated biscuits, and bake slowly.
6. For the sauce: put 7 deciliters wine on the stove, add a piece of sugar and stir until it boils. When boiling, add 8 egg yolks and mix well.
7. Pour the sauce over the unmolded soufflé or serve it in a separate dish and sprinkle the soufflé with sugar.
8. The soufflé is also good when the almonds are neither scalded nor peeled. Hazelnuts can be used instead of almonds, which are also only grated without scalding or peeling. The main thing with this soufflé is that everything is well worked.
