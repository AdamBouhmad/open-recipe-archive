## Ingredients

- cucumbers
- fresh cold water
- salt
- water
- vinegar
- spices
- dill

## Directions

1. Pick cucumbers in the evening and place in a bucket, covering with fresh cold water and let soak briefly
2. Prepare salt water so saturated that an egg floats in it
3. Remove cucumbers from plain water and place in salt water where they stay overnight
4. In the morning remove and dry with cloth, which gives them a nice green color
5. Pack cucumbers into a five-gallon barrel with one end removed, alternating layers of cucumbers and dill until the barrel is full
6. Seal the barrel
7. Boil water and vinegar using three-quarters of a pail of water to one-quarter pint of vinegar
8. Add some spices during boiling
9. When cooled, pour through the bung hole
10. Seal tightly
11. The barrel must be full enough to fill the container completely; a five-gallon barrel requires two pails of cucumbers
12. Cucumbers keep for years in both barrels and bottles if kept airtight and will remain nicely green
