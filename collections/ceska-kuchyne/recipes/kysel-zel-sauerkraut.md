## Ingredients

- peeled and cleaned cabbage heads
- salt
- caraway seeds
- dill seeds
- clean cabbage leaves
- clean cloth
- heavy stone

## Directions

1. Peel and clean the cabbage heads, cut them into quarters and remove the cores
2. Shred the cabbage on a grater and salt it in a large barrel or on a clean cloth spread on the floor
3. For fifty large heads, take one and a half pounds of salt, two tablespoons of cleaned caraway seeds and the same amount of dill seeds, and mix well
4. Cover the bottom of a clean wooden barrel or large stone crock with clean cabbage leaves
5. Place a layer of shredded cabbage in it, press it with a wooden mallet, add cabbage again, press again, and repeat until the barrel is full, but never add too large a layer at once or it cannot compress well
6. Place cabbage leaves on the surface, cover the barrel with a clean white cloth, place a well-fitting lid on it and put a clean washed heavy stone on top
7. Set the barrel in a warm place and let it stand about eight days until it sours
8. Then store it in a cold place protected from frost and cover with a clean cloth moistened in cold water, which should be washed and replaced after each use
9. From time to time you must clean the lid, cloth and stone thoroughly so the cabbage does not spoil
10. If the cabbage dries too much, you must add fresh water
11. When taking cabbage for cooking, scrape it from the surface with your fingers so the cabbage stays completely flat and cover and weight it again
12. Some places also add fennel, ground mustard, crushed pepper or juniper to the salt
13. Between individual layers you can also place peeled and cut sour apples without seeds, which gives the cabbage a particularly good flavor
14. If you layer the cabbage in a barrel with white wine, it will also acquire a very delicate flavor
15. Do not oversalt it, as oversalted cabbage will not sour for a long time
16. It is always better to use several smaller barrels than one large one, and if you want to use the cabbage soon, layer it in stone crocks and set it in a warm place so fermentation begins quickly
17. Many experienced housewives put shredded cabbage in a barrel, salt it, add caraway and then squeeze it with their hands for a long time until it releases water, then store it in a barrel and squeeze it quickly by hand; cabbage prepared this way lasts the longest
