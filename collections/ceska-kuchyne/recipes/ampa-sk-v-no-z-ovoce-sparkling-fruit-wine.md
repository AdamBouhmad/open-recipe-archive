## Ingredients

- Juicy, ripe pears
- Optional: raspberry juice

## Directions

1. Sparkling wine is best made from juicy, ripe pears.
2. Grate and press the pears; pour the juice into a barrel or bottles depending on the quantity, tie the opening with cloth only, and let everything rest peacefully.
3. Within two to three days the juice begins to ferment, foam develops and pushes out of the opening.
4. When the foam subsides, fill the bottles and barrel with fermented fruit juice, seal the opening tightly, and place the containers in a cool cellar for several weeks (five to six).
5. After this time, siphon the clarified juice through a tap into strong bottles, seal them tightly, bind with wire and seal with wax or pitch.
6. This method produces very tasty sparkling wine.
7. If three parts pear juice and one part raspberry juice are taken, with the same treatment a very good, lovely wine is obtained.
8. If the wine is allowed to ferment completely and rest in the cellar for a full year so that it loses carbonation, a very good drinkable wine is obtained.
