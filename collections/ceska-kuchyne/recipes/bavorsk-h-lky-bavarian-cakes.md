## Ingredients

- three small cups flour
- two and a half teaspoons baking powder
- one and a half teaspoons cinnamon
- one and a half teaspoons cloves
- one and a half teaspoons nutmeg
- one small cup white sugar
- one small cup yellow sugar (or two small cups white if yellow unavailable)
- two tablespoons syrup
- two tablespoons brandy
- three pieces chocolate from half pound
- four eggs
- two more small cups flour for kneading
- quarter pound unpeeled whole almonds

## Directions

1. Put three small cups flour in a bowl
2. Add two and a half teaspoons baking powder, one and a half teaspoons cinnamon, one and a half teaspoons cloves, one and a half teaspoons nutmeg, one small cup white sugar, and one small cup yellow sugar (or two small cups white if yellow is unavailable)
3. Mix everything well with the flour
4. Add two tablespoons syrup, two tablespoons brandy, three pieces of chocolate, and four eggs
5. Mix well with the flour
6. Pour dough onto a work surface dusted with two more small cups flour and work thoroughly
7. Take a piece, form into a ball and bake in the oven to test: if it rises upward, it is good
8. If it spreads out, add more flour and roll again
9. Work dough on the work surface, add quarter pound unpeeled whole almonds, and mix again
10. Form dough into long rolls the length of the baking sheet and as thick as an egg, or slightly thinner
11. Place them beside each other, half inch apart
12. Brush with egg white and bake in a not-too-hot oven
13. Must bake for over half an hour, then a piece can be tested
14. If inside it is like coarse grain, it must bake longer with the oven open so it becomes like rye bread when cut
15. Turn out and let cool five minutes, then separate one from another and cut into finger-thick pieces
