## Ingredients

- Eel
- celery
- parsley
- carrot
- onion
- bay leaf
- sage
- peppercorns
- allspice
- salt
- vinegar
- wine
- white flour
- cold water
- egg yolks
- butter
- white pepper
- nutmeg
- lemon

## Directions

1. Fix the eel to the table with a fork
2. Make an incision around the head just below the skin and pull off the skin using a cloth, being careful not to break the gall bladder which is located under the skin near the head
3. Clean and cut the eel into pieces
4. Place celery, parsley, carrot and onion (all sliced) in a pot along with a bay leaf, a piece of sage, several peppercorns and allspice
5. Salt and add enough vinegar and let simmer for half an hour
6. Add the cut eel pieces to this sauce, add a glass of wine and enough more vinegar so the eel is completely covered
7. Continue simmering gently for another quarter hour
8. Arrange the eel on a dish and strain the sauce through a fine sieve into a serving dish
9. For the hollandaise-style sauce: place a spoonful of white flour in a bowl, add a little cold water and mix well, add six egg yolks, add four spoonfuls of fresh butter and mix well, add twelve white peppercorns and a little nutmeg
10. Pour in a pint of the water in which the eel was cooked
11. Place over heat and stir constantly until it is about to boil, but do not let it boil
12. Squeeze in the juice of half a lemon and strain through a fine sieve into a serving bowl
