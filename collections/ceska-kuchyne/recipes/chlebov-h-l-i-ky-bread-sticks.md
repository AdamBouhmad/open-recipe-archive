## Ingredients

- ordinary bread dough
- about 1 pound or 1 large quart of dough
- 1 tablespoon lard

## Directions

1. When making bread with liquid yeast, after the dough made from sourdough is rolled out and worked, take a piece of dough according to how many sticks you want.
2. For a piece of dough about 1 pound or 1 large quart in size, take 1 tablespoon of lard and work it in well and let it rise.
3. When risen, take it to the work surface, roll it a little and then cut into pieces which you roll into long sticks.
4. In the meantime, grease the grooves in the pan with lard, place the rolled sticks into them and let them rise for three quarters of an hour in a warm place.
5. Before putting them in the oven, make a nice hot fire so that the oven is nicely hot at the bottom, which is necessary for the iron pan to heat properly.
6. If needed, cover the top of the pan with paper.
7. Bake for about 25 minutes and when nicely baked golden, place on a plate.
8. They taste best when warm, like biscuits, but when they cool down, they are also eaten.
