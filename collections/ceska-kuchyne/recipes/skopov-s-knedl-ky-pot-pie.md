## Ingredients

- two pounds of mutton from the leg
- half a small onion
- garlic clove
- salt
- water
- two cups of flour
- small spoon of butter
- small spoon of baking powder
- one egg yolk
- small cup of milk

## Directions

1. Place two pounds of mutton from the leg in a pot, add half a small onion cut into pieces, a clove of garlic rubbed with salt, salt, pour water to cover the meat, cover and let it braise.
2. When the meat is tender, prepare dough as follows: Take two cups of flour, crumble into it a small spoon of butter, salt, add a small spoon of baking powder, one egg yolk and a small cup of milk, about enough so that the dough is like for biscuits.
3. Sprinkle the rolling board with flour, place the dough on it, work it a little with your hands, roll it out to a finger's thickness and cut into squares about three fingers wide and long.
4. Check that there is enough broth on the meat; if the meat is not covered with broth, pour hot water over it and then cover the meat with these squares, one next to the other.
5. Cover well so that no steam escapes and let it boil for 10 minutes.
6. Then remove the dough and meat with a fork onto a platter and make a gravy from the remaining broth.
7. Mix a spoon of flour in half a cup of milk and pour into the broth in the pot; if not salty enough, salt and pepper it and pour over the dumplings and meat.
