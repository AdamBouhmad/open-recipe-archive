## Ingredients

- Fresh yeast (beer or pressed)
- water
- sugar
- milk
- flour (1 kilogram)
- 12 dekagrams (8 lots) butter
- grated lemon peel
- 6 dekagrams (4 lots) salt
- 3 dekagrams (2 lots) yeast
- plum jam

## Directions

1. Prepare the yeast: if using beer yeast, rinse with water until clear, then add a piece of sugar, a little milk and flour, and set in a warm place to ferment; if using pressed yeast, add water, milk, sugar and flour, and set in a warm place.
2. Sift 1 kilogram of flour. Cream 12 dekagrams (8 lots) of butter and add grated lemon peel.
3. Add most of the flour, incorporating 3 dekagrams (2 lots) of yeast, and add as much warm milk as the flour will absorb, working until the dough does not stick to the spoon.
4. Scrape down the mixing spoon and bowl, dust the dough with flour, set in a warm place and allow to ferment for about an hour.
5. Roll out the dough and cut vdolky with a small form or shape by hand.
6. Bake on a stove or on a greased sheet, or fry in butter like pastries.
7. Thin plum jam with water, add sugar, cinnamon or lemon peel, simmer briefly, and use to spread on the vdolky.
8. Sprinkle jam-topped vdolky with poppy seeds, gingerbread or cheese curds, and add a dollop of sour cream in the center.
