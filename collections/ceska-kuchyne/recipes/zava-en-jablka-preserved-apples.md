## Ingredients

- beautiful, juicy apples, preferably sweet rather than sour
- sugar
- water
- cinnamon bark

## Directions

1. First the apples must be beautiful, juicy, preferably sweet rather than sour.
2. Peel them, cut into quarters, remove the cores well and anything that is spoiled; if the apple is quite large, cut it into more pieces and immediately throw them into cold water so they do not brown.
3. When finished with this, put a pot on the stove, add water according to the amount of fruit and for one gallon of water add about one quart of sugar and let it boil.
4. Then skim the foam and add the fruit and add a few pieces of broken cinnamon bark and let them boil until they can be easily pierced with a fork.
5. Then immediately pour into prepared jars which you have previously washed well with hot water and then cold water.
6. Most importantly, the lids must be well washed, particularly those that have been used before. Boil them in soda water, then rinse in several clean waters and if anything is stuck on, scrape it well.
7. Also each time when filling use new rubber strips.
