## Ingredients

- cucumbers of medium size
- green dill
- grape leaves or cherry leaves
- water
- salt
- sugar
- rye bread crusts, dried

## Directions

1. Select cucumbers of medium size.
2. Prepare a stone pot.
3. Line the bottom of the pot with green dill and grape leaves (or cherry leaves if grape leaves are unavailable).
4. Arrange a layer of cucumbers in the pot, then add dill and leaves again, and continue layering until the pot is full.
5. Boil water and salt it until it tastes as salty as a well-salted soup.
6. Add a small amount of sugar, about one spoon per four-gallon pot.
7. Let the water cool and pour it lukewarm over the cucumbers.
8. Before weighting down with a stone, add two or three dried rye bread crusts to the cucumbers.
9. Let it sit for one or two days in a warm place until it ferments.
10. Then move it to a cool place such as a cellar.
11. By the fifth or sixth day the cucumbers will be properly aged and over the following 14 days they will be most delicious, though after that they become increasingly sour.
