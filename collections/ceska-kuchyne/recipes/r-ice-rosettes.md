## Ingredients

- four whole eggs
- one egg white reserved
- eight tablespoons sugar
- six tablespoons sweet cream
- a bit of salt
- four tablespoons wine (optional)
- flour

## Directions

1. Reserve one egg white in a bowl for later use
2. Mix together the remaining eggs, sugar, sweet cream, and salt
3. If available, add wine; if not, it can be omitted but the result will be less flavorful
4. Mix well and add enough flour to make the dough slightly thinner than noodle dough
5. Roll out very thin, like noodle dough
6. Use five different cutters of graduated sizes, each noticeably smaller than the next, to cut out circles
7. Place on a cloth dusted with flour
8. Beat the reserved egg white until stiff peaks form
9. Brush the center of the largest circle with beaten egg white and stack the remaining four circles on top, largest to smallest, pressing gently in the center with a finger moistened in egg white to create a dimple and help the petals hold together
10. Be careful not to apply egg white to a large area, or the petals will not separate properly
11. Heat lard in a pan and fry the rosettes carefully until they are red but not burnt
12. Beat one egg white into stiff peaks
13. Place a piece of stiff beaten egg white in the center of each rosette, top with a few grains of red sugar, or fill with jam instead
14. The petals should rise beautifully during frying to resemble an actual rose
