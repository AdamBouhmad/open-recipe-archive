## Ingredients

- meat from four roasted pigeons
- bones from the pigeons
- two quarts beef broth
- one onion
- one parsley root
- one celery stalk
- one carrot
- salt
- pinch of cumin
- pinch of marjoram
- several cloves
- a piece of mace
- several grains of pepper
- a spoon of boiling Madeira wine
- six hard-boiled egg yolks
- a piece of butter

## Directions

1. Remove meat from four roasted pigeons and break the bones, place them in a pot with two quarts of beef broth
2. Add one onion, one parsley root, one celery stalk, and one carrot, all cut into small pieces
3. Salt it and let it cook for one hour, then strain through a cloth into another pot
4. In a small cup, place a pinch of cumin, a pinch of marjoram, several cloves, a piece of mace, and several grains of pepper, pour a spoon of boiling Madeira wine over it, cover and let it steep
5. Pound the pigeon meat in a mortar with six hard-boiled egg yolks and a piece of butter
6. Mix it well into the broth, strain once more through a cloth and place on a hot surface, but it must not boil anymore
7. Strain the wine into the broth, add a piece of good butter, stir well and pour onto a serving dish with fried bread cubes or small croutons
