## Ingredients

- 1 quart water
- 1 pint vinegar
- 2 medium onions
- 1 celery root
- Parsley root
- Carrot root
- 1 clove garlic
- 2 bay leaves
- Thyme
- 15 grains allspice
- 15 cloves
- 2 small pieces ginger
- 2 pounds prunes
- 3 pints beer
- ½ lemon peel
- ½ pound rye bread
- ½ pound gingerbread
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 1 cup raisins
- 1 cup nuts
- Juice of ½ lemon
- ½ cup sugar

## Directions

1. Put water and vinegar in a pot and bring to boil
2. Add chopped onions, celery, parsley root, carrot root, garlic, bay leaves, thyme, allspice, cloves, ginger
3. Simmer for about a quarter hour
4. Place fish in the broth
5. While fish cooks, prepare the sauce: cook 2 pounds of prunes until soft, drain the water into a cup
6. Strain the prunes and chop finely, return to the prune water
7. Add 3 pints beer, long-cut lemon peel, diced rye bread, grated gingerbread, butter, raisins, nuts, lemon juice, and sugar
8. Mix everything together with the prunes
9. When fish is cooked, remove it and strain the broth
10. Return broth to heat and add the sauce
11. Make a light roux with butter, add a spoonful to the sauce and simmer
12. Arrange fish on a platter and pour sauce over it
13. Carp is most commonly prepared this way, but sturgeon and other fish that do not fall apart can also be prepared this way
