## Ingredients

- 1 pike
- pike head or carp head
- rosemary
- thyme
- bay leaf
- peppercorns
- allspice
- saffron
- onion, finely chopped
- 4 hard eggs
- 3 1/2 deciliter (1 spoon) Austrian wine
- vinegar diluted with water, 5 1/4 deciliter (1 1/2 spoons)
- celery, split
- parsley
- carrot
- garlic
- lemon zest
- egg whites

## Directions

1. Scrape the pike, clean thoroughly and cook in salted water
2. Let cool, remove skin from the head, cut off the head, and split the pike lengthwise in half
3. Remove all bones and place pike in a mold or on a platter
4. Wash the pike head or carp head, rosemary, thyme, and bay leaf; add peppercorns, allspice, a little saffron, finely chopped onion, and 4 hard eggs
5. Pound everything in a mortar
6. Pour 3 1/2 deciliter of Austrian wine and vinegar diluted with water (5 1/4 deciliter) on a baking sheet
7. Salt lightly and add split celery, parsley, carrot, a little garlic, chopped onion, lemon zest, and stiff snow from 2 egg whites
8. Add the pounded mixture, boil for a quarter hour
9. Pour it through cloth tied to the legs of an overturned chair, let it drip
10. Pour half the liquid over the pike and put half in a separate container
11. Place both in a cold place
12. When cooled, turn out the aspic from the container, cut decorative pieces with a mold, and arrange around the pike
13. This method can also be used for carp, tench, and salmon; generally all white-fleshed fish
