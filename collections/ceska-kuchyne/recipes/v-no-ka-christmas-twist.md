## Ingredients

- 8 small cups flour
- half pound butter
- two whole eggs or four yolks
- 1 small cup sugar dissolved in milk
- grated lemon zest
- salt
- ground cloves or ground and sifted anise
- piece of pressed yeast, dissolved in sour cream or milk
- 1 small cup sultanas
- half small cup chopped almonds

## Directions

1. Put 8 small cups flour into a bowl, crumble half pound butter in it
2. Beat in two whole eggs or four yolks, add 1 small cup sugar dissolved in milk, a little grated lemon zest, salt, ground cloves or ground and sifted anise, a piece of pressed yeast dissolved in sour cream or milk and make the dough so thick as for dumplings
3. Beat it so long until it does not stick to the bowl or spoon, cover it with a warmed cloth, let it rise
4. Then add 1 small cup sultanas and half small cup chopped almonds
5. Then take the dough out onto a floured board, knead it again, divide it into four larger and five smaller pieces, let them rise a little more, then roll them out
6. From the first four make not too long a braid, place it on a sheet on paper brushed with butter
7. Make then a braid from three smaller pieces, place it straight on the first, press it on the ends
8. Finally twist two smallest pieces, place them on top of the second braid, arrange it nicely, press the ends together
9. Let the Christmas twist rise in warmth, then brush it with beaten egg and bake in moderate oven
10. If the Christmas twist is more risen, it must be baked at stronger heat
11. If you put whole eggs into the batter, it is more moist; with only yolks it is more porous; whole eggs are usually put into the Christmas twist
