## Ingredients

- fresh peas
- water
- salt
- sugar
- butter
- peppercorns (whole grains)
- parsley hearts
- lemon

## Directions

1. Place fresh peas in boiling water
2. Spread on a sieve so water drains
3. Simmer with butter, water, salt, and sugar until half softened
4. Or cook in salted water, and when water drains, pour into jars; per half-liter of peas add 15 grams of crushed sugar, pour with warm water, tie with parchment paper and bladder, wrap the jars with straw, place in a pot on hay and cook for three quarters of an hour
5. Peas can also be stored completely cooked in vessels; per liter of peas add one liter of water, a piece of butter, 25 grams of sugar, a small teaspoon of salt, and cook until peas soften; cover with the water in which they were cooked, seal and cook slowly in water for only half an hour
6. Peas can also be preserved raw with salt; add whole peppercorns, place in jars, cover with butter, tie with bladder and place in a cool location
7. Parsley can be preserved by the last method; remove the hearts, salt the parsley lightly, place in a vessel and cover with butter and tie
8. Lemon zest is peeled from the lemon, freed from white pith, finely cut, salted, filled into a vessel and tied; this way it lasts even a quarter of a year
