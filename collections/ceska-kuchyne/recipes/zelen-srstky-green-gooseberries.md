## Ingredients

- Beautiful, large, not fully ripe gooseberries
- Fresh water
- Sugar

## Directions

1. Select beautiful, large, but not fully ripe gooseberries and clean them.
2. Carefully remove the seeds with straws and place them in fresh water.
3. Mash them thoroughly until they soften.
4. Heat the water with the gooseberries, but do not boil; as soon as the gooseberries float to the surface, remove from heat and cover the pot so they finish cooking away from the stove.
5. When cooled, place in cold water and then into clarified boiled sugar; cover again and let sit quietly.
6. On the second day, drain the sugar, boil it separately, and when it has boiled well, add the gooseberries to it, boil everything again, cover and let sit quietly.
7. Repeat the same on the third day.
8. On the fourth day, boil the sugar separately again until it thickens.
9. On the fifth day, boil it again longer, then add the gooseberries to it, boil with it and skim off any foam that rises, as with each previous boiling.
10. Place the prepared gooseberries in containers and when cooled, bind them well with bladder.
