## Ingredients

- Beefsteak slices, about an inch thick
- Salt and pepper
- Fresh butter
- Fat or lard for greasing (optional)

## Directions

1. Cut the steak slices slightly thicker than usual, almost an inch thick, and pound as for frying.
2. Place in wide, flat wire tongs (broiler) without any other preparation and hold one side over hot coals.
3. When browned on one side, turn the tongs over and cook the other side.
4. When sufficiently cooked, remove to a heated dish, salt and pepper it, and place pieces of fresh butter on top, which will melt on its own.
5. Serve hot.
6. If tongs are not available, you may broil on a griddle, though the flavor will not be quite as good as broiling over coals.
7. Heat the griddle dry over high heat until smoking, then rub with a piece of fat so the steak does not stick.
8. Place the steak slice on it; when cooked on one side, turn it over.
9. When cooked on both sides, salt, pepper, butter it with fresh butter, and serve hot.
