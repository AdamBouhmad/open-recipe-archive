## Ingredients

- 2 pounds beef
- fresh cold water
- salt
- vegetables in season: celery, celery leaves, parsley root, parsley leaves, piece of onion, leek, cabbage, piece of carrot, piece of turnip or cauliflower
- alternatively in winter or early spring: small handful of peas, clove of garlic, pinch of caraway, piece of onion, pinch of dried mushrooms
- optionally: piece of beef liver fried in butter with onion, or raw beef liver
- beef kidney and bones

## Directions

1. Rinse 2 pounds of beef in clean cold water and pound it with a wooden mallet
2. Do not pound the meat before rinsing, as it will lose much of its juices
3. Place the meat in a pot filled with clean fresh water and let it simmer slowly
4. When the water stops foaming, salt it and add vegetables proportionally to the amount of meat
5. In summer add: celery, celery leaves, parsley root, parsley leaves, piece of onion, leek, cabbage, piece of carrot, piece of turnip or cauliflower
6. If vegetables are not available, as in winter or early spring, add: small handful of peas, clove of garlic, pinch of caraway, piece of onion, pinch of dried mushrooms
7. These ingredients may be varied according to taste or preference
8. For improvement, optionally add a piece of beef liver fried in butter with onion, or raw beef liver
9. Beef kidney and bones will also enhance the flavor
10. Once vegetables are added, let the meat simmer slowly for about 2.5 to 3 hours, but not vigorously as this makes the soup unpalatable
11. If water evaporates during cooking, replenish with boiling water so the soup continues to boil without interruption
12. When the meat is tender, strain the soup into a smaller pot and add garnishes according to the following recipes
13. This recipe serves 6 people
