## Ingredients

- rye dough saved from previous baking, or two cups lukewarm water
- dried yeast or compressed yeast or one cup liquid yeast
- rye flour as needed
- baking soda
- salt to taste
- fennel or caraway seeds, optional

## Directions

1. For the rye bread starter, it is best to keep a piece of dough from one baking to the next, about two tablespoons
2. The day before baking, pour about two cups of lukewarm water over the dough to soften it
3. In the evening, mix it well, add flour to make a thin starter like for pancakes and let it rise in a warm place overnight
4. If you do not have dough from the previous baking, take two cups of lukewarm water in the evening before baking, in which you have soaked one square of dry yeast or a piece of compressed yeast, or pour in one cup of liquid yeast
5. Add enough rye flour so that the dough is slightly thicker than for pancakes, mix it well into a smooth starter
6. If desired, add fennel or caraway seeds or both and let it rise well in a warm place until morning
7. In the morning, add one quart of lukewarm water to the starter, in which you have mixed one quarter teaspoon of baking soda, season with salt, add as much flour as needed and work the dough thoroughly with a wooden spoon so that it does not stick to the bowl or hands
8. Dust again with flour, cover with a warm cloth and let it rise again for about one and one-half hours
9. Then roll out the dough on a floured board, shape into a loaf, place on a sheet, let it rise again, place it in a well-heated oven and bake for at least two hours
10. For one loaf, one usually counts one quart of water and four pounds of flour; however, each cook must learn the exact amount of flour from her own experience
