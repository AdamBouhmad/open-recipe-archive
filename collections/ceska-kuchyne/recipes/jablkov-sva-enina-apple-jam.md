## Ingredients

- peeled and sliced apples
- water (one cup water per gallon of apples)
- sugar (half cup sugar per cup of pulp)
- finely chopped lemon zest

## Directions

1. Put peeled and sliced apples in a pot, add water (one cup per gallon of apples) and cook with frequent stirring for about 20 minutes until the apples are cooked into a pulp.
2. Press them through a sieve or thin cloth and put the pulp back in the pot to cook.
3. When the mixture has come to a boil several times, add sugar—half cup per cup of pulp—and a little finely chopped lemon zest.
4. Cook another half hour or three-quarters of an hour with frequent stirring, and when done, pour hot into jars.
