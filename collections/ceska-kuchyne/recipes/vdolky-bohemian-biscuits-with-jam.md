## Ingredients

- 2 cups lukewarm milk
- a piece of butter
- 2 whole eggs
- a little ground cardamom
- a little salt
- a piece of compressed or dried yeast dissolved in milk
- white flour to make a thinner dough than for dumplings
- jam or preserves
- curd cheese, poppy seeds, or grated gingerbread for topping
- melted fresh butter for pouring

## Directions

1. In 2 cups of lukewarm milk, dissolve a piece of butter, crack into it 2 whole eggs, add a little ground cardamom, a little salt, a piece of compressed or dried yeast softened in milk, and enough white flour to make a thinner dough than for dumplings.
2. Work the dough until it is completely smooth, let it rise, then take it out onto a floured work surface, make small dimples, let them rise well, and bake them on a dry or butter-greased sheet on both sides.
3. Or you can fry them in lard.
4. Then spread them with jam, sprinkle with either curd cheese, poppy seeds, or grated gingerbread and pour melted fresh butter over them.
