## Ingredients

- young tender cauliflower
- salt
- water
- white vinegar
- sugar
- mixed spices

## Directions

1. Take young, tender cauliflower (which is most flavorful).
2. Clean it thoroughly and cut it into small pieces, about half an inch to an inch long.
3. Salt it well and let it sit for 16 hours.
4. Pour off the salt water and replace with fresh water, repeating this until all the salt is rinsed out.
5. Spread it on a colander to drain the water.
6. Meanwhile, prepare good white vinegar in an enameled pot and place it on the stove.
7. For every cup of vinegar, add an equal amount of sugar.
8. For every four cups of vinegar, add one spoon of mixed spices.
9. Bring everything to a boil.
10. Add the drained cauliflower (which must not be waterlogged) and boil well for 15 minutes.
11. Pack it hot into jars and seal well.
12. Alternatively, cauliflower preserved in mustard is also good: prepare as above but use less sugar and when it comes to a boil and has cooked for about 10 minutes, thicken it with mustard powder mixed in vinegar in a cup (to prevent lumps), and preserve like a sauce. This will be thicker. For one head of cauliflower use two cups of vinegar, one cup of sugar, a small teaspoon of mustard, and two large spoons of mustard powder.
