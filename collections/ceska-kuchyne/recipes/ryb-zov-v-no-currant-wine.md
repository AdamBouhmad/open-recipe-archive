## Ingredients

- very ripe currant berries
- water
- sugar
- currant wine (when fermented)

## Directions

1. Strip the very ripe currant berries from their stems
2. Press them
3. Add as much water as juice obtained
4. To each liter of this juice, add half a kilogram of sugar if the wine is to last long, or less sugar if the wine is to be drunk soon
5. Pour the prepared juice best into a wine barrel, but not filling it completely, best if a few liters still need to be added
6. Place the barrel in a cellar where it rests peacefully
7. The wine ferments in a short time, and when fermentation is complete, top up with fermented currant wine and close the upper opening so that air has access inside, then after a time hammer the cork in firmly
8. The wine remains in the barrel until February, when it is carefully drawn off, not from the tap but always so that only completely clear wine is obtained
9. All vessels must be very clean, well rinsed with wine
10. Fill bottles up to the neck; the cork must not be pressed in firmly or they easily burst
11. Bottles must be protected from frost or they readily crack
12. Transfer the wine, so that it is more certainly clear, through thick cloth before pouring into bottles
