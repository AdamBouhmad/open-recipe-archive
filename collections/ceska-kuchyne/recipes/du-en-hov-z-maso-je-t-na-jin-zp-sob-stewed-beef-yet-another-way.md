## Ingredients

- three pounds of beef from the hindquarters
- wine vinegar
- bacon, cut into thick strips
- salt
- beef broth
- one onion, sliced
- a piece of carrot
- bay leaf
- peppercorns
- new spice
- clove
- one heaping spoon of butter
- one spoon flour
- one spoon sugar
- two cups of beef broth or hot water
- lemon peel

## Directions

1. Pound about three pounds of beef from the hindquarters well with a wooden mallet, put it in a pot, pour a little wine vinegar over it and let it marinate thus for about twenty-four hours.
2. When you then want to prepare the meat, lard it with bacon cut into thick strips, put it in a pot, salt it, add a little vinegar, a little meat beef broth, one sliced onion, a piece of carrot, bay leaf, several peppercorns, new spice, clove, cover it and let it stew slowly until tender.
3. Meanwhile melt one heaping spoon of butter in another pan, put one spoon of flour and one spoon of sugar in it and brown it to make a roux.
4. To this gradually pour in about two cups of beef broth, from which you have skimmed off all fat, or hot water if you do not have broth, and the sauce from the stewed meat, from which you have also skimmed off all fat.
5. Let it boil about a quarter hour until you have from it a thick, brown, pleasantly sour sauce.
6. When you have skimmed off any additional fat, pour the sauce over the stewed meat and let it boil a little more.
7. Then place the meat on a plate, pour part of the sauce over it, bring to the table and serve the rest of the sauce in a separate bowl.
8. You can also add fried potatoes or dumplings to this.
