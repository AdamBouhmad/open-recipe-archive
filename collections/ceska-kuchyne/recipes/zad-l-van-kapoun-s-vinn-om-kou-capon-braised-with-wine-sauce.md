## Ingredients

- 1 capon, divided into 4 pieces
- butter
- 2 tablespoons water
- 3 1/2 deciliters (jezdlík) Austrian wine
- salt
- carrot
- parsley
- a piece of celery
- cabbage
- capon liver, stomach and heart
- 1/8 kilo (1/8 pound) calf liver
- 2 hard eggs
- fried bread roll
- cloves
- ginger
- pepper
- 2 cloves of garlic
- marjoram
- a piece of onion
- green parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. Divide the capon into 4 pieces, add butter, 2 tablespoons water, 3 1/2 deciliters (jezdlík) Austrian wine, salt, carrot, parsley, a piece of celery and cabbage; braise everything.
2. When everything is tender, make a pale broth, strain it into the sauce and boil it.
3. Cook the capon liver, stomach and heart with 1/8 kilo (1/8 pound) of calf liver, then chop everything as finely as possible with 2 hard eggs and fried bread roll; add a bit of cloves, ginger, pepper, 2 cloves of garlic, marjoram and a piece of onion to this; mix everything together and bake the mixture on a greased baking sheet, stirring carefully so it does not burn.
4. Place the baked mixture on a platter around the capon and garnish with green parsley.
