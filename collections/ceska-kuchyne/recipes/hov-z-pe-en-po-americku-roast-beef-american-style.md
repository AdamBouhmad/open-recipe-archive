## Ingredients

- beef from the loin
- salt
- pepper
- fresh butter
- hot water
- onion

## Directions

1. Roasted beef is best when it can be roasted in a larger piece, 6 pounds or more; for a small family, 4 pounds (from two ribs) is sufficient
2. Have the butcher prepare a piece from the loin, remove the ribs, wrap the meat into a cylindrical shape, skewer, and tie it
3. Salt and pepper the meat, place in a deep baking dish, spread about one spoonful of fresh butter on it, pour in a small cup of hot water, and place in a hot oven to roast
4. At first it requires maintaining a good fire so the meat stays juicy; later reduce the heat
5. When it has been roasting for half an hour, sprinkle chopped onion on it and turn it
6. Calculate that the meat should be in the oven for a quarter hour per pound of weight, so a four-pound piece about one hour, larger pieces longer
7. When the meat is roasted, prepare a gravy from the rendered fat
8. If the meat was nicely fatty and much fat rendered, pour off part of it and add a small cup of boiling water; if not necessary, do not dilute the fat
9. Mix one spoonful of flour in a little water and mix into the fat, let it simmer, and serve in a separate dish as gravy for the meat and potatoes
