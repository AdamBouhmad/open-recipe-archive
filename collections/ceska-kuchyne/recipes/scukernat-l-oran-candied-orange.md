## Ingredients

- orange
- water
- sugar
- lemon juice (optional)

## Directions

1. Using a sharp, pointed knife, carefully peel the orange skin in small pieces, such that alternating strips of peeled and unpeeled skin remain on the orange.
2. Pierce the orange in several places with a thin pointed knife.
3. Place the whole orange in clean fresh water.
4. After several hours, remove the orange from this water and place it in fresh water, in which it should be cooked until soft.
5. Then place it in thin sugar syrup and leave it soaking overnight.
6. On the second day, remove the orange, prepare thick sugar syrup, place the orange in it and boil again. Repeat this on the third and fourth days, until the sugar cooks down into solid crystalline pieces.
7. On the fifth day, the sugar is completely hard and may be transferred to containers.
8. The orange must gradually be cooked according to this method over several days, first in weakly sugared, later in strongly sugared water, so that if it were fully cooked in strongly sugared water on the first day, it would shrivel greatly, whereas in this way it retains its complete shape.
9. A few drops of lemon juice may be added to prevent the sugar from hardening too much.
