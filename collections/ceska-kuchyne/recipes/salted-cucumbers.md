## Ingredients

- Green, medium-sized, firm cucumbers
- Fresh well water
- Salt
- Cherry and grape leaves
- Grape leaves
- Dill greens
- Black rye bread or sourdough starter in a wine leaf (optional)

## Directions

1. Soak green, medium-sized, firm cucumbers in fresh well water for 24 hours, then remove and dry them
2. Rub the barrel where the cucumbers will be stored with salt
3. Line the bottom with cherry and grape leaves, and dill greens
4. Add the cucumbers and cover them again with the same leaves
5. Layer cucumbers and leaves alternately until the barrel is full
6. Fill the barrel completely with salted well water prepared like broth, ensuring it is completely filled
7. Do not over-salt the water as this would interrupt fermentation
8. Seal the barrel completely; keep it in a warm kitchen in the first days, and when cucumbers begin to ferment, move to a cold room
9. To speed up fermentation, add either a piece of black rye bread or sourdough starter wrapped in a wine leaf
10. Daily, rock the barrel in the cellar where it was placed at the beginning of fermentation, and turn it over for eight days
11. When the liquid dries out, add new fresh liquid through the hole where the cork is located
12. The cucumbers will be tasty, keep for a long time, and during use must always remain in the liquid, otherwise they will easily become moldy
