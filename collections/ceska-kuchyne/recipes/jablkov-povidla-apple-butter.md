## Ingredients

- one bushel apples
- one gallon cooked sweet must

## Directions

1. For one bushel of apples, use one gallon of cooked sweet must from the press.
2. Apples must be ripe so they cook easily; peel them nicely, remove any spoiled parts or simply cut them out.
3. Put enough apples in the must so they are submerged.
4. When soft, remove with a skimmer and add fresh ones, continuing until all apples are cooked.
5. Mash and return to the pot, cook over gentle heat with constant stirring.
6. A wooden spoon is best for this.
7. About one hour before it is done, add spices to taste—some cinnamon, clove, and allspice.
8. This butter takes considerable effort as it must be cooked until very thick, but it is then good and quite inexpensive.
