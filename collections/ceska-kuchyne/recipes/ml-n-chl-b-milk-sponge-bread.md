## Ingredients

- two cups boiling water
- one teaspoon sugar
- one quarter teaspoon salt
- one quarter teaspoon baking soda
- flour as needed
- milk for the second mixing

## Directions

1. Pour two cups of boiling water into a pitcher and add one teaspoon sugar, one quarter teaspoon salt, and one quarter teaspoon baking soda
2. Let it cool until you can hold your finger in it
3. Add flour as needed to make a nice thick starter and beat for two minutes
4. Place the pitcher in a pot of hot water, but not so hot that it scalds, and maintain the water at a steady temperature until it rises nicely
5. If you start early in the morning and watch it, you will have the starter risen by eleven o'clock
6. You can then proceed as with previously made starters, but use milk instead of water for the final dough making
7. Let the dough rise nicely, form it into loaves, place in baking pans and let rise again, but do not let them overrise or the bread will be full of holes and unpalatable
8. Bake by the previous method
