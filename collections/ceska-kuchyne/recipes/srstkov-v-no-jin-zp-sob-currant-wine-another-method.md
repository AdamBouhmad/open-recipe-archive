## Ingredients

- unripe but fully developed currants of good flavorful varieties
- water
- finely ground sugar
- cognac

## Directions

1. Select, clean, and crush unripe but fully developed currants of good, flavorful varieties in containers
2. After 24 hours, pour off the juice, pour a little lukewarm water on the currants (about 1 liter of water to 4 liters of currants), mix everything thoroughly again
3. After twelve hours, strain everything and mix all the juices obtained
4. To about 20 liters of juice, add 6 kilograms of finely ground sugar
5. Let it ferment in a warm room
6. After two to three days, pour the juice into a barrel (preferably a wine barrel), adding 2 liters of cognac to every 20 liters of wine
7. Close the barrel and place it in a moderate, not too cold cellar
8. For the wine to be good, it must remain in the barrel for five years, transferring it from time to time
9. If it is to be drunk after a year, always draw it into bottles
