## Ingredients

- fresh morning milk (not collected)
- collected evening milk
- cream (optional)
- rennet
- salt

## Directions

1. Prepare from fresh morning milk not collected, to which collected evening milk is added, and if the cheese is to be especially fatty, cream is also added; everything is heated in a pot and rennet is added.
2. Sometimes only cream is added to morning milk, making the cheese fattier.
3. The fattier the cheese should be, the higher temperature must be given to the milk before rennet is added; for leaner cheese the temperature can be lower, thus the milk temperature before curdling is +26-30° R.
4. The cheese curdled in the pot is broken and cut with wooden knives, then placed into molds with skimming spoons.
5. Molds for brick cheese are made from four small wooden, perforated boards, bottomless, and are placed on little tables fitted with grooves so that the whey, expelled by the weight of the cheese, may drain well.
6. The cheese in molds is turned twice a day, flipped, and salted, which is repeated for about seven days, then the cheese is placed in cellars on shelves to ripen and mature.
