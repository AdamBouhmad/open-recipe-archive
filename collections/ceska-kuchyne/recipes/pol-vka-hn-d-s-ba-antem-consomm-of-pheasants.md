## Ingredients

- fresh bacon sliced into strips
- onions sliced into rounds
- beef and veal slices
- poultry necks
- livers and gizzards from pheasants and partridges
- three parsley roots
- one large celery
- two carrots
- one leek
- beef broth
- one small celery
- parsley root
- three hot salted water-blanched cabbage halves
- one young pheasant salted
- two egg whites
- bread or croutons

## Directions

1. Line a pot or pan with bacon slices, then make a layer of sliced onions, then arrange beef and veal slices, poultry necks, livers and gizzards from pheasants and partridges
2. Add three parsley roots, one large celery, two carrots, one leek, all sliced, cover with a lid and let braise until brown
3. Occasionally check and pour a few spoons of beef broth to prevent burning
4. In a second pot, line the bottom with bacon, sprinkle with thinly sliced onions, add one small celery and parsley root both sliced into small pieces, about three hot salted water-blanched cabbage halves, and place a salted young pheasant on top (if serving more than 12 people, two pheasants are needed)
5. Cover with a lid and let braise until brown, frequently baste with beef broth to prevent burning
6. Remove the cabbage and pheasant, pour the liquid and braised contents from the first pot into a pot, add good beef broth and let cook about half an hour
7. Strain it and wait until it begins to boil again, then add about two egg whites' worth of snow and let cook a moment to clarify the broth
8. Strain either through a thick cloth sieve or through a thin cloth
9. Slice the cabbage into strips and the pheasant neatly and arrange on a platter, around it make a wreath of the cabbage strips and sprinkle with bread cubes fried to golden color
10. This clear broth can be poured into a dish or carried around in pitchers, likewise the pheasant
11. In case of lack of pheasants, partridges work very well for this broth
12. The bread may be cut into half-moons or triangles instead of cubes and fried
13. Good hard bread cubes fried in butter also serve well
