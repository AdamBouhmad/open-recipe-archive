## Ingredients

- oil or butter
- garlic
- rice
- salt
- pepper
- saffron powder
- water or broth

## Directions

1. Heat oil or butter and add a clove of garlic, remove it when it begins to burn
2. Add rice and toast it
3. Mix in the salt, pepper and saffron powder for coloring
4. When the oil has been absorbed by the rice and the grains begin to toast, pour water or broth over it, which immediately begins to boil
5. After the rice grains have puffed up and acquired their full size, consuming the broth in which they were boiling, remove from heat and let rest for half an hour, placing hot coals on top of the pot lid which brown the upper part of the rice
6. The merit of rice à la Valenciana consists in the grains being soft but dry at the same time and separated from each other
7. To the rice can be added all kinds of meat, poultry and fish which should be added moments before adding the broth, after the rice has been browned, taking advantage of leftovers of poultry, ham and meats from the previous day
