## Ingredients

- Plums
- Salt
- Water
- Sugar, five libras per one hundred plums

## Directions

1. Using a thick thorn or spine, make six punctures: three on the back and three on the other side in the groove that runs along the plum.
2. Make a brine with boiling water and pour it over the plums in a dry earthenware pot.
3. Cover well to retain heat; leave in the brine until they release the gum, which usually occurs in three or four days.
4. With a thorn, remove the gum and place the plums to drain in tepid water, changing the water one or two times until they lose the acidity and salty taste.
5. Bring water to a boil in a saucepan and when boiling, place the plums there to cook.
6. Test with a small feather to know if they are well cooked and place them in distilled water, testing again to ensure they have neither acidity nor salty taste.
7. It is not necessary to drain them; simply wash and place to drip in a cloth set over a basket.
8. After dripping well, place in cold syrup. Do not put on the fire.
9. After two days, give a short boil.
10. Give three or four more boils, always short, leaving two days between each one.
11. When finished, the preserve will be at point.
