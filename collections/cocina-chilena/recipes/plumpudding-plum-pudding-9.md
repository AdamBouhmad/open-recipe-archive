## Ingredients

- half a bottle milk
- eight eggs
- one pound finely chopped beef suet, made into crumbs
- one pound flour
- one pound Corinth raisins
- twelve ounces large raisins
- one glass rum
- two tablespoons sugar
- half a teaspoon salt

## Directions

1. Place half a bottle of milk with eight eggs in a pot and beat for a while
2. Add one pound finely chopped beef suet made into crumbs and one pound flour
3. Mix well and add one pound Corinth raisins and twelve ounces large raisins
4. Add one glass of rum
5. Mix everything with two tablespoons sugar and half a teaspoon salt
6. The next day, five hours before serving, boil water in a large pot with a stone in the middle
7. Pour all into a buttered napkin, tie well leaving space for it to rise
8. Boil, cover, and keep it boiling on the stone
9. When removing, place on a serving dish and in the hole left by the stone, pour rum with sugar and set it aflame
