## Ingredients

- Twenty-four egg yolks
- Two egg whites
- One pound of powdered sugar
- Half a bottle of must
- Twenty-two ounces of flour
- One quarter ounce of ground cinnamon
- Wax
- Paper strips
- Wafer paste
- Three pounds of powdered sugar for filling
- Fifteen egg whites for filling

## Directions

1. Beat the yolks with two whites and one pound of powdered sugar
2. When beaten, it takes a white color; add half a bottle of must and twenty-two ounces of flour and one quarter ounce of ground cinnamon
3. Continue beating until all is well incorporated
4. To make the cartuchos, use an iron mold for making wafers
5. Heat the iron on moderate fire so they should not be toasted
6. Coat the iron with a little wax and wipe with a cloth
7. Put a small spoonful of dough on each end of the mold and press to form two wafers
8. Place on fire for a moment, separate the wafer with a knife, and on the same iron wrap the cartucho around the mold
9. Before filling, have cut narrow strips of paper glued with wafers, form rings to place on the cartuchos so they do not break apart with the moisture of the filling
10. To fill them, beat three pounds of powdered sugar with fifteen egg whites until the beaten mixture takes consistency
11. Fill the cartuchos with this beaten mixture and place on baking sheets in a gentle oven
12. To glaze them, make syrup from one pound of sugar; when at point, take each cartucho by the middle and glaze
