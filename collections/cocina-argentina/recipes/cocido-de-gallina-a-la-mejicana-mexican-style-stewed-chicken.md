## Ingredients

- A fat hen
- Fresh lettuce leaves
- Parsley
- Mint
- One onion head
- Salt
- Water
- Offal: gizzard, liver, and heart
- A piece of toasted bread crumbs
- Oil
- Vinegar

## Directions

1. Prepare a fat hen by plucking it carefully, opening it and washing it several times with cold water, as well as its offal: gizzard, liver, and heart
2. Stuff the hen with a piece of toasted bread crumbs, sewing the opening closed with needle and thread
3. Place in a terra-cotta or enameled iron pot to cook in a long boil, accompanied by a bundle of fresh lettuce leaves, parsley, and mint leaves tied with thread so they don't scatter, and an onion head in the center
4. Add very little salt at first, increasing it as the broth reduces and needs water added, either a small cup or two
5. When the flesh of the hen's leg falls apart with pressure, the stew is ready
6. After a short rest, serve the broth in a large porcelain cup to enhance the liquid
7. Serve the hen whole to the diner so they can choose the pieces themselves
8. Accompany with a sauce made from the offal used for stuffing, which should be carefully extracted, ground with the toasted bread crumbs, and seasoned with oil, vinegar, and slices of the onion that cooked with the herbs in the broth
