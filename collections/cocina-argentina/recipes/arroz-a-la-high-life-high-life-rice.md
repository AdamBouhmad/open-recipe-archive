## Ingredients

- one pound of good white rice, well washed three times
- two cups of milk
- a cinnamon stick
- a piece of lemon
- milk for cooking
- one pound of sugar
- one small cup of walnut paste
- walnuts, peeled
- milk for the walnut paste
- cinnamon and powdered sugar for serving

## Directions

1. Soak one pound of good white rice, well washed three times, for six hours. Wash it once more and add to a pan with two cups of milk, a cinnamon stick, and a piece of lemon. Allow to cook, having ready a pitcher of milk to add as the rice absorbs the milk during cooking and begins to swell.
2. When almost cooked, add one pound of sugar without stopping stirring, using a piece of sugarcane cut for this purpose and stirring with only one hand; if another person takes over the stirring, the milk in the rice will curdle. Bring to a high point, then remove from heat and mix in one small cup of walnut paste that has been prepared by grinding the peeled walnuts very well and mixing with milk while grinding to prevent it from turning black. After grinding, add a bit more milk and strain through a sieve. Add to the rice while still off the heat, stir very well, and serve on a round platter with a dusting of cinnamon and powdered sugar.
