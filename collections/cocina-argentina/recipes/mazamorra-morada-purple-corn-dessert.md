## Ingredients

- regular amount of water
- purple corn
- corn cobs
- 6 to 8 pieces of pineapple with skin
- 2 dozen dried cherries
- pineapple, peeled and in slices
- dried cherries
- dried peaches
- quince strips
- sugar
- sweet potato starch
- 1 tablespoon of lemon juice

## Directions

1. In a regular amount of water, add the shelled purple corn, cooking also in the same water with its cobs, as well as 6 to 8 pieces of pineapple with skin and 2 dozen dried cherries
2. Cook 4 hours so that the corn and cobs release all their juice and color, giving the liquid a beautiful purple color
3. Pass through a sieve, and one part of that liquid is put to boil in an enameled pan with sliced peeled pineapple, dried cherries, dried peaches cut into pieces and pitted, strips of quince, and sufficient sugar to sweeten well
4. Boil until cooked well
5. Dissolve sweet potato starch in the purple water and pour little by little, spoonful by spoonful, into the pan in which the cooked is boiling, and when adding the starch, stir constantly so that no clots form
6. Let cook, stirring always, until the mazamorra, cooked, reaches the point of dulce de leche
7. Then, a moment before removing from fire, mix in 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
