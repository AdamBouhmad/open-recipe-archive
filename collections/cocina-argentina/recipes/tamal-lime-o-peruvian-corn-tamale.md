## Ingredients

- four pounds of white corn
- quicklime
- salt to taste
- lard from pork
- four eggs
- cooked pork meat
- hard-boiled eggs
- ground ají
- bacon
- almonds
- ground peanuts
- corn husks
- water

## Directions

1. Cook four pounds of white corn in plenty of water with handfuls of quicklime to remove its skin.
2. After two boils, drain the water and place the corn in a trough or open vessel.
3. Rub and wash the corn alternately and many times until it is clean of skins, white, and without trace of lime.
4. Grind it until reduced to a paste and season with necessary salt, plenty of pork lard, and four eggs, kneading the mass until it has acquired consistency and softness.
5. Divide the mass into pieces of the size the tamales are to have, and fill them with a picadillo of cooked pork meat seasoned with hard-boiled eggs, ground ají, bacon, almonds and ground peanuts.
6. Wrap them in corn husks, which are prepared by soaking them to give them flexibility and washing them well.
7. Once wrapped, place them with little water in a pot, arranging them in squares like building a wall.
8. Cover the mouth of the pot very well, first with burlap folded in four, then with the lid, so that steam helps with cooking.
9. Cook for two hours over slow fire, adding once or twice a little hot water if the water in the pot has diminished.
10. After two hours of slow cooking, the tamales are ready to serve.
