## Ingredients

- un cuarto de cabrito (a quarter of kid goat)
- vinagre (vinegar)
- aceite (oil)
- pimienta (pepper)
- sal (salt)
- orégano (oregano)
- ajo molido (ground garlic)
- harina (flour)
- seis huevos (six eggs)
- agua (water)
- papel fuerte (strong paper)
- mantequilla (butter)
- pan (bread)
- jugo de tomate (tomato juice)
- crema espesa de leche cruda (thick cream of raw milk)
- cucharada de vinagre (spoonful of vinegar)
- tres cucharadas de aceite de oliva (three spoonfuls of olive oil)

## Directions

1. Prepare a quarter of kid goat that has recently finished nursing
2. Place it for two hours in ordinary marinade: vinegar, oil, pepper, salt, oregano, and a little ground garlic
3. Prepare separately a well-consistent paste with flour, six eggs, and necessary water to make it manageable; once the paste is well kneaded, let it rest one hour wrapped in damp linen
4. Extend the paste with a rolling pin, forming a cake about one centimeter thick
5. Remove the kid goat leg from the marinade, dry it very well, and wrap it in the paste, moistening the edges of the mass to join them together
6. Cover the wrapped meat with strong paper untreated in butter and place on the spit with moderate heat
7. After three hours of cooking, remove the paper so the paste browns
8. Serve accompanied separately with a sauce made of bread crumbs dissolved in tomato juice, thick cream of raw milk, one spoonful of vinegar, three spoonfuls of olive oil, salt and pepper; beat all until completely incorporated
