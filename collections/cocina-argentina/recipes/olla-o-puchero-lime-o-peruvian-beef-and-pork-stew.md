## Ingredients

- a piece of beef from the chest
- four legs of white pork, cleaned of hair and scraped
- a piece of salted beef tongue, previously washed and soaked
- a piece of bacon, fresh or salted
- a bouquet of aromatic herbs composed of parsley, mint or lemon balm, basil, rosemary and bay leaf, tied with string around an onion head with green leaves
- a cabbage, well washed and tied in four turns with twine
- a clove of toasted garlic
- peppercorns
- cabbage leaves for the surface
- salt
- two handfuls of chickpeas
- two toasted garlic cloves
- a tomate
- parsley
- meat fat
- paprika
- four cabbage leaves that have cooked on the surface of the pot
- half a teaspoon of pepper
- half a teaspoon of salt
- half a teaspoon of cumin
- three tablespoons of vinegar and oil
- a little broth from the pot
- potatoes
- sweet potatoes
- fruits for garnish

## Directions

1. Choose a piece of beef from the chest part
2. Take four legs of white pork, well cleaned of hair, scraped with a knife, and washed, as well as the meat, in lukewarm water lightly saturated with vinegar
3. Place together with a piece of salted beef tongue, previously washed and soaked in hot water, in a pot with a good quantity of water over a brisk fire
4. Skim carefully, then add a piece of bacon, fresh or salted, and a bouquet of aromatic herbs composed of parsley, mint or lemon balm, basil, rosemary and bay leaf, tied with string around an onion head with green leaves
5. Add a well-washed cabbage tied in four turns with twine to prevent it from falling apart during boiling, a clove of toasted garlic and peppercorns
6. Cover the surface of the pot with a layer of cabbage leaves; being careful to add very little salt because of the salted tongue, cover the pot and let it boil for three hours over a brisk fire
7. Remove from the fire to extract half the broth and place a sausage and an unsalted stuffing or morcilla
8. Prepare the seasoning by grinding separately in a mortar two handfuls of chickpeas with a clove of toasted garlic, a tomato and parsley, meat fat, paprika and four cabbage leaves that have cooked on the surface of the pot
9. When all is well ground, mix; add half a teaspoon of pepper, half a teaspoon each of salt and cumin, three tablespoons each of vinegar and oil, with a little of the broth extracted from the pot
10. Beat lightly and pour over the stew, covering again with cabbage leaves, to cook again over low heat, well covered with a tight lid
11. On the new layer of cabbage leaves, place potatoes, sweet potatoes, and fruits to be cooked by steam, which will serve as garnish for the lima stew
