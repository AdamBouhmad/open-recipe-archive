## Ingredients

- cabbage
- salt
- sausage meat
- poultry offal cooked
- bread crumb
- pepper
- cumin
- two egg yolks
- olive oil
- vinegar
- butter
- chicken livers
- bacon slices
- sauce cooked
- parsley
- shallot
- mushrooms cooked
- truffles cooked
- good broth
- Champagne wine
- pickled gherkins

## Directions

1. Choose a well-formed cabbage and, passing a string around it to prevent it from opening, blanch it in salted water.
2. When it has received this first, light cooking so that the leaves do not lose their firmness, remove it from the water, receive it in a cloth, with which gently press it to dry it; place it standing on a plate; remove the string that closes it, and open it slightly in the shape of a rose.
3. Prepare a filling as follows: grind in a mortar sausage meat, cooked poultry offal, and bread crumb, seasoned with pepper, a pinch of cumin and salt if that of the sausage is not sufficient.
4. Add two half-beaten egg yolks to incorporate and bind the filling. Add half a spoonful of oil, half a spoonful of vinegar and a piece of butter the size of a hazelnut. Mix very well.
5. With this filling, coat the interior and exterior of each cabbage leaf. Then, with light and careful hand, fold the leaves back over the heart, also filled with minced chicken livers. The cabbage thus recovers its form.
6. Tie a string around it again to preserve it; and in a tall pot so as not to crush the cabbage in its cooking, putting on the bottom a layer of bacon slices sprinkled with cooked sauce, place the cabbage always standing; cover it with the lid, with hot coals on top, and moderate heat below, cook slowly.
7. Serve with a sauce made as follows: Put in a pot four spoonfuls of good broth with a little chopped parsley; a shallot; cooked mushrooms and truffles cut into thin slices, and a spoonful of butter, seasoning with salt and pepper. Stir, letting it boil for half an hour during which the broth will have reduced. Then add a spoonful of oil. Stir for a moment, and add half a glass of Champagne wine. Stir for half a minute; remove the pot from the fire and arrange in the serving dish, in which the cabbage is immediately served standing, wedged with pickled gherkins.
