## Ingredients

- kid goat at weaning age
- oil
- vinegar
- mirasol chili pepper
- cumin
- fresh cheese
- aged and buttery cheese from Tafí or Huaraz
- potatoes
- sweet potato
- yucca or cassava
- corn cobs
- plantain leaves
- wood
- stones
- earth
- strong wool or linen cloth

## Directions

1. Skin the kid goat, wash it, and cut off the head and legs.
2. Place it in a marinade of oil, vinegar, and spices (especially mirasol chili pepper and cumin) in a wooden or non-metal vessel for two hours, stirring every half hour to marinate evenly.
3. Prepare thick slices of fresh cheese and aged, buttery cheese from Tafí or Huaraz; potatoes, sweet potato, yucca or cassava, and corn cobs, all whole with their skins.
4. Have ready the largest plantain leaves.
5. Dig a half-meter-deep pit in a dry location in the open air, preferably in sandy soil, with enough width so that the items to be roasted do not hinder the meat, which should be laid out extended, and potatoes, yucca, corn cobs, and cheese piled to one side.
6. Place a large quantity of wood in the pit and cover it with stones, forming an oven and raising it to a height of one meter.
7. Before setting fire to the wood, arrange the plantain leaves on top of the stones in pairs, wrapped to soak and lose their rigidity and become flexible enough to wrap the pieces to be roasted.
8. Light the wood; the flame rises and burns all the wood, which becomes live coals and hot ash, falling to the bottom of the pit and heating the stones.
9. After the initial flames, remove the roasted plantain leaves, unfold them, and wrap in them the kid goat and cheese.
10. With a shovel, move the stones to the edges of the oven.
11. Arrange the kid goat and cheese in the bottom, cleared of coals and embers, and add piled potatoes, yucca, sweet potato, and corn cobs.
12. Pour the coals and embers over them again.
13. Add the extracted earth from the pit and even the stones, and cover everything with a strong cloth of wool or linen.
14. After five hours of cooking, the pachamanca is ready.
15. Carefully remove the cloth, stones, earth, coals, and embers.
16. Carefully extract the roasted pieces and free them from their plantain leaf wrapping.
17. Serve on large platters with spicy sauces.
