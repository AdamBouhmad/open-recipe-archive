## Ingredients

- Good milk
- Cream, one cup
- Sugar
- Cinnamon
- Straw or ice cream paddle

## Directions

1. At five in the morning, fill two tin or zinc cans halfway with milk.
2. Wrap them in sheepskins very thoroughly soaked in water strongly seasoned with saltpeter, or lacking that, salt.
3. Place them on the back of a horse and make it trot one league, and bring it back at the same trot.
4. The milk, which must be carefully covered with a well-adjusted can lid and loose in its container, will shake like the sea in a storm, becoming foam that rises and completely fills the empty space of the can, while ice freezes it solid.
5. After the continued trot of two leagues, arrive at the place where deep dishes await to receive it. Remove the stoppers and two cascades of frozen foam fill the containers.
6. Season with sugar and cinnamon and serve to the table.
7. Alternatively, in the early morning hours, place a quantity of good milk on ice seasoned with saltpeter in a deep tin or zinc dish.
8. You may add one cup of cream and season it with sugar and cinnamon.
9. Make two bundles of straw used for brooms to use as spatulas.
10. Beat the milk from both sides of the dish with these two spatulas.
11. In ten minutes, the milk will have turned to foam and this foam will have reached complete freezing.
