## Ingredients

- one pound of well-ground sugar
- one pound of wheat flour
- one pound of fresh butter
- twelve eggs
- lemon essence
- dulce de leche (milk preserve)
- half a pound of powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Prepare tin molds the size of a large plate with a wire around the edge in the form of a rim or border.
2. Beat the twelve eggs, reserving three egg whites.
3. Beat well the twelve egg yolks and nine egg whites with the butter, and once beaten, add the flour, cupped by cupped, after passing it through a sieve; then add the sugar.
4. All together, beat until it has become a smooth and soft mass.
5. Add a few drops of lemon essence.
6. Lightly butter the tins or molds, and cover with a very thin layer of dough, so thin that we shall call them hosts, and place them in a strong oven, which cooks them momentarily.
7. Once cooked, turn the molds over onto a table so that the hosts come out intact.
8. There should be twelve molds, extracting the hosts, and set aside the twelfth for the base.
9. On the host of this mold, spread a layer of dulce de leche, and on top of this a host.
10. On it another layer of dulce and a host.
11. Thus, alternately, until the twelve leaves that complete the cake, which will end with a host.
12. Beat in meringue point three egg whites with half a pound of powdered sugar; coat the cake with this beaten mixture and place it in a warm oven to dry the coating.
