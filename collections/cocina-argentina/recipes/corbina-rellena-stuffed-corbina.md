## Ingredients

- A tender corbina
- Water, two liters, boiling
- Salt
- Vinegar
- Onion
- Tomato
- Garlic, one clove
- Parsley
- Butter
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Bread crumbs
- Milk
- Pickled olives
- Pickled gherkins
- Pepper
- Cumin

## Directions

1. Choose a tender corbina: one can tell by the fact that it has not yet reached its natural size. After descaling it, opening it and washing it many times with cold water, hang it up, insert a small stick through its mouth to keep it open, and pour two liters of boiling water through it.
2. The water, running along the body from head to tail, shrinks the flesh, thus revealing all the spines, which are quickly removed.
3. Then wash it again and leave it for an hour in water saturated with salt and vinegar.
4. Meanwhile, mince onion and tomato, add one clove of garlic and parsley ground, and cook them in butter.
5. Mince finely hard-boiled eggs; soak bread crumbs in milk; and when the onion being fried takes on a golden color, add the minced eggs and the bread crumbs soaked in milk, add pickled olives and gherkins, sprinkle with pepper and cumin, add one tablespoon of vinegar, and stir, cooking for five minutes more, remove from heat and use this to stuff the corbina.
6. Once the corbina is stuffed, close the opening with stitches of needle, and well buttered, as well as the paper in which it is to be wrapped so that it toasts on both sides, place it in the oven.
