## Ingredients

- corn (choclo)
- milk
- white cornmeal or cornstarch
- salt
- sugar
- butter
- egg yolks

## Directions

1. Grate the corn and grind it very well in a mortar or mill. Pour in one cup of milk, mix well, and strain through loose cloth, squeezing hard to extract the juice.
2. Return the pulp to the mortar and grind again, also adding one cup of milk, and strain.
3. Into this corn juice, add white cornmeal or cornstarch spoon by spoon, stirring as you add the flour and beating until it has thickened.
4. Season with salt and a little sugar, at most one or two spoonfuls, to bring out the natural sweetness of the corn.
5. Melt a large piece of butter and mix it into the mixture, stirring and beating until the butter is incorporated.
6. If the mixture has thickened too much, add a little milk and, always stirring, place it to cook over moderate heat.
7. Taste frequently to know by flavor when it is cooked and ready.
8. Remove from heat, add butter, stir and let cool. Once cold, mix in four egg yolks and stir to incorporate them into the mixture.
9. Butter the bottom of a heat-resistant earthenware dish and spread a layer of the corn mixture. Over this arrange a filling of your choice, either squab prepared in sauce and stewed, or a chopped mixture seasoned with spices, Malaga raisins, almonds and olives.
10. On the filling, arrange symmetrically slices of hard-boiled eggs and olives. Cover the filling with another layer of corn mixture and put in the oven.
11. When the surface of the cake has taken a deep golden color, cooking is done, and it should go from the oven to the table; the hotter this cake is, the more exquisite it becomes.
