## Ingredients

- rabbit
- vinegar
- salt
- butter
- aji (chili pepper)
- sticks
- buttered paper
- yellow aji sauce
- English mustard
- hard-boiled egg yolks
- oil
- vinegar
- parsley

## Directions

1. After peeling, washing, and gutting the rabbit, give it a final bath in vinegar and salt.
2. Open it well, flattening the loin with blows from the pestle or any other weight.
3. Coat it in butter fried with aji, and after a light sprinkling of salt, cross two sticks lengthwise through the interior of the body to keep it open.
4. Wrap it in buttered paper and place it on the spit over a brisk fire, in the open air or in a spacious kitchen, so that it does not become impregnated with the smoke of other foods.
5. When the rabbit is cooked, remove the paper so that it takes on the golden color of perfect cooking.
6. Serve with yellow aji sauce for the creoles and English mustard for foreigners: both seasoned with a mixture of hard-boiled egg yolks dissolved in oil and vinegar, with chopped parsley.
