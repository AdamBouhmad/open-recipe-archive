## Ingredients

- cow tongue
- water
- salt
- two heads of onion
- one clove of garlic
- parsley, oregano and spearmint
- knife
- thick needle
- two hard-boiled eggs
- pepper
- half a head of onion, ground
- half a clove of garlic, ground
- raisins
- almonds, crushed
- butter
- vinegar
- olive oil
- bread and cheese, grated
- paper

## Directions

1. Wash the tongue well, scrape it with a knife, remove the hides, fats and membranes from the upper end, and prick it with a thick needle over its entire surface
2. Boil it for three hours in two liters of water with salt, two heads of onion, one clove of garlic and a small bunch of parsley, oregano and spearmint
3. Once cooked, strike it with the flat of a large knife so the skin loosens, and let it cool
4. Remove the skin and make a deep cut lengthwise in the thickest part
5. Through this cut, with a sharp knife, extract a piece of meat from the center
6. Mince this meat very finely with two hard-boiled eggs
7. Season with salt, pepper, half a head of ground onion and half a clove of ground garlic, raisins and crushed almonds
8. Put it on the fire with a little butter for six minutes, stirring with a spoon
9. Remove from fire and complete the seasoning with a little, very little vinegar and a drop of olive oil
10. Fill the hollow made in the tongue with this mixture
11. Close the edges of the filling with three turns of strong thread
12. Coat the tongue in butter, bread it in grated bread and cheese, and wrapped in a sheet of paper coated with butter, place it in the oven for a quarter hour
13. Serve with purée of fresh peas and pickled cornichons
