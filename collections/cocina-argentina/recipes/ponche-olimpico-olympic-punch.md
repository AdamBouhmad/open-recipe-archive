## Ingredients

- one coconut from Panama or Brazil, or four dozen coconuts from Paraguay
- half spoonfuls of milk
- two dozen blanched and washed almonds
- one liter of hot cream
- sugar
- cinnamon
- nutmeg
- one egg white
- half bottle of Jamaican rum

## Directions

1. Grate first, then grind in a mortar one coconut from Panama or Brazil, or in its absence, four dozen coconuts from Paraguay
2. While grinding in the mortar, add milk little by little, half spoonfuls at a time
3. Add two dozen blanched and washed almonds every four additions, grinding them with the coconut
4. Once ground, strain through a strong cloth sieve
5. Press well to extract the thickest juice
6. Mix the juice with one liter of hot cream sweetened with sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg
7. Immediately and quickly pour a beaten egg white (whipped to stiff peaks) onto this mixture, beating with a molinillo (traditional beater)
8. Add half a bottle of Jamaican rum after the egg white
9. To keep this punch hot, since the coconut prevents reheating over fire, place the bowl over a vessel of boiling water
10. Beat each cup when serving and top with foam
