## Ingredients

- Yellow corn, quantity as needed
- Water
- Straw
- Cabbage leaves or large weeds

## Directions

1. Soak yellow corn in a clay vessel of sufficient capacity for three days
2. Drain the water and empty the corn into a basket to drain well
3. Place to germinate in open vessels and broken clay pots, on straw and covering the surface with cabbage leaves or large weeds
4. If the weather is cold, this germination takes fifteen days; if hot, eight days suffice
5. After this time, remove the sprouted corn and extend it in the sun on cloths to dry, turning it from one side to the other
6. The germinated corn, now called jora, is ground, pulverizing it in the mortar, or if in large quantity, in the mill
7. Dissolve this flour in sufficient water to form a clear broth, and soak for two hours
8. When this infusion has become clear, set aside one glass
9. Then boil all the infusion in a large clay vessel over good heat for three hours
10. While hot, pass through a fine strainer, squeezing forcefully so that the juice is extracted from the bagasse, and let the liquid cool
11. Pour the reserved infusion into the bottom of the clay jar or botija to serve as a base for the already strained liquid, which is placed there to ferment, adding one glass of sediment from fermented chicha to serve as yeast
12. Cover the mouth of the vessel with a cloth folded in four and tied firmly with a rope around its neck
13. Cover the vessel with a wool blanket for three days, after which, upon uncovering, a delicious beverage is found
