## Ingredients

- 6 dozen almonds
- almond milk
- flour
- pure olive oil
- chard leaves
- salt
- water
- potatoes
- green peas
- pepper
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Peel 6 dozen almonds in boiling water and after washing them, grind them in a mortar.
2. Place them in a cup, reduced to paste, and by adding warm water strain them through clear cloth, squeezing well to extract the juice.
3. Grind the pulp still further, and strain and squeeze again.
4. Mix the almond milk that results from this operation with flour, kneading it, adding little by little as you knead, 3 or 4 tablespoons of pure, fresh olive oil; and when the kneaded dough, with the help of the oil, is soft and flexible, stretch it with a rolling pin, fold it six times, making it into a puff pastry, which thinned with the rolling pin, is cut into small squares, and filled with the following preparation:
5. Take chard leaves, wash them and give them a boil in salted water, taking care to cover the vessel to prevent the chard from turning black on contact with air.
6. Once cooked, remove from heat; remove from the leaves stems and fibers, and well cleaned of all this, chop finely.
7. Cook potatoes unpeeled; and once cooked peel them and chop very well, and mix with the chard, adding very well-cooked green peas. Season with pepper, salt, oil and vinegar.
8. With everything well incorporated, fill the little pastries, which are put in a very hot oven, because their cooking should be brief: just the time for the pastries to brown: ten minutes.
