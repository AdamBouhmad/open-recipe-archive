## Ingredients

- One pound peeled walnuts, well ground
- Breadcrumbs from a criolla bread
- Twelve egg yolks
- Half pound pulverized sugar
- Sponge cake (bizcochuelo) slices
- One cup sweetened milk sprinkled with cinnamon
- Four tablespoons ground walnuts
- Three egg whites
- Half pound pulverized sugar for the glaze

## Directions

1. Mix one pound of peeled, ground walnuts with breadcrumbs from a criolla bread, twelve egg yolks, and half pound of pulverized sugar.
2. Stir and beat this mixture well.
3. Make a walnut sauce (nogada) by combining one cup of sweetened milk sprinkled with cinnamon with four tablespoons of ground walnuts, straining the mixture through a sieve.
4. Place sponge cake slices soaked in the walnut sauce as a base in a dish.
5. Arrange the walnut paste on top of this base.
6. Bake in a hot oven for a quarter hour.
7. Remove and coat with a glaze (nevado) made from three beaten egg whites and half pound of pulverized sugar beaten to meringue consistency.
8. Return to a warm oven to set the glaze.
