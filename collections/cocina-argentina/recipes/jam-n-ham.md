## Ingredients

- ham
- vinegar
- water
- four cloves of garlic, ground
- onion head
- mint
- parsley
- rosemary
- onion leaves
- string
- two bottles of red wine
- more rosemary
- mint
- bay leaves
- two cloves of garlic
- four cloves
- piece of cinnamon
- sugar, finely ground
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Wash the ham repeatedly and soak it submerged in water for twenty-four hours
2. Wash again and cook in a terra-cotta pot or enamel porcelain pot, well submerged in water saturated with vinegar, with four crushed cloves of garlic, an onion head, and a large bundle of mint, parsley, rosemary, and onion leaves tied with string
3. When the water reduces during boiling, add more water and continue boiling until the skin swells and is ready to come off
4. Remove from fire, take the ham out of the pot, wash and dry the pot for the second cooking
5. Remove the skin from the ham by hand, dry well with a cloth, and cook in the same pot in two bottles of red wine with another bundle of rosemary, mint, and bay leaves, two cloves of garlic, four cloves, and a piece of cinnamon
6. Cover and cook, turning occasionally to prevent sticking to the bottom
7. Add water at least twice as it boils
8. When the meat feels tender to pressure, remove from fire, transfer to a platter, and let cool in a cool place, covered with a wire cover, before storing
9. If desired caramelized, once cooled, dust finely ground sugar mixed with a little cinnamon and pass a hot iron over it placed on paper
