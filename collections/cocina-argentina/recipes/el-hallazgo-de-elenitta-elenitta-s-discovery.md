## Ingredients

- half a pound of wheat flour
- a glass of good red wine, either Burgundy or Priorat
- three egg yolks
- sugar to moderately sweeten the paste
- a small cup of fresh melted butter
- flour for dusting hands
- high-point syrup
- Burgundy red wine
- powdered cinnamon and cloves
- almonds
- raisins
- walnuts
- pine nuts
- toasted sesame

## Directions

1. Make a mound with half a pound of wheat flour with a hollow in the center
2. Into the hollow, add a glass of good red wine (either Burgundy or Priorat), three egg yolks, sugar to moderately sweeten the paste, and a small cup of fresh melted butter
3. Knead this paste by beating it with your hands, dusting them with flour so the paste does not stick
4. When the paste is united and smooth, extend it with a rolling pin until it is very thin, and cut it into circles the size of the plate in which the filling is to be made
5. Butter the bottom and edges of the plate, and also butter the dough circles
6. Arrange three circles of dough, one on top of the other, in the bottom of the plate
7. Spread the filling on top, composed off the heat with high-point syrup, Burgundy red wine, powdered cinnamon and cloves, almonds, raisins, walnuts, pine nuts, and toasted sesame
8. Decorate the filling with additional raisins, almonds, and strips of candied citron
9. Cover with three more layers of dough, not as thin as those on the bottom
10. Wet the edges around the border to seal both layers of dough and crimp them
11. Form a cord from the same dough by rounding and stretching it between your hands, and make decorative relief designs on the surface with it
12. Brush with a coat of butter and place in the oven
13. When cooked and still hot, dust with sugar
