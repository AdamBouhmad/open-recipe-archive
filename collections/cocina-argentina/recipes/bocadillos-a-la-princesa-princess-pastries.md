## Ingredients

- good beef cuts, proportioned to each diner's portion
- garlic, well ground
- cumin
- pepper
- a slice of bacon
- chopped onion
- tomato
- well ground peanut
- almonds
- walnuts
- salt
- butter
- onions cut into slices
- tomatoes, seeded and cut into pieces
- half bottle of good Priorat wine
- half bottle of vinegar
- a very small amount of salt
- one ounce of grated chocolate
- parsley and mint for garnish

## Directions

1. Cut proportioned pieces from good beef
2. Pound them in a mortar with four strikes, just enough to extend the meat without breaking it apart
3. Place them on a cloth over a board
4. Coat them with ground garlic and cumin
5. Sprinkle with pepper
6. Place at the center a small slice of bacon, chopped onion, tomato, and very well ground peanut, almonds, walnuts and salt
7. Roll each piece and wrap with white thread
8. Arrange in an enameled porcelain pot over a layer of butter, sliced onions, seeded and cut tomatoes
9. Add half a bottle of good Priorat wine, half bottle of vinegar, very little salt, and one ounce of grated chocolate
10. Cover the pot and cook over low heat
11. Uncover frequently to check if the liquid evaporates too much, and add hot water bit by bit
12. After two hours of cooking, let it dry a bit and serve with garnish of parsley and mint leaves
