## Ingredients

- half a pound of rice starch
- one large coconut, or four dozen Paraguay coconuts in its place
- milk
- one liter of milk
- one pound of sugar
- twelve egg yolks
- one teaspoon of cinnamon
- sugar and cinnamon for dusting

## Directions

1. Soak half a pound of rice starch in cold milk. Grate a large coconut, or four dozen Paraguay coconuts if unavailable. Mix the grated coconut with a little milk, enough to moisten it and extract the juice, straining it through cheesecloth. The small amount of milk mixed with the coconut should be hot so that when squeezed through the strainer it yields its thickest juice. Grind and strain the remaining pulp again with another small amount of hot milk.
2. Bring one liter of milk with one pound of sugar to a boil. When it reaches its first boil, remove from heat and mix in vigorously the rice starch already dissolved in the milk that soaked it, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Once cooked and well thickened, remove from heat again and add twelve well-beaten egg yolks, stirring with great vigor, and return to heat once the yolks have been well incorporated into the milk. As soon as it begins to boil, remove from heat for the last time. Add the coconut milk, to which one teaspoon of cinnamon has been mixed, and stirring well to incorporate everything, pour into a flat dish. When cooled, dust thickly with sugar and cinnamon, and pass a heated iron over it to form caramel.
