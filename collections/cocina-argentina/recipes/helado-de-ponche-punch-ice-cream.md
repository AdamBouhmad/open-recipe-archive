## Ingredients

- Four dozen almonds, among which six bitter ones
- One coconut from Panama or Brazil, or lacking these, four dozen coconuts from Paraguay
- Milk
- Cinnamon
- Sugar, necessary amount to sweeten well
- One cup of natural cream
- Half a bottle of good cognac

## Directions

1. Grind four dozen almonds in the mortar, among which introduce six bitter ones.
2. Grind equally one coconut from Panama or Brazil, and lacking these, four dozen coconuts from Paraguay.
3. As they are being ground, successively add drops of milk to prevent them from becoming oily.
4. Once ground, pour hot milk over them separately, and strain them separately through a thin but strong cloth, pressing them so they yield all their juice.
5. Increase the juice by grinding them again and pouring hot milk over them anew, then strain them again.
6. Season with cinnamon and the necessary sugar to sweeten well.
7. Add one cup of natural cream and half a bottle of good cognac.
8. Stir and place to freeze, taking care that the freezing does not harden the liquid and remove its strength.
