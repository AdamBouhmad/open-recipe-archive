## Ingredients

- 1/2 lb. fresh butter
- 1/2 lb. brown sugar
- 1 pt. molasses
- 1/2 pt. milk
- 4 tablespoonfuls ginger
- 1 heaping tablespoonful mixed cinnamon, mace and nutmeg (ground)
- 1 glass brandy
- 1 1/2 lbs. flour
- 6 eggs
- Juice and rind of 2 oranges
- 1 level teaspoonful soda
- 1 small level saltspoonful tartaric acid

## Directions

1. Stir butter and sugar to cream with a spaddle.
2. Add molasses mixed with half a pint of warm milk; ginger; a heaped tablespoonful of mixed powdered cinnamon and powdered mace and nutmeg, and a glass of brandy.
3. Sift in flour.
4. Beat eggs till very light, then mix them alternately with the flour into the pan of butter, sugar, molasses, etc.
5. At the last mix in the yellow rind (grated fine) the oranges and the juice.
6. Stir the whole very hard.
7. Melt in one cup the soda, and in another the tartaric acid.
8. Dissolve them both in lukewarm water and see that both are quite melted.
9. First stir the soda into the mixture and then put in the tartaric acid.
10. On no account exceed the quantity of the two alkalies, as if too much is used they will destroy entirely the flavoring and communicate a very disagreeable taste instead.
11. Having buttered a large round or oblong pan, put in the mixture and bake it in a moderate oven till thoroughly done, keeping up a steady heat, but watching that it does not burn.
