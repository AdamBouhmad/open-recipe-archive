## Ingredients

- white of one egg
- one small teacupful of powdered sugar

## Directions

1. In the first place, the eggs should be cold, and the platter on which they are to be beaten also cold.
2. Break the eggs and throw a small handful of the sugar on them as soon as you begin beating.
3. Keep adding sugar at intervals until it is all used up.
4. The eggs must not be beaten until the sugar has been added in this way, which gives a smooth, tender frosting, and one that will dry much sooner than the old way.
5. Spread with a broad knife evenly over the cake, and if it seems too thin, beat in a little more sugar.
6. Cover the cake with two coats, the second after the first has become dry, or nearly so.
7. If the icing gets too dry or stiff before the last coat is needed, it can be thinned sufficiently with a little water, enough to make it work smoothly.
