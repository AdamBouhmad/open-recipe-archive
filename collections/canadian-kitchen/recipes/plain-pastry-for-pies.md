## Ingredients

- 1 qt. flour
- 1 cupful butter
- 1 cupful cold water
- 1 teaspoonful salt
- Half butter and half lard or cottolene will do

## Directions

1. Sift the salt and flour together, reserving a little flour for the board
2. With a knife, cut the butter into the flour
3. Add the water a little at a time, and mix it in lightly with the knife
4. Turn it on to the board, and roll it twice — that is, after it is rolled out once, fold it together and roll it again
5. If the paste is wanted richer for the top crust, put bits of butter over the paste when rolled; fold and roll it again several times
6. Fold the paste, and put it in the ice-box for an hour before using, keeping it covered
7. In making pastry everything should be cold, the handling light, and the hands used as little as possible
8. Paste will keep several days in a cool place, but should be rolled in a napkin, so it will not dry and form a crust
9. Roll the paste one-eighth inch thick, and a little larger than the tin
10. Dust the pan with flour; place the paste on it, letting it shrink all it will
11. Lift it from the sides to fit into place, and press it as little as possible
12. Cut a narrow strip of paste, and lay around the edge; moisten it so it will stick
13. Brush the top of the bottom crust with white of egg, so the filling will not soak in and make it heavy
14. Put in the filling, and cover with another sheet of pastry
15. Moisten the top of the strip of pastry so the top crust will adhere to it; this gives three layers around the edge
16. Trim and press them lightly together
17. Cut several slits in the top crust to let the steam escape in cooking
18. A thin piece of paste cut into fancy shape can be placed in the centre for ornament if desired
