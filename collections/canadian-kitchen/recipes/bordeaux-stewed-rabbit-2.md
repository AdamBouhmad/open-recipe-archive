## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoonful butter
- 1 sliced onion
- rabbit, jointed, salted and peppered
- flour
- cold water or weak stock
- parsley
- sweet marjoram
- pepper
- salt
- 1 great spoonful brown roux
- 1 teaspoonful Worcestershire sauce
- 1/2 cupful chopped champignons
- 1 glass claret

## Directions

1. Skin, clean, and joint the rabbit.
2. Heat a tablespoonful of butter in a sauce-pan and fry in it a sliced onion.
3. When it is slightly colored put in the pieces of hare, salted, peppered, and dredged with flour, and cook five minutes, turning over and over that all parts may be seared.
4. Cover with cold water or weak stock, add parsley, sweet marjoram, pepper, and salt, and stew gently until tender.
5. Take up the meat with a skimmer and pile upon a dish.
6. Add to the gravy in the sauce-pan a great spoonful of brown roux, a teaspoonful of Worcestershire sauce, and, if you like, half a cupful of chopped champignons.
7. Boil two minutes, take from the fire, add a glass of claret, pour over the meat, cover and set in an open oven for five minutes before serving.
