## Ingredients

- one and a half pounds of good cooking apples
- three-quarters of a pint of water
- four to six ounces of loaf sugar
- bay-leaves
- finely cut peel of one lemon
- three-quarters of an ounce of leaf gelatine
- liquid carmine
- thick cream
- lemon jelly
- angelica
- liquid jelly
- thickly whipped cream
- vanilla essence
- chopped pistachio nuts

## Directions

1. Peel one and a half pounds of good cooking apples, cut them up and cook them in three-quarters of a pint of water with four to six ounces of loaf sugar, according to the sweetness of the apples, and bay-leaves, and the finely cut peel of one lemon.
2. When the apples are perfectly soft, dissolve with them three-quarters of an ounce of leaf gelatine and pass the whole through a sieve.
3. Divide the puree into two parts, and redden one of them with liquid carmine, and whiten the other with a little thick cream.
4. Put them in separate sauce pans to about a quarter of an inch thick, and let them set on broken ice.
5. Cut out in rounds with a plain cutter about the size of a shilling for ornamenting round the mould, and in leaf shapes for the bottom and in the centre of each of the rounds set a little round of angelica with a little liquid jelly.
6. Line a plain round mould with lemon jelly to about one-eighth of an inch thick.
7. Set the cut leaves of apple puree regularly on the bottom of the mould with the stock ends at the centre and the rounds regularly round the sides in alternate colors.
8. Fix these in their places with a little more of the lemon jelly.
9. Separately dissolve the odds and ends of the cuttings of the puree with two tablespoonfuls of lemon jelly, and let them stand till somewhat cool.
10. Add to each a quarter of a pint of thickly whipped cream and pour them into the mould in alternate layers and put to set on broken ice.
11. When required turn out on a dish.
12. Place on the top a ball of stiffly whipped cream sweetened and flavored with vanilla essence, and lightly sprinkle with a little chopped pistachio nuts.
