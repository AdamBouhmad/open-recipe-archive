## Ingredients

- 1 full-grown rabbit or hare
- 1 sliced onion
- hot dripping
- pepper
- salt
- flour
- chopped fat salt pork
- onion
- parsley
- paprica
- fresh tomato
- 1 cupful cold, weak stock
- 1 stalk celery
- browned flour
- 1 tablespoonful currant jelly
- 1 tablespoonful lemon-juice
- 1 glass sherry
- fried hominy

## Directions

1. Skin, clean, and joint a full-grown rabbit, or hare.
2. Cut the back into two pieces and sever every joint.
3. Fry a sliced onion to a pale brown in hot dripping, put in the meat, peppered, salted, and floured, and cook for ten minutes, fast, turning often.
4. Put into the bottom of an agate-iron sauce-pan a layer of chopped fat salt pork, sprinkle with onion, parsley, and paprica.
5. Upon this lay the pieces of hare and cover with another layer of chopped pork and onion.
6. Add a few bits of fresh tomato if desired.
7. Pour in a cupful of cold, weak stock in which a stalk of celery has been boiled, then remove.
8. Fit on a tight top, set in a vessel of cold water, and bring slowly to a boil.
9. Keep this up for three hours, or until the meat is tender.
10. Dish the pieces of rabbit, thicken the gravy with browned flour.
11. Add a tablespoonful of currant jelly and one of lemon-juice, simmer one minute, pour in a glass of sherry and turn all upon the meat.
12. Garnish with triangles of fried hominy, serving a bit with each portion of hare.
