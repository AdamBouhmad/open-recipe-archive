## Ingredients

- paste
- rice, dried peas, beans, cornmeal, or any dry material
- egg
- apricots or peaches
- blanched almond
- fruit juice or water
- arrowroot
- plums or cherries
- apples
- lemon juice
- sugar
- nutmeg
- cinnamon
- berries

## Directions

1. Roll the paste one-eighth of an inch thick, lay it on a deep pie-dish; let it shrink all it will, and use as little pressure as possible in fitting it to the tin.
2. Cut the paste an inch larger than the dish, and fold it under, giving a high twisted edge.
3. Prick the paste on the bottom in several places with a fork.
4. Lay over it a thin paper, and fill the tart with rice, dried peas, beans, cornmeal, or any dry material convenient.
5. Brush the edge with egg, and bake it in a moderate oven.
6. When done remove the rice, or other filling, and the paper.
7. Brush the bottom with white of egg.
8. If apricots or peaches are to be used, peel and cut them in halves, lay them evenly over the tart with the centre side up.
9. Place the half of a blanched almond in each one to represent the pit.
10. Put the juice of the fruit into a saucepan on the fire; if there is no juice use a cupful of water.
11. Sweeten to taste, and when it boils add to each cupful of juice one teaspoonful of arrowroot dissolved in a little cold water, and let it cook until clear.
12. Pour it around the fruit, but not over it, as the fruit should lie on top and show its form.
13. Place in the oven only long enough to cook the fruit tender.
14. If canned fruit is used, cook the juice and arrowroot until a little thickened and clear; then pour it around the fruit, and let cool.
15. When plums or cherries are used, remove the pits carefully, and place the fruit close together, with the whole side up.
16. For apple tarts, cut the apples in even quarters or eighths; stew them in sweetened water, with a little lemon juice added, until tender.
17. Lay them overlapping in even rows or circles in the tart.
18. To a cupful of water in which the apples were stewed add a teaspoonful of arrowroot, and cook until clear; pour it over the apples, sprinkle with sugar, nutmeg, and cinnamon.
19. With berries, the fruit may be stewed or not before being placed in the tart; then strips of paste are laid across it, like lattice-work, and the paste brushed with egg.
20. Bake long enough to cook the fruit and the strips of paste.
21. When cold place a fresh berry on each piece of crust where it crosses; or place a drop of meringue on the crusts, and the berries in the openings.
