## Ingredients

- Twelve Seville oranges
- three pints of cold water per pound of fruit
- one and a half pounds of sugar per pound of boiled fruit
- rind and juice of two lemons

## Directions

1. Cut each orange into eight quarters and slice them very fine.
2. Take out the seeds and put them into a basin and cover with water.
3. To every pound of fruit add three pints of cold water.
4. Let it stand for twenty-four hours, then boil until tender.
5. Put the juice from the seeds in also.
6. Let it stand until the following day.
7. To every pound of boiled fruit add one and a half pounds of sugar and the rind and juice of two lemons.
8. Boil, stirring constantly until the syrup jellies.
