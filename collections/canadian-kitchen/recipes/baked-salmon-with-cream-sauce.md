## Ingredients

- A middle cut of salmon
- 4 tablespoonfuls of butter, melted in hot water
- 1 cup of cream
- 1 teaspoonful of cornflour
- 1 tablespoonful of butter
- Pepper
- Salt
- Parsley
- Butter for paper
- Soda

## Directions

1. Butter a sheet of foolscap paper on both sides
2. Wrap the fish up in it, pinning the ends securely together
3. Lay it in the baking-pan
4. Pour six or seven spoonfuls of butter-and-water over it
5. Turn another pan over all
6. Steam in a moderate oven from three-quarters of an hour to an hour
7. Lift the cover from time to time to baste and assure yourself that the paper is not burning
8. Meanwhile have ready in a saucepan a cup of cream
9. Dissolve a bit of soda a little larger than a pea in the cream
10. Heat this in a double boiler
11. Thicken with a heaping teaspoonful of cornflour
12. Add a tablespoonful of butter, pepper and salt to taste
13. Add a liberal pinch of minced parsley
14. When the fish is unwrapped and dished pour half slowly over it
15. Send the rest to table in a boat
16. If you have no cream use milk and add a beaten egg to the thickening
