## Ingredients

- 1/2 pint water
- 2 tablespoons cornmeal
- salt
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 1/2 pint fresh sweet milk or warm water
- 2 quarts fresh morning's milk
- flour
- lard

## Directions

1. Pour half a pint of water on 2 tablespoons of cornmeal and a generous pinch of salt.
2. Let it stand for 10 minutes, then stir in 2 tablespoons of flour and set it in a warm place to rise over night.
3. In the morning add half a pint of fresh, sweet milk, or warm water — either must be lukewarm — and just flour enough to make the yeast smooth.
4. Put the vessel containing it in a pan or kettle of hot water, hot enough to bear your hand in and be very careful to keep it the same temperature.
5. When this rises pour it into a batter made of 2 quarts of fresh morning's milk, scalded, then cooled to lukewarm, and mixed with flour enough to make a batter.
6. Beat this well, using a long spoon, then set the whole to rise again in a warm place.
7. This will be about twenty-five or thirty minutes if it has been well managed.
8. Now stir in enough flour to make a batter so stiff it cannot be stirred with a spoon.
9. Turn it out on a moulding board well dredged with flour, and before kneading it add a piece of lard the size of a large apple.
10. Mix this well into the dough.
