## Ingredients

- 1 White Swan Yeast Cake
- 2 quarts liquid at 90° F.
- 2 level tablespoons salt
- 2 level tablespoons sugar
- 2 level tablespoons lard or butter
- Flour
- strong flour

## Directions

1. Mix the yeast and half of the sugar in 1/4 cup of warm water, and let it stand at a temperature of 80° F. for 15 or 20 minutes to start fermentation.
2. Measure the shortening, salt, and rest of sugar, into kneading-pan, stir in the rest of the liquid, and mix in flour enough to beat without spattering. Then add the well-fermented yeast and beat with a wooden spoon until it looks smooth and elastic.
3. From this point treat just the same as in the ferment, sponge and dough method: Cover closely, and keep at a temperature of 70° F., until light and spongy. This will take from 9 to 10 hours.
4. When the sponge is ready stir in strong flour until too stiff to use the spoon, then mix in more with a stiff-bladed knife or the hand, until the dough no longer sticks to the fingers. Turn the dough out on the moulding-board to knead, leaving the pan quite clean. Knead lightly until the mass is elastic and velvety, the surface covered with a film of tiny bubbles, and a cut with a sharp knife shows the inside full of fine even bubbles, and free from lumps or unmixed portions. Grease the kneading-pan lightly with lard, warm both pan and cover if they are cold, put in the dough, cover closely, and keep at a temperature of 80° F. until rather more than doubled in volume, or until a gentle slap with the tips of the fingers causes it to fall in. This will take from 2 to 3 hours.
5. Knead lightly in the pan for a minute to get rid of the larger bubbles and return to rise a second time until double in volume. This will take from 1 to 2 hours.
6. Divide into loaves that will half fill the bread tins. Knead each piece only enough to get rid of large bubbles and smooth the surface, and put it into a greased tin. Keep at a temperature of 70° to 80° F. until doubled in volume, when they should have a bold, nicely-rounded appearance.
7. Bake an hour in an oven about 350° F. When done, the loaves should give a hollow sound when tapped on the bottom.
8. When baked remove at once from the pan, and stand on edge or across the top of the pans, that the air may get to all parts and cool it quickly.
