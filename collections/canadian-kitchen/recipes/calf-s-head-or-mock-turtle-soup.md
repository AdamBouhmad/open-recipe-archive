## Ingredients

- One tablespoonful of butter
- Two tablespoonfuls of flour
- One and a half or two quarts of water in which a calf's head has been boiled
- White wine instead of vinegar in the boiling
- Three or four strained tomatoes
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Pieces of boiled calf's head
- Egg balls
- Two or three tablespoonfuls of sherry
- A few very thin slices of lemon

## Directions

1. Make a brown roux by putting in a saucepan one tablespoonful of butter, let it brown, add two tablespoonfuls of flour, and let that brown
2. Add slowly at first, one and a half or two quarts of water in which a calf's head has been boiled
3. Add three or four strained tomatoes and simmer for one-half hour
4. Skim off any fat and season with salt and pepper
5. Add some pieces of boiled calf's head cut in pieces one-half inch square, a few egg balls, two or three tablespoonfuls of sherry, and a few very thin slices of lemon
