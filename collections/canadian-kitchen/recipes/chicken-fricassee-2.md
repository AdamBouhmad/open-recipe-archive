## Ingredients

- chicken
- butter or drippings
- boiling water
- bouquet of herbs
- salt
- pepper
- salt pork
- flour
- cream or milk
- egg yolks
- sherry
- mushrooms
- rice or toast

## Directions

1. Cut a chicken into eleven pieces: two drumsticks, two second joints, two wings, two breasts, three back pieces.
2. Put the pieces in a saucepan with two tablespoonfuls of butter or drippings.
3. Let them brown slightly on both sides, but use care that they do not burn.
4. When a little colored, add enough boiling water to cover them.
5. Add a bouquet of herbs, salt and pepper, and a few slices of salt pork.
6. Simmer until tender.
7. Arrange the pieces neatly on a dish, using the best ones outside.
8. Strain the liquor from the pot and take off the fat.
9. Make a white sauce of one tablespoonful of butter and two of flour and a cupful of the liquor from the pot.
10. Season to taste.
11. Remove from the fire, and when a little cool add a cupful of cream or milk beaten up with two or three yolks of eggs.
12. Place again on the fire until the eggs are a little thickened, but do not let it boil, or they will curdle.
13. A tablespoonful of sherry may be added, if liked, or a half can of mushrooms.
14. Pour the gravy over the chicken pieces.
15. A border of rice may be placed around the chicken, or sippets of toast used.
