## Ingredients

- leg of mutton
- quart of rich gravy
- tomato sauce or catsup
- salt
- 2 tablespoons barberry or currant jelly

## Directions

1. Pick out a fine leg of mutton and let it hang twelve days in a cool place.
2. Wash it thoroughly and put it in a dripping-pan on a grate, so it will heat through thoroughly but will not cook.
3. Remove it from the pan and pour into the pan a quart of rich gravy.
4. Cut the mutton from the bone in slices not quite inch thick, add a little tomato sauce or catsup and salt to suit taste, then lay in the slices of meat and cook about 25 minutes, or until done.
5. When nearly done, add 2 tablespoons barberry or currant jelly.
6. Serve hot as soon as cooked.
