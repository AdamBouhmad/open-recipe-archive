## Ingredients

- chicken weighing from a pound and a half to two pounds
- water
- salt
- two small onions
- butter
- curry powder
- flour
- egg yolk
- hot boiled rice

## Directions

1. Cut up a chicken weighing from a pound and a half to two pounds, as for fricassee, wash it well, and put it in a stewpan with sufficient water to cover it.
2. Boil it closely covered, until tender; add a large teaspoonful of salt, and cook a few minutes longer.
3. Remove from the fire, take out the chicken, pour the liquor into a bowl, and set it one side.
4. Cut up into the stewpan two small onions, and fry them with a piece of butter as large as an egg.
5. As soon as the onions are brown, skim them out and put in the chicken; fry for three or four minutes.
6. Sprinkle over two teaspoonfuls of curry powder.
7. Pour over the liquor in which the chicken was stewed, stir all well together, and stew for five minutes longer.
8. Stir into this a tablespoonful of sifted flour made thin with a little water.
9. Lastly, stir in a beaten yolk of egg.
10. Serve with hot boiled rice laid round on the edge of a platter, and the chicken curry in the centre.
