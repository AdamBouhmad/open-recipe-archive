## Ingredients

- 1 good-sized knuckle of veal
- water
- 1 quart of milk
- 1 piece of fresh butter the size of an egg
- 1/2 teaspoonful of ground mace
- ground red or white pepper
- salt
- 2 and 1/2 tablespoonfuls of flour
- cold water
- yolks of 6 eggs

## Directions

1. Put the knuckle of veal on to boil well covered with water, removing the scum as it comes to a boil
2. Boil till the meat comes freely from the bones, which remove, returning the bones into the pot and boil for another three-quarters of an hour
3. Renew the water when you see fit, so that it may not boil dry
4. Strain all through a colander
5. Set it away in a cool place, and the following day put it on to boil with a quart of milk and a piece of fresh butter the size of an egg
6. Put one-half teaspoonful of ground mace, ground red or white pepper, and salt to taste
7. When all comes to a boil have two and one-half tablespoonfuls of flour well blended in cold water, pour this into the boiling soup
8. Let it boil for three or four minutes
9. Remove the pot from the stove
10. Take the yolks of six eggs well beaten, put a little of the soup with the eggs and mix all together
11. Pour slowly into the soup, stirring it quietly all the while
12. The eggs must not be added to the soup while boiling, as they will curdle
