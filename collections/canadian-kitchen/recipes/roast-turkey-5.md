## Ingredients

- young turkey
- soft butter
- salt
- pepper
- flour
- boiling water
- cranberry sauce

## Directions

1. Select a young turkey; remove all the feathers carefully, singe it over a burning newspaper on the top of the stove
2. Draw it nicely, being very careful not to break any of the internal organs; remove the crop carefully; cut off the neck close to the body
3. Rinse the inside of the turkey out with several waters, and in the next to the last, mix a teaspoonful of baking soda
4. Wipe the turkey dry, inside and out, with a clean cloth
5. Rub the inside with some salt, then stuff the breast and body with Dressing for Fowls
6. Sew up the turkey with a strong thread, tie the legs and wings to the body
7. Rub it over with a little soft butter, sprinkle over some salt and pepper, dredge with a little flour
8. Place it in a dripping pan, pour in a cup of boiling water, and set it in the oven
9. Baste the turkey often, turning it round occasionally so that every part will be uniformly baked
10. When pierced with a fork and the liquid runs out perfectly clear, the bird is done
11. If any part is likely to scorch, pin over it a piece of buttered white paper
12. A fifteen-pound turkey requires between three and four hours to bake
13. Serve with cranberry sauce
