## Ingredients

- One quart or twenty-five oysters in their own liquor
- One cupful of water
- One tablespoonful of butter
- One tablespoonful of flour
- One cupful of scalded milk or cream
- Pepper, salt if necessary, and a dash of cayenne or paprica

## Directions

1. Scald a quart or twenty-five oysters in their own liquor
2. As soon as they are plump, or the gills curl, remove them
3. Add to the liquor a cupful of water
4. Make a roux of one tablespoonful each of butter and flour, dilute it with the liquor, and when it is smooth add a cupful of scalded milk or cream
5. Season with pepper, salt if necessary, and a dash of cayenne or paprica
6. Add the oysters, and as soon as they are heated serve at once
7. Examine oysters carefully, and pass the liquor through a fine sieve before being cooked, in order to remove any pieces of shell
