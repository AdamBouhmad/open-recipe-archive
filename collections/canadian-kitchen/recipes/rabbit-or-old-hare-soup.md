## Ingredients

- One rabbit cut up
- One-half pound salt pork minced fine
- One large onion, chopped
- One stalk celery, chopped
- One sprig parsley, chopped
- One gallon of water
- One heaping tablespoonful brown roux
- One teaspoonful of Worcestershire sauce
- One tablespoonful tomato catsup
- One glassful brown sherry
- Juice of four lemons
- Two tablespoonfuls good drippings
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Directions

1. Fry the onion in the dripping, and when lightly browned, add the pieces of rabbit
2. Cover with cold water and cook very slowly for four hours, or until the meat is in rags
3. Season with salt and pepper
4. Let all get cold together
5. Skim off the fat; strain through a coarse cloth, return to the fire and when it boils thicken with the roux
6. Put in the catsup, wine, lemon-juice, and, if you fancy, a pinch of ground allspice
7. If not brown enough, color with a little caramel
