## Ingredients

- four calves' feet
- one-third of a box of gelatine
- juice of three lemons
- grated rind of one lemon
- one stick of cinnamon
- sugar to taste
- whites of four eggs
- one pint of sherry

## Directions

1. Get the butcher to clean the calves' feet thoroughly and remove the hoof-horns
2. If not sufficiently white, pour boiling water over them and scrape with a knife
3. Divide each foot in half
4. Place over the fire in a preserving pan, three parts full of cold water, adding a pinch of salt
5. Boil till the meat comes to shreds, adding water occasionally, and the bones separate easily
6. Strain, set aside to cool (the liquid should measure about two quarts)
7. When cool skim off every particle of fat, rejecting the sediment beneath the jelly
8. Add one-third of a box of gelatine dissolved in the boiling stock
9. Take off the fire, add the juice of three lemons and grated rind of one, a stick of cinnamon, and sugar to taste
10. Beat up with these the whites of four eggs and broken shells slightly beaten
11. Set on the fire again and boil a few minutes till a thick scum rises to the top
12. Set the pan on the back of the stove one minute, then pour slowly through a flannel jelly bag, returning till the jelly is clear
13. When finished add a pint of sherry
14. Suspend the bag in front of the oven and do not move it, or the jelly will be cloudy
15. Exclude cold air
