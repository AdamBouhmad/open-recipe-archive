## Ingredients

- 1 gallon berries
- cider-vinegar
- water
- sugar
- beeswax
- resin

## Directions

1. Put a gallon of berries into a great crock and crush them well with a potato-beetle or wooden mallet
2. Cover an inch deep in cider-vinegar
3. Set in the hot sunshine for a day and leave all night in the cellar
4. Stir six times during the day of sunning
5. Strain and squeeze the berries dry and throw them away
6. Put another gallon of mashed berries into the strained vinegar
7. Leave again in the sun all day and another night in the cellar
8. Strain and squeeze the berries and measure the liquid thus gained
9. For each quart allow a pint of water, and for every pint of the water thus added, five pounds of sugar
10. Turn into a porcelain-lined or agate-iron kettle and set over the fire, stirring until the sugar melts
11. Heat to boiling, and boil hard one minute to throw up the scum
12. Skim well, take from the fire, strain and, while still warm, bottle
13. Seal the corks with a mixture of beeswax and resin
