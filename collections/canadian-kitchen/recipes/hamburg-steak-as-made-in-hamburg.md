## Ingredients

- thin slices of beef from a round (uncooked)
- thyme or savory
- pepper
- salt
- pounded onions
- veal kidney suet
- flour
- butter or dripping
- boiling water

## Directions

1. Procure some thin slices of beef from a round (uncooked).
2. Pound a little to tender.
3. Divide into portions the size of your hand.
4. On each portion strew a little thyme, or savory, a little pepper, a dust of salt and some pounded onions.
5. Form the bits of beef into rolls with a bit of kidney suet in the inside.
6. Tie with twine, or if possible darn little silver skewers into the flap of each steak to keep rolled.
7. Dust in flour and place in a frying-pan with some butter or nice dripping; roll the steak so that each side may get brown.
8. Carefully pour in a cupful of boiling water, cover the frying-pan tight and set on some part of range where the steaks may cook very slowly.
9. Turn out in ten minutes or longer on a very hot dish and pour gravy over them.
10. A little more gravy may be supplied by browning some flour after lifting the steaks and thinning with boiling water.
