## Ingredients

- haunch of venison
- tepid water
- buttered paper
- paste of flour and water
- stout paper
- twine
- water
- butter or clarified drippings
- flour
- unflavored gravy
- currant jelly

## Directions

1. Wash the haunch of venison slightly in tepid water, and dry it thoroughly by rubbing it with a clean, soft cloth.
2. Lay over the fat side a large sheet of thickly buttered paper, and next a paste of flour and water about three-quarters of an inch thick.
3. Cover this again with two or three sheets of stout paper, secure the whole well with twine, and put down to roast, with a little water, in the dripping-pan.
4. Let the fire be clear and strong; baste the paper immediately with butter or clarified drippings, and roast the joint from three to four hours, according to its weight and quality.
5. Doe venison will require half an hour less time than buck venison.
6. About twenty minutes before the joint is done remove the paste and paper, baste the meat in every part with butter, and dredge it very lightly with flour.
7. Let it take a pale brown color, and serve hot with unflavored gravy made with a thickening, in a tureen and good currant jelly.
