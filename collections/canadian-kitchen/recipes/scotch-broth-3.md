## Ingredients

- 1 qt. stock
- 1/4 cup pearl barley or rice
- 1 medium-sized onion minced
- 1 tablespoonful minced parsley
- 2 tablespoonfuls white roux

## Directions

1. Obtain the stock by boiling down the water in which a leg of mutton was cooked until you have half the original quantity, or by boiling for eight hours the bones left from roast mutton, or the trimmings sent home by the butcher.
2. If raw meat and bones are used, allow one quart of water to each pound.
3. Be careful to skim all the fat from the stock.
4. Wash the barley or rice and soak in cold water one hour.
5. Put the stock over the fire with the onion and bring to a rapid boil.
6. Add the barley (or rice) and simmer for three-quarters of an hour.
7. Put in the parsley and cook five minutes more before stirring in.
