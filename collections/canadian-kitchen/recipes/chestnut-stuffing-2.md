## Ingredients

- one quart of large French chestnuts
- hot water
- one tablespoonful of butter
- one teaspoonful of salt
- one quarter of a teaspoonful of pepper
- one tablespoonful of chopped parsley
- stock
- breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Shell a quart of large French chestnuts.
2. Put them in hot water and boil until the skins are softened.
3. Drain off the water and remove the skins.
4. Replace the blanched chestnuts in water, and boil until soft.
5. Take out a few at a time, and press them through a colander or a potato press.
6. Season the mashed chestnuts with a tablespoonful of butter, a teaspoonful of salt, and a quarter of a teaspoonful of pepper.
7. Some cooks add a tablespoonful of chopped parsley, and moisten it with a little stock, or add a few breadcrumbs.
