## Ingredients

- 1 duck
- 1 pound of shin of beef
- 1 onion
- 2 cloves
- 1 bay-leaf
- thyme
- parsley
- 1 carrot
- 1 small piece of turnip
- pepper
- salt
- 3 pints of stock or water
- 1/2 pound of veal
- 1 little grated nutmeg
- 1/2 pound of green peas
- 1 ounce of gelatine
- whites of 2 eggs
- cooked peas
- cooked carrots
- cooked turnip

## Directions

1. Bone the duck by cutting it open down the back
2. Take out the back bone, the breast and then the leg bones
3. Put the bones, one pound of shin of beef, one onion, two cloves, one bay-leaf, some thyme and parsley, one carrot, a small piece of turnip, pepper and salt into a saucepan with three pints of stock or water
4. Chop one-half pound of veal with an onion, a little grated nutmeg, pepper, salt and one-half pound of green peas
5. Fill the duck with this and sew it up
6. Stew it with the bones for two hours
7. Take out the duck and strain the gravy
8. Put it in another pan and mix in one ounce of gelatine and the whites of two eggs
9. Beat it over the fire until it boils, then let it boil for ten minutes without stirring, with the lid on
10. It will then be clear when again skimmed
11. Cover the bottom of a dish (large enough to hold the duck) with cooked peas, carrots and turnip
12. Put a small cupful of the gravy, and let it get cool
13. Put in the duck (breast downwards)
14. Let the gravy be nearly cold, then pour it over the duck
15. The duck will then set in a firm jelly, and can be turned out on to another dish
