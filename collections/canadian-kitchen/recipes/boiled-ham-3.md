## Ingredients

- ham weighing from eight to ten pounds
- cold water
- bay-leaf
- cloves
- blades of mace
- teaspoonful of sugar
- fresh sweet hay
- beaten egg
- very fine bread crumbs or fine cracker-dust
- watercress or parsley

## Directions

1. Soak all night; wash it carefully before you put it on to boil, removing rust or mould with a small, stiff scrubbing-brush.
2. Lay it in a large boiler and pour over it enough cold water to cover it.
3. Add a bay-leaf, half a dozen cloves, a couple of blades of mace, a teaspoonful of sugar, and, if you can get it, a good handful of fresh, sweet hay.
4. Let the water heat very gradually, not reaching the boil under two hours.
5. It should never boil hard, but simmer gently until the ham has cooked fifteen minutes to every pound.
6. It must cool in the liquor, and the skin should not be removed until the meat is entirely cold, taking care not to break or tear the fat.
7. Brush over the ham with beaten egg, strew it thickly with very fine bread crumbs, or fine cracker-dust, and brown in a quick oven.
8. Arrange a frill of paper around the bone of the shank, and surround the meat with watercress, or garnish the dish with parsley.
