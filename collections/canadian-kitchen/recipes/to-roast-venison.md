## Ingredients

- a saddle of venison
- vinegar
- red pepper
- salt
- strips of salt pork
- seasoned bread crumbs
- sweet marjoram
- sweet basil
- currant jelly
- 1 bottle of claret wine
- flour
- water
- butter
- 1 pound and a half of scraps of venison
- 3 pints of water
- cayenne pepper
- 1 pint currant jelly
- 1 pint claret
- 3/4 pound butter

## Directions

1. Wash a saddle of venison thoroughly in several waters
2. Rub it over with vinegar, red pepper and a little salt
3. Lard with strips of salt pork rolled in seasoned bread crumbs
4. Season if you like with sweet marjoram and sweet basil, 1 teaspoon each, also pepper
5. Rub the whole with currant jelly and pour over it 1 bottle of claret wine
6. Let it stand all night
7. Next morning cover venison with a paste made of flour and water 1 inch thick
8. Cover with a soft paper and secure well with strings
9. Place in dripping pan with claret, butter and water, and baste very often
10. Half an hour before taking it up remove paste and paper
11. Baste it with butter and dredge with flour to make it brown
12. For Sauce: Take a pound and a half of scraps of venison, with 3 pints of water, and salt and cayenne pepper to taste
13. Boil down to a pint and skim off the fat and strain
14. Add 1 pint of currant jelly, 1 pint claret, and 3/4 pound butter, divided into bits and rolled in flour
