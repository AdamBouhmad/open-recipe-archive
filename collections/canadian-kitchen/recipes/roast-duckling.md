## Ingredients

- 3 tablespoonfuls of mashed potatoes
- butter
- 1 tablespoonful of cream
- celery salt
- white pepper
- 3 tablespoonfuls of almonds, blanched and chopped very fine
- 2 young ducks
- cotton-twine
- 1 split onion
- pepper
- salt
- flour
- 1 cupful of boiling water
- gravy
- 1 tablespoonful of browned flour
- 2 tablespoonfuls of currant jelly

## Directions

1. Whip three tablespoonfuls of mashed potatoes to a white cream with butter and a tablespoonful of cream
2. Season with celery salt and white pepper
3. Add three tablespoonfuls of almonds, blanched and chopped very fine
4. Stuff your young ducks when you have cleaned and washed them with this mixture
5. Do not distend the bodies, but fill without packing
6. Truss and bind legs and wings into position with cotton-twine
7. Lay the plump creatures upon the grating of your roaster
8. Rub the breast with a split onion
9. Dust with pepper, salt and flour
10. Put a cupful of boiling water into the pan and cover
11. Set in a very quick oven for the first fifteen minutes
12. Change, then, to a more moderate, and cook, still covered, ten minutes to the pound
13. Uncover, baste well with gravy, then with butter
14. Dredge with flour, and brown
15. Skim the fat from the gravy
16. Thicken with a tablespoonful of browned flour, rubbed up with two tablespoonfuls of currant jelly, and send to table in a boat
