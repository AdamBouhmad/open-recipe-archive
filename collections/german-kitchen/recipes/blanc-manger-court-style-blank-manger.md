## Ingredients

- 1 pound blanched almonds, of which 2 loth must be bitter
- water
- 3 schoppen water
- 1 Seville orange
- 1 lemon
- half a pound sugar
- 3 loth isinglass dissolved in water
- juice of 1 Seville orange
- juice of 2 lemons
- 2 to 3 whole saffron leaves
- 2 spoons full calf's jelly
- 2 tablespoons white wine
- 3 to 4 drops cochineal color
- green carnation petals
- almond oil

## Directions

1. Pound 1 pound blanched almonds (of which 2 loth must be bitter) with water as fine as flour
2. Pour 3 schoppen water to it and press out firmly in a cloth, treating it as if making almond milk
3. Rub a Seville orange and a lemon on sugar, put the rubbed zest with half a pound sugar and 3 loth isinglass dissolved in water, along with the almond milk and juice of 1 Seville orange and 2 lemons, in a brass pan
4. Let everything cook together, press through a fine sieve into a clean dish and let it cool
5. Make 4 colored jellies as follows: for 2 to 3 whole saffron leaves take 2 spoons full calf's jelly and 2 tablespoons white wine; in a second dish pour 3 to 4 drops cochineal color and calf's jelly; in the third dish let calf's jelly melt, pound green carnation petals, press through a fine sieve, pour them with the calf's jelly
6. Brush a mold with almond oil, pour the colored jellies into it, let them set
7. Pour the almond milk over it, place the mold on ice and let everything set
8. Turn the contents onto a plate and garnish to taste
