## Ingredients

- 1 schoppen water and butter
- fine flour
- 3 eggs
- white bread
- hot lard
- 1 cooking spoon flour
- cold water
- 1 schoppen red wine
- sugar
- cinnamon
- 1/4 pound raisins

## Directions

1. Heat 1 schoppen water and butter the size of a hen's egg in a brass pan.
2. Stir in fine flour until very thick, cook until the batter pulls away from the pan, and smooth it with 3 eggs (the batter must be such that it runs off a spoon).
3. Cut a small piece of white bread, shape it round, dip it in the batter and fry golden in a deep pan with hot lard.
4. Stick it on a strong fork, turn it in the batter again, and repeat this 12 to 15 times.
5. Once the bread is large enough, place it on bread slices to drain well.
6. Make an English sauce: stir 1 small cooking spoon flour with cold water, add 1 schoppen red wine, sugar to taste, a knife-tip of ground cinnamon, and 1/4 pound raisins, and bring to a boil.
7. In a small pan, place a piece of sugar, add 1 tablespoon water, cook until it spins threads, add to the sauce, and bring everything to a boil once more.
8. Place the old woman on the plate and pour the sauce over her.
