## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg green beans
- 2 liters water for blanching
- 3/4 liter mutton broth
- 1 sprig bean or pepper herb
- 1 tablespoon melted beef or mutton fat
- 8 g flour
- 1 teaspoon chopped parsley
- 1 pinch pepper and salt

## Directions

1. Set 2 liters of water in a covered pot on the stove. Remove the strings from the beans and break into even pieces; pick over and chop the parsley.
2. When the water boils, put in the beans and blanch several times, pour onto a colander or drain very dry, pour over with cold water and let drip. Put the beans in a pot and cook slowly covered in the mutton broth with the bean or pepper herb until done. Melt the fat in a small bowl, add the flour and stir with the fat until it bubbles, then pour in several spoonfuls of bean broth, add the whole thing to the soft beans and bring the vegetable to a boil again while stirring. Remove the bean herb. Season the beans with pepper, salt and chopped parsley and serve in a bowl.
