## Ingredients

- 4 kg (8 Pfd.) veal knuckle
- 125 g fat and margarine
- 50 g bacon
- 20 g salt
- 1/4 l milk, sour milk or water
- 6 g potato flour
- 3 tablespoons milk or water

## Directions

1. Remove the thick joint bone from the veal knuckle; cut off any loose pieces that would dry out in the oven and spoil the shape.
2. Strike the knuckle slowly on both sides with a damp mallet, then skin it and lard it, taking into account the direction of the muscle fibers so they lie properly on the larding needle.
3. Pour a ladle of boiling water over the meat, salt it on top and bottom, and in the pan cover with boiling brown margarine and fat.
4. Place some clean bacon rinds under the roast.
5. The oven should not be too hot for a veal knuckle, otherwise the roast will dry on the outside and remain raw inside.
6. Roasting time: for every half kilogram (1 Pfd.), allow 8 minutes for knuckles over 5 kg (10 Pfd.), and about 10 minutes per half kilogram for smaller ones.
7. During roasting, gradually pour water or milk into the pan, scraping the bottom, and every 5-10 minutes baste the roast with the liquid fat.
8. Complete the roast by mixing 3 tablespoons milk or water with potato flour, cook it out with the sauce, strain through a sieve, and coat the knuckle with the sauce.
9. If preferring well-done rather than pink meat, allow 10 minutes per half kilogram (1 Pfd.).
