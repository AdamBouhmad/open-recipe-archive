## Ingredients

- 250 g pork cutlets
- 1/2 teaspoon pepper and salt
- 2 tablespoons grated breadcrumbs
- 1 teaspoon flour
- 1/2 egg white
- 1 tablespoon lard
- 1 tablespoon soup vegetables
- 10 g flour
- 10 g onion slices
- 1/2 tablespoon rendered fat
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 3/8 l water
- 1/2 teaspoon mustard

## Directions

1. Trim the meat to a straw's width of fat, remove and chop the backbone (not the rib bone), cut into small pieces and brown; cut the separated fat into cubes and render it, prepare the onion, clean and wash the soup vegetables and cut into pieces, whisk the egg white with 1/2 teaspoon water, mix flour and breadcrumbs.
2. Heat 1/2 tablespoon of the rendered fat in a pot, add the bones, soup vegetables, onion and salt, brown the bones while stirring, add 3/8 l water, cover and let simmer slowly. Lightly wash the pork cutlets, pound them on both sides, sprinkle with pepper and salt, pull them through the beaten egg white on a fork and turn them in the mixture of breadcrumbs and flour. Melt the remaining rendered fat and 1 tablespoon lard in a sauté pan and heat until steaming. Place the breaded cutlets in it and fry them slowly on both sides while turning until a nice color, place them on a hot plate but uncovered so they do not become soft. From the fat in which the cutlets were fried and the flour make a roux, mix some mustard with it and slowly pour in the broth from the boiled bones while stirring, season the sauce with pepper and salt and serve the cutlets with the sauce.
