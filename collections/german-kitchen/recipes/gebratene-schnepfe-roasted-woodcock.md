## Ingredients

- woodcock
- salt
- pepper
- cloves
- onion
- black bread
- fat
- yellow carrots
- parsnip roots
- jus
- giblets
- half a lemon shell
- parsley
- young hen liver
- nutmeg
- white pepper
- flour
- butter
- red wine
- roasting fat
- white bread without crust
- lard

## Directions

1. Pluck the woodcock with its head, though it is better if the head is not marinated.
2. Turn the feet back onto the thighs and remove the entrails.
3. Rub the bird well with salt, pepper, and some cloves.
4. Place an onion and a piece of black bread inside it, tuck the head onto the breast, and press out the eyes.
5. Roast it in a casserole with a piece of fat.
6. Lay onions, yellow carrots, and parsnip roots beside it, covering the casserole so the bird stays white.
7. Pour a little jus over it.
8. For the woodcock farce: Take the giblets (but not the stomach), clean the intestines, and mince them very finely with the shell of half a lemon, parsley, and the liver of a young hen.
9. Add nutmeg, white pepper, and 3 finger-widths of flour.
10. In a small casserole, stir together in melted butter 2 tablespoonfuls of flour, some red wine, and a spoonful of the fat the woodcocks were roasted in, but do not cook it.
11. Cut white bread without crust into slices, fry them in lard, spread the woodcock farce on them, and place the slices on the platter.
12. Put the woodcocks in the center, garnish with parsley and lemon, and serve the sauce separately.
