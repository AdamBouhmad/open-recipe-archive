## Ingredients

- 1 measure of water
- ice
- salt

## Directions

1. Crush the ice as finely as possible
2. Place a hand's height of ice in the freezing bucket and mix in a portion of salt
3. Set the covered tin or tin plate with the mixture into the bucket
4. Crush the remaining ice very finely, mix it with salt, and completely cover the tin with it
5. Sprinkle several handfuls of salt on top to accelerate freezing
6. Let the tin stand in the ice for only a quarter hour and shake it several times by the handle, ensuring it remains in the ice
7. Open the lid and insert the stirring spoon with one hand while driving the tin around the spoon with the other hand as quickly as possible, all while keeping it in the ice, as this is very important so that the mass not only freezes faster but also becomes fine and tender
8. Take care that no ice falls into the tin; wrap a clean cloth around the tin instead
9. During the stirring, work the bottom and sides of the tin well so that any frozen deposits continually detach
10. If the frozen mass adheres too strongly, loosen it with the stirring spoon and press it upward in small pieces
11. Then let the tin stand for a quarter hour, stir and work the mass until the frozen substance is stiff but not lumpy, rather such that it can be cut like butter
12. Finally close the tin, cover it, let it stand in the bucket, and set it in the cellar
13. When the mass is firmly frozen, this ice will keep for 3 to 4 hours
14. When serving, turn it out onto a plate
