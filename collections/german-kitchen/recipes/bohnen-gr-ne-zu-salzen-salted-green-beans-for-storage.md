## Ingredients

- 5 kg (10 Pfd.) beans
- 1 kg (2 Pfd.) salt

## Directions

1. Pick snap beans and string beans while they are still tender and not tough or stringy. Remove the strings, cut them finely or break them, and immediately mix with salt.
2. Spread a layer of salt on the bottom of a large tall stoneware or Bunzlau pot, then layer the salted beans without any other additions and pack them down firmly with a wooden stirring stick, which is important for proper preservation.
3. Lay a piece of clean old linen cloth on top, which prevents mold formation and can later be easily washed if necessary. Then place a porcelain lid or similar on top, which is weighted down with a clean field stone. The pot of beans can be stored in any place that is not too warm.
4. Alternatively: Pack them layer by layer with salt in glasses or pots. Lay a suitable small board on top, weighted down with a stone. As soon as brine has formed, remove the stone and board, pour melted warm fat over the brine, and tie up the pot. Larger quantities, well mixed with salt, can be placed in a stone pot under the weight of a field stone lying on a clean cloth and used as needed. The cloth should be washed frequently.
