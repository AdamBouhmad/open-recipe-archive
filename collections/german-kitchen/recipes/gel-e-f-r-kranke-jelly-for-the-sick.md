## Ingredients

- 2 Loth of isinglass
- half a Maas of water
- 1 Schoppen of good sea wine
- 2 to 3 handfuls of watercress
- 2 egg whites
- 2 to 3 drops of Prussian blue

## Directions

1. Let the isinglass melt in a pan with half a Maas of water over gentle heat.
2. When nothing of the isinglass remains visible, pour in 1 Schoppen of good sea wine.
3. Crush 2 to 3 handfuls of watercress in a mortar, press the juice through a cloth, put it in the pan, let everything cook together, beat 2 egg whites into it.
4. Draw it through a felt hat into a porcelain bowl, add 2 to 3 drops of Prussian blue, let it stand and give the patient one spoonful at a time; it is very good for the chest.
