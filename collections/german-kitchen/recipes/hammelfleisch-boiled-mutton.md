## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg (1 pound) mutton (shoulder)
- 1 liter water
- 8 g or 1 scant tablespoon salt

## Directions

1. Set the water with salt on the stove. Quickly wash and pound the mutton.
2. When the salt water boils, put the meat in and cook it slowly covered until foam forms. After the meat has cooked for a while with the foam, remove the foam and cook the meat covered until tender in 1-1/4 to 1-1/2 hours.
3. Remove it with a spoon, pour a few spoons of the mutton fat from the broth over it, and keep it covered and hot.
4. The broth can be used for cooking beans. You can also cook beans that have been blanched in salt water and cooled in cold water with the mutton, but add them 3/4 hours before the meat is tender, otherwise treat as described. If you want to use the meat broth as soup, add 1 tablespoon finely cut soup vegetables as seasoning to the meat.
