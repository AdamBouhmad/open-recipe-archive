## Ingredients

- 3-1/2 kg (7 Pfd.) cucumbers, weighed after salting
- salt as needed
- beer vinegar as needed
- 1 l wine vinegar
- 10 g salt
- 10 g sugar
- 60 g shallots, peeled and scalded once with warm vinegar
- 30 g pearl onions, peeled and scalded once with warm vinegar
- 3 tablespoons horseradish chunks
- 4 dill flowers
- 1 chili pepper
- 3 muslin bags with various spices
- 4 tablespoons mustard seeds

## Directions

1. Wash the cucumbers thoroughly, peel starting from the tip, cut them through, and remove the seeds with a tablespoon, then cut into uniform pieces of your choice.
2. Salt them with 50 g salt per 1 kg (2 Pfd.) of cucumber pieces by mixing well and let stand until the next day.
3. After they have dried, pour cooled, lukewarm beer vinegar over them; boil the same vinegar again the next day and pour it cooled over the cucumbers.
4. On the next day, bring wine vinegar with 10 g salt and 10 g sugar to a boil per 1 l, remove the cucumbers from the beer vinegar, layer them with all the carefully cleaned various ingredients in glasses or pots, and fill the cooled wine vinegar over them, place cross-sticks on top, and tie the glasses with parchment paper.
5. You can greatly accelerate softening by pouring the vinegar hot over them once.
6. After 8 days, pour off the vinegar again, bring it to a boil, and pour it back over the cucumbers after cooling.
