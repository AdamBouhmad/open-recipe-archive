## Ingredients

- Halfreife Brockelerbsen (Half-ripe brockle peas)
- Salz (Salt)
- Schweinsblase or Bouteille (Pig's bladder or bottle)
- Bindfaden (Twine)
- Stroh or Heu (Straw or hay)

## Directions

1. Select half-ripe brockle peas and remove any wormy ones
2. For one measure of kernels, add 2 handfuls of salt
3. Let them stand overnight
4. Fill a pig's bladder or bottle with the brockle peas and salt together, tie the bladder securely, and make a loop of twine so it can be hung
5. Heat water in a kettle and add straw or hay to it
6. Place the bottle in the water so they do not touch each other; if using a bladder, hang it from a stick and cook it the same length of time as a bottle, but no longer than a couple of hard-boiled eggs
7. Remove from heat and let it stand until the water is cold
8. Hang the bladder in a dry place to preserve
9. If the brockle peas are in a bottle, cork it securely and place it in the cellar
