## Ingredients

- 2 pounds fine flour
- 3 spoonfuls beer yeast
- 1 Schoppen lukewarm milk
- quarter pound butter
- 2 whole eggs and yolks
- flour
- quarter pound butter
- approximately 1 Schoppen milk
- 1 Loth pounded sugar

## Directions

1. In a bowl make a small dough with 2 pounds fine flour, 3 spoonfuls beer yeast and 1 Schoppen lukewarm milk, let it rise well in gentle warmth.
2. Cut quarter pound butter to it, along with 2 whole eggs and yolks, work the dough until it throws bubbles.
3. Let it rise in the warmth, and when it is quite ripe, place cloth on a noodle board, sprinkle flour on it, set the noodles, as large as a hen's egg, on it and let them rise a little more.
4. Let quarter pound butter melt in a suitable dish, but do not let it become hot.
5. Pour approximately 1 Schoppen milk to it, 1 Loth pounded sugar.
6. Set the steamed noodles in the dish and a cover with weak coals on top.
7. Place the dish on a trivet, put a little coals under it and let it cook through.
