## Ingredients

- 200 g cooked meat
- 50 g rice
- 1/4 liter broth
- 15 g margarine
- 1/8 liter meat extract broth
- 10 g onions
- salt to taste
- 5 g Swiss or Parmesan cheese
- 1 pinch white pepper
- 3 g flour

## Directions

1. Prepare a rice porridge from washed, blanched rice and rich meat broth (or 1 teaspoon fat and meat extract broth), 1 onion which is cooked whole and later removed, the necessary salt, and finally the grated cheese.
2. Let the coarsely chopped meat, to which one can also use cooked ham or cured meat, simmer for a few minutes in hot brown margarine along with 1/8 liter meat extract broth, the pepper, and flour.
3. Combine the meat and rice.
4. Alternatively: Layer rice and meat, each prepared separately, in alternating layers so that rice forms the top layer on a butter-brushed dish from which the dish can be brought both to the oven and to table, brush one beaten egg over it, sprinkle the surface with grated cheese, and let the hash brown in the oven.
5. Alternatively: Press both in layers into a well-buttered mold dusted with breadcrumbs and bring the dish turned out, with a piquant brown or Dutch sauce to table. The meat can also be cut into thin slices, sprinkled with margarine pieces and grated cheese, and prepared with the rice according to the various instructions above. (Requires more fat and cheese.)
