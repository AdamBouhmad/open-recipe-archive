## Ingredients

- capon
- bacon
- butter
- onions
- bay leaves
- carrots, yellow
- celery
- crayfish, 25
- asparagus
- salt
- flour
- egg yolks, 3
- water or meat stock
- mace

## Directions

1. Clean and wash the capon, cut off the head in such a way that the skin remains on the body, wrap it in bacon, place it with a piece of butter in a casserole.
2. Add onions, bay leaves, yellow carrots, and celery, cover the casserole, and braise the capon for half an hour.
3. Clean and cook the crayfish as usual, then pound them finely with a quarter pound of butter.
4. Braise them in a casserole until they foam, add 2 ladlefuls of water or meat stock, cook for a quarter hour, remove from heat, and skim off the crayfish butter into cold water.
5. Clean and bundle the asparagus, cook in salted water until tender.
6. For the sauce: melt half the crayfish butter, stir in one strong spoonful of flour, add 3 egg yolks, smooth with cold water, add capon broth and a pinch of mace.
7. Arrange the capon on a platter, lay the asparagus bundles around it, pour the crayfish sauce over it, brush the capon with the remaining crayfish butter, and place the crayfish tails on the capon's breast.
