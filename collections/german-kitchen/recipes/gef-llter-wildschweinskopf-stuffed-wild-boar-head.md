## Ingredients

- wild boar head
- lean wild boar meat
- fresh bacon
- white bread
- eggs
- salt
- pepper
- cloves
- mace
- nutmeg
- truffles
- calf's feet
- pig's feet
- old hen
- beef or cow meat
- roots and herbs
- bay leaves
- wine
- water
- vinegar
- spinach
- lemon
- butter
- lard

## Directions

1. Cut the head not too short and wash it 2 to 3 times in warm water.
2. Brush it clean until it becomes a nice yellow color.
3. Clean the ears thoroughly and cut open the bottom of the head.
4. Carefully remove the skull so that no holes are made in the skin.
5. Chop 2 pounds of lean wild boar meat and the meat removed from the head with 1 pound of fresh bacon and mix to a paste.
6. Soak white bread (roll or semmel) with the crust removed in water, press it firmly out, and work it together with the pork forcemeat, 2 eggs, salt, pepper, cloves, mace, and nutmeg in a mortar until foamy.
7. Fill half of this mixture into the head, lay some thinly sliced truffles on top, then add the other half of the forcemeat.
8. Place a piece of bacon rind on top of the stuffing before the opening to keep the filling secure.
9. Sew the opening closed and wrap the head in cloth.
10. For the aspic: put 8 cleaned calf's feet, 2 pig's feet, an old hen, 2 pounds of beef or cow meat, all sorts of roots and herbs, cloves, pepper, mace, whole cinnamon, onions, bay leaves, and a generous handful of salt into a well-tinned copper pot.
11. Pour in 3 measures of wine, 4 measures of water, and 1.5 measures of vinegar.
12. Place everything together with the pig's head on the fire, cover the pot, cook the head slowly, and turn it often.
13. A large pig's head often takes 3 to 3.5 hours to cook through.
14. Place the cooked head on a dish to drain; pour the drained liquid back into the aspic and let it stand overnight if time allows.
15. Beat 4 eggs into a casserole, carefully remove the fat from the aspic, pour the settled part into the casserole, put it on the fire, and whisk with a whisk until it begins to boil.
16. Remove it from heat, squeeze the juice of a lemon into it, and strain it through a felt hat; if it does not become clear on the first straining, filter it through the felt until it is clear enough.
17. Pour some of this clear aspic onto the plate on which the head is to be served and let it set.
18. Make a green aspic: crush 2 to 3 handfuls of cleaned spinach in a mortar, press the juice through a cloth, let it coagulate in a small vessel on the fire, and pour it through a fine sieve.
19. Stir this spinach cheese into half-set aspic, pour it onto the plate so that it forms a mound, and let it set.
20. Clean the pig's head of all fat and lay it on the mound of green aspic.
21. Cover it with clear aspic until the head is completely embedded in the aspic.
22. Before serving, garnish it with lemons and cut-out decorative herbs.
23. For grand tables, the pig's head can also be prepared as follows: 5 pounds of butter and 5 pounds of lard are stirred together for half an hour until they become like pomade.
24. Make a kind of altar on a large long plate from most of this mass and decorate it with columns formed from the rest of the mass, which can be colored brown with chocolate, yellow with saffron, or green with spinach cheese.
25. A hunter, pilgrim, or other figure formed from the same mass can be placed between two such columns.
26. The pig's head is then placed on this altar.
