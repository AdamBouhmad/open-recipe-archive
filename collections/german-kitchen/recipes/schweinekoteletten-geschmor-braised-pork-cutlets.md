## Ingredients

- 250 g pork shoulder
- 1 pinch salt and pepper
- 5 g flour
- 1/2 spice mixture portion
- 10 g onions
- 1 teaspoon mustard
- 4 g potato starch
- 1 tablespoon vinegar
- 3/16 l water

## Directions

1. Cut off as much of the outer fat as possible and preferably use the piece from the neck because it is more marbled and therefore stays juicier. Cut the pieces 2 cm thick, salt and pepper them and lightly coat them in flour. Dice the onions.
2. Place the cutlets side by side in a wide earthenware braising pot in which you have rendered the fat scraps, brown them on both sides after removing the cracklings, add spices, onions and 1/8 l water, cover the pot and let the cutlets braise slowly for 1 hour, adding a little water from time to time. Degrease the sauce, mix with mustard, potato starch and vinegar, season, strain through a sieve and pour over the cutlets.
