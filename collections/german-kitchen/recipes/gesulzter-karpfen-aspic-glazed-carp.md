## Ingredients

- 1 carp weighing 2 to 3 pounds
- salt and pepper
- 1 measure of water
- 1/2 pound grated hartshorn
- 1 onion
- 3 bay leaves
- whole cloves
- whole peppercorns
- 1/2 lemon, cut into leaves
- 1/2 schoppen of wine, water, or meat broth (if needed)
- 3 to 4 drops of violet juice

## Directions

1. Scale the carp cleanly, wash it, cut open the belly (not too wide), wipe the inside with a cloth, and rub well with salt and pepper.
2. Wrap the carp well in cloth and let it stand for 2 hours.
3. In a pan, slowly simmer 1/2 pound of grated hartshorn in 1 measure of water for 2 to 3 hours (do not turn it or it will become cloudy).
4. Place the fish in a cooking vessel where it will remain straight. Add 1 onion, 3 bay leaves, whole cloves, whole peppercorns, and 1/2 lemon cut into leaves.
5. Pour the clear hartshorn broth through a cloth over the fish. If the broth is insufficient, add 1/2 schoppen of wine, water, or meat broth. Cover and cook until the fish separates from the bones.
6. Let the fish cool on a platter. Strain the aspic through a cloth and divide into 2 parts. Place one part on the serving platter, let it set, then carefully place the carp on it.
7. Add 3 to 4 drops of violet juice to the remaining aspic and pour it over the carp.
