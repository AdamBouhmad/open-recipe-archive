## Ingredients

- Game meat, preferably from the hind leg
- Vinegar for marinating
- Lard
- Onions
- Bacon
- Roots of various kinds
- Bay leaves
- 6 whole cloves
- Mace
- Whole white peppercorns
- Cubebs and cardamom totaling half a loth
- Wine
- Meat broth
- One quarter pound Nuremberg gingerbread
- Black bread
- Pork fat
- Sugar
- 6 to 8 mushrooms
- One spoonful capers
- One cleaned and finely ground truffle
- Lemon

## Directions

1. Marinate the game meat in vinegar for a few days beforehand.
2. Skin the meat well and lard it; pour hot lard over it.
3. Line the bottom of a casserole with onions, bacon, all kinds of roots, bay leaves, 6 whole cloves, mace, whole white peppercorns, and cubebs and cardamom (totaling half a loth).
4. Place all the game meat in the casserole, pour a glass of wine and a ladleful of meat broth over it, cover the casserole tightly (or better yet, seal it with paper), place it on coals, and let it steam slowly for 2 to 3 hours.
5. Do not salt it until serving time.
6. For the sauce: Rub one quarter pound Nuremberg gingerbread and the same amount of black bread on a grater.
7. Roast the grated mixture in a piece of pork fat along with a small piece of sugar until it looks brown like gingerbread.
8. Pour a quart of wine and 2 ladles full of good bouillon over it and let it cook together.
9. Place the game meat on a dish to keep warm, pass the juice through a fine sieve, and pour it into the sauce.
10. Put the meat and sauce into another casserole, cut 6 to 8 mushrooms into small pieces, add one spoonful of capers and one cleaned and finely ground truffle.
11. Let everything cook together for another quarter hour, and when serving, squeeze the juice of half a lemon over it and sprinkle the grated zest on the game.
