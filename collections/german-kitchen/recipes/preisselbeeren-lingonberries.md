## Ingredients

- 3-1/2 kg (7 lbs) lingonberries
- 1/2 l soft, cold water
- 1-3/4 kg (3-1/2 lbs) sugar
- White paper, 1 tablespoon rum

## Directions

1. Prepare the berries as for compote.
2. Cook the berries that fall to the bottom and put them in a bag, which you put into the pot (the juice promotes gelling).
3. Let the berries swimming on top drain well and pour them into the sugar that has meanwhile been cooked with the water and juice until the first degree.
4. As soon as they are clear and skimmed, that is, have a transparent, light red color, remove them with a skimming spoon and let the juice simmer for another 15-20 minutes, pour it over the berries and stir with a spoon for 1/4 hour so that they fill with juice.
5. Fill the berries in glasses or pots, cover with rum paper after they have cooled, and bind with parchment paper.
6. Diced pumpkin, apples or pears can be cooked very advantageously with the lingonberries; carrot cubes are also used for increase, but these must first be cooked separately in water, drained, then added to the raw berries.
