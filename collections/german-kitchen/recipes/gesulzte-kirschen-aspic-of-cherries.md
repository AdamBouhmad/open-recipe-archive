## Ingredients

- half a pound of sour cherries
- juice of the cherries
- one bottle of Roussillon wine
- 3 Schoppen of veal aspic
- 12 Loth of sugar
- 3 egg whites
- 8 cloves
- whole cinnamon

## Directions

1. Stone the cherries, press them through a hair sieve, crush the stones and cook them with the cherry juice, a bottle of Roussillon wine, and 3 Schoppen of veal aspic together with 12 Loth of sugar, 3 egg whites, 8 cloves and some whole cinnamon.
2. Beat with a snow fork in the pan until the contents begin to boil (but it must only boil as long as a soft-boiled egg).
3. Remove the pan from the fire, pour the cherry aspic through a filtering hat, and if it is not a beautiful red, help it along with cochineal color.
4. Remove the stems from another half pound of sour cherries, let them sweat a little in a dish on the embers, then place them in a hair sieve to drain.
5. Pour some of the aspic into a desired mold, let it stand a little, place 6 to 8 cherries in the form, then pour aspic over them, fill the mold halfway with it, let it stand again, place cherries in again, and so a few more times until the mold is full.
6. Let the jelly set on ice and turn it out onto a plate.
