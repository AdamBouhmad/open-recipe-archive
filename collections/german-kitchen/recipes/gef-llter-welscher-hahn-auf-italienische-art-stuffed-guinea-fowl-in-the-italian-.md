## Ingredients

- 1 guinea fowl
- salt
- pepper
- 1 pound macaroni
- half pound grated Parmesan cheese
- half pound finely chopped ham
- quarter pound butter
- 2 eggs
- 2 spoonfuls sour cream
- bacon
- lemons
- 1 ladleful meat broth

## Directions

1. Prepare the aspic as described for the pickled guinea fowl
2. Immediately after killing, strike the breast, pluck while still warm, clean carefully over flame, gut it, remove the crop so there is no hole, remove the breastbone and ribs, and rub the body with salt and pepper
3. Cook 1 pound macaroni in salted water until half-soft, drain well through a sieve
4. Mix together the macaroni with half pound grated Parmesan cheese, half pound finely chopped ham, quarter pound butter, 2 eggs, and 2 spoonfuls sour cream, mixing well in a bowl
5. Fill the guinea fowl and its crop firmly with this mixture, sew it shut, dress it, wrap in bacon and lemons, place in a casserole
6. Pour 1 ladleful of meat broth over it, cover, and braise slowly over coals for 2 hours, taking care not to lay it on its breast
7. Let it cool on a plate; once cool, place it on the serving plate, pour clear aspic over it, let it set, then pour half-set aspic over it again
8. Once this has completely set, garnish the guinea fowl as desired
