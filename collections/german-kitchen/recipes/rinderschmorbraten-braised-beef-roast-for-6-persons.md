## Ingredients

- 1-1/2 kg (3 lbs) beef
- 30 g fatty bacon
- 20 g flour
- 1 spice dose
- 30 g bread crust
- 30 g fat or margarine
- 20 g onions
- 15 g salt
- water as needed

## Directions

1. The meat is beaten and tied with twine into good shape. The bacon is cut into 4 cm long, 8 mm thick strips and turned in the crushed spice mixed with salt. With a pointed wooden stick, the bacon strips are inserted into the meat piece at equal distances and this is then turned in flour mixed with a little salt.
2. In the meantime, fat—also bacon scraps—is made hot and brown in an earthenware pot, the meat is browned on all sides, using a skimming spoon and a ladle for turning, then onions, crumbled bread crust and the remaining spice are added, gradually 1/4 l hot water is poured on, the pot is tightly covered, weighted with a bolt and the meat is slowly braised at the side of the fire. Every half hour it is turned to another side and the reduced sauce is replenished with hot water. For this replenishment, one can add beer or, if the piece lay in a vinegar marinade, some of it as desired. After 3 hours, the soft braised piece is removed from the pot, covered with a deep bowl, the sauce is degreased and checked for its binding, according to which it is either thinned with any liquids or thickened with a little cornstarch, mixed cold. Then it is stirred through a sieve and after everything that has stuck in the braising pot is cooked off, the sauce is returned to the braising pot.
