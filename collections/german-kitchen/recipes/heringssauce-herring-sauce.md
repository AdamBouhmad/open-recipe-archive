## Ingredients

- 1/2 herring
- 30 g flour
- 20 g fat or margarine
- 1/2 l meatball broth or broth
- 10 g onions
- 1 tablespoon vinegar
- 1 pinch pepper and salt

## Directions

1. Soak the herring, divide it, and finely chop one half; set the other half aside. Peel and chop the onion.
2. Heat the fat in an earthenware pot, add the onion and sweat it with the fat, then add the flour. Once the flour is cooked, pour in 1/2 l liquid and cook the sauce uncovered until thickened.
3. Add the chopped herring; the sauce should now only simmer.
4. Season with vinegar, pepper, and salt. If you want it very smooth, strain it through a sieve.
5. The sauce can be colored yellow with saffron or brown with food coloring, but use only a small knife point of either coloring agent.
6. The remaining herring half can be marinated or used for salad or herring potatoes.
