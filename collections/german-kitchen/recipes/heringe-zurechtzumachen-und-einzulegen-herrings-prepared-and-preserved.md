## Ingredients

- 15 herrings
- 1 l vinegar
- 100 g onion slices
- 4 spice portions
- 1 sour cucumber
- 5 g salt

## Directions

1. Soak the herrings. Boil the vinegar. Slice the onions and cucumber. Measure out the spices.
2. Wash the herrings and soak them in cold water for 12-24 hours depending on their firmness. Young Matjes herrings should not be soaked or at most for 10-15 minutes.
3. Place the herrings on a plate, break out the cheeks, cut a thin strip from the belly (which can be used for another herring dish and kept in vinegar until needed), remove the innards with a knife, pull off the back skin after making a straight cut along the back, and place the herrings in a pot.
4. Layer spice portions, onion slices and cucumber slices between the herrings.
5. Pour over the herrings a light vinegar that has been boiled and cooled, in which 5 g salt has been dissolved, and weight them lightly with a saucer so they are evenly covered with liquid.
6. Keep the roe of the herrings separately in a glass, pour vinegar over it and tie it shut; when serving, mix it with fresh vinegar and pour it over the herrings.
