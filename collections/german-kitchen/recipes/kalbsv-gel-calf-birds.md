## Ingredients

- calf's leg meat
- butter
- onion, sliced
- bay leaves
- lemons
- gingerbread, brown
- tarragon or raspberry vinegar, half a glass
- sour cream, a few tablespoons
- salt

## Directions

1. Remove the bones from a calf's leg and cut the meat along the grain into pieces of desired size (usually 3 pieces per pound of meat).
2. Pound the pieces soft, skin and lard them, but not with pieces of bacon that are too long so they look attractive.
3. Place them in lukewarm water.
4. Put a piece of butter with sliced onion, bay leaves, and lemons in a casserole, add the birds, and braise for one hour.
5. Remove the birds, add a piece of brown gingerbread and half a glass of tarragon or raspberry vinegar to the sauce, return the birds, and cook for another quarter hour.
6. Pour in a few tablespoons of sour cream.
7. Strain the sauce through a fine sieve, return it to the casserole with the birds, bring to a boil again.
8. Add the finely chopped zest and juice of half a lemon to the sauce and salt to taste.
9. Note: When making this type of meat dish, it is good to add the skin and cut-out bones as they give the sauce a beautiful gloss.
