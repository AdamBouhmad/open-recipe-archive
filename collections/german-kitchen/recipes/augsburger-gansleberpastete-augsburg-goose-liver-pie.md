## Ingredients

- fine butter dough
- 3 goose livers
- 4 lemon peels
- 1 soaked and pressed rusks
- marrow from 4 lemons
- a handful of chopped and braised onions and parsley
- half a pound lean ham
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- mace
- 2 eggs
- 1 egg yolk
- 3 to 4 goose livers
- 1 spoonful capers
- mushrooms
- quarter pound cleaned truffles
- 2 goose livers
- peeled olives
- bacon slices
- 3 to 4 blanched calf's feet
- wine
- vinegar
- water
- celery
- yellow carrots
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 large onions
- whole cloves
- mace
- whole pepper
- 1 crushed egg
- 1 lemon

## Directions

1. Make a fine butter dough and line a melon mold with it.
2. Weigh 3 goose livers and cover them in a bowl.
3. Weigh 4 lemon peels, take one soaked and pressed rusk, the marrow from 4 lemons, a handful of chopped and braised onions and parsley, half a pound lean ham, salt, pepper, nutmeg, mace, 2 eggs and 1 egg yolk.
4. Mix everything together thoroughly.
5. Line the bottom of the mold with a handful of the mixture.
6. Cut 3 to 4 goose livers lengthwise and place on the mixture.
7. Add 1 spoonful of capers, place a mushroom on top, quarter pound cleaned truffles cut into leaves and crushed.
8. Add another handful of the mixture.
9. Cut 2 more goose livers lengthwise and place on top.
10. Add peeled olives, 1 spoonful capers, 4 to 6 mushrooms, truffles, and the mixture on top.
11. Place bacon slices on top and make a lid from the same dough.
12. Crimp with a pie crimper, brush the pie with eggs, and bake for 2 hours in the oven.
13. Cut an opening so steam can escape, cover again, and make an aspic.
14. Cook 3 to 4 blanched calf's feet with equal parts wine, vinegar, and water, celery, yellow carrots, 2 bay leaves, 2 large onions, whole cloves, mace, and whole pepper for 3 hours.
15. Pour through a hair sieve and let stand for a quarter hour.
16. Dip filter paper in it so the fat sticks to it.
17. Pour the jelly into a saucepan, avoiding the sediment.
18. Add a crushed egg, return to fire, and beat with a whisk until it boils.
19. Remove from fire, press in the juice from 1 lemon, cover with a lid with coals on top, and let stand for a quarter hour.
20. Pour the jelly through a felt hat and, when cold, pour it over the pie (must pour over 3 to 4 times).
21. Place the lid on top and set the pie in a cold place.
