## Ingredients

- salmon trout
- salt
- 3 calf's feet
- 2 pork feet
- 6 Schoppen water
- 3 Schoppen wine
- 1 Schoppen vinegar
- onions
- bay leaves
- mace
- good herbs
- 1 Schoppen wine for cooking
- 2 Schoppen water for cooking
- boiling milk
- lemon juice
- 6 hard-boiled eggs
- 6 fresh egg yolks
- Provence oil
- jelly

## Directions

1. Scale a beautiful salmon trout, cut open the belly and remove the entrails
2. Wash thoroughly, rub with salt inside and outside, wrap in cloth and let stand for 2 hours
3. Cook 3 calf's feet, 2 pork feet, 6 Schoppen water, 3 Schoppen wine, and 1 Schoppen vinegar together with onions, bay leaves, mace, and salt for 3 hours
4. Strain through a fine sieve into a bowl and let stand for half an hour
5. Wipe the slime from the trout and braise in a covered kettle with 1 Schoppen wine, 2 Schoppen water, all kinds of good herbs, and a small handful of salt, braising rather than boiling so it does not burst
6. Allow 1 hour for it to become tender, then let it cool
7. Place the jelly in a casserole, remove the fat from the top and leave the sediment behind
8. Cook the jelly on the fire with 1 Schoppen boiling milk, add the juice of 1 lemon and cook a while longer
9. Remove from heat and let stand for a quarter hour, then strain through a suspended felt hat
10. When properly made, it should be as clear as bright spring water
11. Pour it nail-thick onto the plate to be served, let it set firm
12. For the sauce, press the yolks from 6 hard-boiled eggs through a fine sieve into a bowl, beat in 6 fresh egg yolks
13. Add the juice of half a lemon and stir constantly for half an hour (it is best to stir over ice) with 3 drops Provence oil until quite white
14. Oil must be added drop by drop, along with some jelly, stirring for 1.5 to 2 hours
15. Pour onto the plate with the light jelly so that 2 fingers width of light jelly remains visible all around
16. Let it set firm, carefully pour light jelly over again, make some green jelly and pour it so the fish lies just on the green jelly
17. Then add some clear jelly and let it set
