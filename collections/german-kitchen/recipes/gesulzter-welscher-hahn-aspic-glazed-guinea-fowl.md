## Ingredients

- 1 guinea fowl
- 3 to 4 pounds pork forcemeat
- 1 pound veal forcemeat
- white bread (2 kreuzer worth, crusts removed), soaked in milk and squeezed
- 3 large onions, peeled and chopped
- 1/4 pound bacon, cut into small pieces
- a handful of salt
- 2 knife tips of cloves
- 3 knife tips of mace
- 1 whole nutmeg
- zest of 2 lemons, finely chopped, plus the pulp without seeds
- 1/4 pound anchovies, cleaned and boned
- 1/2 pound truffles, cleaned and skinned
- 2 eggs and 2 egg yolks
- 1/2 pound ham, cut into quill-feather-thick pieces
- 1/2 pound bacon, cut into quill-feather-thick pieces
- 8 chopped veal feet
- 1 measure of wine
- 2 measures of water
- 3 schoppen of vinegar
- 1 loth of mace
- 1 whole lemon, cut into leaves, without seeds
- 2 yellow turnips, cut into pieces
- 4 bay leaves
- 4 onions
- 1 celery, cut into pieces
- cloves and pepper, together 1 loth
- 3 whole eggs
- juice of 3 lemons
- bay leaves and lemons for garnish

## Directions

1. Clean the guinea fowl, wash it, remove the eyes, and scald the head to make it white; do not gut it. Cut off the feet at the knuckle, cut the fowl open along the back, and remove the body in such a way that all the meat remains attached to the skin. Cut off the wings at the second joint and cut through the neck so that the head remains attached to the skin. Remove the wing bones.
2. For the forcemeat: cut the bacon into small pieces and render it in a pan with the chopped onions until they are slightly golden. Mix the pork and veal forcemeat with the bread soaked in milk and squeezed, the cooked onions and bacon, salt, cloves, mace, nutmeg, lemon zest and pulp, anchovies, and truffles. Grind everything together in a mortar with 2 eggs and 2 egg yolks until it forms a foam.
3. Fill one quarter of the forcemeat into the guinea fowl, then layer half a pound of ham and half a pound of bacon cut into quill-feather-thick pieces with truffle slices. Add another layer of forcemeat, then another layer of ham, bacon, and truffle, finishing with a layer of forcemeat. Make sure to fill the neck and thighs well with forcemeat.
4. Sew up the fowl, tuck the head under the wing, and wrap it tightly in clean cloth to maintain its shape.
5. Place the prepared fowl in a large pan with 8 chopped veal feet, add 1 measure of wine, 2 measures of water, and 3 schoppen of vinegar. Add 1 loth of mace, 1 whole lemon cut into leaves without seeds, 2 yellow turnips cut into pieces, 4 bay leaves, 4 onions, 1 celery cut into pieces, and cloves and pepper totaling 1 loth.
6. Cook everything for 2 to 3 hours. If the broth does not cover the fowl, add more water and wine. After 2 hours, remove the fowl and let it cool in the cloth.
7. Continue cooking the remaining ingredients for another hour. Strain through a fine sieve into a clean vessel and let stand overnight.
8. On the day of serving, remove the fat from the aspic, bring the aspic to a boil in a pan, crush 3 whole eggs into it, and stir until it boils. Remove from heat, add the juice of 3 lemons, and strain through a felt hat.
9. Remove the cloth from the fowl, clean it with a clean cloth, place it on a platter, and cover with clear aspic. Let it cool, then cover again, repeating until the fowl is completely covered in aspic.
10. Garnish with bay leaves and lemons.
