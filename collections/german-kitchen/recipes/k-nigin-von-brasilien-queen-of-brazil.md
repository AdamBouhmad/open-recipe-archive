## Ingredients

- 1/2 pound butter
- 10 eggs
- 1/2 schoppen thick, sweet, warm cream
- salt
- 1 loth sugar
- 10 tablespoons fine flour
- 3 tablespoons good white yeast
- 3/4 pound chocolate
- 1 schoppen water
- 24 egg yolks
- 1 maas milk
- 1 cooking spoon flour
- 1/2 pound sugar
- 1 glass water
- cherries
- apricots

## Directions

1. Prepare a fine Gugelhopf or Munich butter loaf the day before.
2. Beat 1/2 pound butter in a bowl for half an hour until foamy.
3. Place 10 eggs in warm water and stir into the butter one by one (each egg must be stirred for 3 minutes).
4. Stir in 1/2 schoppen thick, sweet, warm cream, a knife-tip of salt, and 1 loth sugar for another quarter hour.
5. Stir in 10 tablespoons fine flour until no flour is visible, then 3 tablespoons good white yeast.
6. Grease a tall vessel with butter, fill with the batter, place in gentle warmth, let it rise until it begins to sink again, and bake in gentle heat until golden.
7. Stand the baked loaf upright on a plate and when ready to serve, carefully cut off the lid level with the top.
8. Remove the interior with a spoon so no hole remains.
9. Dissolve 3/4 pound chocolate in a brass pan with 1 schoppen water.
10. Beat 24 egg yolks in a pot, heat the chocolate with 1 maas milk until boiling, pour onto the egg yolks, add 1 cooking spoon flour, cook again in the pan with constant stirring, pour through a hair sieve into another vessel, and let cool slightly.
11. Fill the cream into the hollowed Munich loaf and carefully replace the lid.
12. Place on a large round plate.
13. Beat the snow from 24 egg whites and cover the bread and plate up to the middle band with it, making the mixture as high as possible with a rough, mountain-like appearance, creating a cross-cut at the top to give it a crown shape.
14. Dissolve 3/4 pound chocolate again in a brass pan with 1 glass water, add 1/2 pound sugar, add 1 more glass water, and cook everything together for a quarter hour.
15. Brush the chocolate glaze over a brush onto the snow mountain until completely covered.
16. Place a beautiful cherry at each of the 4 corners of the crown and a beautiful apricot in the middle.
17. Before the snow goes onto the torte, place it in the oven for a quarter hour to warm it again, but not after.
