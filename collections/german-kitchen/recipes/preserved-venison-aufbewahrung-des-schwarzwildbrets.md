## Ingredients

- Venison pieces and legs (not heavier than 5-6 pounds each)
- Vinegar (2 parts)
- Poor quality wine (1 part)
- Water (1 part)
- Calf feet (6-8), hacked up
- Salt
- Juniper berries, crushed
- Various herbs

## Directions

1. Take the best pieces of venison and the strongest legs.
2. Set aside as much as needed for pickling.
3. Cook 2 parts vinegar, 1 part poor quality wine, 1 part water with 6 to 8 hacked calf feet, add salt, a handful of crushed juniper berries, and various herbs.
4. Cook everything together until the feet are soft.
5. Remove the feet and herbs.
6. Strain the broth and let it cool.
7. Bring the venison into a pot as tightly as possible.
8. For this purpose, strew the bottom with salt, juniper berries, and some herbs, lay a layer of venison on top, then sprinkle again, and continue until all venison is in the pot.
9. Pour the cooled broth over it.
10. After the pot has stood for a time, cover it with a board and press down firmly.
11. In this way, the venison keeps for a whole year.
