## Ingredients

- Walnuts (50 nuts)
- 1-1/2 pounds sugar
- Cinnamon
- Cloves
- Cinnamon water
- Cherry spirits for soaking paper

## Directions

1. Break the walnuts around Saint John's Day when they are still soft and milky
2. Cut a little off the top and bottom
3. Pierce 2 to 3 holes with a stick on each side
4. Lay them in fresh spring water for 8 days
5. Cook them in a brass pan for a quarter hour in water
6. Pour off the water, add fresh water, and repeat this three times
7. Test the nuts with a small stick to see if they are soft; if not, cook them once more
8. When soft, place them in cold water
9. Drain the water 3 times
10. Lay them on a hair sieve and let them dry
11. Lard the nuts with cinnamon and cloves
12. Lay them in a sugar jar
13. For 50 nuts, take 1-1/2 pounds sugar
14. Clarify this in a brass pan with cinnamon water until it spins a thick thread
15. Pour the clarified sugar over the nuts
16. Let them stand for 2 days
17. On the third day, pour off the sugar again
18. Let it boil once more, add another piece of sugar
19. Pour it over the nuts again and repeat this 3 to 4 times
20. The last time let the nuts cook along with it for a while
21. Preserve them well in a glass
22. Lay paper soaked in cherry spirits on top
23. Store in a dry place
