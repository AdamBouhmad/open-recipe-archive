## Ingredients

- Game meat of desired size
- One quarter pound bacon
- Salt
- Cloves
- Pepper
- Fat
- Onions
- Lemons
- Bay leaves
- Celery
- Meat broth
- Vinegar (unless long marinated)
- Black bread
- Lard
- Flour
- Wine
- Jus
- Mace
- Cardamom

## Directions

1. Wipe the game meat clean with a cloth and clean it of hair.
2. Cut one quarter pound of bacon into long pieces, turn them in salt, cloves, and pepper, pierce holes in the meat with a knife, and insert a piece of cut bacon into each hole until all the bacon is used.
3. Place the game meat in a casserole with a large piece of fat over the fire, add sliced onions, lemons, bay leaves, and celery, salt to taste.
4. Pour a spoonful of meat broth and some vinegar over it and let it cook covered for 2 hours (but if the game has been marinating for a long time, do not add vinegar).
5. Remove the crust from black bread and bake it in lard until light golden; once the meat is browned, remove it and put the light golden bread crust into the casserole, sprinkle a little flour in it.
6. Pour a quart of wine, a ladleful of jus, and some meat broth over it and let it cook together for a quarter hour.
7. Pass the broth through a fine sieve, pour it back into the casserole, place the meat back into it, and add the grated zest and juice of half a lemon, mace, pepper, and some ground cardamom.
