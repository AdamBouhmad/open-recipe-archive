## Ingredients

- One beautiful fat capon
- Half pound lean ham
- Three eggs
- One schoppen sweet cream
- One nutmeg, grated
- Butter
- One lemon
- Paper
- One lemon wheel

## Directions

1. Pierce the capon and let it bleed thoroughly.
2. Pluck it while warm, carefully to avoid holes.
3. Singe off remaining hairs over a flame.
4. Remove innards carefully, drawing out the crop with the intestines from below, but leave the heart and lungs.
5. Loosen the breast, back, and thighs as you would a young dove.
6. Prepare the filling: mince the lean ham very finely, add three eggs, pour in one schoppen sweet cream, add one grated nutmeg.
7. Fill a syringe with a tube the thickness of a small finger with this mixture.
8. Inject and fill the capon with the mass.
9. Sew the opening closed again.
10. Place the head with beak on the breast, arrange the feet nicely, place a lemon wheel on the breast.
11. Wrap in a sheet of paper thickly brushed with butter.
12. Roast slowly in a braising pan, basting frequently.
13. Can be served as a roast or ragout. For ragout, prepare sauce: melt a piece of butter in a casserole and braise finely cut onions and a small piece of sugar for a good quarter hour.
14. Sprinkle in one cooking spoon of flour, mix, add one large spoon of meat broth and one glass of wine.
15. Let simmer for half an hour.
16. Force the sauce through a hair sieve back into the casserole.
17. Add the yolk and marrow of one finely minced lemon.
18. Let come to a boil once more and rub in one nutmeg.
19. When ready to serve, place the capon carefully on a platter and pour the sauce directly onto the platter, not over the capon.
