## Ingredients

- good apples that do not overcook
- 1 Maas water
- 12 Loth sugar
- zest of half a lemon
- 1 piece whole cinnamon
- 1 glass wine
- butter dough
- raw potatoes or whole apples

## Directions

1. Peel and quarter good apples that do not overcook
2. Cook them with their cores and skins in 1 Maas of water in a brass pan for a quarter-hour
3. Pour through a hair sieve
4. Return the water to the pan with 12 Loth sugar, the zest of half a lemon, a piece of whole cinnamon, 1 glass of wine, and the apples
5. Cook the apples until soft but not overcooked
6. Let them drain on a hair sieve and cook the syrup until it spins threads
7. Line a cake sheet with butter dough, placing raw potatoes or whole apples on top so the dough does not shrink
8. Bake the dough
9. Remove the potatoes or apples
10. Arrange the steamed apples around the cake
11. Pour the syrup over them
12. Serve the cake—it should not be baked further
