## Ingredients

- leftover calf roast, 1 pound
- lemon zest and marrow
- shallot onions, 6 pieces
- bread roll, 1
- butter, quarter pound
- eggs, 4
- salt
- nutmeg
- lemon juice
- ox palates, 4 to 6
- flour
- red Burgundy wine, 1 glass
- good stock, half a ladleful
- mushrooms, 6 to 8 pieces
- truffles, 4 to 5
- pepper
- mace
- young chicken breasts, from 2 birds
- sweetbreads, 2
- morels
- pork forcemeat, quarter pound
- bread, small handful
- egg white
- lard
- consommé

## Directions

1. Grind leftover calf roast very finely with lemon zest and marrow.
2. Add 6 diced, braised shallot onions and a soaked, wrung-out bread roll.
3. Lightly cream quarter pound butter, add the ground meat mixture, and stir in 4 eggs, salt, nutmeg, and lemon juice.
4. Butter a suitable baking dish, pour in the mixture, make a hollow in the center, and bake in the oven until golden.
5. For the ragout: remove the white skin from ox palates, cook very tender, and cut into square pieces.
6. Make a mushroom sauce: brown one spoonful of flour in butter, deglaze with cold water, add one glass red Burgundy and half a ladleful of good stock, and cook the ox palates in this sauce.
7. Add 6 to 8 mushroom pieces, 4 to 5 cleaned, peeled, and sliced truffles, salt, nutmeg, pepper, mace, and lemon juice and zest.
8. Cut the breasts from 2 young chickens, place in lukewarm water until white, clean 2 sweetbreads, lard both beautifully, and braise in butter with half a sliced lemon for a quarter hour.
9. Add them to the ox palates with the braising fat.
10. Make a dumpling batter from cleaned morels, crushed truffles, quarter pound pork forcemeat, a small handful of bread, and an egg white; form small dumplings the size of hazelnuts and fry in lard.
11. Add to the ragout.
12. Place the baked cake on a platter, pour the ragout into the hollow, lay the chicken breasts and mushrooms on top, garnish with lemon, and pour melted consommé over the chicken breasts.
