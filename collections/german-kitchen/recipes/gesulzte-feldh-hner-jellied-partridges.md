## Ingredients

- 4 calf's feet
- 1 Maas water
- 0.5 Maas wine
- 1 Schoppen vinegar
- parsley
- celery
- onions
- bay leaves
- cloves
- mace
- salt
- 2 eggs with shells
- juice of 2 lemons
- 1 partridge breast and meat
- 0.25 pound lard with filling
- bread for 1 Kreuzer
- 4 truffles, skinned
- mace
- cloves
- pepper
- 3 onions
- 2 eggs
- 1 second partridge
- butter
- lemon
- parsley

## Directions

1. Chop 4 calf's feet together and set to fire with 1 Maas water, half Maas wine, and 1 Schoppen vinegar
2. Add parsley, celery, onions, bay leaves, cloves, mace, and a handful of salt
3. Cook for 2 to 3 hours, then strain through a fine sieve and let stand overnight
4. The next day, remove the fat, place the jelly in a brass pan, crush 2 eggs with their shells into it
5. Beat over the fire until it begins to boil, then remove from heat and squeeze in the juice of 2 lemons
6. Strain the jelly through a sugar loaf until it is quite clear
7. From a partridge, cut off the breast and all meat, mince with a quarter pound of filled lard on the chopping block
8. Soak bread (for 1 Kreuzer, de-crusted) in water and press out firmly
9. Add 4 skinned and finely ground truffles, mace, cloves, pepper, and 3 finely minced onions braised in butter
10. Beat in 2 eggs and add jelly as desired
11. Work this all through a wide sieve by hand so all skins remain behind
12. Split a second partridge down the back, loosen the body so all meat remains on the skin
13. Remove head and neck, keep the leg bone attached, cut off only the claws
14. Fill the hollow of the partridge properly with the farce and sew it closed
15. Bind the bird with lard and lemon and braise in a casserole with butter and onions over coals for 1 hour
16. Once the partridge is removed and cooled, let some light jelly set in a melon mold, line it with lemon and parsley
17. Place the partridge on it, pour remaining jelly around it and let it set firm
18. When serving, turn the whole onto a plate and garnish with parsley and lemon
