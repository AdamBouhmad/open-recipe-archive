## Ingredients

- 125 g meat scraps
- 20 g cracklings
- 1/2 old roll
- 1/2 egg
- 1 pinch pepper
- 5 g salt
- 1/2 tablespoon grated breadcrumbs
- 15 g fat or margarine
- 1 knife tip grated onion
- 1/8 l water

## Directions

1. Rub the roll into crumbs, break it up, put it in a pot and pour 1/8 l water over it. Finely chop the cooked lean meat and cracklings on a wooden board with a mincing knife.
2. In a bowl, mix the roll pressed out in a clean cloth or sieve with egg, salt, pepper and grated onion, mix thoroughly with the minced mixture and adjust the seasoning.
3. Heat fat or margarine in a pan until smoking, form 4 equal sized balls from the meat mixture, flatten them, press diagonal squares into them with the back of a knife, roll them in grated breadcrumbs, slowly fry them for 6 minutes on both sides in smoking fat until they have a nice color and serve them poured over with the fat.
