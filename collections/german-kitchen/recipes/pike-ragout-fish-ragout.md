## Ingredients

- 2 to 3 half- or three-quarter-pound pike, cleaned and prepared
- salt
- pepper
- butter
- onions
- lemons
- 1 calf's thymus (sweetbread)
- 8 large cooked crayfish
- 1/4 pound butter
- 1 roll (Mundbrod)
- milk
- 2/3 of the crayfish butter
- nutmeg
- 2 eggs
- flour
- 1/2 glass wine
- meat broth

## Directions

1. Cut the cleaned pike into finger-width pieces and rub them well with salt and pepper
2. Place a piece of butter in a casserole with the fish, some onions and lemons, cover and let steam for half an hour
3. Prepare the calf's thymus by removing the throat, blanch it briefly, cut into pieces and add to the fish
4. From 8 large cooked crayfish, remove the tails and claws, clean the bodies carefully so the upper shell remains whole
5. Pound the remaining crayfish bodies with 1/4 pound butter together, place in a casserole, steam until it foams, then strain through a sieve so the butter runs off well
6. Grate a roll on a grater and soak it in milk
7. Stir 2/3 of the crayfish butter lightly, squeeze out the milk-soaked bread and add it to the butter with salt and nutmeg
8. Stir in 2 eggs one after another
9. Remove the long beard and claw from the crayfish shells, fill the shells with the mixture and place them upside down in a dish
10. Pour a little water or meat broth around them, cover and cook on coals
11. Sprinkle 3 finger widths of flour strongly over the fish, pour on 1/2 glass wine and some meat broth, add the crayfish tails and claws
12. Arrange on a plate and lay the crayfish shells and ragout on top
13. Place the remaining crayfish butter on the ragout
