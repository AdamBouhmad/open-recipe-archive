## Ingredients

- 2 young squabs
- 30 g butter or margarine
- 5 g potato starch
- 2 tablespoons sour milk or cream
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/8 l water or broth
- 1 bread roll
- 1/2 tablespoon fat
- 1 tablespoon currants
- 1 pinch sugar and salt
- 1 egg yolk or egg white
- 9 tablespoons skim milk

## Directions

1. Pluck, singe, clean and lightly wash the squabs; remove wings, neck and legs (to the knee joint). Clean liver, stomach and heart. Rinse the currants and let them swell with a little water on the stove, grate the bread roll, break it into pieces and soften it in milk. Melt the fat and mix all ingredients together well.
2. Rub the squabs with salt and fill them with the stuffing. Sew the openings closed and tie the squabs into good shape.
3. Brown butter or margarine in a pot, place the squabs in and brown them well on all sides. Add water or broth gradually by spoonfuls and braise the squabs slowly covered for 1-1/2 hours.
4. Finally add the milk with potato starch stirred into it and let the sauce cook through. Divide the squabs lengthwise in halves and arrange them side by side on a platter.
5. Taste the sauce, strain through a sieve if necessary, and serve with the squabs.
