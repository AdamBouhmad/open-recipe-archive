## Ingredients

- squabs
- bacon
- onions
- juniper berries
- 1 bay leaf
- lemon
- basil
- piece of pork fat
- salt
- flour
- 1/2 schoppen red wine
- jus or meat broth
- mace
- lemon, chopped
- pepper

## Directions

1. Pluck the still-warm squabs, singe them at the fire and rub with a clean cloth, then gut them but do not wash
2. Cut the wings at the second joint and the feet
3. Wrap the squabs in bacon with the feet and wings, onions, juniper berries, a bay leaf, lemon and basil and a piece of pork fat in a casserole
4. Salt, cover and steam for one hour
5. When the squabs are nicely yellow, place them in a dish to keep warm
6. Take everything else from the casserole, pound it as finely as possible in a mortar and put it back in the casserole
7. Sprinkle one spoon of flour over it, pour on 1/2 schoppen red wine and one ladle of jus or meat broth
8. Cook for a quarter hour, strain through a sieve and pour back into the casserole
9. Put the squabs back in as well, add a knife point of mace, chopped lemon, salt and pepper to taste
