## Ingredients

- 1 ox tongue
- salt water
- quarter pound of bacon
- some raw ham
- 1 kreuzers worth of bread
- water
- half a lemon
- 1 onion
- fat
- salt
- nutmeg
- pepper
- 1 egg
- string
- lard slices
- bay leaf
- lemon juice
- jus

## Directions

1. Wash the tongue and cook it in salt water until not too soft
2. Remove the skin, cut it open at the base of the throat, and remove the meat such that the tongue does not get a hole
3. Mince the removed meat very finely with a quarter pound of bacon and some raw ham
4. Grate a kreuzers worth of bread, soak it in water and press it out firmly, add it to the minced meat
5. Mince half a lemon, steam an onion with fat, and add both to the mixture along with salt, nutmeg, and pepper to taste
6. Work everything together with one egg until well combined
7. Fill the tongue with this mixture and tie it together with string
8. Place it in a saucepan with some lard slices, onions, a bay leaf, and a piece of lemon, cover, and steam for half an hour
9. If serving as a roast, add lemon juice and jus
10. As a ragout it is best with a white lemon sauce: peel the zest from one large or two small lemons and mince it finely together with the pulp of the lemons (without the white skin and seeds) and small onions
11. In a saucepan with a piece of butter, let as much flour as can be held between 3 fingers brown slightly, extinguish it with meat broth, add the lemon mixture and some of the sauce in which the tongue was braised, let everything cook together for half an hour
12. Arrange the sauce on a plate and place the tongue on top
