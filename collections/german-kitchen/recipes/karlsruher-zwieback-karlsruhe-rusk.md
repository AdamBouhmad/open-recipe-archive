## Ingredients

- 4 pounds flour
- 4 strong tablespoons beer yeast
- 3 schoppen warm milk
- 1 maaß warm milk
- 12 loth finely powdered sugar
- 4 loth melted butter
- 1 egg for brushing

## Directions

1. Take 4 pounds flour in a bowl with 4 strong tablespoons beer yeast and 3 schoppen warm milk and make a very strong dough
2. Set the dish in warmth and let the dough rise
3. Place it in another dish
4. Work together 1 maaß warm milk, 12 loth finely powdered sugar, and 4 loth melted butter into a stiff dough
5. Add the first-made, smaller dough to it
6. Work everything again until no flour is visible
7. Take it to the noodle board, work it another 3/4 hour or a whole hour
8. Set it in the bowl in warmth and let it rise beautifully (it needs 1.5 to 2 hours to be ripe)
9. Cover it so it does not form a skin
10. Take it to the noodle board again, make round balls with your hands
11. Roll them out finger-long, dust a black sheet with flour, place them side by side to the end, let the dough rise once more in warmth
12. Brush with beaten egg and bake in the oven until beautifully golden, but do not cut them until the third day
13. Toast the slices, made knife-back thick, on a black sheet in gentle warmth
14. The slices can be kept for a quarter year and are given with tea, coffee, and punch
