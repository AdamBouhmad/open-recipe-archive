## Ingredients

- A nice white and tender veal leg
- Pork
- Lemon
- Onion
- Lard
- Bread
- Salt
- Pepper
- Mace
- One egg
- One egg yolk
- Goose liver
- Smoked tongue
- Half a pound of truffles
- Carrots
- Oil or fat
- Wine
- Tarragon vinegar
- Meat broth

## Directions

1. Remove the shinbone and the marrow bone from a nice white and tender veal leg (best from a cow calf).
2. Cut a hole in the leg and chop the cut-out flesh together with a piece of pork.
3. Grate the rind and marrow of a lemon, an onion browned golden in lard and a piece of soaked bread on a grater until foamy.
4. Mix this in a bowl with the chopped meat, season with salt, pepper and mace and mix it with one egg and one egg yolk to make a forcemeat.
5. Spread half of the forcemeat on the opened-out leg, place a nice large white goose liver, cut into knife-back-thick pieces, on the forcemeat.
6. Lay smoked tongue cut into long pieces on the goose liver, then half a pound of washed, skinned truffles cut into leaves, and on these the rest of the forcemeat.
7. Roll together the leg, sew it closed and place it in a casserole with cut onions, lemons, carrots and a little fat.
8. Pour a glass of wine, a little tarragon vinegar and a little meat broth over it.
9. Let it cook slowly covered for 2 hours.
10. It can be served warm or cold; cold is better however.
