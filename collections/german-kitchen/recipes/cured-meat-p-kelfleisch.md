## Ingredients

- Beef from the brisket or tail piece
- Saltpeter (2 loth)
- Tarragon
- Thyme
- Basil
- Bay leaves
- Lemon balm
- Celery herb
- Rosemary
- Marjoram
- Cloves
- Pepper
- Nutmeg
- Juniper berries
- Salt (1 pound)
- Water (1½ maas)
- Horseradish

## Directions

1. Choose a piece of beef from the brisket for serving warm, or from the tail for serving cold.
2. Rub the meat well with 2 loth of saltpeter.
3. Lay it in a stone pot or tub.
4. Crush the following herbs: tarragon, thyme, basil, bay leaves, lemon balm, celery herb, rosemary, marjoram, cloves, pepper, nutmeg, and juniper berries, and add all to the meat.
5. Cover it and place it in the cellar for 2 to 3 days.
6. On the fourth day, cook the spices in a pan with 1 pound of salt and 1½ maas of water until no salt is visible.
7. Let it cool completely.
8. Pour it again over the meat, cover it firmly, lay 2 stones on top.
9. Let it stand for 3 days, turn it on the third day, add the pickle brine again, and continue this for 3 to 4 weeks.
10. To cook, place it in a not very large pot with water; it must not be salted.
11. Serve it warm with horseradish; it is also very good with sauerkraut, winter cabbage, and beans.
