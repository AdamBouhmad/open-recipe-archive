## Ingredients

- 1 full-grown young hare
- 65 g bacon
- 15 g salt
- 200 g margarine
- 1/4 liter sour cream or milk, or milk with 1 drop of vinegar
- 6 g cornstarch
- 1/4 liter hot water

## Directions

1. Hang the hare by its hind legs on 2 hooks or strong nails; if not yet eviscerated, cut open the body lengthwise from the tail, remove the entrails with the rectum, separate the gallbladder from the liver, keep the liver, heart and lungs, discard the rest, loosen the fur on the belly from the skin, cut around the paws with a sharp knife, pull the paws out of the skin, pull the entire pelt over the back and hindquarters, remove it at the front legs, cut off the ears and pull the skin over the head. Cut off the head, neck, and back; remove the belly skin and front legs, skin the legs and braise them later. Leave the kidneys and fat in the hare, then wash it thoroughly in cold water without soaking. Cut off the hind legs at the joint, break the tailbone so the hindquarters lie well, beat the hare everywhere with a flat mallet, skin it, first the hindquarters from the underside likewise the back, either remove the fillets or place 1 slice of bacon underneath and lard the hare and legs.
2. Once everything is prepared, first place the hindquarters in the roasting pan and, so the roast does not dry out, 15 minutes later add the back and the bent-in legs, sprinkle the hare with salt and pour hot margarine over it. After the hare has browned, gradually pour in the sour cream or milk and some hot water alternately and let it finish roasting with frequent basting. Meanwhile, stir cornstarch and a few spoonfuls of sour cream or milk into the sauce, coat the hare with it after a few minutes and arrange it on a long platter after it has been cut into appropriate pieces (one can also bring the whole hare to table and separate the meat in clean pieces to still use the bone framework for a soup); deglaze the roasting pan with sour cream or milk until the sauce becomes thick and glossy and strain it through a fine sieve into a gravy boat, pouring a few spoonfuls over the arranged hare. The length of roasting time and heat naturally depends on whether the hare is young or old; a young full-grown hare would become tender at medium heat in 35 minutes, the back in 25 minutes, an old hare at lower temperature only in 1-1/2 hours. In the latter case, cover it with butcher paper.
