## Ingredients

- 1 small haddock of 1/2 kg (1 pound)
- 40 g salt
- 2 l water

## Directions

1. Scale the fish by laying it on a moistened board, holding it by the tail with a cloth, and using a knife held at an angle upward to remove the scales. Hold the knife at an angle so the scales do not scatter around the kitchen.
2. Gut the fish by cutting it open, finding the beginning and end of the intestines, loosening them with the knife, and removing all innards with your index finger.
3. If the interior of the fish is clean and the black skin has been removed, wash it carefully with water.
4. Trim the fins and tail neatly with scissors.
5. Place the fish on a plate, put this in the cooking pot and pour enough water over it so it is covered. For 1 l water, use 20 g salt.
6. Bring the prepared whole fish slowly to a boil; let whole fish come to a boil only once and then finish cooking gently.
7. The fish is done when the fins on the back can be pulled out easily and the flesh at the gills is white.
8. Remove the plate with the fish using a skimming spoon, pour off the water from the plate, wipe it clean and serve the fish with brown butter.
