## Ingredients

- 2 pounds flour
- 2 spoonfuls beer yeast
- warm milk
- 2 egg yolks
- 3 loth finely powdered sugar
- 1 knife point salt
- 3 tablespoons raspberry juice
- 1 pound finely powdered sugar
- 1 loth red sandalwood
- 1 loth Trisenet
- 1 loth powdered cinnamon
- 2 finely grated nutmegs
- 1 small knife point cloves
- 1 bottle wine

## Directions

1. In a bowl with 2 pounds flour, make a small dough with 2 spoonfuls beer yeast and warm milk
2. Let it rise and add 2 egg yolks, 3 loth finely powdered sugar, 1 knife point salt, and 3 tablespoons raspberry juice
3. Make a dough from all of this
4. Work it a quarter hour on the noodle board
5. Let it rise again in the bowl
6. When fully ripe, make 2 loaves on the noodle board
7. Roll them out long, place on a flour-dusted sheet, let them rise again
8. Brush with eggs, bake in the oven, and let them sit 2 to 3 days
9. Cut them into regular slices
10. Mix 1 pound finely powdered sugar, 1 loth red sandalwood, 1 loth Trisenet, 1 loth powdered cinnamon, 2 finely grated nutmegs, and 1 small knife point cloves together
11. Pour 1 bottle wine into a dish
12. Dip 4 to 6 slices at once in the wine, rub them well with the mixed spices, and place them in another dish
13. Continue until all are rubbed
14. Let them stand covered overnight
15. The next day, dip and rub them again in the same way
16. Let them dry slowly on a sheet of paper in a cooled oven, and store them in a box
17. They can be kept this way for half a year
