## Ingredients

- A piece of beef from the inner thigh or a fatty tail piece
- Bacon and ham for larding
- Salt and pepper
- 2 bay leaves
- 3 onions
- Yellow carrots
- Celery
- 1 Schoppen (approximately 0.95 liters) wine
- Half a Maass (approximately 1.9 liters) water
- A piece of bacon
- A piece of ham
- 2 cloves of garlic
- Basil
- Thyme
- Tarragon
- Chervil
- 1 tablespoon of jus
- 1 tablespoon flour
- Butter
- Juice of half a lemon

## Directions

1. Lard a piece of beef from the inner thigh or a fatty tail piece with bacon and ham
2. Turn it in salt and pepper
3. Place the meat in a casserole with 2 bay leaves, 3 onions, yellow carrots, and celery
4. Set it over a strong coal fire
5. Pour 1 Schoppen wine and half a Maass water over it
6. Let it stand for 2 to 3 hours
7. Pound very finely a piece of bacon, a piece of ham, 2 cloves of garlic, basil, thyme, tarragon, and chervil
8. When the meat is tender, place it in another casserole
9. Remove the fat from the meat broth
10. Pour the broth back over the meat
11. Add the pounded mixture and 1 tablespoon of jus
12. Let everything come to a boil together
13. Arrange the meat and pour the sauce over it
14. For a French and German style variation: let 1 tablespoon of flour turn yellow in a piece of butter, pour 1 Schoppen wine and 1 tablespoon of good jus over it, let it cook strongly with the meat, arrange the meat, pass the sauce through a fine sieve over it, and squeeze the juice of half a lemon over it
