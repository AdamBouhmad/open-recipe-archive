## Ingredients

- Short crust pastry, half the dose
- Milk, 4 deciliters
- Sugar, 60 grams
- Flour, 30 grams
- Three egg yolks
- Vanilla for flavoring
- 100 grams of sponge cake
- Cedar rosoli liqueur
- Egg white
- Powdered sugar, 130 grams

## Directions

1. Make a short crust pastry.
2. Make a cream with milk, sugar, flour, three egg yolks, and vanilla.
3. Take 100 grams of sponge cake and cut it into slices half a centimeter thick.
4. Use a medium-sized copper pan, butter it and cover the bottom with a sheet of the said pastry; then superimpose on this all around an edge of the same pastry one finger wide and two high, and to attach it well, wet the edge with a wet finger.
5. After making this frame for the pan, cover the bottom pastry with half of the sponge cake slices lightly dipped in cedar rosoli.
6. Over these spread the cream and cover this with the remaining sponge cake slices equally sprinkled with rosoli.
7. Now beat with a whisk two of the three remaining egg whites from the cream and when they are very firm add to them little by little 130 grams of powdered sugar and mix slowly to thus have the meringue with which you will cover the surface of the sweet, leaving uncovered the edge of the short pastry to gild it with egg yolk.
8. Bake it in the oven or country oven and when the meringue has hardened cover it with a sheet so it does not take color.
9. Turn the cake out cold and dust it lightly with powdered sugar.
