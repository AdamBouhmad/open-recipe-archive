## Ingredients

- a quarter of lamb from the hind part
- two cloves of garlic cut into strips
- sprigs of rosemary
- a piece of lard or a slice of cured meat
- oil
- salt
- pepper
- a small piece of butter
- tomato juice or tomato preserve dissolved in broth or water
- peas

## Directions

1. Take a quarter of lamb from the hind part, lard it with two cloves of garlic cut into strips and with some sprigs of rosemary.
2. Take a piece of lard or a slice of cured meat and mince them finely with a knife.
3. Put the lamb on the fire in a pan with this battuto and a little oil; season with salt and pepper and let it brown.
4. When it has taken color, add a small piece of butter, tomato juice or tomato preserve dissolved in broth or water, and cook it until perfectly done.
5. Remove the lamb for a moment, pour the peas into the sauce, and when they have boiled a little, put it back on them, cook them together, and serve them as a side dish.
6. The same method can be used to cook a piece of milk veal in the loin or rump.
7. In Tuscany these dishes are prepared in the same manner, but only oil is used.
