## Ingredients

- Clams in shell, 1.350 kilograms
- Rice, 500 grams
- Oil
- Garlic
- Onion
- Parsley
- Carrot
- Celery
- Dried mushrooms
- Pepper
- Salt
- Butter
- Parmesan cheese
- Tomato juice or tomato preserve

## Directions

1. To remove sand from the clams, wash them first, then place them in fresh salted water or sea water in a bowl with an inverted plate underneath, and after at least two hours, remove them dry and put them on the fire with water in proportion to the rice to be cooked.
2. When they have opened, remove the shells and keep the water, but be careful that at the bottom of the same a deposit of sand will have formed which must be thrown away.
3. Make a soffritto with oil, garlic, little onion, parsley, carrot and celery, all finely chopped with a crescent knife, and when it has browned well, throw in the clams removed from the shell, some pieces of soaked dried mushrooms, a pinch of pepper and some of that reserved water.
4. After a few minutes throw the rice into this sauce and cook it firm with the rest of the aforementioned water.
5. Taste it to see if it is well-flavored with only the natural salt of the clams and the seasonings given to it; if not, add salt with tomato juice or preserve, and also with a piece of butter and a pinch of parmesan.
6. The clams can be substituted with arselle or mussels as in Venice, in whose trattorias if rice with mussels (specialty of the country) were cooked in this manner, it would be much more pleasing.
