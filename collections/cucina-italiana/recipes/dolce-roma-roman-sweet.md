## Ingredients

- Apples of fine quality, not too ripe and medium-sized, 600 grams (5 or 6 apples)
- White alcoholic wine, 2 deciliters
- Sugar, 130 grams
- Milk, 4 deciliters
- Egg yolks, 3
- Sugar, 70 grams
- Flour, 20 grams
- Vanilla sugar flavoring
- Egg whites, 3
- Powdered sugar, 20 grams

## Directions

1. Choose fine apples that are not too ripe and of medium size, weighing about 600 grams.
2. Remove the cores with a tin tube and peel them.
3. Place them to cook with 2 deciliters of white alcoholic wine and 130 grams of sugar, taking care that they do not break while boiling, turning them, and that they do not overcook.
4. Remove them dried and place them with the hole vertical in a decent vessel that can be brought to the table and that withstands fire.
5. Prepare a cream with 4 deciliters of milk, 3 egg yolks, 70 grams of sugar, 20 grams of flour and vanilla sugar flavoring, then pour it over the apples.
6. Whip the three remaining egg whites, and when they are well stiff add 20 grams of powdered sugar and cover the cream with them.
7. Place the sweet in a country oven or only on the hearth with just its cover, with fire above and little below to brown the surface.
8. Before sending it to the table, brush it with the reduced syrup remaining from the cooking of the apples.
9. This will suffice for seven or eight people.
