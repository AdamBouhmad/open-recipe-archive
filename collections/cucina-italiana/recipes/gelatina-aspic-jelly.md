## Ingredients

- 500 grams boneless muscle meat
- One calf's foot or 150 grams of calf's foot
- Feet of two or three chickens
- Two chicken heads with their necks
- 70 grams lean veal meat
- One egg
- A finger's width (of glass) of water
- Two pinches of sugar
- A drop of water
- Salt to taste
- A small glass of Marsala (optional)

## Directions

1. Singe the chicken feet over fire and cut them into pieces
2. Put everything into a pot with two liters of cold water
3. Salt sufficiently and boil, skimming, slowly for seven or eight continuous hours so that the liquid reduces by half
4. Pour the broth into a basin and when it has set, remove the fat from the surface
5. If it does not congeal, return it to the fire to reduce further, or add two sheets of fish glue
6. To clarify and give it amber color, finely mince 70 grams of lean veal meat with a knife and then pound it in a mortar
7. Place it in a small pot with one egg and a finger's width of water, mix everything well and pour in the cold aspic jelly
8. Do not stop beating it with a whisk over the fire until it rises to a boil, then boil slowly for about twenty minutes, during which taste to check the salt and give it color
9. For the color, place two pinches of sugar and a drop of water in a non-tinned metal spoon, hold it over fire until the sugar becomes almost black, pouring it little by little into the boiling aspic jelly to achieve the correct color gradation
10. Take a towel, wet it in water, wring it well and pass the warm aspic jelly through it without squeezing, and pour it immediately into molds
11. In summer, if it does not set well, place the molds on ice
12. When you wish to unmold it, pass a cloth dipped in boiling water lightly around the molds
