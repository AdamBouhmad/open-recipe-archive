## Ingredients

- Hake (cod), 500 to 600 grams
- Salt
- Pepper
- Oil
- Breadcrumbs
- Rosemary (optional)
- Salted anchovies (3 large or 4 small)
- Lemon

## Directions

1. Take a hake weighing 500 to 600 grams, remove all the fins except the tail fin, leaving the head on.
2. Split it along the belly to remove the innards and the spine, flatten it, and season with a little salt and pepper.
3. Turn it to the back side, oil it, season with salt and pepper, bread it, then place it supine with two tablespoons of oil on a heat-proof dish or baking sheet.
4. Take three large salted anchovies (or four if small), clean them of scales and spines, mince them, and put them on the fire with two tablespoons of oil to break them down, being careful they do not boil.
5. Spread this sauce on the fish on the upper part, that is on the belly, and cover it completely with breadcrumbs, sprinkling some rosemary leaves on top if desired.
6. Cook it between two fires and let it form a crust, but be careful it does not dry out too much; for this reason, sprinkle more oil on it, and before removing it, squeeze a large lemon half over it.
7. It should be enough for four or five people if served at table surrounded by croutons with caviar or anchovies.
