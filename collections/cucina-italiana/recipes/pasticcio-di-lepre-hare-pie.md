## Ingredients

- Half hare without head and paws, one kilogram
- Lean veal milk-fed, 230 grams
- Butter, 90 grams
- Salted tongue, 80 grams
- Ham fat, 80 grams
- Fat and lean ham, cut thick half a finger, 50 grams
- Ham, cut fine, 30 grams
- Black truffles, 60 grams
- Flour for the béchamel, 30 grams
- Marsala, 3 deciliters
- Eggs, 2 numbers
- Milk, half a glass
- Broth, as needed

## Directions

1. After washing and drying the hare, remove 80 grams of lean meat from the fillet and set aside, then separate all meat from bones, break the bones and set aside, cut the meat into pieces and with the 80 grams of lean meat left whole, put it in infusion with about two-thirds of the marsala and with the following aromatics cut roughly: a quarter of a large onion, half a carrot, one celery rib about a palm long, several parsley stems and two bay leaves, season with salt and pepper, turn everything well and let it rest for several hours
2. Meanwhile clean the veal of membranes, chop it with a knife and pound it in a mortar as fine as possible
3. Drain the hare meat from the marsala and with all the bones, the indicated aromatics, the ham fat cut into small pieces and 30 grams of the butter, put it in a covered pot and at high heat let it brown well, stirring often with a spoon and basting, when dry, with marsala, also using the remaining infusion, and with broth until completely cooked
4. Then separate the meat from the bones again and set aside the 80 grams of lean for forming with this, with 50 grams of ham and with the tongue, so many fillets thicker than half a finger
5. First pound all the hare meat in a mortar, basting it from time to time to make it more pasty but not too liquid, with the rest of the marsala and with broth and pass it; then pound the bones and ensure that as much as possible of these passes through, using a wire sieve for this purpose
6. Make a béchamel with 30 grams of the butter, the indicated flour and milk and, when cooked, pour into the same pot all the passed meat, both that of the hare and the raw veal, add the two eggs, mix well and taste the mixture to see if it is seasoned correctly, adding salt and the rest of the butter if needed
7. Now line the pie with the pasta described below and to fill it follow recipe 370, cut the truffles into chunks the size of hazelnuts and thus raw and with all the fillets described arrange them in three layers alternated with the well pressed mixture so they are spread regularly and make a fine show when the pie is cut, finally spread the 30 grams of ham in rather thin slices on top and cover it
8. You can cover it with puff pastry at half, like that of recipe 155, or with the following: flour 250 grams, butter 80 grams, wine spirit 2 teaspoons, sugar 2 teaspoons, egg yolks 2 numbers, the juice of one lemon wedge, salt 5 grams, cold water if needed
9. With this method, except for some variations as needed, you can make several other game pies, such as wild boar, fallow deer and roe deer, this should be enough for a meal even for twenty people
