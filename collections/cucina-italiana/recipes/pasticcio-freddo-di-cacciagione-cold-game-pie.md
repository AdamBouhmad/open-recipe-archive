## Ingredients

- One partridge (gray partridge or red partridge), somewhat aged
- Chicken livers, 3
- Egg yolk, 1
- Bay leaves, 2
- Marsala, two fingers of a common glass
- Black truffles, 50 grams
- Salted tongue, 50 grams
- Fat and lean prosciutto, 30 grams
- Butter, 30 grams
- Bread marrow, a fistful
- Small battuto of onion, carrot, and celery
- Broth, a little
- Pastry dough (Recipe No. 372)

## Directions

1. Clean the partridge, wash it, and put it on the fire with the battuto, butter, prosciutto cut into slices, whole bay leaves, and season with salt and pepper.
2. When the onion takes on color, wet it with marsala poured little by little, and if it is not enough to bring the partridge to half-cooked, use broth.
3. Remove the partridge from the fire, take the breast and make eight fillets that you will set aside.
4. Cut the rest into small pieces to cook completely, together with the chicken livers, including that of the partridge.
5. When cooked, remove it dry and put it in a mortar, discarding the bay leaves.
6. Into the remaining sauce, throw the bread marrow and with a little broth, stirring, make a paste that you will pour also into the mortar, as well as the scrapings of the truffles, and pound everything well and fine to pass through a sieve.
7. In this paste, blend the egg yolk and work it well with a spoon to make it creamy.
8. Make the pastry to cover it using recipe No. 372.
9. Take one of those special molds for these pies, which are made in the shape of a boat or round, fluted, of white iron, with a hinge that opens.
10. Grease it with butter and, having rolled out the pastry thin slightly more than a coin, line it with the same and make the bottom which you will place on a copper baking sheet also greased with butter.
11. First throw into the bottom part of the mixture and arrange on top part of the fillets (breast of the partridge and tongue) and some pieces of truffles as big as hazelnuts and raw; then other mixture interspersed with truffles and fillets and so on if the pie were voluminous.
12. Press it well so that it comes together unified and compact and make it a cover of the same pastry with some ornament, leaving in the middle a hole so that the vapor escapes.
13. Gild it on the outside and bake it in the oven or in a country oven, and when you take it out cover the hole with a knot of the same pastry, made to measure and baked separately.
