## Ingredients

- Bread from the day before, grated, not pounded, 130 grams
- Eggs, 4
- Parmigiano cheese, 50 grams
- Nutmeg, to taste
- Salt, a pinch

## Directions

1. Take a wide saucepan and form in it a mixture that is not too stiff with the aforementioned ingredients, adding breadcrumbs if needed.
2. Dissolve it with hot broth, but not boiling, and set aside some to add afterwards.
3. Cook it with coals all around, little or no fire underneath and with a spoon, as it comes to a boil, seek to gather it in the middle moving it away from the sides of the vessel without breaking it apart.
4. When you see it has set, pour it into the soup tureen and serve it.
5. If the panata turned out well you will see it all in clusters with its clear broth around it.
6. If you like it mixed with herbs or with peas cook these things separately and mix them into the mixture before dissolving it with broth.
