## Ingredients

- Thin slices of lard or cured meat (the latter is preferable)
- One large onion
- One carrot
- One celery rib
- Butter
- Lean beef meat in pieces or thin slices
- Salt
- Two cloves
- Small ladle of cold water (repeated three times)
- 1.5 liters hot water or broth made from spongy bones (for approximately 500 grams of meat)
- Chicken necks and heads (optional)

## Directions

1. Cover the bottom of a pot with thin slices of lard or cured meat
2. Above these, slice a large onion, a carrot, and a celery rib
3. Add here and there small pieces of butter and above these ingredients spread lean beef meat in pieces or thin slices
4. Season with only salt and two cloves
5. Place the pot over fire without ever touching it
6. When you smell the odor of burnt onion, turn the meat, and when you see it well browned, indeed almost black, pour cold water from a small ladle
7. Repeat the operation of pouring water three times as it evaporates
8. Finally, if the quantity of meat is about 500 grams, pour into the pot 1.5 liters of hot water or preferably a broth made from spongy bones
9. Let it boil gently for five or six consecutive hours to reduce the gravy and extract all the substance from the meat
10. Pass it through a strainer and when its fat has congealed, forming a large layer on top, remove it all to make the gravy less heavy on the stomach
