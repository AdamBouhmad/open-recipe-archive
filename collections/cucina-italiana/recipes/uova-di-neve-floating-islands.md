## Ingredients

- Milk, one liter
- Sugar, 150 grams
- Eggs, 6
- Powdered sugar, as needed
- Vanilla flavoring

## Directions

1. Put the milk on the fire in a wide pan and while it heats, beat with a whisk the egg whites that you will have already separated from the yolks; but throw into them a pinch of salt if it is true that it serves to strengthen the consistency.
2. When the whites are well beaten, take the perforated shaker where powdered sugar is ordinarily kept and, continuing always to beat them, throw in enough to make them somewhat sweet. Twenty or thirty grams of powdered sugar may suffice, but it is better to taste them.
3. This done, take up the beaten whites with a tablespoon, and giving them the approximate shape of an egg, throw them into the milk when it boils.
4. Turn these so-called eggs to cook them on all sides and when you see them set, take them with a slotted spoon and put them to drain in a colander.
5. Strain that milk and, when it is cold, make with the same, with the yolks and with sugar a cream like that of No. 685, giving it the flavor of vanilla.
6. Arrange on a platter the floating islands in fine display, one on top of the other, pour the cream over them and serve them cold.
