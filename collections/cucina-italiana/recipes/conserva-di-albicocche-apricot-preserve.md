## Ingredients

- Well-ripened apricots of good quality
- White fine sugar in powder, 800 grams per kilogram of apricots

## Directions

1. Take well-ripened apricots of good quality, as it is an error to believe that with inferior fruit one can obtain the same effect.
2. Remove the pit and put them on the fire without water, and while they boil, break them down with a spoon to reduce them to a pulp.
3. When they have boiled for about half an hour, pass them through a strainer to clean them of skins and filaments.
4. Then return them to the fire with white fine sugar in powder in the proportion of eight tenths, that is 800 grams of sugar for every kilogram of apricots passed.
5. Stir them often with a spoon until they reach the consistency of preserve, which is known by pouring a spoonful from time to time onto a plate, on which it should run slowly.
6. Pour it hot into jars and when it is cold cover it with oiled butcher's paper adhering to the preserve, and seal the mouth of the jar with thick paper tied with twine around it.
