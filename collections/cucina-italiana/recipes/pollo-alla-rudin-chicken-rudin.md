## Ingredients

- One young chicken
- One egg
- Water
- Flour
- Pepper
- Salt
- 100 grams of butter
- Lemon

## Directions

1. Take a young chicken, remove the neck, the wing tips, and cut the legs two fingers from the knee; then cut it into six pieces: two with the wings to each of which you will leave attached half of the breast, two with the thighs including the hip, and two with the back with the front part removed.
2. Remove the hip bones and the breast wishbone; crush the two back pieces.
3. Beat an egg with water equal to what fits in half an eggshell, place the chicken (after flouring it) in infusion in this mixture, and season it with pepper and salt in good measure, leaving it until the moment of cooking.
4. Then take the pieces one by one, coat them in breadcrumbs, and with a sauté pan or copper skillet over the fire with 100 grams of butter, cook them in this manner.
5. When the butter begins to sizzle, place the chicken pieces skin side down for a moment, then turn them over, cover the pan with a lid and with much heat above and little below, leave them for about ten minutes.
6. Serve it with lemon wedges.
