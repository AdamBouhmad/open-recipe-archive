## Ingredients

- 160 grams sultana raisins
- 130 grams sugar
- 130 grams shelled walnuts
- 110 grams stale focaccia
- 60 grams shelled sweet almonds
- 60 grams pine nuts
- 35 grams candied citron
- 35 grams candied orange
- 5 grams mixed spices of cinnamon, cloves and mace
- 2 grams salt
- 1 deciliter Cyprus wine
- 1 deciliter rum
- 160 grams flour
- 80 grams butter

## Directions

1. After cleaning the sultana raisins, soak them in the Cyprus wine and rum mixed together; let them sit like this for several hours and remove them when they begin to swell.
2. Cut the pine nuts into three parts crosswise, cut the candied fruits into very small dice, chop the walnuts and almonds with a crescent blade to about the size of rice, and the focaccia, which can be a dough similar to brioches or Milanese panettone, grate or crumble it.
3. Leave the raisins whole and then mix everything together, including the rum and Cyprus wine.
4. This is the filling; now it must be enclosed in puff pastry for which you can use the recipe from N. 155 in the proportion of 160 grams flour and 80 grams butter.
5. Roll it out tight, long and of a thickness a little more than a coin.
6. Spread the filling on it and make it into a roll like a sausage by pulling the pastry on the edges to join it.
7. Give it a circumference of about 10 centimeters, flatten it somewhat or leave it round, place it in a buttered copper pan coiled around itself like a serpent; but not too tight.
8. Finally, with a brush, coat it with a liquid mixture of melted butter and one egg yolk.
9. Instead of one you can make two, if you like, with this same dose, half of which I believe would be enough for seven or eight people.
