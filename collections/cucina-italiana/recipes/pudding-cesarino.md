## Ingredients

- Crumb of fine bread, 200 grams
- Sugar, 250 grams
- Sugar for the mold, approximately 100 grams
- Malaga raisins, 125 grams
- Sultana raisins, 125 grams
- Milk, half a liter
- Marsala and rum combined, three tablespoonfuls total
- Eggs, 5

## Directions

1. Cut the bread crumb into thin slices and throw it into the milk
2. While waiting for this to soak, clean the raisins and remove the seeds from the malaga variety and prepare the form for cooking, which should be the one for puddings, made of worked copper
3. Melt the aforementioned approximately 100 grams of sugar in a saucepan over heat and when it has taken on a hazelnut color, pour it into the mold to coat it completely; when it has cooled, grease the sugar coating with fresh butter
4. To the soaked crumb add the aforementioned 250 grams of sugar, the egg yolks and liqueurs, stirring everything well
5. Finally add the raisins and the beaten egg whites
6. Cook it in a water bath for three full hours, with heat underneath only in the last hour
7. Serve it hot and in flames and therefore water it abundantly with rum and set it alight with a spoonful of lit spirits
8. This will be enough for ten or twelve people
