## Ingredients

- Artichokes
- Flour
- Egg white
- Egg yolk
- Salt
- Lard or oil for frying
- Lemon wedges

## Directions

1. Cut 8 to 10 pieces per artichoke, even if not very large. As you cut them, throw them into fresh water and when they have refreshed well, remove them and dry them roughly or squeeze them only, throwing them immediately into flour so it adheres well.
2. Whisk the egg white to half stiff peaks, as one is enough for two artichokes, then into the white mix the yolk and salt it. Put the artichokes in a small sieve to shake off excess flour and then pass them through the egg, mix and let them sit a little so the egg incorporates. Throw the pieces one by one into a pan with boiling lard and when they have taken on a nice golden color remove them and serve at table with lemon wedges because, as everyone knows, acid on fried foods that are not sweet always adds grace and excitement to good drinking. If you desire that the artichokes remain white, it is better to fry them in oil and squeeze half a lemon in the water when you soak them.
