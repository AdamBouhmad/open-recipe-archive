## Ingredients

- Flour, 80 grams
- Blonde sugar, 30 grams
- Virgin lard, barely warm, 20 grams
- Cold water, seven tablespoons

## Directions

1. Place in a pot the flour, sugar, lard, and cold water.
2. Dissolve first, with the water, the flour and sugar, then add the lard.
3. Place the waffle iron over a hot burner and when it is well heated open it and pour over it each time half a tablespoon of the said batter; press the two parts of the iron together, pass it over the fire on one side and the other, remove the excess with a knife and open it when you know the waffle has taken a hazelnut color.
4. Then detach it somewhat from one side with the knife and immediately while still hot on the iron itself or on a cloth spread on the hearth roll it up with a reed bocciuolo or simply with your hands. This last operation must be done very quickly because if the waffle cools you will no longer be able to roll it up on itself.
5. If the waffles stick to the iron, oil it from time to time with lard, and if they do not all turn out well, add a bit of flour.
6. You already know that cialdoni can be served alone; but it is better to accompany them with cream or whipped cream and also with brûléed milk or Portuguese milk.
