## Ingredients

- Milk, half a liter
- Yellow flour (cornmeal), 100 grams
- Gruyère cheese
- Eggs, 2
- Breadcrumbs
- Lard or oil for frying

## Directions

1. Make a polenta from the milk and cornmeal and before removing it from the fire, salt it
2. While boiling pour it onto a work surface and with a table knife dipped in water spread it to a thickness of slightly less than half a finger
3. When cold, using a stamp cutter of recipe N. 182 or similar, cut as many disks as will yield 30 or 32 if you add the scraps by kneading and flattening them with your hands
4. Pair these disks by putting a slice of Gruyère between them, obtaining thus 15 to 16 frittelle
5. Beat two eggs, which are necessary to be able to gild them with these and with breadcrumbs, and fry them in lard or oil
6. Serve them hot as a side dish to a roast
