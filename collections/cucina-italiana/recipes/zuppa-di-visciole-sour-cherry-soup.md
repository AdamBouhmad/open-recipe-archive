## Ingredients

- Sour cherries, sufficient quantity
- Very little water
- Piece of cinnamon
- Sugar, as needed
- Thin slices of fine toasted bread, or sponge cake or savoiardi biscuits
- Rosolio liqueur

## Directions

1. Remove the pit from the quantity of sour cherries that you think sufficient and put them on the fire with very little water and a piece of cinnamon that you will then throw away.
2. When they begin to boil, add sugar as needed, stir gently so as not to damage them, and when they begin to form a syrup taste them to see if they have enough sugar and remove them from the fire when you see them shriveled and they have lost their rawness.
3. After you have lightly dipped the slices of bread or the savoiardi in rosolio, place them layer by layer together with the cherries in a dish or on a tray so that they form a dome.
4. You can also give this soup a more regular form in a smooth mold and keep it on ice before unmolding it, since in cherry season one begins to appreciate cooling foods.
5. One third of sugar by the weight of the cherries is sufficient.
