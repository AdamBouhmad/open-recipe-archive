## Ingredients

- a piece of beef suitable for roasting
- oil
- salt
- broth
- potatoes

## Directions

1. Cook it over a brisk and well-lit fire from the beginning so the surface is seared immediately.
2. Oil it, then strain the oil from the dripping pan, and finally pass a ladle of broth over it, which, united with the fat that drips from the roast-beef, will serve as sauce for the piece when you send it to the table.
3. Salt it at half cooking, being somewhat sparing because this quality of meat is flavorful by itself.
4. Place it over the fire half an hour before sending the soup course to the table, which is sufficient if the piece is not very large.
5. To check if it is cooked, prick it in the thickest part with a thin larding needle, but do not prick it frequently so it does not dry out. The juice that comes out should be neither the color of blood nor dark.
6. Cook potatoes separately as a side dish, browning them in oil from raw and peeled, whole if small and quartered if large.
7. The roast-beef can also be sent to the oven, but it does not turn out as well as on a spit. In this case season it with salt, oil and a piece of butter, surround it with raw peeled potatoes, and pour a glass of water into the pan.
8. If leftover roast-beef is not pleasing cold, slice it, prepare it again with butter and meat or tomato juice.
