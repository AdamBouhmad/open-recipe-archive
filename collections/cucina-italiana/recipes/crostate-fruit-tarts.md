## Ingredients

- shortcrust pastry (full dose from recipe B No. 589, or half dose from recipe A)
- eggs, 1 whole egg and 1 yolk
- lemon zest or orange flower water for flavoring
- fruit preserves or cream
- butter for greasing the pan

## Directions

1. Take the full dose of the pastry recipe B No. 589, or half of recipe A
2. Beat one whole egg and one yolk together separately; reserve some egg for glazing the surface of the tart
3. Add flavoring to the shortcrust pastry, such as lemon zest or orange flower water; recipe C is best
4. Roll out half the pastry with a smooth rolling pin to form a round disk about the thickness of a shield and place it in a buttered pan
5. Spread the fruit preserves or cream on top, keeping them separate if using both
6. If the preserves are too firm, soften them over heat with a few spoonfuls of water
7. Cut the remaining pastry with a ridged rolling pin into thin strips about one finger-width wide
8. Arrange these strips in a grid pattern over the preserves
9. Cover the ends of the strips with a ring of pastry made from the remaining dough, moistened with water to attach it well
10. Brush the surface of the shortcrust pastry with the reserved egg
11. Bake the tart in an oven or outdoor oven
12. It improves after one or two days
