## Ingredients

- Very little onion
- Prosciutto, both fat and lean
- Butter
- Chicken livers, 3
- Sage leaves, 4-5 for 3 livers
- Salt and pepper
- Flour, 1 teaspoon
- Broth
- Grated Parmesan cheese, 3-4 teaspoons
- Bread, stale crumb, slices about 1 centimeter thick

## Directions

1. Make a mixture (battutino) with very little onion and both fat and lean prosciutto
2. Put it on the fire with a piece of butter and when it is well browned, throw in the finely minced chicken livers together with sage leaves (4 or 5 for 3 livers should be enough)
3. Season with salt and pepper and, once they have released their moisture, add another piece of butter and bind them with a teaspoon of flour
4. Wet them with broth to cook them
5. Before removing from the fire, pour in 3 or 4 teaspoons of grated Parmesan and taste to see if the seasoning is right
6. Form the crostini from the crumb of stale bread, just under 1 centimeter thick, and spread them generously on one side only with the mixture when it is no longer boiling
7. After several hours, when you are about to serve them, either alone or as a side to roasted meat, beat an egg mixed with a drop of water and, taking the crostini one by one, let them touch the flour from the side of the mixture only, then immerse them in the egg and throw them into the pan on the mixture side
