## Ingredients

- Medium-sized eels
- oil
- salt
- pepper
- garlic cloves
- sage leaves
- breadcrumbs
- water

## Directions

1. Clean the eels by making a circular incision under the head, holding it firmly with a cloth to prevent slipping due to the fish's abundant mucus, and pull down the skin which will come off entirely.
2. Cut the eels into pieces about one finger length or slightly less, and season them with oil, salt, and pepper, leaving them to rest for one or two hours.
3. Use an iron pan or earthenware dish, cover the bottom with a thin layer of oil, two whole garlic cloves, and sage leaves.
4. Sauté briefly, then take the eel pieces one at a time, coat them in breadcrumbs, and arrange them in the pan side by side, pouring the remaining seasoning over them.
5. Cook them between two fires, and when they have taken on color, pour a little water into the pan.
