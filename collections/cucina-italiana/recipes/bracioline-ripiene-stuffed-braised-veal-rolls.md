## Ingredients

- thin veal braised rolls, 300 grams
- lean veal or milk-fed veal meat, 70 grams
- rather lean prosciutto, 40 grams
- veal marrow, 30 grams
- grated Parmesan cheese, 30 grams
- egg, 1
- onion
- white celery
- carrot
- dried meat (cured meat), 20 grams
- butter, 20 grams
- salt
- pepper
- tomato juice or preserve
- water
- white wine

## Directions

1. From the braised rolls you will obtain 6 or 7 if you keep them as wide as a hand: beat them very well with a meat beater or with the handle of a knife dipped often in water to widen them.
2. Then mince finely the prosciutto with 70 grams of the second meat and to this mixture unite the Parmesan and the marrow first made pasty with the blade of a knife; lastly add the egg to bind the mixture and a pinch of pepper, salt not being necessary because of the prosciutto and Parmesan.
3. Spread out the braised rolls and on the middle of them distribute the said mixture; then roll them up and tie them crosswise with twine.
4. Now that they have already taken the required form, prepare a light mixture with a little onion, a small piece of white celery, a small piece of carrot and 20 grams of dried meat, and place it on the fire in a saucepan with 20 grams of butter, at the same time as you place the braised rolls.
5. Season them with salt and pepper, and when they have taken on color pour tomato juice or preserve and finish cooking them with water.
6. You can also add, if you like, a drop of white wine.
7. When you send them to the table remove the twine with which you had tied them.
