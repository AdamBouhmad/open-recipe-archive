## Ingredients

- Pork loin, 1 kilogram
- Pork caul, 100 grams
- Lean milk-fed veal, 100 grams
- Fat and lean prosciutto, 50 grams
- Mortadella, 50 grams
- Bone marrow, 30 grams
- Grated Parmesan cheese, 30 grams
- One egg yolk
- Nutmeg flavor to taste

## Directions

1. Brown the milk-fed veal in butter and mince both this and the prosciutto and mortadella with a knife and then pound them in a mortar to reduce them to a fine paste. Pour this paste onto the cutting board, add to it the marrow, the Parmesan and the egg yolk, season sparingly with salt, pepper and nutmeg, and with the blade of a knife reduce it to a uniform paste. Now remove the superficial fat from the loin, debone it and then cut it into seven or eight scaloppine, but in such a way that they all remain joined at the base so that they can be opened like the pages of a book and spread a spoonful of the said paste over each one; then join them together to form a roll that you will sprinkle with salt and pepper and tie tightly with string.
2. Cover it with the pork caul, binding it with thread so that it adheres well, and cook it at low heat in a pot with nothing else. Three hours of cooking should be sufficient and it will serve eight people. It is a good dish, both hot and cold, and not heavy; but if served hot you can send it to the table accompanied by vegetables reheated in its fat. To cut it into slices one must carve it not in the direction of the divisions, but the opposite, which will make it look fine.
