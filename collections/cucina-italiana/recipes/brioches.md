## Ingredients

- Hungarian flour, 300 grams
- Butter, 150 grams
- Beer yeast, 30 grams
- Sugar, 20 grams
- Salt, 5 grams
- Eggs, 6

## Directions

1. Dissolve the beer yeast with lukewarm water in one quarter of the said flour; form it into a round small loaf of proper consistency, make a cross cut in it and place it to rise in a warm place inside a small copper pot with a veil of flour underneath
2. To the remaining flour make a well in the middle, place in it the sugar, salt and one egg, and with your fingers combine these three things together, then add the butter in small pieces and begin to knead the flour, first using the blade of a knife and then your hands, to form a dough which you will place in a bowl to work it better
3. Unite to this the risen small loaf, when it has grown double in size, and using your hand to work it a lot, add the other eggs one at a time
4. Then place the bowl in a warm and well-closed place, and when the dough has risen, spread it out somewhat and with it fill halfway about twenty buttered metal molds with ridges, which beforehand you will have buttered with liquid butter or lard and dusted with flour mixed with powdered sugar
5. Return them to rise, then glaze them and bake them in the oven or country oven
