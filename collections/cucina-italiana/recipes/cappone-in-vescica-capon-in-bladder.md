## Ingredients

- one large, thick bladder of beef or pork without defects
- one capon
- salt, 150 grams (plus a good handful for inside the capon)
- prosciutto, 150 grams (sliced very thin, more lean than fat)
- one wooden tube approximately one palm's length to serve as an air vent, shaped with a whistle-like mouth at top and a notch at bottom for tying

## Directions

1. Wash the bladder well in tepid water and soak it for one or two days.
2. Clean out the capon and remove the neck and legs.
3. Place a good handful of salt inside the capon, tuck in the leg joints, and fold the wings against the body so the tips do not pierce the bladder.
4. Sew closed the openings of the eviscerated cavity and neck.
5. Wrap the capon all over with 150 grams of thinly sliced prosciutto (more lean than fat), binding the slices tightly to the capon with twine.
6. Make a small incision in the bladder just large enough to fit the capon, then place the capon inside.
7. Sew the bladder closed.
8. Insert the wooden tube (with its whistle-mouth end on top and notched end at bottom) into the neck of the bladder and tie it in place.
9. Place the capon in a pot of tepid water and bring to a boil.
10. Boil gently for three continuous hours with the tube protruding, maintaining only small, sparse bubbles rising to the surface—this is the critical point.
11. If fat or other liquid comes from the tube, do not be concerned; collect it in a small pan.
12. Once cooked, let the capon cool in its water.
13. Serve the next day, discarding the prosciutto wrapper which will have lost its flavor.
14. Inside the capon you will find gelatin, and you may add additional gelatin as a garnish if desired.
15. Serves ten people.
