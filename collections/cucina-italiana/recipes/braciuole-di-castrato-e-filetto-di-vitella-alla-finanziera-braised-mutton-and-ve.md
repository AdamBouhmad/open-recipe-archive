## Ingredients

- slice of prosciutto
- butter
- carrot
- celery
- parsley stems
- whole braciuole of mutton from the loin
- salt
- pepper
- chicken gizzards
- chicken livers
- sweetbreads
- fresh or dried mushrooms
- broth
- flour
- good white wine

## Directions

1. Place a slice of prosciutto at the bottom of a pot with some butter, a small bundle made of carrot, celery and parsley stems, and on top of these place whole braciuole of mutton from the loin, which you will season with salt and pepper.
2. Brown them on both sides, add another piece of butter if needed, and add to the braciuole chicken gizzards and then livers, sweetbreads and fresh or dried mushrooms (already softened), all cut into pieces.
3. When these ingredients have also taken color, moisten with broth and cook over low heat.
4. Bind the sauce with a little flour, and lastly pour in half a glass, or even less, of good white wine, first reduced by half in a separate vessel over the fire, and let it boil a little longer so that it incorporates.
5. When ready to serve, remove the prosciutto and the bundle, strain the sauce through a colander and degrease it.
6. The same method can be used for a piece of veal fillet instead of mutton, adding peas to the ingredients mentioned.
