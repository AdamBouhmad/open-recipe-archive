## Ingredients

- a piece of pork loin weighing approximately 500 grams
- 2.5 deciliters of milk
- salt
- fresh milk

## Directions

1. Take a piece of pork loin weighing approximately 500 grams, salt it and place it in a pot with 2.5 deciliters of milk.
2. Cover it and let it boil slowly until the milk is consumed.
3. Then increase the heat to brown it, and once this is done, drain away the fat and remove the piece of meat.
4. Add a little fresh milk to the remaining coagulated milk residue.
5. Mix, bring to a boil and use it to spread on slices of lightly toasted bread to serve as an accompaniment to the pork when serving hot at table.
6. A total of three deciliters of milk will suffice. Cooked this way the pork will have a delicate flavor and will not be cloying.
