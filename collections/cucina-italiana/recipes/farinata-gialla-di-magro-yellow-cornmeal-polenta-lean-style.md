## Ingredients

- Four deciliters of white beans
- Water in proportion
- Half a head of white or savoy cabbage, chopped
- Salt, pepper, and thyme leaves
- Oil in good measure
- Two cloves of garlic, whole and peeled
- Tomato juice or conserve dissolved in water
- Cornmeal flour

## Directions

1. Put the beans on the fire with proportioned water.
2. After they are cooked, pass them through a sieve and mix the paste in the broth of the same beans and in the same put to boil, for about two hours, the chopped cabbage which you will season with salt, pepper and thyme leaves.
3. Put a pan on the fire with oil in good measure and two whole peeled cloves of garlic; when these are well browned throw them away and add to the oil tomato juice or conserve dissolved in water and also here another little salt and pepper.
4. When it has boiled somewhat, pour also this seasoning into the pot where the broth and cabbage are.
5. Finally, when this is cooked, pour with one hand, little by little, the cornmeal flour; with the other hand mix well, so that lumps do not form, and when it has reached a certain consistency, that is somewhat liquid, let it boil a little more and serve it.
