## Ingredients

- 1 kg shoulder or leg of mutton, boned
- 40 grams butter
- Lardoon strips for larding
- Salt and pepper
- Strips of lardoon or prosciutto rind
- 1 bunch tied together of parsley, celery and carrot
- 1 onion of medium size
- Broken bones from the shoulder or leg
- Meat scraps, if available
- 1 glass of broth or half a glass
- 2 or 3 tablespoons of brandy
- Cold water to nearly cover the meat

## Directions

1. Bone the shoulder and lard it with lardoon strips
2. Season inside and out with salt and pepper
3. Roll it up and tie it to achieve a nice shape
4. Place it in a braising pan with 40 grams of butter to brown it
5. Add the lardoon or prosciutto rinds, the tied bunch of herbs, the whole onion, the broken bones, meat scraps if you have them, the glass of broth or half a glass, and 2 or 3 tablespoons of brandy
6. Add enough cold water so the liquid reaches just below the surface of the mutton
7. Cover the pan well and simmer slowly until the meat is cooked, which will take four or more hours if the animal is tough
8. Strain the sauce, degrease it and discard the excess
9. Serve the mutton, optionally garnished with carrots, turnips, or beans prepared according to the instructions given in the text
