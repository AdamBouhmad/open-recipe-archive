## Ingredients

- Oranges
- Water
- Sugar (amount determined by weight of fruit)
- Lemon (optional)
- Rum (optional)

## Directions

1. Pierce the entire peel of the oranges with the tines of a fork.
2. Soak them in water for three days, changing the water morning and evening.
3. On the fourth day, cut them in half and then each half into thick strips about half a centimeter wide, removing the seeds.
4. Weigh them and then regulate the sugar and water according to the indicated proportions.
5. Put them on the fire first with only water, and after ten minutes of boiling add the lemon cut the same way as the oranges.
6. Immediately after, pour in the sugar and stir continuously until the liquid returns to a strong boil, otherwise the sugar will precipitate to the bottom and could stick to the pot.
7. To determine the point of cooking, occasionally pour a few drops onto a plate, blow on it, and if it is slow to flow remove it immediately.
8. Wait until it is lukewarm before adding the rum, and pour it into jars to preserve it like all other fruit preserves.
