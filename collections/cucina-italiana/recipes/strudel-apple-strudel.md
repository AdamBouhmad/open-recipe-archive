## Ingredients

- 500 grams reinette apples or tender apples of good quality
- 250 grams flour
- 100 grams butter
- 85 grams currants
- 85 grams powdered sugar
- zest of one lemon
- two or three pinches of ground cinnamon
- hot milk
- one egg
- salt

## Directions

1. Mix the flour with hot milk, a nut-sized piece of butter, one egg and a pinch of salt to make a rather firm dough and let it rest a little before using it.
2. Roll out with this dough a thin sheet like that of taglierini and, leaving the edges uncovered, spread the apples on top, which you will have first peeled, cleaned of cores and cut into thin slices.
3. On the layer of apples scatter the currants, the lemon zest, the cinnamon, the sugar and finally the 100 grams of melted butter, leaving a little aside for later use.
4. Once done, wrap the sheet over itself to form a filled roll and fit it into a buttered copper pan, accommodating the rounded shape as necessary; with the remaining butter grease the entire outer part of the pastry and send it to the oven.
5. Note that currants, or sultana raisins, are different from raisins. The latter are small and black; the former are twice as large, light chestnut in color and also without seeds. Scrape the lemon with a piece of glass.
