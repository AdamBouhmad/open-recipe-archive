## Ingredients

- Egg yolks, 5
- Bread crumbs (midolla di pane), for 5 slices about a finger thick and as wide as a palm
- Butter
- Milk, 3 scant deciliters
- Flour, 40 grams
- Parmesan cheese, 3 heaping tablespoons
- Cinnamon or nutmeg
- Salt

## Directions

1. Form five square slices of bread crumbs, about a finger thick and roughly the width of a palm, and make a cavity in the center of each, but not all the way through.
2. Fry them in butter and place them on a platter that can be put in the fire.
3. Place one raw, whole egg yolk in the cavity of each.
4. Make a béchamel sauce with 3 scant deciliters of milk, 40 grams of flour, and 40 grams of butter.
5. Remove from the fire and add three heaping tablespoons of Parmesan, the flavor of cinnamon or nutmeg, and salt.
6. Let it cool, then pour it over the platter to cover the egg yolks and the bread pieces.
7. Brown it somewhat under the cover of a country oven so the eggs do not harden too much.
8. Serve it hot.
