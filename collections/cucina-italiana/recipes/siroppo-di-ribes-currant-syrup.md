## Ingredients

- Currants, 3 kilograms (liquid after fermentation)
- Powdered white sugar, 4 kilograms
- Citric acid, 30 grams

## Directions

1. Remove the currants from their bunches as you would do when making grape must and leave them in a cool place in an earthenware or wooden vessel
2. When it has begun to ferment (which can happen even after three or four days), push down the cap and stir it with a ladle twice a day, continuing this operation until it stops rising
3. Pass it through cloth a little at a time, squeezing it well with your hands if you do not have a press, and pass the pressed juice through a filter two or three times and more if necessary to obtain a very clear liquid
4. Put it on the fire and when it begins to boil, pour in the sugar and citric acid in the following proportions: 3 kilograms of liquid, 4 kilograms of finest powdered white sugar, and 30 grams of citric acid
5. Stir continuously with a ladle so the sugar does not stick, let it boil hard for two or three minutes, taste it to add more citric acid if necessary, and when cool bottle it and store it in the cellar
