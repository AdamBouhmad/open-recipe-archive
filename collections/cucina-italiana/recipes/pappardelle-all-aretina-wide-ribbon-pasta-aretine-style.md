## Ingredients

- one domestic duck
- butter
- salt
- pepper
- ham
- onion
- celery
- carrot
- broth and water
- veal or beef spleen
- tomato
- nutmeg
- egg pasta dough
- duck liver
- parmesan cheese

## Directions

1. Put the duck in a casserole with a piece of butter, season it with salt and pepper and, when it has taken color, add a finely chopped battuto of ham, onion, celery and carrot.
2. Let it wilt under the duck, turning it often; then remove a good part of the fat as an indigestible thing, and finish cooking it with broth and water poured little by little, in such a quantity that you have sauce left to season the pappardelle.
3. Get a piece of veal or beef spleen, open it and scrape out the inner part with a knife to boil it under the duck when this is cooked and it will serve as an ingredient for the sauce to which it is good to add also tomato and nutmeg.
4. Make a sheet of egg pasta, thick like that of tagliatelle and with the fluted wheel cut strips wider than a finger.
5. Cook them briefly and season them with the said sauce, with the duck liver in pieces, parmesan and a little butter if needed.
6. These pappardelle serve as a first course and the duck as a second course.
