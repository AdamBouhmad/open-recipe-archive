## Ingredients

- Canned tuna in oil (150 grams)
- Boiling water
- Small onion (half)
- Garlic (one quarter clove)
- White celery stalks (two, one palm long)
- Carrot
- Parsley (abundant handful)
- Oil (three tablespoons)
- Butter (15 grams, plus 15 grams more)
- Water (two fingers of glass)
- Lemon (half)

## Directions

1. Put the canned tuna in boiling water and let it boil gently for half an hour, changing the water every ten minutes, for a total of three changes.
2. Meanwhile, make a finely chopped mixture with half a small onion, a quarter clove of garlic, two white celery stalks each one palm long, a good piece of carrot and an abundant handful of parsley.
3. Put it on heat with three tablespoons of oil and 15 grams of butter.
4. When it has taken color, stop it with two fingers of glass of water and let it boil briefly.
5. Once the tuna is cool, cut it into the thinnest slices you can.
6. In a pan, spread it in layers, alternating with the sauce and 15 grams of butter scattered in pieces.
7. Bring to a boil over heat to liquify the butter.
8. Squeeze half a lemon over it and serve hot.
9. This will be enough for four people as a first course at a meatless lunch or as an intermediate course at a family dinner.
