## Ingredients

- Hungarian flour, 150 grams
- Butter, 40 grams
- Beer yeast, the size of a large walnut
- Eggs, one whole and one yolk
- Sugar, one spoonful
- Salt, a good pinch
- Milk

## Directions

1. Take a handful of the said flour, place it on a work surface and, making a hole in the middle, mix in the beer yeast with lukewarm milk and form a loaf of proper firmness, on which you will make a cross-shaped cut to better know if it has risen.
2. Place this loaf in a small pan or pot in whose bottom there is a very thin layer of milk, cover it and leave it near the fire so that the loaf rises at a very moderate heat: you will see that twenty minutes will be enough.
3. Once risen, put it in the middle of the remaining flour and knead it with the eggs, the melted butter, the sugar, and the salt.
4. If this dough turns out too soft, add enough flour to reduce it so that it can be stretched with a rolling pin to the thickness of half a finger.
5. Thus you will have a sheet from which, with a tin circle, you will cut out many discs the size indicated.
6. Assuming you make 24, take an egg or another tool of similar shape and with the point of it press in the middle of each of the discs to imprint it with a hole.
