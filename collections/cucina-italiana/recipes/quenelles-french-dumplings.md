## Ingredients

- veal, 120 grams
- veal kidney fat, 80 grams
- flour, 50 grams
- butter, 30 grams
- eggs, one whole and one yolk
- milk, 2 deciliters
- salt
- tomato sauce (recipe N. 125)
- fresh or dried mushrooms
- green olives in brine
- butter, 30 grams
- flour, one tablespoon

## Directions

1. Clean the veal well of membranes and the fat of the skin that covers it, and after weighing them, mince them as much as possible with a knife and crescent-shaped knife, then pound them in a mortar until reduced to a very fine paste.
2. Make a béchamel with the flour, butter, and milk mentioned above, and when it has cooled, unite it with the pounded mixture, the eggs, and seasoning of only salt, and mix everything together well.
3. Spread a veil of flour on the work surface, pour the mixture over it, and lightly floured, roll it into a stick shape to obtain 18 or 20 pieces similar to sausages, about one finger long.
4. Put water on the fire in a wide pan, and when it boils throw in the quenelles; let them boil for 8 or 10 minutes and you will see them puff up.
5. With a slightly perforated spoon, remove them dry and serve them swimming in tomato sauce N. 125, to which you will add some fresh or dried mushrooms (which you will have cooked beforehand in the same sauce) and some olives in brine, from which you will remove the pit.
6. You can substitute the tomato sauce with meat gravy, or garnish with the quenelles an offal stew. They can also be made with the white meat of chickens or with fish flesh, and this quantity can be enough for five people.
7. If you use the tomato sauce, which is most suitable for this delicate-flavored dish, bind it with a mixture composed of 30 grams of butter and one tablespoon of flour, pouring it into it when it has taken on a hazelnut color on the fire.
