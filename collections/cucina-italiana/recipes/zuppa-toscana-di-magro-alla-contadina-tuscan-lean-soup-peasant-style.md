## Ingredients

- Stale brown bread of soft dough, 400 grams
- White beans, 300 grams
- Oil, 150 grams
- Water, two liters
- Cabbage or savoy cabbage, half a head of medium size
- Black cabbage, the same amount in volume or more
- A bunch of beet greens and a little thyme
- One potato
- Some strips of dried pork or prosciutto rind

## Directions

1. Put the beans on the fire with the water mentioned, adding the rind.
2. While they boil, make a mixture with a quarter of a large onion and two cloves of garlic, two pieces of celery a palm long and a good pinch of parsley.
3. Chop it finely, put it on the fire with the oil mentioned above and when it has taken color, pour into it the vegetables cut roughly, first the cabbages, then the beet greens and the potato cut into pieces.
4. Season with salt and pepper and then add tomato juice or conserve, and if during boiling they become somewhat dry, moisten them with the bean broth.
5. When the beans are cooked, throw a quarter of them, left whole, among the vegetables, adding the rinds; pass the others through a sieve and dissolve them in the broth, pouring also this into the vessel where the vegetables are.
6. Mix, let boil a little more and pour everything into the tureen where you have already placed the bread cut into thin slices and cover it to serve after about twenty minutes.
7. This quantity may be enough for six people; it is good hot and better cold.
