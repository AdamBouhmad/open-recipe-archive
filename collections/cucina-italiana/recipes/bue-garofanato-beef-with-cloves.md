## Ingredients

- A nice piece of lean beef from the thigh or rump, about 1 kilogram
- Wine for soaking
- Lard for larding
- Four cloves
- Half an onion, sliced thin
- Butter and oil in equal quantities
- Salt

## Directions

1. Take a fine piece of lean from the thigh or rump, beat it, and place it in wine to soak the evening for the following morning. Given that the piece is about one kilogram, lard it with lardons and four cloves, tie it and put it on the fire with half an onion cut into thin slices, butter and oil in equal quantities and salt it. Brown it on all sides and render the onion, pour a glass of water and, covering the mouth of the pot with a sheet of paper in two or three layers held firm by the lid, let it boil gently until cooked. Release it and serve it with its sauce all around, strained and degreased. The lardons of lard, as I have told you other times, it is good to keep them the thickness of a finger and season them with salt and pepper.
