## Ingredients

- Milk, 7 deciliters
- Rice, 100 grams
- Sugar, 50 grams
- Butter, 20 grams
- Salt, a pinch
- Lemon zest flavoring

## Directions

1. Cook the rice in six deciliters of milk and at half cooking time add the aforementioned ingredients into it
2. Stir it frequently with a spoon because it sticks easily and when it has reduced, remove it from the fire and add the deciliter of milk set aside
3. Take a smooth mold with a hole in the middle, into which the mixture comes at least two fingers high, and pour into the bottom and around 50 grams of sugar dissolved on the fire, where you will let it take on a light brown color
4. Pour the rice into this mold and return it to the fire in a water bath so it sets further and the sugar at the bottom dissolves
5. To unmold it, wait until it is cold
6. Now you must fill the hollow in the middle of the rice with a compote that can be made of any fruit; suppose we use apples or dried prunes
7. If apples, prefer rose apples which are hard and fragrant; 200 grams will suffice
8. Peel them, cut them into small wedges, removing the part of the core, and drop them as you peel them into fresh water in which lemon has been squeezed, to keep them white
9. Put these apples in a saucepan with just enough water to cover them and when they have begun to boil, pour in the sugar
