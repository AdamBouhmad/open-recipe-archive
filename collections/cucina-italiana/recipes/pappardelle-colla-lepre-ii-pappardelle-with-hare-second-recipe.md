## Ingredients

- 50 grams of ham, more fat than lean
- One quarter of an onion
- Celery
- Carrot
- Very little parsley
- 40 grams of butter
- Hare meat, same quantity as before
- Salt and pepper
- Broth
- Tomato juice or conserve
- 30 grams of butter
- One spoonful of flour
- 30 grams of butter
- Scent of nutmeg
- Parmesan
- Pappardelle or strips of pasta

## Directions

1. Make a battuto with 50 grams of ham, more fat than lean, a quarter of onion, celery, carrot and very little parsley
2. Put it on the fire with 40 grams of butter and when it has softened, throw in the pieces of meat whole and season them with salt and pepper
3. Brown the meat and then, to cook it, wet it little by little with broth and tomato juice or conserve, so that there remains abundant liquid
4. When the meat is cooked, lift it out dry and chop it not too finely with the crescent blade
5. Make a roux with 30 grams of butter and one spoonful of flour and when it has taken on a blonde color on the fire, pour into it the chopped meat and its broth, adding another 30 grams of butter and the scent of nutmeg
6. Dress the pasta with this sauce and parmesan
