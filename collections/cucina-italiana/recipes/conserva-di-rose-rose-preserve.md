## Ingredients

- White fine sugar, 600 grams
- Rose petals (net), 200 grams
- Water, 6 deciliters
- Half a lemon
- Breton, one teaspoon

## Directions

1. Place the roses in a bowl with 200 grams of the sugar and the juice of half a lemon and with your hands rub them, chop them as much as possible to reduce them to nearly a paste.
2. Dissolve the rest of the sugar in the water over the fire and add the roses to it to boil until the syrup is condensed, which is known if, taking a drop between the fingers, it begins to stick; but be careful that it does not reach the thread stage.
3. Before removing them from the fire, give them color with breton, which you can omit if you do not care about the beautiful color.
4. When the preserve is cooled, put it in small jars to preserve it like all the other similar ones.
