## Ingredients

- Hungarian flour or finest flour, 180 grams
- Butter, 60 grams
- Sugar, 40 grams
- Sweet almonds, 40 grams
- Milk, 2 deciliters
- Eggs, 2 yolks and 1 white
- A pinch of salt
- Beer yeast, about the size of a small egg

## Directions

1. Grease the mold with cold butter, dust it with common flour mixed with powdered sugar, and scatter in the bottom the said almonds, peeled and cut into short strips. If you toast them, you will do better.
2. Mix and knead the yeast with a drop of the said lukewarm milk and a good pinch of Hungarian flour to make a small dough, which you will allow to rise like the Babà in recipe N. 565.
3. Put the remaining flour and the other ingredients, except for the milk which is to be added little by little, in a bowl, and begin to work them with a spoon; then add the yeast, and when the mixture is worked so that it detaches well from the bowl, pour it into the mold over the almonds.
4. Now allow it to rise in a barely warm place, well protected from air, noting that for this second rising you will need four or five hours. Bake it in a common oven or a country oven.
5. In the meantime prepare the following mixture: boil 30 grams of sugar in two fingers' depth of water in a glass, and when you have reduced it to dense syrup, remove it from the fire and, once cooled, add a teaspoon of vanilla sugar and two tablespoons of rum or kirsch.
6. Then turn out the savarin and, while still hot, brush it all over with this syrup using a brush until you have used it all. Serve the cake hot or cold as you prefer.
7. This amount, though small, can serve five or six people. If you see that the dough becomes too liquid with the entire amount of milk, leave some of it aside. It can also be made without the almonds.
