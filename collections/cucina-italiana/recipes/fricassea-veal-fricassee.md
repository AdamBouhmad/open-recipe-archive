## Ingredients

- Breast of milk-fed veal, 500 grams
- Butter, 50 grams
- Flour, 5 grams or one scant tablespoon
- Hot water, not boiling, 2 deciliters
- Two egg yolks
- Half a lemon
- One aromatic bouquet
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- Fresh mushrooms, 100 to 150 grams (if in season), or a pinch of dried mushrooms

## Directions

1. Cut the breast into pieces leaving all the bones.
2. Put a pot on the fire with half the butter and when it begins to melt, pour in the flour stirring until it takes on a hazel color.
3. Then begin to pour the water little by little and then the bouquet which you can compose of some strips of onion and carrot, threads of parsley, celery and basil, all tied together, excluding the leaves because these could fall apart and spoil the fricassee, one of whose merits is to have a beautiful straw color.
4. When the water boils throw in the meat and the rest of the butter and season with salt and white pepper, which is the finest pepper.
5. Cover the pot with a sheet of paper held firm by the lid and let it boil slowly.
6. At two-thirds cooking time remove the bouquet and, if it is mushroom season, you can make it more agreeable with 100 or 150 grams of these cut into thin slices; if not, a pinch of dried mushrooms.
7. When ready to serve, remove the pot from the fire and pour in little by little, stirring, the egg yolks whisked with lemon juice.
