## Ingredients

- Milk, 5 decilitres
- Sugar, 85 grams
- Flour or, better, powdered starch, 40 grams
- Egg yolks, 4
- Vanilla flavoring
- Butter, cold
- Fruit preserve (apricots, peaches, or quinces)
- Savoiardi biscuits, 120-130 grams
- White rosolio liqueur
- Alkermes liqueur

## Directions

1. Work the sugar with the egg yolks first, add the flour and lastly the milk a little at a time.
2. Place over high heat, stirring the spoon continuously; when you see it smoking, cover the coals with a shovelful of ash or move the pan to the corner of the stove if you do not want lumps to form.
3. Once it has thickened, continue to keep it on the fire for eight or ten minutes, then let it cool.
4. Take a fluted mold, grease it well with cold butter and begin to fill it as follows: if you have a good fruit preserve, such as apricots, peaches, or quinces, place this first at the bottom of the mold, then a layer of cream and one of savoiardi dipped in white rosolio.
5. If the mold has, for example, eighteen grooves, dip nine savoiardi in alkermes and nine in white rosolio and fill the grooves with them, alternating them.
6. Pour more cream and lay other savoiardi dipped in rosolio on top and repeat the operation until the mold is full.
7. Be careful not to soak the savoiardi too much in the rosolio or they will give it back; if the liqueur is too sweet, correct it with rum or cognac.
8. If the fruit preserve has hardened over time, soften it on the fire with a few spoonfuls of water, but pour it cold into the mold.
9. Let it sit in ice for at least three hours, then unmold it by immersing the mold in hot water for a moment so the butter melts.
