## Ingredients

- Fish
- Water
- Salt
- Onion
- Cloves
- Celery
- Carrot
- Parsley
- Lemon
- Beets (cooked)
- Potatoes (boiled)
- Hard-boiled eggs

## Directions

1. Put the necessary amount of water (not too much) on the fire and salt it.
2. Before putting in the fish, boil for about a quarter hour with the following aromatics: a quarter or half onion (depending on the quantity of fish), studded with two cloves, pieces of celery and carrot, parsley, and two or three lemon slices.
3. Alternatively, put it on the fire with cold water and the indicated aromatics, and after cooking, leave it hot in its broth until the time to serve it.
4. Rub the fish all over with lemon slices while still raw so the skin remains more intact.
5. The fish is done when the eyes pop out, the skin detaches when touched, and the fish becomes tender from boiling.
6. Serve it hot at the table, not completely dry from the water it was cooked in.
7. If desired, cover it with fresh parsley and arrange it in the center of a mixed side dish of beets (cooked in water if small, or in the oven if large) and boiled potatoes, both cut into very thin slices so they take on the seasoning better, and add a few hard-boiled egg wedges.
