## Ingredients

- Artichokes
- Water
- Vinegar or lemon
- Aromatic herbs (such as thyme, basil, celery leaves)

## Directions

1. Prepare them at the peak of harvest when they cost little; however, choose good quality artichokes at the right degree of maturity.
2. Remove all the tough leaves, trim the tips, clean a good length of the stem and cut them into four wedges, removing the fuzzy center if any.
3. As you cut them, throw them into fresh water acidulated with vinegar or lemon so they do not become black.
4. For the same reason, put them on the fire in an earthenware pot containing boiling water to which it is well to give flavor with a bundle of aromatic herbs, such as thyme, basil, celery leaves and similar.
5. Ten minutes of boiling, or even only five if they are tender, will be sufficient to cook them halfway.
6. Drain them and put them on a grating to dry in the sun; then string them and finish drying them in the shade in a ventilated place.
7. If possible do not keep them so long in the sun lest they acquire the smell of hay.
8. When used for frying or as a side dish to stewed dishes, soften them in boiling water.
