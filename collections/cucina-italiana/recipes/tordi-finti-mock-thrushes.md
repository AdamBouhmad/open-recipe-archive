## Ingredients

- Lean veal without bone for six thrushes, 300 grams
- Juniper berries, 6
- Chicken livers, 3
- Salted anchovies, 3
- Oil, 3 tablespoons
- Lard, as needed
- Sage leaves

## Directions

1. Cut the veal into six thin slices and flatten them, shaping them nicely and setting aside the trimmings.
2. Mince together the trimmings, chicken livers, a piece of lard, the juniper berries, cleaned anchovies, and a sage leaf very finely, seasoning with a little salt and pepper to form the filling.
3. Roll the meat slices around this filling, then wrap each with a thin slice of lard, placing half a sage leaf between the lard and the meat, and tie in a cross pattern. About 60 grams of lard in total should suffice.
4. Heat three tablespoons of oil in a sauté pan or open pot over high heat, place the rolled meat inside and lightly season with salt and pepper.
5. When browned on all sides, drain off the fat, leaving the browned bits on the bottom of the pan, and finish cooking with broth added little by little so they remain almost dry at the end.
6. Serve untied over six slices of lightly toasted bread moistened with the concentrated pan sauce.
7. They are also good served cold.
