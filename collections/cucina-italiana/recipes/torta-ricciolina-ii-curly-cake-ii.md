## Ingredients

- Flour, 170 grams
- Sugar, 70 grams
- Butter, 60 grams
- Lard, 25 grams
- One egg
- Sweet almonds with three bitter ones, blanched, 120 grams
- Sugar, 100 grams
- Butter, 15 grams
- Candied orange, 15 grams
- One egg yolk
- Water
- Very thin noodles
- Butter, 20 grams liquefied
- Candied citron, 10 grams
- Vanilla-powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Make a short crust pastry with flour, sugar, butter, lard, and one egg.
2. Spread one part to the thickness of a coin on the bottom of a copper pan 20 or 21 centimeters in diameter (first buttered) and over it pour a marzipan made with the following proportions: blanched sweet almonds with three bitter ones, sugar, butter, candied orange, one egg yolk.
3. Pound the almonds with sugar in the mortar, add the orange in small pieces, and with the butter, egg yolk and a spoonful of water make everything into a paste.
4. With the rest of the short crust pastry form a circle and with a wet finger attach it all around the edges of the pan.
5. Spread the marzipan evenly and cover it with a layer half a finger high of very thin noodles because these should be like a flowering, not the base of the sweet, and oil them with 20 grams of liquefied butter using a brush.
6. Bake the cake in the oven at moderate heat and after scatter over it 10 grams of candied citron in small pieces.
7. Dust it with vanilla-powdered sugar and serve it one or two days after cooking, because time softens it and makes it more delicate.
8. For the noodles make enough for one egg, but a little more than half will suffice.
