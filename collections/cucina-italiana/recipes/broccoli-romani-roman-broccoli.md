## Ingredients

- broccoli romani (Roman broccoli)
- lardo vergine or strutto (pure lard or lard)
- sale (salt)
- pepe (pepper)
- vino bianco dolce (sweet white wine)
- olio (oil)
- aglio (garlic)
- acqua calda (hot water)

## Directions

1. Clean them from the hardest leaves and boil them. Remove from boiling water and place them in cold water, and after squeezing well, cut them roughly and place them in a pan with virgin lard, seasoning with salt and pepper.
2. Once it has absorbed all the fat, sprinkle with sweet white wine, continue to drag them in the pan until it has been completely absorbed and evaporated, then serve as they will be praised.
3. For another method of cooking these broccoli without boiling them first: use only the flower and the most tender leaves; cut these roughly and the flower into small pieces.
4. Put the pan on the fire with oil in proportion and one garlic clove cut into thin slices lengthwise.
5. When the garlic begins to brown, add the leaves and then the flower raw in the pan, salt and pepper for seasoning, and as they dry by boiling, stirring always, wet them with a drop of hot water and, almost when cooking is complete, with white wine.
6. Since I cannot give you the precise amounts of this dish, have the patience to make some trials (I make many!) to ascertain its best taste.
