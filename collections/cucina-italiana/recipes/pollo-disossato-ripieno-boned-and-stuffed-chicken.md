## Ingredients

- One chicken
- Veal loin, approximately 300 grams (or proportionally less if the chicken is small)
- Bread crumb, one large piece soaked in broth
- Parmesan cheese, grated, one handful
- Egg yolks, 3
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg (optional)
- Ham, fat and lean, 20 grams
- Salted tongue, 20 grams
- Butter
- Onion
- Celery
- Carrot
- Water

## Directions

1. Cut the neck in half, the wing tips, and the legs at the thigh joint.
2. Open the chicken along the back superficially, from the wings to the tail, and with a sharp knife begin to remove the bones of the wings from the inside, scraping the meat well.
3. Remove the bones of the hips and thighs from the inside.
4. Scraping with the knife the external bones of the carcass, remove it all in one piece, including the innards.
5. Leave the small bones of the breastbone, or remove it all and take out the breast fork.
6. Turn the thighs and wings, now stripped of bones, drawing them inward and remove all the tendons you find between the meat.
7. Finely mince the veal, then pound it in a mortar to reduce it very fine.
8. Add to this meat a large piece of bread soaked in broth, a handful of grated Parmesan, three egg yolks, salt, pepper and, if you like, nutmeg flavor.
9. Add to the mixture 20 grams of fat and lean ham and 20 grams of salted tongue, cut into small dice.
10. Fill the chicken and sew it closed.
11. Wrap it tightly in cloth and tie it.
12. Put it to cook in water for a couple of hours over low heat.
13. Remove the wrapping and let it brown first with butter, then in a sauce made as follows:
14. Break all the bones removed from the chicken, the neck and head included, and with dried meat in pieces, butter, onion, celery and carrot put them on the fire in a pot.
15. Season with salt and pepper, draw the sauce with the water in which the chicken has boiled, which has already become a good broth.
16. Before serving, alone or with a side dish, remove the thread with which it was sewn.
