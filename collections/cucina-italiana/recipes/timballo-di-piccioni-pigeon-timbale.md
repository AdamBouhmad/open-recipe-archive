## Ingredients

- Ham
- Onion
- Celery
- Carrot
- Butter
- One or two squabs
- Squab giblets
- Chicken giblets
- Salt and pepper
- Broth
- Macaroni
- Béchamel sauce
- Parmesan cheese
- Ham, fatty and lean
- Nutmeg
- Truffles or dried mushrooms
- Pie dough

## Directions

1. Make a battuto with ham, onion, celery, and carrot, add a small piece of butter and put it on the fire with one or two squabs, depending on the number of people to be served.
2. Add to them their giblets along with chicken giblets if you have them.
3. Season with salt and pepper and, when the squabs are browned, wet them with broth to finish cooking, but make sure some sauce remains.
4. Strain this sauce and throw in the macaroni that you will have already cooked, but not completely, in salted water, and keep them near the fire, stirring from time to time.
5. Make a little béchamel, then break the squabs at their joints, excluding the neck, head, feet, and backbone bones if you don't like to debone them completely, which would be better.
6. Cut the giblets into rather large pieces and remove the tender core from the onions.
7. When the macaroni have absorbed the sauce, season them with Parmesan, small pieces of butter, dice or better slices of fatty and lean ham, nutmeg, slices of truffles or, lacking these, a handful of dried mushrooms softened.
8. Finally add the béchamel and mix.
9. Take a pot of proportional size, grease it entirely with cold butter, and line it with pie dough.
10. Pour in the mixture, cover it with the same dough, and cook it in the oven; turn it out hot and serve immediately.
11. With 300 grams of macaroni and two squabs you will make a timbale for ten or twelve people if they are not big eaters. If you wish, you can also give it the form of a pastry like that in No. 349.
