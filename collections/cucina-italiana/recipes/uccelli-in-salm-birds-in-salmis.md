## Ingredients

- Small birds or thrushes, left whole if small, or cut in four if large
- Salt and oil for initial roasting
- Butter
- Prosciutto
- Meat broth or marsala
- Shallot, 1
- Juniper berries, 1 or 2 (if thrushes) or bay leaf (if other birds)
- Salt and pepper
- Bread, sliced and toasted

## Directions

1. Roast the birds on a spit over a fire, seasoned with salt and oil, until not quite fully cooked.
2. If using small birds or thrushes, leave them whole; if large, cut into four parts.
3. Remove all heads and pound them in a mortar together with either a small roasted bird or scraps from larger birds.
4. In a small pot, heat a battuto made of butter, a few pieces of prosciutto, meat sauce or broth, and marsala in approximately the same amount as the broth.
5. Add a sliced shallot and either one or two juniper berries (if using thrushes) or a bay leaf (if using other birds).
6. Season with salt and pepper, bring to a boil, and let simmer for half an hour.
7. Pass the sauce through a sieve and add the roasted birds to it.
8. Simmer until completely cooked and serve with slices of toasted bread underneath.
