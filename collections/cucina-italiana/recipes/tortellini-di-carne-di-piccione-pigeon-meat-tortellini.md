## Ingredients

- One young pigeon, plucked, weighing approximately half a kilogram
- Grated Parmesan cheese, 80 grams
- Fatty and lean prosciutto, 70 grams
- Nutmeg to taste

## Directions

1. Empty the pigeon of its organs, as the liver and gizzard are not used in this case, and boil it.
2. To boil it, throw it into boiling water and salt it; half an hour of boiling is sufficient, as it should be undercooked.
3. Remove it from the heat, bone it, then mince very finely both this meat and the prosciutto first with a knife then with a lunette, and finally, adding the Parmesan and nutmeg, work the mixture with the blade of the knife to make it all homogeneous.
4. Use the stamp N. 8 to close them, and with three eggs of pasta you will obtain approximately 260.
5. You may serve them in broth, as a soup course, or dry dressed with cheese and butter, or better with sauce and giblets.
