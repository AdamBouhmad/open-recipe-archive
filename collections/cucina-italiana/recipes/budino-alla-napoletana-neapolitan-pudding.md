## Ingredients

- Semolina
- Milk, 3 glasses
- Sugar
- Salt
- Lemon zest for flavoring
- Egg yolks, 3
- Egg whites, 2
- Butter or lard
- Short pastry crust, half the amount of recipe N. 589A
- Fruit preserves of various kinds such as raspberry, quince, apricot, etc.
- Powdered sugar for dusting

## Directions

1. Cook the semolina in three glasses of milk, taking care that it does not become too firm
2. Remove from heat and season it with sugar, a pinch of salt and lemon zest; when it is no longer boiling, add three egg yolks and two egg whites, mixing everything well
3. Take a medium-sized copper baking dish, butter or lard it, and line it with a sheet of short pastry crust about half a scudo thick
4. Pour one third of the semolina into the dish and scatter over it, at some distance from one another, small pieces or spoonfuls of various fruit preserves such as raspberry, quince, apricots, etc.
5. Place a second and third layer on top of this first layer, always adorning them with the said preserves
6. Cover the top of the pudding with a sheet of the same pastry and moisten the edges with a finger dipped in water so that they stick together
7. Make some decorative marks, brush it with egg yolk and cook it in the oven
8. When you unmold it, dust it with powdered sugar and serve it cold
9. The preserves can be replaced with sultana raisins and candied fruit cut into pieces
