## Ingredients

- Dried chickpeas, 300 grams
- Dried chestnuts, 7 or 8
- Sodium carbonate, 3 grams
- Salt, a pinch
- Sapa, necessary amount to soften the mixture
- Mostarda di Savignano, half a small jar
- Candied fruit, 40 grams in small pieces
- Sugar, a little if the sapa has not sweetened enough
- Ground cinnamon, two teaspoons
- Flour, 270 grams
- Butter, 20 grams
- Sugar, 15 grams
- Eggs, 1
- White wine or marsala, about 3 spoonfuls
- Salt, a pinch

## Directions

1. Soak the chickpeas overnight in fresh water.
2. In the morning unite with them 7 or 8 dried chestnuts and put them on the fire with equally fresh water in an earthenware pot with 3 grams of sodium carbonate tied in a small cloth (the people call this the secret and it serves to facilitate the cooking of chickpeas).
3. When they are cooked, remove them dry and pass them through a sieve while hot and boiling, together with the chestnuts; and if, despite the secret, they had remained hard due to the quality of the water, pound them in the mortar.
4. When you have passed them, season and flavor them with a pinch of salt, with sapa in the quantity necessary to make the mixture somewhat soft, half a small jar of mostarda di Savignano, 40 grams of candied fruit in small pieces, a little sugar if the sapa has not sweetened them enough, and two teaspoons of ground cinnamon.
5. For the dough: work together flour, butter, sugar, 1 egg, white wine or marsala, and a pinch of salt.
6. Draw out a sheet of dough about the thickness of half a coin and cut it with the scalloped round stamp.
7. Make the filling abundant in the discs and you will have, joining the edges, the tortelli in the form of a quarter moon.
8. Fry them in lard or oil and when they are no longer bubbling, dust them with powdered sugar.
