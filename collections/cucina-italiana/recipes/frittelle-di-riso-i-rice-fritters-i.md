## Ingredients

- Milk, half a liter
- Rice, 100 grams
- Flour, 100 grams
- Sultana raisins, 50 grams
- Pine nuts chopped to the size of rice grains, 15 grams
- Eggs, three yolks and one white
- Butter, the size of a walnut
- Sugar, two small spoonfuls
- Rum, one spoonful
- Lemon zest flavoring
- Beer yeast, 30 grams
- A pinch of salt

## Directions

1. Prepare the beer yeast as for Krapfen No. 182, mixing it with 40 grams of the said flour.
2. Cook the rice in the milk so that it remains firm, but set aside some milk to add if needed; to prevent it from sticking, stir often and move the pot to the edge of the stove.
3. Once removed from the fire and lukewarm, pour in the already puffed yeast, the eggs, the remaining flour (the 60 grams left), the pine nuts, the rum, and a bit more milk if needed; after working it somewhat, add the raisins and place the pot near the fire so that the entire mixture rises at a very moderate heat.
4. When it has puffed up, drop it into the pan by spoonfuls to form fritters that will turn out large and light.
5. Dust them with powdered sugar when they have lost their first boil and serve them hot.
