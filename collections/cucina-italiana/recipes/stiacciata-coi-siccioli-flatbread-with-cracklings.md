## Ingredients

- Bread yeast, 650 grams
- Powdered sugar, 200 grams
- Cracklings, 100 grams
- Butter, 40 grams
- Lard, 40 grams
- Five eggs
- Scent of orange or lemon zest

## Directions

1. Prepare it the evening before; first work the yeast on the work surface without the seasonings, then in a bowl for more than half an hour by hand, adding the ingredients and eggs gradually
2. Cover well and place in a warm location so it rises during the night
3. The following morning remix it and pour it into a buttered and floured copper baking pan where it should be no more than two fingers thick
4. Send it to the warm chamber for the second rising and pass it to the oven
5. You can also complete the entire operation at home and cook it in a countryside oven, but be warned this is a somewhat difficult dough to succeed with, especially if the season is very cold
6. It is better to make it when the weather is mild, but do not be discouraged at the first attempt
7. If in the morning it has risen little or not at all, add baker's yeast in a quantity slightly more than a walnut, letting it first rise separately with a pinch of flour and lukewarm water
