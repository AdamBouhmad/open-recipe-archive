## Ingredients

- half a kilogram of lean veal without bone
- one slice of fat and lean prosciutto
- salt
- pepper
- spices
- one egg
- flour
- onion (about the size of a walnut)
- parsley
- celery
- carrot
- a small piece of butter
- half a generous glass of water
- half a teaspoon of flour
- half a lemon

## Directions

1. Take half a kilogram of lean veal without bone, clean it of membranes and calluses, and mince it finely first with a knife, then with a crescent-shaped knife, together with a slice of fat and lean prosciutto.
2. Season with a little salt, pepper, and spices, add one egg, mix well, and with wet hands form it into a ball and dust with flour.
3. Make a small battuto with a little onion (about the size of a walnut), parsley, celery, and carrot, put it on the fire with a small piece of butter, and when it has taken color, throw in the meatloaf.
4. Brown it on all sides, then pour into the pan half a generous glass of water in which you have dissolved half a teaspoon of flour; cover it and let it boil over the lowest heat, watching that it does not stick.
5. When serving, squeeze half a lemon over it with its dense sauce around it.
6. If you want it Piedmont style, simply place a peeled hard-boiled egg in the center of the ball when forming it, which serves to give beauty to the meatloaf when sliced.
