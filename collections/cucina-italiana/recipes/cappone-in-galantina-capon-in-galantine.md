## Ingredients

- one large capon, plucked (approximately 1.5 kilograms), emptied and deboned (resulting in 0.7 kilograms)
- Lean veal, 200 grams
- Lean pork, 200 grams
- Half breast of young hen
- Lard, 100 grams
- Salted tongue, 80 grams
- Ham (fat and lean), 40 grams
- Black truffles, 40 grams
- Pistachios, 20 grams
- Pork and veal for the forcemeat, 200 grams total
- Breadcrumb marrow soaked in broth, 60 grams
- one egg
- salt
- pepper
- kitchen twine
- cloth for wrapping

## Directions

1. Cut the truffles into pieces the size of hazelnuts and blanch the pistachios in hot water to remove skins.
2. Cut the veal, pork, ham, and salted tongue into fillets the thickness of a finger and set aside after salting.
3. Make a forcemeat by pounding together 200 grams of pork and veal with 60 grams of breadcrumb marrow soaked in broth.
4. Add one egg, truffle skins, and scraps of tongue and ham to the forcemeat. Season with salt and pepper and pound until fine.
5. Pass the forcemeat through a sieve.
6. Lay out the deboned capon and salt it lightly.
7. Spread a thin layer of forcemeat on the capon, then arrange a layer of fillets interspersed with truffle pieces and pistachios.
8. Continue alternating layers of fillets and forcemeat, placing the young hen breast fillets toward the tail to avoid accumulating the same meat on the capon's breast.
9. Draw the sides of the capon together and sew it closed.
10. Tie it lengthwise with kitchen twine, wrap it tightly in a clean cloth, and tie both ends.
11. Boil in water for two and a half hours.
12. Remove from the cloth, rewrap it, and place it under a weight in a flat position for at least two hours to compress it into a flattened form.
13. The cooking water can be used for broth or gelatin.
