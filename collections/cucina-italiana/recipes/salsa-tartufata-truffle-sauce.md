## Ingredients

- Onion, size of a walnut
- Half a clove of garlic
- A little parsley
- Butter, 20 grams
- Marsala or white wine, two fingers worth
- Flour, 1 heaping teaspoon
- Salt, pepper and spices
- Broth
- Truffles, sliced very thin

## Directions

1. Make a very fine mixture with a piece of onion the size of a walnut, half a clove of garlic and a little parsley.
2. Put it on the fire with 20 grams of butter and when it has taken color pour in two fingers of marsala or white wine in which you have first dissolved a heaping teaspoon of flour.
3. Season the sauce with a pinch of salt, a pinch of pepper and a pinch of spices and stir it always with a spoon.
4. When the flour has thickened, add a little broth and then throw very thin slices of truffles into this sauce.
5. Let it stay another moment on the fire and use it to garnish fried veal cutlets, steaks or other roasted meat.
6. I warn you that wine, as a condiment, does not suit all stomachs.
