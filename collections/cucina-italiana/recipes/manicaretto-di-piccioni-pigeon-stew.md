## Ingredients

- Squabs
- Ham
- Butter
- Herb bouquet
- Salt and pepper
- Broth
- Squab giblets
- Sweetbreads
- Fresh mushrooms or dried mushrooms
- Truffles
- White wine
- Flour
- Lemon

## Directions

1. Cut them into quarters or large pieces at the joints and put them on the fire with a slice of ham, a small piece of butter, and a garnished herb bouquet, seasoning them with salt and pepper.
2. When they begin to dry, wet them with broth and, at half cooking, add their giblets, sweetbreads in pieces, and fresh mushrooms cut into slices, or also dried ones but first softened in hot water, or truffles; these however should be put in near the end of cooking.
3. After wetting them with broth, pour in, if you have two squabs, half a glass of white wine that you will have first reduced by half on the fire in a separate vessel.
4. Continue to simmer gently, then add another small piece of butter mixed in flour, or flour alone, to thicken the sauce, and lastly, before sending them to the table, remove the ham and bouquet, and squeeze a lemon over the squabs.
5. Blanch the sweetbreads first and skin them if they are from a large animal.
6. In this same way young chickens can be cooked, garnishing them with giblets instead of sweetbreads.
