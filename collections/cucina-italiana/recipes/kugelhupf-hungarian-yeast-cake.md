## Ingredients

- 200 grams Hungarian or very fine flour
- 100 grams butter
- 50 grams sugar
- 50 grams sultana raisins
- 30 grams beer yeast
- one whole egg and two egg yolks
- a pinch of salt
- lemon zest
- milk as needed

## Directions

1. Mix the yeast with warm milk and a handful of the said flour to form a rather firm small loaf; make a cross cut in it and place it in a small pan with a veil of milk underneath, covered and near the fire, making sure the fire does not heat it too much.
2. In winter, melt the butter in a water bath then work it somewhat with the whole egg, then pour in the sugar and then the flour, the egg yolks, the salt and the flavoring, mixing well.
3. Now add the yeast which in the meantime will have already swollen and with spoonfuls of warm milk, pour one at a time, work the mixture with a spoon in a bowl for more than half an hour reducing it to a somewhat liquid consistency, but not too much.
4. Finally pour in the raisins and put it in a smooth buttered mold dusted with powdered sugar mixed with flour, where the mixture does not reach half the pan which you will place well covered in a warm place or in a place of warm temperature to rise, which will take two or three hours.
5. When it is well risen to reach the mouth of the pan, put it in the oven at moderate heat, turn it out while warm, dust it with powdered sugar or if you like (this is optional) sprinkle it with rum.
