## Ingredients

- Milk, one liter
- Sugar, 180 grams
- Egg yolks, 8 and two whites

## Directions

1. Put the milk on the fire with 100 grams of the said sugar and let it boil for a full hour, then remove it from the fire so it becomes cold.
2. Dissolve in a pan apart the 80 grams of sugar that remains and when it is well melted, pour into a smooth mold enough to cover the bottom like a veil; what remains in the pan continue to keep on the fire until it becomes black.
3. Then stop it with a ladle of water and you will hear it crackle as it hardens; but continue to keep it on the fire turning the spoon to obtain a thick and dark liquid.
4. Set it aside and beat in a pot the said eggs, then mix everything together, that is: the milk, the eggs and the burnt sugar.
5. Taste it to see if it is sweet enough, pass it through a not too fine tin strainer and pour it into the already prepared mold.
6. Cook it in a water bath with fire above and when the surface begins to color, place under the lid a sheet greased with butter.
7. To ascertain the cooking, immerse a small stick and if this comes out clean and dry it will be a sign that it should be removed from the fire.
8. Let it cool well and before pouring it into the dish, with or without a napkin, detach it all around with a thin knife. In summer, before turning it out, you can freeze it with ice.
