## Ingredients

- Animelle (calf sweetbreads) or milk veal or chicken livers
- Butter, a small piece
- Salt and pepper
- Sauce N. 4 (as needed for cooking)
- Béchamel sauce N. 137, 1 or 2 spoonfuls
- Salted tongue or prosciutto (fat and lean), finely diced
- Parmigiano cheese, a pinch
- Nutmeg, a pinch
- Truffles (in season, for flavoring)

## Directions

1. If using animelle, place them in a pan with a small piece of butter, season with salt and pepper, and when they take on color, finish cooking them with sauce N. 4
2. Cut the cooked animelle to the size of a chickpea or smaller
3. To the animelle add 1 or 2 spoonfuls of béchamel sauce N. 137, some salted tongue or finely diced prosciutto (fat and lean), a pinch of parmigiano, and a pinch of nutmeg
4. Ensure the ingredients are in proportions that render the mixture of pleasant and delicate flavor
5. Allow the mixture to cool thoroughly so it becomes firm and is easier to work with
6. To enclose it in puff pastry N. 154, you may cook the pastry with the filling in the middle (placing the filling in the center of a disc, moistening the edges with a wet finger, covering with another similar disc and cooking), or fill it after cooking
7. For the second method, unite two discs without filling; in the upper disc, before placing it, cut a small circle the size of a 10-centesimi coin using a metal cutter
8. The pastry will naturally puff during cooking and leave a hollow inside; lift the incised circle with the tip of a knife (it will be shaped like a small lid), optionally enlarge the hollow, fill it, and replace the lid
9. Before cooking, always brush the puff pastry surface with egg yolk
10. If filling a vol-au-vent, prepare a sauce instead with chicken giblets and sweetbreads, cut into large pieces
