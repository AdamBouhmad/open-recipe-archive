## Ingredients

- Milk, 7 decilitres
- Pork blood, melted, 330 grams
- Sapa or fine honey, 200 grams
- Sweet blanched almonds, 100 grams
- Sugar, 100 grams
- Very fine breadcrumbs, 80 grams
- Candied fruit, 50 grams
- Butter, 50 grams
- Fine spices, two teaspoons
- Chocolate, 100 grams
- Nutmeg, one teaspoon
- One strip of lemon zest

## Directions

1. Pound the almonds in a mortar together with the candied fruit, which you will have first cut into small pieces, moisten them from time to time with a few spoonfuls of milk and pass them through a sieve.
2. Place the milk on the fire with the lemon zest, which is then to be removed, and let it boil for ten minutes; add the grated chocolate to it, and when this is melted, remove it from the fire and let it cool a little.
3. Then pour the blood, already passed through a sieve, into the same pot along with all the other ingredients, reserving the breadcrumbs for last, of which, if there is too much, you can leave some behind.
4. Put the mixture to cook in a bain-marie and stir it often with a spoon so it does not stick to the pot. The cooking and the degree of density required are known from the spoon which, left in the middle of the mixture, must remain upright. If this does not happen, add the rest of the breadcrumbs, supposing you have not poured it all in.
5. Pour it into a pan lined with Pasta matta and, when it is completely cooled, cut it into almond shapes. Cook the pasta matta briefly so you can cut it easily and do not let the migliaccio dry out over the fire, but remove it when a pomegranate twig immersed in it comes out clean.
6. If you use honey instead of sapa, taste before adding the sugar so it does not turn out too sweet, and note that one of the merits of this dish is that it be well blended, that is, of fine composition.
