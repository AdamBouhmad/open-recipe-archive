## Ingredients

- Water, 180 grams
- Butter, one large walnut-sized piece
- Sugar, 2 teaspoons
- Salt, a pinch
- Flour, 120 grams
- Eggs, 3
- Vanilla extract
- Flour for dusting
- Lard or oil for frying
- Powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Put in a saucepan 180 grams of water, a piece of butter the size of a large walnut, two teaspoons of sugar and a pinch of salt. When the liquid boils, dissolve 120 grams of flour in it, throwing it all at once so no lumps form and stir immediately with a spoon. Remove it quickly from the heat and while it is still boiling break one egg into it and mix strongly until it is well incorporated; then at intervals, when the mixture is cool, add two more eggs, working always and much with the spoon until it is well worked; and this is known by the action of the spoon itself, which, in the spaces it leaves, draws behind itself a thin veil of paste. Give it the scent of vanilla and prepare on a work surface a certain quantity of flour on which you will pour this paste. Then begin to work it with your hands covered in the same flour and roll it in so that it takes enough flour to become manageable, but still rather soft.
2. Divide this dough into 16 or 18 parts, forming as many little balls that will be a little more than walnut-sized: to each of these balls make a hole in the middle by pressing them with the tip of a finger against the work surface and turning them on themselves; flip them and do the same on the opposite side so the hole becomes large and graceful; thus these balls will take the form of little rings. Now put a wide-mouthed pot of water on the fire, and when the water is quite hot, but not boiling, throw the rings into it three or four at a time. If they stick to the bottom lift them slightly with a slotted spoon, turn them and when they come to the surface remove them dry and place them on a cloth, then with the tip of a knife make around and around each one, both on the outside and inside, an incision or even two at a certain distance, so they can puff better.
3. In this state you can leave them for hours if it suits you. Fry them with plenty of fat, lard or oil, over low heat, stirring the pan often; if they have come out well you will see them grow to an extraordinary volume, remaining dry. While still hot, but not boiling, dust them with powdered sugar and serve them.
