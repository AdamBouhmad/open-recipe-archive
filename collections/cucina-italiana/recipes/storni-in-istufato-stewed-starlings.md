## Ingredients

- Six starlings
- One quarter of a large onion
- Lard fat, 30 grams
- Butter, 20 grams
- Three or four strips of fatty and lean prosciutto
- Juniper berries, 2
- Sage leaves
- Salt and pepper
- Dry white wine or marsala and water

## Directions

1. Make a fine battuto (chopped mixture) with a quarter of a large onion and 30 grams of lard fat.
2. Heat this in a pot with 20 grams of butter, three or four strips of prosciutto, and 2 juniper berries.
3. Place the starlings on top without gutting them, garnish with sage leaves, and season with salt and pepper.
4. Turn them often until they absorb the flavors and the onion is well colored.
5. Moisten with a little dry white wine, then add enough wine (or a mixture of water and marsala) so that the total liquid added is three deciliters, or if lacking white wine, use two deciliters water and one of marsala.
6. Cover the pot with a sheet of paper folded four times, held down by a heavy lid, and simmer gently until completely cooked.
7. Serve the birds with their sauce.
