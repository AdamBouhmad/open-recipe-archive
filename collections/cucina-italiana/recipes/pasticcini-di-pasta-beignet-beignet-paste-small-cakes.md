## Ingredients

- Water, 150 grams
- Flour, 100 grams
- Butter, 10 grams
- Eggs, 3 and one yolk
- Salt, as needed

## Directions

1. When the water boils pour all the flour at once and, stirring immediately, add the butter and keep it on the fire for 10 minutes, continuing always to stir it.
2. It should turn out a hard paste which you will spread to the thickness of a finger and pound in a mortar together with one egg to soften it somewhat.
3. Having obtained this, put it in a bowl to work it with a wooden spoon, adding the other eggs one at a time, whipping the whites.
4. Do not tire of working it until it is reduced like an ointment; let it rest for a few hours, and then put it in spoonfuls (which will be about ten or twelve) in a buttered baking pan.
5. Whisk one egg yolk with a little white to make it more fluid, brush them and smooth them with a small brush (but this supplement is not necessary), then put them in a well-heated oven.
6. When they are cooked make a slit on one side with a small knife, or in the shape of a half-circle on the underside, to fill them with cream or fruit preserves, sprinkle them with powdered sugar and serve them.
7. I warn you that when you work pastes that must puff up, the wooden spoon instead of moving it in circles is better to move it from bottom to top.
