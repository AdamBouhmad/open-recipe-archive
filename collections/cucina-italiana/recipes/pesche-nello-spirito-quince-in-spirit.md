## Ingredients

- Quince, 1 kilogram, not too ripe
- White sugar, 440 grams
- Water, 1 liter
- Whole cinnamon, one piece the length of a finger
- Some cloves
- Spirit of wine as needed

## Directions

1. Rub the quince with a cloth to remove the down and prick them in five or six places with a small stick
2. Boil the sugar in the water in an open pan for twenty minutes
3. Throw in the whole quince, stirring often if the syrup does not cover them, and when they have boiled for five minutes from when they return to a boil, remove them and let them dry
4. When the quince and syrup are cold, or better the next day, place them in a glass vase or new glazed earthenware vase, pour the syrup over them and enough spirit of wine or cognac to submerge them in the right measure
5. Add the indicated aromatics and ensure they always remain covered by the liquid, pouring more as needed
6. Keep the vase sealed hermetically and do not begin eating them before a month has passed
