## Ingredients

- Milk-fed veal breast in one piece, 500 grams
- Lean milk-fed veal without bone, 170 grams
- Fat and lean prosciutto, 40 grams
- Bologna mortadella, 40 grams
- Parmesan cheese, 15 grams
- Eggs, 1
- Just under a quarter of a garlic clove and 4 or 5 parsley leaves

## Directions

1. Make a mixture with the lean veal: clean it of tendons and skin if present, and mince it very finely with a small piece of prosciutto fat from the 40 grams. To this minced meat add the garlic and parsley, minced very finely, the Parmesan, the egg, a pinch of pepper, very little salt and mix everything well together. If you have the opportunity, mince a truffle ball into the mixture.
2. Debone the breast of hard bones and leave the tender ones; open it in the connective tissue by passing the knife under the ribs so that it becomes double in surface area like opening a book. Over the half of the breast where the tender bones remain, spread part of the mixture and over this arrange part of the prosciutto or mortadella cut into strips one finger wide, spacing them at small distances from one another. Over this first layer place a second and third, if you have enough filling, always alternating the mixture and cured meats. Once finished, pull the other half of the breast that remained bare over the filling to close it, as it were, like a book, and with a large needle and thread sew the edges so the filling does not burst out; besides this, tie it tightly in a cross with string.
3. Put it on the fire in a pot with a piece of butter, salt and pepper, and when it has taken color on both sides, bring it to cooking temperature with water poured in little by little.
4. Serve it hot with its reduced sauce; but first remove the string and thread. If it has turned out well it should be able to be cut into slices and make a fine appearance with its lardons. You can surround it with fresh peas cooked in their own sauce, or with fennel cut into sections, but first boiled.
