## Ingredients

- One young but large squab
- Lean veal, 100 grams, or one chicken breast
- Thin slices of fatty and lean ham, 40 grams
- Slices of salted tongue, 30 grams
- Butter, 40 grams
- Half a glass of good defatted broth
- One hard-boiled egg
- Flour, 150 grams
- Butter, 50 grams
- Spirit of wine, one teaspoon
- Sugar, one teaspoon
- Lemon juice, one wedge
- One egg yolk
- Salt, as needed
- Pepper, as needed
- Spices, a hint

## Directions

1. Cut the squab into small pieces at its joints, discarding the head and feet.
2. Cut the veal or chicken breast into small rolled pieces and beat them with the back of a knife.
3. Cut the ham and tongue into strips one finger wide.
4. Cut the egg into eight wedges.
5. Take an oval metal or porcelain plate that can withstand fire and arrange in layers one on top of the other: first half of the squab and veal, then half of the ham and tongue, half of the butter scattered here and there in small pieces, and half, that is four wedges, of the egg; season with very little salt, pepper, and a hint of spices, and repeat the operation with the remainder so that the whole makes a mound.
6. Finally pour the aforementioned broth, but cold, which you will see floating on the first edge of the plate and which will remain largely after cooking.
7. For the pastry: mix the flour with the aforementioned ingredients (spirit of wine, sugar, lemon juice, egg yolk, and salt), and if they are not enough, add tepid water to make a rather soft dough.
8. Work it very much by throwing it with force against the pastry board, let it rest a little, and roll it out by folding it four or five times, reducing it finally to the thickness of a shield with the striped rolling pin.
9. Cover the plate with it, adorning it, if possible, with trimmings of the same pastry, then gild it with egg yolk.
10. Cook this pastry (which can be called such) in a country oven and serve it hot.
11. Alternatively, give the squab and other meats a partial cooking first with the butter, seasoning them with salt, pepper, and spices, then arrange them on the platter in the indicated manner, not excluding the cooking juices and broth. You can increase the seasoning and also add chicken giblets, sweetbreads, and truffles.
