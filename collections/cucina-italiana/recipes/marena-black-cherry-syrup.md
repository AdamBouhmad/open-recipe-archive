## Ingredients

- True marasca cherries, very sour even when mature
- Cinnamon stick, one large piece
- Depurated juice, 6 kilograms
- Whitest powdered sugar, 8 kilograms
- Citric acid, 50 grams
- Cherry pits, a handful reserved, 30 grams of kernels extracted

## Directions

1. Take the marasca cherries and remove the stems and crush them like grapes when pressed to make wine.
2. Set aside a handful of pits for later use and place the cherries with a large piece of whole cinnamon in a cool place, inside an earthen vessel to await fermentation, which must last at least forty-eight hours.
3. From the moment the cherries have begun to rise, press them down and mix them from time to time.
4. Extract the juice by squeezing the cherries a few at a time through a thin cloth.
5. When the juice has rested, decant the clear part, and pass the remaining part several times through a wool filter.
6. Once the liquid is purified, put it on the fire in the following proportions with the remembered piece of cinnamon.
7. For pouring the sugar and citric acid wait until the liquid is very hot and then stir often so that the sugar does not settle at the bottom and does not scorch.
8. The boiling must be brief; four or five minutes are sufficient to incorporate the sugar into the liquid.
9. Do not boil for too long as prolonged boiling would cause the fruit to lose its aroma, while insufficient boiling would produce sugar settling over time.
10. When you remove the marena from the fire, pour it into an earthen vessel and bottle it while cold.
11. Cork the bottles with cork without tar and keep them in the cellar where both the marena and the syrups will remain unchanged for several years.
12. For the kernels recalled: dry them in the sun, then crush them and extract 30 grams of almonds, which you will pound very finely in a mortar and mix with the cherries before fermentation; with their pleasant bitter taste these almonds serve to give more elegance to the syrup.
