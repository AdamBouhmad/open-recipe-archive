## Ingredients

- Chicken breast (petto di pollastra o di cappone)
- Butter
- Salt and pepper
- Broth
- Bread crumb (midolla di pane)
- Egg yolks
- Nutmeg
- Flour
- Bread cut into cubes
- Lard or butter for frying

## Directions

1. Cut the chicken breast into small pieces and cook slowly in butter, seasoning with salt and pepper
2. If needed, moisten with broth
3. Remove the cooked chicken and keep aside
4. In the remaining sauce, add a bread crumb about the size of a fist and make a thick paste with broth
5. Place this paste and the cooked chicken in a mortar
6. Add two egg yolks and a pinch of nutmeg, pound everything finely
7. Leave the mixture in a cool place to set
8. When ready to use (can be the next day), dust a work surface with flour
9. Form the mixture into a stick about the thickness of a finger or less
10. Cut with a floured knife into equal pieces
11. Round them into small balls the size of a hazelnut or smaller, using flour-dusted hands
12. Drop into boiling broth and cook for five or six minutes
13. Serve in a bowl lined with bread cubes fried in butter or virgin lard
14. Serves ten to twelve people
