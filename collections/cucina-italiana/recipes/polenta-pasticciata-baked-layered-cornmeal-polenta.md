## Ingredients

- Corn flour
- Milk
- Salt
- Parmesan cheese
- Butter
- Bolognese sauce or similar
- Béchamel sauce

## Directions

1. Make a firm polenta from corn flour cooked in milk.
2. Salt it when you are about to remove it from the fire and pour it onto a work surface, about two fingers high.
3. When cooled, cut it into large almond-shaped pieces about half a centimeter thick, which you will arrange in the following manner in a metal or porcelain tray that can withstand fire.
4. Make a sauce like the one for seasoning Bolognese-style macaroni or similar, and make some béchamel sauce.
5. Dust the bottom of the tray with grated Parmesan and spread a layer of polenta; season it with Parmesan, the sauce, and the béchamel; then on top of this place another layer of polenta and season it equally; and so on until you run out of ingredients.
6. A few small pieces of butter here and there will not hurt, but use sparingly if you do not want it to become cloying from excessive seasoning.
7. Prepared this way with its top, place it in a country oven to brown the polenta and serve it hot as a side dish during a meal in autumn and winter.
8. If it turns out well it will be praised for its delicacy.
9. During hunting season a skilled cook can put it in form, filling it with small birds cooked in their own juices.
