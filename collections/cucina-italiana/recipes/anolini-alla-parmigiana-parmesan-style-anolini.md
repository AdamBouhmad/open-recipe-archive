## Ingredients

- Lean beef from the thigh, boneless, 500 grams
- Lard, about 20 grams
- Butter, 50 grams
- One quarter of a medium onion
- Breadcrumbs from day-old bread, lightly toasted, 100 grams
- Grated Parmesan, 50 grams
- Nutmeg scent
- One egg and the meat broth
- Three eggs for the pasta sheets
- Red wine
- Salt, pepper and spice scent

## Directions

1. Lard the piece of meat, tie it and season with salt, pepper and spice scent, then put it on the fire in an earthenware pot or casserole with butter and finely chopped onion to brown it with the butter.
2. Pour two ladle-fuls of broth into the pot and close it with several sheets of paper held secure by a bowl containing some red wine.
3. Boil the meat gently in this way for eight or nine hours, to obtain four or five spoonfuls of a reduced and flavorful broth which you will strain through a sieve, squeezing well, and which you will save for the next day.
4. Form the filling for the Anolini with: toasted breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan, nutmeg scent, one egg and the meat broth. Make everything into a homogeneous mixture.
5. Make three sheets of pasta kept somewhat tender and fill the scalloped disc, folding it in two to obtain the shape of a small half moon. With this amount you will obtain about one hundred, which will be good in broth or dry like tortellini.
6. Cook the remaining meat alone or with a side of vegetables and it will be a stracotto.
