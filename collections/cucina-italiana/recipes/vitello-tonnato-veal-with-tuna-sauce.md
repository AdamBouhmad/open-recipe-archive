## Ingredients

- 1 kilogram of milk-fed veal from the thigh or rump, all in one piece and boneless
- 2 anchovies
- 1/4 onion studded with 2 cloves
- 1 bay leaf
- celery
- carrot
- parsley
- salt for cooking water
- 100 grams of canned tuna in oil
- 2 anchovies
- fine oil
- lemon juice
- capers

## Directions

1. Take 1 kilogram of milk-fed veal from the thigh or rump, all in one piece and boneless, remove the thin skin and fat, then lard it with two anchovies.
2. Wash the anchovies, split them in two, remove the spine, and cut them crosswise making eight pieces total.
3. Tie the meat not too tightly and put it to boil for an hour and a half in enough water to cover it, in which you have put a quarter of an onion studded with two cloves, a bay leaf, celery, carrot, and parsley.
4. Salt the water generously and wait for it to boil before putting in the meat.
5. When cooked, untie it, dry it, and when cold cut it into thin slices and keep it soaking for a day or two in a narrow vessel in the following sauce in sufficient quantity to cover it.
6. Pound 100 grams of canned tuna in oil and two anchovies, break them down well with a knife blade or better pass them through a sieve, adding fine oil in abundance little by little and the juice of a lemon or even more so that the sauce comes out liquid.
7. Finally mix in a handful of capers squeezed of vinegar.
8. Serve the veal with tuna sauce with its sauce and with lemon wedges.
9. Strain the broth and use it for a risotto.
