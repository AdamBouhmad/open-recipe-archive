## Ingredients

- Fine grain semolina
- Milk, one glass
- Salt, to taste
- Parmigiano cheese, grated
- Butter, a small piece
- Nutmeg, to taste
- Eggs, 2

## Directions

1. Cook fine grain semolina in milk and throw in enough so it turns out quite firm.
2. When you remove it from the fire season it with salt, grated parmigiano, a small piece of butter and a hint of nutmeg and let it cool.
3. Then dissolve the mixture with eggs until you reduce it like a liquid cream.
4. Take a smooth tin mold, grease the bottom well with butter, stick to it an equally greased sheet and pour the said mixture into it to set it in a bain-marie with fire on top.
5. Once cooked and cooled, a knife blade passed all around and the paper at the bottom will help you unmold it.
6. Cut it into small bricks or pieces the thickness of a coin and one or two centimeters wide and throw them into broth making them boil for a few minutes.
7. One glass of milk and two eggs is enough to make soup for four or five people.
