## Ingredients

- Two dozen frogs, large ones
- Two cloves garlic
- Parsley
- Carrot
- Celery
- Basil, if desired
- Onion, if garlic is disliked
- Salt
- Pepper
- Oil
- Tomatoes or tomato conserve
- Water
- Dried mushrooms
- Bread for toasting

## Directions

1. Remove the thighs from the frogs and set them aside.
2. Make a generous battuto (finely chopped mixture) with two cloves of garlic, parsley, carrot, celery, and basil if desired; or use onion if you dislike garlic.
3. Put this mixture on the fire with salt, pepper, and a good measure of oil.
4. When the garlic begins to take color, add the frogs.
5. Stir them from time to time so they do not stick.
6. When they have released a good portion of their liquid, add pieces of tomatoes or, if lacking, diluted tomato conserve with water.
7. Continue to boil, and finally add the necessary water to make soup, keeping everything on the fire until the frogs are cooked and falling apart.
8. Pass everything through a sieve, pressing well so that only the small bones remain.
9. Put the thighs set aside earlier to boil in a little of this strained broth.
10. Debone them when cooked and mix them into the soup along with pieces of dried mushrooms that have been softened.
11. Toast bread into slices, then cut into rather large dice.
12. Serve the soup with the toasted bread dice.
