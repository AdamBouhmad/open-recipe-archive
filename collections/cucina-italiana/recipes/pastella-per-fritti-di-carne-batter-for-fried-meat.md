## Ingredients

- Flour, three heaping teaspoons
- Oil, two teaspoons
- Eggs, 2
- Salt, a pinch
- Pepper, a pinch

## Directions

1. Mix three heaping teaspoons of flour with two teaspoons of oil, add two eggs, a pinch of salt and mix well. This mixture will take on the appearance of a flowing cream and will serve to coat fried brain, sweetbreads, pancreas, lamb testicles, head of milk-fed veal and similar items. These things, some more some less, according to their nature, blanch them all, including the brain and sweetbreads which firm up when boiled. Salt the water and add a pinch of salt and pepper when you remove them from the water. Cut the pancreas into fillets lengthwise. Keep the sweetbreads about half a finger long. If the sweetbreads are from lamb, leave them whole. Cut the brains into pieces the size of a walnut, and keep the heads at a somewhat larger size. Toss the pieces in the batter after flouring them and fry them in pure lard or oil. These white fried items are often served with liver or milk-fed veal cutlets. Slice the liver very thinly, beat the cutlets with the back of a knife or mince the meat with a chopper to reunite it afterward into an elegant shape. Season both with salt and pepper, place them in a bath of beaten egg, and after a few hours, before frying them, roll them in fine breadcrumbs, repeating the operation even twice if necessary. Always accompany these fried items with lemon wedges.
