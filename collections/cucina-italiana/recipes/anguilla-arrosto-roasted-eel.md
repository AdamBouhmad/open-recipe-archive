## Ingredients

- eel
- bread croutons
- sage or laurel leaves
- salt
- lemon

## Directions

1. If the eel is large and you want to cook it on a spit, it is better to skin it.
2. Cut it into pieces three centimeters long and thread them between two croutons with some sage or laurel leaves if you don't fear that the latter, for its too sharp smell, will turn your stomach.
3. Cook it plain over moderate heat and finally give it a good flame to make a crispy crust.
4. For seasoning, salt only and lemon wedges when brought to the table.
5. Alternatively, for medium-sized eel, roast it on a grill with its skin, which when softened with lemon juice at the table can offer a pleasant flavor when sucked.
6. Season with salt and pepper only. The eels require red and dry wine to accompany them.
