## Ingredients

- Porcini mushrooms in nature, half the weight of the rice (to be cleaned and trimmed)
- A little onion, parsley, celery, carrot for a battuto
- Three tablespoons of oil, if the rice is 300 grams (to serve three people)
- Tomato juice and water
- Salt and pepper
- One clove of garlic, whole
- Butter, a piece
- Parmesan cheese

## Directions

1. Make a battuto with a little onion, parsley, celery, carrot and put it on the fire with three tablespoons of oil
2. When the battuto has taken color, stop it with tomato juice and water, season with salt and pepper, and boil a whole clove of garlic in it, which you will then discard before passing the soffritto
3. Put the soffritto back on the fire to cook the mushrooms, first chopped to a size slightly smaller than corn kernels
4. Once cooked, set them aside
5. Brown the raw rice with a piece of butter, then cook it with hot water poured one ladle at a time
6. At half cooking, mix the mushrooms into it and before serving give it flavor with Parmesan cheese
7. It will be willingly eaten also made with a handful of dried mushrooms instead of fresh ones
