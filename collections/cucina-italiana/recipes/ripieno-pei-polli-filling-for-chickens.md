## Ingredients

- Lean milk-fed veal, approximately 100 grams, a small piece of veal breast and the giblets of the same chicken
- Small mixture of shallot or onion, parsley, celery, carrot and butter
- Salt, pepper and spices
- Broth
- Dried mushroom pieces, softened
- A slice of fatty and lean prosciutto
- Bread marrow for one spoonful of thick paste
- Good pinch of Parmesan cheese
- Two eggs

## Directions

1. Cook this meat together with a small mixture of shallot or onion, parsley, celery, carrot and butter. Season with salt, pepper and spices, moistening it with broth. Remove it dry from the heat, remove the tender parts from the gizzard, add several pieces of softened dried mushrooms, a slice of fatty and lean prosciutto and chop everything very finely with a chopper. Into the liquid remaining from cooking the meat, throw a piece of bread marrow to make one spoonful of thick paste. Mix it into the mixture, add a good pinch of Parmesan and two eggs, and fill the chicken with all of this and sew it shut. You can cook it boiled or braised. If you cook it boiled you will have excellent broth, but be careful to carve it to extract the filling all in one piece which you will then slice. Another filling for chickens is that of roasted chicken no. 539.
