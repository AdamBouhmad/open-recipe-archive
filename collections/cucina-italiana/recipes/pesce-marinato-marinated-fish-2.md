## Ingredients

- Sole or large eel
- Oil
- Vinegar
- Sapa (grape must reduction)
- Sage leaves
- Pine nuts
- Raisins
- Garlic
- Candied fruit
- Sugar (if sapa is unavailable)
- Salt
- Pepper

## Directions

1. If using sole, fry it first in oil and salt it. If using eel, cut it into pieces about half a finger long and cook them on a grill or spit without skinning them.
2. When the fish has rendered its fat, season with salt and pepper.
3. In a pot, pour vinegar, sapa (in proportion to the amount of fish), whole sage leaves, whole pine nuts, raisins, a few garlic cloves cut in half lengthwise, and candied fruit in small pieces.
4. If sapa is not available, substitute with sugar and taste to correct the flavor if the vinegar is too strong.
5. Bring this mixture to a boil and pour it over the fish that you have arranged in an earthenware dish, spreading it so the liquid covers it from all sides.
6. Bring it to a boil again with the fish inside, then cover the dish and set it aside.
7. When serving at table, take the quantity needed with some of its sauce, including some of the ingredients in it.
8. If over time the fish dries out, refresh it with a little more of the marinade.
