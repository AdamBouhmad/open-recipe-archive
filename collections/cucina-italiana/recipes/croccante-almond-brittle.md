## Ingredients

- sweet almonds, 120 grams
- powdered sugar, 100 grams
- butter or oil for the mold
- ash for the coals

## Directions

1. Shell the almonds and separate the lobes, which are the two parts naturally joined together
2. Cut each lobe into thin strips, either lengthwise or crosswise as you prefer
3. Heat the cut almonds over the fire and dry them until they take on a yellowish color, without roasting them
4. Meanwhile, place the sugar in a pot, preferably not tinned, and heat until it is perfectly liquefied
5. When the sugar is liquid, pour the well-heated almonds into it and stir
6. To prevent the brittle from becoming bitter from overcooking, throw a shovel of ash onto the coals
7. The exact point of doneness is known by the cinnamon color the brittle acquires
8. Then pour it gradually into a mold previously greased with butter or oil, pressing it with a lemon against the walls and spreading it as thin as you can
9. Unmold while cold; if this proves difficult, immerse the mold in boiling water
10. Alternatively, you may dry the almonds in the sun, grind them finely with a crescent-shaped tool, and add a piece of butter when they are in the sugar
