## Ingredients

- For every egg used to bind it, the following ingredients in these quantities:
- Powdered sugar, 30 grams
- Zibibbo raisins, 30 grams
- Sultana raisins, 30 grams
- Crumb of fine bread, 30 grams
- Suet from wethered mutton, 30 grams
- Candied citron, 15 grams
- Candied orange, 15 grams
- Rum, one tablespoonfull

## Directions

1. Remove seeds from the zibibbo raisins; cut the candied fruits into short, thin strips; if you cannot obtain the suet from a castrated ram, take it from veal, and cut both this and the bread crumb into very small dice, removing the membranes from the suet
2. Make a mixture of everything, having previously beaten the eggs separately, and let it rest for some hours; then place it in a cloth and tie it very tightly with string to form a ball
3. Place a pot of water on the heat and when it boils immerse the pudding in it in such a way that it does not touch the bottom of the pot, letting it boil slowly for as many hours as there are eggs
4. Remove it from the cloth carefully, make a small well on top and, pouring in it a small glass or two of cognac or rum that spreads throughout the pudding, set it alight and thus hot and in flames send it to the table to be cut into slices and eaten when the flame is extinguished
5. Three eggs will be enough for six people
