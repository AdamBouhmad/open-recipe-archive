## Ingredients

- Veal liver, 500 grams
- Butter, 70 grams
- Fresh bread crumb, 50 grams
- Grated Parmesan cheese, 20 grams
- Chicken livers, 4 pieces
- Marsala wine, 1 deciliter
- Meat gravy or broth, 6 tablespoons
- Eggs, 1 whole and 2 yolks
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt and pepper, to taste

## Directions

1. Slice the liver into thin slices and cut the chicken livers in two parts, and throw these two things in a pan with the bay leaf and half the butter and when they have absorbed it add the other half and season with salt and pepper.
2. Pour the Marsala and after 4 or 5 minutes at most on high heat, the liver must remain tender; remove it dry and together with the bay leaf pound it in a mortar.
3. In the sauce that remains in the pan, crumble the bread crumb and make a paste which you will also throw in the mortar, then pass everything through a sieve; then add the Parmesan and the eggs, diluting the mixture with the said gravy or broth.
4. Finally place it in a smooth mold with paper underneath, greased with butter, and set it in a bain-marie.
5. Unmold it warm and when it is cold cover it all with gelatin from recipe 3, inside a mold of greater circumference than the first.
6. It will be enough for twelve people.
