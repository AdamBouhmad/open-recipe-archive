## Ingredients

- 700 grams of fish
- three large cloves of garlic
- fresh ginger, or dried ginger
- oil
- one glass of liquid composed of one third dry white wine or red wine and the rest water
- salt
- tomato juice or tomato paste
- water
- bread slices

## Directions

1. Pound in a mortar three large cloves of garlic and fresh or dried ginger, reducing it to powder.
2. Put this mixture on the fire in a terracotta pan or pot with oil in proportion, and when it has fried gently, pour in a glass of liquid made up of one third dry white wine or red wine and the rest water.
3. Place the fish in it, salt it and shortly after add tomato juice or tomato paste dissolved in a drop of water.
4. Boil over a strong fire, always keeping the pot covered, never touch the fish so as not to break it, and you will find it cooked in a few minutes.
5. Serve it as the previous one, with slices of bread set apart which you will dry first by the fire without toasting them.
6. If the fish, before cooking it, remains raw for several hours, it keeps better if salted; but then it is well to wash it before putting it on the fire.
