## Ingredients

- One capon with only the breast, that is empty, without the neck and legs, killed the day before, weighing about 800 grams
- Black or white truffles, fragrant, 250 grams
- Butter, 80 grams
- Marsala wine, 5 tablespoons
- Salt
- Pepper

## Directions

1. Keep the truffles large as walnuts, lightly peel them and throw the skin thus raw inside the capon; you can also insert some slices of raw truffle under the skin.
2. Put the butter on the fire and when it is melted throw in the truffles with the Marsala, salt and pepper for seasoning and, on high heat, let them boil for only two minutes, stirring them constantly.
3. Removed from the pan, let them cool until the fat is set and then pour everything into the capon, sewing it both in the lower part and in the front where the neck was removed.
4. Keep it in a cool place to cook it after 24 hours, giving it thus three days of aging.
5. If it were a pheasant or a turkey, regulate yourself in proportion. In winter, it is good to keep them filled three or four days before cooking them, indeed for the pheasant you must wait.
