## Ingredients

- 500 grams of small fish of various species
- One quarter of an onion
- Parsley
- Celery
- Oil
- Water
- Tomato juice or conserve
- Salt
- Pepper
- Two eggs
- Three tablespoons Parmesan cheese
- Bread for toasting or frying
- Butter, oil, or lard for frying

## Directions

1. Make a battuto with a quarter of an onion, parsley, and celery.
2. Put it on the fire with oil.
3. When it has colored, add the fish, moistening it when dry with water, tomato juice, or conserve.
4. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Let it cook well, then add the necessary water for the soup: about one liter or slightly more in total can suffice.
6. Pass everything through a sieve or strainer, pressing well, and return it to the fire to bring it to a boil.
7. In the soup tureen, beat together two eggs with three tablespoons of Parmesan cheese beforehand.
8. Pour the broth slowly and gradually into the tureen with the egg mixture.
9. Before sending the soup to the table, add the bread, which, in small dice, can be simply toasted or fried in butter, oil, or lard as you prefer.
10. The eggs and Parmesan can also be beaten separately and poured into the pot when the broth is boiling, stirring strongly, if you do not mind seeing them set in shreds.
