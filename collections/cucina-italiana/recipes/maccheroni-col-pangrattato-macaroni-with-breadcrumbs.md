## Ingredients

- Long macaroni that hold up well to cooking, 300 grams
- Flour, 15 grams
- Butter, 60 grams
- Gruyère cheese, 60 grams
- Parmesan cheese, 40 grams
- Milk, 6 deciliters
- Breadcrumbs, as needed

## Directions

1. Give the macaroni half cooking time, salt them and pour them onto a strainer to drain.
2. Put half the butter and the flour in a pot on the fire, stirring continuously; when the flour begins to take on color, pour the milk a little at a time and let it boil for about ten minutes.
3. Then throw the macaroni and grated or diced Gruyère into this béchamel and move the pot to the edge of the stove so that, simmering gently, they absorb the milk.
4. Then add the rest of the butter and grated Parmesan; pour them into a fireproof dish on which they make a heap and cover them all with breadcrumbs.
5. Prepared in this manner, put them in a country oven or under an iron lid with fire on top and when they are browned, serve them hot as a course or, better, accompanied by a plate of meat.
