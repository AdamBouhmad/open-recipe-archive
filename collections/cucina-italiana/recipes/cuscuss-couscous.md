## Ingredients

- 750 grams veal breast
- 150 grams lean veal without bone
- 300 grams coarse semolina
- One chicken liver
- One hard-boiled egg
- One egg yolk
- Vegetables of various kinds such as onion, savoy cabbage, celery, carrot, spinach, beet or other
- Salt and pepper
- Water
- Oil
- Bread marrow
- Meat broth or tomato sauce or tomato conserve

## Directions

1. Put the semolina in a flat wide earthenware vessel or in a tinned copper pan, season it with a pinch of salt and a dash of pepper and, pouring over it drop by drop two fingers' worth (of a glass) scant of water, knead it with the palm of your hand to make it swell, become granular and loose.
2. When the water is finished, pour over it, little by little, a spoonful of oil and continue manipulating it in the same manner, lasting between the first and second operation more than half an hour.
3. Having conditioned the semolina in this way, put it in a soup bowl and cover it with a cloth, the excess of which, passing it underneath, you will tie tightly with twine.
4. Put the veal breast on the fire with three liters of water to make broth and after skimming the pot, cover its mouth with the bowl already prepared, so that the broth remains at some distance; but make sure that the mouths of the two vessels fit together and do not let smoke escape.
5. Leave the semolina thus for an hour and a quarter so it has time to cook by steam, open the wrapping halfway through cooking to mix it and then put it back as before.
6. Chop with a knife the 150 grams of lean meat, add to it a piece of bread marrow crumbled, season it with salt and pepper, make many small meatballs slightly larger than a hazelnut and fry them in oil.
7. Chop the vegetables somewhat and put first the onion to soften in oil and when it has taken color throw down the others, season them with salt and pepper, stir often and let them draw out the water they release.
8. Reduced almost to dryness, moisten them with meat juice, or with broth and tomato juice or conserve, to bring them to cooking together with the chicken liver cut into pieces and with the meatballs.
9. Remove the semolina from the wrapping, put it on the fire in a saucepan and without letting it boil dissolve into it the egg yolk, pour into it part of the said sauce, mix and pour it into a serving dish, but almost dry so it presents fullness, which you will decorate with the hard-boiled egg cut into small wedges.
10. Mix the rest of the sauce into the broth in the pot and send this broth to table divided into as many cups as there are diners, accompanied, of course, by the serving dish of semolina; thus each one draws down into his plate a portion of semolina and drinks after it the broth by spoonfuls.
11. The veal breast is served afterward as boiled meat.
