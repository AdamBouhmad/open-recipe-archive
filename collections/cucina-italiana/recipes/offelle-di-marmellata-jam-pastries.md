## Ingredients

- Rose apples, 500 grams
- Powdered sugar, 125 grams
- Candied fruit, 30 grams
- Ground cinnamon, two teaspoons
- Short crust pastry

## Directions

1. Cut the apples into four wedges, peel them and remove the core
2. Cut these wedges into the thinnest slices you can and place them in a pot with two glasses of water, breaking them up with a wooden spoon
3. These apples have hard flesh and need water to cook; if they become too dry while boiling, add more water
4. Wait until they are completely broken down before adding the sugar and then taste to check if the sweetness is right, as fruits in general can be more or less acidic depending on ripeness
5. Finally add the candied fruit cut into small pieces and the cinnamon
6. Use the short crust pastry number 589 in the quantity of recipe A, roll it out with a rolling pin to the thickness of a coin and cut it with a round fluted stamp as shown in the previous page; one disc below and one disc above, the latter rolled with a ridged rolling pin, and jam in the middle, moistening the edges so they stick
7. Glaze the offelle with egg yolk and send them to the oven, dusting them afterwards with powdered sugar
