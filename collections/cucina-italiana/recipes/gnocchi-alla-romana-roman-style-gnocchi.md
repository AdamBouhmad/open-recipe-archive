## Ingredients

- Flour, 150 grams
- Butter, 50 grams
- Gruyère cheese, 40 grams
- Parmesan, 20 grams
- Milk, half a liter
- Eggs, 2 pieces

## Directions

1. Mix flour with eggs and milk poured little by little into a saucepan.
2. Add Gruyère cheese in small pieces and place the mixture on the fire, stirring continuously.
3. When it is solidified by the cooking of the flour, salt it and add half the butter.
4. Let the mixture cool, then in the same manner as cornmeal gnocchi, cut it into small pieces in a tray that can withstand fire.
5. Season as you go with the remaining butter in small pieces and the grated Parmesan; but not on the surface, because Parmesan becomes bitter under heat.
6. Brown them under an iron lid or in a country oven and serve them hot.
