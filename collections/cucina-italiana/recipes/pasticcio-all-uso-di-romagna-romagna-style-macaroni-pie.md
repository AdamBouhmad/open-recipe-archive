## Ingredients

- Macaroni, 350 grams
- Parmesan cheese, 170 grams
- Sweetbreads, 150 grams
- Butter, 60 grams
- Truffles, 70 grams
- Fat and lean prosciutto, 30 grams
- A handful of dried mushrooms
- Giblets of 3 or 4 chickens and their gizzards
- Chicken crests, bean-like organs and unborn eggs, if available
- Nutmeg flavoring
- Short crust pastry dough
- Egg yolk for gilding

## Directions

1. Parboil the macaroni in salted water until half-cooked, drain well and pass them through sauce No. 4, then let them absorb the sauce over very low heat until fully cooked.
2. Meanwhile prepare a béchamel sauce at half the dose of recipe No. 137 and cook the giblets with butter, salt and a pinch of pepper, moistening with sauce.
3. Cut the giblets and sweetbreads into pieces the size of a small walnut; after cooking, add the prosciutto in small strips, the truffles in thin slices, the mushrooms (first softened in warm water) and some nutmeg, mixing everything together.
4. Prepare the short crust pastry using the full dose of recipe No. 589, recipe A, flavoring it with lemon zest.
5. Grease a copper pie dish well with butter; drain the macaroni of excess sauce and spread a first layer, seasoning it with grated Parmesan, small pieces of butter scattered here and there and a few spoonfuls of béchamel and giblets; repeat this operation until all ingredients are used, filling the dish.
6. Roll out a sheet of short crust pastry one scudo thick, first with a smooth roller, then with a ridged one, and cover the macaroni up to the rim; then roll out two strips two fingers wide and form a cross over the top to reinforce the covering; wrap the edges with a band as wide as the rim of the dish and make ornaments with any remaining pastry, not forgetting to garnish the top with a nice bow.
7. Brush the entire surface with egg yolk, place the pie in the oven, and in its absence cook it in a country oven; finally serve it hot to those eager to enjoy a good meal.
