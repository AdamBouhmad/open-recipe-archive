## Ingredients

- Milk, half a liter
- Semolina, 130 grams
- Sugar, 70 grams
- Butter, 15 grams
- Eggs, 2
- Salt, a pinch
- Lemon zest for flavoring
- Various fruit preserves

## Directions

1. Cook the semolina in the milk
2. Add the sugar and butter when it is boiling
3. Add the flavoring and salt when you remove it from the fire
4. Beat the eggs when it is still hot and mix very well
5. Prepare a pudding mold, smooth or worked, greased with butter and sprinkled with breadcrumbs
6. Pour the mixture into it little by little while cold, decorating it gradually with preserves in pieces or by spoonfuls according to whether they are liquid or solid; however, be warned that they should not touch the walls of the mold, as they would stick to them, and that there should not be too much of them, as they would become cloying
7. Serve it hot after cooking it on the hearth
8. The preserves that, to my taste, are most suited for this sweet are those of raspberry and quince; but those of apricots, currants, and peaches can also be used
9. For eight or ten persons, double the dose
