## Ingredients

- Beans
- One quarter onion
- One garlic clove
- A pinch of parsley
- A nice piece of white celery
- Oil
- Pepper
- Bean broth
- Tomato juice or paste
- Bread, sliced and toasted
- Cavolo nero (optional)

## Directions

1. Choose beans with fine skin or pass them; those with eyes have less of this defect than others.
2. Make a soffritto in this proportion: take a quarter onion, one garlic clove, a pinch of parsley and a nice piece of white celery. Chop these aromatics very finely with a crescent knife and put them on the fire with a good measure of oil; be generous with pepper.
3. When the soffritto has taken color, add two ladle-fuls of the bean broth, add a little tomato juice or paste, let them come to a boil and pour it into the pot of beans.
4. For those who like a little greens in the soup, you can add cavolo nero, first boiled and then cooked a bit in the liquid of the aforesaid soffritto.
5. Now all that remains is to dampen the bread, already prepared beforehand with thick slices toasted and then cut into cubes.
