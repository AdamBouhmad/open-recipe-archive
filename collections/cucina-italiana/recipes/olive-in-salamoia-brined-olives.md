## Ingredients

- Olives, 1 kilogramm
- Ash, 1 kilogramm
- Quicklime, 80 grams
- Salt, 80 grams
- Water for brine, 8 deciliters
- Wild fennel stems, several large pieces

## Directions

1. Mix the quicklime (in its powdered state after it has slaked and crumbled) with the ash, then add water to form a paste that is neither too thick nor too thin
2. Immerse the olives in this mixture, weighing them down so they remain completely covered, and leave them for twelve to fourteen hours until they become somewhat softened, checking them frequently by tasting
3. Remove the olives from the paste, wash them in plenty of water, and leave them in fresh water for four to five days until the water runs clear and the bitterness is lost, changing the water three times a day
4. Heat the eight deciliters of water with the salt and several pieces of large wild fennel stems, bring to a boil for a few minutes
5. Pour this brine, when cooled, over the olives in a glass jar or glazed earthenware vessel
