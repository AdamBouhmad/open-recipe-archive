## Ingredients

- powdered sugar, 200 grams
- very fine flour, 170 grams
- eggs, 6 whole
- white liqueur for coloring strips
- alkermes liqueur for coloring strips
- white liqueur infused with chocolate for coloring strips
- fruit preserves, liquid
- candied fruit slices
- powdered sugar for frosting
- egg white for frosting
- chocolate powder, 30 grams (for chocolate frosting)

## Directions

1. Make a sponge cake with the given amounts of sugar, flour, and eggs
2. To bake it in the oven, spread it to a depth of about half a finger in a rectangular pan, greased with butter and dusted with flour
3. Beat the egg yolks with sugar for about half an hour
4. Add the well-beaten egg whites and after mixing them gently, let the flour fall from a fine sifter
5. When removed from the oven, cut the hot sponge cake into strips about 2 centimeters wide and as long as the piece of sponge cake
6. To make these strips effective, they must be different colors; spray some with white liqueur and they will remain yellow; spray others with alkermes and they will appear red; make the last ones black with white liqueur in which chocolate has been infused
7. Arrange these colored fillets one on top of the other, alternating them, in the middle of the remaining whole piece of sponge cake, whose surface you have previously spread with liquid fruit preserves, also spreading the fillets so they stick together
8. Pull the edges of the sponge cake over them and form a tightly rolled log which, when sliced, will present a multicolored checkerboard filling
9. For a simpler family version, half the amount suffices even for a large pan
10. Spread the sponge cake with liqueur and fruit preserves, whether quince, apricot, or peach, spread thin slices of candied fruit on top and roll the entire piece like a sheet upon itself
11. To give it a nicer appearance, decorate the surface either with a pattern of sugar or with a chocolate crust as pastry chefs do
12. For a simpler method: mix powdered sugar with egg white, making it very stiff, and spread it uniformly over the dessert, or place it in a cone-shaped pastry bag and squeeze it to form a design
13. For a dark crust, take 60 grams of powdered sugar and 30 grams of chocolate powder, mix, combine with egg white, and spread the mixture on the dessert
14. If it does not dry naturally, place it under moderate heat
