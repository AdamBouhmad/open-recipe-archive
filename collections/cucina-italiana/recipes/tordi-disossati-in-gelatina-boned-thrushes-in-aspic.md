## Ingredients

- Lean veal, 100 grams
- Salted tongue, 40 grams
- Fat and lean prosciutto, 30 grams
- Black truffles, about 30 grams
- Egg yolk, 1
- Marsala, a small amount
- Aspic broth (Recipe No. 3)
- Cheesecloth for wrapping

## Directions

1. Set aside half of the tongue and one third of the prosciutto (more fat than lean), and mince and pound the veal together with the remaining tongue and prosciutto in a mortar with the truffle skin, softening the mixture with a drop of marsala.
2. Pass the mixture through a sieve and add one egg yolk.
3. Bone the thrushes as you would for stuffed chicken (Recipe No. 258), leaving the neck and head attached.
4. Fill them with the described mixture, to which you have already mixed the truffle, tongue, and prosciutto that were set aside, all cut into small cubes.
5. Sew them closed so that the thread can be removed when cooked.
6. Wrap each thrush in a piece of cheesecloth and boil for one hour in aspic broth (Recipe No. 3).
7. Serve as a cold dish on the same aspic, and if you form six molds shaped like nests with the aspic, it will appear that the thrush is sitting on top to brood.
