## Ingredients

- fennel
- salted water
- butter
- salt
- milk
- béchamel sauce
- parmesan cheese
- eggs

## Directions

1. Remove the hardest leaves from the fennel, cut them into small wedges and cook them two-thirds of the way in salted water, then drain them well and put them to brown with a piece of butter
2. Season with salt and when they have absorbed the butter, wet them with a little milk; when they have absorbed this too, add some béchamel
3. Remove from the fire and leave them as they are, or pass them through a sieve; when they are cold mix in grated parmesan cheese and, depending on the quantity, three or four beaten eggs
4. Pour the mixture into a smooth mold, or with a hole in the middle, proceeding as for the other molded dishes; cook it in a water bath and serve it hot as a course between other dishes or with a boiled capon
5. You can also garnish it with a delicate preparation of giblets and sweetbreads
