## Ingredients

- leftover boiled meat
- salt
- pepper
- parmesan cheese
- one or two eggs
- two or three tablespoons of bread paste
- bread marrow cooked in milk, broth, or water with a little butter
- lard or oil for frying
- butter
- two eggs
- salt
- half a lemon

## Directions

1. Remove the fat from the leftover meat and mince the lean part with a crescent-shaped knife.
2. Season it proportionally with salt, pepper, parmesan cheese, one or two eggs, and two or three tablespoons of bread paste made from bread marrow cooked in milk, broth, or water enriched with a little butter.
3. Mix everything together, form it into an oval loaf, and dust it with flour.
4. Fry it in lard or oil until it becomes firm and forms a crust on the surface.
5. Remove from the pan and sauté it in butter on both sides in a pan.
6. When ready to serve, bind it with two beaten eggs, a pinch of salt, and half a lemon.
7. Make this sauce separately in a small pot, treating it like a cream, and pour it over the meatloaf when it is placed on a serving dish.
8. If the meatloaf is large, turn it in the pan using a plate or copper lid as you would for an omelet.
