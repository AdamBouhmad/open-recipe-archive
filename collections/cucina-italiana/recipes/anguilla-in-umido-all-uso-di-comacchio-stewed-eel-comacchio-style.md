## Ingredients

- One kilogram of eels
- three onions
- celery
- carrot
- parsley
- lemon rind
- water
- salt
- pepper
- strong vinegar
- tomato preserve
- bread slices

## Directions

1. Cut all the vegetables except the lemon into large pieces and boil them with water, salt, and pepper.
2. Cut the eels into chunks, leaving the chunks connected to each other by a strip of flesh.
3. Take a suitable pot and make a layer of eel at the bottom, then layer the vegetables mentioned above that are almost cooked (discarding the lemon), then another layer of eel, another of vegetables, and so on until everything is used.
4. Cover everything with the water in which the vegetables boiled.
5. Put the well-sealed pot to boil slowly, shaking and turning it, but never stirring with a spoon as you would break everything up.
6. Surround the pot with ash and embers up to more than halfway, in front of a clear wood fire, always shaking and turning.
7. When the chunks, which were connected by a strip, become detached from each other, they are almost cooked.
8. Add a good spoonful of strong vinegar and tomato preserve, taste the broth to correct the salt and pepper (be generous), give it a few more boils, and serve the pot at table.
9. Serve in hot dishes on bread slices.
