## Ingredients

- Medium-sized ripe tomatoes
- Onion
- Parsley
- Celery
- Butter
- Dried mushrooms
- Milk
- Salt
- Pepper
- Parmesan
- Egg
- Garlic
- Breadcrumbs
- Oil

## Directions

1. Take medium-sized ripe tomatoes and cut them in half equally, remove the seeds, season with salt and pepper and fill the holes formed with the following mixture, so that it overflows and rises heaped on the surface of the tomato.
2. Make a battutino with onion, parsley and celery, put it on the fire with a piece of butter and when it takes color, pour in a handful of softened and finely chopped dried mushrooms; add a spoonful of pap with milk, season with salt and pepper, and boil somewhat, moistening the mixture with milk if needed.
3. Remove from fire and when lukewarm add grated Parmesan and an egg or just the yolk if sufficient to make the mixture not too liquid.
4. Cook the prepared tomatoes in a pan over two fires with a little butter and oil together and serve them as a side dish to any roast or grilled steak.
5. For serving alongside boiled meat they are excellent prepared as follows: take a wide pan or a baking sheet, scatter small pieces of butter on it and on top place, skin-side up, tomatoes cut in half after removing the seeds. Season with salt, pepper and a little oil, scatter more small pieces of butter on top and cook them in an uncovered pan.
