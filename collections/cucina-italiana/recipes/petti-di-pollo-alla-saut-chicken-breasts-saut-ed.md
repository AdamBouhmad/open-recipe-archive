## Ingredients

- chicken breasts
- salt
- pepper
- eggs, beaten
- fine breadcrumbs
- butter
- lemon juice
- optional: truffles or dried mushrooms, oil, grated Parmesan cheese, meat sauce or broth, tomato sauce or conserve

## Directions

1. Cut the breasts into slices as thin as possible, give them the best shape that will be possible and from the small pieces you get when cleaning the breastbone well, form a whole piece by joining them together and pressing them flat.
2. Season them with salt and pepper and put them to soak in beaten eggs.
3. After a few hours, pass them through fine breadcrumbs and cook them with butter in a sauté pan or in a baking dish.
4. If you like them plain, lemon juice is enough; if you want them with truffles, you can treat them as cutlets, or in the following manner:
5. Take a metal small pan, pour into it enough oil to just cover the bottom, spread a layer of truffle slices, sprinkling over it very little grated Parmesan and a pinch of breadcrumbs.
6. Repeat the same operation three or four times, according to the quantity, and finally season with oil, salt, pepper and a few small pieces of butter, all in small amounts so as not to be nauseating.
7. Put the pan on the fire and when it has come to a boil, water it with a ladle of meat sauce or broth and a little lemon juice.
8. Remove this sauce quickly from the fire and pour it over the breasts already browned in the aforementioned manner.
9. If you do not have truffles, use softened dried mushrooms chopped coarsely, and if lemon juice is lacking, have recourse to tomato sauce or conserve.
