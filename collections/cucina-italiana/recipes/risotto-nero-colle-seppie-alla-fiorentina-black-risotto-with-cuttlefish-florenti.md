## Ingredients

- Cuttlefish
- Beet greens, approximately 600 grams
- Rice, 600 grams
- Cuttlefish ink
- Onion, two small or one medium with two garlic cloves
- Oil, fine and abundant
- Salt
- Pepper
- Water
- Parmesan cheese
- Butter
- Peas, optional (as mentioned in the risotto with tench)
- Tomato juice or tomato preserve, alternative version
- Rice, 600 grams

## Directions

1. Peel and clean the cuttlefish to remove useless parts which are the bone, the mouth apparatus, the eyes and the digestive tube; set aside the ink sac, and after washing them well, cut them into small squares and the tails into pieces.
2. Finely chop two small onions, or better one onion and two cloves of garlic, and put them on the fire in a pot with fine oil and in abundance.
3. When the soffritto has taken on a reddish color, throw in the cuttlefish and wait until these, boiling, begin to turn yellow, then throw in approximately 600 grams of beet greens, cleaned of the thickest ribs and chopped somewhat.
4. Mix and let boil for about half an hour; then pour in 600 grams of rice (which will be the weight of the cuttlefish in nature) and their ink and, when the rice has been well impregnated with that sauce, cook it with hot water.
5. The rice, by general rule, should be barely cooked, and when it is said 'dry' it should form a mound on the plate in which you serve it.
6. Always accompany it with grated parmesan; but if you have a delicate stomach abstain from its use when cooked with these and similar ingredients of difficult digestion.
7. Alternative method: No beet greens, no ink, and when the cuttlefish, as stated, begin to take on yellow, pour the rice and cook it with hot water and tomato juice or preserve, giving it more grace and flavor with a piece of butter; when almost cooked add parmesan.
8. For an even better result, add peas at two-thirds of cooking time, as mentioned in the risotto with tench.
