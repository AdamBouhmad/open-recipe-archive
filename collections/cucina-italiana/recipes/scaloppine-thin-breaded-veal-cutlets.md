## Ingredients

- lean meat from beef
- salt
- pepper
- grated Parmesan cheese
- spices
- breadcrumbs
- butter
- tomato juice or tomato preserve
- broth or water

## Directions

1. Take lean beef meat, clean it of tendons and skin, and mince it very finely with a knife and then with a crescent-shaped tool if you do not have a meat grinder.
2. Season the meat with salt, pepper, and grated Parmesan cheese; add the aroma of spices if you like, but be careful it does not taste like a meatball.
3. Mix well and shape the meat into a ball; then with breadcrumbs underneath and on top so it does not stick, roll it out with a rolling pin on a work surface, moving it often to make it a thin flattened shape slightly thicker than a coin.
4. Cut it into square pieces as wide as the palm of a hand and cook them in a pan with butter.
5. When the scaloppine have taken on color, water them with tomato juice or preserve diluted in broth or water and serve them.
6. You can also, without using a rolling pin, flatten them with your hands and give them, for more elegance, the shape of a heart.
7. If you have leftover overcooked meat, it is convenient to make them with this and with raw meat mixed together.
