## Ingredients

- Porcini mushrooms (morecci), the smallest ones possible
- White vinegar
- Water, if needed to dilute strong vinegar
- Oil
- Optional aromatics: garlic cloves, cloves, or bay leaf

## Directions

1. Choose the smallest porcini mushrooms you can find; if there are large ones the size of walnuts, cut them in half
2. Clean them well from dirt and wash them
3. Boil them for twenty-five minutes in white vinegar; if the vinegar is too strong, correct it with a little water
4. Remove from heat and dry them well in a cloth
5. Leave them in the air until the next day
6. Place them in a glass jar or glazed earthenware vessel, cover with oil, and add whichever aroma you prefer: garlic cloves, cloves, or bay leaf (which can be boiled in the vinegar)
7. Serve with boiled meats
