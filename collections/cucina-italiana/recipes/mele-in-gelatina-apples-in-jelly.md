## Ingredients

- Reinette apples or other fine quality, not too ripe, 650-700 grams
- Sugar, 120 grams
- Water, half a litre
- Kirsch, one spoonful
- Lemon, half
- Currant jelly (recipe 739)

## Directions

1. Remove the cores from the apples using a tin tube, peel them and immediately place them in fresh water where half a lemon has been squeezed
2. Dissolve the sugar in half a litre of water on the fire, united with a spoonful of kirsch, and pour it over the apples that you will have placed all together in a saucepan
3. Cook them so that they remain whole, then remove them dry and place them in a fruit dish
4. When cooled, fill and top the holes left by the cores with Currant Jelly (recipe 739), which being red stands out against the white of the apples and makes them beautiful
5. Reduce the remaining liquid in the saucepan over the fire to make a syrup, then pass it through a damp cloth, add another spoonful of kirsch and pour it around the prepared apples, which you will serve cooled
6. If you do not have kirsch, use rosolio and if you lack currant jelly, substitute with preserves
