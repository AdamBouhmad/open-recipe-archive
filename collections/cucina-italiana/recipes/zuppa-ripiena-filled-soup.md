## Ingredients

- Half breast of capon or large chicken
- A slice of fat and lean ham
- A small piece of marrow
- Grated Parmesan cheese
- Nutmeg
- One egg
- Stale bread, a small loaf
- Virgin lard or oil or butter for frying

## Directions

1. Mince together the half breast of capon or chicken, the slice of ham, and the piece of marrow.
2. Season it with grated Parmesan, give it the scent of nutmeg and bind it with an egg. Salt is not necessary as there is ham.
3. Take a small loaf of stale bread, slice it round to the thickness of half a finger, remove the crust from the slices and on half of them spread the aforesaid mixture.
4. On each of these spread slices, lay a slice without mixture and press them together so they stick.
5. Then cut these paired slices into small dice and fry them in virgin lard or oil or butter, according to the taste of the region or your own.
6. When it is time to serve the soup at table, place the fried dice in the tureen and pour boiling broth over them.
