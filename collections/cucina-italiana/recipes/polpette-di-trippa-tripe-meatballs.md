## Ingredients

- Boiled tripe, 350 grams
- Ham, leaner rather than fatty, 100 grams
- Grated parmesan cheese, 30 grams
- Beef marrow, 20 grams
- Eggs, 2
- A good pinch of parsley
- Spice or nutmeg flavoring
- Non-liquid breadcrumb mixture made of bread soaked in broth or milk, two spoonfuls
- Oil or lard for frying
- Onion, just under one quarter of a medium-sized onion
- Butter, 60 grams
- Tomato juice or tomato conserve dissolved in broth
- Parmesan cheese

## Directions

1. Mince the tripe as finely as possible with the chopping tool
2. Do the same with the ham, marrow and parsley, add the eggs, the rest, a little salt and mix
3. Form 12 or 13 meatballs from this mixture, which will be enough for four people, coat well with flour and fry them in oil or lard
4. Make a small soffritto with the onion and place it in a proportioned pan with 60 grams of butter
5. When colored, place the meatballs in it, moisten them after a short time with tomato juice or conserve dissolved in broth, cover and let them boil gently for about ten minutes, turning them
6. Then serve them with some of their sauce and dust with parmesan
7. The author adds raisins and pine nuts to the mixture, but this is optional
