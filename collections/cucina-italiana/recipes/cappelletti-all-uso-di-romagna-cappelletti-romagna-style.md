## Ingredients

- Ricotta, or half ricotta and half cacio raviggiolo, 180 grams
- Half breast of capon cooked in butter, seasoned with salt and pepper, and finely minced with a crescent-shaped knife
- Grated Parmesan cheese, 30 grams
- Eggs, one whole and one yolk
- Nutmeg flavor, few spices, lemon zest for those who like it
- A pinch of salt
- Flour for the pasta sheet, made with eggs only

## Directions

1. Taste the mixture so you can correct it if needed, as the ingredients do not always correspond in the same way. If capon breast is lacking, substitute with 100 grams of lean pork from the loin, cooked and seasoned in the same manner.
2. If the ricotta or raviggiolo are too soft, leave out the egg white or add another yolk if the mixture seems too firm.
3. To close them, make a rather tender pasta sheet of flour with eggs only, using also any remaining egg white, and cut it with a round disk the size of that shown on this page.
4. Place the mixture in the middle of the disks and fold them in two forming a half-moon; then take the two ends of the same, join them together and you will have the completed cappelletto.
5. If the pasta sheet dries out in your hand, dampen with a finger dipped in water the edges of the disks.
6. Cook the cappelletti in capon broth as is customary in Romagna.
7. A discreet eater is satisfied with two dozen.
