## Ingredients

- Two fillets of hare, weighing altogether 180 to 200 grams, including the kidneys
- Butter, 50 grams
- Cured meat (carnesecca), 40 grams
- Half an onion of medium size
- Half a carrot
- A piece of celery one palm long
- Scent of nutmeg
- Parmesan, as needed
- One spoonful of flour
- Meat broth, 6 decilitres
- Pappardelle or strips of pasta
- Salt and pepper

## Directions

1. Peel the fillets of the membrane that wraps them and cut them into small dice
2. Make a battuto with the cured meat, onion, celery and carrot
3. Chop it very finely with the crescent blade and put it on the fire with one third of the said butter and with the hare meat, seasoning it with salt and pepper
4. When the meat is browned, sprinkle flour over it and shortly after wet it and finish cooking it with the said broth
5. Before using this sauce, add the rest of the butter and the nutmeg
6. Cook the pappardelle or strips in salted water, lift them out well dried and dress them on the platter, without putting them back on the fire, with parmesan and the aforesaid sauce
7. In the absence of fillets, use the hind legs
