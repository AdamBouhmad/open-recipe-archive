## Ingredients

- 700 grams of fish
- half an onion, finely chopped
- oil
- parsley
- two cloves of garlic, whole
- 300 grams of tomatoes, in pieces, or tomato paste
- salt
- pepper
- vinegar
- water

## Directions

1. Finely chop half an onion and put it to fry gently with oil, parsley and two whole cloves of garlic.
2. As soon as the onion has taken on color, add 300 grams of tomatoes in pieces, or tomato paste, and season with salt and pepper.
3. Once the tomatoes are cooked, pour over them one finger of vinegar if it is strong, or two if it is weak, diluted in a good glass of water.
4. Let it boil for a few more minutes, then remove the garlic and pass the rest through a sieve, pressing well.
5. Put the strained liquid back on the fire, together with the fish you have ready, such as sole, red mullet, scorpionfish, dogfish, gobies, mantis shrimp, and other seasonal varieties, leaving small fish whole and cutting large ones into pieces.
6. Taste to see if the seasoning is right; but in any case it will not hurt to add a little oil, keeping rather sparing with the initial frying.
7. When the fish is cooked and the cacciucco is made, it is customary to bring it to the table in two separate serving dishes; in one the fish alone, in the other as many slices of bread, thick as a finger, as the remaining broth can soak, but first dry them by the fire without toasting them.
