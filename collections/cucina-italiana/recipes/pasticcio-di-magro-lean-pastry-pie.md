## Ingredients

- fish weighing 300 to 350 grams
- rice 200 grams
- fresh mushrooms 150 grams
- green peas 300 grams
- toasted pine nuts 50 grams
- butter as needed
- Parmesan cheese as needed
- artichokes 6 pieces
- eggs 2
- onion
- celery
- carrot
- parsley
- tomato juice or tomato paste
- salt
- pepper
- flour 400 grams
- white wine or Marsala two spoonfuls

## Directions

1. Cook the rice with 40 grams of butter and a quarter of chopped onion, salt it, and when cooked with the necessary water bind it with the said eggs and 30 grams of Parmesan. Make a soffritto with onion, butter, celery, carrot and parsley and in it cook the mushrooms cut into slices, the peas, and the artichokes cut into wedges and half-cooked. Finish cooking these things with a few spoonfuls of hot water and season them with salt, pepper and 50 grams of grated Parmesan when you have removed them from the fire. Cook the fish, which can be mullet, a type of fish, or even cut fish, in a soffritto of oil, garlic, parsley, tomato juice or tomato paste and season it with salt and pepper. Remove the fish, pass its sauce and in this dissolve the pine nuts that you have first toasted and crushed. Remove from the fish the head, backbone and small bones, cut it into pieces, return it to its sauce and add everything except the rice. Now that the elements of the pie are all ready, make the pastry to enclose it with the following amounts: flour 400 grams, butter 80 grams, eggs 2, white wine or Marsala two spoonfuls, a pinch of salt. Take any mold, grease it with butter and line it with the said pastry rolled thin; then fill it by first pouring in half the rice, then all the filling and on top of the filling the rest of the rice, covering it at the mouth with the same pastry. Cook it in the oven, unmold it and serve it lukewarm or cold. Made in the indicated amounts it will be enough for twelve people.
