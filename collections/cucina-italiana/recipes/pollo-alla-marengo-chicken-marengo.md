## Ingredients

- one young chicken, excluding the neck and legs
- 30 grams of butter
- one spoonful of oil
- salt
- pepper
- a pinch of nutmeg
- one level spoonful of flour
- one decilitre of white wine
- broth
- parsley, finely chopped
- half a lemon

## Directions

1. Take a young chicken and, excluding the neck and legs, cut it into large pieces at the joints.
2. Sauté it with 30 grams of butter, one spoonful of oil and season it with salt, pepper and a pinch of nutmeg.
3. Once the pieces are browned on both sides, drain off the fat and throw into the sauté pan one level spoonful of flour and one decilitre of white wine.
4. Add broth to cook the chicken through, covered, and over low heat.
5. Before removing it from the fire, garnish it with a pinch of chopped parsley and when it is on the platter squeeze half a lemon over it. It turns out to be an appetizing dish.
