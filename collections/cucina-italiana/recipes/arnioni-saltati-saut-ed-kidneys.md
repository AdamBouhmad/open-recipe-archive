## Ingredients

- one large animal kidney (or several from smaller animals)
- salt
- hot water
- flour
- butter
- white wine
- parsley
- broth

## Directions

1. Open the kidney and remove all the fat as it has an unpleasant odor.
2. Cut it crosswise into thin slices, place in a bowl, salt it and pour over it enough boiling water to cover.
3. When the water has cooled, remove it dry and place in a pan to evaporate the water, which you will discard.
4. Sprinkle a pinch of flour over it, add a small piece of butter and, stirring often, let it grill for only five minutes.
5. Season with salt, pepper and a scant half glass of white wine, leave it briefly longer on the heat and when about to remove it add another small piece of butter, a pinch of chopped parsley and a bit of broth if needed.
6. Note: kidneys held too long on the heat become tough. It is good to boil the wine separately first until reduced by a third; if instead of white wine you use marsala or champagne, so much the better.
