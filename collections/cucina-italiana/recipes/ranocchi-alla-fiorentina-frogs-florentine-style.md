## Ingredients

- frogs
- flour
- oil
- salt
- pepper
- eggs
- lemon juice

## Directions

1. Remove the frogs from fresh water where you have placed them after having held them for just a moment in hot water if they have just been killed. Dry them well between the folds of a cloth and coat them in flour. Put a pan on the fire with good oil and when it begins to sizzle throw in the frogs; season them with salt and pepper stirring often because they stick easily. When they are browned on both sides, pour over them beaten eggs, seasoned also with salt and pepper and lemon juice if you like; without touching them, let them set like an omelette and send the pan to table. The gall bladder should always be removed from the frogs. If you want them fried, coat them in flour and, before throwing them in the pan, keep them for a few hours in infusion in egg seasoned with salt and pepper; or, after coating in flour, brown them lightly on both sides and, taking one at a time, immerse them in egg seasoned with pepper, salt and lemon juice, putting them back in the pan to finish cooking them.
