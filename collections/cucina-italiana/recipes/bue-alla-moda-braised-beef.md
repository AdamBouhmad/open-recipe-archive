## Ingredients

- Not less than 1 kilogram of lean meat from the hip or rump of beef
- Thick lardoon strips, wrapped in salt and pepper
- Salt
- 50 grams butter
- Half a calf's foot or a piece of a large calf's foot
- 1 large whole onion
- 2 or 3 whole carrots
- 1 bunch tied of aromatic herbs such as parsley, celery, basil and the like
- Lardoon rinds
- 1 bold glass of water or better 1 glass of degreased broth
- Half a glass of white wine or 2 tablespoons of brandy

## Directions

1. Take not less than 1 kilogram of lean meat from the hip or rump of beef and lard it with thick lardoon strips wrapped in salt and pepper
2. Tie the piece of meat so it takes a nice shape, salt it sufficiently
3. Place it in a braising pan with 50 grams of butter to brown it
4. Add the following ingredients: half a calf's foot or a piece of a large calf's foot, 1 large whole onion, 2 or 3 whole carrots, 1 bunch tied of aromatic herbs such as parsley, celery, basil and the like, some lardoon rinds, 1 glass of water or better 1 glass of degreased broth, and finally half a glass of white wine or 2 tablespoons of brandy
5. Put the well-covered pan on the fire and simmer slowly until the meat is cooked
6. Remove the carrots early so they remain whole
7. Discard the aromatic bunch, strain the sauce and degrease if necessary
8. Serve the meat not overcooked, surrounded by the carrots cut into round slices
