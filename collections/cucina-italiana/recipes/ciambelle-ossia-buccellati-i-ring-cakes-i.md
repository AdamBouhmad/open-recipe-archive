## Ingredients

- Finest flour, 1,700 kilograms
- Sugar, 300 grams
- Yeast, 200 grams
- Butter, 150 grams
- Lard, 50 grams
- Milk, 4 deciliters
- Marsala, 2 deciliters
- Rum, two tablespoons
- Eggs, 6
- Baking soda, one teaspoon
- A pinch of salt
- Lemon zest flavoring

## Directions

1. Dissolve the yeast in a bowl with half the milk, adding enough flour to make a bread of proper consistency
2. After forming it, let it sit surrounded by flour to a depth of more than a finger
3. Place the bowl in a warm place, protected from air, and when that bread is well risen (which will take eight to ten hours depending on the season), break it down and remake it larger with the rest of the milk and flour needed
4. Wait for it to rise again and be well puffed up (which will take about the same time), then pour it onto the work surface and knead it with the rest of the flour and all the ingredients mentioned
5. Work it very well and with force so that the dough refines and becomes completely homogeneous
6. Prepare iron baking sheets or tinned copper pans, greased with lard and floured, and in them place the ring cakes which you will make as large as you like, but so that they have plenty of room
7. Let them rise in the kitchen or in another place of warm temperature, and when they are well puffed up but not over-risen, make long incisions on the surface with the point of a knife
8. Brush them with egg and sprinkle coarsely crushed crystal sugar over them
9. Bake them in the oven at moderate heat
10. In winter it is advisable to knead the yeast with lukewarm milk and send the ring cakes to rise in a warm place. With half the dose you can obtain four beautiful ring cakes of about 350 grams each, if you do not want to make them smaller
