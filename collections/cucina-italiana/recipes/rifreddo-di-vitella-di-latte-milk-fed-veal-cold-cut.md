## Ingredients

- 1 boneless, all lean braciole of milk-fed veal, weighing about 400 grams
- 120 grams additional lean veal
- 50 grams of thick slice of cooked ham, fat and lean
- 20 grams additional cooked ham
- 50 grams of mortadella slice
- 30 grams grated Parmesan
- 20 grams butter
- 1 raw chicken breast
- 1 egg
- salt
- pepper
- 30 grams butter for cooking

## Directions

1. Dampen the braciole with water and beat it with a meat mallet to reduce it to about one centimeter thickness.
2. Mince with a crescent knife the 120 grams of lean meat together with the 20 grams of ham, then pound them in the mortar adding the Parmesan, butter, egg, little salt and little pepper to make from these ingredients the compound to keep together the filling you will form as follows.
3. Cut into strips more than one centimeter thick the chicken breast and the two slices of mortadella and ham.
4. Spread the compound on one part of the braciole and on top of it place one third of the fillets, intercalating them, then spread more on top and thus for two more times.
5. Roll up the braciole with the filling inside and tie it like a salami to put it on the fire with 30 grams of butter, salt and pepper in scant measure.
6. When it has taken color, drain off the fat, which can be used for some other dish, and continue cooking for about three hours with broth added little by little.
