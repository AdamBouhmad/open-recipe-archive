## Ingredients

- One beef cutlet, the thickness of a finger, weighing about 500 grams
- Lean milk veal, 200 grams
- Fat and lean prosciutto, 30 grams
- Salt tongue, 30 grams
- Grated Parmesan cheese, 30 grams
- Butter, 30 grams
- Chicken livers, 2
- Eggs, 1
- One ball of fresh bread crumb about the size of a fist

## Directions

1. Make a fine mixture with onion the size of a walnut, a little celery, carrot and parsley; put it on the fire with the said butter and, once browned, throw in the milk veal in pieces and the livers, a little salt and pepper for seasoning, cooking the meat with a little broth.
2. Remove it dry to mince it finely with the mezzaluna and in the remaining liquid make a thick paste with the bread crumb, moistening it with broth if needed.
3. Now make a single mixture with the minced meat, the paste, the egg, the Parmesan, the prosciutto and the tongue cut into dice.
4. Once the filling is composed this way, just dip the beef cutlet in water to be able to stretch it better, beat it with the back of the knife blade and flatten it with the blade.
5. Place the filling in the middle and form a roll that you will tie tightly like a salami first from the long side and then crosswise.
6. Thread it onto the spit along its length and roast it with oil and salt.
7. You will have a delicate roast which can serve six or seven people.
