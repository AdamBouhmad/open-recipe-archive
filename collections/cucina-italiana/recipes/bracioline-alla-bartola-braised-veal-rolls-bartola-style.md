## Ingredients

- veal or beef meat (fillet, round, rump, or leg), net weight without bone, 500 grams
- fatty and lean prosciutto, 50 grams
- a small clove of garlic
- a small piece of onion
- one rib of celery, about one hand's length
- a good piece of carrot
- a pinch of parsley
- oil
- salt
- pepper
- clove buds
- tomato juice or preserve
- water
- large potato

## Directions

1. Cut the meat into thick slices, almost a finger thick, to obtain no more than seven or eight braised rolls to which you endeavor to give a nice shape, and beat them with the back of a knife.
2. Make a mixture minced very finely with the prosciutto and the ingredients described above, then pour six tablespoons of oil in a sauté pan or copper pan on which, when cold, you will place the braised rolls spreading on each one a pinch of the said mixture.
3. Season them with little salt, pepper and only the flower of four or five cloves, and over high heat brown them from underneath, then turn them one by one with its mixture to brown this as well, and when it has softened sufficiently turn them back over so the mixture returns to the top picking up what has remained stuck to the pan, then bathe them with tomato juice or preserve dissolved in water, cover them and let them boil slowly for almost two hours; but half an hour before sending them to the table cook in the sauce of the same a large peeled potato and cut into ten or twelve pieces placing them in the spaces between one braised roll and the other.
4. It would be better to send them to the table in the vessel where they have been cooked; but if this is unseemly place them as they are in a serving dish with the potatoes around them.
5. This quantity is enough for four or five people, and it is not a dish to be despised, because it does not leave the stomach burdened.
