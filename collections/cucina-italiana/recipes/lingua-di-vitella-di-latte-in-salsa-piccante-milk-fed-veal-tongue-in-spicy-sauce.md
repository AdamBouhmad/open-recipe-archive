## Ingredients

- 1 whole milk-fed veal tongue
- salt for cooking water
- celery
- carrot
- oil
- 2 salted anchovies
- 50 grams capers
- parsley
- 1 piece of bread crumb about the size of an egg
- vinegar
- onion about the size of a hazelnut
- less than half a clove of garlic
- lemon juice from half a lemon
- pepper

## Directions

1. Boil the whole veal tongue in salted water, which will take about two hours.
2. Make a fine battutino (minced mixture) of celery and carrot, bring it to boil with a good measure of oil for five minutes and set aside.
3. Make another battuto with two salted anchovies that have been washed and cleaned of their bones, 50 grams of capers squeezed of vinegar, a good pinch of parsley, a piece of bread crumb about the size of an egg barely dampened in vinegar, onion about the size of a hazelnut, less than half a clove of garlic.
4. When everything is well chopped, work it with a knife blade and a drop of oil to make it united and pasty, then mix it with the previous celery and carrot battuto.
5. To make it liquid, add more oil and the juice of half a lemon, season with pepper and salt if needed. This is the sauce.
6. While the tongue is still hot, peel it, discard the papilla with its small bones, and cut the rest of the tongue into thin slices.
7. Cover the slices with the described sauce and serve it cold.
