## Ingredients

- 10 slices of cured tongue from the thickest part, totaling about 130 grams
- 100 grams of cooked ham, fat and lean
- 70 grams of butter
- 20 grams of white fragrant truffle
- gelatin (recipe No. 3)

## Directions

1. Have your charcutier cut ten thin slices of cured tongue from the thickest part, totaling about 130 grams in weight.
2. Also have cut into thin slices 100 grams of cooked ham, both fat and lean portions.
3. Trim the edges of the tongue slices all around to give them an elegant shape and set the trimmings aside.
4. From the ham, cut out ten slices the same size as those of tongue, and put the trimmings of both the ham and tongue into the mortar with 70 grams of butter and 20 grams of a white fragrant truffle.
5. Pound these things together to reduce them fine as an ointment, which you will use to spread on one side of the tongue slices, and stick the ham slices on top.
6. Now that you have composed these ten pieces, you can take as long as you need to coat them with gelatin (recipe No. 3).
7. There are two ways to decorate these pieces with the gelatin. The first consists in taking a wide plate or pan, pouring a light layer of melted gelatin, and when it begins to set, placing the pieces on it and covering them with another layer of melted gelatin, then removing them one by one after it has set.
8. The second way would be to place the pieces upright in a mold at some distance from each other after pouring a light layer of melted gelatin on the bottom, cover them all with the same gelatin, then unmold and send them to the table all in one piece, which will make a finer appearance.
9. For a meal of several courses this dose could serve ten people, but to be safe it is better not to serve it to more than eight.
