## Ingredients

- Water, 150 grams
- Fine Hungarian or finest flour, 100 grams
- Butter, size of a walnut
- Salt, a pinch
- Lemon zest
- Eggs, 2 whole and 1 yolk

## Directions

1. Put water, butter, and salt on the fire
2. When it boils, add all the flour at once and stir vigorously
3. Keep the paste on the fire until the flour is well cooked (10 minutes), stirring constantly
4. Remove from the pot and flatten to the thickness of a finger so it cools well
5. Begin working it early with an egg yolk; when it is incorporated, add a well-beaten egg white
6. Add another yolk and, working always with a spoon, another beaten egg white, and so on if the dose were double or triple the present one
7. By dint of working it, the mixture must turn out fine as an ointment in the end
8. If making puffs, drop by spoonfuls into a pan giving them round shape
9. If desiring piped paste, pass it through a piping bag with a stellate disc and cut to a length of 9 to 10 centimeters
10. When it has lost its first heat, dust with powdered sugar
11. This dose will serve eight or ten people when doubled or tripled
12. These puffs can also serve as a composed fried dish by making a small incision when cooked to introduce inside a little delicate minced meat; but then do not dust with sugar
