## Ingredients

- Rice, 500 grams
- Tench, approximately 400 grams
- Garlic, two cloves
- Parsley, a pinch
- Basil leaves, if you like its smell
- Carrot, one large
- White celery, two pieces one palm long
- Oil
- Salt
- Pepper
- Tomato juice or tomato preserve
- Water
- Dried mushrooms, optional
- Butter
- Parmesan cheese
- Peas, 200 grams, optional
- Spring onion, one whole, optional

## Directions

1. Make a battuto with two cloves of garlic, a pinch of parsley, some basil leaves if you like its smell, one large carrot and two pieces of white celery one palm long.
2. Put it on the fire in a pot with oil, salt and pepper, adding at the same time the tench already gutted and cut into pieces, including the heads.
3. Turn them often so they do not stick to the bottom, and when they are well browned begin to moisten them first with tomato juice or preserve, then with water poured little by little at first and finally, in quantity such as to cook the rice, but keeping it rather scarce than abundant.
4. Boil until the tench are completely falling apart, then pass everything through a strainer, so that nothing remains but the bones and small bones.
5. This is the sauce that will serve to cook the rice, pulling it dry and of proper cooking.
6. To grace it you can add some pieces of dried mushrooms and a piece of butter and then serve it at table with grated parmesan for those who want it.
7. If using peas instead of mushrooms: cook 200 grams of shelled peas separately with a little oil, a little butter and one whole spring onion. Add the peas when the onion begins to brown, let them fry a little, season with salt and pepper and cook them with little water. Discard the onion and mix the peas with the rice when it is almost cooked.
