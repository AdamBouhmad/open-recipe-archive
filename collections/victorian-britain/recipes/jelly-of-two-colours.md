## Ingredients

- 1-1/2 pint of calf's-feet jelly
- a few drops of prepared cochineal

## Directions

1. Make 1-1/2 pint of jelly by recipe No. 1416, or, if wished more economical, of clarified syrup and gelatine, flavouring it in any way that may be preferred.
2. Colour one-half of the jelly with a few drops of prepared cochineal, and the other half leave as pale as possible.
3. Have ready a mould well wetted in every part; pour in a small quantity of the red jelly, and let this set.
4. When quite firm, pour on it the same quantity of the pale jelly, and let this set.
5. Proceed in this manner until the mould is full, always taking care to let one jelly set before the other is poured in, or the colours would run one into the other.
6. When turned out, the jelly should have a striped appearance.
7. For variety, half the mould may be filled at once with one of the jellies, and, when firm, filled up with the other.
