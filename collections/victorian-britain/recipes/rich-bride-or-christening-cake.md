## Ingredients

- 5 lbs. of the finest flour
- 3 lbs. of fresh butter
- 5 lbs. of currants
- 2 lbs. of sifted loaf sugar
- 2 nutmegs
- 1/4 oz. of mace
- half 1/4 oz. of cloves
- 16 eggs
- 1 lb. of sweet almonds
- 1/2 lb. of candied citron
- 1/2 lb. each of candied orange and lemon peel
- 1 gill of wine
- 1 gill of brandy

## Directions

1. Let the flour be as fine as possible, and well dried and sifted
2. Have the currants washed, picked, and dried before the fire
3. Have the sugar well pounded and sifted
4. Have the nutmegs grated, the spices pounded
5. Have the eggs thoroughly whisked, whites and yolks separately
6. Have the almonds pounded with a little orange-flower water, and the candied peel cut in neat slices
7. Begin working the butter with the hand till it becomes of a cream-like consistency
8. Stir in the sugar
9. When the whites of the eggs are whisked to a solid froth, mix them with the butter and sugar
10. Well beat up the yolks for 10 minutes, and, adding them to the flour, nutmegs, mace, and cloves, continue beating the whole together for 1/2 hour or longer, till wanted for the oven
11. Mix in lightly the currants, almonds, and candied peel with the wine and brandy
12. Having lined a hoop with buttered paper, fill it with the mixture, and bake the cake in a tolerably quick oven, taking care, however, not to burn it
13. Cover the top of it with a sheet of paper to prevent burning
14. To ascertain whether the cake is done, plunge a clean knife into the middle of it, withdraw it directly, and if the blade is not sticky, and looks bright, the cake is sufficiently baked
15. Spread the cake with a thick layer of almond icing, and over that another layer of sugar icing, and afterwards ornament it
