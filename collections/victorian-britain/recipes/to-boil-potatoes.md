## Ingredients

- 10 or 12 potatoes
- To each 1/2 gallon of water allow 1 heaped tablespoonful of salt

## Directions

1. Choose potatoes of an equal size and pare them
2. Take out all the eyes and specks, and as they are peeled, throw them into cold water
3. Put them into a saucepan with sufficient cold water to cover them, with salt in the above proportion
4. Let them boil gently until tender
5. Ascertain when they are done by thrusting a fork in them, and take them up the moment they feel soft through
6. Drain away the water
7. Put the saucepan by the side of the fire with the lid partially uncovered to allow the steam to escape
8. Let the potatoes get thoroughly dry and do not allow them to get burnt
9. Send them to table quickly and very hot, with an opening in the cover of the dish
