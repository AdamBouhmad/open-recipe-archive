## Ingredients

- To each lb. of pumpkin allow 1 lb. of roughly pounded loaf sugar
- 1 gill of lemon-juice
- 1/4 pint of water to every 3 lbs. of sugar used

## Directions

1. Obtain a good sweet pumpkin; halve it, take out the seeds, and pare off the rind
2. Cut it into neat slices, or into pieces about the size of a five-shilling piece
3. Weigh the pumpkin, put the slices in a pan or deep dish in layers, with the sugar sprinkled between them
4. Pour the lemon-juice over the top, and let the whole remain for 2 or 3 days
5. Boil altogether, adding 1/4 pint of water to every 3 lbs. of sugar used until the pumpkin becomes tender
6. Turn the whole into a pan, where let it remain for a week
7. Drain off the syrup, boil it until it is quite thick; skim, and pour it, boiling, over the pumpkin
