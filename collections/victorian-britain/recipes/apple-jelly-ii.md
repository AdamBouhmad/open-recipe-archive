## Ingredients

- apples
- water
- 3/4 lb. of loaf sugar per pint of syrup

## Directions

1. Pare and cut the apples into pieces, remove the cores, and put them in a preserving-pan with sufficient cold water to cover them.
2. Let them boil for an hour; then drain the syrup from them through a hair sieve or jelly-bag, and measure the juice.
3. To every pint allow 3/4 lb. of loaf sugar, and boil these together for 3/4 hour, removing every particle of scum as it rises, and keeping the jelly well stirred, that it may not burn.
4. A little lemon-rind may be boiled with the apples, and a small quantity of strained lemon-juice may be put in the jelly just before it is done, when the flavour is liked.
5. Put it into small pots for storage.
