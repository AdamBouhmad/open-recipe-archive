## Ingredients

- Bones and trimmings of the cooked joint intended for hashing
- 1/4 teaspoonful of salt
- 1/4 teaspoonful of whole pepper
- 1/4 teaspoonful of whole allspice
- a small faggot of savoury herbs
- 1/2 head of celery
- 1 onion
- 1 oz. of butter
- thickening
- sufficient boiling water to cover the bones

## Directions

1. Chop the bones in small pieces, and put them in a stewpan, with the trimmings, salt, pepper, spice, herbs, and celery
2. Cover with boiling water, and let the whole simmer gently for 1-1/2 or 2 hours
3. Slice and fry the onion in the butter till it is of a pale brown, and mix it gradually with the gravy made from the bones
4. Boil for 1/4 hour, and strain into a basin
5. Put it back into the stewpan
6. Flavour with walnut pickle or ketchup, pickled-onion liquor, or any store sauce that may be preferred
7. Thicken with a little butter and flour, kneaded together on a plate
8. The gravy should just boil, to take off the rawness of the flour
