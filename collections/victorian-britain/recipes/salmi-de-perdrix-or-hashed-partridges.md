## Ingredients

- 3 young partridges
- 3 shalots
- a slice of lean ham
- 1 carrot
- 3 or 4 mushrooms
- a bunch of savoury herbs
- 2 cloves
- 6 whole peppers
- 3/4 pint of stock
- 1 glass of sherry or Madeira
- a small lump of sugar
- butter

## Directions

1. After the partridges are plucked and drawn, roast them rather underdone, and cover them with paper, as they should not be browned.
2. Cut them into joints, take off the skin from the wings, legs, and breasts; put these into a stewpan, cover them up, and set by until the gravy is ready.
3. Cut a slice of ham into small pieces, and put them, with the carrots sliced, the shalots, mushrooms, herbs, cloves, and pepper, into a stewpan.
4. Fry them lightly in a little butter, pour in the stock, add the bones and trimming from the partridges, and simmer for 1/4 hour.
5. Strain the gravy, let it cool, and skim off every particle of fat.
6. Put it to the legs, wings, and breasts, add a glass of sherry or Madeira and a small lump of sugar.
7. Let all gradually warm through by the side of the fire, and when on the point of boiling, serve, and garnish the dish with croûtons.
