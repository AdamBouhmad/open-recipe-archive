## Ingredients

- 4 lbs. of cherries
- 3 lbs. of sugar
- 1 pint of white-currant juice

## Directions

1. Let the cherries be as clear and as transparent as possible, and perfectly ripe
2. Pick off the stalks, and remove the stones, damaging the fruit as little as you can
3. Make a syrup with the sugar by the proportions given
4. Mix the cherries with it, and boil them for about 15 minutes, carefully skimming them
5. Turn them gently into a pan, and let them remain till the next day
6. Drain the cherries on a sieve, and put the syrup and white-currant juice into the preserving-pan again
7. Boil these together until the syrup is somewhat reduced and rather thick
8. Put in the cherries, and let them boil for about 5 minutes
9. Take them off the fire, skim the syrup
10. Put the cherries into small pots or wide-mouthed bottles
11. Pour the syrup over, and when quite cold, tie them down carefully, so that the air is quite excluded
