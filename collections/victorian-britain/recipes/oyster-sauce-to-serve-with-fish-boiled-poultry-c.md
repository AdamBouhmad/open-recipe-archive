## Ingredients

- 3 dozen oysters
- 1/2 pint of melted butter, made with milk

## Directions

1. Open the oysters carefully, and save their liquor; strain it into a clean saucepan
2. Put in the oysters, and let them just come to the boiling-point, when they should look plump
3. Take them off the fire immediately, and put the whole into a basin
4. Strain the liquor from them, mix with it sufficient milk to make 1/2 pint altogether, and follow the directions for melted butter
5. When the melted butter is ready and very smooth, put in the oysters, which should be previously bearded, if you wish the sauce to be really nice
6. Set it by the side of the fire to get thoroughly hot, but do not allow it to boil, or the oysters will immediately harden
7. Using cream instead of milk makes this sauce extremely delicious
8. When liked, add a seasoning of cayenne, or anchovy sauce
