## Ingredients

- Venison
- coarse flour-and-water paste
- flour
- butter
- powdered ginger or pepper
- white paper
- twine
- currant jelly
- port wine
- gravy

## Directions

1. Choose a haunch with clear, bright, and thick fat, and the cleft of the hoof smooth and close.
2. Ascertain how long it has been kept by running a sharp skewer into the meat close to the bone.
3. Keep it perfectly dry by wiping it with clean cloths till not the least damp remains.
4. Sprinkle over powdered ginger or pepper, as a preventative against the fly.
5. When required for use, wash it in warm water, and dry it well with a cloth.
6. Butter a sheet of white paper, put it over the fat.
7. Lay a coarse paste, about 1/2 inch in thickness, over this, and then a sheet or two of strong paper.
8. Tie the whole firmly on to the haunch with twine.
9. Put the joint down to a strong close fire.
10. Baste the venison immediately, to prevent the paper and string from burning.
11. Continue this operation without intermission the whole of the time it is cooking.
12. About 20 minutes before it is done, carefully remove the paste and paper.
13. Dredge the joint with flour, and baste well with butter until it is nicely frothed, and of a nice pale-brown colour.
14. Garnish the knuckle-bone with a frill of white paper.
15. Serve with a good, strong, but unflavoured gravy in a tureen, and currant jelly; or melt the jelly with a little port wine, and serve that also in a tureen.
16. Send very hot plates to table, as the venison fat so soon freezes.
