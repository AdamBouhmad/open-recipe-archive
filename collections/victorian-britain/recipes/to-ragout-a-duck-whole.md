## Ingredients

- 1 large duck
- pepper and salt to taste
- good beef gravy
- 2 onions sliced
- 4 sage-leaves
- a few leaves of lemon thyme
- thickening of butter and flour
- about 1-1/2 pint of young green peas (when in season)

## Directions

1. After having emptied and singed the duck, season it inside with pepper and salt, and truss it.
2. Roast it before a clear fire for about 20 minutes, and let it acquire a nice brown colour.
3. Put it into a stewpan with sufficient well-seasoned beef gravy to cover it.
4. Slice and fry the onions, and add these, with the sage-leaves and lemon thyme, both of which should be finely minced, to the stock.
5. Simmer gently until the duck is tender.
6. Strain, skim, and thicken the gravy with a little butter and flour.
7. Boil it up, pour over the duck, and serve.
8. When in season, about 1-1/2 pint of young green peas, boiled separately, and put in the ragoût, very much improve this dish.
