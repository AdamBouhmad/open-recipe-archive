## Ingredients

- 3 gallons of water
- 1 peck of elderberries
- 3 lbs. of sugar per gallon of juice
- 1/2 oz. of ground ginger
- 6 cloves
- 1 lb. of good Turkey raisins
- 1/2 pint of brandy per gallon of wine
- 3 or 4 tablespoonfuls of fresh brewer's yeast to every 9 gallons of wine

## Directions

1. Pour the water, quite boiling, on the elderberries, which should be picked from the stalks
2. Let these stand covered for 24 hours
3. Strain the whole through a sieve or bag, breaking the fruit to express all the juice from it
4. Measure the liquor, and to every gallon allow the above proportion of sugar
5. Boil the juice and sugar with the ginger, cloves, and raisins for 1 hour
6. Skim the liquor the whole time
7. Let it stand until milk-warm
8. Put it into a clean dry cask with the yeast
9. Let it ferment for about a fortnight
10. Add the brandy
11. Bung up the cask and let it stand some months before it is bottled
