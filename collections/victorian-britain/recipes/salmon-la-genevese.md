## Ingredients

- 2 slices of salmon
- 2 chopped shalots
- a little parsley
- a small bunch of herbs
- 2 bay-leaves
- 2 carrots
- pounded mace
- pepper and salt to taste
- 4 tablespoonfuls of Madeira
- 1/2 pint of white stock (No. 107)
- thickening of butter and flour
- 1 teaspoonful of essence of anchovies
- the juice of 1 lemon
- cayenne and salt to taste

## Directions

1. Rub the bottom of a stewpan over with butter, and put in the shalots, herbs, bay-leaves, carrots, mace, and seasoning
2. stir them for 10 minutes over a clear fire, and add the Madeira or sherry
3. simmer gently for 1/2 hour, and strain through a sieve over the fish
4. stew the fish in this gravy
5. As soon as the fish is sufficiently cooked, take away all the liquor, except a little to keep the salmon moist, and put it into another stewpan
6. add the stock, thicken with butter and flour
7. put in the anchovies, lemon-juice, cayenne, and salt
8. lay the salmon on a hot dish, pour over it part of the sauce, and serve the remainder in a tureen
