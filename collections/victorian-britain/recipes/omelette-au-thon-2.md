## Ingredients

- roes of 2 carp
- piece of fresh tunny about the size of a hen's egg
- small shalot, chopped
- very good butter
- butter à discrétion
- parsley and herbs
- lemon
- 12 eggs

## Directions

1. Bleach the roes by putting them for 5 minutes in boiling water slightly salted.
2. Hash up together the roe and the tunny so as to mix them well, and throw the whole into a saucepan with a sufficient quantity of very good butter.
3. Whip it up until the butter is melted.
4. Mix a second piece of butter with parsley and herbs, place it in a long-shaped dish destined to receive the omelet.
5. Squeeze the juice of a lemon over it, and place it on hot embers.
6. Beat up 12 eggs, the fresher the better.
7. Throw up the sauté of roe and tunny, stirring it so as to mix all well together.
8. Make your omelet in the usual manner, endeavouring to turn it out long, thick, and soft.
9. Spread it carefully on the dish prepared for it, and serve at once.
10. Note: The roe and the tunny must be beaten up without allowing them to boil, to prevent their hardening.
