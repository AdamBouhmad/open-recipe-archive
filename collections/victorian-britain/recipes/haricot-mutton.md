## Ingredients

- 4 lbs. of the middle or best end of the neck of mutton
- 3 carrots
- 3 turnips
- 3 onions
- pepper and salt to taste
- 1 tablespoonful of ketchup or Harvey's sauce

## Directions

1. Trim off some of the fat, cut the mutton into rather thin chops, and put them into a frying-pan with the fat trimmings.
2. Fry of a pale brown, but do not cook them enough for eating.
3. Cut the carrots and turnips into dice, and the onions into slices, and slightly fry them in the same fat that the mutton was browned in, but do not allow them to take any colour.
4. Lay the mutton at the bottom of a stewpan, then the vegetables, and pour over them just sufficient boiling water to cover the whole.
5. Give one boil, skim well, and then set the pan on the side of the fire to simmer gently until the meat is tender.
6. Skim off every particle of fat, add a seasoning of pepper and salt, and a little ketchup, and serve.
7. Serve in rather a deep dish.
