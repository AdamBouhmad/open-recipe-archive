## Ingredients

- 10 or 12 potatoes
- To each 1/2 gallon of water allow 1 heaped tablespoonful of salt

## Directions

1. Wash the potatoes well and use a clean scrubbing-brush to remove the dirt from them
2. Choose the potatoes so that they may all be as nearly the same size as possible
3. Fill the saucepan half full with them and just cover the potatoes with cold water, salted in the above proportion
4. Bring them to boil, then draw the pan to the side of the fire and let them simmer gently until tender
5. Ascertain when they are done by probing them with a fork
6. Pour off the water and uncover the saucepan
7. Let the potatoes dry by the side of the fire, taking care not to let them burn
8. Peel them quickly and put them in a very hot vegetable-dish either with or without a napkin
9. Serve very quickly
