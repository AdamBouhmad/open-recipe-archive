## Ingredients

- Salt and water
- 1 lb. of lump sugar
- the rind of 1 lemon
- 1 oz. of ginger
- cucumbers
- cabbage-leaf

## Directions

1. Choose the greenest cucumbers, and those that are most free from seeds
2. Put them in strong salt and water, with a cabbage-leaf to keep them down
3. Tie a paper over them, and put them in a warm place till they are yellow
4. Wash them and set them over the fire in fresh water, with a very little salt, and another cabbage-leaf over them
5. Cover very closely, but take care they do not boil
6. If they are not a fine green, change the water again, cover them as before, and make them hot
7. When they are a good colour, take them off the fire and let them cool
8. Cut them in quarters, take out the seeds and pulp, and put them into cold water
9. Let them remain for 2 days, changing the water twice each day, to draw out the salt
10. Put the sugar, with 1/4 pint of water, in a saucepan over the fire
11. Remove the scum as it rises, and add the lemon-peel and ginger with the outside scraped off
12. When the syrup is tolerably thick, take it off the fire, and when cold, wipe the cucumbers dry, and put them in
13. Boil the syrup once in 2 or 3 days for 3 weeks
14. Strengthen it if required, and let it be quite cold before the cucumbers are put in
