## Ingredients

- 2-1/2 pints of walnut pickle
- 1-1/2 oz. of shalots
- 1 quart of spring water
- 3/4 pint of Indian soy
- 1/2 oz. of bruised ginger
- 1/2 oz. of long pepper
- 1 oz. of mustard-seed
- 1 anchovy
- 1/2 oz. of cayenne
- 1/4 oz. of dried sweet bay-leaves

## Directions

1. Bruise the shalots in a mortar, and put them in a stone jar with the walnut-liquor.
2. Place it before the fire, and let it boil until reduced to 2 pints.
3. Into another jar, put all the ingredients except the bay-leaves, taking care that they are well bruised, so that the flavour may be thoroughly extracted.
4. Put this also before the fire, and let it boil for 1 hour, or rather more.
5. When the contents of both jars are sufficiently cooked, mix them together, stirring them well as you mix them, and submit them to a slow boiling for 1/2 hour.
6. Cover closely, and let them stand 24 hours in a cool place.
7. Then open the jar and add the bay-leaves.
8. Let it stand a week longer closed down, then strain through a flannel bag, and it will be ready for use.
