## Ingredients

- 10 good-sized apples
- the whites of 10 eggs
- the rind of 1 lemon
- 1/2 lb. of pounded sugar

## Directions

1. Peel, core, and cut the apples into quarters, and put them into a saucepan with the lemon-peel and sufficient water to prevent them from burning,--rather less than 1/2 pint
2. When they are tender, take out the peel, beat them to a pulp, let them cool, and stir them to the whites of the eggs, which should be previously beaten to a strong froth
3. Add the sifted sugar, and continue the whisking until the mixture becomes quite stiff
4. Either heap it on a glass dish, or serve it in small glasses
5. The dish may be garnished with preserved barberries, or strips of bright-coloured jelly
6. Serve with a dish of custards or a jug of cream
