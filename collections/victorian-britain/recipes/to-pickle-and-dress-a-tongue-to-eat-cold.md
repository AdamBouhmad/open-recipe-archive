## Ingredients

- 6 oz. of salt
- 2 oz. of bay-salt
- 1 oz. of saltpetre
- 3 oz. of coarse sugar
- cloves, mace, and allspice to taste
- butter
- common crust of flour and water

## Directions

1. Lay the tongue for a fortnight in the above pickle, turn it every day, and be particular that the spices are well pounded
2. Put it into a small pan just large enough to hold it, place some pieces of butter on it, and cover with a common crust
3. Bake in a slow oven until so tender that a straw would penetrate it
4. Take off the skin, fasten it down to a piece of board by running a fork through the root and another through the tip, at the same time straightening it and putting it into shape
5. When cold, glaze it, put a paper ruche round the root, and garnish with tufts of parsley
