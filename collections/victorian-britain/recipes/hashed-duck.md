## Ingredients

- The remains of cold roast duck
- rather more than 1 pint of weak stock or water
- 1 onion
- 1 oz. of butter
- butter and flour for thickening
- salt and cayenne to taste
- 1/2 teaspoonful of minced lemon-peel
- 1 dessertspoonful of lemon-juice
- 1/2 glass of port wine
- sippets of toasted bread for garnish

## Directions

1. Cut the duck into nice joints
2. Put the trimmings into a stewpan
3. Slice and fry the onion in a little butter
4. Add the fried onion to the trimmings
5. Pour in the weak stock or water
6. Stew gently for 1 hour
7. Strain the liquor
8. Thicken it with butter and flour
9. Season with salt and cayenne
10. Add the lemon-peel, lemon-juice, and port wine
11. Boil it up and skim well
12. Lay in the pieces of duck
13. Let them get thoroughly hot through by the side of the fire, but do not allow them to boil
14. Let them soak in the gravy for about 1/2 hour
15. Garnish with sippets of toasted bread
