## Ingredients

- 2 pints of milk
- 6 tablespoonfuls of ground rice
- sugar to taste
- 4 eggs
- flavouring of lemon-rind, nutmeg, bitter almonds or bay-leaf

## Directions

1. Put 1-1/2 pint of the milk into a stewpan, with any of the above flavourings, and bring it to the boiling-point
2. With the other 1/2 pint of milk, mix the ground rice to a smooth batter
3. Strain the boiling milk to this, and stir over the fire until the mixture is tolerably thick
4. Pour it into a basin, leave it uncovered, and when nearly or quite cold, sweeten it to taste, and add the eggs, which should be previously well beaten, with a little salt
5. For boiled pudding: Put the pudding into a well-buttered basin, tie it down with a cloth, plunge it into boiling water, and boil for 1-1/2 hour
6. For baked pudding: Use half the above proportion of ground rice, with the same quantity of all the other ingredients, and bake in a moderate oven for an hour
7. Stewed fruit, preserves, or marmalade may be served with either the boiled or baked pudding
