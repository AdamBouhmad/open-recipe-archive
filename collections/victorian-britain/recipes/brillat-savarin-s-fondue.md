## Ingredients

- Eggs
- Gruyère cheese
- Butter
- Pepper
- Salt

## Directions

1. Take the same number of eggs as there are guests; weigh the eggs in the shell, allow a third of their weight in Gruyère cheese, and a piece of butter one-sixth of the weight of the cheese.
2. Break the eggs into a basin, beat them well; add the cheese, which should be grated, and the butter, which should be broken into small pieces.
3. Stir these ingredients together with a wooden spoon; put the mixture into a lined saucepan, place it over the fire, and stir until the substance is thick and soft.
4. Put in a little salt, according to the age of the cheese, and a good sprinkling of pepper, and serve the fondue on a very hot silver or metal plate.
5. Do not allow the fondue to remain on the fire after the mixture is set, as, if it boils, it will be entirely spoiled.
