## Ingredients

- Woodcocks
- butter
- flour
- toast

## Directions

1. Woodcocks should not be drawn, as the trails are, by epicures, considered a great delicacy.
2. Pluck, and wipe them well outside; truss them with the legs close to the body, and the feet pressing upon the thighs; skin the neck and head, and bring the beak round under the wing.
3. Place some slices of toast in the dripping-pan to catch the trails, allowing a piece of toast for each bird.
4. Roast before a clear fire from 15 to 25 minutes; keep them well basted, and flour and froth them nicely.
5. When done, dish the pieces of toast with the birds upon them, and pour round a very little gravy; send some more to table in a tureen.
