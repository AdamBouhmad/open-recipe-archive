## Ingredients

- Red cabbages
- salt
- water
- vinegar
- ginger well bruised (1/2 oz. to each quart of vinegar)
- whole black pepper (1 oz. to each quart of vinegar)
- cayenne (when liked, a little)
- bruised cochineal (optional)

## Directions

1. Take off the outside decayed leaves of a nice red cabbage, cut it in quarters, remove the stalks, and cut it across in very thin slices.
2. Lay these on a dish, and strew them plentifully with salt, covering them with another dish.
3. Let them remain for 24 hours, turn into a colander to drain, and, if necessary, wipe lightly with a clean soft cloth.
4. Put them in a jar; boil up the vinegar with spices in the above proportion, and, when cold, pour it over the cabbage.
5. Tie down with bladder, and keep in a dry place.
6. It will be fit for use in a week or two. To be really nice and crisp, and of a good red colour, it should be eaten almost immediately after it is made.
