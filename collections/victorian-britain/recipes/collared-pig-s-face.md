## Ingredients

- 1 pig's face
- salt
- 1 gallon of spring water
- 1 lb. of common salt
- 1/2 handful of chopped juniper-berries
- 6 bruised cloves
- 2 bay-leaves
- a few sprigs of thyme
- a few sprigs of basil
- a few sprigs of sage
- 1/4 oz. of saltpetre
- 1/2 lb. of ham
- 1/2 lb. bacon
- 1 teaspoonful of mixed spices
- pepper to taste
- 1/4 lb. of lard
- 1 tablespoonful of minced parsley
- 6 young onions

## Directions

1. Singe the head carefully, bone it without breaking the skin, and rub it well with salt.
2. Make the brine by boiling the ingredients (spring water, common salt, juniper-berries, cloves, bay-leaves, thyme, basil, sage, and saltpetre) for 1/4 hour, and letting it stand to cool.
3. When cold, pour the brine over the head, and let it steep in this for 10 days, turning and rubbing it often.
4. Then wipe, drain, and dry it.
5. For the forcemeat, pound the ham and bacon very finely, and mix with the remaining ingredients (mixed spices, pepper, lard, minced parsley, and young onions), taking care that the whole is thoroughly incorporated.
6. Spread the forcemeat equally over the head, roll it tightly in a cloth, and bind it securely with broad tape.
7. Put it into a saucepan with a few meat trimmings, and cover it with stock; let it simmer gently for 4 hours, and be particular that it does not stop boiling the whole time.
8. When quite tender, take it up, put it between 2 dishes with a heavy weight on the top, and when cold, remove the cloth and tape.
9. Send to table on a napkin, or garnished with a piece of deep white paper with a ruche at the top.
