## Ingredients

- 2 small fowls or 1 large one
- 3 oz. of butter
- a bunch of parsley and green onions
- 1 clove
- 2 blades of mace
- 1 shalot
- 1 bay-leaf
- salt and white pepper to taste
- 1/4 pint of cream
- yolks of 3 eggs
- flour
- button-mushrooms

## Directions

1. Choose a couple of fat plump chickens, and, after drawing, singeing, and washing them, skin, and carve them into joints
2. Blanch these in boiling water for 2 or 3 minutes
3. Take them out, and immerse them in cold water to render them white
4. Put the trimmings, with the necks and legs, into a stewpan
5. Add the parsley, onions, clove, mace, shalot, bay-leaf, and a seasoning of pepper and salt
6. Pour to these the water that the chickens were blanched in, and simmer gently for rather more than 1 hour
7. Have ready another stewpan; put in the joints of fowl, with the above proportion of butter
8. Dredge them with flour, let them get hot, but do not brown them much
9. Moisten the fricassee with the gravy made from the trimmings
10. Stew very gently for 1/2 hour
11. Lift the fowl into another stewpan, skim the sauce, reduce it quickly over the fire, by letting it boil fast, and strain it over them
12. Add the cream, and a seasoning of pounded mace and cayenne
13. Let it boil up, and when ready to serve, stir to it the well-beaten yolks of 3 eggs
14. The sauce should be made hot, but must not boil, or it will instantly curdle
15. A few button-mushrooms stewed with the fowl are by many persons considered an improvement
