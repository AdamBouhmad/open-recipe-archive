## Ingredients

- 1 quart of calf's-feet stock
- 1/2 lb. of sugar
- 1/2 pint of sherry
- 1 glass of brandy
- shells and whites of 5 eggs
- rind and juice of 2 lemons
- 1/2 oz. of isinglass

## Directions

1. Prepare the stock as directed, taking care to leave the sediment, and to remove all the fat from the surface.
2. Put it into a saucepan, cold, without clarifying it; add the remaining ingredients, and stir them well together before the saucepan is placed on the fire.
3. Simmer the mixture gently for 1/4 hour, but do not stir it after it begins to warm.
4. Throw in a teacupful of cold water, boil for another 5 minutes, and keep the saucepan covered by the side of the fire for about 1/2 hour, but do not let it boil again.
5. In simmering, the head or scum may be carefully removed as it rises; but particular attention must be given to the jelly, that it be not stirred in the slightest degree after it is heated.
6. The isinglass should be added when the jelly begins to boil: this assists to clear it, and makes it firmer for turning out.
7. Wring out a jelly-bag in hot water; fasten it on to a stand, or the back of a chair; place it near the fire with a basin underneath it, and run the jelly through it.
8. Should it not be perfectly clear the first time, repeat the process until the desired brilliancy is obtained.
9. Soak the moulds in water, drain them for half a second, pour in the jelly, and put it in a cool place to set.
10. If ice is at hand, surround the moulds with it, and the jelly will set sooner, and be firmer when turned out.
11. When wanted for table, dip the moulds in hot water for a minute, wipe the outside with a cloth, lay a dish on the top of the mould, turn it quickly over, and the jelly should slip out easily.
