## Ingredients

- 1 young fowl
- 3 dozen oysters
- yolks of 2 eggs
- 1/4 pint of cream
- a blade of pounded mace

## Directions

1. Truss a young fowl as for boiling
2. Fill the inside with oysters which have been bearded and washed in their own liquor
3. Secure the ends of the fowl
4. Put it into a jar, and plunge the jar into a saucepan of boiling water
5. Keep it boiling for 1-1/2 hour, or rather longer
6. Take the gravy that has flowed from the oysters and fowl
7. Stir in the cream and yolks of eggs
8. Add a few oysters scalded in their liquor
9. Let the sauce get quite hot, but do not allow it to boil
10. Pour some of it over the fowl, and the remainder send to table in a tureen
11. A blade of pounded mace added to the sauce, with the cream and eggs, will be found an improvement
