## Ingredients

- 1-1/2 lb. of flour
- 1/2 lb. of butter
- 1/2 pint of milk
- 1-1/2 tablespoonful of good yeast
- 3 eggs
- 3/4 lb. of currants
- 1/2 lb. of white moist sugar
- 2 oz. of candied peel

## Directions

1. Put the milk and butter into a saucepan, and shake it round over a fire until the butter is melted, but do not allow the milk to get very hot
2. Put the flour into a basin, stir to it the milk and butter, the yeast, and eggs, which should be well beaten
3. Form the whole into a smooth dough
4. Let it stand in a warm place, covered with a cloth, to rise
5. When sufficiently risen, add the currants, sugar, and candied peel cut into thin slices
6. When all the ingredients are thoroughly mixed, line 2 moderate-sized cake-tins with buttered paper, which should be about six inches higher than the tin
7. Pour in the mixture, let it stand to rise again for another 1/2 hour
8. Bake the cakes in a brisk oven for about 1-1/2 hour
9. If the tops of them become too brown, cover them with paper until they are done through
