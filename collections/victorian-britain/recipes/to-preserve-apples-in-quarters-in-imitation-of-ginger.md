## Ingredients

- apples
- 3/4 lb. of sugar per lb. of apples
- 1-1/2 oz. of the best white ginger per lb. of apples
- 1 oz. of ginger per 1/2 pint of water

## Directions

1. Peel, core, and quarter the apples, and put the fruit, sugar, and ginger in layers into a wide-mouthed jar, and let them remain for 2 days.
2. Infuse 1 oz. of ginger in 1/2 pint of boiling water, and cover it closely, and let it remain for 1 day.
3. Put the apples, &c., into a preserving-pan with the water strained from the ginger, and boil till the apples look clear and the syrup is rich, which will be in about an hour.
4. The rind of a lemon may be added just before the apples have finished boiling; and great care must be taken not to break the pieces of apple in putting them into the jars.
5. Serve on glass dishes for dessert.
