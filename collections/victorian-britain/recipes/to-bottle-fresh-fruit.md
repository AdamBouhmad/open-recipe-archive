## Ingredients

- Fresh fruits, such as currants, raspberries, cherries, gooseberries, plums of all kinds, damsons
- Wide-mouthed glass bottles
- New corks to fit them tightly

## Directions

1. Let the fruit be full grown, but not too ripe, and gathered in dry weather
2. Pick it off the stalks without bruising or breaking the skin, and reject any that is at all blemished
3. Have ready some perfectly dry glass bottles, and some nice new soft corks or bungs
4. Burn a match in each bottle, to exhaust the air, and quickly place the fruit in to be preserved
5. Gently cork the bottles, and put them into a very cool oven, where let them remain until the fruit has shrunk away a fourth part
6. Take the bottles out; do not open them, but immediately beat the corks in tight, cut off the tops, and cover them with melted resin
7. If kept in a dry place, the fruit will remain good for months
