## Ingredients

- full-grown mushroom-flaps
- 1/2 lb. of salt per peck of mushrooms
- 1/4 oz. of cayenne per quart of mushroom-liquor
- 1/2 oz. of allspice per quart of mushroom-liquor
- 1/2 oz. of ginger per quart of mushroom-liquor
- 2 blades of pounded mace per quart of mushroom-liquor
- brandy

## Directions

1. Choose full-grown mushroom-flaps, and take care they are perfectly fresh-gathered when the weather is tolerably dry
2. Put a layer of them in a deep pan, sprinkle salt over them, and then another layer of mushrooms, and so on alternately
3. Let them remain for a few hours, when break them up with the hand; put them in a nice cool place for 3 days, occasionally stirring and mashing them well, to extract from them as much juice as possible
4. Measure the quantity of liquor without straining, and to each quart allow the above proportion of spices
5. Put all into a stone jar, cover it up very closely, put it in a saucepan of boiling water, set it over the fire, and let it boil for 3 hours
6. Turn into a nice clean stewpan the contents of the jar, and let the whole simmer very gently for 1/2 hour
7. Pour it into a jug, where it should stand in a cool place till the next day
8. Pour it off into another jug, and strain it into very dry clean bottles, and do not squeeze the mushrooms
9. To each pint of ketchup add a few drops of brandy
10. Cork well, and either seal or rosin the cork, so as perfectly to exclude the air
