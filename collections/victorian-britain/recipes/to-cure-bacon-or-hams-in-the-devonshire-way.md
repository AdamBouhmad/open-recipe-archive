## Ingredients

- To every 14 lbs. of meat, 2 oz. of saltpetre
- 2 oz. of salt prunella
- 1 lb. of common salt
- 3 gallons of water
- 5 lbs. of common salt
- 7 lbs. of coarse sugar
- 3 lbs. of bay-salt
- dry sawdust

## Directions

1. Weigh the sides, hams, and cheeks, and to every 14 lb. allow 2 oz. of saltpetre, 2 oz. of salt prunella, and 1 lb. of common salt.
2. Pound and mix these together, and rub well into the meat.
3. Lay it in a stone trough or tub, rubbing it thoroughly, and turning it daily for 2 successive days.
4. At the end of the second day, pour on it a pickle made as follows: Put the ingredients into a saucepan, set it on the fire, and stir frequently.
5. Remove all the scum, allow it to boil for 1/4 hour, and pour it hot over the meat.
6. Let the hams, &c., be well rubbed and turned daily; if the meat is small, a fortnight will be sufficient for the sides and shoulders to remain in the pickle, and the hams 3 weeks; if from 30 lbs. and upwards, 3 weeks will be required for the sides, &c., and from 4 to 5 weeks for the hams.
7. On taking the pieces out, let them drain for an hour, cover with dry sawdust, and smoke from a fortnight to 3 weeks.
8. Boil and carefully skim the pickle after using, and it will keep good, closely corked, for 2 years.
9. When boiling it for use, add about 2 lbs. of common salt, and the same of treacle, to allow for waste.
