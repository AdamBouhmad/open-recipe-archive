## Ingredients

- Equal quantities of veal, panada (No. 420), and calf's udder (No. 421)
- 2 eggs
- pepper
- salt
- pounded mace, or grated nutmeg
- a little flour

## Directions

1. Take the fleshy part of veal, scrape it with a knife, till all the meat is separated from the sinews, allowing about 1/2 lb. for an entrée
2. Chop the meat, and pound it in a mortar till reduced to a paste
3. Roll it into a ball
4. Make another ball of panada (No. 420), the same size
5. Make another ball of udder (No. 421), taking care that these three balls be of the same size
6. Pound them altogether in a mortar for some time
7. Moisten with the eggs, whites and yolks, and continue pounding
8. Add a seasoning of pepper, spices, &c.
9. Mould it into balls, or whatever shape is intended
10. Roll them in flour
11. Poach in boiling water, to which a little salt should have been added
12. If the quenelles are not firm enough, add the yolk of another egg, but omit the white
