## Ingredients

- Rather more than 1-1/2 pint of jelly
- a few nice strawberries, or red or white currants, or raspberries, or any fresh fruit that may be in season

## Directions

1. Have ready the above proportion of jelly, which must be very clear and rather sweet, the raw fruit requiring an additional quantity of sugar
2. Select ripe, nice-looking fruit; pick off the stalks, unless currants are used, when they are laid in the jelly as they come from the tree
3. Begin by putting a little jelly at the bottom of the mould, which must harden
4. Arrange the fruit round the sides of the mould, recollecting that it will be reversed when turned out
5. Pour in some more jelly to make the fruit adhere, and, when that layer is set, put another row of fruit and jelly until the mould is full
6. If convenient, put it in ice until required for table, then wring a cloth in boiling water, wrap it round the mould for a minute, and turn the jelly carefully out
7. Peaches, apricots, plums, apples are better for being boiled in a little clear syrup before they are laid in the jelly
8. Strawberries, raspberries, grapes, cherries, and currants are put in raw
9. In winter, when fresh fruits are not obtainable, a very pretty jelly may be made with preserved fruits or brandy cherries
10. It may be garnished with the same fruit as is laid in the jelly
