## Ingredients

- A few thick steaks of cold ribs or sirloin of beef
- 2 oz. of butter
- 1 onion sliced
- pepper and salt to taste
- 1/2 glass of port wine
- a little flour to thicken
- 1 or 2 dozen oysters
- rather more than 1/2 pint of water

## Directions

1. Cut the steaks rather thick, from cold sirloin or ribs of beef; brown them lightly in a stewpan, with the butter and a little water.
2. Add 1/2 pint of water, the onion, pepper, and salt, and cover the stewpan closely, and let it simmer very gently for 1/2 hour.
3. Mix about a teaspoonful of flour smoothly with a little of the liquor; add the port wine and oysters, their liquor having been previously strained and put into the stewpan.
4. Stir till the oysters plump, and serve. It should not boil after the oysters are added, or they will harden.
