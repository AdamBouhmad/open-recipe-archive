## Ingredients

- About 2 lbs. of the prime part of the leg of veal
- a few slices of bacon
- forcemeat No. 417
- cayenne to taste
- egg and bread crumbs
- gravy
- butter
- flour
- 1/4 pint of water
- 2 tablespoonfuls of lemon-juice
- salt
- pepper
- pounded mace

## Directions

1. Cut the veal into long thin collops, flatten them, and lay on each a piece of thin bacon of the same size
2. Spread forcemeat over the bacon, sprinkle over all a little cayenne, roll them up tightly, and do not let them be more than 2 inches long
3. Skewer each one firmly, egg and bread crumb them, and fry them a nice brown in a little butter, turning them occasionally, and shaking the pan about
4. When done, place them on a dish before the fire
5. Put a small piece of butter in the pan, dredge in a little flour, add 1/4 pint of water, 2 tablespoonfuls of lemon-juice, a seasoning of salt, pepper, and pounded mace
6. Let the whole boil up, and pour it over the collops
