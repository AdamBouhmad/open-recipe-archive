## Ingredients

- 6 good-sized apples
- 12 cloves
- pounded sugar
- 1 lemon
- 2 teacupfuls of water
- 1 tablespoonful of gelatine
- a few drops of prepared cochineal

## Directions

1. Choose rather large apples
2. Peel them and take out the cores, either with a scoop or a small silver knife, and put into each apple 2 cloves and as much sifted sugar as they will hold
3. Place them, without touching each other, in a large pie-dish
4. Add more white sugar, the juice of 1 lemon, and 2 teacupfuls of water
5. Bake in the oven, with a dish over them, until they are done
6. Look at them frequently, and, as each apple is cooked, place it in a glass dish
7. When the apples are neatly arranged in the dish without touching each other, strain the liquor in which they have been stewing, into a lined saucepan
8. Add to it the rind of the lemon, and a tablespoonful of gelatine which has been previously dissolved in cold water, and, if not sweet, a little more sugar, and 6 cloves
9. Boil till quite clear
10. Colour with a few drops of prepared cochineal, and strain the jelly through a double muslin into a jug
11. Let it cool a little
12. Pour it into the dish round the apples
13. When quite cold, garnish the tops of the apples with a bright-coloured marmalade, a jelly, or the white of an egg, beaten to a strong froth, with a little sifted sugar
