## Ingredients

- 1 gallon of vinegar
- 1/4 lb. of bruised ginger
- 1/4 lb. of mustard
- 1/4 lb. of salt
- 2 oz. of mustard-seed
- 1-1/2 oz. of turmeric
- 1 oz. of ground black pepper
- 1/4 oz. of cayenne
- cauliflowers
- onions
- celery
- sliced cucumbers
- gherkins
- French beans
- nasturtiums
- capsicums

## Directions

1. Have a large jar, with a tightly-fitting lid, in which put as much vinegar as required, reserving a little to mix the various powders to a smooth paste.
2. Put into a basin the mustard, turmeric, pepper, and cayenne; mix them with vinegar, and stir well until no lumps remain.
3. Add all the ingredients to the vinegar, and mix well.
4. Keep this liquor in a warm place, and thoroughly stir every morning for a month with a wooden spoon.
5. As vegetables come into season, have them gathered on a dry day, and, after merely wiping them with a cloth, to free them from moisture, put them into the pickle.
6. Divide the cauliflowers into small bunches.
7. Put all these into the pickle raw, and at the end of the season, when there have been added as many of the vegetables as could be procured, store it away in jars, and tie over with bladder.
