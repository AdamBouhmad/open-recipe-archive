## Ingredients

- 2 oz. of butter
- 2 large onions
- 2 lbs. of shin of beef
- 2 small slices of lean bacon (if at hand)
- salt and whole pepper to taste
- 3 cloves
- 2 quarts of water
- 2 oz. of butter for thickening
- 3 oz. of flour

## Directions

1. Put the butter into a stewpan; set this on the fire, throw in the onions cut in rings, and fry them a light brown; then add the beef and bacon, which should be cut into small square pieces; season, and pour in a teacupful of water; let it boil for about ten minutes, or until it is of a nice brown colour, occasionally stirring the contents.
2. Now fill up with water in the above proportion; let it boil up, when draw it to the side of the fire to simmer very gently for 1-1/2 hour; strain, and when cold, take off all the fat.
3. In thickening this gravy, melt 3 oz. of butter in a stewpan, add 2 oz. of flour, and stir till of a light-brown colour; when cold, add it to the strained gravy, and boil it up quickly.
4. This thickening may be made in larger quantities, and kept in a stone jar for use when wanted.
