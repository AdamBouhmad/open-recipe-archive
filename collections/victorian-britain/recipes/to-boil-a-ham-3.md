## Ingredients

- Ham
- Water
- Glaze or bread-raspings

## Directions

1. Choose a ham that is perfectly sweet by running a sharp knife into it close to the bone; if it has an agreeable smell, the ham is good
2. If it has been long hung and is very dry and salt, let it remain in soak for 24 hours, changing the water frequently; from 8 to 12 hours would be sufficient for a Yorkshire or Westmoreland ham
3. Wash it thoroughly clean and trim away from the under-side all the rusty and smoked parts
4. Put it into a boiling-pot with sufficient cold water to cover it
5. Bring it gradually to boil and carefully remove scum as it rises
6. Keep it simmering very gently until tender, being careful that it does not stop boiling nor boil too quickly
7. Take it out of the pot and strip off the skin
8. Sprinkle over it a few fine bread-raspings
9. Put a frill of cut paper round the knuckle
10. If to be eaten cold, let the ham remain in the water until nearly cold; do not remain in the saucepan all night
11. Garnish with parsley or cut vegetable flowers
