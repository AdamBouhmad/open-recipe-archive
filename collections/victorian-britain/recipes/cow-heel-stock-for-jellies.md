## Ingredients

- 2 cow-heels
- 3 quarts of water
- 1/2 oz. of isinglass to each quart

## Directions

1. Procure 2 heels that have only been scalded, and not boiled
2. Split them in two, and remove the fat between the claws
3. Wash them well in warm water, and put them into a saucepan with the above proportion of cold water
4. Bring it gradually to boil, remove all the scum as it rises, and simmer the heels gently from 7 to 8 hours, or until the liquor is reduced one-half
5. Strain it into a basin, measuring the quantity, and put it in a cool place
6. Clarify it in the same manner as calf's-feet stock, using about 1/2 oz. of isinglass to each quart
7. This stock should be made the day before it is required for use
