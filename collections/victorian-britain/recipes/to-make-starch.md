## Ingredients

- 1/2 pint of cold water
- 1 quart of boiling water
- 2 tablespoonfuls of starch
- gum Arabic (optional, for extra stiffness)
- wax candle (optional, for shiny appearance)

## Directions

1. Put the starch into a tolerably large basin
2. Pour over it the cold water, and stir the mixture well with a wooden spoon until it is perfectly free from lumps, and quite smooth
3. Take the basin to the fire, and whilst the water is actually boiling in the kettle or boiler, pour it over the starch, stirring it the whole time
4. If the water not be boiling when added to the starch, put it into a clean saucepan, and stir over the fire until it boils
5. Take it off the fire, strain it into a clean basin
6. Cover it up to prevent a skin forming on the top
7. When sufficiently cool that the hand may be borne in it, starch the things
8. To give a shiny and smooth appearance to the linen when ironed, stir round two or three times in the starch a piece of wax candle, which also prevents the iron from sticking
