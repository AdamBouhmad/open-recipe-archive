## Ingredients

- 1/2 lb. of pounded sugar
- the whites of 4 eggs

## Directions

1. Whisk the whites of the eggs to a stiff froth, and, with a wooden spoon, stir in quickly the pounded sugar.
2. Have some boards thick enough to put in the oven to prevent the bottom of the meringues from acquiring too much colour.
3. Cut some strips of paper about 2 inches wide; place this paper on the board, and drop a tablespoonful at a time of the mixture on the paper, taking care to let all the meringues be the same size.
4. In dropping it from the spoon, give the mixture the form of an egg, and keep the meringues about 2 inches apart from each other on the paper.
5. Strew over them some sifted sugar, and bake in a moderate oven for 1/2 hour.
6. As soon as they begin to colour, remove them from the oven.
7. Take each slip of paper by the two ends, and turn it gently on the table, and, with a small spoon, take out the soft part of each meringue.
8. Spread some clean paper on the board, turn the meringues upside down, and put them into the oven to harden and brown on the other side.
9. When required for table, fill them with whipped cream, flavoured with liqueur.
