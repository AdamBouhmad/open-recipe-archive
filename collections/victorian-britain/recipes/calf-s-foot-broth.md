## Ingredients

- 1 calf's foot
- 3 pints of water
- 1 small lump of sugar
- nutmeg to taste
- the yolk of 1 egg
- a piece of butter the size of a nut

## Directions

1. Stew the foot in the water, with the lemon-peel, very gently, until the liquid is half wasted, removing any scum, should it rise to the surface
2. Set it by in a basin until quite cold, then take off every particle of fat
3. Warm up about 1/2 pint of the broth, adding the butter, sugar, and a very small quantity of grated nutmeg
4. Take it off the fire for a minute or two, then add the beaten yolk of the egg
5. Keep stirring over the fire until the mixture thickens, but do not allow it to boil again after the egg is added, or it will curdle, and the broth will be spoiled
