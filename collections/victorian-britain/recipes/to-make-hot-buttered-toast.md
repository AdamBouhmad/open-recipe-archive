## Ingredients

- a loaf of household bread about two days old
- good butter

## Directions

1. Cut as many nice even slices as may be required, rather more than 1/4 inch in thickness, and toast them before a very bright fire, without allowing the bread to blacken, which spoils the appearance and flavour of all toast.
2. When of a nice colour on both sides, put it on a hot plate; divide some good butter into small pieces, place them on the toast, set this before the fire, and when the butter is just beginning to melt, spread it lightly over the toast.
3. Trim off the crust and ragged edges, divide each round into 4 pieces, and send the toast quickly to table.
4. Some persons cut the slices of toast across from corner to corner, so making the pieces of a three-cornered shape.
5. Each slice should be cut into pieces as soon as it is buttered, and when all are ready, pile them lightly on the dish they are intended to be served on.
