## Ingredients

- Green peas
- Water
- Moist sugar
- Salt
- Butter
- Mint (optional)

## Directions

1. Shell the peas, wash them well in cold water, and drain them
2. Put them into a saucepan with plenty of fast-boiling water, to which salt and moist sugar have been added in the proportion of 1 small teaspoonful of moist sugar and 1 heaped tablespoonful of salt to each 1/2 gallon of water
3. Let them boil quickly over a brisk fire, with the lid of the saucepan uncovered, being careful that the smoke does not draw in
4. When tender, pour them into a colander
5. Put them into a hot vegetable-dish, and quite in the centre of the peas place a piece of butter, the size of a walnut
6. Many cooks boil a small bunch of mint with the peas, or garnish them with it, by boiling a few sprigs in a saucepan by themselves
7. For very old peas that are difficult to boil a good colour, a very tiny piece of soda may be thrown in the water previous to putting them in, but this must be very sparingly used
