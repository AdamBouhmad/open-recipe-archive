## Ingredients

- 2 small fowls or 1 large one
- white pepper and salt to taste
- 1/2 teaspoonful of grated nutmeg
- 1/2 teaspoonful of pounded mace
- forcemeat No. 417
- a few slices of ham
- 3 hard-boiled eggs
- 1/2 pint of water
- puff crust
- yolk of an egg
- onion
- bunch of savoury herbs
- blade of mace

## Directions

1. Skin and cut up the fowls into joints
2. Put the neck, leg, and backbones in a stewpan with a little water, an onion, a bunch of savoury herbs, and a blade of mace
3. Let these stew for about an hour
4. When done, strain off the liquor for gravy
5. Put a layer of fowl at the bottom of a pie-dish, then a layer of ham, then one of forcemeat and hard-boiled eggs cut in rings
6. Between the layers put a seasoning of pounded mace, nutmeg, pepper, and salt
7. Proceed in this manner until the dish is full
8. Pour in about 1/2 pint of water
9. Border the edge of the dish with puff crust
10. Put on the cover and ornament the top
11. Glaze it by brushing over it the yolk of an egg
12. Bake from 1-1/4 to 1-1/2 hour, should the pie be very large
13. When done, pour in at the top the gravy made from the bones
14. If to be eaten cold and wished particularly nice, the joints of the fowls should be boned and placed in the dish with alternate layers of forcemeat
15. Sausage-meat may be substituted for the forcemeat
16. When the chickens are boned and mixed with sausage-meat, the pie will take about 2 hours to bake
17. Cover with a piece of paper when about half-done to prevent the paste from being dried up or scorched
