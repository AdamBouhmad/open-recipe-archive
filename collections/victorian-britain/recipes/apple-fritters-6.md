## Ingredients

- 1/2 lb. of flour
- 1/2 oz. of butter
- 1/2 saltspoonful of salt
- 2 eggs
- milk
- apples
- hot lard or clarified beef-dripping
- pounded sugar

## Directions

1. Break the eggs and separate the whites from the yolks; beat them separately
2. Put the flour into a basin and stir in the melted butter
3. Add the salt and moisten with sufficient warm milk to make a batter that will drop from the spoon
4. Stir well and rub down any lumps
5. Add the whisked whites of eggs and beat up the batter for a few minutes
6. Peel and cut the apples into rather thick whole slices without dividing them
7. Stamp out the middle of each slice where the core is with a cutter
8. Throw the slices into the batter
9. Take out the pieces of apple one by one and fry them in hot lard until nice brown, turning when required
10. Lay them on blotting-paper before the fire to absorb the greasy moisture
11. Dish on a white d'oyley piled one above the other
12. Strew over them some pounded sugar and serve very hot
