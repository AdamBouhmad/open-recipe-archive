## Ingredients

- 90 Seville oranges
- 32 lbs. of lump sugar
- water

## Directions

1. Break up the sugar into small pieces, and put it into a dry, sweet 9-gallon cask, placed in a cellar or other storehouse
2. Pare the peel of the oranges quite thin and put into one large pan or wooden keeler
3. Put the pulp after the juice has been squeezed from it into another large pan or wooden keeler
4. Strain the juice through a piece of double muslin, and put it into the cask with the sugar
5. Pour about 1-1/2 gallon of cold spring water on both the peels and pulp; let it stand for 24 hours, and then strain it into the cask
6. Add more water to the peels and pulp when this is done, and repeat the same process every day for a week
7. Apportion the quantity as nearly as possible to the seven days, and stir the contents of the cask each day
8. On the third day after the cask is full, that is, the tenth day after the commencement of making, the cask may be securely bunged down
9. Bottle it in 8 or 9 months, and it will be fit for use in a twelve month after the time of making
