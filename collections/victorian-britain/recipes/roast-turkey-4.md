## Ingredients

- Turkey
- forcemeat No. 417
- sheet of buttered paper
- flour
- butter
- good brown gravy
- bread sauce

## Directions

1. Choose cock turkeys by their short spurs and black legs for young birds; avoid those with long spurs and pale rough legs as they are old
2. Carefully pluck the bird, singe it with white paper, and wipe it thoroughly with a cloth
3. Draw it, preserve the liver and gizzard, and be particular not to break the gall-bag
4. Wash it inside well, and wipe it thoroughly dry with a cloth; the outside merely requires nicely wiping
5. Cut off the neck close to the back, but leave enough of the crop-skin to turn over
6. Break the leg-bone close below the knee, draw out the strings from the thighs, and flatten the breastbone to make it look plump
7. Have ready a forcemeat made by recipe No. 417; fill the breast with this, and sew the neck over to the back with a trussing-needle or use a skewer
8. Run a skewer through the pinion and thigh into the body to the pinion and thigh on the other side, and press the legs as much as possible between the breast and the side bones
9. Put the liver under one pinion and the gizzard under the other
10. Pass a string across the back of the bird, catch it over the points of the skewer, tie it in the centre of the back, and ensure the turkey is very firmly trussed
11. Fasten a sheet of buttered paper on to the breast of the bird, put it down to a bright fire, at some little distance at first (afterwards draw it nearer), and keep it well basted the whole of the time it is cooking
12. About 1/4 hour before serving, remove the paper, dredge the turkey lightly with flour, and put a piece of butter into the basting-ladle
13. As the butter melts, baste the bird with it
14. When of a nice brown and well frothed, serve with a tureen of good brown gravy and one of bread sauce
15. Fried sausages may be used as a garnish, or forcemeat balls should be placed round the dish
