## Ingredients

- 2 dozen apples
- a small pot of apricot-jam
- 1/2 lb. of sugar
- 1 Seville orange
- 1/4 pint of preserved cherries
- 1/4 lb. of raisins
- 1 oz. of citron
- 2 oz. of almonds
- 1 gill of Curaçoa
- 1 gill of Maraschino
- 1 pint of cream

## Directions

1. Peel, core, and cut the apples into quarters, and simmer them over the fire until soft
2. Mix with them the apricot-jam and the sugar, on which the rind of the orange should be previously rubbed
3. Work all these ingredients through a sieve, and put them into the freezing-pot
4. Stone the raisins, and simmer them in a little syrup for a few minutes
5. Add these, with the sliced citron, the almonds cut in dice, and the cherries drained from their syrup, to the ingredients in the freezing-pot
6. Put in the Curaçoa and Maraschino, and freeze again
7. Add as much whipped cream as will be required, freeze again, and fill the mould
8. Put the lid on, and plunge the mould into the ice-pot
9. Cover it with a wet cloth and pounded ice and saltpetre, where it should remain until wanted for table
10. Turn the pudding out of the mould on to a clean and neatly-folded napkin, and serve with iced whipped cream in a sauce-tureen or glass dish
