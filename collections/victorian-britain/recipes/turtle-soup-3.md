## Ingredients

- ham slices
- knuckles of veal
- turtle flesh and members
- sweet basil
- sweet marjoram
- lemon thyme
- winter savory
- bay-leaves
- common thyme
- parsley
- green onions
- large onion
- cloves
- flour
- butter
- Madeira
- sugar
- mace
- calf's udder
- egg yolks
- cayenne pepper
- salt
- spices
- bread crumbs
- milk
- eggs
- lemon juice

## Directions

1. Cut off the head of the turtle the preceding day
2. In the morning open the turtle by leaning heavily with a knife on the shell of the animal's back and cut this off all round
3. Turn it upright on its end that all the water may run out
4. Cut the flesh off along the spine with the knife sloping towards the bones to avoid touching the gall
5. Wash the flesh clean and let them drain
6. Have ready a large vessel full of boiling water and put the shells into it
7. When the shells come easily off take them out of the water and prick them all including those of the back belly fins and head
8. Boil the back and belly till the bones can be taken off without allowing the softer parts to be sufficiently done as they will be boiled again in the soup
9. Lay the slices of ham on the bottom of a very large stewpan over them the knuckles of veal then the inside flesh of the turtle and over the whole the members
10. Moisten with the water in which you are boiling the shell and draw it down thoroughly
11. Ascertain if it be thoroughly done by thrusting a knife into the fleshy part of the meat
12. When no blood appears moisten it again with the liquor in which the bones have been boiling
13. Put in a large bunch of sweet herbs--sweet basil sweet marjoram lemon thyme winter savory 2 or 3 bay-leaves common thyme a handful of parsley and green onions and a large onion stuck with 6 cloves
14. Let the whole be thoroughly done
15. Probe the members to see whether they are done and if so drain and send them to the larder
16. When the flesh is completely done strain it through a silk sieve
17. Make a very thin white roux with flour
18. Moisten the roux with the liquor keeping it over the fire till it boils
19. Ascertain that the sauce is neither too thick nor too thin
20. Draw the stewpan on the side of the stove to skim off the white scum and all the fat and oil that rise to the surface
21. Cut the softer parts to about the size of one or two inches square and throw into the soup
22. Let the soup simmer gently
23. Skim off all the fat and froth
24. Take all the leaves of the herbs from the stock and put these in a stewpan with about 1/4 lb. of fresh butter
25. Let it simmer on a slow fire till quite melted
26. Pour in 1 bottle of good Madeira adding a small bit of sugar and let it boil gently for 1 hour
27. Rub it through a tammy and add it to the soup
28. Let the soup boil till no white scum rises
29. Take with a skimmer all the bits of turtle out of the sauce and put them in a clean stewpan
30. Pour the soup over the bits of turtle through a tammy
31. Make quenelles à tortue by taking the fleshy part of a leg of veal about 1 lb. scraping off all the meat without leaving any sinews or fat
32. Soak in milk about the same quantity of crumbs of bread
33. Squeeze the bread and put it into a mortar with the veal a small quantity of calf's udder a little butter the yolks of 4 eggs boiled hard a little cayenne pepper salt and spices
34. Pound the whole very fine
35. Thicken the mixture with 2 whole eggs and the yolk of another
36. Try this farce or stuffing in boiling-hot water to ascertain its consistency
37. If it is too thin add the yolk of an egg
38. Take half of the farce and put into it some chopped parsley
39. Let the whole cool in order to roll it of the size of the yolk of an egg
40. Poach it in salt and boiling water and when very hard drain on a sieve and put it into the turtle
41. Before you send up squeeze the juice of 2 or 3 lemons with a little cayenne pepper and pour that into the soup
42. When either lemon-juice or cayenne pepper has been introduced no boiling must take place
