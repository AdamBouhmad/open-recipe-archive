## Ingredients

- 1 pound and a half of lean veal from the fillet
- 1 pound of good fresh beef suet
- 6 oz. of panada (bread soaked in milk and boiled till nearly dry)
- 1 teaspoonful of salt
- 1/4 teaspoonful of pepper
- 1/2 teaspoonful of nutmeg
- 4 eggs

## Directions

1. Cut the veal in long thin slices
2. Scrape with a knife till nothing but the fibre remains
3. Put it in a mortar and pound it 10 minutes, or until in a purée
4. Pass it through a wire sieve
5. Skin, shred, and chop the beef suet very fine
6. Put the suet in a mortar and pound it
7. Add the panada with the suet and pound them well together
8. Add the veal
9. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg
10. Work all well together
11. Add four eggs by degrees, continually pounding the contents of the mortar
12. Take a small piece in a spoon, and poach it in some boiling water to test if it is delicate, firm, and of a good flavour
