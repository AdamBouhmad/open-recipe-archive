## Ingredients

- Pig
- 6 oz. of bread crumbs
- 16 sage-leaves
- pepper and salt to taste
- a piece of butter the size of an egg
- salad oil or butter to baste with
- about 1/2 pint of gravy
- 1 tablespoonful of lemon-juice

## Directions

1. After preparing the pig for cooking, stuff it with finely-grated bread crumbs, minced sage, pepper, salt, and a piece of butter the size of an egg, all of which should be well mixed together, and put into the body of the pig.
2. Sew up the slit neatly, and truss the legs back, to allow the inside to be roasted, and the under part to be crisp.
3. Put the pig down to a bright clear fire, not too near, and let it lay till thoroughly dry.
4. Have ready some butter tied up in a piece of thin cloth, and rub the pig with this in every part.
5. Keep it well rubbed with the butter the whole of the time it is roasting, and do not allow the crackling to become blistered or burnt.
6. When half-done, hang a pig-iron before the middle part (if this is not obtainable, use a flat iron), to prevent its being scorched and dried up before the ends are done.
7. Before it is taken from the fire, cut off the head, and part that and the body down the middle.
8. Chop the brains and mix them with the stuffing; add 1/2 pint of good gravy, a tablespoonful of lemon-juice, and the gravy that flowed from the pig.
9. Put a little of this on the dish with the pig, and send the remainder to table in a tureen.
10. Place the pig back to back in the dish, with one half of the head on each side, and one of the ears at each end, and send it to table as hot as possible.
