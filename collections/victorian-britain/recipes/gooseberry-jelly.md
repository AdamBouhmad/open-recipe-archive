## Ingredients

- gooseberries
- loaf sugar (3/4 lb. per pint of juice)
- oiled and egged papers

## Directions

1. Put the gooseberries, after cutting off the tops and tails, into a preserving-pan, and stir them over the fire until they are quite soft
2. Strain them through a sieve, and to every pint of juice allow 3/4 lb. of sugar
3. Boil the juice and sugar together for nearly 3/4 hour, stirring and skimming all the time
4. If the jelly appears firm when a little of it is poured on to a plate, it is done
5. Take it up and put into small pots
6. Cover the pots with oiled and egged papers, the same as for currant jelly No. 1533, and store away in a dry place
