## Ingredients

- Steaks
- A piece of butter the size of a walnut
- Salt to taste
- 1 tablespoonful of good mushroom ketchup or Harvey's sauce

## Directions

1. Ensure the fire is bright and clear, and perfectly free from smoke, and do not add any fresh fuel just before you require to use the gridiron
2. Sprinkle a little salt over the fire and put on the gridiron for a few minutes to get thoroughly hot through
3. Rub it with a piece of fresh suet to prevent the meat from sticking
4. Lay on the steaks, which should be cut of an equal thickness, about 3/4 of an inch, or rather thinner
5. Level them by beating them as little as possible with a rolling-pin
6. Turn them frequently with steak-tongs (if these are not at hand, stick a fork in the edge of the fat, that no gravy escapes)
7. Cook for from 8 to 10 minutes until done
8. Have ready a very hot dish, into which put the ketchup, and, when liked, a little minced shalot
9. Dish up the steaks, rub them over with butter, and season with pepper and salt
10. Garnish with scraped horseradish, or slices of cucumber
