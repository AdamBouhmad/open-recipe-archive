## Ingredients

- 1 lb. of moist sugar
- 1 lb. of common salt
- 2 oz. of saltpetre
- 1 quart of good vinegar

## Directions

1. As soon as the pig is cold enough to be cut up, take the 2 hams and rub them well with common salt, and leave them in a large pan for 3 days.
2. When the salt has drawn out all the blood, drain the hams, and throw the brine away.
3. Mix sugar, salt, and saltpetre together in the above proportion, rub the hams well with these, and put them into a vessel large enough to hold them, always keeping the salt over them.
4. Let them remain for 3 days, then pour over them a quart of good vinegar.
5. Turn them in the brine every day for a month, then drain them well, and rub them with bran.
6. Have them smoked over a wood fire, and be particular that the hams are hung as high up as possible from the fire; otherwise the fat will melt, and they will become dry and hard.
