## Ingredients

- 1 lb. flour
- 1 lb. butter
- not quite 1/2 pint water

## Directions

1. Carefully weigh the flour and butter, and have the exact proportion
2. Squeeze the butter well, to extract the water from it, and afterwards wring it in a clean cloth, that no moisture may remain
3. Sift the flour; see that it is perfectly dry
4. Work the whole into a smooth paste, with not quite 1/2 pint of water, using a knife to mix it with
5. Roll it out until it is of an equal thickness of about an inch
6. Break 4 oz. of the butter into small pieces; place these on the paste, sift over it a little flour, fold it over, roll out again, and put another 4 oz. of butter
7. Repeat the rolling and buttering until the paste has been rolled out 4 times, or equal quantities of flour and butter have been used
8. Every time the paste is rolled out, dredge a little flour over that and the rolling-pin, to prevent both from sticking
9. Handle the paste as lightly as possible, and do not press heavily upon it with the rolling-pin
10. Brush the paste as often as rolled out, and the pieces of butter placed thereon, with the white of an egg, to assist it to rise in leaves or flakes
11. Put it into the oven when it is sufficiently hot to raise the paste
