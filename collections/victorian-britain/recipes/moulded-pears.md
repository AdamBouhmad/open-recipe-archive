## Ingredients

- 4 large pears or 6 small ones
- 8 cloves
- sugar to taste
- water
- a small piece of cinnamon
- 1/4 pint of raisin wine
- a strip of lemon-peel
- the juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1/2 oz. of gelatine

## Directions

1. Peel and cut the pears into quarters; put them into a jar with 3/4 pint of water, cloves, cinnamon, and sufficient sugar to sweeten the whole nicely.
2. Cover down the top of the jar, and bake the pears in a gentle oven until perfectly tender, but do not allow them to break.
3. When done, lay the pears in a plain mould, which should be well wetted, and boil 1/2 pint of the liquor the pears were baked in with the wine, lemon-peel, strained juice, and gelatine.
4. Let these ingredients boil quickly for 5 minutes, then strain the liquid warm over the pears.
5. Put the mould in a cool place, and when the jelly is firm, turn it out on a glass dish.
