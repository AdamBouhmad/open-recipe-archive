## Ingredients

- eggs
- sawdust
- butter or sweet oil
- bran

## Directions

1. Have ready a large saucepan, capable of holding 3 or 4 quarts, full of boiling water.
2. Put the eggs into a cabbage-net, say 20 at a time, and hold them in the water (which must be kept boiling) for 20 seconds.
3. Proceed in this manner till you have done as many eggs as you wish to preserve.
4. Pack them away in sawdust.
5. Alternatively, smear the shells with butter or sweet oil, pack in plenty of bran or sawdust, and ensure the eggs do not touch each other.
6. Or immerse them in lime-water soon after they have been laid, and put the vessel containing the lime-water in a cellar or cool outhouse.
