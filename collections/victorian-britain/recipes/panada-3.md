## Ingredients

- The crumb of 2 penny rolls
- 4 tablespoonfuls of white stock, No. 107
- 1 oz. of butter
- 1 slice of ham
- 1 bay-leaf
- a little minced parsley
- 2 shalots
- 1 clove
- 2 blades of mace
- a few mushrooms
- butter
- yolks of 2 eggs

## Directions

1. Soak the crumb of the rolls in milk for about 1/2 hour
2. Take it out, and squeeze so as to press the milk from it
3. Put the soaked bread into a stewpan with the white stock, and set it on one side
4. In a separate stewpan put 1 oz. of butter, a slice of lean ham cut small, with a bay-leaf, herbs, mushrooms, spices, &c., and fry them gently over a slow fire
5. When done, moisten with 2 teacupfuls of white stock, boil for 20 minutes, and strain the whole through a sieve over the panada in the other stewpan
6. Place it over the fire, keep constantly stirring, to prevent its burning
7. When quite dry, put in a small piece of butter
8. Let this again dry up by stirring over the fire
9. Add the yolks of 2 eggs, mix well
10. Put the panada to cool on a clean plate, and use it when required
