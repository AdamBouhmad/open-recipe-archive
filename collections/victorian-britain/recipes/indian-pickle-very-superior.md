## Ingredients

- vinegar
- 6 cloves of garlic per gallon of vinegar
- 12 shalots per gallon of vinegar
- 2 sticks of sliced horseradish per gallon of vinegar
- 1/4 lb. of bruised ginger per gallon of vinegar
- 2 oz. of whole black pepper per gallon of vinegar
- 1 oz. of long pepper per gallon of vinegar
- 1 oz. of allspice per gallon of vinegar
- 12 cloves per gallon of vinegar
- 1/4 oz. of cayenne per gallon of vinegar
- 2 oz. of mustard-seed per gallon of vinegar
- 1/4 lb. of mustard per gallon of vinegar
- 1 oz. of turmeric per gallon of vinegar
- a white cabbage
- cauliflowers
- radish-pods
- French beans
- gherkins
- small round pickling-onions
- nasturtiums
- capsicums
- chilies
- salt

## Directions

1. Cut the cabbage, which must be hard and white, into slices, and the cauliflowers into small branches
2. Sprinkle salt over them in a large dish, and let them remain two days
3. Dry them, and put them into a very large jar, with garlic, shalots, horseradish, ginger, pepper, allspice, and cloves, in the above proportions
4. Boil sufficient vinegar to cover them, which pour over, and, when cold, cover up to keep them free from dust
5. As the other things for the pickle ripen at different times, they may be added as they are ready: these will be radish-pods, French beans, gherkins, small onions, nasturtiums, capsicums, chilies, &c.
6. As these are procured, they must, first of all, be washed in a little cold vinegar, wiped, and then simply added to the other ingredients in the large jar, only taking care that they are covered by the vinegar
7. If more vinegar should be wanted to add to the pickle, boil it before adding it to the rest
8. When you have collected all the things you require, turn all out in a large pan, and thoroughly mix them
9. Put the mixed vegetables into smaller jars, without any of the vinegar
10. Boil the vinegar again, adding as much more as will be required to fill the different jars, and also cayenne, mustard-seed, turmeric, and mustard, which must be well mixed with a little cold vinegar, allowing the quantities named above to each gallon of vinegar
11. Pour the vinegar, boiling hot, over the pickle, and when cold, tie down with a bladder
