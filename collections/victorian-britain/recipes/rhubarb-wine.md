## Ingredients

- 5 lbs. of rhubarb pulp
- 1 gallon of cold spring water
- 3 lbs. of loaf sugar per gallon of liquor
- 1/2 oz. of isinglass per gallon of liquor
- rind of 1 lemon per gallon of liquor

## Directions

1. Gather the rhubarb about the middle of May; wipe it with a wet cloth, and with a mallet, bruise it in a large wooden tub
2. When reduced to a pulp, weigh it, and to every 5 lbs. add 1 gallon of cold spring water
3. Let these remain for 3 days, stirring 3 or 4 times a day
4. On the fourth day, press the pulp through a hair sieve; put the liquor into a tub, and to every gallon put 3 lbs. of loaf sugar
5. Stir in the sugar until it is quite dissolved, and add the lemon-rind
6. Let the liquor remain, and in 4, 5, or 6 days, the fermentation will begin to subside, and a crust or head will be formed
7. Skim off the crust, or draw the liquor from it, when the crust begins to crack or separate
8. Put the wine into a cask, and if it ferments, rack it off into another cask, and in a fortnight stop it down
9. If the wine should have lost any of its original sweetness, add a little more loaf sugar, taking care that the cask is full
10. Bottle it off in February or March, and in the summer it should be fit to drink
