## Ingredients

- Beef
- Water
- Carrots
- Turnips
- Suet dumplings (optional)

## Directions

1. After the joint has been in salt 5 or 6 days, it will be ready for use.
2. Wash the meat, and if too salt, soak it for a few hours, changing the water once or twice, till the required freshness is obtained.
3. Put into a saucepan or boiling-pot sufficient water to cover the meat.
4. Set it over the fire, and when it boils, plunge in the joint and let it boil up quickly.
5. Draw the pot to the side of the fire, and let the process be very gradual, as the water must only simmer, or the meat will be hard and tough.
6. Carefully remove the scum from the surface of the water, and continue doing this for a few minutes after it first boils.
7. Boil for 2-1/2 hours if the aitch-bone is 10 lbs., or 4 hours if 20 lbs.
8. Garnish with a few of the carrots and turnips, and serve the remainder in a vegetable-dish.
