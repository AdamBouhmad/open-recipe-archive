## Ingredients

- Turkey poult
- butter
- flour
- water-cresses (optional)
- gravy

## Directions

1. Choose a plump bird, and truss it in the following manner: After it has been carefully plucked, drawn, and singed, skin the neck, and fasten the head under the wing; turn the legs at the first joint, and bring the feet close to the thighs, as a woodcock should be trussed, and do not stuff it.
2. Put it down to a bright fire, keep it well basted, and at first place a piece of paper on the breast to prevent its taking too much colour.
3. About 10 minutes before serving, dredge it lightly with flour, and baste well; when nicely frothed, send it to table immediately, with a little gravy in the dish, and some in a tureen.
4. If at hand, a few water-cresses may be placed round the turkey as a garnish, or it may be larded.
5. Time: About 1 hour. Sufficient for 6 or 7 persons.
