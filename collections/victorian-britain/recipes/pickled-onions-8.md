## Ingredients

- 1 gallon of pickling onions
- salt and water
- milk
- 1/2 gallon of vinegar
- 1 oz. of bruised ginger
- 1/4 teaspoonful of cayenne
- 1 oz. of allspice
- 1 oz. of whole black pepper
- 1/4 oz. of whole nutmeg bruised
- 8 cloves
- 1/4 oz. of mace
- 2 large spoonfuls of salt
- olive-oil

## Directions

1. Gather the onions, which should not be too small, when they are quite dry and ripe; wipe off the dirt, but do not pare them
2. Make a strong solution of salt and water, into which put the onions, and change this, morning and night, for 3 days, and save the last brine they were put in
3. Take the outside skin off, and put them into a tin saucepan capable of holding them all
4. Take equal quantities of milk and the last salt and water the onions were in, and pour this to them; to this add 2 large spoonfuls of salt, put them over the fire, and watch them very attentively
5. Keep constantly turning the onions about with a wooden skimmer, those at the bottom to the top, and vice versa; and let the milk and water run through the holes of the skimmer
6. Remember, the onions must never boil, or, if they do, they will be good for nothing; and they should be quite transparent
7. Keep the onions stirred for a few minutes, and, in stirring them, be particular not to break them
8. Turn the onions into a pan with a colander to drain, covering them with a cloth to keep in the steam
9. Place on a table an old cloth, 2 or 3 times double; put the onions on it when quite hot, and over them an old piece of blanket; cover this closely over them, to keep in the steam
10. Let them remain till the next day, when they will be quite cold, and look yellow and shrivelled; take off the shrivelled skins, when they should be as white as snow
11. Put them in a pan, make a pickle of vinegar and the remaining ingredients, boil all these up, and pour hot over the onions in the pan
12. Cover very closely to keep in all the steam, and let them stand till the following day, when they will be quite cold
13. Put them into jars or bottles well bunged, and a tablespoonful of the best olive-oil on the top of each jar or bottle
14. Tie them down with bladder, and let them stand in a cool place for a month or six weeks, when they will be fit for use
