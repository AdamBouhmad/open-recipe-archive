## Ingredients

- 2 calf's feet
- 6 pints of water
- 6 oz. of loaf sugar
- shells and well-whisked whites of 5 eggs

## Directions

1. Procure 2 nice calf's feet from the butcher's
2. Scald them, to take off the hair
3. Slit them in two, remove the fat from between the claws, and wash the feet well in warm water
4. Put them into a stewpan, with cold water, bring it gradually to boil, and remove every particle of scum as it rises
5. When it is well skimmed, boil it very gently for 6 or 7 hours, or until the liquor is reduced rather more than half
6. Strain it through a sieve into a basin, and put it in a cool place to set
7. Measure the liquor to ascertain the proportion for the jelly
8. Carefully remove all the fat from the top, pour over a little warm water to wash away any that may remain, and wipe the jelly with a clean cloth
9. Remove the jelly from the sediment, put it into a saucepan, and add 6 oz. of loaf sugar, the shells and well-whisked whites of 5 eggs
10. Stir these ingredients together cold
11. Set the saucepan on the fire, but do not stir the jelly after it begins to warm
12. Let it boil about 10 minutes after it rises to a head, then throw in a teacupful of cold water
13. Let it boil 5 minutes longer, then take the saucepan off, cover it closely, and let it remain 1/2 hour near the fire
14. Dip the jelly-bag into hot water, wring it out quite dry, and fasten it on to a stand or the back of a chair, which must be placed near the fire
15. Place a basin underneath to receive the jelly
16. Pour it into the bag, and should it not be clear the first time, run it through the bag again
