## Ingredients

- The remains of cold roast fowls
- 3 shalots
- 2 blades of mace
- a faggot of savoury herbs
- 2 or three slices of lean ham
- 1 pint of stock or water
- pepper and salt to taste
- 1 onion
- 1 dessertspoonful of flour
- 1 tablespoonful of lemon-juice
- 1/2 teaspoonful of pounded sugar
- 1 oz. of butter

## Directions

1. Cut the fowls up into neat pieces, the same as for a fricassee
2. put the trimmings into a stewpan with the shalots, mace, herbs, ham, onion, and stock (water may be substituted for this)
3. Boil it slowly for 1 hour
4. strain the liquor, and put a small piece of butter into a stewpan
5. when melted, dredge in sufficient flour to dry up the butter, and stir it over the fire
6. Put in the strained liquor, boil for a few minutes, and strain it again over the pieces of fowl
7. Squeeze in the lemon-juice, add the sugar and a seasoning of pepper and salt, make it hot, but do not allow it to boil
8. lay the fowl neatly on the dish, and garnish with croûtons
