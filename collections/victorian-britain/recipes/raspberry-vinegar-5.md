## Ingredients

- 3 pints of the best vinegar
- 4-1/2 pints of freshly-gathered raspberries
- 1 lb. of pounded loaf sugar per pint of liquor
- 1 wineglassful of brandy per pint of liquor

## Directions

1. Let the raspberries be freshly gathered; pick them from the stalks, and put 1-1/2 pint of them into a stone jar
2. Pour 3 pints of the best vinegar over them, and let them remain for 24 hours
3. Strain the liquor over another 1-1/2 pint of fresh raspberries
4. Let them remain another 24 hours, and the following day repeat the process for the third time
5. Drain off the liquor without pressing, and pass it through a jelly-bag (previously wetted with plain vinegar), into a stone jar
6. Add to every pint of the liquor 1 lb. of pounded loaf sugar; stir them together
7. When the sugar is dissolved, cover the jar; set it upon the fire in a saucepan of boiling water, and let it boil for an hour, removing the scum as fast as it rises
8. Add to each pint a glass of brandy, bottle it, and seal the corks
