## Ingredients

- butter
- bread slices
- very ripe peaches
- fine sugar

## Directions

1. Butter the bottom of a baking pan or fireproof platter
2. Cut bread slices as wide as possible and as long as the pan or platter
3. Place the bread slices on the bottom of the pan so they cover it
4. Take very ripe peaches, open them in half, and cover the bread slices with them, placing the skin side touching the bread and the interior of the fruit facing upward and uncovered
5. Put fine sugar and a piece of butter on each peach half
6. Place the pan over two gentle fires
7. There must be more heat from above than below, renewing the fires as often as necessary
8. Dust the peaches with sugar many times, since this fruit is very acidic
9. Once cooked, serve the picatostes lightly, serving them on a platter in the same manner as they were in the pan, avoiding them sticking together
10. They should equally be sprinkled with the juice found at the bottom of the pan
11. Serve them hot
