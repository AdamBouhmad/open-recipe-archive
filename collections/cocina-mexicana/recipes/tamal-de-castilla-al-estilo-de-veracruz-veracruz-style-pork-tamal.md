## Ingredients

- one real of masa (corn dough) for tortillas
- broth from two reals of pork meat mixed with ears and ribs
- salt to taste
- six ounces of lard
- one hoja santa or epazote leaf
- three types of chiles: ancho, pasilla, and mulato (one tlaco of each)
- eight to ten tomatoes
- lard for frying
- pieces of hoja santa or epazote

## Directions

1. Mix the masa with the broth where the pork has been cooked, season with sufficient salt and add six ounces of lard
2. Cook while stirring constantly so it does not stick
3. Test the masa and when cooked, remove it; the consistency should be slightly thicker than ponche
4. Add one hoja santa or epazote leaf to the masa
5. Take three types of chiles, one tlaco of each (ancho, pasilla, and mulato), remove veins and toast them very well, then grind
6. Fry eight to ten tomatoes in lard, then grind and combine with the chile
7. Fry well and add the meat and a little of its broth, with pieces of hoja santa or epazote
8. In a large casserole greased with much lard, layer the masa, then meat and mole, alternating until finished, ensuring the last layer is masa
9. Leave some mole without meat to place on top
10. Place on a well-heated burner with a comal with fire on top
11. Insert a popote (straw) to test; when it comes out clean, it is done
12. Remove and let rest all night
13. In the morning, reheat in the same manner; if made during the day, do so very early so it can rest
