## Ingredients

- pork blood
- salt
- four ounces of rice
- water
- six broad chiles
- ginger
- black pepper
- caraway
- onion
- pork mesentery and diaphragm
- pork intestines

## Directions

1. Receive the pig's blood in a suitable bowl with a little salt, stirring by hand at the same time until it cools so it remains liquid and clean without straining
2. Wash four ounces of rice, tie it in a cloth napkin, and hang it inside a pot with water so that the rice bundle does not touch the bottom
3. Let it boil until the rice is half-cooked, then remove and let it drain
4. Devein and toast six broad chiles, then grind them until they become fine powder
5. Do the same with a little ginger, black pepper, and caraway
6. Chop a good amount of onion very finely in the raw state
7. Blanch it in a small basket or strainer by pouring boiling water over it so it passes through to the other side, leaving the onion blanched
8. Also chop the pork mesentery and diaphragm
9. Once everything is prepared with the necessary anticipation, mix it with the already cool blood, seasoning with the corresponding salt
10. With this mixture fill the thick pork intestines that have been well cleaned without removing the fat
11. Tie to leave the sausages the size desired and place to cook in boiling water
12. Eat them fried or grilled with a good chile sauce, or with another appropriate sauce if wine is to be drunk over them
