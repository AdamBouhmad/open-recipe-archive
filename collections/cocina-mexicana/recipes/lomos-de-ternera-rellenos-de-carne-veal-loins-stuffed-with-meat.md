## Directions

1. Slice the small inner loins that they call for roasting, and rubbed them with lemon and salt, leaving them thus for the rest of the day
2. At night, place them in water, and the following morning, remove them from it, drain them, pat them dry and coat them with lard
3. For the filling, fry in lard with salt, garlic, onions, tomatoes and green chiles, all chopped, and season with ground clove, pepper, cinnamon and ginger
4. Add chopped parsley, a little broth, slices of chorizo, longaniza, sausage, ham, chopped hard-boiled egg, capers, large capers, olives, chopped pickled chiles, raisins and almonds
5. After frying all these things, well seasoned and dry, spread and arrange them on the loins to be wrapped, trying to keep their original form and tie them with twine or thread
6. Place them in a dish, and if it is all in one single piece, which is best, the dish should be deep and compact to preserve its shape
7. Add salt, water, wine, vinegar and lard, and place them to cook over two fires, being careful to turn them and that water does not run out so they are well cooked
8. After the broth is consumed, let them fry in their own grease, removing from the fire when they are done
9. Let them cool, whether they be eaten the same day or the next, and untying them and removing the twine, slice them
10. For serving, prepare a sauce: fry garlic and onions chopped very fine in lard, then finely chopped cooked and ground tomatoes mixed with almonds
11. Season everything with salt, clove, pepper and cinnamon powder, and add a little broth, vinegar, raisins and oil, and when removing from the fire add cured olives and cured chiles
