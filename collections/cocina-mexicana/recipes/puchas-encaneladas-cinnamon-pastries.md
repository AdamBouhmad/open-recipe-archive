## Ingredients

- one pound flour
- thirty-four egg yolks and three egg whites, beaten separately until stiff, then combined
- one ounce sifted sugar
- one ounce melted and cooled butter
- one small bowl of good Málaga wine
- additional flour if needed
- boiling water
- very thick syrup mixed with abundant cinnamon powder

## Directions

1. Mix the flour with the beaten egg yolks and whites
2. Add the sifted sugar, melted butter, and Málaga wine
3. Incorporate everything and add more flour if necessary, kneading the dough so it is neither too hard nor too soft
4. Cut and shape the puchas
5. Let them air-dry a little
6. Drop them into boiling water and remove them as soon as they rise to the surface
7. As they are removed from the water, cover them with napkins or cloths, being careful they do not stick together
8. Once the water has dried, pat them dry, place them on baking sheets, and bake in the oven
9. After baking, cover them again with napkins
10. When cooled, coat them with very thick syrup mixed with abundant cinnamon powder
11. Return the coated puchas to a warm oven to dry
