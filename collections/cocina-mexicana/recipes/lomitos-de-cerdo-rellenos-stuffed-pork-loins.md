## Ingredients

- large pork loin
- pork meat
- tomato
- xitomate
- garlic
- parsley
- clove
- cinnamon
- pepper
- vinegar
- salt
- ham pieces
- cooked egg
- choncitos meat
- longaniza meat
- olives
- small green chiles
- thyme
- rosemary
- bay leaves
- white wine
- water
- toasted almond shells
- bread
- butter

## Directions

1. Cut a large loin into pieces of one eighth in length and thin them like cecina
2. Mince pork meat with tomato, xitomate, garlic, and parsley; add ground clove, cinnamon, and pepper, a little vinegar, salt, small pieces of ham and cooked egg, cooked choncitos and longaniza meat, olives, and small chiles; mix everything well
3. Fill the loin pieces with this hash so they are not too bulky
4. Tie with thread or cord and place in a pot with minced tomato, xitomate, garlic, and parsley; few whole green chiles, a sprig of thyme, one of rosemary, and three or four bay leaves; a little vinegar, half a quarter of white wine, and enough water to cover the loins
5. Season with salt if needed, ground clove, cinnamon, and pepper, and some whole peppercorns
6. Cover the pot with paper and place a small appropriate pot with water on top
7. When the loins are cooked, empty everything into a pot, separating the thyme, rosemary, and bay leaf, and allowing the loins to brown over two fires
8. Serve with sauce made from toasted almond shells, a slice of bread fried and browned in lard, clove, cinnamon, and pepper, all ground and later fried in lard
