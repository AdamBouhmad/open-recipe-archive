## Ingredients

- half pound of flour
- three eggs
- half a tablespoon of lard
- a bit of salt
- a bit of tequesquite water
- one pound of butter
- flour for dusting
- another half pound of flour
- butter and lard for brushing
- sugar

## Directions

1. On a very clean surface place half pound of flour; add three eggs, half a tablespoon of lard, a bit of salt and a bit of tequesquite water
2. Knead everything until it forms eyes and extend with a rolling tool making the dough as thin as possible
3. Take one pound of butter that should have been in water to harden and make small balls with it, placing them over half the dough, covering with the other half as if making a quesadilla
4. Repeat this operation until the size of the quesadilla becomes very small
5. Extend again with the rolling tool and to prevent it from sticking to the dough, dust flour on another pile of half pound of flour and repeat the same operation with the balls until everything is finished
6. Extend again with the rolling tool, leaving the dough the thickness of a finger, a little more or less
7. Cut with molds, and extend the scraps again with the rolling tool without touching them as they will spoil
8. Bake in the oven, and when they begin to rise, brush with butter and lard using a feather, then dust with sugar powder to form little crusts
9. When serving, dust more powdered sugar on top
