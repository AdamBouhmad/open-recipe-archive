## Ingredients

- Six dried ancho chiles per chicken
- Garlic
- Clove
- Pepper
- Lard
- Pork loin
- Chorizos
- Chicken broth or water
- Salt
- Twelve ounces sesame seeds
- Four ounces almonds with shell
- Just over half an eggshell of coriander egg
- Two jars of cooked tomatoes
- Two jars of xitomates
- Cinnamon
- Clove
- Pepper
- Two roasted garlic cloves
- Sherry wine
- Oregano
- Sugar
- One well-toasted tortilla
- Cinnamon
- Clove
- Three peppercorns
- Lard or pork loin fat

## Directions

1. Devein and wash six dried ancho chiles well for each chicken
2. Clean garlic, grind together the chiles and garlic with clove and pepper very well
3. Fry the ground mixture in a little lard with two pieces of pork loin and some chorizos
4. Add chicken broth or water with sufficient salt
5. Place the chickens in the broth and cook in that stock, adding more if it reduces too much
6. When the meat is very tender, add a bottle of sherry wine and a little oregano, and let the stock reduce so the chickens can brown in the remaining fat
7. Toast the cleaned chiles and separately soak some
8. Toast twelve ounces of sesame seeds, four ounces of almonds with shell, and just over half an eggshell of coriander egg
9. Grind two jars of cooked tomatoes and two of xitomates, adding cinnamon, clove, little pepper, and two roasted and dry-ground garlic cloves
10. Test grinding by placing a small amount on an inverted plate to check for any skin, regrinding if necessary to avoid cutting the clemole
11. Grind in the following order: first the toasted and soaked chiles, then the tomatoes, then the xitomates, and lastly the spices
12. In a pot with just enough water to skim the chickens, place the chickens with a piece of pork loin
13. After skimming, pour the chile and all ground ingredients, stirring well, and let boil for one or two hours on low heat, stirring constantly and not leaving the spoon in to prevent it from breaking
14. Leave in the open air overnight to prevent souring
15. The next morning put on low heat to cook, and when boiling add half an ounce of sugar, removing the chickens when tender and setting aside to avoid them falling apart
16. Finally grind a well-toasted tortilla with a little cinnamon, one clove, and three peppercorns, adding this to the clemole one hour before serving
17. Return the chickens to cook and give one boil, and if the pork loins are fatty, add only half a pound of lard
18. Set aside this fat to distribute over the plates when bringing to the table
