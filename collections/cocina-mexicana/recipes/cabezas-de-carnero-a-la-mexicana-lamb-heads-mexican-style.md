## Ingredients

- Lamb heads
- Salt
- Wide chiles, soaked
- Garlic
- Cumin
- Lard or oil
- Lettuce
- Radishes
- Small chiles
- Olives
- Onion
- Pasilla chiles, soaked
- Pulque or water

## Directions

1. Skin the lamb heads and boil them in salted water until well cooked.
2. Split the skull with a machete so that the brains can be removed after roasting.
3. Coat with an adobo made from soaked wide chiles, garlic, and cumin, all ground and seasoned with salt.
4. Spread a small amount of lard on top of the chile and place in the oven on metal sheets, turning frequently so they brown on all sides.
5. Serve hot over lettuce cut into quarters, decorated with radishes, small chiles, olives, and chopped onion.
6. Serve separately a sauce made from soaked wide and pasilla chiles ground with pulque (or water with a little oil) in the molcajete, seasoned with salt, pieces of aged cheese, small chiles, and olives.
7. Alternatively, they can be roasted in the oven without the chile coating, coating with lard after cooking and sprinkling with salt and pepper.
