## Ingredients

- Three pounds of scraps or leftovers of meat, such as beef trimmings from the neck, breast, loins, leg, shanks, or ribs, or remains of poultry
- Some carrots
- Some onions
- A small bunch of parsley and small onions
- Three bay leaves
- Three cloves
- Water or broth
- Salt
- Butter
- Wheat flour
- Mushrooms

## Directions

1. Place three pounds of meat scraps in a saucepan with some carrots, onions, a small bunch of parsley and small onions, three bay leaves, and three cloves
2. Add one ladle of water in place of broth and place the saucepan over fairly strong heat
3. Skim the small amount of broth well, preventing the meat from sticking when the broth has been consumed
4. Fill the saucepan almost completely with water if broth is not available, adding the necessary salt
5. Bring the sauce to a boil, skimming it immediately
6. Place the saucepan on the edge of the stove so the sauce simmers gently for about two hours
7. When the meat is cooked, strain the broth through a silk sieve
8. Make a white substance by melting butter in a saucepan and adding as much wheat flour as it can absorb, stirring constantly over the fire without letting it take color until it is well heated
9. Add mushrooms to this white substance, stirring for ten minutes
10. Add the broth in which the meat was cooked, being careful that the substance dissolves gradually to prevent lumps from forming
11. Bring the sauce to a boil, skim it, and place it in a corner of the stove to continue simmering gently for an hour and a half
12. Remove the fat and pass it through a fine sieve, ensuring it does not take on color, as it is better the whiter it remains
