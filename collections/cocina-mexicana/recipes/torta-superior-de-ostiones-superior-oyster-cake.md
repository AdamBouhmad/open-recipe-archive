## Ingredients

- oysters
- xitomates
- garlic
- green chiles
- parsley
- alcaparras
- cloves
- pepper
- vinegar
- beaten eggs
- saffron
- lard
- raisins
- almonds

## Directions

1. Soak oysters from the evening in very hot water, keeping them until the next day
2. Clean oysters of the dark mass in the middle
3. Chop finely with xitomates, garlic, green chiles and parsley
4. Fry the mixture
5. Add the chopped oysters in a casserole with alcaparras, cloves, pepper and a little vinegar
6. When half-cooked, mix with three or four beaten eggs
7. Add a little saffron, ensuring there is no broth to split the cake
8. Separate whites and yolks of sufficient eggs according to the amount of picadillo
9. Heat enough lard to set the cake on the bottom
10. Pour the picadillo into half the beaten eggs, being careful not to mix
11. Add raisins, almonds, parsley and a little egg so the cake is covered on top
12. When the lard is hot, pour in the cake
13. Check with a knife if it sets on the bottom
14. When set, move away from the fire without turning it over
15. Place a comal with fire on top
16. As soon as the egg begins to rise, pour in the remaining egg that was left in the lard
17. Once the cake is cooked on top, finish cooking over embers
18. For the sauce: grind twelve xitomates and fry with sliced garlic, green chile, parsley and salt
19. Add three or four beaten egg yolks, a little lemon and water to make a thin sauce
20. When beginning to boil, stir very quickly with a spoon, beating constantly until yolks are dissolved
21. Add alcaparras, cloves, cinnamon and pepper
22. Let boil until foam is gone and it takes an orange color
23. When ready, add saffron, parsley, raisins, almonds and one or two sliced tornachiles, without vinegar
24. Serve adorned with olives and sliced hard-boiled egg yolks
