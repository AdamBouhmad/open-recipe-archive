## Ingredients

- two pounds of rice
- a little milk
- one quarter quart of milk
- two full tablespoons of lard
- a grain of salt
- one tablespoon of lard
- a little anise
- twenty-nine eggs
- three pounds of lard
- two pounds of sugar
- two orange leaves

## Directions

1. The day before making the fritters, soak two pounds of rice
2. The next day, grind it very well, adding a little milk as you grind
3. In a pan, dissolve one quarter quart of milk, not too thick nor too loose, and two full tablespoons of lard and a grain of salt
4. Put it on the heat without stopping stirring
5. When half cooked, add another tablespoon of lard and a little anise
6. It is well cooked when it is slightly yellow, then remove from heat to cool
7. Put it on a table to knead it, and add twenty-nine eggs with whites and yolks, adding them little by little
8. Knead well, giving it many blows
9. Put a fryer with a wide bottom that covers the heat with three pounds of lard
10. When well hot, coat the palms of your hands with lard, take a ball of dough and make a hole in the middle with your finger
11. Fry them in the lard, constantly turning them and striking them from above with a small stick
12. Clarify two pounds of sugar and after straining, put it on the heat and add two orange leaves
13. When at the proper point, pour it over the fritters
