## Ingredients

- Suckling pig, cleaned and whole or in pieces
- Wide chiles, soaked and ground
- Toasted garlic
- Cumin
- Oregano
- Bread crumb, soaked in vinegar
- Water
- Raw onion, sliced
- Small chiles
- Olives

## Directions

1. Make the adobo with soaked and ground wide chiles, toasted garlic, cumin, and oregano (which can be omitted if desired), and a piece of bread crumb soaked in vinegar, adding sufficient water so that after the meat is cooked, the broth becomes thick.
2. Place the clean whole suckling pig, or in pieces if it is large, in this adobo and let it cure for six, twelve, or twenty-four hours, according to its size.
3. It can be served just after cooking; but it is better to let the fire consume the broth enough so it is not dry, and then serve it hot with raw sliced onion, small chiles, and olives on top.
