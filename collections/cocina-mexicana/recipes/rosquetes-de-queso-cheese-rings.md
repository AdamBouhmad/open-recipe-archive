## Ingredients

- Fresh cheese, not buttery
- Seven egg yolks per pound of cheese
- A little flour
- Cinnamon
- Sugar

## Directions

1. Choose fresh cheese that does not form strings when cooked
2. Crumble it and soak in water to remove salt
3. Strain through a sieve and let drain until dry
4. Grind the cheese
5. Mix seven egg yolks with each pound of cheese and a little flour so the dough is not too hard
6. To test consistency, coat the palm of the hand with butter, take a bit of dough and make a hole in the middle with your finger
7. Fry it; if it does not spread, rise, or burst, it has good consistency; otherwise add more flour until it is right
8. Form rosquetes like the test piece, or extrude them through a piping bag and fry
9. Boil them in clarified syrup made from two pounds of sugar, boiled with cinnamon and brought to medium consistency
10. When the rosquetes are well soaked in the syrup and dry, arrange on plates and decorate with pearl sugar, cinnamon powder, and anise seeds
11. They can also be left covered with syrup after boiling it more, in which case decorate only with cinnamon
