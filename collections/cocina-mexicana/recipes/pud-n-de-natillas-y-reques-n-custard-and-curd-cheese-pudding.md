## Ingredients

- Milk
- Considerable sugar
- Small strips of cinnamon
- Sixteen egg yolks beaten
- Two cups well filled with custard
- Ten ounces of curd cheese from cow milk
- Twelve hard biscuits
- Four ounces of clean almonds
- Raisins
- Pine nuts
- Pieces of almond
- Pieces of walnut
- Candied citron
- More sugar if necessary
- Butter

## Directions

1. Sweeten the milk on the fire with considerable sugar
2. Add small strips of cinnamon and let it boil until it thickens
3. Remove from fire, strain and let cool
4. Mix three cups of milk with sixteen beaten egg yolks
5. Add two cups well filled with custard
6. Add ten ounces of curd cheese from cow milk, twelve hard biscuits and four ounces of clean almonds, all ground
7. Add raisins, pine nuts, pieces of almond, pieces of walnut, candied citron, and more sugar if necessary
8. Stir everything with a spoon
9. If it is too loose add biscuit; if too firm add some beaten egg yolks
10. Have a small stove ready with much fire and well lit with a comal on top, which should already be hot when the paste is made
11. Turn the paste and pour it into a pan well coated with butter, so that it does not fill and the paste reaches only halfway up its height
12. Quickly remove the coals from the stove, arrange the pan, cover it with the hot comal and sufficient fire, and let the pudding cook
13. Be careful that it does not burn
14. When a popote comes out clean, you will know it is cooked
15. Remove and leave until the next day
16. The next day, bring it near the heat of a small stove to melt the congealed fat so that the pudding detaches from the pan, and place it on a plate to be taken to the table
