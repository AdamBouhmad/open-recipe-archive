## Ingredients

- Bergamot pears, ripe and without cloves
- Sugar, six pounds, clarified and strained
- Water
- Ground almonds, one pound
- Custard without composition, two to three cuartillos
- Coconut paste
- Brick or stones
- Comal (griddle)

## Directions

1. Select ripe bergamot pears without cloves, peel them and hollow them out, removing the core and more
2. Weigh the pears to make six pounds and place them in warm honey and water made with six pounds of clarified and strained sugar
3. Boil for two or three hours, then set aside and keep for the next day, repeating the same operation the following day and again on the third day
4. On the third day, when they have boiled a little, bring the honey to half point and remove the pears, placing them on a platter to drain
5. To the honey remaining in the pot, add one pound of ground almonds and two to three cuartillos of custard without composition, allowing it to reach the point of cajeta
6. Cover the pears with this mixture after filling them with coconut paste
7. Place the platter on the ground, leaving it hollow so it does not burst, and form a tlecuilli with bricks or stones on top
8. Place a comal with heat on top so it browns on the upper part
