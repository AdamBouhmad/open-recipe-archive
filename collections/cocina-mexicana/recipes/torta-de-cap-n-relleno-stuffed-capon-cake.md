## Ingredients

- capon
- salt
- water
- longaniza or sausage
- butter
- garlic
- onions
- tomatoes
- poblano chiles or tornachiles
- eggs
- raisins
- almonds
- olives
- capers
- ham
- bread

## Directions

1. Cook the capon in salted water
2. Prepare a mixture by slicing cooked longaniza or sausage and frying it in butter with salt, minced garlic, onions, tomatoes, and poblano chiles or tornachiles in strips
3. After the seasoning has sautéed in butter, add the longaniza or sausage and beaten eggs so that as it sets and dries it forms the mixture, to which add raisins, almonds, olives, capers and ham pieces
4. Arrange the capon's legs so they do not slip and straighten its wings which should remain in natural position
5. Fry the capon in butter with ground garlic, removing from the frying when browned
6. In a proportionally sized pan or casserole well lined with butter, pour a few beaten eggs, then place a layer of fried toasts on which to arrange the capon, covering the eggs with compound picadillo
7. Between the casserole and the picadillo, pour beaten eggs with a spoon so that as they set, everything is bound together
