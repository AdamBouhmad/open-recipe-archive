## Ingredients

- half a pound of flour
- 3 egg yolks
- sifted sugar, as much as comes out
- a little melted and cooled lard
- generous wine as needed to moisten the dough
- generous wine (for filling)
- clove and cinnamon powder
- ground mamón (a type of bread)
- raisins
- almonds
- walnuts
- pine nuts
- toasted sesame
- pieces of candied pumpkin or citron
- butter

## Directions

1. Make a ring with half a pound of flour on a table
2. Into the center put 3 egg yolks, sifted sugar, a little melted and cooled lard, and generous wine as needed to moisten
3. The dough should be tender; extend it with a rolling pin
4. Cut very thin tortillas the size of the dish in which it will be made
5. Butter the dish and place 3 or 4 of these tortillas on the bottom, also buttered
6. For the filling: make a paste away from heat with generous wine syrup, generous wine, clove and cinnamon powder, ground mamón, raisins, almonds, walnuts, pine nuts, and toasted sesame
7. Season and make it somewhat thick; place on the tortillas
8. Arrange according to taste, flat or elevated, and stud with more raisins, almonds, walnuts, pine nuts, and pieces of candied pumpkin or citron
9. Place 2 or 3 more very thin tortillas on top and seal at the edge of the dish
10. Decorate with figures and designs made of the same dough
11. Brush with sufficient butter using feathers and put in a well-tempered oven
12. As soon as it is done, before cooling, sprinkle sifted sugar or small candies on the designs on the upper side
13. Note: Adding cooked bone marrow to the filling gives it another flavor
