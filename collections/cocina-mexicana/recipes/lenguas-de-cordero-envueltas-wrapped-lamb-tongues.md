## Ingredients

- Dozen lamb tongues
- Twenty-four mushrooms, selected and clean
- Parsley
- Shallots
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Nutmeg
- Four ounces of butter
- One glass of white wine
- Breadcrumbs
- Thin slices of pork loin
- Warm water
- Tomato sauce or pepper sauce

## Directions

1. Cook a dozen lamb tongues in a pan after soaking in water and removing the ganyote.
2. Once cooked, remove the skins and place on a plate to drain, covering with a pot lid and plate to shape them uniformly.
3. When cool, divide in half lengthwise while keeping the two halves united, forming small costillas.
4. Prepare the filling by finely chopping twenty-four selected, clean mushrooms with parsley, shallots, salt, coarse pepper, and nutmeg.
5. Cook this filling in a pot with four ounces of butter and one glass of white wine until almost no broth remains.
6. Add a little grated breadcrumbs to the filling.
7. Spread thin slices of cooked and flattened pork loin on a napkin after soaking in warm water.
8. Place each tongue with its filling on the pork slices and wrap.
9. Ten minutes before serving, place on a grill to brown.
10. Arrange on a plate in the form of a crown and serve with tomato or pepper sauce.
