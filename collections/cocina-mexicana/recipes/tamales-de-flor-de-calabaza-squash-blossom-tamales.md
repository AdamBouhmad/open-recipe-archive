## Ingredients

- Half almud of corn
- Lard
- Two or three egg yolks
- Anise
- Salt
- Cold water
- Squash blossoms
- Epazote
- Fresh green chiles
- Corn leaves

## Directions

1. For half almud of corn, prepare nixtamal as for atole
2. Wash much, dry slowly in the sun, and grind
3. Sift through an ayate
4. Take the amount of lard according to the quantity of corn
5. Place the lard in a cazuela and with it two or three egg yolks, beat very well
6. Add the sifted corn, boil a little anise with salt and add to the dough when it is lukewarm
7. Stir much and if it is thick add a little cold water and keep beating
8. If it needs salt add it, and to know if it is well beaten, put a little bit in water and if it stays on top it is done
9. Add plenty of squash blossoms, plenty of stripped epazote, green chiles of the season without veins and split
10. Mix everything and form the tamales the size desired
11. Take a pot and place some small sticks in it like supports, and on top some leaves of the same tamales
12. Add water until it reaches the sticks and place on heat
13. Once boiling place the tamales, cover the opening with a napkin and a plate and tie it
14. To know if they are cooked, unwrap one and if it does not stick to the leaf they are done
