## Ingredients

- one loin of veal
- one piece of pork meat
- one loaf of bread soaked
- sufficient salt
- coarse and fine pepper
- onion chopped and sautéed with butter
- half of fat ham chopped very finely
- a little melted bone marrow
- capers
- eight egg yolks
- four egg whites
- four ounces of fried lard
- four ounces of butter melted in the lard
- eight ounces of butter
- one tablespoon of flour
- a little broth from the pot without chili
- pepper
- chopped parsley
- capers
- salt

## Directions

1. Take one loin of veal and one piece of pork meat, and after cleaning both well, chop and grind them
2. Place the mixture in a casserole and stir in one soaked loaf of bread, sufficient salt, coarse and fine pepper, chopped onion sautéed with butter, half of finely chopped fat ham, a little melted bone marrow and capers
3. Mix in eight egg yolks and four egg whites
4. Beat four more egg whites and add them to the meat, mixing well
5. Grease a mold with lard, dust it with breadcrumbs, empty the paste into it and cook for three hours in a pot of water, being careful that the water does not penetrate the mold
6. Remove at mealtime and serve hot with the following sauce
7. For the sauce: fry eight ounces of butter in a sauce dish, brown one tablespoon of flour there and dilute with a little broth from the pot but without chili, ensuring it reaches a regular thickness
8. Add a pinch of pepper, chopped parsley, capers and salt; let everything boil so it seasons well
9. This pudding can also be eaten cold, and the next day the leftover can be sliced and fried
