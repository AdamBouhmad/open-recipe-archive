## Ingredients

- a piece of beef
- bacon for larding
- salt
- pepper
- white wine
- a piece of veal knuckle without bone
- a bouquet of fine herbs
- onion, chopped
- two whole cloves
- broth
- four to six carrots, cut into pieces

## Directions

1. Lard the beef with bacon and season well on all sides with salt and pepper.
2. Two hours before cooking, soak the meat in white wine.
3. After one hour, turn the piece so the white wine penetrates both sides.
4. Place the prepared meat in a pot with a piece of veal knuckle without bone, a bouquet of fine herbs, plenty of chopped onion, and two whole cloves.
5. Pour one cup of broth over everything and let cook for one hour over low heat.
6. Turn the meat, add the remaining white wine in which the meat was soaked, along with four to six carrots cut into pieces, and continue cooking.
7. Use a pot that is not too large so the meat remains always covered with the cooking liquid.
8. This dish is as good cold and even better than hot. If you wish to serve it cold, it is best to prepare it the day before.
9. Once prepared, arrange it circularly on the platter, arranging the carrot pieces around it; then pour the sauce over it, which will soon become a jelly. Prepared this way it is an exquisite and elegant dish.
