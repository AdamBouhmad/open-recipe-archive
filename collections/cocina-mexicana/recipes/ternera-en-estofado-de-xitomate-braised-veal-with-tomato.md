## Ingredients

- Veal loins
- Pieces of fat ham
- Sliced garlic
- Clean garlic
- Tomatoes (xitomate)
- Tomatoes
- Onions
- Clove
- Cinnamon
- Black pepper
- Cumin
- Lard
- Tomato (xitomate)
- Thyme
- Laurel (bay leaf)
- Salt
- Cooking oil
- Lard
- Nutmeg shavings
- Oregano
- Embers
- Olives
- Tornachiles
- Parsley
- Fried potatoes in butter

## Directions

1. Grease a pot with lard
2. Slice veal loins and stud them with pieces of fat ham and slices of garlic
3. Slice clean garlic, sufficient tomatoes, and onions, and grind dry clove, cinnamon, pepper and a few cumin seeds
4. Place in the greased pot a layer of tomato and more ingredients, dusting with a little of the ground spices
5. Then place another layer of slices in the meat, and add a little salt
6. On this layer place another of the ingredients, dusted with the spices, adding thyme, laurel and a little salt, and in this way alternate the layers until finishing in one of ingredients
7. Over it pour cooking oil and about half less lard, and cover the pot with a new cazuela, sealing the joints with paste and paper
8. Bury the pot in embers up to the neck, and also put charcoal inside the cazuela, putting in charcoal from time to time to maintain the fire
9. Leave the pot there until nighttime, when it is placed over the steam of another pot that is boiling, and thus it is maintained until the next day when it is served
10. After cooking, place the covered pot over the steam of another pot at the fire, stirring it from time to time until it is time to serve, and then arrange the platter with olives, tornachiles, chopped parsley and sliced potatoes fried in butter
