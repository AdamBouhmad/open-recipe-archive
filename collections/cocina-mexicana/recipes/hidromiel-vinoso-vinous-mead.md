## Ingredients

- one and a half jars of spring or river water
- four quartillos of the purest white honey
- one ounce of refined brandy

## Directions

1. Bring the water and honey to a boil until an egg can float on the surface of the liquid, which indicates the liquid has acquired sufficient consistency to be preserved for a long time
2. Add one ounce of refined brandy
3. Fill the barrel or cask only to two-thirds of its capacity so that the liquid, becoming rarefied during fermentation, has enough space to expand
4. Cover the barrel with linen and paper, for fear it might burst from fermentation
5. Expose to the sun or place in heated stoves so that the liquid ferments more quickly and its elements are better disposed to enter into the new combinations that form the spirituous parts
6. Some may mix aromas before fermentation begins
7. Others may add juice of mulberries, prickly pear, and other fruits for different tastes and colors
8. Can be stored for ten or twelve years
