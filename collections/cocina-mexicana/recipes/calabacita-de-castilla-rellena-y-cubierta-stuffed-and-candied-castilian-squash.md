## Ingredients

- Calabacita squash
- Lime water
- Clarified syrup
- Conserve, coconut paste, almond paste, or other filling
- Cinnamon sticks

## Directions

1. Remove the skin so as not to damage the flesh, after cutting a round slice at the stem end to remove the insides and seeds, leaving the inside perfectly clean
2. Place both the squash and the round slice removed in settled lime water so they take body
3. Once this is achieved, remove and place in natural water, washing in several waters without damaging them so no taste of lye or lime remains
4. Partially cook in boiling water, remove to drain, and place in syrup that has been prepared, already clarified and hot
5. Let it boil a little in it each day for two consecutive days
6. On the third day, when the squash will be ready and the syrup at the proper point, do not put it on the fire but let it rest another two or three days
7. After these days have passed, remove the squash from the syrup, making sure it drains and dries
8. Fill it with conserve, coconut paste, almond paste, or other paste, putting as a cover the round slice that was cut from it, securing it with small cinnamon sticks
9. Cover it immediately with syrup at high point, the same as the other candied sweets, and place to dry on the wire in the sun until it forms a consistent crust
