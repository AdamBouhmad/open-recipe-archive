## Ingredients

- Soconusco cacao, 2 pounds
- Maracaybo cacao, 2 pounds
- Caracas cacao, 2 pounds
- Sugar, 4 to 6 pounds according to taste
- Cinnamon, an equal number of ounces as sugar, also according to individual preference
- Egg yolks (optional, quantity varies according to taste)

## Directions

1. The first operation is to roast the cacao, and for this it is best to substitute a sheet of tin or another thin iron plate, such as a griddle with all paint and varnish removed, for the porous comal that is generally used, since the cacao loses all its oily part in it.
2. The degree of roasting should be as for coffee, at the moment when the grain begins to release its oil, because if removed earlier, it will remain raw and discolored, giving bad appearance to the chocolate and making it indigestible; but if left on the fire longer, the grain will dry in part and communicate to the chocolate its harshness or acridity.
3. Once the cacao is roasted to the point described, sift or winnow it to separate the grain from the husk.
4. Place a brazier with good fire beneath the metate on which it is to be ground, and when the metate is already hot, begin to grind the grain, making one pass over all the quantity of it.
5. Mix the ground cacao with the sugar, crushed with a mallet, and grind the two things together.
6. At this stage, if egg yolks are to be mixed in, as many people do, and it can be done without inconvenience since it gives good flavor to the chocolate without altering it, place only the yolks, separated from the whites, on the metate, varying the quantity according to the taste of each person.
