## Ingredients

- one pound of apricot almonds
- four ounces of peach almonds
- sixteen cuartillos of refined aguardiente
- three cloves
- ten grains of white pepper
- two small sticks of cinnamon the size of a finger
- two pounds of sugar
- one cuartillo of water

## Directions

1. Pound one pound of apricot almonds and four ounces of peach almonds in a marble mortar after having soaked them for some time in cold water (not hot water as is customary) to peel them
2. Put them in infusion in sixteen cuartillos of refined aguardiente, protecting them from light and giving them a mild temperature
3. Place inside a small bag containing three cloves, ten grains of white pepper, and two small sticks of cinnamon the size of a finger, noting that the infusion must be made in a large bottle with a narrow neck or in an unglazed sandstone jug
4. After fourteen to twenty days, strain the aguardiente and mix it with two pounds of sugar dissolved in one cuartillo of water over a gentle fire, after having let it cool
5. Store in well-sealed bottles
