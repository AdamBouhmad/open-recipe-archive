## Ingredients

- Sturgeon roe, in good condition
- Water
- Fine salt
- Pepper
- Wicker brushes

## Directions

1. Take the roe of many sturgeon, ensuring they are in good condition, which is known when a small white point can be distinguished in the eggs
2. Place them in a bucket of water and remove the fibers, using wicker brushes like those used to beat egg whites
3. Beat the roe in the water, and as the fibers stick to the brush, shake them off each time you have them
4. Change the water for the roe and continue beating and changing the water until not a single fiber or mud remains, so that the eggs are clearly distinguished
5. Place them to drain in sieves and season with fine salt and pepper
6. Mix everything well and deposit in a piece of cloth, tying the four corners with thread, giving it the shape of a ball
7. Serve with toasted bread, fried or placed on the grill, and with chopped shallots
