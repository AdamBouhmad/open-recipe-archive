## Ingredients

- Pork tail
- Ham
- Other meat
- Garbanzos (chickpeas), soaked
- Fine salt
- Two heads of garlic, whole
- Two heads of onion
- Two parsnips
- One branch of mint
- Half a cabbage
- Two or three turnips, peeled
- Chorizones (large chorizos)
- One piece of longaniza (Spanish sausage)
- One or two sausages
- Twelve grains of pepper
- Twelve grains of clove
- One handful of dried coriander, toasted
- Saffron, the amount that can be grasped with three fingers

## Directions

1. Place the meat in a pot with sufficient water
2. Clean the pork tail, ham and other meat well with a knife and hot water
3. Put all meat in the pot except the ham, which should be left soaking
4. Add the soaked garbanzos and the necessary fine salt
5. Place the pot on the fire and remove all the foam it produces
6. When it has finished foaming, add two whole heads of garlic, two heads of onion, two parsnips and one branch of mint
7. After one hour of boiling with these ingredients, add half a cabbage, two or three peeled turnips, the ham, the chorizones, one piece of longaniza, and one or two sausages
8. Boil everything in vigorous fire with the pot well covered
9. When about one hour or a little more remains before serving, grind twelve grains of pepper, as many grains of clove, one handful of dried toasted coriander, and the amount of saffron that can be grasped with three fingers
10. Add all these ground spices to the pot and cover it again
11. Always use boiling water when adding liquid to the broth, as cold water makes the meat red
12. To serve, arrange the meats in groups on a platter or large plate, place the garbanzos and ham on another plate, the chorizones and sausages on another, and the vegetables on another platter
