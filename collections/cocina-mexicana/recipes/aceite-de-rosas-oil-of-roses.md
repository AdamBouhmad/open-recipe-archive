## Ingredients

- Seven pounds of Castilian rose petals
- Sixteen quartillos of common aguardiente
- Four pounds of sugar
- Six quartillos of rose distilled water
- One pound of double water
- Cochineal dye

## Directions

1. On one of the beautiful mornings of spring, strip the petals from Castilian roses and put seven pounds of petals in infusion at atmospheric temperature in sixteen quartillos of common aguardiente
2. Distill afterwards until half of the liquor is obtained
3. Dissolve four pounds of sugar in six quartillos of rose distilled water to which is added one pound of double water, that is, water as loaded as possible with the aroma of this flower
4. Mix everything exactly
5. Give it rose color with cochineal
6. Filter through a cloth sleeve
7. Keep it where light does not reach it
