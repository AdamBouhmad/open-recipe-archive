## Ingredients

- egg white
- water
- sugar

## Directions

1. Beat one egg white in half a quartillo of water for every four to five pounds of sugar; two egg whites in one quartillo of water for ten to twelve pounds of sugar, maintaining the same proportion for greater or lesser quantities
2. Bring the syrup to a boil until it rises three different times, calming the boil with a little cold water added when it rises
3. Remove from heat, let it rest and skim; add another small amount of water and boil again
4. Skim again and strain through a sieve or fine cloth over a frame
5. For purer syrup, as needed for sweetening liqueurs, after the aforementioned operations tilt the pan or vessel containing it, let it rest and decant, or in other words, separate from the sediment with the least possible movement
6. The whiter and purer the sugar employed, the less work it will take for the syrup to clarify and the better the sweets made with it will turn out
