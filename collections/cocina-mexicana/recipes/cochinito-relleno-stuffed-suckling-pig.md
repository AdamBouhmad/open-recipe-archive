## Ingredients

- One suckling pig, milk-fed
- Salt
- Lemon
- Pepper powder
- Raw garlic
- Vinegar
- Ripe xitomates (tomatoes)
- Parsley
- Onion
- Green poblano chiles, peeled and deveined
- Capers
- Lard
- Chicken breasts
- Longaniza (Spanish sausage)
- Morcillas (blood sausages)
- Chorizos
- Chorizones (large chorizos)
- Pork feet
- Mutton feet
- Ham
- Pork jowl
- Water from teques (a type of corn dough)
- Paper, thick and buttered with lard

## Directions

1. After washing the suckling pig well, remove the innards
2. Cut off the head and feet and let drain well
3. Salt all over
4. Coat with lemon and a considerable amount of pepper powder
5. Hang it and after twenty-four hours, place in a pot of boiling water
6. Remove and wash well
7. Rub inside and outside with raw garlic, ground with a little vinegar
8. Fill with the following preparation:
9. Make a picadillo as for stuffed chiles: coarsely chop many ripe xitomates, parsley, garlic and onion; cut green poblano chiles that are peeled and deveined; add capers
10. Fry all this well in lard
11. When fried, add the meat picadillo to it, add a little mild vinegar, and let it boil until it thickens
12. Make a frying of chicken breasts, longaniza, morcillas, chorizos and chorizones, all coarsely chopped, with cooked pork and mutton feet, ham and pork jowl
13. After browning this in lard with much chopped onion, fill the suckling pig with it and sew it closed
14. Coat the outside of the skin with water from teques
15. Once settled, wrap in thick papers coated with lard
16. Place in the oven at not very strong heat
17. When the meat is very tender, remove the paper and finish cooking
