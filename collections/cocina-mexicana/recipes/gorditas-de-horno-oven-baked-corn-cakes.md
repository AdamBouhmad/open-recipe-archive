## Ingredients

- Nixtamal or cacahuatzincle corn, cleaned and dried
- Corn flour (sifted)
- Sugar (sifted)
- Egg yolks (as needed for color)
- Salt
- White tequesquite water, settled
- Orange blossom water
- Water (as needed)
- Melted and cooled butter

## Directions

1. Prepare corn the same way as for atole or tamales
2. Rub and remove the tips of the dried corn, then dry in the sun
3. Grind and sift the corn into flour
4. Mix the clean flour with sifted sugar so it is not too sweet
5. Add enough egg yolks to give the dough good color
6. Add a little salt, white tequesquite water, orange blossom water, and natural water as needed to bring the flour together
7. Knead the dough with a stick, gradually adding melted and cooled butter until the dough is soft but not so soft that it falls apart
8. Place small portions on papers, separated from one another
9. Transfer to tin sheets and place in a well-tempered, not very hot oven to bake
