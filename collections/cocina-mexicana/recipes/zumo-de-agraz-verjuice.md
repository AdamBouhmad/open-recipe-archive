## Ingredients

- Agraz (unripe grapes)
- Salt
- Spirit of wine (optional, for preservation or beverages)

## Directions

1. Pick the agraz before it begins to ripen
2. Separate the grapes from the bunches
3. Remove the stems or stalks from the grapes
4. Place the grapes in a marble mortar with a little salt
5. Pound the grapes
6. Press the juice through a linen cloth by hand force or in a press
7. Filter the juice one or two times through flannel or cotton cloth, dampened and coated with flour on the napped side of the cloth, hung open
8. Pour the verjuice repeatedly through the filters so that when filtered it becomes as clean and clear as distilled or stone-filtered water
9. Prepare bottles by scrubbing them well or use new ones, which is better, so they do not smell bad or impart bad taste to the verjuice
10. Sulfur the bottles in this manner: choose a cork that fits all of them, pierce it with an iron wire, fasten it at the top of the cork and make a clasp or hook at the other end
11. Ensure the wire does not pass through the middle of the bottle
12. Attach a sulfur stick to the clasp or hook, light it and insert it into the bottles one by one
13. When the bottles have filled with vapor or smoke, remove the sulfur stick and stand them in the wine cellar or bottle rack
14. After a moment, pour the verjuice into the bottles and cork them well
15. When using the verjuice, remove the film or membrane that will have formed at the neck of the bottles
16. The verjuice can be employed in place of lemon in all compositions where the latter is used, and also for fresh beverages and punches, adding a little spirit of wine to it
17. It is good for preventing the inconveniences of falls, taking for this a glass when the accident has just occurred
