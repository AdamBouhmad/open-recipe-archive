## Ingredients

- little more than one quarter pound of flour
- three whole eggs
- a bit of salt
- half a tablespoon of lard
- a bit of tequesquite water
- one pound of butter
- sugar

## Directions

1. Spread on a table a little more than one quarter pound of flour, three whole eggs and a bit of salt, half a tablespoon of lard, a bit of tequesquite water, and work everything together until it forms eyes or blisters
2. Extend with a rolling tool to regular thickness, and begin placing small balls of butter until they cover half the dough
3. Fold over the other half as if making ravioli, fold again and roll
4. Make some holes in the dough and add the rest of the pound of flour, using up the pound of butter
5. Do not touch the dough with your hand, only with the rolling tool or a knife
6. Once well kneaded, extend to the thickness of a finger or a little less, cut with a mold and place over low heat
7. Place in a casserole over the fire, and when they begin to cook, brush the pastries with butter using feather barbs, and cover with powdered sugar
8. Cover the casserole with a comal until they finish cooking, being careful they do not brown too much
9. Once cooked, cover again with sugar
