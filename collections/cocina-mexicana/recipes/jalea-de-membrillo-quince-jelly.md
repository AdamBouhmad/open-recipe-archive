## Ingredients

- twenty ripe quinces of good size
- water (eighteen cuartillos)
- clarified syrup (equal quantity to juice)
- grana (for red color, optional)

## Directions

1. Choose twenty ripe quinces of good size, and after cleaning and dividing into pieces, put them to cook in eighteen cuartillos of water, which should be reduced to six cuartillos
2. Pour this juice and the quinces over a sieve, placing a wide earthenware pan underneath
3. Let the quinces drain there, pass the juice through a strainer and weigh it
4. Weigh an equal quantity of clarified syrup and cook until the breaking point
5. Add the quince juice and let the mixture boil until the same degree as for texocote or perón jellies
6. If a red jelly is desired, tint it with a little prepared grana, skim off foam and pour into jars
