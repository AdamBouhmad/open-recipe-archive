## Ingredients

- Young hare
- Liver
- Butter, one piece the size of an egg
- Parsley
- Small onions
- Shallots
- Garlic
- Cloves
- Laurel
- Thyme
- Savory
- Flour
- Three quartillos of broth
- One quartillo of red wine
- One tablespoon of vinegar
- Salt
- Fine whole capers, half a handful

## Directions

1. When emptying and cleaning the young hare, be very careful not to lose the blood, which is set aside separately
2. Divide the young hare into quarters, which may be larded with ham if desired
3. Place them in a pot with the liver, the butter, a bunch of parsley, small onions, shallots, garlic, cloves, laurel, thyme, and savory
4. Pass everything over the fire and add a small amount of flour
5. Moisten with three quartillos of broth, one quartillo of red wine, and one tablespoon of vinegar
6. Season with salt and let it boil until the young hare is well cooked and only very little sauce remains
7. Set aside the liver, grind it well, and mix it with the blood that was set aside
8. At the moment of serving, add the blood mixture to the stew, thickening it without boiling, as one would with egg yolks
9. Add a half handful of fine whole capers
10. Arrange the young hare on the plate
