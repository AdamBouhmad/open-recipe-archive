## Ingredients

- One beef head, cooked
- Strips of lard or ham
- Pan essence (sartenada)
- One bottle of Madeira wine
- One and a half quarts of Spanish sauce
- One bottle of dry Madeira wine
- Beef stock (caldo consumado)
- Ground pepper
- Beef quenelles
- Beef tongue
- Rooster crests and kidneys
- Small lardoons and pieces of veal sweetbread
- Eight to ten cooked egg yolks
- Twelve egg white bottoms
- Turned cucumber batons
- Turned mushrooms
- Crayfish
- Nasturtium seeds, pickled in vinegar
- Salt

## Directions

1. Cook the beef head naturally as indicated in the following article
2. Divide into pieces and garnish with strips of lard or ham
3. After garnishing, wrap all pieces in clean linen
4. Place in the proper vessel for cooking over coals with pan essence and a bottle of Madeira wine
5. Cook over two fires and let boil for three hours
6. Separately, place over fire about one and a half quarts of Spanish sauce with one bottle of dry Madeira wine and a little beef stock and ground pepper
7. Let reduce to half
8. Add to this sauce beef quenelles, sliced beef tongue, rooster crests and kidneys, small lardoons and pieces of veal sweetbread, eight to ten cooked egg yolks, twelve egg white bottoms, turned cucumber batons, turned mushrooms, crayfish, and pickled nasturtium seeds
9. Ensure the dish is very hot but not boiling, with good seasoning
10. Pour over the head and arrange in pyramid form
