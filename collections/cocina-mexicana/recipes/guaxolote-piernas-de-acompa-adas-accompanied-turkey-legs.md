## Ingredients

- Two raw turkey legs
- Beef
- Mushrooms
- Ham
- Parsley
- Onion
- Basil
- Shallots
- Salt
- Pepper
- Two egg yolks
- White wine
- Broth
- Bouquet garni
- Butter
- Flour
- Lemon juice or vinegar

## Directions

1. Place the legs in cold water to bleed, then blanch in boiling water with a small piece of beef cut into dice and a good portion of mushrooms
2. Knead everything together with grated ham, parsley, onion, basil and shallots, all chopped, salt, pepper and two egg yolks
3. Choose two raw, well-cleaned turkey legs and bone them completely, leaving only the extremity of the bone that joins them with the feet
4. Fill them with the forcemeat, sewing them closed so the filling does not escape
5. Cook over low heat, barded with ham, in a sauce made with a glass of white wine, the same amount of broth, a bouquet of parsley and onion and little salt
6. When cooked and the sauce well reduced, remove the fat, take off the ham strips and the bouquet
7. Add two spoonfuls of clear broth and a small piece of butter the size of a walnut, kneaded with a pinch of flour and a little parboiled, chopped parsley
8. Let the sauce thicken over the fire and pour over the turkey legs
9. Serve with a little lemon juice or a splash of vinegar
