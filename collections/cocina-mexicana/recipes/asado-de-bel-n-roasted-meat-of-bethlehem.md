## Ingredients

- a leg or quarter of mutton, veal, pork, or similar
- a small sprig of laurel
- oregano
- one large head of garlic
- three parts water
- one part vinegar (or more if weak)
- five sliced lemons
- salt
- ground garlic
- lard
- a little flour
- a small amount of wide chile, soaked and ground
- broth from the roast
- eating oil

## Directions

1. Soak a leg or quarter of mutton, veal, pork, etc. in water to bleed it
2. Break the bones
3. Put it to cook in a suitable pot or vessel with a small sprig of laurel, oregano, and one large head of garlic, three parts water and one part vinegar (if the vinegar is weak, increase the proportion)
4. Add five sliced lemons and the corresponding salt
5. Cover with a comal and let it boil until more than parboiled
6. Place the piece to roast at two fires or in the oven, basting it meanwhile with ground garlic mixed with lard and salt
7. For the sauce: fry some garlic in lard with a little flour and a small amount of wide chile that has been soaked and ground, adding broth from the roast, salt, and eating oil
