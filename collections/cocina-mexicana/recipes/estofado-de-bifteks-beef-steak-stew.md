## Ingredients

- thick beef steaks
- butter
- flour
- good broth
- fine herbs
- onion
- carrot
- turnip
- salt
- pepper
- flour mixed with pepper
- four or five small onions

## Directions

1. Take thicker beef steaks than those that should be cooked on grills and brown them in butter inside a pan, turning them so they take color on both sides.
2. Remove them from the pan and add to the butter it contains one tablespoon of flour; stir actively so it makes a good-colored roux, and mix with good broth, in sufficient quantity so the steaks, placed back in the pan, are completely covered.
3. When the sauce begins to boil, add a bouquet of fine herbs, one onion, one carrot, and half a turnip, all cut into very thin slices.
4. Season with salt and pepper and let cook gradually for two or three hours.
5. Half an hour before serving, add to the sauce three tablespoons of flour mixed with pepper, and four or five small onions browned separately in butter. This stew is very delicious if not overloaded with spices.
