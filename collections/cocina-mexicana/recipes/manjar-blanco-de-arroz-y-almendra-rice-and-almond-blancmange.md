## Ingredients

- 3 pounds rice
- 30 cuartillos of milk, strained through a cloth
- 7 pounds white sugar, coarsely ground
- 2 caldron cups of cold water (approximately)
- grains of well-washed salt
- almond, coarsely ground

## Directions

1. Clean 3 pounds of rice, wash and leave in water until the next day
2. Drain the rice, grind very well in a metate, strain through a sieve into a new earthenware bowl
3. Place in a cazo and add 30 cuartillos of milk strained through a cloth, previously sweetened with 7 pounds of very white sugar, coarsely ground
4. Leave for one hour, carefully removing the fat that forms on the milk
5. Add about 2 caldron cups of cold water and grains of well-washed salt
6. Place over high fire to boil vigorously, then add a little coarsely ground almond
7. Stir without removing from fire so it does not burn, and do not touch what sticks to the sides of the cazo until it is about to be poured into plates
8. Pour into dishes when a small amount placed on the back of a well-glazed ceramic plate comes off easily
