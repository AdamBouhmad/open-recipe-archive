## Ingredients

- Good piece of sturgeon
- Ham, thin sliced or anguila (eel) and anchovies
- Beef slices
- Ham slices
- Carrots
- Onions
- Aromatic herbs
- White wine
- Butter
- Spanish sauce, three spoonfuls

## Directions

1. Lift the skin and bony strips from a good piece of sturgeon, which will be lightly beaten with the flat of a machete.
2. If the fricandeau is to be made with meat, lard the sturgeon with thin ham; but if it is to be cooked in the lean version, lard it with eel and anchovies.
3. For the first case, line a pot with beef slices, slices of ham, some carrots, onions, and aromatic herbs.
4. Place the fish on top, moistening with white wine and covering with buttered paper, letting the fricandeau cook.
5. After this operation is complete and the sturgeon is well set or iced, pass the cooking liquid through a silk sieve, and once defatted, let it reduce with three spoonfuls of Spanish sauce.
6. Pour it over the fricandeau, which is already arranged on the plate in which it is to be served.
