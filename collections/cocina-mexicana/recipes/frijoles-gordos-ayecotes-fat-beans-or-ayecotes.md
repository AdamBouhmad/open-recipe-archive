## Ingredients

- cleaned ayecotes beans
- a little tequesquite water
- lard
- garlic
- onion, chopped
- a handful of the same beans
- pepper
- clove
- cinnamon
- hard biscuit
- pork meat, in small pieces
- longaniza
- morcón
- chicharrones
- cooking oil
- oregano powder
- onion, sliced
- avocado
- small chiles in vinegar
- olives
- radishes
- lettuce hearts

## Directions

1. Clean the ayecotes and put them to cook with a little tequesquite water.
2. In a pot with lard on the fire, fry plenty of garlic and chopped onion.
3. Add the ayecotes without broth to parboil there; then add the corresponding water and fine salt.
4. Grind a handful of the same beans with pepper, clove, and cinnamon, adding a hard biscuit; add all the ground mixture to the beans.
5. While they boil, fry well the pork meat, divided into small pieces, with longaniza, morcón, and chicharrones, all well cooked.
6. Let it boil on low heat, and when it has thickened enough, remove from heat.
7. Arrange on a small plate and decorate on top with cooking oil, oregano powder, and sliced onion, avocado, small chiles in vinegar, olives, radishes, and lettuce hearts cut in quarters.
