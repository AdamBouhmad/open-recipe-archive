## Ingredients

- Beef head
- Salt
- Wide chiles, deveined
- Garlic cloves
- Cloves
- Black pepper powder
- Vinegar
- Oregano
- Thyme
- Cooked ham
- Lard
- Paper
- Burlap or mallow sacking
- Water

## Directions

1. Early in the morning, prepare a pit in the earth measuring two-thirds of a square unit, or two feet wide, long, and deep
2. Pile the excavated earth around the pit so it heats at the same time as the interior
3. Arrange united stones inside to serve as a bed or base
4. Light plenty of firewood in the pit, which should burn all day long, feeding it with fresh logs as needed
5. As evening falls, push the coals aside, separating the larger part along with some of the stones, which will be replaced after the head is arranged
6. Remove the skin from the head without destroying it, split it in half, and clean well
7. Slit the tongue and lard it with cooked ham, garlic cloves, and cloves
8. Coat the tongue with salt, wide deveined chiles that have been soaked and ground, pepper powder, vinegar, oregano, and thyme
9. Once the tongue is larded and marinated, rejoin the head to restore its natural form, then lard the outside and marinate it the same way as the tongue
10. Wrap it in papers coated with lard so it is well covered, place the skin back on, and wrap again in a piece of burlap or mallow sacking thoroughly soaked in water
11. Place in the pit, which should now be hot as described, moving the separated coals to one side and placing them on the other
12. Put the separated stones with the coals on top, and add the earth that should have been heated at the pit edges, filling it to ground level or slightly below to form a small oven
13. Keep wood fire burning in this small oven all night, leaving the head buried until serving time
