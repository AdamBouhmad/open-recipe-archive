## Ingredients

- texocotes
- water
- sugar
- grains of uva

## Directions

1. Cook whatever quantity of texocotes you wish without peeling, and after they are cooked, peel them, dry them and give them a boil in clean water, then leave them in the same water for twenty-four hours so they release the phlegm.
2. Make clarified syrup in the proportion of one pound of sugar for each cuartillo and a half of the phlegmy water of the texocotes, and with the syrup at point, mix both things and put them to boil until they have the necessary degree of cooking, which is known by taking with the spoon a little of the syrup and throwing it in cold water, from where it will be taken by grasping with the fingers, between which it should make small threads.
3. In this case remove the syrup from the heat, and carefully remove all the foam that forms when the bubbles rise to the surface of the pot.
4. Once the jelly is well skimmed, empty it into the plates or molds, which will be placed uncovered in the sun so they form a crust.
5. You can mix with the jelly to give it novelty, some grains of uva, and the same can be done with the other fruits.
