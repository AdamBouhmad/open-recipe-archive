## Ingredients

- nixtamal, washed and dried
- carmine
- egg yolks
- lard
- four cuartillos of milk
- four ounces of starch
- two pounds of ground camote
- one pound of crushed sugar
- four ounces of ground almond
- ground carmine
- orange blossom water
- raisins
- almonds
- pine nuts
- sesame

## Directions

1. Sift the washed nixtamal through a cloth, grind it dry, tint it with a bit of carmine, and mix with three egg yolks and necessary lard
2. Using this dough, form the box inside a lard-greased pot
3. Bring four cuartillos of milk to cook, and before it boils add four ounces of starch, stirring well and straining immediately
4. Add two pounds of ground camote and, when boiling, one pound of crushed sugar
5. When it is almost at soft-ball stage, add four ounces of ground almond and a bit of ground carmine dissolved in orange blossom water until the paste becomes nacreous
6. Once at soft-ball stage, remove from heat and fill the dough box formed inside the pot with this paste, adding raisins, almonds, pine nuts, and sesame
7. Cover with layers of dough made thin and brushed with lard, and place another layer on top to form a crust
8. Decorate with dough ornaments and figurines as desired, and bake in an oven or over two fires
9. Once the turco is well browned, brush with lard and sprinkle with sugar and cinnamon
