## Ingredients

- one large, very white cabbage
- water
- salt
- crushed garlic
- a small piece of white tequezquite
- one chopped tomato
- onion
- a little parsley
- cooked chickpeas
- broth
- ham
- chorizo
- chorizones
- longaniza
- peritas
- green peas
- fresh fava beans
- one soaked ancho chile
- garlic
- cumin

## Directions

1. Take a large, very white cabbage and remove the stalks and first leaves
2. Scoop it out from the top like a watermelon, tie the scoop with a small cord
3. Take a pot proportioned to the cabbage so it does not fall apart, with enough water, salt, crushed garlic, and a small piece of white tequezquite
4. When the water in the pot is boiling, add the cabbage to cook
5. Once cooked, remove it very carefully so it does not fall apart and remove the scoop
6. For the filling: fry a chopped tomato, onion, a little parsley, and cooked chickpeas from the pot with a little broth so it cooks and is seasoned
7. Add ham, chorizo, chorizones, longaniza, and peritas, all sliced, along with cooked green peas and fava beans
8. Before filling the cabbage, season everything
9. Place a cazuela or skillet in which the cabbage fits and fry it with a little soaked ancho chile, garlic and cumin, a little broth from the pot, and in this sauce put the cabbage, bathing it with a spoon from above
10. At low heat, season until it dries, making sure it is neither too salty nor bland, and bring it whole and hot to the table to be served
