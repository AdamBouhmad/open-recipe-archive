## Ingredients

- one pound of flour
- thirty egg yolks
- three fingers of aniseed powder
- half a small cup of tequesquite water, settled
- three tablespoons of aguardiente
- lard for frying
- three pounds of sugar
- one and a half pounds of piloncillo
- Jerez wine

## Directions

1. In a cazuela, combine one pound of flour and thirty egg yolks, mixing with three fingers of aniseed powder, half a small cup of tequesquite water that has settled, and three tablespoons of aguardiente.
2. Knead the mixture, and if it remains loose, add flour until it no longer sticks to the hand.
3. Beat the dough vigorously on a metate and form long thin sticks that are twisted in the hand.
4. Fry the sticks in lard (the sticks are made on a table).
5. Make a syrup with three pounds of sugar and one and a half pounds of piloncillo, bringing it to a high glossy point and beating until it crystallizes.
6. Sprinkle the sticks with Jerez wine, place them in the crystallized syrup, arrange in small boxes, and press them down with a damp napkin on top to smooth them.
7. Cut into quarter-pound pieces before they harden, without removing them from the box.
