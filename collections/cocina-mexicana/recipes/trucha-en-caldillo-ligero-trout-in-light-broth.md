## Ingredients

- trout
- onion slices
- four cloves
- four bay leaves
- sprigs of thyme
- handful of parsley sprigs
- salt
- six bottles of white wine

## Directions

1. Clean the trout without opening the belly, wash it, dry it very well, tie the head with thread, and place it in a fish kettle
2. Add onion slices, four cloves, four bay leaves, some sprigs of thyme, a handful of parsley sprigs, salt, and six bottles of white wine
3. Allow to cook over low heat for one hour, or longer if the fish is large and fat
4. The cooked trout can be served in various ways: roasted in a napkin, folded on a plate; surrounded by parsley à la genovese; moistened with red wine; with a little salt in the same broth in which it cooked, which for this is strained through silk; the same as salmon à la genovese (see page 761); and in short, the cooked trout can be served in the various ways in which salmon is served
