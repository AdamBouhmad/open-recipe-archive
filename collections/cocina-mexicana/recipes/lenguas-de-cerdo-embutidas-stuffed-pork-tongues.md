## Ingredients

- pork tongues
- salt
- saltpeter
- aromatic plants
- beef, cow, or pork intestines
- juniper branches

## Directions

1. After preparing them and removing the small glands at the thickest end, parboil them in boiling water; remove the skin that covers them and arrange them in an earthenware jar on a bed of salt with one-sixth part saltpeter and chopped aromatic plants.
2. Sprinkle the tongues with this mixture and place successive layers of one and the other until filling two-thirds of the jar.
3. Cover with a wooden lid that can fit easily in the same jar with sufficient weight on top to force the tongues to be continuously bathed in the brine, leaving them thus for eight days.
4. At the end of this time, remove them, let them drain, stuff them into beef, cow, or pork intestines, tying them at both ends, and smoke them with burning juniper branches while they finish burning.
