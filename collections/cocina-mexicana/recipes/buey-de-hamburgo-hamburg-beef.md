## Ingredients

- beef hindquarter, not too fatty
- saltpeter, one pound
- bay leaf
- thyme
- garlic
- cloves
- cilantro
- salt
- water
- vegetables: turnips, parsnips, carrots

## Directions

1. Debone a hindquarter of beef that is not very fatty and press it.
2. Rub all the lean parts with a pound of saltpeter.
3. Arrange this piece of beef in a vessel with bay leaf, thyme, garlic, cloves, and cilantro.
4. Cover with salt and seal the vessel well so that air does not penetrate.
5. After eight or nine days, remove the meat, wash it in many waters, and wrap it in a white cloth.
6. Put it in a pot with appropriate water and some roots, such as turnips, parsnips, and carrots, letting it boil for seven or eight hours.
7. Then drain and eat cold.
