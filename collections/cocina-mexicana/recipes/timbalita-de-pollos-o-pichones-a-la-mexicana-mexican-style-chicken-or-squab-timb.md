## Ingredients

- Lard
- Timbale dough (masa para timbales)
- Onions, finely chopped
- Garlic cloves, sliced
- Tomatoes, chopped
- Chicken or squab quarters
- Ground pepper
- Ground clove
- Bay leaves
- Salt
- Flour

## Directions

1. Grease a pot with lard and place on top, well-arranged, sheets of timbale dough (masa para timbales) well-extended and made very thin.
2. Prepare a filling by frying together in lard finely chopped onions, sliced garlic cloves, chopped tomatoes, and quarters of squab or chicken.
3. When everything has taken on color or become golden as is said, add ground pepper and clove, some bay leaves, and the corresponding salt.
4. When well-browned, remove from heat, add a good handful of flour that is mixed very well, with a little water so that the stew cooks, and return to fire, letting the broth reduce until only the fat remains.
5. Place the filling in the prepared pot, which is then covered with another sheet of the same dough.
6. Let cook immediately over two gentle fires and as soon as it is cooked and well-browned, remove and serve.
