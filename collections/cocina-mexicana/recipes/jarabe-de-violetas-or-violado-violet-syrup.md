## Ingredients

- half a pound of violets
- three cuartillos of water
- three pounds of powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Clean half a pound of violets (those from the woods are better than those from gardens)
2. Place them in a clay jar or vessel that can be covered
3. Boil three cuartillos of water and remove from heat
4. Ten minutes later, pour the water over the violets (the infusion should be a beautiful violet color and would turn green if boiling water were poured directly on the flowers)
5. Place the infusion in a warm place to keep it hot until the next morning
6. Remove the flowers, pressing them well in a napkin to extract the tint
7. Pour the tint into a bowl or casserole with three pounds of powdered sugar and dissolve
8. Return the bowl to the warm place and leave for twenty-four hours, stirring from time to time
9. Keep the warm place hot during all this time, as for rock candy
10. These operations will yield two bottles of syrup
11. Before bottling, ensure it has the cooking point called high glaze (see syrup at high glaze point, page 25), so it will keep and not ferment at all
12. This is the only syrup made without boiling on the fire
