## Ingredients

- 1 pheasant
- 1.5 pounds ground truffle
- 4 ounces butter
- 0.5 pound grated ham
- 4 ounces oil
- salt
- coarse pepper
- cinnamon
- clove
- fine pepper
- nutmeg
- 2 truffles, sliced
- 3 tablespoons Spanish sauce
- 3 tablespoons game sauce or pheasant broth

## Directions

1. Empty and singe the pheasant at the flame
2. Break the two breast bones and remove from the neck opening to avoid damaging the tail
3. Finely mince 1.5 pounds of ground truffle that has been washed and trimmed thinly on the outside, setting the thin parts aside separately
4. Heat 4 ounces butter, 0.5 pound grated ham, and 4 ounces oil in a pan
5. In this mixture, soften or render the separated truffle parts cut into walnut-sized pieces, adding salt, coarse pepper, cinnamon, clove, fine pepper and nutmeg
6. Let them boil for five minutes in the ham, then add the other finely minced truffles and let cool
7. When the picadillo is cold, fill the pheasant through the neck opening, closing the hole with a thin slice of ham covered with pheasant skin
8. After folding the legs as done with fattened hens, tie them and secure the neck skin well to prevent the truffles from escaping
9. Place the pheasant in a pan between two layers of ham slices and cover with pan sauce without lemon, covering all with a wheel of buttered paper
10. Cook at low heat for one hour
11. When ready to serve, drain the pheasant and untie it carefully, arrange it on a plate
12. For the sauce: fry two sliced truffles in butter, then add 3 tablespoons Spanish sauce and 3 tablespoons game sauce or the broth in which the pheasant cooked, reducing until half remains and degreasing afterwards
