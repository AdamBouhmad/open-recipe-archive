## Ingredients

- Sturgeon, sliced
- Bread crumbs
- Butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Shallot
- Parsley
- Onion
- Flour
- Red wine, one quarter liter
- Capers
- Oil or butter for frying

## Directions

1. Make small rounds of fresh bread crumbs, the size of a peso or smaller, and fry them in butter until well browned, then drain them.
2. Take a piece of sturgeon and cut it into somewhat thin slices, arranging them on a fish platter so they do not overlap, with a piece of butter, salt, and coarse pepper.
3. Let them cook over low heat, and as they cook on one side, turn them to the other; a quarter hour is enough for complete cooking.
4. Remove the sturgeon from the platter and add flour to it, which will be stirred with the butter, and then shallot, parsley, and onion, all chopped, moistening with a quarter liter of red wine.
5. Let this light sauce boil for another quarter hour and place the sturgeon in it so that it heats without boiling.
6. Add some chopped capers and decorate the edges of the platter with the fried toasts, sprinkling some of the same sauce or light sauce over them.
