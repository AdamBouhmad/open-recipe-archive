## Ingredients

- white and sour texocotes
- water
- sugar

## Directions

1. Select texocotes that are white and sour, wash them very well and put them to cook.
2. Once cooked, pass them through a sieve and add a little water so it releases what filth it may have, then mash them so they are open and pour into a pot filled halfway; fill with water, cover, and leave for two days, stirring with a spoon, and leave another day.
3. Check and taste to see if it has become sour, and if you wish to abbreviate the operation somewhat, place it where the sun reaches it.
4. To be ready, it must have five or six days, and even if it has eight it does no harm; taste that broth which should be sour; but if it is very strong, add a little water; and for thickness it should be like syrup at medium point: having these conditions, strain through cloth, put the clarified honey to reach point, which is tested with the spoon by turning until it forms a mirror.
5. Mix ten cuartillos of texocote broth with half an arroba of sugar made into syrup in a capable pot, because it rises much; then give it point again, which is examined with the spoon, and being thick and cutting itself only with the edge of the spoon, remove from heat, and let it rest a little, so that the foam on top is called together with the spoon; and bringing it all together to one side, remove it and pour into a cold pot, to empty it into the molds.
6. This can be done in an entire morning or afternoon, because if left longer time, the jelly will set in the pot.
7. You can get more jelly from the texocote that already served by adding new water and doing it as the first time.
