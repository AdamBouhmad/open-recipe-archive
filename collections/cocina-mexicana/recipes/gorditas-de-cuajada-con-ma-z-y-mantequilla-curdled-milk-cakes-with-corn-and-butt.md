## Ingredients

- Four calderas of curdled milk (pressed, drained, and ground)
- Two calderas of corn flour (prepared as in previous recipes)
- Twelve egg yolks (or as needed)
- Sifted sugar
- One small bowl of melted and lukewarm butter
- Salt
- Tequesquite

## Directions

1. Mix four calderas of pressed, drained, and ground curdled milk with two calderas of corn flour
2. Add twelve egg yolks or as many as needed to moisten the dough
3. Add sifted sugar
4. Dissolve a little salt and tequesquite in the melted butter
5. Add the butter mixture gradually as the dough absorbs it
6. Dust the dough with ground cinnamon and clove
7. Beat the dough until it reaches the proper consistency, which is known by dropping a small amount in water—it should rise immediately to the surface
8. If the dough does not have the necessary consistency to form little cakes, add flour and beat again
9. Form the gorditas on corn husks
10. Place on tin sheets and bake in a well-tempered oven where bran immediately browns
