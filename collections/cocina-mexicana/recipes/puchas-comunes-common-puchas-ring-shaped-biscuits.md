## Ingredients

- Sixty-four egg yolks
- Five ounces of sifted sugar
- Five ounces of melted and cooled butter
- One quarter of refined Spanish brandy, or half a quarter of cane brandy and another half of mescal
- One quarter ounce of very white tequesquite powder
- Fine wheat flour as needed

## Directions

1. Beat sixty-four egg yolks the same way as for mamón and pour into a bowl dusted with very fine flour
2. Add five ounces of sifted sugar, five ounces of melted and cooled butter, one quarter of refined Spanish brandy or half a quarter of cane brandy and another half of mescal, and one quarter ounce of very white tequesquite powder
3. Mix everything very quickly so that the dough does not cook, adding flour as necessary so that the dough remains smooth and does not stick to the hands
4. Once everything is well incorporated, beat it a little and spread it on a table to air it sufficiently so that the puchas can be formed in the desired size, cutting proportioned pieces of dough
5. Place them on baking sheets, separated from each other, and bake in the oven, which should be somewhat hotter than for mamones, covering the opening immediately
6. When the puchas burst, remove them, turn them quickly, and put them back in the oven to cook on the other side
7. Remove them and place them in a basket lined with a cloth where they will be covered until they cool completely, then store for use
