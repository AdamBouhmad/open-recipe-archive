## Ingredients

- Quails
- Ham, streaked
- Cooked chard
- Goat or veal liver, grated
- A regular piece of fresh cheese curds
- Salt
- Pepper
- Clove
- Cinnamon
- Lard
- Ripe tomatoes, roasted and ground
- Diluted vinegar
- Sugar
- Water
- Two wide chiles, whole
- Three or four onions, sliced
- Oregano
- Thyme
- Breadcrumbs
- Equal parts lard and oil
- Toasted pine nuts
- Parsley
- Four to six hard-boiled egg yolks
- Finely chopped onion
- Bread soaked in vinegar

## Directions

1. After emptying the quails, dry and clean their intestines well, removing the gall and dirty parts.
2. Chop these intestines with an equal amount of streaked ham, cooked chard, a bit of grated goat or veal liver, and a regular piece of fresh cheese curds.
3. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, clove and cinnamon, all ground and well mixed.
4. Place a saucepan on the fire with lard and fry in it plenty of ripe roasted and well ground tomatoes.
5. Add the chopped mixture, sufficient diluted vinegar and a lump of sugar.
6. Let the broth reduce regularly.
7. Then stuff the quails, sew the openings well closed, place them in a saucepan with enough water to cover them well, sufficient salt, two whole wide chiles, three or four sliced onions and a bit of oregano and thyme.
8. Bring to a boil and let the broth reduce until only the fat remains.
9. Roll in breadcrumbs and return to the pan to brown before serving with the following sauce.
10. In a saucepan on the fire with equal parts lard and oil, fry some toasted pine nuts and parsley, with four to six hard-boiled egg yolks, very finely chopped onion, a piece of bread soaked in vinegar, sufficient salt, and a splash of vinegar.
11. Then add the fat in which the quails were roasted, a little water, and when seasoned, pour over the birds and serve.
