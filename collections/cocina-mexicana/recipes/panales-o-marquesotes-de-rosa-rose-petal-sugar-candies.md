## Ingredients

- very white sugar
- egg white
- rose petals
- lemon juice

## Directions

1. Sugar must be very white and is made into syrup clarified with a beaten egg white added while still thin, so it boils well and collects the dark foam it releases.
2. Set aside, strain and leave until the next day.
3. Afterwards, pour the honey into another pan, separating the sediment and place it on heat.
4. Once the boil is thick, test the point by first dipping your finger in cold water and removing it from the pan, dip it back in cold water and when it begins to break add the rose petals stripped of their yellow parts and lemon juice, little or much according to taste, beating everything together briefly.
5. The first time it rises, but no note is taken, and continue beating and at the second time it rises again, and pour it into a box lined with a napkin and paper.
6. Soon as they cool a little, separate the honeycombs, because once well cooled they crumble.
7. The frosting is made with an egg white for each pound of sugar, and if you want them more spongy, add more egg whites.
8. This frosting is made with ground and sifted sugar and is whitened with a little lemon.
