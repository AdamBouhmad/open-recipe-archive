## Ingredients

- chicken
- lard
- tomatoes
- onions
- garlic
- parsley
- rice
- oil
- water
- salt

## Directions

1. Cut the chickens in half and fry them raw in abundant lard and set aside from the heat.
2. In another pan fry in oil two or three large tomatoes, cut in pieces, two chopped onions, some chopped garlic cloves and parsley.
3. When the preparation is almost done, add the rice dry, that is, unwashed and clean only of all debris, and let it toast without burning.
4. Then add the chicken pieces with their lard to finish frying with the rice and the preparation that was fried in oil.
5. Have ready a pot with proportionate boiling water that will be added to the pan with the meat and preparation, and cover with a comal after adding the corresponding salt.
6. It should cook over high heat and it takes twenty minutes or at most half an hour for the rice to be cooked and whole.
7. Remove from heat and let rest for a while to finish cooking with its own steam, without uncovering the pan.
8. Remember that the rice cannot be wetted before adding it to the pan, as its own starch helps it cook well without falling apart and turning into a kind of atole that makes it lose its form and flavor.
