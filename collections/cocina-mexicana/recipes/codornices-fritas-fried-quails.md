## Ingredients

- Quails
- Water
- Salt
- Lard
- Two onions, finely chopped
- One red chile, whole
- Two or three cloves of garlic
- Vinegar
- Sugar
- Toasted pine nuts
- Pepper
- Clove
- Eating oil

## Directions

1. Pluck, clean and empty the quails.
2. Clean all their intestines, removing only the small intestines.
3. If the quails are sufficiently fat, place them alone in a saucepan covered well with water.
4. If they are somewhat lean, spread them with a bit of lard, and add sufficient salt to the water.
5. Add two finely chopped onions, one whole red chile, and two or three clean cloves of garlic, and let them boil until well cooked.
6. Then remove the intestines and let the water reduce, adding a bit of vinegar and a small lump of sugar.
7. Brown them in the same fat that they release when boiling.
8. In the meantime, prepare the following sauce: grind the intestines with an equal amount of toasted pine nuts, and fry both in lard with sufficient salt, adding pepper and clove, and a touch of vinegar.
9. When the sauce has thickened well, place the quails on a platter, sprinkle a bit of salt and pepper on them.
10. Pour over them the fat in which they were fried, and then coat them with the sauce, drizzled with eating oil.
