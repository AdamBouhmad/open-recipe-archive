## Ingredients

- Four small teal ducks, regularly aged
- The ducks' livers
- A good piece of butter
- Salt
- Pepper
- A little clove
- Cinnamon
- Nutmeg
- Lemon zest, scraped and chopped
- One or two slices of lemon
- A slice of ham
- Paper well buttered
- Spanish sauce
- Juice of two bitter oranges
- Or lemon if bitter oranges are not available

## Directions

1. Take four small teal ducks, regularly aged
2. Clean them carefully, removing the entrails
3. Singe them lightly over flames so that only the down burns, not other feathers
4. Clean them carefully so no quill feathers remain that could conceal feathers
5. Crush the ducks' livers with the back of a machete to make a light stuffing
6. Mix the livers with a good piece of butter, salt, pepper, a little clove, cinnamon, nutmeg, and chopped scraped lemon zest
7. Stuff the four ducks with this mixture so their bellies are gently rounded
8. Twist their legs and thread them skillfully to give them a nice and appetizing appearance
9. Cover the belly with one or two slices of lemon and a slice of ham on top
10. Cover everything with paper well buttered
11. Be careful to tie them in this state at both ends so the substance does not escape
12. Skewer all four on a fork, which is fastened to a large roasting spit
13. Roast for half an hour until perfectly cooked
14. When ready to serve, unwrap and untie them neatly
15. Drain the butter from them
16. Remove the lemon slices
17. Serve with well-seasoned Spanish sauce to which the juice of two bitter oranges has been added, or lemon if bitter oranges are not available
