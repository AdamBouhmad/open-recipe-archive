## Ingredients

- costillas (ribs)
- pulque
- vino de Jerez (Sherry wine)
- agua (water)
- ajos (garlic)
- tomillo mexicano (Mexican thyme)
- pimienta (pepper)
- leche (milk)
- harina (flour)
- pan rallado (breadcrumbs)
- manteca (lard)
- jitomates cocidos (cooked tomatoes)
- cebolla (onion)
- perejil (parsley)

## Directions

1. Tie the ribs to the mouth of a pot
2. Add one quarter of pulque for every six ribs, half a small cup of Sherry wine and the same amount of water, crushed garlic, Mexican thyme and crushed pepper
3. Cover the pot with cloth
4. Make a sauce with one quarter milk and a handful of flour, and add it little by little as the ribs are heating so they become very coated
5. Then add breadcrumbs and fry them in lard that has been well burned, but is only warm so they do not brown too much
6. Make another sauce with cooked tomatoes that are ground and brought down from the metate with clean water
7. Fry some onion, fry the tomato very much with salt and a little finely minced parsley
8. When serving add Sherry wine
