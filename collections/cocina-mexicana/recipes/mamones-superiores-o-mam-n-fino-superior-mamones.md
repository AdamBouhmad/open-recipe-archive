## Ingredients

- Eggs (number not specified)
- Ground sugar (one ounce per egg)
- Ground and sifted starch (one ounce per egg)
- Lard for greasing the pan
- Clarified syrup for coating
- White wine
- Honey thinned with water
- Ground cinnamon
- Sugar for rolling

## Directions

1. First beat the egg whites as if for frying
2. Then beat the egg yolks in equal number
3. Once both are mixed together, combine with ground sugar and well-ground and sifted starch, in the proportion of one ounce of each for each egg
4. Before putting in the oven, pour everything well mixed into a lard-greased pan
5. When it begins to rise, place a paper on top
6. After the mamón is cooked, let it cool and cut into pieces of desired size
7. For toasted versions, return to the oven once it has cooled somewhat
8. To coat them, bring well-clarified syrup to mirror glaze point, add white wine, and keep on the fire
9. Dip the mamones there, and when removed, place in sugar with ground cinnamon, separated from each other
10. If during the operation the syrup rises in point, add thinned honey and wine
11. Soletas should be dusted with sugar on top before going into the oven
12. In some places they are now made round and not dusted with sugar
