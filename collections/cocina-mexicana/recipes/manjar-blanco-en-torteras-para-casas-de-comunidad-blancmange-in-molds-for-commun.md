## Ingredients

- 4 cubos of milk
- 8 pounds rice
- 1 pound 6 ounces corn (nixtamal)
- 0.5 arroba of sugar
- 4 ounces lard
- 1 whole chicken breast, cooked without salt

## Directions

1. Grind 8 pounds of rice; wash it well, place on a cloth in the sun, and when very dry, sift through a fine sieve until it becomes like powder
2. Place 4 cubos of milk in a cazo and mix 4 pounds of the sifted rice powder (accounting for weight loss from 8 pounds ground)
3. Take some prepared corn called nixtamal, and as soon as it begins to husk, place in a chiquihuite and wash very well
4. Place in the sun like the rice, and when dry, grind and sift in the same manner as the rice, doing both separately
5. Add 1 pound 6 ounces of this corn to the cazo with the rice, and 0.5 arroba of sugar
6. If the milk is very fatty, remove the cream and do not strain again; alternatively, set aside one cubo to dissolve the sugar and strain it for any debris, then combine everything
7. When it begins to thicken, add 4 ounces of lard and 1 whole chicken breast that has been cooked without salt and shredded finely (the chicken should be killed the day before and wrapped in a damp cloth to shred the breast)
8. Add the shredded chicken when the blancmange begins to boil
9. Once it has boiled well, test the point by inserting a knife and letting it cool; if it does not stick to the hand, it is ready
10. Remove from heat, and pour while not too hot because it will rise and overflow the molds
11. The molds should not be burned, but rather placed in the sun for 2 or 3 days after scrubbing
