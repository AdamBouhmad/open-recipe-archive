## Ingredients

- chiles
- cebollas de Cholula (Cholula onions)
- orégano (oregano)
- laurel (bay leaf)
- tomillo (thyme)
- pimientas enteras (whole peppercorns)
- pimienta en polvo (ground pepper)
- sal (salt)
- cabezas de ajo (garlic heads)
- agua de sal (salted water)
- aceite (oil)
- vinagre (vinegar)
- lienso (linen cloth)

## Directions

1. Devein the chiles and open them on one side without tearing them
2. Roast them just enough so they can be peeled
3. In the bottom of a pot, place a layer of chiles and Cholula onions, oregano, bay leaf, thyme, whole peppercorns, ground pepper, and salt
4. Continue layering chiles with the seasonings until finished
5. Add garlic heads that have been briefly boiled in salted water
6. Add oil in proportion to the quantity of chiles (for example, for thirty chiles, half a quarter-liter of oil and half of vinegar)
7. Cover the pot with linen cloth and tie it very tightly, placing a plate upside down on top
8. Place the pot on a very gentle fire for one and a half to two hours
9. When cooked, remove with a wooden spoon and let cool
10. Sprinkle with vinegar
