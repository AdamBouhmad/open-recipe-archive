## Ingredients

- Young partridge
- Butter
- Fine herbs for wrapping
- Oil
- Very thin ham slices
- Paper

## Directions

1. After cleaning the young partridge and passing it through flames, divide it in two halves from the neck to the tail, separating the pieces
2. Partially cook them in a casserole with butter and leave them in it about seven or eight minutes, which will be enough for them to be almost cooked
3. Let them cool on a plate and pour fine herbs arranged for wrapping over them
4. When the pieces are cold, cut square papers of the appropriate size so that each can contain one half of the partridge
5. Spread oil on the paper and place a very thin slice of ham, and on top the half of the partridge with its herbs and another slice of ham to cover it
6. Fold the paper and fold it in the shape of cornets
7. Half an hour before serving, place them on the grill over a very gentle fire
8. To bring to table, arrange them on a plate in the form of a crown with some watery or loose substance underneath
