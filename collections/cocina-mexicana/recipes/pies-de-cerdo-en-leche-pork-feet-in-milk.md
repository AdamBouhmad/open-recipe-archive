## Ingredients

- Pork feet
- Water
- Salt
- Bread, two or three days old
- Eggs, beaten
- Reduced milk
- Egg yolks
- Sugar
- Orange blossom water
- Custard (if available)
- Cinnamon

## Directions

1. One day before serving, cook the pork feet with water and salt until extremely tender.
2. Remove the bones.
3. The next day, fry them, cut in half and well pressed, rolled in finely ground old bread and coated with beaten egg.
4. As they fry, immediately place them in very hot water from a pot kept boiling, so they release the fat from frying.
5. Place them in a basket.
6. If fat still remains, fry them again without new lard and place in another hot water.
7. Prepare reduced milk in proportion to the quantity of feet.
8. When cold so it does not curdle, add egg yolks so it takes on some color.
9. Place over gentle fire until it thickens.
10. Then add sugar, orange blossom water, and custard if available, taking care that it does not taste of egg.
11. Incorporate the feet, sprinkle cinnamon on top, and serve so they are neither cold nor very hot.
