## Ingredients

- one pig's head
- salt
- black pepper
- allspice
- thyme
- laurel
- basil
- six cloves
- culantro
- half nutmeg
- two garlic cloves
- one bunch of parsley
- vinegar
- ham pieces
- parsley leaves
- white wine
- two cuartillos
- onion
- carrot

## Directions

1. Clean the pig's head well, debone it, and remove all the meat and fat without damaging the skin.
2. Cut the meat into very thin strips; do the same with the fat, placing each in separate dishes.
3. Cut the ears also into very thin strips, and season everything on top and bottom with salt, black pepper, allspice, thyme, laurel, basil, six cloves of spice, a little culantro, and half nutmeg, all ground or chopped very fine, adding two garlic cloves and one whole bunch of parsley.
4. Place the pig's head skin in a round pot and arrange in it all the seasoned meat strips, with a little vinegar, some small pieces of ham if available, and some parsley leaves, which are arranged very neatly, continuing the same way to the end.
5. Sew up the skin leaving it like a bag; wrap it in clean linen, tie it very well with twine or hemp cord.
6. Put this cheese in a pot or marmite that fits its size well, and cook for about six or seven hours with broth, two cuartillos of white wine, onion, carrot, thyme, laurel, basil, one garlic clove, salt, and pepper.
7. When cooked, place it to drain and put it in a round vessel that serves as a mold, cover it with a lid and a large weight on top so it takes the shape of a cheese, leaving it thus until it is cold.
