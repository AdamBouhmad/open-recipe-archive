## Ingredients

- twelve egg whites
- twenty-eight ounces of sifted sugar
- thirty-two egg yolks
- two pounds of dry, sifted starch
- additional sifted sugar for dusting

## Directions

1. Beat twelve egg whites until well risen
2. Mix in twenty-eight ounces of sifted sugar, then thirty-two beaten egg yolks that have been beaten until thick and stiff
3. Incorporate everything near the oven, which should be less hot than for mamones
4. With a clean, tight brush to prevent loose fibers or debris, while one person beats, another dusts with dry, sifted starch, scraping it with the brush until two pounds are incorporated
5. Once everything is well combined, place small portions with a spoon on paper, separated from each other, in whatever size and shape desired, even forming letters to write someone's name
6. Dust with sifted sugar, or if papers are medium-sized, quickly turn them over into a dish with ground sugar, and place on baking sheets
7. Put in the oven for the soletas to bake
