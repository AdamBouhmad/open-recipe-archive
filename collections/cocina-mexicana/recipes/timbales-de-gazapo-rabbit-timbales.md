## Ingredients

- Two young rabbits, divided into pieces
- Butter
- Salt
- Pepper
- Spices
- Truffles
- Mushrooms
- Shallots
- Parsley, chopped
- Ground or pounded aromatics
- Armada dough (pasta de armar)
- Flour
- Pigeon meat
- Spanish sauce, thick

## Directions

1. Half-cook two young rabbits, divided into pieces, in a pot with butter, salt, pepper, spices, truffles, mushrooms, chopped shallots and parsley, and a few ground or pounded aromatics, then let this stew cool.
2. Butter a mold or casserole and cover the interior with armada dough (pasta de armar), and extend with a rolling pin another portion of the same dough.
3. Moisten the dough placed in the mold and line the bottom with the dough that was extended.
4. Take some of the prepared rabbit mixture and make small balls, rolling them in flour, and with these cover the bottom of the timbale.
5. Place on top and fill the gaps with the cooked pieces of rabbit, mixed with pigeon meat and some whole truffles.
6. Moisten the edges of the dough, cover the timbale with another sheet of extended dough and put it in the oven to cook for an hour and a half.
7. When cooked and well-colored, remove it, turn it over onto a plate, cover with a lid of appropriate size, and pour thick Spanish sauce over it.
