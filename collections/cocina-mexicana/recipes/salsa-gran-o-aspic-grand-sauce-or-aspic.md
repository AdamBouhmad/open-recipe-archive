## Ingredients

- 1 or 2 beef knuckles
- 1 old partridge
- 1 hen
- poultry feet
- 2 or 3 slices of ham
- 2 carrots
- 2 large onions
- 1 well-seasoned bunch of herbs
- caldo consumado (concentrated broth)
- 2 eggs with whites, yolks and shells
- 1 tablespoon tarragon vinegar
- 1 good glass of white wine

## Directions

1. Put beef knuckles, old partridge, hen, poultry feet and ham in a pot, tying them together
2. Add 2 carrots, 2 large onions and a well-seasoned bunch of herbs
3. Moisten with a little concentrated broth
4. Let sweat slightly over fire
5. When the sauce is becoming gelatin and turning yellow, moisten with broth or water and allow to reduce more than before
6. Skim when it reaches boil and add necessary salt
7. Let cook three consecutive hours
8. Degrease and strain through a wet, wrung-out cloth and let cool
9. Break 2 eggs with whites, yolks and shells, beat them
10. Moisten with a little of the small broth and add 1 tablespoon of tarragon vinegar with a good glass of white wine
11. Mix all this with the grand sauce, put it on the fire and stir with a boxwood brush
12. When it begins to boil, move it to the edge of the stove so it only simmers without boiling
13. Cover the pan and place fire on its lid
14. When the aspic is seen to be liquid, strain it through a wet, wrung-out cloth, tying it to the four feet of a frame or stool
15. Empty into the pan, cover it again with its lid, placing a little fire on top
16. Once the fire is finished, the grand sauce is complete and ready to use
