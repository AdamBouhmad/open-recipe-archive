## Ingredients

- loin meat
- some fat from the flank
- one ounce of salt per pound of meat
- one quarter ounce of pepper per pound of meat

## Directions

1. Take loin meat and some fat from the flank so it does not come out dry and mince very well
2. For each pound of meat add one ounce of salt and one quarter ounce of pepper
3. Mix and fill the tripe not very tight so they do not burst
4. Prick them so the air comes out and cannot be put in the pot unless when the water is boiling in bubbles
5. Keep them for half an hour more or less and prick them frequently while in the water so they throw off all excess fat
6. You will know they are cooked when pricking them they do not throw out a small white broth which is what they throw when they begin to cook
7. Then remove and let drain, hanging them so they do not touch one another
8. They can be eaten this way and are excellent; they can also be eaten by frying them in an egg cake or alone; if you put them in the stew they make good broth
