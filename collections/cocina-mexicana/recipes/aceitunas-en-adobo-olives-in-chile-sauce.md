## Ingredients

- one dozen dried guajillo chiles
- half a head of garlic
- a few cumin seeds
- sufficient salt
- Spanish oil
- olives
- onion slices
- aged cheese
- oregano

## Directions

1. Remove the veins from the chiles and soak them so they are not too spicy
2. Grind the chiles dry with half a head of garlic and a few cumin seeds with sufficient salt
3. Dissolve this mixture from the metate with a little water
4. Pour everything into a pot (not one with lard) with Spanish oil
5. Crush the olives well so they do not fall apart
6. Add them to the chile mixture together with onion slices
7. Leave for three or four days
8. Garnish with aged cheese and oregano
