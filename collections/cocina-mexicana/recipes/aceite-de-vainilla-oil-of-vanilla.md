## Ingredients

- Four ounces of the finest vanilla pods
- Eighteen quartillos of good refined aguardiente
- Twenty-one pounds of sugar
- Essence of amber
- Cloth sleeve

## Directions

1. Cut into small pieces four ounces of the finest vanilla
2. Put them with eighteen quartillos of good refined aguardiente in a cucurbit placed in a water bath and cover with a blind head or cap
3. Seal the joints with clay or paste
4. Heat the liquid afterwards and keep it for eight days at a temperature that keeps it more than lukewarm
5. After this time, leave it for five or six more days in the vessel
6. Meanwhile make the syrup with twenty-one pounds of sugar, leaving it a little less thick than caramel point
7. After this operation, remove the vessel from the fire and expose it for four or five days in the open air so the syrup takes on good taste and does not absorb the perfume of the vanilla
8. Being thus arranged, dismantle the vessel and pour the tincture and vanilla into the syrup
9. Add, if desired, five or six drops of essence of amber
10. Shake the mixture strongly
11. Keep in well-sealed vessels and let rest for fifteen days
12. Then clarify, bottling the clear part and passing the dregs through a cloth sleeve to collect what drips and keep it with the rest
