## Ingredients

- doscientas nueces (two hundred walnuts)
- cincuenta chiles (fifty chiles)
- agua (water)
- dientes de ajo (garlic cloves)
- manteca (lard)
- cebolla (onion)
- caldo (broth)
- pan remojado (soaked bread)
- canela (cinnamon)
- clavo (clove)
- pimienta (pepper)
- tomillo (thyme)
- perejil (parsley)
- manzana (apple)
- durazno (peach)
- terrón de azúcar (sugar lump)
- vino de Jerez (Sherry wine)

## Directions

1. Carefully split fifty chiles and soak them in water
2. Peel and clean them well and place in a pot with clean water
3. Grind one cup of cleaned walnuts in the metate until very finely ground
4. Peel garlic cloves and fry them in lard
5. Once well fried, remove and toast them
6. In the same lard, fry a little finely minced onion and add the ground walnuts to fry well
7. Add broth in which the chile meat was cooked, without including the sediment
8. Fry the broth briefly and add water to cover the chiles
9. Add soaked and ground bread, a little cinnamon, clove and pepper (all ground), a little thyme and parsley
10. May also add sliced apple and peach
11. Add a lump of sugar and a little Sherry wine
