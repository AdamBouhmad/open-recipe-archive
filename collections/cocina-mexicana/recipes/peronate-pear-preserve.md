## Ingredients

- Half a pound of sugar
- Eight pounds of cooked pears

## Directions

1. Make a syrup with half a pound of sugar, clarify it, strain it, and bring it to the point of hardening in water.
2. Place eight pounds of cooked, well-drained, and trimmed pears in it and let the mixture reach a higher point than cajeta.
3. Then set it aside, let it cool, and pour it into boxes lined with paper, smoothing the surface with a damp hand and letting it rest until the next day.
4. On the next day, remove the papers from the box and place them in the sun so the peronate forms a crust.
5. If you wish to remove the papers afterward, dampen them on the outside to facilitate the operation.
6. If peronate in small pieces is desired, divide it with a damp knife, placing it in the sun for one day and afterward in a well-ventilated place.
