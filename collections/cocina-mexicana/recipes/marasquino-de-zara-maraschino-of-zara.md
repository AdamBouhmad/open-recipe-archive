## Ingredients

- Three and a half jars of refined brandy
- Two and a quarter arrobas of sour cherries, well ripe
- Five pounds of cherry leaves
- Sufficient quantity of water
- Eighteen pounds of refined and crushed sugar
- One jar of kirchwasser
- Six ounces of rose spirit
- Six ounces of orange blossom spirit
- One and a half ounces of jasmine spirit

## Directions

1. Place in infusion in refined brandy two and a quarter arrobas of sour cherries, well ripe, in a hermetically sealed vessel, and leave for two or three days, then proceed to distillation in a water bath to obtain forty to forty-five cuartillos of spirituous liquor, according to the strength of the brandy used.
2. Then distill five pounds of cherry leaves, which will have been kept in sufficient quantity of water, to obtain after distillation forty-five to fifty cuartillos; in this distilled water dissolve eighteen pounds of refined and crushed sugar, and mix with the spirituous liquor obtained from the cherries, with one jar of kirchwasser, six ounces of rose spirit, another six ounces of orange blossom spirit and one and a half ounces of jasmine spirit.
3. This liquor should have a taste and perfume of the most pleasant kind, and it is necessary to note that the jasmine spirit that should be used must be made with fresh oil, because when it has been kept a long time, it tends to be rancid, and in that case it imparts to the liquor a very acrid and disagreeable taste.
