## Ingredients

- Long, straight carrots
- Water
- Honey, in proportion to the weight of carrots (one quartillo per pound)
- Ginger, ground and sifted
- Cinnamon, ground and sifted
- Clove, ground and sifted
- Black pepper, ground and sifted
- Raisins
- Blanched and toasted almonds, sliced
- Walnut pieces
- Toasted sesame seeds

## Directions

1. Select the longest and straightest carrots and scrape them with a knife
2. Divide them in half lengthwise, remove the cores and trim the ends so all are equal with four corners each
3. Weigh the carrots and place them in a pot with boiling water, not adding too much so they do not break against each other
4. Once tender, empty the pot into a basket, cover with cloth and let drain
5. Place in a cooking pot as many quartillos of honey as pounds the carrots weighed, skim, strain, and when cooled somewhat, add the carrots
6. Place over a wood fire, stirring carefully so they do not fall apart, and let them reach the proper point
7. Remove the preserve and sprinkle with ginger, cinnamon, clove and pepper, all ground and sifted, stirring with a wooden spoon
8. Transfer to a platter or preserve dish and decorate the top with raisins, strips of blanched toasted almonds, walnut pieces and toasted sesame seeds
