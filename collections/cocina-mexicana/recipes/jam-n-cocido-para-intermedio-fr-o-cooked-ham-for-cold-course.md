## Ingredients

- ham
- six quartillos of water
- six quartillos of red wine
- carrots
- turnips
- onions
- herbs
- parsley
- pepper
- bread crumbs

## Directions

1. Trim the ham to remove any bad parts, without removing the skin or crust
2. Leave it three days in water to desalt, increasing or decreasing the time depending on whether it is new or old and how much salt it has
3. Wrap it in clean cotton cloth and place it in a pot or marmite of its size
4. Add six quartillos of water and the same amount of red wine with carrots, turnips, onions and a good bunch of assorted fine herbs
5. Let the ham cook for five or six hours over moderate fire
6. When cooked, let it cool in its own broth, then remove it and gently remove the covering without separating the fat
7. Place chopped parsley with a bit of pepper on top
8. Cover with bread crumbs and pass a shovel heated to coals over it so the crumbs absorb some fat and take on a beautiful color
9. Serve cold on a napkin as a thick course
