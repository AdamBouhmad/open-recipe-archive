## Ingredients

- solid meat in large pieces
- cloves
- pepper
- cinnamon
- salt
- ham strips
- garlic cloves
- dry thyme
- one bottle of Mexican vinegar
- a little Castilian vinegar
- eighteen to twenty heads of garlic
- a handful of Castilian pepper
- a little bay leaf
- thyme with stalks
- one bottle of oil

## Directions

1. Cut solid meat into large pieces and remove the skin.
2. Soak in cold water to bleed, then wash and slice.
3. Clean with cloth so it has no blood.
4. Grind cloves, pepper, cinnamon, and a little salt.
5. Cut ham into strips and peel garlic cloves.
6. Mix thyme with everything.
7. Stud the meat pieces with ham mixed together, ground spices, and garlic.
8. Tie the meat pieces with strong thread and put in a pot with sufficient water, one bottle of Mexican vinegar and a little Castilian vinegar, eighteen to twenty heads of garlic, a handful of Castilian pepper, a little bay leaf, thyme with stalks, and salt.
9. Heat, cover the pot and stir from time to time so the meat does not stick.
10. When cooked, remove and let cool.
11. Once cold, remove the thread, cut into slices the thickness of a finger, and put in a dish with Mexican vinegar and a little Castilian vinegar and one bottle of oil.
12. Drain the herbs, garlic, and other things from the pot where the meat was cooked.
13. Add salt to taste and in a small barrel or in the pot place a layer of meat and another of garlic and spices and leave ready to eat.
