## Ingredients

- Turkey
- One hundred wide dried chile peppers
- One quarter ounce of cinnamon
- Eighteen cloves
- Twelve peppercorns
- Culantro (a small handful, toasted)
- Lard (one pound)
- Salt
- Water
- Two small baskets of soaked tomatoes

## Directions

1. Kill the turkey the day before it is to be eaten, and devein and toast without burning one hundred wide chile peppers
2. Grind the chiles with one quarter ounce of cinnamon, eighteen cloves, twelve peppercorns, and what can be grasped with three fingers of toasted culantro
3. Cut the turkey into pieces, wash them, and place them in a suitable pot with one pound of lard, salt, and a little water to parboil
4. Once parboiled, add the ground chile and let it cook a little with it
5. Add the spices and a little water, and after three boils remove from heat and let it sit overnight in the open air
6. The next day, cook two small baskets of soaked tomatoes, grind them, and mix well with the mole
7. Return to heat and cook until the turkey is well done; if desired, add pork neck or jowl
8. Care should be taken to add water as it is consumed, so that in the end the mole is thick
