## Ingredients

- pork feet
- hen
- salt
- all spices
- garlic
- chorizos
- picadillo
- four to six beaten eggs
- saffron
- broth from the hen
- all spices
- vinegar
- ten to twelve eggs
- tongue slices
- pork feet pieces without bones
- hen pieces
- chorizo pieces
- sliced hard-boiled eggs
- raisins
- almonds
- olives
- capers
- lard
- tomatoes
- chiles
- parsley
- eight egg yolks
- lemon juice
- molleja or liver, or small fish pieces
- mint

## Directions

1. When the pork feet are half-cooked, add a hen so that everything finishes cooking together.
2. Skim the broth and add salt, all spices, ground garlic, and chorizos.
3. Do this the day before the cake is to be eaten.
4. On the day it is to be served, make a picadillo with all its requisites, adding four to six beaten eggs stirred so they are set, and a little saffron.
5. To the broth in which the hen cooked, well seasoned with all spices, add a little vinegar.
6. Beat ten to twelve eggs separately, beating first the whites and then adding the yolks.
7. Put the picadillo in a pan over the fire, gradually adding sliced tongue, pieces of pork feet without bones, hen, chorizo, sliced hard-boiled eggs, raisins, almonds, olives, and capers.
8. Pour this over half the beaten egg in a pan with very hot lard so it sets, doing so carefully so the pieces of picadillo do not mix with the egg.
9. After adding the picadillo, cover it completely with the other half of the beaten egg.
10. When recognized with a knife that the cake is set, lower it to the ground by placing a comal on top so it cooks without lard.
11. When well browned, if the pan is not full, add more well-puffed beaten eggs and put the comal on top again.
12. If there is suspicion that the cake is not well cooked, put it back on gentle fire until it is.
13. For the sauce, fry from morning twelve ground tomatoes with garlic, chiles, and parsley, then adding a little broth, eight egg yolks, and the juice of two lemons, stirring quickly so the yolks break down.
14. Let it simmer for a while and add all spices and more well-washed capers, or in their place molleja or small liver pieces, or small fish pieces, mint, parsley, and saffron.
15. When bringing to the table, add olives and any other garnishes desired.
