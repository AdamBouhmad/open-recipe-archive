## Ingredients

- sugar
- egg whites
- water
- lemon juice

## Directions

1. Clarify the syrup in the common manner, with the only difference that for one pound of sugar add four egg whites, or more if desired, beating them in water with some switches.
2. Place to cook over good heat, stirring until it has acquired the high point of 'flower' or nearly so, but without skimming.
3. Strain through a sieve, pour into a pan, and if you want it more toasted, let it cook until on the walls of the pan the sugar appears reddish or yellowish.
4. Afterwards remove it from the heat, and placing it on a firm surface, add four drops of lemon juice for each pound of sugar.
5. Beat strongly and without ceasing, until the sugar has puffed up and risen high.
6. Let it rest again, and with a small saw within the same pan divide it into long pieces of half a quarter and the thickness of an inch or so in square, more or less.
7. Once everything is divided in this way, put it back on the heat so it becomes detached and so it can be removed without breaking, taking care not to mistreat it.
8. Being warm equally, turn the vessel carefully onto a paper placed on a table, where the pieces are finished separating.
9. For this type of sugar candy, brown sugar can be used.
