## Ingredients

- Beef tongue
- Pepper
- Saltpetre
- Fine salt
- Cloves
- Thyme
- Bay leaf
- Onions
- Water

## Directions

1. Remove the root and all cartilaginous parts from the tongue
2. Place on the grill over hot coals to remove the tough skin, being careful not to let it taste of smoke
3. Continue removing all skin by placing it repeatedly on the grill
4. Place in a well-sealing vessel
5. Rub with pepper and a little saltpetre, place a good layer of fine salt underneath, and cover with salt after rolling it in salt so it becomes well salted
6. Place some cloves, a little thyme and bay leaf around it
7. After twenty-four hours, rub again with salt and replace the salt at the bottom and on top daily until the tongue is well impregnated, keeping it in this brine for twelve to fifteen days at least, turning it daily
8. Then cook it or let it dry in the fireplace
9. When you want to cook it, put it in a pot full of water with some onions, two cloves, a little thyme and bay leaf, without any salt or pepper
10. Cook slowly for six or seven hours, then let it cool in the same broth and drain
11. Serve whole as a cold appetizer, or in slices for a side dish, being careful that it cooks gently so the broth does not reduce too much and become too salty
