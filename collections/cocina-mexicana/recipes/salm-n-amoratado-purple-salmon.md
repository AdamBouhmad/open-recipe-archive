## Ingredients

- Salmon
- Eight bottles of wine
- Seven or eight carrots
- Onions, sliced
- Four cloves
- Six bay leaves
- A little thyme
- Salt
- A handful of parsley sprigs
- Butter
- Flour
- Brown fish or beef stock
- Whole pepper

## Directions

1. Empty the salmon without cutting the belly
2. After washing and drying well, put it in a fish kettle with eight bottles of wine, seven or eight carrots, sliced onions, four cloves, six bay leaves, a little thyme, salt and a handful of parsley sprigs
3. The salmon must be bathed in its broth and left to cook over low heat for two hours
4. When ready to serve, drain it, place a napkin on the fish kettle with the salmon on top surrounded with parsley
5. If it is to be served as a main course, put a good piece of butter in a pot and mix three tablespoons of flour and one large tablespoon of brown fish or beef stock
6. Put the sauce on the fire, stirring until it boils, and at that moment add whole pepper and let it reduce
