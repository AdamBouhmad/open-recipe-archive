## Ingredients

- Good bread, sliced lengthwise and thin
- Lean bacon
- Kid or poultry offal
- Parsley, minced
- Hard-boiled eggs, minced
- Grated cheese
- Cloves
- Cinnamon
- Broth from the cooked pot or meat substance
- Egg beaten

## Directions

1. Cut slices of good bread lengthwise and thin, and toast them on a wire rack without burning them.
2. Fry other thin pieces of bread with lean bacon and kid or poultry offal. When the frying is done, add a little minced parsley and hard-boiled eggs, a little grated cheese, cloves and cinnamon in small quantity, and remove from heat.
3. In a casserole or soup bowl, place a layer of the toasted bread mentioned first and another layer of the minced mixture made afterward, and continue in this manner until finished.
4. Then pour broth from the cooked pot or meat substance so it becomes moistened. Then place over heat, adding a little more broth so it boils, and remove immediately. If it becomes too dry, a little more broth may be added, but in small quantity.
5. Once the soup is cooked, make a beaten egg mixture and pour it on top, covering the soup bowl or casserole with a lid and placing some fire embers on top so it browns. If desired to garnish, artichoke hearts, fried birds, poultry breasts, egg slices, etc. may be placed on top.
