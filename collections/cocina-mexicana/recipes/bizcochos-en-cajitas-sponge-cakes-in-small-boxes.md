## Ingredients

- half a pound of sugar
- twelve egg yolks
- egg whites
- six ounces of fine flour
- two ounces of powdered sugar
- the zest of one lemon

## Directions

1. Put half a pound of sugar in a small baking pan, beat the egg whites with a small willow or white poplar switch for just over half an hour in a confiture pan or any earthenware vessel
2. Meanwhile, another person stirs the egg yolks and sugar with a wooden spatula the entire time
3. Once the whites are whipped as thick as firm snow, add the yolks with the sugar to them, stirring gently with the spatula
4. Then add six ounces of fine flour, two ounces of powdered sugar, and the zest of one lemon
5. The flour and sugar powder should have been passed through a sieve to mix well and remove debris
6. Mix everything well but gently, as this precaution is necessary so the beaten whites do not deflate and fall
7. Fill half-full the small tin or paper boxes with this batter, as it will puff up considerably in the oven
8. Before filling tin boxes, rub the interior with butter, and when they come out of the oven shake them to remove the hot bizcochos; but if these are made in white paper boxes, do not grease them with butter, and serve the bizcochos in the same boxes
9. Before putting them in the oven, dust them with sugar using a fine sieve
10. Bake at good heat until they turn a bright yellow
