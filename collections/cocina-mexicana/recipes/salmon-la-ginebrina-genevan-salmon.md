## Ingredients

- one salmon steak of two or three pounds
- red wine
- two carrots
- three onions
- two cloves
- bay leaf
- salt
- pepper
- parsley sprigs
- whole scallions
- butter
- flour

## Directions

1. Choose a good salmon steak of about two or three pounds, clean and scale it, and place in a saucepan.
2. Moisten with red wine and add two carrots, three sliced onions, two cloves, a bay leaf, salt, pepper, parsley sprigs, and some whole scallions.
3. Let the salmon cook for an hour and a half over low heat.
4. Ten minutes before serving, pass the cooking liquid through a sieve and pour into a saucepan with a good piece of butter kneaded with flour.
5. Place over heat and stir strongly until it boils, letting it reduce until only enough sauce remains to cover the salmon steak, skimming and degreasing as it reduces.
6. Drain the fish, place on a fish platter, and cover with the sauce after passing it through cheesecloth, finishing with two small pats of butter.
