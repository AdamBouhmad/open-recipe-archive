## Ingredients

- meat
- wide chile
- mulato chile
- roasted garlic
- aromatic herbs
- fine spices
- cilantro
- cumin seeds
- ginger
- grated nutmeg
- creole vinegar
- egg yolk vinegar
- veal, chicken, or other meat
- ham slices
- chorizos and large chorizos
- fried garlic
- olives
- capers
- fried oil with garlic
- carrots in vinegar infusion
- sliced cheese
- onion flowers

## Directions

1. For each part of meat, prepare one part wide chile and one part mulato chile by soaking to remove heat, then grind with roasted garlic, aromatic herbs, fine spices, a little cilantro, cumin, a small piece of ginger, all toasted, and a little grated nutmeg.
2. Mix everything together and grind on the metate with creole vinegar without adding water.
3. Once ground, add salt and a little egg yolk vinegar.
4. Cook the meat (veal, chicken, or as desired), ham slices, chorizos, and large chorizos.
5. Once cooked, remove the ham, slice the large chorizos, and dry the meats with a napkin.
6. Fry garlic cloves and let cool.
7. In a baking dish, spread a little ground chile as described, then layer: a layer of meat, a layer of ham, chorizos and large chorizos, olives, capers, and oil fried with garlic, and another layer of chile, making sure the meats are well moistened.
8. Continue layering as desired, but this should be done three or four days before eating.
9. When ready to serve, remove from the pan, place on a platter, and garnish with carrots in vinegar infusion, sliced cheese, and onion flowers.
