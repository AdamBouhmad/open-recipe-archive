## Ingredients

- one large squash, well-ripened in the sun
- water
- salt
- butter
- nutmeg
- bread crumbs
- cream or milk

## Directions

1. Peel and clean the inside of a well-ripened squash.
2. Cut it into equal slices.
3. Boil lightly salted water and blanch the squash slices for about a quarter hour.
4. Drain in a colander and trim so all pieces are the same size.
5. Put the trimmings in a saucepan with butter, salt, nutmeg, and a little bread crumb.
6. Moisten with a little cream or milk.
7. Place the saucepan on the fire, stirring constantly so it does not stick.
8. Pour half of this mixture into a deep dish.
9. Arrange the squash slices and bread slices in a crown shape, having used one pound of bread crumbs.
10. Cover the crown with the rest of the mixture from the saucepan.
11. Place the dish on hot coals to form a crust.
12. Serve with hot milk and fresh butter, and hot milk on the side.
