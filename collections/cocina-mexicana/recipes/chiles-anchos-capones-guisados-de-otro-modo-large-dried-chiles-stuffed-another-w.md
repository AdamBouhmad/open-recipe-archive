## Ingredients

- Large dried chiles
- Aged cheese, ground or grated
- Fresh cheese, if available
- Lard
- Small onions with heads
- Salted water
- Salt

## Directions

1. Heat the dried chiles slightly so they can be deveined without breaking, which should be done from one side without removing the stem
2. Soak them and then drain them
3. Once aired, fill them with ground or grated aged cheese, or fresh cheese if available, taking care that they remain flat rather than bulging
4. Fry them in lard, making sure they do not open or burn
5. Remove from the frying fat and fry small whole onions that have been cooked in salted water
6. Add the water in which the onions were cooked, and when it begins to boil, carefully place the fried chiles in this broth so they do not open
7. Let them soften in the broth, which should be brought to a boil; if necessary, add more water if the chiles do not soften quickly, as it is essential that when eaten they can be easily peeled
8. The broth should not be too thick, nor should it be left too watery
