## Ingredients

- one quarter of a quart of flour
- two drachms of beer foam
- a little warm water
- two drachms or quarter ounce of salt
- one ounce of sugar
- four ounces of butter
- six eggs
- hot cream
- two small glasses of good brandy

## Directions

1. Place one quarter quart of flour in a bowl and separate a quarter of it to make a light yeast with two drachms of beer foam and a little warm water
2. Let the yeast ferment at the bottom of the bowl, and once well fermented, add two drachms or a quarter ounce of salt, one ounce of sugar, four ounces of butter and six eggs
3. Mix everything well at once and finish this operation by moistening with hot cream, being necessary that the dough becomes as liquid as frying batter
4. Cover the bowl and let it ferment for two hours in a warm place, adding two small glasses of good brandy when an hour and a half has passed
5. Then knead thoroughly so it breaks or becomes corrupted and then heat the wafer iron to cook the wafers at serving time, which is done by bringing them to the table with powdered sugar on top
