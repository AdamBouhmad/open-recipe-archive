## Ingredients

- Twelve cuartillos of good white Castilian vinegar
- One and a half pounds of tarragon leaves, wilted in the shade
- A small bundle of cloves
- Stripped peels of two lemons

## Directions

1. Pour twelve cuartillos of good white Castilian vinegar into a large bottle
2. Add one and a half pounds of tarragon leaves that have been left to wilt in the shade, taking care to spread them out so they do not scald
3. When the leaves have become limp, add them to the bottle with the vinegar
4. Add a small bundle of cloves and the stripped peels of two lemons
5. Seal the bottle very well
6. Place it daily in the sun for fifteen days, or put it in the oven two or three times after the bread has been removed
7. After this time has passed, the vinegar is ready to use and it is unnecessary to add salt as is generally done
8. Decant the vinegar and press out the sediment or leaves remaining at the bottom of the vessel, filtering the vinegar through brown paper or a piece of cotton or bombazine cloth, or place the vinegar over a large horsehair sieve with a wheel of brown paper made of two sheets spread one over the other covering the entire bottom of the sieve and protruding two or three inches from the edges, and gently pour the vinegar through until it is perfectly clarified
9. Once this operation is complete, bottle it and seal it very well
