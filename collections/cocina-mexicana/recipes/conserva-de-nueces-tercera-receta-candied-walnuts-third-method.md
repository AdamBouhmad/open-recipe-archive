## Ingredients

- Walnuts, gathered at the end of May
- Cinnamon stick
- Clarified and strained syrup
- Flower water or orange blossom water, a small amount

## Directions

1. Gather walnuts at the end of May
2. Remove the thin skin and pierce ten or twelve times with a basting needle
3. Place in water
4. The next day, change the water, boil once, and return to fresh water; repeat this for three consecutive days until they lose their tint and become sweet
5. Insert a small piece of fine cinnamon in each hole
6. Boil in water to soften the flesh, then gradually remove with a skimmer and place in a pot
7. Heat the necessary amount of clarified and strained syrup to cover all the walnuts until it is almost at full stage and begins to form a thin film
8. Remove from heat and, while more warm than lukewarm, pour over the walnuts
9. The next day, remove the syrup from the walnuts and return it to heat to reach a higher stage that forms a thread or film, then pour it back over the walnuts, adding more if needed from loss during the operations; the new syrup must be made with the same requirements as before
10. Finally, add a small amount of rose water or orange blossom water to everything and store the preserve in jars
