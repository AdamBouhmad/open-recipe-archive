## Ingredients

- 14 ounces flour
- 1 egg
- 1 common spoonful melted lard cold
- water as needed
- cooked chard
- salt
- ground pepper
- grated cheese
- butter or lard for frying
- chicken or pot broth seasoned
- grated cheese
- ground pepper
- beef marrow cooked in the same broth
- small pieces of butter
- parsley leaves

## Directions

1. Make the dough with 14 ounces of flour, one egg, one spoonful of cold melted lard and the water needed for the flour to absorb
2. For the filling, make it with cooked chard chopped only in the green part, squeezed and seasoned with salt and ground pepper and mixed with grated cheese
3. Form the rabioles the same as in the preceding articles
4. Leave them on a board or tray to air until the next day
5. Fry them in butter or lard so they are not very golden, and cook them in seasoned chicken or pot broth
6. After cooking, separate them, and in the tureen or platter where they will be served, place a layer of grated cheese with ground pepper on top and another layer of rabioles, pouring a little of the same broth in which they were cooked
7. Continue placing layers until filling the tureen
8. On top of the last layer, in addition to cheese and pepper, place some small pieces of beef marrow that will have been cooked in the same rabioles broth, and other small pieces of butter
9. Place the platter over the steam of a pot or the tureen over hot coals so they stay hot without boiling and without the broth being consumed while they are carried to the table
10. Add some parsley leaves before serving
