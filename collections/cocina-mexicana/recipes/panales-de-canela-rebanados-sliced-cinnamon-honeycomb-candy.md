## Ingredients

- White and fluffy sugar, or little compact sugar
- Water, one cuartillo for each pound of sugar
- Egg whites for clarifying the syrup
- Lemon juice
- Cinnamon powder, one tablespoon per two pozuelos of syrup

## Directions

1. Choose white and fluffy sugar and make syrup with it, calculating one cuartillo of water for each pound of sugar, but setting aside some water both for beating the egg whites to clarify the syrup and to calm the boiling twice
2. Dissolve the sugar on the fire, stirring with a spoon until completely dissolved
3. Mix two beaten egg whites in water for each pound of sugar and continue stirring until the sugar is entirely dissolved
4. Place the pan on high heat so it quickly comes to a boil; when it begins to rise, add some of the separated water to calm it, using this opportunity to remove the foam quickly
5. When it rises again, add the remaining water and remove the pan from the heat
6. Outside the fire, skim the syrup again until it is perfectly clean and strain it through a fine cloth, letting what is strained rest until the next day, tilting the pan so the syrup can decant or separate from the sediment
7. The next day before beginning the operation, prepare two or three small pans or skillets the size of a small plate, the same number of spoons, a small brazier or anafe with fire, a small table with clean napkin, sheets of paper, a bowl or cup with cold water, cut lemons, cinnamon powder and clean popotes
8. When everything is ready, pour two pozuelos of unskimmed syrup into one of the small pans, placing it on the anafe with moderate heat, and as the syrup begins to boil, skim without inserting the spoon and add sixty drops of lemon
9. When there is no more foam to remove and the syrup begins to reach its point, test it with the popote, which when inserted in the syrup and then quickly in cold water, the sugar adhering to it should crack between the teeth and break like glass
10. At this point remove from the fire, beat it, and when it begins to rise, quickly pour it onto the paper, slicing as the fire cools before it hardens, but without separating the slices, which is left for when the honeycomb has cooled well
11. When this operation is finished, repeat in another pan with another spoon, because there is not time to wash the first one well, and for this reason several are prepared
12. Decrease the amount of lemon if the honeycomb has too much or add it if it has too little
13. When beating the two pozuelos of syrup, while one is being worked on, add one tablespoon of cinnamon powder to the other, which is the quantity proportioned to that syrup
14. In this way the pores of the honeycomb remain very fine and it is solid and of a delicate flavor
