## Ingredients

- Butter
- Toasted ground sponge cake
- Eggs
- Cinnamon sticks
- Clove
- Sugar
- Clarified syrup

## Directions

1. Melt the butter and remove from heat
2. Mix in as much toasted and ground sponge cake as needed to form a paste that is thick enough to hold together but not so compact that it cannot be eaten
3. Mix in eggs, beating the egg whites separately from the yolks and then combining both in the proportion of twelve eggs per pound of paste
4. Once everything is well incorporated, pour into a casserole dish lined with butter placed over gentle heat
5. Immediately cover with a comal or tin sheet, well fitted, so the cake does not ventilate, with hot coals on top, being careful that it does not burn but only browns
6. It is ready when it begins to smell; to be sure, use the straw test by inserting a straw into the cake—if it comes out coated, it has not set well; if it comes out dry, the cake is ready
7. Remove from heat and drain off excess fat
8. Pierce it with cinnamon sticks all the way to the bottom and with clove
9. Have ready clarified syrup at low point in the necessary quantity according to the size of the cake; generally make it with two and a half pounds of sugar, which is sufficient for regular-sized common cakes
10. Place the cake in this syrup, which is returned to heat to boil a while with the cake so it absorbs the sweetness
11. Remove and let rest until the next day, when it is returned to heat, letting the syrup reduce and basting the cake with it
12. When the syrup reaches a very high point, almost drying, remove from heat, take off the cinnamon sticks and cloves from the cake, place a plate on top, and flip it over
