## Ingredients

- one goose, not very tender
- ham
- parsley, finely chopped
- onions, finely chopped
- one head of garlic, finely chopped
- bay leaf
- thyme
- basil, finely chopped as powder
- salt
- pepper
- two heads of garlic, cleaned
- oil
- two glasses of water
- two glasses of white wine
- half glass of refined brandy
- coarse pepper
- nutmeg, grated
- thread
- one egg

## Directions

1. Empty the goose and gather its feet against the body, singe it over fire and clean it
2. Lard it with ham seasoned and flavored with parsley, onions, one head of garlic, bay leaf, thyme, basil finely chopped as powder, salt, and pepper
3. Prepare two heads of garlic cleaned, dissolve with oil and add salt and pepper; coat the goose with this well-mixed sauce
4. Return the goose to the grill until it takes on good color
5. Tie the goose with thread and place it in a pot of the same size with two glasses of water, two glasses of white wine, and half glass of refined brandy, with more salt and coarse pepper
6. Cover the pot well and let it cook over low heat for three or four hours
7. When cooked and the sauce sufficiently reduced so it can set and gel, arrange the goose on a plate, and when almost cold, pour the sauce over it
8. Do not bring to the table until it has set and taken on the consistency of jelly, and serve as a cold intermedio
