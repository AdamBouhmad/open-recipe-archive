## Ingredients

- Corn
- Nine ounces of white sugar
- One pound of lard
- Fine salt
- A little anise
- Warm water
- Corn leaves
- Grass or hay (optional)

## Directions

1. Prepare the corn as for nixtamal and remove the hull
2. Wash and grind entirely as if for atole
3. Once well ground, pass the dough through an ayate cloth
4. After sifting, mix nine ounces of white sugar and one pound of lard with corresponding fine salt and a little anise
5. Moisten the dough with warm water and beat well
6. Place quantities of the dough on washed, soaked, drained and dried corn leaves
7. Wrap and cover with them, fold in half, and form the tamales
8. Put a pot of sufficient capacity on the heat with very little water, optionally placing a little grass in the water to control the boil
9. Form a wooden grate above it and arrange the tamales so the water does not reach them and they cook only by steam
10. Cover the pot opening with a cazuela of its size and a cloth tied very well
11. When the dough detaches from the leaf it is a sign they are cooked
12. Remove from heat and serve hot, alone or with milk atole
