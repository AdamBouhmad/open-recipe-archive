## Ingredients

- Butter
- Lard
- Oil
- Flour
- Bread
- Aged cheese
- Garlic head
- Cinnamon
- Clove
- Black pepper
- Broth in which cod was cooked
- Cooked cod
- Oil

## Directions

1. In a pan over the fire, place four ounces of butter, two of lard and a little oil, and toast there less than an ounce of flour.
2. When toasted, add a loaf of bread grated into four pieces, and half a pound of aged grated cheese.
3. Add afterwards, separately, a cleaned garlic head, cinnamon, clove and pepper, all ground together.
4. Let everything toast, and add a little of the broth in which the cod was cooked, in the quantity necessary so it is neither too thin nor too thick.
5. When it has boiled well, add three pounds of already-cooked fish.
6. Once it gives two or three boils, remove and let rest, adding at serving time the cooking oil that is lacking to complete half a quarter of a liter, with what was put in the pan at the beginning.
