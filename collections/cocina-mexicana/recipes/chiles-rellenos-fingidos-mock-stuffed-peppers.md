## Ingredients

- chiles en conserva (preserved peppers)
- pasta ó conservilla de coco, de almendra (coconut or almond paste or preserve)
- huevo batido (beaten egg)
- manteca (lard)
- almíbar de medio punto (medium-soft sugar syrup)
- canela (cinnamon)
- almendras ó yemas cocidas (almonds or cooked egg yolks)
- canela en polvo (powdered cinnamon)
- cebolla picada (chopped onion)
- aceitunas (olives)
- chilítos (small peppers)
- huevo cocido (cooked egg)
- pasta de los colores convenientes (colored paste)

## Directions

1. Remove the preserved peppers from the syrup and drain
2. Allow them to air dry a little
3. Fill with coconut, almond, or other appropriate paste or preserve
4. Coat with beaten egg, cut somewhat short so they don't bulge too much
5. Fry in lard
6. Drain the chiles and remove the lard from the pan
7. Pour in medium-soft sugar syrup with cinnamon and almonds or cooked egg yolks, ground to thicken the syrup and imitate the broth
8. Place the chiles in the pan and let them boil once
9. Transfer to a serving dish or preserve bowl
10. Garnish on top with powdered cinnamon, chopped onion, olives, small peppers, and cooked egg, all imitated with appropriately colored paste
