## Ingredients

- beef pulp
- salt
- vinegar, a good bottle or more
- onion tails
- garlic heads, 4 crushed
- Parras or Málaga wine, one good glass
- cumin, 4 ounces
- garlic, 4 or 6 heads, well cleaned
- onion
- vinegar, 2 quartillos

## Directions

1. Remove all fat from the beef pulp, make cuts like those for cecina (dried beef), and apply plenty of fine salt to them.
2. Transfer to a dry cooking pot and add a bottle or more of good vinegar, onion tails, four crushed garlic heads, and place over the fire.
3. When the vinegar is nearly consumed, add warm water that covers the meat well and a good glass of Parras or Málaga wine.
4. Let boil until the meat is well cooked; pour into a bowl until it cools.
5. Grind four ounces of cumin, four or six well-cleaned garlic heads, and one onion.
6. Add two quartillos of vinegar and leave the meat in this marinade for two days.
7. After this time, remove it, wrapping it in a new petate (woven mat), in which wrapped it is pressed for eight days, then remove and hang in the air.
