## Ingredients

- Three pounds of beef pulp
- Three or four old partridges
- Two pounds of veal shank
- One pheasant
- Carrots
- Onions
- Three or four stalks of celery
- Three cloves
- Small bunch of fennel
- Three young partridges
- Breadcrumbs
- Broth

## Directions

1. Place three pounds of beef pulp, three or four old partridges, two pounds of veal shank, one pheasant, carrots, onions, three or four stalks of celery, three cloves, and a small bunch of fennel in a pot or medium marmite and let everything cook together
2. Roast three young partridges on a spit and after cooling, pound them in a mortar with breadcrumbs three times larger than an egg, soaked in broth
3. Moisten the pounded mixture with the same broth and, mixing well, pass it through a sieve
4. Mix the broth of the beef and partridges with this substance so that it is neither too thick nor too liquid
5. Place over gentle heat without letting it boil
