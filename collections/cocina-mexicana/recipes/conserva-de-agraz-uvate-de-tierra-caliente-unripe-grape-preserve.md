## Ingredients

- unripe grapes (agraz)
- cold water
- boiling water
- clarified syrup (of small lizo point)

## Directions

1. Choose unripe grapes that are neither too green nor too ripe, meaning that by opening them on one side, you can easily remove the small pits with a needle
2. As you perform the operation of removing the pits, add them to cold water
3. Then place in a pot in boiling water, taking care that the boil is not too strong so it does not reduce to marmalade, and remove from heat the moment it rises to the surface of the water
4. Let cool in this same water, and when cool, return it to a very gentle heat to make it turn green again, then add to cold water again
5. Prepare clarified syrup in a quantity proportional to what you want to make of preserve, at the point of small lizo
6. Let the unripe grapes drain on a sieve and add them immediately to the syrup, letting them rest there until the following day
7. On that day, removing them from the syrup let them drain a second time, returning to the syrup the point it had before, adding the unripe grapes to it as on the previous day, and repeating these operations for four consecutive days
8. On the fifth day let the syrup reach the high pearled point to add the unripe grapes, and give it only one boil with them
9. Skim and pour into jars or pots, which are covered with a saucer the size and measure of the mouth, gluing and papering the joints
