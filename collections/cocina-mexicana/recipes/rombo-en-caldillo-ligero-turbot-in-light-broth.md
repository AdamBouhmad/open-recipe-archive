## Ingredients

- Large turbot, thick and white
- Salt
- Water
- Three or four quartillos of milk
- Lemon juice
- Parsley
- Butter sauce or cream sauce

## Directions

1. Select the largest turbot, which should be thick and of very white flesh
2. Remove the roe and entrails by making an opening near the head
3. Wash many times, changing the water
4. Remove two or three vertebrae from the spine by opening it slightly on the dark side, and tie the jaw to the bone of the opening
5. Rub the entire surface with lemon juice
6. Place to cook in salt water, to which three or four quartillos of milk can be added
7. Apply strong heat at the stove, and when it begins to boil, reduce the heat so it finishes cooking without boiling
8. Keep hot and ten minutes before serving, drain it
9. Serve in fish platters on napkins adorned with fresh parsley and accompanied by a sauce boat with butter sauce or cream sauce
10. For the cream sauce: place four ounces of butter in a pan, one tablespoon of flour, salt, coarse pepper and half a quartillo of good milk; stir this sauce over the fire, and if it is too thick, add milk until it reaches the right consistency
11. Divide the cooked turbot into pieces, place in a pan and pour the sauce over it
12. Fry all of it making it jump, and keep it hot until serving time
