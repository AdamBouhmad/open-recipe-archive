## Ingredients

- rice
- water
- salt
- lard
- pepper
- cloves
- cinnamon
- cumin
- saffron
- tomatoes
- garlic
- onions
- parsley
- mint
- chile peppers
- olives
- ham
- longaniza sausage
- chorizones
- sausages
- vinegar
- eggs

## Directions

1. After washing and soaking the rice, dry it and cook with very little water and necessary salt
2. A little before it finishes cooking, add a small amount of lard with pepper, cloves, cinnamon, and cumin, all in small quantity and ground
3. When cooked, add a small amount of ground saffron, let it boil until no broth remains, remove from heat and save for the next day in a cool place
4. Chop very finely raw tomatoes, garlic, onions, parsley, mint, chile peppers, and olives, and fry in plenty of lard
5. When the seasoning is well fried, add saffron and cumin ground with a piece of bread soaked in vinegar, put in the mortar and set aside
6. Cut into short pieces lean ham and a small amount of fat, and mix with small pieces of longaniza and the filling from some chorizones and sausages
7. Return the pot to heat and fry the ham with sausages and chorizones with a little good vinegar
8. When everything is fried and well mixed, remove from heat, grease the bottom of a mold with lard and add well-beaten eggs with whites and yolks beaten separately then combined
9. Add the rice and stir so it does not have too much egg
10. Put a layer of this rice in the pot, another of minced meat, and alternate layers until finishing with a layer of rice
11. Put the cake to cook over gentle heat, and cover on top with a comal with a little more heat
12. From time to time brush the top with lard using a feather and leave on heat until the cake sets, then remove it leaving gentle heat in the comal
13. With the feather brush egg yolk on top, and when it has dried, dust with sugar
