## Ingredients

- two pounds of fine flour
- half cup of butter
- half cup of lard, both melted and cold
- three ounces of sifted sugar
- two ounces of yeast dissolved in water
- one cup of milk
- a little salt
- less tequesquite dissolved in water and strained through a napkin
- three egg yolks and one egg white
- water as needed

## Directions

1. Place two pounds of fine flour on the table and in the hollow or hole made in the middle, add half cup of butter and another half cup of lard, both melted and cold
2. Add three ounces of sifted sugar, two ounces of yeast dissolved in water, one cup of milk, a little salt, and tequesquite dissolved in water and strained through a napkin
3. Add three egg yolks and one egg white and the necessary water to bind it
4. Knead everything and beat until the dough is smooth and sticky
5. Extend it then on the table dusted with flour, cutting the wafers with a wheel cutter
6. Place these to air on napkins, and before they dry, fry them in hot lard, twisted or wrapped around reeds, which will be placed in the lard using a wooden spatula
7. When removed from the frying, remove the reeds
