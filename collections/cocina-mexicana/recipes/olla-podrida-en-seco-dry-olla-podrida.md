## Ingredients

- Rice
- Lamb
- Chickpeas
- Fat cured beef or pork
- Pieces of ham
- One or two pig tails
- A piece of bacon
- Three or four chorizos
- Several sausages
- Pieces of longaniza sausage
- One or two chickens
- Cabbage leaves
- Lettuce
- Chard
- Garlic heads
- Onion heads
- Four to six wide chiles
- Turnips
- Carrots
- Cumin
- Clove
- Cinnamon
- Ginger
- Saffron
- Black pepper
- Sea salt

## Directions

1. One day before serving, wash rice early in the morning, changing the water several times and let it soak for about an hour
2. Drain it well and place it in the sun to dry on a napkin
3. In cold water, soak lamb and chickpeas
4. In separate water, soak fat cured beef or pork, pieces of ham, one or two pig tails, a piece of bacon, three or four chorizos, sausages and pieces of longaniza
5. In the afternoon, wash and remove fat from the lamb, divide it into regular portions
6. Divide one or two chickens into quarters
7. Clean the ham or bacon with hot water
8. Prepare vegetables of all kinds, wash them well and let everything drain
9. Cover the bottom of a proportioned pot with tender cabbage leaves
10. On them place a layer of desalted fat cured meat, and on that another layer of thick slices of cabbage, lettuce and chard, well cleaned
11. Add four to six whole garlic heads, the same number of onion heads, four to six wide chiles, an equal number of cleaned turnips and carrots, with a good portion of chickpeas and rice that have been soaking
12. On this layer place another of lamb portions and chicken quarters, pieces of bacon and ham
13. Form another layer with all the substances that were put with the cabbage and other vegetables, repeating on top the lamb, chicken and ham
14. Cover this with vegetables, and the last layer is formed with all the pork meats, such as tails, bacon, cured meat, chorizos, sausages and longaniza
15. Once the pot is arranged this way, grind cumin, clove, cinnamon, ginger, saffron, black pepper and sea salt in proportionate quantity and dissolve in a cup of water, which is poured into the pot without adding more water
16. Cover with a dish that fits well to the mouth, and seal the joints around with starch paste
17. In the evening of the day before the olla is to be used, place it on a very low fire covered with hot ashes, and maintain it on that fire until midday the following day, trying to keep the fire even
18. To prevent burning, put a little water in the dish covering the pot, without allowing this water to dampen the paste sealing it
19. Some, after arranging all the layers in the olla-podrida, dissolve the spices in a quarter-liter of white wine and another quarter-liter of Castilian vinegar, distributing the resulting broth in the vegetable layers, then covering the pot as described
