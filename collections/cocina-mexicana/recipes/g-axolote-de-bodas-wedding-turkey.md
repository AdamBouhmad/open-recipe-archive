## Ingredients

- large, fat güaxolote (turkey)
- lamb or beef sweetbreads, cooked
- lamb tongues and kidneys, cooked
- güaxolote giblets
- giblets of various birds
- potato or chestnut pieces
- salt
- pepper
- fine breadcrumbs
- lard
- large chorizos
- small chorizos
- longaniza sausage
- morcón sausage
- oil
- onion, sliced
- lamb liver
- capers
- parsley
- clove
- cinnamon
- vinegar
- mint, chopped
- raisins, pitted
- whole onions
- wide chiles, deveined
- garlic heads, whole
- ham slices
- white wine
- good broth
- parsley bundle
- whole onion
- garlic head
- cloves of spice
- thyme
- bay leaf
- basil
- sliced onions
- carrot
- turnip or parsnip
- egg yolks
- pine nuts, toasted
- parsley, finely chopped
- wine from Parras or Malaga

## Directions

1. Select the largest and fattest turkey available and empty it from the bottom.
2. After cleaning well, cut off the feet and neck, lowering the skin downward so that the excess remains and can cook, covering three fingers of neck.
3. Two days after slaughtering, prepare the following filling.
4. Cook the sweetbreads, tongues, kidneys and giblets, then cut them into equal dice pieces.
5. Add equal pieces of potato or chestnut, dust well with salt and pepper and roll in very fine breadcrumbs.
6. Fry in lard until perfectly golden, then set aside.
7. In another pan with lard, fry the chorizos, small chorizos, longaniza and morcón, also browning them in lard.
8. Remove them, empty the large chorizos and slice the longaniza into thin slices; halve the small chorizos.
9. Mix everything with the sweetbread mixture and place a clean pan on the fire with equal parts lard and oil.
10. Fry much sliced onion in it, then grind a lamb liver with a good handful of capers and parsley, frying everything in the lard.
11. Add salt, clove, cinnamon, a little vinegar and oil, with a handful of chopped mint.
12. Let it boil until it thickens, and just before removing from heat, add pitted raisins and set aside.
13. When it has cooled a little, mix all the fried, chopped meat into that broth.
14. If the gravy is somewhat dry, increase it with oil; but so that it does not become watery.
15. Sew the neck skin to the turkey, turn it over on it, and gradually fill it, adjusting the meat well in the hollows and pouring the broth equally.
16. When it is half filled, place some hard-boiled egg yolks inside.
17. When fully filled, sew the opening, taking care that it closes completely so no oil escapes.
18. Place the turkey in a large pot that covers it completely and add adequate salt, half a dozen whole onions, eight wide deveined chiles and three whole garlic heads.
19. Let it boil, taking care to turn it from time to time and adding boiling water if it reduces much, until it is cooked.
20. Let it reduce, and brown it in the remaining fat, increasing it if very little remains with cooking oil, until well browned, which can be achieved faster by placing a comal on top with some fire.
21. Once the turkey is browned, serve it with the following sauce.
22. Take a little of the fat in which the turkey was fried, and in a pan with it fry all the leftover gravy and filling, ground, with ten or twelve egg yolks, a cup of toasted pine nuts, finely chopped parsley and adequate salt.
23. When everything is well fried, add a little vinegar, and as soon as it has thickened enough, loosen the gravy with wine from Parras or Malaga and pour into the sauce boat.
