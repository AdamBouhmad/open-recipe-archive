## Ingredients

- twelve eggs, whites and yolks beaten separately then together
- two tablespoons of flour
- half a pound of cracked almond not very finely ground
- some toasted sesame
- lard for coating pan
- two pounds of sugar for clarified syrup

## Directions

1. Beat the whites and yolks of twelve eggs separately and then together; mix in two tablespoons of flour, half a pound of cracked almond not very finely ground, and a little toasted sesame; pour all into a pan coated with lard and let it set over two heats; then slice the mamón and place in clarified syrup made with two pounds of sugar, cooking on the heat until it reaches the preserve point. Serve with its syrup and decorate with pine nuts and cinnamon. They are also made without sesame; but in that case cook the syrup with fig leaves.
