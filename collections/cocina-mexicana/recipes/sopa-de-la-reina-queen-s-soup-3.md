## Ingredients

- Four fat hens
- Twelve ounces of lean ham, cut into pieces
- Two turnips, in pieces
- Three carrots
- One onion
- A dozen cloves stuck in the onion
- A little parsley and thyme
- Two bay leaves
- A little celery and one garlic clove
- Sufficient water

## Directions

1. Kill the four fat hens the day before they are to be used, clean them well and cook them in a clean pot with the ham, turnips, carrots, onion with cloves, parsley, thyme, bay leaves, celery, and garlic clove.
2. Add sufficient water and place on the fire, taking great care to skim them, as this considerably depends on the good taste of the soup.
3. As soon as boiling begins, leave them on a gentle fire, taking care that they cook continuously until they are well cooked.
4. Remove and let cool, resting on the lower jaw, strip them of their skin and grind the meat on a metate until it becomes a compact paste, moistening it from time to time with a little of the water in which they were cooked.
5. Pass through a sieve and then take half a pound of very white rice, wash it and half cook it, discard this water and with fresh water cook it again until it is tender.
6. Grind it the same as the hens, pass through the sieve and mix everything together, add the water in which the hens were cooked, heat well and serve.
7. The soup will turn out better if you add half a pound of veal leg, as the broth will be stronger. One or two chicken breasts should be set aside to make croquettes and place them in the soup.
