## Ingredients

- 25 to 30 tender corn ears
- Twelve ounces of lard
- Sugar
- Salt
- Corn leaves
- Water

## Directions

1. Select the most tender corn ears and remove the heads with a knife
2. Separate the best leaves and cut the kernels
3. Grind the kernels well and lower them from the metate with a little milk
4. Add sugar and salt to taste
5. Heat the lard until very hot and pour it over the prepared corn
6. Beat until well incorporated
7. Join two leaves at the head and place the necessary quantity to form the tamal
8. Wrap and prepare a pot with some corn ears at the bottom and a little salted water
9. Place over heat and once it boils, arrange the tamales inside
10. Cover with the remaining leaves and a well-fitting lid
11. Let boil for one or two hours, ensuring water does not run out at the bottom
