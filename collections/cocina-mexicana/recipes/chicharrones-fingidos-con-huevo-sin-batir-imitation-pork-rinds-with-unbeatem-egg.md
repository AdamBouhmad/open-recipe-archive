## Ingredients

- Two large cups of milk
- One level cup of flour (rasada)
- Four eggs
- Lard
- Hot syrup (almíbar)
- Raisins
- Pine nuts
- Almonds

## Directions

1. In a bowl, combine the milk, flour, and four eggs
2. Work the mixture by hand until no lumps remain
3. Pour the mixture into a frying pan greased with lard, placed over coals with a griddle on top
4. When cooked, divide into squares and mark smaller squares with a knife to imitate the cuts of pork rind, leaving the bottom layer about the thickness of a finger
5. Fry in very hot lard in sufficient quantity to bathe the pieces vigorously so they puff up
6. Place them in hot syrup without letting them boil
7. Remove as soon as the syrup penetrates them, ensuring more syrup covers the top than the bottom
8. Serve adorned with raisins, pine nuts, and almonds
