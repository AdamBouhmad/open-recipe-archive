## Ingredients

- duck
- parsley
- onion
- garlic head
- cloves
- thyme
- bay leaf
- basil
- cilantro
- onion slices
- carrot
- parsnip
- butter
- 2 large caldron cups of broth
- 1 cup white wine

## Directions

1. After emptying and plucking the duck, singe the down over a flame and bend the legs forward as if the duck were lying down
2. Place in a casserole of appropriate size with a small bundle of parsley, onion, a head of garlic, two cloves, thyme, bay leaf, basil and a little fresh cilantro
3. Add onion slices, a carrot, a parsnip, a piece of butter, two large caldron cups of broth and another cup of white wine
4. Cook over low heat, and when the duck meat is tender to the touch, strain the broth through a sieve
5. Skim the fat and reduce until it reaches the consistency of a sauce
6. Pour the sauce over the duck to serve. The duck may be divided into quarters before cooking if desired.
