## Ingredients

- two pounds almonds with skin
- a little water
- half ounce cinnamon
- fragrant water
- pieces of candied citron or lemon
- one pound clarified syrup cooked to the crack stage
- cinnamon and syrup for the glaze

## Directions

1. Grind two pounds of almonds with their skin, sprinkling the mass from time to time with a little water to prevent it from turning to oil.
2. Place it then in a pot over low fire to dry, and add half an ounce of cinnamon passed through a drum sieve, a little fragrant water, and some pieces of candied citron or lemon.
3. When everything is dry, gradually add and mix one pound of clarified syrup cooked to the crack stage, little by little with a spoon, stirring the paste constantly so it does not stick to the pot.
4. When the dough is manageable, that is, when it does not stick to the hand, remove from the fire and empty it onto a plate to cool.
5. Then form the mostachones as described in the previous article (oval shape, pointed at the ends, four or five fingers long and three fingers wide).
6. These mostachones can be glazed, although many neither glaze nor bake them, as either way they are very good.
7. If you want them glazed, mix a little cinnamon with syrup and let it cook until it reaches the crack stage.
8. Then glaze the mostachones one by one, place them on paper, and set the glaze with the oven door cover.
