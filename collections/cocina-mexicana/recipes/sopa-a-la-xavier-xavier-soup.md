## Ingredients

- three-quarters of a quarter-liter of flour
- six egg yolks
- two whole eggs
- salt
- broth
- one tablespoon of finely chopped parsley
- one-quarter of a grated nutmeg
- a little coarse pepper

## Directions

1. Dissolve three-quarters of a quarter-liter of flour with six egg yolks and two whole eggs, a little salt, and broth in sufficient quantity so that the dough becomes very liquid and can be passed through a slotted spoon or a colander with small holes
2. Add one tablespoon of finely chopped parsley, which is mixed well with the dough, and then one-quarter of a grated nutmeg and a little coarse pepper
3. When everything is well incorporated, fill a saucepan up to three-quarters of its height, put it on the fire with good broth, and when it is boiling, pass the prepared dough over it through the slotted spoon, being careful that the broth continues boiling so that the dough sets, and skim it so that the soup remains clean
4. This can be made lean, using lean broth instead of meat broth, and then it needs only boil for a quarter of an hour
