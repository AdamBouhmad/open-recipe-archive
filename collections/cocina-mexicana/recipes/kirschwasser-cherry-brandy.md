## Ingredients

- ripe black cherries
- wet ash or thick mortar
- 

## Directions

1. Remove the stems from ripe black cherries
2. Fill a medium barrel with them
3. Cover with wet ash or thick mortar to form a crust that maintains the spirituous essence of the fruit in fermentation and prevents it from evaporating
4. After one month or six weeks, remove the covering and prepare the remaining cherries for distillation
5. Place the grate in the cucurbit, fill it to two-thirds with the juice and pulp of the cherries
6. Assemble the still and conduct the fire with caution and gradually until only a thin stream drips
7. Obtain a very clear liquor, ceasing the operation as soon as you perceive that the tails (weak flavorless product) begin to pass
8. Discard the refuse from the first product and place the same quantity of cherries as the first time back in the cucurbit, continuing until everything is distilled
9. Rectify the kirschwasser afterward in a bain-marie, which should reach twenty-two to twenty-three degrees after this second distillation
