## Ingredients

- pichones (squabs)
- manteca (lard)
- tajadas de jamón (ham slices)
- agua caliente (hot water)
- sal (salt)
- vinagre (vinegar)
- tornachiles crudos (raw chile peppers)
- azúcar (sugar)
- xitomate asado y molido (roasted and ground tomatoes)
- alcaparras (capers)
- peregil picado (chopped parsley)
- vino de Málaga (Málaga wine)
- canela molida (ground cinnamon)

## Directions

1. After cleaning the squabs, fry them with ham slices and plenty of lard
2. When they are already browned, slice plenty of onion and garlic, add hot water and salt with a little vinegar, letting it boil until cooked
3. Cut some raw chile peppers and add them with a small piece of sugar, letting the sauce reduce so the squabs brown
4. Then add roasted and ground tomatoes fried separately with capers and chopped parsley, frying the tomato again with the squabs
5. When almost dry, add a glass of Málaga wine, and dust with a little ground cinnamon
