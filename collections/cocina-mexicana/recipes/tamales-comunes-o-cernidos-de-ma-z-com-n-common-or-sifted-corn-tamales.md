## Ingredients

- corn prepared as nixtamal
- nine and a half ounces of white sugar
- one pound of lard
- fine salt
- a little anise
- warm water
- corn husks, washed, soaked, drained, and dried

## Directions

1. Prepare the corn as nixtamal, remove the husks, wash it and grind it completely the same way as for making atole
2. Once the mass is well ground, pass it through a cloth or ayate, and after sifting, mix nine and a half ounces of white sugar and one pound of lard with the corresponding fine salt and a little anise, moistening the mass as needed with warm water
3. Beat it well, and on washed corn husks that have been soaked, drained, and dried, place the quantities of mass that they can hold, wrapping and covering with them, and folding in half to form the tamales
4. Put a pot of sufficient capacity on the fire with very little water; you can put a little grass inside this water to stop the boiling; above this, form a small lattice or framework of wooden strips, and arrange the tamales so that the water does not reach them, cooking them only by steam
5. Cover the mouth of the pot with a bowl of its size and a cloth that is tied well
6. When the mass separates from the husk, it is a sign that it is cooked; then the tamales are done, removed from the fire and served hot, alone or with milk atole
