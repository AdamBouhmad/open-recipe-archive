## Ingredients

- Veal
- Parsley
- Onions
- Good oil
- Salt
- Pepper
- Paper
- Lard
- Flour
- Broth
- Vinegar

## Directions

1. Prepare a marinade with a good handful of parsley and one or two well-chopped onions, thinned with several tablespoons of good oil, and seasoned with salt and pepper.
2. Soak the veal in the marinade on all sides so that it absorbs all the oil when placed on the spit.
3. Spread the fine herbs over the entire surface of the veal piece and hold them in place with greased paper tied with string so the herbs do not fall.
4. When the roast is almost cooked, so that it only needs to take color, carefully remove the paper and scrape off with a knife the herbs adhering to the paper or to the surface of the roast.
5. Put the herbs in a pot with proportioned lard and a tablespoon of flour to make a light brown roux, moistened with a little fat-free broth and a few drops of vinegar.
6. Serve this sauce in a sauceboat at the same time as the veal tied with fine herbs.
