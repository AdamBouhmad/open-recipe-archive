## Ingredients

- 1 large turkey
- 1 pound mulato chile
- 4 ounces pasilla chile
- 4 ounces ancho chile
- lard
- tomatoes
- 1 cuartilla sesame seeds
- 1 real almonds
- 1 pork knuckle
- pork meat scraps
- hot water
- chile seeds
- anise

## Directions

1. Devein all the chiles and place lard in a skillet over low heat so the chile browns little by little
2. Grind tomatoes according to the quantity of chile and grind well
3. Toast a cuartilla of sesame seeds on the comal and grind dry until it yields; brown a real of almonds in lard and grind also dry with shells and combine with the sesame
4. Cut the turkey into quarters, wash it, dry with a cloth, and brown the portions in lard until golden
5. The day before, cook a pork knuckle with pork meat scraps and remove from heat when half-cooked
6. The next morning early, place the turkey in a clay pot
7. When ready to make the mole, heat water in a pot until very hot then lower heat
8. Place an empty clay pot on the fire and once hot add a little lard; when well hot add the chile to fry well, adding hot water little by little and breaking it up with a spoon; once well fried transfer to the pot with the turkey
9. In the same manner fry the tomato, break it up with a spoon in hot water and add to the pot with the turkey
10. Rinse the pot where the chile was fried with hot water where the turkey is cooking; brown the knuckle and scraps with all the broth in which it cooked
11. Place on the fire with everything together and add water in regular quantity but not so it becomes watery, adding it hot
12. Grind the almonds with a spoon and add to the mole
13. Toast some chile seeds and a little anise on the comal, taking care not to burn them
