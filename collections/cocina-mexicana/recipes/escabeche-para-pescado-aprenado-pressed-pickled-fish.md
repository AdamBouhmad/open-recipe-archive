## Ingredients

- Fresh fish, any kind
- Sour orange or lemon
- Oil
- Garlic cloves with skin, crushed
- Bay leaves, raw
- Thyme, raw
- Oregano, raw
- Pepper
- Salt
- Oil from frying
- Strong vinegar
- Wood wheel
- Heavy weight

## Directions

1. Cut the fish into regular-sized slices and begin to marinate with sour orange or lemon.
2. A little before frying, place in water to clean and soak.
3. Heat oil in a pan over the fire, and when very hot, add crushed garlic cloves with skin in sufficient quantity to layer between the fish slices in the barrel.
4. When the garlic is fried, remove it, and fry the fish in the same oil without browning or toasting so it does not harden, then let cool.
5. After about twelve hours, barrel the fish, first placing a layer of bay leaves, thyme, and oregano raw, some of the fried garlic, with pepper, salt, oil from the frying, and strong vinegar.
6. Place another layer of fish on top, and continue thus until the barrel is full, with the last layer being fish.
7. Place a wooden wheel on top with a heavy weight to press the fish, which can be eaten after two days.
