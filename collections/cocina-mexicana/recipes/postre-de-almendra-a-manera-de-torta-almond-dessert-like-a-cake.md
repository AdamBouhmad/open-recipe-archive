## Ingredients

- sugar
- water
- egg (for clarifying syrup)
- lemon
- almonds, crushed
- biscuit, ground
- white honey
- egg yolks

## Directions

1. Dissolve a large quantity of sugar in proportionate water over heat
2. Clarify the syrup with egg and lemon, then remove the foam
3. Add eight pounds of crushed almonds (neither very finely ground nor whole) and stir so they do not burn
4. Test the point on a spoon; when the syrup thickens it is ready
5. Store for another day
6. When ready to use, mix in some ground biscuit, a little white honey, and as many egg yolks as desired
7. Burn the cake molds two or three days before use
8. Pour the dessert into them and heat the oven until it is hot coals, then spread the coals and place the molds with plenty of cold butter on top
9. Spread the mass so the bottom is neither too thick nor too thin
10. When slightly cool, adjust the edges so the sides join the bottom to prevent the cakes from falling apart
11. The oven should burn for two or three hours and rest for the same amount of time, maintaining good heat and being well-settled
12. When the oven is in coals, pour the paste into the molds (not filling them completely to prevent overflow)
13. Cut strips of dough from the wheel slightly thicker than floor strips and slightly less than a finger's width
14. Moisten the edge and arrange as a rolled border, sprinkling sesame seed on top
15. Place them in a clean oven without water, using one as a test; if it boils very briefly it is not ready, so wait until the oven is at good temperature before placing the rest
