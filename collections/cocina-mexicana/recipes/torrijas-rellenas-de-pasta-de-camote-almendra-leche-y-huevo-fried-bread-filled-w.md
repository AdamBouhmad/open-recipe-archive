## Ingredients

- ground sweet potato
- sugar
- milk without water
- six egg yolks per pound of sugar
- four ounces of ground almond per pound of sweet potato
- orange flower water
- white wine
- raisins
- almonds
- pine nuts
- toasted sesame seeds
- ground cinnamon
- bread slices

## Directions

1. Mix ground sweet potato with sugar in equal proportion and two gourds of milk for each pound of sugar
2. Cook over heat and before reaching medium consistency, add six well-beaten egg yolks per pound of sugar, adding one at a time while stirring the paste quickly so it does not stick
3. Add four ounces of ground almond per pound of sweet potato, dissolved in a little orange flower water
4. Cook to a point slightly lower than cajeta, then remove from heat and pour onto a plate to rest until the next day
5. Meanwhile, boil two or three pounds of sugar with water according to the quantity of fried bread needed
6. Clarify and when the syrup reaches medium consistency, remove from heat and add cinnamon, a little clove, a quarter or less of white wine, raisins, almonds, pine nuts, toasted sesame seeds and orange flower water; set aside until the next day
7. Cut bread slices carefully so they do not fall apart
8. Mix with the paste made the previous day, raisins, almonds, pine nuts and sesame seeds
9. Place a little of this mixture on each slice and cover with its companion slice
10. Take each fried bread carefully in hand and coat vigorously with beaten egg that has been prepared with the yolks and whites beaten separately
11. Fry in lard of regular temperature (if too cold the fried breads will become greasy, if too hot they will burn)
12. Remove from lard and place where they can drain
13. When serving, pour syrup with gragea and ground cinnamon over them
