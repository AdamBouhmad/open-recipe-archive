## Ingredients

- Water
- White salt

## Directions

1. Fill a bronze pot or other pot of appropriate size with water
2. Place it on the fire and when it boils, gradually add white salt, letting it dissolve
3. To determine the necessary quantity, first place an egg in the boiling water; when it rises to the surface and holds itself there by itself, it is a sign that the water is well salted and the brine is made
4. Remove from heat and let cool in the pot, or in a bronze or tin pot, and pour into earthenware vessels if it is a copper pot, because the verdigris that would form in it is very harmful
5. Only pour into the curing place when it is cold, as otherwise it would give an unpleasant taste to the meats to be cured
6. If the brine is not to be used immediately but saved for when needed, skim it, remove from heat, let it cool and settle, strain without disturbing the sediment, and store for use
