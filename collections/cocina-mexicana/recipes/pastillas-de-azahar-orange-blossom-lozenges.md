## Ingredients

- three or four pounds of superfine white sugar
- double quantity of orange blossom water

## Directions

1. Pound three or four pounds of superfine white sugar in a marble mortar, separating the finest part for other compositions as described.
2. Dissolve the remaining sugar in a clean earthenware vessel with double quantity of orange blossom water, using a small spatula while pouring the water gently and at intervals until the paste condenses.
3. If too much water was added and the paste is liquid, thicken it with a little powdered sugar kept in reserve.
4. It is ready when a portion taken with the spatula and tilted falls off by itself.
5. Place four ounces of this paste in a spouted pan on a hot burner and heat the paste until it liquefies, stirring with a flat stick shaped like a spatula.
6. When the paste begins to boil, remove the pan and stir two or three more times, then pour onto tin sheet plates as follows:
7. Hold the pan with the left hand and tilt its spout gently, allowing the paste to flow while using a knitting needle attached to a wooden handle held in the right hand to guide it, directing the pan or needle alternately so the paste falls onto the plates in the shape of small buttons about the size of a coin, which are called lozenges.
8. Endeavor to line them up as they are made and that all have the same quantity of paste, which is only achieved by keeping the left hand steady.
9. After one hour, remove from the plates and place on papers in the oven, where they should remain one day, as more time would diminish the perfume.
10. These lozenges can produce twenty pounds or more per day.
