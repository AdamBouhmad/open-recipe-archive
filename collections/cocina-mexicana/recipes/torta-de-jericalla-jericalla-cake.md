## Ingredients

- Milk, boiled until very concentrated and sweet
- Three egg yolks for each cup of milk
- Sugar
- Egg white and lemon for clarification
- Marquesote (sponge cake), in slices
- White wine
- Cinnamon, raisins, almonds, and pine nuts for decoration
- Sugar for syrup

## Directions

1. Boil the milk until very concentrated and well sweetened, then measure it in cups
2. For each cup, lightly beat three egg yolks, which are stirred with the milk
3. In a pan coated with butter, place marquesote in layers, wetting each layer with the prepared milk and filling the pan in this way
4. After the last layer is well moistened, put the pan of milk on the fire so it curdles more and remains with skin, like jericalla, and pour it over the marquesote in the pan
5. Cook at two fires or in the oven
6. Test with a knife or a straw if the cake is cooked, and once done, leave for another day
7. On the next day, clarify syrup made at medium point, strain through a serviette and put back on the fire
8. Divide the cake into slices of the size desired, pierce with a straw and put in the honey to boil on low heat
9. Once the syrup is at point, remove from heat, put the cake on plates and decorate with raisins, almonds, pine nuts, and powdered cinnamon on top
