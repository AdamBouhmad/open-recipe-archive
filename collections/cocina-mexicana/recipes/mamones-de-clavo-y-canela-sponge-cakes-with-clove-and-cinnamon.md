## Ingredients

- clarified syrup at the point of thread
- one and a half pounds of sugar
- half a pound of ground almond
- clove and cinnamon powder to taste
- twenty egg yolks
- almond preserve
- a little sifted starch if needed
- lard for coating pan

## Directions

1. Make clarified syrup at the thread point, combining it in water with one and a half pounds of sugar and away from the heat mix in half a pound of ground almond, clove and cinnamon powder according to taste, for some like the mamones more aromatic and others less; return the mixture to the heat and as soon as it dries, break it apart and let it cool immediately; meanwhile, beat twenty egg yolks until they become hard; dissolve the almond preserve in a little of this beaten mixture, then mix all the remaining egg, thickening everything with a little sifted starch if it becomes too liquid; pour into a pan coated with lard, so that it does not fill but the mass remains to half its height, and let it set over two heats, taking care that it does not burn; then pour it onto a plate, cut into slices that are placed in syrup at half point, putting it on the heat precisely the time needed for the syrup to reach the point; then arrange it on an appropriate plate, studding it with pine nuts and powdering with cinnamon.
