## Ingredients

- Thirty well-cleaned carrots
- Half a pound of butter
- Seven or eight onions
- Broth
- Sugar
- Curled sauce

## Directions

1. Cut thirty well-cleaned carrots lengthwise into slices and place in a saucepan with half a pound of butter and seven or eight onions cut into quarters, after having removed the stems and roots
2. When the butter has melted, fry the carrots, taking care to stir them so they do not stick
3. When slightly fried, moisten with broth, adding a small piece of sugar the size of a walnut
4. After having been allowed to cook over gentle heat for three hours, check if they fall apart easily when pressed gently between the fingers
5. Remove the substance from the heat and pour it into a sieve through which it will be passed, having removed the broth and pounded the roots
6. To strain it easily, moisten it from time to time with the same broth in which the carrots were cooked, being necessary that it remain quite liquid and that it does not boil for long if you want it not to turn bitter
7. Once the substance is in the saucepan, add four tablespoons of curled sauce, and if any broth from the carrots remains, add it to the substance, then let it reduce sufficiently, skimming and degreasing it, until it becomes so thick that it can cover the entrees for which it was prepared
