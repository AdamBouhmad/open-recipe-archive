## Ingredients

- half a pound of flour
- three egg yolks
- powdered sugar in sufficient quantity to make the dough somewhat sweet
- a little melted and cooled butter
- generous wine as much as necessary to moisten the dough
- syrup at high point
- generous wine
- clove and cinnamon powder
- ground ladyfinger biscuit
- raisins
- almonds
- walnuts
- pine nuts
- toasted sesame
- candied citron pieces
- ground powdered sugar
- dragée

## Directions

1. Make the dough with half a pound of flour heaped on the table with a hole in the middle, into which pour three egg yolks, powdered sugar in sufficient quantity so the dough becomes somewhat sweet, a little melted and cooled butter, and generous wine as much as necessary to moisten the dough, which should remain tender
2. Knead everything together, and when smooth, extend with a rolling pin so that the dough becomes very thin
3. Cut some round discs the size of the platter in which the cake is to be served, and arrange three or four of them stacked on top of each other to serve as a base on the same plate after it has been greased with butter, with the dough sheets for the base greased the same way
4. Add on top a filling composed away from the heat with syrup at high point, generous wine, clove and cinnamon powder, ground ladyfinger biscuit, raisins, almonds, walnuts, pine nuts and toasted sesame
5. Once this filling is well arranged, stud it with more raisins, walnuts, almonds, pine nuts and candied citron pieces, and cover everything with two or three other very thin sheets of the same dough
6. Wet the edges so the covering adheres and pinch the edges all around
7. Decorate on top with small figures of the same dough and brush with butter using feathers
8. Place in the oven, and when cooked and still hot, dust the upper part with ground powdered sugar and decorate the figures or designs with which it has been adorned with dragée
