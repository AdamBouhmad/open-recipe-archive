## Ingredients

- young pigeons
- one sprig of parsley
- onion
- one head of garlic
- two cloves
- salt
- black pepper
- a small piece of butter
- one glass of white wine
- two handfuls of flour
- fine salt
- a little oil

## Directions

1. Prepare the young pigeons by scalding them, and after emptying them, leaving the wings, head and legs, pierce each one with a skewer across the legs to prevent them from spreading too much when briefly blanching them in boiling water
2. Once well cleaned, place them to cook in a pot with one glass of white wine, a small bunch of parsley, onion, one head of garlic, two cloves, salt, black pepper and a small piece of butter
3. Once cooking is finished, drain the pigeons and let them cool to cover or soak them afterwards with batter and fry them until they turn a good color
4. Serve hot with fried parsley as an appetizer
5. For the batter: put in a pot two handfuls of flour, fine salt and a little oil, adding little by little white wine to dissolve the batter, until it reaches regular consistency, neither too watery nor too thick; that is, it needs to form a thread when poured from the spoon
