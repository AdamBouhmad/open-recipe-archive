## Ingredients

- Egg yolks
- Sifted starch
- Clarified syrup
- Cloves
- Ground cinnamon
- Cinnamon powder
- Pine nuts or almond paste

## Directions

1. Beat egg yolks with a little sifted starch until stiff, using as many yolks as needed for the number of mock pears desired.
2. Pour the beaten mixture into small cups up to half their height and place them to cook in a water bath, that is, in a pan with boiling water, so the water level is lower than the cups, to prevent water from entering, covering with a comal and fire.
3. When the egg is cooked, which is known by inserting a straw and seeing if it comes out dry, remove the cups from the fire and let them cool.
4. Round them out with a knife to shape the pears, and place a clove of spice without the seed or globule at the top as a crown.
5. Immediately put them in clarified syrup, bringing it to the consistency of a high preserve.
6. Then remove the mock pears, arrange them on a platter with the crown on top, and decorate with ground cinnamon, with pine nuts, or with almond paste.
