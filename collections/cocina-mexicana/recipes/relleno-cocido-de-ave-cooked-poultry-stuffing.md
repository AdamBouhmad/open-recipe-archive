## Ingredients

- chicken breasts
- butter
- salt
- black pepper
- nutmeg
- bread crumb
- broth
- parsley
- cow udder or butter

## Directions

1. Cut chicken breasts into dice and place in a casserole with a little butter, salt, black pepper and nutmeg
2. Simmer gently over very low heat for ten minutes
3. Remove the breasts, drain them and let them cool
4. In the same casserole, add a piece of bread crumb with broth and a little finely chopped parsley
5. Stir everything with a wooden spoon, pressing and reducing it to a paste
6. When the broth is consumed and the bread is well cooked, set aside and let cool
7. Prepare a cooked and cold cow udder, or use butter as a substitute
8. Pound the poultry breasts and when well ground, pass them through a quenelle sieve and set aside
9. Similarly pound the bread crumb and pass through the sieve, setting also apart
10. Pound the udder, pass through the sieve and keep separate
11. Make three equal portions of breast, bread and udder, and pound them together in the mortar
12. After grinding for three quarters of an hour, add five or six egg yolks according to the quantity of stuffing
13. Pound the yolks together with everything else as they are added
14. This stuffing can be used equally in crusts with all kinds of dishes
