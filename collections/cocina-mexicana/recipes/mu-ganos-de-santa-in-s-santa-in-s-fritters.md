## Ingredients

- three pounds of sugar
- one panela
- half a gill of virgin honey
- one pound of flour
- thirty egg yolks
- one eggshell of Jerez wine
- one eggshell of anise water
- half an eggshell of water of tequesquite
- a pinch of salt
- lard for frying
- sesame seeds

## Directions

1. Clarify three pounds of sugar, one panela, and half a gill of virgin honey together
2. Strain and return to heat until it reaches soft-ball stage when tested in water
3. Mix one pound of flour with thirty egg yolks without any whites
4. Add one eggshell of Jerez wine, one of anise water, and half an eggshell of tequesquite water, plus a pinch of salt
5. Mix everything with the hands and test a small piece in hot lard to see if it puffs; if not, add more of everything except eggs
6. Form into small sticks, not very thick nor very thin
7. Leave to air dry, then fry in lard
8. Once all are fried, place in hot syrup and stir until well coated
9. Remove and place on a damp board, sprinkle with sesame seeds and empty the pot contents there
10. Press with a spoon to the desired height and cut as desired
11. While coating, sprinkle with water
