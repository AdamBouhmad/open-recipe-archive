## Ingredients

- fourteen ounces of flour
- six ounces of fresh cream
- one pound of powdered sugar
- half an ounce of orange blossom water
- fresh butter, melted

## Directions

1. Beat fourteen ounces of flour with six ounces of fresh cream, and when it no longer forms lumps, add one pound of powdered sugar
2. Mix everything and add half an ounce of orange blossom water, beating so that the mixture becomes as liquid as cooked milk
3. Heat the wafer iron and grease it with a brush dipped in fresh butter, melted in an earthenware pot
4. Pour one and a half spoonfuls of the batter into the mold to form the wafer and press the wafer iron slightly to make it thinner
5. Place the instrument over charcoal fire on a stove, turning it to the other side when the wafer is cooked on the first side
6. To know its degree of cooking, half-open the wafer iron, and if the wafer has taken on a good color, open it completely
7. Remove the wafer with the help of a knife passed underneath, rolling or wrapping itself as it is peeled off
8. Still hot, shape it as needed and immediately put it in a drying oven so it remains well dried
