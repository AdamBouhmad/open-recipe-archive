## Ingredients

- One old goose
- Bacon strips wrapped in breadcrumbs
- Parsley, chopped
- Onions, chopped
- Garlic, chopped
- Bay leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg
- Two glasses of water
- Two glasses of white wine
- Half glass of aguardiente
- Onions
- Carrots

## Directions

1. Preferably take an old goose, empty it and hold its legs inside the body, blanch and pluck it.
2. Lard the entire body with bacon strips wrapped in breadcrumbs and seasoned with parsley, onions, garlic all finely chopped; a bay leaf, thyme, basil almost reduced to powder, salt, pepper and nutmeg.
3. After larding, tie it and place it in a pot of its size with two glasses of water, two of white wine, half glass of aguardiente, onions and carrots, salt and pepper.
4. Cover the pot well and cook over low heat for four hours.
5. Once cooked, place the marinated goose on a platter; if the sauce is too thin, reduce it and pour it over the goose, after it has been garnished with carrots and onions cooked with it.
6. One can also let it cool, pour over it the same sauce and not serve it until the sauce is made into aspic: but this method is not the most used. The goose is always preferred hot.
