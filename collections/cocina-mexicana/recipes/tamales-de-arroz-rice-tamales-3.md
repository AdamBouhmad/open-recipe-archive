## Ingredients

- rice
- butter, melted and clarified
- sugar, finely ground
- milk
- egg yolks
- salt
- corn husks
- raisins
- almonds
- manjar blanco (dulce de leche) or yellow milk

## Directions

1. Wash the rice very well, spread it on a napkin and place it in the sun
2. Once it is completely dry, grind it very finely and pass it through a sieve
3. In a bowl where it can be beaten well, gradually add melted and clarified butter
4. Add finely ground sugar and beat until the sweet paste is very thick, as it will thin during cooking
5. Once sweetened, add an amount of milk equal to the amount of water used, and one or two egg yolks
6. The milk should be lukewarm so the paste does not become hard
7. Add a little salt and continue beating until the batter makes an eye or the spoon stands alone in the middle of the paste
8. Paint the corn husks with red and yellow stripes made with bread crumbs and yellow milk with egg yolks respectively
9. Place the tamales in the husks with a spoon and fill them with raisins, almonds, or manjar blanco or yellow milk
10. Cook them and do not unwrap until they are cold, or they will fall apart
