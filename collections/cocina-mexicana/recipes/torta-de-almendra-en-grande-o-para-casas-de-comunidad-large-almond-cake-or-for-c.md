## Ingredients

- Eight pounds of blanched and ground almonds
- One arroba of sugar
- Egg white and lemon for clarification
- A little ground biscuit
- A little white honey
- Egg yolks as desired
- Lard, abundant and cold
- Dough for the base as described in the following article
- Sesame seeds

## Directions

1. Make a syrup with one arroba of sugar, clarified with egg white and lemon, then skim it
2. Add the almonds ground and not too whole into the syrup and let it boil until it reaches the mirror point, stirring constantly so it does not burn
3. Store for another day
4. When ready to put into the cake pans, add a little ground biscuit, a little white honey, and as many egg yolks as desired
5. Prepare the cake pans two or three days in advance, light the oven, and once it is reduced to embers, place the pans with abundant cold lard on top
6. Put a base of dough like that described in the following article, not too thick nor too thin, and once the base is slightly cool, put the border which will adhere to the base so they do not separate
7. The oven must burn for two or three hours and rest for as many more; it must be well heated and very calm
8. When the oven is at embers, put the paste in the pans without filling them so they do not spill
9. Extend the dough of the same as the bases, though thinner, and with a pastry wheel cut strips less than a finger's width
10. Wet the border and put it in small latticed strips rolled up, sprinkling sesame seeds on top
11. Put the pans into the oven, swept without water, testing with one; if it boils very briefly, the others should not yet be put in so they do not spill, and let rest a little more until it is of good temperament
12. Once golden, remove from the oven
