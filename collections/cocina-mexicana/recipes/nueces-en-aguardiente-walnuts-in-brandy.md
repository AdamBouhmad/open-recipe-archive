## Ingredients

- tender walnuts
- water
- powdered alum
- clarified syrup made with two pounds of sugar for every three pounds of walnuts
- refined Spanish brandy

## Directions

1. Peel the tender walnuts and place them in cold water as you remove the shell
2. Place them in water that is close to boiling and let them blanch slightly
3. Finally place them in boiling water in which a little powdered alum has been dissolved, letting them boil until they are easily pierced with a pin
4. Remove and place in cold lemon water
5. Prepare clarified syrup made with two pounds of sugar for every three pounds of walnuts, cooked to the soft-crack stage, and pour over the drained walnuts in a large earthenware bowl, leaving them overnight
6. The next day, recook the syrup to the hard-crack stage and pour again over the walnuts in the bowl, leaving them for a full day
7. On the third day, cook the syrup to the low pearl stage and pour over the walnuts
8. On the fourth and final day, cook the syrup until it reaches the high pearl stage and pour over the walnuts, then mix with equal quantities of refined Spanish brandy
9. Heat everything so it crackles without boiling, and after cooling slightly, bottle, making sure the walnuts are well coated
