## Ingredients

- Mutton feet
- Flour
- Butter or lard
- Defatted broth
- Butter
- Small onions
- Parsley
- Egg yolks

## Directions

1. Clean the feet thoroughly by passing them several times through water.
2. Boil for at least four or five hours.
3. Fry flour with butter or lard without browning it.
4. Add a quantity of defatted broth proportionate to the number of feet to be cooked.
5. Add the feet to the broth after they have been blanched, cleaned, the bone removed, and cooled in fresh water.
6. Cook for four or five hours at a gentle boil.
7. Remove from heat and take out of the casserole.
8. Add new butter, some small onions and finely chopped parsley to the sauce remaining in the casserole.
9. If the sauce is not thick enough, add a little flour mixed with defatted broth.
10. Put the feet back in the casserole.
11. When the sauce is very hot but not boiling, beat in two or three egg yolks. This should be done moments before serving.
