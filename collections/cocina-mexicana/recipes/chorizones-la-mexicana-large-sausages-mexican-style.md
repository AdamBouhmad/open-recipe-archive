## Ingredients

- Pork loins, nerves and skins removed
- Salt, to taste
- Clove, to taste
- Pepper, to taste
- Cumin, to taste
- Ginger, to taste
- Garlic, to taste
- Wide chile, soaked and deveined
- Strong vinegar
- Cured beef casings or cured pork casings

## Directions

1. Remove nerves and skins from pork loins and mince them
2. Season with salt, clove, pepper, cumin, ginger, garlic, and wide soaked and deveined chile, all ground so that the aromas stand out
3. Dampen all with strong vinegar and mix so the spices incorporate well
4. Let the mixture rest in this state for one full day
5. Place a little in a pan without butter and cook to test if it is well seasoned, adding anything it lacks from the spices added
6. When it tastes good, fill cleaned beef casings with it using a funnel
7. Tie making divisions at distances of an eighth or sixth part as seems appropriate
8. Prick with a thick needle and put to air in a well-ventilated place where the sun does not hit
9. Leave to dry for use, though they can be used immediately
10. For cured pork casings, fill and tie each sausage to be one and a half to two inches long, and these can be used immediately
