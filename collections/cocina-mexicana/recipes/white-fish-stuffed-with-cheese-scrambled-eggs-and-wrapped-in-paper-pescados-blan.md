## Ingredients

- onion, much, chopped
- garlic
- parsley
- eggs
- dried wide chiles, toasted
- salt
- aged cheese, grated
- lard
- roasted garlic
- oil
- pepper
- white fish
- papers, buttered
- thread
- hard-boiled egg yolks
- vinegar, diluted

## Directions

1. In a pot over heat, chop much onion, garlic, and parsley and mix with beaten eggs
2. Grind two or three dried wide chiles that have been toasted without burning and sprinkle over the egg with adequate salt
3. Mix well and fry while stirring to form scrambled eggs
4. Just before removing, sprinkle a bit of grated aged cheese over them
5. When the eggs begin to brown in lard, remove them and use to fill the cleaned prepared white fish
6. Grind roasted garlic roasted in lard, dilute in oil to form a loose paste, sprinkle a bit of the toasted chile that was ground, and season with salt and pepper
7. Coat the fish with this broth, wrap each one in buttered papers and tie with thread so they do not fall apart
8. Place in oil over low heat and cook well
9. From the sauce used to coat them, form a broth with plenty of parsley, hard-boiled egg yolks, and a bit of diluted vinegar; fry this and serve the fish either bathed with this sauce or, better yet, bring to the table wrapped in paper with the sauce in a sauce boat
