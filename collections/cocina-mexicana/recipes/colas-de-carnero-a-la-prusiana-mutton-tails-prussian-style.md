## Ingredients

- four to five mutton tails
- half a large white and firm cabbage
- half pound of fat pork meat
- six large onions
- two cloves
- half a head of garlic
- small sprig of fennel
- salt
- pepper
- broth
- breadcrumb rounds
- butter
- flour
- clear substance

## Directions

1. Blanch for about a quarter hour in boiling water four or five mutton tails, half a large white firm cabbage, and half a pound of fat pork meat
2. Remove and refresh in cold water
3. Squeeze the cabbage, divide it into four parts, and tie each quarter with string
4. Cut the fat into many pieces without separating them from the meat and tie like the cabbage
5. Place everything in a pot with six large onions, two cloves, half a head of garlic, a small sprig of fennel, salt and pepper on top
6. Moisten with broth and let cook over low heat
7. Cut rounds of breadcrumb the size of a peso and fry in butter until well browned and drain them
8. Fry a handful of flour in the butter until it browns, then add broth from that in which the cabbage was cooked
9. Let it boil half an hour so the flour has time to cook and forms with it a light substance, which is skimmed and passed through a sieve
10. When the tails are cooked and their broth is consumed, place them to drain, the same with the cabbage and fatty meat
11. Dry everything with a cloth and dress the tails, interspersed with the untied cabbage, placing the onions around them, the browned bread pieces and the fat over the cabbage, and pour over them the strained broth that was made with the flour
