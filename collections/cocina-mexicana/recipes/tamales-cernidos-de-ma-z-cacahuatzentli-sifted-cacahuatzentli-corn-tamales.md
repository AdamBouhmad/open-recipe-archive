## Ingredients

- three pounds of cacahuatzentli corn
- three pounds or slightly more of lard
- warm water
- salt, or sugar if making sweet
- corn husks, soaked, drained, and dried

## Directions

1. Make nixtamal or cook three pounds of cacahuatzentli corn the same way as for common tamales
2. Wash it well, dry it and put it to dry in the sun
3. When completely dry, grind it, and then sift it through a thin ayate cloth
4. To the sifted portion add three pounds or slightly more of lard and a little warm water
5. Beat this mass with the necessary salt, or if you want to make it sweet, with sugar, beating until it makes eyes and is at the right point, which you will know by forming a small ball from it that when dropped in water floats
6. Form the tamales in corn husks, making sure these have well drained the water in which they were soaked, and once made, put them to cook the same way as in the previous article
7. If you do not want to make them sweet, do not add sugar to the mass, but season well with salt, and fill the tamales with the stews you want made of meat or fish, chile or spice
8. If they are sweet, they can be filled with cooked egg yolks, ground sugar, pine nuts, citron, cinnamon and sesame
