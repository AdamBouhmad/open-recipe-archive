## Ingredients

- Duck livers
- Salt and pepper in proportion of one and a half drachm per pound of livers
- Offal from the livers
- Lard, double the weight of the offal
- Cooked fat ham, double the weight of the offal
- Fine herbs, finely chopped
- Butter
- Two egg yolks
- Truffle slices, somewhat thick
- Lard or fat to cover and surround the casserole
- Concentrated poultry stock, well clarified

## Directions

1. Soak and prepare the duck livers, removing any bile stains
2. Cut the livers into thick slices and season with salt and pepper at the proportion of one and a half drachm per pound
3. Chop and pound or grind the offal remaining after slicing the livers, having weighed it beforehand to mix with double its weight of lard and cooked fat ham
4. When pounding or grinding, season also with salt and pepper and with fine herbs finely chopped and heated in a small pan with a little butter
5. Add two egg yolks, and when this stuffing is well pounded or ground, remove from the mortar or metate
6. Cut truffle slices somewhat thick
7. Cover and line a fire-resistant casserole with lard
8. Make a layer of truffle slices and season lightly
9. Over them put another layer of the stuffing, spreading and leveling it well
10. On top put another layer of the liver slices, and begin again with the truffles, following alternately with layers of stuffing and liver, up to the height of the casserole, leaving a difference of about an inch and a half unfilled
11. Cover all with slices of lard or fat and a round of paper
12. Place the casserole in the oven one hour after it has heated
13. When removed from the oven, drain the fat and to fill the space it leaves, replace it with good concentrated poultry stock, well clarified, and serve the casserole once this stock has set well
