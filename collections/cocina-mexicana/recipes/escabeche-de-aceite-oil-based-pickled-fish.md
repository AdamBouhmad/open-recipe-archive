## Ingredients

- Fish, cleaned and desalted if salt-cured
- Oil for frying
- Garlic heads, crushed
- Thyme
- Bay leaves
- Chinese oregano
- Lemon, sliced
- Salt (if fish is not salted)
- Vinegar
- Water
- Tabasco pepper, whole
- Raw oil to cover

## Directions

1. Clean and desalt the fish if salt-cured, then cut into pieces that fit in a barrel.
2. Fry the fish in oil until well fried, then set aside and let cool.
3. In fresh oil, fry crushed garlic heads, thyme, bay leaves, Chinese oregano, and lemon slices.
4. When everything is well fried, add vinegar (tempered with water if it is too strong); add whole Tabasco pepper.
5. When everything is well seasoned, remove from heat and let cool.
6. Once cooled, layer the fish in a barrel with a sprinkling of oregano and thyme, bay leaves, some of the fried lemon slices, and bathe with the sauce and fried herbs.
7. Continue layering until the barrel is full, then cover completely with raw oil, seal well, and after eight days the fish can be eaten.
