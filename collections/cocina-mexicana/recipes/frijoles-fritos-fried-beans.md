## Ingredients

- Sufficient lard
- Bread, cut into small pieces (optional)
- Onion, chopped
- Beans
- Broth

## Directions

1. Heat a pot with sufficient lard over the fire.
2. If desired, fry small pieces of cold bread in the lard until golden, then remove them.
3. Fry sufficient chopped onion in the same lard.
4. When the onion begins to take on color, extract a portion of beans without broth from the pot and fry them with the onion.
5. Add broth that makes up at least two-thirds of the bean stew to be made, and let it boil, stirring frequently so they do not burn, until the broth has thickened somewhat more.
6. Then move to very low heat and leave at a gentle boil for a couple of hours.
7. When they have thickened, there is no need to stir them, and it is better to let them form a skin on top.
8. If needed to moisten the beans as they become very thick, they need two or three boils so the water incorporates and they do not remain tasteless.
