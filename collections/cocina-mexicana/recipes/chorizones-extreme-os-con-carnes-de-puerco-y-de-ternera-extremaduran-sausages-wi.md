## Ingredients

- 17 pounds pork loin, cut into small pieces
- 8 pounds veal, cut into small pieces
- 2 pounds dried red chile, toasted
- 1 cup peeled garlic cloves
- 2 cups salt
- 2 cups Jerez wine
- Cured cow intestines

## Directions

1. Cut seventeen pounds of pork loin and eight pounds of veal into very fine pieces and place in a proportioned earthenware vessel
2. Toast two pounds of dried red chile in the sun and grind it dry
3. Add this chile powder to the minced meat
4. On the same stone grinder used for the chile, grind peeled garlic cloves measured in a cup until the cup is full
5. Add the ground garlic to the minced meat along with two cups of salt
6. Mix everything together very well, cover the vessel with a cloth and let the meat cure for three days
7. On the third day, add two cups of Jerez wine
8. Fill cleaned cow intestines with the mixture, pricking them with a steel needle or fistol to release air, then tying them
9. Leave each sausage a quarter vara in length
10. Hang them in the kitchen three varas away from the fire
11. Smoke them for nine nights with straw
12. After this time, take them down and store them in bran
