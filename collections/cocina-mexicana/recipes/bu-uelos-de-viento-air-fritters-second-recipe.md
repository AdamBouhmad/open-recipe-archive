## Ingredients

- flour
- hot water
- salt
- anise
- butter
- eggs

## Directions

1. To each cup of flour being measured and poured into the pan, add one cup of hot water and stir so it does not form lumps
2. Once one and a half to two pounds is measured and well broken up, add ground salt, a little anise and a quarter pound of butter without ceasing to stir
3. Once it thunders and becomes difficult to stir, take a little of that dough between the fingers and make a small ball without sticking to the fingers
4. Once cooked, pour into a container to cool a little and add to each pound fourteen eggs, seven whole and seven only yolks
5. Knead much giving pats until it is smooth and fluffy
6. Grease the hands with butter and make a ball of the dough a little larger than a walnut, making a hole in the middle that comes out like a little ring
7. Drop in hot butter to fry, giving little taps on top with the spoon so they puff up; once golden, place on the plate, pour syrup over them and a little granulated sugar
