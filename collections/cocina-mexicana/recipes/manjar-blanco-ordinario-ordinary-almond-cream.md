## Ingredients

- eighteen cuartillos of milk
- one pound and a half of clean rice
- four and a half pounds of white sugar
- one cooked chicken breast, ground
- garlic

## Directions

1. Weigh the clean rice, then wash it very well in a metate
2. Grind it so that no grains remain
3. Dissolve all of this in the milk, placed in a pan
4. Strain through a cloth into another pan, and a second time through finely woven fabric, taking care that all cloths used for this are well washed so the milk does not curdle
5. Rub the outside of the pan where it will cook with a well-ground garlic head, and do the same on the bottom to prevent burning
6. Put on the fire and stir constantly on all sides with force using a stick with a cloth so that the manjar does not stick
7. Have a cooked and ground chicken breast ready, dissolved in a little of the same milk, and add it a little before it reaches the point
8. If desired, one can use instead of the breast one pound of almonds, which are blanched, ground and added in the same manner as the breast, and it is more delicious with the almonds
9. When a spoonful placed on a plate, if when cold it pulls away leaving it clean, the manjar has reached a good point
