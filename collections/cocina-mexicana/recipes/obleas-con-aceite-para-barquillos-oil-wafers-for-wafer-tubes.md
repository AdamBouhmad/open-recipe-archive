## Ingredients

- half a quarter quart of flour
- two tomas of fine salt
- one spoonful of brandy
- one spoonful of olive oil
- three eggs
- good milk
- powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Place half a quarter quart of flour in a bowl and make a hole in the middle, pouring into it two tomas of fine salt, one spoonful of brandy, one spoonful of olive oil and three eggs
2. Dissolve the flour by pouring it toward the middle without disturbing the edges, being necessary that the whole forms only a single lump
3. Gradually moisten with good milk until the dough becomes like a thin pap
4. Heat the wafer iron over a fire that makes no smoke
5. When it is heated without turning red, grease its interior with a brush dipped in oil
6. Pour the batter with a measuring spoon and heat the wafer iron for two minutes on each side
7. The wafers should turn out yellow and of equal color and detach from the mold by themselves, and when removed sprinkle them with ground sugar
8. Although the first one may be ruined, a little practice will teach the appropriate degree of heat
