## Ingredients

- one good cabbage
- three or four large potatoes
- one small calabash
- two carrots
- two lettuces
- a pair of parsnips
- four or six turnips
- the same number of kohlrabies
- two sweet potatoes
- a handful of very tender peas
- a handful of green beans
- a handful of string beans, trimmed and shredded
- salt
- two heads of garlic
- vinegar
- powdered pepper
- butter and oil in equal parts
- chorizos
- longaniza sausage
- regular sausages
- long plantains, sliced
- chickpeas
- ham
- cured beef
- mutton
- veal
- chicken

## Directions

1. Boil one good cabbage, three or four large potatoes, one small calabash, two carrots, two lettuces, a pair of parsnips, four or six turnips, the same number of kohlrabies, and two sweet potatoes, a handful of very tender peas, a handful of green beans, and a handful of string beans trimmed and shredded, adding necessary salt
2. When everything is well cooked, refresh it in clean water
3. Roughly chop half the potatoes and carrots, the turnips and kohlrabies, the larger leaves of cabbage, and all the lettuces
4. Combine the chopped vegetables with the string beans, peas and beans, and fry in a casserole that has equal parts butter and oil in which two cleaned cloves of garlic have been browned
5. Add a little vinegar and powdered pepper
6. When well dried, remove this vegetable from the fire
7. Place another casserole on the fire with butter and fry chorizos, longaniza, and sausages whole in it
8. When these are well fried, remove them from the butter and fry in it long plantains sliced lengthwise
9. With everything thus prepared, place all the chopped vegetable at the bottom of a platter, and on top all the whole pieces, peeling the sweet potatoes, potatoes and everything that has a skin that does not please the eye
10. Also place on top of the fried vegetables the chickpeas, ham, chorizos, longaniza and fried sausages, the cured beef and plantain, placing only mutton, veal and chicken on the meat part
