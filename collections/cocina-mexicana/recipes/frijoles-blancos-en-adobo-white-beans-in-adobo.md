## Ingredients

- White beans
- Salt of the earth
- Lard
- Wide chiles, deveined and washed
- Garlic heads, cleaned and roasted
- A few beans
- A little cumin
- Good vinegar
- Pork meat broth
- Clove
- Black pepper
- Fine salt
- Pork meat
- Longaniza sausage
- Small chorizos
- Morcilla
- Salt
- Chopped or sliced onion (for serving)
- Little chiles in vinegar (for serving)
- Olives (for serving)

## Directions

1. Cook the white beans with the necessary salt of the earth.
2. Heat a pot with lard over the fire.
3. Make a sauce with competent wide chiles deveined and washed, some cleaned roasted garlic heads, a few beans, and a little cumin, all ground and loosened with a little good vinegar.
4. Fry this sauce in the lard.
5. Then add the beans, mixing with the chile and increase the broth with pork meat broth.
6. Season with a little clove, pepper, and fine salt.
7. Let them boil until they thicken well.
8. The pork meat with longaniza, small chorizos, and morcilla are fried separately with a little salt; as soon as it has browned, it is either added to boil in the beans when the broth is added, or reserved to adorn the plate on top, adding chopped or sliced onion, little chiles in vinegar, and olives.
