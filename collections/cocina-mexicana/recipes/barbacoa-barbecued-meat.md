## Ingredients

- meat
- petate (woven mat)
- water
- firewood
- stones

## Directions

1. Dig a pit in the earth about one yard deep and proportioned to the size of the meat to be cooked.
2. Place stones at the bottom of the pit and on top of them branches and small logs to form a fire, which must be maintained for at least two hours so that the earth becomes well heated.
3. Remove the firewood, leaving the stones, which should remain very hot.
4. Moisten a new petate thoroughly and wrap the meat in it, which should be prepared as roasts are ordinarily prepared.
5. Cover the meat with sticks, on top of which place another moistened petate, ensuring that no part of the meat remains uncovered.
6. Cover the top petate with a light layer of earth, sufficient so that the petate does not burn.
7. Light a good fire on top of it and keep it burning.
8. After eight hours the meat will be cooked and without having lost its substance, which makes it extremely delicious.
