## Ingredients

- one deboned beef shoulder
- lard for each pound of meat
- spice powder
- four bay leaves
- thyme
- parsley
- small onions
- broth or water and salt
- thick peppercorns
- four spices
- ham strips
- tongue in scarlet
- truffles
- ground veal feet
- beef bones
- six carrots
- eight to ten onions
- eggs

## Directions

1. Debone an entire beef shoulder
2. Make a hash with part of the meat, adding for each pound another pound of lard, both chopped
3. Mix in spice powder, four bay leaves, a little thyme, and a good bunch of parsley and small onions
4. Moisten with broth, or lacking this, with water and salt
5. Extend the hash to the thickness of a finger over the shoulder
6. Place strips of ham, scarlet tongue, and truffles on top, adding strips of carrot if desired
7. To mottle the galantine, cover all ingredients with hash and place strips of ham, truffles, etc. on top again, covering again with hash
8. Roll the shoulder lengthwise, reuniting and pressing the meats well
9. Tie with strong thread and cover with lard
10. Wrap tightly in canvas, then in clean cloth and tie to maintain shape
11. Place in a braising pan lined with lard slices, veal slices, two veal feet, shoulder bones, six carrots, and eight to ten onions of those of spice
12. Moisten with reduced broth and cook, examining if the shoulder is done before removing from heat
13. Press to extract all liquid and let cool
14. Strain the gelatin through a fine cloth and put eggs in a pan, beating well
15. Pour the gelatin over and mix both thoroughly
16. Add thick peppercorns and four spices, a bay leaf, a little thyme, and a small handful of parsley leaves
17. Incorporate all and let boil
18. When it boils, place the pan at the edge of the stove, cover with its lid and put fire on top
19. Leave half an hour in this position so everything simmers gently
20. Strain the gelatin through a cloth without pressing, only moving the gelatin inside so it passes to the other side
21. Let cool, and when set, surround the galantine with it after it is arranged on a plate
22. Do not forget to season the hash with salt, thick pepper, a little four spices and finely chopped parsley, and when about to use, mix in egg yolks, blending well
