## Ingredients

- Ducks, cleaned with tail removed
- Water treated with tequesquite
- Onion tops
- Salt water
- Duck broth
- Fat from duck
- Ripe tomato, ground
- Pepper, ground
- Clove, ground
- Cinnamon, ground
- Nutmeg, ground
- Vinegar
- Carrot, cooked
- Beet, cooked
- Onion, cooked
- Tomatillos, cooked
- Oil
- Vinegar
- Salt and pepper
- Olives
- Tomatillos
- Capers

## Directions

1. Clean the ducks, remove the tail, and infuse in water treated with tequesquite and onion tops for twenty-four hours.
2. Cook in salted water, using only the necessary amount of water.
3. Once cooked, fry ripe ground tomato mixed with ground pepper, clove, cinnamon, and nutmeg in some of the duck's broth with its fat.
4. When well fried, add a little vinegar and an equal amount of the duck broth and let boil well, seasoning with salt.
5. Separately, season a salad of carrot, beet, onion, and tomatillos (all cooked separately) with oil, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
6. When serving, remove the ducks from their cooking broth, top with the salad, shake the platter to spread well, and pour the tomato broth over.
7. Garnish with olives, tomatillos, and capers.
