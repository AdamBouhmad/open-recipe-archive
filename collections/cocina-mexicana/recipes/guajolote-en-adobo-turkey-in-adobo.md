## Ingredients

- Old turkey
- Thick slices of well-seasoned ham
- Veal scraps
- Ham strips
- Veal shank
- One cuartillo of white wine or one layer of brandy
- Broth
- Roots
- Onions
- Parsley
- Fine herbs
- Salt
- Pepper
- Garlic
- Bay leaf

## Directions

1. Pluck and empty an old turkey, and tuck its feet into the body
2. Turn it a few times over the coals
3. Cover with thick slices of well-seasoned ham and wrap in cloth, which is sewn
4. Place in a pot just right for its size with veal scraps, ham strips and a veal shank
5. Moisten with one cuartillo of white wine or one layer of brandy, broth, roots, onions, a bundle of parsley and fine herbs, salt, pepper, garlic, and bay leaf
6. Cook over slow fire and turn
7. Once cooked, strain the broth through a sieve and reduce it until it becomes aspic
8. Leave it to cool, spread it over the turkey, place on a platter over a napkin, garnished with green parsley
9. It can also be served hot, reducing part of the aspic to sauce
