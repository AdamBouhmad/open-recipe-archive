## Ingredients

- Seven cups of corn
- One pound four ounces of lard
- Six egg yolks
- One cup of strong tequesquite water
- Water
- Salt
- Pork meat for mole

## Directions

1. Prepare seven cups of corn as nixtamal.
2. The next day, wash and dry in the sun.
3. Once dry, grind and sift through an ayate.
4. In a cazuela, place one pound four ounces of lard, six egg yolks, and beat together with a spoon.
5. Once well beaten, incorporate the corn.
6. Add one cup of strong tequesquite water and if the masa becomes thick, add pure water and salt to taste.
7. Let rest for two or more hours and beat well.
8. Make mole with pork that is thick and mix with the masa.
9. In a pot, make a tapextle with sticks and leaves and add water so it does not overflow the sticks.
10. Place the tamales on this base and cover with leaves and cloth so the pot is well sealed.
11. Cook over regular heat.
12. For sweet tamales, fill with custard instead. The same operation can be done with rice.
