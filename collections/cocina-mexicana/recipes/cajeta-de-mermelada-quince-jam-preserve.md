## Ingredients

- white quinces with skin
- water
- straw
- salt
- 2 pounds of white crushed sugar per pound of fruit
- glass jars or well-sealed pots

## Directions

1. Cook the white quinces with their skin in water and straw
2. Peel and core them, placing them in cold water with a little salt for half an hour
3. Wring them out and change the water; change the water every hour or so up to four times
4. Grate or pass through the metate, then through a sieve or strainer
5. Clarify the sugar well and place the quince in a pan on the fire to dry, as the drier the better, using gentle heat
6. As soon as the moisture is consumed, bring the syrup to a point where it sets in water
7. Remove from heat and shortly after, stir in the paste and beat until it whitens and cools
8. Return to gentle heat, give it one boil while beating, remove and continue beating until it cools
9. Place in glass jars or well-sealed pots and finish in the sun until they are good
