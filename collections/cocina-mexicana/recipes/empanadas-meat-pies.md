## Ingredients

- 1 pound melted and cooled lard
- 1 small cup water with salt
- 1/4 cuartillo of generous wine
- some yeast dissolved in salted water
- flour

## Directions

1. To 1 pound of melted and cooled lard, add 1 small cup of water with salt, 1/4 cuartillo of generous wine, and some yeast dissolved in the salted water, beating everything together until incorporated.
2. Gradually add flour, mixing until the dough is soft and can be extended.
3. Place the dough in a lard-greased bowl and cover it.
4. If the dough is made in the morning, make the empanadas in the afternoon; if made in the afternoon, make them the following morning.
5. Extend the dough with a rolling pin on flour dust, cut to desired size, and fill with picadillo, adding pieces of ham and cooked egg, olives and chiles in quarters.
6. For fish versions: fry onions, garlic, tomatoes, and chopped green chile in lard, then add a little water (if not using catfish), salt, cooked fish pieces proportioned to the empanadas, saffron, cloves, and ground pepper, chopped parsley, oil, and vinegar, simmering until reduced.
7. In each empanada place a bit of the sauce, a piece of fish, some capers, olives, and chopped chiles (if small empanadas) or quarters of chiles or whole chiles (if large ones).
8. Fold and crimp the edges with the thumb all around.
9. Dampen the edges with a little water to seal well and press the top onto the bottom.
10. Beat an egg (yolk and white) and brush the entire upper surface with a feather.
11. Place on tin sheets and bake in a well-tempered oven until cooked and dried, then sprinkle with sifted sugar.
