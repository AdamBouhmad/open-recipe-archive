## Ingredients

- Squabs
- Salt
- Pepper
- Pork loin with fat
- Garlic
- Parsley
- Mint (yerbambuena)
- Breadcrumbs
- Vinegar
- Cloves
- Cinnamon
- Saffron
- Capers
- Eggs
- Chorizos (chorrizones), small chorizos (choricitos)
- Sausage (longaniza)
- Ham
- Water
- Onions
- Oil

## Directions

1. Clean the squabs without breaking the skin or cutting anything
2. Make a hole on the underside
3. Wash through the hole and with fingers or a small round stick loosen the skin
4. Season inside and out with a little salt and ground pepper
5. Make a filling of pork loin with its fat, very finely chopped and mixed with garlic, parsley, mint, breadcrumbs soaked in vinegar, cloves, pepper, cinnamon, and saffron all ground
6. Mix well with parsley, garlic browned in lard, and capers all ground
7. When the mixture is well combined, add four raw eggs and divide in two parts
8. Mix one part with small pieces of chorizos, small sausages, longaniza, and ham
9. Fill the inside with this mixture and place the remainder between skin and meat
10. Sew the holes well so the squabs do not empty
11. Cook in a pot with water, two or three whole heads of garlic and onion, a little vinegar, and necessary salt
12. Cover the pot well and when cooked, coat with garlic ground with vinegar and oil
13. Brown on two fires, repeating the coating from time to time until golden
14. Serve with a poultry sauce or with the following: Fry finely chopped onion in oil until golden, add a little flour to fry until it begins to color, fry a handful of parsley and the same of capers, grind both, add necessary water and salt, a little vinegar, and plenty of oil, and serve when the sauce has thickened well
