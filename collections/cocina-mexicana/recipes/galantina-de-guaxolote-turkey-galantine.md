## Ingredients

- Guaxolote (turkey)
- Carnes de dos pollas or vaco (meat from two hens or beef)
- Jamón puramente gordo (pure fat ham)
- Sal (salt)
- Pimienta (pepper)
- Yerbas finas (fine herbs)
- Mechas medianas de jamón (medium ham lards)
- Especias molidas (ground spices)
- Criadillas de tierra (truffles)
- Almendras (almonds)
- Tajadas de jamón (ham slices)
- Hojas de laurel (bay leaves)
- Jarretes de vuca (veal shanks)
- Manojito de peregil (bunch of parsley)
- Cebolla (onion)
- Tomillo (thyme)
- Clavos de especia (cloves)
- Caldo (broth)
- Huevos (eggs)

## Directions

1. Singe the turkey over a flame and bone it, starting from the loin
2. Remove all nerves from the legs
3. Lift some meat from the legs and breast and combine with meat from two hens or beef
4. Weigh the meat and add an equal amount of pure fat ham
5. Chop all together and season with salt, pepper, and fine herbs
6. Season medium ham lards with ground spices, salt, and pepper, and lard the turkey meat with them
7. Spread a layer of filling about one inch thick, smoothing it evenly
8. Place on top truffles cut lengthwise, ham lards, strips of turkey and other poultry meat, and thin slices of clean almonds
9. Cover with another layer of filling
10. Alternate layers until all filling is used
11. Roll the turkey to contain all the filling without spilling, and sew the meat closed with a needle to give it an elongated shape
12. Cover with ham slices sprinkled with a little salt
13. Wrap in a napkin with four or five bay leaves and tie the napkin at the ends
14. Bind the galantine on top to maintain its shape
15. Place in a braising pan lined with ham slices
16. Add two veal shanks, a bunch of parsley and onion, the scraps from the turkey, four bay leaves, a little thyme, three cloves, and three ladles of broth
17. Leave to cook over low heat for five hours
18. Remove the galantine from the pan and press gently to extract the broth
19. Keep in the same napkin until it cools to serve
20. Strain the broth through a fine napkin and break in one or two eggs according to quantity, beating them in and tasting for flavor
21. Place over the fire, stirring the galantine without stopping until it boils
22. Set aside at the edge of the stove, cover with a lid, keeping a hot fire on top for half an hour
23. Strain through a fine napkin and let cool for serving
