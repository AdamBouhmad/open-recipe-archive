## Ingredients

- two and one-quarter pounds of sugar
- one pound of cleaned almond, pounded
- twelve ounces of ground biscuit
- ten egg whites and four egg yolks
- cinnamon, clove, and a little pepper, all ground
- orange flower water
- one ounce of fresh butter
- five egg whites
- one pound of finely ground sugar
- a few drops of lemon

## Directions

1. Make a well-clarified syrup with two and one-quarter pounds of sugar, removing from heat when it reaches the thread stage
2. Add one pound of cleaned, pounded almond and give it three boils, removing from heat again
3. Mix in twelve ounces of ground biscuit
4. Add gradually ten egg whites and four egg yolks, stirring the dough by hand to prevent lumps from forming
5. Return to heat and add cinnamon, clove, a little pepper all ground, orange flower water, and one ounce of fresh butter
6. Cook the dough until it is neither tough nor dry
7. Dust a board with flour and empty the dough onto it to cool
8. Cut the mostachones in the style of small hams
9. Dust with flour so they do not stick and leave on the board until the next day
10. The next day, beat five egg whites and mix very well with one pound of finely ground sugar
11. Heat this beaten mixture briefly on the heat, add a few drops of lemon, and beat perfectly
12. Using feathers, coat the mostachones on three sides with this icing
13. Place on a shovel and put in the oven, which should be gentle, removing as soon as they dry
14. Coat the remaining three unbathed sides and return to the oven to finish drying so they can be stored
