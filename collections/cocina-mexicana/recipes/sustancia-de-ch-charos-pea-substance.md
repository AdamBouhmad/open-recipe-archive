## Ingredients

- One quart to one and a half quarts peas
- Four ounces butter
- Parsley leaves
- Green parts of onion tops
- Salt
- Concentrated broth or pot broth
- Spinach green (if needed)

## Directions

1. Soak one quart or quart and a half of peas in water and add four ounces of butter, kneading and working the peas with it
2. After draining the water, strain them in a colander and put them immediately in a pot placed on a burner that does not have very strong heat
3. Add a handful of parsley leaves and a little green from onion tops
4. Fry everything together with the peas, making them jump for a quarter hour
5. Then add salt and half a ladle of concentrated broth (see concentrated broth, page 131) or pot broth if the other is not available, and let them boil at less heat, taking care to cover the pot with its lid
6. After three quarters of an hour, remove from the heat and put them in a mortar to pound them
7. While pounding, pass them through a strainer, using pot broth or cold concentrated broth if available, and keep the substance in a pot
8. If it is not of a good color, add spinach green (see spinach green, page 322) and heat it at the moment of serving so it does not turn yellowish
