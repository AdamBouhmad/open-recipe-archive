## Ingredients

- One pound flour
- Three cauldron cups cold water
- Two ounces raw lard
- One cauldron cup anise water
- A piece of sugar
- Salt
- Eggs

## Directions

1. For each pound of flour, add three cauldron cups of cold water, crumbling it well so no lumps remain
2. Add two ounces raw lard and one cauldron cup of anise water, a piece of sugar, and sufficient salt
3. Place everything over heat, stirring continuously
4. When the mixture reaches the right point (forming a hard dough like bread that makes a crust in the pan), remove and let cool
5. Once cooled, add eggs until the mixture loosens so it is neither too thick nor too thin
6. Beat well with the hand or a paddle until it forms an eye and is neither too thick nor thin
7. With a small spoon, drop spoonfuls into very hot lard and fry until golden
8. Serve in syrup or dusted with sugar
