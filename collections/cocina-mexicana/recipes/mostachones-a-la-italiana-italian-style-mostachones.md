## Ingredients

- three pounds brown sugar
- two pounds almonds with skin
- one and one-half ounces cinnamon
- nutmeg, pepper, and clove to taste
- four pounds flour
- cold water
- a little candied lemon peel
- three pounds brown sugar for the glaze
- one and one-half ounces cinnamon for the glaze
- fragrant water

## Directions

1. Grind three pounds of brown sugar, two pounds of almonds with skin, one and one-half ounces of cinnamon, nutmeg, pepper, and clove to taste according to how spiced you want the mostachones.
2. After grinding these ingredients well, mix with four pounds of flour and sift through a sieve.
3. Knead with cold water in a suitable pot, adding a little candied lemon peel while kneading.
4. After the dough is well worked and manageable, place it on a flour-dusted plate and let it rest two or three hours.
5. Cut out three or four mostachones to test in the oven to check if it has the right consistency, which you know when they puff up slightly with heat; if they spread instead, add more flour to the dough and knead again.
6. When ready, cut the mostachones in an oval shape, pointed at the ends, four or five fingers long and three fingers wide.
7. Bake in the oven; after baking, remove and glaze as follows.
8. For the glaze: mix three pounds of brown sugar with one and one-half ounces of cinnamon, pass through a sieve, add a little fragrant water, and make it so that when dropped from a spoon it forms a thread.
9. Brush the glaze onto the mostachones with a brush using this bath.
10. Place on paper on a table and cover with the hot oven door cover so it sets; when set, store in a humid and cool place, as they are naturally hard.
11. They can also be glazed with chocolate dissolved in water and made very thick.
