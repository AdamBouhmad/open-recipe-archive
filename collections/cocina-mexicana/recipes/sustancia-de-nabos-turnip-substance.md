## Ingredients

- Four ounces butter
- A dozen large turnips, sliced
- Curried sauce
- Brown beef substance
- Flour (for economical version)
- Beef broth
- Sugar

## Directions

1. Put four ounces of butter and a dozen large sliced turnips in a pot
2. Place the pot over a fairly strong fire, taking care to turn the turnips with a wooden spoon
3. When they have browned, add a ladle of curried sauce (page 766) and another full of brown beef substance (see)
4. Let everything reduce until well bound, taking care to degrease it, pass through a strainer and keep the substance in a pot until the moment it is to be used
5. For a less costly version: after browning the turnips in butter, add a good spoonful of flour, stir it with them, and two ladles of broth from the pot
6. Let everything boil together until it thickens, add a lump of sugar, and immediately pass the substance through a strainer or sieve
7. Return to the fire in a pot, let it boil, skim the foam and set aside for the use intended
