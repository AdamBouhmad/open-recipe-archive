## Ingredients

- flour (quarter of total amount for starter)
- beer yeast
- warm water
- butter (one good quarter for each pound of flour)
- three eggs per pound of flour
- fine salt (two grams per pound of flour)

## Directions

1. Make a starter: place a quarter of the flour on a table, make a hole in the middle, add beer yeast and pour warm water over it, knead well
2. Place the starter dough in a trough, make two cuts in the form of a cross, cover with a cloth and let ferment
3. Use one ounce of yeast per pound of flour mixed with it, or otherwise one ounce per every four pounds of dough
4. When the starter has acquired the desired quality, knead separately the rest of the flour with butter, eggs and fine salt
5. Combine the starter with the flour mixture, fold the dough many times, sprinkling with flour
6. Let rest for at least eight hours before making the rolls
