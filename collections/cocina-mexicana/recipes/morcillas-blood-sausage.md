## Ingredients

- lard from the belly, finely chopped
- onion, chopped
- salt
- pepper
- clove
- cinnamon
- blood, added gradually
- wide intestines

## Directions

1. Chop the lard from the belly into small pieces and place in a large bowl
2. Add the chopped onion, salt, pepper, clove, and cinnamon
3. Mix everything well so the spices blend thoroughly
4. Add the blood little by little, stirring with a wooden spoon
5. Fry a small amount in a pan to check if it is well prepared and seasoned; adjust salt or spices as needed
6. Fill the intestines, leaving them somewhat slack so they do not burst when cooked
7. Place the mixture in a large pot prepared with warm water
8. Add the morcillas little by little, ensuring they are not too crowded
9. Bring to a boil over strong heat
10. After the morcillas cook for a while, prick them with a needle to release the air inside
11. Reduce the heat moderately
12. When no blood comes out when pricked, they are cooked
13. Remove carefully and place on a cloth spread on a table
14. Dry with another cloth and hang to dry
15. The broth is good for soup and breadcrumbs, and can also be used to make cakes
