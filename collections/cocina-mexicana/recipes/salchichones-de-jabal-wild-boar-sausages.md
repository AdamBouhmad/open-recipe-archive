## Ingredients

- Wild boar meat
- Lard, half a pound for each pound of meat
- Salt, one ounce per pound of meat
- Spices in proportion
- Saltpeter, a little, crushed
- Muscatel wine
- Pork intestines of desired thickness
- White wine
- Thyme
- Laurel
- Basil
- Garlic
- Shallots
- Salt

## Directions

1. Mince the wild boar meat with half a pound of lard for each pound of meat
2. Season with one ounce of salt per pound of meat and spices in proportion
3. Add a little crushed saltpeter to make the meat red
4. Place the seasoned mixture in a bowl with a little muscatel wine
5. Marinate for twenty-four hours
6. Prepare intestines of the desired thickness and soak them to remove the tripe flavor in white wine with thyme, laurel, basil, garlic, shallots, and salt
7. After draining well, fill the intestines with the wild boar meat using a funnel
8. Tie the ends
9. Hang in the chimney to smoke until the sausages are completely dry
10. Cook over coals for one hour, although some people eat them raw
