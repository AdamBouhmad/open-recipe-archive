## Ingredients

- Ham with good lean meat, in thin slices
- Lard
- Very ripe tomatoes
- Half a cuartillo of good vinegar
- Panocha, size of an egg
- Water with a little salt
- Cooked chicken breasts
- Miscellaneous poultry pieces, chopped
- Meat from legs and wings of hens, boned
- Capers, desalted
- Egg yolks, ground
- Cloves, ground
- Cinnamon, ground
- Pepper, ground
- Salt as needed
- Orange leaf powder or orange peel
- Sifted sugar
- Tender dough

## Directions

1. Cut thin slices of ham with good lean meat and fry in lard until cooked
2. Roast very ripe tomatoes, grind them well, and dissolve in half a cuartillo of good vinegar
3. Add to the pot where the ham is frying
4. Add a piece of panocha the size of an egg and a small amount of water with salt
5. Boil everything until the broth is consumed leaving only the grease
6. Remove the ham and fry the cooked chicken breasts and chopped miscellaneous poultry pieces and meat from the legs and wings of hens, deboned
7. Once everything is browned, remove the meat and fry a sauce made of desalted capers and ground egg yolks, adding ground cloves, cinnamon, pepper, and necessary salt
8. Chop half of the ham slices, all the meat and poultry remnants that were fried, except the breasts
9. Mix the chopped mixture with the sauce and place in a platter
10. Arrange the breasts and remaining ham slices on top
11. Cover the platter with a sheet made of the tender dough explained before
12. Form thin strips of the same dough and place them around the edge of the platter in four to six layers, one on top of another
13. Using the thumb, cut them along the edge all around so they rise when cooked and look good
14. Apply fire from above until the dough browns
15. When browned, dust with very fine orange leaf or orange peel powder and sifted sugar
16. Apply fire from above again for two or three minutes to melt it, then remove
