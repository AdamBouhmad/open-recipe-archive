## Ingredients

- rabbit or hare legs
- bread crumb
- milk
- broth
- hot water
- butter
- salt
- coarse pepper
- nutmeg
- salsa rizada
- egg yolks
- whole eggs

## Directions

1. Lift the slices from the rabbits and take the legs, separating the meat from the sinews with the tip of the knife
2. Pound the meat well and pass it through a quenelles sieve and gather it into a pile
3. Soak bread crumb in milk, broth and hot water, and when well soaked place it in a clean new cloth and squeeze as much as possible so no liquid remains
4. Pound it perfectly afterward and pass it through the same quenelles sieve as the meat, making another pile separate
5. If the butter is very hard, pound it also, making a third pile, ensuring the three portions are equal in quantity of bread, meat, and butter
6. Mix the three piles together and pound them jointly, then add salt, coarse pepper, a little grated nutmeg and one spoonful of salsa rizada
7. Pound the mixture again, adding from time to time an egg yolk until three are complete, then add two whole eggs
8. When the mass is at the right consistency, make a small ball and throw it into a pot or kettle with boiling water to test if it is too firm, in which case add a small piece of butter to the mass; but if it is too soft or delicate, add an egg yolk
9. If you want quenelles with more body and better quality, put beef udder in place of butter, cooking it in the pot and passing it through the quenelles sieve after frying; put more quantity of udder in the mass than the amount of butter that would have been used
10. After everything is mixed, conclude the operation as with common quenelles
