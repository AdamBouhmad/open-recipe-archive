## Ingredients

- corn
- water
- quicklime

## Directions

1. Soak the corn in a proportionate quantity of water
2. Add a small amount of water in which quicklime has been slaked and allowed to settle, by which operation the corn becomes yellow and in this state is called nixtamal
3. Put the nixtamal on the fire with its own water
4. When the pot begins to steam and testing shows that the corn kernels peel easily, remove from the heat
5. Let it rest and when well cooled, wash in many waters, rubbing strongly with the hand until well husked, white and clean
6. Put in the metate, where it is ground and reground
7. Dissolve the mass with water and strain through a horsehair sieve, pouring it into a pot or pan, which placed on the fire is left until the atole takes the proper consistency, stirring frequently so that it does not stick or burn
8. The spoon or stick with which it is stirred should not have any grease and should not be new, because in the first case the atole breaks, and in the second it acquires an unpleasant taste
