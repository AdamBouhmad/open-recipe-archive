## Ingredients

- Thirty egg whites
- Thirty egg yolks
- Seven ounces of ground sugar
- One small glass of white wine
- Seven ounces of butter
- A little ground salt
- Four pounds of fine wheat flour
- Three pounds of ground sugar for glazing

## Directions

1. Beat thirty egg whites, letting them rest until they have a thick sediment
2. Separately and without mixing with them, beat well the thirty yolks with seven ounces of ground sugar and a small glass of white wine, then add seven ounces of butter and a little ground salt
3. Pour this mixture (not the egg whites) into four pounds of fine flour, beating everything so that the dough remains loose; if it is too loose, add a little more flour and knead until it forms eyes
4. Form the puchas and immediately drop them into a pot prepared with boiling water, leaving them there until they rise and float on the water
5. Remove them and place them between two cloths to dry, then put them in the oven where they are kept until their smell is noticed, which is the sign that they are cooked
6. When removing them, coat them with the sediment of beaten egg whites mixed with three pounds of ground sugar
