## Ingredients

- Custard apples, somewhat firm but ripe
- Lye water from wood ash, weak and strained
- One pound sugar for each large custard apple

## Directions

1. Select custard apples that are somewhat firm but ripe and not overripe
2. Cut in half and peel, removing also some of the bitter flesh near the skin
3. Carefully separate the small pieces covering the pits
4. Remove the pits so the small pieces remain whole
5. Add to weak strained lye water from wood ash
6. Let boil over gentle heat until it rises in point, reaching a point somewhat higher than common preserves
7. The proportion of sugar is one pound for each large custard apple
