## Ingredients

- Oregano
- Water
- Tomatoes (xitomate)
- Onions
- Parsley
- Ham slices
- Thyme
- Breadcrumbs
- Cilantro (culantro), toasted
- Saffron
- Cloves
- Cinnamon
- Pepper
- Squabs (raw)
- Lard (manteca)
- Salt
- Almond shells
- Poultry livers
- Cinnamon (additional)
- Cloves (additional)

## Directions

1. Boil oregano in a little water and remove at first boil
2. Fry a good amount of tomatoes with onions and parsley, all chopped very fine, ham slices, and a sprig of thyme in a pot
3. When well fried, add the oregano water, a little breadcrumbs, toasted ground cilantro, saffron, cloves, cinnamon, and pepper all ground
4. Mix well with the oregano broth and let boil until it thickens considerably
5. Coat the raw squabs with this mixture and cover well, then roll them in breadcrumbs or toasted ground breadcrumbs
6. Place them in papers greased with lard, with sufficient salt, wrapping them well leaving only the legs exposed
7. Place in a pot with the sauce on two fires, with the upper fire stronger
8. Cook gently, being careful to turn them; if the broth dries too much, add a little lard and a very small amount of oregano broth
9. When cooked, remove the paper and brown with only the upper fire
10. Serve with poultry sauce or with the following sauce: Toast almond shells and grind with poultry livers, chop onion very finely and fry everything together, adding a little ground cinnamon and cloves, necessary salt, and a little water
