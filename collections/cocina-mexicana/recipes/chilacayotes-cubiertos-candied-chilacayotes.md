## Ingredients

- Chilacayotes
- Lime water
- Clarified syrup without point
- New clarified syrup

## Directions

1. Peel the chilacayote with the point of a knife and scrape until it becomes white
2. Cut into pieces slightly more than a finger's height
3. Soak in settled lime water for half an hour
4. Rinse in clean water and cook so they are neither too hard nor too soft
5. Place immediately in clarified syrup without point and put on the fire so it only boils a few times
6. Repeat this operation daily so the chilacayote penetrates well until the syrup acquires a higher point than that of preserves through being put on the fire so many times
7. Remove the chilacayote and let it drain
8. Put a new well-clarified syrup on the fire until it reaches the point of making threads as thick as a finger, when tested in water
9. Place the chilacayote there and keep it in the syrup until the boil closes or it boils evenly
10. Set aside the pan and on its edge polish the pieces of chilacayote
11. Place them on wires to drain and dry
