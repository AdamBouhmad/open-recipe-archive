## Ingredients

- pulp of a veal leg
- butter
- oil
- three bay leaves
- crushed garlic
- whole peppercorns
- cloves
- orange leaves
- one lemon
- Castilian vinegar
- red wine
- hot water

## Directions

1. Divide the pulp of a veal leg into three pieces and bruise with the flat of a cleaver without grinding the meat, which is drawn without water so that no skin remains
2. Place in a pot to parboil with butter and oil, three bay leaves, crushed garlic, and whole peppercorns and cloves
3. Add more oil, orange leaves, a sliced lemon, some Castilian vinegar, another of red wine and another of hot water, until the liquid covers the meat
4. Cook over low heat, taking care that it does not fall apart, until the broth is consumed
5. Remove the cooked pieces of beef and finish reducing the cooking liquid
6. Add a little oil and red wine to the reduced liquid to fry the cooked pieces there, one by one, turning on both sides
7. Place on a platter over orange leaves and cover with the sediment from the pot with some strips of cooked ham
