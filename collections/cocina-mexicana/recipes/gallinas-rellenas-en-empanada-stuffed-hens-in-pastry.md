## Ingredients

- Cooked hens
- Picadillo or stuffing
- Lard
- Breadcrumbs
- Half pound flour
- One egg
- Two ounces butter
- Half a pozuelo of settled tequesquite water
- A small amount of yeast dissolved in a pozuelo of wine
- Juice of one sour lime
- One tablespoon lard
- Water as needed

## Directions

1. After cooking the hens, stuff them with any of the prepared picadillos or as turkeys are stuffed
2. Coat them with lard and cover completely on all sides with breadcrumbs
3. Wrap them in the prepared dough and place on sheet metal to cook in the oven, ensuring the dough browns well and the hens cook without burning
4. To make the dough: mix half a pound of flour with one egg, two ounces of butter, half a pozuelo of settled tequesquite water, a small amount of yeast dissolved in a pozuelo of wine, the juice of one sour lime, one tablespoon of lard, and water as needed to dissolve the flour
5. Knead everything until soft, adding more lard if necessary
6. Extend the dough with a rolling pin to regular thickness, place the hens on top already covered with breadcrumbs and sufficient lard, wrap them in the dough and cut away excess pieces
