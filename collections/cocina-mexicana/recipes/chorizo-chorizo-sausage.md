## Ingredients

- lean meat in small pieces
- ground salt
- hot and sweet chile
- oregano ground and sifted
- crushed garlic dissolved in a little water
- oregano infusion for preparing intestines
- a little salt for the infusion

## Directions

1. Chop the lean meat into small pieces and place in a large earthenware bowl
2. Season with ground salt, hot and sweet chile for good color, finely ground oregano passed through a sieve, and garlic crushed and dissolved in a little water
3. Knead well so the meat becomes impregnated with the adobo
4. Keep for three or four days, stirring once or twice a day and kneading with your fists
5. When time is up, fry a little in the pan to test if well seasoned and has good color, increasing the amount of ingredients that were found to be scarce, and ensuring the spicy flavor stands out
6. Taste another sample if convenient
7. Once seasoned, stuff the meat into beef or pork intestines prepared ahead of time with an oregano infusion, a little salt, and enough water to cover them, draining well when ready to use
8. Once filled, tie off at intervals according to the size desired for the chorizos
9. Hang them to air dry or place them in smoke
