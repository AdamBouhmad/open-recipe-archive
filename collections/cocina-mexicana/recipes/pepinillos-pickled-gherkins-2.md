## Ingredients

- Small cucumbers (the size of a small finger)
- Salt
- Vinegar of grapes or wine (Castilian vinegar)
- Tarragon
- Saxifrage
- Exotes

## Directions

1. Clean the gherkins by cutting off the stem and removing the flower parts that may remain attached
2. If preparing a small quantity, rub each one individually with a somewhat rough rod; if preparing many at once, place them in a linen sack and shake vigorously for several minutes
3. Place in earthenware vessels, sprinkle with salt and shake strongly so the salt distributes evenly over all
4. Cover the vessels with linen and move and shake several times so the gherkins penetrate well with the salt, forming a brine with the salt and the water they release
5. The next morning, discard this brine, let the gherkins drain well and sprinkle again with fresh salt, leaving them for another twenty-four hours, shaking the vessels many times throughout the day
6. Pour good grape or wine vinegar over the gherkins and let them soak in it for eight days, adding the plants or herbs desired, such as saxifrage and exotes
7. After eight days, remove the first vinegar and replace it with new vinegar; after another week, remove the second vinegar (which is kept with the first for kitchen use), adding aromatics and spices such as tarragon, mint, bay leaf, and pepper
8. Pour new vinegar over the gherkins and store in glass jars or wide-necked bottles
