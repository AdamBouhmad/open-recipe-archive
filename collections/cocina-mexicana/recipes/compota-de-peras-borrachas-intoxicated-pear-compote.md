## Ingredients

- Large bergamot pears
- Ground cinnamon and sugar
- White wine
- Grease-free pot
- Tortilla and paste for sealing

## Directions

1. Select the largest bergamot pears, wash them and with a knife remove the core and seeds from the bottom
2. Fill this hollow with ground cinnamon and sugar, then add white wine with a small spoon
3. Cover with the piece of the bottom that was removed to hollow them
4. Arrange them in a grease-free pot with the stems facing down, ensuring the pear stands upright so it does not break
5. Cover the pot with a tortilla and a little paste at the joints so it does not evaporate
6. Place on a small stove over embers and cover a comal with little heat
7. When you prudently judge the pears are well saturated, uncover the pot; if they are not, add cinnamon, sugar and wine, and cover again until they are well penetrated
8. Do not remove them immediately from the pot so they do not burst
