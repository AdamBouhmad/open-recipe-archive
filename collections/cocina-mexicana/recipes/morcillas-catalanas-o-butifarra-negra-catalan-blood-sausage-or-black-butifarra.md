## Ingredients

- muscular extremities of pork
- bloody and veined fatty tissues
- nervous tissues
- kidneys and other scraps not suitable for white sausages
- lard, more than in the offal
- lungs, heart, belly, and scraps of skin and tripe, cooked beforehand
- blood
- salt
- pepper
- optional spices and seasonings for blood sausages, but not onion
- wide veal or pork intestines

## Directions

1. While chopping pork meats for longanisa, chorizos, and other white sausages, set aside the muscular extremities, bloody and veined fatty tissues, kidneys, and other scraps
2. Chop these scraps raw with slightly more lard than in the offal
3. Also include the lungs, heart, belly, and scraps of skin and tripe
4. Cook everything beforehand
5. Add a portion of blood so the mixture becomes somewhat thick
6. In Catalonia, season this sausage only with salt and pepper; however, one may season it with other spices and seasonings used for blood sausages, but under no circumstances add onion
7. Stuff in wide veal or pork intestines
8. Cook like the others and can be stored for some time
