## Ingredients

- Three pounds of cacahuazentle corn
- More than three pounds of lard
- Water
- Ground sugar
- Egg yolks
- Piñones
- Acitrón
- Canela
- Sesame seeds
- Corn leaves

## Directions

1. To three pounds of cacahuazentle corn add more than three pounds of lard
2. Prepare the corn as for atole, wash it well and dry with a cloth in the sun
3. Grind and sift in an ayate
4. Add a little softened water, beating until it makes like bubbles, and ground sugar
5. Make the dough thicker because the sugar makes it watery
6. To know if it is good, make a little ball of dough and put it in water; if it rises it is because it is good
7. The filling is made of cooked egg yolks, ground sugar, piñones, acitrón, canela and sesame seeds
8. Place the leaves to drain from the morning so they are not wet
9. Cook the tamales in a pot like the previous ones and when they sound hollow, detach the leaves they are cooked in
10. This dough without sugar and without salt can be filled with chile, spices, etc.
