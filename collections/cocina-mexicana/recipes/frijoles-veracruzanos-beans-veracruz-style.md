## Ingredients

- Black beans, small and full
- Water
- A little lard
- Half a small cup of tequisquite water settled (optional)
- Oil
- Garlic cloves, sliced
- Onion, chopped
- Lard
- Salt
- Chopped little onions (for serving)
- Grated cheese (for serving)
- Oil for eating (for serving)

## Directions

1. After washing the sierra beans to remove dust, put them to cook in water with a little lard, and if desired, although not necessary, with half a small cup of settled tequisquite water.
2. When they are cooked, fry sliced garlic cloves in oil and as soon as they brown, remove them from the oil.
3. Fry in the same oil a little chopped onion, which is not removed.
4. Pour the beans with the water in which they were cooked over this, setting aside a handful of them to be ground.
5. Add lard, the handful of ground beans, quarters of onion if desired, and the corresponding salt, not forgetting that if you overdo it, the beans will be salty when they thicken.
6. Let most of the broth reduce and once the beans are well fried, serve them, putting aside chopped little onions, grated cheese, and an oil cruet, so that each person can season them to their taste.
