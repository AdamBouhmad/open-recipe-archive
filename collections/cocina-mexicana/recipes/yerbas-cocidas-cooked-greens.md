## Ingredients

- Sorrel (acedera)
- Chard (acelga) and different species of orache (armuelle) to correct acidity
- Parsley, chervil, and small onions (cebollita) for seasoning
- Salt and necessary spices

## Directions

1. Clean, wash, and drain the greens well in a basket
2. Chop the greens and fill a caldron with them
3. Use tinned copper caldrons or cast iron if possible
4. When greens begin to stick to the bottom, stir vigorously with a wooden spoon or spatula to prevent burning
5. If sticking persists, remove the caldron from heat, empty the greens into a bowl, and wash the caldron well before continuing
6. Return to heat and continue stirring and turning with a wooden spoon or spatula
7. Cook until the mixture is so thick that when a small amount is cooled on a plate and the plate is tilted, no liquid runs and the mixture remains compact
8. Season with salt and necessary spices
9. Place in dry earthenware jars, leaving about an inch of space at the top
10. Cover the greens with melted butter, lard, or olive oil, which when cooled prevents air from entering
