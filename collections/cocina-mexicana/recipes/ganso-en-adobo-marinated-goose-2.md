## Ingredients

- Old goose
- Bacon strips
- Breadcrumbs
- Parsley
- Onions
- Garlic
- Bay leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg
- Water
- White wine
- Brandy
- Carrots

## Directions

1. Prefer an old goose, empty it and hold the legs inside the body
2. Scald and pluck it
3. Lard the entire body with bacon strips wrapped in breadcrumbs and seasoned with chopped parsley, onions, garlic, bay leaf, thyme, basil reduced almost to powder, salt, pepper and nutmeg
4. After larding, tie it and place in a pot of appropriate size with two glasses of water, two of white wine, half glass of brandy, onions and carrots, salt and pepper
5. Cover the pot well and cook over low heat for four hours
6. Once cooked, place the adobo in a platter
7. If the sauce is too clear, reduce it and pour it over the goose, after garnishing with carrots and onions cooked with it
8. It can also be left to cool, poured with the same sauce and not served until this becomes jelly, but this method is not most commonly used
9. Hot goose is always preferred
