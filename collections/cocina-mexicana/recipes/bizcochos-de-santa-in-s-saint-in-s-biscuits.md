## Ingredients

- one quarter of flour
- one pound of butter
- one pound and four ounces of sugar
- ten egg yolks
- salt
- leaven
- water

## Directions

1. To one quarter of flour add one pound of butter, one pound and four ounces of sugar, ten egg yolks and salt to taste; mix and beat a lot with your hands to unite it and allow it to rise well; then add the butter and mix again
2. Take a handful of leaven, add two handfuls of flour and mix with a little water; if it is stiff, form a ball immediately and cover with a napkin; leave overnight in a vessel
3. The next day, dissolve the leaven with water until loose and incorporate it with everything else with a little water and knead again in a trough
4. Knead and work the dough on a table as if washing, break into pieces with a knife and put back in the trough; knead again and when united put on the table and repeat the washing motion, repeating this four or five times so the dough becomes very smooth and soft
5. Leave in the trough until the next day, then knead once more and mix twice
6. To shape, weigh six and a half ounces, knead and form sticks half a vara long with proportionate thickness
7. Place on a board covered with coarse bran so the sticks do not burn
8. Space them six fingers apart from each other to allow for rising; when risen, cut in half with a knife
9. Bake in an oven that is not as hot as for bread to prevent burning
10. Cool, cut into thin slices and return to the oven to toast completely
