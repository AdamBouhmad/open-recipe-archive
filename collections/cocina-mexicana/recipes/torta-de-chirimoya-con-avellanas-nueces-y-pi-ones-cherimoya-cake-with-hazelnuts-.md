## Ingredients

- 2 large ripe cherimoyas
- beaten eggs
- 80 well-peeled walnuts
- avellanas in equal quantity to the walnuts
- equal quantity of pine nuts
- 20 hard biscuits or cambray biscuits
- butter
- clarified syrup at medium point
- raisins, almonds, and candied citron

## Directions

1. Select two large ripe cherimoyas and peel them so completely that no bitterness from the skin remains
2. Grind them on a metate
3. Separately grind eighty well-peeled walnuts, an equal weight of hazelnuts, an equal quantity of pine nuts, and twenty hard biscuits or cambray biscuits, setting each aside
4. Butter a new glazed earthenware dish and melt the butter in it
5. Arrange a paper in the dish and distribute all the ground ingredients in separate layers
6. Mix well with beaten eggs
7. Bake the cake at two gentle fires and when cooked, slice it
8. Place the slices in clarified syrup at medium point and boil gently over low heat until it reaches the consistency of a conserve
9. Remove from heat and stud the cake slices with raisins, almonds, and candied citron
