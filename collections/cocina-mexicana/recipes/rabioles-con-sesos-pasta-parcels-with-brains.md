## Ingredients

- spinach
- tequesquite water
- fat ham
- beef marrow
- brains
- salt
- black pepper
- cloves
- cinnamon
- grated Flemish cheese
- flour
- two or three eggs
- melted lard
- beef broth
- toasted bread
- fresh butter
- parsley

## Directions

1. Cook a portion of spinach with a little tequesquite water
2. Form a filling by mixing the spinach with some fat ham, beef marrow, and brains, all cooked separately, adding fine salt
3. Add ground black pepper, cloves, and cinnamon to the filling, along with grated Flemish cheese, mixing well
4. Make a dough with flour, two or three eggs, and fine salt
5. Extend the dough on a table greased with melted lard until it forms very thin sheets
6. Place small pieces of spinach filling on the sheets at intervals
7. Cover with another sheet of dough and cut around the edges with a wheel or knife to form small pastries
8. Dust with flour so they do not stick and store for another day
9. The next day, cook the pastries gently in water with lard and fine salt so they do not fall apart
10. When cooked, drain well and place in the following broth
11. In a pot, add beef broth and toasted bread that has been ground well
12. Boil with necessary salt and when seasoned, add ground cinnamon, cloves, and enough pepper to stand out
13. Add a quarter of fresh butter and when it comes to a good boil, add the pastries
14. Keep on low heat until the broth thickens slightly
15. Remove from heat and serve with chopped parsley on top
