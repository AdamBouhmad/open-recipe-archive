## Ingredients

- Oranges with thick skin
- Water
- Sugar syrup (clarified, at low point)
- Coconut paste, almond paste, or other filling

## Directions

1. Select oranges with thick skin
2. With a knife, form desired figures or turn them, making parallel lines around them with equal spacing until the end so that when some strips are removed, others remain attached to the orange, surrounding it completely
3. When removing these strips, be careful not to insert the knife too deeply, only to half the thickness of the skin to avoid breaking or piercing the oranges
4. Complete the operation by removing a small wheel from the crown to remove seeds later after cooking
5. Cook with sufficient water so there is no need to add more while the oranges cook
6. When done, remove and cool
7. Remove all seeds with a finger without leaving any, as even one would give bad flavor to the preserve
8. Place in fresh water, changing it twice daily for three consecutive days, leaving them in sun and open air
9. After three days, place in clarified sugar syrup at low point, bringing to a gentle boil over low heat for two or three hours daily for another three days, taking care to preserve also the wheels and strips that are removed
10. After this time and when the oranges are well penetrated with syrup, remove carefully, drain the syrup from inside, and fill with coconut paste, almond paste, or other filling
11. Cover with the small wheel and serve with the syrup and the little strips loose or braided; but if they are to be served dry as is also customary, let them drain and air on hurdles or sieves
