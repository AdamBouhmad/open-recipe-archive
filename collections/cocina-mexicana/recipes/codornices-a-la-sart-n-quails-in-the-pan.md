## Ingredients

- Quails
- Rendered lard, scraped
- Raw ham in small pieces
- Truffles
- Mushrooms
- Some fat livers
- Raw egg yolk
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg
- Fine herbs, chopped
- Fat ham slices
- Beef slices
- Lean ham slices
- Broth
- Ground pepper
- Lemon juice

## Directions

1. After opening the quails along the back, fill them with the following stuffing: mix scraped rendered lard, raw ham in small pieces, truffles, mushrooms, some fat livers, and a raw egg yolk, chopping everything together and seasoning with salt, pepper, nutmeg and finely chopped herbs.
2. Line a saucepan with slices of fat ham, slices of beef and lean ham.
3. Place the quails on top and cover with more slices of the same meats.
4. Cover the saucepan and let the ham stew over embers for two hours.
5. Remove the slices and allow the quails to finish cooking on a small stove.
6. As soon as they take color and the juices stick to the bottom of the pan, remove them.
7. Skim the fat from the juices and moisten with broth what has stuck to the pan so it comes loose.
8. Pass through a sieve, add ground pepper and lemon juice, and pour over the quails.
