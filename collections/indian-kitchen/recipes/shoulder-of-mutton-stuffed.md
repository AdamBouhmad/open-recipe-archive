## Ingredients

- shoulder of mutton
- pepper
- salt
- thin slices of cold cooked bacon
- turkey stuffing
- four ounces of butter
- pint of good broth made from the bones and trimmings
- two Bombay onions
- clove of garlic
- carrot
- six pepper corns
- blade of mace
- good piece of celery
- dessert-spoonful of sugar
- dessert-spoonful of salt
- tumbler of chablis or sauteme
- half a glass of brandy
- half a glass of Madeira
- dessert-spoonful of red currant jelly
- juice of a lime
- few drops of chilli vinegar
- small white onions

## Directions

1. Order a shoulder of the best mutton you can get, bone it carefully, wash it, dry it, and flatten it out upon a clean board.
2. Dust it over with pepper and salt, and lay over it a number of thin slices of cold cooked bacon.
3. Make a good bowl of turkey stuffing and spread it evenly over the bacon.
4. Roll the meat carefully up, and secure it in shape with tapes.
5. Put four ounces of butter into a stew-pan, and turn the roll of mutton over in it till it takes colour.
6. Pour in a pint or so of good broth made from the bones and trimmings, with two Bombay onions cut up, a clove of garlic, a carrot sliced, six pepper corns, a blade of mace, a good piece of celery, a dessert-spoonful of sugar, a dessert-spoonful of salt, a tumbler of chablis or sauteme, and half a glass of brandy.
7. Braise the mutton in this until it is done.
8. Dish it on a hot dish, and brown its surface with a hot iron.
9. Strain off the gravy, remove the fat, flavour it with half a glass of Madeira, a dessert-spoonful of red currant jelly, the juice of a lime, and a few drops of chilli vinegar.
10. Let it boil up, pour it round the mutton, and serve.
11. Garnish the dish with small white onions glazed, and send round potatoes à la maître d'hôtel.
