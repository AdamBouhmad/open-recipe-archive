## Ingredients

- tongue of from three to four pounds weight
- best bazar salt (one and a half lb)
- saltpetre (one oz)
- bay-salt (one oz)
- spiced pepper (one oz)
- moist sugar (five oz)
- juice of three limes
- sherry-glass of good vinegar
- cold water (one pint and a half)

## Directions

1. Cleanse and wipe the tongue thoroughly
2. Rub the tongue with the bazar salt and spiced pepper, expending about a quarter of a pound of salt
3. Put the tongue aside and let it drain for the rest of the day to get rid of any blood
4. Take a roomy enamelled sauce-pan and put into it the remaining bazar salt (about a pound), the bay-salt, saltpetre, the lime juice, vinegar, and a pint and a half of cold water
5. Boil these over a low fire, removing all scum as it rises
6. When clear of scum and well boiled, set the liquor to get cold
7. Make a separate syrup with the sugar, diluting it with water in a small sauce-pan, and heating it gently till free from scum and smooth
8. Let the syrup get cold
9. Amalgamate the salt liquor and syrup with a wooden spoon
10. Pour the completed brine into the tub
11. After the tongue has been drained for six or eight hours, place it in the brine in the evening
12. Let it remain for a couple of days, being frequently turned over and basted during that period
13. On the evening of the second day's soaking, drain the tongue from the brine
14. Hang it to dry in the smoke of the kitchen fire for a couple of days
15. Wrap it in paper during the smoking stage
16. Soak it a little before cooking in cold water
