## Ingredients

- one pound of salmon from the tin
- three-quarters of a pound of forcemeat
- eight ounces of fresh uncooked whiting, pomfret, or other fresh fish
- four ounces of fresh butter
- four ounces of fine white crumbs
- salt and pepper
- a cupful of rich poulette sauce
- two raw eggs
- paste
- white of egg
- a coffee-cupful of rich fish broth made from the bones and trimmings of the fresh fish
- a coffee-cupful of essence of truffles

## Directions

1. Take one pound of salmon from the tin, and drain it from the tin liquor.
2. If nice and firm, cut it into fillets with a dessert-knife.
3. Make three-quarters of a pound of forcemeat as follows: choose either some fresh uncooked whiting, pomfret, or other fresh fish and having taken eight ounces of it pass it through a wire sieve.
4. Add four ounces of fresh butter, and four of fine white crumbs, pound all together in a mortar.
5. Season the puree with salt and pepper; moisten it with a cupful of rich poulette sauce, and bind the mixture with two raw eggs.
6. Line a raised pie tin with paste as for pork pie.
7. Fill the bottom with a layer of the forcemeat, then a layer of the salmon, an inch thick, continuing the packing till the pie is filled.
8. Put a cover of puff-paste over the top, brush it over with white of egg, and bake the pie slowly.
9. When done, let it cool for half an hour, and then pour in, through a hole made in the top, a coffee-cupful of rich fish broth, made from the bones and trimmings of the fresh fish, reduced to a glaze, and mix with a coffee-cupful of essence of truffles.
10. After having poured the essence into the pie, let it get quite cold, then turn it out of the tin, put it upon a napkin, and serve.
