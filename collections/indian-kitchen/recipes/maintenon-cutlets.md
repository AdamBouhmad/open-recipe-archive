## Ingredients

- eight nicely trimmed choplets of mutton
- scraps of bacon
- onion
- carrot
- spices
- pepper
- salt
- grated nutmeg
- pint of good gravy
- glass of sherry
- minced onion
- capers
- two or three truffles
- chopped parsley
- eggs
- papers
- oil or butter
- red currant jelly
- anchovy vinegar
- spoonful of sherry
- mashed potatoes
- celery puree

## Directions

1. Put eight nicely trimmed choplets of mutton into a stew-pan with some scraps of bacon, onion, carrot, spices, pepper, salt, and a pinch of grated nutmeg
2. Add a pint of good gravy, and a glass of sherry
3. Gently stew the little chops till done
4. Lay them out on a large dish, covered by another with a weight upon it
5. When quite cold, trim them finally into shape if necessary
6. Strain the gravy in which they were stewed
7. Remove all fat
8. Mince an onion very small, and a few capers, with two or three truffles also
9. Fry the onions a golden brown
10. Add the minced capers and truffles with pepper, salt, and a spoonful of chopped parsley
11. Moisten with a little of the gravy
12. Thicken it with a couple of eggs
13. Put the mince away to get cold
14. Cut some papers for your cutlets and oil or butter them
15. Spread your cold thick mince over your cutlets liberally
16. Fold them in their papers most carefully
17. Broil them on the grid-iron sufficiently long to heat them thoroughly, or heat them in the oven
18. Serve your cutlets in their papers
19. Hand round a rich sauce made of the remains of the gravy originally got from the cooking of the cutlets, slightly thickened with butter and flour, flavoured with red currant jelly, anchovy vinegar, and a spoonful of sherry
20. Place the cutlets round a ring of mashed potatoes filled with celery puree
