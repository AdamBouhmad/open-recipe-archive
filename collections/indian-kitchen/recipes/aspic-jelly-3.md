## Ingredients

- ox-foot
- bacon bone or slice or two of ham or bacon
- scraps of raw meat
- young fowl
- mixed vegetables
- cold water
- tea-spoonful of caramel
- sugar
- tarragon vinegar

## Directions

1. Put into a stew-pan an ox-foot cleaned and cut up with a bacon bone or a slice or two of ham or bacon
2. Add any scraps of raw meat such as cutlet trimmings or a young fowl cut up as for fricassee with a few mixed vegetables as for soup
3. Add a cup of cold water and cover the pan
4. Set it on the fire, shaking it occasionally
5. When the pieces of meat begin to take colour, add a little more water
6. In about half an hour, pour in enough water to cover the contents of the pan completely
7. Put in a tea-spoonful of caramel and let the vessel simmer for three hours very gently
8. When ready, strain the liquor off into a bowl and let it get cool
9. Skim it carefully and add a tea-spoonful of sugar and table-spoonful of tarragon vinegar
10. Clarify it and strain it finally through a clean cloth
11. This produces a clear, amber-coloured liquid which will set of its own accord without isinglass if put in the ice-box
