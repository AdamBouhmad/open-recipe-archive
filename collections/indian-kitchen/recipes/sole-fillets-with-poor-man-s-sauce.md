## Ingredients

- a couple of nice soles
- egg
- minced parsley
- minced shallot
- broth made from sole bones and trimmings
- a spoonful of chablis or sauterne
- a coffee-cupful of stock
- a couple of finely minced onions
- butter
- a coffee-cupful of the gravy in which the fillets were cooked
- a spoonful of vinegar
- sauce blanche

## Directions

1. Fillet a couple of nice soles
2. Brush the fillets over on one side with egg
3. Dust over that some minced parsley and shallot
4. Roll up your fillets, securing each in shape with a tiny skewer
5. Simmer these fillets carefully in a broth made from their own bones and trimmings, assisted by a spoonful of chablis or sauterne, and a coffee-cupful of stock
6. When done, place the fillets on a hot dish and cover them up
7. For the sauce: Fry a couple of finely minced onions in butter till they assume a golden tint
8. Stir into this, by degrees, a coffee-cupful of the gravy in which the fillets were cooked
9. When boiling, sharpen this by adding a spoonful of vinegar
10. Strain and pour it into another sauce-pan by degrees in which some good sauce blanche has been made
11. Mix thoroughly over the fire, and serve round the fillets
