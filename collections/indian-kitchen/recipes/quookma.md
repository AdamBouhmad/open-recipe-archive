## Ingredients

- about a pound of very tender mutton without any bone
- a dessert-spoonful of pounded green ginger
- salt
- a quarter of a pound of butter
- a couple of white onions
- a couple of cloves of garlic
- a tea-spoonful of pounded coriander-seed
- one tea-spoonful of pounded black pepper
- half a tea-spoonful of pounded cardomoms
- half a tea-spoonful of pounded cloves
- four ounces of well-pounded almonds
- a breakfast-cupful of cream
- a dessert-spoonful of turmeric powder
- a tea-spoonful of sugar
- the juice of a couple of limes

## Directions

1. Cut up about a pound of very tender mutton without any bone, and stir the pieces about in a big bowl with a dessert-spoonful of pounded green ginger, and a sprinkling of salt.
2. Melt a quarter of a pound of butter in a stew-pan, and throw into it a couple of white onions cut into rings, and a couple of cloves of garlic finely minced.
3. Fry for about five minutes, and then add a tea-spoonful of pounded coriander-seed, one of pounded black pepper, half one of pounded cardomoms, and half one of pounded cloves.
4. Cook this for five minutes, then put in the meat, and stir over a moderate fire until the pieces seem tender, and have browned nicely.
5. Take the pan from the fire, and work into it a strong infusion obtained from four ounces of well-pounded almonds, and a breakfast-cupful of cream.
6. Mix thoroughly, adding a dessert-spoonful of turmeric powder, and a tea-spoonful of sugar.
7. Put the pan over a very low fire, and let the curry simmer as gently as possible for a quarter of an hour, finishing off with the juice of a couple of limes.
