## Ingredients

- Rice |- seer
- Fried moog dal ^ seer
- Ghee i powa
- Onion i powa
- A dozen eggs
- Salt 2^ tolas
- Pepper ^ tola
- Coriander i tola
- Aniseed ^ tola
- Ginger i tola
- Garam-masalla ^ tola
- Turmeric |- tola
- Cumin ^ tola
- A few cassia leaves
- Red chillies
- Saffron (if desired)
- Akhni water prepared by boiling pepper, coriander, aniseed and a few cloves and cassia leaves (2 seers)

## Directions

1. Wash and dry the rice and pulse, and brown them lightly in |- powa of ghee
2. Pour in 2 seers of akhni water prepared by boiling the pepper, coriander, aniseed and a few cloves and cassia leaves
3. Fry the pieces of onions and add them with salt, pasted or powdered ginger, turmeric and cumin when the rice begin to boil
4. Add a little saffron if desired
5. Slow down the fire
6. Add the contents of several fresh eggs and stir incessantly so that they may not coagulate
7. Boil |- powa of ghee with cassia leaves and red chillies, and pour it in the khechuri when the boiling nears completion
8. Add powdered garam-masalla before taking down
9. Sprinkle some finely chopped onions while serving on the khechuri in every dish
10. If potatoes are to be added, cut them in pieces, brown a little, and add to the khechuri with the spices
