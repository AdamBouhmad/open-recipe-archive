## Ingredients

- hare
- marinade (port wine, vinegar, ketchup, red currant jelly)
- stuffing as for turkey
- hare kidneys
- hare heart
- fat bacon
- onion
- thin slices of bacon
- melted butter or clarified beef suet
- flour
- best gravy
- hare liver
- butter
- onion finely shredded
- chopped liver
- blood from hare

## Directions

1. Skin, clean, and wash the hare well, saving the heart, liver, kidneys and blood
2. Wipe the carcass inside and out
3. Let it soak in the marinade all day, turning it every now and then
4. Mince the kidneys and heart, and fry in fat bacon with a little onion
5. Pour the contents of the pan into a bowl to cool, and when cold, pound to a paste and mix with the stuffing
6. Fill the hare with the well-made stuffing
7. Lard the back of the hare, or cover with thin slices of bacon pinned down with little skewers
8. Roast the hare with constant basting of melted butter or clarified beef suet throughout the process
9. When nearly done, remove the bacon strips and lightly dredge the back with flour
10. Allow the skin to brown and run into crisp blisters
11. Prepare the sauce: Make a good pint of the best gravy you can
12. Cut the liver into dice
13. Melt an ounce of butter in a small sauce-pan
14. Throw in an onion finely shredded, toss the onion till it colours nicely
15. Throw in the chopped liver, shaking the pan for a minute or two
16. Add a little gravy, stir well, pour in all the gravy and simmer till the liver is cooked
17. Strain the gravy
18. Pour into it the marinade of port wine, vinegar, ketchup, and red currant jelly in which the hare was soaked
19. Put it on the fire in a sauce-pan
20. Pour in very slowly as it warms the blood you saved; continue stirring, and the sauce will thicken
21. Throw into it the liver pounded to a paste, stir and serve very hot
22. Serve the hare with the prepared sauce
