## Ingredients

- half a pound of prunes
- two ounces of white sugar
- a slice or two of a lime
- a little cinnamon
- claret and water, mixed half and half
- about an ounce of gelatine
- a liqueur glass of cherry brandy
- a glass of good red wine
- blanched almonds
- whipped cream

## Directions

1. Put half a pound of prunes into a sauce-pan with two ounces of white sugar, a slice or two of a lime, a little cinnamon, and sufficient claret and water, mixed half and half, to cover them
2. Stew gently till the fruit is quite tender
3. Lift the sauce-pan from the fire, drain off the liquor, stone the prunes
4. Pass the fruit through the sieve, and save the pulp in a basin
5. Crack the stones, and throw the prune kernels into the pulp
6. Steep about an ounce of gelatine in the liquor that you strained off, put it on the fire and let it dissolve
7. Mix it with the prune pulp, and pour in a liqueur glass of cherry brandy and a glass of good red wine
8. Decorate a plain border mould with blanched almonds, fill the mould with the prune liquid, and set it upon ice to form
9. When required, turn out the pain, and fill the centre of the mould with whipped cream
