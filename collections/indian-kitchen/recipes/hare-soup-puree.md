## Ingredients

- shin and a half of beef
- hare
- bacon or ham bone
- lean slices of bacon or ham
- anchovy
- Bombay onions
- carrots
- marjoram
- thyme
- parsley
- rind of limes
- pepper corns
- butter
- flour
- hare blood
- half pint of portwine
- red currant jelly
- crystal vinegar or lime juice

## Directions

1. Make your ordinary amount of stock from a shin and a half of beef
2. As the stock is being made, clean, and cut up the hare, saving the blood
3. Let the pieces soak for half an hour, or so, in cold water
4. Put the pieces of hare into a large stew-pan with a bacon, or ham bone, or a few lean slices of either, an anchovy, three Bombay onions cut into quarters, three carrots sliced, a bag containing a dessert-spoonful each of marjoram, thyme, and parsley, the rind of two limes, and a dozen pepper corns
5. Cover the whole with the stock
6. Let this simmer very gently for three hours, after it has once slowly attained boiling point
7. Strain the liquor from the meat, &c., and set it to get cool
8. Pick the meat out, and lay the pieces on a large dish
9. Choose the back fillets, pick the meat from the bones, and pound it to a paste in a mortar, moistening it with a little soup
10. Work it through the sieve, and save the pulp
11. Take another stew pan, place it on the fire, melt a couple of ounces of butter in it, and work into it a good table-spoonful of flour
12. Add a little soup with which the blood has been mixed, continue stirring, and add soup and pounded meat till you have exhausted the whole, stirring without ceasing
13. Add half a pint of portwine, a table-spoonful of red currant jelly, and a table-spoonful of crystal vinegar, or lime juice
14. Let the soup come to the boil, so as to thicken properly, and become thoroughly blended
15. Serve
