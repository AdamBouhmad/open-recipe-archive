## Ingredients

- hare
- half pound of butter
- sliced onion
- muslin bagful of herbs
- pepper
- salt
- butter
- table spoonful of flour
- quart and a half of beef consomme
- four glasses of portwine or claret
- blood from hare

## Directions

1. Skin, clean, and thoroughly wash the hare, saving all the blood in a cup
2. Cut the hare up into small pieces and put them into a stew-pan with half pound of butter, a sliced onion, and a muslin bagful of herbs
3. Season with pepper and salt
4. Fry the meat over a brisk fire for five minutes
5. Make a roux in another stew-pan with a pat of butter and a table spoonful of flour
6. Moisten the roux with a quart and a half of beef consomme and add four glasses of portwine or claret
7. Pour this into the pan containing the pieces of hare
8. Let them stew very slowly till thoroughly done
9. Drain off the liquor from the bones, put the neatest pieces of hare on one side for serving with the soup
10. Return the liquor to the stew-pan and let it throw up all grease in the form of scum, which skim off carefully
11. Mix the saved blood with some of the soup from the stew-pan and thoroughly amalgamate these
12. Add the mixture slowly through a pointed tin strainer to the gradually re-heating soup
13. Let it come nearly to the boil
14. Serve it over the pieces of hare you preserved
