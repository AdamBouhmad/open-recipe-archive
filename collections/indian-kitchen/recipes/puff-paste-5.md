## Ingredients

- 1 pound flour
- ½ ounce salt
- ¼ pint cold water
- 1 good ripe lime
- 1 pound clarified suet
- iced water

## Directions

1. Weigh a pound of flour and turn it out upon your cold marble slab
2. Make a hollow in its centre and fill it with half an ounce of salt and a quarter of a pint of the cold water
3. Mix the flour gradually with the water
4. When half mixed, sprinkle over it another quarter pint of water in which the lime has been squeezed
5. Mix it all thoroughly until it ceases to adhere to the slab and pat it into a round ball
6. Take one pound weight of the clarified suet, cut it up into dice
7. Flatten out the ball of paste to a thickness of about two inches, spreading the suet evenly over its surface
8. Fold the four sides of the paste to the centre enclosing the suet, and form a square piece
9. Roll this evenly out a yard long
10. Fold over one-third of the length towards the centre and fold the other third over it (one turn)
11. Let it rest for ten minutes in the ice-box or on a very cold slab
12. Give it two turns, rest ten minutes, then two turns more (five rolls out in all)
13. Gather the paste into a lump and roll it according to your requirements
