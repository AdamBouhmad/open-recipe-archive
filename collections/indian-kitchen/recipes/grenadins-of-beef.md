## Ingredients

- fillet of beef or tender meat from the ribs
- fat bacon
- meat and bones
- glass of Marsala
- yolks of seven eggs
- five ounces of butter
- salt
- pepper
- dessert-spoonful of tarragon vinegar
- table-spoonful of chopped parsley
- water
- potato
- crisply fried bacon
- flageolets or petits pois

## Directions

1. Trim fillet into thick heart-shaped fillets large enough for one person each
2. Lard them with fat bacon
3. Set them to marinade all day as described in Chapter VIII, page 65
4. Drain them and stew them gently in as rich a stock as you can make from the meat and bones
5. Add a glass of Marsala to the stock
6. Keep them hot in the bain-marie when done
7. Put into a stew-pan the yolks of seven eggs, one ounce of butter, one pinch of salt, and a little pepper
8. Stir over a low fire till the yolks begin to thicken
9. Take the pan off the fire and stir in one ounce of butter more
10. Stir over the fire for two minutes and again remove the pan, adding another ounce of butter
11. Repeat the process again twice, using in all five ounces of butter
12. Stir till the sauce is rich and creamy looking
13. Finally add a dessert-spoonful of tarragon vinegar and a table-spoonful of chopped parsley
14. Add a little water with the butter to prevent curdling
15. Dish the grenadins round a ring of carefully mashed potato, introducing a slice of crisply fried bacon between each of them
16. Serve the sauce piping hot in a boat
17. Put flageolets a la maitre d'hotel or petits pois in the hollow formed by the potato ring
