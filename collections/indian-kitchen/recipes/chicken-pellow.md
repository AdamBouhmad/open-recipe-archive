## Ingredients

- a good-sized chicken
- one pound of beef
- two cupfuls of clean water
- onions
- ginger
- salt
- twelve onions
- two chittacks or four ounces of ghee
- half a pound or a coonkee of the best bassrauttee or cheeneesuckur
- a few cloves
- four or five cardamoms
- half a dozen small sticks of cinnamon
- peppercorns
- a blade or two of mace
- one dessertspoonful of salt
- ghee or butter
- two hard-boiled eggs
- half a dozen bits of finely-sliced and fried bacon

## Directions

1. Take a good-sized chicken and clean, truss, and boil it with one pound of beef in two cupfuls of clean water, seasoning it with onions, ginger, and salt
2. When sufficiently cooked, but yet quite firm, remove the chicken, and set it and the gravy aside
3. Cut up twelve onions lengthways into fine slices
4. Warm your pot
5. Melt in it two chittacks or four ounces of ghee, and as it bubbles, throw in the sliced onions and fry to a light brown
6. Remove and set aside the fried onions
7. Put in half a pound or a coonkee of the best bassrauttee or cheeneesuckur, having drained away all the water in which it was washed, and fry
8. On the rice absorbing the ghee, throw in a few cloves, four or five cardamoms, half a dozen small sticks of cinnamon, some peppercorns, a blade or two of mace, and one dessertspoonful of salt
9. Mix up the whole, and pour over it the gravy in which the chicken and beef were boiled, or as much of it only as will entirely cover the rice
10. Close the pot immediately with a close-fitting cover, and set on a slow fire
11. As the gravy continues to decrease or to be absorbed, keep reducing the fire
12. Shake up the pot occasionally, or stir its contents, to prevent the pellow from burning
13. Brown the boiled chicken in a pan with ghee or butter
14. Place the chicken, either whole or cut up, on the centre of a dish, covering it with the pellow
15. Strew over it the fried onions, garnishing it besides with two hard-boiled eggs, cut into halves, or in some device, and with half a dozen bits of finely-sliced and fried bacon
