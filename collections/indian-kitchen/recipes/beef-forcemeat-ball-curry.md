## Ingredients

- more than two pounds of good fat beef
- lard or ghee
- salt
- pepper
- garden herbs
- bread-crumbs
- broth or milk
- egg

## Directions

1. Get rather more than two pounds of good fat beef; wash it thoroughly, and cut it into pieces, rejecting all veins and scraggy portions
2. Put about two pounds of it into a mortar and pound it fine, removing all fibres, veins, &c.
3. Mix with the pounded beef a teaspoonful of salt, pepper, and garden herbs, and two tablespoonfuls of bread-crumbs
4. Add a little of the broth, or in its absence some milk
5. Mix the whole well together
6. Beat up the yolk and white of the egg, add it to the mixture, and make into balls about the size of large walnuts
7. Roll them in bread-crumbs
8. After heating the pot, melt the lard or ghee, and fry brown the ground ingredients, sprinkling a tablespoonful of cold water over them
9. Add the coftas or balls with salt to taste, and fry or brown them
10. Pour into the pot either a cup of broth or of water, and allow to simmer for about two hours
