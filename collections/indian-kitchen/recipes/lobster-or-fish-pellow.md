## Ingredients

- one or two hilsa or beckty fishes
- eight or ten large long-legged lobsters with the roe or coral
- salt
- onions
- sliced ginger
- peppercorns
- a dozen bay-leaves
- a tablespoonful of unroasted dhuniah or coriander seed
- water

## Directions

1. Take out the centre bones of the fish
2. Thoroughly wash the fish and lobsters in several waters with salt
3. Boil with plenty of seasoning of onions, sliced ginger, peppercorns, a dozen bay-leaves, a tablespoonful of unroasted coriander seed, and salt, with water sufficient to give the required quantity of gravy
4. When ready, remove and shell the lobsters, reserving the roe or red coral in the heads
5. Bruise down the roe with a little unroasted coriander seed and mix with the fish gravy
6. Make the pellow in all other respects the same as prawn pellow, using the gravy of the fish instead of cocoanut or other gravy
7. Garnish with the lobsters
