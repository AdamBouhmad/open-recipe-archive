## Ingredients

- one coonkee or half a pound of bassmuttee or cheeneesuckur rice
- two cups of pure cow's milk
- two or three small sticks of cinnamon
- one tablespoonful and a half to two tablespoonfuls of fine-quality white sugar

## Directions

1. Thoroughly boil one coonkee or half a pound of the bassmuttee or the cheeneesuckur rice
2. Drain the water away
3. Add two cups of pure cow's milk, and put over a slow fire
4. As the rice begins to absorb the milk, add two or three small sticks of cinnamon, with one tablespoonful and a half to two tablespoonfuls of fine-quality white sugar
5. On the milk being entirely absorbed, the kheer is either turned out upon a dish and eaten hot, or put into a buttered mould, served up in shape, and eaten cold
