## Ingredients

- one pound of well-dried and sifted flour
- a quarter of a pound of cold clarified suet
- a tea-spoonful of salt
- iced water

## Directions

1. Put one pound of well-dried and sifted flour on the slab, or in an enamelled basin
2. make a hollow in the centre, and work into it a quarter of a pound of cold clarified suet cut into pieces, adding a tea-spoonful of salt
3. When mixed, sprinkle over it as much iced water as will bind it thoroughly
4. dredge some flour over the slab, and roll the paste out half an inch thick
5. Cut up another quarter of a pound of clarified suet, and dot it over the surface of the paste
6. fold the paste over it, and roll it out again very thin
7. fold it in three, set it in the ice-box for ten minutes
8. give it three more turns, and then roll it out half an inch thick when it may be cut to cover the pie
