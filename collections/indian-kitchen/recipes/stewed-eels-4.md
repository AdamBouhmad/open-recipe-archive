## Ingredients

- a couple of eels
- two ounces of butter
- a Bombay onion
- half a pint of claret
- half a pint of stock
- a clove of garlic
- a carrot
- sweet herbs
- salt
- spiced pepper
- the rind of a lime
- sugar
- parsley

## Directions

1. Skin a couple of eels, clean, parboil, and divide them into two inch fillets for stewing
2. Put into your stew-pan two ounces of butter, with a Bombay onion sliced
3. Stir over the fire for five minutes
4. Add half a pint of claret, and half a pint of stock, with a clove of garlic, a carrot cut up, and a bag of sweet herbs
5. Boil this for ten minutes, stirring it well with your wooden spoon
6. Throw in a salt-spoonful of salt, a tea-spoonful of spiced pepper, the rind of a lime, and place the pieces of eel in the midst
7. Simmer this for half an hour
8. Arrange the pieces of the fish upon the hot dish, strain the gravy rapidly, thicken it
9. Re-heat it almost to boiling point, add a pinch of sugar, and a table-spoonful of chopped parsley
10. Pour it round the fish
