## Ingredients

- yolks of three large, or four ordinary Indian eggs
- salt
- juice of two limes
- salad oil
- tarragon vinegar
- black pepper
- lettuce leaves
- broken aspic jelly
- gherkins
- turned olives
- capers
- fillets of anchovy
- balls of green or yellow butter
- hard-boiled yolks of egg

## Directions

1. Put the yolks of three large, or four ordinary Indian eggs (raw) in a flat joint dish with a tea-spoonful of salt, and the juice of two limes, and beat them well
2. Tip the dish on end at an angle of about 35 degrees, by slipping a thick book under the rim at one end
3. Open a fresh bottle of salad oil, and get an assistant to let the oil fall, in rapidly succeeding drops, upon the centre of the dish, whilst you continue beating the egg mixture upwards to make it pass under the stream of dripping oil
4. Half a pint of salad oil may be thus expended, until you have a sauce as thick as treacle, and of a golden yellow tint
5. Arrange your mayonnaise in its dish
6. Shake a few drops of tarragon vinegar over it, with a dust of black pepper
7. Arrange some well dried lettuce leaves, trimmed with a silver dessert knife, over the mayonnaise
8. Garnish as you like, with broken aspic jelly, gherkins, turned olives, capers, fillets of anchovy, balls of green or yellow butter, plain hard-boiled yolks of egg whole
9. Ice the sauce, and pour it over all
