## Ingredients

- 4 lb. or 2 seers butter, perfectly free of water
- 2 lb. or 1 seer good clean sugar
- 2 lb. or 1 seer raisins, cleaned, stoned, and dried
- 2 lb. or 1 seer currants, cleaned, stoned, picked, and dried
- 2 lb. or 1 seer Jordan almonds, blanched and sliced very fine
- preserved ginger, orange-peel, lemon-peel, and pumpkin in small pieces and well dried, mixed
- 1 tablespoonful cinnamon, finely pounded and sifted
- 1 tablespoonful nutmegs, finely grated
- 1 tablespoonful dried orange-peel, finely pounded and sifted
- 2 tablespoonfuls English caraway-seeds, cleaned and picked
- 1 tablespoonful mace, finely pounded and sifted
- 1 lb. or 1/2 seer finely-sifted flour
- 1 lb. or 1/2 seer soojee
- 40 eggs, new or fresh laid
- 1 claret-glass brandy of the best quality

## Directions

1. Have two large glazed earthen preserving-pans; put the sugar into one and bruise it well down, breaking all the lumps
2. Add to it three pounds and three-quarters of butter
3. Throw in one by one all the yolks of the forty eggs, and throw the whites into the other preserving-pan
4. Mix the sugar, butter, and the yolks the whole while briskly and without ceasing
5. While one man is mixing these ingredients another ought to beat up the whites of the eggs unceasingly for nearly an hour
6. After the butter has been well mixed with the sugar and eggs, dredge in all the finely-pounded spices and the caraway-seeds
7. After a while dredge in the flour and soojee in small quantities at a time, well mixed
8. Add the currants, raisins, and preserves, with the almonds
9. Pour in the brandy very gradually and in small quantities at a time
10. Last of all add the well-beaten whites of the forty eggs, stirring very briskly to effect a perfect mixture
11. Fill quickly into the moulds, lined with paper and well buttered
12. Bake without a moment's delay in a brisk baker's oven
