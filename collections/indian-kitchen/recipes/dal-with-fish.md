## Ingredients

- pulses of grams and moog
- big sized shrimps and fish
- salt
- powdered turmeric
- oil
- moog-dal or chhola-dal
- water
- cumin 1½ tola per seer
- pepper 1 tola per seer
- turmeric 1 tola per seer
- salt 2 tolas per seer
- cassia leaves
- red chillies
- ghee
- powdered garam-masala

## Directions

1. Prepare big sized shrimps and fishes of the higher classes
2. Keep the scales on the heads of shrimps and fry in oil
3. Use pieces of big fish and its head, entire or divided, preferably that of Rohit, Katla or Bhetki fish
4. Fry and break the head in small fragments
5. Smear salt and powdered turmeric on the pieces before frying
6. Wash fried moog-dal or chhola-dal and boil with water
7. Chhola-dal may be fried a little before boiling
8. Add cumin, pepper, turmeric and salt all powdered for every seer of raw pulse when they are half-boiled
9. Add the fried shrimps or pieces of fish or the broken head with the bones or both fish and head
10. Stir thoroughly
11. Fry a few cassia leaves and red chillies with some ghee and pour these into the boiling dal
12. Boil till the dal is moderately thick
13. Add ghee and powdered garam-masala, and stir before taking down
