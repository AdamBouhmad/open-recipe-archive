## Ingredients

- yolks of two hard-boiled eggs
- a salt-spoonful of salt
- a dessert-spoonful of mustard powder
- a tea-spoonful of finely-minced shallot
- white pepper
- oil
- yolks of two raw eggs
- tarragon vinegar

## Directions

1. Put into a soup-plate or slop-basin the cold, very hard-boiled yolks of two eggs, a salt-spoonful of salt, a dessert-spoonful of mustard powder, a tea-spoonful of finely-minced shallot, and a dust of white pepper
2. Bruise these together thoroughly with the back of a silver spoon
3. Add a little oil, and work your materials to a paste, dropping in the oil patiently by degrees until you get it nice and moist
4. Throw in the yolks of two raw eggs, and continue your working
5. Add oil without measure, judging by your eye when you think you have made enough sauce
6. Finally add tarragon vinegar, not more than a good table-spoonful
7. When the vinegar is added, the sauce will assume a creamy appearance
8. When worked sufficiently, pass through a block tin strainer to get rid of onion atoms and lumps of egg
9. Ice the sauce, if only for a few minutes, before serving
