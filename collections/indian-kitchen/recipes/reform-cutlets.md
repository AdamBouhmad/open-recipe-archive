## Ingredients

- ten neck chops
- marinade
- ham
- bread crumbs
- mashed potato
- puree de pois verts
- Bellary onion
- curled parsley
- pounded lean ham or bacon
- garlic
- carrot
- thyme leaves
- Espagnole sauce
- anchovy vinegar
- chilli vinegar
- red currant jelly
- mushroom ketchup
- Madeira
- hard-boiled eggs
- black Leicestershire mushrooms
- gherkins
- lean ham
- white pepper

## Directions

1. Set ten nice neck chops, very neatly trimmed, in marinade all day.
2. Towards evening lift them out, wipe them dry, and proceed to bread-crumb them as laid down at page 67.
3. Mix very finely minced ham with the crumbs at a proportion of half and half.
4. When the time arrives, the cutlets must be very delicately fried in plenty of boiling fat, and served as soon as they reach that bright golden tint.
5. Place the cutlets round a ring of mashed potato, with puree de pois verts in the centre.
6. For the sauce: Put into a sauce-pan, one Bellary onion cut into rings, two sprigs of curled parsley, two ounces of pounded lean ham or bacon, a clove of garlic, a carrot cut up, and a tea-spoonful of thyme leaves.
7. Cover them with a pint of Espagnole sauce and add two tea-spoonfuls of anchovy vinegar, and one of chilli vinegar.
8. Boil up, simmer for ten minutes, skimming carefully, then boil again reducing the sauce to the thickness of cream.
9. Add a dessert-spoonful of red currant jelly and one of good mushroom ketchup.
10. Stir till the jelly is dissolved, adding half a glass of Madeira.
11. Pass the sauce through the strainer.
12. Keep it hot in the bain-marie, and add just before serving, the following mixture: the whites of two hard-boiled eggs, four black Leicestershire mushrooms, two gherkins, and half an ounce of lean ham, all chopped up into small dice, and dusted over with white pepper.
13. Send the sauce round in a boat separately.
