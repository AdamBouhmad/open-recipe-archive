## Ingredients

- two handsome slices of seer fish
- bones and trimmings for broth
- one onion
- one carrot
- a bunch of parsley
- one dessert-spoonful preserved thyme and marjoram
- one minced anchovy
- a dozen pepper corns
- one table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup
- one table-spoonful of Harvey sauce
- one table-spoonful of vinegar
- one table-spoonful of chablis or sauteme
- two table-spoonfuls of chopped truffles
- one ounce butter
- one table-spoonful of Madeira
- well browned gravy

## Directions

1. Stew two handsome slices of seer fish in a good broth made from bones and trimmings, assisted by an onion, a carrot, a bunch of parsley, a dessert-spoonful of preserved thyme and marjoram, a minced anchovy, a dozen pepper corns, a table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup, a table-spoonful of Harvey sauce, a table-spoonful of vinegar, and a table-spoonful of chablis or sauteme
2. Let the fish slices cook slowly in this broth
3. When done, drain and place them in a very hot dish, carefully covered up
4. Strain the broth in which they were cooked, thicken it, and add a little well browned gravy
5. Toss two table-spoonfuls of chopped truffles in a frying-pan in an ounce butter and a table-spoonful of Madeira, then throw into the sauce
6. Let the sauce simmer for ten minutes to extract the flavour of the truffles
7. Pour it over the slices and serve
