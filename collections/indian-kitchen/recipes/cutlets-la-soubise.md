## Ingredients

- one neck of mutton
- pepper and salt
- one Bombay onion
- two handfuls of sorrel
- one lettuce
- two table-spoonfuls of butter
- half a pint of gravy slightly thickened with flour
- one tea-spoonful of white sugar
- three salt-spoonfuls of salt
- black pepper
- mashed potato
- water-cress

## Directions

1. Choose a first rate neck of mutton and divide it into the neatest cutlets you can
2. Give them a dusting of pepper and salt
3. Place them on marinade for the rest of the day
4. Take a light sauce-pan and melt the butter at the bottom of it
5. Throw into it the onion very finely shredded
6. Toss this about till it turns a pale yellow
7. Add the whole of the sorrel and the lettuce also finely cut up
8. Stir the vegetables about in the melted butter till they begin to change colour
9. Pour into the sauce-pan about half a pint of gravy slightly thickened with flour
10. Stir this well and put in a tea-spoonful of white sugar, three salt-spoonfuls of salt, and a good dusting of black pepper
11. Let the vegetables boil for about five minutes
12. If the sauce is too thick, dilute it with a little more gravy till it assumes the consistency of a rich puree
13. Ease the fire and let the sorrel simmer for half an hour
14. Drain the cutlets from their marinade, dip them in melted butter, and grill them over a bright clear fire
15. Prepare a circle of mashed potato, fill it with the puree burning hot
16. Arrange the cutlets round the outside of the circle with bunches of water-cress for garnish
