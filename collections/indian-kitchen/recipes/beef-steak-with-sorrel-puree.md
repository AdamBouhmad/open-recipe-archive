## Ingredients

- butter
- onion
- beef steak
- gravy made from scraps and trimmings
- vegetables
- two good handfuls of carefully picked sorrel
- salt
- pinch of soda
- two ounces of butter
- flour
- teaspoonful of sugar
- yolks of three eggs
- milk

## Directions

1. Make some gravy with scraps and trimmings and strain it off and keep it handy
2. Take a frying-pan, melt a piece of butter at the bottom of it, and shred an onion therein
3. Lay the steak upon it, and turn it about until it is well browned on both sides
4. Lift it up and place it in your stew-pan, with the gravy and vegetables, to simmer gently till done, which you can test with a fork
5. Prevent fast cooking; the slow simmering process is the thing needful
6. Strain the vegetables from the gravy, and cut up into neat dice, to garnish the top of the steak
7. Save the gravy for the sorrel
8. Put two good handfuls of carefully picked sorrel in plenty of boiling water, with a little salt, and a pinch of soda, blanch for five minutes, drain, and chop the leaves small on a board
9. Melt two ounces of butter in a sauce-pan to which add a little flour, the sorrel, a tea-spoonful of sugar, and the steak gravy stirring vigorously to prevent the leaves catching at the bottom of the pan
10. When the gravy and butter are thoroughly well absorbed, turn the sorrel out, stir in the yolks of three eggs beaten up in a little milk, and pour the puree round the steak, which should have been kept hot
11. The sorrel should be got ready beforehand
