## Ingredients

- a bright clear consomme
- six snipes
- a pinch of salt
- chopped parsley
- a little thyme
- Nepaul pepper or spiced pepper
- a very little red currant jelly
- a few drops of portwine
- a little bread-crumb soaked in stock
- two eggs
- half a glass of Madeira

## Directions

1. Prepare a bright clear consomme for your eight covers
2. Slightly roast six snipes; let them get cold
3. Divide them into convenient pieces, mash them in a mortar and throw the whole, with the exception of the livers of the birds, into the consomme
4. Let it come to the boil, skimming carefully, and then remain simmering all the afternoon
5. Towards evening, strain the liquor from the bones, and set it to cool
6. Pick from the remnants of the snipe sufficient meat to form a dozen tiny quenelles the size of an olive
7. Pound this meat in a mortar with the saved livers, a pinch of salt, some chopped parsley, a little thyme, and a dust of Nepaul pepper or spiced pepper
8. Stir in a very little red currant jelly and a few drops of portwine
9. Stiffen this with a little bread-crumb soaked in stock, and bind the whole with two eggs
10. Let it get cold, roll it out, and divide it into little portions
11. Form them between two tea-spoons, and poach till done just before serving the soup
12. Put them into the soup at the last minute
13. Meanwhile clarify the consomme, assisted by half a glass of Madeira, and again heat almost to boiling point
