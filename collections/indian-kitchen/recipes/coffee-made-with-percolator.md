## Ingredients

- coffee berries
- butter or salad oil
- boiling water
- boiling milk

## Directions

1. Get really good coffee berries and be willing to pay more than the usual price for them
2. Roast a tablespoonful of berries at a time in a frying-pan over a very low fire
3. Melt a little butter, sufficient to lubricate the berries
4. Stir the berries about until they turn a light Havannah brown, discarding any berry that takes a darker tint
5. Grind them at once as coarsely as your mill will admit, with grains quite as large as those of rifle gun-powder
6. Make the coffee as soon afterwards as possible
7. Heat the coffee-pot thoroughly
8. Fill the upper chamber of the percolator according to your requirements
9. Ram the coffee down firmly
10. Pour boiling water in tea-spoonfuls at a time through the upper strainer upon the powder
11. Add water slowly so that the coffee will become thoroughly soaked and the essence will be as strong as possible
12. As soon as the coffee has run through, pour the rich essence into your cups
13. For cafe au lait, fill the cups with boiling milk; for cafe noir, fill with a little boiling water
14. For a party, make the essence just before dinner and keep it covered, then reheat gently at the time it is wanted
