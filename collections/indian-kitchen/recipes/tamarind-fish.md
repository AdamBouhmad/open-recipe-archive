## Ingredients

- ripe tamarinds
- good English vinegar
- salt
- hilsa fish
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Make a thick pickle of ripe tamarinds, good English vinegar, and a little salt
2. Pass through a seive, rejecting all stones and fibres
3. Select really good fresh hilsa fish, full size, with roes
4. Remove all the scales and fins, cut away the heads and tails, remove the roes, clean out the fish inside, and then slice up, an inch thick
5. Wipe away all blood, &c., with a clean dry towel
6. Lay out the slices of fish and roe on a clean dish, sprinkle thickly with salt, and place over them a wire dish-cover to keep away the flies
7. Four or five hours afterwards put a layer of the pickle into a wide-mouthed bottle or jar, and a thick coating of pickle over each slice of fish and the roes, after washing away the salt with a little vinegar
8. Lay them in order in the jar, until the last of the fish is put in
9. Put in a very thick layer of the pickle
10. Cork the jar securely, and tie it down with a good bladder to keep it air-tight
11. In three weeks it will be fit for use
