## Ingredients

- pines
- sugar
- cinnamon sticks
- bay-leaves

## Directions

1. Take care that the pines are not green, nor yet quite ripe
2. Remove the peel, cutting it deeply, and then all the seeds and eyes
3. Cut each pine into six slices
4. Lay them in a preserving-pan, and sprinkle over each layer a good quantity of sugar, a few sticks of cinnamon, and a few bay-leaves, covering the uppermost layer with a larger quantity of sugar
5. Allow them to simmer over a tolerably brisk fire until the sugar has all melted
6. Reduce the fire, and continue to simmer until the pines have quite changed colour and become tender
7. Remove them out of the syrup into a colander, and allow them to drain, but continue to boil the syrup with all that drops from the fruit until it has thickened
8. Return the fruit into the syrup and finish the boiling
9. Bottle when quite cool
10. Before corking them for good, ascertain the state of the syrup every two or three days
11. If it shows signs of fermentation, remove it from the fruit and reboil it
12. This operation must be continued until the syrup has ceased to ferment
13. The fruit is not to be reboiled, but only returned into the syrup when the boiling is about to be finished
