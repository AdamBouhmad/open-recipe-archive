## Ingredients

- a snipe for each head
- beef gravy
- an onion
- a stick of celery
- a carrot
- sweet herbs in a bag
- a clove of garlic
- salt
- a tea-spoonful of sugar
- the peel of a lime
- bread soaked in stock
- a lightly roasted chicken with its liver
- two raw eggs
- spiced pepper
- a table-spoonful of butter
- a dessert-spoonful of flour
- a glass of portwine
- a dessert-spoonful of vinegar or lime juice
- a dessert-spoonful of red currant jelly
- a tin of black Leicestershire mushrooms

## Directions

1. Roast the snipes lightly, then cut off the meat of the breasts whole in fillets
2. Mash the rest, trails and all, and throw the debris into a large sauce-pan with a pint and a half of well made beef gravy, an onion cut up, a stick of celery, a carrot, sweet herbs in a bag, a clove of garlic, salt to taste, a tea-spoonful of sugar, and the peel of a lime
3. Simmer for an hour or more to get the essence out of the snipe bones
4. When done, strain off your gravy, and set it to get cool
5. Pick all the snipe meat from the bones of the thighs and back, with the livers, &c, and pound the whole with half its bulk of bread (soaked in stock) in a mortar
6. Add the pounded meat of a lightly roasted chicken with its liver, and mix them thoroughly with two raw eggs
7. Season the mixture with spiced pepper, and dilute it with some of the gravy
8. When worked sufficiently, put it into a border mould, and steam it gently
9. For the gravy: place a sauce-pan on the fire, with a table-spoonful of butter at the bottom of it
10. Work into the melted butter a dessert-spoonful of flour, and then add your cool gravy, stirring all the time
11. Pour in a glass of portwine, a dessert-spoonful of vinegar or lime juice, and a dessert-spoonful of red currant jelly
12. Amalgamate the whole thoroughly, heat up the fillets and heads in it, and the mushrooms strained from their own gravy
13. Turn the turban out when done, garnish the top with the snipe heads and beaks, and with a fillet (heated separately in the gravy) between each head
14. Put the mushrooms inside the mould, pour the gravy over the whole composition, and serve piping hot
