## Ingredients

- pork from the fattest parts
- vinegar
- garlic bruised
- dry ginger powdered
- turmeric powdered
- peppercorns roasted and powdered
- coriander-seeds roasted and powdered
- cumin-seeds roasted and powdered
- dry salt
- mustard oil
- bay-leaves

## Directions

1. Select the fattest parts of pork; satisfy yourself that the meat is fresh and sound, and that it has not been washed with water in the butcher's shop.
2. Cut the meat into two-inch squares, wash thoroughly in vinegar (no water), rub over with the condiments, and then steep them in really good English vinegar for twenty-four hours.
3. Melt a large quantity of the best mustard oil in an earthen pot, and according to the quantity of meat, take additional condiments mentioned above, but in the proportion given in recipe No. 61; grind in vinegar, and fry in the oil.
4. Put in the meat, and all the vinegar, &c., in which it had been steeped, together with some more salt, a little more vinegar, a few bay-leaves and peppercorns, and allow to simmer until the meat is quite tender.
5. Remove from the fire and allow it to get quite cold; then put it into dry stone jars, with patent screw tops, well filled with plenty of the oil in which the vindaloo was cooked.
6. Take care that all the meat is well covered over with oil, which latter ought to be at least from two to three inches above the meat in the jar.
7. Screw down the lid, and cover it over with a good sound bladder to render it perfectly air-tight.
8. When required for use, take out only as much as will suffice, and simply warm it in a little of its own gravy.
