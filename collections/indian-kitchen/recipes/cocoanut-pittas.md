## Ingredients

- one cocoanut, finely scraped
- jagree
- a few grains of black cardamom seed
- finely-sifted rice-flour
- boiling-hot water

## Directions

1. Scrape finely a cocoanut, brown it with some jagree and a few grains of the black cardamom seed, and set aside
2. Prepare a pastry of finely-sifted rice-flour, kneaded with boiling-hot water
3. Take as much as the size of a duck's egg, and press it out flat in the palm of your hand to the size of a large saucer
4. Put a tablespoonful of the fried cocoanut into it, and close it up in a half-moon shape, with the help of a little water
5. Have a wide-mouthed large earthen pot of boiling water; stretch and tie over its mouth a napkin, and steam the pittas or cakes over them
6. They will be ready in half an hour, and may be eaten hot or cold
