## Ingredients

- yolks of four hard-boiled eggs
- half a teaspoonful of pepper
- one teaspoonful of salt
- one tablespoonful of sugar
- two to three teaspoonfuls of prepared mustard
- four tablespoonfuls of oil
- a dessertspoonful of Lee and Perrin's Worcestershire sauce
- one tablespoonful and a half of white wine vinegar
- a dessertspoonful of tarragon vinegar

## Directions

1. Bruise down when quite cold the yolks of four hard-boiled eggs
2. Rub into them half a teaspoonful of pepper, one teaspoonful of salt and one tablespoonful of sugar, with two to three teaspoonfuls of prepared mustard
3. When well rubbed together, add very gradually four tablespoonfuls of oil, stirring it the whole while
4. When well mixed add a dessertspoonful of Lee and Perrin's Worcestershire sauce
5. Add one tablespoonful and a half of white wine vinegar and a dessertspoonful of tarragon vinegar
6. If a thicker sauce is required, take either a larger number of eggs or a teaspoonful or dessertspoonful of corn or other flour
7. Put the flour into a cup and pour over it the quantity of vinegar prescribed
8. Place the cup in a saucepan of boiling water over the fire and stir until the vinegar thickens to the desired consistency
9. Mix it gradually into the preparation of eggs, oil, &c.
