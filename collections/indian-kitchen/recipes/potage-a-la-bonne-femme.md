## Ingredients

- one quart of stock
- one good-sized (Bombay) onion
- two ounces of good tinned butter
- a quarter of a pound of sorrel leaves
- a lettuce
- a bunch of parsley
- pepper
- salt
- half an ounce of flour
- a dessert-spoonful of pounded loaf-sugar
- half a cupful of the stock, freed from fat, and not coloured
- bread
- crust along one side

## Directions

1. Prepare a quart of stock and keep by your side
2. Cut up a good-sized (Bombay) onion into very thin rounds
3. Place the onion in a sauce-pan with two ounces of good tinned butter
4. Take care not to let the onion get brown
5. When the onion is half done, throw in a quarter of a pound of sorrel leaves, a lettuce, and a bunch of parsley, all finely shred
6. Add pepper, salt, and half an ounce of flour
7. Keep stirring for five minutes
8. Add a dessert-spoonful of pounded loaf-sugar and half a cupful of the stock, freed from fat, and not coloured
9. Let the mixture reduce nearly to a glaze
10. Gradually stir in about a quart of the stock
11. Let the soup simmer for a quarter of an hour
12. Prepare about a dozen pieces of bread cut very thin about two inches long, and an inch wide, taking care that there is crust along one of their long sides
13. Dry these bread pieces thoroughly in the oven
14. When it is time to send up the soup, remove the superfluous fat from it
15. Set it to simmer for a quarter of an hour
