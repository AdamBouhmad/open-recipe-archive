## Ingredients

- a well fed fowl
- buttered paper
- clear stock
- a glass of sherry
- vegetables, especially celery
- a bag of savoury herbs
- lime peel
- a pinch of sugar
- six pounded sweet almonds
- one bitter almond
- pellets of tongue
- sweetbreads
- button mushrooms
- oysters
- a few slices of truffle
- the liver of the fowl
- grated lean bacon or ham
- a table-spoonful of cream or the yolks of two raw eggs

## Directions

1. Slightly roast a well fed fowl, covering the breast with buttered paper to prevent its burning
2. When done, put it on a dish, and let it get cold
3. Carve it neatly, cutting the white meat off in nice little round fillets, and saving the wings, merry-thought, &c.
4. Take the bones, skin, giblets, trimmings, and coarser meat of the drumsticks, and beat them well with your pestle
5. Throw them into a sauce-pan, with as much clear stock as will cover them, a glass of sherry, and the usual proportion of vegetables, especially celery, a bag of savoury herbs, lime peel, and a pinch of sugar
6. Make an excellent fowl consomme, adding six pounded sweet and one bitter almond
7. When quite ready, strain it off, and thicken it as for veloute
8. Put into the white sauce all your pieces of fowl, with some pellets of tongue cut like gun wads rather thickly, some sweetbreads, some button mushrooms, a few oysters trimmed free from their beards, a few slices of truffle, the liver of the fowl minced, and some grated lean bacon or ham
9. Gently heat up the blanquette, and let it simmer for half an hour over a low fire
10. Add a table-spoonful of cream, or the yolks of two raw eggs beaten in a cup with a little of the sauce, and stir well in as a finishing touch
