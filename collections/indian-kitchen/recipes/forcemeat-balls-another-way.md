## Ingredients

- half a pound of beef or veal
- half a pound of udder
- finely-grated crumbs of a slice of stale bread
- a tablespoonful of finely-chopped parsley
- milk or gravy
- a teaspoonful of finely-pounded pepper
- a teaspoonful of salt
- whites and yolks of two raw eggs

## Directions

1. Mince and pound beef or veal and udder to a pulp in a mortar
2. Remove all gristle and parts not pulped
3. Mix with it the finely-grated crumbs of a slice of stale bread and a tablespoonful of finely-chopped parsley
4. Soften down the whole with some milk or gravy
5. Add a teaspoonful of finely-pounded pepper and a teaspoonful of salt
6. Rub down the whole well together
7. Add the whites and yolks of two raw eggs, well beaten up
8. Make into balls, the size of small nutmegs for soup, or the size of large walnuts or ordinary croquets or rissoles for garnishing made dishes
