## Ingredients

- 4 pounds soup-meat (3 pounds flesh to 1 pound bone)
- 2 ounces salt
- Water
- A couple of large white onions
- A clove of garlic
- Two large or three small carrots
- Two or three turnips
- Six leeks
- One head of celery
- A bunch of curly parsley
- Two cloves
- Thyme
- Marjoram
- A handful of whole pepper
- A dessert-spoonful of sugar
- A table-spoonful of Harvey sauce
- Two table-spoonfuls of mushroom ketchup

## Directions

1. Put a piece of soup-meat of four pounds weight, in the proportion of three pounds of flesh to one of bone, tightly bound with a string, with two ounces of salt and the bones separately broken up, into a stock pot filled with water, so as to completely cover the meat.
2. Put the pot by the side of the fire and let it become gradually heated.
3. As this takes place, a scum will form upon the surface which must be carefully removed as it rises.
4. When nearly boiling, a coffee cupful of cold water should be thrown into the pot to accelerate the rising of the scum.
5. Keep the water from boiling point until all the scum is removed.
6. Put into the saucepan the following vegetables which should have been previously carefully cleaned and cut up: a couple of large white onions, a clove of garlic, two large or three small carrots, two or three turnips, six leeks, one head of celery, a bunch of curly parsley, and two cloves in the onion.
7. Put in, tied up in a piece of muslin, some thyme, marjoram, and a handful of whole pepper (a tea-spoonful each of dried thyme and marjoram will be quite enough).
8. Watch the vegetables carefully, and remove them when they are done.
9. Remove the muslin bag also.
10. Put in a dessert-spoonful of sugar, a table-spoonful of Harvey sauce, and two of mushroom ketchup.
11. Let it simmer slowly from three to four hours.
12. Strain the soup into a basin.
