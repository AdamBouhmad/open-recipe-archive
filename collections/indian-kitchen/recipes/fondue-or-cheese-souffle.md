## Ingredients

- one ounce of butter
- one table-spoonful of flour
- one breakfast-cupful of hot milk
- three ounces of grated Parmesan cheese
- one clove of garlic
- one tea-spoonful of mustard powder
- grated nutmeg
- black pepper
- salt
- yolks of four eggs
- one table-spoonful of milk
- whites of five eggs

## Directions

1. Melt an ounce of butter in an enamelled sauce-pan
2. Stir into it a table-spoonful of flour
3. When the two are well amalgamated, put in a breakfast-cupful of hot milk, and about three ounces of grated Parmesan cheese
4. Stir the mixture on a very slow fire till it assumes the appearance of thick cream, being careful that it does not become too hot or boil
5. Put in one clove of garlic, a tea-spoonful of mustard powder, a dash of grated nutmeg, and some black pepper
6. Mix thoroughly, and if upon tasting you find that it is required, add a little salt
7. Keep on stirring the mixture at a very moderate heat for quite ten minutes
8. Remove the garlic, take the sauce-pan from the fire, stirring occasionally till the contents are nearly cold
9. Add the yolks of four eggs previously beaten up with a table-spoonful of milk, and well strained
10. Mix well
11. Incorporate swiftly with the mixture the whites of five eggs beaten to a froth
12. Pour this into a deep round tin, and put it into the oven which must not be too hot
13. Bake for twenty to thirty minutes until the fondue is ready for the table
14. Send it quickly to the table in its tin, with a napkin folded round it
