## Ingredients

- a cut of seer-fish
- lime juice or vinegar
- pepper
- salt
- onion
- sweet herbs
- yolks of two eggs
- brandy
- salad oil
- water
- imported flour
- boiling fat
- fried parsley
- slices of lime
- sauce-Hollandaise

## Directions

1. Divide a cut of seer-fish into six nice collops about two inches long, half an inch thick
2. Marinate in a little lime juice or vinegar, pepper, salt, onion, and sweet herbs
3. Half fry, or bake them, and let them get cold
4. Beat up together the yolks of two eggs with one table-spoonful of brandy, one of salad oil, and four or five table-spoonfuls of water
5. Amalgamate with this three table-spoonfuls of imported flour, and a pinch of salt
6. Beat the mixture well for a minute or two, and bring it to the right consistency by adding water, or flour, as the case may require
7. Add, at the last moment, the whites whipped to a froth
8. Dip your collops into this, and lay them one by one in your frying basket, dipping it immediately into a deep saute-pan, filled with boiling fat
9. As soon as they turn a nice deep yellow, lift them out, drain them on a sheet of blotting paper
10. Serve them, crisp and dry on a napkin garnished with fried parsley, and slices of lime. With sauce-Hollandaise in a boat
