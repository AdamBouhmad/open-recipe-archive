## Ingredients

- a nice steak
- butter
- half a pint of water or broth
- one Bombay onion
- a good handful of French beans
- a plant of celery
- two carrots
- a table-spoonful of walnut ketchup
- a salt-spoonful of sugar
- a glass of Marsala
- pepper
- salt
- flour

## Directions

1. Choose a nice steak, fry it to begin with in butter till it turns a nice brown colour on both sides
2. Put it into your stew-pan, with half a pint of water (or broth), one Bombay onion cut into quarters, a good handful of French beans, a plant of celery and two carrots sliced, a table-spoonful of walnut ketchup, a salt-spoonful of sugar, a glass of Marsala, pepper, and salt
3. Cover the pan, and stew the steak ever so gently till the meat parts easily when tested by the fork
4. Lift it out, strain the gravy, place the steak on a hot silver dish, arrange the vegetables cut up into very small dice tastefully over it
5. Thicken the gravy with a little butter and flour, add a dessert-spoonful of Marsala, and pour it boiling hot over the steak
