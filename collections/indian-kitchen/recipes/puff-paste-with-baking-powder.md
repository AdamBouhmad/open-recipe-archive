## Ingredients

- 3 breakfast-cupfuls flour
- 2 cupfuls butter
- 1 heaped teaspoonful baking powder
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 teaspoonful salt
- iced water
- broken ice

## Directions

1. Choose a cool place to work in
2. Ensure the flour is good and dry and the butter is firm and free from moisture
3. Fill two shallow baking-tins with broken ice
4. Put the flour on a cool slab and mix into it a heaped teaspoonful of the baking powder
5. Form the flour in a ring and in the centre throw the yolk of an egg and a teaspoonful of salt
6. Add a little iced water and gradually work the flour into it from the inside of the ring, sprinkling additional water as required (about one breakfast-cupful altogether) until you have a smooth, fine dough free from all stickiness
7. Pat this into a lump and put it in the ice-box for a quarter of an hour
8. Roll it out about the size of a dinner plate
9. Put the butter upon it and wrap the edges of the dough over it, carefully covering it
10. Turn it upside down and roll it out very thin
11. Reverse it again and fold it in three
12. Place it on a baking-sheet over one of the pans of broken ice and put the other pan of ice upon it
13. Repeat this cooling process between each double turn
14. Use as soon as possible when five turns have been completed
