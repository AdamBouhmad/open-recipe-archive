## Ingredients

- rather more than three-quarters of a coonkee of bassmuttee or cheeneesuckur
- half a coonkee of dal
- twelve large curry onions
- two chittacks or four ounces of ghee
- a few slices of green ginger
- some peppercorns
- salt to taste (say one dessertspoonful)
- a few cloves
- three or four cardamoms

## Directions

1. Take rather more than three-quarters of a coonkee of bassmuttee or cheeneesuckur and half a coonkee of dal
2. Take twelve large curry onions and cut them up lengthways into fine slices
3. Warm up two chittacks or four ounces of ghee (but before doing so be careful to warm the pot)
4. While bubbling, throw in the sliced onions, removing them immediately they become of a bright brown colour
5. Set the fried onions aside, and throw in the dal and rice (having previously allowed all the water in which they were washed to drain through a colander)
6. Fry until the dal and rice have absorbed all the ghee
7. Add a few slices of green ginger, some peppercorns, salt to taste (say one dessertspoonful), a few cloves, three or four cardamoms
