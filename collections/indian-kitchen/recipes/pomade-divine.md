## Ingredients

- beef marrow, one pound and a half
- spring water
- rose-water, one pint
- flowers of benzoin, one ounce
- cyprus-root, one ounce
- odoriferous thorn, one ounce
- Florentine iris-root, one ounce
- cinnamon, half an ounce
- cloves, a quarter of an ounce
- nutmeg, a quarter of an ounce
- flour
- white of egg
- boiling water

## Directions

1. Clear beef marrow thoroughly from bone and fibre, and put it in an earthen vessel of spring water
2. Change the water every night and morning for eight or ten days
3. Steep the marrow in a pint of rose-water for twenty-four hours
4. Drain it dry through a linen cloth
5. Take an ounce each of flowers of benzoin, cyprus-root, odoriferous thorn, and Florentine iris-root, half an ounce of cinnamon, and a quarter of an ounce each of cloves and nutmeg
6. Pound all these very fine
7. Mix them well with the marrow
8. Put all into a pewter digester which holds three pints with closely fitted top
9. Spread on linen a paste made of flour and white of egg, and fix it over the top so that there can be no evaporation
10. Suspend the digester by the handles in the middle of a pot of boiling water
11. Keep it boiling, adding more boiling water as often as necessary
12. Strain the pomade into small wide-mouthed bottles
13. Cover it down when quite cold
