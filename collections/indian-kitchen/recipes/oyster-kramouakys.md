## Ingredients

- yolks of three eggs
- one tablespoonful of brandy
- one tablespoonful of olive oil
- three or four tablespoons of cold water
- three tablespoonfuls of flour
- a salt-spoonful of salt
- whites of three eggs
- about a dozen and a half oysters
- juice of three or four limes
- a sliced onion
- whole pepper
- a few cloves
- yolks of eggs
- thick white sauce
- six slices of bacon
- white of egg
- curly parsley

## Directions

1. Beat up the yolks only of three eggs with a tablespoonful of brandy, one of olive oil, and three or four of cold water
2. Mix into this three tablespoonfuls of flour and a salt-spoonful of salt
3. Beat this to a paste for ten minutes, preparing a rather thicker batter than you would for ordinary fritters
4. At the time of using, stir in the whites of the three eggs whipped to a stiff froth
5. Wash your oysters early in the day
6. Parboil them if fresh, beard them, and put them to marinade for two hours in the juice of three or four limes, a sliced onion, some whole pepper and a few cloves
7. Lift them out of this when wanted, cut them up, bind the mince together with a liaison of yolks of eggs and thick white sauce, and let it get cold and firm
8. Divide it into six portions, each the size of an ordinary wine cork, and place them on six slices of previously boiled bacon
9. Roll up your bacon fillets and fix them with white of egg
10. Dip them into the batter and lay them gently in your frying basket
11. Plunge this into a seething bath of fat and fry a golden yellow
12. Serve prettily garnished with curly parsley
