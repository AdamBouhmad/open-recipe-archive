## Ingredients

- beef collops
- boiled bacon
- oysters
- spiced pepper
- finely minced lime peel
- beef gravy
- oyster liquor from tin
- lime rind
- blade of mace
- glass of sherry
- tablespoonful of walnut or mushroom ketchup

## Directions

1. Follow the recipe for beef cutting and roll a nice piece of boiled bacon within each collop
2. Place at the bottom a layer of beef collops, then a layer of oysters drained from the tin liquor, beef again, oysters again, and so on, till the dish is packed
3. Season with spiced pepper and finely minced lime peel
4. Make the gravy thus: To about a pint of good beef gravy, add the liquor strained from the tin of oysters, the rind of a lime, a blade of mace, a glass of sherry and a tablespoonful of walnut or mushroom ketchup
5. Heat up gently, skimming off the scum which the oyster liquor may throw up
6. When thoroughly blended, strain the gravy off, and pour it into your packed pie-dish, reserving about a coffee-cupful to pour through the top of the crust at the end of the baking
