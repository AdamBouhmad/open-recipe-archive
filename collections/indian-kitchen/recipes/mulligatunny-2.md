## Ingredients

- large sweet onion
- two ounces good butter
- three table-spoonfuls Barrie's Madras mulligatunny paste
- veal stock
- table-spoonful red currant jelly
- few drops lemon juice
- dessert-spoonful Madras chutney
- four ounces sweet almonds
- milk
- two ounces butter
- two ounces flour
- coffee-cupful cream

## Directions

1. Cut up a large sweet onion into fine rings, and fry them in two ounces of good butter, till about to turn yellow
2. Stir in three table-spoonfuls of Barrie's Madras mulligatunny paste, adding sufficient stock to bring the mixture to the consistency of mayonnaise sauce
3. Taste the mixture, and if it requires a little sub-acid, administer a table-spoonful of red currant jelly and a few drops of lemon juice
4. Stir in well a dessert-spoonful of Madras chutney, and add stock enough to produce a thin soup—about three pints in all
5. Allow to simmer for a quarter of an hour to extract the flavours of the various ingredients
6. Pound four ounces of sweet almonds in a mortar with a little milk, using a breakfast-cupful altogether
7. Strain the almond milk into the soup, and stop the simmering
8. Pass the whole of the liquid through a tin strainer into a clean bowl to catch up lumps of onion, chutney, &c.
9. Skim the mulligatunny
10. Thicken with two ounces of butter and two of flour
11. Bring the soup to boiling-point
12. Just before serving, off the fire, stir a coffee-cupful of the best cream into the tureen as the soup is poured into it
