## Ingredients

- breast of mutton
- white wine
- water
- onion
- tomatoes
- carrots
- celery
- whole pepper
- salt
- garlic
- parsley
- dried thyme
- egg
- stale bread-crumbs
- fat
- spinach
- hard-boiled eggs
- butter
- flour
- caramel colouring
- sherry
- red currant jelly
- cornichons or gherkins

## Directions

1. Braise a breast of mutton in a stew-pan with some white wine and water, an onion, two tomatoes, carrots, celery, whole pepper, salt, a clove of garlic, some parsley, and a tea-spoonful of dried thyme
2. When sufficiently done to enable you to remove the bones, draw the pan from the fire and take out the breast
3. Pick out the bones and place it flat on a dish with a heavy weight upon it
4. Strain the broth and vegetables in which the meat was cooked, putting the vegetables aside and setting the gravy to cool
5. When the breast has become thoroughly cold, remove the weight and divide the meat into eight nice collops
6. Brush them over with egg and bread-crumb them with some very finely sifted stale bread-crumbs crisped in the oven
7. Let them stand for an hour and repeat the process, recrumbing them again
8. Fry them in boiling fat a nice golden yellow, take them out and drain them dry
9. Arrange them round a ring of spinach garnished with hard-boiled eggs neatly cut up and fried parsley
10. For the sauce: skim every atom of fat from the gravy, put a lump of butter in a sauce-pan and work a little flour into it when melted
11. Gradually add your gravy and stir well and let the sauce thicken
12. Add some caramel colouring, a dessert-spoonful of sherry, and a tea-spoonful of red currant jelly
13. Put this into a hot sauce boat and serve
14. A few dice of sliced cornichons or minced mushrooms may be mingled with the sauce
