## Ingredients

- a dozen mushrooms
- an ounce of butter
- half an ounce of flour
- half a pint of made gravy
- chopped stalks from mushrooms
- a table-spoonful of chopped curled parsley
- a tea-spoonful of minced onion
- a salt-spoonful of salt
- pepper
- a spoonful of cream

## Directions

1. First, be careful in cleaning them; cut the stalks down, leaving only a little of them within the hollow of the mushroom
2. Peel off the skin that covers the convex side of the fungus
3. Brush away any particles of earth or sand that may adhere to the pink gills on the concave side, and by patting the top of each mushroom, shake out all grit
4. When satisfactorily prepared, clean the stalks you cut off, and chop them up
5. Put into a sauce-pan an ounce of butter and stir into it half an ounce of flour
6. When mixed, add half a pint of made gravy
7. Stir well and throw in the chopped stalks, a table-spoonful of chopped curled parsley, a tea-spoonful of minced onion, a salt-spoonful of salt, and a dusting of pepper
8. Simmer the sauce until it is thick and rich
9. Add a spoonful of cream, and then strain it
10. Butter a silver dish liberally, place the mushrooms upon it head downwards
11. Fill their hollow parts with the thick sauce
12. Set the dish in the oven (a brisk one) for ten minutes
13. As soon as the mushrooms flatten themselves, they are done
14. Serve on the same dish wrapping a napkin round it
