## Ingredients

- endive leaves
- salt
- consomme
- rich béchamel
- butter
- cooked chicken

## Directions

1. Trim endives by picking off the outside leaves, and cutting off the green tips
2. Sever the heads by a cut across the stalk, detaching all the leaves therefrom
3. Examine and clean every leaf
4. Drain leaves dry
5. Cast them into a roomy vessel full of boiling water with half an ounce of salt
6. Boil for twenty-five minutes
7. When tender, drain and press out the water
8. Chop fine on a clean board
9. Simmer the endive leaves in consomme for two hours
10. Increase the fire, stirring till the leaves have absorbed the broth
11. Moisten them with some rich béchamel and a pat of butter
12. Use as foundation for a blanquette à la Talleyrand with neat pieces of cooked chicken heated up in the endive sauce
