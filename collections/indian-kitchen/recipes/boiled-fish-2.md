## Ingredients

- fish
- salt
- vinegar
- water

## Directions

1. After having thoroughly cleansed and wiped the fish, rub it over with a little vinegar, and place it on the drainer of the fish-kettle, so that when done it may be lifted out without risk of breaking up.
2. Put plenty of salt, and a dessert-spoonful of vinegar, into the water in which the fish is to be boiled.
3. Let the water be cold, and in sufficient quantity to cover the fish.
4. Place the kettle on a brisk fire, and boil the fish as fast as possible.
5. Skim off all scum that rises, and take care to suspend operations, the moment the fish is done.
6. Allow ten minutes per pound as a fair average of the time required for this operation; but the cook should test it now and then with the point of a skewer, and as soon as the flesh parts easily from the bone, let him decide that it is ready.
7. Never let your fish, after it is done, remain soaking in the water in which it has been cooked; drain it at once.
8. If ready too soon, let it rest on the drainer over the hot kettle, and cover it with a napkin.
9. Be very particular to drain every drop of water from the fish before you serve it.
