## Ingredients

- a good fat tender goose
- a dessertspoonful each of mustard, sweet oil, and mixed sauces
- bones and giblet of the goose (liver excepted)
- pepper
- salt
- soup herbs
- bay-leaves
- three pounds of beef
- a quarter of a pound of beef suet
- a quarter of a pound of fat bacon
- the liver of the goose
- a tablespoonful of chopped garden herbs
- a dessertspoonful of powdered black pepper
- a dessertspoonful of mixed hot spices finely powdered
- two tablespoonfuls of finely-grated bread-crumbs
- a dessertspoonful of salt
- one teaspoonful of essence of anchovies
- two chittacks and a half or five ounces of ghee
- boiled turnips
- carrots
- onions
- potatoes
- English pickles

## Directions

1. Take a good fat tender goose; feather, clean, and bone it carefully without destroying the skin.
2. When every bone has been removed, pour into the goose a mixture composed of a dessertspoonful each of mustard, sweet oil, and mixed sauces.
3. Take all the bones and the giblet, the liver excepted, and make a good gravy seasoned with pepper, salt, soup herbs, and bay-leaves.
4. Mince very fine three pounds of beef, a quarter of a pound of beef suet, a quarter of a pound of fat bacon, and the liver of the goose.
5. Take of chopped garden herbs a tablespoonful, powdered black pepper a dessertspoonful, mixed hot spices finely powdered a dessertspoonful, finely-grated bread-crumbs two tablespoonfuls, salt a dessertspoonful, and essence of anchovies, if liked, one teaspoonful.
6. Mix the above well together, and stuff the goose.
7. Melt two chittacks and a half or five ounces of ghee.
8. Put in the goose, and pour over it the soup made of the bones and giblet, and allow it to stew until quite tender.
9. Then glace the goose, as also some boiled turnips, carrots, onions, and potatoes, and serve up hot, surrounded with the vegetables and some English pickles.
