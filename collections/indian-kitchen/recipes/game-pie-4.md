## Ingredients

- snipe, quails, partridges, hares, pigeons, teal, wild duck
- tender beef or good mutton collops
- slices of bacon, ham, or Bologna sausage
- game livers
- truffles
- one pound and a half of gravy beef
- mashed game bones
- bacon or ham bone or slices
- two sheep's feet
- vegetables for soup-making
- bouquet of sweet herbs
- clove of garlic
- mixed spice
- pepper corns
- caramel
- salt
- one glass of Madeira or fruity sherry
- teaspoonful of reduced vinegar
- one table-spoonful of red currant jelly
- half a wine-glass of mushroom ketchup
- flour
- spiced pepper
- shallot
- paste

## Directions

1. Make a forcemeat from the livers of all the game, prepared as foie-gras forcemeat with truffles
2. Bone all the game, mashing the bones with a pestle
3. Make a rich gravy from the gravy beef, mashed game bones, bacon or ham bone, two sheep's feet, soup vegetables, sweet herbs, garlic, mixed spice, pepper corns, and caramel
4. Strain the broth and add one glass of Madeira or fruity sherry, one teaspoonful of reduced vinegar, one table-spoonful of red currant jelly, and half a wine-glass of mushroom ketchup
5. Boil the gravy again and set it to cool
6. Carefully bone the game and cut into neat pieces if large
7. Cast skin, scraps, and trimmings into the stock-pot
8. Arrange the trimmed meat on a large flat dish, dredge with flour, and dust with spiced pepper
9. Rub the pie-dish well with a shallot
10. Spread a thin coating of forcemeat over the bottom and up the sides of the pie-dish
11. Place a good layer of tender beef or mutton collops, each rolled round a slice of bacon, over the forcemeat
12. Pack the game meat with slices of ham, bacon, or Bologna sausage dotted in and frequent dustings of spiced pepper until the dish is filled
13. Pour in the gravy
14. Cover with a nice light paste and bake
