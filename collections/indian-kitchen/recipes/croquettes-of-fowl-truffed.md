## Ingredients

- a fair sized fowl
- buttered paper
- stock
- two onions
- a clove of garlic
- a carrot
- sweet herbs
- pepper
- salt
- half a glass of sherry
- butter
- flour
- the yolks of two eggs
- the juice of a lime

## Directions

1. Roast a fair sized fowl, protecting the breast from burning by a buttered paper
2. When partly done, carve the bird carefully, picking on all the white meat you can
3. Remove the skin from these pieces and put them aside
4. Break up the carcass, and throw it, with all fragments of skin and bone, and the giblets of the fowl previously saved, into a sauce-pan, with as much stock as will cover the whole
5. Add two onions, a clove of garlic, a carrot, sweet herbs, pepper, and salt, and simmer the contents of your pan as long as you can, finally adding half a glass of sherry, and bringing the broth to the boil
6. Strain it, remove all fat that may rise, and reduce the sauce a little
7. Thicken it with butter and flour, take it off the fire, and stir in the yolks of two eggs beaten up with the juice of a lime
