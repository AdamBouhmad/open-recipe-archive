## Ingredients

- potatoes of a good mealy kind
- spring water

## Directions

1. Choose some potatoes of a good mealy kind, peel and wash them (raw)
2. Grate them to pulp with a strong bread grater, emptying the grated stuff into a large bowl of clean spring water
3. Stir the potato and water together briskly for a few minutes, and then let the former settle
4. After resting ten minutes, strain off the water
5. Repeat the process with another basinful of water, and let it rest after stirring for ten minutes, again straining off the water
6. Continue the washings until the water remains quite clear after the sediment has settled at the bottom of the bowl
7. When satisfied that the grated potato has been thoroughly cleansed, take it out of the bowl, drain it
8. Spread it out upon dishes to dry and bleach in the sun, turning it frequently
9. When quite dry, pound it in a mortar, and pass the flour so obtained through a silk or hair sieve
10. Bottle it securely in dry bottles, and cork it down tightly
