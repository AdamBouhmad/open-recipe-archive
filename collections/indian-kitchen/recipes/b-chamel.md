## Ingredients

- an onion
- an ounce of melted butter
- meat and bones
- water
- a tea-spoonful of powdered sugar
- vegetables
- a quarter of a pound of preserved butter
- a sliced carrot
- a sliced onion
- two ounces of flour
- clear broth
- half a pint of cream
- a small tinful of mushrooms chopped
- salt
- pepper
- a gill of cream

## Directions

1. Slice up the onion, and shake the rings in an ounce of melted butter at the bottom of the stew-pan
2. Do not let them take colour, but add your meat and bones at once
3. Cover them with water, omitting the browning stage
4. Make a clear consommé, adding a tea-spoonful of powdered sugar plain, and all the vegetables
5. Carefully prepare and skim to obtain a pellucid broth
6. Strain and keep ready for use
7. Take a sauce-pan, and melt a quarter of a pound of preserved butter in it
8. Fry gently in that for ten minutes a sliced carrot and a sliced onion
9. Before they take colour, add two ounces of flour
10. Stir for five minutes, and add by degrees your clear broth
11. Add half a pint of cream, a small tinful of mushrooms chopped, salt, and pepper
12. Stir over the fire till boiling
13. Permit the sauce to simmer slowly for an hour, taking off all fat that may rise
14. At the end of the hour, strain the Béchamel into a sauce-pan, and place it in the bain-marie
15. Before using, a gill of cream may be finally stirred into the sauce as you take it from the bain-marie
