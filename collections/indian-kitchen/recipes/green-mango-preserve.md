## Ingredients

- mangoes slightly under the middling size
- clean water
- lime-water
- good syrup (two pounds of sugar to every twenty-five mangoes)

## Directions

1. Select mangoes slightly under the middling size, taking care that they are not bruised or injured in any way
2. Steep them in clean water
3. Grate the outer coat, or peel very finely, so as to remove thoroughly a fine coat of green from the surface
4. Cut them sufficiently lengthways to extract the stones
5. Throw them into lime-water
6. Remove them into a copper preserving-pan with clean water
7. Parboil them, skimming them well
8. Throw them into a sieve, and allow all the water to drain away
9. Have a large quantity of good syrup prepared, allowing two pounds of sugar to every twenty-five mangoes
10. Throw the mangoes into the syrup, and allow them to simmer
11. Cut away the scum until the sugar inclines to crystallize
12. Remove the pan from the fire, and put the preserve into wide-mouthed bottles
13. Before corking them down, examine the syrup every two or three days, and if it is becoming thin, reboil it
14. Just as the boiling is about to be finished, put the mangoes into it to warm up
15. Repeat this precaution every time the syrup has been reboiled, until there is no further appearance of fermentation
16. Then securely cork down the bottles
