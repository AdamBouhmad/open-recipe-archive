## Ingredients

- a fine pomfret
- egg
- pepper
- salt
- finely-chopped parsley
- minced mushroom
- butter
- minced onion
- chablis or light white wine
- a cupful of broth
- veloute
- liquor of a tin of oysters
- broth made from the bones and trimmings of the pomfret
- oysters
- black Leicestershire mushrooms
- croutons of bread

## Directions

1. Clean and trim a fine pomfret
2. Draw off the dark skin and detach the flesh from the bone with a sharp knife
3. Take the two sides obtained and season them on their respective inner sides with a well beaten egg, pepper, salt, a little finely-chopped parsley, and some minced mushroom
4. Lay them together again so the fish resumes its former appearance
5. Butter a flat silver dish or one that will stand the fire
6. Strew over the butter some minced onion
7. Place the fish thereon
8. Moisten it with a little chablis or a light white wine of that class and a cupful of broth
9. Bake it in the oven
10. Whilst baking, make a good veloute in which you should pour the liquor of a tin of oysters, and use the broth made from the bones and trimmings of the pomfret
11. When the fish is nearly done, take it out of the oven
12. Pour the liquor from its dish into your veloute
13. Garnish the fish with the oysters of the tin previously mentioned and some black Leicestershire mushrooms
14. Pour your veloute over all
15. Set the dish in the oven again for five minutes
16. Serve with croutons of bread, buttered on each side and coloured a pale brown in the oven
