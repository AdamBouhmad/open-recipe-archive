## Ingredients

- a good full-sized fowl
- water
- a Bombay onion
- fresh vegetables
- a bag of mixed sweet herbs
- a clove of garlic
- a dozen pepper corns
- a pinch of parsley seed
- a few drops of celery essence
- a table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup
- a tea-spoonful of sugar
- a dessert-spoonful of salt
- caramel
- a dessert-spoonful of Marsala
- a dissolved dessert-spoonful of Julienne

## Directions

1. Kill a good full-sized fowl
2. cut it up
3. put it, giblets and all, into a stew-pan
4. cover it with water
5. let it come very slowly to the boil
6. skim off the scum which may rise during that process
7. when the boiling stage has been attained, take the pan from the fire for a minute
8. throw into it a Bombay onion, cut into quarters
9. add any fresh vegetables you may have brought out
10. add a bag of mixed sweet herbs, a clove of garlic, a dozen pepper corns, a pinch of parsley seed, a few drops of celery essence, a table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup, a tea-spoonful of sugar, and a dessert-spoonful of salt
11. let the pan boil again till the onion is soft
12. reduce the fire for the simmering stage
13. When the pieces of fowl are nice and tender, the broth is ready
14. lift up your pan and strain off the broth into a bowl
15. tint slightly with caramel and serve hot with a dessert-spoonful of Marsala and a dissolved dessert-spoonful of Julienne
