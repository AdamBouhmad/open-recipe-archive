## Ingredients

- one pound and a half of cleaned and picked raisins
- half a pound of currants
- three-quarters of a pound of finely-grated bread-crumbs
- half a pound of finely-sliced mixed peel
- three-quarters of a pound of finely-minced suet
- three-quarters of a pound of sugar
- half a teaspoonful of finely-powdered mixed spices (cinnamon, nutmeg, and mace)
- half a pound of butter free of water
- twelve eggs well beaten
- a wineglassful of brandy
- flour
- brandy sauce

## Directions

1. Mix cleaned and picked raisins, currants, finely-grated bread-crumbs, finely-sliced mixed peel, finely-minced suet, and sugar well together
2. Mix with half a teaspoonful of finely-powdered mixed spices (cinnamon, nutmeg, and mace)
3. Moisten the mixture with half a pound of butter free of water, twelve eggs well beaten, and a wineglassful of brandy, stirring well the whole time
4. Butter a large piece of cloth or napkin and dredge it well with flour
5. Put the mixture into it and tie it down tightly
6. Boil it steadily for seven hours
7. Take it out of the boiler and hang it up immediately until the day it is intended to be eaten
8. Boil again for fully two hours, ensuring the water is boiling before the pudding is put into it
9. Turn it out of the towel and serve up with brandy sauce
