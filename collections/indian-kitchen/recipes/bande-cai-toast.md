## Ingredients

- bande-cai pods
- butter
- white pepper
- salt
- anchovy sauce
- bread
- cream
- egg yolk
- Parmesan cheese
- Nepaul pepper

## Directions

1. Boil the bande-cais, and when cold, scrape out the seeds and pulp from each pod into a small basin, using a silver spoon for the operation.
2. Give the pulp a dusting of white pepper, and salt to taste, with a few drops of anchovy sauce.
3. Fry rounds, or slices of bread, according to the number you want, in butter, and set them to keep crisp and hot in the oven.
4. Take a small sauce-pan, place it in the bain-marie, or over a very moderate fire; melt a dessert-spoonful of butter in it.
5. Stir into it the bande-cai pulp, and two good table-spoonfuls of cream with the yolk of one egg.
6. Continue stirring one way until the contents of your sauce-pan look nice and thick, and steaming hot.
7. Pour the mixture over the toasts, and serve.
8. Dust grated Parmesan cheese over the surface of the toasts as an embellishment, and serve with Nepaul pepper handed round.
