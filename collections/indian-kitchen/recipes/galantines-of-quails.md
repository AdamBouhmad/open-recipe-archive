## Ingredients

- boned quails
- spiced pepper
- fat bacon
- lamb's liver
- birds' livers
- onion
- salt
- pepper
- grated nutmeg
- parsley
- dessert-spoonful of spiced pepper
- grated lime peel
- chopped truffles
- cold sheep's tongues
- red currant jelly
- glass of sherry
- half a wine-glass of vinegar

## Directions

1. Trim cold sheep's tongues and cut eight nice fillets the size of a walnut from the centre of them
2. Place boned birds breast downwards on a board and dust them over with spiced pepper
3. Melt a couple of thick slices of fat bacon in a frying-pan
4. When melted, throw in three table-spoonfuls of the lamb's liver with those of the birds too, cut into dice, and one onion shredded finely
5. Cook the liver in this, and when done, empty the contents of the pan, melted bacon and all, into a mortar
6. Throw in the remains of the sheep's tongues which were left after making the fillets, a pinch of salt, pepper, and grated nutmeg
7. Work this to a paste and pass it through the sieve
8. Add some finely minced parsley, a dessert-spoonful of spiced pepper, a little grated lime peel, and a table-spoonful of chopped truffles
9. Work this again thoroughly and spread a layer of it over the flattened quails
10. Place one of the tongue fillets in the centre of each quail and spread another layer of forcemeat over each
11. Gather the birds into shape neatly, sewing the skin together securely
12. Stew the little galantines in the previously made gravy, with a little red currant jelly, a glass of sherry, and half a wine-glass of vinegar
13. When done, lift them out and drain off the gravy
14. Reduce the gravy to a glaze
15. Paint the galantines over with the glaze when cold
16. Arrange them upon a dish which should be set upon ice
17. Garnish the dish with parsley and slices of lime
18. Serve with sauce tartare, also iced
