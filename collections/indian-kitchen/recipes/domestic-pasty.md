## Ingredients

- saddle of mutton
- cold-boiled pickled pork, about a pound
- gravy beef, about a pound
- chicken
- six shallots
- a dozen pepper corns
- peel of a lime
- two carrots
- bunch of parsley
- coarse stalk of celery with its leaves
- table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup
- bouquet of sweet herbs
- warm water
- anchovies, a couple
- hard-boiled eggs
- spiced pepper
- thyme and marjoram, about a tea-spoonful in all

## Directions

1. Cut as many slices as possible from the meat that remained untouched at the tail end of the saddle, trimming each slice free from burnt skin and laying upon a separate plate until about a pound and a half of slices are obtained
2. Cut off all remnants of good lean that still adhered to the bones and put them into a bowl
3. Break the bones and cast into a large stew-pan with every atom of skin, fat, gristle that can be found left in the dish after the trimming operation
4. Into the stew-pan with the mutton bones and scraps, throw six shallots, a dozen pepper corns, the peel of a lime, two carrots cut up, a bunch of parsley, a coarse stalk of celery with its leaves, a table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup, a bouquet of sweet herbs, the pound of gravy beef cut up, and all the trimmings of the chicken - neck, legs, feet, pinions and giblets
5. Cover with warm water and simmer gently for about three hours until the ingredients produce a pint and a half of excellent broth
6. Strain off the broth and set to cool
7. Pound the lean remnants which had been saved in the bowl with a couple of anchovies in the mortar and pass through the sieve
8. When the broth is quite cold, skim off the fat and make a regular puree with it and the pounded mutton
9. Pack the pie-dish by first sprinkling finely minced parsley, then a double layer of sliced mutton, over that a layer of sliced lean pork, another of mutton, and so on alternately until there is just enough space left to accommodate the joints of the raw chicken
10. Neatly dispose the chicken joints on the top with little bits of lean pork dotted in between them
11. Garnish the surface with hard-boiled eggs cut into quarters
12. Sprinkle a little spiced pepper over the layers of meat and some very finely chopped thyme and marjoram, about a tea-spoonful in all
13. Patiently pour the puree over everything, giving time for the liquid to settle in and amongst the contents of the pie-dish until the gravy comes within an inch of the top
14. Shake minced small parsley over the surface as a last touch
15. Make the paste and cover in the pasty
16. Bake the pie and at the end of baking, gently pour some of the puree which had been saved for the purpose into the pie through the vent in the centre of the crust
17. Place an ornamental flower cut in paste over the aperture
18. Glaze the crust
