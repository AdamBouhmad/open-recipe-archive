## Ingredients

- very-hard boiled yolks of two eggs
- tea-spoonful of powdered mustard
- scant salt-spoonful of salt
- pinch of sugar
- tea-spoonful of minced shallot
- salad oil
- raw yolks of three eggs
- dessert-spoonful of tarragon or other aromatic vinegar

## Directions

1. Put the very-hard boiled yolks of two eggs into a slop basin, with a tea-spoonful of powdered mustard, a scant salt-spoonful of salt, a pinch of sugar, and a tea-spoonful of minced shallot.
2. Bruise these with a wooden or silver spoon, and work them to a paste with a little salad oil.
3. Add oil by degrees till your paste is about the consistency of batter, then toss into it one by one the raw yolks of three eggs, continue the working, and add oil, till you have a nice rich sauce coating the spoon pretty thickly.
4. Dole out a dessert-spoonful of tarragon or other aromatic vinegar, and mix it thoroughly with the other ingredients.
5. Taste your sauce by dipping a leaf of lettuce into a spoonful of it, and finish it off, as regards further addition of oil or vinegar, according to discretion.
6. Strain it through the little block tin strainer to get rid of every lump, and the little bits of onion.
7. Put the sauce-boat into the ice box as soon as it is filled.
