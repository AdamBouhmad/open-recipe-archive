## Ingredients

- neck cutlets
- melted butter or salad oil
- two or three fair sized Bombay onions
- a ladleful of butter
- a pinch of sugar
- pepper and salt
- a table-spoonful of boiled rice or pearl barley
- a cupful of broth
- a heaped up table-spoonful of finely grated cheese
- a table-spoonful of rich cream
- savoury rice
- French beans a la maitre d'hotel

## Directions

1. Trim the neck cutlets neatly.
2. Dip each cutlet into melted butter, or brush over with salad oil before placing on the grid-iron.
3. Grill over a very fast fire.
4. For the sauce: Parboil two or three fair sized Bombay onions, cut them up roughly and put them into a sauce-pan with a ladleful of butter, a pinch of sugar, and pepper and salt to fancy.
5. Let them cook slowly so that they do not take colour.
6. Add a table-spoonful of boiled rice or pearl barley, and a cupful of broth and let the simmering go on till the lumps of onion are quite soft.
7. Add a heaped up table-spoonful of finely grated cheese, stir this in well for a minute or two.
8. Work the mixture through the sieve as you would a puree.
9. Heat it up gently in the bain-marie.
10. At the last stir in a table-spoonful of rich cream.
11. Serve the cutlets round a wall of savoury rice and fill in the centre with French beans a la maitre d'hotel.
12. Let the sauce go round in a boat with the entree.
