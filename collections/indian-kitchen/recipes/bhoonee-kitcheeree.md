## Ingredients

- twelve large curry onions
- two chittacks or four ounces of ghee
- dal
- rice
- a few slices of green ginger
- peppercorns
- salt to taste (say one dessertspoonful)
- a few cloves
- three or four cardamoms
- half a dozen bay-leaves
- small sticks of cinnamon
- water

## Directions

1. Cut the twelve large curry onions lengthways into fine slices
2. Warm the pot carefully
3. Warm up two chittacks or four ounces of ghee
4. While bubbling, throw in the sliced onions, removing them immediately they become of a bright brown colour
5. Set the fried onions aside
6. Throw in the dal and rice (having previously allowed all the water in which they were washed to drain through a colander)
7. Fry until the dal and rice have absorbed all the ghee
8. Add a few slices of green ginger, some peppercorns, salt to taste (say one dessertspoonful), a few cloves, three or four cardamoms, half a dozen bay-leaves, and as many small sticks of cinnamon
9. Mix well together
10. Add as much water only as will entirely cover over the whole of the rice and dal
11. Put a good-fitting cover on, and set over a slow fire, reducing the same from time to time as the water is being absorbed
12. Occasionally shake the pot, or stir its contents with a wooden spoon to prevent the kitcheeree from burning
13. Serve up quite hot, strewing over it the fried onions
