## Ingredients

- dry dates
- khobanee or Arabian apricots
- allohlokara
- English prunes
- Normandy dry pippins
- French vinegar
- good clean sugar
- finely-sliced ginger
- peppercorns
- sticks of cinnamon
- salt

## Directions

1. Take equal quantities of dry dates, khobanee or Arabian apricots, allohlokara, English prunes, and Normandy dry pippins
2. Wash and clean them thoroughly, particularly the Arabian dry fruits
3. Dry them well in the sun
4. Stew the dry dates for ten to fifteen minutes
5. Cut them up into rings, and throw away the stones
6. Make a syrup of good French vinegar, in the proportion of a quarter of a pound of good clean sugar to a quart of French vinegar
7. Quarter the pippins
8. Arrange them and the other fruit in a wide-mouthed bottle in alternate layers, with finely-sliced ginger, peppercorns, sticks of cinnamon, and small sprinklings of salt
9. Pour over the whole as much of the vinegar syrup as will entirely cover the fruit
10. Cork the bottle well down
11. Expose it in the sun for a few days
12. It will be fit for use in a month
