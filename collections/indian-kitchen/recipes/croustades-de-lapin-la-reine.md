## Ingredients

- eight stale dinner rolls
- butter
- small rabbit
- tongue ham or lean bacon
- sweet-breads
- cocks' combs
- sliced mushrooms
- grated lime peel
- truffle
- onion
- carrot
- milk of almonds

## Directions

1. Take eight stale dinner rolls, which have been lightly baked in cylindrical tins about two inches and a half in diameter.
2. Cut them so as to leave a case about an inch and a half deep.
3. Scoop out every atom of crumb, and trim the tops that you cut off neatly, so as to form a lid for the top of the hollowed rolls.
4. Fry the cases and their lids in butter till of a golden yellow, and set them on one side to dry.
5. Choose a small rabbit, some tongue ham or lean bacon, a few sweet-breads if you can get them, and have ready some cocks' combs, sliced mushrooms, a little grated lime peel, and a slice of truffle for the top of each croustade.
6. Remove the fillets from the rabbit's back and divide them into pieces, or cut them with a cutter about the size of a shilling.
7. Put the rest of the rabbit into a stew-pan with the usual vegetables to yield a good broth, and with it make a rich white sauce flavoured with some milk of almonds.
8. Butter a saute-pan, and cast into it your pieces of rabbit fillet with some slices of onion and carrot.
