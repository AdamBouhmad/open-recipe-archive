## Ingredients

- a couple of good handfuls of spinach
- six full sized anchovies from the tin
- butter
- curled parsley sufficient to fill a table-spoon generously
- capers sufficient to fill a tea-spoon, heaped up

## Directions

1. Boil a couple of good handfuls of spinach, drain them thoroughly, pass the leaves through the hair sieve, and save all the pulp so obtained, in a bowl.
2. Take six full sized anchovies from the tin, wipe them free from oil, pick out their back bones, pass them through the hair sieve, and save the pulp.
3. Mince as finely as possible sufficient curled parsley to fill a table-spoon generously.
4. Mince also as finely as possible capers sufficient to fill a tea-spoon, heaped up.
5. First colour the butter by working into it, as lightly as you can, enough spinach-greening to secure the tint you require, then add the other things by degrees, and when thoroughly incorporated, trim the butter into a tasteful mould, or sundry pretty patlets, and set it in the ice-box.
