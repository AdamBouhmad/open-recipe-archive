## Ingredients

- a young chicken
- flour
- onion
- carrot
- half a dozen pepper corns
- celery
- salt
- sugar
- cold water
- milk of cocoanut or almond
- six good shallots or two small white onions
- two ounces of Denmark, Normandy, or other good tinned butter
- garlic
- curry stock powder
- curry paste
- wine-glassful of cocoanut milk
- breakfast-cupful of broth
- an ounce of butter or clarified beef suet
- bay-leaf
- chutney
- sweet-acid
- pounded almond and cocoanut
- spice
- grated green ginger

## Directions

1. Choose a nice young chicken and cut it up neatly as for a fricassee, place the pieces aside, and dredge over them a little flour.
2. Take all the trimmings, neck, pinions, leg bones, feet, head, and any scraps of meat that can be spared, and cast them into a sauce-pan with an onion sliced, a carrot sliced, half a dozen pepper corns, a bit of celery, a pinch of salt and one of sugar.
3. Cover them with cold water and make the best broth you can.
4. When ready, strain the contents of the sauce-pan into a bowl, and skim it clean to obtain a good breakfast-cupful of weak stock.
5. Make a breakfast-cupful of milk of cocoanut, or almond.
6. Take your stew-pan, and having sliced up six good shallots, or two small white onions, cast the rings into it with two ounces of tinned butter.
7. Add a finely-minced clove of garlic, and fry till the onions turn a nice yellow brown.
8. Add a heaped-up table-spoonful of the stock powder, and one of the paste, or, if you have not made the latter, two table-spoonfuls of the powder.
9. Cook the curry-stuff with the onions and butter for a minute or two, slowly, adding by degrees a wine-glassful of the cocoanut milk, and then also by degrees the breakfast-cupful of broth.
10. Simmer for a quarter of an hour until you have a rich, thick curry gravy or sauce.
11. Place the stew-pan en bain-marie.
12. Take a frying-pan and melt in it an ounce of butter, or clarified beef suet, add a shallot cut up small, and fry for a couple of minutes.
13. Put the pieces of chicken into the frying-pan, and lightly fry them.
14. As soon as slightly coloured, transfer the pieces of chicken to the stew-pan in which they should rest for at least half an hour, marinading in the curry gravy.
15. Place the stew-pan over a gentle fire, and if the liquid be found insufficient to cover the pieces of chicken, stock, if available, or water, should be added.
16. Encourage a gentle simmering process, during which add the bay-leaf, chutney, and sweet-acid.
17. If powder without fresh paste has been used, put in the pounded almond and cocoanut with a little spice and grated green ginger.
18. Taste the curry gravy, and if a little more acid or sweet be found necessary, make the proper correction.
19. As soon as the pieces of chicken have become tender and thoroughly stewed, stir in a coffee-cupful of cocoanut milk, and in three minutes the operation will be complete.
20. If a semi-dry or dry curry be required, further reduce the gravy by simmering with the lid off, stirring the pieces of meat continually with a wooden spoon to prevent their catching at the bottom of the pan.
21. When the proper amount of absorption has been attained, remove the pan and serve.
