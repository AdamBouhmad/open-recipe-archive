## Ingredients

- a good fat duck
- a teaspoonful each of mustard, sweet oil, and mixed sauce
- bones and giblet of the duck
- pepper
- salt
- soup herbs
- bay-leaves
- two pounds and a half of good beef
- the liver of the duck
- half a pound of beef suet
- a dessertspoonful of chopped garden herbs
- a tablespoonful of grated bread-crumbs
- half a teaspoonful of mixed hot spices pounded
- a teaspoonful each of black pepper and salt
- half a teaspoonful of essence of anchovies
- one chittack and a half or three ounces of ghee
- ready-boiled mixed vegetables
- hot West-Indian pickle

## Directions

1. Take a good fat duck; feather, clean, and bone it without hurting the skin.
2. Pour into it a mixture made up of a teaspoonful each of mustard, sweet oil, and mixed sauce.
3. Make a gravy of the bones and giblet, seasoning it with pepper, salt, soup herbs, and a few bay-leaves.
4. Mince together with the liver of the duck two pounds and a half of good beef, half a pound of beef suet, a dessertspoonful of chopped garden herbs, a tablespoonful of grated bread-crumbs, half a teaspoonful of mixed hot spices pounded, a teaspoonful each of black pepper and salt, and, for those who like it, half a teaspoonful of essence of anchovies.
5. Mix these well together, and stuff the duck.
6. Melt one chittack and a half or three ounces of ghee.
7. Put in the duck.
8. Pour over it the giblet gravy, and allow it to cook until tender.
9. Then glace the duck, as also some ready-boiled mixed vegetables, and serve up, surrounding the duck with the vegetables and some hot West-Indian pickle.
