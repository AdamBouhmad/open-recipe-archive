## Ingredients

- two undercuts of the sirloin
- fat bacon
- trimmings of the meat
- two ounces of carrot
- two ounces of onion
- salt
- sugar
- pepper
- half a pint of good gravy
- a pint more gravy

## Directions

1. Get two undercuts of the sirloin, if one be too small, trim them into a neat shape, and attach them together by two good skewers.
2. Lard them freely with fat bacon. If you cannot lard, having no needle, you must introduce a slice of bacon into each fillet by making therein a longitudinal incision; slip into it your slice of bacon, and pin the lips of the incised meat together with a small skewer.
3. Put the trimmings of the meat, two ounces of carrot sliced, two ounces of onion sliced, a pinch of salt, a pinch of sugar, and a pinch of pepper into the stew-pan.
4. Place your fillets upon the vegetables, and pour into the pan half a pint of good gravy.
5. Let this cook up to boiling point, and keep it on the fire till the broth has somewhat reduced and thickened.
6. Add a pint more gravy, and let it simmer for an hour with the pan half covered.
7. Close your pan: put some live charcoal on the lid, which lift every five minutes or so to admit of your basting the meat under it.
8. Continue this until you have glazed the fricandeau, then take it out, and dish it on a very hot dish.
9. Strain the gravy from the stew-pan, skim off any grease there may be, pour it over the meat, and serve.
