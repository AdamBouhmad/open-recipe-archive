## Ingredients

- a couple of sheep's trotters chopped in pieces
- a ham or bacon bone, or a few lean slices of either
- any raw cutlet trimmings
- two pounds of beef gravy meat cut into squares
- a couple of onions
- an ounce of butter
- a breakfast-cupful of broth, or water
- water
- two carrots sliced
- a turnip
- a good piece of celery
- a clove of garlic
- half a dozen pepper corns
- a spoonful of dried sweet herbs
- a bunch of parsley
- burnt sugar colouring
- salt to taste
- two ounces of butter
- two ounces of flour

## Directions

1. Cut up a couple of onions and throw them into a stew-pan with an ounce of butter, fry them a golden yellow
2. Add a breakfast-cupful of broth, or water, and the pieces of meat previously prepared
3. Shake the pan every now and then, and let the meat take colour
4. Add water enough to cover the meat completely
5. Reduce the fire, and let the contents of the pan come slowly to the boil, skimming carefully during that period
6. When the surface seems nicely clear of grease and scum, add a cupful of cold water and two carrots sliced, a turnip, a good piece of celery, a clove of garlic, half a dozen pepper corns, a spoonful of dried sweet herbs tied up in a bag, a bunch of parsley, some burnt sugar colouring, and salt to taste
7. As soon as the vegetables have been cooked, remove the pan from the fire and pick out the vegetables
8. Place it so that it may simmer slowly for a couple of hours
9. Lift it up, and strain off your gravy
10. Take a sauce-pan and melt two ounces of butter at the bottom of it
11. Stir in two ounces of flour and make a roux
12. When the colour satisfies you, add by degrees, stirring as you do so, the pint or so of strong gravy that you strained from the stew-pan
13. Let the contents of your sauce-pan come to the boil, stirring the whole time
14. Strain the sauce through your tin strainer into a clean sauce-pan
15. Set the vessel in the bain-marie to remain hot till wanted
