## Ingredients

- Equal weights of fine rice, atap or siddha
- Equal weights of fried moog-dal
- Sliced onions
- Potatoes, fried or raw, if desired
- Powdered turmeric 2 tolas per seer of rice
- Powdered cumin 1 tola per seer of rice
- Salt 2 1/2 tolas per seer of rice
- A few cloves
- Seeds of a few cardamoms
- Some red chillies
- Cassia leaves
- Ghee
- Ghee prepared from cow-milk

## Directions

1. Wash equal weights of fine rice, atap or siddha, and fried moog-dal, and drop them in boiling water.
2. After a few minutes add sliced onions and potatoes, fried or raw, if desired.
3. When the rice are half boiled add powdered turmeric, powdered cumin and salt.
4. Take the cooking-pot off the fire when the khechuri is fully boiled and is tolerably thick.
5. Fry a few cloves, the seeds of a few cardamoms, some red chillies broken in two, and cassia leaves in ghee in another pot.
6. Pour in the khechuri when the smell of the spices comes out, and stir.
7. Take down after a few minutes.
8. Serve while hot with plenty of ghee, prepared from cow-milk, on the khechuri.
9. Use slow heat and constant stirring especially at the finishing time.
