## Ingredients

- one duck
- black pepper
- salt
- an onion
- one clove of garlic
- a table-spoonful of melted butter
- a breakfast-cupful of strong gravy
- the rind of a lime
- a dozen pepper corns
- a pinch of sugar
- water
- flour
- a tumblerful of sauterne
- minced bacon, lean and fat in equal shares
- the bird's liver
- truffle

## Directions

1. Roast the bird lightly and set it to cool, then cut off all the meat from the bones, saving every atom of skin, bone, liver, &c.
2. Put the meat away for awhile, giving it a good dusting with black pepper and salt
3. Mince an onion, and one clove of garlic and stir the mince into a table-spoonful of melted butter at the bottom of a small sauce-pan
4. Let it fry a pale brown, now throw in all your fragments of duck, having mashed the bones in a mortar, a breakfast-cupful of strong gravy, the rind of a lime; a dozen pepper corns, a pinch of sugar, and salt to taste
5. Add water enough to cover the bones well, and let the contents of the sauce-pan simmer, skimming off the scum that rises
6. In half an hour you will have a well-flavoured broth
7. When quite satisfied with the broth, strain it off, let it cool, skim it, and thicken it plainly with flour and butter
8. Place the sauce-pan in the bain-marie-pan, and let it keep hot there
9. Take a tumblerful of sauterne, put it into a small sauce-pan with a table-spoonful of minced onion, a salt-spoonful of black pepper, and a pinch of salt
10. Boil till the liquid has been reduced to half its original quantity
11. Strain it into the previously made sauce
12. Give the whole a simmer for ten minutes and serve
