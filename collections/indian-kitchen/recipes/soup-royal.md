## Ingredients

- shin of beef
- calf's head
- calf's tongue
- pepper
- salt
- soup herbs
- lemon
- red carrots
- green peas
- boiled potatoes
- turnip
- Patna onion
- roasted and ground split peas or gram dal
- orange
- water
- French prunes
- port wine
- brain cakes
- egg balls
- forcemeat balls

## Directions

1. Take a shin of beef, the best parts of meat cut off from a calf's head, and the tongue
2. Cut the beef into small particles, but leave the tongue and the meat from the calf's head whole
3. Add pepper and salt and boil well, clearing the scum as it rises
4. Remove the tongue and the meat of the calf's head when sufficiently tender
5. Continue to boil the shin of beef until it is well dissolved
6. Strain it and cut away all the fat
7. Put it up again with plenty of soup herbs and more salt and pepper if necessary
8. Boil it well up
9. Squeeze into the soup the juice of half a lemon and skim it well
10. Strain it once more and set it aside
11. Cut the tongue into slices of an eighth of an inch thick
12. Trim them into the shape of large diamonds and set aside
13. Cut up the meat of the calf's head into one-inch squares and strips of an inch and a half long and half an inch wide
14. Add to these a few ready-fried circular flat brain cakes
15. Make also a few egg balls and forcemeat balls, and after cooking, add them to the rest of the meat, tongue, etc., and set aside
16. Take four red carrots, one pound of green peas, half a pound of boiled potatoes, one large turnip, one large Patna onion, a quarter of a pound of roasted and ground split peas or gram dal, some soup herbs, pepper, and salt, the pulp of one orange, and the peels of half an orange and half a lemon
17. Put these into a stewpan with water sufficient to cover the whole
18. Boil them thoroughly, skimming all the while
19. When perfectly dissolved, turn them out into a colander and allow all the water to drain away
20. Turn the contents of the colander into a sieve and pass the vegetables, etc., through it, rejecting all such as will not pass
21. Add the whole or a part of the strained vegetables to the soup, which should not be thicker in consistency than a good thick potato soup
22. Stew one dozen good French prunes in a claret-glassful of port wine
23. Strain through a sieve, rejecting stones, etc., and add the strained portion to the soup
24. Boil the whole and strain it once more
25. Add to it all the forcemeat and egg balls, the brain cakes, tongue, etc., and serve up, adding to it more salt, wine, or sauce, if needed
