## Ingredients

- one large, or two small pomfrets
- finely minced (cooked) onion
- parsley
- trimmings of mushrooms
- a breakfast-cupful of stock
- a spoonful of chablis
- a spoonful of vinegar
- flour
- butter
- the strained yolks of four eggs
- a dozen button mushrooms
- a little white pepper and salt
- a table-spoonful of cream

## Directions

1. Divide one large, or two small pomfrets into fillets as neatly as you can
2. Season them on one side with a very little finely minced (cooked) onion, parsley, and the trimmings of the mushrooms you are about to use for the sauce
3. Roll them up enveloping the seasoning within each fillet
4. Set them to simmer gently till done, in a court bouillon, or broth made from their own bones and trimmings, assisted by a breakfast-cupful of stock, a spoonful of chablis and a spoonful of vinegar
5. Lift them out when done, strain off the broth
6. Thicken it as carefully as you can in the bain-marie with a little flour, and butter, and the strained yolks of four eggs
7. Add a dozen button mushrooms cut into quarters, a little white pepper and salt
8. As you take it off the fire, stir in a table-spoonful of cream
9. Pour this over your fillets which have been kept hot in the bain-marie whilst the sauce was being made
