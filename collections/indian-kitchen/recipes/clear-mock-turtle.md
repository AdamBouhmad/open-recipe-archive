## Ingredients

- stock meat
- half an ox-head unskinned or a sheep's head
- water
- an ounce of salt
- two Bombay onions in quarters
- a head of celery
- a couple of carrots
- the rind of two limes
- a large bunch of parsley
- a quarter ounce of dried basil
- a teaspoonful of dried thyme
- a teaspoonful of marjoram
- a dozen pepper corns
- two anchovies
- half a wine-glass of mushroom ketchup
- a tablespoonful of sugar
- a teaspoonful of caramel
- a glass of Madeira
- the juice of a lime

## Directions

1. Procure the stock meat as usual, and either half an ox-head unskinned, or a sheep's head, well, clean the head thoroughly, keeping the skin on, and scalding it to get rid of the hair or wool
2. Remove every atom of brain and wash the meat in several waters
3. Set it to soak awhile in cold water, and then place it in your soup kettle
4. Cover it with water and let it simmer slowly for an hour, skimming all fat and scum that may rise
5. Take it up (it will be about half done), remove the bones and set it, with a weight upon it, on a dish to flatten and get cold
6. Throw your shin, &c., into the soup kettle, adding to the cold head liquor already there, the bones just removed, sufficient cold water to cover the bone and meat completely and an ounce of salt
7. Proceed as for consomme skimming very carefully, and retarding the boiling point as much as you can by periodical additions of water
8. When boiling takes place, ease off the fire, and add two Bombay onions in quarters, a head of celery, a couple of carrots, the rind of two limes, a large bunch of parsley, a quarter ounce of dried basil, a teaspoonful of dried thyme, and of marjoram (the herbs in a muslin bag), a dozen pepper corns, two anchovies cut up, half a wine-glass of mushroom ketchup, a tablespoonful of sugar, and a teaspoonful of caramel
9. Simmer slowly now for about three hours, skimming the surface occasionally, and on no account permitting the vessel to boil
10. Strain very carefully, and set the soup in a bowl to cool and throw up all grease
11. Cut the cold head into one-and-a-half-inch squares, skim the cool soup well, and pour it into a large sauce-pan adding the pieces of head
12. Let the pieces cook slowly for half an hour, then drain them, and again strain the soup
13. Clarify it if necessary adding a glass of Madeira, and the juice of a lime
14. Heat this up finally when required, and pour the soup into the tureen over a dozen or so carefully selected pieces of the head arranged therein
15. Serve with limes cut into quarters, which should be handed round followed by the Madeira
