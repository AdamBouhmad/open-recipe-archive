## Ingredients

- nice brisket or fleshy piece of the flank
- salt
- brine
- half an ounce of cloves
- half an ounce of pepper corns (ground)
- half an ounce of mace
- a tablespoonful of finely minced parsley
- half a tablespoonful of marjoram
- chopped rind of a nice yellow lime, peeled very finely
- a dust of Nepaul pepper
- a couple of glasses of Madeira

## Directions

1. Remove all bones.
2. Rub the joint well with salt, and let it lie in the brine for about two days.
3. Spread it out flat and dry it.
4. Lay a coating of the spices over the inside in the same manner as you would spread jam over paste before making a rolypoly pudding.
5. Roll up the brisket and tie it tightly with a string.
6. Stew it patiently until tender.
7. Set it under a weight when done.
8. When cold, remove the string and trim the joint neatly.
9. Glaze it, and when the glaze is dry, the beef may be considered ready for the table.
