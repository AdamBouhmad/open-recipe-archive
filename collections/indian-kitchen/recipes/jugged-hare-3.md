## Ingredients

- hare
- flour
- butter
- pint and a half of good strong stock
- two carrots
- two or three large onions
- an ounce of celery
- juice of two limes
- a table-spoonful of sugar
- salt
- a wine-glass of brandy
- a bumper glass of port
- a dessert-spoonful of red currant jelly
- a lump of butter rolled in flour
- stuffing as for roast hare

## Directions

1. Skin and clean the hare
2. Cut the hare up into neat pieces
3. Dredge them with flour
4. Give them a few turns in the frying-pan with some butter till they take colour
5. Prepare beforehand a pint and a half of good strong stock
6. Choose a vessel that you can close securely with paste
7. Put the pieces of hare into it, with two carrots, two or three large onions, an ounce of celery, the juice of two limes, a table-spoonful of sugar, and one of salt
8. Pour in the stock
9. Throw in a wine-glass of brandy
10. Seal the vessel as closely as possible
11. Place it in a pan of cold water
12. Set it to boil, steaming the covered pot for three hours
13. When done, open the pot
14. Stir into it a bumper glass of port, a dessert-spoonful of red currant jelly, and a lump of butter rolled in flour
15. Serve with a dozen balls of stuffing made as for roast hare, and fried in butter
16. Alternatively, the jar may be placed in the oven and baked for two or three hours
