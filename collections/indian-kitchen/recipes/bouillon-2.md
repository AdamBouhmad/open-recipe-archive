## Ingredients

- piece of the ribs of beef, weighing about five pounds
- two carrots
- a turnip
- a handful of French beans
- a large Bombay onion
- half a head of celery
- two leeks
- a bunch of parsley
- salt
- cold water
- caramel
- sugar
- pepper corns
- mixed sweet herbs
- Madeira
- tarragon

## Directions

1. Purchase a nice piece of the ribs of beef, weighing about five pounds, with two carrots, a turnip, a handful of French beans, a large Bombay onion, half a head of celery (leaves and all), two leeks, and a bunch of parsley.
2. Detach the meat from the bones en masse, roll it up compactly, and tie it firmly with a string.
3. See that your soup kettle is thoroughly clean, put in the bones (chopped up) first, and place your bundle of meat above them.
4. Pour in cold water enough to cover the top of the meat with one and a half ounce of salt and nothing else.
5. Set the pot near the fire, and let the contents come as slowly as possible to the boil, skimming off every atom of scum as it rises, and adding from time to time a little cold water to retard ebullition.
6. Keep the vessel open throughout the operations.
7. Once boiling with a clean surface, cast in your vegetables (the carrots first, followed by the turnip) all nicely washed, trimmed, and cut up.
8. Give the soup a tea-spoonful of caramel (burnt sugar colouring), a dessert-spoonful of sugar, a dozen pepper corns, and a bag containing mixed sweet herbs (a tea-spoonful of each).
9. Watch the vessel narrowly, and as soon as boiling begins again, ease off the fire, and let the contents of the kettle simmer till the vegetables are nicely done.
10. Remove the vegetables and put the meat broth and bone back on a low fire to simmer slowly until the meat is nice and tender.
11. Strain the soup from the meat, and set it to cool.
12. When the broth is cold, take off any fat that may have risen to its surface, and return the liquor to the sauce-pan.
13. Cut up some of the vegetables into nice pieces and place them in the soup tureen.
14. Let the contents of your sauce-pan come almost to the boil, and pour them over the vegetables.
15. Add a dessert-spoonful of Madeira and a dried leaf or two of tarragon to improve it.
