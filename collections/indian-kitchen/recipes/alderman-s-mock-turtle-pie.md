## Ingredients

- a calf's head
- brain cakes
- forcemeat and egg balls
- eight calves' feet
- soup herbs
- salt
- ground black pepper
- pie-pastry
- about twenty or thirty green leaves of spinach

## Directions

1. Make an extra rich hash of a calf's head, cutting the pieces from the cheeks two and a half to three inches long, and one and three-quarters to two inches wide.
2. Slice the tongue, and cut into large-sized shapes.
3. Prepare brain cakes, and plenty of forcemeat and egg balls.
4. Make an extra strong stock with eight calves' feet.
5. Season it highly with soup herbs, salt, and plenty of ground black pepper.
6. Simmer until the meat begins to drop away from the bones.
7. Strain through a coarse sieve, in order to get a very thick stock, passing as much of the dissolved meat through as possible.
8. Line a deep pie-dish with a thick and rich pie-pastry, and arrange in it the hash, egg and meat balls, and brain cakes, with some twenty or thirty green leaves of spinach, cut up to about the size and shape of the meat.
9. Pour over the whole as much stock as will fill the dish, cover over with pastry, and bake.
