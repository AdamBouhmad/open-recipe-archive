## Ingredients

- six mutton kidneys
- salt
- dried thyme
- marjoram
- spice
- one well washed anchovy
- one ounce of butter
- one table-spoonful of flour
- consomme
- a glass of port
- a tea-spoonful of red currant jelly
- a tea-spoonful of good vinegar
- caramel coloring
- croutons of fried bread

## Directions

1. Wash the kidneys, dry them, slit them in halves, and plunge them immediately into boiling water well salted
2. Let them remain in this bath for one minute only, take them out, and dry them in a napkin
3. Having thus blanched the kidneys, proceed to boil them gently with some dried thyme, marjoram, and a bag containing spice, &c., till they are very tender
4. Take them out, and pound them in the mortar, with one well washed anchovy
5. When sufficiently pounded, pass the paste through a wire sieve to get rid of fibre, gristle, &c.
6. Place the consomme in a bowl handy, and keep the kidney paste ready in a soup plate
7. Take a roomy sauce-pan, melt an ounce of butter at the bottom of it over the fire, stir in a table-spoonful of flour
8. When nice and creamy, add a cupful of consomme, stirring vigorously, and continue to add consomme and kidney paste by degrees, working them well together, till you have used them up
9. Let the puree come to the boil
10. Add a glass of port, a tea-spoonful of red currant jelly, a tea-spoonful of good vinegar, and sufficient caramel coloring to give the soup a warm brown tint
11. Stir well for a minute over the fire, and serve with croutons of fried bread
