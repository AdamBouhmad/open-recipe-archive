## Ingredients

- Fine atap rice 1 seer
- Fried moog-dal 1 seer
- Potato 1 seer
- Ghee 1/2 seer
- Onion 1 powa
- Turmeric 1 tola
- Salt 3 1/2 tolas
- Pepper 1 tola
- Cumin 1/2 tola
- Coriander 2 tolas
- Aniseed 1/2 tola
- Cloves 1/2 tola
- Cardamom 1/8 tola
- Cinnamon 1/2 tola
- A few red chillies and cassia leaves
- Water 4 seers

## Directions

1. Pulverise the coriander, aniseed, half the pepper and half the cloves, bind them in a piece of clean cloth, and boil with some cassia leaves in 4 seers of water till it is reduced to 2 1/2 seers.
2. Throw away the boiled spices and keep the akhni water covered.
3. Wash and dry the rice and pulse, and brown them in half the ghee for 5 or 6 minutes.
4. Add akhni water and put the lid on.
5. Do not stir much.
6. Apply slow heat.
7. Brown the pieces of potatoes and onions in another pan and drop them with turmeric, pepper and cumin, all powdered, and salt in the boiling rice when they are half boiled.
8. Take down when the mass is fully boiled.
9. Boil the rest of the ghee with the cassia leaves and red chillies, and pour the mass in.
10. Finish by adding the powdered cloves, cardamoms and cinnamon.
11. Try to keep the grains of rice and pulse entire.
12. If meat is to be added, boil the meat while preparing akhni water, brown the boiled meat and drop them in the khechuri with the browned potatoes.
