## Ingredients

- two freshly boiled fowl's livers
- tea-spoonful of anchovy sauce or flesh of one fish pounded
- pinch of sugar
- plenty of fresh butter
- yolk of one raw egg
- spiced pepper
- four squares of bread
- coffee-cupful of good cream
- yolks of two raw eggs

## Directions

1. Pound the liver to a paste, mixing with it a tea-spoonful of anchovy sauce or the flesh of one fish pounded
2. Add a pinch of sugar, plenty of fresh butter, and the yolk of one raw egg
3. Dust into it a little spiced pepper
4. Pass it through the sieve and set it aside on a clean plate
5. Prepare four squares of golden-tinted, crisply-fried bread, about half an inch thick
6. Spread the liver paste over them and set them in a moderate oven to retain their heat, but not to burn
7. Pour a coffee-cupful of good cream into a sauce-pan, which must either be dipped into a bain-marie, or placed over a very low fire
8. Stir into it, as it warms, the carefully strained yolks of two raw eggs
9. Continue stirring one way till the cream thickens nicely and is quite hot without boiling
10. Pour it over your toasts
11. The egg whites should be whisked to a stiff froth and laid on the top of all
12. Send the dish up without delay
