## Ingredients

- two good-sized chickens
- flour
- bones, skin, necks, pinions
- onion
- celery
- carrot
- bunch of parsley
- almonds
- pepper
- salt
- butter
- flour
- aspic or plain strong meat jelly
- yolks of three eggs
- thick cream
- creme de topinambours
- carrots
- green peas
- truffle
- cock's comb

## Directions

1. Cover the breasts of two good-sized chickens with paper, and roast them without letting them take colour
2. When cold, remove the breast meat as neatly as you can, also the flesh of the thighs and drumsticks
3. Out of the pieces thus obtained, trim a number of neat fillets as nearly the same size as possible
4. Dredge a little flour over them and cover them up
5. Take all the bones left after the above operation, skin, necks, pinions, &c., and make a strong chicken broth with them, adding an onion, some celery, a carrot, a bunch of parsley, a few almonds, pepper, and salt, to flavour it well
6. When the broth is ready, strain it into a bowl and skim it
7. Proceed to make with it a rich veloute sauce, using for a pint of broth an ounce of butter and one of flour
8. Strain it and let it get cold, then add to its thickness by stirring into it one-third of its quantity of liquefied aspic, or plain strong meat jelly, and reducing it over the fire till it coats the spoon
9. Take it from the fire, and add the yolks of three eggs, and (if available) a table-spoonful of thick cream
10. Pour some of this over the pieces of chicken completely coating each piece with a thick white glaze
11. Put them away so that the glaze may cool and set
12. Prepare a border of creme de topinambours decorating the top of the mould with carrots and green peas
13. After steaming it, let the mould get cold, and then set it in ice
14. When quite cold, turn it out carefully upon a cold silver dish
15. Fill the hollow in the centre with the pieces of glazed chicken, introducing a slice of truffle here and there as you arrange them
16. Garnish the top of the chicken with cold boiled cock's comb and truffles
17. Let the dish remain in the ice-box until the time arrives for it to go to table
