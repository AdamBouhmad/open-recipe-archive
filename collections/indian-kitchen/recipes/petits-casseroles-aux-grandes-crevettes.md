## Ingredients

- a pound of the best table rice
- a quart of water
- an onion
- ounces of clarified suet
- a tablespoonful of grated Parmesan
- butter
- pepper and salt
- the yolks of three eggs
- spiced pepper
- egg
- bread-crumbs

## Directions

1. Put a pound of the best table rice into a stew-pan with a quart of water, an onion, and ounces of clarified suet
2. Simmer till the rice is soft, yet quite whole
3. Drain it, and pound it to a paste in a mortar with a tablespoonful of grated Parmesan, some butter, pepper and salt
4. Stir into this the yolks of three eggs, and powder it with a little spiced pepper
5. Pass all through the sieve, and pat it to a paste on your pastry slab, about one and a half inch thick
6. When quite cold, cut it into cylinders two inches long, and an inch and a half in diameter
7. Egg and bread-crumb them, fry them, a golden yellow in butter
