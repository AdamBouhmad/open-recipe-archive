## Ingredients

- firm pieces of lobster from the tin
- buttered saute-pan
- riz a la Turque
- sweet onion or shalots
- two ounces of butter
- one table-spoonful of good curry powder or paste
- one pint of plain gravy
- quarter of a pound of sweet almonds
- one bitter almond
- milk
- boiling water
- yolks of two eggs

## Directions

1. Choose the firm pieces of a lobster from the tin and trim them neatly
2. Set them in a buttered saute-pan and warm them thoroughly
3. Arrange the pieces in a circle in a hot silver dish filling the centre with riz a la Turque
4. Cut up a sweet onion or half a dozen shalots and throw the pieces into a small stew-pan with two ounces of butter
5. Fry till the onions begin to take color, then stir in a table-spoonful of good curry powder or paste
6. Cook this for five minutes, then add a pint of plain gravy
7. Let the contents of the pan simmer for a quarter of an hour
8. Blanch and peel a quarter of a pound of sweet almonds with one bitter one
9. Pound them in a mortar with a little milk
10. When well pounded, pour some boiling water upon them
11. Stir well and then squeeze the milk through muslin
12. Stir this into the sauce, adding the yolks of two eggs off the fire before serving
13. Pour some of the sauce over the pieces of lobster but not over the rice and serve
