## Ingredients

- well made liquid aspic jelly
- green butter
- prawn butter
- balls of fancy butters
- turned olives
- strips of anchovy
- hard-boiled egg yolks
- fish or fowl
- lettuce leaves from the golden heart
- whole boiled prawns in their shells

## Directions

1. In an oval, or round cylinder-mould placed in ice, pour a very little well made liquid aspic jelly
2. As soon as it is all but set, dispose neatly therein a number of little balls of green butter, and prawn butter alternately
3. Cover them with some more of the jelly, set them, and repeat the process in layers until the mould is packed
4. Ice the balls of butter before putting them in, and alternate them differently in each layer, so that the colours may be checkered
5. Leave the mould in ice to consolidate thoroughly
6. When turned out, garnish it externally with balls of the same fancy butters, turned olives, strips of anchovy, and hard-boiled yolks of egg
7. Pack its hollow centre with the fish, or fowl, of which your mayonnaise is composed
8. Crown the centre with lettuce leaves culled from the golden heart, and arrange with some whole boiled prawns in their shells round the margin of the dish outside the mould
9. Keep the mould of jelly and its sauce in the ice box till the nick of time before serving, when the sauce should be poured over the contents in the centre of the mould
