## Ingredients

- one seer and a quarter of soojee
- half a seer of flour
- half a seer of suet
- leg of mutton
- ground pepper
- salt
- butter
- flour for dredging

## Directions

1. Prepare a good pie-crust with one seer and a quarter of soojee, half a seer of flour, and half a seer of suet, as per recipe No. 199.
2. Clean and trim the leg, cutting away the end of the knuckle-bone, and any other projections likely to injure the dumpling.
3. Sprinkle it well with ground pepper and some salt, and confine it securely in the pastry, closing all joinings with the aid of a little water.
4. Place the dumpling into a strong napkin, previously buttered and dredged with flour.
5. Tie it securely, and allow it to boil from three to four hours.
6. Care must be taken that during the whole process of boiling the dumpling remains suspended in the water, and not resting on the bottom of the pan.
7. On removing it from the boiler, plunge it immediately into a large tureen of cold water for two or three minutes. This will strengthen the pastry and prevent its bursting or breaking while it is being served up.
