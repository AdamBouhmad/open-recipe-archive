## Ingredients

- large fowl or two good sized chickens
- two sheep's feet
- giblets
- two ounces of fat bacon
- pepper
- salt
- crumb of bread
- stock
- half a tin of white mushrooms
- four whole raw eggs
- truffles

## Directions

1. Cut off all the white meat from the fowl for the puree
2. Make a strong essence with all the bones, skin, scraps, two sheep's feet cleaned and cut up, and the giblets
3. Strain the essence when done, let it get cold, and skim it
4. Thicken the essence until as thick as a rich mayonnaise sauce, strain it, and set it in the bain-marie
5. Melt two ounces of fat bacon in a sauce-pan
6. Throw in pepper, salt, and the meat of the fowl and work them together for five minutes over a bright fire
7. Empty the contents into a mortar and pound the meat and bacon to a paste
8. Add half their bulk of crumb of bread soaked in stock and half a tin of white mushrooms
9. Pass the whole through a hair sieve
10. Moisten the puree with some of the sauce while pounding it
11. When finished, add four whole raw eggs
12. Butter a plain mould and fill it with the puree, introducing a good allowance of truffles cut into dice
13. Steam it as you would a pudding
14. Let the mould get cold and turn it out
15. Reduce the remainder of the sauce to a white glaze and let it get cool
16. Pour the glaze gently over the mould until it coats it completely
