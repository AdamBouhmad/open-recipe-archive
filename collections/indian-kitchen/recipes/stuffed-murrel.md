## Ingredients

- murrel fish
- turkey forcemeat (page 108)
- chicken stock
- white wine (hock, chablis, or sauterne)
- onion
- vegetables
- maitre d'hotel butter

## Directions

1. After having been carefully cleansed and trimmed, stuff the fish with turkey forcemeat
2. Sew it up and trim it in a circular shape with its tail in its mouth
3. Bake it in a pie-dish surrounded by chicken stock about half an inch deep
4. A glass of any white wine like hock, chablis, or sauterne may be put into the stock, an onion also, and any vegetables you can spare
5. The fish should be basted every now and then
6. When it has absorbed the gravy and seems soft, take it out of the oven
7. Put a pat of maitre d'hotel butter on the top of it
8. Serve in its own dish with a napkin folded round it
9. A good sized murrel will take from twenty minutes to half an hour in baking
