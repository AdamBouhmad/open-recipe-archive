## Ingredients

- any sort of small fish, such as small pomfret, whitings, soles, mullets, or robal
- two pounds of mixed fish
- two Bombay onions, sliced
- two fairly large tomatoes, emptied of their seeds and cut into quarters
- one carrot
- two slices of lime with the pithy skin cut off
- six cloves
- a dozen pepper corns
- a clove of garlic
- the peel of a lime pared as finely as possible
- a salt-spoonful of saffron
- a tea-spoonful of salt
- one pod of capsicum, sliced
- a wine-glass of salad oil
- a wine-glass of chablis
- a pint and a half of cold water
- a heaped up table-spoonful of freshly-chopped curled parsley

## Directions

1. Put the whole into a stew-pan
2. Add to it a wine-glass of salad oil, one of chablis, and a pint and a half of cold water
3. Boil for about half an hour
4. Just before serving, add a heaped up table-spoonful of freshly-chopped curled parsley
5. Remove the bag of condiments before serving
6. Serve the fish in a deep dish with some of the broth round it
7. Send up the rest of the broth first, poured boiling hot over some slices of stale bread
