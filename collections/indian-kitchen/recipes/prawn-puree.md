## Ingredients

- beef
- bone
- vegetables
- herbs
- cold boiled prawns
- butter
- salt
- spiced pepper
- ounce of butter
- table-spoonful of flour
- bread-crumb
- stock
- consomme
- dice of fried bread
- boiling cream or milk

## Directions

1. Make a strong clear consomme with beef, bone, vegetables and herbs, as usual.
2. When done, strain it into a bowl to be ready when wanted.
3. Pick enough cold boiled prawns to fill a half-pint pot to the brim.
4. Pound these in a mortar with a good allowance of butter till you get them to a pulp.
5. Flavour this with salt, and a little spiced pepper to taste.
6. Melt an ounce of butter in a sauce-pan, and incorporate therewith a table-spoonful of flour.
7. Mix this with the prawn pulp.
8. Take about the same quantity of bread-crumb well soaked in stock (the consomme) that you have of prawn, and add it to the prawn pulp also, off the fire, mixing the two together by degrees thoroughly, and gradually adding the consomme till you find you have a soup a little less thick than that you want eventually to get.
9. Set the sauce-pan on the fire, and stir vigorously till it boils, and thickens.
10. Take it off the fire then, and let it get cool, to enable you to remove any fat that may rise.
11. The puree should be pressed through a hair sieve into a bowl.
12. When wanted, it must be re-heated, and served with dice of fried bread.
