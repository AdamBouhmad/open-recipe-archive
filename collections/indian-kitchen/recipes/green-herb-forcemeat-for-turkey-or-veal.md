## Ingredients

- one lime
- six ounces dry, well sifted, stale bread-crumbs
- one dessert-spoonful of chopped thyme (green), or one table-spoonful of dried thyme leaves powdered
- one dessert-spoonful of chopped marjoram (green), or one table-spoonful of dried marjoram leaves powdered
- one heaped table-spoonful minced parsley
- three ounces fresh beef-suet or butter
- salt
- pepper
- three well-beaten eggs

## Directions

1. Pare a good sized lime as finely as possible avoiding the slightest particle of white pith, and mince the peel as small as possible
2. Weigh six ounces of dry, well sifted, stale bread-crumbs
3. Measure a dessert-spoonful of chopped thyme (green) and one of marjoram (green), or take a table-spoonful of the dried leaves powdered — half and half, and powder the leaves to get rid of atoms of stalk and stick
4. Mince the parsley fine to the extent of a heaped up table-spoonful
5. Chop up three ounces of fresh beef-suet, or butter if suet cannot be got
6. Mix all these together with two spoons in a large dish
7. Dust the whole well with salt and pepper
8. Bind the mixture with three well-beaten eggs
9. Work this together, and the stuffing will be fit to use
