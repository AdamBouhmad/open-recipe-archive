## Ingredients

- a cut of seer fish
- butter
- half a pint of broth made from the fish trimmings
- a glass of chablis or hock
- pepper and salt
- minced parsley
- a little shallot
- a quarter of a pint of cream
- a table-spoonful of walnut catsup
- half a table-spoonful of anchovy sauce
- a bit of butter the size of a walnut rolled in flour
- a thought of red pepper

## Directions

1. Divide a cut of seer fish into eight nice collops about half an inch thick
2. Flatten them by a board
3. Butter a pie-dish and pour into it half a pint of broth made from the fish trimmings, and a glass of chablis or hock
4. Lay the slices therein
5. Pepper and salt them
6. Sprinkle them with some minced parsley, and a little shallot
7. Spread a sheet of buttered paper over them
8. Bake for about ten minutes in a quick oven
9. When done, remove the paper, and arrange the slices neatly in a hot dish
10. For the sauce: To the liquor from the pie-dish add a quarter of a pint of cream, a table-spoonful of walnut catsup, and half one of anchovy sauce
11. Boil these together
12. Just before you remove the sauce-pan from the fire, stir in well a bit of butter the size of a walnut rolled in flour, with a thought of red pepper
