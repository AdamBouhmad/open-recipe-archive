## Ingredients

- two nice chickens
- onion
- pepper corns
- meat gravy
- lime peel
- ketchup
- salt
- carrot
- French beans
- sweet almonds
- blade of mace
- yolks of three eggs

## Directions

1. Cut up two nice chickens as if for fricassee
2. Steep the pieces in cold water for half an hour, then drain them
3. Select the following pieces: the four wings, the four legs, two breasts, and four thighs, and put them aside covered up
4. Take all that remains (the backs, pinions, necks, livers, gizzards, and trimmings), and with an onion cut up, some pepper corns, a few spoonfuls of meat gravy, a bit of lime peel, a tea-spoonful of ketchup, and salt, make as good a pint of broth as you can
5. When ready, fish out the livers, and strain the broth
6. Put into the stew-pan the selected joints of the chickens, cover them with the strained broth, and stew them gently till done, with one carrot, and a handful of French beans
7. Pick out the chicken, strain the broth, and put the vegetables aside
8. Proceed with the broth to make a nice veloute (rich white sauce) flavoured with a dozen sweet almonds pounded, and a blade of mace
9. Thicken in the bain-marie with the yolks of three eggs
10. When satisfactory, strain and put in the pieces of chicken, heating them without boiling
11. Cut the carrot, French beans, with the livers you saved, into dice
12. When you dish the chicken, garnish your entree with them. A casserole of mashed potatoes, shaped like the pastry case of a vol-au-vent, and nicely ornamented, can hold the chicken, and the garnish should be sprinkled over the surface
