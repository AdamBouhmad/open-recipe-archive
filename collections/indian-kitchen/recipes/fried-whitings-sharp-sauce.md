## Ingredients

- three nice whitings
- batter
- boiling fat
- Bombay onion
- clove of garlic
- butter
- table-spoonful of chopped parsley
- tea-spoonful of sugar
- coffee-cupful of vinegar
- pint of beef gravy
- tea-spoonful of red currant jelly
- table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup
- table-spoonful of minced cornichons

## Directions

1. Divide three nice whitings in fillets
2. Dip them in batter and fry them a golden yellow in a bath of boiling fat
3. Drain them
4. Fry a Bombay onion finely minced with one clove of garlic also minced in butter at the bottom of a sauce-pan
5. When turning brown, put in a table-spoonful of chopped parsley, a tea-spoonful of sugar, a coffee-cupful of vinegar, and a pint of beef gravy
6. Stir in a tea-spoonful of red currant jelly and a table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup
7. As soon as the mixture becomes nicely flavoured and the juices appear to be extracted, strain off the sauce
8. Cut up a table-spoonful of minced cornichons and add the mince to the strained gravy
9. Heat it up and send it round with your fried fish in a boat
