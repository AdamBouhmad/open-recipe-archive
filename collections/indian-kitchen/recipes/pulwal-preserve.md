## Ingredients

- two seers or four pounds of large full-grown pulwals
- half a seer of sugar
- a quarter of a seer or half a pound of green ginger

## Directions

1. Take two seers or four pounds of large full-grown pulwals without any decay
2. Peel, slit, remove the seeds, and throw them into cold water
3. Wash them thoroughly, and parboil them in clean water
4. Put them in a colander, and set them aside to cool
5. Prepare a good strong syrup of half a seer of sugar and a quarter of a seer or half a pound of green ginger well bruised
6. Throw the pulwals in, and allow them to simmer until the syrup thickens
7. Remove them immediately the colour becomes quite brown, but keep the syrup boiling till it has acquired the proper consistency
8. Return the pulwals into the syrup, and if necessary, reboil it two or three days after, if it appears to have become thin, or inclined to ferment
