## Ingredients

- round of beef
- four pounds of lard or fat bacon
- half a tablespoonful of cinnamon powdered
- half a seer or one pound of finely-powdered pepper
- one tablespoonful of cloves powdered
- four tablespoonfuls of chutnee strained through muslin
- claret-glassful of mixed sauces such as Harvey, walnut, Worcestershire, tap, tomato
- plain water
- half a pint of vinegar
- bay-leaves
- peppercorns
- claret or champagne

## Directions

1. Corn a round of beef in every particular as directed in recipe No. 123
2. Twenty-four hours previously to its being cooked lard it as follows with the undermentioned ingredients
3. Mix the ground pepper, ground hot spices, and strained chutnee with a claret-glassful of mixed sauces
4. Cut up into long narrow slips the lard or bacon to correspond in thickness with the larding-pin
5. Lay the slips into the mixture of spices, sauces, etc., for an hour or two before larding the beef
6. Lard the beef through and through, and as closely as possible
7. Cook it the next day, either in plain water with half a pint of vinegar and with bay-leaves and peppercorns, or in a preparation of claret or champagne with vinegar, bay-leaves, etc.
