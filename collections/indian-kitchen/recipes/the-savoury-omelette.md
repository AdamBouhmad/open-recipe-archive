## Ingredients

- four ordinary country fowl's eggs
- two country fowl's eggs (yolks only)
- salt-spoonful of salt
- tea-spoonful of very finely minced shallot
- heaped up table-spoonful of minced curly parsley
- table-spoonful of really rich cream
- butter

## Directions

1. Break four ordinary country fowl's eggs whole into a slop-basin, and add the yolks only of two more eggs
2. Thoroughly mix the six yolks and four whites by using two forks, without beating them at all
3. Flavour the mixture with a salt-spoonful of salt, a tea-spoonful of very finely minced shallot, a heaped up table-spoonful of minced curly parsley, and a table-spoonful of really rich cream
4. Stir this for a minute
5. Melt a pat of butter in a shallow pan about ten inches in diameter over a good charcoal fire, sufficient in quantity to thoroughly lubricate the whole surface and leave a coating of moisture about an eighth of an inch deep
6. When the butter is quite burning hot, having ceased to splutter and beginning to brown, pour in the mixture with one good stir round
7. At the moment of contact, lift the underpart of omelette formed with a spoon, and allow the unformed portion to run beneath it, while holding and playing the pan from side to side with the left hand
8. With one good shake, lift the pan from the fire in less than a minute and roll its contents into a hot silver dish that has been prepared with a little melted butter, minced parsley, and shallot
