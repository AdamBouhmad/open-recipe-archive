## Ingredients

- two pounds of potatoes
- yolks of five eggs
- grated mace
- salt
- egg and bread-crumbs for coating
- ragout a la financiere
- fried parsley for garnish

## Directions

1. Peel two pounds of potatoes and cook them as usual
2. When done, stir into them the yolks of five eggs, add a little grated mace and a little salt, and stir them over the fire for five minutes
3. Pass them through the sieve, pat them to a paste, and flatten that out on your slab about two inches thick
4. Let it get cold
5. With your cutter, cut it into cylinders (or little drums) two inches in diameter
6. Egg and bread-crumb each cylinder, and fry these potato drums till they are a bright golden yellow
7. Carefully slice off the top of each drum (say) a quarter of an inch thick
8. Place the caps so obtained on one side, and scoop out the interior of your drums very carefully
9. Put the cases out of the way, well covered up
10. As dinner time draws near, fill up each cassolette with a portion of ragout a la financiere
11. Place the caps on the top of each, and put them in the oven for a few minutes to bring them to perfection
12. Serve the cassolettes upon a napkin, and garnish them with fried parsley
