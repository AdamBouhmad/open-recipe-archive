## Ingredients

- neck of mutton, trimmed into chops
- onion
- pepper corns
- scraps of beef
- chicken bones
- cold game
- lean ham or bacon
- two Bombay onions
- carrot
- young turnip
- celery
- two tomatoes
- curled parsley
- liqueur glass of brandy
- table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup
- salt-spoonful of salt
- salt-spoonful of sugar
- black pepper

## Directions

1. Choose a good neck of mutton, and trim the little chops as neatly as possible.
2. With the trimmings of meat and bone make a broth, assisted by an onion, some pepper corns, any scraps of beef, chicken bones, cold game, lean ham or bacon, in short any useful sundries.
3. When done, skim, and strain it, you ought to have a breakfast-cupful of it.
4. Scald the tin and cool it in cold water.
5. Cover the bottom of the tin with slices of onion, and arrange the chops thereon.
6. Cover the chops with two Bombay onions sliced fine, a carrot sliced, a young turnip sliced, a stick of celery cut into half inch lengths, two tomatoes sliced and drained, and a bunch of curled parsley.
7. Pepper the chops pretty freely with black pepper before covering them.
8. Add to the broth a liqueur glass of brandy, a table-spoonful of mushroom ketchup, a salt-spoonful of salt, and the same of sugar.
9. Pour in the broth, and seal the lid of the tin, all round the rim, with stiff paste, fixing it securely.
10. Put the tin into boiling water, and keep it on the fire for two hours.
11. At the time of serving, the lid should be cut off, and the tin, wrapped in a napkin, should be placed upon an ordinary dish, and sent to table immediately.
