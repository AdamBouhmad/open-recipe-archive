## Ingredients

- sweet and ripe tal fruit
- 1 seer of milk
- 1/2 seer of tal fruit pulp
- raisins
- almonds
- pistachios
- cardamom seeds
- aniseeds
- camphor
- bread

## Directions

1. Skin a sweet and ripe tal fruit and separate the seeds.
2. Beat each a little with the hands and rub against a sieve or a small basket of bamboo or iron placing a plate beneath it.
3. The pulp of the fruit will be collected on the plate.
4. The pulp may be used now as it is but it tastes better if it is mixed with a little water and held high in a bag of linen till the whole of the water is drained off in drops.
5. Heat 1 seer of milk on very mild heat till it is reduced to 3 powas when add 1/2 seer of the pulp and stir constantly.
6. Take down when the mass is thick and gluey after mixing with raisins, pieces of almonds and pistachios, powders of cardamoms and aniseeds 1/2 tola each and very little camphor.
7. Serve when cool with bread.
