## Ingredients

- a quarter of a pound of the best rice
- a pint and a half of good boiling milk
- six ounces of sugar
- a coffee-cupful of boiling cream
- mixed dessert fruits (greengages, cherries, apricots)
- preserved ginger
- citron
- a table-spoonful of maraschino or noyeau
- eight eggs
- two whites of egg
- two ounces of sugar
- a coffee-cupful of whipped cream
- a liqueur-glass of liqueur

## Directions

1. Gently boil a quarter of a pound of the best rice till about half done, then drain it
2. Put the rice into a stew-pan with a pint and a half of good boiling milk, and six ounces of sugar
3. Add a coffee-cupful of boiling cream, stir well, and simmer till the rice is done
4. Let it get cold
5. Mince up some mixed dessert fruits, greengages, cherries, apricots, &c., (preserved ginger, and citron if you like)
6. Moisten the fruit mince with a table-spoonful of maraschino or noyeau and put the mince away upon a plate
7. Strain the milk and cream from the rice, add milk enough to fill the mould you have chosen, and turn it to a rich custard with eight eggs
8. Set the freezing pail in the ice, put the custard into it, and work it with the spatula till nearly frozen
9. Make meringue with two whites of egg: put two ounces of sugar into an enamelled sauce-pan, and heat it with a little water till nearly boiling
10. Whip two whites of egg, and add them to the sugar, whipping all the time
11. Add the rice and meringue to the nearly frozen custard
12. Continue working the spatula, and when the mixture is all but frozen, add a coffee-cupful of whipped cream, the minced fruit, and a liqueur-glass of liqueur
13. Stir well, freeze a little longer, then fill your ice mould, and bury it in ice until it is required
