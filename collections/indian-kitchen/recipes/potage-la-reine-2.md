## Ingredients

- stock
- a cold roast fowl
- bread-crumbs
- twelve sweet almonds
- three bitter almonds
- the hard-boiled yolks of four eggs
- bones
- uncoloured beef stock
- an ounce of butter
- a tablespoonful of flour
- a coffee-cupful of cream or milk with the strained yolk of an egg

## Directions

1. Prepare your stock as usual but without colouring
2. Remove the flesh from a cold roast fowl, excluding all skin, and browned parts
3. Add to the meat so obtained, half its bulk of bread-crumbs soaked in stock
4. Pound both together in a mortar, with twelve sweet, and three bitter almonds, and the hard-boiled yolks of four eggs
5. Mash and cast all the bones into as much uncoloured beef stock as you think you will require for eight basins, and let them simmer for two or three hours
6. Pass your pounded fowl and crumbs through the sieve to get rid of lumps, gristle, &c., moistening it with a spoonful or so of stock to assist the operation
7. When near the dinner hour, strain off your stock from the bones, and place it to get cool, removing all the fat that may rise to the surface
8. Take a sauce-pan and melt an ounce of butter at the bottom of it, stirring in a tablespoonful of flour
9. Add a little stock, and work the paste so obtained without ceasing, gradually pouring in stock, and adding pounded fowl, until you have exhausted your supply
10. Let the puree now come to the boil
11. Remove the sauce-pan from the fire, and as you pour it into the tureen, stir into it a coffee-cupful of cream, (or that quantity of milk with which the strained yolk of an egg has been mixed) and serve
