## Ingredients

- ham
- cold water
- Madeira or Marsala (optional, for festivals)
- three carrots
- one head of celery
- three Bombay onions
- one clove of garlic
- a dozen whole peppers
- pieces of lime peel
- bunches of parsley
- thyme
- marjoram
- fine crust raspings or pounded baked crumbs

## Directions

1. Soak the ham for twenty-four hours, changing the water at least three times
2. Scrub the ham well and trim it, scraping off all discolorations
3. Place it in your ham-kettle and cover it with cold water (for a festival a bottle of Madeira or Marsala should be poured in with the water)
4. Let it come gently to the boil, removing all scum that may rise
5. When quite clear, throw in three carrots, a head of celery cut up (leaves and all), three Bombay onions, a bag containing a clove of garlic, a dozen whole peppers, and some pieces of lime peel, with bunches of parsley, thyme, and marjoram
6. Boil on till the vegetables are done, and then let the kettle simmer gently for four or five hours
7. When done, let it remain till nearly cold
8. Lift the ham from the water and detach the outer skin (it will roll off easily)
9. Dredge some fine crust raspings or some pounded baked crumbs over it
