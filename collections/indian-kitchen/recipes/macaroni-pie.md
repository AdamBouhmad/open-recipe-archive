## Ingredients

- half a pound of macaroni
- milk
- two pounds of beef
- ground white pepper
- salt
- soup herbs
- two ounces of English cheese
- a dessertspoonful of very dry mustard
- half a teaspoonful of very finely powdered white pepper
- about two teaspoonfuls of salt
- two chittacks or four ounces of butter
- a couple of crisp biscuits

## Directions

1. Take half a pound of macaroni, boil and throw away the first water.
2. Then boil it again in some milk, and remove when it is quite tender.
3. Prepare a strong gravy or soup with two pounds of beef, well seasoned with ground white pepper, salt, and soup herbs.
4. Bruise into fine powder two ounces of some good English cheese.
5. Pound very fine a couple of crisp biscuits.
6. Pour over the boiled macaroni sufficient beef gravy or stock to entirely cover it.
7. Put in all the pepper, salt, and mustard, but only half the ground cheese.
8. Set it to simmer over a slow fire until the gravy begins to dry, and the macaroni acquires some consistency.
9. With three ounces of butter butter well the baking-dish.
10. Pour into it the macaroni.
11. Mix the remaining ground cheese with the powdered biscuit, and strew it over the pie.
12. Cut into small pieces the remaining ounce of butter, and throw that also over the pie.
13. Put the dish into an oven, and bake to a fine light but rich brown colour for ten to fifteen minutes.
