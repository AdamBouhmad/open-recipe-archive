## Ingredients

- a well-nurtured chicken or young fowl
- cold water
- a couple of good-sized onions
- two table-spoonfuls of butter
- a couple of table-spoonfuls of mulligatunny paste or curry-powder
- a couple of pints of warm water
- a couple of ounces of almonds
- a coffee-cupful of milk
- a pinch of sugar
- a dessert-spoonful of good chutney
- a tea-spoonful of red currant jelly
- a tea-spoonful of lime-juice
- a table-spoonful of butter
- one of flour
- muslin

## Directions

1. Cut up a well-nurtured chicken or young fowl as if for fricassee, soak the pieces in cold water for a quarter of an hour
2. Slice up a couple of good-sized onions, and put them, with two table-spoonfuls of butter, into a stew-pan on a good fire
3. Fry the chicken and onions together till slightly browned
4. Pick out the chicken, and stir into the butter a couple of table-spoonfuls of mulligatunny paste or curry-powder
5. Cook the paste or powder with the butter and onions for five minutes
6. Stir in a couple of pints of warm water
7. Add the chicken; and if the pieces are not quite covered, put in water enough to do so
8. Let the contents come to the boil, then ease off the fire, and simmer for half an hour very gently
9. Pound a couple of ounces of almonds in a mortar, with a coffee-cupful of milk, give it a pinch of sugar, and let the mixture stand till wanted
10. Ascertain that the chicken is quite tender, then stir in a dessert-spoonful of good chutney, a tea-spoonful of red currant jelly, and a tea-spoonful of lime-juice
11. After five minutes' simmering, strain off the whole of the liquid into a bowl
12. Pick out the nicest pieces of chicken for garnish, and put them aside
13. Skim the surface of the liquid, and when quite clear of grease, proceed to thicken it, using a table-spoonful of butter and one of flour, and stirring in the soup slowly
14. Strain into the sauce-pan the almond milk, using a piece of muslin in order to catch up the bits of nut
15. Let the mulligatunny come to the boil, and serve
