## Ingredients

- a teal
- oil
- lime juice
- sliced onion
- sweet herbs
- gravy making concomitants
- butter
- flour
- a tea-spoonful of red currant jelly
- a dessert-spoonful of lime juice
- caramel colouring
- a glass of red wine or Madeira
- a few drops of tabasco or chilli vinegar

## Directions

1. Cut off the breasts of the teal, whole, bones and all, and divide each in half
2. Marinade these pieces all day in a little oil, lime juice, sliced onion, and sweet herbs
3. Grill them over a bright clear fire
4. Send them up piping hot, and full of juice straight from the gridiron
5. Throw every atom of the backs, legs, giblets, etc., into a sauce-pan with the usual concomitants of gravy making
6. Distil their essence by gentle simmering
7. Strain when done
8. Pick all the meat from the fragments and pound it a la puree
9. Add it, with butter and flour thickening, to the gravy of the bones
10. Judiciously introduce a tea-spoonful of red currant jelly, a dessert-spoonful of lime juice, some caramel colouring and a glass of red wine, or Madeira, with a few drops of tabasco or chilli vinegar
11. Send the sauce round in a boat, as hot as possible, following the grilled breasts
