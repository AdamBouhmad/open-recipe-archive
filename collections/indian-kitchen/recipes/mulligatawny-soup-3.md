## Ingredients

- a shin of beef
- pepper
- salt
- water
- a full-sized curry chicken
- two chittacks or four ounces of ghee
- onions
- four teaspoonfuls of ground onions
- one teaspoonful of ground turmeric
- one teaspoonful of ground chilies
- half a teaspoonful of ground ginger
- a quarter of a teaspoonful of ground garlic
- half a teaspoonful of roasted and ground coriander-seed
- a quarter of a teaspoonful of roasted and ground cumin-seed
- two teaspoonfuls of salt
- one chittack or two ounces of roasted and ground poppy-seeds
- half a dozen of the kurreeah fool leaves
- limes

## Directions

1. Prepare a shin of beef soup as above, omitting the sauce, wine, and white of egg
2. Set the soup aside
3. Take a full-sized curry chicken; cut it up into sixteen or eighteen pieces, and wash them thoroughly
4. Warm a pot and melt in it two chittacks or four ounces of ghee
5. Fry in it some finely-sliced onions, and set aside
6. Then fry in the melted ghee the following condiments: four teaspoonfuls of ground onions, one teaspoonful of ground turmeric, one teaspoonful of ground chilies, half a teaspoonful of ground ginger, a quarter of a teaspoonful of ground garlic, half a teaspoonful of roasted and ground coriander-seed, and a quarter of a teaspoonful of roasted and ground cumin-seed
7. Sprinkle a little water over these while frying
8. Then add the cut up chicken with two teaspoonfuls of salt
9. When nearly brown, add one chittack or two ounces of roasted and ground poppy-seeds
10. Pour in the beef soup, add the fried onion and half a dozen of the kurreeah fool leaves
11. Close the pot, and allow the whole to simmer over a slow fire until the chicken be perfectly tender
12. Serve up hot, with limes cut in slices on a separate plate
