## Ingredients

- shell oysters
- salt
- butter

## Directions

1. Improvise a steamer by the help of a cullender and a pot-lid fitting closely into it, at a little distance from the top.
2. Wash some shell oysters and lay them in such a position in the bottom of the cullender that the liquor will not escape from them when the shell opens, that is, with the upper shell down.
3. Cover with a cloth thrown over the top of the cullender, and press the lid hard down upon this to exclude the air.
4. Set over a pot of boiling water so deep that the cullender, which should fit into the mouth, does not touch the water.
5. Boil hard for twenty minutes, then make a hasty examination of the oysters.
6. If they are open, you are safe in removing the cover.
7. Serve on the half-shell, or upon a hot chafing dish.
8. Sprinkle a little salt over them and a few bits of butter; but be quick in whatever you do, for the glory of the steamed oyster is to be eaten hot.
