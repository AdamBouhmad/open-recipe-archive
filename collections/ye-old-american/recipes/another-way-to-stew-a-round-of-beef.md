## Ingredients

- a round of fresh beef
- water
- vinegar
- salt
- two or three large onions
- a few cloves
- a little whole black pepper
- a large glass of port or claret
- butter
- flour

## Directions

1. The day before you cook it, lay the beef in a pickle made of equal proportions of water and vinegar with salt to your taste.
2. Next morning take it out of the pickle, put it into a large pot or stew-pan, and just cover it with water.
3. Put in with it two or three large onions, a few cloves, a little whole black pepper, and a large glass of port or claret (if a whole round of beef allow two glasses of wine).
4. Stew it slowly for at least four hours or more, in proportion to its size. It must be thoroughly done, and tender all through.
5. An hour before you send it to table take the meat out of the pot, and pour the gravy into a pan.
6. Put a large lump of butter into the pot, dredge the beef with flour, and return it to the pot to brown, turning it often to prevent its burning.
7. Or put it into a Dutch oven. Cover the lid with hot coals, renewing them as they go out.
8. Take the gravy that you poured from the meat, and skim off all the fat.
9. Put it into a sauce-pan, and mix with it a little butter rolled in flour, and add some more cloves and wine.
10. Give it a boil up. If it is not well browned, burn some sugar on a hot shovel, and stir it in.
11. Send it to table hot, with the gravy poured round it.
