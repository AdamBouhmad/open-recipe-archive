## Ingredients

- lean slices from the leg of pork or bits left from trimming
- cold water
- Irish potatoes
- pepper
- salt
- minced shallot
- pungent catsup
- aromatic herbs

## Directions

1. Cut into dice an inch square, put into a pot with enough cold water to cover them.
2. Stew gently for three-quarters of an hour, closely covered.
3. Meanwhile parboil half a dozen Irish potatoes, cut in thick slices, in another vessel.
4. When the pork has stewed the allotted time, drain off the water from the potatoes and add to the meat.
5. Season with pepper, salt, a minced shallot, a spoonful of pungent catsup, and a bunch of aromatic herbs.
6. Cover again, and stew twenty minutes longer, or until the meat is tender throughout.
7. You can stew cutlets in the same way.
