## Ingredients

- a pint of new milk
- thin rind of a lemon
- a small pinch of salt
- three-quarters of an ounce of the finest isinglass or gelatine
- five ounces of powdered sugar
- half a pint of rich cream
- yolks of five eggs
- orange-flower water or vanilla
- two ounces of citron cut in thin strips
- two ounces of candied cherries

## Directions

1. Throw into a pint of new milk the thin rind of a lemon, heat it slowly by the side of the fire and keep at the boiling point until strongly flavored
2. Sprinkle in a small pinch of salt and three-quarters of an ounce of the finest isinglass or gelatine
3. When dissolved, strain through muslin into a clean saucepan with five ounces of powdered sugar and half a pint of rich cream
4. Give the whole one boil, stir it briskly and add by degrees the well-beaten yolks of five eggs
5. Thicken the mixture as a custard over a slow fire, taking care not to keep it over the fire a moment longer than necessary
6. Pour it into a basin and flavor with orange-flower water or vanilla
7. Stir until nearly cold, then add two ounces of citron cut in thin strips and two ounces of candied cherries
8. Pour into a buttered mold
9. Use any kind of fruit syrup for sauce
