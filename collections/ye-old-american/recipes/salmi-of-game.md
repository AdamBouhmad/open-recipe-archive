## Ingredients

- Cold roast partridges, grouse, or quails
- 1 small onion, minced
- A bunch of sweet herbs
- 1 pint of water
- Gravy
- A few bits of pork
- Brown Sherry
- Lemon-juice
- A pinch of nutmeg
- Brown flour

## Directions

1. Cut cold roast partridges, grouse, or quails into joints, and lay aside while you prepare the gravy.
2. Put the scraps—bones, dressing, skin, and general odds and ends—into a saucepan, with one small onion, minced, and a bunch of sweet herbs.
3. Pour in a pint of water, and whatever gravy you may have, and stew, closely covered, for nearly an hour.
4. A few bits of pork should be added if you have no gravy.
5. Skim and strain, return to the fire, and add a little brown Sherry and lemon-juice, with a pinch of nutmeg.
6. Thicken with brown flour, if the stuffing has not thickened it sufficiently, boil up, and pour over the reserved meat, which should be put into another saucepan.
7. Warm until all is smoking-hot, but do not let it boil.
8. Arrange the pieces of bird in a symmetrical heap upon a dish, and pour the gravy over them.
