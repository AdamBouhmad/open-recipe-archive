## Ingredients

- Limes or small lemons
- Alum-water
- Cold water
- Ginger tea
- Vine-leaves

## Directions

1. Take limes or small lemons that are quite ripe and all about the same size
2. With a sharp penknife scoop a hole at the stalk end of each and loosen the pulp all around the inside, taking care not to break or cut through the rind
3. Hold the lime over a bowl and extract all the pulp and juice, saving them in the bowl
4. Boil the empty limes half an hour or more in alum-water till the rinds look clear and nearly transparent
5. Drain them and lay them for several hours in cold water, changing the water nearly every hour
6. At night, change the water once more and let the limes remain in it till next day
7. If the taste of the alum is not removed, boil them in some weak ginger tea
8. If you wish them very green, line the sides and bottom of a preserving-kettle with fresh vine-leaves placed very thickly, put in the limes, and pour on as much clear cold water as will cover them
9. Fill up with a very thick layer of vine-leaves
10. Boil them slowly an hour or more
11. If they are not sufficiently green, repeat the process with fresh vine-leaves and fresh water
12. They must boil till a twig can pierce them
