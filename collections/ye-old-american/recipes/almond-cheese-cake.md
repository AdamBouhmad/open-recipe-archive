## Ingredients

- a piece of rennet about two inches square
- a quart of milk
- half a pound of currants
- flour
- three ounces of sweet almonds
- one ounce of bitter almonds
- rose water
- the yolks of eight eggs
- five table-spoonfuls of cream
- a tea-spoonful of mixed spice
- puff paste

## Directions

1. Cut a piece of rennet about two inches square, wash off the salt in cold water, and wipe it dry.
2. Put it into a tea-cup, pour on it sufficient luke-warm water to cover it, and let it soak all night, or at least several hours.
3. Take a quart of milk, which must be made warm, but not boiling.
4. Stir the rennet-water into it.
5. Cover it, and set it in a warm place.
6. When the curd has become quite firm, and the whey looks greenish, drain off the whey, and set the curd in a cool place.
7. While the milk is turning, prepare the other ingredients.
8. Wash and dry half a pound of currants, and dredge them well with flour.
9. Blanch three ounces of sweet and one ounce of bitter almonds, by scalding and peeling them.
10. Then cool them in cold water, wiping them dry before you put them into the mortar.
11. Beat them, one at a time, in the mortar to a smooth paste, pouring in with every one a few drops of rose water to prevent their being oily, dull-coloured, and heavy.
12. Mix, as you do them, the sweet and bitter almonds together.
13. Then beat the yolks of eight eggs, and when light, mix them gradually with the curd.
14. Add five table-spoonfuls of cream, and a tea-spoonful of mixed spice.
15. Lastly, stir in, by degrees, the pounded almonds, and the currants alternately.
16. Stir the whole mixture very hard.
17. Bake it in buttered dishes, laying puff paste round the edges.
18. It must be put in the oven immediately.
