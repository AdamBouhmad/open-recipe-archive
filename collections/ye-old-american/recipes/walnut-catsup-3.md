## Ingredients

- one hundred walnuts
- six ounces of shallots
- one head of garlic
- half a pound of salt
- two quarts of vinegar
- two ounces of anchovies
- two ounces of pepper
- a quarter of an ounce of mace
- half an ounce of cloves

## Directions

1. Beat in a large mortar a hundred green walnuts until they are thoroughly broken; then put them into a jar with six ounces of shallots cut into pieces, a head of garlic, two quarts of vinegar and the half pound of salt.
2. Let them stand for a fortnight, stirring them twice a day.
3. Strain off the liquor, put into a stewpan with the anchovies, whole pepper, half an ounce of cloves and a quarter of an ounce of mace.
4. Boil it half an hour, skimming it well.
5. Strain it off, and, when cold, pour it clear from any sediment into small bottles, cork it down closely and store it in a dry place.
6. The sediment can be used for flavoring sauces.
