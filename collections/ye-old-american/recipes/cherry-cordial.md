## Ingredients

- a bushel of fine ripe cherries
- a quart of water per quart of juice
- a pound of sugar per quart of juice
- a quart of brandy per quart of juice
- two ounces of cloves
- four ounces of cinnamon

## Directions

1. Stone the cherries and put them into a clean wooden vessel.
2. Mash them with a mallet or beetle.
3. Boil them about ten minutes and strain the juice.
4. To each quart of juice allow a quart of water, a pound of sugar, and a quart of brandy.
5. Boil in the water two ounces of cloves and four ounces of cinnamon.
6. Strain out the spice.
7. Put the mixture into a stone jug or a demijohn and cork it tightly.
8. Bottle it in two or three months.
