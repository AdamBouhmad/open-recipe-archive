## Ingredients

- fine large pine-apples
- fine loaf-sugar
- water
- white of egg

## Directions

1. Take fine large pine-apples; pare them, and cut off a small round piece from the bottom of each; let the freshest and best of the top leaves remain on.
2. Have ready on a slow fire, a large preserving kettle with a thin syrup barely sufficient to cover the fruit.
3. Allow a pound of fine loaf-sugar to every quart of water, and half the white of a beaten egg; all to be mixed before it goes on the fire.
4. Boil and skim the syrup, and when the scum ceases to rise, put in the pine-apples, and simmer them slowly an hour.
5. Take them out to cool, cover them carefully and put them away till next day; saving the syrup in another vessel.
6. Next day, put them into the same syrup, and simmer them again an hour.
7. On the third day, repeat the process.
8. The fourth day, make a strong fresh syrup, allowing but a pint of water to each pound of sugar, and to every two pounds the beaten white of one egg.
9. When this syrup has boiled, and is completely skimmed, put in the pine-apples, and simmer them half an hour.
10. Take them out to cool, and set them aside till next morning.
11. Boil them again half an hour in the same syrup, and repeat this for seven or eight days, or till you can pierce through the pine-apple with a straw from a corn-broom.
12. At the last of these boilings enrich the syrup by allowing to each pound of sugar a quarter of a pound more; and, having boiled and skimmed it, put in the pine apples for half an hour.
13. Take them out, and when quite cold put each into a separate glass jar, and fill up with the syrup.
