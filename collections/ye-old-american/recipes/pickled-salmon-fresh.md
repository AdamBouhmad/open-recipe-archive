## Ingredients

- salmon
- salted water
- cloth
- vinegar
- mace blades
- white peppers
- cloves
- mustard
- white sugar
- water from boiling the fish
- earthenware jar
- bell-metal kettle
- glass jars
- shallot
- celery
- cayenne pepper

## Directions

1. Having cleaned your fish, cut into pieces of a convenient size to go into the fish-kettle, and boil in salted water as for the table.
2. Drain it very dry, wipe it with a clean cloth, and set it aside in a cool place until next morning.
3. Make pickle enough to cover it in the following proportions: 2 quarts vinegar, a dozen blades of mace, dozen white peppers, dozen cloves, two teaspoonfuls made mustard, three tablespoonfuls white sugar, and a pint of the water in which the fish was boiled.
4. Let them boil up once hard, that you may skim the pickle.
5. Should the spices come away with the scum in large quantities, pick them out and return to the kettle.
6. Set the liquor away in an earthenware jar, closely covered, to keep in the flavor.
7. Next morning hang it over a brisk fire in a bell-metal kettle (covered), and heat to boiling.
8. Meanwhile, prepare the salmon by cutting into pieces an inch and a half long and half an inch wide.
9. Cut cleanly and regularly with a sharp knife.
10. When they are all ready, and the liquor is on the boil, drop them carefully into the kettle.
11. Let the pickle boil up once to make sure the salmon is heated through.
12. Have ready some air-tight glass jars, such as you use for canning fruit and tomatoes.
13. Take the salmon from the kettle, while it is still on the stove or range, with a wire-egg-beater, taking care you do not break the pieces.
14. Drop them rapidly into the jar, packing closely as you go on; fill with the boiling pickle until it overflows, screw on the top, and set away in a dark, cool place.
15. Proceed in the same way with each can until all are full.
