## Ingredients

- half-pound paper of corn-starch flour
- quart of milk
- four heaping table-spoonfuls of corn-starch
- three eggs
- tea-spoonful of extract of bitter almonds, or of vanilla, or a wine-glass of rose-water
- quarter of a pound of powdered loaf-sugar

## Directions

1. Boil a quart of milk, taking out of it a large tea-cup-full, which you may put into a pan
2. While the milk is boiling, mix with the cold milk four heaping table-spoonfuls of the corn-starch
3. Beat three eggs very light, and stir them into the mixture
4. Flavour it with a tea-spoonful of extract of bitter almonds, or of vanilla, or a wine-glass of rose-water
5. Add a quarter of a pound of powdered loaf-sugar, and stir the whole well together
6. When the other milk is boiling hard, pour it gradually on the mixture in the pan, which mixture will thicken while the milk is pouring
7. Transfer it to blancmange moulds, first wetting them with cold water, and set them in a cold place till dinner-time
8. Eat it with cream and serve up sweetmeats at the same time
