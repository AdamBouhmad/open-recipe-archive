## Ingredients

- cucumbers, very small
- salt and water
- fresh cabbage leaves
- fresh vine leaves
- alum, a small piece
- vinegar, best quality
- cloves
- mace
- mustard seed

## Directions

1. Make a brine of salt and water strong enough to bear an egg
2. Pour it over your cucumbers, cover them with fresh cabbage leaves, and let them stand for a week, or till they are quite yellow, stirring them at least twice a day
3. When they are perfectly yellow, pour off the water
4. Take a porcelain kettle, and cover the bottom and sides with fresh vine leaves
5. Put in the cucumbers with a small piece of alum and cover them closely with vine leaves all over the top, and then with a dish or cloth to keep in the steam
6. Fill up the kettle with clear water, and hang it over the fire when dinner is done, but not where there is a blaze
7. The fire under the kettle must be kept very moderate
8. The water must not boil, or be too hot to bear your hand in
9. Keep them over the fire in a slow heat till next morning
10. If they are not then of a fine green, repeat the process
11. When they are well greened, take them out of the kettle, drain them on a sieve, and put them into a clean stone jar
12. Boil for five or six minutes sufficient of the best vinegar to cover the cucumbers well, putting into the kettle a thin muslin bag filled with cloves, mace, and mustard seed
13. Pour the vinegar scalding hot into the jar of pickles, which should be secured with a large flat cork, and an oil-cloth or leather cover tied over it
