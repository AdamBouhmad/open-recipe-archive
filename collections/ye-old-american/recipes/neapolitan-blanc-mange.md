## Ingredients

- 1 quart of milk
- 1 oz. Cooper's gelatine
- 3 ozs. of almonds, blanched and pounded in a mortar
- 1 tablespoonful of rose-water
- ¾ cup sugar
- 1 great tablespoonful chocolate, wet with a very little boiling water
- Yolk of an egg beaten light
- 1 great tablespoonful of currant jelly

## Directions

1. Make according to the Almond Blanc-Mange receipt, and, after straining, separate into four different portions, allowing about a cupful of the mixture for each.
2. Beat the chocolate into one portion, mixing it well; the jelly into another, the egg into a third, returning this and that flavored with chocolate, to the fire, and stirring until very hot, but not boiling.
3. Leave the fourth uncolored.
4. When quite cold and a little stiff, pour carefully into a wet mould—the white first; then the pink; next the yellow; and the chocolate last.
5. Set in a cold place.
6. Loosen, when firm, by dipping the mould for a moment in warm water, and working the top free from the edge with a few light touches of your fingers.
