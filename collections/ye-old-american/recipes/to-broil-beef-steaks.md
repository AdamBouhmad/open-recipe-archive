## Ingredients

- beef steaks from the ribs or inside of the sirloin
- suet
- salt
- pepper
- butter
- shallot or small onion
- catchup

## Directions

1. Cut beef steaks about three quarters of an inch thick from the ribs or from the inside of the sirloin.
2. Beat them on both sides with a steak mallet, or with a rolling-pin.
3. Do not season them till you take them from the fire.
4. Have ready on your hearth a fine bed of clear bright coals, entirely free from smoke and ashes.
5. Set the gridiron over the coals in a slanting direction, that the meat may not be smoked by the fat dropping into the fire directly under it.
6. When the gridiron is quite hot, rub the bars with suet, sprinkle a little salt over the coals, and lay on the steaks.
7. Turn them frequently with a pair of steak-tongs, or with a knife and fork.
8. A quarter of an hour is generally sufficient time to broil a beef-steak.
9. When the fat blazes and smokes very much as it drips into the fire, quickly remove the gridiron for a moment, till the blaze has subsided.
10. After they are browned, cover the upper side of the steaks with an inverted plate or dish to prevent the flavour from evaporating.
11. Rub a dish with a shallot, or small onion, and place it near the gridiron and close to the fire, that it may be well heated.
12. In turning the steak drop the gravy that may be standing on it into this dish, to save it from being lost.
13. When the steaks are done, sprinkle them with a little salt and pepper, and lay them in a hot dish, putting on each a piece of fresh butter.
14. Season them with a very little raw shallot, minced as finely as possible, and moistened with a spoonful of water.
15. Stir a tea-spoonful of catchup into the gravy.
16. Send the steaks to table very hot, in a covered dish.
