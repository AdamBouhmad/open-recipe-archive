## Ingredients

- aitch-bone or brisket
- cold water
- horse-radish
- pickles
- cabbage
- white turnips
- carrots
- parsnips
- potatoes
- beets
- vinegar
- salt pork

## Directions

1. Put the beef into a pot and cover with sufficient cold water
2. Place over a brisk fire and let come to a boil in half an hour
3. Just before boiling remove all the scum from the pot
4. Place the pot on the back of the fire and let it boil very slowly until quite tender
5. A piece weighing eight pounds requires two and a half hours' boiling
6. If not eating hot, let remain in the pot until nearly cold, then lay in a colander to drain
7. Lay a cloth over it to retain its fresh appearance
8. To serve with vegetables as an old-fashioned boiled dinner, about three-quarters of an hour before dishing up skim the liquor free from fat and turn part into another kettle
9. Put a cabbage carefully prepared and cut into four quarters into the kettle
10. Add half a dozen peeled medium-sized white turnips cut into halves
11. Scrape four carrots and four parsnips each cut into four pieces
12. About half an hour before serving, pour more boiling water into the kettle with the meat and add peeled medium-sized potatoes
13. Cook cabbage separately from the meat
14. Cook carrots, parsnips and turnips separately, boiling about an hour
15. Cook beets separately, slice hot and add vinegar
16. Serve with horse-radish and pickles
