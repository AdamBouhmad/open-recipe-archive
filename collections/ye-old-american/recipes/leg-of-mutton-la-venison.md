## Ingredients

- mutton
- salt
- celery-salt
- brown sugar
- black pepper
- English mustard
- allspice
- sweet herbs
- vinegar
- water
- flour
- currant jelly

## Directions

1. Remove all the rough fat from the mutton and lay it in a deep earthen dish; rub into it thoroughly one tablespoonful of salt, one each of celery-salt, brown sugar, black pepper, English mustard, allspice, and some sweet herbs, all powdered and mixed.
2. Pour over it slowly a teacup of good vinegar, cover tightly, and set in a cool place four or five days, turning it and basting often with the liquid each day.
3. To cook, put in a kettle a quart of boiling water, place over it an inverted shallow pan, and on it lay the meat just as removed from the pickle; cover the kettle tightly and stew for four hours.
4. Do not let the water touch the meat.
5. Add a cup of hot water to the pickle remaining and baste with it.
6. When done, thicken the liquid with flour and strain through a fine sieve, to serve with the meat; also a relish of currant jelly, the same as for venison.
