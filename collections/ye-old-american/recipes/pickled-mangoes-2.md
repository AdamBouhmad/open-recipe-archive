## Ingredients

- young musk or nutmeg melons
- English mustard-seed, two handfuls
- scraped horseradish, one handful
- mace and nutmeg pounded, 1 teaspoonful
- chopped garlic, 2 teaspoonfuls
- ginger
- whole pepper-corns, 1 dozen
- ground mustard, ½ tablespoonful to a pint of the mixture
- sugar, 1 teaspoonful to a pint of the mixture
- best salad oil, 1 teaspoonful to a pint of the mixture
- celery-seed, 1 teaspoonful
- strong brine
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Cut a slit in the side of the melon; insert your finger and extract all the seeds.
2. If you cannot get them out in this way, cut a slender piece out, saving it to replace,—but the slit is better.
3. Lay the mangoes in strong brine for three days.
4. Drain off the brine, and freshen in pure water twenty-four hours.
5. Green as you would cucumbers, and lay in cold water until cold and firm.
6. Fill with the stuffing; sew up the slit, or tie up with pack thread; pack in a deep stone jar, and pour scalding vinegar over them.
7. Repeat this process three times more at intervals of two days, then tie up and set away in a cool, dry place.
8. They will not be "ripe" under four months, but are very fine when they are.
