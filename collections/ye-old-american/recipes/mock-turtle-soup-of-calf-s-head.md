## Ingredients

- a well-cleansed calf's head
- butter
- a quarter of a pound of lean ham
- fine herbs
- parsley
- one onion
- a pint of the broth
- flour
- a quarter bottle of Madeira or sherry
- cayenne pepper
- salt
- juice of one lemon
- a quarter teaspoonful pounded mace
- a dessert-spoon sugar
- force meat balls

## Directions

1. Scald a well-cleansed calf's head.
2. Remove the brain and tie it up in a cloth.
3. Boil an hour, or until the meat will easily slip from the bone.
4. Take out and save the broth.
5. Cut it in small square pieces and throw them into cold water.
6. When cool, put it in a stewpan and cover with some of the broth.
7. Let it boil until quite tender and set aside.
8. In another stewpan melt some butter.
9. Put in a quarter of a pound of lean ham, cut small, with fine herbs to taste, parsley and one onion.
10. Add about a pint of the broth.
11. Let it simmer for two hours.
12. Dredge in a small quantity of flour.
13. Add the remainder of the broth, and a quarter bottle of Madeira or sherry.
14. Let all stew quietly for ten minutes.
15. Rub it through a medium sieve.
16. Add the calf's head.
17. Season with a very little cayenne pepper, a little salt, the juice of one lemon, and if desired, a quarter teaspoonful pounded mace and a dessert-spoon sugar.
18. Add force meat balls.
19. Serve hot five minutes after.
