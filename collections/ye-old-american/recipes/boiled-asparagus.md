## Ingredients

- Asparagus
- salt
- bread
- melted butter

## Directions

1. Set on the fire a pot with plenty of water and sprinkle into it a handful of salt
2. Select large or full grown asparagus all of the same size
3. Scrape the stalks till they are perfectly nice and white
4. Cut them all of equal length, short, so as to leave them but two or three inches below the green part
5. As you scrape them, throw them into a pan of cold water
6. Tie them up in small bundles with bass or tape
7. When the water is boiling fast, put in the asparagus and boil it an hour; if old it will require an hour and a quarter
8. When it is nearly done boiling, toast a large slice of bread, cut off the crust, and dip it into the asparagus water
9. Lay it in a dish and place the drained asparagus on the toast with all the heads pointed inwards towards the centre and the stalks spreading outwards
10. Serve up melted butter with it
