## Ingredients

- milk
- cream
- salt
- white sugar
- wooden spoon
- clean earthen basin
- clean pans

## Directions

1. Immediately after the cow is milked, strain the milk into clean pans, and set it over a moderate fire until it is scalding hot; do not let it boil
2. Set it aside; when it is cold, skim off the cream; the milk will still be fit for any ordinary use
3. When you have enough cream put it into a clean earthen basin; beat it with a wooden spoon until the butter is made, which will not be long
4. Take it from the milk and work it with a little cold water, until it is free from milk
5. Drain off the water, put a small tablespoonful of fine salt to each pound of butter and work it in
6. A small teaspoonful of fine white sugar, worked in with the salt, will be found an improvement
7. Make the butter in a roll; cover it with a bit of muslin and keep it in a cool place
