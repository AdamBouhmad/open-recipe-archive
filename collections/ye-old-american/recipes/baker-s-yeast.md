## Ingredients

- a gallon of soft water
- two quarts of wheat bran
- one quart of ground malt
- two handfuls of hops
- two large tea-cups of molasses
- half a pint of strong yeast
- pearl-ash
- warm water

## Directions

1. To a gallon of soft water put two quarts of wheat bran, one quart of ground malt, and two handfuls of hops.
2. Boil them together for half an hour.
3. Strain it through a sieve, and let it stand till it is cold.
4. Put to it two large tea-cups of molasses, and half a pint of strong yeast.
5. Pour it into a stone jug, and let it stand uncorked till next morning.
6. Pour off the thin liquid from the top, and cork the jug tightly.
7. When you are going to use the yeast, if it has been made two or three days, stir in a little pearl-ash dissolved in warm water, allowing a lump the size of a hickory-nut to a pint of yeast.
8. This will correct any tendency to sourness, and make the yeast more brisk.
