## Ingredients

- old potatoes
- salt
- butter
- milk
- pepper

## Directions

1. Pare potatoes and let them lie in cold water from half to three-quarters of an hour.
2. Boil in hot or cold water according to the toughness of texture, putting in a pinch of salt.
3. Drain thoroughly when done and sprinkle with salt.
4. Mash them in the pot with a potato-beetle or whip with a split spoon, working in a tablespoonful of butter and enough milk to make the paste about the consistency of soft dough.
5. Leave no lumps in it, and when smooth, dish.
6. Form into a mound with a wooden spoon and leave dots of pepper here and there on the surface, as large as a half-dime.
7. Alternatively, brown by setting in the oven until a crust is formed, glaze with butter, and serve.
