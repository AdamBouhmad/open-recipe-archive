## Ingredients

- 2½ lbs. veal chopped fine
- ¼ lb. pearl sago
- 1 pt. milk
- 4 eggs
- 3 qts. water
- pepper
- salt

## Directions

1. Put on the veal and water, and boil slowly until the liquid is reduced to about one-half the original quantity.
2. Strain out the shreds of meat, and put the soup again over the fire.
3. Meanwhile wash the sago in several waters, and soak half an hour in warm water enough to cover it.
4. Stir it into the strained broth and boil—stirring very often to prevent lumping or scorching—half an hour more.
5. Heat the milk almost to boiling.
6. Beat the yolks of the eggs very light.
7. Mix with the milk gradually, as in making boiled custard, and pour—stirring all the while—into the soup.
8. Season with pepper and salt.
9. Boil up once to cook the eggs, and serve.
10. Should the liquid be too thick after putting in the eggs, replenish with boiling water.
