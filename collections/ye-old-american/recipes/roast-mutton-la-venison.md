## Ingredients

- saddle of mutton
- vinegar
- cloth
- butter
- water
- walnut or mushroom or tomato catsup
- Madeira wine
- flour
- currant jelly or other tart jelly

## Directions

1. Wash it well, inside and out, with vinegar
2. Do not wipe it, but hang it up to dry in a cool cellar
3. When the vinegar has dried off, throw a clean cloth over it, to keep out the dust
4. On the next day but one, take down the meat and sponge it over again with vinegar, then put it back in its place in the cellar
5. Repeat this process three times a week for a fortnight, keeping the meat hung in a cold place, and covered, except while you are washing it
6. When you are ready to cook it, wipe it off with a dry cloth, but do not wash it
7. Roast—basting for the first hour with butter and water; afterward with the gravy, and keeping the meat covered with a large tin pan for two hours
8. A large saddle of mutton will require four hours to roast
9. When it is done, remove to a dish, and cover to keep it hot
10. Skim the gravy, and add half a teacupful of walnut, mushroom or tomato catsup, a glass of Madeira wine, and a tablespoonful of browned flour
11. Boil up once, and send to table in a sauce-boat
12. Send around currant or some other tart jelly with roast mutton
