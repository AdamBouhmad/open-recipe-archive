## Ingredients

- a stale five cent loaf of bread
- a quart of rich milk
- a quarter of a pound of butter
- a quarter of a pound of sugar
- a glass of wine and brandy mixed, or a glass of rose water, or the grated peel of a large lemon
- a table-spoonful of mixed cinnamon and nutmeg powdered
- seven or eight eggs

## Directions

1. Take a stale five cent loaf of bread; cut off all the crust, and grate or rub the crumb as fine as possible.
2. Boil a quart of rich milk, and pour it hot over the bread.
3. Stir in a quarter of a pound of butter, and the same quantity of sugar, a glass of wine and brandy mixed, or a glass of rose water, or the grated peel of a large lemon.
4. Add a table-spoonful of mixed cinnamon and nutmeg powdered.
5. Stir the whole very well, cover it, and set it away for half an hour.
6. Let it cool.
7. Beat seven or eight eggs very light, and stir them gradually into the mixture after it is cold.
8. Butter a deep dish, and bake the pudding an hour.
9. Send it to table cool.
