## Ingredients

- four maccaroons per cup
- three spoonfuls of white wine per cup
- one pint of cream
- one pint of milk
- one large stick of cinnamon
- a small bunch of peach leaves or a handful of broken bitter almonds
- a quarter of a pound of white sugar
- eight eggs (omitting the whites of four)

## Directions

1. Put four maccaroons into each china custard cup
2. Pour three spoonfuls of white wine on them
3. Mix together a pint of cream and a pint of milk, and boil them with a large stick of cinnamon broken up and a small bunch of peach leaves or a handful of broken bitter almonds
4. Strain the milk and stir in a quarter of a pound of white sugar, and set it away to cool
5. Beat very light eight eggs, omitting the whites of four, and stir them gradually into the cream and milk when quite cold
6. Fill your cups with the mixture, leaving the maccaroons at the bottom
7. Set them in a Dutch oven or iron baking pan which must be half full of boiling water
8. Heat the oven-lid first by standing it up before a hot fire, then put it on, spreading coals over the top
9. Place sufficient coals under the oven and bake the custards about ten minutes
10. When cold, heap beaten white of egg on the top of each
