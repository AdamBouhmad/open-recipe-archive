## Ingredients

- small white cabbage
- cauliflower
- cucumbers
- carrots
- beets
- button-onions
- string-beans
- radish pods
- barberries
- cherries
- green grapes
- nasturtians
- capsicums
- bell-peppers
- salt
- water
- vinegar
- white ginger root
- long pepper
- peeled shalots or little button-onions
- peeled garlic
- turmeric
- mustard seed or mustard powder
- sweet oil

## Directions

1. Have ready a small white cabbage sliced with the stalk removed; a cauliflower cut into neat branches; sliced cucumbers; sliced carrots; sliced beets nicked round the edges; button-onions; string-beans; radish pods; barberries; cherries; green grapes; nasturtians; capsicums; bell-peppers, &c.
2. Sprinkle all these things with salt, put them promiscuously into a large earthen pan, and pour scalding salt and water over them
3. Let them lie in the brine for four days, turning them all over every day
4. Take them out, wash each thing separately in vinegar, and wipe them carefully in a cloth
5. Lay them on sieves before the fire, and dry them thoroughly
6. To every two quarts of the best vinegar, put an ounce and a half of white ginger root scraped and sliced; the same of long pepper; two ounces of peeled shalots or little button-onions, cut in pieces; half an ounce of peeled garlic; an ounce of turmeric; and two ounces of mustard seed bruised, or of mustard powder
7. Let all these ingredients mixed with the vinegar infuse in a close jar for a week, setting in a warm place, or by the fire
8. Put the vegetables all into one large stone jar, or into smaller jars, and strain the pickle over them, keeping them well covered with liquid
9. Put a table-spoonful of sweet oil on the top of each jar, and secure them well with a large cork and a leather
10. If the vegetables have absorbed the liquor, prepare some more seasoned vinegar and pour it over them
