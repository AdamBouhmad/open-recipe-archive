## Ingredients

- beef-tongue
- lean veal
- pepper
- powdered sweet herbs
- made mustard
- nutmeg
- cloves
- salt
- melted butter
- parsley or blanched celery tops

## Directions

1. Boil a beef-tongue the day before it is to be used, and a like number of pounds of lean veal
2. Grind first one, then the other, in a sausage-cutter, keeping them in separate vessels until you are ready to pack
3. If you have no machine for this purpose, chop very fine
4. Season the tongue with pepper, powdered sweet herbs, a teaspoonful of made mustard, a little nutmeg, and cloves—just a pinch of each; the veal in like manner, with the addition of salt
5. Pack in alternate spoonfuls, as irregularly as possible, in cups, bowls, or jars, which have been well buttered
6. Press very hard as you go on, smooth the top, and cover with melted butter
7. When this cools, close the cans, and keep in a cool, dry place
8. Turn out whole, or cut in slices for tea
9. Garnish with parsley or the blanched tops of celery
