## Ingredients

- haunch of venison
- fresh butter or lard
- flour
- water
- white wrapping-paper
- pack-thread
- Claret
- butter
- flour
- pound of scraps of raw venison
- quart of water
- pinch of cloves
- blades of mace
- half a nutmeg
- cayenne
- salt
- currant jelly
- glass of claret
- two tablespoonfuls of butter
- browned flour

## Directions

1. If the outside be hard, wash off with lukewarm water
2. Rub all over with fresh butter or lard
3. Cover it on the top and sides with a thick paste of flour and water, nearly half an inch thick
4. Lay upon this a large sheet of thin white wrapping-paper well buttered, and above this thick foolscap
5. Keep all in place by greased pack-thread
6. Put down to roast with a little water in the dripping-pan
7. Let the fire be steady and strong
8. Pour a few ladlefuls of butter and water over the meat now and then, to prevent the paper from scorching
9. If the haunch is large, it will take at least five hours to roast
10. About half an hour before you take it up, remove the papers and paste, and test with a skewer to see if it is done
11. If the skewer passes easily to the bone through the thickest part, set it down to a more moderate fire and baste every few minutes with Claret and melted butter
12. At the last, baste with butter, dredge with flour to make a light froth, and dish
13. It should be a fine brown by this time
14. Twist a frill of fringed paper around the knuckle
15. For gravy, put into a saucepan a pound or so of scraps of raw venison left from trimming the haunch, a quart of water, a pinch of cloves, a few blades of mace, half a nutmeg, cayenne and salt to taste
16. Stew slowly to one-half the original quantity
17. Skim, strain, and return to the saucepan when you have rinsed it with hot water
18. Add three tablespoonfuls of currant jelly, a glass of claret, two tablespoonfuls of butter, and thicken with browned flour
19. Send to table in a tureen
20. Send around currant jelly with venison always
