## Ingredients

- table-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- tea-spoonful of powdered mace
- two nutmegs
- glass of white wine
- glass of brandy
- glass of rose water
- one pound of finest flour
- one pound of loaf-sugar
- one pound of fresh butter
- ten eggs
- juice of two lemons or three large oranges

## Directions

1. Prepare a table-spoonful of powdered cinnamon, a tea-spoonful of powdered mace, and two nutmegs grated or powdered.
2. Mix together in a tumbler, a glass of white wine, a glass of brandy, and a glass of rose water.
3. Sift a pound of the finest flour into a broad pan, and powder a pound of loaf-sugar.
4. Put the sugar into a deep pan, and cut up in it a pound of fresh butter.
5. Warm them by the fire till soft; and then stir them to a cream.
6. When they are perfectly light, add gradually the spice and liquor, a little at a time.
7. Beat ten eggs as light as possible, and stir them by degrees into the mixture alternately with the flour.
8. Then add the juice of two lemons or three large oranges.
9. Stir the whole very hard; put it into a deep tin pan with straight or upright sides, and bake it in a moderate oven from two to three hours.
