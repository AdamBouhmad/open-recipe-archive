## Ingredients

- remains of roasted game or birds scarcely more than half roasted
- two sliced onions
- a bay-leaf
- a small blade of mace
- a few peppercorns
- a pint or more of good veal gravy or strong broth
- a little cayenne and lemon juice
- fried bread

## Directions

1. Carve the game very neatly
2. Strip every particle of skin and fat from the legs, wings and breasts
3. Bruise the bodies well and put them with the skin and other trimmings into a very clean stewpan
4. Add two sliced onions, a bay-leaf, a small blade of mace and a few peppercorns
5. Pour in a pint or more of good veal gravy or strong broth and boil it briskly until reduced nearly half
6. Strain the gravy, pressing the bones well to obtain all the flavor
7. Skim off the fat and add a little cayenne and lemon juice
8. Heat the game very gradually in it but do not allow it to boil
9. Place pieces of fried bread around a dish
10. Arrange the birds in good form in the centre
11. Give the sauce a boil and pour it on them
