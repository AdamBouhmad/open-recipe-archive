## Ingredients

- plums
- sugar
- water

## Directions

1. Use a pound of sugar for a pound of plums
2. Wash the plums and wipe dry
3. Put the sugar on a slow fire in the preserving kettle, with as much water as will melt the sugar and let it simmer slowly
4. Prick each plum thoroughly with a needle, or a fork with fine prongs, and place a layer of them in the syrup
5. Let them cook until they lose their color a little and the skins begin to break
6. Lift them out with a perforated skimmer and place them singly in a large dish to cool
7. Put another layer of plums in the syrup and let them cook and cool in the same manner, until the whole are done
8. As they cool, carefully replace the broken skins so as not to spoil the appearance of the plums
9. When the last layer is finished, return the first to the kettle, and boil until transparent
10. Do the same with each layer
11. While the latest cooked are cooling, place the first in glass jars
12. When all are done, pour the hot syrup over them
13. When they are cold, close as usual
