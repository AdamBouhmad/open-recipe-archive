## Ingredients

- one pound of flour
- the yolk of one egg
- the juice of one lemon
- half a saltspoonful of salt
- cold water
- one pound of fresh butter

## Directions

1. Put the flour onto the paste-board; make a hole in the centre, into which put the yolk of the egg, the lemon juice and salt.
2. Mix the whole with cold water (this should be iced in summer if convenient) into a soft, flexible paste with the right hand, and handle it as little as possible.
3. Squeeze all the buttermilk from the butter, wring it in a cloth and roll out the paste.
4. Place the butter on this and fold the edges of the paste over, so as to hide it.
5. Roll it out again to the thickness of a quarter of an inch.
6. Fold over one-third, over which again pass the rolling-pin; then fold over the other third, thus forming a square.
7. Place it with the ends, top and bottom before you, shaking a little flour both under and over, and repeat the rolls and turns twice again as before.
8. Flour a baking-sheet, put the paste on this and let it remain on ice or in some cool place for half an hour.
9. Then roll twice more, turning it as before; place it again upon the ice for a quarter of an hour.
10. Give it two more rolls, making seven in all, and it is ready for use when required.
