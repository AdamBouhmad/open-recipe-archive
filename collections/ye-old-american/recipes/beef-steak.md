## Ingredients

- steak, at least three-quarters of an inch in thickness
- lemon-juice
- butter
- salt
- pepper
- onion

## Directions

1. If the meat is doubtful, lay it on a clean cloth, take a blunt heavy carving-knife, if you have not a steak mallet, and hack closely from one end to the other; then turn and repeat the process upon the other side. The knife should be so dull you cannot cut with it, and the strokes not the sixtieth part of an inch apart.
2. Wipe all over on both sides with lemon-juice, cover, and leave it in a cool place for one hour.
3. Lay on a buttered gridiron over a clear fire, turning very often as it begins to drip.
4. Do not season until it is done, which will be in about twelve minutes, if the fire is good and the cook attentive.
5. Rub your hot chafing dish with a split raw onion, lay in the steak, salt and pepper on both sides, and put a liberal lump of butter upon the upper.
6. Put on a hot cover, and let it stand five minutes to draw the juices to the surface before it is eaten.
7. If you have neither chafing-dish nor cover, lay the steak between two hot platters for the same time, sending to table without uncovering.
8. If a gridiron is not at hand, rub a little butter upon the bottom of a hot, clean frying-pan, put in the meat, set over a bright fire, and turn frequently.
