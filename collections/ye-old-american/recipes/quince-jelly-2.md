## Ingredients

- fine ripe yellow quinces
- clear spring water
- loaf-sugar

## Directions

1. Take fine ripe yellow quinces, wash them and remove all the blemishes.
2. Cut them in pieces, but do not pare or core them.
3. Put them into a preserving-pan with clear spring water. If you are obliged to use river water, filter it first; allowing one pint to twelve large quinces.
4. Boil them gently till they are all soft and broken.
5. Then put them into a jelly-bag, and do not squeeze it till after the clear liquid has ceased running.
6. Of this you must make the best jelly, allowing to each pint a pound of loaf-sugar.
7. Having dissolved the sugar in the liquid, boil them together about twenty minutes, or till you have a thick jelly.
8. In the mean time squeeze out all that is left in the bag. It will not be clear, but you can make of it a very good jelly for common purposes.
