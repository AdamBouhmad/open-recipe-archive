## Ingredients

- largest and finest oysters
- yolk of egg
- milk
- stale bread
- fresh butter

## Directions

1. Get the largest and finest oysters.
2. After they are taken from the shell wipe each of them quite dry with a cloth.
3. Then beat up in a pan yolk of egg and milk, (in the proportion of two yolks to half a jill or a wine glass of milk,) and have some stale bread grated very fine in a large flat dish.
4. Cut up at least half a pound of fresh butter in the frying-pan, and hold it over the fire till it is boiling hot.
5. Dip the oysters all over lightly in the mixture of egg and milk, and then roll them up and down in the grated bread, making as many crumbs stick to them as you can.
6. Put them into the frying-pan of hot butter, and keep it over a hot fire.
7. Fry them brown, turning them that they may be equally browned on both sides.
8. Serve them dry in a hot dish, and do not pour over them the butter that may be left in the pan when they are fried.
