## Ingredients

- one large calf's head
- one knuckle of veal
- hock of a ham or a few slices of bacon
- six potatoes
- three turnips
- parsley
- sweet marjoram
- sweet basil
- cayenne pepper
- minced veal
- beef-suet
- pepper
- salt
- sweet herbs
- grated lemon-peel
- powdered nutmeg
- powdered mace
- beaten yolk of egg
- flour
- butter
- half a pint of Madeira
- yolks of hard boiled eggs
- raw yolk of egg
- lemon

## Directions

1. Take a large calf's head, and having cleaned, washed, and soaked it, put it into a pot with a knuckle of veal, and the hock of a ham, or a few slices of bacon; but previously cut off and reserve enough of the veal to make two dozen small force-meat balls.
2. Put the head and the other meat into as much water as will cover it very well, so that it may not be necessary to replenish it: this soup being always made very rich.
3. Let it boil slowly four hours, skimming it carefully.
4. As soon as no more scum rises, put in six potatoes, and three turnips, all sliced thin; with equal proportions of parsley, sweet marjoram, and sweet basil, chopped fine; and cayenne pepper to your taste.
5. An hour before you send the meat to table, make about two dozen small force-meat balls of minced veal and beef-suet in equal quantities, seasoned with pepper and salt; sweet herbs, grated lemon-peel, and powdered nutmeg and mace.
6. Add some beaten yolk of egg to make all these ingredients stick together.
7. Flour the balls very well, and fry them in butter.
8. Before you put them into the soup, take out the head, and the other meat.
9. Cut the meat from the head in small pieces, and return it to the soup.
10. When the soup is nearly done, stir in half a pint of Madeira.
11. Have ready at least a dozen egg-balls made of the yolks of hard boiled eggs, grated or pounded in a mortar, and mixed with a little flour and sufficient raw yolk of egg to bind them.
12. Make them up into the form and size of boy's marbles.
13. Throw them into the soup at the last, and also squeeze in the juice of a lemon.
14. Let it get another slow boil, and then put it into the tureen.
