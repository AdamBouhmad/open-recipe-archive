## Ingredients

- a quart of small, white beans
- soda the size of a bean
- a tablespoonful of salt
- a pound of salt pork

## Directions

1. Pick over carefully the beans.
2. Let them soak over night in cold water.
3. In the morning wash and drain in another water.
4. Put on to boil in plenty of cold water with a piece of soda the size of a bean.
5. Let them come to a boil, then drain again, cover with water once more, and boil them fifteen minutes, or until the skin of the beans will crack when taken out and blown upon.
6. Drain the beans again, put them into an earthen pot, adding a tablespoonful of salt.
7. Cover with hot water, place in the centre a pound of salt pork, first scalding it with hot water, and scoring the rind across the top, a quarter of an inch apart to indicate where the slices are to be cut.
8. Place the pot in the oven, and bake six hours or longer.
9. Keep the oven a moderate heat.
10. Add hot water from the tea-kettle as needed, on account of evaporation, to keep the beans moist.
11. When the meat becomes crisp and looks cooked, remove it, as too long baking the pork destroys its solidity.
