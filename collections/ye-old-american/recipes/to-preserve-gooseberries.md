## Ingredients

- six quarts of gooseberries
- water
- loaf-sugar

## Directions

1. Select three quarts of the largest and finest gooseberries to preserve whole, reserving the others for the jelly.
2. Put the whole ones into a pan with sufficient water to cover them, and simmer them slowly till they begin to be tender, but do not keep them on the fire till they are likely to burst.
3. Take them out carefully with a perforated skimmer to drain the warm water from them, and lay them directly in a pan of cold water.
4. Put those that you intend for the jelly into a stew-pan, allowing to each quart of gooseberries half a pint of water.
5. Boil them fast till they go all to pieces, and stir and mash them with a spoon.
6. Put them into a jelly-bag that has been first dipped in hot water, and squeeze through it all the juice.
7. Measure the juice, and to each pint allow a pound and a half of loaf-sugar.
8. Break up the sugar, and put it into a preserving kettle, pour the juice over it, and let it stand to melt, stirring it frequently.
9. When it has all dissolved, set it over the fire, put the gooseberries into it, and let them boil twenty minutes, or till they are quite clear, and till the jelly is thick and congeals in the spoon.
10. When all is done, put up the gooseberries and the jelly together in glass jars.
