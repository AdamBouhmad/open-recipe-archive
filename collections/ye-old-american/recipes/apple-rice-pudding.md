## Ingredients

- half a pint of rice
- six large juicy apples
- water
- six table-spoonfuls of brown sugar
- five eggs
- five or six drops of essence of lemon
- grated nutmeg
- grated peel and juice of one large lemon
- flour
- sweetened cream
- beaten butter and sugar

## Directions

1. Wash half a pint of rice, and boil it till soft and dry.
2. Pare, core, and cut up six large juicy apples, and stew them in as little water as possible.
3. When they are quite tender, take them out, and mash them with six table-spoonfuls of brown sugar.
4. When the apples and rice are both cold, mix them together.
5. Have ready five eggs beaten very light, and add them gradually to the other ingredients, with five or six drops of essence of lemon, and a grated nutmeg.
6. Or you may substitute for the essence, the grated peel and the juice of one large lemon.
7. Beat the whole very hard after it is all mixed.
8. Tie it tightly in a cloth, leaving but a very small space for it to swell, and stopping up the tying place with a lump of flour moistened to paste with water.
9. Put it into a pot of boiling water, and boil it fast for half an hour.
10. Send it to table hot, and eat it with sweetened cream, or with beaten butter and sugar.
