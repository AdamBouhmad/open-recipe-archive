## Ingredients

- one pound of the very best and lightest bread-dough
- quarter of a pound of fresh butter
- half a pint of milk
- well-beaten egg
- sufficient flour
- heaping tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- soda or contents of the blue paper
- lukewarm water
- tartaric acid, or contents of the white paper
- lard or fresh butter
- brown sugar
- butter
- powdered cinnamon
- four heaping table-spoonfuls of sugar
- two large spoonfuls of butter
- one heaped tea-spoonful of cinnamon
- powdered white sugar

## Directions

1. Take as much of the very best and lightest bread-dough as will weigh a pound
2. The dough must have risen perfectly, so as to have cracked all over the surface
3. Put it into a pan, and mix into it a quarter of a pound of fresh butter, melted in half a pint of milk, adding a well-beaten egg, and sufficient flour to enable you to knead the dough over again
4. Mix in a heaping tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
5. Melt the soda or contents of the blue paper in as much lukewarm water as will cover it
6. When thoroughly melted, mix it into the dough
7. Dissolve in another cup the tartaric acid, or contents of the white paper, and stir that in also
8. Knead the dough a little while, till the whole is well mixed
9. Spread the dough thick and evenly in a square pan greased with lard or fresh butter
10. With a knife make deep cuts all through it
11. Prepare in a bowl a mixture of brown sugar, moistened with butter, and highly flavoured with powdered cinnamon, in the proportion of four heaping table-spoonfuls of sugar to two large spoonfuls of butter and one heaped tea-spoonful of cinnamon
12. Fill the cuts with this mixture, pressing it down well into the dough
13. Bake the cake half an hour or more, in a rather quick oven
14. When done, set it to cool
15. When cold, cut it in squares, and sift powdered white sugar over it
