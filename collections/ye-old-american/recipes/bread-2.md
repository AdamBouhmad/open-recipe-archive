## Ingredients

- one peck or two gallons of fine wheat flour
- half a pint of warm water
- half a pint of strong fresh yeast
- three quarters to a whole pint home-made yeast
- three or four table-spoonfuls fine salt
- soft water

## Directions

1. Sift flour into a kneading trough, or into a small clean tub, or a large broad earthen pan
2. Make a deep hole in the middle of the heap of flour
3. Have ready half a pint of warm water, which in summer should be only lukewarm, but even in winter it must not be hot or boiling
4. Stir water well into half a pint of strong fresh yeast
5. Pour it into the hole in the middle of the flour
6. With a spoon work in the flour round the edges of the liquid, so as to bring in by degrees sufficient flour to form a thin batter
7. Stir the batter well about for a minute or two
8. Take a handful of flour, and scatter it thinly over the top of this batter, so as to cover it entirely
9. Lay a warmed cloth over the whole, and set it to rise in a warm place
10. When the batter has risen so as to make cracks in the flour on the top, scatter over it three or four table-spoonfuls of fine salt
11. Begin to form the whole mass into a dough, commencing round the hole containing the batter, and pouring as much soft water as is necessary to make the flour mix with the batter
12. Knead it hard, turning it over, pressing it, folding it, and working it thoroughly with your clenched hands for twenty minutes or half an hour, till it becomes perfectly light and stiff
13. Form the dough into a lump in the middle of the trough or pan, and scatter a little dry flour thinly over it
14. Cover it, and set it again in a warm place to undergo a farther fermentation for about twenty minutes or half an hour
15. Heat the oven with faggots or small light wood
16. When the bread is ready, clear out the coals, and sweep and wipe the floor of the oven clean
17. Try the heat of the bottom by throwing in some flour
18. Put the dough on the paste-board, which must be sprinkled with flour
19. Divide it into loaves, forming them of a good shape
20. Place them in the oven, and close up the door
21. Bake for two hours and a half to three hours, or more, according to their size
22. When the loaves are done, wrap each in a clean coarse towel, and stand them up on end to cool slowly
