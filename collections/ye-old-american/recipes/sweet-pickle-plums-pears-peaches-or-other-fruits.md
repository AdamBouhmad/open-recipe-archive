## Ingredients

- 7 lbs. fruit, pared
- 4 lbs. white sugar
- 1 pint strong vinegar
- mace
- cinnamon
- cloves

## Directions

1. Pare peaches and pears; prick plums and damsons, tomatoes, "globes" or husk-tomatoes (otherwise known as ground-plums).
2. Put into the kettle with alternate layers of sugar.
3. Heat slowly to a boil; add the vinegar and spice; boil five minutes.
4. Take out the fruit with a perforated skimmer and spread upon dishes to cool.
5. Boil the syrup thick; pack the fruit in glass jars, and pour the syrup on boiling hot.
6. Examine every few days for the first month, and should it show signs of fermenting set the jars (uncovered) in a kettle of water, and heat until the contents are scalding.
