## Ingredients

- 2 qts. of oysters
- 2 eggs
- 1 qt. of milk
- 1 teacupful of water
- cayenne pepper
- salt
- nutmeg
- mace
- cloves
- butter
- sliced lemon
- crackers

## Directions

1. Strain the liquor from the oysters into a saucepan, pour in with it the water
2. Season with cayenne pepper and a little salt, a teaspoonful of mingled nutmeg, mace, and cloves
3. When the liquor is almost boiling, add half the oysters chopped finely and boil five minutes quite briskly
4. Strain the soup and return to saucepan
5. Make force-meat balls from the yolks of the eggs boiled hard and rubbed to a smooth paste with a little butter, mixed with six raw oysters chopped very finely, a little salt, and a raw egg well beaten
6. Flour your hands well and roll the force-meat into pellets, laying them upon a cold plate
7. Put the reserved whole oysters into the hot soup, and when it begins to boil again, drop in the force-meat marbles
8. Boil until the oysters ruffle, by which time the balls will also be done
9. Add the hot milk and a liberal tablespoonful of butter stirred in gently at the last
10. Serve with sliced lemon and crackers
