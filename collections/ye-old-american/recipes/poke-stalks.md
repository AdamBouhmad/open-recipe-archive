## Ingredients

- young poke stalks
- salt
- water
- butter
- pepper
- buttered toast

## Directions

1. When the young stalks are not larger than a man's little finger, and show only a tuft of leaves at top a few inches above ground, is the time to gather them.
2. Scrape the stalks, but do not cut off the leaves.
3. Lay in cold water, with a little salt, for two hours.
4. Tie in bundles, as you do asparagus, put into a saucepan of boiling water, and cook fast three-quarters of an hour.
5. Lay buttered toast in the bottom of a dish, untie the bundles, and pile the poke evenly upon it, buttering very well, and sprinkling with pepper and salt.
