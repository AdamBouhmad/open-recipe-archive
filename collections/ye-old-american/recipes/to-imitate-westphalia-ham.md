## Ingredients

- pork
- powdered saltpetre
- brown sugar
- fine salt
- black pepper
- cloves
- sal prunella
- stale strong beer or porter
- bran

## Directions

1. Mix together an equal quantity of powdered saltpetre and brown sugar, and rub it well into the hams
2. Next day make a pickle in sufficient quantity to cover them very well with a pound of fine salt, mixed with a pound of brown sugar, an ounce of black pepper and an ounce of cloves pounded to powder, a small bit of sal prunella, and a quart of stale strong beer or porter
3. Boil them all together, so as to make a pickle that will bear up an egg
4. Pour it boiling hot over the meat, and let it lie in the pickle two weeks, turning it two or three times every day, and basting or washing it with the liquid
5. Then take out the hams, rub them with bran and smoke them for a fortnight
6. When done, keep them in a barrel of fine charcoal
7. To imitate the shape of the real Westphalia hams, cut some of the meat off the under side of the thick part, so as to give them a flat appearance, first loosening the skin and afterwards sewing it on again
8. In cooking these hams simmer them slowly for seven or eight hours
