## Ingredients

- ripe yellow quinces
- powdered loaf-sugar
- water
- cochineal

## Directions

1. Take ten pounds of ripe yellow quinces; and having washed them clean, pare and core them, and cut them into small pieces.
2. To each pound of the cut quinces allow half a pound of powdered loaf-sugar.
3. Put the parings and cores into a kettle with water enough to cover them, and boil them slowly till they are all to pieces, and quite soft.
4. Then having put the quinces with the sugar into a porcelain preserving kettle, strain over them, through a cloth, the liquid from the parings and cores.
5. Add a little cochineal powdered, and sifted through thin muslin.
6. Boil the whole over a quick fire till it becomes a thick smooth mass, keeping it covered except when you are skimming it; and always after skimming, stir it up well from the bottom.
7. When cold, put it up in glass jars.
