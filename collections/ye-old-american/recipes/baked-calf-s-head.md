## Ingredients

- A calf's head
- Bits of butter the size of a nutmeg
- Pepper
- Flour
- A cup of water
- Wheat flour or batter
- Hot lard or beef drippings
- Salt
- Slices of lemon
- A tablespoonful of browned flour

## Directions

1. Boil a calf's head until tender, then split it in two, and keep the best half
2. Cut the meat from the other half in uniform pieces, the size of an oyster
3. Put bits of butter, the size of a nutmeg, all over the best half of the head
4. Sprinkle pepper over it, and dredge on flour until it looks white
5. Set it on a trivet or muffin rings in a dripping-pan
6. Put a cup of water into the pan, and set it in a hot oven
7. Turn it that it may brown evenly; baste once or twice
8. Dip the prepared pieces of the head in wheat flour or batter, and fry in hot lard or beef drippings a delicate brown
9. Season with pepper and salt and slices of lemon, if liked
10. When the roast is done put it on a hot dish, lay the fried pieces around it, and cover it with a tin cover
11. Put the gravy from the dripping-pan into the pan in which the pieces were fried, with the slices of lemon, and a tablespoonful of browned flour, and, if necessary, a little hot water
12. Let it boil up once, and strain it into a gravy boat, and serve with the meat
