## Ingredients

- young chickens, a pair
- water
- salt pork, half a pound
- onion, one large or two or three small
- allspice and cloves, a quarter teaspoonful
- walnut or dark catsup, a great spoonful
- browned flour, nearly three tablespoonfuls
- parsley
- brown Sherry, a glass

## Directions

1. Clean, wash, and cut up a pair of young chickens
2. Lay in clear water for half an hour
3. Put them in a saucepan with enough cold water to cover them well, and set over the fire to heat slowly
4. Cut half a pound of salt pork into strips, and fry crisp
5. Chop fine and put into the pot with the chicken
6. Fry in the fat left in the frying-pan one large onion or two or three small ones, cut into slices, until they brown well
7. Add them to the chicken with a quarter teaspoonful of allspice and cloves
8. Stew all together slowly for an hour or more until the meat is very tender
9. Take out the pieces of fowl and put in a hot dish, covering closely
10. Add to the gravy a great spoonful of walnut or other dark catsup, and nearly three tablespoonfuls of browned flour, a little chopped parsley, and a glass of brown Sherry
11. Boil up once; strain through a cullender to remove the bits of pork and onion
12. Return to the pot with the chicken; let it come to a final boil, and serve, pouring the gravy over the pieces of fowl
