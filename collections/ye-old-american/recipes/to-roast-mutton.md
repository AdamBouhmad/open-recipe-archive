## Ingredients

- mutton
- salt
- water
- flour
- butter
- currant jelly
- mashed turnips

## Directions

1. Trim off every part that cannot be eaten.
2. Wash the meat well.
3. Take off the skin and skewer it on again before putting the meat on the spit, or tie paper over the fat, having soaked the twine in water to prevent the string from burning.
4. Put a little salt and water into the dripping-pan, to baste the meat at first, then use its own gravy for that purpose.
5. A quarter of an hour before you think it will be done, take off the skin or paper, dredge the meat very lightly with flour, and baste it with butter.
6. Skim the gravy and send it to table in a boat.
7. Roast a leg of mutton from two hours to two hours and a half in proportion to its size.
8. Roast a chine or saddle from two hours and a half to three hours.
9. Roast a shoulder from an hour and a half to two hours.
10. Roast a loin from an hour and three quarters to two hours.
11. Roast a haunch in not less than four hours.
12. Serve with currant jelly and mashed turnips.
