## Ingredients

- a turtle
- a pound of middling or flitch of bacon
- four onions chopped

## Directions

1. Kill it at night in winter, and in the morning in summer
2. Hang it up by the hind fins
3. cut off the head and let it bleed well
4. Separate the bottom shell from the top
5. with great care, lest the gall bladder be broken
6. which must be cautiously taken out and thrown away
7. Put the liver in a bowl of water
8. Empty the guts and lay them in water
9. if there be eggs, put them also in water
10. It is proper to have a separate bowl of water for each article
11. Cut all the flesh from the bottom shell
12. and lay it in water
13. then break the shell in two
14. put it in a pot after having washed it clean
15. pour on as much water as will cover it entirely
16. add one pound of middling, or flitch of bacon
17. with four onions chopped
18. and set it on the fire to boil
19. Open the guts, cleanse them perfectly
20. take off the inside skin
21. and put them in the pot with the shell
22. let them boil steadily for three hours
23. and if the water boils away too much, add more
24. Wash the top shell nicely after taking out the flesh
25. cover it, and set it by
26. Parboil the fins
27. clean them nicely--taking off all the black skin
28. and put them in water
29. cut the flesh taken from the bottom and top shell
30. in small pieces
31. cut the fins in two
32. lay them with the flesh in a dish
33. sprinkle some salt over
34. and cover them up
35. When the shell, &c. is done
36. take out the bacon
37. scrape the shell clean
38. and strain the liquor
39. about one quart of which must be put back in the pot
40. reserve the rest for soup
41. pick out the guts
42. and cut them in small pieces
43. take all the nice bits that were strained out
44. put them with the guts into the gravy
45. lay in the fins cut in pieces with them
46. and as much of the flesh as will be sufficient to fill the upper shell
47. add to it, (if a large turtle,) one bottle of white wine
48. cayenne pepper, and salt, to your taste
49. one gill of mushroom catsup
50. one gill of lemon pickle
51. mace, nutmegs and cloves, pounded, to season it high
52. Mix two large spoonsful of flour in one pound and a quarter of butter
53. put it in with thyme, parsley, marjoram and savory, tied in bunches
54. stew all these together
55. till the flesh and fins are tender
56. wash out the top shell
57. put a puff paste around the brim
58. sprinkle over the shell pepper and salt
59. then take the herbs out of the stew
60. if the gravy is not thick enough
61. add a little more flour
62. and fill the shell
63. should there be no eggs in the turtle
64. boil six new laid ones for ten minutes
65. put them in cold water a short time
66. peel them
67. cut them in two
68. and place them on the turtle
69. make a rich forcemeat, (see receipt for forcemeat,)
