## Ingredients

- large young walnuts
- spring or pump water
- salt
- fresh spring water
- board
- wooden ladle
- cold spring water
- cloth
- soft flannel
- stone jar
- blades of mace
- sliced nutmeg
- best vinegar

## Directions

1. Take large young walnuts while their shells are quite soft so that you can stick the head of a pin into them
2. Pare them very thin till the white appears, and as you do them throw them into spring or pump water in which some salt has been dissolved
3. Let them stand in that water six hours, with a thin board upon them to keep them down under the water
4. Fill a porcelain kettle with fresh spring water, and set it over a clear fire, or on a charcoal furnace
5. Put the walnuts into the kettle, cover it, and let them simmer but not boil for about ten minutes
6. Have ready a vessel with cold spring water and salt, and put your nuts into it, taking them out of the kettle with a wooden ladle
7. Let them stand in the cold salt and water for a quarter of an hour, with the board keeping them down, for if they rise above the liquor or are exposed to the air, they will be discoloured
8. Take them out, and lay them on a cloth covered with another, till they are quite dry
9. Rub them carefully with a soft flannel, and put them into a stone jar
10. Lay among them blades of mace, and sliced nutmeg, but no dark-coloured spice
11. Pour over them the very best vinegar
