## Ingredients

- 1-1/2 lbs. of lean veal from the fillet
- 1 lb. of good fresh beef suet
- 6 oz. of panada (bread soaked in milk, and boiled till nearly dry)
- 1 teaspoonful of salt
- 1/4 teaspoonful of pepper
- 1/2 teaspoonful of nutmeg
- Four eggs

## Directions

1. Cut the veal in long thin slices
2. Scrape with a knife till nothing but the fibre remains
3. Put it in a mortar and pound it ten minutes or until in a purée
4. Pass it through a wire sieve
5. Skin, shred and chop the beef suet very fine
6. Put it in a mortar and pound it
7. Add the panada with the suet and pound them well together
8. Add the veal and season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg
9. Work all well together
10. Add four eggs by degrees, continually pounding the contents of the mortar
11. Take a small piece in a spoon and poach it in some boiling water to test if it is delicate, firm, and of good flavor
