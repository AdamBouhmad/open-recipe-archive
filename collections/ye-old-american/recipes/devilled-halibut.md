## Ingredients

- cold boiled or baked halibut, one pound
- hard-boiled eggs, yolks of three
- salad oil, tablespoonful
- white sugar, two teaspoonfuls
- made mustard, teaspoonful
- cayenne, pinch
- salt, teaspoonful
- Worcestershire sauce, teaspoonful
- anchovy paste
- vinegar in which celery-seed have been steeped, small teacupful
- pickled beet

## Directions

1. Mince a pound of cold boiled or baked halibut, or the fragments of halibut steak.
2. Rub the yolks of three hard-boiled eggs smooth with the back of a silver spoon, or in a Wedgewood mortar, until there remain no lumps in it.
3. Work into a soft paste with a tablespoonful salad oil.
4. Beat in two teaspoonfuls white sugar, a teaspoonful made mustard, a pinch of cayenne, teaspoonful salt, one of Worcestershire sauce, a little anchovy paste if you have it.
5. Finally, a little at a time to prevent lumping, add a small teacupful of vinegar in which celery-seed have been steeped.
6. Stir all thoroughly into the minced fish.
7. Garnish with a chain of the whites of the eggs cut into rings, with a small round slice of pickled beet laid within each link.
