## Ingredients

- leg of mutton
- water
- butter
- capers or nasturtians
- mashed turnips
- onions
- carrots
- whole turnips
- suet
- flour
- cold water

## Directions

1. Wash the leg of mutton clean, cut a small piece off the shank bone, and trim the knuckle.
2. Put it into a pot with water enough to cover it, and boil it gently for three hours, skimming it well.
3. Take it from the fire, and keeping the pot well covered, let it finish by remaining in the steam for ten or fifteen minutes.
4. Serve it up with a sauce-boat of melted butter into which a tea-cup full of capers or nasturtians have been stirred.
5. Serve with mashed turnips.
6. A neck or a loin of mutton will require about three hours slow boiling.
7. Serve with carrots and whole turnips.
8. Make suet dumplings of finely chopped suet mixed with double its quantity of flour, and a little cold water, to eat with the meat.
