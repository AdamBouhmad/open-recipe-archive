## Ingredients

- a pound of butter or chopped suet
- two pounds and a quarter of flour
- fine juicy acid apples, pared, cored, and sliced
- brown sugar
- a few cloves
- slips of lemon-peel
- a very little water

## Directions

1. Make a paste, allowing a pound of butter, or of chopped suet to two pounds and a quarter of flour.
2. Have ready a sufficient quantity of fine juicy acid apples, pared, cored, and sliced.
3. Mix with them brown sugar enough to sweeten them, a few cloves, and some slips of lemon-peel.
4. Butter the sides of an iron pot, and line them with paste.
5. Put in the apples, interspersing them with thin squares of paste, and add a very little water.
6. Cover the whole with a thick lid of paste, cutting a slit in the centre for the water to bubble up.
7. Let it boil two hours.
8. When done, serve it up on a large dish, and eat it with butter and sugar.
