## Ingredients

- peaches
- white sugar
- water
- peach kernels

## Directions

1. Select peaches that are ripe but not soft
2. Cut them in halves, take out the stones and pare them neatly
3. Take as many pounds of white sugar as of fruit
4. Put to each pound of sugar a teacupful of water
5. Stir it until it is dissolved
6. Set it over a moderate fire
7. When it is boiling hot, put in the peaches
8. Let them boil gently until a pure, clear, uniform color
9. Turn those at the bottom to the top carefully with a skimmer several times
10. Do not hurry them
11. When they are clear, take each half up with a spoon and spread them on flat dishes to become cold
12. When all are done, let the syrup boil until it is quite thick
13. Pour it into a large pitcher and let it set to cool and settle
14. When the peaches are cold put them carefully into jars and pour the syrup over them, leaving any sediment which has settled at the bottom, or strain the syrup
15. Some of the kernels from the peach-stones may be put in with the peaches while boiling
16. Let them remain open one night, then cover
