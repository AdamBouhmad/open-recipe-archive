## Ingredients

- young fine fowls
- celery
- yolks of eight hard-boiled eggs
- fine salt
- cayenne pepper
- made mustard
- vinegar
- sweet oil

## Directions

1. The fowls for this purpose should be young and fine.
2. You may either boil or roast them.
3. They must be quite cold.
4. Remove all the skin and fat, and disjoint the fowls.
5. Cut the meat from the bones into very small pieces, not exceeding an inch.
6. Wash and split two large fine heads of celery, and cut the white part into pieces also about an inch long.
7. Mix the chicken and celery together, and put them into a deep china dish, cover it and set it away.
8. Have ready the yolks of eight hard-boiled eggs.
9. Put them into a flat dish, and mash them to a paste with the back of a wooden spoon.
10. Add to the egg a small teaspoonful of fine salt, the same quantity of cayenne pepper, half a jill of made mustard, a jill or a wine-glass and a half of vinegar, and rather more than two wine-glasses of sweet oil.
11. Mix all these ingredients thoroughly; stirring them a long time till they are quite smooth.
12. Pour the dressing on a few minutes before the salad is sent in.
13. Mix the whole well together with a silver fork.
