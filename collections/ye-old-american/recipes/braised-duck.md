## Ingredients

- a pair of fine young ducks
- two or three slices of bacon
- a carrot
- an onion
- two cloves
- thyme
- parsley
- pepper
- broth
- a gill of white wine
- a turnip
- butter

## Directions

1. Prepare a pair of fine young ducks, the same as for roasting
2. Place them in a stewpan together with two or three slices of bacon, a carrot, an onion stuck with two cloves, and a little thyme and parsley
3. Season with pepper, and cover the whole with a broth, adding to the broth a gill of white wine
4. Place the pan over a gentle fire and allow the ducks to simmer until done, basting them frequently
5. When done remove them from the pan, and place them where they will keep hot
6. Cut up a turnip and fry in some butter
7. When nicely browned, drain the pieces and cook them until tender in the liquor in which the ducks were braised
8. Strain and thicken the gravy
9. Dish up the ducks and pour the gravy over them, garnishing with the pieces of turnip
