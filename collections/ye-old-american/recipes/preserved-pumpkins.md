## Ingredients

- one pound of pumpkin
- one pound of roughly pounded loaf sugar
- one gill of lemon juice
- half a pint of water to every three pounds of sugar used
- a little bruised ginger and lemon rind, thinly pared

## Directions

1. Obtain a good, sweet pumpkin; halve it, take out the seeds and pare off the rind; cut it into neat slices
2. Weigh the pumpkin, put the slices in a pan or deep dish in layers, with the sugar sprinkled between them
3. Pour the lemon juice over the top, and let the whole remain for two or three days
4. Boil all together, adding half a pint of water to every three pounds of sugar used until the pumpkin becomes tender
5. Turn the whole into a pan, where let it remain for a week
6. Drain off the syrup, boil it until it is quite thick, skim, and pour it boiling over the pumpkin
7. A little bruised ginger and lemon rind, thinly pared, may be boiled in the syrup to flavor the pumpkin
