## Ingredients

- the crumb of a stale six cent loaf
- half as much beef suet
- a grated nutmeg
- two large table-spoonfuls of sugar
- four eggs
- four table-spoonfuls of white wine or brandy

## Directions

1. Grate the crumb of a stale six cent loaf, and mix it with half as much beef suet, chopped as fine as possible
2. Add a grated nutmeg, and two large table-spoonfuls of sugar
3. Beat four eggs with four table-spoonfuls of white wine or brandy
4. Mix all well together to a stiff paste
5. Flour your hands, and make up the mixture into balls or dumplings about the size of turkey eggs
6. Have ready a pot of boiling water
7. Put the dumplings into cloths, and let them boil about half an hour
8. Serve them hot, and eat them with wine sauce
