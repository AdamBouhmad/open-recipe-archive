## Ingredients

- 1 tea-cup full of arrow root
- cold water
- 1/2 pint of unskimmed milk
- white sugar
- 1 beaten nutmeg
- 8 or 9 blades of mace
- juice and grated peel of 1 lemon
- 1 1/2 pints of very rich milk or cream
- 1/4 pound of sugar

## Directions

1. Take a tea-cup full of arrow root, put it into a large bowl, and dissolve it in a little cold water.
2. When it is melted, pour off the water, and let the arrow root remain undisturbed.
3. Boil in half a pint of unskimmed milk, made very sweet with white sugar, a beaten nutmeg, and eight or nine blades of mace, mixed with the juice and grated peel of a lemon.
4. When it has boiled long enough to be highly flavoured, strain it into a pint and a half of very rich milk or cream, and add a quarter of a pound of sugar.
5. Boil the whole for ten minutes; then strain it, boiling hot, over the arrow root.
6. Stir it well and frequently till cold; then put it into moulds and let it set to congeal.
