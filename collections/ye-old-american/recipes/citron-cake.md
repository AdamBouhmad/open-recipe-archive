## Ingredients

- a pound of candied citron
- sifted flour
- fourteen ounces flour
- a pound of fresh butter
- a pound of powdered loaf-sugar
- a glass of wine
- a glass of brandy
- a table-spoonful of powdered mace and cinnamon mixed
- a powdered nutmeg
- twelve eggs

## Directions

1. Cut a pound of candied citron into slips and spread it on a large dish.
2. Sprinkle it thickly with sifted flour till it is entirely white with it, tumbling the citron about with your hands till every piece is well covered with flour.
3. Sift into a pan fourteen ounces (two ounces less than a pound) of flour.
4. Beat together in a deep pan, till perfectly light, a pound of fresh butter cut up in a pound of powdered loaf-sugar.
5. Add, by degrees, a glass of wine, a glass of brandy, and a table-spoonful of powdered mace and cinnamon mixed, and a powdered nutmeg.
6. Beat twelve eggs in a shallow pan till very smooth and thick.
7. Stir the beaten egg into the beaten butter and sugar, alternately with the flour and citron, a little at a time of each.
8. Stir the whole very hard.
9. Butter a large tin pan (one with a tube in the centre will be best), put in the mixture, set it directly in a moderate oven, and bake it at least four hours.
10. Put it on an inverted sieve to cool.
11. When the cake is cool, ice and ornament it.
