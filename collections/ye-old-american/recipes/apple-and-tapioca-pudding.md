## Ingredients

- 1 teacupful tapioca
- 6 apples, juicy and well-flavored pippins, pared and cored
- 1 quart water
- 1 teaspoonful salt
- sugar
- cloves

## Directions

1. Cover the tapioca with three cups of lukewarm water, and set it in a tolerably warm place to soak five or six hours, stirring now and then.
2. Pack your apples in a deep dish, adding a cup of lukewarm water; cover closely and steam in a moderate oven until soft all through, turning them as they cook at bottom.
3. If the dish is more than a quarter full of liquid, turn some of it out before you pour the soaked tapioca over all.
4. Unless your apples are very sweet fill the centre with sugar and stick a clove in each, just before you cover with the tapioca.
5. Bake, after the tapioca goes in, one hour.
6. Eat warm, with sweet hard sauce.
