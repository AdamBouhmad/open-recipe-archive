## Ingredients

- half a pound of fresh butter
- half a pound of brown sugar
- two large table-spoonfuls of ginger
- a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- a tea-spoonful of powdered cloves
- a pint of West India molasses
- three pints of sifted flour
- six well-beaten eggs
- a small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash
- a pint of sour milk

## Directions

1. Beat to a cream half a pound of fresh butter cut up in a deep pan, among half a pound of brown sugar, setting it near the fire to soften it a little, but not to melt it
2. Add two large table-spoonfuls of ginger, a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon, and a tea-spoonful of powdered cloves
3. Stir into it, alternately, a pint of West India molasses, and three pints of sifted flour, and six well-beaten eggs
4. Dissolve a small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash in a pint of sour milk, and stir it, while foaming, into the mixture
5. Put it immediately into shallow square tin pans, well buttered
6. Place it in an oven not too hot
7. Bake, giving it long beating and much baking
