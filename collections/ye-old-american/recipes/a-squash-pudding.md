## Ingredients

- a yellow winter squash
- a quarter of a pound of butter
- two pounded crackers, or an equal quantity of grated stale bread
- a quarter of a pound of powdered sugar
- a quart of rich milk
- nine eggs
- a glass of white wine
- a glass of brandy
- a glass of rose water
- a table-spoonful of mixed spice, nutmeg, mace, and cinnamon powdered

## Directions

1. Pare, cut in pieces, and stew in a very little water, a yellow winter squash
2. When it is quite soft, drain it dry, and mash it in a cullender
3. Put it into a pan, and mix with it a quarter of a pound of butter
4. Prepare two pounded crackers, or an equal quantity of grated stale bread
5. Stir gradually a quarter of a pound of powdered sugar into a quart of rich milk, and add by degrees, the squash, and the powdered biscuit
6. Beat nine eggs very light, and stir them gradually into the mixture
7. Add a glass of white wine, a glass of brandy, a glass of rose water, and a table-spoonful of mixed spice, nutmeg, mace, and cinnamon powdered
8. Stir the whole very hard, till all the ingredients are thoroughly mixed
9. Bake it three quarters of an hour in a buttered dish
10. When cold, grate white sugar over it
