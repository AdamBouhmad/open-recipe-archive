## Ingredients

- salmon
- pepper
- salt
- cayenne
- powdered mace
- butter
- white paper
- hot water
- arrow-root, rice, or wheat flour
- cold water
- tomato sauce
- lemon
- parsley
- celery
- currant jelly
- Sherry
- cream sauce

## Directions

1. Wash and wipe dry, and rub with pepper and salt.
2. Some add a soupçon of cayenne and powdered mace.
3. Lay the fish upon a grating set over your baking-pan, and roast or bake, basting it freely with butter, and, toward the last, with its own drippings only.
4. Should it brown too fast, cover the top with a sheet of white paper until the whole is cooked.
5. When it is done, transfer to a hot dish and cover closely, and add to the gravy a little hot water thickened with arrow-root, rice, or wheat flour,—wet, of course, first with cold water,—a great spoonful of strained tomato sauce, and the juice of a lemon.
6. Boil up and serve in a sauce boat, or you can serve with cream sauce, made as for boiled salmon.
7. Garnish handsomely with alternate sprigs of parsley and the bleached tops of celery, with ruby bits of firm currant jelly here and there.
