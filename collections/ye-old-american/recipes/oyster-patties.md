## Ingredients

- thin pastry
- bread or paper
- egg white
- oysters
- butter
- flour
- oyster liquor
- milk or cream
- egg yolks

## Directions

1. Line patty-pans with thin pastry, pressing it well to the tin
2. Put a piece of bread or a ball of paper in each
3. Cover them with paste and brush them over with the white of an egg
4. Cut an inch square of thin pastry, place on the centre of each, glaze this also with egg, and bake in a quick oven fifteen to twenty minutes
5. Remove the bread or paper when half cold
6. Scald as many oysters as you require, allowing two for each patty, three if small, in their own liquor
7. Cut each oyster in four and strain the liquor
8. Put two tablespoonfuls of butter and two of flour into a thick saucepan and stir them together over the fire till the flour smells cooked
9. Pour half a pint of oyster liquor and half a pint of milk into the flour and butter, or use cream instead of milk
10. Stir till it is a thick, smooth sauce
11. Put the oysters into it and let them boil once
12. Beat the yolks of two eggs
13. Remove the oysters for one minute from the fire, then stir the eggs into them till the sauce looks like thick custard
14. Fill the patties with this oyster fricassee, taking care to make it hot by standing in boiling water before dinner on the day required, and to make the patty cases hot before you fill them
