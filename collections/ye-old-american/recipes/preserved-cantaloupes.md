## Ingredients

- ripe cantaloupes
- salt
- water
- weak alum-water
- loaf-sugar
- fine ripe oranges
- white of egg
- spring water

## Directions

1. Shave a thin paring off the whole outside of each cantaloupe
2. Cut out a small piece or plug about an inch square, and through it extract all the seeds from the middle
3. Return the plugs to the hole from whence you took them, and secure them with a needle and thread, or by tying a small string round the cantaloupe
4. Lay the cantaloupes for four or five hours in salt and water
5. Put them into spring water to extract the salt, changing the water till you find it salt no longer
6. Scald them in weak alum-water
7. Make a syrup in the proportion of a pint of water to a pound of loaf-sugar, and boil the cantaloupes in it till a straw will go through them
8. Take them out, and set them in the sun to harden
9. Prepare some fine ripe oranges, paring off the yellow rind very thin, and cutting it into slips, and then laying it in scalding water to extract the bitterness
10. Cut the oranges into pieces; allow a pint of water to each orange, and boil them to a pulp
11. Strain them, and allow to each pint of the liquid, a pound of the best loaf-sugar, and stir in a little beaten white of egg (one white to two pounds of sugar)
12. Boil the peel in this second syrup, skimming it well
13. When the peel is soft, take it all out
14. Put the cantaloupes into your jars, and pour over them the hot syrup
15. Cover them closely, and keep them in a dry cool place
