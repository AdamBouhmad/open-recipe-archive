## Ingredients

- ham, thinly sliced
- lard
- eggs

## Directions

1. Cut the ham into very thin slices.
2. Soak them in hot water at least half an hour, changing the water several times, and always pouring it on scalding hot, to draw out some of the salt.
3. After soaking, dry the slices in a cloth.
4. Heat your gridiron, and broil them over a clear fire.
5. If serving with eggs, put some lard into a very clean frying-pan, and make it boiling hot.
6. Break the eggs separately into a saucer.
7. Slip each egg gently into the frying-pan.
8. Do not turn them while they are frying, but keep pouring some of the hot lard over them with an iron spoon.
9. They will be done enough in about three minutes.
10. Take them up with a tin slice, drain off the lard, and trim off any discoloured or ragged parts.
11. Lay a fried egg upon each slice of the broiled ham, and send them to table hot.
