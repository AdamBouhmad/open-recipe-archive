## Ingredients

- the largest and finest ripe strawberries
- bees-wax
- rosin
- brick-dust

## Directions

1. Take the largest and finest ripe strawberries and hull them.
2. Put them immediately into large wide-mouthed bottles, filling them quite up to the top.
3. Cork them directly and wire the corks.
4. Set the bottles into a large preserving-kettle full of cold water.
5. Place them over the fire and let the water boil around them for a quarter of an hour after it has come to a boil.
6. Take out the bottles, drain them, and wipe the outside dry.
7. Proceed at once to seal the corks hermetically with red cement made of one-third bees-wax cut up and two-thirds rosin, melted together in a skillet over the fire.
8. When the cement is completely liquid, take it off the fire and thicken it to the consistence of sealing-wax by stirring in sufficient finely powdered brick-dust.
9. Spread this cement on hot over the wired corks.
10. Keep the bottles in boxes of dry sand.
11. When opened, the strawberries will be found fresh and highly flavoured, and must be used as soon as they are opened.
