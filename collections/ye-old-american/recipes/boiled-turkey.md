## Ingredients

- oysters
- dressing
- butter
- turkey
- cloth
- flour
- oyster-sauce
- liquor
- parsley
- rice or wheat flour
- cold milk
- tablespoonful of butter
- hot milk
- celery

## Directions

1. Chop about two dozen oysters, and mix with them a dressing compounded as for roast turkey, only with more butter.
2. Stuff the turkey as for roasting, craw and body.
3. Baste about it a thin cloth, fitted closely to every part.
4. The inside of the cloth should be dredged with flour to prevent the fowl from sticking to it.
5. Allow fifteen minutes to a pound, and boil slowly.
6. Make oyster-sauce by adding to a cupful of the liquor in which the turkey was boiled, eight oysters chopped fine.
7. Season with minced parsley, stir in a spoonful of rice or wheat flour, wet with cold milk, a tablespoonful of butter.
8. Add a cupful of hot milk.
9. Boil up once and pour into an oyster-tureen.
10. Send around celery with it.
