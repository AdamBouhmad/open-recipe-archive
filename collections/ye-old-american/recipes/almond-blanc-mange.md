## Ingredients

- 1 quart of milk
- 1 oz. Cooper's gelatine
- 3 ozs. of almonds, blanched and pounded in a mortar
- 1 tablespoonful of rose-water
- ¾ cup sugar

## Directions

1. Heat the milk to boiling, having previously soaked the gelatine in a cup of it for an hour.
2. Turn in the gelatine when the milk is scalding hot; add the pounded almond-paste, and stir all together ten minutes before putting in the sugar.
3. When the gelatine has dissolved, remove the blanc-mange from the vessel of boiling water in which you have cooked it, and strain through a thin muslin bag, pressing it well to get out the flavor of the almonds.
4. Wet a mould with cold water, put in the blanc-mange, and set in a cold place until firm.
