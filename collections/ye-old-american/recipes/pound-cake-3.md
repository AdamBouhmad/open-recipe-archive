## Ingredients

- a glass of rose water
- a pound of the finest flour
- a pound of loaf-sugar
- a pound of fresh butter
- spice
- ten eggs
- juice of two lemons or three large oranges

## Directions

1. Sift a pound of the finest flour into a broad pan, and powder a pound of loaf-sugar.
2. Put the sugar into a deep pan, and cut up in it a pound of fresh butter.
3. Warm them by the fire till soft; and then stir them to a cream.
4. When they are perfectly light, add gradually the spice and liquor, a little at a time.
5. Beat ten eggs as light as possible, and stir them by degrees into the mixture alternately with the flour.
6. Then add the juice of two lemons or three large oranges.
7. Stir the whole very hard; put it into a deep tin pan with straight or upright sides, and bake it in a moderate oven from two to three hours.
8. If baked in a Dutch oven, take off the lid when you have ascertained that the cake is quite done, and let it remain in the oven to cool gradually.
9. If any part is burnt, scrape it off as soon as cold.
10. It may be iced either warm or cool; first dredging the cake with flour and then wiping it off.
11. It will be best to put on two coats of icing; the second coat not till the first is entirely dry.
12. Flavour the icing with essence of lemon, or with extract of roses.
