## Ingredients

- herrings
- brine left from winter stock for beef
- coarse alum salt
- saltpetre
- salt

## Directions

1. Take the brine left of your winter stock for beef to the fishing place
2. when the seine is hauled, pick out the largest herrings and throw them alive into the brine
3. let them remain twenty-four hours
4. take them out and lay them on sloping planks that the brine may drain off
5. have a tight barrel and put some coarse alum salt at the bottom
6. put in a layer of herrings taking care not to bruise them
7. sprinkle over it alum salt and some saltpetre
8. continue with fish, salt, and saltpetre till the barrel is full
9. keep a board over it
10. should they not make brine enough to cover them in a few weeks, add some
11. when they are to be used, take a few out of brine and soak them an hour or two
12. scale them nicely, pull off the gills
13. wash them clean and hang them up to dry
14. when to be broiled, take half a sheet of white paper and rub it over with butter
15. put the herring in and double the edges securely
16. broil without burning it
