## Ingredients

- large egg plums, all quite ripe
- four pounds and a half of loaf-sugar, broken small or powdered
- one quart of clear hard water

## Directions

1. Take large egg plums that are all quite ripe, and prick them all over with a small silver fork, leaving on the stems.
2. Put the plums and sugar into a preserving kettle, and pour in one quart of clear hard water.
3. Hang the kettle over a moderate fire, and boil and skim it.
4. As soon as the skin begins to crack or shrivel, take out the plums one at a time, leaving the syrup on the fire, and spread them on large dishes to cool.
5. Place them in the open air, and as soon as they are cool enough to be touched with your fingers, smooth the skin down where it is broken or ruffled.
6. When quite cold, return them to the syrup, which in the mean time must have been kept slowly simmering, and boil the plums again till they are quite clear, but not till they break.
7. Put them warm into large glass or queen's-ware jars, and pour the syrup over them.
