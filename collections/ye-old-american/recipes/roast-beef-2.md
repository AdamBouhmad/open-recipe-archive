## Ingredients

- rib piece or loin roast of seven or eight pounds
- butter or suet fat
- salt
- pepper
- sifted flour
- boiling water

## Directions

1. Take a rib piece or loin roast of seven or eight pounds.
2. Wipe it thoroughly all over with a clean wet towel.
3. Lay it in a dripping-pan, and baste it well with butter or suet fat.
4. Set it in the oven.
5. Baste it frequently with its own drippings, which will make it brown and tender.
6. When partly done season with salt and pepper, as it hardens any meat to salt it when raw, and draws out its juices.
7. Dredge with sifted flour to give it a frothy appearance.
8. It will take a roast of this size about two hours' time to be properly done, leaving the inside a little rare or red.
9. Remove the beef to a heated dish, set where it will keep hot.
10. Skim the drippings from all fat, add a tablespoonful of sifted flour, a little pepper and a teacupful of boiling water.
11. Boil up once and serve hot in a gravy boat.
12. Serve with mustard or grated horse-radish and vinegar.
