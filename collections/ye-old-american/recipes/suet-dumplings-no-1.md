## Ingredients

- One pint bowl of fine bread crumbs
- One-half cupful of beef suet chopped fine
- Whites and yolks of four eggs
- One teaspoonful of cream of tartar
- Half a cupful of flour
- Half a teaspoonful of soda
- One teaspoonful of salt
- Milk
- Cloth
- Wine sauce or syrup and butter for serving

## Directions

1. Sift cream of tartar into half a cupful of flour.
2. Dissolve half a teaspoonful of soda in a little water.
3. Beat the whites and yolks of four eggs separately and very light.
4. Combine the bread crumbs, chopped suet, beaten eggs, flour mixture, soda, and salt.
5. Wet it all together with milk enough to make a stiff paste.
6. Flour your hands and make into balls.
7. Tie up in separate cloths that have been wrung out in hot water and floured inside; leave room when tying for them to swell.
8. Drop them into boiling water and boil about three-quarters of an hour.
9. Serve hot with wine sauce, or syrup and butter.
