## Ingredients

- a part of a round of fresh beef
- salt
- water
- skewers
- poultry necks and trimmings
- root of tongue
- four or five carrots
- a head of celery
- four or five turnips
- a bunch of sweet herbs
- black peppercorns
- muslin rag
- a dozen very small onions
- capers or nasturtians
- cloves
- catchup
- made mustard

## Directions

1. Rub the beef with salt.
2. Place skewers in the bottom of the stew-pot, and lay the meat upon them with barely water enough to cover it.
3. To enrich the gravy you may add the necks and other trimmings of whatever poultry you may happen to have; also the root of tongue, if convenient.
4. Cover the pot, and set it over a quick fire.
5. When it boils and the scum has risen, skim it well, and then diminish the fire so that the meat shall only simmer; or set the pot on hot coals.
6. Put in four or five carrots sliced thin, a head of celery cut up, and four or five sliced turnips.
7. Add a bunch of sweet herbs, and a small table-spoonful of black peppercorns tied in a thin muslin rag.
8. Let it stew slowly for four or five hours, and then add a dozen very small onions roasted and peeled, and a large table-spoonful of capers or nasturtians (you may stick a clove in each onion if you choose).
9. Simmer it half an hour longer.
10. Take up the meat, and place it in a dish, laying the vegetables round it.
11. Skim and strain the gravy; season it with catchup, and made mustard, and serve it up in a boat.
