## Ingredients

- a pair of large fat fowls
- two pounds of the lean of veal, or four calf's feet
- a tea-spoonful of salt
- a half tea-spoonful of cayenne pepper
- a dozen blades of mace
- water
- the grated crumb of about one quarter of a loaf of stale bread
- a little warm milk
- the yolks of four hard boiled eggs
- a dozen sweet almonds
- half a dozen bitter almonds
- a quart of cream or rich milk

## Directions

1. Cut up a pair of large fat fowls.
2. Butter the inside of the soup-pot, and put in the pieces of fowl with two pounds of the lean of veal, cut into pieces, or with four calf's feet cut in half.
3. Season them with a tea-spoonful of salt, a half tea-spoonful of cayenne pepper, and a dozen blades of mace.
4. Cover them with water, and stew it slowly for an hour, skimming it well.
5. Take out the breasts and wings of the fowls, and having cut off the flesh, chop it fine.
6. Keep the pot covered, and the veal and the remainder of the fowls still stewing.
7. Mix the chopped chicken with the grated crumb of about one quarter of a loaf of stale bread, having soaked the crumbs in a little warm milk.
8. Have ready the yolks of four hard boiled eggs, a dozen sweet almonds, and half a dozen bitter ones blanched and broken small.
9. Mix the egg and almonds with the chopped chicken and grated bread, and pound all in a mortar till it is well incorporated.
10. Strain the soup from the meat and fowl, and stir this mixture into the liquid, after it has stewed till reduced to two quarts.
11. Having boiled separately a quart of cream or rich milk, add it hot to the soup, a little at a time.
12. Cover it, and let it simmer a few minutes longer.
13. Send it to table.
