## Ingredients

- butter
- half a pound of ham
- three pounds of lean fresh beef
- three pounds of veal cut from the bones
- bones
- cold water
- pint of water
- three large white onions
- three large turnips
- three carrots
- three large heads of celery

## Directions

1. Having well buttered the inside of a nicely tinned stew-pot, cut half a pound of ham into slices, and lay them at the bottom, with three pounds of the lean of fresh beef, and as much veal, cut from the bones, which you must afterward break to pieces, and lay on the meat.
2. Cover the pan closely, and set it over a quick fire.
3. When the meat begins to stick to the pan, turn it; and when there is a nice brown glaze at the bottom, cover the meat with cold water.
4. Watch it well, and when it is just coming to a boil, put in a pint of water.
5. This will cause the scum to rise. Skim it well, and then pour in another pint of water.
6. Skim it again; pour in water as before, a pint at a time, and repeat this till no more scum rises.
7. In skimming, carefully avoid stirring the soup, as that will injure its clearness.
8. Peel off the outer skin of three large white onions and slice them.
9. Pare three large turnips, and slice them also.
10. Wash clean and cut into small pieces three carrots, and three large heads of celery.
11. Put the vegetables into the soup.
