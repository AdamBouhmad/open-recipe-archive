## Ingredients

- nine large pippin apples
- water
- three table-spoonfuls of flour
- a quart of milk
- a quarter of a pound of white sugar
- a tea-spoonful of mixed spice
- seven eggs
- white sugar
- a slice of fresh lemon
- a small handful of peach-leaves

## Directions

1. Take nine large pippin apples
2. Pare and core them whole
3. Set them in the bottom of a large deep dish, and pour round them a very little water, just enough to keep them from burning
4. Put them into an oven, and let them bake about half an hour
5. Mix three table-spoonfuls of flour with a quart of milk, a quarter of a pound of white sugar, and a tea-spoonful of mixed spice
6. Beat seven eggs very light, and stir them gradually into the milk
7. Take out the dish of apples, which by this time should be half baked
8. Fill up the holes from whence you extracted the cores, with white sugar
9. Press down into each a slice of fresh lemon
10. Pour the batter round the apples
11. Put the dish again into the oven, and let it bake another half hour
12. Do not bake long enough for the apples to fall to pieces
13. Send it to table warm
14. Improve it by previously boiling in the milk a small handful of peach-leaves
15. Let it get cold before you stir in the eggs
