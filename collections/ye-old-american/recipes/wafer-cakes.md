## Ingredients

- half a pound of powdered sugar
- a quarter of a pound of butter
- six beaten eggs
- sifted flour
- a powdered nutmeg
- a tea-spoonful of cinnamon
- juice of a lemon or a table-spoonful of rose water
- butter for greasing
- powdered sugar for strewing
- whipt cream (optional)
- preserved strawberries or other small sweetmeat (optional)

## Directions

1. Mix together half a pound of powdered sugar and a quarter of a pound of butter
2. Add to them six beaten eggs
3. Beat the whole very light
4. Stir into it as much sifted flour as will make a stiff batter, a powdered nutmeg, and a tea-spoonful of cinnamon and the juice of a lemon or a table-spoonful of rose water
5. The batter must be very smooth when it is done and without a single lump
6. Heat your wafer iron on both sides by turning it in the fire, but do not allow it to get too hot
7. Grease the inside with butter tied in a rag, repeating this previous to the baking of every cake
8. Put in the batter, allowing to each wafer two large table-spoonfuls, taking care not to stir up the batter
9. Close the iron and when one side is baked, turn it on the other
10. Open it occasionally to see if the wafer is doing well
11. They should be coloured of a light brown
12. Take them out carefully with a knife
13. Strew them with powdered sugar and roll them up while warm, round a smooth stick, withdrawing it when they grow cold
14. If preparing for company, fill up the hollow of the wafers with whipt cream and stop up the two ends with preserved strawberries or with any other small sweetmeat
