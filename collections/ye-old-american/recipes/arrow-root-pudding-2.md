## Ingredients

- a large tea-cup of arrow root
- half a pint of rich milk
- half a pint of milk
- cinnamon
- bitter almonds or peach-leaves
- three eggs
- four large table-spoonfuls of powdered sugar
- nutmeg
- fresh lemon-peel, grated
- slices of preserved quince or peach, or whole strawberries or raspberries

## Directions

1. Take a large tea-cup of arrow root, and melt it in half a pint of rich milk
2. Boil another half pint of milk with some cinnamon, and a few bitter almonds or peach-leaves
3. Strain the milk hot over the dissolved arrow root; stir it to a thick, smooth batter, and set it away to cool
4. Beat three eggs very light, and stir them into the batter, alternately with four large table-spoonfuls of powdered sugar
5. Add some nutmeg, and some fresh lemon-peel, grated
6. Put the mixture into a buttered dish, and bake it half an hour
7. When cold, ornament the top handsomely, with slices of preserved quince or peach, or with whole strawberries or raspberries
