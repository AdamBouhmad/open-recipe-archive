## Ingredients

- two quarts of large oysters
- tea-spoonful of whole pepper
- tea-spoonful of grated nutmeg
- whole cloves
- seven or eight blades of mace
- salt
- hard-boiled yolk of egg
- quarter of a pound of butter
- flour

## Directions

1. Take two quarts of large oysters
2. Strain their liquor into a soup pan; season it with a tea-spoonful of whole pepper, a tea-spoonful of grated nutmeg, the same quantity of whole cloves, and seven or eight blades of mace
3. If the oysters are fresh, add a large tea-spoonful of salt; if they are salt oysters, none is requisite
4. Set the pan on hot coals, and boil it slowly (skimming it when necessary) till you find that it is sufficiently flavoured with the taste of the spice
5. Cut out the hard part and chop the oysters fine, with some hard-boiled yolk of egg
6. Take the liquor from the fire, and strain out the spice from it
7. Return it to the soup pan, and put the chopped oysters into it, with whatever liquid may have continued about them
8. Add a quarter of a pound of butter, divided into little bits and rolled in flour
9. Cover the pan, and let it boil hard about five minutes
