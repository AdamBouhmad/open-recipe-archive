## Ingredients

- a pound of loaf-sugar
- half a pint of water
- the beaten white of an egg
- an ounce of isinglass
- boiling water
- a quart of white wine
- a table-spoonful or a spoonful and a half of old Jamaica spirits

## Directions

1. Clarify a pound of loaf-sugar, by mixing it with half a pint of water and the beaten white of an egg
2. Boil and skim it
3. Put an ounce of isinglass (with as much boiling water as will cover it) into a small sauce-pan
4. Set it in hot coals till the isinglass is thoroughly dissolved
5. When the syrup has been taken from the fire, mix the melted isinglass with it
6. Add a quart of white wine
7. Stir in a table-spoonful or a spoonful and a half of old Jamaica spirits
8. Stir the mixture very hard, and pour it into a mould
9. When it has congealed, wrap a cloth dipped in warm water round the outside of the mould
10. Turn out the jelly, and eat it with ice-cream
