## Ingredients

- 1 pound currants
- flour for dredging
- 2 nutmegs
- 1 large table-spoonful powdered cinnamon
- ½ pound and 2 ounces flour
- 6 ounces fresh butter
- ½ pound powdered white sugar
- 2 wine glasses brandy
- 4 eggs
- ½ pint brisk cider
- 1 small tea-spoonful pearl-ash or sal-aratus

## Directions

1. Pick, wash, and dry a pound of currants, and sprinkle them well with flour
2. Prepare two nutmegs and a large table-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
3. Sift half a pound and two ounces of flour
4. Stir together till very light, six ounces of fresh butter, and half a pound of powdered white sugar
5. Add gradually the spice, with two wine glasses of brandy
6. Beat four eggs very light, and stir them into the mixture alternately with the flour
7. Add by degrees half a pint of brisk cider
8. Stir in the currants, a few at a time
9. Stir in a small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash or sal-aratus dissolved in a little cider
10. Stir the whole very hard, put it in a buttered tin pan
11. Bake it in a brisk oven an hour or more
