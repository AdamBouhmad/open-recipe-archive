## Ingredients

- young soft walnuts
- coarse flannel or tow cloth
- salt
- water
- boiling water
- stone jars
- vinegar
- cloves
- allspice
- black pepper
- mace
- nutmeg
- thin muslin rag
- mustard seed
- scraped horse-radish
- large needle

## Directions

1. Gather walnuts while young and soft, so that you can easily run a pin through them, and when the sun is upon them
2. Rub them with a coarse flannel or tow cloth to get off the fur of the outside
3. Mix salt and water strong enough to bear an egg, and let them lie in it a week, changing it every two days, and stirring them frequently
4. Take them out, drain them, spread them on large dishes, and expose them to the air about ten minutes, which will cause them to blacken the sooner
5. Scald them in boiling water, but do not let them lie in it
6. Rub them with a coarse woollen cloth, and pierce every one through in several places with a large needle
7. Put them into stone jars
8. To a hundred walnuts allow a gallon of vinegar, an ounce of cloves, an ounce of allspice, an ounce of black pepper, half an ounce of mace, and half an ounce of nutmeg
9. Boil the spice in the vinegar for fifteen minutes, then strain the vinegar, and pour it boiling hot over the walnuts
10. Tie up in a thin muslin rag, a tea-cupful of mustard seed, and a large table-spoonful of scraped horse-radish, and put it into the jars with the walnuts
11. Cover them closely with corks and leathers
