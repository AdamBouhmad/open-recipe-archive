## Ingredients

- terrapins
- water
- flour
- salt
- black pepper
- cayenne pepper
- mace
- brandy
- sherry wine
- rich cream
- sweet butter

## Directions

1. Plunge the terrapins alive into boiling water, and let them remain until the sides and lower shell begin to crack
2. Remove them and let them get cold
3. Take off the shell and outer skin, being careful to save all the blood possible in opening them
4. If there are eggs in them put them aside in a dish
5. Take all the inside out, and be very careful not to break the gall
6. Remove the gall immediately or it will make the rest bitter
7. Cut up the liver and all the rest of the terrapin into small pieces, adding the blood and juice that have flowed out in cutting up
8. Add half a pint of water
9. Sprinkle a little flour over them as you place them in the stewpan
10. Let them stew slowly ten minutes, adding salt, black and cayenne pepper, and a very small blade of mace
11. Add a gill of the best brandy and half a pint of the very best sherry wine
12. Let it simmer over a slow fire very gently
13. About ten minutes or so before you are ready to dish them, add half a pint of rich cream, and half a pound of sweet butter, with flour, to prevent boiling
14. Two or three minutes before taking them off the fire peel the eggs carefully and throw them in whole
