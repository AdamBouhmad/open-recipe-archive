## Ingredients

- large, fine, juicy apples
- water
- butter
- flour
- brown sugar
- lemon-juice
- marmalade
- cream sauce

## Directions

1. Take large, fine, juicy apples, and pare and core them, leaving them as whole as possible.
2. Put them into a kettle with sufficient water to cover them, and let them parboil a quarter of an hour.
3. Take them out, and drain them on a sieve.
4. Prepare a paste in the proportion of a pound of butter to two pounds of flour, as for plain pies.
5. Roll it out into a sheet, and cut it into equal portions according to your number of apples.
6. Place an apple on each, and fill up the hole from whence the core was extracted with brown sugar moistened with lemon-juice, or with any sort of marmalade.
7. Cover the apple with the paste, closing it neatly.
8. Place the dumplings side by side in buttered square pans, not so as to touch, and bake them of a light brown.
9. Serve them warm or cool, and eat them with cream sauce.
