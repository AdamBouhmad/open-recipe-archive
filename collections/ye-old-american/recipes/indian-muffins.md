## Ingredients

- a pint and a half of yellow Indian meal
- a handful of wheat flour
- a quarter of a pound of fresh butter
- a quart of milk
- four eggs
- a small tea-spoonful of salt
- molasses
- honey

## Directions

1. Sift and mix together a pint and a half of yellow Indian meal, and a handful of wheat flour.
2. Melt a quarter of a pound of fresh butter in a quart of milk.
3. Beat four eggs very light, and stir into them alternately (a little at a time of each) the milk when it is quite cold, and the meal; adding a small tea-spoonful of salt.
4. The whole must be beaten long and hard.
5. Butter some muffin rings; set them on a hot griddle, and pour some of the batter into each.
6. Send the muffins to table hot, and split them by pulling them open with your fingers, as a knife will make them heavy.
7. Eat them with butter, molasses or honey.
