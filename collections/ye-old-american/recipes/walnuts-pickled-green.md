## Ingredients

- walnuts
- vine leaves
- strong vinegar
- cider vinegar
- salt
- mace
- nutmeg
- horse-radish

## Directions

1. Gather walnuts while the shells are very soft, and rub them all with a flannel
2. Wrap them singly in vine leaves
3. Lay a few vine leaves on the bottom of a large stone jar
4. Put in the walnuts, seeing that each of them is well wrapped up so as not to touch one another
5. Cover them with a thick layer of leaves
6. Fill up the jar with strong vinegar
7. Cover it closely, and let it stand three weeks
8. Pour off the vinegar, take out the walnuts, renew all the vine leaves
9. Fill up with fresh vinegar, and let them stand three weeks longer
10. Pour off the vinegar again, and renew the vine leaves
11. Take the best cider vinegar; put salt in it till it will bear an egg
12. Add to it mace, sliced nutmeg, and scraped horse-radish, in the proportion of an ounce of each and a gallon of vinegar to a hundred walnuts
13. Boil the spice and vinegar about ten minutes
14. Pour it hot on the walnuts
15. Cover the jar closely with a cork and leather, and set it away, leaving the vine leaves with the walnuts
