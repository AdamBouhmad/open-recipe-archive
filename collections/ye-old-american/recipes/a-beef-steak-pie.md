## Ingredients

- one pound of butter
- two pounds of sifted flour
- two pounds of the best beef-steak, cut thin and well beaten
- pepper and salt
- sliced potato
- small bits of butter rolled in flour
- mushrooms, or chopped clams or oysters (optional)
- half a pint of cold water

## Directions

1. Make a good paste in the proportion of a pound of butter to two pounds of sifted flour.
2. Divide it in half, and line with one sheet of it the bottom and sides of a deep dish, which must first be well buttered.
3. Have ready two pounds of the best beef-steak, cut thin, and well beaten; the bone and fat being omitted.
4. Season it with pepper and salt.
5. Spread a layer of the steak at the bottom of the pie, and on it a layer of sliced potato, and a few small bits of butter rolled in flour.
6. Then another layer of meat, potato, &c., till the dish is full.
7. Add mushrooms, or chopped clams or oysters if desired, leaving out the hard parts.
8. If you use clams or oysters, moisten the other ingredients with a little of their liquor. If not, pour in, at the last, half a pint of cold water, or less if the pie is small.
9. Cover the pie with the other sheet of paste as a lid, and notch the edges handsomely, having reserved a little of the paste to make a flower or tulip to stick in the slit at the top.
10. Bake it in a quick oven an hour and a quarter, or longer, in proportion to its size.
11. Send it to table hot.
