## Ingredients

- currants
- sugar

## Directions

1. Pick over currants, but do not remove stems; wash and drain.
2. Mash a few in the bottom of a preserving kettle, using a wooden potato masher; so continue until berries are used.
3. Cook slowly until currants look white.
4. Strain through a coarse strainer, then allow juice to drop through a double thickness of cheese-cloth or a jelly bag.
5. Measure, bring to boiling-point, and boil five minutes; add unequal measure of heated sugar, boil three minutes, skim, and pour into glasses.
6. Place in a sunny window, and let stand twenty-four hours.
7. Cover, and keep in a cool, dry place.
