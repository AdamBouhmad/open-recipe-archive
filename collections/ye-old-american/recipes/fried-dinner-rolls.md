## Ingredients

- light raised bread dough
- melted butter
- soda
- water
- cold butter
- lard

## Directions

1. When making light raised bread, save out a piece of dough nearly the size of a small loaf
2. Roll it out on the board
3. Spread a tablespoonful of melted butter over it
4. Dissolve a quarter of a teaspoonful of soda in a tablespoonful of water and pour that also over it
5. Work it all well into the dough
6. Roll it out into a sheet not quite half an inch thick
7. Cut it in strips three inches long and one inch wide
8. Lay them on buttered tins
9. Cover with a cloth and set away in a cool place until an hour before dinner time
10. Set them by the fire where they will become light
11. While they are rising, add to a frying-pan a tablespoonful of cold butter and one of lard
12. When it boils clear and is hot, lay as many of the rolls in as will fry nicely
13. As soon as they brown on one side turn them over and brown the other
14. Turn them on the edges and brown the sides
15. Add fresh grease as is needed
16. Eat them warm in place of bread
