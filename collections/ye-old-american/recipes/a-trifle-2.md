## Ingredients

- half a pound of maccaroons or Naples biscuits
- white wine
- bitter almonds or peach leaves
- marmalade or jam
- one quart of cream
- a quarter of a pound of sugar
- half a pint of white wine
- preserved water-melon rind
- red currant jelly
- fresh flowers for decoration

## Directions

1. Place half a pound of maccaroons or Naples biscuits at the bottom of a large glass bowl
2. Pour on them as much white wine as will cover and dissolve them
3. Make a rich custard flavoured with bitter almonds or peach leaves and pour it when cold on the maccaroons
4. Add a layer of marmalade or jam
5. Take a quart of cream, mix with it a quarter of a pound of sugar and half a pint of white wine, and whip it with rods to a stiff froth
6. Lay the froth as you proceed on an inverted sieve with a dish under it to catch the cream that drips through, which must be saved and whipped over again
7. Pile the frothed cream upon the marmalade in a high pyramid
8. Take preserved water-melon rind that has been cut into leaves or flowers, split them nicely to make them thinner and lighter, and place a circle or wreath of them round the heap of frothed cream, interspersing them with spots of stiff red currant jelly
9. Stick on the top of the pyramid a sprig of real flowers
