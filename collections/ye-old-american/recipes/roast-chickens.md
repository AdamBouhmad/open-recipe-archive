## Ingredients

- chickens
- soda
- water
- bread-crumbs
- butter
- pepper
- salt
- gravy
- giblets
- necks
- browned flour
- crab-apple jelly or tomato sauce

## Directions

1. Pick and draw the chickens, wash out well in two or three waters, adding a little soda to the last but one should any doubtful odor linger about the cavity.
2. Prepare a stuffing of bread-crumbs, butter, pepper, salt, &c.
3. Fill the bodies and crops of the chickens, which should be young and plump; sew them up.
4. Roast an hour or more, in proportion to their size.
5. Baste two or three times with butter and water, afterward with their own gravy.
6. If laid flat within the dripping pan, put in at the first a little hot water to prevent burning.
7. Stew the giblets and necks in enough water to cover them.
8. When you have removed the fowls to a hot dish, pour this into the drippings.
9. Boil up once; add the giblets, chopped fine; thicken with browned flour; boil again, and send to table in a gravy-boat.
10. Serve with crab-apple jelly or tomato sauce.
