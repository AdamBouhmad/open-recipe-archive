## Ingredients

- green sage leaves
- onion
- butter
- pepper
- salt
- duck livers
- duck gizzards
- duck hearts
- salt and water
- flour
- onion-sauce
- chopped sage leaves
- stewed cranberries
- green peas

## Directions

1. After the ducks are drawn, wipe out the inside with a clean cloth, and prepare your stuffing.
2. Mince very fine some green sage leaves, and twice their quantity of onion, (which should first be parboiled,) and add a little butter, and a seasoning of pepper and salt.
3. Mix the whole very well, and fill the crops and bodies of the ducks with it, leaving a little space for the stuffing to swell.
4. Reserve the livers, gizzards, and hearts to put in the gravy.
5. Tie the bodies of the ducks firmly round with strings, (which should be wetted or buttered to keep them from burning,) and put them on the spit before a clear brisk fire.
6. Baste them first with a little salt and water, and then with their own gravy, dredging them lightly with flour at the last.
7. They will be done in about an hour.
8. After boiling the livers, gizzards and hearts, chop them, and put them into the gravy; having first skimmed it, and thickened it with a little browned flour.
9. Send to table with the ducks a small tureen of onion-sauce with chopped sage leaves in it.
10. Accompany them also with stewed cranberries and green peas, if in season.
