## Ingredients

- fine ripe pippin or bell-flower apples
- loaf-sugar (a pound to each pound of apples)
- water
- lemons (two lemons to a dozen apples)
- lemon juice
- cochineal (optional)

## Directions

1. Take fine ripe pippin or bell-flower apples. Pare and core them, and either leave them whole, or cut them into quarters. Weigh them.
2. Put the apples into a stew-pan with just water enough to cover them, and let them boil slowly for about half an hour. They must be only parboiled.
3. Strain the apple water over the sugar into a preserving kettle, and when the sugar is melted put it on the fire with the yellow rind of some lemons pared thin.
4. Boil the syrup till clear and thick, skimming it carefully; then put in the apples.
5. After they have boiled slowly a quarter of an hour, add the juice of the lemons.
6. Let it boil about fifteen minutes longer, or till the apples are tender and clear, but not till they break.
7. When they are cold, put them into jars, and covering them closely, let them set a week. At the end of that time give them another boil in the same syrup.
8. You may colour them red by adding, when you boil them in the syrup, a little cochineal.
