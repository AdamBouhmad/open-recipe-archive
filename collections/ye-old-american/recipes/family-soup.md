## Ingredients

- shin or leg of beef, newly killed
- water
- salt
- carrots
- parsnip
- large onion
- small turnips
- tomatas
- head of celery
- small head of cabbage
- bunch of sweet marjoram
- ochras (optional)
- Lima beans (optional)
- green peas (optional)

## Directions

1. Take a shin or leg of beef that has been newly killed; the fore leg is best, as there is the most meat on it. Have it cut into three pieces, and wash it well.
2. To each pound allow somewhat less than a quart of water; for instance, to ten pounds of leg of beef, nine quarts of water is a good proportion.
3. Put it into a large pot, and add half a table-spoonful of salt.
4. Hang it over a good fire, as early as six o'clock in the morning, if you dine at two.
5. When it has come to a hard boil, and the scum has risen, skim it well.
6. Do not remove the lid more frequently than is absolutely necessary, as uncovering the pot causes the flavour to evaporate.
7. Set it on hot coals in the corner, and keep it simmering steadily, adding fresh coals so as to continue a regular heat.
8. About nine o'clock, put in four carrots, one parsnip, and a large onion cut into slices, and four small turnips, and eight tomatas, also cut up; add a head of celery cut small.
9. Put in a very small head of cabbage, cut into little pieces.
10. Put in also a bunch of sweet marjoram, tied up in a thin muslin rag to prevent its floating on the top.
11. Let the soup simmer unceasingly till two o'clock, skimming it well.
12. Take it up, and put it into a tureen.
13. If you wish to send the meat to table, take the best part of it out of the soup, about two hours before dinner. Have ready another pot with a dozen tomatas and a few cloves. Moisten them with a little of the soup, just sufficient to keep them from burning.
14. When the tomatas have stewed down soft, put the meat upon them, and let it brown till dinner time over a few coals, keeping the pot closely covered.
15. Let the remainder of the meat be left in the large pot till you send up the soup.
16. If you do not intend to serve up the meat separately, break to pieces all the bones with a mallet or kitchen cleaver when you first begin the soup.
