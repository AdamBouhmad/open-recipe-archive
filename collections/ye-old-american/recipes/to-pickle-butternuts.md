## Ingredients

- butternuts
- lye
- hickory broom
- salt
- water
- large needle
- black pepper
- ginger root
- cloves
- mace
- nutmeg
- mustard seed
- cider vinegar

## Directions

1. Gather the nuts when young and soft, when the sun is hot upon them
2. Put them into a tub with sufficient lye to cover them, and stir and rub them about with a hickory broom till they are clean and smooth on the outside
3. Alternatively, scald them and then rub off the outer skin
4. Put the nuts into strong salt and water for one week, changing the water every other day, and keeping them closely covered from the air
5. Drain and wipe them, piercing each nut through in several places with a large needle
6. For a hundred large nuts, take of black pepper and ginger root of each an ounce; and of cloves, mace and nutmeg of each a half ounce
7. Pound all the spices to powder, and mix them well together, adding two large spoonfuls of mustard seed
8. Put the nuts into jars, having first stuck each of them through in several places with a large needle, strewing the powdered seasoning between every layer of nuts
9. Boil for five minutes a gallon of the very best cider vinegar, and pour it boiling hot upon the nuts
10. Secure the jars closely with corks and leathers
