## Ingredients

- a quart of rich milk or cream
- a vanilla bean
- a stick of the best Ceylon cinnamon
- yolks of twelve eggs
- a quarter of a pound or more of powdered white sugar
- half a pound of fine flour
- half a pound of fresh butter
- a pint of rich milk
- eight whole eggs
- powdered loaf-sugar

## Directions

1. From a quart of rich milk or cream take half a pint, and put it into a small saucepan, with a vanilla bean, and a stick of the best Ceylon cinnamon, broken in pieces.
2. Cover the saucepan closely, and let it boil till the milk is highly flavoured with the vanilla and cinnamon.
3. Strain it, take out the vanilla bean, wipe it, and put it away.
4. Mix the flavoured milk with the other pint and a half, and let it get quite cold.
5. Beat very light the yolks only of twelve eggs, and stir them into the milk alternately with a quarter of a pound, or more, of powdered white sugar.
6. Put this custard mixture into a tin pan, set it in a Dutch oven or something similar, pour round the pan some boiling water, enough to reach half-way up its sides, and bake the custard ten minutes.
7. Sift half a pound of fine flour, cut up half a pound of fresh butter in a pint of rich milk, and set it on a stove or near the fire till the butter is soft but not melted.
8. Stir it well and take it off.
9. Beat eight whole eggs very light, and stir them gradually into the milk and butter, in turn with the flour.
10. Ensure the batter is very smooth and quite free from lumps.
11. Put the batter in equal portions into deep pattypans with plain unscolloped sides, filling them but little more than half.
12. Sprinkle powdered loaf-sugar over the top of each.
13. Set them immediately into a brisk oven, and bake them about a quarter of an hour, or twenty minutes, until well browned.
14. When done, take them out, and open in the side of each (while quite hot) a slit or cut, large enough to admit a portion of the custard.
15. Put in with a spoon as much of the custard as will amply fill the cavity or hollow in the middle of each cake.
16. Close the slit nicely, by pinching and smoothing it with your thumb and finger, and set the cakes to cool.
17. Eat fresh.
