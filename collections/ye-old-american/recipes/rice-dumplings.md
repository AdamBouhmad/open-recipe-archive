## Ingredients

- a pound of rice
- two quarts of water
- a dozen pippins or other large juicy apples
- marmalade or lemon and sugar

## Directions

1. Pick and wash a pound of rice, and boil it gently in two quarts of water till it becomes dry, keeping the pot well covered, and not stirring it
2. Take it off the fire, and spread it out to cool on the bottom of an inverted sieve, loosening the grains lightly with a fork, that all the moisture may evaporate
3. Pare a dozen pippins or other large juicy apples, and scoop out the core
4. Fill up the cavity with marmalade, or with lemon and sugar
5. Cover every apple all over with a thick coating of the boiled rice
6. Tie up each in a separate cloth, and put them into a pot of cold water
7. They will require about an hour and a quarter after they begin to boil
8. Turn them out on a large dish, being careful not to break the dumplings
9. Eat them with cream sauce, or with wine sauce, or with butter, sugar, and nutmeg beaten together
