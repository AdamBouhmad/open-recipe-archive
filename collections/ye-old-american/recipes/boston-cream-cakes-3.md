## Ingredients

- half a cup of butter
- one cup of hot water
- a pint of sifted flour
- five eggs
- half a teaspoonful of soda
- a teaspoonful of salt

## Directions

1. Put into a large-sized saucepan half a cup of butter and one cup of hot water.
2. Set it on the fire; when the mixture begins to boil, turn in a pint of sifted flour at once.
3. Beat and work it well with a vegetable masher until it is very smooth.
4. Remove from the fire.
5. When cool enough add five eggs that have been well beaten, first the yolks and then the whites, also half a teaspoonful of soda and a teaspoonful of salt.
6. Drop on buttered tins in large spoonfuls about two inches apart.
7. Bake in a quick oven about fifteen minutes.
8. When done and quite cold, open them on the side with a knife or scissors and put in as much of the custard as possible.
9. For cream filling: Put two-thirds of a pint of milk over the fire in a double boiler.
10. In a third of a pint of milk, stir two eggs, three tablespoonfuls of sifted flour (or half cup of cornstarch) and one cup of sugar.
11. As soon as the milk looks like boiling, pour in the mixture and stir briskly for three minutes, until it thickens.
12. Remove from the fire and add a teaspoonful of butter.
13. When cool, flavor with vanilla or lemon and fill your cakes.
