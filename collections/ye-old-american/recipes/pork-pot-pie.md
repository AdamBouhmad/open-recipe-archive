## Ingredients

- lean pork
- bones
- light paste
- parboiled potatoes
- pepper
- salt
- cold water

## Directions

1. Cut lean pork from any part of the pig (the chine is best) into riblettes two inches long and crack the bones well.
2. Line a round-bottomed, well greased pot with a good light paste.
3. Put in the meat, then a layer of parboiled potatoes split in half, seasoning with pepper and salt as you go on.
4. When the pot is nearly full, pour in a quart of cold water and put on the upper crust, cutting a small round hole out of the middle.
5. Slips of paste may also be strewed among the meat and potatoes.
6. Put on the pot-lid and boil from one hour and a half to two hours.
7. When done, remove the upper crust carefully and turn out the meat and gravy into a bowl.
8. Lay the lower crust upon a hot dish, put the meat and gravy in order upon it, and cover with the top crust.
9. Brown with a red-hot shovel or oven-lid.
