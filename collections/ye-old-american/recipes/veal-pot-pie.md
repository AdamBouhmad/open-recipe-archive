## Ingredients

- a nice breast or brisket of veal, well jointed
- water
- pepper
- salt
- flour
- butter
- milk
- yeast

## Directions

1. Procure a nice breast or brisket of veal, well jointed, put the pieces into the pot with one quart of water to every five pounds of meat.
2. Put the pot over a slow fire; just before it comes to a boil, skim it well and pour in a teacupful of cold water.
3. Then turn over the meat in order that all the scum may rise.
4. Remove all the scum, boil quite hard, season with pepper and salt to your taste, always remembering that the crust will take up part of the seasoning.
5. When this is done cut off your crust in pieces of equal size, but do not roll or mould them.
6. Lay them on top of the meat, so as to cover it.
7. Put the lid on the pot closely, let the whole boil slowly one hour.
8. If the lid does not fit the pot closely, wrap a cloth around it, in order that no steam shall escape.
9. By no means allow the pot to stop boiling.
10. To make the crust: To three pints of flour add two ounces of butter, a little salt, and wet with milk sufficient to make a soft dough.
11. Knead it well and set it away to rise.
12. When quite light, mould and knead it again, and let it stand, in winter, one hour, in summer, one-half hour.
