## Ingredients

- Indian meal
- salt
- milk
- butter
- molasses

## Directions

1. Have ready on the fire a pot of boiling water.
2. Stir into it by degrees (a handful at a time) sufficient Indian meal to make it very thick.
3. Add a very small portion of salt.
4. Keep the pot boiling on the fire all the time you are throwing in the meal.
5. Between every handful, stir very hard with the mush-stick, (a round stick flattened at one end,) that the mush may not be lumpy.
6. After it is sufficiently thick, keep it boiling for an hour longer, stirring it occasionally.
7. Cover the pot, and hang it higher up the chimney, so as to simmer slowly or keep hot for another hour.
8. Eat it warm; either with milk, or cover your plate with mush, make a hole in the middle, put some butter in the hole and fill it up with molasses.
