## Ingredients

- a pound of flour
- a pound of butter
- a pound of powdered white sugar
- six eggs
- a large table-spoonful of mixed mace and cinnamon
- two grated nutmegs
- juice of two lemons, or a wine glass of rose water
- sugar for grating

## Directions

1. Sift a pound of flour into a large pan.
2. Cut up a pound of butter into a pound of powdered white sugar, and stir them to a cream.
3. Beat six eggs till very light, and then pour them all at once into the pan of flour.
4. Add the butter and sugar, with a large table-spoonful of mixed mace and cinnamon, two grated nutmegs, and the juice of two lemons or a wine glass of rose water.
5. Stir the mixture very hard with a broad knife.
6. Flour your hands and spread some flour on the paste-board.
7. Make the dough into long rolls, all of equal size.
8. Form them into rings by joining the two ends very nicely.
9. Lay them on buttered tins.
10. Bake them in a quick oven from five to ten minutes.
11. Grate sugar over them when cool.
