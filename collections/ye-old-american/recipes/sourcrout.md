## Ingredients

- white and firm cabbages
- coarse salt
- coriander
- juniper berries
- water
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Slice very white and firm cabbages in fine shreds with a machine
2. At the bottom of a small barrel place a layer of coarse salt
3. Alternately layer cabbage and salt, being careful to have one of salt on the top
4. As each layer of cabbage is added, press it down by a large and heavy pestle
5. Add fresh layers as soon as the juice floats on the surface
6. Season the cabbage with a few grains of coriander, juniper berries, etc.
7. When the barrel is full, put it in a dry cellar
8. Cover with a cloth, under a plank, and place heavy weights on this
9. At the end of a few days it will begin to ferment
10. Draw off the pickle and replace by fresh, until the liquor becomes clear
11. Do this every day
12. Renew the cloth and wash the cover, put the weights back and let stand for a month
13. By that time the sourcrout will be ready for use
14. Take care to let the least possible air enter the sourcrout and to have the cover perfectly clean
15. Each time the barrel has to be opened it must be properly closed again
