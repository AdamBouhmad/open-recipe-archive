## Ingredients

- raw venison
- bones
- skin
- fat
- refuse bits
- shallot
- pepper
- salt
- nutmeg
- sweet herbs
- cold water
- puff-paste
- butter
- cupful of liquor from stewing meat
- Port wine
- tablespoonful of butter
- juice of a lemon
- browned flour
- beaten egg
- clean paper

## Directions

1. Set aside bones, skin, fat, and refuse bits for gravy and put them into a saucepan with a shallot, pepper, salt, nutmeg and sweet herbs, cover well with cold water, and set on to boil.
2. Cut the better and fairer pieces of meat into squares an inch long, and cook in another saucepan until three-quarters done.
3. Line a deep dish with good puff-paste.
4. Put in the squares of venison, season with pepper, salt, and butter, and put in half a cupful of the liquor in which the meat was stewed, to keep it from burning at the bottom.
5. Cover with a lid of prepared pastry an inch thick.
6. Cut a round hole in the middle, and if you have not a small tin cylinder that will fit this, make one of buttered paper; stiff writing-paper is best.
7. Bake steadily, covering the top with a sheet of clean paper so soon as it is firm, to prevent it from browning too fast.
8. When all the substance has been extracted from the bones, strain the liquor back into the saucepan; let it come to a boil, and when you have skimmed carefully, add a glass of Port wine, a tablespoonful of butter, the juice of a lemon, and some browned flour to thicken.
9. Boil up once, remove the plug from the hole in the pastry, and pour in through a small funnel as much gravy as the pie will hold.
10. Brush the crust over with beaten egg and put back in the oven until it is a delicate brown, or rather, a golden russet.
11. Bake two or three hours, guiding yourself by the size of the pie.
