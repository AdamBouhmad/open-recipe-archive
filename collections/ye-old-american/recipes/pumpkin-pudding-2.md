## Ingredients

- a pint of pumpkin stewed soft and pressed through a cullender
- half a pint of warm milk
- a quarter of a pound of butter
- a quarter of a pound of sugar
- a pint of rich cream
- eight eggs
- a wine glass of rose water
- a glass of wine
- a large tea-spoonful of powdered mace and cinnamon mixed
- a grated nutmeg

## Directions

1. Take a pint of pumpkin that has been stewed soft, and pressed through a cullender.
2. Melt in half a pint of warm milk, a quarter of a pound of butter, and the same quantity of sugar, stirring them well together. If you can conveniently procure a pint of rich cream it will be better than the milk and butter.
3. Beat eight eggs very light, and add them gradually to the other ingredients, alternately with the pumpkin.
4. Then stir in a wine glass of rose water and a glass of wine mixed together, a large tea-spoonful of powdered mace and cinnamon mixed, and a grated nutmeg.
5. Having stirred the whole very hard, put it into a buttered dish and bake it three quarters of an hour.
6. Eat it cold.
