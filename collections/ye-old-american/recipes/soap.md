## Ingredients

- lye strong enough to bear an egg
- clean grease, three quarters of a pound per gallon of lye
- salt, one pint to three gallons

## Directions

1. Put any quantity of lye on the fire that is strong enough to bear an egg
2. To each gallon add three quarters of a pound of clean grease
3. Boil it very fast and stir it frequently
4. A few hours will suffice to make it good soap
5. When you find by cooling a little on a plate that it is a thick jelly and no grease appears, put in salt in the proportion of one pint to three gallons
6. Let it boil a few minutes and pour it in tubs to cool
7. Next day cut out the soap, melt it, and cool it again
8. This takes out all the lye and keeps the soap from shrinking when dried
