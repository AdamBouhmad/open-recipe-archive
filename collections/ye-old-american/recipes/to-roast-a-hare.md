## Ingredients

- hare
- water
- heart and liver
- force-meat
- bread-crumbs
- claret
- butter
- cream
- milk
- beaten yolk of egg
- flour
- drippings
- lemon-juice
- currant jelly

## Directions

1. Wash and soak it in water for an hour, and change the water several times, having made a little slit in the neck to let out the blood.
2. Take out the heart and liver, and scald them.
3. Drain, dry, and truss the hare.
4. Make a force-meat richer and more moist than usual, and add to it the heart and liver minced fine.
5. Soak the bread-crumbs in a little claret before you mix them with the other ingredients.
6. Stuff the body of the hare with this force-meat, and sew it up.
7. Put it on the spit, rub it with butter, and roast it before a brisk fire.
8. For the first half hour baste it with butter; and afterwards with cream, or with milk thickened with beaten yolk of egg.
9. At the last, dredge it lightly with flour.
10. The hare will require about two hours roasting.
11. For sauce, take the drippings of the hare mixed with cream or with claret, and a little lemon-juice, a bit of butter, and some bread-crumbs.
12. Give it a boil up, and send it to table in a boat.
13. Garnish the hare with slices of currant jelly laid round it in the dish.
