## Ingredients

- whitest and closest full-grown cauliflowers
- salt
- water
- coriander seed
- turmeric
- mustard seed
- ginger
- cider vinegar

## Directions

1. Take the whitest and closest full-grown cauliflowers, cut off the thick stalk, and split the blossom or flower part into eight or ten pieces
2. Spread them on a large dish, sprinkle them with salt, and let them stand twenty-four hours
3. Wash off the salt, drain them, put them into a broad flat jar or pan, scald them with salt and water, allowing a quarter of a pound of salt to a quart of water, cover them closely and let them stand in the brine till next day
4. Drain them in a hair sieve, and spread them on a cloth in a warm place to dry for a day and a night
5. Mix together three ounces of coriander seed, three ounces of turmeric, one ounce of mustard seed, and one ounce of ginger, and pound the whole in a mortar to a fine powder
6. Put it into three quarts of the very best cider vinegar, set it by the side of the fire in a stone jar, and let it infuse three days
7. Put the cauliflower carefully, piece by piece, into clean broad jars and pour over them the prepared pickle, keeping it well covered by the liquid
8. Secure the jars closely from the air
