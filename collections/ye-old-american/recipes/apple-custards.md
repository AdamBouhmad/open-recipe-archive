## Ingredients

- fine juicy apples, sufficient when stewed to fill two soup plates
- a lump of butter, about the size of a walnut
- grated peel of a lemon
- water
- fine brown sugar
- juice of a lemon or a wine-glass of rose-water
- a grated nutmeg
- a table-spoonful of brandy
- a table-spoonful of cream
- a table-spoonful of finely-grated bread crumbs
- the well-beaten yolk of an egg
- thin puff-paste

## Directions

1. Pare, core, and slice the apples
2. Add a lump of butter about the size of a walnut and the grated peel of a lemon
3. Stew them with as little water as can possibly keep them from burning until quite soft all through, but not broken
4. Mash them well with the back of a spoon and make them very sweet with fine brown sugar
5. Squeeze in the juice of a lemon or add a wine-glass of rose-water
6. When the apple is quite cold, add a grated nutmeg, a table-spoonful of brandy, and a table-spoonful of cream mixed with a table-spoonful of finely-grated bread crumbs and the well-beaten yolk of an egg
7. Stir the whole very hard
8. Cover the bottom and sides of two soup plates with thin puff-paste and put a thick paste round the edges, notching it handsomely
9. Fill up with the mixture and bake it about half an hour
