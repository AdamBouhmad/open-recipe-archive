## Ingredients

- flour
- two gills and a half of water for each quart
- a tea-spoon of salt for each quart
- a large spoonful of yeast for each quart

## Directions

1. Empty flour into a chest or box
2. sift the quantity you intend to make up
3. put into a bowl two gills and a half of water for each quart, with a tea-spoon heaped up with salt, and a large spoonful of yeast for each quart
4. stir this mixture well
5. put into another bowl one handful of flour from every quart
6. pour a little of the mixture on to wet it, then more, until you get it all in
7. beat it some minutes
8. take one-third of the flour out of the kettle, pour on the batter, and sprinkle over it the dry flour
9. stop the kettle, and set it where it can have a moderate degree of warmth
10. when it has risen well, turn it into a bowl, mix in the dry flour, and knead it on a board till it looks quite light
11. return it to the kettle, and place it where it can have proper heat
12. in the morning, take the dry crust carefully from the top
13. put the dough on a board, knead it well, make it into rolls
14. set them on tin sheets
15. put a towel over, and let them stand near the fire till the oven is ready
16. bake it and rasp when baked
