## Ingredients

- currant, blackberry, strawberry, or similar fruit
- sugar
- lemon juice (for strawberry)

## Directions

1. Put the fruit into a stone jar; set this in a kettle of tepid water, and put it upon the fire
2. Let it boil, closely covered, until the fruit is broken to pieces
3. Strain, pressing the bag (a stout coarse one) hard, putting in but a few handfuls at a time, and between each squeezing turning it inside out to scald off the pulp and skins
4. To each pint of juice allow a pound of sugar
5. Set the juice on alone to boil, and while it is warming divide the sugar into several different portions, and put into shallow pie-dishes or pans that will fit in your ovens
6. Heat in these, opening the ovens now and then to stir it and prevent burning
7. Boil the juice just twenty minutes from the moment it begins fairly to boil
8. Throw the sugar into the boiling juice, stirring rapidly all the while
9. Withdraw your spoon when you are sure it is dissolved
10. Let the jelly just come to a boil, to make all certain, and take the kettle instantly from the fire
11. Roll your glasses or cups in hot water, and fill with the scalding liquid
12. For strawberry jelly, add a little lemon-juice to that of the fruit
13. If less firm, set it in the sun, filling one cup from another as the contents shrink
14. Put brandied tissue-paper over the top of each glass when cold and firm, paste a thick paper over it, and keep in a dry place
