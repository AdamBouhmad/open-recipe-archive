## Ingredients

- two quarts of tomato pulp (or two cans of canned tomatoes)
- one onion, cut fine
- two tablespoonfuls of salt
- three tablespoonfuls of brown sugar
- two tablespoonfuls of mustard
- one tablespoonful of allspice
- one tablespoonful of black pepper
- one tablespoonful of cinnamon
- one teaspoonful of ground cloves
- half a teaspoonful of cayenne pepper
- one grated nutmeg
- one pint of good vinegar

## Directions

1. Put into two quarts of tomato pulp (or two cans of canned tomatoes) one onion, cut fine, two tablespoonfuls of salt and three tablespoonfuls of brown sugar.
2. Boil until quite thick; then take from the fire and strain it through a sieve, working it until it is all through but the seeds.
3. Put it back on the stove, and add two tablespoonfuls of mustard, one of allspice, one of black pepper and one of cinnamon, one teaspoonful of ground cloves, half a teaspoonful of cayenne pepper, one grated nutmeg, one pint of good vinegar.
4. Boil it until it will just run from the mouth of a bottle.
5. Watch it, stir often, that it does not burn.
6. If sealed tight while hot, in large-mouthed bottles, it will keep good for years.
