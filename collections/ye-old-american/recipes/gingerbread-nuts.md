## Ingredients

- half a pound of butter
- a pound and a half of sifted flour
- half a pound of brown sugar
- three table-spoonfuls of ginger
- a tea-spoonful of powdered cloves
- a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- a pint of molasses
- the grated peel of a large lemon
- a very small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash
- tepid water

## Directions

1. Rub half a pound of butter into a pound and a half of sifted flour
2. Mix in half a pound of brown sugar, crushed fine with the rolling-pin
3. Add three table-spoonfuls of ginger, a tea-spoonful of powdered cloves, and a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
4. Stir in a pint of molasses, and the grated peel of a large lemon, but not the juice
5. Add at the last a very small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash dissolved in tepid water
6. Stir the whole mixture very hard with a spaddle or with a wooden spoon
7. Make it into a lump of dough just stiff enough to roll out into a sheet about half an inch thick
8. Cut it out into small cakes about the size of a quarter dollar, or make it up with your hands well floured into little round balls, flattening them on the top
9. Lay them in buttered pans
10. Bake them in a moderate oven
