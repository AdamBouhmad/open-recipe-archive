## Ingredients

- gooseberries
- boiling water
- sugar

## Directions

1. Top and tail the gooseberries.
2. Pour some boiling water on the gooseberries, cover them up, and let them set about half an hour, or till the skin is quite tender, but not till it bursts.
3. Pour off the water, and mix with the gooseberries an equal quantity of sugar.
4. Put them into a porcelain stew-pan or skillet, and set it on hot coals, or on a charcoal furnace.
5. In a few minutes you may begin to mash them against the side of the pan with a wooden spoon.
6. Let them stew about half an hour, stirring them frequently.
7. They must be quite cold before they are used for any thing.
