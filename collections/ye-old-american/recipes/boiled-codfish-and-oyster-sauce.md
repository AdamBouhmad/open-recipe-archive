## Ingredients

- codfish
- salt
- flour
- whole cloves
- peppers
- lemon peel
- six oysters per pound of fish
- oyster liquor
- mace
- butter
- milk
- gill of milk
- half teaspoonful of flour

## Directions

1. Lay the fish in cold, salted water half an hour before it is time to cook it
2. Roll it in a clean cloth dredged with flour
3. Sew up the edges in such a manner as to envelop the fish entirely, yet have but one thickness of cloth over any part
4. Put the fish into boiling water slightly salted
5. Add a few whole cloves and peppers and a bit of lemon peel
6. Pull gently on the fins, and when they come out easily the fish is done
7. Arrange neatly on a folded napkin, garnish and serve with oyster sauce
8. For oyster sauce: Take six oysters to every pound of fish and scald (blanch) them in a half-pint of hot oyster liquor
9. Take out the oysters and add to the liquor, salt, pepper, a bit of mace and an ounce of butter
10. Whip into it a gill of milk containing half of a teaspoonful of flour
11. Simmer a moment
12. Add the oysters, and send to table in a sauce boat
