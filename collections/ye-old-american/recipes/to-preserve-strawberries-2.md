## Ingredients

- large ripe strawberries
- a pound of powdered loaf-sugar to each pound of strawberries
- apple jelly

## Directions

1. Gather strawberries in dry weather, when there has been no rain for at least two days
2. Hull, or pick off the green, select the largest and firmest
3. Spread them out separately on flat dishes
4. Weigh them, and allow to each pound of strawberries a pound of powdered loaf-sugar
5. Sift half the sugar over them
6. Take the inferior strawberries and those that are over-ripe, mix with them an equal quantity of powdered sugar, and mash them
7. Put them into a basin covered with a plate, and set them over the fire in a pan of boiling water, till they become a thick juice
8. Strain it through a bag, and mix with it the other half of the sugar
9. Put it into a porcelain kettle, and boil and skim it till the scum ceases to rise
10. Put in the whole strawberries with the sugar in which they have been lying, and all the juice that may have exuded from them
11. Set them over the fire in the syrup, just long enough to heat them a little
12. In a few minutes take them out, one by one, with a tea-spoon, and spread them on dishes to cool, not allowing them to touch each other
13. Repeat this several times, taking out the strawberries and cooling them till they become quite clear
14. When quite cold, put them with the syrup into tumblers, or into white queen's-ware pots
15. If intended to keep a long time put at the top a layer of apple jelly
