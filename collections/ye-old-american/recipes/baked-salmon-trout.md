## Ingredients

- salmon-trout
- water
- butter
- cream
- hot water
- parsley
- salt
- nasturtium blooms

## Directions

1. Handle the beauty with gentle respect while cleaning, washing, and wiping him, and lay him at full length, still respectfully, in a baking-pan, with just enough water to keep him from scorching.
2. If large, score the back-bone with a sharp knife, taking care not to mar the comeliness of his red-spotted sides.
3. Bake slowly, basting often with butter and water.
4. By the time he is done, have ready in a saucepan a cup of cream diluted with a few spoonfuls of hot water, in which have been stirred cautiously two tablespoonfuls of melted butter and a little chopped parsley.
5. Heat this in a vessel set within another of boiling water, add the gravy from the dripping-pan, boil up once to thicken.
6. When the trout is laid in a hot dish, pour the sauce around him.
7. Garnish with a wreath of crimson nasturtium blooms and dainty sprigs of parsley, arranged on the edge of the dish.
8. Salt him lightly, should he need it.
