## Ingredients

- Fifty heads of asparagus
- Half a dozen or more stale biscuit or rolls
- Pint of milk
- Four well-whipped eggs
- Great spoonful of butter
- Salt
- Pepper

## Directions

1. Cut off the tender tops of fifty heads of asparagus; boil and drain them.
2. Have ready half a dozen (or more) stale biscuit or rolls, from which you have cut a neat top slice and scraped out the crumb.
3. Set them in the oven to crisp, laying the tops beside them that the cavities may be well dried.
4. Put into a saucepan a sugarless custard made of a pint of milk, and four well-whipped eggs.
5. Boil the milk first, before beating in the eggs.
6. Set over the fire and stir until it thickens.
7. Add a great spoonful of butter, a little salt and pepper.
8. Add the asparagus tops, minced fine.
9. Do not let it boil, but take from the fire so soon as the asparagus is fairly in.
10. Fill the rolls with the mixture, put on the tops, fitting them accurately.
11. Set in the oven three minutes, and arrange on a dish.
