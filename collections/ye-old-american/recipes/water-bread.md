## Ingredients

- 2 cups boiling water
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 1 tablespoon lard
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1½ teaspoons salt
- ¼ yeast cake dissolved in ¼ cup lukewarm water
- 6 cups sifted flour

## Directions

1. Put butter, lard, sugar, and salt in bread raiser, or large bowl without a lip; pour on boiling water
2. When lukewarm, add dissolved yeast cake and five cups of flour
3. Stir until thoroughly mixed, using a knife or mixing-spoon
4. Add remaining flour, mix, and turn on a floured board, leaving a clean bowl
5. Knead until mixture is smooth, elastic to touch, and bubbles may be seen under the surface
6. Return to bowl, cover with a clean cloth kept for the purpose, and board or tin cover
7. Let rise over night in temperature of 65° F
8. In morning cut down by cutting through and turning over dough several times with a case knife, which checks fermentation for a short time
9. Dough may be again raised, and recut down if it is not convenient to shape into loaves or biscuits after first cutting
10. Toss on board slightly floured, knead, shape into loaves or biscuits, place in greased pans, having pans nearly half full
11. Cover, let rise again to double its bulk, and bake in hot oven
