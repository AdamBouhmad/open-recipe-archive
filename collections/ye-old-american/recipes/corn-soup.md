## Ingredients

- 1 large fowl, cut into eight pieces
- 1 doz. ears green corn—cut from the cobs
- 1 gallon water
- Pepper and salt
- Parsley
- Rice or wheat flour

## Directions

1. Boil the chicken with the cobs in a gallon of water until the fowl is tender—if tough, the boiling must be slow and long.
2. Then, put the corn into the pot, and stew an hour longer—still gently.
3. Remove the chicken with a cupful of the liquid, if you wish to make other use of the meat.
4. Set this aside, take out the cobs, season the corn-soup with pepper, salt, and parsley; thicken with rice or wheat flour, boil up once, and serve without straining, if the corn be young and tender.
