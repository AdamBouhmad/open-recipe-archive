## Ingredients

- venison cutlets
- cup of cold water
- half a pound of venison for gravy
- bones
- scraps of fat
- slips of fat salt pork
- young onion
- minced parsley
- thyme
- pepper
- nutmeg
- butter
- currant jelly
- Worcestershire or other piquant sauce
- half a glass of wine
- browned flour

## Directions

1. Trim the cutlets nicely, and make gravy of the refuse bits in the proportion of a cup of cold water to half a pound of venison
2. Put in bones, scraps of fat, etc., and set on in a saucepan to stew while you make ready the cutlets
3. Lard with slips of fat salt pork a quarter of an inch apart, and projecting slightly on either side
4. When the gravy has stewed an hour, strain and let it cool
5. Lay the cutlets in a saucepan, with a few pieces of young onion on each
6. Allow one onion to four or five pounds
7. Scatter also a little minced parsley and thyme between the layers of meat, with pepper, and a very little nutmeg
8. The pork lardoons will salt sufficiently
9. When you have put in all your meat, pour in the gravy, which should be warm—not hot
10. Stew steadily twenty minutes
11. Take up the cutlets and lay in a frying-pan in which you have heated just enough butter to prevent them from burning
12. Fry five minutes very quickly, turning the cutlets over and over to brown, without drying them
13. Lay in order in a chafing-dish, and have ready the gravy to pour over them without delay
14. This should be done by straining the liquor left in the saucepan and returning to the fire, with the addition of a tablespoonful of currant jelly, a teaspoonful Worcestershire or other piquant sauce, and half a glass of wine
15. Thicken with browned flour, boil up well and pour over the cutlets
16. Let all stand together in a hot dish five minutes before serving
