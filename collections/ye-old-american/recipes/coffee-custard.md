## Ingredients

- Fresh ground coffee
- Cold water
- Eggs
- Rich milk or cream
- Powdered loaf-sugar

## Directions

1. Take a large half pint of fresh ground coffee of the best quality, roasted that day
2. Put it into a grecque or French coffee pot with strainers inside
3. Lay the coffee on the upper strainer and pour on it half a pint of cold water
4. Press it down with the inner cover and put on the outer or top-lid
5. Stop the mouth of the spout with a roll or wad of soft white paper or with a closely-fitting cork
6. When the coffee liquid has filtered down through both strainers, pour it off into a bowl and return it to the upper strainer to filter down a second time
7. Make a custard-mixture of eight well-beaten eggs stirred gradually into a pint of cold rich milk or cream and three or four table-spoonfuls of powdered loaf-sugar
8. Stir the cold liquid coffee gradually into the custard-mixture
9. Put it into cups and set them in an iron oven or bake-pan with boiling water round them reaching rather more than half-way up the sides of the cups
10. Bake them ten minutes or more
11. Set them on ice and send them to table quite cold
