## Ingredients

- two quarts of oysters
- cayenne
- dozen crackers
- large lump of fresh butter
- quart of milk
- grated nutmeg
- dozen blades of mace
- head of celery
- pepper
- toasted bread

## Directions

1. Season two quarts of oysters with a little cayenne
2. Take them out of the liquor
3. Grate and roll fine a dozen crackers
4. Put them into the liquor with a large lump of fresh butter
5. When the grated biscuit has quite dissolved, add a quart of milk with a grated nutmeg, and a dozen blades of mace; and, if in season, a head of celery split fine and cut into small pieces
6. Season it to your taste with pepper
7. Mix the whole together, and set it in a closely covered vessel over a slow fire
8. When it comes to a boil, put in the oysters; and when it comes to a boil again, they will be sufficiently done
9. Before you send it to table put into the tureen some toasted bread cut into small squares, omitting the crust
