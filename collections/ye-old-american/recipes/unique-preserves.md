## Ingredients

- young cucumbers
- strong brine
- fair water
- alum
- vine-leaves
- ice-water
- raisins
- citron
- fine thread
- sugar
- ginger-root

## Directions

1. Gather young cucumbers, a little longer than your middle finger, and lay in strong brine one week
2. Wash them and soak a day and a night in fair water, changing this four times
3. Line a bell-metal kettle with vine-leaves, lay in the cucumbers, with a little alum scattered among them
4. Fill up with clear water; cover with vine-leaves, then with a close lid, and green as for pickles
5. Do not let them boil
6. When well greened, drop in ice-water
7. When perfectly cold, wipe, and with a small knife slit down one side
8. Dig out the seeds; stuff with a mixture of chopped raisins and citron
9. Sew up the incision with fine thread
10. Weigh them, and make a syrup, allowing a pound of sugar for every one of cucumbers, with a pint of water
11. Heat to a lively boil, skim, and drop in the fruit
12. Simmer half an hour, take out and spread upon a dish in the sun while you boil down the syrup, with a few slices of ginger-root added
13. When thick, put in the cucumbers again; simmer five minutes and put up in glass jars
14. Tie them up when cold
