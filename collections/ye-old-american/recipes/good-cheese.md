## Ingredients

- Three buckets of milk warm from the cow
- Half a tea-cupful of infusion of rennet or rennet-water
- Salt to taste
- Lard for greasing

## Directions

1. Strain the milk immediately into a large tub or kettle
2. Stir into it half a tea-cupful of infusion of rennet or rennet-water
3. Cover it and set it in a warm place for about half an hour, or till it becomes a firm curd
4. Cut the curd into squares with a large knife or wooden slitting-dish, and let it stand about fifteen minutes
5. Break it up fine with your hands, and let it stand a quarter of an hour longer
6. Pour off from the top as much of the whey as you can
7. Tie up the curd in a linen cloth or bag, and hang it up to drain out the remainder of the whey, setting a pan under it to catch the droppings
8. After all the whey is drained out, put the curd into the cheese-tray, and cut it again into slices
9. Chop it coarse, put a cloth about it, place it in the cheese-hoop or mould, and set it in the screw press for half an hour, pressing it hard
10. Take it out, chop the curd very fine, add salt to your taste, and put it again into the cheese-hoop with a cloth about it, and press it again
11. Wet the cloth all over to prevent its sticking to the cheese and tearing the surface
12. Let it remain in the press till next morning, then take it out and turn it
13. Wrap it in a clean wet cloth and replace it in the press, where it must remain all day
14. On the following morning take out the cheese, turn it, renew the cloth, and put it again into the press
15. Continue pressing for three days total
16. When you finally take it out of the press, grease the cheese all over with lard
17. Put it on a clean shelf in a dry dark room or in a wire safe
18. Wipe, grease, and turn it carefully every day until firm, then continue to rub it all over every day with a clean dry cloth
19. Continue this for five or six weeks; the cheese will then be fit to eat
