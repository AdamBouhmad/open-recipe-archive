## Ingredients

- a peck of button tomatoes
- a pound of the best double-refined loaf-sugar for each pound of tomatoes
- ten lemons
- a quarter of a pound of root ginger
- water

## Directions

1. Take button tomatoes, full grown but quite green, and weigh them.
2. Scald and peel the tomatoes.
3. Roll ten lemons under your hand on a table to increase the juice.
4. Grate off the yellow surface of the rind onto lumps of sugar, scraping up the zest with a spoon and transferring it to a bowl.
5. Squeeze the juice of the lemons through a strainer over the zest.
6. Scrape off the outside of the root ginger, grate the ginger and mix it with the lemon and zest.
7. Put the sugar into a large preserving kettle and pour water on it, allowing half a pint of water to each pound of sugar.
8. Stir with a large, clean wooden spoon until the sugar melts.
9. Set it over a clear fire and boil and skim it.
10. When it has boiled and is very clear and the scum has ceased to rise, put in the tomatoes and boil them till every one has slightly burst.
11. Add the lemon and ginger mixture, and boil about a quarter of an hour longer.
12. Take them out and spread them on large dishes to cool.
13. Boil the syrup by itself ten minutes longer.
14. Put the tomatoes into jars about half full and fill up with the syrup.
15. Cover the jars closely and paste paper round the lids, or tie bladders over them.
