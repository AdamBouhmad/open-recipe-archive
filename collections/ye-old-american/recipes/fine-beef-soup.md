## Ingredients

- fresh beef, newly killed
- water
- pepper
- salt
- carrots
- turnips
- onions
- celery
- tomatas

## Directions

1. Begin this soup the day before it is wanted.
2. Take a good piece of fresh beef that has been newly killed: any substantial part will do that has not too much fat about it; a fore leg is very good for this purpose. Wash it well.
3. Cut off all the meat, and break up the bones.
4. Put the meat and the bones into a large pot, very early in the day, so as to allow eight or nine hours for its boiling.
5. Proportion the water to the quantity of meat--about a pint and a half to each pound.
6. Sprinkle the meat with a small quantity of pepper and salt.
7. Pour on the water, hang it over a moderate fire, and boil it slowly: carefully skimming off all the fat that rises to the top, and keeping it closely covered, except when you raise the lid to skim it.
8. Do not put in additional water to this soup while it is boiling; and take care that the boiling goes steadily on.
9. After the meat is reduced to rags, and the soup sufficiently boiled, remove the pot from the fire, and let it stand in the corner for a quarter of an hour to settle.
10. Take it up, strain it into a large earthen pan, cover it, and set it away in a cool dry place till next day.
11. On the following morning, boil separately, carrots, turnips, onions, celery, and whatever other vegetables you intend to thicken the soup with.
12. Prepare tomatas by taking off the skin, cutting them into small pieces, and stewing them in their own juice till they are entirely dissolved.
13. Put on the carrots before any of the other vegetables, as they require the longest time to boil.
14. Scrape off carefully from the cake of jellied soup whatever fat or sediment may still be remaining on it; divide the jelly into pieces.
15. About half an hour before it is to go to table, put it into a pot, add the various vegetables, having first sliced them, in sufficient quantities to make the soup very thick.
16. Hang it over the fire and let it boil slowly, or simmer steadily till dinner time.
