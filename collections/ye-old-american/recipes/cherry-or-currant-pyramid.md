## Ingredients

- cherries or currants
- good light crust
- sugar

## Directions

1. Wash and stone the cherries, or pick the currants from their stems.
2. Make some good light crust, roll it out a quarter of an inch thick, and cut for the bottom a round piece about the size of a tea-plate.
3. Spread your fruit upon this, and sprinkle with sugar, leaving a half inch margin all around.
4. Roll out a second sheet an inch less in diameter than the first, lay it carefully upon the fruit, and turn up the margin of the lower piece over the edge of this.
5. Spread this, in turn, with fruit and sugar, and cover with a third and lessening round; proceeding in this order until the sixth and topmost cover is not more than three inches across.
6. Have ready a conical cap of stout muslin adapted to the proportions and dimensions of your pile; dip it in boiling water, flour inside, and draw gently over all.
7. Boil two hours, and eat with sweet sauce.
