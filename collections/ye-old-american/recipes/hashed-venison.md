## Ingredients

- remains of cold roast venison
- bones
- fat and scraps
- large teacupful of cold water
- small onion
- parsley
- thyme
- pepper
- salt
- three or four whole cloves
- gravy from the roast
- tablespoonful currant jelly
- tablespoonful tomato or mushroom catsup
- teaspoonful of anchovy sauce
- browned flour

## Directions

1. Put the bones with the fat and other scraps in a saucepan, with a large teacupful of cold water, a small onion minced, parsley and thyme, pepper and salt, and three or four whole cloves.
2. Stew for an hour.
3. Strain and return to the saucepan, with whatever gravy was left from the roast, a tablespoonful currant jelly, one of tomato or mushroom catsup, a teaspoonful of anchovy sauce, and a little browned flour.
4. Boil for three minutes.
5. Slice the meat from the bones and lay in the venison, cut into slices about an inch long, and let all heat over the fire for eight minutes, but do not allow the hash to boil.
6. Stir frequently, and when it is smoking hot, turn into a deep-covered dish.
