## Ingredients

- Calves' feet
- Water
- Vanilla bean
- Milk
- Rich cream
- Eggs
- Loaf-sugar
- Lady-fingers or finger biscuits
- White of egg

## Directions

1. The day before you want the Charlotte russe, make a stiff plain jelly by boiling four calves' feet in a gallon of water till the meat drops from the bone
2. Boil slowly till the liquid is reduced to less than two quarts
3. Strain it and measure three pints of the liquid into a pan, cover it, and set it away to congeal
4. Next morning it should be a solid cake; carefully scrape off all the fat and sediment
5. Boil a vanilla bean in half a pint of milk till the milk is very highly flavoured with the vanilla
6. Strain it and set it away to get cold
7. Take three pints of rich cream, put it into a shallow pan, set it on ice, and beat it to a stiff froth with rods or a whisk
8. Add to the cream the vanilla milk and beat both together
9. Melt the jelly in a pan over the fire
10. Beat very light the yolks of six eggs and stir gradually into the beaten egg half a pound of powdered loaf-sugar
11. Add by degrees the melted jelly to the egg and sugar, stirring very hard
12. Keep the vessel sitting on ice and continue stirring till the mixture is firm enough to retain the mark of the spoon
13. Stir in the cream as quickly as possible
14. Have ready the tin mould lined with lady-fingers or finger biscuits brushed over with beaten white of egg, laid closely across each other on the bottom and arranged to stand up in a circle round the sides of the mould
15. Carefully put in the mixture and cover the top with lady-fingers laid closely across
16. Set it on ice till wanted
17. When you wish to turn out the Charlotte russe, wrap round the outside of the mould a coarse towel dipped in cold water and lift it off
