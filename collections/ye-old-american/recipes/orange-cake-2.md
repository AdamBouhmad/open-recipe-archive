## Ingredients

- four ripe oranges
- a pound of the best loaf-sugar
- orange juice
- a pound of the best fresh butter
- fourteen ounces of fine flour
- ten eggs

## Directions

1. Take four ripe oranges, and roll them under your hand on the table.
2. Break up a pound of the best loaf-sugar, and on some of the pieces rub off the yellow rind of the oranges.
3. Cut the oranges, and squeeze their juice through a strainer.
4. Powder the sugar, and mix the orange-juice with it; reserving a little of the juice to flavour the icing.
5. Wash, and squeeze in a pan of cold water, a pound of the best fresh butter, till you have extracted whatever milk and salt may have been in it, as they will impede the lightness of the cake.
6. Cut up the butter in the pan of sugar and orange, and stir it hard till perfectly light, white, and creamy.
7. Sift into a pan fourteen ounces (two ounces less than a pound) of fine flour.
8. Beat ten eggs till they are as thick and smooth as a fine boiled custard.
9. Stir them, by degrees, into the butter and sugar, alternately with the flour, a little of each at a time.
10. Continue to beat the whole very hard for some time after all the ingredients are in, as this cake requires a great deal of beating.
11. Have ready a large square, shallow pan, well buttered.
12. Put in the mixture, and set it immediately into a brisk oven.
13. Bake thoroughly, as otherwise it will be heavy, streaked, and unfit to eat.
