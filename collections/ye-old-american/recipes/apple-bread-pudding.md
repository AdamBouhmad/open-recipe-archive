## Ingredients

- a dozen or more fine juicy pippins or bell-flowers
- the yellow rind of a large lemon pared very thin
- the juice of a lemon or a tea-spoonful of essence of lemon
- brown sugar
- the best fresh butter
- grated bread-crumbs
- a grated nutmeg

## Directions

1. Pare, core, and slice thin a dozen or more fine juicy pippins, or bell-flowers
2. Strew among them some bits of the yellow rind of a large lemon that has been pared very thin
3. Squeeze over them the juice of the lemon, or substitute a tea-spoonful of essence of lemon
4. Cover the bottom of a large deep dish with a thick layer of the sliced apples
5. Strew it thickly with brown sugar
6. Scatter on a few very small bits of the best fresh butter
7. Strew over it a thin layer of grated bread-crumbs
8. Repeat layers of apple, followed by sugar, butter, and bread-crumbs
9. Continue till you get the dish full, finishing with a thin layer of crumbs
10. Put the dish into a moderate oven, and bake the pudding well until the apples are thoroughly done and as soft as marmalade
11. Send it to table either hot or cold
12. Eat it with cream-sauce, or with butter, sugar, and nutmeg, stirred to a cream
