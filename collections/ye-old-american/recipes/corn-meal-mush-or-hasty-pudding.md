## Ingredients

- two quarts of water
- tablespoonful of salt
- sweet, fresh yellow or white corn meal

## Directions

1. Put two quarts of water into a clean dinner-pot or stewpan, cover it and let it become boiling hot over the fire
2. Add a tablespoonful of salt, take off the light scum from the top
3. Have sweet, fresh yellow or white corn meal; take a handful of the meal with the left hand and a pudding-stick in the right
4. With the stick, stir the water around and by degrees let fall the meal
5. When one handful is exhausted, refill it; continue to stir and add meal until it is as thick as you can stir easily, or until the stick will stand in it
6. Stir it awhile longer; let the fire be gentle
7. When it is sufficiently cooked, which will be in half an hour, it will bubble or puff up
8. Turn it into a deep basin
9. This is eaten cold or hot, with milk or with butter and syrup or sugar, or with meat and gravy, the same as potatoes or rice
