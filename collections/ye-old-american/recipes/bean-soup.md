## Ingredients

- two quarts of dried white beans
- five pounds lean fresh beef
- bones
- pound of bacon
- leftover roasted beef
- pepper
- six quarts of water
- head of celery
- table-spoonful of pounded celery-seed
- small squares of toasted bread

## Directions

1. Put two quarts of dried white beans into soak the night before you make the soup, which should be put on as early in the day as possible.
2. Take five pounds of the lean of fresh beef--the coarse pieces will do.
3. Cut them up, and put them into your soup-pot with the bones belonging to them, (which should be broken to pieces,) and a pound of bacon cut very small.
4. If you have the remains of a piece of beef that has been roasted the day before, and so much under-done that the juices remain in it, you may put it into the pot, and its bones along with it.
5. Season the meat with pepper only, and pour on it six quarts of water.
6. As soon as it boils take off the scum, and put in the beans (having first drained them) and a head of celery cut small, or a table-spoonful of pounded celery-seed.
7. Boil it slowly till the meat is done to shreds, and the beans all dissolved.
8. Then strain it through a cullender into the tureen, and put into it small squares of toasted bread with the crust cut off.
