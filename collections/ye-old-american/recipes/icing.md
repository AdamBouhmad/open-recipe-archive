## Ingredients

- Whites of 4 eggs
- 1 pound powdered white sugar
- Lemon, vanilla, or other seasoning

## Directions

1. Break the whites into a broad, clean, cool dish.
2. Throw a small handful of sugar upon them, and begin whipping it in with slow, steady strokes of the beater.
3. A few minutes later, throw in more sugar, and keep adding it at intervals until it is all used up.
4. Beat perseveringly until the icing is of a smooth, fine, and firm texture.
5. Half an hour's beating should be sufficient, if done well.
6. If not stiff enough, put in more sugar.
7. If you season with lemon-juice, allow, in measuring your sugar, for the additional liquid.
8. Lemon-juice or a very little tartaric acid whitens the icing.
9. Pour the icing by the spoonful on the top of the cake and near the centre of the surface to be covered.
10. If the loaf is of such a shape that the liquid will settle of itself to its place, it is best to let it do so.
11. If you spread it, use a broad-bladed knife, dipped in cold water.
12. You may set it in a moderate oven for three minutes, if you are in great haste.
13. The better plan is to dry in a sunny window, where the air can get at it, and where there is no dust.
14. Color icing yellow by putting the grated peel of a lemon or orange in a thin muslin bag, straining a little juice through it, and squeezing it hard into the egg and sugar.
15. Strawberry-juice colors a pretty pink, as does also cranberry-syrup.
