## Ingredients

- cold roast veal
- veal gravy
- fresh butter
- tomata catchup

## Directions

1. Always save the gravy of roast meat
2. Skim off the fat, and pour the gravy through a strainer into a jar
3. Cover it closely, and set it away in a refrigerator, or some very cold place, till next day
4. Take some cold roast veal, and cut it into small mouthfuls
5. Put it into a skillet or stew-pan, without a drop of water
6. Add to it the veal gravy that was left the preceding day, and a small lump of fresh butter
7. Cover the skillet, and let the hash stew over the fire for half an hour
8. Put to it a large table-spoonful of tomata catchup, or more, according to the quantity of meat
9. Cover the hash, and let it stew half an hour longer
