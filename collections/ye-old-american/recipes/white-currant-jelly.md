## Ingredients

- ripe white currants
- best double refined loaf-sugar
- clear spring water or river water filtered

## Directions

1. Select currants that are quite ripe and gathered on a dry day
2. Strip them from the stalks and put into a close stone jar
3. Set it in a kettle of boiling water
4. When all the currants are broken, take them out and strain through a linen cloth
5. To each pint of juice allow a pound and a quarter of the best double refined loaf-sugar
6. Break the sugar small and put it into a porcelain preserving pan with barely sufficient water to melt it
7. Use not quite half a pint to a pound and a quarter of sugar of either clear spring water or river water filtered
8. Stir up the sugar while it is dissolving
9. When all is melted, put it over a brisk fire
10. Boil and skim it till clear and thick
11. When the scum ceases to rise, put in the white currant juice and boil it fast for ten minutes
12. Put it warm into tumblers and when cold, cover with double white tissue paper
13. Use only a silver spoon and carefully observe all precautions so it may be transparent and delicate
14. If not quite clear and bright when done boiling, run it again through a jelly-bag
