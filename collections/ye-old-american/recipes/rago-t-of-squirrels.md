## Ingredients

- A pair of fine young squirrels
- Salt water
- 1 onion
- 1 tablespoonful of butter
- 5 tablespoonfuls of boiling water
- 2 teaspoonfuls of browned flour
- A quarter of a pound of bacon cut into slips
- Pepper and salt
- Half a cupful of tepid water
- A glass of wine
- The juice of half a lemon

## Directions

1. Skin, clean, and quarter a pair of fine young squirrels, and soak in salt and water to draw out the blood.
2. Slice an onion and fry brown in a tablespoonful of butter.
3. Stir into the frying-pan five tablespoonfuls of boiling water, and thicken with two teaspoonfuls of browned flour.
4. Put the squirrels into a saucepan, with a quarter of a pound of bacon cut into slips; season with pepper and salt to taste, add the onion and gravy, and half a cupful of tepid water.
5. Cover and stew for forty minutes, or until tender.
6. Pour in a glass of wine and the juice of half a lemon, shake around well, and turn into a deep covered dish.
