## Ingredients

- a quart of rich milk
- a small loaf of baker's bread
- four eggs
- a table-spoonful and a half of fine wheat flour
- half a pound of whole raisins (optional)
- wine sauce, or sugar and butter, or molasses

## Directions

1. Boil a quart of rich milk.
2. While it is boiling, take a small loaf of baker's bread, such as is sold for five or six cents.
3. Pare off all the crust, and cut up the crumb into very small pieces.
4. Put the bread into a pan; and when the milk boils, pour it scalding hot over the bread.
5. Cover the pan closely, and let it steep in the hot steam for about three quarters of an hour.
6. Remove the cover, and allow the bread and milk to cool.
7. Beat four eggs till they are thick and smooth.
8. Beat into them a table-spoonful and a half of fine wheat flour.
9. Beat the egg and flour into the bread and milk, and continue to beat hard till the mixture is as light as possible.
10. Have ready over the fire a pot of boiling water.
11. Dip your pudding-cloth into it, and shake it out.
12. Spread out the cloth in a deep dish or pan, and dredge it well with flour.
13. Pour in the mixture, and tie up the cloth, leaving room for it to swell.
14. Tie the string firmly and plaster up the opening with flour moistened with water.
15. See that the water boils when you put in the pudding, and keep it boiling hard.
16. If the pot wants replenishing, do it with boiling water from a kettle.
17. Boil it an hour and a half.
18. Turn it out of the bag the minute before you send it to table.
19. Eat it with wine sauce, or with sugar and butter, or molasses.
