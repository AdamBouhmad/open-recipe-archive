## Ingredients

- large fine ripe lemons
- loaf-sugar
- water
- lemon-juice
- white of egg

## Directions

1. Take large fine ripe lemons, that have no blemishes, choosing those with thin, smooth rinds.
2. With a sharp knife scoop a hole in the stalk end of each, large enough to admit the handle of a tea-spoon.
3. Put them into a preserving kettle with clear water, and boil them gently till you find them tender, keeping the kettle uncovered.
4. Take them out, drain, and cool them, and put them into a small tub.
5. Prepare a thin syrup of a pound of loaf-sugar to a quart of water.
6. Boil and skim it, and pour it over the lemons and cover them.
7. Let them stand in the syrup till next day.
8. Pour the syrup from the lemons, and spread them on a large dish.
9. Boil it a quarter of an hour, and pour it over them again, having first returned them to the tub.
10. Cover them, and let them again stand till next day.
11. Boil the syrup and pour it over them again.
12. Repeat this process every day till you find that the lemons are quite clear, and that the syrup has penetrated them thoroughly.
13. If the syrup becomes too weak, add a little more sugar to it.
14. Finally, make a strong syrup in the proportion of half a pint of water to a pound of sugar, adding a jill of raw lemon-juice squeezed from fresh lemons, and allowing to every two pounds of sugar the beaten white of an egg.
15. Mix all well together in the kettle.
16. Boil and skim it, and when the scum ceases to rise, pour the syrup boiling hot over the lemons; and covering them closely, let them stand undisturbed for four days.
17. Look at them, and if you find that they have not sucked in enough of the syrup to make the inside very sweet, boil them gently in the syrup for a quarter of an hour.
18. When they are cold, put them up in glass jars.
