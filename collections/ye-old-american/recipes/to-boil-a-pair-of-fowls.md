## Ingredients

- grated bread-crumbs
- chopped sweet herbs
- butter
- pepper
- salt
- yolk of egg
- water
- flour
- egg-sauce
- parsley

## Directions

1. Make a force-meat in the usual manner, of grated bread-crumbs, chopped sweet herbs, butter, pepper, salt, and yolk of egg.
2. Fill the bodies of the fowls with the stuffing, and tie a string firmly round them.
3. Skewer the livers and gizzards to the sides, under the wings.
4. Dredge them with flour, and put them into a pot with just enough of water to cook them; cover it closely, and put it over a moderate fire.
5. As soon as the scum rises, take off the pot and skim it.
6. Then cover it again, and boil it slowly half an hour.
7. Afterwards diminish the fire, and let them stew slowly till quite tender.
8. An hour altogether is generally sufficient to boil a pair of fowls, unless they are quite old.
9. By doing them slowly (rather stewing than boiling) the skin will not break, and they will be whiter and more tender than if boiled fast.
10. Serve them up with egg-sauce in a boat.
