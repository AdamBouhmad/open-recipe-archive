## Ingredients

- two fowls or chickens
- one-half onion
- sprig of parsley
- bit of bay leaf
- boiling water
- one-half tablespoon salt
- one-eighth teaspoon pepper
- one-third cup flour
- cold water
- pie-crust

## Directions

1. Dress, clean, and cut up two fowls or chickens.
2. Put in a stewpan with one-half onion, sprig of parsley, and bit of bay leaf; cover with boiling water, and cook slowly until tender.
3. When chicken is half cooked, add one-half tablespoon salt and one-eighth teaspoon pepper.
4. Remove chicken, strain stock, skim off fat, and then cook until reduced to four cups.
5. Thicken stock with one-third cup flour diluted with enough cold water to pour easily.
6. Place a small cup in centre of baking-dish, arrange around it pieces of chicken, removing some of the larger bones; pour over gravy, and cool.
7. Cover with pie-crust in which several incisions have been made, that there may be an outlet for escape of steam and gases.
8. Wet edge of crust and put around a rim, having rim come close to edge.
9. Bake in a moderate oven until crust is well risen and browned.
10. Roll remnants of pastry and cut in diamond-shaped pieces, bake, and serve with pie when reheated.
