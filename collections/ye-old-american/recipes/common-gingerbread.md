## Ingredients

- a pound of butter
- a quart of West India molasses
- half a pound of brown sugar
- three table-spoonfuls of ginger
- a large tea-spoonful of powdered cloves
- a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- sufficient flour
- a small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash
- a little sour milk

## Directions

1. Cut up a pound of butter in a quart of West India molasses, which must be perfectly sweet
2. Warm it slightly, just enough to melt the butter
3. Crush with the rolling-pin, on the paste-board, half a pound of brown sugar
4. Add it by degrees to the molasses and butter
5. Stir in three table-spoonfuls of ginger, a large tea-spoonful of powdered cloves, and a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
6. Add gradually sufficient flour to make a dough stiff enough to roll out easily
7. Add a small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash melted in a little sour milk
8. Mix and stir the dough very hard with a spaddle, or a wooden spoon, but do not knead it
9. Divide it with a knife into equal portions
10. Having floured your hands, roll it out on the paste-board into long even strips
11. Place them in shallow tin pans, that have been buttered, either laying the strips side by side in straight round sticks, or coil them into rings one within another
12. Bake them in a brisk oven, taking care that they do not burn
