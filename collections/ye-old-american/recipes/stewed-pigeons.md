## Ingredients

- pigeons
- cold water
- pepper
- salt
- a few blades of mace
- a little sweet marjoram
- a good piece of butter
- a tablespoonful of browned flour
- salt pork or ham

## Directions

1. Pick, draw, clean and stuff as directed
2. Put the pigeons in a deep pot with enough cold water to cover them, and stew gently for an hour, or until, testing them with a fork, you find them tender
3. Then season with pepper, salt, a few blades of mace, a little sweet marjoram, and a good piece of butter
4. Stew, or rather simmer, for five minutes longer
5. Stir in a tablespoonful of browned flour
6. Let it boil up once; remove the pigeons, draw out the strings with which they were sewed up, and serve, pouring the hot gravy over them
7. A little salt pork or ham, cut into strips, is an improvement and should be put in when the pigeons have stewed half an hour
