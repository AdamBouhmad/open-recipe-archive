## Ingredients

- calf's head
- set of feet
- brains
- sweet herbs
- sliced onions
- pepper
- salt
- butter rolled in flour
- water
- cayenne pepper
- madeira
- lemon, juice and grated peel
- grated bread-crumbs
- nutmeg
- beaten yolk of egg
- lard

## Directions

1. Take a calf's head and a set of feet, and boil them until tender, having first removed the brains.
2. Cut the flesh off the head and feet in slices from the bone, and put both meat and bones into a stew-pan with a bunch of sweet herbs, some sliced onions, and pepper and salt to your taste; also a large piece of butter rolled in flour, and a little water.
3. After it has stewed awhile slowly till the flavour is well extracted from the herbs and onions, take out the meat, season it a little with cayenne pepper, and lay it in a dish.
4. Strain the gravy in which it was stewed, and stir into it two glasses of madeira, and the juice and grated peel of a lemon.
5. Pour some of the gravy over the meat, lay a piece of butter on the top, set it in an oven and bake it brown.
6. Clean and wash the brains (skinning them and removing the strings) and parboil them in a sauce-pan.
7. Make them into balls with chopped sweet herbs, grated bread-crumbs, grated lemon-peel, nutmeg, and beaten yolk of egg.
8. Fry them in lard and butter mixed.
9. Send them to table laid round the meat (which should have the tongue placed on the top) and garnish with sliced lemon.
