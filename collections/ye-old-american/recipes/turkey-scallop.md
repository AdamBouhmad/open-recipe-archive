## Ingredients

- cold boiled or roasted turkey
- cracker or bread-crumbs
- milk
- stuffing
- pepper
- salt
- butter
- gravy
- hot water
- Worcestershire sauce or catsup
- eggs

## Directions

1. Cut the meat from the bones of a cold boiled or roasted turkey left from yesterday's dinner.
2. Remove the bits of skin and gristle, and chop up the rest very fine.
3. Put in the bottom of a buttered dish a layer of cracker or bread-crumbs; moisten slightly with milk, that they may not absorb all the gravy to be poured in afterward.
4. Spread a layer of the minced turkey, with bits of the stuffing, pepper, salt, and small pieces of butter.
5. Another layer of cracker, wet with milk, and so on until the dish is nearly full.
6. Pour in the gravy left from the turkey, diluted—should there not be enough—with hot water, and seasoned with Worcestershire sauce, or catsup, and butter.
7. Have ready a crust of cracker-crumbs soaked in warm milk, seasoned with salt, and beaten up light with two eggs.
8. Spread this mixture smoothly over the top of the scallop.
9. Stick bits of butter plentifully upon it, and bake.
10. Turn a deep plate over the dish until the contents begin to bubble at the sides, showing that the whole is thoroughly cooked.
11. Remove the cover, and brown.
12. A large pudding-dish full of the mixture will be cooked in three-quarters of an hour.
