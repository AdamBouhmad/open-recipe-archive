## Ingredients

- fourteen ounces of the finest flour
- a table-spoonful of beaten cinnamon
- a tea-spoonful of mace
- two beaten nutmegs
- half a glass of white wine
- half a glass of brandy
- half a glass of rose water
- a pound of loaf-sugar
- a pound of fresh butter
- ten eggs
- juice of two lemons

## Directions

1. Sift fourteen ounces of the finest flour, being two ounces less than a pound.
2. Prepare a table-spoonful of beaten cinnamon, a tea-spoonful of mace, and two beaten nutmegs; and mix them all together when powdered.
3. Mix in a tumbler, half a glass of white wine, half a glass of brandy, and half a glass of rose water.
4. Powder a pound of loaf-sugar, and sift it into a deep pan; cut up in it a pound of fresh butter; warm them by the fire, and stir them to a cream.
5. Add gradually the spice and the liquor.
6. Beat ten eggs very light, and stir them into the mixture in turn with the flour.
7. Stir in the juice of two lemons, and beat the whole very hard.
8. Butter some little tins; half fill them with the mixture; set them into a brisk oven, and bake them about a quarter of an hour.
9. When done, they will shrink from the sides of the tins.
10. After you turn them out, spread them on an inverted sieve to cool.
11. If you have occasion to fill your tins a second time, scrape and wipe them well before they are used again.
12. Make an icing, flavoured with lemon juice or with extract of roses; and spread two coats of it on the queen cakes.
13. Set them to dry in a warm place, but not near enough the fire to discolour the icing and cause it to crack.
