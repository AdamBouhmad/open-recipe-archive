## Ingredients

- oysters (12 if large; double the number of small ones)
- eggs (6)
- milk (1 cup)
- butter (1 tablespoonful)
- chopped parsley
- salt
- pepper

## Directions

1. Chop the oysters very fine.
2. Beat the yolks and whites of the eggs separately as for nice cake—the white until it stands in a heap.
3. Put three tablespoonfuls of butter in a frying-pan, and heat while you are mixing the omelet.
4. Stir the milk into a deep dish with the yolk, and season.
5. Next put in the chopped oysters, beating vigorously as you add them gradually.
6. When they are thoroughly incorporated, pour in the spoonful of melted butter.
7. Finally, whip in the whites lightly and with as few strokes as possible.
8. If the butter is hot, put the mixture into the pan.
9. Do not stir it, but when it begins to stiffen—to "set," in culinary phrase, slip a broad-bladed, round-pointed dinner-knife around the sides, and cautiously under the omelet, that the butter may reach every part.
10. As soon as the centre is fairly "set," turn out into a hot dish.
11. Lay the latter bottom upward over the frying-pan, which must be turned upside-down dexterously.
12. This brings the browned side of the omelet uppermost.
