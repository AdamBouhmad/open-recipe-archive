## Ingredients

- three quarters of a pound of shelled sweet almonds
- three ounces of shelled bitter almonds
- rose water
- a pint of rich milk
- cinnamon sticks
- mace blades
- two stale rusks
- three quarters of a pound of fresh butter
- three quarters of a pound of powdered loaf-sugar
- ten eggs

## Directions

1. Blanch the almonds, and beat them in a mortar to a fine paste, mixing them well, and adding by degrees a tea-cup full, or more, of rose water.
2. Boil in a pint of rich milk a few sticks of cinnamon broken up, and a few blades of mace.
3. When the milk has come to a boil, take it off the fire, strain it into a pan, and soak in it two stale rusks cut into slices. They must soak till quite dissolved.
4. Stir to a cream three quarters of a pound of fresh butter, mixed with the same quantity of powdered loaf-sugar.
5. Beat ten eggs very light, yolks and whites together, and then stir alternately into the butter and sugar, the rusk, eggs, and almonds.
6. Set it on a stove or a chafing dish, and stir the whole together till very smooth and thick.
7. Put it into a buttered dish and bake it three quarters of an hour.
8. It must be eaten quite cold.
