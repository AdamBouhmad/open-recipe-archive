## Ingredients

- twenty-five large fine oysters
- half a pint of grated bread-crumbs
- a little sweet marjoram
- a quarter of a pound of butter
- two table-spoonfuls of cream or rich milk
- beaten yolks of three eggs
- a turkey
- flour
- cold water
- the liver and gizzard of the turkey
- oyster-sauce

## Directions

1. Take twenty-five large fine oysters, and chop them.
2. Mix with them half a pint of grated bread-crumbs, a little sweet marjoram, a quarter of a pound of butter, two table-spoonfuls of cream or rich milk, and the beaten yolks of three eggs.
3. Stuff the craw of the turkey with it, and sew up the skin.
4. Dredge it with flour, put it into a large pot or kettle, and cover it well with cold water.
5. Place it over the fire, and let it boil slowly for half an hour, taking off the scum as it rises.
6. Remove the pot from over the fire, and set it on hot coals to stew slowly for two hours, or two hours and a half, according to its size.
7. Just before you send it to table, place it again over the fire to get well heated.
8. When you boil a turkey, skewer the liver and gizzard to the sides, under the wings.
9. Send it to table with oyster-sauce in a small tureen.
