## Ingredients

- ripe raspberries
- fine loaf sugar
- cold water
- brandy

## Directions

1. Set aside half of the raspberries, selecting the largest and firmest
2. Put the remainder into a preserving pan and mash them
3. Set them over the fire
4. When they come to a boil, take them out and let them cool
5. Squeeze them through a bag
6. Prepare sugar allowing a pound of sugar to every quart of whole raspberries
7. Wash the kettle clean and put the sugar into it
8. Allow half a pint of cold water to two pounds of sugar
9. When sugar has melted in the water, put it on the fire
10. Boil it till the scum ceases to rise and it is a thick syrup, skimming well
11. Put in the whole raspberries and boil them rapidly a few minutes but not long enough to burst
12. Take them out with a skimmer full of holes and spread on a large dish to cool
13. Mix with the syrup the juice of the boiled raspberries
14. Let it boil about ten or fifteen minutes
15. Put in the whole fruit and give it one more boil, seeing that it does not break
16. Put it warm into glass jars or tumblers
17. When quite cold cover closely with paper dipped in brandy, tying another paper tightly over it
