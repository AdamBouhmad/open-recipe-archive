## Ingredients

- a duck
- salt
- pepper
- three pints bread crumbs
- six ounces butter
- salt pork
- two chopped onions
- one teaspoonful each of sage, black pepper and salt
- water
- butter
- flour
- necks and gizzards
- a quart of cold water
- one tablespoonful of Madeira or sherry
- a blade of mace
- one small onion
- cayenne pepper

## Directions

1. Pick, draw, clean thoroughly, and wipe dry
2. Cut the neck close to the back
3. Beat the breast-bone flat with a rolling pin
4. Tie the wings and legs securely
5. Stuff with three pints bread crumbs, six ounces butter, or part butter and salt pork, two chopped onions and one teaspoonful each of sage, black pepper and salt
6. Do not stuff very full, and sew up the openings firmly to keep the flavor in and the fat out
7. If not fat enough, it should be larded with salt pork, or tie a slice upon the breast
8. Place in a baking pan, with a little water, and baste frequently with salt and water
9. Turn often, so that the sides and back may all be nicely browned
10. When nearly done, baste with butter and a little flour
11. Young ducks should roast from twenty-five to thirty minutes, and full-grown ones for an hour or more, with frequent basting
12. Make a gravy out of the necks and gizzards by putting them in a quart of cold water, that must be reduced to a pint by boiling
13. When done, the giblets may be chopped fine and added to the juice
14. Add one tablespoonful of Madeira or sherry, a blade of mace, one small onion, and a little cayenne pepper
15. Strain through a hair sieve
16. Pour a little over the ducks and serve the remainder in a boat
17. Serve with jellies or any tart sauce
