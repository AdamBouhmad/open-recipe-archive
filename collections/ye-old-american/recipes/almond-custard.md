## Ingredients

- half a pound of shelled sweet almonds
- three ounces of shelled bitter almonds
- rose water
- a quarter of a pound of loaf-sugar
- a quart of cream or rich milk
- ten eggs
- powdered nutmeg
- a tea-spoonful of powdered mace and cinnamon mixed
- cream or white of egg

## Directions

1. Scald and blanch half a pound of shelled sweet almonds, and three ounces of shelled bitter almonds; throwing them as you do them into a large bowl of cold water
2. Pound them one at a time in a mortar; pouring in frequently a little rose water to prevent their oiling, and becoming dark-coloured and heavy
3. Melt a quarter of a pound of loaf-sugar in a quart of cream or rich milk, and stir in by degrees the pounded almonds
4. Beat ten eggs very light, and stir them gradually into the mixture; adding a powdered nutmeg, and a tea-spoonful of powdered mace and cinnamon mixed
5. Put the whole into a pitcher, and place it in a kettle or pan of boiling water, the water coming up to the lower part of the neck of the pitcher
6. Set it over hot coals, and let it boil (stirring it all the time) till it is quite thick, but not till it curdles
7. Take the pitcher out of the water; pour the custard into a large bowl, and stir it till it cools
8. Put it into glass cups, and send it to table cold
9. Sweeten some cream or white of egg
10. Beat it to stiff froth and pile it on the top of the custards
