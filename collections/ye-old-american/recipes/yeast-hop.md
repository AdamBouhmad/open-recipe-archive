## Ingredients

- 4 large potatoes, or six small
- 2 quarts cold water
- Double handful hops, tied in a coarse muslin bag
- 4 tablespoonfuls flour
- 2 tablespoonfuls white sugar
- 4 tablespoonfuls lively yeast

## Directions

1. Peel the potatoes, and put them with the hop-bag into a saucepan containing two quarts cold water.
2. Cover and boil until the potatoes break and fall apart.
3. Take these out with a perforated skimmer, leaving the water still boiling, mash them fine with a potato-beetle, and work in the flour and sugar.
4. Moisten this gradually with the boiling hop tea, stirring it to a smooth paste.
5. When all the tea has been mixed in, set it aside to cool.
6. While still slightly warm, add four tablespoonfuls of lively yeast, and turn all into a large open vessel to "work."
7. Keep this in a warm place until it ceases to bubble up, or until next day.
8. In summer it will work well in a few hours.
9. When quite light, put in earthen jars with small mouths, in which fit corks, or bottle it, and remove to ice-house or cellar.
10. It will keep good for a fortnight—longer in winter.
11. When you wish to use it for baking, send a small vessel to the cellar for the desired quantity, and re-cork at once.
