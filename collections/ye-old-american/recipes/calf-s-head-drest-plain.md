## Ingredients

- calf's head
- warm water
- salt
- sage leaves
- parsley
- brains
- melted butter
- yolks of hard-boiled eggs
- pepper
- beaten egg
- grated bread-crumbs
- butter
- ham, broiled and sliced thin
- parsley-sauce

## Directions

1. Wash the head in warm water.
2. Lay it in clean hot water and let it soak awhile to blanch it.
3. Take out the brains and the black part of the eyes.
4. Tie the head in a cloth, and put it into a large fish-kettle, with plenty of cold water, and add some salt to throw up the scum, which must be taken off as it rises.
5. Let the head boil gently about three hours.
6. Put eight or ten sage leaves, and as much parsley, into a small sauce-pan with a little water, and boil them half an hour.
7. Chop them fine, and set them ready on a plate.
8. Wash the brains well in two warm waters, and then soak them for an hour in a basin of cold water with a little salt in it.
9. Remove the skin and strings, and then put the brains into a stew-pan with plenty of cold water, and let them boil gently for a quarter of an hour, skimming them well.
10. Take them out, chop them, and mix them with the sage and parsley leaves, two table-spoonfuls of melted butter, and the yolks of four hard-boiled eggs, and pepper and salt to your taste.
11. Put the mixture into a sauce-pan and set it on coals to warm.
12. Take up the head when it is sufficiently boiled, score it in diamonds, brush it all over with beaten egg, and strew it with a mixture of grated bread-crumbs, and chopped sage and parsley.
13. Stick a few bits of butter over it, and set it in a Dutch oven to brown.
14. Serve it up with the brains laid round it.
15. Garnish with very thin small slices of broiled ham, curled up.
16. Have parsley-sauce in a boat.
