## Ingredients

- a pint and a half of cream
- six yolks of eggs
- two table-spoonfuls of powdered arrow-root or fine flour
- six ounces of chocolate
- two ounces of powdered loaf-sugar
- a quarter of a pound of maccaroons
- a dozen bitter almonds
- four ounces of shelled sweet almonds or pistachio nuts
- rose-water
- an ounce of citron
- four ounces of powdered sugar
- two large spoonfuls of spinach juice
- a large sponge-cake
- six eggs
- twelve drops of oil of lemon
- powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Boil over a slow fire a pint and a half of cream.
2. Have ready six yolks of eggs, beaten up with two table-spoonfuls of powdered arrow-root, or fine flour.
3. Stir this gradually into the boiling cream, taking care to have it perfectly smooth and free from lumps.
4. Allow ten minutes for the egg and cream to boil together.
5. Divide the mixture by putting it into two separate sauce-pans.
6. In one pan, mix six ounces of chocolate scraped fine, two ounces of powdered loaf-sugar, and a quarter of a pound of maccaroons, broken up.
7. When it has come to a hard boil, take it off, stir it well, pour it into a bowl, and set it away to cool.
8. For the other sauce-pan of cream and egg, blanch a dozen bitter almonds, and four ounces of shelled sweet almonds or pistachio nuts, and pound them in a mortar with rose-water to a smooth paste, and mix with an ounce of citron also pounded.
9. Add four ounces of powdered sugar.
10. To colour it green, add two large spoonfuls of spinach juice that has been strained through a sieve.
11. Stir this mixture into the other half of the cream, and let it come to a boil.
12. Put it aside to cool.
13. Cut a large sponge-cake into slices half an inch thick.
14. Spread one slice thickly with the chocolate cream, and cover another slice with the almond cream.
15. Do this alternately, piling them evenly on a china dish, till all the ingredients are used up.
16. Arrange it in the original form of the sponge-cake before it was cut, or in a pyramid.
17. Whip the whites of the six eggs to a stiff froth, and gradually mix in six ounces of powdered sugar, and twelve drops of oil of lemon.
18. With a spoon heap this meringue all over the pile of cake.
19. Sift powdered sugar over it.
20. Set it in a very slow oven till the outside becomes a light brown colour.
21. Serve it up cold, ornamented according to your taste.
