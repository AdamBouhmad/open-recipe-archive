## Ingredients

- six or eight peach leaves
- a quart of milk
- a large stick of cinnamon
- eight eggs
- a quarter of a pound of sugar
- nutmeg

## Directions

1. Tie together six or eight peach leaves, and boil them in a quart of milk with a large stick of cinnamon broken up
2. If you cannot procure peach leaves, substitute a handful of peach-kernels or bitter almonds, or a vanilla bean split in pieces
3. When it has boiled hard, strain the milk and set it away to cool
4. Beat very light eight eggs, and stir them by degrees into the milk when it is quite cold, (if warm, the eggs will curdle it, and cause whey at the bottom,) and add gradually a quarter of a pound of sugar
5. Fill your cups with it; set them in a Dutch oven, and pour round them boiling water sufficient to reach nearly to the tops of the cups
6. Put hot coals under the oven and on the lid, (which must be previously heated by standing it up before a hot fire,) and bake the custards about fifteen minutes
7. Send them to table cold, with nutmeg grated over each
8. Or you may bake the whole in one large dish
