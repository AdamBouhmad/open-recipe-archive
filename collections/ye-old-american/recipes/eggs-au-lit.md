## Ingredients

- cold fowl (chicken, turkey, or duck)
- cold boiled veal and ham
- Wedgewood mortar
- melted butter
- pepper
- salt
- minced parsley
- two beaten eggs
- hot water
- poached eggs
- buttered toast

## Directions

1. Mince some cold fowl—chicken, turkey, or duck (or some cold boiled veal and ham in equal quantities)—very fine, and rub in a Wedgewood mortar, adding by degrees some melted butter, pepper, salt, minced parsley, and two beaten eggs.
2. Warm in a frying-pan when it is well mixed, stirring in a little hot water should it dry too fast.
3. Cook five minutes, stirring to keep it from scorching or browning.
4. Form, on a hot platter or flat dish, into a mound, flat on top, with a ridge of the mixture running all around.
5. It is easily moulded with a broad-bladed knife.
6. In the dish thus formed, on the top of the mince-meat, lay as many poached eggs as it will hold, sprinkling them with pepper and salt.
7. Arrange triangles of buttered toast in such order, at the base of the mound, that they shall make a pointed wall against it.
