## Ingredients

- lemons
- six pounds of the best loaf-sugar
- whites of two eggs
- a quart of clear soft water

## Directions

1. Squeeze lemons with a wooden lemon-squeezer to yield about a quart of juice
2. Break up the sugar into pieces and rub off the yellow rind or zest of the lemons onto the sugar pieces
3. Put all the sugar into a large porcelain preserving-kettle
4. Beat to a stiff froth the whites of two eggs, mix it gradually with a quart of clear soft water, and add it to the sugar
5. Stir the sugar while it is melting in the water
6. When all is dissolved, place the kettle over the fire, and boil and skim it till perfectly clear, and the scum ceases to rise, and the particles of lemon zest are no longer visible
7. Squeeze the lemons through a strainer into a large pitcher, till you have a quart of juice
8. When the sugar has boiled sufficiently, and is quite clear, stir in gradually the lemon-juice
9. Cover the kettle and let it boil ten minutes longer
10. When cool put it into clean, clear glass bottles that have been rinsed with brandy
11. Cork them tightly and seal the corks
