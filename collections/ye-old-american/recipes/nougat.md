## Ingredients

- a pound of shelled sweet almonds
- two pounds of the best loaf-sugar
- the juice of two or three lemons
- sweet oil

## Directions

1. Blanch a pound of shelled sweet almonds; and with an almond-cutter, or a sharp penknife, split each almond into two slips.
2. Spread them over a large dish, and place them in a gentle oven.
3. Powder two pounds of the best loaf-sugar, and put it into a preserving pan without a drop of water.
4. Set it on a chafing-dish over a slow fire, or on a hot stove, and stir it with a wooden spoon till the heat has entirely dissolved it.
5. Take the almonds out of the oven, and mix with them the juice of two or three lemons.
6. Put them into the sugar a few at a time, and let them simmer till it becomes a thick stiff paste, stirring it hard all the while.
7. Have ready a mould, or a square tin pan, greased all over the inside with sweet oil; put the mixture into it.
8. Smooth it evenly, and set it in a cold place to harden.
9. When almost hard cut it into long slips.
