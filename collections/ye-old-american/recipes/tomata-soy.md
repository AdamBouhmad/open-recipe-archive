## Ingredients

- tomatas
- salt
- cloves
- mace
- black pepper
- cayenne

## Directions

1. Have the best and ripest tomatas, gathered on a dry day
2. Do not peel them, but merely cut them into slices
3. Strew some salt over the bottom of a tub
4. Put in the tomatas in layers
5. Sprinkle between each layer, which should be about two inches in thickness, a handful of salt
6. Repeat this till you have put in eight quarts or one peck of tomatas
7. Cover the tub and let it set for three days
8. Early in the morning, put the tomatas into a large porcelain kettle, and boil it slowly and steadily till ten at night
9. Frequently mash and stir the tomatas
10. Put it out to cool
11. Next morning strain and press it through a sieve
12. When no more liquid will pass through, put it into a clean kettle with two ounces of cloves, one ounce of mace, two ounces of black pepper, and two table-spoonfuls of cayenne, all powdered
13. Let it boil slowly and steadily all day
14. Put it to cool
