## Ingredients

- fine ham, preferably Westphalia
- lukewarm water
- Madeira or sherry
- hot water
- fine bread-raspings
- lemon juice
- parsley
- carrots
- beets
- turnips

## Directions

1. Take a very fine ham and soak it in lukewarm water for a day or two, changing the water frequently.
2. The day before cooking, take the ham out of the water, and having removed the skin, trim it nicely.
3. Pour over it a bottle of Madeira or sherry and let it steep till next morning, frequently washing the wine over it.
4. Put it on the spit in time to allow at least six hours for slowly roasting it.
5. Baste it continually with hot water.
6. When done, dredge it all over with fine bread-raspings shaken through the top of the dredging box, and set it before the fire to brown.
7. For gravy, take the wine in which the ham was steeped, and add to it the essence or juice which flowed from the meat.
8. Squeeze in the juice of two lemons, put it into a sauce-pan, and boil and skim it.
9. Send it to table in a boat.
10. Cover the shank of the ham with bunches of double parsley, and ornament it with a cluster of flowers cut out with a penknife from raw carrots, beets, and turnips.
