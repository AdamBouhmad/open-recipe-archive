## Ingredients

- two pailfuls of boiling water
- twenty pounds of potash
- thirty pounds of clean grease

## Directions

1. Pour two pailfuls of boiling water upon twenty pounds of potash and let it stand two hours
2. Have ready thirty pounds of clean grease, upon which pour one pailful of the lye, adding another pail of water to the potash; let it stand three or four hours, stir it well
3. then pour a gallon of the lye upon the grease, stir it well; and in half an hour another gallon of the lye, stir it thoroughly; in half an hour repeat the process, and thus proceed until you have poured off all the lye
4. then add two pails of boiling hot water to the remainder of the potash, and let it stand ten hours
5. then stir the mixture, and if it has become stiff and the grease has disappeared from the surface, take out a little and see whether the weak lye will thicken it; if it does, add the lye; if it does not, try water, and if that thickens it, let it stand another day, stirring it well five or six times during the day
