## Ingredients

- one ounce of isinglass
- a pint of boiling water
- a quart of milk
- two ounces sweet almonds blanched and pounded
- sugar
- raspberry cream
- thin slips of blanched almonds
- syllabub
- egg yelk
- spinach juice
- Cochineal and alkanet root
- brandy

## Directions

1. Break one ounce of isinglass into very small pieces
2. Wash it well, and pour on a pint of boiling water
3. Next morning, add a quart of milk, boil it till the isinglass is dissolved
4. Strain it, put in two ounces sweet almonds, blanched and pounded
5. Sweeten it, and put it in the mould
6. When stiff, turn them into a deep dish, and put raspberry cream around them
7. For a change, stick thin slips of blanched almonds all over the blanc mange, and dress round with syllabub, nicely frothed
8. For moulds requiring colouring: for an ear of corn, mix the yelk of an egg with a little of the blanc mange, fill the grains of the corn with it, and when quite set, pour in the white, but take care it is not warm enough to melt the yellow
9. For a bunch of asparagus, colour a little with spinach juice, to fill the green tops of the heads
10. Fruit must be made the natural colour of what it represents
11. Cochineal and alkanet root pounded and dissolved in brandy, make good colouring
12. Blanc mange should never be served, without raspberry cream or syllabub to eat with it
