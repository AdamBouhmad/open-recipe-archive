## Ingredients

- pork
- powdered saltpetre
- cayenne pepper
- brown sugar
- fine salt
- soap-suds

## Directions

1. Hogs should be well fattened on corn for about eight weeks, allowing ten bushels to each hog.
2. They are best for curing when from two to four years old, and should not weigh more than one hundred and fifty or one hundred and sixty pounds.
3. The first four weeks feed them on mush, or on Indian meal moistened with water.
4. The remaining four weeks feed them on corn unground, giving them always as much as they will eat.
5. Soap-suds may be given to them three or four times a week, or oftener if convenient.
6. When killed and cut up, begin immediately to salt them.
7. Rub the outside of each ham with a tea-spoonful of powdered saltpetre, and the inside with a tea-spoonful of cayenne pepper.
8. Mix together two pounds brown sugar and fine salt, in the proportion of a pound and a half of brown sugar to a pint of salt.
9. Rub the pork well with this mixture; this quantity will be sufficient for fifty pounds of meat.
10. Have ready some large tubs, the bottoms sprinkled with salt, and lay the meat in the tubs with the skin downward.
11. Put plenty of salt between each layer of meat.
12. After it has lain eight days, take it out and wipe.
