## Ingredients

- quinces
- white sugar (equal quantity to fruit by weight)
- water
- fresh lemons (two)

## Directions

1. Pare, core and quarter your fruit, then weigh it and allow an equal quantity of white sugar
2. Take the parings and cores and put in a preserving kettle
3. Cover them with water and boil for half an hour
4. Strain through a hair-sieve, and put the juice back into the kettle
5. Boil the quinces in it a little at a time until they are tender
6. Lift out as they are done with a drainer and lay on a dish
7. If the liquid seems scarce add more water
8. When all are cooked, throw into this liquor the sugar, and allow it to boil ten minutes before putting in the quinces
9. Let them boil until they change color, say one hour and a quarter, on a slow fire
10. While they are boiling occasionally slip a silver spoon under them to see that they do not burn, but on no account stir them
11. Have two fresh lemons cut in thin slices, and when the fruit is being put in jars lay a slice or two in each
