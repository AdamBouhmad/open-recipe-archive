## Ingredients

- three pounds of sifted flour
- two pounds of powdered white sugar
- four nutmegs
- a quarter of an ounce of mace powdered fine
- two pounds of stoned raisins
- two pounds of currants, picked, washed, and dried
- half a pound of citron cut large
- half a pint of white wine
- two pounds of the best fresh butter
- six eggs
- a pint of warm new milk
- half a pint of fresh strong yeast

## Directions

1. Prepare the day before: sift flour, powdered white sugar, nutmegs, and mace powdered fine together
2. Prepare two pounds of stoned raisins, two pounds of currants, picked, washed, and dried, and half a pound of citron cut large
3. Spread the raisins, currants, and citron on a large dish, and dredge thickly over with flour, mixing well among them with your hands so as to coat them all completely
4. Put the spice into half a pint of white wine, cover it, and let it infuse all night
5. Next morning, cut two pounds of the best fresh butter into small pieces
6. Beat six eggs well
7. Rub half the butter into the flour, adding half the sugar
8. Wet it with the milk, and add half of the eggs, and the wine, and the yeast
9. Stir and mix it thoroughly
10. Cover it and set it to rise until perfectly light by evening
11. Add the remainder of the butter and the sugar, and the rest of the egg
12. Mix it well, and set it again to rise till early next morning
13. Add gradually the fruit, setting it again to rise for two or three hours
14. When it is perfectly light for the last time, butter a large deep pan, and put in the mixture
15. Make the oven very hot, then allow it to cool down so as to bake rather slowly
16. Bake from three to four hours
