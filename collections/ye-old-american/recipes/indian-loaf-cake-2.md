## Ingredients

- a teacupful of powdered white sugar
- a quart of rich milk
- two ounces of butter
- a saltspoonful of salt
- yellow Indian meal
- three eggs
- a teacupful of good strong yeast

## Directions

1. Mix a teacupful of powdered white sugar with a quart of rich milk
2. cut up in the milk two ounces of butter, adding a saltspoonful of salt
3. Put this mixture into a covered pan or skillet, and set it on the fire till it is scalding hot
4. take it off, and scald with it as much yellow Indian meal (previously sifted) as will make it of the consistency of thick boiled mush
5. Beat the whole very hard for a quarter of an hour
6. set it away to cool
7. While it is cooling, beat three eggs very light, and stir them gradually into the mixture when it is about as warm as new milk
8. Add a teacupful of good strong yeast and beat the whole another quarter of an hour
9. have ready a tin mold or earthen pan with a pipe in the centre
10. The pan must be very well-buttered as Indian meal is apt to stick
11. Put in the mixture, cover it and set it in a warm place to rise
12. It should be light in about four hours
13. bake it two hours in a moderate oven
14. turn it out with the broad surface downwards and send it to table hot and whole
15. Cut it into slices and eat it with butter
