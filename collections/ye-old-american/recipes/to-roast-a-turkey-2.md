## Ingredients

- grated bread-crumbs
- minced suet
- sweet marjoram
- grated lemon-peel
- nutmeg
- pepper
- salt
- beaten yolk of egg
- grated cold ham
- writing paper
- a turkey
- flour
- cold lard
- butter
- neck, liver, and gizzard of the turkey
- browned flour
- ham or tongue
- mushroom-sauce
- stewed cranberries

## Directions

1. Make a force-meat of grated bread-crumbs, minced suet, sweet marjoram, grated lemon-peel, nutmeg, pepper, salt, and beaten yolk of egg.
2. You may add some grated cold ham.
3. Light some writing paper, and singe the hairs from the skin of the turkey.
4. Reserve the neck, liver, and gizzard for the gravy.
5. Stuff the craw of the turkey with the force-meat, of which there should be enough made to form into balls for frying, laying them round the turkey when it is dished.
6. Dredge it with flour, and roast it before a clear brisk fire, basting it with cold lard.
7. Towards the last, set the turkey nearer to the fire, dredge it again very lightly with flour, and baste it with butter.
8. It will require, according to its size, from two to three hours roasting.
9. Make the gravy of the giblets cut in pieces, seasoned, and stewed for two hours in a very little water; thicken it with a spoonful of browned flour, and stir into it the gravy from the dripping-pan, having first skimmed off the fat.
10. A turkey should be accompanied by ham or tongue.
11. Serve up with it mushroom-sauce.
12. Have stewed cranberries on the table to eat with it.
