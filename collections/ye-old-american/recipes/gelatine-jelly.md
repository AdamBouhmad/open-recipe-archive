## Ingredients

- two ounces of gelatine
- cold water
- two quarts of boiling water
- four lemons
- two large sticks of the best cinnamon
- a pound of the best double refined loaf-sugar
- the beaten white of an egg
- a pint of Malaga or any other good white wine

## Directions

1. Soak two ounces of gelatine for twenty-five minutes in as much cold water as will cover it
2. Take it out, lay it in another vessel, pour on it two quarts of boiling water, and let it thoroughly dissolve
3. Set it to cool
4. Roll lemons under your hand on a table and pare off very thin the yellow rind and cut it into small bits
5. Break up into little pieces two large sticks of the best cinnamon and a pound of the best double refined loaf-sugar
6. Mix together in a large bowl the sugar, the lemon-rind, and the cinnamon, adding the juice of the lemons, the beaten white of an egg, and a pint of Malaga or any other good white wine
7. Add to these ingredients the dissolved gelatine when it is cool but not yet cold
8. Mix the whole very well, put it into a porcelain kettle or a very clean bell-metal one, and boil it fifteen minutes
9. Pour it warm into a white flannel jelly-bag and let it drip into a large glass bowl
10. Do not squeeze or press the bag
11. After it has congealed in the bowl, set it on ice
