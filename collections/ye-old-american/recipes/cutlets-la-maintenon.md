## Ingredients

- neck of mutton
- oiled butter
- hard-boiled egg yolk
- sweet-herbs
- grated bread
- pepper
- salt
- nutmeg
- onion
- white paper
- mushroom sauce
- brown gravy
- red wine
- flour
- bones and trimmings

## Directions

1. Cut a neck of mutton into steaks with a bone in each; trim them nicely, and scrape clean the end of the bone.
2. Flatten them with a rolling pin or a meat beetle, and lay them in oiled butter.
3. Make a seasoning of hard-boiled yolk of egg and sweet-herbs minced small, grated bread, pepper, salt, and nutmeg; and, if you choose, a little minced onion.
4. Take the chops out of the butter, and cover them with the seasoning.
5. Butter some half sheets of white paper, and put the cutlets into them, so as to be entirely covered, securing the paper with pins or strings; and twisting them nicely round the bone.
6. Heat your gridiron over some bright lively coals.
7. Lay the cutlets on it, and broil them about twenty minutes.
8. Serve them up hot, with mushroom sauce in a boat, or with a brown gravy, flavoured with red wine.
9. To make the gravy, stew the bones and trimmings in a little water, skim well, and strain when sufficiently stewed; thicken it with flour browned in a Dutch oven, and add a glass of red wine.
10. Alternatively, bake these cutlets in a Dutch oven without the papers, moistening them frequently with a little oiled butter.
