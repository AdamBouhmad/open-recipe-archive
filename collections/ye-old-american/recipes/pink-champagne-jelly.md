## Ingredients

- the white of an egg
- three wine-glasses of filtered water
- twelve ounces of the best double-refined loaf-sugar
- twelve grains of cochineal powder
- an ounce and a half of isinglass
- a pint of pink champagne
- the juice of a large lemon

## Directions

1. Beat up the white of an egg to a stiff froth.
2. Stir it hard into three wine-glasses of filtered water.
3. Put twelve ounces of the best double-refined loaf-sugar (powdered fine and sifted) into a skillet lined with porcelain.
4. Pour on it the white of egg and water and stir it till dissolved.
5. Add twelve grains of cochineal powder.
6. Set it over a moderate fire and boil it and skim it till the scum ceases to rise.
7. Strain it through a very fine sieve.
8. Have ready an ounce and a half of isinglass that has been boiled in a little water till quite dissolved.
9. Strain it and while the boiled sugar is lukewarm mix it with the isinglass.
10. Add a pint of pink champagne and the juice of a large lemon.
11. Run it through a linen bag into a mould.
12. When it has congealed so as to be quite firm, wrap a wet cloth round the outside of the mould and turn out the jelly into a glass dish or serve it broken up in jelly glasses or glass cups.
