## Ingredients

- potatoes
- salt
- pepper
- butter (piece the size of an egg)
- rich sweet milk (a cup)

## Directions

1. Boil potatoes whole with the skins on in plenty of water, well salted; they are much better for being boiled the day before needed.
2. Strip off the skins (not pare them with a knife) and slice them nearly a quarter of an inch thick.
3. Place them in a chopping-bowl and sprinkle over them sufficient salt and pepper to season them well.
4. Chop them all one way, then turn the chopping-bowl half way around and chop across them, cutting them into little square pieces the shape of dice.
5. About twenty-five minutes before serving time, place on the stove a saucepan containing a piece of butter the size of an egg.
6. When it begins to melt and run over the bottom of the dish, put in a cup of rich sweet milk.
7. When this boils up put in the chopped potatoes (about a quart of them).
8. Stir them a little so that they become moistened through with the milk.
9. Cover and place them on the back of the stove, or in a moderate oven, where they will heat through gradually.
10. When heated through, stir carefully from the bottom with a spoon and cover tightly again.
11. Keep hot until ready to serve.
