## Ingredients

- four cupfuls of white sugar
- one cupful of water

## Directions

1. Put four cupfuls of white sugar and one cupful of water into a bright tin pan on the range and let it boil without stirring for ten minutes
2. If it looks somewhat thick, test it by letting some drop from the spoon, and if it threads, remove the pan to the table
3. Take out a small spoonful, and rub it against the side of a cake bowl; if it becomes creamy, and will roll into a ball between the fingers, pour the whole into the bowl
4. When cool enough to bear your finger in it, take it in your lap, stir or beat it with a large spoon, or pudding-stick
5. It will soon begin to look like cream, and then grow stiffer until you find it necessary to take your hands and work it like bread dough
6. If it is not boiled enough to cream, set it back upon the range and let it remain one or two minutes, or as long as is necessary, taking care not to cook it too much
7. Add the flavoring as soon as it begins to cool
8. It can be made into rolls, and sliced off, or packed in plates and cut into small cubes, or made into any shape imitating French candies
