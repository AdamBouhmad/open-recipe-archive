## Ingredients

- cabbage
- salt
- cabbage-leaves
- black peppers
- cloves
- garlic
- mace
- pork

## Directions

1. Shred or chop the cabbage fine
2. Line a barrel, keg, or jar with cabbage-leaves on the bottom and sides
3. Put in a layer of the cut cabbage, three inches in depth
4. Press down well and sprinkle with four tablespoonfuls of salt
5. When you have packed five layers in this way, press hard with a board cut to fit loosely on the inside of the barrel or jar
6. Put heavy weights on this, or pound with a wooden beetle until the cabbage is a compact mass
7. Remove the board and put in more layers of salt and shred cabbage, repeating the pounding every four or five layers, until the vessel is full
8. Cover with leaves and put the board on the top of these with a heavy weight to keep it down
9. Set all away to ferment
10. In three weeks remove the scum, and if need be, cover with water
11. Keep in a cool, dry cellar
12. It can be eaten raw or boiled, and seasoned with pork
