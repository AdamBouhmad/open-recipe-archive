## Ingredients

- one quart of white, preserving, fine Batavia brandy
- for every pound of fruit, three-quarters of a pound of granulated sugar
- strawberries, raspberries, blackberries, apricots, cherries (sweet and sour), peaches, plums, currants, grapes, and at least one pound of black cherries

## Directions

1. Put one quart of white, preserving, fine Batavia brandy into a two-gallon stone jar that has a tightly fitting top
2. For every pound of fruit in prime condition and perfectly dry, use three-quarters of a pound of granulated sugar
3. Plums and grapes should be peeled and seeded; apricots and peaches peeled and cut in quarters or eighths or dice; cherries also must be seeded; quinces may be steamed until tender
4. Stir every day so that the sugar will be dissolved, using a clean, wooden spoon kept for the purpose
5. You may use as much of one sort of fruit as you like, and it may be put in from day to day, just as you happen to have it
6. Keep the jar in a cool, dry place, and never forget the daily stirring, for that is the secret of success
7. The preserve will be ready for use within a week after the last fruit is put in
