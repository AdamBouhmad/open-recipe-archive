## Ingredients

- one large cold fowl
- half a dozen or more hard-boiled eggs
- salt
- cayenne pepper
- made mustard
- salad oil
- cider vinegar
- one large head of lettuce
- boiled red beets
- green pickled cucumbers

## Directions

1. Remove the skin and fat from the fowl, and cut the flesh from the bones in very small shreds, not more than an inch long
2. Cut up the yolks of the hard-boiled eggs upon a plate, and with the back of a wooden spoon mash them to a paste, adding a small salt-spoonful of salt, rather more of cayenne pepper, and a large tea-spoonful of made mustard
3. Mix them well together; then add two large table-spoonfuls of salad oil, and one of the best cider vinegar
4. Mix all these ingredients for the dressing to a fine, smooth, stiff, yellow paste
5. Lay the shred chicken in a nice even heap upon the middle of a flat dish, smoothing it and making it circular or oval with the back of a spoon, and flattening the top
6. Cover it thickly and smoothly with the dressing or paste of seasoned yolk of egg
7. Pick and wash a large head of lettuce in cold water; cut up the best parts of it very small and mix the lettuce with a portion of the hard-boiled white of egg minced fine
8. Lay the chopped lettuce all round the heap of shred chicken
9. Ornament the surface with very small bits of boiled red beets and green pickled cucumbers, cut into slips and dots, and arranged in a pretty pattern upon the yellow ground of the coating that covers the chicken
10. After taking on your plate a portion of each part of the salad, mix all together before eating it
