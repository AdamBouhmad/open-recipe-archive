## Ingredients

- beef steaks from the ribs or sirloin
- salt
- pepper
- butter or beef-dripping
- onions
- mushroom catchup

## Directions

1. Cut beef-steaks thinner than for broiling from the ribs or sirloin, and remove the bone.
2. Beat them to make them tender.
3. Season them with salt and pepper.
4. Put some fresh butter, or nice beef-dripping into a frying-pan, and hold it over a clear bright fire till it boils and has done hissing.
5. Put in the steaks, and some sliced onions.
6. Fry them about a quarter of an hour, turning them frequently.
7. After they are browned, cover them with a large plate to keep in the juices.
8. Take out the steaks and onions and lay them in a hot dish with another dish on the top, to keep them hot.
9. Give the gravy in the pan another boil up over the fire.
10. Add to it a spoonful of mushroom catchup.
11. Pour the gravy over the steaks, and send them to table as hot as possible.
