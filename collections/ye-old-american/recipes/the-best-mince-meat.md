## Ingredients

- large fresh tongue
- salt
- brown sugar
- powdered cloves
- three pounds beef suet
- six pounds sultana raisins
- six pounds pippin apples
- three pounds currants
- two large table-spoonfuls powdered cinnamon
- juice of four large lemons
- grated rinds of four large lemons
- one pound sweet almonds
- one ounce bitter almonds
- half a pint rose water
- four powdered nutmegs
- two dozen beaten cloves
- dozen blades mace powdered
- one pound powdered white sugar
- one pound citron
- one quart Madeira
- one pint brandy

## Directions

1. Take a large fresh tongue, rub it with a mixture, in equal proportions, of salt, brown sugar, and powdered cloves.
2. Cover it, and let it lie two days, or at least twenty-four hours.
3. Then boil it two hours, and when it is cold, skin it, and mince it very fine.
4. Chop also three pounds of beef suet, six pounds of sultana raisins, and six pounds of the best pippin apples that have been previously pared and cored.
5. Add three pounds of currants, picked, washed and dried; two large table-spoonfuls of powdered cinnamon; the juice and grated rinds of four large lemons; one pound of sweet almonds, one ounce of bitter almonds, blanched and pounded in a mortar with half a pint of rose water; also four powdered nutmegs; two dozen beaten cloves; and a dozen blades of mace powdered.
6. Add a pound of powdered white sugar, and a pound of citron cut into slips.
7. Mix all together, and moisten it with a quart of Madeira, and a pint of brandy.
8. Put it up closely in a stone jar with brandy paper; and when you take any out, add some more sugar and brandy; and chop some fresh apples.
9. Bake this mince-meat in puff paste.
