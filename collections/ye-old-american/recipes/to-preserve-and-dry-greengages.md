## Ingredients

- sugar (one pound to each pound of fruit)
- water (one quarter pint to each pound of sugar)

## Directions

1. To every pound of sugar allow one pound of fruit, one quarter pint of water
2. Use the fruit before it is quite ripe and part of the stalk must be left on
3. Weigh the fruit, rejecting all that is in the least degree blemished
4. Put it into a lined saucepan with the sugar and water, which should have been previously boiled together to a rich syrup
5. Boil the fruit in this for ten minutes
6. Remove it from the fire, and drain the greengages
7. The next day boil up the syrup and put in the fruit again
8. Let it simmer for three minutes, and drain the syrup away
9. Continue this process for five or six days
10. The last time place the greengages, when drained, on a hair-sieve, and put them in an oven or warm spot to dry
11. Keep them in a box, with paper between each layer, in a place free from damp
