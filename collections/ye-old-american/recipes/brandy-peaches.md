## Ingredients

- large white or yellow free-stone peaches
- flannel
- boiling water
- white French brandy
- loaf-sugar
- water
- beaten white of egg

## Directions

1. Rub off the down with a flannel
2. Score them down the seam with a large needle and prick every peach to the stone in several places
3. Scald them with boiling water and let them remain in the water till it becomes cold, keeping them well covered
4. Repeat the scalding three times to make them white
5. Wipe them and spread them on a soft table-cloth, covering them over with several folds
6. Let them remain in the cloth to dry
7. Put them into a tureen or a large jar and pour on as much white French brandy as will cover them well
8. Carefully keep the air from them and let them remain in the brandy for a week
9. Make a syrup in the usual manner, allowing to each pound of peaches a pound of loaf-sugar and half a pint of water mixed with a very little beaten white of egg
10. When the syrup has boiled and been well skimmed, put in the peaches and boil them slowly till they look clear
11. Do not keep them boiling more than half an hour
12. Take them out, drain them, and put them into large glass jars
13. Mix the syrup, when it is cold, with the brandy in which you had the peaches, and pour it over them
