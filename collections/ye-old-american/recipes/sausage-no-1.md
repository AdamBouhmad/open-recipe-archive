## Ingredients

- 6 lbs. lean fresh pork
- 3 lbs. fat fresh pork
- 12 teaspoonfuls powdered sage
- 6 teaspoonfuls black pepper
- 6 teaspoonfuls salt
- 2 teaspoonfuls powdered mace
- 2 teaspoonfuls powdered cloves
- 1 grated nutmeg
- melted lard
- stout muslin
- hog intestines
- soda and water

## Directions

1. Grind the meat, fat and lean, in a sausage-mill, or chop it very fine.
2. Mix the seasoning in with your hands and taste to be sure all is right.
3. Pack down in stone jars, pouring melted lard on top.
4. Alternatively, make long, narrow bags of stout muslin and fill with the meat, dip in melted lard, and hang from the beams of the cellar.
5. If packing in hog intestines, empty them, cut them in lengths, and lay for two days in salt and water.
6. Turn them inside out and lay in soak one day longer.
7. Scrape them, rinse well in soda and water, wipe, and blow into one end, having tied up the other with a bit of twine.
8. Stuff with the meat, tie up and hang in the store-room or cellar.
9. Fry the sausages in the cases in a clean, dry frying-pan until brown, or make into small, round flat cakes and fry in the same way.
10. Cook for ten to twelve minutes, turning when one side is done.
11. Serve dry and hot.
