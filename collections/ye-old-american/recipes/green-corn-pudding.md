## Ingredients

- twelve ears of green corn
- a quart of rich milk
- a quarter of a pound of fresh butter
- a quarter of a pound of sugar
- four eggs
- butter and white sugar in equal proportions
- grated nutmeg

## Directions

1. Take twelve ears of green corn, as it is called, (that is, Indian corn when full grown, but before it begins to harden and turn yellow,) and grate it
2. Have ready a quart of rich milk, and stir into it by degrees a quarter of a pound of fresh butter, and a quarter of a pound of sugar
3. Beat four eggs till quite light; and then stir them into the milk, &c. alternately with grated corn, a little of each at a time
4. Put the mixture into a large buttered dish and bake it four hours
5. It should be eaten quite warm
6. For sauce, beat together butter and white sugar in equal proportions, mixed with grated nutmeg
7. Alternatively, you may, if more convenient, boil the corn and cut it from the cob; but let it get quite cold before you stir it into the milk
8. If the corn has been previously boiled, the pudding will require but two hours to bake
