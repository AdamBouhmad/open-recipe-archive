## Ingredients

- asparagus
- water
- salt
- bread
- butter
- white sauce

## Directions

1. Scrape the stems of the asparagus lightly, but very clean; throw them into cold water and when they are all scraped and very clean, tie them in bunches of equal size.
2. Cut the large ends evenly, that the stems may be all of the same length, and put the asparagus into plenty of boiling water, well salted.
3. While it is boiling, cut several slices of bread half an inch thick, pare off the crust and toast it a delicate brown on both sides.
4. When the stalks of the asparagus are tender (it will usually cook in twenty to forty minutes) lift it out directly, or it will lose both its color and flavor and will also be liable to break.
5. Dip the toast quickly into the liquor in which it was boiled and dish the vegetable upon it, the heads all lying one way.
6. Pour over white sauce, or melted butter.
