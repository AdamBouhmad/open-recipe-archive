## Ingredients

- three jills of whole rice
- a pint of milk
- a quarter of a pound of butter
- another pint of milk
- a pound and a half of raisins or currants
- flour
- a powdered nutmeg
- a table-spoonful of mixed mace and cinnamon powdered
- a wine glass of rose water
- a wine glass of brandy or white wine
- six eggs
- a quarter of a pound of sugar

## Directions

1. Take three jills of whole rice; wash it, and boil it in a pint of milk.
2. When it is soft, mix in a quarter of a pound of butter, and set it aside to cool.
3. When it is quite cold, stir it into another pint of milk.
4. Prepare a pound and a half of raisins or currants; if currants, wash and dry them; if raisins, seed them and cut them in half.
5. Dredge them well with flour, to prevent their sinking.
6. Prepare also a powdered nutmeg; a table-spoonful of mixed mace and cinnamon powdered; a wine glass of rose water; and a wine glass of brandy or white wine.
7. Beat six eggs very light, and stir them into the mixture, alternately with a quarter of a pound of sugar.
8. Then add by degrees the spice and the liquor, and lastly, stir in, a few at a time, the raisins or currants.
9. Put the pudding into a buttered dish and bake it an hour and a half.
10. Send it to table cool.
