## Ingredients

- quinces
- water
- sugar

## Directions

1. Choose fine yellow quinces
2. Pare, quarter, and core them, saving both skins and cores
3. Put the quinces over the fire with just enough water to cover them, and simmer until they are soft, but not until they begin to break
4. Take them out carefully, and spread them upon broad dishes to cool
5. Add the parings, seeds, and cores, to the water in which the quinces were boiled, and stew, closely covered, for an hour
6. Strain through a jelly-bag, and to every pint of this liquor allow a pound of sugar
7. Boil up and skim it, put in the fruit and boil fifteen minutes
8. Take all from the fire and pour into a large deep pan
9. Cover closely and let it stand twenty-four hours
10. Drain off the syrup and let it come to a boil; put in the quinces carefully and boil another quarter of an hour
11. Take them up as dry as possible, and again spread out upon dishes, setting these in the hottest sunshine you can find
12. Boil the syrup until it begins to jelly; fill the jars two-thirds full and cover with the syrup
13. Cover with brandied tissue-paper
