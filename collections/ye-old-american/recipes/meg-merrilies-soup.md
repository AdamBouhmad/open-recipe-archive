## Ingredients

- four pounds of venison, or lean fresh beef or mutton
- pepper and salt
- four quarts of water
- a hare or a rabbit
- a pair of partridges
- a pair of grouse
- a dozen small onions
- a couple of heads of celery
- half a dozen sliced potatoes

## Directions

1. Take four pounds of venison, or if you cannot procure venison you may substitute the lean of fresh beef or mutton.
2. Season it with pepper and salt, put it into a large pot with the bones broken and laid on the meat.
3. Pour in four quarts of water, and boil it three hours, skimming it well.
4. Strain it, and put it into another pot.
5. Cut up a hare or a rabbit, a pair of partridges, and a pair of grouse; or one of each, with a pheasant, a woodcock, or any other game that you can most easily obtain.
6. Season them and put them into the soup.
7. Add a dozen small onions, a couple of heads of celery cut small, and half a dozen sliced potatoes.
8. Let the soup simmer till the game is sufficiently done, and all the vegetables tender.
