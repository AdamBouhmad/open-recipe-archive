## Ingredients

- fine citron melons
- six pounds of fine loaf-sugar to every six pounds of melon
- the juice and yellow rind of four lemons
- half a pound of race ginger
- strong alum water
- three pints of clear spring or pump water

## Directions

1. Pare, core, and cut the citron melons into long broad slices
2. Weigh them, and to every six pounds of melon allow six pounds of fine loaf-sugar, and the juice and yellow rind pared off very thin of four lemons, also half a pound of race ginger
3. Put the slices of melon into a preserving-kettle and cover them with strong alum water
4. Boil them half an hour, or longer, till they are quite clear and tender
5. Drain them, lay them in a broad vessel of cold water, cover them and let them stand all night
6. Next morning, tie up the race ginger in a piece of thin muslin, and boil it in three pints of clear spring or pump water, till the water is highly flavoured
7. Having broken up the sugar, put it into a clean preserving-kettle, and pour the ginger water over it
8. When the sugar is all melted, set it over the fire, add the lemon parings, and boil and skim it, till no more scum rises
9. Take out the lemon peel, stir in the juice, and put in the citron slices
10. Boil them in the syrup till they are transparent and soft, but not till they break
11. When done, put the citron slices and syrup into a large tureen, set it in a dry, cool, dark place, and leave it uncovered for two or three days
12. Put the slices carefully into wide-mouthed glass jars, and gently pour in the syrup
13. Lay inside the top of each jar a double white tissue paper cut exactly to fit, and close the jars carefully with corks and cement
