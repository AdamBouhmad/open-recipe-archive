## Ingredients

- large fine juicy apples
- brown sugar
- chips of lemon-peel
- lemon juice
- a pound of suet, chopped fine
- two pounds and a half of sifted flour
- water

## Directions

1. Take large fine juicy apples
2. Pare them, and extract the cores without dividing the apple
3. Fill each hole with brown sugar, and some chips of lemon-peel
4. Squeeze in some lemon juice
5. Have ready a paste, made in the proportion of a pound of suet, chopped as fine as possible, to two pounds and a half of sifted flour, well mixed, and wetted with as little water as possible
6. Roll out the paste to a moderate thickness, and cut it into circular pieces, allowing two pieces to each dumpling
7. Lay your apple on one piece, and put another piece on the top, closing the paste round the sides with your fingers, so as to cover the apple entirely
8. Boil each dumpling in a small coarse cloth, which has first been dipped in hot water
9. Tie them tightly, leaving a small space for the dumpling to swell
10. Plaster a little flour on the inside of each tying place to prevent the water from getting in
11. Put in the dumplings and boil them steadily for an hour
12. Send them to table hot in a covered dish
