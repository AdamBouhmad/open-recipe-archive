## Ingredients

- free-stone peaches
- salt
- water
- garlic
- horse-radish
- mustard seed
- cloves
- ginger-root
- pack-thread
- cinnamon
- cochineal
- vinegar
- mustard
- nutmeg

## Directions

1. Take free-stone peaches of the largest size, when they are full grown but not quite ripe, and lay them in salt and water for two days, covered with a board to keep them down
2. Take them out, wipe them dry, cut them open, and extract the stones
3. Mix together to your taste minced garlic, scraped horse-radish, bruised mustard seed, and cloves, and a little ginger-root soaked in water to soften and then sliced
4. Fill the cavity of the peaches with this mixture
5. Tie them round with pack-thread and put them into a stone jar till it is two-thirds full
6. Strew among them some whole cloves, broken cinnamon, and a little cochineal
7. Season some cold vinegar, allowing to each quart a jill of fresh made mustard and a little ginger and nutmeg
8. Mix this pickle well and fill up the jar with it
