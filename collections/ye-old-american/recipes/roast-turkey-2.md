## Ingredients

- young turkey
- baking soda
- salt
- pepper
- Dressing for Fowls
- soft butter
- flour
- cup of boiling water
- cranberry sauce

## Directions

1. Select a young turkey; remove all the feathers carefully, singe it over a burning newspaper on the top of the stove
2. Draw it nicely, being very careful not to break any of the internal organs
3. Remove the crop carefully; cut off the head, and tie the neck close to the body by drawing the skin over it
4. Rinse the inside of the turkey out with several waters, and in the next to the last, mix a teaspoonful of baking soda
5. After washing, wipe the turkey dry, inside and out, with a clean cloth
6. Rub the inside with some salt, then stuff the breast and body with Dressing for Fowls
7. Sew up the turkey with a strong thread, tie the legs and wings to the body
8. Rub it over with a little soft butter, sprinkle over some salt and pepper, dredge with a little flour
9. Place it in a dripping-pan, pour in a cup of boiling water, and set in the oven
10. Baste the turkey often, turning it around occasionally so that every part will be uniformly baked
11. When pierced with a fork and the liquid runs out perfectly clear, the bird is done
12. If any part is likely to scorch, pin over it a piece of buttered white paper
13. A fifteen pound turkey requires between three and four hours to bake
14. Serve with cranberry sauce
