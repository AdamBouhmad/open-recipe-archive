## Ingredients

- three handfuls of fresh damask rose-leaves
- three handfuls of sweet pinks
- three handfuls of wall-flowers
- three handfuls of stock gilly-flowers
- other fragrant flowers
- powdered orris-root
- lavender
- knotted marjoram
- rosemary
- lemon-thyme
- balm of Gilead
- lemon-peel
- laurel leaves
- mint
- sliced orris-root
- powdered cloves
- cinnamon
- nutmeg

## Directions

1. Take a china jar, and put into it three handfuls of fresh damask rose-leaves; three of sweet pinks, three of wall-flowers, and stock gilly-flowers, and equal proportions of any other fragrant flowers that you can procure
2. Place them in layers; strewing powdered orris-root thickly between each layer
3. You may fill another jar with equal quantities of lavender, knotted marjoram, rosemary, lemon-thyme, balm of Gilead, lemon-peel, and smaller quantities of laurel leaves and mint; and some sliced orris-root
4. Mix with the herbs (which must all be chopped) powdered cloves, cinnamon, and nutmeg; strewing powdered orris-root between the layers
5. Flowers, herbs, and spice may all be mixed in the same jar; add always some orris root
6. Ensure every thing that is put in is perfectly free from damp
7. Keep the jar closely covered, except when the cover is occasionally removed for the purpose of diffusing the scent through the room
