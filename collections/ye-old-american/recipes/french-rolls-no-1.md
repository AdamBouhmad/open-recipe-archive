## Ingredients

- risen sponge
- heaping tablespoonful of lard or butter

## Directions

1. After adding and working in the risen sponge to kneading dough for the day's baking, set aside enough for a loaf of tea-rolls
2. Work into this a heaping tablespoonful of lard or butter, and let it stand in a tolerably cool place (not a cold or draughty one) for four hours
3. Knead it again and let it alone for three hours longer
4. Make into rolls by rolling out, very lightly, pieces of the dough into round cakes and folding these not quite in the centre like turn-overs
5. Let the third rising be for one hour, then bake steadily half an hour or less if the oven is quick
