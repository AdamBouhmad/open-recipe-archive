## Ingredients

- fresh tongue
- water
- salt
- small red pepper
- vinegar
- sugar

## Directions

1. Wash a fresh tongue and just cover it with water in the pot.
2. Put in a pint of salt and a small red pepper.
3. Add more water as it evaporates, so as to keep the tongue nearly covered until done--when it can be easily pierced with a fork.
4. Take it out, and if wanted soon, take off the skin and set it away to cool.
5. If wanted for future use, do not peel until it is required.
6. A cupful of salt will do for three tongues, if you have that number to boil; but do not fail to keep water enough in the pot to keep them covered while boiling.
7. If salt tongues are used, soak them over night, of course omitting the salt when boiling.
8. Or, after peeling a tongue, place it in a saucepan with one cup of water, half a cup vinegar, four tablespoonfuls sugar, and cook until the liquor is evaporated.
