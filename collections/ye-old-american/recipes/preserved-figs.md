## Ingredients

- figs when perfectly ripe
- water
- hickory wood-ashes
- the finest loaf-sugar

## Directions

1. Take ripe figs and wipe them carefully, leaving the stem about half an inch long.
2. Boil them rapidly for about ten minutes in water that has a small bag of hickory wood-ashes laid at the bottom of the preserving kettle.
3. Take them out carefully so as not to break the skins.
4. Wash out the kettle and boil the figs a second time in clean hot water for ten minutes.
5. Take them out, spread them separately on large dishes, and let them rest till next morning.
6. Prepare a syrup by allowing to every pound of the finest loaf-sugar half a pint of water.
7. When melted together, place the kettle over the fire.
8. When the syrup has boiled and is thoroughly skimmed, put in the figs and boil them about twenty-five minutes or half an hour.
9. Take them out and spread them to cool on large dishes.
10. Put them up in glass jars, pouring the syrup over them.
11. Cover the jars closely and set them in the hot sun all next day.
12. Seal the corks with red cement made of melted rosin and bees-wax, thickened with fine brick-dust.
