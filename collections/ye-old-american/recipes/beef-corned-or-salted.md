## Ingredients

- quarter of beef
- coarse salt, half a peck per hundred weight
- quarter pound saltpetre
- quarter pound saleratus
- one quart molasses or two pounds coarse brown sugar
- mace
- cloves
- allspice
- water

## Directions

1. Cut up a quarter of beef
2. For each hundred weight take half a peck of coarse salt, a quarter of a pound of saltpetre, the same weight of saleratus and a quart of molasses, or two pounds of coarse brown sugar
3. Mace, cloves and allspice may be added for spiced beef
4. Strew some of the salt in the bottom of a pickle-tub or barrel
5. Put in a layer of meat
6. Strew this with salt
7. Add another layer of meat
8. Repeat salt and meat alternately until all is used
9. Let it remain one night
10. Dissolve the saleratus and saltpetre in a little warm water
11. Put it to the molasses or sugar
12. Put it over the meat
13. Add water enough to cover the meat
14. Lay a board on it to keep it under the brine
15. The meat is fit for use after ten days
16. Towards spring take the brine from the meat, make it boiling hot and skim it clear
17. When it is cooled, return it to the meat
