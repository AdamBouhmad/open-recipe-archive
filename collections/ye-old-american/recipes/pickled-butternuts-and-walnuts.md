## Ingredients

- butternuts and walnuts
- strong brine
- vinegar
- sugar
- whole cloves
- black pepper-corns
- allspice
- blades of mace

## Directions

1. Gather them when soft enough to be pierced by a pin.
2. Lay them in strong brine five days, changing this twice in the meantime.
3. Drain, and wipe them with a coarse cloth; pierce each by running a large needle through it, and lay in cold water for six hours.
4. To each gallon of vinegar allow a cup of sugar, three dozen each of whole cloves and black pepper-corns, half as much allspice, and a dozen blades of mace.
5. Boil five minutes; pack the nuts in small jars and pour over them scalding hot.
6. Repeat this twice within a week; tie up and set away.
7. They will be good to eat in a month—and very good too.
