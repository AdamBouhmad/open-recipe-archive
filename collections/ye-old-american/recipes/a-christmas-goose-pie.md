## Ingredients

- one pound of butter
- a pint and a half of water
- four pounds of flour
- beaten yolks of three or four eggs
- a large goose
- a fowl
- a smoked tongue
- a powdered nutmeg
- a quarter of an ounce of powdered mace
- a tea-spoonful of pepper
- a tea-spoonful of salt
- beaten white of egg
- pieces of the flesh of pigeons, partridges, quails, or any game
- bits of ham
- force-meat balls
- half a pound of butter
- beaten yolk of egg
- white paper

## Directions

1. Put into a sauce-pan one pound of butter cut up, and a pint and a half of water; stir it while it is melting, and let it come to a boil.
2. Skim off whatever milk or impurity may rise to the top.
3. Have ready four pounds of flour sifted into a pan.
4. Make a hole in the middle of it, and pour in the melted butter while hot.
5. Mix it with a spoon to a stiff paste, adding the beaten yolks of three or four eggs, and then knead it very well with your hands, on the pasteboard, keeping it dredged with flour till it ceases to be sticky.
6. Set it away to cool.
7. Split a large goose, and a fowl down the back, loosen the flesh all over with a sharp knife, and take out all the bones.
8. Parboil a smoked tongue; peel it and cut off the root.
9. Mix together a powdered nutmeg, a quarter of an ounce of powdered mace, a tea-spoonful of pepper, and a tea-spoonful of salt, and season with them the fowl and the goose.
10. Roll out the paste near an inch thick, and divide it into three pieces.
11. Cut out two of them of an oval form for the top and bottom; and the other into a long straight piece for the sides or walls of the pie.
12. Brush the paste all over with beaten white of egg, and set on the bottom the piece that is to form the wall, pinching the edges together, and cementing them with white of egg.
13. The bottom piece must be large enough to turn up a little round the lower edge of the wall piece, to which it must be firmly joined all round.
14. Put in first the goose, then the fowl, and then the tongue.
15. Fill up what space is left with pieces of the flesh of pigeons, or of partridges, quails, or any game that is convenient.
16. Add some bits of ham, or some force-meat balls.
17. Cover the other ingredients with half a pound of butter, and put on the top crust, which must be of an oval form to correspond with the bottom.
18. Place the lid not quite on the top edge of the wall, but an inch and a half below it.
19. Close it very well, and ornament the sides and top with festoons and leaves cut out of paste.
20. Notch the edges handsomely, and put a paste flower in the centre.
21. Glaze the whole with beaten yolk of egg, and bind the pie all round with a double fold of white paper.
22. Set it in a regular oven, and bake it four hours.
