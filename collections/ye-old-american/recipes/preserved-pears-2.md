## Ingredients

- pears (Seckle variety preferred)
- sugar (one pound to one pound of fruit)
- water (nearly one cupful)
- cloves
- lemon juice and rind (juice and thinly pared rind of one lemon to each five pounds of fruit, optional)

## Directions

1. Pare off the peeling thin from one pound of fruit
2. Make a nice syrup of nearly one cupful of water and one pound of sugar
3. When clarified by boiling and skimming put in the pears and stew gently until clear
4. Stick a clove in the blossom end of each pear
5. As an alternative, add the juice and thinly pared rind of one lemon to each five pounds of fruit
6. If the pears are hard and tough, parboil them until tender before beginning to preserve, and from the same water take what you need for making their syrup
7. If you can procure only large pears to preserve, cut them into halves, or even slices, so that they can get done more quickly
