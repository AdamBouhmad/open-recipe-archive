## Ingredients

- oranges
- three pounds of granulated sugar to each gallon of juice
- the white and shell of one egg to each gallon
- one-third of a gallon of cold water to each gallon
- a small cake of compressed yeast to about ten gallons of wine
- a glass of brandy to each gallon of wine

## Directions

1. Wipe the oranges with a wet cloth
2. Peel off the yellow rind very thin
3. Squeeze the oranges and strain the juice through a hair-sieve
4. Measure the juice after it is strained and for each gallon allow three pounds of granulated sugar, the white and shell of one egg and one-third of a gallon of cold water
5. Put the sugar, the white and shell of the egg (crushed small) and the water over the fire and stir them every two minutes until the eggs begin to harden
6. Boil the syrup until it looks clear under the froth of egg which will form on the surface
7. Strain the syrup and pour it upon the orange rind
8. Let it stand over night
9. Next add the orange juice and again let it stand over night
10. Strain it the second day and put it into a tight cask with a small cake of compressed yeast to about ten gallons of wine
11. Leave the bung out of the cask until the wine ceases to ferment
12. When fermentation ceases, close the cask by driving in the bung
13. Let the wine stand about nine months before bottling it
14. Three months after it is bottled, it can be used
15. A glass of brandy may be added to each gallon of wine after fermentation ceases
