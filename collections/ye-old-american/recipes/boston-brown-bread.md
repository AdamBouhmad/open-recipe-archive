## Ingredients

- 1 cup yeast
- 6 potatoes, mashed fine with three cups of flour
- 1 quart warm water
- 2 tablespoonfuls lard
- 2 tablespoonfuls brown sugar
- 1 quart rye-flour
- 2 quarts Indian meal
- 1 tablespoonful salt
- 1 teaspoonful soda or saleratus
- half a teacupful of molasses

## Directions

1. Set a sponge over night with potatoes or white flour in the following proportions: 1 cup yeast, 6 potatoes mashed fine with three cups of flour, 1 quart warm water, 2 tablespoonfuls lard, 2 tablespoonfuls brown sugar
2. Beat up well and let it rise five or six hours
3. When light, sift into the bread-tray 1 quart rye-flour, 2 quarts Indian meal, 1 tablespoonful salt, 1 teaspoonful soda or saleratus
4. Mix this up very soft with the risen sponge, adding warm water if needed, and working in gradually half a teacupful of molasses
5. Knead well and let it rise from six to seven hours
6. Work over again and divide into loaves, putting these in well-greased, round, deep pans
7. The second rising should last an hour
8. Bake in a moderate oven about four hours
9. If put in late in the day, let it stay in the oven all night
