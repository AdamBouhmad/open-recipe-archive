## Ingredients

- 1 quart flour
- 2 tablespoonfuls lard, or half butter
- 2 cups of milk
- 1 teaspoonful soda, dissolved in hot water
- 2 teaspoonfuls cream-tartar
- 1 saltspoonful salt
- juicy, tart apples, pared and cored
- beaten egg
- powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Chop the shortening into the flour after you have sifted this and the cream-tartar together; put in the soda and wet up quickly—just stiff enough to roll into a paste less than half an inch thick.
2. Cut into squares, and lay in the centre of each a juicy, tart apple, pared and cored; bring the corners of the square neatly together and pinch them slightly.
3. Lay in a buttered baking-pan, the joined edges downward, and bake to a fine brown.
4. When done, brush over with beaten egg, and set back in the oven to glaze for two or three minutes.
5. Sift powdered sugar over them, and eat hot with rich sweet sauce.
