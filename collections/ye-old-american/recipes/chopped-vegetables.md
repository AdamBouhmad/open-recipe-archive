## Ingredients

- remains of vegetables from a boiled dinner such as cabbage, parsnips, potatoes
- pepper
- butter (piece the size of a hickory nut)
- hot water

## Directions

1. Chop rather coarsely the remains of vegetables left from a boiled dinner, such as cabbage, parsnips, potatoes, etc.
2. Sprinkle over them a little pepper
3. Place in a saucepan or frying pan over the fire
4. Put in a piece of butter the size of a hickory nut
5. When it begins to melt, tip the dish so as to oil the bottom and around the sides
6. Put in the chopped vegetables
7. Pour in a spoonful or two of hot water from the tea-kettle
8. Cover quickly so as to keep in the steam
9. When heated thoroughly take off the cover and stir occasionally until well cooked
10. Serve hot
