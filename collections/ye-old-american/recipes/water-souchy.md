## Ingredients

- four flounders or half a dozen perch
- two onions
- bunch of parsley
- three quarts of water
- pepper
- salt
- mace
- half a pint of white wine or two table-spoonfuls of vinegar
- parsley roots
- parsley leaves
- mushroom catchup
- bread and butter

## Directions

1. Cut up four flounders or half a dozen perch, two onions, and a bunch of parsley
2. Put them into three quarts of water and boil them till the fish go entirely to pieces and dissolve in the water
3. Strain the liquor through a sieve and put it into a kettle or stew-pan
4. Have ready a few more fish with the heads, tails, and fins removed, and the brown skin taken off
5. Cut little notches in them and lay them for a short time in very cold water
6. Put them into the stew-pan with the liquor or soup-stock of the first fish
7. Season with pepper, salt, and mace, and add half a pint of white wine or two table-spoonfuls of vinegar
8. Boil it gently for a quarter of an hour and skim it well
9. Have parsley roots cut into slices and boiled till very tender
10. Have a quantity of parsley leaves boiled nice and green
11. After the fish-pan has boiled moderately fifteen minutes, take it off the fire and put in the parsley roots and a little mushroom catchup
12. Take out the fish and lay them in a broad deep dish or in a tureen
13. Pour on the soup very gently for fear of breaking them
14. Strew the green parsley leaves over the top
15. Serve with plates of bread and butter
