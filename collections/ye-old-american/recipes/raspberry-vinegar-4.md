## Ingredients

- raspberries
- cider-vinegar
- 1 pint of water
- 5 lbs. of sugar (best white) for every 3 pints of this liquid, juice and water mingled

## Directions

1. Put the raspberries into a stone vessel and mash them to a pulp
2. Add cider-vinegar—no specious imitation, but the genuine article—enough to cover it well
3. Stand in the sun twelve hours, and all night in the cellar
4. Stir up well occasionally during this time
5. Strain, and put as many fresh berries in the jar as you took out
6. Pour the strained vinegar over them; mash and set in the sun all day
7. Strain a second time next day
8. Place over a gentle fire and stir until the sugar is dissolved
9. Heat slowly to boiling, skimming off the scum, and as soon as it fairly boils take off and strain
10. Bottle while warm, and seal the corks with sealing wax, or bees'-wax and rosin
