## Ingredients

- water
- milk
- cream
- salt
- butter
- white pepper
- arrow-root, rice-flour, or corn-starch
- oyster-shells or clam-shells
- oysters

## Directions

1. Pour into your inner saucepan a cup of hot water, another of milk, and one of cream, with a little salt.
2. Set into a kettle of hot water until it boils.
3. Stir in two tablespoonfuls of butter and a little salt, with white pepper.
4. Take from the fire and add two heaping tablespoonfuls of arrow-root, rice-flour, or corn-starch, wet with cold milk.
5. By this time your shells should be washed and buttered, and a fine oyster laid within each.
6. Use oyster-shells for this purpose; but clam-shells are more roomy and manageable, because more regular in shape.
7. Range these closely in a large baking-pan, propping them with clean pebbles or fragments of shell, if they do not seem inclined to retain their contents.
8. Stir the cream very hard and fill up each shell with a spoon, taking care not to spill any in the pan.
9. Bake five or six minutes in a hot oven after the shells become warm.
10. Serve on the shell.
11. Alternatively, substitute oyster-liquor for the water in the mixture, and use all milk instead of cream.
