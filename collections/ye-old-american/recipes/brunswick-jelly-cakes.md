## Ingredients

- one cup of powdered white sugar
- one-half cup of butter
- three eggs
- three cups of flour
- one teaspoonful of mixed spice (nutmeg, mace and cinnamon)
- half a glass of rose-water or wine
- fruit jelly or marmalade
- powdered loaf sugar
- strong essence of lemon

## Directions

1. Stir one cup of powdered white sugar and one-half cup of butter together, till perfectly light
2. Beat the yolks of three eggs till very thick and smooth
3. Sift three cups of flour and stir it into the beaten eggs with the butter and sugar
4. Add a teaspoonful of mixed spice (nutmeg, mace and cinnamon) and half a glass of rose-water or wine
5. Stir the whole well and lay it on your paste-board, which must first be sprinkled with flour
6. If you find it so moist as to be unmanageable, throw in a little more flour
7. Spread the dough into a sheet about half an inch thick and cut it out in round cakes with a biscuit-cutter
8. Lay them in buttered pans and bake about five or six minutes
9. When cold, spread over the surface of each cake a liquor of fruit jelly or marmalade
10. Beat the whites of three or four eggs till they stand alone
11. Beat into the froth, by degrees, a sufficiency of powdered loaf sugar to make it as thick as icing
12. Flavor with a few drops of strong essence of lemon, and with a spoon heap it up on each cake, making it high in the centre
13. Put the cakes into a cool oven, and as soon as the tops are colored a pale brown, take them out
