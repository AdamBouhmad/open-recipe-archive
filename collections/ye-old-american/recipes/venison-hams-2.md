## Ingredients

- hams
- salt
- brown sugar
- dry salt
- fine salt
- molasses
- saltpetre
- cider vinegar
- cold water
- tepid water
- bran
- brown paper
- muslin cover

## Directions

1. Mix together in equal proportions, salt and brown sugar, and rub them hard into the hams with your hand.
2. Pack them in a cask, sprinkling dry salt between them, and let them lie eight days, rubbing them over every day with dry salt and sugar.
3. Mix equal parts of fine salt, molasses, and a teaspoonful of saltpetre to every two hams.
4. Take the hams out of the pickle, go over them with a brush dipped in cider vinegar, then in the new mixture.
5. Empty the cask, wash it out with cold water, and repack the hams, dripping from the sticky bath, scattering fine salt over each.
6. Let them lie eight days longer in this.
7. Wash off the pickle first with tepid water, until the salt crystals are removed; then sponge with vinegar, powder them with bran while wet, and smoke a fortnight, or, if large, three weeks.
8. Wrap in brown paper that has no unpleasant odor, stitch a muslin cover over this, and whitewash, unless you mean to use at once.
9. Chip or shave for the table.
