## Ingredients

- goose
- pepper
- salt
- onions
- green sage leaves
- grated bread-crumbs
- butter
- egg yolks
- apple-sauce
- mashed potatoes

## Directions

1. Having drawn and singed the goose, wipe out the inside with a cloth, and sprinkle in some pepper and salt.
2. Make a stuffing of four good sized onions minced fine, and half their quantity of green sage leaves minced also, a large tea-cupful of grated bread-crumbs, a piece of butter the size of a walnut, and the beaten yolks of two eggs, with a little pepper and salt.
3. Mix the whole together, and incorporate them well.
4. Put the stuffing into the goose, and press it in hard; but do not entirely fill up the cavity, as the mixture will swell in cooking.
5. Tie the goose securely round with a greased or wetted string; and paper the breast to prevent it from scorching.
6. Fasten the goose on the spit at both ends.
7. The fire must be brisk and well kept up.
8. It will require from two hours to two and a half to roast.
9. Baste it at first with a little salt and water, and then with its own gravy.
10. Take off the paper when the goose is about half done, and dredge it with a little flour towards the last.
11. Having parboiled the liver and heart, chop them and put them into the gravy, which must be skimmed well and thickened with a little browned flour.
12. Send apple-sauce to table with the goose; also mashed potatoes.
