## Ingredients

- 2 young fowls, or one full-grown
- ½ lb. corned ham
- 1 gallon of water
- four hard-boiled eggs
- handful of fine bread-crumbs
- salt
- white pepper
- bunch of chopped parsley
- pint of rich milk
- flour

## Directions

1. Cut the fowls into pieces as for fricassee.
2. Put these with the ham into the pot with a quart of water, or enough to cover them fairly.
3. Stew for an hour, if the fowls are tender; if tough, until you can cut easily into the breast.
4. Take out the breasts, leaving the rest of the meat in the pot, and add the remainder of the water—boiling hot.
5. Keep the soup stewing slowly while you chop up the white meat you have selected.
6. Rub the yolks of four hard-boiled eggs smooth in a mortar or bowl, moistening to a paste with a few spoonfuls of the soup.
7. Mix with these a handful of fine bread-crumbs and the chopped meat, and make it into small balls.
8. When the soup has boiled in all, two hours and a half, if the chicken be reduced to shreds, strain out the meat and bones.
9. Season with salt and white pepper, with a bunch of chopped parsley.
10. Drop in the prepared force-meat, and after boiling ten minutes to incorporate the ingredients well, add, a little at a time, a pint of rich milk thickened with flour.
11. Boil up once and serve.
