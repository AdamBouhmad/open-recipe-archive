## Ingredients

- sirloin or rib pieces of beef
- boiling water
- salt
- flour
- butter

## Directions

1. Make your butcher remove most of the bone, and skewer the meat into the shape of a round
2. If you roast in an oven, dash a small cup of boiling water over the meat in first putting it down, letting it trickle into the pan
3. If there is much fat upon the upper surface, cover with a paste of flour and water until it is nearly done
4. Baste frequently, at first with salt and water, afterward with the drippings
5. Allow about a quarter of an hour to a pound, if you like your meat rare; more, if you prefer to have it well done
6. When the meat is almost done, dredge with flour and baste with butter—only once
7. Remove the beef, when quite ready, to a heated dish; skim the drippings; add a teacupful of boiling water, boil up once, and send to table in a gravy-boat
8. Serve with mustard, or scraped horse-radish and vinegar
