## Ingredients

- sweet new cider fresh from the press, not yet fermented
- fine juicy apples
- cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg

## Directions

1. Fill a very large kettle with cider, and boil it till reduced to one half the original quantity.
2. Have ready some fine juicy apples, pared, cored, and quartered; and put as many into the kettle as can be kept moist by the cider.
3. Stir it frequently, and when the apples are stewed quite soft, take them out with a skimmer that has holes in it, and put them into a tub.
4. Add more apples to the cider, and stew them soft in the same manner, stirring them nearly all the time with a stick.
5. At night leave them to cool in the tubs, which must be covered with cloths, and finish next day by boiling the apple and cider again till the consistence is that of soft marmalade, and the colour a very dark brown.
6. Twenty minutes or half an hour before you finally take it from the fire, add powdered cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg to your taste.
7. When it is cold, put it into stone jars, and cover it closely.
