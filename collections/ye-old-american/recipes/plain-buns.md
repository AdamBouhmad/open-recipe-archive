## Ingredients

- 3 cups sweet milk
- 1 cup yeast
- Flour to make thick batter
- 1 cup sugar
- ½ cup butter, melted
- ½ nutmeg
- 1 saltspoonful salt
- Flour
- 2 or 3 handfuls currants
- white of an egg beaten up stiff with white sugar

## Directions

1. Set 3 cups sweet milk, 1 cup yeast, and flour to make thick batter as a sponge over night.
2. In the morning add 1 cup sugar, ½ cup butter melted, ½ nutmeg, and 1 saltspoonful salt.
3. Add flour enough to knead like biscuit-dough, taking care not to get it too stiff.
4. After the five-hour rising, work in two or three handfuls of currants which have been previously well washed and dredged with flour.
5. Mould with your hands into round balls, set these closely together in a pan, that they may form a loaf when baked.
6. Let them stand nearly an hour, or until very light.
7. Bake from half to three-quarters of an hour until brown.
8. Wash them over while hot with the beaten egg and sugar.
