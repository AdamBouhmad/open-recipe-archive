## Ingredients

- 1 lb. sweet almonds, blanched in boiling water, stripped of skins, and pounded when perfectly cold in a Wedgewood mortar with rose-water
- 1 generous tablespoonful essence of bitter almonds
- 1 lb. powdered sugar
- The beaten whites of eight eggs
- 1 teaspoonful nutmeg
- 1 teaspoonful arrowroot

## Directions

1. Prepare the almonds the day before by blanching them in boiling water, stripping off the skins, and pounding them when perfectly cold a few at a time in a Wedgewood mortar, adding rose-water from time to time
2. When beaten to a smooth paste, stir in the essence of bitter almonds and cover closely, set away in a cold place until the morrow
3. Stir the sugar and white of egg lightly together
4. Whip in gradually the almond-paste
5. Line a broad baking-pan with buttered white paper
6. Drop upon this spoonfuls of the mixture at such distances apart as shall prevent their running together
7. Sift powdered sugar thickly upon each
8. Bake in a quick oven to a delicate brown
