## Ingredients

- Half a pound of rice
- Two ounces of butter
- Three ounces of sugar
- Half a saltspoonful of mixed ground spice
- Salt
- Yolks of two eggs
- Tablespoonful or two of cream
- Flour for shaping
- Four ounces of sugar
- Two ounces of butter
- Yolks of two eggs
- Gill of cream

## Directions

1. Boil half a pound of rice, drain and mash it moderately fine.
2. Add to it two ounces of butter, three ounces of sugar, half a saltspoonful of mixed ground spice, salt and the yolks of two eggs.
3. Moisten a trifle with a tablespoonful or two of cream.
4. With floured hands shape the mixture into balls, and tie them in floured pudding cloths.
5. Steam or boil forty minutes.
6. For the custard sauce: Mix together four ounces of sugar and two ounces of butter (slightly warmed).
7. Beat together the yolks of two eggs and a gill of cream; mix and pour the sauce in a double saucepan.
8. Set this in a pan of hot water and whisk thoroughly three minutes.
9. Set the saucepan in cold water and whisk until the sauce is cooled.
10. Send the dumplings to table with the custard sauce.
