## Ingredients

- half a pound of butter
- two pounds of sifted flour
- three quarters of a pound of powdered sugar
- a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- a grated nutmeg
- a large table-spoonful of rose water
- six eggs
- boiling lard for frying
- white sugar for coating

## Directions

1. Rub half a pound of butter into two pounds of sifted flour
2. Mix in three quarters of a pound of powdered sugar
3. Add a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon and a grated nutmeg with a large table-spoonful of rose water
4. Beat six eggs very light and stir them into the mixture
5. Mix it with a knife into a soft paste
6. Put it on the paste-board and roll it out into a sheet an inch thick
7. If you find it too soft, knead in a little more flour and roll it out over again
8. Cut it into long slips with a jagging iron or with a sharp knife and twist them into various fantastic shapes
9. Have ready on hot coals a skillet of boiling lard
10. Put in the crullers and fry them of a light brown, turning them occasionally by means of a knife and fork
11. Take them out one by one on a perforated skimmer, that the lard may drain off through the holes
12. Spread them out on a large dish and when cold grate white sugar over them
