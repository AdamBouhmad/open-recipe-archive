## Ingredients

- string beans
- water
- salt
- butter
- bacon

## Directions

1. Break off the tops and bottoms and string carefully.
2. Pare both edges with a sharp knife, to be certain that no remnant of the tough fibre remains.
3. Cut the beans thus cleared of their troublesome attachés, in pieces an inch long, and lay in cold water with a little salt for fifteen or twenty minutes.
4. Drain them, and put into a saucepan of boiling water.
5. Boil quickly, twenty minutes if well-grown—less if small—at any rate, until tender.
6. Drain in a cullender until the water ceases to drip from them.
7. Dish with a great spoonful of butter stirred in.
8. A bit of bacon boiled with them—whole, or chopped into bits that dissolve in the boiling, mellows the rank taste.
