## Ingredients

- calves or hogs' feet
- one quart of chopped apples
- one quart of currants, washed and picked
- raisins stoned and cut
- good brown sugar
- suet nicely chopped
- cider
- a pint of brandy
- a tea-spoonful of pounded mace
- a tea-spoonful of cloves
- a tea-spoonful of nutmegs
- a tea-spoonful of pounded black pepper
- a tea-spoonful of salt
- citron sliced thin
- paste

## Directions

1. Boil either calves or hogs' feet till perfectly tender, rub them through a colander
2. When cold, pass them through again, and it will come out like pearl barley
3. Take one quart of this, one of chopped apples, the same of currants, washed and picked, raisins stoned and cut, of good brown sugar, suet nicely chopped, and cider, with a pint of brandy
4. Add a tea-spoonful of pounded mace, one of cloves and of nutmegs
5. Mix all these together intimately
6. When the pies are to be made, take out as much of this mixture as may be necessary
7. To each quart of it, add a tea-spoonful of pounded black pepper, and one of salt
8. Cover the moulds with paste, put in a sufficiency of mince-meat, cover the top with citron sliced thin, and lay on it a lid garnished around with paste cut in fanciful shapes
9. They may be eaten either hot or cold, but are best when hot
