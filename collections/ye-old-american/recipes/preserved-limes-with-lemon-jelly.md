## Ingredients

- limes
- alum
- weak ginger tea
- fresh vine-leaves
- spring or pump-water
- loaf-sugar, double refined
- water
- white of egg
- lemon juice from saved juice
- lemons, ripe
- loaf-sugar, double refined
- isinglass

## Directions

1. Change the water nearly every hour
2. At night, change the water once more and let the limes remain in it till next day to remove alum taste, or boil in weak ginger tea if needed
3. To make them very green, line the sides and bottom of a preserving-kettle with fresh vine-leaves placed very thickly
4. Put in the limes and pour on as much clear cold water as will cover them
5. Fill up with a very thick layer of vine-leaves
6. Boil them slowly an hour or more
7. If not sufficiently green, repeat the process with fresh vine-leaves and fresh water
8. They must boil till a twig can pierce them
9. After greening, give the kettle a complete washing or take another
10. Weigh the limes and allow to every pound of them a pound of the best double refined loaf-sugar and half a pint of very clear water
11. Break up the sugar and put it into the kettle
12. Pour on the water, which must previously be mixed with some beaten white of egg, allowing the white of one egg to three pounds of sugar
13. Let the sugar dissolve in the water before setting it over the fire, stirring it well
14. Boil and skim the sugar, and when the scum ceases to rise, put in the limes
15. Add the juice that was saved from them, strained from the pulp, seeds, &c.
16. Boil the limes in the syrup till they are very tender and transparent
17. Take them out carefully and spread them on flat dishes
18. Put the syrup into a tureen and leave it uncovered for two days
19. Get several dozen of fine ripe lemons
20. Roll them under your hand on the table to increase the juice
21. Cut them in half and squeeze them through a strainer into a pitcher
22. To each pint of the lemon juice allow a pound and a quarter of the best double refined loaf-sugar
23. Put the sugar mixed with the lemon-juice into a preserving-kettle
24. When they are melted set it over the fire and boil and skim it till it becomes a thick, firm jelly
25. Try if it will congeal by taking out a little in a spoon and placing it in the open air
26. If it congeals immediately, it is sufficiently done
27. Put the jelly at once into a large bowl and leave it uncovered
28. When the lemon-jelly, the syrup and the limes are thoroughly done and all grown cold, finish by filling the limes with the jelly
29. Put them with the open part downwards into wide-mouthed glass jars
30. Gently pour on them the syrup
31. Cover the jars closely and paste strong paper over the covers or seal the corks
