## Ingredients

- fifty fine large oysters
- four or five small pickled cucumbers
- a bunch of parsley
- two tea-cupfuls of stale bread-crumbs
- yolks of four eggs
- cayenne
- powdered mace
- salt
- a pound of lard

## Directions

1. Take fifty fine large oysters, and mince them raw.
2. Chop also four or five small pickled cucumbers, and a bunch of parsley.
3. Grate about two tea-cupfuls of stale bread-crumbs, and beat up the yolks of four eggs.
4. Mix the whole together in a thick batter, seasoning it with cayenne and powdered mace; and with a little salt if the oysters are fresh.
5. Have ready a pound of lard, and melt in the frying-pan enough of it to fry the oysters well.
6. When the lard is boiling hot in the pan, put in about a table-spoonful at a time of the oyster-mixture, and fry it in the form of small fritters; turning them so as to brown on both sides.
7. Serve them up hot, and eat them with small bread rolls.
