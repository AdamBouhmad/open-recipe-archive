## Ingredients

- six apples
- one ounce of butter
- about two ounces of white powdered sugar
- two tablespoonfuls of water
- two or three slices of bread, half an inch thick
- two ounces of butter
- a little white sugar

## Directions

1. Cut six apples into quarters, take the core out, peel and cut them in slices
2. Put in the saucepan an ounce of butter, then throw over the apples about two ounces of white powdered sugar and two tablespoonfuls of water
3. Put the saucepan on the fire, let it stew quickly, toss them up, or stir with a spoon; a few minutes will do them
4. When tender cut two or three slices of bread half an inch thick
5. Put in a frying pan two ounces of butter, put on the fire; when the butter is melted put in your bread, which fry of a nice yellowish color
6. When nice and crisp take them out, place them on a dish, a little white sugar over, the apples about an inch thick
7. Serve hot
