## Ingredients

- twelve small cat-fish
- pound and a half of ham
- bunch of parsley
- sweet marjoram
- pepper
- head of celery
- large table-spoonful of celery seed
- two quarts of water
- quart of rich milk
- quarter of a pound of butter
- flour
- beaten yolks of four eggs
- toasted bread

## Directions

1. Cut off the heads of cat-fish, skin them, and clean them, and cut them in three
2. Cut the ham into small pieces, or mouthfuls, and scald it two or three times in boiling water, lest it be too salt
3. Chop together a bunch of parsley and some sweet marjoram stripped from the stalks
4. Put these ingredients into a soup kettle and season them with pepper
5. Add a head of celery cut small, or a large table-spoonful of celery seed tied up in a bit of clear muslin to prevent its dispersing
6. Put in two quarts of water, cover the kettle, and let it boil slowly till every thing is sufficiently done, and the fish and ham quite tender
7. Skim it frequently
8. Boil in another vessel a quart of rich milk, in which you have melted a quarter of a pound of butter divided into small bits and rolled in flour
9. Pour it hot to the soup, and stir in at the last the beaten yolks of four eggs
10. Give it another boil, just to take off the rawness of the eggs, and then put it into a tureen, taking out the bag of celery seed before you send the soup to table, and adding some toasted bread cut into small squares
11. Before you send it to table, remove the back-bones of the cat-fish
