## Ingredients

- a pound of sifted flour
- three-quarters of a pound of fresh butter
- a tea-cup of fine brown sugar
- two large table-spoonfuls of strong ginger
- two table-spoonfuls of carraway seeds
- five eggs
- a pint of strained honey
- a small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Rub together a pound of sifted flour and three-quarters of a pound of fresh butter.
2. Mix in a tea-cup of fine brown sugar, two large table-spoonfuls of strong ginger, and two table-spoonfuls of carraway seeds.
3. Beat five eggs and add them to the mixture alternately with a pint of strained honey.
4. Stir in towards the last a small tea-spoonful of pearl-ash that has been melted in a very little vinegar.
5. Beat or stir the mixture long enough to make it perfectly light.
6. Transfer it to a square iron or block-tin pan which must be well buttered.
7. Put it into a moderate oven and bake it an hour or more, in proportion to its thickness.
8. When cool, cut it into squares.
