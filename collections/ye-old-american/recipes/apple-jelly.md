## Ingredients

- twenty large ripe juicy pippins
- yellow rind of four lemons, pared thin
- lemon juice
- loaf-sugar (a pound to each pint of mixed juice)

## Directions

1. Take twenty large ripe juicy pippins. Pare, core, and chop them to pieces.
2. Put them into a jar with the yellow rind of four lemons, pared thin and cut into little bits.
3. Cover the jar closely, and set it into a pot of hot water.
4. Keep the water boiling hard all round it till the apples are dissolved.
5. Strain them through a jelly-bag, and mix with the liquid the juice of the lemons.
6. To each pint of the mixed juice allow a pound of loaf-sugar.
7. Put them into a porcelain kettle, and when the sugar is melted, set it on the fire, and boil and skim it for about twenty minutes, or till it becomes a thick jelly.
8. Put it into tumblers, and cover it with double tissue paper nicely fitted to the inside of the top.
