## Ingredients

- large firm apples (pippins are best)
- water
- sugar (three-quarters of a pound to each pint of water)
- lemon peel, orange peel or mace

## Directions

1. Peel and core large firm apples
2. Throw them into water as you pare them
3. Boil the parings in water for fifteen minutes, allowing a pint to one pound of fruit
4. Strain and, adding three-quarters of a pound of sugar to each pint of water, as measured at first, with enough lemon peel, orange peel or mace, to impart a pleasant flavor, return to the kettle
5. When the syrup has been well skimmed and is clear, pour it boiling hot over the apples, which must be drained from the water in which they have hitherto stood
6. Let them remain in the syrup until both are perfectly cold
7. Covering closely, let them simmer over a slow fire until transparent
