## Ingredients

- a pound of fresh butter
- three pounds of sifted flour
- a pound of fine brown sugar
- half an ounce of cloves ground or powdered
- sufficient West India molasses
- a small tea-spoonful of sal-aratus
- tepid water

## Directions

1. Rub a pound of fresh butter (cut up) into three pounds of sifted flour
2. Add by degrees a pound of fine brown sugar, half an ounce of cloves ground or powdered, and sufficient West India molasses to wet the whole into a stiff dough
3. Dissolve a small tea-spoonful of sal-aratus in tepid water and mix in at the last
4. Roll the dough out into a sheet of paste
5. Cut out the cakes with a tin stamp, or with the edge of a tumbler
6. Put them in buttered pans
7. Bake them a quarter of an hour or more
