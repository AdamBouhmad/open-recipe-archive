## Ingredients

- quinces
- water
- orange juice
- sugar

## Directions

1. Pare, core, and slice the quinces, stewing the skins, cores, and seed in a vessel by themselves, with just enough water to cover them
2. When this has simmered long enough to extract all the flavor, and the parings are broken to pieces, strain off the water through a thick cloth
3. Put the quinces into the preserve-kettle when this water is almost cold, pour it over them and boil, stirring and mashing the fruit with a wooden spoon as it becomes soft
4. The juice of two oranges to every three pounds of the fruit imparts an agreeable flavor
5. When you have reduced all to a smooth paste, stir in a scant three-quarters of a pound of sugar for every pound of fruit
6. Boil ten minutes more, stirring constantly
7. Take off, and when cool put into small jars, with brandied papers over them
