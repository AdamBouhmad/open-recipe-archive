## Ingredients

- tenderloin of beef
- water
- Irish potatoes
- carrots
- turnips
- celery
- pounded allspice
- butter
- lean piece of beef
- onion
- pepper
- salt
- eggs
- grated cracker or fine bread crumbs

## Directions

1. Wash the tenderloin well and put it in an oven
2. Add about a pint of water
3. Chop up a good handful each of Irish potatoes, carrots, turnips and a large bunch of celery
4. Wash, peel and chop up the vegetables raw, then add them to the meat
5. Let the whole slowly simmer
6. When nearly done, add a teaspoonful of pounded allspice
7. Put in a tablespoonful of butter to give richness to the gravy
8. Skim off some of the melted suet if the gravy looks too greasy
9. Boil a lean piece of beef separately until perfectly done
10. Chop the boiled beef fine, flavoring with a very small quantity of onion, pepper and salt to taste
11. Make into small balls
12. Wet them on the outside with eggs and roll in grated cracker or fine bread crumbs
13. Fry these force meat balls a light brown
14. When serving, put the balls around the tenderloin and pour over the whole the rich gravy
