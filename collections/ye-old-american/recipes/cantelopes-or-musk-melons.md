## Ingredients

- very small cantelopes
- salt
- water
- alum
- loaf-sugar

## Directions

1. Take very small cantelopes before they are ripe.
2. Shave a thin paring off the whole outside.
3. Cut out a small piece or plug about an inch square, and through it extract all the seeds, &c. from the middle.
4. Return the plugs to the hole from whence you took them, and secure them with a needle and thread, or by tying a small string round the cantelope.
5. Lay the cantelopes for four or five hours in salt and water.
6. Put them into spring water to extract the salt, changing the water till you find it salt no longer.
7. Scald them in weak alum-water.
8. Make a syrup in the proportion of a pint of water to a pound of loaf-sugar, and boil the cantelopes in it till a straw will go through them.
9. Take them out, and set them in the sun to harden.
