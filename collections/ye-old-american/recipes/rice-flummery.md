## Ingredients

- two quarts of milk
- half a pound of ground rice
- a bunch of peach leaves or a few peach-kernels
- sugar
- mace
- rose brandy
- juice of a large lemon

## Directions

1. To two quarts of milk allow half a pound of ground rice.
2. Take out one pint of the milk, and mix the rice gradually with it into a batter; making it quite smooth and free from lumps.
3. Put the three pints of milk into a skillet, (with a bunch of peach leaves or a few peach-kernels,) and let it come to a boil.
4. Then while it is still boiling, stir in by degrees the rice batter, taking care not to have it lumpy; add sugar, mace, and rose brandy to your taste; or you may flavour it with the juice of a large lemon.
5. When it has boiled sufficiently, and is quite thick, strain it, and put it into a mould to congeal.
6. Make a rich boiled custard, (flavoured in the same manner,) and send it to table in a pitcher, to eat with the flummery.
7. Both should be cold.
8. If you mould it in tea-cups, turn it out on a deep dish, and pour the custard round it.
