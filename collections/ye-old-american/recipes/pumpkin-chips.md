## Ingredients

- pumpkin
- loaf-sugar
- fine ripe lemons
- lemon-juice
- brandy
- white paper

## Directions

1. Take a fine pumpkin of a rich deep colour, pare off the outer rind
2. Remove the seeds
3. Slice the best part and cut it into chips of equal size, and as thin as you can do them, in long narrow pieces, two inches in breadth, and four in length
4. Prepare the pumpkin the day before; and having weighed the chips, allow to each pound of them a pound of the best loaf-sugar
5. Have several dozen of fine ripe lemons, sufficient to furnish a jill of lemon-juice to each pound of pumpkin
6. Roll the lemons under your hand on a table to make them yield as much juice as possible
7. Pare off the yellow rind and put it away for some other purpose
8. Cut the lemons, squeeze out all the juice into a pitcher
9. Lay the pumpkin chips in a large pan or tureen, strewing the sugar among them
10. Measure the lemon-juice in a wine-glass (two common wine-glasses making one jill), pour it over the pumpkin and sugar
11. Cover the vessel, and let it stand all night
12. Next day transfer the pumpkin, sugar, and lemon-juice to a preserving kettle, and boil it slowly for an hour or more, or till the pumpkin becomes all through tender, crisp, and transparent
13. Skim it thoroughly
14. Some very small pieces of the lemon-paring may be boiled with it
15. Take up the pumpkin chips in a perforated skimmer that the syrup may drain through the holes back into the kettle
16. Spread the chips to cool on large dishes, and pass the syrup through a flannel bag that has been first dipped in hot water
17. When the chips are cold, put them into glass jars or tumblers, pour in the syrup, and lay on the top white paper dipped in brandy
18. Tie up the jars with leather, or with covers of thick white paper
19. If when cold the chips are not perfectly clear, crisp, and tender, give them another boil in the syrup before you put them up
