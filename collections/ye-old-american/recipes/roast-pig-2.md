## Ingredients

- young pig about six weeks old
- dressing for fowls
- half an onion
- baking soda
- salt
- butter
- hot salted water
- gravy
- flour
- water
- pepper
- wine
- parsley or celery tops
- celery

## Directions

1. Prepare dressing as for DRESSING FOR FOWLS, adding half an onion chopped fine
2. Take a young pig about six weeks old and wash it thoroughly inside and outside
3. In another water put a teaspoonful of baking soda and rinse out the inside again
4. Wipe it dry with a fresh towel
5. Salt the inside and stuff it with the prepared dressing, making it full and plump
6. Sew it up and place it in a kneeling posture in the dripping-pan, tying the legs in proper position
7. Pour a little hot salted water into the dripping-pan
8. Baste with butter and water a few times as the pig warms, afterwards with gravy from the dripping-pan
9. When it begins to smoke all over rub it often with a rag dipped in melted butter
10. Roast for from two to three hours
11. Make the gravy by skimming off most of the grease
12. Stir into the grease remaining in the pan a good tablespoonful of flour
13. Turn in water to make it the right consistency
14. Season with pepper and let all boil up once
15. Strain and add half a glass of wine if desired
16. Turn it into a gravy boat
17. Place the pig upon a large, hot platter surrounded with parsley or celery tops
18. Place a green wreath around the neck and a sprig of celery in its mouth
19. In carving, cut off its head first, split down the back, take off its hams and shoulders, and separate the ribs
