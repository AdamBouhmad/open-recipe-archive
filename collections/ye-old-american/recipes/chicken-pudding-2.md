## Ingredients

- two young chickens
- water
- salt
- pepper
- butter
- eight eggs
- one quart of milk
- three tablespoonfuls of melted butter
- a teaspoonful of salt
- two large teaspoonfuls of baking powder
- sifted flour
- a tablespoonful of flour
- a third of a cup of melted butter

## Directions

1. Cut up two young chickens into good-sized pieces; put them in a saucepan with just enough water to cover them well
2. When boiled quite tender, season with salt and pepper; let them simmer ten or fifteen minutes longer
3. Take the chicken from the broth and remove all the large bones
4. Place the meat in a well-buttered pudding dish, season again, if necessary, adding a few bits of butter
5. Beat eight eggs light and mix with one quart of milk, three tablespoonfuls of melted butter, a teaspoonful of salt and two large teaspoonfuls of baking powder, added to enough sifted flour to make a batter like griddle-cakes
6. Pour the batter over the chicken
7. Bake one hour in a moderate oven
8. Make a gravy of the broth that remained from the cooking of the chicken, adding a tablespoonful of flour stirred into a third of a cup of melted butter
9. Let it boil up, putting in more water if necessary
10. Serve hot in a gravy boat with the pudding
