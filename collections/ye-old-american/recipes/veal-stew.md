## Ingredients

- Two or three pounds of veal
- Two quarts of water
- Pepper and salt to taste
- A small piece of butter
- Twelve small Irish potatoes
- Two eggs
- Two spoonfuls of cream or milk
- A little salt
- Flour

## Directions

1. Cut up veal into pieces three inches long and one thick
2. Wash it, put it into your stewpan with two quarts of water
3. Let it boil, skim it well, and when all the scum is removed, add pepper and salt to your taste, and a small piece of butter
4. Pare and cut in halves twelve small Irish potatoes, put them into the stewpan
5. When it boils, have ready a batter made with two eggs, two spoonfuls of cream or milk, a little salt, and flour enough to make it a little thicker than for pancakes
6. Drop this into the stew, a spoonful at a time, while it is boiling
7. When all is in, cover the pan closely so that no steam can escape
8. Let it boil twenty minutes and serve in a deep dish
