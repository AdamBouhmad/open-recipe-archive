## Ingredients

- two and a half to three pounds of the thick ends of a loin of pork
- very little fat
- a handful of parsley
- an onion
- pepper
- salt
- nutmeg
- a cupful of stock or water
- a spoonful or two of catsup
- good plain paste

## Directions

1. Make a good plain paste.
2. Cut pork into very thin slices three inches long by two inches wide.
3. Put a layer at the bottom of a pie-dish.
4. Wash and chop finely parsley and onion.
5. Sprinkle a small portion of these over the pork, and a little pepper and salt.
6. Add another layer of pork, and over that some more of the seasoning, only be sparing of the nutmeg.
7. Continue this till the dish is full.
8. Pour into the dish a cupful of stock or water, and a spoonful or two of catsup.
9. Put a little paste around the edge of the dish.
10. Put on the cover and place the pie in a rather hot oven.
11. When the paste has risen and begins to take color, place the pie at the bottom of the oven, with some paper over it, as it will require to be baked at least two hours.
