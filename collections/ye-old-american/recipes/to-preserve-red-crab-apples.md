## Ingredients

- red or Siberian crab apples, quite ripe with black seeds
- water
- loaf-sugar, a pound and a half for each pound of crab apples
- cochineal powder
- white brandy
- lemon-juice and lemon peel, one large lemon or two smaller ones to two pounds of crab apples (optional)

## Directions

1. Wash and wipe the crab apples, and put them into a kettle with sufficient water to cover them.
2. Simmer them very slowly till you find that the skin will come off easily.
3. Take them out and peel and core them; extract the cores carefully with a small knife, so as not to break the apples.
4. Weigh them, and to every pound of crab apples allow a pound and a half of loaf-sugar and a half pint of water.
5. Put the sugar and water into a preserving kettle, and when they are melted together, set it over the fire and let it boil.
6. After skimming it once, put in the crab apples, adding a little cochineal powder rubbed with a knife into a very small quantity of white brandy till it has dissolved.
7. Cover them and let them boil till clear and tender, skimming the syrup when necessary.
8. Spread them out on dishes, and when they are cold, put them into glass jars and pour the syrup over them.
9. If using lemon-juice and peel, add them during the boiling and put a smaller quantity of water to the sugar.
10. If the syrup becomes dry or candied after keeping awhile, give it another boil with the crab apples in it, adding a tea-cup full of water to about three or four pounds of the sweetmeat.
