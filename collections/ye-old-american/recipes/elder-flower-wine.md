## Ingredients

- elder flowers
- water
- white sugar
- lemon juice
- yeast
- eggs
- isinglass
- raisins

## Directions

1. Take the flowers or blossoms of the elder tree, and strip them from the stalks.
2. To every quart of flowers allow one gallon of water, and three pounds of white sugar.
3. Boil and skim the sugar and water, and then pour it hot on the flowers.
4. When cool, mix in with it some lemon juice and some yeast; allowing to six gallons of the liquor the juice of six lemons, and four or five table-spoonfuls of good yeast stirred in very hard.
5. Let it ferment for three days in a tub covered with a double blanket.
6. Then strain the wine through a sieve, and add six whites of eggs beaten to a stiff froth, or an ounce of melted isinglass, and put it into a cask, in the bottom of which you have laid four or five pounds of the best raisins, stoned.
7. Stop the cask closely, and in six months the wine will be fit to bottle.
