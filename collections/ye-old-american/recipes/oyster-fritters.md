## Ingredients

- finest and largest oysters
- six eggs
- six table-spoonfuls of fine sifted flour
- pint and a half of rich milk
- grated nutmeg
- butter or lard

## Directions

1. Have ready some of the finest and largest oysters; drain them from the liquor and wipe them dry.
2. Beat six eggs very light, and stir into them gradually six table-spoonfuls of fine sifted flour.
3. Add by degrees a pint and a half of rich milk and some grated nutmeg, and beat it to a smooth batter.
4. Make your frying-pan very hot, and put into it a piece of butter or lard.
5. When it has melted and begins to froth, put in a small ladle-full of the batter, drop an oyster in the middle of it, and fry it of a light brown.
6. Send them to table hot.
