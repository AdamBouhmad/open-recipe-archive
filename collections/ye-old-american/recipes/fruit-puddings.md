## Ingredients

- green gooseberries or apples or rhubarb
- paste
- sugar
- cloth

## Directions

1. Butter a basin and line with a paste, rolling it round to the thickness of half an inch
2. Get a pint of gooseberries and three ounces of sugar
3. Take half the fruit and lay it at the bottom of your basin
4. Add half your sugar, then put the remainder of the gooseberries in and the remainder of the sugar
5. Draw your paste to the centre, join the edges well together, put the cloth over the whole, tying it at the bottom
6. Boil in plenty of water for an hour
7. Take out of the saucepan, untie the cloth, turn out on a dish, or let it remain in the basin and serve with sugar over
8. Ripe cherries, currants, raspberries, greengages, plums and such like fruit, will not require so much sugar, or so long boiling
9. These puddings are also very good steamed
