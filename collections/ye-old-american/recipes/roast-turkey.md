## Ingredients

- turkey
- soda
- bread-crumbs
- butter
- pepper
- salt
- thyme or sweet marjoram
- beaten yolks of two eggs
- chopped sausage
- oysters
- flour
- water
- gravy
- giblets
- browned flour
- cold water
- cranberry sauce
- fried oysters

## Directions

1. After drawing the turkey, rinse out with several waters, and in next to the last mix a teaspoonful of soda.
2. Fill the body with this water, shake well, empty it out, and rinse with fair water.
3. Prepare a dressing of bread-crumbs, mixed with butter, pepper, salt, thyme or sweet marjoram. You may, if you like, add the beaten yolks of two eggs. A little chopped sausage is esteemed an improvement when well incorporated with the other ingredients. Or, mince a dozen oysters and stir into the dressing.
4. Stuff the craw with this, and tie a string tightly about the neck, to prevent the escape of the stuffing.
5. Fill the body of the turkey, and sew it up with strong thread.
6. Dredge it with flour before roasting.
7. In roasting, if your fire is brisk, allow about ten minutes to a pound.
8. Baste often; at first with butter and water, afterward with the gravy in the dripping-pan.
9. If you lay the turkey in the pan, put in with it a teacup of hot water.
10. Many roast always upon a grating placed on the top of the pan. In that case the boiling water steams the underpart of the fowl, and prevents the skin from drying too fast, or cracking.
11. Roast to a fine brown, and if it threaten to darken too rapidly, lay a sheet of white paper over it until the lower part is also done.
12. Stew the chopped giblets in just enough water to cover them.
13. When the turkey is lifted from the pan, add these, with the water in which they were boiled, to the drippings.
14. Thicken with a spoonful of browned flour, wet with cold water to prevent lumping.
15. Boil up once, and pour into the gravy-boat.
16. If the turkey is fat, skim the drippings well before putting in the giblets.
17. Serve with cranberry sauce. Some lay fried oysters in the dish around the turkey.
