## Ingredients

- one quart of sifted flour
- two teaspoonfuls of baking powder
- a teaspoonful of salt
- one teacupful of butter
- one teacupful of lard, hard and cold
- ice-water, say half a cupful
- a beaten white of egg

## Directions

1. Into one quart of sifted flour mix two teaspoonfuls of baking powder and a teaspoonful of salt; then sift again.
2. Measure out one teacupful of butter and one of lard, hard and cold.
3. Take the lard and rub into the flour until a very fine smooth paste.
4. Put in just enough ice-water, say half a cupful, containing a beaten white of egg, to mix a very stiff dough.
5. Roll it out into a thin sheet, spread with one-fourth of the butter, sprinkle over with a little flour, then roll up closely in a long roll, like a scroll.
6. Double the ends towards the centre, flatten and re-roll, then spread again with another quarter of the butter.
7. Repeat this operation until the butter is used up.
8. Put it on an earthen dish, cover it with a cloth and set it in a cold place, in the ice box in summer.
9. Let it remain until cold; an hour or more before making out the crust.
