## Ingredients

- a teacupful of lard
- a pint of milk
- flour
- a small cupful of yeast
- three cupfuls of sugar
- the whipped whites of six eggs
- half a teaspoonful of soda
- a spoonful of milk
- one teaspoonful of salt
- a teaspoonful of ground cinnamon
- half of a nutmeg grated
- a large Sultana raisin, seeded
- fine powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Warm a teacupful of lard in a pint of milk
2. when nearly cool add enough flour to make a thick batter and add a small cupful of yeast
3. beat it well and set it to rise
4. when light work in gradually and carefully three cupfuls of sugar, the whipped whites of six eggs, half a teaspoonful of soda dissolved in a spoonful of milk, one teaspoonful of salt, a teaspoonful of ground cinnamon and half of a nutmeg grated
5. work in gradually enough flour to make it stiff enough to roll out
6. let it rise again and when very light roll it out in a sheet an inch thick
7. cut into rounds
8. put into the centre of each round a large Sultana raisin, seeded, and mold into perfectly round balls
9. flatten a little
10. let them stand a few minutes before boiling them
11. have plenty of lard in the pot and when it boils drop in the cakes
12. when they are a light brown take them out with a perforated skimmer
13. drain on soft white paper and roll, while warm, in fine powdered sugar
