## Ingredients

- One month-old pig
- bread-crumbs
- chopped onion
- powdered sage
- melted butter
- pepper
- grated nutmeg
- salt
- well-beaten eggs
- flour
- hot water

## Directions

1. As soon as it is really cold, make ready a large boiler of scalding water.
2. Lay the pig in cold water for fifteen minutes; then, holding it by the hind-leg, plunge it into the boiling water, and shake it about violently until you can pull the hair off by the handful.
3. Take it out, wipe it dry, and with a crash cloth or whisk broom rub the hair off, brushing from the tail to the head, until the skin is perfectly clean.
4. Cut it open, take out the entrails, and wash very thoroughly with cold water, then with soda and water, to remove any unpleasant odor; next with salt and water.
5. Rinse with fair water and wipe inside.
6. Then wrap in a wet cloth, and keep this saturated with cold water until you are ready to stuff it.
7. For stuffing, take a cupful of bread-crumbs, half a chopped onion, two teaspoonfuls powdered sage, three tablespoonfuls melted butter, a saltspoonful of pepper, half a grated nutmeg, half a teaspoonful of salt, two well-beaten eggs.
8. Mix all these ingredients, except the egg, together, incorporating them well; beat in the eggs, and stuff the pig into his natural size and shape.
9. Sew him up, and bend his fore-feet backward, his hind-feet forward, under and close to the body, and skewering them into the proper position.
10. Dry it well, and dredge with flour.
11. Put it to roast with a little hot water, slightly salted, in the dripping-pan.
12. Baste with butter and water three times, as the pig gradually warms, afterward with the dripping.
13. When it begins to smoke or steam, rub it over every five minutes or so, with a cloth dipped in melted butter.
14. A month-old pig will require about an hour and three-quarters or two hours—sometimes longer—to roast, if the fire be brisk and steady.
