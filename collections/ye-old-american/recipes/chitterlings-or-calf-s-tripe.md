## Ingredients

- chitterlings
- pepper
- salt
- water
- eight or ten white onions
- butter
- cream or rich milk
- nutmeg
- flour
- buttered toast
- vinegar

## Directions

1. See that the chitterlings are very nice and white. Wash them, cut them into pieces, and put them into a stew-pan with pepper and salt to your taste, and about two quarts of water.
2. Boil them two hours or more.
3. In the mean time, peel eight or ten white onions, and throw them whole into a sauce-pan with plenty of water.
4. Boil them slowly till quite soft; then drain them in a cullender, and mash them.
5. Wipe out your sauce-pan, and put in the mashed onions with a piece of butter, two table-spoonfuls of cream or rich milk, some nutmeg, and a very little salt.
6. Sprinkle in a little flour, set the pan on hot coals (keeping it well covered) and give it one boil up.
7. When the chitterlings are quite tender all through, take them up and drain them.
8. Place in the bottom of a dish a slice or two of buttered toast with all the crust cut off.
9. Lay the chitterlings on the toast, and send them to table with the stewed onions in a sauce-boat.
10. When you take the chitterlings on your plate season them with pepper and vinegar.
