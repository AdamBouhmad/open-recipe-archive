## Ingredients

- half a pound of the finest flour
- a quart of rich milk
- half a pound of fresh butter
- eight eggs
- a powdered nutmeg
- a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- white sugar
- sweetened cream flavoured with wine and nutmeg, or molasses and butter, or sugar and wine

## Directions

1. Sift half a pound of the finest flour.
2. Cut up in a quart of rich milk, half a pound of fresh butter, and set it on the stove, or near the fire, till it has melted.
3. Beat eight eggs very light, and stir them gradually into the milk and butter, alternately with the flour.
4. Add a powdered nutmeg, and a tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon.
5. Mix the whole very well to a fine smooth batter, in which there must be no lumps.
6. Butter some large common tea-cups, and divide the mixture among them till they are half full or a little more.
7. Set them immediately in a quick oven, and bake them about a quarter of an hour.
8. When done, turn them out into a dish, and grate white sugar over them.
9. Serve them up hot, with a sauce of sweetened cream flavoured with wine and nutmeg; or you may eat them with molasses and butter; or with sugar and wine.
10. Send them round whole, for they will fall almost as soon as cut.
