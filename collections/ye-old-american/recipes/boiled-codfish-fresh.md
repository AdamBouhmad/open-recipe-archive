## Ingredients

- Fresh codfish
- Cold water
- Salt
- Flour
- One gill boiling water
- Milk
- Two tablespoonfuls of butter
- Two beaten eggs
- Chopped parsley
- Capers or pickled nasturtium seeds or vinegar in which celery-seeds have been steeped
- Sprigs of parsley
- Hard-boiled eggs

## Directions

1. Lay the fish in cold water, slightly salted, for half an hour before it is time to cook it
2. Wipe free of the salt and water, wrap it in a clean linen cloth
3. Dredge the cloth with flour, to prevent sticking
4. Sew up the edges in such a manner as to envelop the fish entirely
5. Put into the fish-kettle, pour on plenty of hot water, and boil briskly—fifteen minutes for each pound
6. To one gill boiling water add as much milk, and when it is scalding-hot, stir in two tablespoonfuls of butter, rolled thickly in flour
7. As this thickens, add two beaten eggs
8. Season with salt and chopped parsley
9. After one good boil, withdraw from the fire and add a dozen capers, or pickled nasturtium seeds, or a spoonful of vinegar in which celery-seeds have been steeped
10. Put the fish into a hot dish, and pour the sauce over it
11. Garnish with sprigs of parsley and circles of hard-boiled eggs, laid around the edge of the dish
