## Ingredients

- half a package of Cox's gelatine
- a teacupful of cold water
- a pint of boiling water
- whites of four eggs
- two teacupfuls of sugar
- one teaspoonful of vanilla extract, or the grated rind and the juice of a lemon
- yolks of three eggs
- half a cupful of sugar
- a pint of milk

## Directions

1. Soak half a package of Cox's gelatine in a teacupful of cold water one hour
2. Add a pint of boiling water and stir it until the gelatine is thoroughly dissolved
3. Beat the whites of four eggs to a stiff froth
4. Put two teacupfuls of sugar in the gelatine water first, then the beaten white of egg and one teaspoonful of vanilla extract, or the grated rind and the juice of a lemon
5. Whip it some time until it is all quite stiff and cold
6. Dip some teacups or wine-glasses in cold water and fill them
7. Set in a cold place
8. Make a boiled custard of the yolks of three of the eggs, with half a cupful of sugar and a pint of milk
9. Flavor with vanilla extract
10. After the meringue in the cups has stood four or five hours, turn them out of the molds
11. Place them in a glass dish and pour the custard around the base
