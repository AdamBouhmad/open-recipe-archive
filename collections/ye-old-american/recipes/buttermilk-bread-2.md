## Ingredients

- 1 pint buttermilk heated to scalding
- flour
- half a gill of yeast
- tablespoonful of white sugar
- tablespoonful of soda, dissolved in hot water
- salt
- two tablespoonfuls melted butter

## Directions

1. Stir in flour while buttermilk is hot to make a tolerably thick batter
2. Add half a gill of yeast and let it rise five or six hours
3. If making over night, put in a tablespoonful of white sugar instead of yeast
4. In the morning, stir into the sponge a tablespoonful of soda dissolved in hot water, a little salt, and two tablespoonfuls melted butter
5. Work in just flour enough to enable you to handle the dough comfortably
6. Knead well, make into loaves, and let it rise until light
