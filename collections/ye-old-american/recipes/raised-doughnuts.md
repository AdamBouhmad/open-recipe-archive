## Ingredients

- a pint of warm water or milk
- a large half cupful of yeast
- half a cupful of butter or sweet lard
- a coffeecupful of sugar
- a teaspoonful of salt
- one small teaspoonful of soda
- one tablespoonful of cinnamon
- a little grated nutmeg
- two well-beaten eggs
- sifted flour
- powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Make a sponge as for bread, using a pint of warm water or milk, and a large half cupful of yeast
2. when the sponge is very light, add half a cupful of butter or sweet lard, a coffeecupful of sugar, a teaspoonful of salt and one small teaspoonful of soda dissolved in a little water, one tablespoonful of cinnamon, a little grated nutmeg
3. stir in now two well-beaten eggs
4. add sifted flour until it is the consistency of biscuit dough
5. knead it well, cover and let rise
6. roll the dough out into a sheet half an inch thick
7. cut out with a very small biscuit-cutter, or in strips half an inch wide and three inches long
8. place them on greased tins, cover them well and let them rise before frying them
9. Drop them in very hot lard
10. Sift powdered sugar over them as fast as they are fried, while warm
