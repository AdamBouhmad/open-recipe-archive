## Ingredients

- a pound and a half of raisins
- half a pound of currants
- two pounds of flour
- a pound of brown sugar
- a pound of fresh butter
- two table-spoonfuls of the best ginger
- one table-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- one table-spoonful of powdered cloves
- six eggs
- a quart of molasses
- a tea-spoonful of pearl-ash
- lukewarm water

## Directions

1. Stone a pound and a half of raisins, and cut them in two
2. Wash and dry half a pound of currants
3. Sift into a pan two pounds of flour
4. Put into another pan a pound of brown sugar and cut up in it a pound of fresh butter
5. Stir the butter and sugar to a cream
6. Add to it two table-spoonfuls of the best ginger, one table-spoonful of powdered cinnamon, and one of powdered cloves
7. Beat six eggs very light
8. Add them gradually to the butter and sugar, in turn with the flour and a quart of molasses
9. Stir in a tea-spoonful of pearl-ash dissolved in lukewarm water
10. Add by degrees the fruit, which must be well dredged with flour
11. Stir all very hard
12. Put the mixture into a buttered pan
13. Bake it in a moderate oven
