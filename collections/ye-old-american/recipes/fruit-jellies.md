## Ingredients

- fruit
- a pound of loaf sugar per pint of juice

## Directions

1. Take a stone jar and put in the fruit, place this in a kettle of tepid water and set on the fire
2. Let it boil, closely covered, until the fruit is broken to pieces
3. Strain, pressing the bag, a stout, coarse one, hard, putting in a few handfuls each time, and between each squeezing turning it inside out to scald off the pulp and skins
4. To each pint of juice allow a pound of loaf sugar
5. Set the juice on alone to boil
6. While it is boiling, put the sugar into shallow dishes or pans, and heat it in the oven, watching and stirring it to prevent burning
7. Boil the juice just twenty minutes from the time it begins fairly to boil
8. Throw the sugar into the boiling juice, stirring rapidly all the time
9. Withdraw the spoon when all is thoroughly dissolved
10. Let the jelly come to a boil to make all certain
11. Withdraw the kettle instantly from the fire
12. Roll your glasses and cups in hot water, and fill with the scalding liquid
13. The jelly will form within an hour; when cold, close and tie up as you do preserves
