## Ingredients

- chicken
- soda
- stuffing
- hot water
- butter
- salt
- pepper
- flour
- necks
- gizzards
- livers
- hearts
- cranberry sauce or tart sauce

## Directions

1. Pick and draw the chicken and wash out well in two or three waters, adding a little soda to the last but one to sweeten it if there is doubt as to its being fresh
2. Dry it well with a clean cloth and fill the crop and body with a stuffing
3. Lay it in a dripping-pan and put a pint of hot water and a piece of butter in the dripping-pan
4. Add to it a small tablespoonful of salt and a small teaspoonful of pepper
5. Baste frequently and let it roast quickly without scorching
6. When nearly done put a piece of butter the size of a large egg to the water in the pan
7. When it melts baste with it, dredge a little flour over, baste again, and let it finish
8. Half an hour will roast a full grown chicken if the fire is right
9. Stew the necks, gizzards, livers and hearts in a very little water
10. Strain it and mix it hot with the gravy that has dripped from the fowl, which must be first skimmed
11. Thicken it with a little browned flour and add the livers, hearts and gizzards chopped small
12. Send the fowl to the table with the gravy in a boat with cranberry sauce or any tart sauce
