## Ingredients

- a pound of fresh rose leaves
- a quart of lukewarm water
- half a pound of loaf-sugar
- half a pint of white brandy
- an ounce of broken cinnamon
- an ounce of coriander seeds

## Directions

1. Put a pound of fresh rose leaves into a tureen, with a quart of lukewarm water.
2. Cover the vessel, and let them infuse for twenty-four hours.
3. Then squeeze them through a linen bag till all the liquid is pressed out.
4. Put a fresh pound of rose leaves into the tureen, pour the liquid back into it, and let it infuse again for two days.
5. You may repeat this till you obtain a very strong infusion.
6. Then to a pint of the infusion add half a pound of loaf-sugar, half a pint of white brandy, an ounce of broken cinnamon, and an ounce of coriander seeds.
7. Put it into a glass jar, cover it well, and let it stand for two weeks.
8. Then filter it through a fine muslin or a blotting paper (which must be white) pinned on the bottom of a sieve; and bottle it for use.
