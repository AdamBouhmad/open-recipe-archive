## Ingredients

- coffeecupful of fine white sugar
- whites of six eggs
- sifted sugar
- whipped cream
- liquor or vanilla
- pounded sugar

## Directions

1. Whisk the whites of the eggs to a stiff froth and with a wooden spoon stir in quickly the pounded sugar
2. Have some boards put in the oven thick enough to prevent the bottom of the meringues from acquiring too much color
3. Cut some strips of paper about two inches wide
4. Place this paper on the board and drop a tablespoonful at a time of the mixture on the paper, taking care to let all the meringues be the same size
5. In dropping it from the spoon, give the mixture the form of an egg and keep the meringues about two inches apart from each other on the paper
6. Strew over them some sifted sugar and bake in a moderate oven for half an hour
7. As soon as they begin to color, remove them from the oven
8. Take each slip of paper by the two ends and turn it gently on the table and with a small spoon take out the soft part of each meringue
9. Spread some clean paper on the board, turn the meringues upside down and put them into the oven to harden and brown on the other side
10. When required for table, fill them with whipped cream, flavored with liquor or vanilla and sweeten with pounded sugar
11. Join two of the meringues together and pile them high in the dish
