## Ingredients

- flour
- yeast (half a teacupful to two quarts of flour)
- milk or water
- butter (size of an egg, if using water)
- salt (one teaspoonful)

## Directions

1. Sift the flour into a large bread-pan or bowl; make a hole in the middle of it, and pour in the yeast
2. Stir the yeast lightly, then pour in your wetting, either milk or water, as you choose, which use warm in winter and cold in summer; if you use water as wetting, dissolve in it a bit of butter of the size of an egg; if you use milk, no butter is necessary
3. Stir in the wetting very lightly, but do not mix all the flour into it
4. Cover the pan with a thick blanket or towel, and set it in winter in a warm place to rise (this is called putting the bread in sponge)
5. In the morning add a teaspoonful of salt and mix all the flour in the pan with the sponge, kneading it well
6. Let it stand two hours or more until it has risen quite light
7. Remove the dough to the molding-board and mold it for a long time, cutting it in pieces and molding them together again and again, until the dough is elastic under the pressure of your hand, using as little flour as possible
8. Make it into loaves, put the loaves into baking-tins; the loaves should come half way up the pan
9. Allow them to rise until the bulk is doubled
10. When the loaves are ready to put into the oven, prick them three or four times with a fork
11. Bake in a quick oven from forty-five to sixty minutes
