## Ingredients

- a large quantity of tomatas
- salt
- powdered ginger
- nutmeg
- mace
- cloves
- cayenne

## Directions

1. Take a large quantity of tomatas, and scald and peel them
2. Press them through a fine hair-sieve
3. Boil the pulp in either a porcelain or a bell-metal preserving-kettle as tin or iron will blacken it
4. Cover the kettle closely, and keep it at a slow boil during four hours
5. Measure the pulp of the tomatas, and to every two quarts allow a tea-spoonful of salt
6. Boil it an hour after the salt is in, stirring it frequently
7. Have ready, in equal proportions, a mixture of powdered ginger, nutmeg, mace, and cloves; and to every two quarts of the liquid, allow a large tea-spoonful of these mixed spices, adding a small tea-spoonful of cayenne
8. Stir in this seasoning
9. Boil the catchup half an hour longer
10. Strain it carefully into a large pitcher, avoiding the grounds or sediment of the spices
11. While hot, pour it through a flannel into clean bottles
12. Cork them tightly, and seal the corks
13. Keep it in a dry, cool place
