## Ingredients

- a quarter of a pound of ground rice
- a pint of cold milk
- one pint of milk
- a quarter of a pound of butter
- a quarter of a pound of white sugar
- eight eggs
- a grated nutmeg
- a small tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
- the juice and the grated peel of a lemon, or a small tea-spoonful of essence of lemon
- slips of citron
- powdered sugar

## Directions

1. Mix a quarter of a pound of ground rice with a pint of cold milk, till it is a smooth batter and free from lumps
2. Boil one pint of milk; and when it has boiled, stir in gradually the rice batter, alternately with a quarter of a pound of butter
3. Keep it over the fire, stirring all the time, till the whole is well mixed, and has boiled hard
4. Then take it off, add a quarter of a pound of white sugar; stir it well, and set it away to cool
5. Beat eight eggs very light, and stir them into the mixture when it is quite cold
6. Then strain it through a sieve, (this will make it more light and delicate,) add a grated nutmeg, and a small tea-spoonful of powdered cinnamon
7. Stir in the juice and the grated peel of a lemon, or a small tea-spoonful of essence of lemon
8. Put it into a deep dish or dishes, and bake it an hour
9. As soon as it comes out of the oven, lay slips of citron over the top; and when cold, strew powdered sugar on it
