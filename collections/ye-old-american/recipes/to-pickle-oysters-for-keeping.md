## Ingredients

- finest and largest oysters
- salt
- fresh water
- oyster liquor
- best vinegar
- whole cloves
- whole black pepper
- whole mace

## Directions

1. After oysters are opened, separate them from their liquor and put them into a bucket or large pan
2. Pour boiling water upon them to take out the slime
3. Stir them about in the water, then take them out and rinse them well in cold water
4. Put them into a large kettle with fresh water, barely enough to cover them, mixing with it a table-spoonful of salt to every hundred oysters
5. Give them a boil up, just sufficient to plump them
6. Take them out, spread them on large dishes or on a clean table, and cover them with a cloth
7. Take the liquor of the oysters and with every pint of it mix a quart of the best vinegar, a table-spoonful of salt, a table-spoonful of whole cloves, the same of whole black pepper, and a tea-spoonful of whole mace
8. Put the liquid over the fire in a kettle, and when it boils throw in the oysters and let them remain in it five minutes
9. Take the whole off the fire, stir it up well, and let it stand to get quite cold
10. If you have a large quantity, put it into a keg which must first be well scalded, a new keg is best, and fill it as full as it can hold
11. Do not put a weight on the oysters to keep them down in the liquor, as it will crush them to pieces if the keg should be moved or conveyed to a distance
12. If you do not have enough to fill a keg, put them into stone jars when they are perfectly cold and cover them securely
