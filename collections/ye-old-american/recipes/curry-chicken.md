## Ingredients

- chicken weighing from a pound and a half to two pounds
- water
- salt
- two small onions
- butter as large as an egg
- two teaspoonfuls of Curry Powder
- tablespoonful of sifted flour
- beaten yolk of egg
- hot boiled rice

## Directions

1. Cut up the chicken as for fricassee, wash it well, and put it into a stewpan with sufficient water to cover it
2. Boil it, closely covered, until tender
3. Add a large teaspoonful of salt, and cook a few minutes longer
4. Remove from the fire, take out the chicken, pour the liquor into a bowl, and set it one side
5. Cut up into the stewpan two small onions, and fry them with a piece of butter as large as an egg
6. As soon as the onions are brown, skim them out and put in the chicken
7. Fry for three or four minutes
8. Sprinkle over two teaspoonfuls of Curry Powder
9. Pour over the liquor in which the chicken was stewed, stir all well together, and stew for five minutes longer
10. Stir into this a tablespoonful of sifted flour made thin with a little water
11. Stir in a beaten yolk of egg
12. Serve with hot boiled rice laid around on the edge of a platter, and the chicken curry in the centre
