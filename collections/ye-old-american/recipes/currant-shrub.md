## Ingredients

- currants
- loaf-sugar
- white brandy

## Directions

1. Pick the currants from the stalks, and squeeze them through a linen bag.
2. To each quart of juice allow a pound of loaf-sugar.
3. Put the sugar and juice into a preserving kettle, and let it melt before it goes on the fire.
4. Boil it ten minutes, skimming it well.
5. When cold, add a jill of the best white brandy to each quart of the juice.
6. Bottle it, and set it away for use; sealing the corks.
