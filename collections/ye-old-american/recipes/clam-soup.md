## Ingredients

- fifty clams
- three quarts of water
- knuckle of veal
- large bunch of sweet herbs
- beaten nutmeg
- tea-spoonful of mace
- table-spoonful of whole pepper
- quarter of a pound of butter
- flour

## Directions

1. Put clams into a pot of boiling water to make them open easily
2. Take clams from the shells, carefully saving the liquor
3. To the liquor of fifty opened clams, allow three quarts of water
4. Mix the water with the liquor of the clams and put it into a large pot with a knuckle of veal, the bone of which should be chopped in four places
5. Let it simmer slowly three hours
6. Put in a large bunch of sweet herbs, a beaten nutmeg, a tea-spoonful of mace, and a table-spoonful of whole pepper, but no salt
7. Stew it slowly an hour longer, and then strain it
8. Return the liquor to the pot and add a quarter of a pound of butter divided into four and each bit rolled in flour
9. Put in the clams, having cut them in pieces, and let it boil fifteen minutes
10. Send it to table with toasted bread cut into dice
