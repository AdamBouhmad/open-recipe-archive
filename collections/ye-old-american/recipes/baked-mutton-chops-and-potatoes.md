## Ingredients

- potatoes
- salt
- pepper
- cold water
- loin chops
- hot water

## Directions

1. Wash and peel some good potatoes and cut them into slices the thickness of a penny-piece
2. Allow two, or even three, potatoes for each person
3. After the potatoes are sliced, wash them in two or three waters to thoroughly cleanse them
4. Arrange them neatly in layers in a brown stone dish proper for baking purposes
5. Sprinkle a little salt and pepper between each layer, and add a sufficient quantity of cold water to prevent their burning
6. Place the dish in a very hot oven with oil on the top shelf so as to brown the potatoes in a few minutes
7. Have ready some nice loin chops, say one for each person
8. Trim off most of the fat and make them into a neat round shape by putting a small skewer through each
9. When the potatoes are nicely browned, remove the dish from the oven, and place the chops on the top
10. Add a little more salt and pepper, and water if required, and return the dish to a cooler part of the oven
11. Allow it to remain until sufficiently cooked, which will be in about three-quarters of an hour
12. When the upper sides of the chops are a nice crisp brown, turn them over so as to brown the other side also
13. If the potatoes appear to be getting too dry, pour in a little more hot water at one corner of the dish
