## Ingredients

- a stale six cent loaf
- a quart of rich milk
- half a pound of currants, picked, washed, and dried
- half a pound of raisins, stoned and cut in half
- a quarter of a pound of citron cut in large slips
- two nutmegs beaten to a powder
- a table-spoonful of mace and cinnamon powdered and mixed together
- half a pound of sugar
- half a pound of butter
- a glass of brandy
- a glass of white wine
- eight eggs

## Directions

1. Grate all the crumb of a stale six cent loaf
2. Boil a quart of rich milk, and pour it boiling hot over the grated bread
3. Cover it, and let it steep for an hour
4. Set it out to cool
5. Prepare half a pound of currants, picked, washed, and dried
6. Prepare half a pound of raisins, stoned and cut in half
7. Prepare a quarter of a pound of citron cut in large slips
8. Beat two nutmegs to a powder
9. Powder and mix a table-spoonful of mace and cinnamon together
10. Crush with a rolling-pin half a pound of sugar
11. Cut up half a pound of butter
12. When the bread and milk is uncovered to cool, mix with it the butter, sugar, spice and citron
13. Add a glass of brandy, and a glass of white wine
14. Beat eight eggs very light
15. When the milk is quite cold, stir them gradually into the mixture
16. Add, by degrees, the raisins and currants, which must be previously dredged with flour
17. Stir the whole very hard
18. Put it into a buttered dish, and bake it two hours
19. Send it to table warm, and eat it with wine sauce, or with wine and sugar only
