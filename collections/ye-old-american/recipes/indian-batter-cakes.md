## Ingredients

- a quart of sifted Indian meal
- a handful of wheat flour
- a quart of milk
- a small tea-spoonful of salt
- two large table-spoonfuls of the best fresh yeast
- three eggs
- molasses

## Directions

1. Mix together a quart of sifted Indian meal, (the yellow meal is best for all purposes,) and a handful of wheat flour.
2. Warm a quart of milk, and stir into it a small tea-spoonful of salt, and two large table-spoonfuls of the best fresh yeast.
3. Beat three eggs very light, and stir them gradually into the milk in turn with the meal.
4. Cover it, and set it to rise for three or four hours.
5. When quite light, bake it on a griddle in the manner of buckwheat cakes.
6. Butter them, cut them across, and send them to table hot, with molasses in a sauce-boat.
