## Ingredients

- 1 gallon medium-sized cucumbers
- salt
- cider vinegar
- piece of alum as large as a walnut
- 1 teacup horse-radish root cut up fine
- 1 teaspoonful mustard
- 1 teaspoonful ground cloves
- 1 teaspoonful cinnamon

## Directions

1. Put cucumbers into a jar or pail
2. Put into enough boiling water to cover them a small handful of salt
3. Turn it over them and cover closely
4. Repeat this three mornings
5. The fourth morning scald enough cider vinegar to cover them
6. Put into it a piece of alum as large as a walnut and a teacup of horse-radish root cut up fine
7. Tie up in a small muslin bag one teaspoonful of mustard, one of ground cloves, and one of cinnamon
8. Slice up the cucumbers half of an inch thick
9. Place them in glass jars and pour the scalding vinegar over them
10. Seal tight and they will keep good a year or more
