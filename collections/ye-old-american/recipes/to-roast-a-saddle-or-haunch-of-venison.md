## Ingredients

- saddle or haunch of venison
- lard or nice dripping
- sheets of paper
- packthread
- flour
- water
- claret
- butter
- salt
- currant jelly
- white paper
- cloves
- mace
- nutmeg
- cayenne
- scraps and trimmings of venison

## Directions

1. Wipe it all over with a sponge dipped in warm water
2. Then rub the skin with lard or nice dripping
3. Cover the fat with sheets of paper two double, buttered, and tied on with packthread that has been soaked to keep it from burning
4. You may cover the first sheets of paper with a coarse paste of flour and water rolled out half an inch thick, and then cover the paste with the second sheets of paper, securing the whole well with the string to prevent its falling off
5. Place the venison on the spit before a strong clear fire, such as you would have for a sirloin of beef, and let the fire be well kept up all the time
6. Put some claret and butter into the dripping-pan and baste the meat with it frequently
7. If wrapped in paste, it will not be done in less than five hours
8. Half an hour before you take it up, remove the coverings carefully, place the meat nearer to the fire, baste it with fresh butter and dredge it very lightly with flour
9. Send it to table with fringed white paper wrapped round the bone, and its own gravy well skimmed
10. Have currant jelly to eat with it
11. As venison chills immediately, the plates should be kept on heaters
12. If you wish another gravy, put a pound and a half of scraps and trimmings or inferior pieces of venison into a sauce-pan with three pints of water, a few cloves, a few blades of mace, half a nutmeg; and salt and cayenne to your taste
13. Boil it down slowly to a pint
14. Then skim off the fat, and strain the gravy into a clean sauce-pan
15. Add to it half a pint of currant jelly, half a pint of claret, and near a quarter of a pound of butter divided into bits and rolled in flour
16. Send it to table in two small tureens or sauce-boats
17. If you wish to keep venison before cooking, wash it well with milk and water, and then dry it perfectly with cloths till there is not the least damp remaining on it
18. Then mix together powdered ginger and pepper, and rub it well over every part of the meat
