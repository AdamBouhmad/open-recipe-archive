## Ingredients

- good strong stock
- white and shell of one egg per quart of stock
- a tablespoonful of water per quart of stock
- salt

## Directions

1. Take good strong stock, remove all fat from the surface
2. For each quart of the stock allow the white and shell of one egg and a tablespoonful of water, well whipped together
3. Pour this mixture into a saucepan containing the stock
4. Place it over the fire and heat the contents gradually, stirring often to prevent the egg from sticking to the bottom of the saucepan
5. Allow it to boil gently until the stock looks perfectly clear under the egg, which will rise and float upon the surface in the form of a thick white scum
6. Remove it and pour it into a folded towel laid in a colander set over an earthen bowl, allowing it to run through without moving or squeezing it
7. Season with more salt if needed, and quickly serve very hot
