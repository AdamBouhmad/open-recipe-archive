## Ingredients

- small myrtle oranges
- weak salt and water
- cold water
- green vine-leaves
- powdered alum
- water
- double-refined sugar

## Directions

1. Lay the oranges three days in weak salt and water.
2. Then lay them three days in cold water, changed at least three times a day.
3. Put a layer of green vine-leaves at the bottom of the preserving kettle and round the sides.
4. Put in a layer of oranges, sprinkling among them a very little powdered alum, allowing not more than a heaped salt-spoonful of alum to the whole kettle of oranges and vine-leaves.
5. Fill up with water and hang over the fire till they are of a fine green.
6. Boil them till they are so tender that you can pierce them through with a twig from a whisk broom.
7. When clear and crisp, take them out of the kettle and spread them on flat dishes.
8. Throw away the vine-leaves.
9. Wash out the kettle and, having weighed the oranges, allow to each pound one pound of double-refined sugar, broken small.
10. Put the sugar into the preserving-kettle and pour on half a pint of water to each pound of sugar.
11. When it is quite dissolved, hang it over the fire and boil and skim it till it is very clear and no more scum appears on the surface.
12. Put in the oranges and boil them slowly in the syrup till they slightly burst.
