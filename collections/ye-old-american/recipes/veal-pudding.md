## Ingredients

- about two pounds of lean veal
- a piece of butter the size of an egg
- a few slices of bacon
- a small sprig of thyme
- pepper
- salt
- warm water
- a good suet crust
- a piece of paste

## Directions

1. Cut about two pounds of lean veal into small collops a quarter of an inch in thickness
2. Put a piece of butter the size of an egg into a very clean frying pan to melt
3. Lay in the veal and a few slices of bacon, a small sprig of thyme and a seasoning of pepper and salt
4. Place the pan over a slow fire for about ten minutes, then add two or three spoonfuls of warm water
5. Just boil it up and then let it stand to cool
6. Line a pudding-dish with a good suet crust
7. Lay in the veal and bacon, pour the gravy over it
8. Roll out a piece of paste to form a lid, place it over, press it close with the thumb
9. Tie the basin in a pudding cloth and put it into a saucepan of boiling water, keeping continually boiling until done, or about one hour
