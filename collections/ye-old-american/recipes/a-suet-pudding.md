## Ingredients

- beef suet
- bread-crumbs
- a quart of milk
- eight eggs
- flour
- powdered nutmeg and cinnamon mixed
- a glass of mixed wine and brandy

## Directions

1. Mince very finely as much beef suet as will make two large table-spoonfuls.
2. Grate two handfuls of bread-crumbs.
3. Boil a quart of milk and pour it hot on the bread.
4. Cover it, and set it aside to steep for half an hour; then put it to cool.
5. Beat eight eggs very light.
6. Stir the suet, and six table-spoonfuls of flour alternately into the bread and milk.
7. Add, by degrees, the eggs.
8. Stir in a table-spoonful of powdered nutmeg and cinnamon mixed, and a glass of mixed wine and brandy.
9. Pour it into a square cloth dipped in hot water, and floured; tie it firmly.
10. Put it into a pot of boiling water, and boil it two hours.
11. Do not take it up till immediately before it is wanted, and send it to table hot.
