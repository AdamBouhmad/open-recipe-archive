## Ingredients

- a chicken
- a very little salt
- three quarts of water
- grated lemon-peel
- nutmeg

## Directions

1. Cut up a chicken, season it with a very little salt, and put it into three quarts of water.
2. Let it simmer slowly till the flesh drops to pieces.
3. To make chicken panada or gruel, take out the white meat as soon as it is tender, mince it fine, and then pound it in a mortar, adding as you pound it, sufficient of the chicken water to moisten the paste.
4. Thin it with water till it becomes liquid enough to drink.
5. Put it into a sauce-pan and boil it gently a few minutes.
6. You may add to it a little grated lemon-peel and nutmeg.
