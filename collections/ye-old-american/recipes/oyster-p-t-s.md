## Ingredients

- paste
- oysters
- beaten egg
- milk
- butter
- corn starch
- salt

## Directions

1. Cut the paste into round cakes: those intended for the bottom crust less than an eighth of an inch thick; for the upper, a little thicker.
2. With a smaller cutter, remove a round of paste from the middle of the latter, leaving a neat ring.
3. Lay this carefully upon the bottom crust; place a second ring upon this, that the cavity may be deep enough to hold the oysters.
4. Lay the pieces you have extracted also in the pan with the rest, and bake to a fine brown in a quick oven.
5. When done, wash over with beaten egg, around top and all, and set in the oven three minutes to glaze.
6. Boil half the liquor from a quart of oysters.
7. Put in all the oysters, leaving out the uncooked liquor; heat to boiling, and stir in ½ cup of hot milk, 1 tablespoonful butter, 2 tablespoonfuls corn starch wet with a little milk, and a little salt.
8. Boil four minutes, stirring all the time until it thickens.
9. Fill the cavity in the paste shells with the mixture.
