## Ingredients

- thick piece of halibut
- whites of two eggs
- one and one-fourth cups heavy cream
- salt
- pepper
- cayenne
- buttered fish-mould
- buttered paper
- skin and bones of fish
- three slices carrot
- one slice onion
- sprig of parsley
- bit of bay leaf
- one-fourth teaspoon peppercorns
- two cups cold water
- two tablespoons butter
- three tablespoons flour
- one-third cup heavy cream
- yolks of two eggs
- Sauterne

## Directions

1. Remove skin and bones from a thick piece of halibut, finely chop fish, and force through a sieve (there should be one and one-third cups).
2. Pound in mortar, adding gradually whites of two eggs.
3. Add one and one-fourth cups heavy cream, and salt, pepper, and cayenne to taste.
4. Turn into a buttered fish-mould, cover with buttered paper, set in pan of hot water, and bake until fish is firm.
5. For Normandy Sauce: Cook skin and bones of fish with three slices carrot, one slice onion, sprig of parsley, bit of bay leaf, one-fourth teaspoon peppercorns, and two cups cold water, thirty minutes, and strain; there should be one cup.
6. Melt two tablespoons butter, add three tablespoons flour, fish stock, one-third cup heavy cream, and yolks of two eggs.
7. Season with salt, pepper, cayenne, and Sauterne.
8. Turn fish on serving dish and surround with Normandy Sauce.
