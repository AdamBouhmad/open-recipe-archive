## Ingredients

- one quart of pastry flour
- one pint of butter
- one tablespoonful of salt
- one tablespoonful of sugar
- one and a quarter cupfuls of ice-water

## Directions

1. Wash the hands with soap and water and dip them first in very hot and then in cold water.
2. Rinse a large bowl or pan with boiling water and then with cold.
3. Half fill it with cold water.
4. Wash the butter in this, working it with the hands until it is light and waxy.
5. Shape the butter into two thin cakes and put in a pan of ice-water to harden.
6. Mix the salt and sugar with the flour.
7. With the hands, rub one-third of the butter into the flour.
8. Add the water, stirring with a knife.
9. Stir quickly and vigorously until the paste is a smooth ball.
10. Sprinkle the board lightly with flour and turn the paste on this.
11. Pound quickly and lightly with the rolling-pin, do not break the paste.
12. Roll from you and to one side; or if easier to roll from you all the time, turn the paste around.
13. When it is about one-fourth of an inch thick, wipe the remaining butter, break it in bits and spread these on the paste.
14. Sprinkle lightly with flour.
15. Fold the paste, one-third from each side, so that the edges meet.
16. Fold from the ends, but do not have these meet.
17. Double the paste, pound lightly and roll down to about one-third of an inch in thickness.
18. Fold as before and roll down again.
19. Repeat this three times if for pies and six times if for vol-au-vents, patties, tarts, etc.
20. Place on the ice to harden, when it has been rolled the last time.
21. It should be in the ice chest at least an hour before being used.
22. In hot weather, if the paste sticks when being rolled down, put it on a tin sheet and place on ice.
23. As soon as it is chilled, it will roll easily.
