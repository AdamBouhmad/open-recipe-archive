## Ingredients

- lobsters
- salt
- water
- pepper-corns
- cloves
- blades of mace
- vinegar
- oil
- mustard
- vinegar
- bread
- butter

## Directions

1. Take half a dozen fine lobsters.
2. Put them into boiling salt and water.
3. When they are all done, take them out and extract all the meat from the shells, leaving that of the claws as whole as possible.
4. Cut the flesh of the body into large pieces nearly of the same size.
5. Season a sufficient quantity of vinegar very highly with whole pepper-corns, whole cloves, and whole blades of mace.
6. Put the pieces of lobster into a stew-pan, and pour on just sufficient vinegar to keep them well covered.
7. Set it over a moderate fire; and when it has boiled hard about five minutes, take out the lobster, and let the pickle boil by itself for a quarter of an hour.
8. When the pickle and lobster are both cold, put them together into a broad flat stone jar.
9. Cover it closely, and set it away in a cool place.
10. Eat the pickled lobster with oil, mustard, and vinegar, and have bread and butter with it.
