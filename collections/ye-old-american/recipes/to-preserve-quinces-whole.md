## Ingredients

- large smooth yellow quinces
- water
- loaf-sugar

## Directions

1. Take those that are large, smooth, and yellow; pare them and extract the cores, carefully removing all the blemishes.
2. Boil the quinces in a close kettle with the cores and parings, in sufficient water to cover them.
3. In half an hour take them out, spread them to cool, and add to the cores and parings some small inferior quinces cut in quarters, but not pared or cored; and pour in some more water, just enough to boil them.
4. Cover the pan, and let them simmer for an hour.
5. Then take it off, strain the liquid, measure it, and to each quart allow a pound of loaf-sugar.
6. Put the sugar to melt in the liquid, and let it set all night.
7. Next day boil the quinces in it for a quarter of an hour, and then take them out and cool them, saving the syrup.
8. On the following day repeat the same; and the fourth day add a quarter of a pound more sugar to each pint of the syrup, and boil the quinces in it twelve minutes.
9. If by this time they are not tender, bright, and transparent all through, repeat the boiling.
10. When they are quite done, put quince jelly or marmalade into the holes from whence you took the cores; put the quinces into glass jars and pour the syrup over them.
