## Ingredients

- turtle
- salt
- water
- pepper
- onion
- sweet herbs
- spiced sauce or catsup (walnut or mushroom)
- butter
- flour
- brown Sherry
- egg

## Directions

1. Plunge the turtle into a pot of boiling water, and let him lie there five minutes.
2. Skin the underpart and pull off the horny parts of the feet.
3. Lay him for ten minutes in cold salt and water.
4. Put into more hot water—salted, but not too much.
5. Boil until tender, time depending upon size and age.
6. Take him out, drain, and wipe dry.
7. Loosen the shell carefully, not to break the flesh.
8. Cut open with care, lest you touch the gall-bag with the knife.
9. Remove the gall-bag with the entrails and sand-bag.
10. Cut up all the rest of the animal into small bits.
11. Season with pepper, salt, a chopped onion, sweet herbs, and a teaspoonful of some spiced sauce, or a tablespoonful of catsup—walnut or mushroom.
12. Save the juice that runs from the meat, and put all together into a saucepan with a closely fitting top.
13. Stew gently fifteen minutes, stirring occasionally.
14. Add a great spoonful of butter, a tablespoonful of browned flour wet in cold water, and a glass of brown Sherry.
15. Add the beaten yolk of an egg, mixed with a little of the hot liquor, that it may not curdle.
16. Boil up once, and turn into a covered dish.
17. Serve around with green pickles and delicate slices of dry toast.
