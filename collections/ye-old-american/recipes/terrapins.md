## Ingredients

- terrapins
- salt
- cayenne pepper
- nutmeg
- mace
- butter
- flour
- Madeira
- eggs

## Directions

1. Have ready a pot of boiling water.
2. When it is boiling very hard put in the terrapins, and let them remain in it till quite dead.
3. Take them out, pull off the outer skin and the toe-nails.
4. Wash the terrapins in warm water and boil them again, allowing a tea-spoonful of salt to two terrapins.
5. When the flesh becomes quite tender so that you can pinch it off, take them out off the shell.
6. Remove the sand-bag, and the gall, which you must be careful not to break, as it will make the terrapin so bitter as to be uneatable.
7. Cut up all the other parts of the inside with the meat, and season it to your taste with cayenne pepper, nutmeg, and mace.
8. Put all into a stew-pan with the juice or liquor that it has given out in cutting up, but not any water.
9. To every two terrapins allow a quarter of a pound of butter divided into pieces and rolled in flour, one glass of Madeira, and the yolks of two eggs.
10. Beat the eggs, and do not stir them in till a moment before it goes to table.
11. Keep it closely covered.
12. Stew it gently till every thing is tender, and serve it up hot in a deep dish.
