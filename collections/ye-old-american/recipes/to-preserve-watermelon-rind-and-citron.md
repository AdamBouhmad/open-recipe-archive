## Ingredients

- watermelon rind
- loaf sugar (a pound and a half to each pound of rind)
- green vine-leaves
- water
- alum
- white of egg (beaten)
- lemon juice (one lemon to a quart of syrup)

## Directions

1. Pare off the green skin, cut the watermelon rind into pieces
2. Weigh the pieces and allow to each pound a pound and a half of loaf sugar
3. Line your kettle with green vine-leaves, and put in the pieces without the sugar
4. Layer vine-leaves to cover each layer of melon rind
5. Pour in water to cover the whole and place a thick cloth over the kettle
6. Scatter a few bits of alum amongst the fruit
7. Simmer the fruit for two hours
8. Spread the melon rind on a dish to cool
9. Melt the sugar, using a pint of water to a pound and a half of sugar, and mix with it some beaten white of egg
10. Boil and skim the sugar
11. When quite clear, put in the rind and let it boil two hours
12. Take out the rind, boil the syrup again, pour it over the rind, and let it remain all night
13. The next morning, boil the syrup with lemon juice, allowing one lemon to a quart of syrup
14. When it is thick enough to hang in a drop from the point of a spoon, it is done
15. Put the rind in jars and pour over it the syrup
16. Citrons may be preserved in the same manner, first paring off the outer skin and cutting them into quarters
