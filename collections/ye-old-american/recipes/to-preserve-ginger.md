## Ingredients

- root of green ginger
- sugar
- beaten whites of egg
- spring or pump water
- lemon-juice

## Directions

1. Take root of green ginger, and pare it neatly with a sharp knife, throwing it into a pan of cold water as you pare it.
2. Boil it till tender all through, changing the water three times.
3. Each time put on the ginger in quite cold water to take out the excessive heat.
4. When it is perfectly tender, throw it again into a pan of cold water, and let it lie an hour or more; this will make it crisp.
5. For every six pounds of ginger root, clarify eight pounds of the best double-refined loaf-sugar.
6. Break up the sugar, put it into a preserving kettle, and melt it in spring or pump water, (into which you have stirred gradually the beaten whites of four eggs,) and half a pint of water to each pound of sugar.
7. Boil and skim it well.
8. Let the syrup stand till it is cold; and having drained the ginger, pour the syrup over it, cover it, and do not disturb it for two days.
9. Pour it from the ginger, and boil the syrup over again.
10. As soon as it is cold, pour it again on the ginger, and let it stand at least three days.
11. Boil the syrup again, and pour it hot over the ginger.
12. Proceed in this manner till you find that the syrup has thoroughly penetrated the ginger, (which you may ascertain by its taste and appearance when you cut a piece off,) and till the syrup becomes very thick and rich.
13. Put it all into jars, and cover it closely.
14. It will be greatly improved by adding some lemon-juice at the close of the last boiling.
