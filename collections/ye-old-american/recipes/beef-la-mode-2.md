## Ingredients

- ten pounds of the upper part of a round of beef
- three teaspoonfuls of salt
- one teaspoonful of pepper
- one teaspoonful of ginger
- one teaspoonful of mace
- one teaspoonful of cinnamon
- two teaspoonfuls of cloves
- a pint of fine bread crumbs
- half a pound of fat salt pork
- one teaspoonful of ground thyme or summer savory
- two teaspoonfuls sage
- half a teaspoonful of pepper
- one teaspoonful of nutmeg
- a little cloves
- one onion
- milk or water
- flour
- tape
- one pint of hot water

## Directions

1. Mix together three teaspoonfuls of salt, one of pepper, one of ginger, one of mace, one of cinnamon, and two of cloves
2. Rub this mixture into ten pounds of the upper part of a round of beef
3. Let this beef stand in this state over night
4. In the morning, make a dressing or stuffing of a pint of fine bread crumbs, half a pound of fat salt pork cut in dice, a teaspoonful of ground thyme or summer savory, two teaspoonfuls sage, half a teaspoonful of pepper, one of nutmeg, a little cloves, an onion minced fine, moisten with a little milk or water
5. Stuff this mixture into the place from whence you took out the bone
6. With a long skewer fasten the two ends of the beef together so that its form will be circular
7. Bind it around with tape to prevent the skewers giving way
8. Make incisions in the beef with a sharp knife
9. Fill these incisions very closely with the stuffing
10. Dredge the whole with flour
11. Put it into a dripping-pan and pour over it a pint of hot water
12. Turn a large pan over it to keep in the steam
13. Roast slowly from three to four hours, allowing a quarter of an hour to each pound of meat
14. If the meat should be tough, stew it first in a pot with water enough to cover it until tender, then put into a dripping-pan and brown in the oven
15. If the meat is to be eaten hot, skim off the fat from the gravy, into which, after it is taken off the fire, stir in the beaten yolks of two eggs
16. If onions are disliked you may omit them and substitute minced oysters
