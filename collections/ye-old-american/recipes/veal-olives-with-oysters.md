## Ingredients

- fillet of veal
- veal chops
- forced-meat
- bread-crumbs
- chopped pork
- pepper
- salt
- chopped oysters
- boiling water
- melted butter
- oyster-liquor
- browned flour

## Directions

1. Cut large, smooth slices from a fillet of veal, or veal chops will do quite as well.
2. Trim them into a uniform shape and size, and spread each neatly with forced-meat made of bread-crumbs and a little chopped pork, seasoned with pepper and salt.
3. Over this spread some chopped oysters, about three to a good-sized slice of veal.
4. Roll them up carefully and closely, and pin each with two small tin or wooden skewers.
5. Lay them in a dripping-pan; dash a teacupful of boiling water over them, and roast, basting at least twice with melted butter.
6. When they are brown, remove to a chafing-dish, and cover, while you add a little oyster-liquor to the gravy left in the dripping-pan.
7. Let this simmer for three or four minutes; thicken with a teaspoonful of browned flour, and boil up at once.
8. Withdraw the skewers cautiously, so as not to break the olives; pour the gravy over and around them, and serve.
