## Ingredients

- birds (grouse and quails or any birds from foregoing receipts)
- salt pork
- giblets
- pint and a half of water (for six birds)
- puff-paste
- livers
- parsley
- sweet marjoram
- thyme
- lemon juice
- pepper
- shallot
- bread-crumbs
- warm milk
- corned ham
- butter
- wine
- pastry leaves

## Directions

1. Clean and wash the birds; cut the quails in half, the grouse into four pieces.
2. Trim off bits of the inferior portions, necks, lower ribs, etc., and put them with the giblets into a saucepan, with a pint and a half of water, if your pie requires six birds.
3. While this is stewing make a good puff-paste and line a large pudding-dish, reserving enough for a lid half an inch thick.
4. When the livers are tender, take them out, leaving the gravy to stew in the covered saucepan.
5. Lard the breasts of the birds with tiny strips of salt pork, and mince a couple of slices of the same with the livers, a bunch of parsley, sweet marjoram, and thyme, also chopped fine, the juice of a lemon, pepper, and a very small shallot.
6. Make a force-meat of this, with bread-crumbs moistened with warm milk.
7. Put some thin strips of cold corned (not smoked) ham in the bottom of the pie, next to the crust; lay upon these pieces of the bird, peppered and buttered, then a layer of the force-meat, and so on, until you are ready for the gravy.
8. Strain the gravy, return to the fire, and season with pepper and a glass of wine.
9. Heat to a boil, pour into the pie, and cover with the upper crust, cutting a slit in the middle.
10. Ornament with pastry leaves, arranged in a wreath about the edge, and in the middle a pastry bird, with curled strips of pastry about it.
11. This last should be baked separately and laid on when the pie is done, to cover the hole in the middle.
12. Bake three hours if your pie be large, covering with paper if it threaten to brown too fast.
