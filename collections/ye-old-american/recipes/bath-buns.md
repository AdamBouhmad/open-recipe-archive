## Ingredients

- saffron
- water
- half a pound of fresh butter
- a pound of sifted flour
- four eggs
- a large wine glass of yeast
- a quarter of a pound of powdered and sifted loaf-sugar
- a grated nutmeg
- two or three spoonfuls of carraway seeds
- carraway comfits

## Directions

1. Boil a little saffron in sufficient water to cover it, till the liquid is of a bright yellow
2. Strain it, and set it to cool
3. Rub half a pound of fresh butter into a pound of sifted flour, and make it into a paste with four eggs that have been well beaten, and a large wine glass of the best and strongest yeast
4. Add the infusion of saffron to colour it yellow
5. Put the dough into a pan, cover it with a cloth, and set it before the fire to rise
6. When it is quite light, mix into it a quarter of a pound of powdered and sifted loaf-sugar; a grated nutmeg; and, if you choose, two or three spoonfuls of carraway seeds
7. Roll out the dough into a thick sheet, and divide it into round cakes with a cutter
8. Strew the top of each bun with carraway comfits, and bake them on flat tins buttered well
9. They should be eaten the day they are baked
