## Ingredients

- a round of fresh beef
- sweet marjoram
- sweet basil
- small onions or shalots
- parsley
- marrow from the bone of the beef
- a quarter of a pound or more of suet
- two penny rolls of stale bread
- pepper, mace, and nutmeg
- beaten yolks of four eggs
- slices of ham
- four large onions
- four carrots
- four turnips
- half a pint to a pint of water
- two calves' feet (if convenient)
- port wine
- pickled mushrooms
- beaten white of egg
- boiled potato
- cold boiled carrot
- beet-root
- raw turnips and carrots
- double parsley
- flowers

## Directions

1. Take the bone out of a round of fresh beef, and beat the meat well all over to make it tender.
2. Chop and mix together equal quantities of sweet marjoram and sweet basil, the leaves picked from the stalks and rubbed fine.
3. Chop also some small onions or shalots, and some parsley; the marrow from the bone of the beef; and a quarter of a pound, or more of suet.
4. Add two penny rolls of stale bread grated; and pepper, mace, and nutmeg to your taste.
5. Mix all these ingredients well, and bind them together with the beaten yolks of four eggs.
6. Fill with this seasoning the place from whence you took out the bone; and rub what is left of it all over the outside of the meat.
7. Proportion the quantity of stuffing to the size of the round of beef.
8. Fasten it well with skewers, and tie it round firmly with a piece of tape, so as to keep it compact and in good shape.
9. It is best to prepare the meat the day before it is to be cooked.
10. Cover the bottom of a stew-pan with slices of ham.
11. Lay the beef upon them, and cover the top of the meat with more slices of ham.
12. Place round it four large onions, four carrots, and four turnips, all cut in thick slices.
13. Pour in from half a pint to a pint of water, and if convenient, add two calves' feet cut in half.
14. Cover the pan closely, set it in an oven and let it bake for at least six hours; or seven or eight, according to the size.
15. When it is thoroughly done, take out the beef and lay it on a dish with the vegetables round it.
16. Remove the bacon and calves' feet, and having skimmed the fat from the gravy carefully, strain it into a small sauce-pan.
17. Set it on hot coals, and stir into it a teacup-full of port wine, and the same quantity of pickled mushrooms.
18. Let it just come to a boil, and then send it to table in a sauce-tureen.
