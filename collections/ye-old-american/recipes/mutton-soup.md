## Ingredients

- shoulder part of a fore quarter of mutton
- two quarts of water
- remainder of the mutton
- boiling water
- salt
- half a dozen turnips
- four carrots
- three onions
- small dumplings
- chopped parsley
- scrag of mutton

## Directions

1. Cut off the shoulder part of a fore quarter of mutton, and having cut all the meat from the bone, put it into a soup pot with two quarts of water.
2. As soon as it boils, skim it well, and then slacken the fire and simmer the meat for an hour and a half.
3. Then take the remainder of the mutton, and put it whole into the soup-pot with sufficient boiling water to cover it well, and salt it to your taste.
4. Skim it the moment the fresh piece of meat begins to boil, and about every quarter of an hour afterwards.
5. It should boil slowly five hours.
6. Prepare half a dozen turnips, four carrots, and three onions, (all cut up, but not small,) and put them in about an hour and a half before dinner.
7. You may also put in some small dumplings.
8. Add some chopped parsley.
9. Cut the meat off the scrag into small pieces, and send it to table in the tureen with the soup.
10. The other half of the mutton should be served on a separate dish, with whole turnips boiled and laid round it.
