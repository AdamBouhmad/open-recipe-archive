## Ingredients

- cucumbers
- alum water (2 teaspoons powdered alum to each quart of water)
- ice-water
- 2 pounds sugar
- 1 pint vinegar
- 2 tablespoons whole cloves
- 2 tablespoons stick cinnamon

## Directions

1. Cut cucumbers in halves lengthwise.
2. Cover with alum water, allowing two teaspoons powdered alum to each quart of water.
3. Heat gradually to boiling-point, then let stand on back of range two hours.
4. Remove from alum water and chill in ice-water.
5. Make a syrup by boiling five minutes two pounds sugar, one pint vinegar, with two tablespoons each of whole cloves and stick cinnamon tied in a piece of muslin.
6. Add cucumbers and cook ten minutes.
7. Remove cucumbers to a stone jar, and pour over the syrup.
8. Scald syrup three successive mornings, and return to cucumbers.
