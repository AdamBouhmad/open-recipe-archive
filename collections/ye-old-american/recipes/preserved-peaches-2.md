## Ingredients

- peaches
- sugar
- water
- pineapple slices

## Directions

1. Weigh the fruit after it is pared and the stones extracted, and allow a pound of sugar to every one of peaches
2. Crack one-quarter of the stones, extract the kernels, break them to pieces and boil in just enough water to cover them, until soft, when set aside to steep in a covered vessel
3. Put a layer of sugar at the bottom of the kettle, then one of fruit, and so on until you have used up all of both
4. Set it where it will warm slowly until the sugar is melted and the fruit hot through
5. Strain the kernel-water and add it
6. Boil steadily until the peaches are tender and clear
7. Take them out with a perforated skimmer and lay upon large flat dishes, crowding as little as possible
8. Boil the syrup almost to a jelly—that is, until clear and thick, skimming off all the scum
9. Fill your jars two-thirds full of the peaches, pour on the boiling syrup, and, when cold, cover with brandy tissue-paper, then with cloth, lastly with thick paper tied tightly over them
10. The peaches should be ready to take off after half an hour's boiling; the syrup be boiled fifteen minutes longer, fast, and often stirred, to throw up the scum
