## Ingredients

- currants
- water
- powdered loaf sugar

## Directions

1. Gather full ripe currants on a dry day, pick them from the stalks, and weigh them
2. Crush them with your hands, leaving none whole
3. For every two pounds of currants put one quart of water
4. Stir all well together, and let it stand three hours, and strain the liquor through a sieve
5. For every three pounds of currants, put one pound of powdered loaf sugar
6. Stir it till the sugar is dissolved, boil it, and keep skimming it, as long as any scum will rise
7. Let it stand sixteen hours to cool, before you put it in the cask
8. Stop it very close
9. If the quantity be twenty gallons, let it stand three weeks before you bottle it; if it be thirty gallons, it must remain a month
10. It should be perfectly clear when drawn off
11. Put a lump of sugar in each bottle, cork it well, and keep it in a cool place, or it will turn sour
