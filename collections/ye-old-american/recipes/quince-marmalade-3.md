## Ingredients

- quinces
- powdered loaf-sugar (half a pound to each pound of cut quinces)
- water
- cochineal powder

## Directions

1. Wash the quinces clean, pare and core them, and cut them into small pieces.
2. Put the parings and cores into a kettle with water enough to cover them, and boil them slowly till they are all to pieces, and quite soft.
3. Put the quinces with the sugar into a porcelain preserving kettle, strain over them through a cloth the liquid from the parings and cores.
4. Add a little cochineal powdered and sifted through thin muslin.
5. Boil the whole over a quick fire till it becomes a thick smooth mass, keeping it covered except when skimming; always after skimming, stir it up well from the bottom.
6. When cold, put it up in glass jars. If you wish to use it soon, put it warm into moulds, and when it is cold, set the moulds in luke-warm water, and the marmalade will turn out easily.
