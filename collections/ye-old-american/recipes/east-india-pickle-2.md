## Ingredients

- small cucumbers
- very small common white onions
- snap beans
- gherkins
- hard white cabbage
- plums
- peaches
- pears
- lemons
- green tomatoes
- strong brine
- pure water
- vinegar
- one ounce of turmeric to the gallon
- two ounces of mace
- two ounces of cloves
- two ounces of cinnamon
- two ounces of allspice
- two ounces of celery seed
- a quarter of a pound of white race ginger
- half a pound of white mustard seed
- half a pint of small red peppers
- quarter of a pound of grated horse-radish
- half a pint of flour mustard
- two ounces of turmeric
- half a pint of garlic
- two gallons of cider vinegar
- brown sugar

## Directions

1. Lay in strong brine for two weeks, or until convenient to use them, small cucumbers, very small common white onions, snap beans, gherkins, hard white cabbage quartered, plums, peaches, pears, lemons, green tomatoes and anything else you may wish
2. When ready, take them out of the brine and simmer in pure water until tender enough to stick a straw through
3. If still too salt, soak in clear water
4. Drain thoroughly and lay them in vinegar in which is dissolved one ounce of turmeric to the gallon
5. For five gallons of pickle, take two ounces of mace, two of cloves, two of cinnamon, two of allspice, two of celery seed, a quarter of a pound of white race ginger craked fine, half a pound of white mustard seed, half a pint of small red peppers, quarter of a pound of grated horse-radish, half a pint of flour mustard, two ounces of turmeric, half a pint of garlic if you like and soak in two gallons of cider vinegar for two weeks, stirring daily
6. After the pickles have lain in the turmeric vinegar for a week, take them out and put in jars or casks, one layer of pickle and one of spice out of the vinegar, till all is used
7. If the turmeric vinegar is still good and strong, add it and the spiced vinegar
8. If the turmeric vinegar be much diluted do not use it, but add enough fresh to the spiced to cover the pickles
9. Put it on the fire with a pound of brown sugar to each gallon
10. When boiling, pour over the pickle
11. Repeat this two or three times as your taste may direct
