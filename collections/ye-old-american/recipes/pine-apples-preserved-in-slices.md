## Ingredients

- pine-apples
- loaf-sugar
- spring or pump water
- white of egg

## Directions

1. Pare the pine-apples, and cut them into round pieces near an inch thick, and take out the core from the centre of each slice.
2. Allow a pound of loaf-sugar to every pound of the sliced pine-apple.
3. Powder the sugar, and strew it in layers between the slices of pine-apple.
4. Cover it and let it set all night.
5. Next morning measure some clear spring or pump water, allowing half a pint to each pound of sugar.
6. Beat some white of egg, (one white to two pounds of sugar,) and when it is a very stiff froth, stir it gradually into the water.
7. Mix with it the pine-apple and sugar, and put the whole into a preserving kettle.
8. Boil and skim it well, till the pine-apple is tender and bright all through.
9. Take it out, and when cold, put it up in wide-mouthed glass jars, or in large tumblers.
