## Ingredients

- three pounds mutton cut from fore-quarter
- three pints cold water
- one-half cup barley soaked in cold water over night
- two tablespoons butter
- one-fourth cup carrot cut in one-half inch dice
- one-fourth cup turnip cut in one-half inch dice
- one-fourth cup onion cut in one-half inch dice
- one-fourth cup celery cut in one-half inch dice
- salt and pepper to taste
- two tablespoons butter
- two tablespoons flour
- one-half tablespoon finely chopped parsley

## Directions

1. Wipe three pounds mutton cut from fore-quarter and cut lean meat in one-inch cubes.
2. Put in kettle and cover with three pints cold water.
3. Bring quickly to boiling-point and skim.
4. Add one-half cup barley which has been soaked in cold water over night.
5. Simmer one and one-half hours, or until meat is tender.
6. Put bones in a second kettle, cover with cold water, heat slowly to boiling-point, skim, and boil one and one-half hours.
7. Strain water from bones and add to meat.
8. Fry five minutes in two tablespoons butter the carrot, turnip, onion, and celery, cut in one-half inch dice.
9. Add vegetables to soup with salt and pepper to taste.
10. Cook until vegetables are soft.
11. Thicken with two tablespoons each of butter and flour cooked together.
12. Add one-half tablespoon finely chopped parsley just before serving.
