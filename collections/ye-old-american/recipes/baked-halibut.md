## Ingredients

- halibut, five or six pounds
- salt and water
- butter
- water
- walnut catsup, tablespoonful
- Worcestershire sauce, teaspoonful
- lemon, juice of
- browned flour
- cold water

## Directions

1. Take a piece of halibut weighing five or six pounds, and lay in salt and water for two hours.
2. Wipe dry and score the outer skin.
3. Set in the baking-pan in a tolerably hot oven, and bake an hour, basting often with butter and water heated together in a saucepan or tin cup.
4. When a fork will penetrate it easily it is done.
5. Take the gravy in the dripping-pan—add a little boiling water should there not be enough.
6. Stir in a tablespoonful of walnut catsup, a teaspoonful of Worcestershire sauce, the juice of a lemon, and thicken with browned flour, previously wet with cold water.
7. Boil up once and put into sauce-boat.
