## Ingredients

- saddle of venison of about ten pounds
- salt pork
- two carrots
- one onion
- salt pork in thin slices
- two bay-leaves
- two cloves
- four kernels of allspice
- half a lemon
- salt
- pepper
- one quart of good stock
- butter
- jelly or wine sauce
- cream sauce or cream

## Directions

1. Wash the saddle carefully and see that no hairs are left dried on to the outside
2. Cut salt pork in strips about two inches long and an eighth of an inch thick
3. Lard the saddle with two rows on each side
4. In a large dripping-pan cut two carrots, one onion and salt pork in thin slices
5. Add two bay-leaves, two cloves, four kernels of allspice, half a lemon sliced
6. Season with salt and pepper
7. Place the saddle of venison in the pan with a quart of good stock boiling hot and a small piece of butter
8. Let it boil about fifteen minutes on top of the stove
9. Put it in a hot oven and bake, basting well every five minutes, until it is medium rare
10. Serve with jelly or a wine sauce
11. If venison is desired well done, cook much longer and use a cream sauce or stir cream into the venison gravy
