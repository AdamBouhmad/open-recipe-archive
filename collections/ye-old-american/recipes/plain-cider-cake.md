## Ingredients

- pound and a half of flour
- half a pound of butter
- three-quarters of a pound of powdered white sugar
- small tea-spoonful of sal-aratus or pearl-ash
- pint of the best cider

## Directions

1. Sift into a large pan a pound and a half of flour, and rub into it half a pound of butter.
2. Mix in three-quarters of a pound of powdered white sugar, and melt a small tea-spoonful of sal-aratus or pearl-ash in a pint of the best cider.
3. Pour the cider into the other ingredients while it is foaming, and stir the whole very hard.
4. Have ready a buttered square pan, put in the mixture, and set it immediately in a rather brisk oven.
5. Bake it an hour or more, according to its thickness.
6. Cut it into squares, split and butter them.
