## Ingredients

- one ten-pound turkey
- salt
- one-third cup butter
- one-fourth cup flour
- flour
- two cups boiling water
- one-half cup butter
- one-half cup boiling water
- parsley or celery tips or curled celery
- rings and discs of carrots

## Directions

1. Dress, clean, stuff, and truss a ten-pound turkey.
2. Place on its side on rack in a dripping-pan and rub entire surface with salt.
3. Spread breast, legs, and wings with one-third cup butter rubbed until creamy and mixed with one-fourth cup flour.
4. Dredge bottom of pan with flour.
5. Place in a hot oven.
6. When flour on turkey begins to brown, reduce heat and baste with fat in pan.
7. Add two cups boiling water.
8. Continue basting every fifteen minutes until turkey is cooked, which will require about three hours.
9. For basting, use one-half cup butter melted in one-half cup boiling water, and after this is used baste with fat in pan.
10. During cooking turn turkey frequently that it may brown evenly.
11. If turkey is browning too fast, cover with buttered paper to prevent burning.
12. Remove string and skewers before serving.
13. Garnish with parsley or celery tips or curled celery and rings and discs of carrots strung on fine wire.
