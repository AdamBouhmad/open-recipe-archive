## Ingredients

- fine ripe plums
- loaf-sugar, a pound and a half for each pound of plums
- boiling water
- plum kernels, blanched and broken in half (optional)

## Directions

1. Weigh the plums, and to each pound allow a pound and a half of loaf-sugar.
2. Put them into a pan, and scald them in boiling water to make the skins come off easily.
3. Peel them, and throw them as you do so into a large china pitcher.
4. Let them set for an hour or two, and then take them out, saving all the juice that has exuded from them while in the pitcher.
5. Spread the plums out on large dishes, and cover them with half the sugar you have allotted to them (it must be previously powdered), and let them lie in it all night.
6. Next morning pour the juice out of the pitcher into a porcelain preserving kettle, add the last half of the sugar to it, and let it melt over the fire.
7. When it has boiled skim it, and then put in the plums.
8. Boil them over a moderate fire for about half an hour.
9. Take them out one by one with a spoon, and spread them on large dishes to cool.
10. If the syrup is not sufficiently thick and clear, boil and skim it a little longer till it is.
11. Put the plums into glass jars and pour the syrup warm over them.
12. If desired, boil a handful or more of blanched plum kernels in the syrup with the fruit, and take them out before pouring the syrup into the jars.
13. You may preserve plums whole, without peeling, by pricking them deeply at each end with a large needle.
