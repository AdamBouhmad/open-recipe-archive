## Ingredients

- butter
- sugar
- orange
- fourteen ounces fine flour
- ten eggs
- beaten white of egg
- powdered loaf-sugar
- orange juice

## Directions

1. Cream up the butter in the pan of sugar and orange, and stir it hard till perfectly light, white, and creamy.
2. Sift into a pan fourteen ounces (two ounces less than a pound) of fine flour.
3. Beat ten eggs till they are as thick and smooth as a fine boiled custard.
4. Stir them, by degrees, into the butter and sugar, alternately with the flour, a little of each at a time.
5. Continue to beat the whole very hard for some time after all the ingredients are in.
6. Put the mixture into a large square, shallow pan, well buttered, and set it immediately into a brisk oven.
7. Bake thoroughly until it shrinks from the sides of the pan. Test by sticking a twig from a corn-broom into the middle down to the bottom; if it comes out dry and clean, the cake is done.
8. Make an icing of beaten white of egg and powdered loaf-sugar, mixed with a spoonful or more of orange juice.
9. Dredge the cake with flour, then wipe off the flour and spread on the icing thick and evenly, scoring it in large squares.
10. Cut the cake into squares about the usual size of a Spanish bun. Eat fresh, best the day it is baked.
