## Ingredients

- 1 quart of rich unskimmed milk
- 2 ounces of bitter almonds, blanched and pounded
- 1/2 nutmeg
- 1 large stick of cinnamon
- 8 or 9 blades of mace
- 1/2 handful of carrageen
- 1/2 pound of powdered sugar

## Directions

1. From a quart of rich unskimmed milk take half a pint.
2. Add to the half pint two ounces of bitter almonds, blanched and pounded; half a nutmeg; and a large stick of cinnamon, broken up; also eight or nine blades of mace.
3. Set it in a closed pan over hot coals, and boil it half an hour.
4. In the mean time, wash through two or three cold waters half a handful of carrageen, and add it to the pint and a half of cold milk.
5. When it is sufficiently flavoured, stir in the boiled milk, adding gradually half a pound of powdered sugar, and mix the whole very well.
6. Set it over the fire, and keep it boiling hard five minutes from the time it has come to a boil.
7. Strain it into a pitcher; wet your moulds or cups with cold water, put the blanc-mange into them, and leave it undisturbed till it congeals.
