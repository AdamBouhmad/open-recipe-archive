## Ingredients

- one quart of milk
- four large tablespoonfuls of cornstarch
- a teaspoonful of salt
- whites of two eggs
- two tablespoonfuls of powdered sugar
- half of a large tumbler of currant jelly or any other bright jelly or any kind of preserved fruit

## Directions

1. Reserve half a cupful of milk from a quart and put the remainder on the stove in a double boiler
2. Mix four large tablespoonfuls of cornstarch and a teaspoonful of salt with the half cupful of milk
3. Stir the mixture into the boiling milk and beat well for two minutes
4. Cover the boiler and cook the pudding for twelve minutes
5. Pour it into a pudding-dish and set in a cool place for half an hour
6. Beat the whites of two eggs to a stiff, dry froth, and beat into this two tablespoonfuls of powdered sugar
7. As soon as the sugar has been well mixed with the whites, add half of a large tumbler of currant jelly, or any other bright jelly, or any kind of preserved fruit
8. Serve
