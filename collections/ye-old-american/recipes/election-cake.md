## Ingredients

- 2 pounds and a half flour
- 1 pound flour
- 2 table-spoonfuls West India molasses
- 5 jills strong fresh yeast
- 1 jill water
- 2 pounds fresh butter
- 2 pounds powdered sugar
- 1 table-spoonful powdered cinnamon
- 1 tea-spoonful powdered mace
- 2 powdered nutmegs
- ½ pint rich milk
- 14 eggs

## Directions

1. Sift into a pan two pounds and a half of flour; and into a deep plate another pound
2. Stir two table-spoonfuls of the best West India molasses into five jills of strong fresh yeast; adding a jill of water, warm, but not hot
3. Stir gradually into the yeast the pound of flour that you have sifted separately
4. Cover it, and let it set by the fire three hours to rise
5. While it is rising, stir in a deep pan two pounds of fresh butter and two pounds of powdered sugar, till they are quite light and creamy
6. Add to them a table-spoonful of powdered cinnamon; a tea-spoonful of powdered mace; and two powdered nutmegs
7. Stir in also half a pint of rich milk
8. Beat fourteen eggs till very smooth and thick, and stir them gradually into the mixture, alternately with the two pounds and a half of flour
9. When the sponge is quite light, mix the whole together, and bake it in buttered tin pans in a moderate oven
