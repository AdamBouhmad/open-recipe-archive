## Ingredients

- crab apples, full grown
- vine leaves
- cold water
- loaf-sugar, a pound and a half for each pound of crab apples
- lemon juice and yellow peel (optional)

## Directions

1. Cover the bottom and sides of your preserving kettle with vine leaves, and put crab apples in; spreading a thick layer of vine leaves over them.
2. Fill up the kettle with cold water, and hang it over a slow fire early in the morning; simmer them slowly, but do not allow them to boil.
3. When they are quite yellow, take them out, peel off the skin with a penknife, and extract the cores very neatly.
4. Put them again into the kettle with fresh vine leaves and fresh water, and hang them again over a slow fire to simmer, but not to boil.
5. When they have remained long enough in the second vine leaves to become green, take them out, weigh them, and allow a pound and a half of loaf-sugar to each pound of crab apples.
6. Wash and wipe the kettle well, put the apples into it with a thick layer of sugar between each layer of apples, and about half a pint of water for each pound and a half of sugar.
7. You may add the juice and yellow peel of some lemons.
8. Boil them gently till they are quite clear and tender throughout.
9. Skim them well, and keep the kettle covered when you are not skimming.
10. When done, spread them on large dishes to cool, and then tie them up in glass jars with brandy papers.
