## Ingredients

- ham
- wine
- vinegar
- white sugar
- made mustard
- hot water
- cracker-crumbs
- milk
- butter
- beaten egg
- good wine
- catsup or walnut catsup
- lemon juice
- nutmeg

## Directions

1. Take the ham out and wash very well, scrubbing the under part hard, and trimming away the black and rusty edges.
2. Skin with care, lest you mangle the meat and spoil the symmetry of the shape.
3. Lay in a dish and sponge with a cloth dipped in a mixture of wine, vinegar, sugar, and mustard—about a tablespoonful of white sugar, a saltspoonful of made mustard, and a glass of wine to half a gill of vinegar.
4. Do this at intervals of an hour, washing every part of the ham well, all day and until bed-time.
5. Renew the process next morning until six hours before you need the meat.
6. Put it upon the spit or in the dripping-pan, with a cup of hot water to prevent burning.
7. Add to the mixture—or what is left of it in the dish—a cupful of boiling water.
8. Keep this on the stove and baste continually with it until the liquor flows freely from the ham as it cooks; then substitute the gravy.
9. When done, cover with cracker-crumbs, worked to a paste with milk, butter, and a beaten egg, and return to the oven to brown.
10. Skim the gravy; add a glass of good wine, a tablespoonful of catsup, the juice of a lemon, and a little nutmeg.
11. Boil up, and send to table in a boat.
