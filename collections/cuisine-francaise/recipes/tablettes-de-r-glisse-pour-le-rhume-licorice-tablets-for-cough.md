## Ingredients

- One pint of river water
- One pound of scraped green licorice cut into very small pieces
- Two handfuls of barley
- Four rainette apples
- One pound of clarified sugar
- Two ounces of gum tragacanth

## Directions

1. Put the water, licorice, barley, and apples in an earthenware pot and bring to boil over very low heat for four to five hours, until well cooked and reduced to less than a chopine of water.
2. Crush everything together and force through a sieve with all your strength.
3. Add the clarified sugar and the melted gum tragacanth to what you have strained.
4. Dry this composition over the fire, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon until it no longer sticks to your fingers.
5. Pour it onto slate tablets or copper sheets rubbed with a little oil.
6. When cold, cut into tablets and put them to dry in a moderately warm place.
