## Ingredients

- crayfish, fifty
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- butter, a quarter pound
- rice
- broth or water
- bread crusts

## Directions

1. You will take fifty crayfish that you will wash in eight or ten waters; you will put them in a saucepan, adding salt, coarse pepper, a little grated nutmeg and a quarter pound of butter; you will put them on a fairly hot fire; you will sauté or stir them with a spoon for a quarter hour. When your crayfish are cooked, you will let them drain, and you will remove the meat that you will pound. You must make rice burst for a quarter hour in broth or water; you will drain it and put it in the mortar with your crayfish meat; when it is all well pounded, you will put it in a saucepan, and thin it with a little soup broth that you will pass through a fine sieve; when your purée is made, you will thin it with broth so that it is neither too thick nor too clear, then you will pound the crayfish shells; when they are well pounded, you will put juice in them, or the butter in which your crayfish were cooked, and you will pass this purée through a fine sieve. It should then have a red color. You will put it in a saucepan on gentle heat; try that neither one nor the other purées boil, but that they are very hot; you will have crusts from the top of the bread, that you will put in your soup tureen, and you will pour a little extremely hot broth over your bread before serving; then, at the moment of service, you will pour your first purée over your bread, and as for the one of shells, you will pour it over your soup with a large spoon, so that it has a beautiful color. This soup is made with fat as well as lean. (See Lean Broth.)
