## Ingredients

- Lamb forequarters
- Lamb shoulders
- Lamb breast and cutlets
- Atelet sauce or salt and coarse pepper
- Warm butter
- Breadcrumbs
- Tender loin

## Directions

1. Take the forequarters of lamb, or both if you wish, so your entrée is substantial
2. Remove the shoulders
3. Cut the breasts so your cutlets are not damaged
4. Cook your shoulders on the spit and let them cool
5. Cook your breasts as explained for shoulders of lamb with cucumbers
6. When they are cooked, place them between two covers to take on a uniform shape and let them cool
7. Cut them into oval pieces the size of a crouton, pointed on one end
8. Leave a small breast bone showing
9. Coat them with atelet sauce if you have it, or season with a little fine salt and coarse pepper
10. Dip them in warm butter
11. Put them in breadcrumbs and give them a beautiful form
12. Your pieces should be slightly larger than your cutlets
13. Place them on a shallow pan
14. You may also bread your tendons in the English manner and fry them
15. Cut and trim your cutlets
16. Season them with a little salt and a little coarse pepper
17. Place them in your sautoir with warm butter on top
