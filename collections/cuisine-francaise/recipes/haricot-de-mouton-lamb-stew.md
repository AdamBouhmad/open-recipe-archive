## Ingredients

- lamb shoulder
- butter
- flour
- bouillon or water
- salt
- pepper
- parsley
- chives
- bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- three cloves
- garlic clove
- turnips

## Directions

1. Cut a lamb shoulder into pieces the width of two fingers and a bit longer
2. Make a roux with little butter and a spoonful of flour
3. Brown it on a small fire, stirring always with a spoon, until it is a dark cinnamon color
4. Add the meat and pass it over the fire five or six times, stirring from time to time
5. Add bouillon (or about a pint of warm water if no bouillon), little at a time so the roux can dissolve well, stirring always with the spoon until all is added
6. Season the meat with salt and pepper, a bouquet of parsley, chives, a bay leaf, thyme, basil, three cloves, and a clove of garlic
7. Cook on a small fire
8. Halfway through cooking, tilt the casserole so the fat comes to the top; remove it with a spoon
9. Add turnips cut into pieces and a bouquet
10. Tilt the casserole again to remove the remaining fat
11. When done, the sauce should be neither too clear nor too thick, that is to say of medium consistency
12. Dress your pieces in the serving dish, pour the sauce over them, and sprinkle with chopped parsley
