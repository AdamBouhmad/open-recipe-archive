## Ingredients

- Twenty chestnuts from Limoges or Auvergne
- Slices of veal
- Lard
- Two bay leaves
- Three cloves
- Six carrots
- Six onions
- A bouquet of fresh celery leaves
- Coarse pepper
- Bouillon
- Bread
- Salt

## Directions

1. Remove the first shell of the chestnuts
2. Put them in water and heat until the water boils; remove and check if the skin lifts
3. Peel them completely so no inner skin remains
4. Put slices of veal and lard at the bottom of a saucepan
5. Add two bay leaves, three cloves, six carrots, six onions, and a bouquet of fresh celery leaves
6. Add the chestnuts seasoned with coarse pepper
7. Cover the chestnuts with lard slices
8. Moisten with bouillon and simmer for three-quarters of an hour to an hour until the chestnuts are cooked
9. Drain and cut in half
10. Make a layer of bread, then a layer of chestnuts in your dish until full
11. Form several lines of chestnuts on your garbure
12. Strain the bouillon in which the chestnuts cooked
13. Pour the bouillon over your garbure and let it boil until it forms a crust
14. Serve a pot of bouillon
15. Alternatively, cook the chestnuts only in bouillon with carrots, onions, cloves, bay leaves, and coarse pepper
