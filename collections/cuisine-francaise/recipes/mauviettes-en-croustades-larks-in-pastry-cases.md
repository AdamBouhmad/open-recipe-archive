## Ingredients

- Larks
- Forcemeat, cooked
- Nutmeg, grated
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Fine herbs
- Lard strips
- Butter
- Four spices
- Bread croutons
- Italienne sauce

## Directions

1. Debone twelve larks; take the interior, except the gizzard; chop it and mix this hash with an equal amount of cooked forcemeat, a little grated nutmeg, a little salt and coarse pepper, and a little fine herbs
2. Place this forcemeat on a silver platter, with a strip of lard on top; place it on a gentle stove with a camp oven over it; once your forcemeat is cooked, put it in a casserole
3. Pour in about two skimming-spoonfuls of velouté and mix well with your forcemeat; fill the interior of your larks with this; give them the shape of a small ball or an oval if you wish; sew them with thread so they do not lose their shape
4. Place a piece of butter in a casserole; warm it gently; add your larks with a little salt, coarse pepper, and a little quatre épices; place them on a gentle fire for seven or eight minutes
5. Once well stiffened, drain them; prepare croutons made with thick-cut bread about an inch and a half thick; make an incision inside at a line from the edge; pass them through butter; when they have a nice color, remove them from the butter and drain them on white linen; hollow out the bread; put some of your forcemeat inside, and your lark from which you have removed the thread; leave a little space above your forcemeat to add some sauce
6. Fifteen minutes before serving, place your croutons garnished with larks on a tourtière and put them in the oven or under a not-too-hot camp oven; at the moment of serving, arrange your croutons on the plate and put a little italienne sauce in each one
