## Ingredients

- Fifteen mutton tongues
- Lard bardes
- Four carrots
- Four onions
- One onion stuck with two cloves
- Some veal slices
- Two bay leaves
- Thyme
- A bouquet of parsley and chives
- Medium lardons
- Broth or water
- Salt

## Directions

1. Put mutton tongues to soak to disgorge them
2. Rub and wash them carefully to remove clotted blood
3. Blanch them for one and a half quarter hours
4. Refresh them, drain them, dry them, and cut the cornet
5. You may lard them with medium, seasoned lardons
6. Put lard bardes, four carrots cut into pieces, four onions (one of which is stuck with two cloves), some veal slices, two bay leaves, a little thyme, and a bouquet of parsley and chives in a saucepan
7. Place your tongues on this seasoning
8. Cover them with lard bardes
9. Moisten them with broth
10. Braise them gently for five hours
11. If you have neither veal nor broth, arrange them as described and moisten with water and salt instead
12. Be careful that the liquid is not too abundant so the tongues develop flavor
