## Ingredients

- Pork head, boned entire
- Pork meat, fresh, fatty and lean
- Salt
- Peppercorns
- Pounded aromatic herbs
- Four spices
- Parsley and chives, chopped
- Pork bones
- Rinds of pork
- Eight to ten carrots
- Ten onions
- Eight bay leaves
- Seven to eight sprigs of thyme
- Basil
- Parsley and chives
- Six cloves
- Lard cut in long strips
- White cloth
- Water

## Directions

1. Bone the head entirely; take pieces of fresh pork meat, fatty and lean, that you put with your head
2. Season it with salt, peppercorns, pounded aromatic herbs, four spices, chopped parsley and chives; let it sit in a vessel for eight or ten days
3. When it has well absorbed the seasoning, drain it; gather all your pieces lengthwise in your head, such as the tongue, the fillets, the pieces of lard that you cut lengthwise; arrange them so that your head is filled, and regains its original form
4. With a trussing needle and string sew the opening where you have boned it, and tie it with string so that in cooking it does not deform; wrap it in white linen that you tie at both ends
5. Then place it in a braising pan with the bones of your head, some rinds, eight to ten carrots, ten onions, eight bay leaves, seven or eight sprigs of thyme, basil, a large bouquet of parsley and chives, six cloves, a large handful of salt, and some pork scraps if you have any, or other meat
6. Moisten your head cheese with water until it is covered; braise it eight to ten hours over a gentle fire
7. Test it before to see if it is cooked; and if your larding needle has difficulty entering, it is not cooked
8. When you remove it from the fire, leave your head cheese two hours in its cooking liquid; then remove it with another white cloth; press it with your two hands, always keeping its form, but only to extract any liquid that remained in it
9. Let it cool in its cloth: when it is quite cold, unwrap it, prepare it, and place it on a folded napkin on a plate; be careful to remove the strings attached to it
10. You may also chop the meats inside; but the head cheese is more generally esteemed this way: the pieces are more whole, the interior more marbled
