## Ingredients

- four or five lamb tails
- bouillon
- parsley
- chives
- two cloves
- half a clove of garlic
- half a bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- salt
- pepper
- six ounces of well-cleaned rice
- egg yolk, beaten

## Directions

1. Cook the lamb tails with bouillon, a bouquet of parsley, chives, two cloves, half a clove of garlic, half a bay leaf, thyme, basil, salt, and pepper over low heat.
2. Remove from heat and let drain and cool.
3. Wash six ounces of well-cleaned rice several times in lukewarm water, rubbing it with your hands.
4. Put the rice in a small pot with the bouillon used to cook the tails, strained through a sieve without removing the fat; if there is not enough bouillon, add more.
5. Cook the rice over low heat; it should remain quite thick without being overcooked.
6. When half cold, cover the bottom of the serving dish with a little rice and arrange the tails on top so they do not touch.
7. Cover them completely with the remaining rice, preserving the shape of each tail.
8. Glaze the top lightly with beaten egg yolk.
9. Place the dish on a bed of hot ashes with a tourte lid covered with good fire.
10. Let cook until the tails are a golden color and the rice forms a crust.
11. Tilt the dish slightly to drain the fat, wipe the edges, and serve.
