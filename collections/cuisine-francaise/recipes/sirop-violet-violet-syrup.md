## Ingredients

- 1/4 pound violets
- 1/2 setier boiling water
- 2.5 pounds sugar
- 1/2 setier water

## Directions

1. Put 1/4 pound of cleaned violets in a terrine and pour 1/2 setier of boiling water over them
2. Put something clean on the violets to keep them submerged in the water, cover them and place on hot ashes for two hours
3. Strain the violets through a cloth, pressing hard to extract the water
4. This quantity of violets should yield about 1 pint of liquid
5. If you have 1 pint, put 2.5 pounds of sugar in a pan with 1/2 setier of water, boil and skim
6. Continue boiling until when you dip your fingers in water and then in the sugar and redip in water, the sugar breaks clean
7. Then pour in your violet water, being very careful that the syrup does not boil
8. When well combined, put the syrup in a terrine, cover it and place on hot ashes for three days, maintaining the most even heat possible without being too hot
9. You will know the syrup is done by putting two fingers in it and drawing them together; if a thread forms that does not break, put it in bottles
