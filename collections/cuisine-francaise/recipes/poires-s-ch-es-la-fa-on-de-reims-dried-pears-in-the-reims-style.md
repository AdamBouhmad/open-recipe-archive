## Ingredients

- Rousselet or Doyenné pears
- Sugar
- Water

## Directions

1. Peel the pears from top to bottom, gather the stem slightly and cut off the small end.
2. Throw them into fresh water as you peel them, then bring them to boil until they yield under the fingers.
3. Remove them with a skimmer as they are done and throw them into fresh water.
4. Once drained, for every fifty pears, put one pound of sugar with a pint of water.
5. When the sugar is melted, put the pears in to let them steep for two hours.
6. Remove them and dress them on wicker baskets, round end up, to pass the night in an oven of gentle heat.
7. The next day, re-dip the pears in the sugar and return them in the same way to the oven, continuing this for four days, and the last time do not remove them until they are completely dry.
8. Keep them in a dry place as long as desired.
