## Ingredients

- one half-setier of cream
- one chopine of milk
- nearly one quarteron of sugar
- tea or tarragon or celery or parsley as desired
- five egg yolks
- a pinch of flour
- rennet or chicken gizzard skin

## Directions

1. Put in a saucepan one half-setier of cream and one chopine of milk, nearly one quarteron of sugar
2. Bring to boil and reduce by a third
3. If it is a tea cream, add enough tea to make five cups; let it boil a moment and pass through a sieve
4. If you wish to make a tarragon cream, take two branches of tarragon that you will boil for half a quarter-hour in water, and then put it to boil in the cream; leave it only long enough to give the taste, and remove it promptly, for fear the taste does not dominate
5. Do the same for celery and parsley cream
6. The cream being passed through a sieve, dilute five egg yolks with a pinch of flour, and cook it in a bain-marie as the coffee one
7. If you wish to serve them cold, put no eggs or flour in them; after they are passed through a sieve and the cream is lukewarm, dilute rennet or chopped chicken gizzard skins in it, pass it through a sieve and put it in the dish you must serve, which you put on some hot ashes; cover with a lid in which you also put some hot ashes, leave until the cream is set, and put it in the cold until you serve
