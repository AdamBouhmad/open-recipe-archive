## Ingredients

- filet (beef filet)
- gros lard (large lard)
- beurre (butter)
- quatre carottes (four carrots)
- cinq oignons (five onions)
- deux feuilles de laurier (two bay leaves)
- bouquet de persil et de ciboules (bouquet of parsley and chives)
- trois clous de girofle (three cloves)
- gelée ou bouillon (aspic or broth)
- papier beurré (buttered paper)
- espagnole (espagnole sauce)
- consommé (consommé)
- farine (flour)

## Directions

1. Lard your filet as the previous one and lard it with large lard underneath
2. Tie it in such a way that it forms a spiral
3. Butter the bottom of your casserole and place your filet in it with four carrots, five onions, two bay leaves, a bouquet of parsley and chives, and three cloves
4. Add a spoonful of aspic or broth and a sheet of buttered paper cut in a round shape
5. Bring your filet to a boil; when it releases some broth, place it over low heat and place coals also on the lid of your casserole
6. Check from time to time, ensuring your filet takes on a beautiful color
7. It takes two hours to cook, and the cooking liquid must reduce to a glaze so your filet is glazed when you remove it
8. Remove your vegetables from the casserole along with the fat
9. Add four spoonfuls of espagnole and one of consommé to your casserole and detach the glaze from your filet
10. Strain your sauce through cheesecloth and pour it under your filet
11. If you lack sauce, glaze your filet with the glaze in your casserole; add about a teaspoon of flour that you dissolve with your glaze; add also a glass of broth; bring your sauce to a boil and strain it through cheesecloth; pour it over your filet and ensure your sauce has good seasoning
