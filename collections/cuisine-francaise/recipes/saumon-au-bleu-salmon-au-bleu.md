## Ingredients

- Salmon
- Wine, eight or ten bottles
- Carrots, seven or eight
- Onions, sliced
- Cloves, four
- Bay leaves, six
- Thyme, a little
- Salt
- Parsley, a handful in sprigs
- Butter, a good piece
- Flour, three tablespoons
- Fish or veal stock, one tablespoon each of court-bouillon and blond
- Pepper
- Gherkins, diced
- Anchovies, eight or ten
- Capers
- Pickled nasturtiums

## Directions

1. Empty your salmon without cutting the belly; wash and dry it well.
2. Place it in a fish kettle; add eight or ten bottles of wine, seven or eight carrots, onions cut into slices, four cloves, six bay leaves, a little thyme, salt, and a handful of parsley in sprigs; your fish must be immersed in the court-bouillon.
3. Simmer for two hours.
4. When you want to serve it, let it drain; place a napkin on your plate and the salmon on top, with parsley around it.
5. If you serve it as a relevé, put a good piece of butter in a saucepan; mix three tablespoons of flour and one tablespoon each of the fish court-bouillon and fish or veal stock into it.
6. Place your sauce on the fire, stirring until it boils, then add pepper and reduce it by half.
7. Strain it through fine silk into a saucepan.
8. Cut gherkins into dice, along with eight or ten anchovies, capers, and pickled nasturtiums, and put them in your sauce; keep it warm without boiling.
9. Mask your salmon with this sauce; you can also use anchovy butter instead of gherkins if you prefer.
