## Ingredients

- whole mutton from the shoulder defect to the leg
- salt
- coarse pepper
- aromatic spices
- meat from a leg of mutton
- lard strips
- mutton bones and leg bones
- six carrots
- six onions
- three cloves
- four bay leaves
- thyme
- parsley and chives bouquet
- bouillon or water
- buttered paper

## Directions

1. Cut the mutton in its entirety from the shoulder defect to the leg
2. Debone the ribs down to the spine
3. Ensure the saddle is perfectly well covered with fat and skin
4. Season the interior with salt, coarse pepper, and pounded aromatics
5. Put the meat from a leg of mutton inside, without leaving the skins, to fill it
6. Give the saddle its original form by rolling each side of the flanks and closing the meat inside so that the saddle is evenly rounded
7. Tie it with string so it does not lose its fine form
8. Put lard strips in the bottom of your braisière
9. Place your saddle there; if you wish, cook it in a pan so it is very white, or put the bones from your leg and ribs, six carrots, six onions, three cloves, four bay leaves, a little thyme, a strong bouquet of parsley and chives
10. Moisten it with bouillon or water if you do not have any, then add salt
11. Cover the saddle with lard strips and buttered paper
12. Braise it for five hours over and under fire
13. At the moment of serving, drain it, untie it, remove the skin from above; take care that the fat is bare
14. Sprinkle very finely chopped parsley on it, clear juice underneath, or glaze it
15. Put around it, if you wish, glazed onions or turnips, large carrots, potatoes, or tomato sauce
