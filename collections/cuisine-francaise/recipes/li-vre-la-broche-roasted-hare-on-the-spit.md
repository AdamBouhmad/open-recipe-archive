## Ingredients

- Skinned and gutted hare
- The hare's blood
- Hare liver
- Butter
- Shallots
- Parsley
- Thyme
- Bay leaf
- Flour
- White wine
- Bouillon
- Salt
- Pepper

## Directions

1. When your hare is skinned and gutted, sear it on a hot stove
2. The meat must be somewhat firm so the larding needle enters more easily
3. Remove from the stove and dip your hand in the hare's blood
4. Pass your hand over the back and thighs
5. Lard the hare from the neck to the end of the thighs
6. Leave a distance of one inch between the loins and the thighs
7. Roast on the spit for one hour to cook the young hare
8. For the sauce: pound the raw liver with the back of your knife
9. Pass it through with a small piece of butter, a little shallots, parsley branches, a little thyme and bay leaf
10. Put three-quarters of a tablespoon of flour that you have heated with your seasoning
11. Add a glass of white wine and two glasses of bouillon
12. Stir your sauce until it boils
13. Add salt and pepper, enough so that pepper dominates
14. Reduce your sauce to more than half
15. When reduced, pass it through a sieve while pressing lightly
16. Serve your sauce in a sauce boat beside your roasted hare dish
17. Alternatively, you can roast a young hare on the spit without larding it; instead bard it from the neck to the thighs
