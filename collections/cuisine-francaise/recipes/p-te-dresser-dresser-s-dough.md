## Ingredients

- two litrons of flour
- half an ounce of salt
- three quarterons of butter
- six egg yolks
- one glass of water

## Directions

1. Put two litrons of flour on a table
2. Make a hollow in the middle
3. Add half an ounce of salt, three quarterons of butter, six egg yolks, and one glass of water
4. Work the butter with the water, eggs and salt, ensuring the butter is well softened
5. Mix your flour little by little and combine everything together
6. When your dough is assembled, knead it with your fists until well kneaded
7. If there is not enough moisture, add more
8. Once you have kneaded twice, that is enough, as more kneading risks burning it, especially in summer, and the dough would no longer hold together and would break when working it
9. For larger quantities, for every quarter use one and a half pounds of butter, one ounce of salt, and ten egg yolks for half a bushel, three pounds of butter for a bushel, and so on
10. Your dough should be quite firm so as not to be caught off guard
11. Add one glass of water in several additions
12. If you use this dough for meat pies, make it softer
