## Ingredients

- Hen
- Lard strips
- Scarlet tongue
- Salt
- Pepper
- Four spices
- Veal slices
- Carrots
- Onions
- Cloves
- Parsley
- Chives
- Bay leaf
- Thyme
- Lemon juice
- Bouillon
- Red tongue
- Butter
- Nutmeg
- Velouté
- Consommé
- Meat glaze

## Directions

1. When the hen is singed and emptied, prepare lardons of fat lard with scarlet tongue
2. Season them with salt, pepper, and four spices, mixing well
3. Remove the bones from inside the breast of your hen
4. Cut off the legs and truss the thighs inside
5. Lard your hen correctly, alternating lard and tongue lardons from the breast to the kidneys, spacing them equally to form an oval pattern
6. Truss your hen making the breast dome nicely and give it a pleasant shape
7. Place lard strips in a saucepan with several veal slices, some carrots cut into slices, four onions (one stuck with two cloves), a bouquet of parsley and chives, and a bay leaf with a little thyme
8. Rub with lemon juice several lard strips with which you will cover your hen
9. Place it on your seasoning with a round of buttered paper to cover it
10. Pour in about a small spoonful of bouillon and a little salt
11. Put it on the fire an hour before serving and let it simmer
12. Take a piece about the size of an egg from the reddest of the tongue you have used
13. Pound it until pulverized
14. Add a piece of butter the size of half an egg, a little coarse pepper, and a little grated nutmeg; pound all this again
15. Put it in a saucepan and dilute with a spoonfuls of velouté
16. Heat your purée without boiling so it dilutes; pour in about two spoonfuls of consommé in which you will have melted a piece of meat glaze the size of half a walnut
17. When your purée is well diluted, strain it through a sieve, working it through; this purée should represent a sauce, not a purée, so it must be clear
18. Keep it warm in a bain-marie
19. At the moment of serving, drain your hen, untruss it, arrange it on your plate, and put your sauce underneath; ensure it is a good red color and well seasoned
