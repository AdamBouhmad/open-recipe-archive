## Ingredients

- one litron of flour
- warm water
- more than half an ounce of brewer's yeast or a small piece of bread yeast
- two litrons of flour
- one and a half pounds of butter
- ten eggs
- half a glass of water
- nearly one ounce of fine salt

## Directions

1. Put one litron of flour on a table and knead it with a little warm water and more than half an ounce of brewer's yeast, or if you do not have it, a small piece of bread yeast in its place
2. Wrap this paste in a cloth and let it rise in a warm place for a quarter hour in summer and one hour in winter
3. Then put two litrons of flour on a table with the paste you have made as leaven, one and a half pounds of butter, ten eggs, half a glass of water, and nearly one ounce of fine salt
4. Knead everything together with the palm of your hands three times
5. Dust with flour and wrap in a cloth, letting it rise for nine or ten hours
6. Cut this paste according to the size of brioche cakes you want to make
7. Moisten them by rounding them with your hands and flatten the top slightly
8. Glaze with beaten egg
9. Bake in the oven for half an hour for small ones and one and a half hours for large ones
