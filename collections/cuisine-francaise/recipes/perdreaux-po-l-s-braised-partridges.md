## Ingredients

- Three fresh partridges of the same size
- Three-quarters of a pound of butter
- Salt
- Juice of one lemon
- Coarse pepper
- Aromatic herbs, pounded
- Lard strips
- Lemon slices
- Three spoonfuls of Spanish sauce
- Three spoonfuls of game essence

## Directions

1. Select three fresh partridges of equal size, not skinned on the breast or bruised
2. Empty them through the vent, removing the intestines, gizzard, and liver to avoid making incisions elsewhere
3. Singe them lightly when well plucked
4. Mix three-quarters of a pound of butter with a little salt, the juice of one lemon, some coarse pepper, and pounded aromatic herbs, all well combined
5. Fill your partridges with this butter
6. Truss and tie them as you would a braised hen
7. Place strips of lard in a casserole, put the partridges on top
8. Cover them with lemon slices and strips of lard
9. Pour a poêle over them
10. Simmer for a good half hour
11. Drain them
12. At the moment of serving, remove the trussing strings and arrange on the platter
13. Place a fine crayfish between each partridge
14. Add three spoonfuls of Spanish sauce and three spoonfuls of game essence reduced by half, and pour under the partridges
15. If you do not have sauce or game essence, make a light roux and strain through silk sieve the cooking liquid from the partridges
16. Moisten it and add half a glass of white wine
17. If your sauce is too thin, reduce it until it is thick enough to sauce the partridges
18. Be sure to skim it and strain it through a fine sieve
19. Check that it is properly salted
