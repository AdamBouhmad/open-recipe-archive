## Ingredients

- Hare fillets
- Salt
- Pepper
- Laurel leaves
- Thyme
- Parsley in sprigs
- Chives whole
- One large glass of vinegar
- Butter
- Flour
- Herbs from the marinade
- Bouillon
- Vinegar from the marinade

## Directions

1. Trim and lard your fillets as in the previous recipe.
2. Place them in a terrine with salt, pepper, two laurel leaves, thyme, parsley sprigs, whole chives, and a large glass of vinegar.
3. Leave them to marinate for up to eight days.
4. When you wish to serve them, remove them from the marinade, trim them, drain them, and shape them as desired.
5. Place them in your sauté pan and coat them with warm butter so they are bathed in it.
6. At the moment of service, place your sauté pan over a high heat so your fillets do not sweat.
7. When they are stiffened on one side, turn them.
8. When you feel your fillets are firm to the touch, remove them, drain them, and arrange them on your plate.
9. Place a poivrade sauce underneath.
10. If you have no sauce, heat your butter until what is in the sauté pan begins to stick.
11. Remove three quarters of the butter.
12. Add a small spoonful of flour, which you stir with your butter.
13. Add some of the herbs from your marinade, a small pot-spoonful of bouillon, half of the vinegar that marinated your fillets, and enough pepper to dominate.
14. Reduce your sauce by half or more if it is too thin.
15. Strain it through a sieve into another pan.
16. Check the seasoning for salt and place it under your fillets.
