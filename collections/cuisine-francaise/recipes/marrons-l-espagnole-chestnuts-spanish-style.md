## Ingredients

- fifty chestnuts
- half a quarter pound of butter
- four spoonfuls of espagnole sauce
- two glasses of consommé
- bay leaf
- grated nutmeg

## Directions

1. Take fifty chestnuts (they should not be from Lyon, as they are not good for cooking).
2. Blanch them in hot water like almonds.
3. Take great care to remove all the second skin so they have no bitterness.
4. When well blanched, put them in a saucepan with half a quarter pound of butter, four spoonfuls of espagnole sauce, two glasses of consommé, a bay leaf, and a little grated nutmeg.
5. Boil the chestnuts for half an hour.
6. Then remove them from their sauce and put them in another saucepan.
7. Let the sauce reduce and pass it through a fine cloth over the chestnuts.
8. Keep them hot in a bain-marie.
9. At the moment of serving, arrange them on the dish with the sauce underneath.
10. You can also serve them with various sauces.
