## Ingredients

- two veal tails
- half a pound of bacon cut in slices
- half a large cabbage cut in four pieces
- a bouquet of parsley
- chives
- half a clove of garlic
- three cloves
- a small piece of nutmeg
- bouillon
- salt
- coarse pepper
- coulis or cooking liquid from the cabbage
- butter
- flour

## Directions

1. Cut the two veal tails in two and blanch them briefly with the bacon slices
2. Blanch the cabbage quarters separately for a quarter hour, then refresh in cold water and press well
3. Remove the cores and tie the cabbage pieces
4. Place the veal tails in a small pot with the tied bacon and cabbage, add the bouquet of parsley and chives, half a clove of garlic, three cloves, and a small piece of nutmeg
5. Moisten with bouillon, add a little salt and coarse pepper
6. Boil on a low fire until the veal tails are cooked
7. Remove everything from the pot to drain and wipe off the fat
8. Arrange the veal tails interspersed with cabbage, with the bacon on top
9. Make a sauce by heating coulis (or the strained, defatted cooking liquid from the cabbage) in a pan with a little butter and coarse pepper, thickened with flour if needed
10. Pour the sauce over the cabbage and meat and serve
