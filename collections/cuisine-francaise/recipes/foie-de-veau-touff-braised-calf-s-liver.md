## Ingredients

- Calf's liver (the blondest are best)
- Lard strips
- Four spices
- Parsley and chives, chopped
- Pepper
- Salt
- Carrots
- Onions
- Cloves
- Bay leaves
- Thyme
- White wine
- Poivrade sauce

## Directions

1. Take a calf's liver and lard it with thick lardons.
2. Season it with four spices, chopped parsley and chives, pepper, salt.
3. When your liver is larded, put lard strips in a braising pan or casserole.
4. Put your liver with four carrots, four onions (one of which is studded with three cloves), three bay leaves, a little thyme, a bouquet of parsley and chives, salt.
5. Moisten your liver with a bottle of white wine.
6. Cover it with lard strips and a round of paper.
7. Put it on a stove.
8. When it comes to a boil, place it on another braising pan with fire on the cover.
9. Let it simmer for two hours and you can serve it.
10. Add about four spoonfuls to degrease of poivrade sauce; strain the cooking liquid from your liver and add four spoonfuls of it.
11. Reduce your sauce by half and pour it under your liver.
12. If you do not have poivrade sauce, make a small roux; pass through a silk sieve the cooking liquid in which your liver cooked; moisten your roux with it.
13. Your sauce must be thin so you can reduce it by half.
14. Degrease it and place it under your liver.
