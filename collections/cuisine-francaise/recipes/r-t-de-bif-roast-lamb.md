## Ingredients

- lamb cut to the second rib on the flank
- larding bacon
- thread for trussing

## Directions

1. Cut your lamb up to the second rib of the flank, which makes half the lamb with the two thighs held together
2. Lard the saddle and part of the thighs with fine bacon
3. Give your saddle a well-rounded shape and place it on the spit, securing it with skewers or small metal pins
4. Also take care to secure your last ribs to the spit with a trussing needle so that your roast does not take on a bad shape and does not turn
5. Two hours are sufficient to cook it
6. You can serve the roast without larding it if you cover it with lard bacon, or you can do without that as well
