## Ingredients

- filet (sirloin)
- gros lardons (large lardons)
- épices (spices)
- thym (thyme)
- deux feuilles de laurier (two bay leaves)
- sel fin (fine salt)
- bardes de lard (bacon strips)
- deux tranches de veau (two veal slices)
- deux tranches de bœuf (two beef slices)
- carottes (carrots)
- oignons (onions)
- bouquet garni (bouquet garni)
- demi-bouteille de vin de Malaga ou trois quarts de bouteille (half bottle or three quarters of a bottle of Malaga wine)
- bouillon (broth)
- grande espagnole (espagnole sauce)

## Directions

1. Trim your filet as in previous recipes
2. Lard it with large lardons containing spices, a little thyme, two minced or crushed bay leaves, and fine salt
3. Tie the filet securely with string
4. Place bacon strips, two veal slices, and two beef slices in the bottom of a casserole with vegetables such as carrots, onions, and a bouquet garni
5. Place your filet in the casserole and cover with bacon strips
6. Add half a bottle or three quarters of a bottle of Malaga wine, depending on the size of your filet, and half a spoonful of broth
7. Bring to a boil; once it releases some broth, simmer gently over low heat for two and a half hours
8. When cooked, strain the cooking liquid through a silk sieve
9. Add three spoonfuls of espagnole sauce and pour the strained liquid into your sauce
10. Reduce to a glaze, meaning you produce only enough juice to sauce your filet
11. Remove and untie the filet, glaze it, and pour your reduction underneath
12. Alternatively, you may cook this filet by placing it between bacon strips with vegetables moistened with Malaga wine and a little broth
