## Ingredients

- one duck
- poultry forcemeat or godiveau forcemeat
- veal round the size of an egg
- beef fat twice the amount of veal
- parsley
- chives
- mushrooms
- two raw eggs
- salt
- pepper
- half a setier of cream

## Directions

1. Singe and clean the duck through the vent, and bone it entirely without piercing the skin.
2. Begin deboning from the vent, turning it inside out as you remove the bones.
3. Fill it halfway with poultry forcemeat or godiveau.
4. To make godiveau forcemeat: take veal round the size of an egg and twice as much beef fat, chop them together; add parsley, chives, and mushrooms all chopped, two raw eggs, salt, pepper, and half a setier of cream; mix well together and place in the body of the duck.
5. Tie it up so nothing escapes.
6. Cook it in a braise like beef tongue (page 29).
7. When cooked, wipe off the fat and serve with a good sauce or a ragout of chestnuts.
8. For the chestnut ragout: cook chestnuts with half a setier of white wine, a little coulis, and a pinch of salt, and serve as you see fit.
