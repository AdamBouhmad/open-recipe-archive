## Ingredients

- Young spinach
- Water
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Nutmeg, grated
- Butter
- Croutons

## Directions

1. Blanch young spinach, as old spinach does not suit this preparation.
2. Boil water in a large pot; add a handful of salt.
3. After washing the spinach well, put it in the pot; push it down well so it does not take on a smoky taste.
4. When it blends with the water, feel with your fingers to see if it yields; then refresh it as you would chicory.
5. Chop it and put it in a saucepan with salt, coarse pepper, and a little grated nutmeg.
6. With a wooden spoon, stir it over the fire; when the spinach is well heated, add a good piece of butter.
7. Mix it with the spinach without placing it on the fire, to prevent the butter from turning to oil.
8. Dress it on the plate with croutons around it.
