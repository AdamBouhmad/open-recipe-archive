## Ingredients

- Eight pheasant fillets
- Pheasant tenderloin fillets (filets mignons)
- Fine lard for larding
- Lard strips (bardes)
- Pheasant trimmings
- Several slices of veal
- Two carrots
- Four onions
- Two bay leaves
- Two cloves
- Buttered paper
- One glass white wine
- Two glasses of consommé
- Salt
- Espagnole sauce
- Butter
- Croûtons

## Directions

1. Remove eight fillets from the pheasant
2. Set aside the tenderloin fillets which you will sauté and arrange in the middle
3. Trim your fillets and lard them with fine lard
4. Place lard strips in a saucepan with the trimmings of your pheasants, several slices of veal, two carrots, four onions, two bay leaves, two cloves
5. Arrange your fillets well so they keep a beautiful form
6. Place over them a round of buttered paper, one glass of white wine and two glasses of consommé, a little salt
7. There should be no liquid over your fillets, but liquid up to the larding
8. Place them on the fire one hour before serving
9. When they boil, place them over gentle heat, and much heat on the lid so they glaze
10. At the moment of serving, drain them, glaze them, pass through silk sieve the liquid in which they cooked
11. Reduce it almost to a glaze
12. Pour over it three spoonfuls of espagnole sauce which you boil with your reduction
13. Pass it through cheesecloth and arrange your fillets flat on croûtons passed in butter and three lines thick
14. Pour your sauce underneath; you can also place lard strips on a baking pan, and your fillets with a glass of white wine, salt, a little coarse pepper, a little aromatic spices, a round of buttered paper, and put them in the oven or under the oven
