## Ingredients

- Two common chickens in good meat
- Butter
- Parsley
- Chives (ciboule)
- Bay leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- Two cloves
- Mushrooms
- Ham (optional)
- Flour
- Warm water
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Three egg yolks
- Cream or milk
- Lemon juice or vinegar

## Directions

1. Singe and pluck the chickens, then gut them; cut them into pieces and soak them in lukewarm water to disgorge them; also soak the livers after removing the bitter part, the gizzards which you split to remove the insides, the feet which you place on coals to remove the skin, you must cut the spurs, the necks of which you cut half the head
2. Drain the chickens well on a sieve or strainer
3. Put them in a casserole with a piece of good butter, bouquet of parsley, chives, a bay leaf, a little thyme, basil, two cloves, mushrooms, and a slice of ham if you have it; cook over good heat until there is almost no sauce left
4. Add a good pinch of flour and moisten with a little hot water, season with salt and black pepper, cook and reduce to little sauce
5. When ready to serve, add a liaison of three egg yolks diluted with cream or milk, thicken over heat without boiling so the sauce does not break, add lemon juice or a dash of vinegar
6. Arrange the fricassee with the giblets at the bottom, the thighs and wings on top, pour the sauce and mushrooms all over
