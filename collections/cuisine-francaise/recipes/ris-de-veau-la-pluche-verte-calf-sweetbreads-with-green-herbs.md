## Ingredients

- Three or four calf sweetbreads
- bouillon
- white wine
- parsley
- chives
- garlic
- clove
- bay leaf
- basil
- salt
- pepper
- verjuice
- butter mixed with flour

## Directions

1. Soak three or four calf sweetbreads in lukewarm water to disgorge them and blanch for a quarter hour in boiling water
2. Plunge them in fresh water, remove the cornet, keep the throat lobes, and place the sweetbreads and throat lobes in a pot with a little bouillon, a glass of white wine, a bouquet of parsley and chives, a clove of garlic, a clove, half a bay leaf and a few basil leaves
3. Add salt and pepper and cook until done, then pass the sauce through a sieve
4. Reduce the sauce if it is too thin, then add half a spoonful of verjuice with a piece of butter mixed with a pinch of flour the size of a walnut
5. Make the sauce bind over the fire until it has the consistency of double cream, add a good pinch of blanched parsley finely chopped
6. Dress the sweetbreads in the plate and pour the sauce over, serving as an hors-d'œuvre or side dish
