## Ingredients

- Chicken breast whites, cut in small dice
- A small piece of butter
- A little salt
- A little pepper
- A little grated nutmeg
- A piece of bread crumb
- Bouillon
- A little finely chopped parsley
- A cooked and cold veal breast, or butter in default
- Five or six egg yolks, according to the quantity of forcemeat

## Directions

1. Cut chicken breast whites into small dice
2. Put a small piece of butter in a saucepan with your raw chicken whites, a little salt, a little pepper, and a little grated nutmeg
3. Cook them on a low fire for ten minutes
4. Drain your whites and let them cool
5. Put a piece of bread crumb in the same saucepan with bouillon and a little finely chopped parsley
6. Stir it with a wooden spoon, mashing and reducing it to a paste
7. When your bouillon is reduced and your bread crumb is well softened, let it cool
8. Have a cooked and cold veal breast; if not available, use butter instead
9. Pound your chicken whites; when well pounded, pass them through a quenelle sieve and set aside
10. Pound your bread crumb the same way, pass it through the sieve and set apart
11. Pound your veal breast, pass it through the sieve and set to the side
12. Make three equal portions of chicken whites, bread crumb, and veal breast
13. Pound it all together
14. When you have pounded it for three quarters of an hour, add five or six egg yolks according to the quantity of forcemeat
15. Pound your yolks with your forcemeat as you add them
16. When your forcemeat is made, remove it from the mortar and put it in a terrine
17. Use it as needed, either with butcher's meat, poultry, or game; use the same procedure and also employ this forcemeat for the gravies
