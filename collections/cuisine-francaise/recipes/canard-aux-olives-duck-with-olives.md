## Ingredients

- duck
- olives
- Spanish sauce (espagnole)
- consommé or bouillon
- coarse pepper
- light roux
- silk sieve

## Directions

1. Truss and cook your duck as you would cook duck in a pan
2. Turn your olives, that is to say remove the flesh from the pit in a corkscrew manner
3. Keep them whole so they resume their original form
4. If you wish, let them boil in water, or put them in a saucepan
5. Put in four spoonfuls of Spanish sauce (espagnole), twice as much consommé or bouillon, a little coarse pepper
6. Cook your olives on a hot fire; when the sauce is reduced to one third, remove them from the fire
7. Keep them warm in a bain-marie
8. If you have no sauce, make a light roux, which you will moisten with the cooking liquid from your duck
9. Pass it through a silk sieve
10. If your sauce is not thick enough, add bouillon
11. At the moment of serving, drain your duck, remove the trussing string, arrange it on your plate, and put your ragout of olives underneath
