## Ingredients

- Thirty or forty onions
- Lard or butter
- Three to four pounds of pork fat, diced
- Parsley
- Chives
- Spices
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Four pints of blood
- One pint of cream
- Intestines, cleaned

## Directions

1. Cut thirty or forty onions into dice; remove the head and tail, and let them melt gently in white by passing them over the fire with lard or butter
2. When well melted, add three or four pounds of pork fat also cut into dice; combine with your onions
3. Add parsley and chives, spices, salt, and black pepper, stirring into four pints of blood
4. Add one pint of cream; mix everything together so that your fat does not remain in lumps
5. Have intestines that you will have washed, and funnel your mixture into your intestines; avoid trapping air inside
6. When your intestine is full, tie it at the end to the size you desire
7. Have a pot full of water whose heat is too hot to hold your finger in; place your boudin in it
8. Do not let the water boil, to avoid bursting it: test it; remove it when it begins to be firm, and when pricked it no longer runs blood
9. Then slice your boudin and grill it
