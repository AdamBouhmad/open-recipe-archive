## Ingredients

- five quarterons of sugar
- half a setier of water
- half a pound of orange blossoms
- alum
- juice of one lemon

## Directions

1. Put five quarterons of sugar in a pan with half a setier of water. Bring to a boil and skim well. Continue boiling until dipping the skimming spoon in and shaking it over the sugar, blowing through the holes produces little sparks of sugar. Then add your orange blossom prepared as follows: take half a pound of peeled orange blossoms and boil for half a quarter hour in water. Remove this water from the fire and add a small pinch of alum. Put fresh water on the fire. When it boils, add the juice of one lemon, put your orange blossom in it, and remove with the skimming spoon letting it drain. Boil it in the lemon water until it is tender under the fingers. Then put it for half an hour in fresh water with a little lemon juice. Then press the orange blossom in a cloth to remove the water, and pound in a mortar to make jam. Put this jam in the sugar to blend them together, pouring the sugar several times over a very small fire, without letting it boil or even simmer. Put in pots and sprinkle a little fine sugar on top when cold.
