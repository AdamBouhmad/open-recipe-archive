## Ingredients

- mortified lamb square
- fine lard
- two or three cucumbers
- vinegar
- salt
- butter
- ham slice
- flour
- jus
- bouillon
- coulis

## Directions

1. Have a mortified lamb square that you trim neatly, that is to say, remove the skin and nerves found on the filet, and cut the bones at the base of the ribs.
2. Lard the top of the filet with fine lard; you may also put it on the spit without being larded, but this method is more common.
3. Take two or three cucumbers that you peel, hollow out, and cut into dice.
4. Let them marinate for two hours with a small spoonful of vinegar and a little salt.
5. Then press them with your clean hands to remove all the water and put them progressively in a saucepan with a piece of butter and a slice of ham.
6. Pass them on the fire, turning them often with a spoon, until they begin to color, then add a pinch of flour and moisten them with half jus and half bouillon.
7. If you have no jus, blanch the cucumbers before passing them.
8. Cook on low heat and degrease them when they are cooked.
9. Add a little coulis to bind them, and if you have no coulis, add a little more flour before moistening them.
10. When your ragout is finished, serve it under the lamb square.
11. If you wish to serve a ragout of lamb dishes with cucumbers, cut the cucumbers into very thin slices and let them marinate and cook in the same fashion as above.
12. When the ragout is finished and tastes good, take a lamb leg cooked on the spit that has been removed from the table, cut it into very thin filets, and warm it in the ragout without letting it boil.
13. Do the same with the leftovers of a lamb square and a lamb shoulder, and indeed all sorts of meats that have been cooked on the spit.
