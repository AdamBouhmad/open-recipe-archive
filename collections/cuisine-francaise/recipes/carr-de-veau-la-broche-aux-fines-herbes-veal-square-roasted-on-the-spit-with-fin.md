## Ingredients

- veal square fillet
- parsley
- ciboule (chives)
- a bit of fennel
- mushrooms
- bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- two shallots
- salt
- coarse pepper
- grated nutmeg
- a bit of oil
- white paper
- butter
- a bit of juice
- two spoonfuls of verjuice
- butter kneaded with a pinch of flour
- egg yolk
- breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Lard the entire fillet of a veal square after having trimmed it properly
2. Place it in a terrine to marinate for three hours with parsley, ciboule, a bit of fennel, mushrooms, a bay leaf, thyme, basil, two shallots, all chopped very fine, salt, coarse pepper, grated nutmeg, and a bit of oil
3. When it has taken on flavor, spit the square, put all its seasoning on top, and wrap it in two sheets of white paper well buttered
4. Tie it so that the small herbs cannot escape, and cook it over a small fire
5. When cooking is done, remove the paper and scrape off with a knife all the small herbs that cling to the paper and the meat, to put them in a saucepan with a bit of juice, two spoonfuls of verjuice, butter kneaded with a pinch of flour, a bit of salt, and coarse pepper
6. Make the sauce bind over the fire to serve under the square
7. Before binding the sauce, melt a bit of butter and mix in an egg yolk to rub on top of the square and bread it with breadcrumbs, and brown it nicely; you can also serve it without being breaded if you are indifferent about appearance
