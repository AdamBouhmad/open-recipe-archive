## Ingredients

- Sheep tails
- Veal sweetbreads
- Artichoke bottoms
- Chestnuts
- Veal quenelles
- Mushrooms
- Butter
- Espagnole sauce
- Consommé
- Flour
- Bouillon
- Jus

## Directions

1. When your tails are cooked as described for braised tails, keep them somewhat firm so they do not break in your ragout
2. Cut them in two
3. Put them in a saucepan with veal sweetbreads and four artichoke bottoms cut in quarters, or else chestnuts and a few veal quenelles
4. Turn a handful of mushrooms that you sauté in butter
5. Drain them
6. Put with your mushrooms a spoonful of espagnole, and half of consommé
7. Reduce your sauce by one third and pour it over your ragout
8. If you do not have sauce, put with your mushrooms two spoonfuls of flour, moisten it with bouillon, and add a little jus to give color
9. Reduce it until it is bound enough to put with your ragout
10. You may serve it in a terrine, in a silver saucepan, or in a hot pâté
