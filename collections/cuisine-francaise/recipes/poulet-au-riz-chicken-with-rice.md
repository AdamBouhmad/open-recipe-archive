## Ingredients

- One chicken
- Rooster crests and kidneys
- Mushrooms
- Lard slices
- Veal rouelle slices
- Two carrots
- Three onions
- Two cloves
- One bay leaf
- Thyme
- Consommé
- One pound of rice
- Bouillon
- Pâte round
- Butter
- Velouté
- Three eggs
- Coarse pepper

## Directions

1. When your chicken is singed and gutted, cut off the legs and truss the thighs inside; debone the interior of the breast of your chicken without damaging the exterior; put inside a ragout of rooster crests and kidneys, mushrooms, and add a strong liaison to your ragout; when it is cold, put it in your chicken; take care to wrap the ends well with lard slices so your ragout does not escape; when your chicken cooks, pour lemon juice over its breast; cover it with lard slices; put some also in the bottom of your saucepan with some slices of veal rouelle, two carrots cut in slices, three onions, two cloves, a bay leaf, a little thyme, two tablespoons of consommé; place your chicken on this seasoning; put it on the fire: three-quarters of an hour is enough to cook it if it is young; pass through a silk sieve the cooking liquid in which your chicken cooked; add to it one pound of well-cleaned and well-washed rice; put good bouillon in case there is not enough; boil your rice for a quarter of an hour; leave it to drain on a hair sieve; butter a saucepan large enough to contain it; put a round of pâte in the middle, your rice over it; place your chicken so it cannot be seen; fill your saucepan with rice; one hour before serving, make it take color like a rice cake; put fire on the lid: at the moment of serving, turn over your rice loaf; remove the round of pâte, and clear out the interior of your loaf without damaging the edges or bottom; pour in a full tablespoon of reduced velouté, mushrooms, the rest of your chicken ragout, coarse pepper, a liaison of three eggs, a piece of butter the size of an egg; put them in the interior of your rice loaf; make sure your sauce is properly salted.
