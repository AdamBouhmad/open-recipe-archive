## Ingredients

- Seven or eight calf's tails
- Butter
- Flour, four spoonfuls
- Beef consommé, four spoonfuls
- Mushrooms, turned
- Parsley and chives
- Veal quenelles
- Small pieces of veal shoulder
- Veal sweetbreads, in pieces
- Butter for sautéing the sweetbreads
- Velouté, one spoonful
- Egg yolks, five

## Directions

1. Cut your tails at the joints; only the section from the thick end to the middle of the tail yields four or five pieces
2. Sauté them in a good piece of butter without letting them brown, as this ragout must be white
3. When your meat is well browned in the butter, add four spoonfuls of flour and blend it with your butter and tail
4. Then add four spoonfuls of consommé
5. Stir your ragout until it boils; take care to skim it well
6. Add a portion of turned mushrooms, a bouquet of parsley and chives
7. Cook your ragout and degrease it when it is three-quarters cooked
8. When your ragout is completely cooked, remove the pieces of tail with a slotted spoon, along with your mushrooms, and place them in another saucepan
9. Pour one spoonful of velouté into the sauce of your tails
10. Reduce this sauce; when you see it is sufficiently reduced and well thickened, strain it through a sieve
11. Poach veal quenelles in your ragout; drain them at the moment of serving and place them in your terrine
12. Mix in small pieces of veal shoulder and pieces of veal sweetbreads that you have sautéed in butter; drain them when cooked and place them in your terrine
13. Add to your tail ragout a liaison of five egg yolks
14. Stir it gently on the stove until your ragout is thickened; be careful it does not boil for fear it will curdle
15. Pour it into your terrine over your garnishes, which you will have kept warm; the sauce of your ragout must be abundant so that all the garnishes are bathed in it
