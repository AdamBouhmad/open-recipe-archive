## Ingredients

- One well-cleaned pig head, boned
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Thyme
- Bay leaf
- Basil
- Six cloves
- Two pinches of coriander
- Half a nutmeg
- Two garlic cloves
- Four shallots
- Half a handful of parsley
- Vinegar
- Ham slices
- Bouillon
- One pint white wine
- Onion
- Roots

## Directions

1. Take a well-cleaned pig head and bone it completely
2. Remove all the meat and lard without cutting the skin; cut the meat into very thin fillets
3. Do the same with the lard
4. Place lean meat on one plate, fat on another
5. Cut the ears into fillets as well
6. Season all meat on both sides with salt, coarse pepper, thyme, bay leaf, basil, six cloves, two pinches of coriander, and half a nutmeg, all finely chopped
7. Add two minced garlic cloves and four minced shallots
8. Add half a handful of parsley leaves left whole
9. Place the pig head skin in a round pot
10. Arrange all your meat fillets, adding a bit of vinegar and some ham slices if available, with neatly arranged parsley leaves
11. Continue this layering until complete
12. Sew the skin closed, pleating it like a purse
13. Wrap in a white cloth and tie tightly with string
14. Place in a pot sized exactly to fit, and cook for six to seven hours with bouillon, one pint white wine, onion, roots, thyme, bay leaf, basil, one garlic clove, salt, and pepper
15. Once cooked, drain and place in a vessel exactly its size and well-rounded
16. Cover with a lid and place a very heavy weight on top until cold, to give it the shape desired
17. Serve as a large entremets
