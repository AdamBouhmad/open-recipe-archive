## Ingredients

- Six or seven quail
- Butter
- Bouquet of parsley
- Chives
- Half a clove of garlic
- Two cloves
- Half a bay leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- Mushrooms
- Flour
- White wine
- Bouillon
- Salt
- Pepper
- Sweetbread of veal
- Quail livers
- Parsley
- Chives
- Breadcrumbs
- Butter
- Salt
- Pepper
- Egg yolks

## Directions

1. Flame and clean six or seven quail, pass them in a saucepan over the fire with a piece of butter, a bouquet of parsley, chives, half a clove of garlic, two cloves, half a bay leaf, thyme, basil, and mushrooms.
2. Add a good pinch of flour, moisten with white wine, bouillon, and juice as needed to give color, salt, and coarse pepper.
3. At half the cooking time, add a sweetbread of veal blanched and cut into large dice, finish cooking and reduce the sauce to the point of a bound sauce.
4. For the gratin: chop the quail livers with parsley and chives, mix with a little breadcrumbs, a piece of butter, salt, coarse pepper, and two egg yolks.
5. Place this forcemeat in the bottom of the serving dish and put it over a small fire until the forcemeat is gratinéed.
6. Serve the ragout over it.
