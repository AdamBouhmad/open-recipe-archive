## Ingredients

- Two medium cabbages
- Water
- Salt
- Half pound veal, minced
- One pound pork fat
- Coarse pepper
- Four-spice
- Aromatics
- Seven or eight egg yolks
- Lard strips
- Veal slices
- Ham
- Three carrots
- Four onions
- Thyme
- Bay leaf
- Two cloves
- Bouillon
- Spanish sauce or light roux

## Directions

1. Blanch two medium cabbages in water and salt; when they have been twenty minutes in boiling water, refresh them and let them drain; remove the hearts.
2. Chop half a pound of veal; add one pound of pork fat, salt, coarse pepper, a little four-spice, and a little crushed aromatics; when all is well chopped, add seven or eight egg yolks that you will mix well with your stuffing.
3. Put the stuffing inside each cabbage; tie them well.
4. Arrange lard strips in the bottom of a saucepan, some veal slices, a little ham, three carrots, four onions, a little thyme, a bay leaf, two cloves; put the cabbages on top and cover them with lard.
5. Moisten them with bouillon from the bottom of the pot; add a little salt and pepper; let them simmer for an hour and a half.
6. When they are cooked, drain them on white cloth; extract the juice by pressing them a little; then untie them and give them an agreeable shape; you can dress them on the plate; glaze them and put Spanish sauce underneath; if you have no sauce, make a light roux, moisten it with the cooking liquid, and pass it through silk strainer.
