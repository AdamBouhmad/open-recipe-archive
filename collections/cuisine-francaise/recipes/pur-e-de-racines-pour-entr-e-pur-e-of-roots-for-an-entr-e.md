## Ingredients

- thirty carrots
- seven or eight onions
- half a pound of butter
- a piece of sugar the size of a walnut
- good bouillon
- four degreasing spoonfuls of velouté

## Directions

1. When your carrots are clean, cut them into slices
2. Place half a pound of butter in a pot
3. Add seven or eight onions from which you have removed the top and bottom, cut them in four forming a circle
4. When your butter is melted, add your vegetables
5. Stir them so they do not stick
6. When you see your vegetables are slightly softened, moisten them with good bouillon
7. Add a piece of sugar the size of a walnut
8. Let your purée simmer for three hours
9. Test with two fingers if your vegetables crush easily
10. Remove your purée from the fire to place it in your sieve
11. Drain the liquid
12. Crush your vegetables and pass them through a sieve
13. Moisten it from time to time to help it pass
14. Your purée must not be too thin and must not boil for a long time if you want it not to become acrid
15. When your purée is in the pot, add four degreasing spoonfuls of velouté
16. If you have any remaining liquid from your vegetables, pour it into your purée
17. Reduce it while skimming and degreasing until it is thick enough to mask your entrées
