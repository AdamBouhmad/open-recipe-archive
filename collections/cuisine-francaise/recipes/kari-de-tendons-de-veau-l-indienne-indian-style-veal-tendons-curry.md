## Ingredients

- Veal tendons (from one breast)
- Butter (3 quarters pound)
- Saffron or turmeric (half a spoonful of powder)
- Small hot pepper (10 cloves)
- Salt pork, cut in flat squares (1 pound)
- Bay leaves (2)
- Flour (4 spoonfuls)
- Bouillon
- Mushrooms
- Cloves (2) in an onion
- Artichoke bottoms
- Small onions
- Rice (1.5 pounds)
- Water
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg

## Directions

1. Cut and prepare your tendons as in the blanc method
2. Put butter, saffron or turmeric powder, small hot pepper, salt pork cut in flat squares, and bay leaves in a pot
3. Sauté your tendons with butter and pepper until well browned
4. Add four spoonfuls of flour and mix with the tendons and butter
5. Moisten the tendons with bouillon; there should be plenty of sauce
6. Add mushrooms and two cloves stuck in an onion, which you will remove when the curry is cooked
7. When the ragoût is half cooked, add artichoke bottoms that are three-quarters cooked and small onions
8. Do not degrease this ragoût because of its richness
9. For the rice bread: wash one and a half pounds of rice in five or six warm waters
10. Bring water to a boil in a pot; put the rice in to blanch for ten minutes
11. Drain on a sieve
12. Butter a pot of the right size to hold the rice; fill it with rice
13. Put it on a gentle heat with fire on the lid and around it so the rice dries, forms into bread, and takes color
14. At the moment of serving, pour the ragoût into a deep dish such as a tureen or salad bowl; turn over the rice bread and place on a plate beside the curry, as rice is placed on the plate where curry is served
15. If you do not have bouillon to moisten the curry, use water; then add salt, pepper, and a little grated nutmeg
16. The curry must be very yellow
17. Put nothing in the rice
