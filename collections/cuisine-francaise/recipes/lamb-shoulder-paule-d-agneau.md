## Ingredients

- lamb shoulder
- tendons from breast
- cutlets
- bread crumbs
- butter
- salt
- coarse pepper
- sauce with skewers or seasoning
- egg yolks

## Directions

1. From the spitted lamb shoulder, remove the meat and mince it for a blanquette
2. Ensure there is no skin or nerves
3. When the blanquette is prepared, keep it warm in a bain-marie
4. Meanwhile, prepare the tendons from the breast: leave a small bone from the chest
5. Coat with skewer sauce if available, or season with fine salt and coarse pepper
6. Dip in warm butter, place in breadcrumbs, giving them a nice shape
7. They should be slightly larger than the cutlets; place on a baking sheet
8. Alternatively, bread the tendons in English style and fry them
9. Prepare the cutlets: cut and trim; season with salt and coarse pepper
10. Place in a sauté pan with warm butter over them for serving
11. At service time, grill the breast tendons gently over low fire
12. Sauté and glaze the cutlets
13. Arrange in crowns alternating tendons and cutlets
14. When the crown is formed, place the blanquette in the middle
