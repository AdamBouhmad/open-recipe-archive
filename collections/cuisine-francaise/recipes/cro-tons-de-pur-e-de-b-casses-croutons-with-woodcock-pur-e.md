## Ingredients

- Three or four woodcocks
- Cooked lard the size of one and a half eggs
- Aromatic herbs
- White wine
- Parsley
- Bay leaf
- Clove
- Bouillon
- Velouté sauce
- Bread for croutons

## Directions

1. Roast three or four woodcocks on the spit; when cooked, let them cool
2. Remove the flesh and interior of your woodcocks and place them in the mortar with cooked lard the size of one and a half eggs and a little pounded aromatic herbs
3. Put your trimmings in a casserole with a glass of white wine, a little parsley leaves, a bay leaf, a clove, two glasses of bouillon, and about six spoonfuls of velouté sauce
4. Reduce this sauce by half and strain it through cloth, pressing it lightly
5. When it is cold, pound the flesh of your woodcocks and add this sauce to it
6. If your purée is difficult to pass, moisten it with a little consommé
7. Place it in a casserole and keep it warm without boiling, or use a bain-marie
8. Make oval-pointed croutons, thick about an inch and a half; before browning them
