## Ingredients

- Two young rabbits, cut into equal pieces
- Three-quarters pound of butter
- One pound of small bacon, cut into small square flat pieces
- Two teaspoons of Indian saffron or turmeric
- Ten small hot peppers, pounded with a little salt
- Two bay leaves
- Two cloves
- Three spoonfuls of flour
- Bouillon or water
- Salt
- Mushrooms
- Small onions
- Artichoke hearts, if available
- Eggplants
- Haricots verts, cauliflower, tomatoes, cucumbers, and other vegetables as in India
- Five or six eggs for liaison

## Directions

1. Cut two young rabbits into equal pieces
2. In a casserole, heat three-quarters pound of butter
3. Add one pound of small bacon cut into small square flat pieces and brown it in the butter
4. Add two teaspoons of Indian saffron or turmeric
5. Add ten small hot peppers pounded with a little salt
6. Add two bay leaves and two cloves
7. Once all the seasoning is well browned in the butter, add the rabbit pieces, which should be well dried to prevent blood
8. Remove the lungs
9. Brown the rabbit well
10. When the pieces are firm, add three spoonfuls of flour and mix well with the ragout
11. Moisten with bouillon or water
12. Add salt; there must be plenty of liquid so it can cook on high heat and reduce
13. Add mushrooms
14. When three-quarters cooked, add small onions, artichoke hearts if available, and eggplants
15. In India, all sorts of vegetables are added: haricots verts, cauliflower, tomatoes, cucumbers, etc.
16. When the curry is cooked, it must be swimming in sauce and fat; consequently, do not degrease it
17. You can serve it in this state, or if you wish, remove the meat and garnish and place them in the dish for serving
18. Make a liaison of five or six eggs in the sauce
19. Stir the sauce over the fire without letting it boil, as it would break
20. When thickened, pass it through a sieve over the ragout
21. Check that it is well spiced and well seasoned
22. Serve with rice bread in water alongside the curry dish, as the person serving the ragout places rice bread on the plate
