## Ingredients

- suckling pig
- veal liver
- lard
- sage
- four spices
- aromatics
- salt
- pepper
- large lardons
- white cloth
- lemon
- bay leaves
- good white wine
- bouillon

## Directions

1. Scald your pig; singe it and debone it up to the head which you leave whole
2. Have a nice light veal liver which you will chop
3. Put one pound of lard for one pound of liver
4. Mix in a little ground sage, a little of the four spices, a little ground aromatics, salt, coarse pepper
5. When everything is well chopped, put this forcemeat in the body of your pig
6. Have large lardons which you will put surrounded by forcemeat in the limbs and along the spine of the back of your pig
7. Fill it well with this forcemeat; sew the skin of the belly so that it has a firm appearance
8. Wrap it in a white cloth, tie it up, and rub it with lemon
9. Put in your pot a little sage, 4 bay leaves
10. Cover the back with lard, and wrap everything in a white cloth, of which you tie both ends
11. Mark a pan which you moisten with half good white wine, half bouillon, which you pour over the pig that you will have put in a braising pan
12. Let it simmer for three and a half hours on a low fire, barely boiling
13. When it is cooked, remove it and leave it one hour in its cooking liquid
14. Take it out and press it gently; try to preserve its shape
15. Let it cool completely
16. Unwrap it; tidy it up; put a napkin on a plate, and arrange your suckling pig on top
