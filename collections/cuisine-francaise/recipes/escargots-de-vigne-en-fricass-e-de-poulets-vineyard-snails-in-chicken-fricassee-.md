## Ingredients

- Vineyard snails
- Wood ashes
- River water
- Butter
- Bouquet of parsley
- Ciboule
- One clove of garlic
- Two cloves
- Thyme
- Laurel
- Basil
- Flour
- Bouillon
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Egg yolks
- Cream
- Parsley
- Mustard
- Nutmeg

## Directions

1. Put a good handful of ashes in a medium pot with river water and bring to a boil
2. Throw in the snails and leave for a quarter hour
3. When they come easily out of their shells, remove them to lukewarm water to clean
4. Place them again in clear water and boil for an instant, then drain
5. In a casserole, put a piece of butter with a bouquet of parsley, ciboule, one clove of garlic, two cloves, thyme, laurel, basil, and salt
6. Heat gently and coat the snails lightly with flour mixed with a little salt and pepper
7. Moisten with bouillon and a little white wine
8. Let cook until the snails are tender and the sauce is reduced
9. When serving, add three yolks of eggs mixed with a little cream and finely chopped parsley
10. Bind without boiling and add a little white mustard with a pinch of nutmeg
