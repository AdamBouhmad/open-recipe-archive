## Ingredients

- frogs, about fifty
- butter, a good piece
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- bread crumb, tender, weighing a quarter pound
- milk or broth

## Directions

1. You cut the head of the frog; you pull the body from its skin, observing to remove the entrails; the thighs and the neck, which you will make disgorge, must be very clean; you need about fifty; you put them in a saucepan with a good piece of butter, salt, coarse pepper, a little nutmeg; you sauté your frogs on the fire; when they have been on a good fire for ten minutes, you then put them on a gentler heat for half an hour so that they finish cooking; you must drain them and put them cooked in a mortar; you add a piece of tender bread weighing a quarter pound; you soak it in milk or broth; you pound it all together; when you have pounded it for a quarter hour, and your frogs are well in paste, you put them in a saucepan, and you thin them with the juice they have rendered; you pass it all through a fine sieve; if this were not liquid enough, you will take broth that you intend for your soup; pass your purée to make it liquid, and put it on gentle heat; try that it does not boil: you will have bread crusts that you prepare as for bread soup; you will boil a little broth, which you will pour over your crusts a quarter hour before serving; and at the moment of serving your soup you will pour your frog purée over your bread; try that your soup is neither too thick nor too clear. This soup is made with fat as well as lean. (See Lean Broth.)
