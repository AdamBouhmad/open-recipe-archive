## Ingredients

- Cooked cod
- Butter, a good piece
- Flour, one small tablespoon
- Salt
- Pepper
- Nutmeg, grated, a little
- Parsley, finely chopped and washed
- Cream, three-quarters of a glass of good cream
- Breadcrumbs
- Parmesan, a little
- Butter, melted

## Directions

1. Cook the cod as in the preceding recipe.
2. Cut it into small pieces and remove the bones; place them in a saucepan.
3. Place a good piece of butter in a saucepan; add one small tablespoon of flour, a little salt, pepper, a little grated nutmeg, and a little finely chopped and washed parsley.
4. Mix three-quarters of a glass of good cream with your seasoning.
5. Place your sauce on the fire, stirring constantly until it comes to a boil; it should be more bound or thicker than the preceding one.
6. Pour it over your cod and stir with a wooden spoon so it mixes with the sauce; then let it cool.
7. Glue a correct ring of breadcrumbs cut into small cubes around the edge of your plate, and arrange your cod so it forms a slight dome; smooth it with your knife.
8. Take breadcrumbs in which you place a little parmesan if you wish, and put it on your cod.
9. Have a small brush, dip it in lukewarm butter, and drain it over your breadcrumbs; coat once more.
10. Pour drops of butter a quarter hour before serving.
11. Put your dish on a stand with fire underneath; place a very hot field oven above it to give it a nice color.
12. When you serve, remove the croutons that are around the edge; also remove the butter turned to oil, and put croutons sautéed in butter to replace the others.
