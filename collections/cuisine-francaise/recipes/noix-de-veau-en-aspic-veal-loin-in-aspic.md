## Ingredients

- Veal loin, larded and glazed
- Aspic
- Cold ragout of rooster crests and kidneys
- Béchamel

## Directions

1. Prepare a larded and glazed veal loin the day before your dinner.
2. Pour melted aspic into your mold to the thickness of one inch.
3. When the aspic in your mold is well congealed, cut your veal into slices all of the same thickness and size.
4. Arrange your pieces in crowns, one on top of the other until your round is complete; there must be half an inch of distance between the meat and your mold.
5. Have a small cold ragout of rooster crests and kidneys in a béchamel, which you will place in the middle of the meat.
6. Put the mold in ice or in a cold place so that the gelatin sets firm.
7. Fill the mold with melted but cold aspic and let it congeal.
8. Put the mold in a cold place, and at the moment of serving, soak it in more than lukewarm water.
9. Remove it quickly into slightly warm water.
10. Dry your mold by putting your plate on top and turning it over.
11. If your aspic does not detach, heat a cloth and rub your mold with it.
12. Remove it gently.
13. If there is any melted gelatin on your plate, suck it out with a straw.
14. If your aspic was turned over some time before serving, it must be kept in a cold place.
