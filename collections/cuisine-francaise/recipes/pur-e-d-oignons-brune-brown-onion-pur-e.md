## Ingredients

- thirty or forty onions, depending on size
- one and a half quarter pounds of butter
- two tablespoons of espagnole sauce
- one large spoonful of broth
- flour (optional, if no sauce is available)
- a piece of sugar the size of a walnut

## Directions

1. Peel thirty or forty onions according to their size
2. Cut them in two from top to bottom
3. Remove the two ends to prevent your purée from being acrid
4. Put one and a half quarter pounds of butter in your saucepan
5. Cut your onions into slices forming half-circles
6. Put them in your butter
7. Cook your onions until they are blonde
8. Add two tablespoons of espagnole sauce and one large spoonful of broth
9. Let your purée reduce
10. When it is sufficiently thickened, pass it through cheesecloth
11. Do not let it boil anymore to prevent it from becoming acrid
12. Keep it hot in a bain-marie
13. If you have no sauce, put one spoonful of flour in when the onion is blonde, then add broth and a piece of sugar the size of a walnut
14. When your purée is reduced, pass it through cheesecloth
