## Ingredients

- One litron of flour
- A little water
- Half a glass of olive oil
- Quarter pound of lard
- Two egg yolks
- A little salt
- Meat or fish ragout (cooked, cooled, and in short sauce)
- Optional: leftover ragouts

## Directions

1. Place one litron of flour on a table and make a well in the center.
2. Add a little water, half a glass of olive oil, a quarter pound of lard, two egg yolks, and a little salt.
3. Knead this dough until it is very firm.
4. Take two-thirds of the dough and beat it with a rolling pin to the thickness of a small coin.
5. Place this dough in a small saucepan and spread it in the bottom and around the sides, taking care not to pierce it and ensuring it takes the shape of the pan well.
6. Add any cooked meat or fish ragout you wish, provided it is cooked, cooled, and in short sauce (you can even use this method to disguise all sorts of ragouts that have already been served).
7. Beat the remaining dough to the same thickness.
8. Cover the meat or fish with this dough and moisten the edges to seal them together, as you would for a pie.
9. Pinch all the edges to seal them together.
10. Cook in the oven or on the coals by burying the saucepan in hot ashes with fire underneath a cover.
11. When the pastry of your timbale is cooked, carefully turn it upside down into the serving dish.
12. Make a hole in the middle and serve.
