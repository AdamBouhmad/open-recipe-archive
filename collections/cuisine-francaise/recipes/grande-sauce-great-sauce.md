## Ingredients

- The underside of the veal round (four or one piece according to desired quantity of sauce)
- Two pot-spoonfuls of consommé
- Four or five large turned carrots
- Three large onions
- Four noix (pieces of veal)
- Four or five turned carrots
- Four or five onions, one of which is studded with two cloves
- Two pot-spoonfuls of consommé
- Roux (see Roux recipe)
- Mouillement (moistening liquid)
- Mushrooms
- A bouquet of parsley and chives
- Two bay leaves

## Directions

1. Take the underside of the veal round (four or one piece according to the quantity of sauce you want)
2. Put them in a large casserole with two pot-spoonfuls of consommé
3. Make them sweat over a somewhat hot fire
4. Be careful to remove as much foam as possible
5. Also be careful to wipe with a white cloth what sticks to the inside of your casserole so your sauce is not cloudy
6. When the consommé is reduced, prick your pieces of veal with your knife to draw out the juice
7. Put your casserole on a gentle fire so your meat and glaze attach slowly
8. When the glaze at the bottom of your casserole becomes blonde, remove it from heat and leave it covered
9. After ten minutes, fill it with grand bouillon in which you have put four or five large turned carrots and three large onions
10. Let your pieces of veal simmer for two hours
11. During this time, take the four noix and put them in a casserole with four or five turned carrots and four or five onions, one of which is studded with two cloves
12. Put two pot-spoonfuls of consommé in it
13. Put the casserole with your noix on a somewhat hot fire so the moistening reduces and becomes glaze
14. When you see your glaze is more than blonde, transfer the moistening from your pieces of veal that have boiled for two hours and pour it over your noix
15. Let them detach slowly, then make them start, that is to say boil
16. Make a roux and dilute it with the moistening in your casserole
17. When your roux is well dissolved, pour it over your veal noix
18. Add some mushrooms, a bouquet of parsley and chives, and two bay leaves
19. Be careful to foam well when it begins to boil
20. Skim again when you bind your sauce with your roux
21. Try to make your sauce neither too clear nor too thick; if it is too thick, add some moistening; if it is not thick enough, dilute a little roux and put it in your sauce
22. When your sauce has boiled an hour and a half, degrease it, that is to say remove the grease found on the back of your sauce
23. When your meat is cooked, pass your sauce through a sieve
