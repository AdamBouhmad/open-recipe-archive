## Ingredients

- ten or twelve red potatoes
- four chicken breasts
- six to eight raw egg yolks
- nutmeg
- coarse pepper
- optional: double cream
- bouillon or salted water with a little butter

## Directions

1. Cook ten or twelve red potatoes in hot ashes
2. When they are cooked, peel them; remove all the roasted part and anything hard, taking only the mealy part which you pound dry
3. Add four chicken breasts that you pound well together
4. When everything is well mixed and without lumps, add six or eight raw egg yolks, which you pound with your potatoes and fillets several times, along with a little nutmeg and coarse pepper
5. When everything is well combined, if your dough is too thick, add a little double cream so that you can place your dough on a spoon (like quenelles) or roll it like small balls
6. Poach them in bouillon or in salted water to which you add a little butter
7. After they have been poached (that is, cooked half an hour in boiling water), drain them
8. Put good bouillon in your tureen and add your quenelles
9. Ensure everything has proper seasoning
