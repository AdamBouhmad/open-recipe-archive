## Ingredients

- one macreuse (scoter duck)
- butter
- lean broth
- one glass red wine
- salt
- pepper
- parsley
- chives
- one garlic clove
- two cloves
- savory
- turnips
- flour
- bread crumbs

## Directions

1. Pluck the macreuse and gut it, brown it over coals like poultry you wish to put on the spit
2. Cut it into four pieces and put it in a saucepan to cook over the fire with a little butter for one hour
3. Put it in a small pot with lean broth, a glass of red wine, salt, pepper, a bouquet of parsley, chives, one garlic clove, two cloves, and a little savory
4. Cook over low heat for four or five hours
5. Cut turnips neatly and blanch them for half an hour in boiling water
6. Make a small roux with flour and butter, moisten it with the cooking liquid from the macreuse, and cook the turnips in it
7. When the turnips and macreuse are well cooked, cut bread pieces the size of a small coin, cook them over the fire with a little butter until they are golden colored
8. Arrange the macreuse, turnips, and croutons on the platter, pour the well-degreased and well-seasoned sauce over them
