## Ingredients

- butter the size of an egg
- two grated green lemons
- orange flower water, one full teaspoonful
- a quarteron of sugar
- a little salt
- a large half-setier of water
- flour as needed

## Directions

1. Put in a saucepan butter the size of an egg, two grated green lemons, one full teaspoonful of orange flower water, a quarteron of sugar, a little salt, and a large half-setier of water
2. Boil everything together for a moment
3. Add enough flour to make a well-bound and very thick paste
4. Stir it well on the fire with a wooden spoon until it sticks to the saucepan
5. Transfer it promptly to another saucepan and mix in two eggs at a time, stirring well with your wooden spoon
6. Continue adding two eggs at a time until your paste becomes soft without being runny
7. Spread it on a plate to the thickness of a finger with a knife
8. Heat some fat, not too hot
9. Dip the handle of a ragout spoon in the fat and take paste with it the size of a walnut
10. Drop it in the pan by striking the edge
11. Fry at a gentle fire, stirring constantly
12. When they are well-puffed and a beautiful color, serve them hot after dusting with fine sugar
13. Alternatively, make small mounds of paste the size of a walnut close together on white paper
14. Turn them into fat more than half-hot
15. When your fritters detach, remove the paper and fry them the same way
16. With this same paste, you can make small choux, with only the changes of putting more butter in the paste and cooking them in the oven
