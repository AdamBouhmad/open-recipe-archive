## Ingredients

- lamb rack
- bouillon
- parsley
- chives
- half a bay leaf
- thyme
- salt
- pepper
- turnips
- water
- jus
- coulis
- glaze

## Directions

1. Take a rack of lamb and cut it by ribs so they are thicker, with each one being thicker than just one rib
2. Cut them and pare them neatly, flatten them a little with the flat of the knife
3. Cook them in a casserole with bouillon, a bouquet of parsley and chives, half a bay leaf, a little thyme, and salt and pepper
4. Have turnips which you will blanch; cut them round, and blanch them about a quarter hour in water and remove them to fresh water
5. Put them to cook with bouillon and jus to color them a little
6. When they are almost cooked, add two or three spoonfuls of coulis
7. When your chops are cooked, degrease the sauce and pass it through a sieve to put it in the ragout of turnips
8. Take care that the ragout is neither too clear nor too thick; reduce it to the point of a glaze
9. Dress the chops in the serving dish that you will serve, and the ragout of turnips over them
