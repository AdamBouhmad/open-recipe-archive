## Ingredients

- A good pinch of tarragon leaves
- Two chickens
- Butter
- Salt
- Pepper
- Two pieces of good butter the size of a walnut
- Flour
- Two egg yolks
- Half a glass of juice
- Two spoonfuls of bouillon
- A dash of vinegar

## Directions

1. Blanch a good pinch of tarragon leaves for half a quarter hour
2. Drain in fresh water, chop finely after pressing, flame and pluck two chickens
3. Empty them and take the livers which you chop, and mix with a piece of butter, a quarter of the chopped tarragon, salt, and pepper
4. Put this small forcemeat into the body of the chickens
5. Place them in a saucepan, truss with their legs, and brown them in fat or butter
6. Put a strip of lard on the breast and cook them on the spit wrapped in paper
7. When cooked, put the remaining chopped tarragon in a saucepan with two pieces of good butter the size of a walnut, worked with a pinch of flour, two egg yolks, half a glass of juice, two spoonfuls of bouillon, and a dash of vinegar
8. Thicken the sauce without boiling, for fear the eggs turn
9. Serve over the chickens
