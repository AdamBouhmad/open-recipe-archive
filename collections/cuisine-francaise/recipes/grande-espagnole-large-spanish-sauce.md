## Ingredients

- 2 knuckles of veal
- 1 pheasant or 4 partridges
- half a ham knuckle
- 4 or 5 large carrots
- 5 onions, one of which studded with 5 cloves
- 1 bottle of dry Madeira white wine
- one pot spoon full of aspic
- a white roux
- 2 or 3 bay leaves
- a little thyme
- mushrooms
- a bouquet of parsley and chives
- a few shallots

## Directions

1. Place all the meats and vegetables into a casserole
2. Moisten your meats with a bottle of dry Madeira wine and one pot spoon full of aspic
3. Place your casserole on a high fire
4. When your moisture is reduced, place it on a gentle fire
5. When your glaze is more than blonde, remove your casserole from the fire and leave it outside for ten minutes so the glaze detaches well
6. Make a roux to dilute with the moistening liquid and pour it over your meat
7. Add 2 or 3 bay leaves, a little thyme, mushrooms, a bouquet of parsley and chives, and a few shallots
8. When your sauce boils, place it on the corner of the stove so it boils gently for 2 or 3 hours until your meats are cooked
9. When skimming and boiling, be careful to skim well what you place on the fire and wipe the interior of your casserole beforehand to avoid cloudy sauces
10. Ensure your sauce is neither too brown nor too pale, neither too clear nor too thick, and that it has a gentle salt
