## Ingredients

- five small pigeons
- bouillon
- half glass of white wine
- parsley
- chives
- half clove of garlic
- thyme
- bay leaf
- basil
- two cloves
- salt
- pepper
- veal round
- beef fat
- dried breadcrumbs
- milk or cream
- mushrooms
- three egg yolks
- egg whites
- caul fat
- egg
- coulis

## Directions

1. Have five small pigeons scalded and blanched briefly in boiling water
2. After tucking their feet into the body, cook them for half an hour with a little bouillon, half a glass of white wine, a bouquet of parsley, chives, half a clove of garlic, thyme, bay leaf, basil, two cloves, salt, and pepper
3. Let them cool
4. Make a forcemeat with veal round, beef fat, breadcrumbs dried with milk or cream, parsley, chives, and chopped mushrooms, salt, and pepper
5. Bind with three egg yolks and beaten egg whites
6. Wrap each pigeon with forcemeat and a piece of caul fat
7. Hold the caul in place with beaten egg, bread them, and cook them under a pastry lid cover for half an hour over low heat so the forcemeat has time to cook
8. Wipe them of their fat and serve with the sauce from the pigeon cooking that you degrease, pass through a tamis, add a little coulis to bind it
