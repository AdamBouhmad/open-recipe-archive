## Ingredients

- One pheasant
- One and a half pounds of truffles
- Half pound of lard
- Quarter pound of butter
- Quarter pound of oil
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Four spices
- Lard bacon

## Directions

1. Empty and singe your pheasant; break the two breast bones; it must be emptied by the pocket so as not to damage the croup of your pheasant
2. Have a pound and a half of truffles which you clean and peel, that is to say remove the top layer of your truffles which you set aside
3. Chop them very finely; place then a half pound of grated lard in a casserole, a quarter pound of butter, a quarter pound of oil which you will heat
4. Put your truffles cut into pieces about the size of a large nut; sauté them; add a little salt, a little coarse pepper, a little of the four spices
5. When the truffles have boiled in your lard for five minutes, put your chopped trimmed pieces in; let them cool, and put everything by the pocket in the body of the pheasant
6. Arrange a very thin slice of lard bacon on the place where your truffles entered, and put the skin back on top
7. Truss the legs as for a roasted hen; bind them well; fasten the skin of the pocket so the truffles do not come out while cooking
8. Put lard bacon in a casserole, your pheasant on top; cover it well with bacon; mark a braising pan without lemon which you will put on top; cover it
