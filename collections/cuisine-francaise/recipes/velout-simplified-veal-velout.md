## Ingredients

- 3 or 4 pounds of veal trimmings, either neck, breast, shoulder, leg, shank, cutlet trimmings or poultry scraps
- a few carrots
- onions
- bouquet of parsley and chives
- 3 bay leaves
- 3 cloves
- water, one pot spoon full (or broth if available)
- salt to taste
- white roux
- mushrooms or mushroom trimmings

## Directions

1. Place 3 or 4 pounds of meat trimmings in a casserole with a few carrots, onions, bouquet of parsley and chives, 3 bay leaves, and 3 cloves
2. Add one pot spoon full of water (lacking broth)
3. Place your casserole with your meat on a fairly hot fire
4. Skim your moisture well, and when it is reduced, prevent it from sticking
5. Fill your casserole almost full with water if you do not have broth
6. Add the appropriate salt
7. Let your sauce boil, then skim it, then place it on the edge of the stove so it simmers for 2 hours
8. When your meat is cooked, pass this moisture through a silk sieve
9. Make a white roux, when it reaches its point, add mushrooms or mushroom trimmings which you will stir for ten minutes in your white roux
10. Then pour the moisture in which your meat cooked into it
11. Be careful to dilute your roux little by little so it does not become lumpy
12. When you have added everything, let your velouté boil
13. Skim it and place it on the edge of the stove so it simmers for an hour and a half
14. Degrease it and pass it through cheesecloth
15. Be careful to avoid color in your velouté; the whitest is the most beautiful
