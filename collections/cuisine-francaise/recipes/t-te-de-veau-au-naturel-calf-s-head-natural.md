## Ingredients

- scalded calf's head
- water for boiling and cooling
- blanc (cooking liquid)
- lemon
- lard barding
- tongue
- salt
- coarse pepper
- butter
- parsley
- vinegar
- fine pepper
- chives or shallots

## Directions

1. Take a scalded calf's head; bone it down to the eyes; remove the lower jaws and cut the upper jaw to the eye.
2. Soak your head for two or three hours to disgorge.
3. Boil water in a large pot and put the head in it.
4. Ensure it is well immersed in the boiling water so it does not blacken.
5. Skim your water well so it does not soil your head.
6. When it has boiled for half an hour, remove it from the pot and put it in a bucket of cold water.
7. Leave it there for half an hour to cool; then remove it from your bucket and dry it well.
8. Flame your head over a well-heated stove to burn any remaining hairs.
9. Dry it, remove the tongue, the white and hard skins that are inside the mouth.
10. Gather the skins together; truss the head with string so it appears whole.
11. Rub it with lemon and cover it with a barding of lard.
12. Put it to cook in a blanc with the tongue.
13. When your head is in your blanc, bring it to a boil, skim it, put a round of buttered paper over it, and let it boil gently; three hours suffice to cook it.
14. Test by pressing with your finger; if the flesh yields, your head is cooked.
15. At the moment of serving, remove it from your blanc, drain it, dress it on the platter.
16. Cut the skin on the skull with the point of your knife; open the skull by separating it in two; remove the two bones covering the brain and leave it uncovered.
17. Remove the hard skin from the tongue; split it in two lengthwise.
18. Powder it with fine salt and coarse pepper; dip it in butter; grill it; place it on the snout of the head.
19. Powder your head with finely chopped parsley and serve with an oil cruet, or heat vinegar in a pan with salt, fine pepper, chives or shallots, and put this sauce in a sauceboat.
20. This head is served to follow the soup course.
