## Ingredients

- Salt
- coarse pepper
- parsley
- minced shallot
- half a bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- two tablespoons of brandy
- three egg yolks
- three egg whites
- calf's tongue
- calf's eyes
- calf's jowls
- calf's head skin
- calf's ears
- half a septier of white wine
- twice as much bouillon
- bouquet of parsley
- chives
- one garlic clove
- three cloves
- carrots
- onions
- vinegar
- coulis

## Directions

1. Season with salt, coarse pepper, parsley, minced shallot, half a bay leaf, thyme and basil chopped as powder
2. Add two tablespoons of brandy
3. Bind this stuffing with three egg yolks and three beaten egg whites
4. Take the tongue, eyes from which you remove all the dark part, and jowls; clean everything well after blanching in boiling water, and cut them into fillets or large pieces
5. Mix them into your stuffing
6. Place the calf's head skin without blanching into a pot, ears underneath, and fill with your stuffing
7. Sew it up pleating it like a purse and tie it all around to restore its natural shape
8. Put it to cook in a vessel just its size with half a septier of white wine, twice as much bouillon, a bouquet of parsley, chives, one garlic clove, three cloves, carrots, onions, salt and pepper
9. Cook on low heat for three hours
10. When cooked, let it drain of its fat and dry it well with a cloth after removing the string
11. Strain part of its cooking liquid through a sieve
12. Add a little coulis if you have it, and add a dash of vinegar
13. Reduce it on the fire to the consistency of a sauce and serve over the calf's head
