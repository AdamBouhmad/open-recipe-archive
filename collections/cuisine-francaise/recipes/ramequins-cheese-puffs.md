## Ingredients

- A good piece of well-aged Brie cheese
- A piece of butter about one quarter pound
- A half setier of cold or hot water
- Very little salt
- One anchovy
- Flour
- Eggs

## Directions

1. Crush a good piece of Brie cheese in a saucepan with a piece of butter of about one quarter pound, a half setier of cold or hot water, very little salt, and one chopped anchovy.
2. Make everything boil together, and add as much flour as the sauce can absorb.
3. Let it dry on the fire until your paste is very thick.
4. Transfer it to another saucepan and blend in as many eggs as the paste can absorb without becoming liquid; the paste must hold together without running.
5. Shape this paste into small pieces the size of a pigeon egg on a baking sheet and bake them in the oven.
6. The ramequins should be light and a beautiful golden color when done.
