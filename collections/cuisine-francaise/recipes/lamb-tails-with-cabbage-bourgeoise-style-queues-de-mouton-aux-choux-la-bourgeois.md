## Ingredients

- five or six lamb tails
- bouillon
- salt
- pepper
- parsley
- chives
- two cloves
- half a clove of garlic
- half a large head of cabbage
- quarter pound of small lard or half a pound
- flour
- butter

## Directions

1. Cook five or six lamb tails in a light braise made with a little bouillon, little salt, pepper, a bouquet of parsley, chives, two cloves, and half a clove of garlic.
2. Blanch half a large head of cabbage for a quarter hour in boiling water, then remove to fresh water and press it.
3. Remove the core and chop the cabbage.
4. Cut a quarter pound of small lard or half a pound into small dice.
5. Mix the lard and cabbage together in a roux made with a good pinch of flour and butter.
6. Pass them together and moisten with a little bouillon without salt.
7. Let cook for an hour over low heat until the cabbage and lard are well cooked and the ragout is well bound.
8. Drain the tails and wipe them with a cloth.
9. Arrange them in the dish at a small distance from one another.
10. Cover each tail with ragout and serve hot.
