## Ingredients

- four or five lamb tails
- half a head of cabbage
- half a pound of small lard
- six large onions
- parsley
- chives
- two cloves
- half a clove of garlic
- very small branch of fennel
- salt
- coarse pepper
- bouillon
- bread crumbs
- butter
- flour
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Blanch the lamb tails, cabbage, and small lard for a quarter hour in boiling water, then remove to fresh water.
2. Press the cabbage and cut it into several pieces, tying each separately.
3. Cut the small lard into several pieces without separating them from the rind, and tie them.
4. Place the tails at the bottom of a small pot, then add the cabbage, lard, and six large onions.
5. Add a bouquet of parsley, chives, two cloves, half a clove of garlic, a very small branch of fennel, salt, and coarse pepper.
6. Cover with bouillon and cook over low heat in a braise.
7. Cut bread crumbs into rounds the size of a small coin and pass them over the fire with butter until they are a beautiful golden color; set aside to toast.
8. Make a roux with a good pinch of flour in the remaining butter from the croutons and brown it.
9. Moisten with bouillon from the cooking of the cabbage and a dash of vinegar; boil for half an hour so the flour cooks and forms a light coulis to your taste.
10. Degrease and strain the sauce through a sieve.
11. When the tails are cooked and no sauce remains, drain them and wipe with a cloth.
12. Arrange the tails interspersed with the cabbage, onions around them, the lard and croutons on top of the cabbage.
13. Serve the sauce on top.
