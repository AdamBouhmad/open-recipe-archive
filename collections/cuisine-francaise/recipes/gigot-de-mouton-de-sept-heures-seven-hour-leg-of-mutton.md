## Ingredients

- one leg of mutton
- lardons (lard strips)
- salt
- coarse pepper
- thyme
- bay leaf
- lard for barding
- ham slices
- mutton bones, crushed
- mutton slices
- four carrots
- six onions
- three bay leaves
- cloves
- parsley and chives bouquet
- one spoonful of broth

## Directions

1. Debone the leg of mutton halfway up the shank
2. Season with lardons, salt, coarse pepper, thyme and bay leaf, all pounded
3. Lard the interior of the leg, ensuring the lardons do not protrude on top
4. Restore the leg to its original shape and tie it with twine so that the deboning is not apparent
5. Place lard strips in the bottom of a braising pot, followed by ham slices, crushed bones, mutton slices, four carrots, six onions, three bay leaves, a little thyme, three cloves, and a bouquet of parsley and chives
6. Add one spoonful of broth and place all this mixture above the leg of mutton
7. Cover the leg with lard and a buttered paper to cover it
8. Place the leg in the coals to cook for seven hours if it is large, at a gentle fire
9. Also place coals on the lid of the braising pot
10. When ready to serve, drain the cooking liquid, untie the leg, glaze it, and serve with the reduced cooking liquid in which it cooked
