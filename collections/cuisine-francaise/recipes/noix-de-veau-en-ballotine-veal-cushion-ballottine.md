## Ingredients

- One veal cushion, with or without teat
- Thick lard for larding
- Half a pound of butter
- Salt and coarse pepper
- One quarter-pound of grated lard
- One quarter-pound of oil
- Shallots, finely chopped, one spoonful
- A dozen mushrooms, finely chopped
- Parsley, one spoonful
- Nutmeg, grated
- Six oiled sheets of paper
- Thin lard bards
- String

## Directions

1. Lard the veal cushion with thick lard in the same manner as veal in the bourgeoise style, with the same seasoning in the larding pieces
2. Place half a pound of butter in a casserole and put the cushion in it
3. Sauté over fire for three-quarters of an hour without allowing it to take color
4. Dust with salt and coarse pepper
5. Remove from the casserole and place on a plate
6. If there is juice in the casserole with the butter, reduce it until the butter becomes oil
7. Add the grated lard and oil
8. Add finely chopped shallots (one spoonful) and let them fry a little in the butter
9. Add a dozen finely chopped mushrooms, pass them with the butter, and add parsley (one spoonful)
10. When all is well combined, add grated nutmeg and coarse pepper
11. Pour the fine herbs over the veal cushion
12. When cooled, oil six sheets of paper
13. Wrap the cushion with a thin lard bard on top and bottom, then wrap it with a sheet of paper so the seasoning does not escape, then cover with another sheet, continuing until all six sheets are used
14. Ensure the cushion is hermetically sealed, the bundle has a fine shape, and the folds are well made
15. Tie the bundle with string as one ties a piece of beef, so the paper does not come undone on the grill
16. One hour before serving, place the cushion on the grill with very gentle heat
17. Watch that the paper does not burn and the seasoning does not escape
18. When the cushion is grilled, remove only the string and serve the cushion in the paper with the sauce inside
19. If the first sheet is too dark, it should be removed
