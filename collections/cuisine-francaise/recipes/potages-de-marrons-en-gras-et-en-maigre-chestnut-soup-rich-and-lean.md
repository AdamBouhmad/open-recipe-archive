## Ingredients

- a piece of butter
- three onions
- two roots
- one parsnip
- one celery root
- three leeks
- half a clove of garlic
- two cloves
- water
- salt
- one hundred or one hundred fifty large chestnuts

## Directions

1. Put butter in a saucepan with three onions cut into slices, two roots, a parsnip, a celery root, three leeks, all cut into small pieces, half a clove of garlic, and two cloves
2. Cook all together until they are slightly colored
3. Moisten with water and boil for one hour
4. Pass your broth through a sieve and season with salt
5. Take one hundred or one hundred fifty large chestnuts, remove the first skin, and put them over the fire in a perforated pan, stirring always until you can remove the second skin
6. When well peeled, cook them with part of the broth
7. Set aside those that are whole to garnish the soup, and crush the others and pass them through a sieve to make a coulis that you will moisten with the broth from their cooking
8. Simmer the soup with the root broth, and when serving add the chestnut coulis
9. The rich version is made the same way, using good rich broth in place of the lean
