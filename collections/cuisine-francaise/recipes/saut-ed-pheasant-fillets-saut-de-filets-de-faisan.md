## Ingredients

- Eight pheasant fillets
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Butter
- Spanish sauce
- Game stock
- Glazed croutons
- Truffles (optional)

## Directions

1. Remove and trim eight pheasant fillets; if the skin side still has a nervous skin, place that side on the table and slide your knife between the skin and meat so none remains
2. Trim the edges of your fillets so they are correct; place them in your sauté pan with a little salt and coarse pepper
3. Remove the nerves from the tenderloins and place them the same way
4. Warm about three-quarters of a pound of butter and pour it over them
5. At serving time, place your fillets on a hot fire; when they are stiffened on one side, turn them to the other; leave them only a moment; touch them with your finger; if the meat resists, remove them
6. Arrange them in crowns, one fillet, one glazed crouton, and so on; place your tenderloins in the middle
7. For sauce, use a Spanish sauce worked with game stock; if you do not have any, remove the butter in your sauté pan; leave the juice your fillets have given and put your sauce in it
8. Make it boil five or six times; then pour it under your fillets; you can put a sauté of truffles in the middle over your tenderloins
