## Ingredients

- 3 artichokes
- 1/4 pound lard, minced
- butter and oil in equal amounts
- mushrooms
- 1 tablespoon shallots, finely chopped
- 1 tablespoon parsley
- spices
- salt
- coarse pepper
- thyme
- bay leaf
- 1 glass bouillon
- veal slices
- lard strips
- espagnole sauce or roux

## Directions

1. Cut off the tips of the leaves; place three artichokes in water
2. When well washed, place them in a pot of boiling water and blanch for twenty minutes
3. Refresh them and remove the choke; let them drain
4. Take hot fat and place the artichokes in it with the leaf side down; when the leaves are well fried, drain them
5. Add a quarter pound of minced lard to a pot, along with equal amounts of butter and oil
6. Chop mushrooms very fine and pass them through the pot
7. Add a tablespoon of finely chopped shallots, the same of parsley, a little spice, salt, and coarse pepper
8. Pass the herbs well and let them cool, then place them inside the artichokes
9. Line the bottom of a pot with lard strips and a few veal slices
10. Truss your artichokes and place them in the pot with thyme, bay leaf, and a glass of bouillon
11. Cover with lard strips and a round of buttered paper
12. When boiling, place on low heat and put hot coals on the lid
13. After braising for three-quarters of an hour, remove the twine and serve on a platter
14. Pour a little espagnole sauce inside; if you have no sauce, make a light roux that you moisten with the braising liquid from the artichokes and pass through a silk sieve
