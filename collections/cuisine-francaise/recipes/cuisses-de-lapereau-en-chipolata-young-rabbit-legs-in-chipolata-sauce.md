## Ingredients

- Young rabbit legs
- 1/4 pound of butter
- 1 and 1/2 spoonfuls of flour
- 2 small pot spoons of bouillon, strained through a silk tamis
- Mushrooms, sautéed in water and lemon juice
- 1 bay leaf
- Parsley and chives
- Small lard
- 20 small onions
- 15 chestnuts (not from Lyon)
- 6 sausages
- String
- 4 egg yolks for liaison
- Salt

## Directions

1. Bone your legs as in the previous recipe.
2. Throw them into boiling water for two minutes; then remove them and trim them.
3. Put 1/4 pound of butter in a casserole; place your legs in it.
4. Sauté them for ten minutes.
5. Then pour 1 and 1/2 spoonfuls of flour, which you blend with your butter.
6. Add about 2 small pot spoons of bouillon strained through a silk tamis.
7. Turn your ragout until it boils.
8. Add mushrooms sautéed in water and lemon juice to prevent them from darkening, a bay leaf, and a bouquet of parsley and chives.
9. Cook your ragout over high heat so it reduces.
10. Add small lard that you have blanched.
11. When your ragout is three-quarters cooked, add twenty small onions that you have blanched.
12. You can also add fifteen chestnuts not from Lyon.
13. Join six sausages, which you will tie in the middle with string so they are not too long.
14. Put them in boiling water for five minutes; after refreshing them, put them in your ragout.
15. Be careful to degrease it well.
16. When it is cooked, add a liaison of four egg yolks.
17. Arrange your legs on your plate and your ingredients on top. Your ragout should be properly salted. You can serve this ragout in a tureen to serve as a flan.
