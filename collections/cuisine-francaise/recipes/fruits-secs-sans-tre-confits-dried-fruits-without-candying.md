## Ingredients

- Ripe cherries
- Ripe plums
- Peaches
- Apricots
- Pears

## Directions

1. For cherries: Choose well-ripened ones that are not turned, arrange them on wicker baskets without piling one on top of another, leave the stems on and put them to dry in an oven of gentle heat like that where bread has just been taken out. Leave them as long as the oven has heat, then remove them to turn them over and put them back in the oven with the same heat until you judge they are dry enough. Let them cool to tie in small bunches and keep in a dry place.
2. For plums: Dry them the same way; pick them very ripe as those that fall by themselves are better because they have more flesh and better flavor.
3. For peaches: Dry as plums, except those picked from the tree are better than those that have fallen. Split them lengthwise and remove the pit. When they are half dry, put them on a clean table and flatten them so they dry evenly, then return them to the oven until dry.
4. For apricots: Make them the same way, except remove the pit without opening them.
5. For pears: Dry them peeled and unpeeled; the first way is better. You take the peels and put them with the pears in a pot full of water, boil them until they begin to soften, being careful when peeling to leave the stems on, and then dry them in the oven the same way as plums.
