## Ingredients

- one young hare with blood reserved
- coarse lard for larding (optional)
- hare liver
- butter, the size of an egg
- bouquet of parsley
- chives
- one garlic clove
- two shallots
- three cloves
- one bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- a good pinch of flour
- three glasses of bouillon
- one half-setier of red wine
- one spoonful of vinegar
- salt
- coarse pepper
- fine capers

## Directions

1. When plucking and cleaning a young hare, be careful not to lose the blood; set it aside
2. Cut the hare into pieces and lard with coarse lard if desired
3. Put it in a saucepan with the liver and butter the size of an egg, a bouquet of parsley, chives, one garlic clove, two shallots, three cloves, one bay leaf, thyme, basil
4. Pass over the fire and add a good pinch of flour
5. Moisten with three glasses of bouillon, one half-setier of red wine, one spoonful of vinegar, salt, coarse pepper
6. Let boil until the young hare is cooked and a little sauce remains
7. Take the cooked liver, crush it well and mix with the blood you kept
8. When ready to serve, add the blood to make it bind on the fire without boiling, like an egg yolk liaison
9. Then add a half-handful of whole fine capers
10. Serve hot
