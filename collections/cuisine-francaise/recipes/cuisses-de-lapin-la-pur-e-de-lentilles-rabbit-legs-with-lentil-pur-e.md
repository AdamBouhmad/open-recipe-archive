## Ingredients

- 8 rabbit legs
- Medium lardons, seasoned with aromatic herbs, salt and pepper
- Lard slices for lining
- A few slices of veal
- 2 carrots
- 2 onions
- 1 bay leaf
- Thyme
- Lard strips for covering
- Butter
- 1 small pot spoon of bouillon
- Lentil purée

## Directions

1. Take rabbit legs; eight are sufficient for an entrée.
2. Bone them, that is, remove the bone up to the joint of the foreleg; try not to cut the top.
3. Lard the inside of your legs with medium lardons seasoned with aromatic herbs, salt and pepper. Since the leg is generally very dry, put many lardons in it.
4. When they are well larded, throw them into boiling water for two minutes.
5. Remove them, trim them, and put lard strips in a casserole with a few slices of veal, two carrots, two onions, a bay leaf, and a little thyme.
6. Place the legs on this seasoning. Cover them with lard strips and a round of buttered paper.
7. Pour about one small pot spoon of bouillon with a little drippings from the cooking pot.
8. Let them simmer for two hours, more or less, depending on how tender your rabbit is.
9. At the moment of serving, drain them. Arrange them on your plate and mask them with a lentil purée.
