## Ingredients

- Calf's shoulder, well covered
- Meat from the shoulder for farce
- Lard, one pound for each pound of meat
- Bacon lardons
- Tongue at scarlet
- Truffles
- Spinach omelette, if desired
- Carrots
- Lard for covering
- Veal lardons, additional
- Vegetables and aromatics for the braising liquid: veal slices, two veal feet blanched, shoulder bones, six carrots, eight to ten onions (one studded with four cloves), four bay leaves, thyme, parsley and chives
- Beef broth or water and salt

## Directions

1. Select a calf's shoulder that is well covered; the top should not be damaged
2. Bone it entirely
3. Remove some of the meat with which you will make your farce, if you do not have a slice of veal
4. For every pound of meat, add a pound of lard that you will chop; when your farce is well chopped, spread it to a thickness of one inch on your shoulder
5. Lay lardons and pieces of scarlet tongue, truffes if you have them; some people add an omelette made with spinach green, and if you wish, carrots also cut into lardons
6. To marble your galantine, cover all these ingredients with farce, and add more lardons, truffes, etc., then another layer of farce
7. When you have used it all, roll your shoulder lengthwise; join your flesh well together; tie it and cover it with lard
8. Wrap it tightly in canvas or a clean white cloth; tie it once more so that as it cooks it maintains a beautiful form
9. Place it in a braising pan where you have laid some slices of lard, some slices of veal, two blanched veal feet, the bones from your shoulder, six carrots, eight or ten onions, one of which is studded with four cloves, four bay leaves, a little thyme, a large bouquet of parsley and chives
10. Moisten with beef broth; if you do not have it, use water and salt
11. Bring your cooking liquid to boil and braise for three hours; test with a skewer to determine doneness
