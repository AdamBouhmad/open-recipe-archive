## Ingredients

- lamb shoulder, well covered with meat
- lardons seasoned with salt and pepper
- lard strips
- carrots
- onions
- cloves
- bay leaves
- thyme
- bouillon or water
- salt
- espagnole sauce
- flour for roux

## Directions

1. Have a well-covered lamb shoulder
2. Bone it halfway down the handle
3. Lard the interior with seasoned lardons
4. Season with salt and pepper
5. Once larded, truss it into a ball or lengthwise shape
6. Bridge it so it retains the form you wish to give it
7. Then tie it with string
8. Place lard strips in a braising pan
9. Place your shoulder in it
10. Add three or four carrots, five onions (one stuck with two cloves), two bay leaves, a little thyme, the shoulder bones, and a few other pieces if you have them
11. Moisten with bouillon or water
12. Add salt and simmer your shoulder for three and a half hours
13. At serving time, drain your shoulder, remove the bridge, and untie it
14. Glaze it and arrange it on the platter
15. Place glazed onions around it
16. Use a reduced espagnole sauce
17. If you do not have sauce, make a light roux with the braising liquid in which you cooked your shoulder
18. Ensure your sauce is thin enough to be reduced
19. Check that it is properly seasoned
20. You may serve around this shoulder lettuce, small turnips, small root vegetables, lentil purée, or peas
