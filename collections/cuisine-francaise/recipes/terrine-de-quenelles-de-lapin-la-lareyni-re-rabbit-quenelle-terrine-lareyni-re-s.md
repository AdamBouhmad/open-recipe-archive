## Ingredients

- Rabbit forcemeat, slightly firmer than standard
- Butter, one-sixth less than in standard forcemeat
- Eight or ten truffles
- Kidneys
- Chicken crests
- Small pieces of veal
- Lamb sweetbreads
- Truffles
- Two spoonfuls of velouté
- One spoonful of game stock
- Half-bottle of dry Madeira wine
- Mushrooms, turned and sautéed
- Lemon juice and water
- Four egg yolks
- Butter, the size of an egg
- Salt and pepper

## Directions

1. Make forcemeat slightly firmer than the standard recipe by using one-sixth less butter
2. Poach quenelles with a spoon as you would the standard recipe
3. Drain the quenelles
4. Cut eight or ten truffles into small lardons
5. Insert the truffle lardons into the quenelles at equal distances and of the same size, arranging four rows on each quenelle
6. Once all quenelles are studded, place them in a casserole with kidneys, chicken crests, small pieces of veal, lamb sweetbreads, and truffles
7. Pour two spoonfuls of velouté and one spoonful of game stock into the casserole
8. Add half-bottle of dry Madeira wine
9. Add mushrooms that have been turned and sautéed in lemon juice and water
10. Reduce the sauce by half
11. Remove the mushrooms and set aside with the garnish
12. Make a liaison with four egg yolks and butter the size of an egg
13. Turn the liaison over the fire to thicken it, but do not let it boil for fear it will break
14. Strain the liaison through a sieve over the garnish, ensuring the garnish is well heated
15. Serve the ragout in a terrine
16. You may use an espagnole instead of velouté, working the sauce the same way
17. Cook the truffles in the sauce
18. Check that the seasoning is correct
