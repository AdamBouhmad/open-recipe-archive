## Ingredients

- Small pieces of lard
- Veal round
- Onions
- Root vegetables
- Bouillon
- Butter
- Flour
- One tablespoon of flour and butter per half-septier

## Directions

1. Put small pieces of lard and veal round in the bottom of a casserole according to the quantity of coulis desired; for good coulis, use one pound per half-septier
2. Add two or three onions and as many root vegetables
3. Cover the casserole well and cook over low heat so the meat has time to release its juices
4. Then increase the heat until the meat is ready to brown
5. Lower to low heat so the meat browns gently to the casserole, creating a good gratin
6. Remove the meat and vegetables to a plate
7. Put a piece of butter and flour in the casserole according to the quantity of coulis desired, about one tablespoon per half-septier
8. Cook over heat until the roux is beautiful
9. Add hot bouillon
10. Return the meat to make it cook for another two hours over very low heat; degrease the coulis often
11. When finished, pass it through fine cloth or a sieve for use
