## Ingredients

- turkey
- chicken or veal meat
- lard
- salt
- pepper
- four spices
- fine herbs
- aromatic flavoring
- medium lardons
- truffles
- scarlet tongue
- poultry fillets
- foie gras
- lard slices (bardes)
- canvas cloth
- bay leaves
- veal knuckles
- carrots
- onions
- parsley and chives bouquet
- turkey scraps
- thyme
- cloves
- bouillon

## Directions

1. Singe your turkey; make sure it has good meat and is well nourished
2. Begin to bone it through the back; be careful not to damage the breast
3. When it is entirely boned and the sinews of the thighs are removed, lift some of the meat from the breast half an inch from the skin
4. Do the same for the thighs
5. Put with the meat you have cut from the top of your turkey that of two or three chickens, or simply veal or other meat; this is optional
6. If you have two pounds of meat, put two pounds of the fattest lard possible; chop everything together
7. Add salt, pepper, four spices, fine herbs
8. If you wish, when your forcemeat is finely chopped, season medium lardons with pounded aromatics, salt, and pepper, and lard the meat of your turkey
9. Make a layer of forcemeat one inch thick, which you press down well and evenly
10. Put on this layer of forcemeat truffles cut lengthwise, scarlet tongue, lard lardons, the filets of your turkey and poultry, foie gras if you have it
11. Add another layer of forcemeat, and as before, truffles, tongue, lardons, etc., until you have no more forcemeat; perform the same procedure
12. When your forcemeat is used up, roll your turkey so that it contains all the forcemeat without any escaping from any side
13. With a trussing needle and string, sew the meat together as it was in its original form
14. Give your galantine an elongated shape; cover it with lard slices, a little salt
15. Wrap it in canvas cloth into which you put four or five bay leaves
16. Tie your two ends and tie your galantine on top of the canvas so it keeps its shape
17. Put lard slices in a braising pan, your galantine on top
18. Add two veal knuckles, six carrots, six onions, a strong bouquet of parsley and chives, the scraps of your turkey, four bay leaves, a little thyme, three cloves, three pot spoonfuls of bouillon, more if your piece is large
19. Put it on the fire and simmer for three hours
