## Ingredients

- Good milk, one chopine
- Crumb of bread, a good handful
- Half-dozen onions
- Butter, a piece
- Pork belly fat (panne), half a pound, chopped
- Six egg yolks
- Cream, a little more than half a setier
- Fine salt
- Fine spices
- Pork intestines (boyaux), well washed

## Directions

1. Put on the fire a chopine of good milk that you bring to a boil and add a good handful of bread crumbs, pass through a sieve
2. Boil everything together turning often, principally toward the end, until the bread crumb has absorbed all the milk and is well thick
3. Set it to cool
4. Cut half a dozen onions into small dice and cook them on a small fire without coloring them, with a piece of butter
5. After they are removed from the fire, add the bread crumb with six egg yolks and a little more than half a setier of cream
6. Mix everything together, season with fine salt and fine spices
7. Take pork intestines well washed, cut them to the length you wish to make the sausages, fill them only three-quarters full, tie the end
8. When they are all finished, boil water; when it boils vigorously, gently put in the sausages and boil them until they are cooked, which takes only a quarter hour
9. You will know they are cooked by pricking them with a pin, fat will come out
10. Remove them gently with a skimmer, put them in fresh water, let them drain, and grill them in a paper case, then remove them from the case to serve them hot
