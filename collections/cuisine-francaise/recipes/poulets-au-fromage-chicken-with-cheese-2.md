## Ingredients

- Two chickens
- Butter
- Half a glass of white wine
- Bouillon
- Bunch of parsley
- Chives
- Half a clove of garlic
- Two cloves
- Half a bay leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- Salt
- Pepper
- Good butter
- Flour
- Gruyère cheese, grated

## Directions

1. Flame and pluck two chickens; after having emptied them and tucked the legs into the body, split them slightly on the back and flatten them with the cleaver
2. Brown them in a saucepan with a little butter
3. Moisten with half a glass of white wine and as much bouillon
4. Add a bunch of parsley, chives, half a clove of garlic, two cloves, half a bay leaf, thyme, basil, salt, and pepper
5. Cook for one hour over low heat
6. Remove the chickens and put into the sauce a piece of good butter the size of a walnut, worked with a pinch of flour
7. Thicken the sauce over the fire
8. Prepare the serving dish with a layer of this sauce on the bottom, and on the sauce a small handful of grated Gruyère cheese
9. Place the chickens on top, and on the chickens put the remaining sauce, then as much grated Gruyère cheese as you put beneath
10. Place the dish over low heat with a pie-dish cover with fire on top
11. When they are a beautiful golden color, serve hot
12. If your cheese is very salty, do not put salt in the cooking of the chickens
