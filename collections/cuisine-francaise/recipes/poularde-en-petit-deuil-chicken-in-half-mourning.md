## Ingredients

- poularde (hen)
- citron (lemon)
- bardes de lard (lard strips)
- truffes (truffles)
- beurre (butter)
- velouté (velouté sauce)
- consommé (consommé)
- sel (salt)
- gros poivre (coarse pepper)

## Directions

1. Flame, pluck, and clean your hen; cut off its legs and tuck the thighs inside; truss it so that the breast protrudes well; cover it with lemon slices and lard strips
2. Place lard strips in a casserole, your hen on top; add a poêle (cooking vessel); cook it three-quarters of the way and remove from the fire
3. For best results, let it cool; with a small piece of wood, make holes on the breast at equal intervals, and in each hole place a small truffle lardon; arrange them neatly so they form an oval covering the full width of the breast
4. Cover your studded piece with lard; place it in a casserole and pass the cooking liquid through a silk sieve
5. One hour before serving, let it simmer; at the moment of serving, drain and untruss it; place it on your plate
6. Chop a truffle and pass it with a small piece of butter; add three or four spoonfuls of velouté, one of consommé, very little salt, and a bit of coarse pepper
7. Bring your sauce to a boil a few times; place it under your hen
