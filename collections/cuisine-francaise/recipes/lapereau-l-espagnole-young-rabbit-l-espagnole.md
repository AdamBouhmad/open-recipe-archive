## Ingredients

- 1 young rabbit, dressed and cleaned, cut into pieces
- 1/4 pound of butter
- 2 large spoonfuls of flour
- 4 pot spoons of bouillon
- Mushrooms, sautéed in water and lemon juice
- Parsley and chives
- 1 bay leaf
- Thyme
- Coarse pepper
- About 1/2 pound of lard, cut into small pieces and blanched
- 24 small, peeled onions of the same size
- 3-4 egg yolks for liaison
- Water (optional)
- Salt

## Directions

1. To avoid blanching the young rabbit and losing some of its flavor, put a 1/4 pound of butter in a casserole.
2. Heat it slightly and put your young rabbit in it.
3. Brown it over high heat.
4. When you see that all your pieces are stiffened, pour in about two large spoonfuls of flour, which you blend with your butter and rabbit.
5. Add about four pot spoons of bouillon.
6. Stir your ragout well until it boils.
7. Add mushrooms that you have sautéed in water and lemon juice, a bouquet of parsley and chives, a bay leaf, a little thyme, and a little coarse pepper.
8. Add about 1/2 pound of lard that you will cut into small pieces and blanch.
9. Cook your ragout over high heat so the liquid reduces.
10. When it is three-quarters cooked, degrease it and add twenty-four small, well-peeled onions of the same size.
11. When your ragout is cooked and reduced, degrease it and add a liaison of three or four egg yolks, depending on the size of your ragout.
12. You can moisten it with water if needed; then add salt.
13. Serve it very hot.
