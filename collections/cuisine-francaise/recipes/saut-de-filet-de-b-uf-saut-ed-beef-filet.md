## Ingredients

- filet de bœuf (beef filet)
- sel (salt)
- gros poivre (coarse pepper)
- beurre (butter)
- sauce espagnole (espagnole sauce)
- sauce piquante (piquant sauce)
- sauce tomate (tomato sauce)
- beurre d'anchois (anchovy butter)
- roux (roux)
- parures de sauté (trimmings from sauté)
- carotte (carrot)
- deux oignons (two onions)
- clou de girofle (clove)
- feuille de laurier (bay leaf)
- fond ou bouillon (broth or stock)
- cornichons (gherkins)
- vinaigre (vinegar)

## Directions

1. Cut your beef filet in four lengthwise
2. Cut your pieces into short meat of the thickness of three lines
3. Flatten them and make them round, about the size of a six-franc coin or slightly larger
4. Be careful when rounding them not to leave tough skin
5. Your pieces should be roughly the same size
6. Arrange them flat in a sauté pan or something that can replace it
7. When all your pieces are flattened, trimmed, and arranged in your pan, season them with salt and coarse pepper
8. Melt a good piece of butter and pour it over them
9. At the moment of serving, place your pan over a hot fire; when they are stiffened on one side, stiffen them on the other
10. Test with your finger if your piece is cooked; if too soft, leave it longer
11. When your pieces are cooked, arrange them in a miroton around the platter, putting the smallest in the middle
12. You may pour your sauté with a well-worked and well-corseted espagnole sauce
13. You may mix with a piquant sauce a tomato sauce or anchovy butter sauce
14. If you lack sauce, make a light roux in which you put the trimmings of your sauté, a carrot cut in small dice, two onions, a clove, a bay leaf
15. Pass everything through your roux, which you moisten with a little stock or a little broth
16. Cook and reduce this sauce for three-quarters of an hour
17. Pass it through cheesecloth
18. Add a little coarse pepper, gherkins, a dash of vinegar
19. In short, give your sauce whatever taste you want
20. You may also add anchovy butter or serve it quite simply, ensuring it has good seasoning and good taste
