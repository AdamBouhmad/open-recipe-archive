## Ingredients

- Thrushes
- Fine forcemeat
- Aromatic herbs, pounded
- Coarse pepper
- Salt
- Butter
- Parsley
- Shallots
- Flour
- White wine
- Bouillon

## Directions

1. Pluck the thrushes whole; singe them lightly; debone them starting from the back; once well deboned, take the livers and pound them with the back of the knife; mix them with fine forcemeat, a little pounded aromatic herbs, and coarse pepper
2. Fill your thrushes with this forcemeat, but first pass a leg through the middle of the body, having cut off a large piece so that it represents the tail of a cherry; once the body is filled with forcemeat, bring the flesh together with a needle and secure it
3. Place your thrushes in a casserole with salt and coarse pepper; warm a piece of butter and pour it over your thrushes
4. Half an hour before serving, put them on the fire to simmer in the butter; at the moment of serving, drain them and remove the strings holding them; arrange them on the plate like cherries in a compote dish
5. Sauce them with an italienne; if you do not have any, reduce the juice your thrushes have rendered; remove three-quarters of the butter; add a little parsley and finely chopped shallots, which you will pass a moment in your butter; add about half a spoonful of flour, the equivalent of half a glass of white wine, and as much bouillon; bring your sauce to a boil a few times; degrease it; check that it is well bound and properly seasoned; use it for your thrushes
