## Ingredients

- two large handfuls of breadcrumbs
- chopine of milk
- butter
- parsley
- chives
- salt
- pepper
- six egg yolks
- cod tail
- mushrooms
- shallots
- garlic
- flour
- milk
- three egg yolks
- buttered paper
- beaten egg
- breadcrumbs
- bouillon
- beurre manié
- verjus

## Directions

1. In a casserole, put breadcrumbs with a chopine of milk and bring to a boil
2. Dry on the fire until the breadcrumbs are very thick
3. Let cool
4. Add butter the size of an egg, chopped parsley, chives, salt, pepper, and six egg yolks
5. Cook the cod tail in water as previously described
6. Drain it and remove all the flesh, leaving only the bone
7. Separate into fillets
8. In a casserole, put butter the size of an egg with mushrooms cut into fillets, parsley, chives, two shallots, and half a clove of garlic, all chopped
9. Pass on the fire and add half a spoonful of flour
10. Moisten with half a setier of milk, add coarse pepper
11. Boil until the sauce is thick
12. Add the cod with three egg yolks and thicken on the fire without boiling
13. Let cool
14. On the serving dish, place the bone of the tail and wrap the small end of the tail in buttered paper
15. Make a border around all sides of the bone with some of the stuffing
16. Put the cod ragout in the middle
17. Cover over with the remaining stuffing so that the ragout is not visible and forms a cod tail
18. Smooth everywhere with a knife
19. Dip in beaten egg and coat with breadcrumbs
20. Cook and brown in the oven or under a country stove cover
21. When cooked to a nice color, serve with clean dish edges
22. You can put at the bottom a sauce made with a glass of good bouillon, butter the size of a walnut worked with flour, a spoonful of verjus, little salt, and coarse pepper, thickened on the fire
