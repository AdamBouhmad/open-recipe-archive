## Ingredients

- calf's head with skin, well blanched and scalded
- calf's brains
- veal round
- beef fat
- salt
- coarse pepper
- parsley
- chives
- half a bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- eau-de-vie
- egg yolks
- egg whites
- calf's tongue
- calf's eyes

## Directions

1. Use a calf's head with skin that is well blanched and well scalded.
2. Carefully remove the skin without cutting it.
3. Bone the head to extract the brains, tongue, eyes, and cheeks.
4. Make a forcemeat with the brains, veal round, and beef fat, all chopped very fine.
5. Season with salt, coarse pepper, parsley, chives (chopped), half a bay leaf, thyme and basil (chopped like powder).
6. Add two spoonfuls of eau-de-vie and bind the forcemeat with three egg yolks and three beaten egg whites.
7. Blanch the tongue, eyes (from which you remove all the dark part), and cheeks in boiling water, clean them properly, then cut them into fillets or large dice.
8. Mix these pieces into your forcemeat.
9. Place the calf's head skin, unblanced, in a casserole with the ears underneath, and fill it with your forcemeat.
10. Sew the head closed.
