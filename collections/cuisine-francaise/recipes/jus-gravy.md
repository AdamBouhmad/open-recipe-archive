## Ingredients

- Three pounds of sliced beef
- Two rabbits, that is the hind legs and saddle only
- One calf knuckle
- Five carrots
- Six onions
- Two cloves
- Two bay leaves
- A bouquet of parsley and chives
- Two spoonfuls of broth
- Great broth or water

## Directions

1. Put in a saucepan three pounds of sliced beef, two rabbits (that is to say the hind legs and the saddle, not the breast nor the head), one calf knuckle, five carrots, six onions, two cloves, two bay leaves, a bouquet of parsley and chives
2. Put two spoonfuls of broth in your saucepan which you place on a good fire
3. When the broth is reduced, smother your stove and put your saucepan on it so that your meat releases its jus and it attaches very gently
4. The glaze at the bottom of your saucepan must be almost black
5. When it reaches this point, take your saucepan from the fire and wait ten minutes without moistening it
6. Fill it with great broth or water, but in a lesser quantity
7. Then let your jus simmer for three hours on the fire
8. Let it be well skimmed and seasoned; if you moisten with water the cooked meats, pass your jus through a hair sieve
9. You can also make it with meat scraps: cut slices of onions that you put at the bottom of your saucepan, your meat on top (and the seasoning of the jus above), put two or three glasses of water; reduce it as the jus: when the bottom of your saucepan is almost black, fill it almost with water according to the quantity of meat; add the salt needed and let your jus boil for two hours; after pass it through the hair sieve
