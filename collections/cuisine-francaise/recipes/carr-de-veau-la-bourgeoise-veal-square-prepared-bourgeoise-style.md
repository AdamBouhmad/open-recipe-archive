## Ingredients

- Half a pound of lard
- parsley
- ciboule (chives)
- a small clove of garlic
- bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- salt
- coarse pepper
- veal square fillet
- lard bard for the bottom
- onion slices
- carrot and parsnip zests
- one glass of bouillon
- three spoonfuls of brandy
- water

## Directions

1. Cut the half pound of lard into lardons and mix them with parsley, ciboule, a small clove of garlic, all chopped, a bay leaf, thyme, and basil chopped to a powder, salt, and coarse pepper
2. Lard with this mixture the fillet of a veal square, after having cut the bones at the bottom of the fillet
3. Place it in a terrine or small pot with a bard of lard in the bottom and a few slices of onion; zests of carrots and parsnips
4. Make it sweat for half an hour over a small fire
5. Then moisten it with one glass of bouillon and three spoonfuls of brandy; cook it over a small fire
6. When the cooking is done and the sauce is short, degrease it to serve over the square; if you want this square cold, in the manner of beef à la mode, arrange it on the plate with the sauce on top without degrassing it, and let it cool
