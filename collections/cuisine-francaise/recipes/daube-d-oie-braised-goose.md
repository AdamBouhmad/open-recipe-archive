## Ingredients

- one goose
- lardons of seasoned lard
- parsley
- chives
- two shallots
- half a clove of garlic
- bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- two glasses of water
- as much white wine
- half a glass of eau-de-vie

## Directions

1. Scald the goose and pluck it; make it refirm on the fire, and pluck it again
2. Lard it throughout with lardons of seasoned lard kneaded with parsley, chives, two shallots, half a clove of garlic, all chopped fine, a bay leaf, thyme, basil chopped to a powder, salt, coarse pepper, and a little grated nutmeg
3. After having larded the goose, truss it and place it in a pot just fitting its size with two glasses of water, as much white wine, and half a glass of eau-de-vie, add a little salt and coarse pepper
4. Seal the pot well and cook over a very low fire for three or four hours
5. When cooked and the sauce is very reduced so that it can set in aspic, arrange the daube in its serving dish
6. When it is almost cold, pour the sauce over it, and serve only when it is completely set as a cold entremet
