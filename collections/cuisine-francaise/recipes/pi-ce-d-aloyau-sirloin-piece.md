## Ingredients

- whole sirloin piece from the thick end of the tenderloin to the first rib
- string
- skewers
- piquant sauce with many cornichons

## Directions

1. Take a whole sirloin piece, that is to say from the thick end of the tenderloin to the first rib.
2. Have it removed as squarely as possible, without removing the fat that is on the tenderloin.
3. Take care to remove the surface and leave three fingers thick of fat so that your tenderloin is well covered.
4. Spit your piece so that it does not turn; for this you must pass the spit along the bones attached to the filet.
5. Secure your meat with small skewers and make sure there is not more meat on one side than the other.
6. If your piece were not spitted straight, you would put a large skewer on the hard filet and attach it at each end with string, tightening it as much as possible; it is the tenderloin that is in view.
7. This sirloin is served to follow large pieces.
8. Serve also a piquant sauce with many cornichons underneath.
9. This piece is excellent stewed in a braising pan, cooked without liquid, with fire above and below.
