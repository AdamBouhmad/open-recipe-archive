## Ingredients

- Twenty carrots
- Twenty turnips
- Twelve onions
- Six celery hearts
- Twelve leeks
- Six lettuces
- A handful of sorrel
- A pinch of chervil, more or less according to strength desired
- Three-quarters pound of butter
- Bouillon
- A piece of sugar the size of half an egg
- Bread
- Coarse pepper
- Salt

## Directions

1. Cut your root vegetables into medium-sized dice and chop the herbs
2. First fry the carrots in three-quarters pound of butter
3. When they are lightly fried, add the turnips and let them fry with the carrots
4. Then add the leeks and onions
5. When everything is golden, add the sorrel and stir with all the vegetables
6. When the sorrel is well melted, moisten with bouillon, not too much as it would be unnecessary
7. Let the vegetables boil for an hour until cooked
8. Add a piece of sugar the size of half an egg
9. Make a layer of bread, then a layer of vegetables, continuing until the dish is full
10. Add a little coarse pepper to each layer
11. Moisten with the bouillon from the vegetables without degreasing
12. Simmer until it forms a golden crust
13. Spare the salt due to reduction
14. This garbure can be made lean by moistening the vegetables with water seasoned with salt and pepper
