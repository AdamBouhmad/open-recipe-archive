## Ingredients

- Quail
- Truffles
- Lard
- Butter
- Salt
- Pepper
- Four spices
- Parsley
- Veal
- Carrot
- Small onions
- Clove
- Bay leaf
- White wine
- Bouillon

## Directions

1. Clean your quail through the cavity and singe them lightly.
2. Peel your truffles and cut them into large dice.
3. Grate a little lard and put it in a saucepan with as much butter, salt, pepper, four spices, and a little parsley.
4. Chop a truffle and mix it with the rest.
5. Pass this over the fire for seven or eight minutes and let it cool.
6. Fill the body of your quail with this mixture.
7. When well filled, truss and bard them and give them an agreeable shape.
8. Put lard fat in a saucepan with your quail on top and cover them with lard fat.
9. Cut small pieces of veal into dice, a carrot, seven or eight small onions, a clove, and half a bay leaf.
10. Add your truffle peelings and a piece of butter.
11. Put all this in a saucepan and cook your seasoning for a good quarter hour over a moderate fire.
12. When your seasoning is well browned, add a glass of white wine and as much bouillon.
13. Let it come to a boil two or three times, then pour this seasoning over your quail when you put them on the fire.
14. A good half hour is sufficient to cook them.
15. At the moment of serving, drain them, unbard them, and arrange them on the plate.
16. In the middle, put a sauté of truffles arranged in a reduced Spanish sauce.
