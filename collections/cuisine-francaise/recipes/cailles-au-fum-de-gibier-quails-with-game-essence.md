## Ingredients

- quails
- quail fillets
- salt
- coarse pepper
- parsley
- butter
- lemon
- bay leaf
- bacon strips
- white wine
- espagnole sauce
- game essence
- silk tamis
- quail livers
- farce

## Directions

1. Remove the breast bones from your quail from the inside without damaging the outside
2. After cleaning it through the cavity, singe it lightly
3. Take fillets from other quails and season them with a little salt, coarse pepper, a little finely chopped parsley, a quarter pound of butter, and the juice of a lemon
4. Put two in each quail; you need eight quails for an entrée
5. Truss them and round them as well as possible
6. Baste them so they keep their shape while cooking
7. Put a good piece of butter in a saucepan with the juice of a lemon and a bay leaf
8. Stiffen your quails for a moment in your butter
9. Put strips of bacon in a saucepan, your quails on top of them
10. Cover them also with strips of bacon and put a pan moistened with white wine over your quails
11. Cook them over heat for a good half hour
12. Before serving and at the moment of doing so, drain them, remove the trussing strings, and arrange them on your plate
13. Put an espagnole sauce prepared with game essence on top; if you have no sauce, make a light roux and pass through a silk sieve the cooking liquid in which your quails cooked
14. Pour it over your roux and add a spoonful of jus
15. Reduce your sauce by half, skim off the fat and pass it through a sieve, then place it under your quails
16. If your sauce had too much salt, add the juice of half a lemon and a piece of fresh butter the size of a walnut, melting it by stirring into your sauce without putting it on the fire
17. If the fillets are too difficult to obtain for putting inside your quails, you may chop the livers which you will mix with a little farce
