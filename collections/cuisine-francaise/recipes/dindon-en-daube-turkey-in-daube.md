## Ingredients

- old turkey
- salt
- pepper
- four spices
- fine herbs
- lard lardons
- lard slices (bardes)
- string and trussing needle
- veal knuckles
- turkey legs
- carrots
- onions
- cloves
- bay leaves
- thyme
- parsley and chives bouquet
- buttered paper
- bouillon
- egg

## Directions

1. Pluck and gut the turkey
2. Cut off its legs and truss the thighs inside
3. Secure them with string and a trussing needle
4. Singe and clean it
5. Season large lardons with salt, pepper, four spices, and fine herbs
6. If you wish, lard the breast and thighs of your turkey as finely as possible
7. Put lard slices in a braising pan, your turkey on top, two veal knuckles, the turkey legs, four carrots, six onions, three cloves, two bay leaves, thyme, a bouquet of parsley and chives
8. Cover your turkey with lard slices and a piece of buttered paper
9. Moisten it with bouillon, four pot spoonfuls, more if the turkey is large, a little salt
10. Simmer for three and a half hours
11. When cooked, remove the braising pan from the fire; do not remove your turkey for half an hour after, so it does not break apart
12. Pass your cooking liquid through a fine cloth
13. Reduce it by a quarter, or more if it is too thin
14. Break an egg into a saucepan; beat it well, and pour the jelly into it; beat it well with your egg
15. Check if it tastes good; if it lacks flavor, add a little aromatic flavoring, some parsley leaves, some chive stems
16. Put it on the fire, stirring vigorously until it is about to boil
17. When it reaches its first boil, place it on the edge of the stove with a lid and fire on top
18. Let it simmer for half an hour
19. Then pass it carefully through a fine cloth and let it cool to serve around the daube
