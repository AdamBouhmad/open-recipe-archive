## Ingredients

- Currants
- Water
- Sugar

## Directions

1. Separate the currants from the stems so the wood of the stems does not give a bitter taste
2. Place them in a pot with a glass of water
3. When they have boiled a few times and burst, place them in a horsehair sieve
4. Pass the juice a second time over the pulp so it is very clear
5. Squeeze it to extract all the liquid
6. For thirty pounds of juice, clarify thirty pounds of sugar and bring it to the crack stage
7. Pour the juice over the sugar and boil for a quarter hour
8. Skim well and pour into pots
9. Or put the currant juice on the fire and add sugar in pieces
10. Let the preserves boil well and skim
11. Place a few drops on a plate and tilt it to see if the preserve sets
12. You can use only three-quarters of a pound of sugar per pound of fruit, but the preserves must cook longer
13. If using cassonade, it must be clarified
