## Ingredients

- All sorts of small herbs, such as cress, tarragon, salad burnet, and chervil
- Ten cloves of garlic
- As many shallots
- Six onions
- A handful of mustard seed
- Twenty cloves
- A half-gros of mace
- One gros of long pepper
- One lemon cut into slices with its peel
- Vinegar

## Directions

1. In spring, around the month of June, gather all sorts of small herbs such as cress, tarragon, salad burnet, and chervil.
2. Dry these small herbs in the sun.
3. When they are dry, put them in a jug of about six pints capacity, with ten cloves of garlic, as many shallots, six onions, a handful of mustard seed, twenty cloves, a half-gros of mace, one gros of long pepper, and one lemon cut into slices with its peel.
4. Fill the jug with vinegar; after having well stopped it, expose it for eight days in the heat of the sun.
5. Pass it through a strainer to clarify it, put it in bottles that you will well stop, and you will use it afterwards as you judge fit.
