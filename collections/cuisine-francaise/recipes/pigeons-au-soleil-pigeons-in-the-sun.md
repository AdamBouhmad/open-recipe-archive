## Ingredients

- small young pigeons
- white wine
- one glass
- parsley
- chives
- one clove of garlic
- two cloves
- salt
- coarse pepper
- a small piece of butter
- flour
- two handfuls
- fine salt
- a little oil
- white wine for thinning the batter
- fried parsley

## Directions

1. Have small young pigeons well scalded and emptied; they must retain their wings, head, and feet
2. Pass a skewer through the thighs of each to prevent them from spreading too much
3. Blanch them briefly in boiling water
4. After thoroughly cleaning them, place them in a saucepan with one glass of white wine, a bouquet of parsley, chives, one clove of garlic, two cloves, salt, coarse pepper, and a small piece of butter
5. When cooked, drain them and let cool, then dip them in a batter and fry until golden
6. Serve hot with fried parsley around them
7. For the batter: put two handfuls of flour and fine salt in a saucepan with a little oil, and gradually add white wine to thin the batter until it is neither too thin nor too thick, meaning it should run from the spoon when poured
