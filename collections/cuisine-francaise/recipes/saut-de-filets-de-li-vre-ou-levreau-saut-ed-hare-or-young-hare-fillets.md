## Ingredients

- ten fillets of hare or young hare
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- butter
- white wine
- espagnole sauce
- flour

## Directions

1. Ten fillets are sufficient to make a sauté
2. Cut them into flat slices and flatten them with the blade of your knife
3. Trim them by cutting the angles and rounding them as well as possible
4. When your piece is trimmed, place it in the sauté pan or on a hollow cooking vessel successively until the end of your fillets
5. Season them with fine salt, coarse pepper, and a little grated nutmeg
6. Put warm butter on top
7. At the moment of serving, place your sauté pan over a hot fire
8. Take care to move it to detach your pieces of fillets
9. When they are stiffened on one side, turn them to the other side
10. It takes only a moment for your sauté to be cooked
11. Then put it in a saucepan
12. Remove the butter that is in your sauté pan without removing the juice from the fillets
13. Add a glass of white wine to detach what is stuck to the sauté pan
14. Add four spoons of espagnole sauce
15. Reduce everything by half and pass your sauce through a cloth onto your sauté
16. Take care to drain the juice from the fillet so that your sauce is not too thin, for the sauce must cling to your sauté
17. If you do not have sauce, heat the butter that is in the sauté pan, and if there is too much, remove half of it
18. When you see that what is on the bottom of the sauté pan begins to stick, add a spoonful of flour that you will mix with your butter
19. Add a glass of white wine, a glass of bouillon, a bay leaf, and a little thyme
20. Reduce everything by half and pass your sauce through a cloth onto your sauté
21. Before passing it, see if it has good seasoning
