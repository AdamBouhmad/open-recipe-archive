## Ingredients

- four calf's ears
- clear broth
- lemon or verjuice in grain
- salt
- pepper
- a bouquet of parsley
- cloves
- a hint of garlic
- a bay leaf
- one and a half liters of small peas
- a piece of butter
- a bouquet of parsley and chives
- a pinch of flour
- half juice and half broth
- sugar the size of a walnut
- fine salt
- coulis (optional)

## Directions

1. Boil the four calf's ears for a moment in hot water, then remove to cold water
2. When well cleaned, cook them with clear broth, a little lemon or verjuice in grain, salt, pepper, a bouquet of parsley, cloves, a hint of garlic, and a bay leaf
3. When cooked and white, remove and set aside
4. For the peas: pass them over the fire with a piece of butter, a bouquet of parsley and chives
5. Add a pinch of flour and moisten with half juice and half broth
6. Cook over low heat
7. When cooked, add sugar the size of a walnut and a little fine salt
8. If you have coulis, add a spoonful
9. The ragout should not be thin
10. Serve over the calf's ears
