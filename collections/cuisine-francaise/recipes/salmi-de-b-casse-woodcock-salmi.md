## Ingredients

- Woodcock
- Parsley
- Shallots
- Bay leaf
- Garlic
- Pepper
- Butter
- White wine
- Spanish sauce
- Consommé
- Clove
- Bouillon

## Directions

1. Put three woodcocks on the spit; when they are cold, remove the members as correctly as possible, pare them, and put them in a saucepan.
2. Crush your scraps in a mortar and add a pinch of parsley leaves, six shallots, a little bay leaf, a clove of garlic if you like, and coarse pepper.
3. Give a few blows with the pestle on these scraps.
4. Put a piece of butter in a saucepan and place it on the fire.
5. Put your pounded scraps in it and let them brown for ten minutes.
6. Pour in a glass of white wine, six spoonfuls of Spanish sauce, and three spoonfuls of consommé.
7. Reduce everything to almost half.
8. Make sure your sauce is well thickened and pass it through a strainer, pressing it.
9. Put it over your woodcock members which you keep warm without letting them boil.
10. At the moment of serving, arrange your members and put croutons between, underneath, or on top as you wish.
11. If you have no sauce, make a light roux and put your pounded scraps in it, then pass them through; pour in a glass of white wine, two glasses of bouillon, and a clove; reduce your sauce to half and pass it as described above.
