## Ingredients

- small onions, roots, turnips, cabbage, or celery (choice of one for garnish)
- butter
- water
- salt
- onion
- carrots
- parsnips
- celery root
- garlic clove
- bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- three cloves
- parsley
- chives
- bread crumb filets
- egg white

## Directions

1. Choose the vegetable you wish to use for the soup garnish
2. Cut it neatly and blanch it for a quarter hour in boiling water
3. Remove it to fresh water and cook it with a piece of butter, water, and salt
4. In a separate pot, place a piece of butter with onion, carrots, parsnips, celery root, garlic clove, bay leaf, thyme, basil, three cloves, parsley, and chives, all cut into small pieces
5. Ensure you do not put in this pot the same vegetable you are cooking separately for garnish
6. Toss these vegetables over the fire, turning them from time to time until they are cooked and colored
7. Add water and boil for half an hour with the vegetables
8. This should make a flavorful broth of beautiful color as if it contained meat juices
9. Pass it through a sieve and add it to the pot where the vegetable for garnish is cooking
10. When it is cooked and the broth seasoned to taste, use it to simmer the soup gently in a pot
11. Place the serving dish on the fire and arrange small filets of bread crumb on the edges, dipped in a little egg white, to make them stick
12. Arrange your vegetable garnish neatly on top
13. Pour the soup and serve, or if serving in a bowl, simply place the vegetable on top of the poured soup without elaborate arrangement
