## Ingredients

- Eight partridges
- Four spoonfuls of Spanish sauce
- Coarse pepper
- Bitter orange juice and zest
- Bouillon
- Partridge kidneys
- Light roux
- White wine
- Bay leaf

## Directions

1. Put eight partridges on the spit three-quarters of an hour before serving
2. When they are cooked, still slightly undercooked (in their own juice), remove them from the spit
3. Correctly remove the fillets
4. On the platter, arrange a glazed crouton of approximately the same shape as your fillets, but flat
5. In a casserole, put four spoonfuls of Spanish sauce, a little coarse pepper, the juice of a bitter orange with a little of the zest
6. Make your sauce bubble with a little bouillon and pour it under your fillets
7. If you do not have sauce, make a light roux
8. Pound some kidneys from your partridges and put them in your roux
9. Moisten with bouillon, half a glass of white wine, and a bay leaf
10. Reduce by half
11. Skim it and strain through a fine sieve
12. Squeeze your bitter orange into it as explained
