## Ingredients

- five hens
- salt
- pepper
- finely chopped parsley
- four skimming spoons of béchamel
- one spoon of consommé
- very good butter the size of half an egg
- croûton

## Directions

1. Remove the filets from five hens and pare them
2. Remove the nerve skin, place this side on the table, and slip your knife blade between this skin and meat without cutting too much into the flesh
3. Arrange them on your sauté pan
4. Season with salt and pepper
5. Dust with finely chopped parsley that has been washed so your whites do not take on harshness or a greenish color
6. At the moment of service, put them over high heat
7. Let them stiffen on one side, then turn them to the other
8. Do not leave them on the heat long
9. Tilt your sauté pan and put your filets on the edge so the butter separates from them
10. Add four skimming spoons of béchamel and one of consommé
11. When it has released three or four boils, add parsley chopped the size of half an egg of very good butter
12. Stir it into your sauce, dip your filets in it and arrange them in crowns with a glazed croûton between on your dish
