## Ingredients

- Three or four calf sweetbreads
- lard
- bouillon
- parsley
- chives
- garlic
- cloves
- tarragon
- salt
- verjuice
- butter mixed with flour
- egg yolks

## Directions

1. Soak and blanch three or four calf sweetbreads, then take half a pound of well-cooked lard, cut it into lardons and place in a casserole to render over low heat until almost cooked
2. Lard the sweetbreads across and place them in a casserole with good bouillon, a bouquet of parsley and chives, half a clove of garlic, two cloves, five or six tarragon leaves, and a pinch of salt
3. Cook the sweetbreads for half an hour, then pass the cooking liquid through a sieve and skim off the fat
4. Return it to the fire to reduce it into a glaze to coat the top of the sweetbreads
5. Put half a glass of bouillon in a casserole with two spoonfuls of verjuice, scrape up what remains in the pan, then add a piece of butter mixed with a pinch of flour the size of a walnut and two egg yolks
6. Make the sauce bind over the fire without boiling and serve under the sweetbreads as an hors-d'œuvre or side dish
