## Ingredients

- Turkey
- Three or four pounds of truffles
- Two pounds of lard, scraped
- One pound of best oil
- One pound of fine clarified butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Four spices
- Parsley, finely chopped
- Shallot, finely chopped
- Lard bard
- Oiled paper
- Truffles for sauce
- Espagnole
- Consommé

## Directions

1. Begin several days in advance for best results
2. Singe the turkey lightly and clean it well
3. Gut it through the vent
4. Remove the breastbone
5. Wash and clean three or four pounds of truffles according to the turkey's size
6. Chop the truffle peelings finely or pound them
7. Scrape two pounds of lard and put it in a saucepan with one pound of best oil and one pound of fine clarified butter (clarified because otherwise it would give a sour taste to the truffles; it would be better not to use it then)
8. Add your chopped or pounded truffle peelings and your whole truffles, salt, coarse pepper, four spices, finely chopped parsley and finely chopped shallot
9. Put your saucepan on the fire
10. When your preparation boils, let it cook for ten minutes
11. Stir your truffles from time to time
12. Place them to cool
13. When they are set in the fat, put them in your turkey
14. If time permits, prepare it eight days in advance
15. When putting it on the spit, truss it and cover it with a bard of lard
16. Oil paper and wrap it
17. Secure your turkey on the spit so it does not turn, because your truffles would fall into the dripping pan
18. Half an hour before removing it from the spit, remove the paper and give it a fine color
19. If serving as a relevé, chop two or three truffles and pass them through a little butter, and add five spoonfuls of espagnole and two of consommé, a little four spices and coarse pepper
20. When your sauce is somewhat reduced and degreased, pour it in a sauce boat
21. For roasting it is served without sauce
