## Ingredients

- Rabbit
- Lard and meat
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Aromatic spices, ground
- Truffles, chopped
- Lardons of scarlet tongue
- Lard strips (bardes)
- Calf knuckle (jarret de veau)
- Two carrots
- Three onions
- Two cloves
- Two bay leaves
- Thyme
- Parsley and chives bouquet
- Half-bottle white wine
- Bouillon

## Directions

1. Chop together more lard than meat; add salt, coarse pepper, a little ground aromatic spices, and chopped truffles
2. Extend the rabbit and season it; place a layer of stuffing, and over it lardons of scarlet tongue, pieces of truffles, and lardons
3. Place another layer of stuffing and continue thus
4. Once the rabbit is well filled, give it its original form, cover it with lard strips, tie it, and wrap it in white linen, tying it again
5. Place lard strips in a braising pan; put your rabbit in it with the calf knuckle cut in pieces along with rabbit trimmings, two carrots, three onions (one studded with two cloves), two bay leaves, a little thyme, a bouquet of parsley and chives, half a bottle of white wine, one spoonful of bouillon, and a little salt
6. Simmer your rabbit for two hours over very low heat
7. When cooked, remove the braising pan from the fire; after half an hour, carefully remove your rabbit without breaking it
8. Let it cool in its linen
9. Serve it glazed or with breadcrumb coating
10. If you need aspic for decoration, pass the cooking liquid through a cloth, clarify it like aspic, let the aspic cool, and serve it with your rabbit
