## Ingredients

- One good fat, well-fleshed chicken
- About a quarter pound of butter
- Flour (one tablespoon)
- Bouillon or water (one pot spoon)
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- One bay leaf
- A bouquet of parsley and chives
- Mushrooms
- Small onions
- Three egg yolks (for the liaison)
- Lemon juice

## Directions

1. Flame the chicken and disjoint it as correctly as possible, lifting the members without hacking or skinning them so they retain a beautiful form
2. Cut the end of the thigh at the side of the leg; break and remove the bone to the joint so your thigh has a beautiful form
3. Warm about a quarter pound of butter in a saucepan
4. Arrange the thighs, then the wings, breast, kidneys and the rest on top
5. Put your fricassee on the fire and stiffen the chicken members at the bottom of your saucepan
6. After they have remained an instant on the fire, sauté them
7. When they are all stiffened, pour a spoon of flour that you will mix with your chicken and butter
8. Add a pot spoon of bouillon; if you have none, moisten it with water
9. Add salt, coarse pepper, a bay leaf, a bouquet of parsley and chives, and mushrooms
10. Bring it to a boil, stirring constantly
11. Skim it and let it go to a high fire
12. One hour is sufficient to cook your fricassee, unless your chicken is tough
13. When it is three-quarters cooked, degrease it
14. Add small onions, all equal and well cleaned, as you judge appropriate
15. Once your fricassee is cooked, remove the bouquet and bay leaf
16. Add a liaison of three egg yolks; take care that it does not boil
17. Add lemon juice if you wish
