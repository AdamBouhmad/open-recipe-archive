## Ingredients

- one chicken, flamed, emptied, and plucked
- a veal knuckle of about one and a half pounds
- two pints of water
- a slice of green lemon, skin removed
- or white vinegar
- a quarter pound of sugar
- two or three grains of salt
- two pinches of coriander
- a very small piece of cinnamon
- three eggs with whites and yolks and shells

## Directions

1. Put in a pot a chicken that you have flamed, emptied, and plucked, a veal knuckle of about one and a half pounds, and two pints of water.
2. Bring them to boil and skim well, then simmer on very low heat for three hours.
3. Degrease your broth well and pass it through a fine tamis, put it in a casserole on a good stove with a slice of green lemon, skin removed.
4. If you have no lemon, put in a few drops of white vinegar, a quarter pound of sugar, two or three grains of salt, two pinches of coriander, a very small piece of cinnamon.
5. Boil for a quarter hour, and put in three eggs with whites and yolks and shells, boil gently while stirring often until your jelly is clear and reduced to about three half-setiers.
6. Pass it through a white cloth that is moistened, press it well so that it does not smell of lye and no water remains.
7. Put your jelly in the vessels where it must stay, let it set in a cool place or on ice.
