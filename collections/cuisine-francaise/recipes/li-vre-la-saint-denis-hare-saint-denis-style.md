## Ingredients

- One hare, plucked and cleaned
- Medium lardons, well seasoned with crushed aromatics, salt and pepper
- Salt
- Pepper
- Parsley sprigs
- Thyme
- Laurel
- Whole chives
- Two onions, sliced
- Hare liver, finely chopped
- Lard fat, the same amount as the liver, finely chopped
- Salt
- Crushed aromatics
- Coarse pepper
- Forcemeat for quenelles, twice the amount of the liver forcemeat
- Three egg yolks
- Lard strips
- Veal slices
- Two carrots, sliced
- Three onions
- Bouquet of parsley and chives
- Thyme
- Laurel
- Two cloves
- One bottle of good white wine
- Salt
- Espagnole sauce or light roux
- Butter
- Lemon juice

## Directions

1. When you have plucked your hare, clean it and make the smallest possible hole in its belly.
2. Cut off its head.
3. Season medium lardons to lard the fillets and thighs.
4. Place it to marinate for two or three days with salt, fine pepper, parsley sprigs, thyme, laurel, whole chives, and two sliced onions.
5. When you wish to serve it, finely chop the liver and chop an equal amount of fat lard together.
6. Add a little salt, some crushed aromatics, and coarse pepper.
7. Mix this forcemeat with twice as much quenelle forcemeat and blend them together.
8. Add three egg yolks and mix thoroughly.
9. Place this forcemeat in the body of the hare.
10. Sew up the belly skin so the forcemeat does not escape.
11. Place lard strips in a braising pan and the hare on top.
12. Cover it with lard strips.
13. Arrange around it some veal slices, two sliced carrots, three onions, a bouquet of parsley and chives, thyme, laurel, two cloves, a bottle of good white wine, and a little salt.
14. Simmer your hare for two hours or more if it is tough.
15. At the moment of serving, drain it, glaze it, and reduce the braising liquid until almost glazed.
16. Add two spoonfuls of espagnole sauce.
17. Place your hare on a plate with the sauce underneath.
18. If it is too seasoned, add a piece of butter the size of an egg and lemon juice.
19. If you have no espagnole, make a light roux and moisten it with the braising liquid in which your hare cooked.
20. Your sauce should be quite thin so you can reduce it by more than half.
21. At the moment of serving, strain it through a sieve and check the salt seasoning.
22. You can wrap this hare in lard strips, buttered paper, and put it on a spit, serving a poivrade underneath.
