## Ingredients

- Half pig's feet
- Thyme
- Bay leaf
- Carrots
- Onions
- Cloves
- Bouquet of parsley and chives
- Saltpeter
- Half bottle of white wine
- Bouillon
- Cooked chicken breasts
- Bread crumbs
- Veal udder (dried in bouillon)
- Egg yolks (three or four)
- Truffles
- Four spices
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Cream
- Pork or veal caul
- Butter

## Directions

1. Prepare your half pig's feet and cook them in the same seasoning as those prepared à la Sainte-Menehould.
2. Simmer them for eight hours.
3. Remove them from their cooking liquid when they are half cold.
4. Unwrap your feet and remove the bones.
5. Make a forcemeat with cooked chicken breasts; chop and pound bread crumbs; dry in bouillon over the fire the veal udder, using as much bread as chicken, as much udder as bread and chicken combined, all well pounded.
6. Mix in three or four egg yolks, chopped truffles, a little four spices, salt, coarse pepper, and a little cream.
7. When your forcemeat is finished, add truffles cut in slices.
8. Fill the interior of the foot in place of the bones with your forcemeat.
9. Cover this forcemeat with pork or veal caul.
10. Take care to preserve the shape of your feet.
11. Dip them in warm butter and coat them with breadcrumbs.
12. Fifteen minutes and a half before serving, place them on a grill over a gentle fire.
13. Pay attention that they are grilled on both sides.
14. Arrange them on the plate without sauce.
15. If you do not have chicken, use other meat such as veal, young rabbit, pheasant, etc.
