## Ingredients

- beef cut of desired size
- two or three carrots
- one parsnip
- three or four onions
- bouquet of parsley and chives
- one clove garlic
- three cloves
- one bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- bouillon or water
- salt
- pepper
- one large cabbage
- farce of godiveau or other meat farce
- coulis

## Directions

1. Take a cut of beef of the size you judge appropriate and tie it
2. Put it in a pot proportioned to its size with two or three carrots, one parsnip, three or four onions, a bouquet of parsley and chives, one clove garlic, three cloves, one bay leaf, thyme, and basil
3. Moisten with bouillon or water, season with salt and pepper
4. Cook at low fire in court bouillon
5. At half the cooking time, prepare small cabbage heads: take a large cabbage, remove the greenest leaves, and blanch it whole in boiling water for half an hour
6. Remove it to fresh water and press it firmly without breaking the leaves
7. Remove the leaves one by one and put a little farce of godiveau or other meat farce inside
8. Stack three or four leaves together as you fill them, one on top of the other, to form small cabbage heads slightly bigger than an egg
9. Tie them and cook them with the beef
10. When cooking is done, drain everything and wipe it of its fat
11. Arrange the beef in the serving dish, cut each cabbage head in half, and arrange them around the beef with the cut side out
12. For the sauce, take some of the cooking liquid, pass it through a sieve, degrease it, add a little coulis to bind it, reduce it over the fire to sauce consistency
13. Serve on the meat and on the cabbage heads
