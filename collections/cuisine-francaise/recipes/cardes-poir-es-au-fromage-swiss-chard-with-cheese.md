## Ingredients

- Swiss chard whites
- Water
- Salt
- Butter
- White sauce
- Gruyère cheese
- Parmesan cheese
- Breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Cut the white parts of Swiss chard to a length of four inches
2. Fill a saucepan with water and add salt and butter the size of an egg
3. When water boils, add the Swiss chard
4. Boil for three-quarters of an hour until the chard bends under your fingers
5. Drain well
6. Make a slightly bound white sauce with grated Gruyère and Parmesan cheese mixed in
7. Mix the drained chard into the sauce and sauté
8. Butter a dish and arrange in alternating layers of chard and grated cheese until full
9. Cover with grated cheese
10. Dip feathers in warm butter and drizzle over the cheese
11. Cover the chard with breadcrumbs
12. When well breaded, drizzle a little more butter over
13. Half an hour before serving, place the dish on red coals with a hot camp oven on top until it takes on a nice color
14. Blot the butter from the edges with soft breadcrumbs
