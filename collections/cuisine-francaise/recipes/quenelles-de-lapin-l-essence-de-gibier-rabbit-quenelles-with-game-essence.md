## Ingredients

- Rabbit forcemeat
- Butter
- Bread
- Meat
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Nutmeg
- Aromatics
- Eggs
- Game forcemeat or rabbit scraps
- Espagnole sauce
- White wine

## Directions

1. Fill a tablespoon with forcemeat and dip your knife in boiling water; smooth the forcemeat with the knife blade giving it a rounded shape
2. With another spoon also dipped in boiling water, remove the forcemeat from the first spoon and place it successively into a large buttered casserole
3. Prepare as many quenelles as needed
4. When the bottom of your casserole is full, place a round of buttered paper over your quenelles
5. Three quarters of an hour before serving, gently pour boiling salted water into your casserole
6. Let it boil very gently so your quenelles poach but do not burst
7. At the moment of serving, drain them on white linen
8. Prepare the sauce: put four tablespoons of degreased espagnole in a casserole, six tablespoons of game forcemeat, and half a glass of white wine
9. Reduce everything to one third, skim well, and pass through a sieve
10. Sauce and coat your quenelles with this preparation
