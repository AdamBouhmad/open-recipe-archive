## Ingredients

- Sorrel, according to the quantity of purée desired
- Three or four lettuce hearts
- A handful of chervil
- A good piece of butter
- Chopped mushrooms
- Shallots
- Parsley
- Four spoonfuls of velouté, or more according to the quantity of purée
- Or one spoonful of flour if velouté is not available
- Bouillon
- A little salt
- Pepper
- Five or six egg yolks

## Directions

1. Prepare the sorrel, lettuce hearts, and chervil, cleaning them well
2. Chop them well and press them to extract the juice
3. Put a good piece of butter in your saucepan with chopped mushrooms, shallots, and parsley, and cook them in the butter
4. Put the sorrel over your fine herbs and cook
5. When they are at their point, add four spoonfuls of velouté, or more according to the quantity of purée (see Velouté); if you do not have it, put one spoonful of flour and moisten your purée with bouillon, a little salt, and pepper
6. Make it reduce; when it is reduced enough, add five or six egg yolks
7. Pass it through a sieve and place it in a saucepan to use as needed
