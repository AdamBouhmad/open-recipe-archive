## Ingredients

- eel
- salt
- pepper
- bay leaves
- parsley
- chives
- white wine
- butter
- small onions
- flour
- mushrooms
- egg yolks
- crayfish

## Directions

1. After stripping your eel, cut it into sections of three inches
2. Put them in a saucepan with salt, coarse pepper, two bay leaves, sprigs of parsley, chives, and a bottle of white wine
3. Put your eel on the fire; when it is cooked, remove the pieces and put them in another saucepan
4. Pass its cooking liquid through a silk sieve
5. Put a piece of butter in a saucepan and pass twenty small onions in white; add a spoonful of flour that you mix with your butter
6. Add your cooking liquid with your onions; add mushrooms, a bouquet of parsley and chives
7. When your onions are cooked, remove them with a slotted spoon and put them on your eel
8. Make sure the sauce tastes good; degrease it and reduce it
9. If it is too thin, add a liaison of three egg yolks; be careful that it does not boil, and pass it through cheesecloth over your eel
10. At the moment of serving, put croutons fried in butter at the bottom of the dish; arrange your eel on top; cover it with your garnish and garnish it with crayfish
