## Ingredients

- One young, tender goose
- Goose liver
- Two shallots
- Parsley
- Chives
- Laurel leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- Butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Mustard
- Breadcrumbs
- Flour
- Vinegar

## Directions

1. Singe, pluck, and eviscerate a young tender goose
2. Chop the liver after removing the bitter part and mix it with two chopped shallots, parsley, chives, a laurel leaf, thyme and basil (chopped like powder), a good piece of butter, salt, and coarse pepper
3. Stuff the goose and sew it closed
4. Roast it on a spit, basting from time to time with a little butter
5. As you baste, hold a plate underneath to catch what falls
6. When the goose is almost cooked, mix one spoonful of mustard into the butter used for basting
7. Spread it back on the goose and bread it gradually until the entire top is well covered with breadcrumbs
8. Finish cooking until it has a beautiful golden color
9. For the sauce: in a casserole, place butter the size of half an egg mixed with two pinches of flour, one good spoonful of mustard, one teaspoonful of vinegar, and a small glass of bouillon, salt, and coarse pepper; bind over the fire
10. Serve the sauce under the goose
