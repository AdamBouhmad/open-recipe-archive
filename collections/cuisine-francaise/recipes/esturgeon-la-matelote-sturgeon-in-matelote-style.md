## Ingredients

- A piece of sturgeon
- Bread crumbs
- Butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Flour
- Shallot
- Parsley
- Chives
- Two glasses of red wine
- Capers

## Directions

1. Cut bread crumbs into rounds the size of a small coin.
2. Pass them over the fire with butter until they are a beautiful golden color and let them drain.
3. Cut a piece of sturgeon into small slices, slightly thin.
4. Put them in a plate, arranged so they are not on top of each other, with a piece of butter, salt, and coarse pepper.
5. Cook them over low heat, and as they are cooked on one side, turn them to the other.
6. Cooking takes only a quarter hour.
7. Remove them from the plate and put a little flour in it, which you stir with the butter, and then add finely chopped shallot, parsley, and chives.
8. Moisten with two glasses of red wine.
9. Let the whole boil together for a quarter hour.
10. Put the sturgeon back in the sauce to heat it without boiling.
11. Throw in a little chopped capers.
12. Garnish the edges of the plate with your fried bread croutons, being careful to sprinkle them a little with sauce.
