## Ingredients

- poularde (hen)
- beurre (butter)
- sel (salt)
- gros poivre (coarse pepper)
- épices (spices)
- bardes de lard (lard lardons)
- lard fin (fine lard)
- tranches de veau (veal slices)
- carottes (carrots)
- oignons (onions)
- clous de girofle (cloves)
- feuille de laurier (bay leaf)
- thym (thyme)
- persil (parsley)
- ciboules (chives)
- bouillon (broth)
- papier beurrée (buttered paper)
- ragoût de crêtes et de rognons de coqs (ragout of cock combs and kidneys)

## Directions

1. Flame, gut, and remove the breastbone of your hen
2. Mix butter, salt, coarse pepper, and a little spices
3. Fill your hen with this mixture
4. Bridle it as for an entrée
5. Make the stomach swell as much as possible
6. Lard it with fine lard a second thick, or end to end well garnished
7. Arrange lard lardons in the bottom of an oval saucepan
8. Put your hen there and around it a few veal slices, three carrots, three onions, two cloves, a bay leaf, a little thyme, a bouquet of parsley and chives
9. Fill with a pot spoon of grand broth
10. Cover with a buttered paper sheet
11. Put it on the fire an hour before serving
12. Place a hot fire on the lid
13. Take care that your fire does not glaze your piece too much
14. At the moment of serving, drain your hen, unbridle it, and dress it on your platter
15. Put underneath a ragout of cock combs and kidneys
