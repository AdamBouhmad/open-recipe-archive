## Ingredients

- one beef rump of ten to twelve pounds
- one pound lard cut into lardoons
- fine spices
- fine salt
- one-quarter pound powdered saltpeter
- one ounce juniper berries, somewhat crushed
- three bay leaves
- thyme
- basil
- one pound salt
- three chopines red wine
- one pint water
- five or six onions
- two cloves garlic
- four or five carrots
- two parsnips
- four or five cloves
- one-quarter nutmeg
- parsley
- chives

## Directions

1. Bone the beef rump as needed, cut one pound of lard into lardoons, season them with fine spices and fine salt
2. Lard the piece of beef all over without touching the top which is covered
3. Take one-quarter pound powdered saltpeter and rub the meat with it to make it red
4. Put the rump in a terrine with one ounce juniper berries somewhat crushed, three bay leaves, a little thyme and basil, and one pound salt
5. Cover the terrine and leave the meat in it for eight days
6. When it has taken on the salt, wash it with hot water
7. Put some lard on top of the rump on the side that is covered and defatted
8. Wrap it in a white cloth and tie it
9. Cook it at low fire for five hours with three chopines of red wine, one pint water, five or six onions, two cloves garlic, four or five carrots, two parsnips, one bay leaf, basil, thyme, four or five cloves, one-quarter nutmeg, parsley, and chives
10. When it is cooked, remove it from the fire and let it cool in its cooking liquid until completely cold
