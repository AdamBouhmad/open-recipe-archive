## Ingredients

- one turbot
- water to fill a large pot
- one pound of salt
- twenty bay leaves
- one handful of thyme
- one large handful of parsley branches
- twenty chives
- ten onions cut into slices
- lemon juice
- two pints of milk (if lemon is not used)

## Directions

1. After washing the turbot well, remove the gills and entrails from its pouch without damaging it
2. Make an incision on the dark side near the head along the line
3. Uncover and remove a piece of three joints to give flexibility to the turbot so it does not crack while cooking
4. With a larding needle and string, secure the thick part of the head with the bone that holds the pouch
5. Pour water into a large pot, one pound of salt, twenty bay leaves, one handful of thyme, one large handful of parsley branches, twenty chives, ten onions cut into slices
6. Boil the court-bouillon for a quarter hour
7. Strain it through a sieve and let it rest until it is completely clear
8. Place the turbot in a turbot cooker
9. Rub the white side well with lemon juice; if you do not use lemon, add two pints of milk, and pour the court-bouillon over it
10. Place on the fire and let it simmer without boiling for one hour, or longer if the turbot is very large
11. Ten minutes before serving, remove it with its sheet and let it drain
12. Arrange a napkin on a platter and slide the turbot onto it
13. If there are any cracks, fill them with parsley leaves and place some around it
14. Take care to unbind it
