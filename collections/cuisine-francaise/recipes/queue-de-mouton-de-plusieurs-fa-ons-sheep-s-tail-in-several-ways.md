## Ingredients

- sheep's tails, five or six
- bouillon
- onions, two
- roots, two
- bouquet of fine herbs
- salt
- pepper
- eggs, two
- bread crumbs
- fine oil or fat from the back of the pot
- sauce à l'échalotte

## Directions

1. Cook the tails in a small braise composed of bouillon, two onions and two roots, a bouquet of fine herbs, salt, pepper for three or four hours
2. When cooked this way, they can serve you for different preparations
3. If you want to put them on the grill when they are cold, dip them in two eggs beaten as for an omelette, bread them with bread crumbs
4. When all are breaded, dip them after in fine oil or fat from the back of the pot that is warm
5. Bread them a second time and put them to grill on a small fire
6. Be careful to baste them on the grill with the rest of the oil or fat
7. When grilled to a beautiful color, serve them dry or with a little sauce à l'échalotte
8. If it is to serve them fried, when they are cooked and cooled, bread them simply in beaten eggs, dip them in bread crumbs, and fry them to a beautiful color; serve them with fried parsley
9. When cooked à la braise, they are served with a coulis of lentils and small bacon, or a ragout of cabbage and small bacon
