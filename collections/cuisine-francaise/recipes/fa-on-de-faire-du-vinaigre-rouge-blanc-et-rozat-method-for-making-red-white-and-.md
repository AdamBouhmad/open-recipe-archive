## Ingredients

- A barrel
- The strongest vinegar
- Good vinegar
- Clean wine suitable for making vinegar
- Juice of ripe wild blackberries (optional, for color)

## Directions

1. According to the quantity of vinegar you wish to make, take a vessel more or less large; to make twenty pints, take a barrel of that size that is new; if it is old wood, you must have it planed inside.
2. Take one pint of the strongest vinegar that you make boil, and put it boiling into the barrel, which you will plug well with the bung, and roll it while shaking until it is completely cold.
3. Six hours later, remove this vinegar and place the barrel in a warm location.
4. After having bunged it, make a hole in the top of the barrel above the jable, large enough to insert a large funnel.
5. Pour in through the funnel two pints of good vinegar.
6. Eight days later, add one pint of clean wine suitable for making vinegar.
7. Every eight days add one pint of wine until the barrel is half full, then you may add more.
8. You must take care that the wine is always of the same strength as the first that you put in, because if it were weaker, the increase you would make would not have the same strength.
9. Your barrel being full and the vinegar in its state, withdraw two-thirds of it which you put in another vessel.
10. Then you will gradually return wine to the barrel as described above; by this means you always have vinegar.
11. For white vinegar: put ten pints of vinegar on the fire and let it boil until it is reduced to eight; then have it distilled in an alembic, putting in more or less according to the quantity you desire.
12. For rose vinegar: dry an ounce of musk roses in the sun for two days, which you will put in one pint of vinegar; place the roses and vinegar in the sun for fifteen days in a well-stopped bottle.
13. For tarragon, elder, or carnation vinegar: make the same way; for orange flower vinegar, put the flower without being dried.
14. For garlic vinegar: take four pints of white vinegar for one ounce of garlic, twelve cloves, and one nutmeg cut into pieces.
