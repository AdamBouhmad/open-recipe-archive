## Ingredients

- butter
- small pieces of bacon
- hare, cut into pieces
- white or red wine, or vinegar
- bouillon or water
- salt
- pepper
- bay leaves
- parsley
- chives
- mushrooms
- small onions

## Directions

1. Make a roux with more butter than is ordinarily used
2. When it is three-quarters made, add pieces of small bacon and make them turn in your roux over high heat for a moment
3. Then add your hare cut into pieces and when they are well browned, add a bottle of white or red wine, or a glass of vinegar according to taste
4. Finish moistening your civet with bouillon if you have it, or water
5. Add salt, taking care that your small bacon should give salt to your stew
6. Add pepper, two bay leaves, a bouquet of parsley and chives, and mushrooms
7. Your stew must be bathed, that is to say your meat must swim in the sauce
8. Cook it over high heat so that your liquid reduces to three-quarters
9. Have small onions, well peeled and all equal in size, that you will sauté in butter over high heat
10. When they are well browned and your civet is almost cooked, put them in
11. After a quarter hour and a half, remove your civet from the fire
12. Taste to see if it has good seasoning
13. Keep it warm on hot ashes until the moment of serving
