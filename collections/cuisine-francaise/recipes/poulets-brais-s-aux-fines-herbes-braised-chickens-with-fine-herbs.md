## Ingredients

- Two chickens
- Chicken livers
- Butter
- Parsley
- Chives
- Two leaves of tarragon
- Two or three branches of chervil
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Lard
- Paper
- Two roots or zests
- Two onions
- One clove of garlic
- Two cloves
- One bay leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- One glass of white wine
- Bouillon
- Herbs for salad garnish: tarragon, burnet, chervil, chives, watercress with walnuts

## Directions

1. Singe and pluck two chickens, empty them and chop the livers which you mix with a piece of butter, parsley, chives, two leaves of tarragon, two or three branches of chervil, all chopped, salt, coarse pepper
2. Stuff the chickens with this mixture and truss with the legs
3. Brown over the fire with a little butter or pot fat
4. Cook on the spit wrapped in lard and paper
5. Put in a saucepan the butter used to brown the chickens, with two roots or zests, two onions in slices, one clove of garlic, two cloves, one bay leaf, thyme, basil
6. Pass over the fire without browning
7. Moisten with one glass of white wine and as much bouillon
8. Boil gently for half an hour and strain through a sieve
9. Take herbs for salad garnish such as tarragon, burnet, chervil, chives, watercress with walnuts, each according to taste, to make about half a handful which you chop very fine
10. Put in the sauce to infuse for half an hour on hot ashes without boiling
11. Strain through a sieve and press the herbs to extract their essence
12. Add to the sauce the size of two walnuts of good butter mixed with a good pinch of flour, salt, coarse pepper
13. Bind over the fire without boiling
14. Serve over the chickens
