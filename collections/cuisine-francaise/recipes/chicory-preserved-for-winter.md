## Ingredients

- Water
- Salt
- Chicory
- Brine
- Clarified butter
- Parchment or paper

## Directions

1. Fill a large pot with boiling water and add two large handfuls of salt
2. When the water boils, add well-washed and drained chicory, pushing it down into the boiling water so it does not blacken
3. After boiling for ten minutes, remove the chicory and place it in cold water
4. When well cooled, remove and press it in your hands to extract the water
5. Arrange it in jars or pots
6. Make a brine and when it is cold and clear, pour it over the chicory
7. Cover with clarified butter, and when frozen, seal the pot with parchment or paper
8. Alternatively, when chicorries are trimmed, washed and well-drained, make a layer in a pot or tub, sprinkle salt on top, and repeat until full, then cover with a wooden round that fits inside the tub and place a stone on top
9. To use, soak in fountain water for two hours, then place in a pot with cold water and cook, testing from time to time until it yields under the fingers
