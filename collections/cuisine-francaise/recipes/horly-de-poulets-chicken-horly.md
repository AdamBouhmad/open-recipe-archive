## Ingredients

- Two fat chickens
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Parsley leaves
- Scallions
- Two bay leaves
- Juice of two lemons
- A dozen large onions
- Flour
- Oil for frying
- Aspic

## Directions

1. Singe and butcher the two chickens as you would for a fricassée and place the pieces in a vessel.
2. Season the chicken pieces with salt, coarse pepper, a pinch of parsley leaves, some broken scallions, two bay leaves, and the juice of two lemons.
3. Toss the chicken pieces in this seasoning.
4. Peel a dozen large onions of equal size and cut them into slices of equal thickness to form rings, discarding the centers.
5. Half an hour before serving, drain the chicken pieces and dust them with flour.
6. Toss them in a cloth and shake them well.
7. Place them in oil that is not too hot.
8. When they have a beautiful color and you judge them cooked, drain them on white cloth.
9. Heat your oil well.
10. Dust the onion rings with flour, toss them in a cloth, and place them in very hot oil.
11. When they have a beautiful color, drain them on white cloth.
12. Arrange the chicken pieces in a mound on a platter.
13. Place the onions on top and hot aspic underneath.
