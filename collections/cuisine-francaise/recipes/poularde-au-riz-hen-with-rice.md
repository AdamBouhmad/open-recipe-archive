## Ingredients

- poularde (hen)
- riz (rice)
- bouillon (broth)
- consommé (consommé)
- beurre (butter)
- muscade (nutmeg)
- sel (salt)
- gros poivre (coarse pepper)
- jaunes d'oeufs (egg yolks)
- jus de citron (lemon juice)
- bardes de lard (lard strips)
- espagnole (espagnole sauce)

## Directions

1. After flaming your hen, bone it entirely from the back, taking care not to damage the breast
2. Boil half a pound of well-washed and cleaned rice in broth for ten minutes; drain it on a horsehair sieve
3. Place a quarter pound of butter in a casserole and warm it; add your rice; grate a little nutmeg, add a little salt and coarse pepper, and mix everything together
4. Add four egg yolks and mix them well with your rice; let it cool; fill your hen with this mixture as much as it can hold
5. Sew up the back and give it its original shape; rub the breast with lemon juice; cover it with lard strips and tie it
6. Place lard strips in a casserole with your hen and a cooking vessel containing no lemon; place it on the fire one hour before serving
7. Take half a pound of clean rice and place it in a casserole with three times as much consommé or good broth (if the half pound of rice fills one glass, use three glasses of consommé); simmer your rice for three-quarters of an hour and drain it on a horsehair sieve
8. Mix in four spoonfuls of well-reduced espagnole sauce; add butter the size of an egg
9. At the moment of serving, drain your hen, remove the string, place it on the plate, and make a ring of your hot rice; pour a clear espagnole sauce with a piece of glace the size of half a walnut underneath your hen
10. If you do not have sauce, make a light roux; pass through a silk sieve the liquid in which your hen cooked and pour it over your roux; reduce it and use it in place of espagnole sauce; check that it has good seasoning
