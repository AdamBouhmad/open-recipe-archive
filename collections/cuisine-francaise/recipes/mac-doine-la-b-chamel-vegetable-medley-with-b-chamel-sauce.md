## Ingredients

- carrots
- turnips
- small onions
- peas
- asparagus
- white beans
- green beans
- artichoke hearts
- eggplants
- cauliflower
- cucumbers
- small fava beans
- béchamel sauce
- velouté or reduced cooking liquid
- three egg yolks
- butter
- bay leaf
- mushrooms

## Directions

1. Prepare many small roots and vegetables in equal and uniform portions, as described for vegetable medleys in entrées: carrots, turnips, small onions, peas, asparagus, white beans, green beans, artichoke hearts, eggplants, cauliflower, cucumbers, small fava beans, and all sorts of roots and vegetables.
2. When the vegetables are cooked in appropriate seasoning, drain them until completely dry.
3. Put everything in a saucepan.
4. Make a somewhat reduced and bound béchamel sauce and pour it over your vegetable medley.
5. Toss to mix the sauce with the vegetables.
6. If you do not have béchamel, reduce velouté and add a liaison of three egg yolks.
7. Pass the velouté through a fine cloth.
8. Put on top an amount of butter the size of an egg.
9. If not using velouté, clarify the cooking liquid from your roots and make a white roux.
10. Moisten with the clarified cooking liquid.
11. Add a bay leaf and mushrooms.
12. Reduce your sauce so it is somewhat bound.
13. Add a liaison of three egg yolks and pass the sauce through a fine cloth over the vegetables, adding an amount of butter the size of an egg.
14. Toss the vegetables in the sauce so the vegetable medley is well coated.
15. Keep it hot without boiling.
