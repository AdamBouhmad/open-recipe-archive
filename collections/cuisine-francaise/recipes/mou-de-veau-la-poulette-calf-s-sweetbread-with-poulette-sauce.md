## Ingredients

- One calf's sweetbread
- Half a pound of butter
- Two tablespoons of flour
- Broth
- Two bay leaves
- A bouquet of parsley and chives
- A handful of mushrooms
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Small onions
- A little nutmeg
- Three egg yolks

## Directions

1. Cut the sweetbread into pieces the size of a large onion
2. Soak it to disgorge
3. Blanch it for a quarter hour, pressing it well into the water so it does not blacken
4. Put it in cold water and drain
5. Melt half a pound of butter in a saucepan and add your sweetbread
6. Let it turn for a quarter hour
7. Pour in two tablespoons of flour and stir well with the sweetbread to mix
8. Moisten it with plenty of broth so it will reduce
9. Add two bay leaves, a bouquet of parsley and chives, a handful of mushrooms, salt, and coarse pepper
10. When the sweetbread is three-quarters cooked and reduced, add small, well-peeled onions
11. When the ragout is entirely cooked, keep it warm
12. At the moment of serving, prepare a liaison of three egg yolks with a little grated nutmeg
13. Add this liaison to your sweetbread, stirring constantly over the fire
14. Ensure it does not boil so the liaison does not break
15. Check that the seasoning is good
16. Be sure to degrease your ragout before binding it
