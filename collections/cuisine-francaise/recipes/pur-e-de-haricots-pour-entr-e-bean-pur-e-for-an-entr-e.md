## Ingredients

- One liter of beans
- Water
- Salt
- Butter
- A dozen onions
- Four spoonfuls of espagnole sauce
- One glass of bouillon
- Bouillon or cooking stock

## Directions

1. Cook one liter of beans in water, salt, and butter
2. Put three pounds of butter in a saucepan; clean a dozen onions, cut off the head and tail, and thin them
3. Put them with your butter and cook on a fairly hot fire
4. When your onions are nicely browned, add four spoonfuls of espagnole sauce and one glass of bouillon
5. Let your onions simmer for half an hour or three quarters of an hour
6. Add your cooked beans when your sauce is well reduced
7. Stir your beans well with your sauce
8. When the mixture is done, pass it through a sieve; if your purée is too thick, moisten it with a little consommé or bouillon
9. Once passed, do not boil it again to avoid bitterness; keep it hot in a bain-marie or on a gentle stove
10. If you do not have espagnole, put one spoonful of flour with your onions, stir it, and moisten your purée with bouillon or some cooking stock if you have it, and make your purée as explained
