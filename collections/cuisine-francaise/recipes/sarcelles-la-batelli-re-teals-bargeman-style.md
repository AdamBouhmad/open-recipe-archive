## Ingredients

- Teals
- Butter
- Shallots
- Parsley
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Nutmeg
- Flour
- White wine
- Lemon juice
- Lemon zest
- Bouillon

## Directions

1. Remove the thighs, fillets, and rump from your teals
2. Cut your fillets into three pieces lengthwise of the same thickness
3. Put a good piece of butter in a saucepan with your pieces of teal, chopped shallots, parsley, salt, coarse pepper, a little grated nutmeg
4. Put your saucepan on a hot fire
5. Sauté what is in your saucepan for ten to twelve minutes
6. Taste if your pieces are well browned
7. Then take a full spoonful of flour and mix it well with your ragout
8. Add a glass of white wine
9. Stir your ragout an instant until it comes to a boil
10. If your sauce was too thick, add a half glass of white wine
11. As soon as your ragout comes to a boil, remove it from the fire to eat right away
12. If you are in the city, when your teals are sautéed enough, add half a spoonful of flour, the juice of two lemons, a bit of its zest, and three or four spoonfuls of bouillon
13. Turn your ragout on the fire when it is thickened
14. Check that it is well seasoned and serve it
