## Ingredients

- veal tendons
- asparagus tips
- salt water
- velouté
- butter
- egg yolks
- flour
- coarse pepper

## Directions

1. Prepare your tendons as those said poêlés (pan-fried); your tendons must be whole so that it forms a well
2. At the moment of serving, drain them, glaze them, and dress them on the plate
3. Have asparagus tips from which you remove the leaves up to the button; cut them to a length of eight or ten lines
4. Blanch them in salt water until they are three-quarters cooked; drain them and put them in cold water so they keep their green color
5. Take reduced velouté and put your asparagus in it, making sure to drain them well; let them boil two or three times in your velouté
6. At the moment of serving, bind your ragout with two egg yolks; at the same instant add butter the size of half an egg; pour your bound ragout in the middle of your tendons
7. If you do not have velouté, when your asparagus are blanched, put half a quarteron of butter in a pan; sauté your asparagus in it; when you see they are well heated, put a spoonful of flour; sauté everything together so the flour mixes with the asparagus and butter
8. Take the cooking liquid from your tendons, pass it through a silk sieve, and moisten your asparagus tips with it
9. The sauce must be short, as it should only boil three or four times; then add a liaison of two egg yolks, and pour your ragout in the middle of your tendons; ensure it has good salt; add some coarse pepper
