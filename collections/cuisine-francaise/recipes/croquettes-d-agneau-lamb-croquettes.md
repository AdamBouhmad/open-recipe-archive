## Ingredients

- a lamb leg or other lamb meat
- lamb fat
- veal udder cooked
- mushrooms cooked
- nutmeg grated
- coarse pepper
- six spoonfuls of velvet sauce
- four spoonfuls of aspic
- three whole eggs and two yolks
- fine salt
- breadcrumbs
- butter

## Directions

1. Cook a lamb leg on the spit or other lamb meat that has been roasted
2. Cool and remove the meat
3. Remove skin and nerves; cut the meat into small dice
4. Take some lamb fat, cut into small dice, and mix with the meat
5. If no fat available, use cooked veal udder
6. Add cooked mushrooms cut into small dice
7. Add a little grated nutmeg and coarse pepper
8. Take six spoonfuls of velvet sauce and four spoonfuls of aspic, mix together
9. Reduce the sauce by more than half; it must be somewhat thick
10. When reduced, add a liaison of three egg yolks
11. Stir the sauce on fire without leaving it so the liaison does not break
12. When the sauce is bound, add butter the size of an egg and melt while stirring well
13. Check seasoning and pass through a sieve over the meat
14. Stir well when the sauce is added
15. Cool the ragout
16. Stir it and place spoonfuls on a tile, successively until finished, making eighteen or twenty portions
17. If the ragout is not cold enough, let it cool further to make it workable
18. Take the portions and place breadcrumbs on a table
19. Shape as desired: pear-shaped, ball-shaped, oval or elongated
20. Roll well in breadcrumbs
21. Beat three whole eggs and two yolks with a little fine salt and coarse pepper like an omelet
22. Dip the croquettes in the egg so it covers all sides
23. Place in breadcrumbs and ensure they are well breaded so they do not dissolve in frying
24. Leave on a tile
25. Just before serving, put frying oil on the fire
26. When very hot, place the croquettes in it
27. When colored, remove and drain on white linen
28. Dress in a pyramid and fry a little parsley
