## Ingredients

- Two fat, white chickens of equal form and without blemishes
- Butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Grated nutmeg
- Lemon juice
- Fine lard for larding
- Lard slices
- Veal slices
- Two carrots
- Three onions
- Two cloves
- One bay leaf
- Parsley and chives bouquet
- Buttered paper round
- Grand bouillon
- Spanish sauce
- Glace

## Directions

1. Take two fat white chickens of equal form without blemishes; singe and gut them; remove the breastbone; put a good piece of butter in a saucepan with salt, coarse pepper, a little grated nutmeg, lemon juice; mix the seasoning with your butter; fill your two chickens with it; truss them as for an entrée; lard them with fine lard on the breast; put lard slices in the bottom of a saucepan; place your two chickens there, lard slices around them, veal slices to fill the gaps, two carrots, three onions, two cloves, a bay leaf, a bouquet of parsley and chives, a round of buttered paper, about one tablespoon of grand bouillon; put them on the fire three-quarters of an hour before serving, and fire also on the lid: at the moment of serving, drain them, unbind them, glaze them, and dress them on the platter; you will serve a clear Spanish sauce in which you will add about the size of half a walnut of glace; one can serve underneath an aspic, tomato sauce, sautéed truffles, a small mixed ragout, a sautéed mushrooms.
