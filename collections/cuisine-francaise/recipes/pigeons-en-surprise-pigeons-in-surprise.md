## Ingredients

- Five small pigeons
- Five fine head lettuces
- Pigeon livers
- Parsley
- Chives
- Five or six tarragon leaves
- A little chervil
- Two shallots
- A piece of butter or grated lard
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Two egg yolks
- Bouillon, slightly fatty
- A bouquet of parsley
- Chives
- Two cloves
- Two onions
- One carrot
- One parsnip
- Veal coulis
- Butter mixed with flour
- Two egg yolks

## Directions

1. Scald the pigeons, empty them, and truss the legs into the body; set the livers aside
2. Blanch the pigeons briefly in boiling water
3. In the same water, blanch five fine head lettuces and boil them for a good quarter hour
4. Remove the lettuces to fresh water and press them firmly so no water remains
5. Split them in two without detaching the leaves
6. Make a farce with the pigeon livers, parsley, chives, five or six tarragon leaves, a little chervil, two shallots, all chopped very finely and mixed with a piece of butter or grated lard, salt, coarse pepper, bound with two egg yolks
7. Place the farce on top of each lettuce half
8. Wrap each pigeon with a lettuce so they are not visible
9. Tie them with string and put them to cook in slightly fatty bouillon with a bouquet of parsley, chives, two cloves, two onions, one carrot, one parsnip, salt, and pepper
10. Cook for one hour over low heat
11. When cooked, drain the pigeons and untie them
12. Wipe them of their fat with a cloth
13. Serve a good veal coulis over them; if you have no coulis, use slightly less salt in the cooking, pass the sauce through a sieve and degrease it
14. Reduce the sauce to the consistency of a sauce and add a knob of butter mixed with flour and two egg yolks
15. Finish on the fire without boiling
16. Serve the pigeons wrapped in lettuce
