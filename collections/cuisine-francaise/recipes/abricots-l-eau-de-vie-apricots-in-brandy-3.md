## Ingredients

- Apricots, not fully ripe
- Cold water
- Three pounds of sugar for every twelve pounds of fruit
- Five or six boils in the syrup
- Nine pints of brandy at 18 degrees

## Directions

1. Choose apricots that are not quite ripe and place them in a skillet with cold water
2. Put the skillet on the fire; as soon as the water begins to simmer and the apricots rise to the surface, carefully remove them with a skimmer
3. Throw them into cold water and let them boil once more; let them cool
4. Drain them on a sieve
5. Clarify three pounds of sugar for every twelve pounds of fruit; when it reaches the pearl stage, add the apricots
6. Let them boil five or six times; remove them from the syrup
7. Remove the apricots from the fire and let them drain; when they are cold, put them carefully into a jar without damaging them
8. If your sugar is not sufficiently reduced, let it boil a few more times; when you see the syrup will pearl, remove it from the fire and skim off the foam
9. Pour nine pints of good brandy at 18 degrees into the syrup; when the syrup is well combined with the brandy, pour it over your apricots
10. Put a cork stopper on the jar and a wet parchment to cover it; tie it with string
