## Ingredients

- Wild boar haunch
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Fine pepper
- Peppercorns
- Juniper
- Thyme
- Bay laurel
- Basil
- Onions
- Parsley
- Chives
- Lard lardoons
- Aromatic herbs
- Sage
- Quatre épices (four spices)
- White linen cloth
- Buttered braising pan
- Brine from marinade
- White wine
- Water
- Carrots
- Cloves
- Bouquet of parsley and chives
- Breadcrumbs

## Directions

1. Burn away the bristles on your haunch well
2. Clean it as best as possible
3. Bone it up to the joint of the shank
4. Lard it with thick lardoons seasoned with ground aromatics, a little sage, quatre épices, salt, and coarse pepper
5. When well larded, place ground aromatics inside
6. Place it in a terrine or tub with plenty of salt, fine pepper, peppercorns, juniper, thyme, bay laurel, basil, onions cut into slices, parsley in branches, and whole chives
7. Leave to marinate four or five days if you have time
8. When you wish to cook it, remove the aromatics from inside your haunch
9. Wrap it in white linen
10. Truss it like a piece of beef
11. Place it in a braising pan with the brine in which it has marinated, six bottles of white wine, as much water, six carrots, six onions, four cloves, a strong bouquet of parsley and chives, and salt
12. If you believe the brine is not sufficient to give it good seasoning, let it simmer for six hours
13. Test it with a skewer to ensure it is cooked; if not, cook it another hour
14. After that, let it rest half an hour in its cooking liquid and remove it
15. Leave it in its skin, or if desired, cover it with breadcrumbs, or if your haunch is fatty, remove the skin and leave it white
16. Glaze it and ensure it has a beautiful shape
