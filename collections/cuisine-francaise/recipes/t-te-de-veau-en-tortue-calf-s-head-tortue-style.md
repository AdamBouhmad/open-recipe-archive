## Ingredients

- calf's head
- lard for barding
- fine white linen
- one bottle dry Madeira wine
- four spoonfuls of large Spanish sauce
- three spoonfuls of consommé
- ten cloves of hot chili pepper, well crushed
- veal quenelles
- calf's tongue
- rooster crests and kidneys
- small pieces of veal
- veal sweetbreads in pieces
- eight or ten hard-boiled egg yolks
- twelve egg ends (whites cut into small cup shapes)
- cornichons cut into sticks
- turned mushrooms
- crayfish
- nasturtium seeds preserved in vinegar

## Directions

1. Prepare your head in pieces as described in the previous recipe
2. Have a fine, white linen cloth, washed in clean water, laid on the table with lard bards
3. Take the pieces of head, tie them with string, place them on the lard bards, and cover them with more lard bards
4. Wrap the pieces with the linen cloth and tie both ends with string
5. Place this wrapped head in a braising pan or casserole and put a lid on top
6. Add one bottle of dry Madeira wine
7. Bring to a boil; when it reaches a boil, reduce to a gentle simmer
8. Put fire on your cover and let your head cook gently: three hours of cooking is sufficient; test with a larding needle
9. If the pieces are still too firm, cook longer
10. At the moment of serving, take both ends of your linen cloth, remove it from the casserole, untie it, drain your pieces and brain which are inside, and arrange them on the plate
11. Pour the ragout over them
12. For the ragout: take four or more spoonfuls of large Spanish sauce (the sauce should be plentiful) and put in a casserole
13. Pour in one bottle of dry Madeira wine, three spoonfuls of consommé, ten cloves of hot chili pepper well crushed
14. Reduce your sauce to half
15. When finished, add veal quenelles, tongue cut into pieces, rooster crests and kidneys, small pieces of veal, veal sweetbreads in pieces and other cooked garnishes
16. You can add eight or ten hard-boiled egg yolks, twelve egg ends (the whites with the tip cut off forming a small cup), cornichons cut into sticks, turned mushrooms, crayfish, nasturtium seeds preserved in vinegar
17. Ensure the ragout is very hot but does not boil
18. Pour it over the head arranged in a pyramid; the ragout must be well seasoned
19. If you do not have Spanish sauce, make a somewhat strong roux so your sauce is somewhat plentiful; moisten it with a little cooking liquid and Madeira wine
20. You could also use the cooking liquid in which your head was cooked; if nothing else, add the garnishes you have available, but always cornichons, hard-boiled eggs, quenelles and chili pepper
