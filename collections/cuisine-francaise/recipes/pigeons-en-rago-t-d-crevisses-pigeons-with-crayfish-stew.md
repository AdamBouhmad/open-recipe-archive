## Ingredients

- three or four medium pigeons
- good bouillon
- white wine
- bouquet of parsley and chives
- garlic clove
- cloves
- salt
- pepper
- mushrooms
- good butter
- crayfish
- flour
- cream
- nutmeg

## Directions

1. Blanch the pigeons after gutting them, split them slightly on the back to open the breast a bit, and cook them with good bouillon, a glass of white wine, a bouquet of parsley and chives, a garlic clove, two cloves, salt, and pepper.
2. When cooked, put mushrooms (the size of half an egg), good butter, and a dozen peeled crayfish in a casserole.
3. Pass them over the fire and add a pinch of flour, moisten with the cooking liquid from the pigeons that you pass through a sieve.
4. Let the stew boil for half an hour until little sauce remains.
5. Add a liaison of three egg yolks with cream, a little nutmeg, and a small pinch of very finely chopped parsley; thicken without boiling over a moderate fire while stirring constantly.
6. Drain the pigeons to dress them in the serving dish and put the crayfish stew on top.
