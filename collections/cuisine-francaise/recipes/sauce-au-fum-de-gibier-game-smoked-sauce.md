## Ingredients

- Three or four partridges or wild rabbits
- Two carrots
- Three or four onions
- Two cloves
- Two bay leaves
- A little thyme
- Half a bottle of white wine
- Game essence or consommé
- Six to eight tablespoons of Spanish sauce or other suitable sauce

## Directions

1. Put the partridges or rabbits, carrots, onions, cloves, bay leaves, and thyme in a saucepan
2. Moisten everything with half a bottle of white wine
3. Reduce the wine until it is a glaze
4. Moisten with game essence if you have it; otherwise use consommé
5. When your game is cooked, pass this essence through a cloth
6. Work in six to eight tablespoons of Spanish sauce or another suitable sauce
7. When your sauce is reduced and degreased to the right point, pass it through muslin and put it in a saucepan in a bain-marie
8. You can also use game scraps for this sauce; if you lack Spanish sauce or essence, moisten the game scraps with white wine that you reduce to a glaze, or with bouillon; incorporate this into a brown sauce that you reduce; your sauce should be of good salt and flavor
