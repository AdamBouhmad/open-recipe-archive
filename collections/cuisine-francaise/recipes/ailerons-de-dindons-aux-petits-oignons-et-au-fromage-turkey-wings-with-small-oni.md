## Ingredients

- six or eight turkey wings
- a bouquet of parsley, chives
- two cloves
- half a bay leaf
- a little basil
- one glass of white wine
- an equal amount of broth
- at least a dozen small onions
- salt, coarse pepper
- beurre manié with flour
- Gruyère or Parmesan cheese, grated

## Directions

1. Blanch the turkey wings, clean them, and place them in a saucepan with a bouquet of parsley, chives, two cloves, half a bay leaf, and a little basil
2. Moisten with one glass of white wine and an equal amount of broth
3. Cook over low heat
4. After half an hour, add at least a dozen small onions blanched for a quarter-hour in boiling water and well cleaned
5. Season lightly with salt and coarse pepper
6. Finish cooking and remove the wings and onions from the pan to drain them
7. Strain the sauce through a sieve and reduce it if too thin
8. Add a piece of butter worked with a pinch of flour and thicken over the fire
9. Put a little sauce in the bottom of the serving plat and cover with a handful of grated Gruyère or Parmesan
10. Arrange the wings and small onions on top, pour the remaining sauce over everything, and cover with more grated cheese
11. Place the plat on a small stove to simmer over low heat until almost no sauce remains
12. Brown the top with a hot shovel or a tourière cover with good heat and serve hot
