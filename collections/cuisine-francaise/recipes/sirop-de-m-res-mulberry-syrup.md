## Ingredients

- two hundred of beautiful black mulberries
- one large glass of water
- two pounds of sugar
- one half-setier of water

## Directions

1. Put the mulberries on the fire with one large glass of water
2. Let them boil five or six times until they have rendered all their juice and pass through a sieve
3. Let it rest and pass it a second time through a finer sieve
4. Put two pounds of sugar on the fire with a half-setier of water
5. Let it boil and skim
6. Continue boiling until dipping two fingers in water and putting them in the sugar, you redip them in fresh water, the sugar remaining on your fingers breaks cleanly
7. Add your mulberry water and heat until it is incorporated with the sugar, taking care that it does not boil
8. Put it afterwards in a well-covered terrine to place on warm ashes for three days
9. Maintain it with equal heat as much as you can without it being burning
10. You will know it is at its point when taking syrup with one finger and pressing it against the other, and opening both, a thread forms that does not break easily
11. Put it in bottles and do not cork it until it is completely cold
