## Ingredients

- poularde (hen)
- moules (mussels)
- persil (parsley)
- ciboules (scallions)
- beurre (butter)
- béchamelle (béchamel sauce)
- velouté (velouté sauce)
- consommé (consommé)
- muscade râpée (grated nutmeg)
- jaunes d'oeufs (egg yolks)
- farine (flour)
- bouillon (broth)

## Directions

1. Truss and cook your hen as you would a poêlée hen; rinse the mussels thoroughly in several waters and place them in a casserole over a hot fire to detach them; do not let them boil so they do not shrivel
2. Drain them in a casserole; pass some parsley and scallions through a small piece of butter; add four spoonfuls of béchamel (or if you have none, velouté), one spoonful of consommé, and a little grated nutmeg
3. Bring your sauce to a boil a few times and pour it over your mussels
4. At the moment of serving, drain your hen, untruss it, and place it on your plate; add to your mussels a liaison of three egg yolks
5. If using velouté, place them on the fire to bind; do not let them boil
6. If you do not have velouté, add a little flour to your herbs and butter; moisten with broth and proceed as described above
