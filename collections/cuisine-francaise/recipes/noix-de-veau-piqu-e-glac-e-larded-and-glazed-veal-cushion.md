## Ingredients

- One whole veal cushion
- Thin larding fat
- Lard bards
- Veal slices
- Two or three carrots
- Four onions, one studded with two cloves
- One bay leaf
- A bouquet of parsley and chives
- Consommé
- Chicory
- Cream
- Cucumbers
- Glazing sauce or tomato sauce

## Directions

1. Beat the veal cushion well
2. Remove the skin and teat covering the cushion
3. Place the meat skin-side down on the table
4. With a sharp knife, slip it between the top nervous layer and the flesh, as if lifting a bard, being careful not to cut too deeply into the meat to minimize waste and keep the cushion well-formed
5. Remove any remaining skin as gently as possible so no knife marks are visible
6. Lard the prepared cushion with thin larding fat
7. Place lard bards in a casserole with a few veal slices, two or three carrots, four onions (one studded with two cloves), one bay leaf, a bouquet of parsley and chives
8. Position the cushion in the casserole so it rounds in the middle
9. Cover with a double sheet of buttered paper
10. Add one spoonful of consommé
11. Bring to a boil; when it has thrown off a few bubbles, place on gentle heat and put fire on the lid
12. Cook for two hours
13. At serving time, drain it well, glaze it, and serve underneath with chicory, cream, cucumbers, and either glazing sauce or tomato sauce as you see fit
