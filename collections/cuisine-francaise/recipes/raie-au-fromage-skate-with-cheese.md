## Ingredients

- half of a fine bouclée skate
- milk (demi-setier)
- butter (size of half an egg)
- flour (two pinches)
- garlic clove
- two cloves
- two shallots
- bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- salt
- pepper
- Gruyère cheese, grated
- twelve small white onions
- two small pieces of fried bread, cut in rounds
- parmesan cheese (optional)

## Directions

1. Skin a fine half of the bouclée skate and cut it into four equal pieces
2. Wash it and cook with a demi-setier of milk and butter kneaded with two pinches of flour, a garlic clove, two cloves, two shallots, a bay leaf, thyme, basil, little salt, and pepper
3. Boil before adding the skate (cooking requires little time)
4. Remove the skate from the sauce to drain it
5. Strain the sauce through a tamis and reduce it to the consistency of a bound sauce
6. Put half the sauce in the bottom of the serving dish
7. Sprinkle a small handful of grated Gruyère cheese over it
8. Arrange the skate pieces on top
9. Between the pieces, arrange a dozen small white onions cooked in broth and well drained, and two small pieces of fried bread cut in rounds, interspersing them neatly
10. Pour the remaining sauce over everything
11. Cover with grated Gruyère or Parmesan cheese
12. For best results, place the dish over a low fire that boils gently until there is almost no sauce left
13. Glaze the top with a hot shovel or a pie-dish cover with a good fire underneath
14. When the top is a beautiful golden color, serve
