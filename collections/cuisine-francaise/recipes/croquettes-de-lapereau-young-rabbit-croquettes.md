## Ingredients

- Three young rabbits
- Béchamelle, one small spoonful
- Butter, the size of an egg
- Coarse pepper
- Salt
- Nutmeg, grated
- Breadcrumbs
- Eggs, beaten with salt and coarse pepper
- Parsley for frying

## Directions

1. Roast three young rabbits on the spit
2. Remove the fillets and the largest part of the thighs
3. Cut the meat into small dice, removing nerves and skin
4. Place the meat in a casserole with one small spoonful of béchamelle
5. Reduce the béchamelle to almost half
6. Add butter the size of an egg to the sauce without placing it on the fire, allowing it to melt
7. Pass the sauce through a sieve over the rabbit meat
8. Add a little coarse pepper, a small amount of salt, and a little grated nutmeg
9. Mix the meat well with the sauce; it should be slightly thick
10. When cold, form small portions with a spoon, slightly smaller than an egg
11. Shape the croquettes as desired: egg-shaped, pear-shaped, or rounded sticks
12. Roll them in breadcrumbs
13. Dip them in beaten eggs with a little salt and coarse pepper
14. Ensure the eggs coat all the croquettes
15. Roll them again in breadcrumbs, ensuring they are well coated all over to prevent them from bursting
16. At the moment of serving, put them in fairly hot fat
17. When they have a nice color, remove them and drain on white linen
18. Fry a handful of parsley and place it on top of and around the croquettes
19. If you do not have béchamelle, reduce velouté into which you put a liaison of three eggs
