## Ingredients

- Sheep's feet, deboned
- Poultry quenelle farce with finely chopped herbs, salt, coarse pepper, and grated nutmeg
- Or godiveau with the same seasoning
- Half a pound of grated lard
- Half a pound of beef fat
- Half a pound of veal round cut into dice
- Butter, the size of two eggs
- Two lemons cut into slices without the white
- Two carrots cut into dice
- Two onions
- Two cloves
- Two bay leaves
- Thyme
- Salt
- Water
- Velouté, reduced
- Two eggs
- Coarse pepper
- Grated nutmeg
- Lemon juice

## Directions

1. After deboning your sheep's feet, fill them with poultry quenelle farce in which you put finely chopped herbs, a little salt (because in cooking it will lose its seasoning), a little coarse pepper, a little grated nutmeg
2. If you have no quenelle farce, use godiveau with the seasoning described
3. Sew the end closed with a needle and thread so the farce does not come out
4. Blanch them, refresh them, drain them, dry them
5. Flame them and remove the hair found in the cleft of the foot
6. Put in a casserole half a pound of grated lard, half a pound of beef fat, half a pound of veal round cut into dice, butter the size of two eggs, two lemons cut into slices without the white, two carrots cut into dice, two onions, two cloves, two bay leaves, and thyme
7. Pass everything together over the fire
8. When it has browned a bit, add salt and one spoonful of water
9. When your blanc boils, arrange your twelve or fifteen sheep's feet in a casserole and pour your blanc over them
10. Put a double round of buttered paper so your feet do not blacken
11. Let them simmer on very low heat for four hours
12. After this time, test them to see if they are cooked
13. At the moment of service, drain them, cut the two extremities, remove the thread, arrange them on the plate
14. Coat them with a velouté that has been reduced, in which you put a liaison of two eggs, a little coarse pepper, a little grated nutmeg, and the juice of a lemon
15. Check that your sauce is properly salted
16. These feet can be put with different sauces, such as tomato sauce, Spanish sauce, onion purée, or Provençale style
