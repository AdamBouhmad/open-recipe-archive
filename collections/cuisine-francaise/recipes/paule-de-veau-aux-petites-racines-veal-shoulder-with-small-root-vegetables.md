## Ingredients

- veal shoulder
- lard for larding, cut into strips
- fine salt
- coarse pepper
- parsley, finely chopped
- two bay leaves
- thyme, finely chopped
- four spices
- lard for bardes
- veal slices
- shoulder bones
- six carrots
- seven or eight onions
- two bay leaves
- three cloves
- parsley and chives bouquet
- bouillon or water and salt
- small root vegetables (carrots and turnips), turned into olives or small sticks
- brown sauce or light roux
- lettuce or mushroom purée (optional)

## Directions

1. Bone the veal shoulder as you would for a galantine
2. Prick the shoulder internally with lard strips seasoned with fine salt, coarse pepper, finely chopped parsley, two bay leaves, finely chopped thyme, and a little four spices
3. When the shoulder is well larded, roll it lengthwise
4. Tie it the same way as the galantine
5. Put lard bardes, several slices of veal, and the shoulder bones in the bottom of a braising pan
6. Place the tied shoulder on top, cover it with lard
7. Add six carrots, seven or eight onions, two bay leaves, three cloves, a bouquet of parsley and chives
8. Moisten the shoulder with bouillon; if you have none, use water and salt
9. Cover with buttered paper
10. Bring to a boil and place over a gentle fire
11. Also place fire on the braising pan's lid
12. Three hours are sufficient for it to be cooked
13. At the moment of serving, drain it, untie it, glaze it, and place it on a long plat
14. Arrange many small root vegetables, turned into olives or small sticks, around the shoulder; they should be cooked in brown sauce
15. If you do not have lard or veal to cook it, simply cook it with the bones, vegetables and aromatics, moistening with water in place of bouillon and adding salt
16. If you do not have sauce for your small root vegetables, make a light roux which you will moisten with the cooking liquid from your shoulder, and add your small root vegetables (after blanching them) with a small piece of sugar to remove any bitterness
17. You can also place lettuces around this shoulder, a mushroom purée, etc.
