## Ingredients

- Sheep's feet, scalded
- Blanc (white cooking liquid)
- Six spoonfuls of velouté
- Four spoonfuls of consommé
- Two egg yolks
- Nutmeg
- Coarse pepper
- Butter, the size of two eggs
- Chives or shallots, finely chopped
- Parsley, finely chopped
- One spoonful of flour
- One small spoonful of bouillon
- Lemon juice
- Salt

## Directions

1. Have sheep's feet that have been scalded
2. Clean them up to the joint and blanch them; refresh them
3. Remove the tuft of wool found in the cleft of the feet
4. Dry them well and put them to cook in a blanc
5. Let them simmer on the fire for four hours
6. Test them to see if they are cooked; if the flesh yields under the fingers, remove them to drain
7. Trim the extremities so that your feet are whole and clean
8. Put them in a casserole and pour in six spoonfuls of defatted velouté and four of consommé
9. Reduce it almost by half
10. Just before serving, add a liaison of two egg yolks to your sauce with a little nutmeg
11. When thickened, pass it through fine cloth over your feet
12. Keep them warm without letting them boil
13. Add a little coarse pepper
14. If you have no velouté, take butter the size of two eggs in a casserole with finely chopped chives or shallots and let them warm in the butter
15. Add a good pinch of finely chopped parsley and stir with the hot butter
16. Add one spoonful of flour and stir everything together
17. Add one small spoonful of bouillon, a little coarse pepper, and a little nutmeg
18. Add a liaison of two eggs to your sauce
19. When it has boiled a little, pour it over your feet
20. At the moment of serving, add lemon juice and check that your sauce is properly salted
