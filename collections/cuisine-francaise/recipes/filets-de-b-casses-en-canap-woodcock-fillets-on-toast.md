## Ingredients

- Fillets from four woodcocks
- Salt
- Pepper
- Butter
- Interior of the woodcocks except the gizzard
- Cooked lard the size of an egg
- Parsley
- Shallots
- Aromatic herbs
- Bread for croutons

## Directions

1. Remove and trim the fillets from four woodcocks; arrange them in your sauté pan
2. Season them with salt and pepper; warm a piece of butter and pour it over your fillets
3. Take the interior of your woodcocks, except the gizzard; mix together cooked lard the size of an egg, finely chopped parsley and shallots, a little pounded aromatic herbs, salt, and coarse pepper; chop everything together with your knife
4. When your forcemeat is ready, make croutons slightly larger and in the same shape as your fillets, thick about half an inch
5. Before browning them in butter, make an incision one line from the edge inside all around; when your crouton is browned, hollow it out so it can hold forcemeat
6. Fill each crouton completely so you can place your fillet on top
7. About a quarter hour before serving, put your croutons in the oven or on a grill with gentle heat
8. Sauté your fillets, arrange your croutons flat, and place one fillet on each crouton
9. Cover with the same sauce as before, adding about a walnut-sized piece of glaze
