## Ingredients

- ten or fifteen onions
- butter
- lamb square
- bouillon
- salt
- coarse pepper
- caul
- egg yolks
- egg
- bread crumbs
- olive oil or fat
- white wine
- jus

## Directions

1. Cut into slices about ten or fifteen onions, depending on their size.
2. Pass them on the fire with a piece of butter, stirring often with a spoon, until they are completely cooked.
3. Cut a lamb square into cutlets and cook them on low heat with bouillon, a little salt, and coarse pepper.
4. When they are cooked, reduce the sauce until it clings entirely to the cutlets and remove them on a plate.
5. Drain the fat in the saucepan and put half a glass of bouillon in the same saucepan to detach what sticks to it.
6. Pass this small sauce through a sieve to mix with the onions and three egg yolks.
7. Bind them on the fire without boiling, for fear they turn.
8. Take caul that you have soaked in water; press it well to divide it into as many pieces as you have cutlets.
9. Put on each piece of caul one cutlet with the onion all around the cutlet; wrap everything with caul and seal all the edges with beaten egg.
10. Then dip them everywhere in beaten egg to coat them with bread crumbs.
11. Arrange them on a plate that can go in the fire.
12. Baste all the top with good fat or olive oil.
13. Put them in the oven or under a pastry lid cover until they have taken on a beautiful golden color and remove them on a cloth to drain them of their fat.
14. Serve with a sauce made as follows: Put in a saucepan half a glass of white wine, as much bouillon, a little jus, salt, and coarse pepper.
15. Boil and reduce by half; serve under the cutlets.
