## Ingredients

- veal breast
- needle for trussing
- gelée or bouillon
- lard bardes
- buttered paper
- three or four carrots
- four onions
- two bay leaves
- two cloves
- bouillon or water
- salt
- parsley and chives bouquet
- grande espagnole sauce
- flour
- coarse pepper

## Directions

1. Cut your breast as squarely as possible
2. Bone it to within two finger-widths of the tendons
3. Secure the meat with a trussing needle so that your breast is well rounded on top
4. Take care to cut the tender bones that extend beyond the tendons
5. Place your breast with the tendons facing down in your pan with a pot spoon of gelée
6. Cover your breast with lard bardes and buttered paper
7. Bring to a boil, then place over a gentle fire
8. Also place fire on the braising pan's lid
9. Two and a half hours of cooking are sufficient
10. If you do not have gelée to moisten your breast, add three or four carrots, four onions, two bay leaves, two cloves, a pot spoon of bouillon; if you have none, use water with salt, and a bouquet of parsley and chives
11. When your breast is three-quarters cooked, remove your vegetables and reduce your cooking liquid to a glaze
12. When your breast is cooked and the glaze is at the bottom of your pan, turn your breast so that the top side glazes
13. At the moment of serving, untie your breast and place it on your plat
14. Add two large spoonfuls of defatted grande espagnole sauce to your glaze with a spoonful of bouillon
15. Detach your glaze over the fire and place this sauce under your breast
16. If you do not have sauce, remove the fat from your glaze, add a little flour and a little bouillon, or water if you have none
17. Detach your glaze over the fire, stirring with a wooden spoon
18. Add a little coarse pepper and salt if your sauce is too mild
19. Pour this sauce under your breast
