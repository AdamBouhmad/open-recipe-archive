## Ingredients

- Two chickens
- Butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Juice of one lemon
- Lard
- Citron slices

## Directions

1. When your two chickens are plucked, singe them lightly, pluck them clean, and gut them through the cavity to avoid making a hole at the croup; remove the breast bones; put a good piece of butter in a saucepan with salt, coarse pepper and the juice of a lemon; mix this seasoning with your butter; fill your chickens with it; cut off the neck near the kidneys; truss them with a trussing needle and string that you pass from one thigh to the other, putting one leg between your string and securing it on the thigh; make the breast swell and give your chicken as much grace as possible; arrange the skin of the cavity so the butter does not escape; put lard slices in a saucepan; cut very thin slices of citron; place them on the breast of your chickens; place them in your saucepan; cover them with lard and put a lid on top to cook them; three-quarters of an hour before serving, put them on the fire; take care that they always simmer; at the moment of serving, drain them and unbind them; trim the hole near the croup; dress them on the platter; you will serve with hot aspic, thickened velouté, tomato sauce, sautéed mushrooms, a ragout of rooster crests and kidneys, etc.
