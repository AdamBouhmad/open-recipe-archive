## Ingredients

- Larks
- Butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Pounded aromatic herbs
- Parsley, finely chopped
- Shallots, finely chopped
- Mushrooms, chopped
- Espagnole sauce
- Bouillon
- Flour
- White wine

## Directions

1. Pluck, truss and singe your larks; place a good piece of butter in a casserole with fifteen or eighteen larks, salt, coarse pepper, and a little pounded aromatic herbs; place them on a hot fire
2. Once you have sautéed them in your butter for seven or eight minutes, add about one spoonful of finely chopped parsley, the same amount of finely chopped shallots, and chopped mushrooms; sauté them with fine herbs for seven or eight minutes
3. Pour in about two skimming-spoonfuls of espagnole and one spoonful of bouillon; stir them in their sauce over the fire; at the first boil, remove them and serve them
4. If you do not have sauce, add one spoonful of flour, mix it well with your birds; add half a glass of white wine and as much bouillon; bring your sauce to a single boil; check that your ragout is properly seasoned
