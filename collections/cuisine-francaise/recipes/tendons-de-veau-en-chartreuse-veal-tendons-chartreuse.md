## Ingredients

- veal tendons
- carrots
- turnips
- small onions
- lettuce
- green beans
- green peas
- salt water
- consommé
- butter
- espagnole sauce
- meat glaze

## Directions

1. Prepare and cut your tendons into oyster pieces; cook them as those said à la poêle (pan-fried)
2. Turn thirty carrots and thirty turnips of the same thickness and length, with the rounds the size of a liard
3. Cook your carrots separately and your turnips in consommé
4. Cook forty small onions, all the same size and well peeled, taking care they are not too cooked so they remain whole
5. Cook twenty lettuce
6. When your roots are cooked, cut them the size of a liard and arrange them in a miroton pattern in the bottom of your buttered mold; make a border of carrots, one of turnips, one of small onions, one of green beans, and small peas which you will blanch in salt water to keep them green; arrange them like pearls in your mold
7. When the bottom is well decorated, put your carrots and turnips in sticks around it, ensuring it is regular so your chartreuse has a nice appearance at the bottom
8. Over your decoration, drain your lettuce, cut them in two, and put them in your mold so your decoration has a solid body to hold it
9. When you have well placed lettuce in the interior of your mold, drain your tendons and put them over your lettuce, placing them so there are no voids in your mold; ten to twelve tendons are sufficient, or fewer if your mold is small
10. Fill it with lettuce and the rest of your vegetables; your mold must be completely full so that when you turn it over on the plate, your roots are well supported from inside
11. Take the cooking liquid from your roots; clarify it; pass it through fine linen and reduce it
12. Add half a spoonful of reduced espagnole sauce and an amount of meat glaze the size of a walnut
13. At the moment of serving, pour your chartreuse onto your plate; if there is some liquid, aspirate it with a straw; pour the reduction over it as sauce
