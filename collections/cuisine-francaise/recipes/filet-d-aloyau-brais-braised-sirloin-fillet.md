## Ingredients

- sirloin fillet
- fat to remove
- large lardons
- thyme
- bay leaf
- quatre épices
- salt
- pepper
- lard strips
- veal slices
- beef slices
- four to six onions
- cloves
- parsley
- chives
- bouillon

## Directions

1. Remove the sirloin fillet and remove all the fat from it
2. Lay your fillet on the table on the side where the skin is; make sure the meat lies flat on the table with your hand
3. Take a large sharp knife; place the blade between the skin and the meat so well that by moving your knife back and forth, as if you were lifting a strip of lard, you separate the skin with the fillet and it becomes well unified and trimmed
4. If any skin remains, you must remove it by sliding the blade of your knife as close as possible to the skin to avoid hollows in your fillet
5. When it is well trimmed, take large lardons in which you put a little thyme, a little crushed or finely chopped bay leaf, a little quatre épices, and a little salt and pepper
6. Lard your fillet and tie it, giving it the shape you want
7. Put in the bottom of your pot strips of lard, slices of veal and beef, four or six onions, one of which is studded with three cloves, two bay leaves, a little thyme, a bouquet of parsley and chives
8. Place your fillet in your pot where your braising base is marked; cover it with lard and place a few pieces of meat around it
9. Pour in two large spoonfuls of bouillon and very little salt
10. Bring your braise to a boil, then place it over a gentle fire to simmer for two and a half hours
11. Take the cooking liquid in which your fillet has cooked; pass it through a silk sieve and reduce it
12. When your fillet is drained, untie it and glaze it
13. Dress it on your serving dish and pour the reduced cooking liquid over it
14. If you do not have everything needed to braise your fillet, trim it and lard it with large lard; put your trimmings in the bottom of your pot; tie your fillet; place it on your trimmings; add your carrots, onions, bouquet of aromatics, two spoonfuls of bouillon; if you have none, add water and salt, not too much so that you can use the cooking liquid for what you have to do; when it is cooked, serve it as above: make sure your fillet looks good and is well seasoned
