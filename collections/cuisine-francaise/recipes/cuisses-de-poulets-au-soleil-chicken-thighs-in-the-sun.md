## Ingredients

- Twelve chicken thighs from good chickens
- Butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- One bay leaf
- One onion studded with a clove
- Parsley and scallion bouquet
- Flour
- Hot bouillon
- Mushrooms
- Three egg yolks
- Bread crumbs
- Beaten and seasoned eggs
- Oil for frying
- Fried parsley

## Directions

1. Break the bone of each thigh up to the inner joint and cut off the large end at the leg.
2. Put a good piece of butter in a casserole and heat it gently.
3. Arrange the thighs in it so they take on a beautiful shape.
4. Add salt, coarse pepper, a bay leaf, an onion studded with a clove, and a bouquet of parsley and scallions.
5. Place them over high heat and sauté until well browned.
6. Add a spoonful of flour and a pot-spoon of hot bouillon along with mushrooms.
7. Boil for three-quarters of an hour.
8. Degrease the ragout before the time expires.
9. When removing the thighs, the sauce should be reduced to three-quarters of its volume.
10. Remove the bouquet, onion, and bay leaf.
11. Add a liaison of three egg yolks.
12. Arrange the thighs on a platter with sauce on top.
13. When they are cold, coat them well with their sauce.
14. Roll them in breadcrumbs, then dip in beaten and seasoned eggs.
15. Coat them again with breadcrumbs, making sure they are well covered everywhere.
16. At serving time, place them in hot oil.
17. When they have a beautiful color, drain them on white cloth.
18. Arrange them in a crown shape.
19. Fry a good handful of parsley and place it in the center.
