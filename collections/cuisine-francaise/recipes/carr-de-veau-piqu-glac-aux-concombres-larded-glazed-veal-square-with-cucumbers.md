## Ingredients

- Whole square of veal from the rib near the kidney to the second rib near the collar
- Fine lard for larding
- Lard strips
- Slices of veal
- Carrots
- Onions
- Laurel leaves
- Parsley
- Chives
- Cloves
- Consommé or bouillon
- Salt
- Paper
- Butter

## Directions

1. Take a whole square of veal from the rib near the kidney to the second rib near the collar.
2. Trim and cut it as squarely as possible.
3. Remove the bones that are under the fillet to the end of the rib of the chapelet.
4. Uncover the fillet, that is to say lift the skins that cover it.
5. Remove the nerves that are on it by introducing your knife between the nerve and the flesh, so that your fillet is well uncovered and no skin remains on it.
6. Then lard it with fine lard.
7. Fasten the skins covering the ribs with a trussing needle and string.
8. When your square is larded and trussed, place strips of lard in a small braising pan, slices of veal, two or three carrots, four onions, two laurel leaves, a bouquet of parsley and chives, two cloves.
9. Place your square over this seasoning so that your larded fillet can receive the heat of the fire on top of your braising pan.
10. Cover the skins covering the ribs with strips of lard.
11. Have a sheet of paper folded in double and buttered, which you place on your square.
12. Add two spoonfuls of consommé or bouillon.
13. Add a little salt.
14. Cook on low heat for three hours.
15. Be careful to put fire on top of the lid to glaze your fillet.
16. At the moment of serving, drain your square and untie it, that is to say remove the string.
