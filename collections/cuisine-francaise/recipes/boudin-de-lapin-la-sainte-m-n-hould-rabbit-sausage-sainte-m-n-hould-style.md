## Ingredients

- Flour
- Rabbit quenelle forcemeat
- Butter
- Sauce à atelet or other bound sauce
- Breadcrumbs
- Warm butter
- Game forcemeat or fumé de gibier
- Velouté or espagnole sauce

## Directions

1. Spread flour on a clean table
2. Place rabbit quenelle forcemeat (see forcemeat recipe) on the flour in a size large enough to represent a piece of sausage
3. Roll it in the flour and place it in the bottom of a buttered casserole
4. Make as many pieces as you judge appropriate, but three or four are sufficient
5. Poach them the same way as the preceding quenelles
6. When they are poached, let them cool
7. Pare them and give them a square, long and flat shape, fifteen lines thick
8. Coat them with a sauce à atelet if you have it, or another bound sauce
9. Dip them in breadcrumbs and then in warm butter
10. Bread them a second time and give them an attractive shape
11. Half an hour before serving, place them on the grill over a gentle blue fire
12. Place a hot camp oven over them to give them color
13. At the moment of serving, arrange them on the dish and sauce with a reduced velouté, game fumé, or espagnole
