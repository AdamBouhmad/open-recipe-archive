## Ingredients

- Plums (such as Perdigon, Reine Claude, Mirabelle, or other varieties)
- Five pounds of sugar per hundred plums

## Directions

1. Blanch the plums
2. When they are soft to the touch, remove them with a skimmer and place them in fresh water
3. Clarify five pounds of sugar for every hundred plums
4. Place the plums one by one in a clean vessel so they do not crush, and add the sugar slightly warmer than lukewarm
5. For four or five days, morning and evening, drain the plums on a sieve and boil the sugar, skimming it each time, then return the plums to the vessel with the sugar poured over, always slightly warmer than lukewarm
6. The Reine Claude plums should remain green, and other plums should keep their color
7. At the end, if the sugar is not syrupy enough at the final cooking, cook it further by adding two glasses of water to degrease it, then pour it boiling hot over the plums
