## Ingredients

- Fillets from four woodcocks
- A good piece of butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- A little powdered rosemary
- Half a glass of white wine
- Bay leaf
- Clove
- Six spoonfuls of espagnole sauce
- A glass of bouillon
- Juice of one lemon

## Directions

1. Remove the fillets from four woodcocks, trim them, and place them in your sauté pan
2. Warm a good piece of butter and pour it over them; add salt, coarse pepper, and a little powdered rosemary
3. When ready to serve, place your fillets on a hot fire; when they are stiffened on one side, turn them; leave them on the fire just a moment so they are not overcooked
4. Drain them and arrange them in crowns with a crouton between each
5. Sweat the trimmings of your woodcocks with half a glass of white wine, a bay leaf, and a clove, and let your sauce reduce to a glaze
6. When reduced, add half a glass of white wine, a glass of bouillon, and six spoonfuls of espagnole sauce; reduce everything by half
7. Strain your sauce through cloth and place it under your fillets
8. If you do not have espagnole sauce, add a glass of white wine and three glasses of bouillon to your trimmings; let it simmer and make a light roux that you will moisten with your reduction; add salt and coarse pepper, reduce your sauce, skim it, and strain it through cloth
9. When serving, add the juice of one lemon and check that it is well-seasoned
