## Ingredients

- two large onions
- butter
- one calf's liver
- three quarterons of beef fat
- dried bread crumb
- half a septier of milk
- parsley
- chives
- mushrooms
- salt
- coarse pepper
- four egg yolks
- four egg whites
- lard slices
- pig's caul
- fresh water
- egg
- paper
- sauce
- half a spoonful of verjuice

## Directions

1. Cut two large onions into small dice and put them in a saucepan with butter, cooking over the fire until done
2. Chop one calf's liver finely with three quarterons of beef fat
3. Put the chopped liver into a mortar with the cooked onion, dried bread crumb that has been dried over the fire with half a septier of milk, chopped parsley, chives, and mushrooms, salt, coarse pepper, four egg yolks, and four beaten egg whites
4. Pound everything together
5. Take a medium saucepan and place lard slices on the bottom
6. Place a pig's caul on top that you have soaked for half an hour in fresh water and well pressed with your hands to remove the water
7. Put all the forcemeat into the caul and seal all the edges with beaten egg to hold them together
8. Cover the saucepan with a sheet of paper and a lid
9. Cook over low heat between two beds of hot ashes for half an hour
10. Drain off the fat and gently pull the caul onto a white cloth so as not to break it, and wipe off the remaining fat
11. Arrange on the serving dish and put a good somewhat thin sauce on top where you have put half a spoonful of verjuice
