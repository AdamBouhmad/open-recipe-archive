## Ingredients

- Roe deer loin
- Vinegar
- Salt
- Fine pepper
- Bay leaves
- Cloves
- Thyme
- Onions
- Parsley
- Chives
- Lard strips
- Roe deer slices
- Carrots
- White wine
- Bouillon
- Roux
- Juice
- Vinegar

## Directions

1. Remove the two loins from your roe deer
2. Trim them as explained for beef loin
3. Lard them and place them in a terrine with two glasses of vinegar, salt, fine pepper, four bay leaves, six cloves, six or eight branches of thyme, four onions cut into slices, a small handful of parsley in branches, and a few whole chives
4. Leave to marinate forty-eight hours or longer if you have time
5. When you wish to serve them, remove them from your marinade and prepare them properly
6. Spread strips of lard in a casserole with a few slices of roe deer, three carrots, three onions, two cloves, two bay leaves, and a little thyme
7. Arrange your loins in spirals or otherwise
8. Place them on your seasoning, cover them with buttered paper
9. Pour over them half a bottle of white wine and as much bouillon with a little salt
10. Let them simmer over heat above and below for one hour
11. At the moment of serving, drain them, remove the string or skewers if there are any
12. Glaze them and dress them on your platter
13. Serve a piquant sauce underneath; if you have none, make a light roux that you will moisten with the cooking liquid from your loins, add a little juice so your sauce has good color, three spoonfuls of vinegar, salt, and fine pepper
14. Reduce your sauce by half so it takes on flavor
15. You can also roast these same loins on a spit if you do not wish to braise them
