## Ingredients

- Teals
- Lemon zest
- Butter
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Juice of a lemon
- Lemon slices
- Lard strips
- Buttered paper
- Three spoonfuls of worked Spanish sauce
- One spoonful of consommé
- Glaze the size of a walnut
- Zest of a quarter lemon

## Directions

1. Pluck and clean your teals
2. Singe them lightly
3. Clean them carefully so no down or quills remain
4. Truss them and fix them with a larding needle and string
5. Chop a little lemon zest and mix it with a piece of butter, salt, coarse pepper, and the juice of a lemon
6. Put this seasoned butter inside the body of your two teals
7. Give them a pleasing shape
8. Put them on the spit
9. Cover them with lemon slices
10. Put lard strips on them and wrap them in buttered paper, on which you sprinkle salt
11. Tie your paper well so it can contain your seasoning
12. When on the spit, three-quarters of an hour before serving, put them on the fire
13. At the moment of serving, unbridle them and drain them (that is, let the butter they have in their body come out), and remove the lemon slices on top
14. Arrange them on your plate
15. Pour in three spoonfuls of worked Spanish sauce, one spoonful of consommé, glaze the size of a walnut, the zest of a quarter lemon, a bit of coarse pepper
16. Make your sauce come to a boil and put it under your teals
