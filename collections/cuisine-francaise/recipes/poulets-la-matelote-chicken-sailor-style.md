## Ingredients

- A dozen small white onions
- Two medium carrots
- One parsnip
- Butter
- Flour
- One glass of white wine
- Bouillon
- Bunch of parsley
- Chives
- Half a clove of garlic
- Two cloves
- One bay leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- Salt
- Pepper
- One large chicken or two small ones
- Anchovy
- A pinch of capers

## Directions

1. Cut the head and tail from a dozen small white onions and blanch them half a quarter hour in boiling water
2. Drain in fresh water to remove the first skin
3. Cut two medium carrots and one parsnip the length of two fingers and cut them around like batons
4. Put in a saucepan a small piece of butter with two pinches of flour and brown to a cinnamon color while stirring over the fire
5. Moisten with one glass of white wine and as much bouillon
6. Add the carrots, small onions, a bunch of parsley, chives, half a clove of garlic, two cloves, one bay leaf, thyme, basil, salt, and pepper
7. Boil over low heat for half an hour
8. Flame, pluck, and empty one large chicken (or two small ones), brown it on the fire and cut it in four, put it in the ragout
9. Add, if you wish, the liver, neck, wings, and legs
10. Boil over low heat for one hour
11. When cooked, there should be little sauce remaining; degrease it and add a chopped anchovy and a pinch of capers
12. Serve hot
