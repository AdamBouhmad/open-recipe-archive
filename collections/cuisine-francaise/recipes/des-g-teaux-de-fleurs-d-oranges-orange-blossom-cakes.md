## Ingredients

- 1 pound of sugar
- 1 glass of water
- 1 quarterons of orange blossom petals
- Sugar mixed with egg white, consistency of double cream
- Fine sugar

## Directions

1. Make a paper mold with white paper kept double and folded around to make a border two fingers high, like a box, of medium cake size
2. Put 1 pound of sugar in a pan with a glass of water
3. Boil and skim, continuing until, when dipping the skimming spoon in the sugar and blowing through the holes, large sparks fly out that hold together
4. Immediately add a quarterons of orange blossom petals and boil until the sugar returns to the point it was at when you added the flowers
5. Remove from the fire and stir quickly with a spatula, rubbing all around the pan and in the middle, until you see the sugar beginning to rise
6. Quickly throw in a little fine sugar mixed with egg white no thinner than double cream that you have ready
7. Stir quickly and pour your cake into the mold
8. Hold the bottom of the pan on the cake while it is hot, because this prevents it from falling
9. Violet cakes are made the same way, except that for a quarterons of violets only three quarterons of sugar are needed
10. Roasted orange blossom cakes are made the same way, except you roast the orange blossoms with a little fine sugar before putting them in the other sugar
