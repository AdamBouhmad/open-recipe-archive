## Ingredients

- six calf's ears
- a glass of white wine
- twice as much broth
- salt
- pepper
- a bouquet of parsley and chives
- a clove of garlic
- two cloves
- a half bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- a little butter
- a handful of crumb of bread
- a half-setier of milk
- a bit of grated Gruyère cheese
- a little butter
- four raw egg yolks
- hot butter for breading
- a mixture of bread crumb and grated Gruyère cheese

## Directions

1. Scald six calf's ears well
2. Blanch them for a quarter hour in boiling water
3. Remove to cold water and clean off any remaining hairs
4. Put them to cook with a glass of white wine and twice as much broth, salt, pepper, a bouquet of parsley and chives, a clove of garlic, two cloves, a half bay leaf, thyme, basil, and a little butter
5. When cooked, drain them
6. Make a stuffing: heat a handful of bread crumb over the fire with a half-setier of milk and a bit of grated Gruyère cheese
7. Stir until the bread crumb is well thickened
8. Let cool, then add a little butter and four raw egg yolks
9. Mix together and stuff the inside of the ears
10. Dip the ears in warm butter to bread them, using a mixture of half bread crumb and half grated Gruyère cheese
11. Arrange in the serving dish
12. Brown them nicely under a pastry cover
13. Wipe the edges of the dish and serve without sauce
