## Ingredients

- Half a pound to three-quarters pound of meat (beef tenderloin, veal round, leg of mutton, game, or poultry)
- Beef fat (equal amount to the meat)
- Parsley
- Chives
- Mushrooms
- Salt
- Mixed spices
- Two whole eggs
- Half a septier of cream
- Pastry dough
- Good clear coulis

## Directions

1. Choose meat without small bones or tendons, removing any if found.
2. Mince the meat finely with chopping knives.
3. Mix with an equal amount of good beef fat, parsley, chives, and mushrooms, all chopped very fine.
4. Season with fine salt and a little mixed spices.
5. Mix everything well and add two whole eggs and moisten with half a septier of cream.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning to good flavor.
7. Line a pie dish with pastry dough.
8. Spread the forcemeat on top to a thickness of one inch.
9. Cover with pastry dough and finish as for other pies.
10. Cook for two hours; if using beef or mutton, cook longer.
11. When cooked and degreased, cut the forcemeat into small squares with a knife.
12. Top with good clear coulis and serve.
