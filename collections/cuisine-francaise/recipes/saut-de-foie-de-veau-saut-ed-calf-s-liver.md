## Ingredients

- One calf's liver
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- A little spice
- Fine herbs if desired
- Half a pound of butter
- One glass of Champagne wine
- Four tablespoons of prepared Spanish sauce
- Or one tablespoon of flour, two glasses of white wine, and a little broth

## Directions

1. Cut the liver in half lengthwise
2. Cut pieces across the width, two or three lines thick
3. Cut off the angles and shape the liver pieces like a matelote crouton, making them oval and pointed on one side
4. Season the prepared pieces with salt, coarse pepper, a little spice, and fine herbs if desired
5. Put half a pound of butter in your sauté pan or cooking vessel and warm it gently
6. Arrange your pieces in the sauté pan
7. At the moment of serving, place it on high heat
8. When the pieces are stiffened on one side, turn them to the other
9. Press your finger on the piece; when it is somewhat firm, remove the liver from the fire
10. Deposit your sauté into a saucepan
11. Remove the butter from your sauté pan and put in a full glass of Champagne wine and four tablespoons of prepared Spanish sauce
12. Reduce this sauce by half and strain it through a fine sieve over your sauté
13. Keep it warm without letting it boil
14. When serving, arrange your pieces in coronets and pour your sauce over them
15. If you do not have Spanish sauce, put one tablespoon of flour in your sauté pan where the butter from your sauté remains; when the flour is thinned out, add two glasses of white wine and a little broth; if the sauce is too thick, season with salt and pepper; when it is somewhat reduced, pour it over your liver
