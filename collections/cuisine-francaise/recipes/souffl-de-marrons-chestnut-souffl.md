## Ingredients

- chestnuts
- water
- butter
- powdered sugar
- six egg yolks
- six egg whites

## Directions

1. Blanch your chestnuts only in water.
2. Then cook them in water, adding whatever flavoring you wish.
3. When cooked, drain them on a white cloth.
4. Dry them well and put them in a mortar.
5. Pound them thoroughly and pass them through a quenelle sieve.
6. Put the chestnuts back in the mortar.
7. Add half as much butter as chestnuts.
8. Add powdered sugar.
9. After pounding everything together well, add six egg yolks, or more according to the quantity of your soufflé.
10. Pound them also with your chestnuts.
11. If your paste is sufficiently liquid, remove it from the mortar to put it in a saucepan.
12. A good half hour before serving, whip the whites of the six eggs.
13. When they are stiff like for a biscuit, mix them lightly with the mixture.
14. Pour it into a silver saucepan and cook the soufflé as described for other soufflés.
15. It must only be served at the moment of eating.
