## Ingredients

- poularde (hen)
- lard râpé (grated lard)
- beurre (butter)
- huile (oil)
- échalotes (shallots)
- champignons (mushrooms)
- persil (parsley)
- quatre épices (four spices)
- sel (salt)
- gros poivre (coarse pepper)
- papier huilé (oiled paper)

## Directions

1. Have a beautiful hen, flame and clean it from the breast; remove the breastbone; cut off the legs and tuck the thighs inside; cut off the wings
2. Place in a casserole half a pound of grated lard, as much butter, and four spoonfuls of oil; place your hen on a low fire for three-quarters of an hour; it need not take color
3. Remove it; then place in your casserole two spoonfuls of finely chopped shallots, which you will let brown briefly with six spoonfuls of finely chopped mushrooms, two spoonfuls of chopped parsley, a little of the four spices, salt, and coarse pepper
4. When everything has browned for ten minutes, pour it over your hen and let it cool
5. Oil six large sheets of paper; on the first one place your hen with a very thin lard strip and half of the herbs that you will arrange in its body, with the rest underneath and on top with a very thin lard strip
6. Wrap your piece as hermetically as possible with the remaining oiled sheets; ensure your campine has a beautiful shape; tie it with string over the paper
7. Place it in a hot oven for one hour, or on a grill over a low fire and in a hot country oven for three-quarters of an hour
8. At the moment of serving, untie it; remove the first and second sheets if they are too browned, and serve it in the paper
