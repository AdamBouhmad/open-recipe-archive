## Ingredients

- Fillets of five teals
- Salt
- Coarse pepper
- Pinch of ground aromatic herbs
- Butter
- White wine
- One spoonful of consommé
- Four spoonfuls of Spanish sauce
- Zest of bigarade
- Juice of bigarade
- Glaze the size of a walnut
- Flour
- Bouillon

## Directions

1. Take the fillets of five teals
2. Remove the nerves found on the side of the skin
3. Cut them into aiguillettes (cut the fillet into four or five lengthwise, all the same thickness)
4. Place them in a sauté pan
5. Season with salt, coarse pepper, a small pinch of ground aromatic herbs
6. Warm a piece of butter and pour it over
7. Sweat your scraps with a glass of white wine
8. When they have reduced to a glaze, put in a full spoonful of consommé
9. Simmer for one hour and pass your gravy through a silk sieve
10. Put into a saucepan a full four spoonfuls of Spanish sauce
11. Add your gravy and let it reduce by half
12. Pass it through a cloth
13. Add a bit of bigarade zest and the juice of the bigarade, and glaze the size of a walnut
14. Heat your sauce well without boiling it
15. At the moment of service, put your sauté pan on a hot stove
16. When your fillets are stiffened on all sides and you judge them cooked, tilt your sauté pan and put your fillets on top so you do not take butter with the spoon
17. Put them on the plate in the shape of a bush and sprinkle them with your sauce
