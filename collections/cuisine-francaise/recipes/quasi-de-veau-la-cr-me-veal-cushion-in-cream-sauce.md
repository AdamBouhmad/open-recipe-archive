## Ingredients

- one pint of milk
- a good piece of butter mixed with flour
- two cloves of garlic
- four shallots
- parsley
- chives
- endive
- one bay leaf
- thyme
- basil
- four cloves
- two onions in slices
- salt
- pepper
- coulis
- flour for the sauce

## Directions

1. Place in a vessel just the size of the quasi, a pint of milk with a good piece of butter mixed with flour, two cloves of garlic, four shallots, parsley, chives, endive, one bay leaf, thyme, basil, four cloves, two onions in slices, salt, and pepper.
2. Heat this marinade and simmer it on the fire until the butter is melted.
3. Remove from the fire and place the quasi in it, letting it take on flavor for twelve hours.
4. Then drain it and dry it well.
5. Cover it with well-oiled paper and roast it on the spit.
6. Serve it with a piquant sauce made as follows: pass on the fire two onions in slices with a piece of butter; when well colored, add a pinch of flour, moisten with coulis, salt, and pepper.
7. Boil for a quarter hour, degrease the sauce and pass it through a sieve.
8. Serve the sauce under the quasi.
