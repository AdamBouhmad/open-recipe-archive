## Ingredients

- Two large cucumbers
- Two tablespoons of vinegar
- Salt
- A piece of butter
- A bouquet of parsley
- Chives
- One clove of garlic
- Two shallots
- One bay leaf
- Thyme
- Basil
- Two pinches of flour
- Two glasses of good broth
- Coarse pepper
- Young rabbit fillets

## Directions

1. Cut the cucumbers into thin slices as fine as possible
2. Place them in a pot with two tablespoons of vinegar and salt, and marinate for two hours, turning them from time to time
3. When they have released their water, press them firmly so that no water remains
4. Put them in a pot with a piece of butter, a bouquet of parsley, chives, a clove of garlic, two shallots, a bay leaf, thyme, and basil
5. Pass them over the fire, turning them often until they are slightly colored
6. Add two pinches of flour
7. Moisten with two glasses of good broth
8. Let cook over low heat for a good half hour until the ragout is slightly thickened
9. Remove the bouquet and add young rabbit fillets that have been sliced and cut like the cucumbers
10. Heat without boiling
11. Season with salt and coarse pepper
12. Serve. The fillets should be the leftovers from roasted rabbits that have been removed from the table.
