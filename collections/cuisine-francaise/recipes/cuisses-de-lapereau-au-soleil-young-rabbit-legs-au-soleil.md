## Ingredients

- Young rabbit legs
- Medium lardons, seasoned with salt, pepper and pounded aromatic herbs
- Butter
- 1 spoon of flour
- 1 pot spoon of bouillon
- 2 spoonfuls of velouté to degrease
- 1 bay leaf
- Mushrooms
- 1/2 bottle of good white wine
- Parsley and chives
- 5 egg yolks
- Fine butter
- Breadcrumbs
- 5 whole eggs
- Fine salt
- Coarse pepper

## Directions

1. After boning your legs, lard them closely with medium lardons seasoned with salt, pepper and pounded aromatic herbs.
2. Put butter in a casserole; heat it a little, and put your legs in it.
3. Place them on a hot stove; sauté them for ten minutes.
4. Add about one spoon of flour and one pot spoon of bouillon, two spoonfuls of velouté to degrease, a bay leaf, some mushrooms, half a bottle of good white wine, and a bouquet of parsley and chives.
5. Cook over high heat to make the liquid reduce.
6. Be careful to degrease your ragout.
7. When it is cooked and reduced, add a liaison of five egg yolks and a small piece of fine butter.
8. Place your twelve legs on a platter to cool; drizzle them with their sauce.
9. When they are cold, remove the bone of the foreleg, soak them well in their sauce, and put them in breadcrumbs one after the other.
10. Place them on a platter.
11. Break five whole eggs; add a little fine salt and a little coarse pepper.
12. Beat your eggs as you would for an omelet.
13. Dip your legs in it; make sure they are coated all over.
14. Roll them in breadcrumbs; give them a pleasing shape.
15. Put them on a platter; at the moment of serving, you fry them.
