## Ingredients

- 1/2 kg flour
- 30 g yeast
- 1/2 liter warm milk
- 35 g butter or margarine
- 35 g sugar
- 75 g currants, raisins and candied citrus peel
- 15 g snippets
- some lemon juice and grated lemon zest
- 1 sour apple
- 1 egg
- 10 g salt
- frying fat

## Directions

1. Add to the flour enough warm milk so it no longer clouds.
2. Stir out the lumps from this batter.
3. Add the remaining milk, salt, well-washed currants and raisins stripped of their stems, the shredded candied citrus peel, sour apples, the softened butter or margarine, snippets, and optionally some lemon juice and grated lemon zest.
4. Mix the yeast with some warm milk or sugar, add it to the batter and let this rise covered on a lukewarm place for 3 to 4 hours.
5. In the meantime, heat the frying fat in an iron pot.
6. Add the batter in small portions with the help of two spoons and fry the balls light brown and cooked through (approximately 5 to 7 minutes).
7. Test if they are cooked by inserting a knitting needle, which should come out dry.
8. Let the balls drain thoroughly on gray paper, then dust with powdered sugar.
