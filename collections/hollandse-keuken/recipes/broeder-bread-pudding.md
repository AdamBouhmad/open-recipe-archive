## Ingredients

- 400 g flour
- approximately 4½ dl lukewarm milk
- 30 g yeast
- 200 g currants, raisins and mixed peel
- 2 eggs (optional)
- salt
- 20 g lard or beef fat

## Directions

1. Mix the flour with enough lukewarm milk so it does not fly about
2. Stir out the lumps from this batter and add the remaining milk, salt, well-washed currants and raisins with stems removed, the chopped mixed peel and the yeast mixed with lukewarm milk (optionally also the beaten eggs)
3. Let the batter rise, covered, in a warm place for three quarters of an hour to 1 hour
4. Let the lard or beef fat melt in an iron pot and grease the sides with it
5. Put the batter in and let the broeder bake in a moderately warm oven until light brown and cooked through (approximately 1 hour)
6. Lay a lid on it if necessary
7. Check if it is cooked by pricking with a knitting needle, which should come out dry
8. Serve this dish with butter and sugar or syrup sauce
9. Alternatively, let the batter rise in the greased iron pan (3/4 hour) and then bake it
