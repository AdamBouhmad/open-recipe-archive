## Ingredients

- 100 g rice
- salt
- 300 g veal for braising
- optionally 100 g ground veal
- 4 dl boiling water
- salt
- 40 g flour
- 40 g butter or margarine
- optionally 1 egg
- mace
- thyme
- piece of cayenne pepper

## Directions

1. Wash the veal and let it cook gently in the boiling water with salt (approximately 1 hour) or braise it until done.
2. Let the herbs cook along tied in a cloth.
3. If using ground veal, mix it with some soaked old bread, pepper, salt and nutmeg, shape into small balls and cook these separately in a little boiling water so the stock does not become too fatty.
4. Take the meat and herbs out of the stock and gradually add this, stirring vigorously, to the butter and flour that have been brought to a boil together and from which all lumps have been carefully rubbed out.
5. Let the sauce cook through, stirring, for 10 minutes and then warm the veal (and the meatballs) in it.
6. Cook the rice in the usual manner and press it as firmly as possible into a ring mold buttered or rinsed with water.
7. Turn the rice out as quickly as possible onto a heated plate and place the braised veal in the middle.
8. Note: If desired, curry can be cooked with the rice. In this case only mace is cooked with the veal. One can also add a little lemon juice to the sauce and finish it with an egg or egg yolk.
