## Ingredients

- 300 g flour
- 150 g currants, raisins and candied citrus peel
- 1 egg
- 20 g yeast
- 2 dl lukewarm milk
- salt to taste

## Directions

1. Prepare the batter as for ketelkoek (see previous recipe).
2. Make sure that in the meantime a linen sack is rinsed with lukewarm water and sprinkled with flour on the inside.
3. Put the dough in it, tie the sack closed, but make sure there is sufficient room for the dough to rise.
4. Cook the pudding about 2 hours in a pan of boiling water, but place a small plate at the bottom to prevent sticking.
5. Make sure the water never stops boiling.
6. Test if the pudding is done by inserting a knitting needle, which must come out dry.
7. Let it dry without the sack and serve with a syrup sauce, or butter or margarine and syrup, or butter or margarine and brown sugar.
