## Ingredients

- 1 hare
- 250 g butter or margarine
- 150 g larding bacon
- salt

## Directions

1. Clean the hare or (preferably) wipe it with a damp cloth.
2. Cut the larding bacon into very thin strips and lard the back with one row of bacon strips, the hind quarters with two rows.
3. Rub the hare inside and outside with some salt.
4. Heat the butter or margarine thoroughly (but not brown) in a hare pan or roasting pan, place the hare in it, baste it on all sides and put it in the oven.
5. Place the lid over the hare pan, over the roasting pan place a piece of gray paper smeared with butter or margarine and baste the meat frequently.
6. Add some water to the butter when it threatens to brown.
7. After 2 to 2½ hours, depending on the weight of the hare, test whether the hare is done by seeing if the meat from the hind quarter can be easily removed with a spoon.
8. Remove it from the pan, make the butter so hot that a crust forms on the bottom of the pan, let it cool on low heat and gradually add hare blood mixed with some red wine, cream or water while stirring.
9. Let the gravy simmer briefly while stirring.
10. Remove the strings and pegs from the hare before serving and serve with apple sauce or red cabbage.
11. Note: You can also first cut the hare into pieces, place them for 24 hours in water and vinegar with a couple of bay leaves, some cloves and some white peppercorns, then brown and roast them. The gravy can optionally be finished with some of the liquid in which the hare was soaked.
