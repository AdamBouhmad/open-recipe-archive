## Ingredients

- 2 sweetbreads
- 500 g veal
- 500 g fresh fatted pork
- 2 egg whites
- 100 g smoked beef tongue
- 100 g ham
- truffles and mushrooms
- 60 g butter or margarine
- 3/4 l soubise sauce

## Directions

1. Cook the sweetbreads, which have been standing in cold water for half an hour, for 10 minutes in boiling water
2. Rinse them with cold water, remove the membranes and fat and press them between two plates with a weight for 15 minutes
3. Lard the sweetbreads with strips of truffle and braise them gently in melted butter or margarine until done (approximately 1/2 hour)
4. Grind the washed veal with the pork (washed in lukewarm water) twice through a meat grinder, mix it with pepper, salt, nutmeg and one egg white
5. Divide this mixture into 3 equal parts
6. Mix one part with some finely chopped ham, the second with some finely chopped tongue and leave the third part unmixed
7. Form these mixtures with 2 spoons into flat, pear-shaped little figures, brush them with egg white and garnish them in a diamond pattern with thin strips of tongue and ham
8. Cook 3 to 4 of these quenelles at a time for 10 minutes in boiling salted water, keep them warm over the steam of boiling water while the other quenelles are cooked
9. Slice the sweetbreads diagonally, arrange them on a platter, pour the soubise sauce (prepared in the usual way according to recipe 353) over them and arrange the chopped meat balls around them
10. Serve the remaining soubise sauce in a sauce bowl
