## Ingredients

- 12 oranges or blood oranges
- 3 lemons
- 1.75 liters water
- sugar in the amount that the fruit weighs after cooking
- jam jars
- parchment paper
- twine
- rubber bands
- cellophane
- labels

## Directions

1. Brush the fruit clean, wash them and put them on with the cold water.
2. Let them cook until they are so soft that one can easily prick them with a silver fork or wooden spoon handle.
3. Take the fruit out of the liquid, weigh it and add its weight in sugar to the remaining liquid.
4. Cut the fruit in quarters, carefully remove from the flesh the pits and fibers and put the flesh in the sugar solution.
5. Cut off the white part of the peel and cut the peels into strips as thin and as long as possible.
6. Put these too in the sugar solution and now let the marmalade cook down, stirring occasionally, until it reaches the required thickness (see the rules on page 369).
7. Treat it further as strawberry jam (recipe 983).
8. Note: In this marmalade the same weight of sugar as fruit is used, because otherwise the marmalade would be too bitter.
