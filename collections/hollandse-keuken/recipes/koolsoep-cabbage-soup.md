## Ingredients

- 1 savoy cabbage
- 2 hectograms lean bacon
- salt and pepper
- parsley, thyme, and bay leaf
- 1 hectogram carrots
- 1 hectogram onions
- 2 cloves
- 4 liters water
- 2.5 decagrams bread
- Liebig's Meat Extract

## Directions

1. Remove the outer leaves of the savoy cabbage as they are usually tough, cut the cabbage into 4 pieces, and wash them in plenty of water.
2. Boil them for 10 minutes with 2 hectograms of lean bacon in boiling water.
3. Then place them for one hour in plenty of cold water. Through this treatment you will have made the cabbage easily digestible.
4. Take the cabbage and carefully press all the water out, then season each piece with some salt and pepper.
5. Make a bundle of parsley, thyme and bay leaf, 1 hectogram carrots and 1 hectogram onions and stick 2 cloves in one of the onions.
6. Place the four pieces of cabbage in a pot or cooking pan that is large enough to contain four liters of water with the bundle and the vegetables.
7. Place the 2 hectograms of lean bacon that have been cooked with the cabbage on top of the cabbage. Pour 4 liters of water over it.
8. Let this come to a boil together, skim it carefully and let it simmer gently for 3 hours.
9. Then remove the vegetables and cabbage from the liquid. Cut the four pieces of cabbage into small pieces so that they distribute well in the soup.
10. Place them in the soup tureen, in which you have placed 2.5 decagrams of bread (cut into pieces). Pour the cooking liquid over this and add Liebig's Meat Extract to taste.
