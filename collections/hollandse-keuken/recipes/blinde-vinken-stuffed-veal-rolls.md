## Ingredients

- 4 oblong-shaped veal cutlets of approximately 75 g
- 75 g veal mince or half veal, half pork mince
- 1 egg
- Old bread
- Pepper
- Salt
- Nutmeg
- ½ lemon
- Approximately 80 g butter or margarine

## Directions

1. Wash or scrape the cutlets and rub them with salt.
2. Make the mince with the egg yolk, some soaked old bread, pepper, salt and nutmeg.
3. Divide it into four portions and spread it over the cutlets, making sure it does not stick out at the edges.
4. Roll up the cutlets, tie them closed with a very thin string, roll them in egg white that has been beaten with one tablespoon of water and then roll them in fine breadcrumbs.
5. Make the butter or margarine light brown in the frying pan, fry the blinde vinken carefully until brown in it and braise them in the finished jus, with lemon slices without pips, over a gentle fire for another 45 minutes to 1 hour, but ensure the crust does not come loose.
6. Remove the strings before serving.
