## Ingredients

- melons that are not fully ripe
- vinegar
- white sugar
- whole cloves
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Take melons that are not fully ripe, cutting off the small round parts but especially not the skin; cut them further into slices the thickness of a finger, remove the seed, and lay the slices in a pot
2. Pour over as much vinegar as is needed for them to be well covered and let stand covered for eight days
3. Remove the melons, arrange them in a new earthenware pan and add the same vinegar; let them cook until they can be pierced with a straw
4. Layer them in an earthenware pot, placing a layer of white sugar on the bottom and repeating this between each layer of melon, adding some whole cloves and some cinnamon
5. The vinegar in which the melon cooked must be heated again and poured over boiling; seal the pot at once with a bladder
