## Ingredients

- 300 g old bread without crust
- 0.5 l milk
- 4 egg yolks
- 2 egg whites
- 75 g butter or margarine
- 100 g sugar
- jam
- grated lemon zest
- 2 egg whites (for garnish)
- 100 g sugar (for garnish)
- angelica
- candied fruits

## Directions

1. Make the bread, freed of crusts, fine in the boiling milk, so there are no lumps
2. Add the butter or margarine
3. Stir the egg yolks with the sugar and grated lemon zest
4. Add the bread with the butter or margarine to this
5. Fold in the two stiffly beaten egg whites as lightly as possible at the end
6. Grease a warm pudding mold (also the lid) with some butter or margarine, dust it with powdered sugar and add the mixture, but ensure the mold is filled only 3/4 full
7. Close the mold firmly and cook it 1.5 hours au bain-marie, but ensure the water is always refilled to just below the rim of the mold
8. Let the pudding dry out a little without the lid
9. Turn it out onto a fireproof dish and quickly coat it with the jam and then with the two stiffly beaten egg whites with sugar, sprinkle some sugar over it and quickly place it in a hot oven to brown it lightly
10. Garnish the pudding afterwards with strips of angelica and the candied fruits, which should already be ready
11. Bring the pudding to the table as hot as possible and serve with warm vanilla sauce or wine sauce
