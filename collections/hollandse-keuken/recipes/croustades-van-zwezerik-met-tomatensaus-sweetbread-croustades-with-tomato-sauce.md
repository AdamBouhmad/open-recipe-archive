## Ingredients

- For the croustades: approximately 200 g rice
- 1 egg
- 50 g butter or margarine
- pepper and salt
- egg white for coating
- breadcrumbs
- frying fat
- For the filling: 2 sweetbreads
- 20 g flour
- 20 g butter or margarine
- 1 dl bouillon
- 1 dl cream
- 1 to 2 egg yolks
- pepper and salt
- For the sauce: 3 to 4 large tomatoes
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 onion
- 1/2 large carrot
- 4 cloves
- 15 g flour
- approximately 25 g butter or margarine
- 2 dl bouillon
- some lean jus
- 1/2 dl cream

## Directions

1. Cook the rice in the usual way until done and while warm mix it with egg, butter or margarine, pepper and salt. Let this mixture spread on a plate cool down.
2. Form large pyramid-shaped croquettes from it, the underside of which is so broad that they can easily stand. Coat them twice and fry them in frying fat until brown. Let them drain well on grey paper, cut off the top cap, hollow them out and fill them with the sweetbread ragout.
3. Cook the sweetbreads in the usual way (see recipe 122), cut them into small pieces. Make a small sauce from butter or margarine, flour and bouillon, let it cook until done and add the cream and 1 to 2 egg yolks. Stir the sweetbread pieces through last. Place the caps on the sweetbread croquettes and serve them warm with the sauce, which is prepared as follows: crush the tomatoes, chop the onion and carrot and let this together with cloves and bay leaves cook gently in butter or margarine (approximately 15 minutes), but make sure it does not brown.
4. Add the flour and the bouillon, jus and cream and if the colour is not nice, some tomato purée. Let the sauce cook until done, press it through a sieve.
