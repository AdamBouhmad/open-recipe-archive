## Ingredients

- 12 thin slices of old bread without crust (approximately 200 g)
- 4 1/2 deciliters milk
- 1 to 2 eggs
- 50 g currants
- 50 g raisins
- 50 g candied citron
- 60 g sugar
- 175 g butter (or 50 g butter) or margarine
- 2 teaspoons cinnamon

## Directions

1. FIRST METHOD. Beat the eggs with the sugar, add the lukewarm milk to it. Wash the currants and raisins, which have been removed from the stems, chop the candied citron.
2. Spread the thin bread slices on both sides with some butter or margarine and layer them alternately with the mixture in the fireproof dish, but ensure that the last layer consists of bread.
3. Moisten each layer of bread with a portion of the milk and egg sauce.
4. Sprinkle some sugar mixed with cinnamon over the last layer of buttered bread.
5. Let a crispy crust form on the dish in the oven.
6. SECOND METHOD. Soak the bread in boiling milk and rub it completely smooth.
7. Add the egg yolks, the sugar, the well-washed currants and raisins and the chopped candied citron to it and lastly stir in the stiffly beaten egg white as lightly as possible.
8. Put the mixture in a fireproof dish, sprinkle some fine cinnamon mixed with sugar over it, place small lumps of butter or margarine here and there on it and let the dish brown and rise in a moderately warm oven.
