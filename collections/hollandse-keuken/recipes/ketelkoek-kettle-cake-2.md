## Ingredients

- 300 g flour
- 150 g currants, raisins and candied citrus peel
- 1 egg
- 20 g yeast
- 3 dl lukewarm milk
- salt to taste

## Directions

1. Put the flour in a bowl, add the egg and lukewarm milk and stir out the lumps from this batter.
2. Add the well-washed currants and raisins, which have been stripped of their stems, the chopped candied citrus peel, the salt, and lastly the yeast mixed with a little lukewarm milk.
3. Let the batter rise, covered, in a lukewarm place (1/2 hour to 3/4 hour).
4. Grease the inside of a hot pudding mold with butter (also the lid) and sprinkle it with fine breadcrumbs.
5. Put the batter in it, but make sure the mold is only filled 2/3 to 3/4.
6. Close the mold firmly and cook the pudding about 2 hours in a water bath, but make sure the water is constantly filled to just below the rim of the pudding mold.
7. Check if the pudding is done by inserting a knitting needle, which must come out dry.
8. Let it dry without the lid and serve the pudding with butter or margarine and syrup or sugar, or if preferred with a syrup sauce.
