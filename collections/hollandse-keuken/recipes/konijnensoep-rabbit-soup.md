## Ingredients

- 1 or 2 rabbits
- butter
- parsley
- onions sliced into rings
- salt and pepper
- 1.5 hectograms flour
- 1 glass red wine
- 3 liters water
- Liebig's Meat Extract
- rabbit blood
- Cayenne pepper

## Directions

1. Take one or two rabbits from which you have saved the blood for thickening. After they are well cleaned, cut them into pieces and let them brown slightly in butter, parsley and sliced onions. Add some salt and pepper.
2. When the rabbit pieces are well colored, make a roux of 1.5 hectograms flour, 1 glass of red wine and 3 liters of water.
3. Let the rabbits become done, and when done, pour them through a strainer, clean each piece well, taking care that no small bone fragments remain.
4. Put all the rabbit pieces in a pot. Let the cooking liquid simmer gently on the side of the stove so you can skim it well. Keep the rabbit pieces warm.
5. After the cooking liquid has simmered gently for half an hour, add Liebig's Meat Extract to it and thicken it with the blood, after you have skimmed off the foam. Place the rabbit pieces (without heads) in the soup tureen, pour the cooking liquid over with a little Cayenne pepper.
