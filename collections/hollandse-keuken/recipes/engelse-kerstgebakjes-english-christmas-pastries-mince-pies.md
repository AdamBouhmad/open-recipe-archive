## Ingredients

- 250 g flour
- 125 g butter or margarine
- water
- 100 g currants
- 100 g raisins
- 75 g citron peel
- 25 g candied peel snippets
- 2 sour apples
- 100 g calf's suet
- 80 g sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/2 teaspoon crushed cloves
- juice of 2 lemons
- grated zest of 1 lemon

## Directions

1. Sift the flour into a bowl, add the butter or margarine and cut it into the flour until it is as fine as breadcrumbs.
2. Add water quickly and lightly while stirring until the dough hangs together as a firm ball.
3. Roll it out on a floured board into a thin sheet and let the dough, which has become slack from handling, rest under a damp cloth for half an hour.
4. Remove the suet from its membrane, wash it, as well as the currants, raisins and peeled and cut into quarters sour apples.
5. Chop this all together with the citron peel until it becomes a fine mixture.
6. Stir in the sugar, ginger powder, lemon zest, lemon juice and crushed cloves.
7. Butter approximately 30 small tartlet molds, line them with a piece of dough and fill them with a little of the mixture.
8. Lay a layer of dough on top, press the edges firmly together and place the molds on a baking sheet in a moderately warm oven.
9. Let the pastries become light brown and done (approximately 20 minutes), turn them out and keep them in a tin for several days before serving.
10. Serve them sprinkled with powdered sugar and decorate the platter with a sprig of holly.
