## Ingredients

- 2 sweetbreads
- approximately 5 dl bouillon infused with mace, thyme and cayenne pepper
- 40 g flour
- 40 g butter or margarine
- 1 egg
- breadcrumbs for coating
- salad oil for frying
- 1 egg yolk
- 1/2 dl cream
- 1 kg asparagus
- nutmeg

## Directions

1. After soaking the sweetbreads for half an hour in cold water, cook them in the usual way in plenty of boiling salted water with herbs (25 to 30 minutes)
2. Rinse them with cold water, remove the membrane and slice them diagonally about 1 cm thick
3. Make a thick, light sauce in the usual way from butter or margarine, flour and strained sweetbread bouillon, mix it away from the heat with the egg yolk beaten with the cream
4. Dip the sweetbread slices in this sauce and let them cool
5. Double-bread them and fry them in fairly hot frying fat until brown and crispy
6. Let them drain briefly on porous paper and insert a piece of macaroni in the point, around which a paper collar is tied
7. Arrange the cutlets in a ring on a heated platter
8. Place the cooked asparagus tips in the center, pour a little melted butter or margarine over them and grate some nutmeg over them
9. Note: stewed asparagus (see recipe 463) can also be placed in the center of the cutlets
