## Ingredients

- 600 g flour
- 6 eggs
- 125 g butter or margarine
- 100 g sugar
- 50 g yeast
- approximately 1 dl lukewarm milk
- some salt
- some jam or marmalade

## Directions

1. Make a firm batter that can be rolled out from the flour, eggs, melted butter or margarine, sugar, mixed yeast, milk and salt.
2. Let this rise, covered, in a lukewarm place (approximately 1 hour).
3. Roll it out on a flour-dusted board with a flour-dusted rolling pin to a thickness of approximately 1 cm.
4. Cut out an even number of not-too-large circles with rings or glasses.
5. Brush the outside of the circles with some egg white, indent half of the circles slightly in the middle and place some marmalade or jam in this hollow.
6. Stick two circles firmly together at the edges.
7. Let the balls rise, covered with a somewhat damp cloth, for another 20 minutes to ½ hour.
8. Heat lard, frying fat or salad oil in a deep iron pot until blue steam rises, then place it on a gentle heat, add 4 to 5 balls at a time and fry them, turning occasionally, light brown and cooked (approximately 10 minutes).
9. Let the balls drain well on porous paper and sprinkle them with powdered sugar or roll them through sugar and cinnamon after cooling.
