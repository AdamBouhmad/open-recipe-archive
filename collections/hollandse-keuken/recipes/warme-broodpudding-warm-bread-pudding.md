## Ingredients

- 200 g old bread without crust
- 3.5 dl milk
- 2 to 3 eggs
- 50 g butter or margarine
- 35 g currants
- 35 g raisins
- 25 g candied citrus peel
- 50 g sugar
- 50 g almonds (optional)

## Directions

1. Make the bread, carefully freed of crusts, fine in the boiling milk, ensuring there are no lumps at all
2. Add the butter or margarine
3. Stir the egg yolks with the sugar
4. Add to this the bread with the butter or margarine, the well-washed currants and raisins, freed from their stems, the shredded candied citrus peel and if desired the peeled and chopped almonds
5. Fold in the stiffly beaten egg white as lightly as possible
6. Grease a warm pudding mold (also the lid) with some butter or margarine, sprinkle some fine breadcrumbs in it and add the mixture, but ensure the mold is filled only 3/4 full
7. Close the mold firmly and cook the pudding 1.5 hours au bain-marie; ensure the water is always refilled to just below the rim of the mold
8. Let the pudding dry out a little without the lid and serve with foam sauce or warm vanilla sauce
