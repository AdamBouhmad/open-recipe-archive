## Ingredients

- 1¼ kg thin pork cutlets of about 75 g per piece
- 900 g thin smoked beef
- thyme
- Spanish pepper
- bay leaves
- cloves
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Wash the cutlets and rub them in with some pepper and salt.
2. Cut the smoked beef, which has been soaked in lukewarm water for several hours, into approximately the same shape as the cutlets, place the meat in it, roll it up together and tie the resulting rolls closed with a string.
3. Cook the rolls for 3 hours in 2/3 water and 1/3 vinegar with a piece of cloth in which the spices are tied.
4. Scoop them with the liquid into well-cleaned Cologne pots. Make sure they are under the liquid and close the pots with damp parchment paper. Serve the smoked beef rolls with the jelly cold with the sandwich.
