## Ingredients

- 100 g butter or hard margarine or calf suet
- 100 g flour
- some salt
- approximately 0.5 dl water

## Directions

1. Sift the flour into a bowl, add salt, place butter, margarine or fat in it and cut these with 2 knives into fairly coarse pieces.
2. Add, stirring as gently as possible, drippings of cold water until everything sticks together, but especially do not use too much water, as otherwise the dough would stick when rolling out.
3. Roll out the ball, turning it over each time, on a flour-dusted board or table with a flour-sprinkled rolling pin to a thin layer.
4. Carefully brush off excess flour that clings to it and fold the dough lengthwise into thirds. (Larger quantities can be folded into thirds lengthwise and widthwise.)
5. Let the dough, which has become soft from handling, rest in a cool place until it is stiff again (approximately 15 minutes) and repeat rolling out three times and folding three times.
6. Then fold it into quarters and let it rest as long as possible.
7. Roll it out to the desired thickness and give it the desired shape.
