## Ingredients

- 1 cod tail
- 100 g butter or margarine
- 1 lemon
- salt
- breadcrumbs
- 1 Maggi's Bouillon cube
- water
- potatoes (optional)

## Directions

1. METHOD 1: Remove the backbone from the tail by loosening it along the entire length and pulling it out, or by cutting the fish open along one side and removing the backbone. Place the scaled, washed and salted fish in a fireproof dish with some salted water in which lemon juice has been added and in which, if desired, 1 Maggi's Bouillon cube has been dissolved. Distribute the butter or margarine in pieces on top and let the fish braise in a moderately warm oven, covered with a lid, until almost cooked (approximately 30 minutes). Baste it from time to time with the liquid. When the fish is almost cooked, sprinkle some breadcrumbs over it, lay a few thin slices of lemon on top and let it brown and cook without a lid.
2. METHOD 2: First cook the tail whole until almost cooked and then braise it in a fireproof dish. The braising can be completed more quickly in this case than in the first method, but it is not advisable to place boiled potatoes with the fish, as these might not cook through.
