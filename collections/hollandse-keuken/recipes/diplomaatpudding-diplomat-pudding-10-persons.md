## Ingredients

- 1 l milk
- 150 g sugar
- ½ vanilla pod
- 5 egg yolks
- 100 g currants
- 100 g raisins
- 100 g candied citron
- 100 g biscuits or fruit in syrup
- 40 g gelatin or 35 g powdered gelatin
- 2½ dl whipped cream

## Directions

1. Stir the egg yolks smooth with the sugar.
2. Remove the stems from the currants and raisins, rinse them with lukewarm water, cover with boiling water and cook for 10 minutes. Let them drain well in a sieve.
3. Chop the candied citron finely.
4. Bring the milk slowly to a boil with the vanilla, soak the biscuits in it briefly once it has cooled. Pour it while stirring to the yolks, let the mass bind slightly while stirring well, but do not boil.
5. Soak the leaf gelatin for 10-15 minutes in cold water, then dissolve it in the liquid taken from the heat and let it cool somewhat.
6. Add the mixed dried fruit to it.
7. Add the mass to the stiffly beaten cream, let it become jelly-like stirring occasionally and layer it alternately with the biscuits in a mold rinsed with unbeaten egg white, but ensure that the first and last layer do not consist of biscuits.
8. Turn out the pudding after cooling onto a damp plate and serve without sauce.
9. Note: Instead of with mixed dried fruit and biscuits, the pudding can also be prepared with fruit in syrup. The biscuits can be soaked in rum or maraschino instead of in the milk.
