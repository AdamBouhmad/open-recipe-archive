## Ingredients

- quinces
- equal weight sugar as quinces
- cochineal for coloring
- water
- cinnamon (optional)

## Directions

1. Weigh the fruits and take the same weight of sugar
2. Peel the fruits and remove the seed cores, set them just under water and with half the sugar on the fire, with the addition of a little cochineal for coloring
3. When the quinces are done, remove them from the pan and place them with the other half of the sugar in a glazed pot, adding sufficient water
4. Put the pits and seed cores in a muslin cloth and let this cook in the liquid from which you removed the quinces; if the water has cooked down too much, add more, and let it cook until all the sugar has properly melted
5. Pour this over the quinces and seal the pot well with a bladder
6. For every 25 quinces use cochineal worth ten cents and if desired also some cinnamon
