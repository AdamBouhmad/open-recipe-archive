## Ingredients

- 500 g ground veal
- 1 egg
- 50 g old bread without crust
- pepper, salt and nutmeg to taste
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped parsley
- 3 hard-boiled eggs
- 100 g butter or margarine
- egg white
- breadcrumbs
- scalloped lemon slices
- parsley

## Directions

1. Beat the egg with salt, pepper and nutmeg.
2. Soak the bread in some water or milk, squeeze it firmly between two spoons and stir it through the egg until it is evenly mixed with it.
3. Add the parsley and the ground meat and stir everything with two spoons until it becomes an even mass and no bread pieces are visible.
4. Press the ground meat into a 1 1/2 cm thick layer on a floured dish.
5. Lay the hard-boiled eggs in the middle, with the ends cut off on both sides to fit them well together, in a row one after the other, after the eggs without ends have been passed through egg white loosened with water.
6. Fold the ground meat around them, but make sure the eggs do not protrude at the sides.
7. Roll the ball through the egg white loosened with water, then through breadcrumbs and fry it in the butter or margarine, which has been made light brown in the roasting pan, brown and done (45 to 50 minutes).
8. Remove the ground meat from the roasting pan, slice it thinly with a sharp knife when it has cooled completely and arrange these slices overlapping on the dish.
9. Garnish with scalloped lemon slices, fresh sprigs of parsley and, if desired, some chopped aspic in different colors.
