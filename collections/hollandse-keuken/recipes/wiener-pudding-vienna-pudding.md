## Ingredients

- 125 g old bread without crust
- 150 g sugar
- 3 1/2 dl milk
- 3 large eggs
- 100 g raisins
- 50 g candied citrus peel
- juice and zest of one lemon

## Directions

1. Cut the bread into small cubes and mix it with the chopped candied citrus peel, the raisins washed with lukewarm water, the grated lemon zest and the lemon juice.
2. Beat the eggs with 100 g sugar.
3. Place the remaining sugar in a fairly large pan, let it stand on a moderate heat until it becomes caramel and begins to foam over the entire surface.
4. Pour the boiling milk slowly but in one stream over the caramel off the heat and, if necessary, let the caramel that is not yet dissolved dissolve further on the heat.
5. Pour this liquid, stirring well, into the beaten eggs, add the bread with the remaining ingredients and let it soak for 1 1/2 hours.
6. Coat a warm pudding mold with some butter or margarine.
7. Add the mixture to it, close it firmly and cook the pudding 1 1/2 to 2 hours in a bain-marie, but make sure the water is continuously topped up to just below the rim of the mold.
8. Let the pudding dry slightly and serve it with warm vanilla or caramel sauce.
