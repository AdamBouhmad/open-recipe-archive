## Ingredients

- 250 g flour
- salt
- 2 to 2½ dl milk
- 15 g yeast
- 50 g sugar
- 75 g butter or margarine
- 2 egg yolks
- 50 g currants
- 75 g raisins
- 75 g candied peel
- 15 g candied orange peel snippets
- grated zest of 1 lemon

## Directions

1. Place flour in a warmed bowl, make a well in the center, add yeast, half the milk warmed to lukewarm and half a teaspoon sugar
2. Stir with half the flour until a firm dough forms and the other half of the flour remains as a ring around it
3. Let rise for 15-20 minutes in a lukewarm place, covered
4. Add egg yolks, melted butter or margarine, remaining lukewarm milk, salt and sugar, stir in remaining flour and beat out lumps in the usual manner
5. Add currants and raisins (washed in lukewarm water and stems removed), chopped candied peel and snippets
6. Dry currants and raisins in a cloth first
7. Place dough in a greased tulband mold dusted with fine breadcrumbs, let rise covered in a lukewarm place (approximately 1 hour)
8. Bake in a fairly warm oven until light brown and cooked through (½ hour to ¾ hour)
9. Test for doneness by inserting a knitting needle, which should come out dry
10. Turn out onto a cooling rack, let cool and dust with powdered sugar
