## Ingredients

- 250 g veal cutlets
- 250 g pork cutlets
- 50 g smoked ox tongue
- 50 g fresh bacon
- 1 egg
- 50 g old bread without crust
- 10 g salt
- some pepper and nutmeg
- 1/2 tin of truffles
- 100 g pork caul
- 1/2 litre aspic in two colours
- notched lemon slices
- parsley
- capers

## Directions

1. Soak the pork caul for some time in lukewarm water and wash it thoroughly clean. Wash the cutlets, pass them twice through a meat grinder and chop them finely. Cut the bacon, which has been stripped of rind and washed, into cubes, do the same with the tongue and the truffles. Beat the egg with pepper, salt and nutmeg.
2. Stir through the bread soaked in water or milk and squeezed out between two spoons and the remaining ingredients, until no bread pieces are visible. Form a firm, oblong ball from it, place this on the pork caul and fold it around it on all sides.
3. Roll the meat in a doubled cloth and tie it as tightly as possible. Bring water with plenty of salt to a boil in a large pan with a small plate in the bottom, place the galantine in it and cook it for 1 1/2 hours.
4. Press it afterwards (with the cloth around it) for 24 hours between 2 flat plates with a heavy weight (weights or stones) on top. Then take it out of the cloth and cut it into thin slices.
