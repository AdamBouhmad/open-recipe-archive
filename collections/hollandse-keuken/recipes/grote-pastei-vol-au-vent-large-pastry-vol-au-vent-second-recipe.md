## Ingredients

- 100 g hard butter or calf kidney fat
- 100 g flour
- salt
- water (approximately 1/2 dl)
- white paper
- egg yolk

## Directions

1. Prepare puff pastry (feuilletée) in the usual manner (see recipe 846)
2. Roll it out the last time into a large, broad sheet to a thickness of 1/4 cm
3. Cut out a circle with a diameter of 18 cm (for example by laying a small plate or lid on it) and place it on a baking sheet
4. Cut long, narrow strips from thin white paper, roll them into a ball and place this on the dough
5. Cut a larger circle from the remaining dough (for example with a diameter of 24 cm)
6. Lay this over the paper and glue the edge with water to the lower circle, making sure the folds are evenly distributed
7. Roll out the leftover dough thinly, cut long, narrow strips from it, lay these crosswise over the pastry, making sure that a triangle or quadrangle is formed in the middle of the pastry, which will later be cut out to serve as the lid
8. Place a small ball of dough in the middle as a knob for the lid, glue the strips and knob with water and brush everything with egg yolk beaten with water
9. Bake the pastry in a hot oven
10. Do not look at it during the first 5 minutes, as there is a risk of it sinking
11. Bake until light brown and cooked through (approximately 20 minutes)
12. Cut out the little lid, carefully remove the paper (for example with scissors), fill it with ragout and place the lid back on
