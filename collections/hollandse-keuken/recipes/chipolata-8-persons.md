## Ingredients

- 2½ dl cream
- 2½ dl milk
- 6 eggs
- 80 g currants
- 80 g raisins
- 50 g almonds
- 30 g candied peel strips
- 30 g sugar
- 20 g white gelatin or 17½ g powdered gelatin
- 100 g biscuits
- 1 dl maraschino or persico

## Directions

1. Remove the stems from the currants and raisins, rinse them with lukewarm water, cover with boiling water and cook for 10 minutes. Let them drain well in a sieve.
2. Chop or grind the peeled almonds finely, chop the candied peel strips.
3. Soak the biscuits in the liqueur.
4. Stir the egg yolks smooth with the sugar, carefully add the cream mixed with the milk that has been brought to a boil, and let the mixture bind in a bain-marie while stirring well, but ensure it does not boil.
5. Soak the gelatin for 10-15 minutes in cold water, dissolve it in the liquid taken from the heat, let the mass cool and add the currants, raisins, almonds and candied peel strips.
6. Stir occasionally in the mixture until it begins to become jelly-like. Then mix it as lightly as possible with the stiffly beaten egg whites and layer it alternately with the biscuits in a pudding mold smeared with unbeaten egg white, but ensure that the first and last layer do not consist of biscuits.
7. Turn out the pudding after cooling onto a damp plate and serve without sauce.
