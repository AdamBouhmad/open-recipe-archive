## Ingredients

- 1/2 liter vanilla ice cream
- 1/2 liter coffee ice cream
- French candied fruits

## Directions

1. Make vanilla and coffee ice cream in the known manner (see recipe 880 and recipe 883).
2. Then divide an ice mold, either with tins or with cardboard plates, into vertical parts, carefully fill these with the ice cream types, remove the partitions.
3. Press the ice cream as firmly as possible.
4. Close the mold well, for example with a strip of surgical tape, so that no salt water can enter.
5. Place it in a bucket in which finely crushed raw ice and salt have been added.
6. Wrap the bucket in one or more blankets or molton cloths.
7. Let the pudding stand like this for at least 2 hours.
8. If the raw ice has already largely melted, then it is necessary to remove the water and refill the remainder with newly crushed ice and salt.
9. After the indicated time, remove the mold from the bucket and rinse it with cold water to prevent salt water from entering.
10. Hold it just before turning out in very lukewarm water.
11. The pudding then comes loose from the mold and can be easily turned out.
12. This is done on a chilled plate.
13. Quickly garnish the pudding with halved French candied fruits and if desired whipped cream.
14. Serve it immediately and provide waffle cookies.
