## Ingredients

- 350 g flour
- 200 g butter
- 4 eggs
- 25 g sugar
- 25 g yeast
- approximately 1 dl lukewarm milk
- fruits

## Directions

1. Make a fairly slack starter dough from approximately 100 g flour, yeast and some lukewarm milk. Let it rise in a warm place for approximately 20 minutes. Make a dough from the rest of the flour, the eggs, the sugar and possibly the rest of the milk, add the butter in small pieces at a time and knead the dough as for bread dough; then work the risen starter dough into it. Place the dough in a ring mold brushed with butter and sprinkled with breadcrumbs. Let it rise for 1 hour and then bake the savarin for approximately 3/4 hour in a hot oven. Meanwhile prepare the fruits. Catch the fruit juice and add some liqueur to it or dissolve extra sugar in it. Turn out the savarin and prick holes in it with a larding needle. Soak the ring with the sweet and aromatic fruit juice. Let the ring cool, fill it with the fruits and garnish the savarin with fruits and with cream. If the savarin is served hot (and with warm compote) then the cream garnish should be omitted, but if for example a small pitcher of cream is served separately.
