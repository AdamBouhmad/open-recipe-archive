## Ingredients

- 3 kg quinces
- 3 liters water
- 2¼ kg sugar
- 4 cloves (optional)
- 6 cm cinnamon stick (optional)
- Lemon juice (optional)

## Directions

1. Wash the pears carefully, cook them for several minutes in boiling water, then peel them, cut them into quarters and remove the cores.
2. Bring the water and sugar to a boil, add the fruit and if desired the spices and some of the peels and pits bound in a piece of cheesecloth.
3. Let everything simmer gently together until the pears are cooked and have become a nice red color (1 to 2 hours).
4. Place them with the liquid in a bowl and let them stand until the next day.
5. See if the liquid has become syrupy.
6. If not, cook the syrup down until it coats a cold spoon dipped into it.
7. Cook the fruit for 5 minutes more and place the mass in a well-cleaned Cologne pot.
8. Check after several days to see if the syrup is the right consistency and cook it down again if necessary.
9. Add lemon juice if desired.
10. Close the pot with damp parchment paper and label it with name and date.
