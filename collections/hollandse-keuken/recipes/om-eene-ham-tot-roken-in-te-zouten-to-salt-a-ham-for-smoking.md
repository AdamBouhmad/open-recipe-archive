## Ingredients

- ham
- salt
- saltpeter
- optional: coarsely ground cloves, pepper, Jamaica pepper, bay leaves

## Directions

1. Meat must lie for at least 24 hours and be completely cooled before cutting
2. Rub each piece individually with salt and saltpeter carefully, calculating one pound salt and one and a half loot saltpeter for 16 pounds meat
3. Lay the pieces close together in a sturdy oak barrel with the bottom strewn with mixed salt
4. Sprinkle salt and saltpeter between each layer and arrange so salt comes on top and the specified amount is distributed evenly
5. For a more piquant taste, mix coarsely ground cloves, pepper and Jamaica pepper with the salt to rub in, and strew some bay leaves between each layer
6. When the barrel is well filled, seal it properly and turn it daily a few times on its bottom
7. Large pieces such as hams must lie four weeks before they are well penetrated with brine; smaller pieces such as tongues are ready in three weeks
8. For the ham, make an incision underneath at the large bone and put salt there so no spoilage occurs at the bone
9. When the barrel is opened and you want part of the meat to remain, place a wooden lid with a stone on top, turn the meat daily and baste it with its brine
