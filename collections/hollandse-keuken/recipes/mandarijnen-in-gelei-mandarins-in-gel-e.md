## Ingredients

- juice of 6 large oranges
- juice of 3 lemons
- juice of 6 small mandarins, all made up with white wine, wine and water or water and some lemonade syrup to 1 liter liquid
- sugar to taste, at least 200 grams
- 2 egg whites
- 35 g white gelatin or 30 g powdered gelatin
- 6 mandarins
- a few green French fruits

## Directions

1. Dissolve the sugar in a little water, add this mixture to the remaining liquid.
2. Add the (washed and soaked) gelatin and the lightly beaten egg whites.
3. Prepare the liquid as indicated in recipe 555.
4. Pour a thin layer of the clarified gelée into a pudding mold rinsed with cold water.
5. Let it set firm.
6. Peel the mandarins, carefully remove all white membranes, divide them into segments and remove as many pits as possible.
7. Place some on the cooled gelée, alternating with small pieces of candied fruit, glue them with a little lukewarm gelée, let the mass cool again, pour gelée over until the segments are covered and let everything set.
8. Continue this way until the mold is filled, turn the pudding out onto a damp plate after cooling and garnish it with whipped cream.
9. Note: If the pudding does not come out of the mold easily, it is best to dip it briefly in warm water. The gelée can also be made in a flat dish, filled with pineapple for example, and instead of juice pressed from fresh fruit, use canned fruit juice.
