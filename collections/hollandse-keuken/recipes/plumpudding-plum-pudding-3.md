## Ingredients

- 250 g old bread without crust
- 125 g raisins
- 125 g currants
- 125 g sultana raisins
- 125 g sugar
- 150 g candied citrus peel
- 250 g calf suet
- 4 eggs
- grated zest of 1/2 lemon
- 3/4 grated nutmeg
- 25 g cinnamon
- 15 g almonds
- 1/2 dl rum

## Directions

1. Remove the stems from the currants and raisins, wash them with lukewarm water.
2. Chop the candied citrus peel, chop the peeled almonds finely.
3. Crumble the bread, chop the washed suet, from which the membranes have been removed, finely.
4. Stir the egg yolks as smoothly as possible with the sugar, mix them with all ingredients, the last as lightly as possible with the stiffly beaten egg white.
5. Fill a warm pudding mold coated with butter and sprinkled with fine breadcrumbs 3/4 full with the mixture.
6. Close it firmly and cook the pudding at least 8 hours in a bain-marie. Make sure the water is continuously topped up to just below the rim of the mold.
7. Let the pudding dry without the lid, pour rum over it and bring it in flaming, or stick a sprig of holly with berries in it.
8. Serve plumpudding in small portions and serve rum sauce with it.
