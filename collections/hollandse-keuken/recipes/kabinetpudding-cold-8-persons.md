## Ingredients

- 1 l milk
- 125 g sugar
- ½ vanilla pod or vanilla sugar
- 1 dl rum or arak
- 4 eggs
- 100 g currants
- 100 g raisins
- 50 g candied citron
- 35 g white gelatin or 30 g powdered gelatin
- angelica and candied fruit pieces

## Directions

1. Remove the stems from the currants and raisins, rinse them with lukewarm water, cover with boiling water and cook for 10 minutes. Let them drain well in a sieve.
2. Chop the candied citron finely.
3. Beat the egg yolks with the sugar vigorously together, add as much of the milk mixed with the vanilla that has been brought to a boil until they are thoroughly warmed and the sugar is dissolved.
4. Return everything to the pan, let it bind in a bain-marie while stirring well, but ensure it does not boil.
5. Remove the mass from the heat and stir in the soaked gelatin, mixed dried fruit and rum or arak.
6. Let the mixture cool, stirring occasionally, add the stiffly beaten egg whites, let everything together, also stirring occasionally, become jelly-like and pour it into a pudding mold rinsed with unbeaten egg white.
7. Let the pudding cool and set firmly, turn it out onto a damp plate and serve without sauce.
8. Note: The pudding mold rinsed with egg white can be garnished with strips of angelica and candied fruit pieces.
