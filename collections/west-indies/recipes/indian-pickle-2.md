## Ingredients

- cauliflowers
- French beans
- peas
- gherkins
- small onions
- capsicums
- bird's-eye chilies
- garlic
- eschalots
- white hearts of summer cabbage
- tomato skins
- vinegar
- salt
- currie powder
- mustard seed

## Directions

1. Wash the vegetables with vinegar and dry them
2. Put them on large dishes, sprinkled over with salt, and left for twenty-four hours
3. Dry them carefully and put into a large jar
4. Boil as much vinegar as will be required to cover them, putting to each quart of vinegar one tablespoonful and a half of currie powder (or two if the currie powder is not very hot), and an ounce of mustard seed
5. Pour this vinegar boiling over the vegetables, and when cold tie the jar down, and leave it for twelve hours or longer
6. Turn the vegetables out of the jar into a large basin, and put them, free of liquid, into smaller bottles for use, taking care to place the tomato skins at the sides of the bottles
7. Boil the vinegar again, adding more and currie powder in proportion, if you have not enough to fill every bottle quite full, and pour it boiling hot over the vegetables in the bottles
8. When cold, tie down with bladders; it must be kept a year
