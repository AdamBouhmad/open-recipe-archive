## Ingredients

- large cucumbers or melons (green)
- strong salt and water
- vine leaves
- alum and water
- cold spring water
- one pound of loaf sugar
- half an ounce of powdered ginger
- water
- the juice and rind of a lemon
- rum

## Directions

1. Take large cucumbers or melons, such as are green, put them into a jar of strong salt and water, with vine leaves on the top
2. Set them by the fireside until they turn yellow, then wash them
3. Put them on a slow fire in alum and water, covered with vine leaves
4. Let them boil till they become green, take them off and leave them in the liquor until they are cold
5. Quarter them and take out the soft part and seeds, if there are any
6. Put them in cold spring water, changing it twice a day for three days
7. Have ready a syrup made as follows: To one pound of loaf sugar add half an ounce of powdered ginger, and as much water as will moisten it
8. Boil it and skim it well
9. When it is quite free from scum and boiling fast, add the juice and rind of a lemon
10. When this syrup is quite cold pour it over the cucumber or melon
11. Should the syrup be too thin after standing two or three days, boil it again with a little more sugar
12. A spoonful of rum gives it the West Indian flavour
