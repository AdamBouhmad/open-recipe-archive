## Ingredients

- two pounds of good mutton
- one onion
- butter
- one clove of garlic
- one tablespoonful (or two if required very hot) of currie powder
- two ounces of butter
- good gravy
- tamarind juice or lemon

## Directions

1. Wash and cut up into pieces, about an inch square, two pounds or rather less of good mutton
2. Put the pieces of meat into a stewpan
3. Add an onion previously fried in butter, and a clove of garlic, chopped fine
4. Sprinkle over the meat one tablespoonful (or two if the currie is required to be very hot) of currie powder
5. Brown two ounces of butter in a frying-pan, and pour it over the meat
6. Add sufficient good gravy to cover it
7. Let it stew gently for two hours
8. Add some tamarind juice, or lemon to give the required acid
9. Thicken the gravy and serve
