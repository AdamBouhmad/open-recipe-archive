## Ingredients

- half a pound or a little more of raisins stoned and cut in halves
- two onions
- one garlic
- one eschalot
- about twenty chilies well pounded in a mortar
- half a pound of mustard
- a quarter of a pound of fine ginger
- a quarter of a pound of salt or not quite as much
- one pound of sour apples
- a pint of vinegar
- one pound of brown sugar previously boiled to a syrup, or the same quantity of treacle or golden syrup
- about three pints of vinegar

## Directions

1. Put into a large basin or pan, half a pound, or a little more, of raisins stoned and cut in halves, two onions, one garlic, one eschalot, and about twenty chilies well pounded in a mortar
2. add half a pound of mustard, a quarter of a pound of fine ginger, and a quarter of a pound of salt, or not quite as much
3. peel and slice one pound of sour apples, put them in a jar with a pint of vinegar
4. cover the jar well, and put it into the oven and bake until the apples are rather soft
5. When they are cold, add them to the other ingredients with one pound of brown sugar previously boiled to a syrup, or the same quantity of treacle or golden syrup, and about three pints of vinegar, including the quantity the apples were baked in
6. Stir all the ingredients well together, and bottle for use
