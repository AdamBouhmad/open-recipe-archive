## Ingredients

- large, fully ripe, peeled quince
- sugar equal in weight to the quince
- four deciliters of water
- warm water

## Directions

1. Cut the whole ripe peeled quince into four pieces, or six if larger. Cut off the core. In a preserving basin, cook sugar equal in weight to the quince with four deciliters of water until the sugar dissolves. Add the quince slices and, turning them frequently, cook until completely soft but not falling apart. While the sugar is dissolving, the quince slices must lie in warm water to stay beautiful white in color. Remove the slices and continue cooking the liquid until it begins to thicken. Remove from heat, return the slices to the basin, and shake it strongly so the liquid covers everything. Cool slightly and pour while warm into clean preserving jars, leaving to stand for twenty-four hours. The liquid must completely cover the slices after settling. If the liquid is not thick enough after complete cooling, strain it slowly, cook it again, and pour it warm over the slices.
