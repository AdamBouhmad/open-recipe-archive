## Ingredients

- well-worked black bread dough made with yeast or sourdough
- water
- clean wheat bran
- rice
- eggs
- egg yolks
- sour cream
- butter
- cream of tartar

## Directions

1. From well-worked black bread dough made with yeast or sourdough, set aside about one liter
2. Let it rise in a slightly warm place for 6-8 hours
3. Place in an 8-9 liter earthenware pot and mix by hand with 2 liters of water
4. When well mixed, thicken with clean wheat bran to form a soft dough consistency
5. Cover with a lid and leave in a warm place for 48 hours, stirring three times daily with a wooden spoon
6. On the third day, fill the pot with cold water and let it settle for a couple of hours
7. When settled, strain off as much as needed into an earthenware pot and bring to a boil
8. For each liter of czibere, cook a handful of rice, and when tender, strain off the water
9. For each liter of czibere, beat an egg and egg yolk with a tablespoon of sour cream
10. Temper by first adding a small amount of hot czibere so it does not curdle, then add to the whole pot
11. Stir well, season with salt, add the cooked rice and bring to a boil
12. When boiling, add a tablespoon of melted butter per liter, and if not sour enough, a little cream of tartar
13. Pour into small bread cubes and serve
14. Serve oil at the table and those who like it can pour a spoonful into their bowl
15. Refill the pot with the same amount of clean water and keep in a warm place for later use
16. One starter can be strained eight times if kept in a good place
