## Ingredients

- four deciliters good cream
- fish glue
- four deciliters currant juice
- juice of half a lemon
- eighteen dekagrams sugar
- two dekagrams dissolved fish glue
- fourteen dekagrams swollen, finely broken almonds
- a few bitter almonds among them
- four deciliters best cream
- sugar to taste
- two dekagrams dissolved fish glue

## Directions

1. Prepare a chocolate aspic from four deciliters of good cream, mixing it with fish glue
2. Pour into a mold and freeze on ice
3. Press currants through a cloth, take four deciliters of currant juice
4. Add the juice of half a lemon to the currant juice
5. Dissolve eighteen dekagrams sugar in the liquid
6. Mix in two dekagrams dissolved fish glue
7. Strain the mixture through a cloth stretched on four chair legs with blotting paper in the middle
8. When the chocolate aspic is completely frozen, pour the currant mixture on top
9. Soak fourteen dekagrams almonds, including a few bitter ones, in four deciliters of best cream
10. Let it stand with the almonds for a few minutes, mixing well
11. Strain through cloth and add enough sugar to make it sweet enough
12. Mix in two dekagrams dissolved fish glue
13. Strain once more and slowly pour over the already frozen currant aspic
14. Freeze the whole mixture
15. When unmolding, pay special attention so the form is not left in water too long, lest the stripes melt and the colors mix together
