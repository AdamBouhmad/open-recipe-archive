## Ingredients

- twenty-eight dekagrams of hazelnuts
- twenty-eight dekagrams of sugar
- eleven egg yolks
- five egg whites
- three identical cake leaves and their hoops
- butter for coating
- apricot syrup for filling
- preserved raspberries for filling
- lemon icing for coating
- quince paste or candied quince
- green almond for decoration
- preserved apricot or a beautiful apple for decoration

## Directions

1. Shell the hazelnuts, rub well in cloth, and grind them.
2. Similarly grind twenty-eight dekagrams of sugar and sift it.
3. Place the sugar in a basin with eleven egg yolks and beat until white and well foamy.
4. Add the ground hazelnuts and beat well.
5. Fold in the stiff foam of five egg whites.
6. Coat three identical cake leaves and their hoops with butter.
7. Fill with the mixture and bake over gentle heat.
8. To test if well baked, after half an hour insert a large needle into the center of the cake; if the needle remains clean, turn the cake out onto a sieve and cool it.
9. When completely cooled, coat the first layer with apricot syrup, place the second on top, fill it with preserved raspberries, and cover with the third layer.
10. Cover the entire cake evenly with lemon icing.
11. Cut long slices from quince paste or candied quince.
12. Slice green almond lengthwise and arrange a star in the center of the cake.
13. Place a whole preserved apricot or a beautiful apple in the center of the almond star.
14. Arrange the quince paste strips at finger-width intervals around the edge of the cake, placing the green almond leaves beside them as if serving as a stem.
15. Place the cake on a plate covered with a neatly folded napkin.
