## Ingredients

- one and a half kilograms quince pulp
- one and a half kilograms fine sugar
- water, four deciliters
- aspic

## Directions

1. A portion of quince ham consists of one and a half kilograms of quince pulp and the same amount of fine sugar; cook the latter in a brass pan with four deciliters of water until it forms heavy drops.
2. Then add eighteen to twenty pressed quinces to the cooked sugar and cook with continuous stirring until it begins to gel slightly; this must be tested several times so the quince does not lose its white color by cooking too long.
3. If the test succeeds, put enough of it in a tin ham mold to form a layer of fat; spread it evenly quickly with a silver spoon and cool slightly; meanwhile pour enough aspic into the remaining mixture to give it a meat color; carefully place the red quince paste on the white, but the two must not mix; leave in a warm room for three or four days.
4. Test whether it could be turned out; if not, leave standing two or three more days, then turn out onto a clean board covered with tissue paper and keep in a dry place.
5. If the paper becomes damp after several days, replace it with dry paper. This ham is an excellent delicacy, as when cut into thin slices it has a beautiful appearance, and if the quince is pure and spotless, it perfectly resembles natural ham.
