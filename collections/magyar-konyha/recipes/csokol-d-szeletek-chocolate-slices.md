## Ingredients

- 14 dekagrams softened almonds
- 14 dekagrams grated chocolate
- 3 tablespoons water
- 14 dekagrams butter
- 14 dekagrams sugar
- 7 egg whites
- 14 dekagrams granulated and sifted sugar
- 4 egg yolks
- 4 egg whites
- 14 dekagrams fine flour
- 14 dekagrams butter
- orange zest
- 7 dekagrams finely ground and sifted sugar
- 2 teaspoons raspberry or currant marmalade

## Directions

1. Finely grind the softened almonds, moistening several times during grinding to prevent it from becoming oily
2. Cook the grated chocolate with three tablespoons water in a brass pan with continuous stirring into a thick paste
3. Beat butter until fluffy, finely grind the sugar
4. Mix the almonds and chocolate with the butter and sugar, stirring together for a good quarter hour
5. Fold in the stiff foam of seven egg whites
6. Spread the mixture thinly on a buttered baking sheet to pinky-finger thickness and bake slowly
7. Prepare a second mixture: beat granulated and sifted sugar rubbed with orange zest with four egg yolks for a quarter hour
8. Lightly fold in the stiff foam of four egg whites with 14 dekagrams fine flour
9. Meanwhile, melt 14 dekagrams butter, discard the whey, and when cooled, slowly fold in, stirring only until combined
10. Spread this mixture on a buttered baking sheet to pinky-finger thickness, sprinkle with sugar, and bake slowly
11. Once the second mixture is baked and cooled, cut into card-sized pieces
12. Spread the chocolate layer with peach marmalade and top with a white layer of the same size
13. Continue until all are filled
14. Mix 7 dekagrams finely ground and sifted sugar with 2 teaspoons raspberry or currant marmalade into icing
15. Fill a paper cone with a pinhead-sized opening with this icing
16. Decorate the slices and let dry
