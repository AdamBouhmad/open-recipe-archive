## Ingredients

- 36-40 medium-sized crayfish
- lightly salted water
- parsley
- piece of red onion
- crushed pepper
- thyme
- bay leaves
- basil
- half a liter light table wine
- fish broth (consommé)
- 100-150 grams white bread crumb

## Directions

1. Wash the crayfish carefully
2. Put lightly salted water in a casserole and add parsley, sliced red onion, crushed pepper, a little thyme, a few bay leaves, and a pinch of basil
3. Add half a liter of light table wine
4. Before the liquid boils, add the crayfish and let cook until completely done, stirring frequently
5. Remove the tails from the cooked crayfish and extract the meat from them
6. Grind the cooked torsos with their meat and the claws finely in a mortar
7. Place this ground mixture in a pot and pour the liquid in which the crayfish were cooked over it
8. Add 2 liters of fish broth (consommé) and 100-150 grams of white bread crumb
9. Bring the whole to a boil and cook for a good hour on a careful fire so it does not boil away
10. After one hour, strain through a fine sieve and set the broth aside
