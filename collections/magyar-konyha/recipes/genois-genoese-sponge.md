## Ingredients

- 14 dekagramm butter
- 14 dekagramm broken and sifted sugar
- orange
- 4 egg yolks
- 4 egg whites
- 14 dekagramm fine dry flour
- apricot marmalade
- white frosting
- lemon juice
- redcurrant or raspberry preserves
- sugar

## Directions

1. Melt 14 dekagramm butter, remove the sediment and set aside to clarify completely but remain liquid, not solidify
2. Rub 14 dekagramm broken and sifted sugar on an orange, then beat in a bowl with 4 egg yolks for a quarter hour until foamy
3. Make stiff foam from 4 egg whites
4. At almost the same time, mix in 14 dekagramm fine dry flour with the mixture
5. Then quickly stir in the butter sediment only until well combined with the rest
6. Coat a cake tin with its ring with butter
7. Spread the mixture onto it in two knife-blade thicknesses smoothly
8. Bake at gentle heat
9. When baked and cooled, either cut with a two-forint-sized cutter or cut large boats from it
10. Coat with apricot marmalade
11. Using a small paper cone with an opening no larger than a pin head, pipe small dots of beautiful white frosting around the edge in an orderly pattern, leaving space for another dot between every two dots
12. Fill another cone with redcurrant or raspberry preserves that have been pushed through and cooked with sugar, which should be beautifully colored, and pipe red dots from the preserves between the white dots
13. If cutting the dough into boats, decorate the same way and arrange on a plate in a large star shape
