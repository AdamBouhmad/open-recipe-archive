## Ingredients

- medium-sized cucumbers
- fresh water
- walnut-sized alum
- dill seeds
- currant leaves
- cucumber herb
- ironwort
- half vinegar
- half good white wine
- several handfuls of salt
- 3 dekagrams whole pepper
- 3 dekagrams white ginger

## Directions

1. Place medium-sized cucumbers, preferably small rather than large, in fresh water and leave them there for 2-3 hours, pouring fresh water over them several times
2. Drain them, place on cloth, and dry them
3. The barrels in which you place them must be very clean
4. Put a walnut-sized piece of alum in each barrel and the usual herbs: currant leaves, cucumber herb, and especially a lot of ironwort, as this preserves the firmness and green color of the fruit
5. After lining the bottom of the barrel with this, place a good layer of cucumbers on top, then herbs again, then cucumbers, and so on until the barrel is full
6. Boil equal parts vinegar and good white wine with several handfuls of salt, three dekagrams of whole pepper, and three dekagrams of white ginger in a clean earthenware pot
7. Pour this mixture hot over the cucumbers, then seal tightly immediately
8. Check occasionally, and if there is barrel mold on top, remove it; if necessary, pour a little more wine and vinegar
9. Shake this container several times during use
10. For households where only small quantities of cucumbers are needed, it is better to preserve them in glass jars rather than barrels; the procedure remains the same, except that the jar, since the vinegar is not absorbed, should be tied tightly closed with paper and a bladder and stored in a dry place like cooked fruit
