## Ingredients

- puff pastry dough as described in recipe 372
- egg white
- 28 dekagrams veal
- 3-4 veal sweetbreads
- 6 button mushrooms
- green parsley, finely chopped
- 20-30 shelled crayfish tails
- asparagus or artichoke, boiled in salted water
- nutmeg flower
- butter sauce

## Directions

1. Prepare puff pastry dough as described in recipe 372 and stretch it to knife-thickness
2. Cut out a round or rectangular piece and brush the outer edge with egg white to one finger's width
3. Stretch another piece of dough similarly and place it on top
4. For round pastry: cut small donut-sized pieces and arrange them on the edge of the pastry, overlapping so the second piece always covers half of the first
5. For rectangular pastry: cut two-finger-width strips from the dough and place on the edge, trimming neatly at the corners
6. Make a round incision in the center of the pastry the size of a drinking glass using a warm knife
7. Place a double button made from dough on top
8. Brush the pastry with egg white and bake in the oven until golden
9. Once baked, cut out the lid with a sharp knife and carefully remove it
10. Quickly extract the dough that spreads between the two pieces
11. Fill the pastry with the prepared filling
12. For the filling: cook 28 dekagrams veal and 3-4 veal sweetbreads, then braise 6 chopped button mushrooms with finely chopped green parsley; separately cut 20-30 cooked crayfish tails and boiled asparagus or artichoke into cubes, and braise these in butter sauce with nutmeg flower until the sauce reduces completely
13. Carefully fill the pastry with the filling using a spoon
14. Replace the lid and serve on the table
15. Alternative: the pastry can also be filled with rabbit or venison instead
