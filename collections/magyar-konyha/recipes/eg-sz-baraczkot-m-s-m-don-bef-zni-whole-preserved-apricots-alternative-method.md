## Ingredients

- apricots
- slaked lime
- white alum
- sugar
- water

## Directions

1. Use the best type of beautiful yellow apricots, not yet completely ripe.
2. Prepare clean water in a clean container, and strain slaked lime through clean cloth into the water until it becomes milky.
3. Peel the apricots, cut them in half, remove the pit, and place them in the lime water so they do not blacken.
4. While peeling, boil 7-10 liters of clean water with 10-12 dekagrams of white alum in a clean container.
5. Remove the peeled apricots from the lime water, wash them two or three more times in clean water, and boil them in the alum water for 3-4 minutes with constant stirring without softening them.
6. Remove them still firm and place on a sieve so the water drains well, then arrange on a clean tablecloth spread on a table.
7. For 1.5 kilograms of fruit, take 1 kilogram of sugar and boil it with three glasses of water until it throws heavy drops.
8. Arrange the apricots in a porcelain dish in a beautiful order and pour the boiled and cooled sugar over them.
9. Leave for twenty-four hours.
10. After this time, pour off the sugar, boil it again until it thickens, cool it, and pour it over the apricots again, leaving for another twenty-four hours.
11. On the third day, place the apricots in glasses and use the liquid that was on them for various purposes.
12. Again boil 1 kilogram of sugar with three glasses of water for 1.5 kilograms of fruit until it throws heavy drops, cool it, and pour over the apricots in the glasses.
13. Tie the glasses well and steam for 2-3 minutes.
