## Ingredients

- one suckling pig
- salt
- ground pepper
- lard
- clean cloth
- twine

## Directions

1. After killing and scalding the pig, use water that is not too hot—only hot enough that you can safely insert your finger into it.
2. Remove the entrails and clean the pig thoroughly.
3. Salt it well and coat the interior with ground pepper.
4. Splay the thighs apart; bend the front legs inward at the joints so they lie backward and tie them with twine to maintain their natural position.
5. Thread the pig lengthwise onto a spit so that the spit passes through the neck and comes out through the nose without making a hole.
6. Stuff clean cloth into the mouth and sew it closed.
7. Roast for one hour over medium heat or in the oven, frequently basting with a piece of lard, then wiping clean with cloth after a few moments, and turning frequently to prevent blistering.
8. The skin should turn a beautiful golden-brown and be crispy and brittle.
9. When done, remove the spit, cloth, and twine, and quickly bring to the table.
