## Ingredients

- five-times folded puff pastry made from approximately eighty dekagrams of butter
- cooked apricots for filling
- cooked currants
- four egg whites
- fine sugar
- pistachios, finely chopped
- coarse sugar
- egg wash
- cooked whole cherries for decoration

## Directions

1. Prepare five-times folded puff pastry from approximately eighty dekagrams of butter
2. Roll the pastry in three layers to the thickness of two knife-backs
3. Cut out two completely equal round leaves with a warm knife, sized as a 38 dekagram pastry leaf
4. Cut out a large circle from the center with a large doughnut cutter, leaving only a hand-width-sized smooth edge
5. Cut out two more completely identical leaves and cut them the same way, continuing until eight pairs are made, with each pair being about half a small finger-width smaller than the other
6. Brush every leaf with beaten egg, prick with a knife point, dust with finely ground sugar, and bake in a slightly cooled oven
7. Once baked, coat the largest leaf with cooked apricots to a knife-back thickness and place its pair on top
8. Fill the second pair with apricots and continue until all eight pairs are filled
9. Coat the edges of the joined leaves with foamy icing made from four egg whites to a knife-back thickness and roll over finely chopped pistachios on rice paper so the entire edge appears green with powder
10. Coat the second leaf similarly and roll it in coarsely ground sugar, the third again in pistachios, and so on until all eight pairs are powdered
11. Return to a cooled oven only until the foamy icing is dried, with the sugar remaining completely white and the pistachios a nice green
12. Once cooled, place the first two joined leaves on the intended plate, coat with cooked apricots, place the second double leaf on top so it occupies exactly the center of the first, coat its surface again with apricots and place the third leaf on top, continuing until all eight are stacked on top of each other
13. Decorate the final leaf with cooked whole cherries
