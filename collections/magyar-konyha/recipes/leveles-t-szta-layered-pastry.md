## Ingredients

- 1 large lemon
- 280 grams sugar
- 280 grams butter
- 6 egg yolks
- 280 grams fine flour
- 6 egg whites
- 3 torte sheets with rings
- butter
- preserved fruit
- 5 egg whites
- 2 tablespoons peach marmalade
- 100 grams finely ground sugar

## Directions

1. Rub the sugar on a large lemon, finely grind the sugar
2. Whip the butter until foamy and add egg yolks one by one
3. Once the eggs are well beaten, add the sugar and beat for a quarter hour
4. Add the fine flour and the stiffly beaten egg whites
5. Butter three torte sheets with rings
6. Divide the batter into three equal parts on the torte sheets
7. Bake until a beautiful light yellow color
8. When baked and cooled, one sheet remains on the torte sheets, two are set aside on a sieve
9. Fill each sheet with preserved fruit and leave the top one empty
10. A quarter hour before serving, beat stiffly the egg whites of five eggs with two tablespoons peach marmalade and 100 grams finely ground sugar
11. Gradually fold in the egg whites and coat the top and sides of the pastry with a knife
12. Fill the remaining meringue into a Spanish pastry bag and pipe hazelnut-sized meringues in three rows around the edge of the top sheet
13. Place a large button in the center of the sheet, surround it with smaller meringues so they touch the button, keeping them small but beautifully tall, which requires a light and steady hand
14. Sprinkle with fine sugar and place in a cool oven or cold cooled oven so the meringue retains its color and does not harden too much
