## Ingredients

- 5 brittle kiflick
- 10 dekagramm softened and finely ground almonds
- 10 dekagramm ground sugar with vanilla
- 4 deciliters cream
- 7 dekagramm butter
- 6 egg yolks
- 4 egg whites
- citronade slices
- preserved sour cherry or medlar
- raspberry sauce

## Directions

1. Gently grate the crusts of 5 brittle kiflick and cut into slices.
2. Place in a pan with the almonds, sugar with vanilla, and cream.
3. Heat over coals, stirring constantly, until smooth paste forms.
4. Remove from heat and stir in 7 dekagramm butter while still warm, then let cool.
5. Once completely cold, beat in 6 egg yolks one after another.
6. Fold in the stiffly beaten whites of 4 eggs.
7. Butter a pan and line the bottom with white paper, then butter the paper again.
8. On the bottom of the pan, arrange a wreath of citronade slices, and inside arrange another wreath of preserved sour cherry or medlar pieces the size of a silver coin.
9. Carefully pour the paste over this and cook in a bain-marie for three-quarters of an hour.
10. If making the raspberry sauce from cooked fruit, clean it, press through cloth, and cook 4 deciliters of juice with 18 dekagramm sugar until the liquid is slightly thick.
11. Carefully turn the paste out onto a serving dish, carefully remove the paper lining, and pour the raspberry sauce over the bottom of the dish.
