## Ingredients

- a pigeon
- a little marrow
- finely chopped parsley
- lemon zest
- bread soaked in milk
- two eggs
- two whole eggs
- a full tablespoon sour cream
- several shelled and diced crayfish tails
- bacon
- red onion
- carrots
- oil-brushed paper
- flour
- good broth

## Directions

1. After cleaning and soaking the pigeon in water, cut it open along the back and remove the meat all the way to the head, legs and wings
2. Finely chop this meat together with a little marrow, finely chopped parsley, lemon zest, bread soaked in milk, and a mixture made from two eggs
3. Place in a bowl, then add two whole eggs, a full tablespoon of sour cream, and several shelled crayfish tails diced into cubes
4. Stuff the pigeons with this mixture and sew them shut so they regain their former shape
5. Place bacon, red onion, and carrots cut into slices in a pot, place the pigeons on top
6. Cover with oil-brushed paper and a lid and braise well, frequently pouring a little broth over them so the pigeons do not dry out
7. Remove the pigeons and braise the vegetables left in the pot until nicely brown
8. Sprinkle a little flour over them, dilute with good broth and cook down
9. Strain the sauce through a sieve, place the pigeons back in and serve on the table
