## Ingredients

- seven large, ripe quinces
- water
- sugar, same amount as quince, half kilogram

## Directions

1. Cook or bake seven large ripe quinces until soft; be careful they do not crack as they will then become watery.
2. When soft, cool slightly on a plate; then peel with a silver spoon, as they would darken from a knife, and press through a sieve.
3. For half a kilogram of quince use the same amount of sugar; cook this with one glass of water until it threads, then add the pressed quince, mix well with the sugar, and cook over gentle charcoal heat until the syrup separates from the silver spoon with which you must stir continuously so it does not burn.
4. Then while still warm place on coffee trays, spread nicely with a spoon, and leave on the tray for five or six days.
5. Then turn out onto a clean plate, let stand for six to eight days, turning over daily so it dries on all sides. The longer it stands, the better.
