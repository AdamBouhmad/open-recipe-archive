## Ingredients

- beautiful veal loin with ribs and cartilage cleaned
- finely chopped bacon in five or six rows
- ham pieces
- onion or nutmeg onion
- brown sauce
- 28 dekagrams veal
- bread soaked in milk and grated
- two or three egg yolks
- finely chopped onion
- fresh parsley finely chopped
- flour

## Directions

1. Blanch the veal loin, ribs, and cartilage as described in the first part, page 40
2. Lard it with finely chopped bacon in five or six rows
3. Cut the bacon with large sharp scissors so the whole resembles a spiny hedgehog
4. Place in a pot with ham pieces, onion or nutmeg onion, and fill with brown sauce
5. Cover and braise until tender
6. Remove the loin, strain the sauce through a sieve back into the pot, and braise until it becomes glacé with only a small amount of sauce remaining
7. Return the loin to the pot and coat well on all sides, especially where it is larded
8. Place in the center of the serving dish and pour the small amount of sauce over it
9. Prepare a wreath as follows: make a forcemeat from 28 dekagrams veal and bread soaked in milk and grated
10. Chop finely and grind to breadcrumbs
11. Add two or three egg yolks to make it into a paste
12. Add finely chopped onion and fresh parsley
13. Place on a flour-dusted board and make a thick sausage
14. Cook in beef broth, then cut into knife-thickness slices
15. Arrange these slices around the edge of the dish to form a nice golden wreath
