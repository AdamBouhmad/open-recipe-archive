## Ingredients

- 28 dekagrams fine flour
- 2 deciliters cream
- 5 egg yolks
- 1 dekagram dissolved yeast
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- salt
- 28 dekagrams firm butter
- preserved apricots, currants or poppy seeds for filling
- egg white
- ground sugar
- almonds

## Directions

1. Divide the flour into two parts
2. From the larger part, prepare dough on a board with cream, egg yolks, dissolved yeast, sugar and salt; this dough should be firmer than puff pastry
3. Knead it with your hand until it separates from the hand, form into a ball and set aside covered with a cloth
4. Salt the other half of the flour, place firm butter in the middle, cover with flour and smooth with a rolling pin until the flour and butter are completely mixed, being careful not to touch the butter but always separating it with a wide knife and stretching it into a long strip
5. When done, place the dough on a board dusted with flour, roll out to the thickness of two knife-backs, place the butter in the middle and fold the dough over it from all four sides
6. Roll out and fold together four times like puff pastry
7. Note that this dough should not rest but should be rolled out and folded quickly one after another
8. This dough is especially good for pot pies and yeast cake, but particularly for rolls: roll out to knife-back thickness, cut out round leaves with a large cutter and brush the edges with egg white
9. Fill with preserved apricot, currants or poppy seeds, fold together and form into roll shapes
10. Place on a baking sheet and let rise slightly
11. In the meantime, blanch almonds, dry and chop finely and mix with an equal amount of ground sugar
12. When the rolls have risen, brush with egg yolk, sprinkle thickly with the almond sugar and when baked, sprinkle again with sugar and serve on the table
