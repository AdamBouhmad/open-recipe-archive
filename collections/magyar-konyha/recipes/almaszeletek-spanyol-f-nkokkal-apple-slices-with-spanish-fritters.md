## Ingredients

- 14 dekagrams butter
- 17 dekagrams flour
- 10 dekagrams finely ground sugar
- 9 dekagrams softened almonds
- 3 hard-boiled egg yolks
- 1 additional egg yolk
- 3 tablespoons thick cream
- Masánszki apples
- sugar
- 1 tablespoon preserved peach
- 3 egg whites
- 16 dekagrams sugar
- a little vanilla

## Directions

1. Mix butter, flour, and finely ground sugar well together
2. Crush the softened almonds and hard-boiled egg yolks to crumbs
3. Work these together with the butter mixture using a rolling pin until it becomes dough
4. Add one more egg yolk and three tablespoons thick cream, working well with a wide knife
5. Roll the dough to knife-edge thickness and cut into strips the length of a small span and two fingers wide
6. Place on a baking sheet, brush with sugared water, and bake at gentle heat
7. Peel Masánszki apples and cut into thin wedges
8. Cook the apple wedges with sugar and one spoonful of preserved peach into marmalade with continuous stirring
9. Draw shapes on parchment paper matching the dough strips but slightly narrower
10. Prepare Spanish fritter dough from three egg whites, 16 dekagrams sugar, and a little vanilla
11. Fill a pastry tube with this dough and pipe three fine strips lengthwise along the drawing, not at the edges so they don't expand too much during baking
12. Pipe cross-strips to create a lattice pattern
13. Sprinkle with fine sugar and place in a cooled oven to dry but remain white
14. Once dry enough to lift from the paper, spread apple marmalade knife-edge thickness on the slices
15. Top with the lattice made from Spanish fritter dough before serving
