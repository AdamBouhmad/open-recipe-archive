## Ingredients

- ripe but not soft rose hips
- seventy dekagrams rose hips
- fifty-six dekagrams sugar
- two deciliters of water

## Directions

1. Select rose hips when completely ripe but not yet soft. Cut them in half and carefully scrape away the coarse inner part with a knife. Place in a pot that has already been boiled out. Pour enough water so they do not burn. Cook over gentle heat with continuous stirring until all the water has evaporated and the rose hips are soft, then press through a fine sieve. For seventy dekagrams of rose hips, take fifty-six dekagrams of sugar and cook it with two deciliters of water until it threads. Stir in the rose hip pulp and cook with continuous stirring for ten minutes, then pour hot into jars.
