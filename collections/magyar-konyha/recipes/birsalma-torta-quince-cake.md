## Ingredients

- 0.5 kilogramm sugar
- 2 deciliter water
- 0.5 kilogramm quince pulp
- 18 dekagramm swollen and finely broken almonds
- 10 dekagramm candied citron, chopped
- 1 lemon, zest and juice finely chopped
- wafer paste sheets
- coarsely broken sugar
- lemon icing
- Spanish pastries
- green preserved fruits

## Directions

1. Cook 0.5 kilogramm of sugar with 2 deciliter of water in a brass bowl until it threads and produces large bubbles.
2. Prepare 0.5 kilogramm of pulp from well-ripened quinces, cook it, and press through a sieve.
3. Add the pulp to the well-cooked sugar, along with 18 dekagramm of swollen and finely broken almonds, 10 dekagramm of chopped citron, and the finely chopped zest and juice of 1 lemon.
4. Cook together over gentle heat with constant stirring until it becomes jelly-like.
5. Line a cake layer with large round wafers and spread the mixture to a two-finger height with a knife dipped in water, smoothing it evenly.
6. Sprinkle with coarsely broken sugar and leave in a cooled oven for 24 hours.
7. Coat with lemon icing and decorate the edge with Spanish pastries fastened with hardened sugar and green preserved fruits.
8. For the lemon icing: prepare it as pistachio icing, but rub the sugar on the lemon zest, finely crush it, and when the icing is well worked and clear and thick, incorporate the rubbed sugar and 2 teaspoons of lemon juice, stirring well.
9. Test the icing by placing a few drops on paper; if it does not spread and becomes firm in a few moments so it can be lifted off intact, it is good; if not, continue working it longer.
