## Ingredients

- a stuffed, crumbly-fleshed capon
- seven dekagrams marrow
- seven dekagrams bacon
- two tablespoons fine herbs
- salt
- a handful of capers
- pickled tongue cut in pencil-like pieces
- equal amount of venison mushroom
- butter
- cloth
- acidic root vegetables
- aspic with cutout leaves

## Directions

1. After cleanest plucking, cut open along the back
2. With a sharp knife carefully remove the ribs from the flesh so the skin is not cut anywhere
3. Draw the legs into the body above the knee, develop the wings only to the first joint, similarly remove the rest
4. Spread the whole capon on a clean board and press as evenly as possible
5. Finely chop together seven dekagrams marrow, seven dekagrams bacon, two tablespoons fine herbs and a little salt
6. Pound into a paste, place in a bowl
7. Mix well with a handful of capers, the same amount of pickled tongue cut in pencil-like pieces, and an equal amount of venison mushroom
8. Spread on the capon skin, then roll it up in a cloth brushed with butter
9. Tie very securely and cook in acidic root vegetables for one and a half to two hours
10. Set aside in a cool place
11. For serving, remove the roll from the broth, unwrap it and cut into beautiful even slices
12. Arrange on a long platter and decorate with cutout aspic leaves
