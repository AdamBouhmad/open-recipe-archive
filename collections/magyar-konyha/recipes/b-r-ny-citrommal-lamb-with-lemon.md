## Ingredients

- two liters of water
- one quarter of a lamb from the front
- salt
- a little parsley root
- carrot
- celery
- one small head of red onion
- several peppercorns
- one tablespoon of flour
- one egg-sized amount of lard
- half a glass of cold water
- two lemons

## Directions

1. Put one quarter of lamb from the front, cut into pieces, into two liters of water with salt, a little parsley root, carrot, celery, a small head of red onion, and several peppercorns
2. Cook the lamb for three-quarters of an hour
3. While it cooks, prepare a roux from one tablespoon of flour and one egg-sized amount of lard
4. When the roux is at its best, pour half a glass of cold water into it and immediately add it to the simmering lamb broth
5. After cooking with the roux for two minutes, strain through a sieve
6. Remove the meat from among the vegetables and place it in the clear broth
7. At serving time, squeeze the juice of two lemons into it, but not before, as it will become bitter
