## Ingredients

- sponge cake dough
- butter for greasing
- strong dough for border
- apricot or raspberry syrup
- 3 egg whites
- 16 dekagrams sugar
- small amount of vanilla
- orange preserve
- 42 dekagrams fine sugar

## Directions

1. Prepare sponge cake dough and grease a large sheet with butter
2. Make a border around the edge of the sheet from strong dough to prevent the sponge cake dough from running off
3. With the help of a large knife, spread the sponge cake dough on the sheet to the thickness of a knife blade and bake it at gentle heat
4. Once baked, use a small pastry cutter to cut out crescent shapes or croissants, fill them with apricot or raspberry syrup, place another on top, and continue until all are filled
5. Prepare Spanish thread dough from 3 egg whites, 16 dekagrams sugar, and a small amount of vanilla
6. Fill a pastry bag with the mixture; the tube must have a very small opening and the lines must be very fine
7. Press thin lines crosswise into the croissants, with the space between lines only as wide as a knife blade, then add another line
8. Once all are piped, place in the oven to dry as quickly as possible without browning
9. Meanwhile, prepare orange preserve from 42 dekagrams fine sugar, with the difference that when the sugar that has been worked for a long time is white and has the consistency of child-paste, add 2 teaspoons of finely ground sugar first rubbed on an orange and 4 teaspoons of orange juice
10. Once the croissants are dried, dip the upper side entirely in the preserve and place them upper-side down on paper until they harden, then pile them high on a plate
11. The surest test that the sugar is properly made into preserve is if during cooking it forms large bubbles, and during the working you see that what flows off the spoon is white and thick
