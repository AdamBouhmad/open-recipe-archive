## Ingredients

- sponge cake batter
- fine sugar for sprinkling
- twenty-eight dekagrams coarsely ground sugar
- two deciliters water
- two teaspoons lemon juice
- two teaspoons sugar rubbed on lemon
- two teaspoons good rum
- preserved apricots or raspberries for filling

## Directions

1. Fill a pastry cone with sponge cake batter
2. Pipe small coin-sized cakes onto white paper
3. Sprinkle with fine sugar and place on baking sheet
4. Bake at low heat
5. When cooled, peel off with knife
6. Fill half of each cake with preserved apricot or raspberry at the bottom, cover with another cake
7. For the filling: pour two deciliters water onto twenty-eight dekagrams coarsely ground sugar in a deep brass pan
8. Place on charcoal fire, stir several times and cook until sugar completely dissolves and thickens slightly
9. Test by passing a slotted spoon through it and blowing hard on it; if many small bubbles flow through like soap bubbles, the sugar is ready
10. Remove from heat
11. Take a new cooking spoon, dip halfway into the sugar and continuously rub against the pan's side, keeping the spoon in the empty pan area, working only what flows from the dipped spoon, doing this quickly and without pause
12. When sugar begins to develop a white skin, quickly stir it off and continue the previous method until sugar becomes white and thick
13. Add two teaspoons lemon juice, two teaspoons sugar rubbed on lemon, and two teaspoons good rum
14. Mix well and thoroughly until well combined with the sugar
15. The filling is good when white and thick like light baby pap
16. Dip the filled cakes one by one into the filling, turning both sides with two four-pronged forks
17. Place on white writing paper with the forks and leave until firm
18. Remove and arrange neatly on a plate
