## Ingredients

- suckling pig
- veal shoulder
- marrow
- bread roll
- milk
- four whole eggs
- salt
- black pepper
- nutmeg flowers
- cooked, pickled or smoked tongue
- pickled cucumber
- red onion
- carrot
- peppercorns
- allspice
- white wine
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Clean the pig thoroughly, cut off the head and legs, and slit the belly lengthwise.
2. Separate the meat and bones from the skin carefully with a sharp knife without damaging the skin.
3. Chop finely 28 dekagrams each of veal shoulder, 14 dekagrams of marrow, and bread roll soaked in milk.
4. Pound this mixture in a mortar, pour four whole eggs over it, pound again, add salt, little crushed black pepper, and nutmeg flowers.
5. Pass through a fine sieve and spread the mixture inside the pig to the thickness of a thumb.
6. Cut cooked, pickled or smoked tongue into pieces the size of a small finger, and similarly cut pickled cucumber.
7. Arrange these in strips over the forcemeat, leaving spaces the width of a finger between them.
8. Roll up like a strudel, wrap in clean cloth, and tie both ends securely.
9. Place in a long pot with sliced red onion, carrot, a few peppercorns and allspice, and the cut-off head and legs.
10. Pour two liters of white wine and four deciliters of vinegar over it, salt it, cover, and cook until the head is tender.
11. Cool it, and when it has cooled and set, pour the cooking liquid over it and set aside until ready to use.
12. Before serving, remove from the cloth, dry it clean, and slice thinly.
