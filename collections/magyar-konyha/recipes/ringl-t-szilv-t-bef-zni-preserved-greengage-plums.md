## Ingredients

- greengages
- water
- vinegar
- sugar

## Directions

1. Boil the greengages in two parts water and one part vinegar, but allow only a few boils so the skin does not crack and the stem does not fall out.
2. Then place in fresh water.
3. For 500 grams of sugar, allow 25 dekagrams of fruit.
4. Boil the sugar with one glass of water, clarify it, and boil until it throws heavy drops.
5. Place the greengages on a sieve so they drain well, then into the sugar, and bring to a boil with it for one moment and remove from the fire, pour over them, and leave open for twenty-four hours.
6. After this time, strain the syrup from them well and boil it again, cool it, pour over the fruit; repeat this procedure for three days.
7. The greengage must not be completely ripe, and for this reason those at the end of July are the best and most suitable.
