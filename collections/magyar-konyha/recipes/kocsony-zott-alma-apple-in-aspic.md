## Ingredients

- apples
- champagne
- water
- sugar
- isinglass
- cochineal juice
- preserved sour cherries

## Directions

1. Peel the apples and scoop out the centers slightly
2. Stew them in 2 parts genuine French champagne and 1 part water with 21 decagrams of sugar without letting them fall apart
3. When well softened, place on a prepared dish and put the approximately 7 deciliter sauce back on embers
4. Add 4.5 decagrams dissolved isinglass, or if you have ice, 3.5 decagrams is sufficient
5. Add cochineal juice for color, and strain the aspic through cheesecloth and blotting paper as usual
6. Fill the apples with whole preserved sour cherries and pour the aspic over them, being careful not to stain the apples, keeping them white
7. In the absence of good champagne, use only 7 deciliters of water, juice of 2 good lemons, and enough sugar to sweeten sufficiently
8. In summer, when fresh fruit is available, smaller apples can be used, and raspberry or currant syrup can be used instead of champagne
