## Ingredients

- 42 dekagrams sifted flour
- salt
- 1 spoonful powdered sugar
- 2 deciliters lukewarm sour cream
- 5 egg yolks
- 7 dekagrams melted butter
- 1.5 dekagrams fresh brewer's yeast, softened

## Directions

1. Since these fritters are mainly made at carnival time and require a warm oven to rise properly, it is especially necessary to place the flour near a heated oven one day before use so it is dry and warm
2. Put 42 dekagrams sifted flour in a bowl, salt it, add 1 spoonful powdered sugar
3. Take 2 deciliters lukewarm sour cream, 5 egg yolks, 7 dekagrams melted butter and 1.5 dekagrams softened fresh brewer's yeast, mix everything well together
4. Take as much flour as needed for working the fritters
5. Since not all flour is the same, it is better to take more of it so the dough is not too hard; you can always add more flour later if necessary
6. Sift the above mixture through a sieve into a bowl and stir with the flour until the dough separates from the spoon and beat well for some time
7. Note that the dough should not be hard, although it is difficult to beat
8. Place the dough on a flour-dusted board and stretch to three knife-edge thicknesses, cut out the fritters with a floured fritter cutter and place them on a flour-dusted board
