## Ingredients

- 7 dekaliters milk (lukewarm)
- flour (approximately 14 dekaliters by volume)
- 1.25 dekagrams fresh yeast dissolved in milk
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- salt as needed
- 1 egg yolk
- 18 dekagrams butter
- For finer brioche: 3 liters flour, 1.5 liters milk, 6 dekagrams softened cheese yeast, salt, 1 whole egg, 2 egg yolks, 28 dekagrams butter
- For economical good brioche: 1 liter fine flour, 1 whole egg, 1 egg yolk, 1 tablespoon sugar, 2 dekagrams cheese yeast dissolved in milk, 4 dekaliters lukewarm good milk, 7 dekagrams pure lard

## Directions

1. Prepare a starter (pâte) from 4 dekaliters lukewarm milk and 1.25 dekagrams fresh yeast dissolved in milk
2. Mix finely with more milk (this is essential with yeast)
3. Place in bowl and beat well, adding only as much flour as 4 dekaliters milk will wet
4. Set in a warm place until well risen
5. Warm the remaining milk and add 2 tablespoons sugar
6. Salt the flour and prepare the dough with the milk
7. Once the dough is well worked so no more flour or milk is needed, add the starter to the center and knead well
8. Note: knead with open fingers, not with fists as for bread
9. After kneading awhile, add 1 egg yolk and continue kneading
10. When dough separates from hand, move it to one side of the trough
11. Clean the trough and melt 18 dekagrams butter
12. Coat the trough and your hands with it and continue kneading
13. Coat the other side of the trough and repeat until all butter is kneaded in and dough forms bubbles
14. Then knead lightly with fists for a few minutes, fold together, dust with flour, cover and let rise well
15. Once well risen, form small pieces
16. Let them rest 15 minutes, then braid the brioche
17. Heat the oven while the dough rises so it is ready when the brioche is formed
18. The brioche should rise for at most 15 minutes before baking
19. Place on buttered paper and brush with beaten egg before putting in the oven
20. For finer brioche: use 3 liters flour to 1.5 liters milk; make starter from 4 dekaliters milk and 6 dekagrams softened cheese yeast; when well risen, salt flour, sweeten well, add 1 whole egg and 2 egg yolks; prepare dough with remaining milk; knead 28 dekagrams butter in three portions using same method
21. For economical brioche: salt 1 liter fine flour in a bowl; in a pitcher whisk 1 whole egg, 1 egg yolk, and 1 tablespoon sugar; mix in 2 dekagrams cheese yeast dissolved in milk; add 4 dekaliters lukewarm good milk; pour this onto the flour and knead with right hand until dough is smooth
22. Now add 7 dekagrams pure cold lard and knead until hand is clean and dough forms small bubbles; cover with cloth and let rise in warm place
23. Divide the entire mass into 4 pieces on a flour-dusted board, knead them, and form two brioche from them
