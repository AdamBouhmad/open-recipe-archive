## Ingredients

- one or two calf brains
- salt
- water
- butter
- fresh parsley
- onion
- pepper
- rusk
- beef broth
- two whole eggs

## Directions

1. Cook the calf brains in salted water and clean completely from fibrous parts
2. Chop finely
3. Melt a piece of butter and add chopped fresh parsley and finely chopped onion, browning slightly
4. Add the brain, a little salt, a little ground pepper, and one or two rusks crumbled fine, the quantity of brain should be about twice that of rusk
5. Pour on a little beef broth and cook until reduced, then cool
6. Mix in two whole eggs and place in a buttered mold
7. Set in a water bath and keep at even heat for half an hour
8. Meanwhile, prepare thin veal cutlets the size and shape of mulberry leaves using a wide knife
9. Soak the knife frequently in water to shape them easily
10. Once all are prepared and salted, coat well with melted butter, sprinkle with fine breadcrumbs
11. Place on a buttered pot lid and bake until nicely brown
12. When serving, turn the brain paste onto a round dish so the pieces overlap, with one edge resting on the brain to form a crown, or cut half-moons from butter pastry using a medium pastry cutter, coat with egg white, fry until golden brown and arrange around the turned-out brain paste glazed with good glaze
13. If the paste is made from two calf brains, use four eggs
