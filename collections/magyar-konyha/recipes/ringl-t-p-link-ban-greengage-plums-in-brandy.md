## Ingredients

- 150 slightly unripe greengages
- cloth for cleaning
- 2 parts fresh water
- 1 part white vinegar
- 1 kilogram sugar
- 1 liter water
- 1.5 liters good brandy

## Directions

1. Clean 150 slightly unripe greengages carefully with a cloth and prick them all over with a needle.
2. Soften them in a mixture of 2 parts fresh water and 1 part white vinegar for three to four hours.
3. Cook 1 kilogram sugar with 1 liter water and skim it well.
4. When the syrup is sufficiently clear, place the greengages in a clean basin, let the syrup cool slightly, and pour it over them.
5. Leave for 24 hours so the fruit absorbs the sugar well.
6. Strain the syrup, place the fruit in glass jars, cook the syrup again until it becomes thick, let it cool, and pour it over the fruit.
7. Pour 1.5 liters of good brandy over this quantity, tie the jars closed, prick the paper with a needle, and store.
8. When the fruit settles to the bottom of the jar, it is ready to eat.
