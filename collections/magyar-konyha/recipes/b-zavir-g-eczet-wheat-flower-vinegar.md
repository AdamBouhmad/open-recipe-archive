## Ingredients

- wheat flowers in bloom
- good vinegar

## Directions

1. When the flower is in full bloom, carefully pull off the petals, remove the white part, and let it dry.
2. Place it in a glass bottle, pour good vinegar over it, bind it well, and set it in the sun or in a warm place.
3. When the wheat flower begins to turn white in the vinegar, place fresh petals, pulled off just as carefully, in another bottle, strain the vinegar from the first flower onto it, and pour fresh vinegar on, bind the bottle, and similarly set it in a warm place.
4. Continue in this manner with fresh flowers until the vinegar has the desired color.
5. If you use good strong vinegar for this, not only will the color be much more beautiful, but you will need to pour it onto fresh flowers only two or three times instead of more.
