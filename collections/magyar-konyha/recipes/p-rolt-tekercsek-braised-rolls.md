## Ingredients

- beef for broth
- beef marrow
- salt
- water
- green parsley
- butter
- red onion
- breadcrumbs
- bacon
- carrot
- thread
- flour
- vinegar
- lemon zest
- sour cream
- mushrooms

## Directions

1. Cut pieces from beef broth meat that are as long as a knife is wide but not thicker than a knife spine
2. Beat them and salt them
3. Pour boiling salted water over beef marrow, peel off the membrane cleanly, chop it finely, and braise it in butter with green parsley
4. Add a little chopped red onion, salt, pepper, a handful of breadcrumbs, and some liquid, and bring to a slight boil and cool
5. Spread this filling on the meat, roll it up, and tie it with thread
6. Place bacon, red onion, and carrot in a pot, put the rolls in, and braise until their sauce has evaporated and they brown
7. Remove them, place one to two spoonfuls of flour in the pot, brown it, dilute it with good liquid, add vinegar and lemon zest, cook the sauce well, strain it, place the rolls back in, add two spoonfuls of sour cream, and braise slightly
8. Arrange the rolls in a line on a serving dish, strain the sauce over them, and border the edge of the dish with crescents made from short pastry, or after cutting off both ends of the rolls, pile them high in the center of the dish, pour the scant sauce underneath, and surround them with a wreath of mushroom or truffle, which has been browned in butter first
