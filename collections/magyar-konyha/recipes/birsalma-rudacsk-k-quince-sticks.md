## Ingredients

- several large, ripe quinces
- 28 dekagrams sugar per 28 dekagrams quince pulp
- 1 glass water
- 18 dekagrams blanched and finely ground almonds
- 14 dekagrams citron peel, finely chopped
- wafer sheets
- lemon preserve

## Directions

1. Cook the quinces until soft but not bursting
2. Drain and let rest for a while
3. Peel and pass the flesh through a sieve
4. Take equal weights of sugar to quince pulp
5. Cook the sugar with a glass of water until very thick and forming large bubbles
6. Quickly add the quince pulp with the almonds and citron peel
7. Cook with continuous stirring for a few minutes until very thick
8. Cut wafer sheets into strips the length of a finger and two fingers wide
9. Spread the mixture onto the strips with a wet knife to the thickness of a little finger
10. Place on wax-coated baking sheets
11. Leave in a cooled oven until dried and easily peeled off
12. Coat with lemon preserve
