## Ingredients

- roasted rabbit or venison
- two rolls, sliced wide
- lard
- bones from the meat
- a few bacon slices
- carrots
- onions
- one tablespoon flour
- one part white wine
- two parts good strong broth
- puff pastry
- egg yolk

## Directions

1. After roasting the rabbit or venison, cut out the most beautiful back pieces knife-thin and set aside
2. Remove remaining meat from bones and fry it
3. Slice two rolls into wide slices and fry in lard with the minced meat
4. Pound together in a large mortar until it resembles paste, then place in a pot
5. Meanwhile, braise the bones with a few bacon slices, carrots, and onions until nicely brown
6. Sprinkle with one tablespoon flour and when brown, cook well with one part white wine and two parts good strong broth
7. Strain the sauce, add one part to the ground meat, bring to a boil, stir well and pass through a sieve
8. Add the reserved back slices and boil together for a few minutes
9. The forcemeat should be pea-thick
10. Prepare puff pastry and cut out a round piece the size of a plate with a warm knife
11. Coat the outer edge with egg yolk and place strips of pastry around it
12. Cut remaining pastry into coin-sized pieces and arrange on the strips so each overlaps the previous one to form a tall crown
13. Coat everything carefully with egg and bake nicely
14. When removed from the oven, fill the crown with the forcemeat, pile the slices on top and serve quickly
