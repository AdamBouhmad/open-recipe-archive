## Ingredients

- firm head of kale
- salted water
- bacon slices
- ham slices
- red onion
- carrot
- black peppercorns
- clove-pepper
- brown broth
- sour cream
- beef broth
- rice
- warm water
- butter or goose fat
- thin ham slices
- lean ham
- butter paper

## Directions

1. Wash a beautiful firm head of kale cleanly and boil in salted water for a few minutes without making it too soft
2. Strain and place on a sieve
3. In a larger pot put several bacon slices, the same amount of ham slices, chopped red onion and carrot, eight peppercorns and five clove-peppers
4. Cut the kale lengthwise in half and place it cut-side down in the pot
5. Cover and braise slowly, occasionally pouring a little brown broth on it so it does not burn
6. When the vegetable has a nice light-brown color on the bottom of the pot, carefully remove the kale
7. Mix a spoonful of sour cream with it and brown it with frequent stirring
8. Thin it with good beef broth and cook for a few more minutes until soft but not falling apart
9. Remove and set aside
10. Prepare the braised rice: soften ten decagrams of cleaned rice in warm water half an hour before use
11. Melt a piece of butter or goose fat and add the drained rice to it
12. Put half a red onion, a few peppercorns and salt in the middle of the rice and braise slowly, occasionally pouring one or two spoonfuls of good broth over it so the rice braises but does not boil, keeping the grains beautiful, white and whole while becoming soft without becoming soupy
13. Cover the bottom of a pot buttered with a paper that fits exactly
14. Cut thin slices from beautiful ham and leave a narrow strip of bacon on it
15. Cut the ham slices into triangular pieces and arrange them on the bottom of the pot so no space remains and the triangles fit together well
16. Finely chop a piece of lean ham
17. Remove the red onion and peppercorns from the braised rice
18. With a spoon, layer the rice smoothly and firmly in the pot, sprinkle thickly with chopped ham, then rice again and so on until the pot is full
19. Cover and steam for three-quarters of an hour
20. Before serving, strain the sauce in which the kale braised and let it braise a little
21. Carefully turn the kale out onto the plate, pour a little sauce on the bottom of the plate and serve with a beautifully prepared veal carbonade
