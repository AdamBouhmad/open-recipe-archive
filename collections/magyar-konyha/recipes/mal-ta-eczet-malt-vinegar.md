## Ingredients

- 15 liters barley
- water
- 40 liters water
- 7 deciliters yeast

## Directions

1. Soak 15 liters of barley in water.
2. When it swells, pour it onto a floor, cover it with something light until it begins to sprout, then spread it out, dry it again, and grind it coarsely.
3. Soak the ground malt in lukewarm water for several hours, then heat it well in hot water, then boil it for one hour.
4. For this amount of malt, calculate 40 liters of water.
5. After boiling, filter the liquid through a clean sack or sieve, at the bottom of which you have placed straw, into a container, and when it is half as warm as fresh milk, place it in a small barrel or trough, mix it with 7 deciliters of yeast, then cover and let it stand in a warm place.
6. Fermentation will soon begin, and when it no longer rises and no bubbling can be observed, and you have removed the yeast on top, draw off the fermented liquid through a tap, which is applied 3-5 centimeters above the bottom of the barrel, so that only clear vinegar flows out without the yeast and other cloudy parts.
7. The barrel into which you draw it off should first be rinsed with vinegar.
