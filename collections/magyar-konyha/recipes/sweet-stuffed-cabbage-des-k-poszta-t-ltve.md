## Ingredients

- 1 soft head of cabbage
- salt water
- 28 dekagrams braised veal, chopped into pieces
- 1/2 goose liver, breaded and fried
- breadcrumb coating
- a little fresh parsley
- 1/2 red onion, grated
- 1/2 bread roll, soaked in milk and squeezed out
- salt
- ground pepper
- 1-2 tablespoons good sour cream
- egg yolk
- butter for lining the mold
- boiled cabbage leaves

## Directions

1. Cook the soft head of cabbage in salted water, cool it and separate into individual leaves
2. Prepare the filling: chop finely together the braised veal cut into pieces, half a goose liver breaded and fried, a little fresh parsley, grated half a red onion, and half a bread roll soaked in milk and squeezed out
3. Place in a bowl and season with salt and ground pepper
4. Add 1-2 tablespoons of good sour cream and egg yolk and mix well
5. Line a butter-greased mold completely with boiled cabbage leaves
6. Spread part of the filling smoothly on top
7. Add cabbage leaves again and continue alternating until the mold is full
8. Cover the top with cabbage leaves and braise in steam for 45 minutes
9. Turn out onto a platter
10. Serve with a sauce prepared as follows: melt a piece of butter the size of half an egg, mix in a spoonful of flour and brown to a dark yellow; dilute with a tablespoon of broth, add 4-5 whole peppercorns, a little nutmeg, and 2 tablespoons of brown broth; cook well, strain through a sieve and cook until thick; pour the thin sauce on the bottom of the platter, place the stuffed cabbage in the center and arrange nice cutlets around it
