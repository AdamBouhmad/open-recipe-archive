## Ingredients

- 12 kilograms water
- 75 dekagrams honey
- 25 dekagrams tartar

## Directions

1. Take sweet water obtained from honey pomace, place it in sour earthenware pots, and set them next to the oven, by which means after 4-6 weeks you will obtain good vinegar.
2. Alternatively, take 12 kilograms of water with 75 dekagrams of honey (this amount is necessary for twelve liters), place them together on the fire, remove the foam, and add 25 dekagrams of tartar.
3. Boil the mixture for a time, then strain it through cloth into a barrel and place it in a warm spot so it boils for a few moments.
4. Draw off the now-ready vinegar, clarify the cloudy part in a pot with bladder, and pour the vinegar together with the clarified part into a small barrel.
5. Once it has sufficiently soured, draw it off into bottles, which, well-stoppered, will keep for a long time.
