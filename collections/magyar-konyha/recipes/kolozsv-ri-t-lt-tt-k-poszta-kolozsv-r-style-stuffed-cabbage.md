## Ingredients

- 3 or 4 heads of fermented head cabbage
- 1/2 kilo pork loin
- 1/2 kilo beef brisket
- 1/4 kilo bacon for larding
- 2 deciliters rice
- 1 whole egg
- 1/4 teaspoon ground pepper
- salt to taste
- 4 fillér worth of whole peppercorns
- 1/4 kilo smoked bacon
- 1 pig's ear
- 1/2 kilo pork with skin
- 1/2 kilo beef sirloin
- white wine (small glass if needed)
- sour cream

## Directions

1. Use fermented head cabbage, not shaved cabbage, as it has better flavor
2. Remove the leaves from 3 or 4 heads of fermented cabbage and trim the core from the back of each leaf
3. Set aside the thinner leaves and chop the remaining leaves thinly
4. Prepare the filling: finely chop 1/2 kilo pork loin and 1/2 kilo beef brisket
5. Finely chop 1/4 kilo bacon for larding
6. Combine the meat and bacon, then add 2 deciliters rice, 1 whole egg, 1/4 teaspoon ground pepper, and salt to taste
7. Mix the filling well by hand
8. Place filling in the reserved thin cabbage leaves, roll, and tuck in the ends
9. In one roll, wrap about 4 fillér worth of whole peppercorns and tie with string so it doesn't fall apart
10. In a 7-8 liter earthenware pot, layer 1/4 kilo smoked bacon on the bottom
11. Add a layer of chopped cabbage
12. Place on top: 1 pig's ear, 1/2 kilo pork with skin, and 1/2 kilo beef sirloin (these must be boiled first, skimmed, strained through a sieve, and the broth added)
13. Layer cabbage again, then the cabbage rolls, then cabbage until the pot is full
14. Pour water to cover the cabbage
15. If the cabbage is not sour enough, add a small glass of good white wine
16. Bring to heat and cook until tender, shaking and turning occasionally
17. When cooked, move to a cold place, covered with a sieve, until the next day
18. Reheat the next day
19. For serving: arrange the cabbage on the bottom of the dish, arrange the stuffed cabbage rolls, meat, and bacon on top
20. Pour the cabbage liquid into a pot, mix with sour cream, bring to a boil, and pour over the arranged cabbage
21. If cabbage remains in the pot, it can be reheated on the third day
