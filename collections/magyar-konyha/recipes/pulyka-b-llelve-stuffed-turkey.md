## Ingredients

- young large turkey
- veal
- goose liver
- bacon
- fresh mushrooms
- button mushrooms
- butter
- parsley
- onion
- salt
- pepper
- eggs
- white wine

## Directions

1. Blanch a young but fairly large turkey and split its skin lengthwise down the back.
2. Using a sharp knife, carefully extract the legs to the thigh and wing, being careful not to damage the skin.
3. Blanch twenty-eight dekagrams of veal, one goose liver, a small amount of bacon cut to the size of peas, several finely chopped fresh mushrooms and button mushrooms.
4. Braise with butter and green parsley, add finely minced onion, salt and pepper.
5. Place everything in a bowl, beat in two whole eggs and egg yolk, mix well with the rest.
6. Stuff the turkey with this mixture and sew the skin closed at the back.
7. Cover the turkey with thin bacon slices, wrap well with string and place in a pot that should not be larger than the turkey.
8. Pour three spoonfuls of good broth over it, two deciliters of white wine and cook slowly.
9. When the turkey is sufficiently tender, remove it and place where it will not cool.
10. Skim the fat from the sauce, strain through a sieve and cook until only a small amount remains.
11. Remove the bacon from the turkey, place on the plate, pour the small amount of sauce underneath and arrange small braised potatoes around the edge.
