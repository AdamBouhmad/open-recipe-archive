## Ingredients

- 210 grams blanched almonds, soaked
- 5 deciliters water
- sugar to taste
- 3 tablespoons fine flour
- 1 egg for brushing
- breadcrumbs
- 4 deciliters milk
- 4 tablespoons fine flour
- 4 grated tablets chocolate
- 100 grams sugar

## Directions

1. Grind the soaked almonds fine and place in a soup bowl
2. Pour 5 deciliters water over it and let stand for an hour with frequent stirring
3. Wring out in a cloth and pound the almonds again, pour the almond milk over it, mix well and strain a second time for a strong almond milk
4. Mix in enough sugar so the taste is distinctly sweet
5. In a pot slowly mix 3 tablespoons fine flour with the almond milk, cook to a smooth paste with continuous stirring
6. When well cooked, spread on buttered sheet metal to finger thickness, cool and carefully turn onto a flour-dusted board
7. Cut out in crescent shapes with a flour-dusted cutter, brush with beaten egg and coat with fine breadcrumbs
8. Fry in fat to a beautiful golden color and dust with powdered sugar
9. For a more elegant presentation, make the following fritter to combine with it: in a pot set over low heat, slowly mix 4 deciliters milk with 4 tablespoons fine flour, 4 grated tablets chocolate and 100 grams sugar with continuous stirring
10. Cook until thick and smooth, then cool
11. Spread the paste evenly on buttered parchment with a knife moistened in water, cool completely, heat the parchment for a moment, then carefully turn onto a flour-dusted board and cut into crescents
12. Brush with egg and coat half with flour and half with breadcrumbs
13. Fry in hot fat and dust densely with finely grated chocolate
14. Arrange the white almond milk fritters and chocolate fritters alternately on a platter and serve
