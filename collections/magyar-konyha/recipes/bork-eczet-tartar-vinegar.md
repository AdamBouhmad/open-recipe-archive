## Ingredients

- Good vinegar
- Boiling water
- 42 dekagrams alcohol
- Wine tartrate
- Burnt sugar or raspberry

## Directions

1. Prepare two small wooden barrels made of beech or oak wood with iron hoops
2. On the front side of the first barrel, above the tap, make an opening 3-4 centimeters from the barrel rim to serve as an air hole, with a diameter of 1½ centimeters
3. Place both barrels in a cellar that can be kept warm in winter
4. Fill the first barrel with good vinegar up to the air hole (approximately 7 liters)
5. Let the barrel rest for 14 days so the vinegar penetrates the wood well
6. Draw off 2 liters and place in the second barrel, replacing it with boiling water to which 42 dekagrams of alcohol are added
7. Every 3 weeks, draw off 2 liters and pour into the vinegar reserve in the other barrel, replacing with boiling water and alcohol in the same manner
8. If you wish to make this vinegar entirely equal to wine vinegar, dissolve a small amount of wine tartrate in it and color it with burnt sugar or raspberry
