## Ingredients

- almonds when still soft
- half kilogram sugar
- almonds equal in quantity to the sugar
- one part white vinegar
- two parts water
- one glass of water
- silver spoon

## Directions

1. Preserve almonds when their kernel is still soft. For half kilogram of sugar, take an equal amount of almonds. Clean them with cloth so the coarse part comes off. Cook them in one part white vinegar and two parts water until they can easily be pierced with a pin, then place on a sieve to dry. Cook the sugar with one glass of water until it threads, then cook the almonds in it for a quarter hour, turning them with a silver spoon on all sides so they absorb the sugar well. Remove them and cook the syrup a bit longer. Place the almonds in jars, pour the slightly cooled syrup over them, leave the jars open, and for four days every twenty-four hours bring the syrup to a boil. On the fifth day, when the preserve is completely cool, seal the jars.
