## Ingredients

- 40 grams isinglass
- water
- egg white
- 2 jars canned apricots
- sugar
- 1 jar small muscat pears
- lemon juice
- 1 jar Spanish cherries
- 1 jar autumn apricots

## Directions

1. Beat 40 grams isinglass well and tear into small pieces
2. Wash thoroughly in cold water and soak for several hours
3. Cook in 400-500 ml water until dissolved
4. Add fresh egg white during cooking, simmer for a few minutes, remove from heat
5. Let settle and strain the clear part through cloth
6. Divide into four parts and keep in a warm place
7. Select 12 finest apricots from a jar and place on a plate
8. Sprinkle well with crushed sugar and turn several times
9. Drain juice and sprinkle again so sugar penetrates well
10. Place remaining apricots with their juice in a bowl
11. Cook 140 grams sugar with 400 ml water until dissolved
12. Pour over apricots in bowl and let sit for several hours
13. Strain without pressing, add juice of a small lemon and one quarter of isinglass
14. Pass through stretched cloth
15. Put half in a pan, bury in ice and freeze
16. Once frozen, layer the sugared apricots on top
17. Pour remaining aspic over and freeze again
18. Select the finest pears from a jar and set aside
19. Place others in a bowl with 100 grams sugar and 200 ml water with half the pears
20. Once boiling, pour over the pears and let sit
21. Add juice of 1.5 lemons and another quarter of isinglass, strain through cloth
22. Pour over apricot layer and arrange pears same way as apricots
23. Select largest cherries from a jar and set aside on a plate
24. Sprinkle with sugar and strain
25. Place remaining cherries in a bowl with 140 grams sugar boiled in 100 ml water
26. Mix with another quarter of isinglass and strain through cloth
27. Pour over pear layer and arrange cherries
28. Select 12 finest autumn apricots on a plate
29. Sprinkle well with sugar and prepare like the first apricots
30. Add 1 lemon to the juice and remaining isinglass
31. Strain through cloth
32. Once cherry layer is frozen, pour and layer the final aspic
33. Note: the final aspic must be added slowly and only poured when already frozen to prevent fruit from floating up and mixing with other layers
34. Keep on ice until serving, use caution and speed when unmolding
