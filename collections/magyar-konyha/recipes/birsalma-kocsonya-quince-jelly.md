## Ingredients

- 1 liter water
- 42 dekagrams sugar
- 4 large ripe and beautiful yellow quinces
- 2 lemons
- 1.25 dekagrams dissolved isinglass

## Directions

1. Cook the sugar in water only until it dissolves
2. Wipe the four large ripe and beautiful yellow quinces clean with a cloth, but do not peel them
3. Cut them into slices as thick as a knife blade and cook them in the sugar until soft
4. When the sugar has well absorbed the quince flavor and the color is beautiful yellow, strain through a sieve but do not press
5. Press in the juice of two lemons
6. Mix in the dissolved isinglass
7. Strain through a stretched cloth
8. If it is beautifully clear, pour into a mold and freeze on ice
9. Alternatively, thin slices of quince preserved in sugar may be placed in the jelly in two or three rows, and then treated as other layered jellies
