## Ingredients

- tender beans
- water
- 2 parts white vinegar
- 1 part water
- 14 dekagrams salt per 2 liters
- fresh beef fat or butter

## Directions

1. Clean the tender beans by removing the strings
2. Bring water to a boil in a pot and add the beans
3. Cook for a short quarter hour, then drain quickly and place in cold water
4. When completely cooled, remove and spread on clean cloth to dry
5. When dry, place in an earthenware pot
6. Take two parts water and one part white vinegar with fourteen dekagrams salt per two liters
7. Boil the beans only a few times so they don't become too soft
8. Boil the water only until the salt is completely dissolved, then remove from heat
9. When cooled, pour over the beans
10. Pour melted fresh beef fat or butter over it so that the fat forms a hard protective layer that air cannot penetrate
11. When the fat has hardened, tie the pots closed and store in a cool place
12. When using, take out as much as needed and place in lukewarm water an hour before use
13. Close the pot with freshly melted lard, otherwise the rest would spoil
