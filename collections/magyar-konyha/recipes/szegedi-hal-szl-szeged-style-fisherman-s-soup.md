## Ingredients

- fish (carp, catfish, sturgeon and other river fish)
- fresh bacon
- onion
- salt
- paprika
- water

## Directions

1. For ten to twelve people, procure at least six to seven kilos of fish, which should include carp, catfish and sturgeon, and may also include two or three other types of live river or stream fish
2. Clean the fish alive and cut off their tails below the anus; pour the fish blood into the cauldron, remove the intestines and rinse the fish
3. Cook the fisherman's soup best outdoors or at an open hearth in an iron cauldron hung on a chain
4. For each kilo of fish, chop 2-3 decagrams of fresh bacon into small cubes and for each kilo of fish add one to two medium onions cut into thin slices, which should be lightly fried in the fat
5. At the bottom of the cauldron, layer the fish heads and firmer-fleshed fish so that there is a small layer of onion at the bottom, then a little salt and paprika and a row of fish, thus all the fish and onion are layered and salted and paprikaed in layers
6. When everything is layered, pour very little water over it and do not stir during cooking, but occasionally rock the cauldron by its handles in a circle
7. The fisherman's soup cooks for just half an hour and is best served at the table in the cauldron itself, where the chef who knows where each type of fish is located can best serve each person individually
