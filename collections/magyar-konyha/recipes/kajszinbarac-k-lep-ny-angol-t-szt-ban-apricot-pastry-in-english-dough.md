## Ingredients

- 280 grams fine flour
- 3 hard-boiled egg yolks
- salt
- 2 tablespoons broken sugar
- walnut-sized piece of butter
- 2 raw egg yolks
- 1 coffee cup fresh sour cream
- water if needed
- 280 grams clarified butter, chilled
- well-ripened apricots
- sugar

## Directions

1. Place 280 grams flour on a work surface
2. Press 3 hard-boiled egg yolks through a sieve and add with salt and 2 tablespoons broken sugar, mixing well with flour
3. Make a well in the flour, add a walnut-sized piece of butter, 2 raw egg yolks, and a coffee cup fresh sour cream
4. Knead into dough, adding water if not moist enough; the dough should be firmer than strudel dough
5. Knead until it separates from your hands, fold together, cover with cloth, and let rest half an hour
6. Roll out to little-finger thickness
7. Work 280 grams chilled clarified butter until smooth, shape into a long sheet with your palm, and wrap in dough folding the dough over from all four sides as for butter dough
8. Cover and let rest 15 minutes; in summer place between two plates on ice
9. Roll out as thin as possible, fold together again, let rest, and repeat this process three times total
10. For the fourth rolling, roll to knife-blade thickness, cut 5 or 6 round leaves with a warm knife
11. Each leaf should be slightly smaller than your little finger in diameter
12. Make two-finger-width incisions of equal depth with a warm knife, brush incision edges with egg white, and fold the two sides up and together so the leaf looks woven into tabs
13. Brush with egg white and bake to beautiful roll color
14. One hour before baking, peel well-ripened apricots and cut in half at the center, or use canned apricots
15. Place on a large plate, sprinkle generously with sugar, turn several times
16. Strain the canned apricot liquid
17. When the leaves are beautifully baked, arrange apricots on the leaf up to the tabs, sprinkle well with sugar, and return to the oven for 8-10 minutes
18. Cook the apricot liquid with sugar in a pan, stirring frequently until it becomes light syrup
19. Arrange the filled leaves on a cloth-covered plate, largest leaf first and smallest last (so place the most beautiful apricots on the smallest leaf)
20. Coat the fruit with syrup using a knife, sprinkle the whole pastry with sugar, and serve warm
