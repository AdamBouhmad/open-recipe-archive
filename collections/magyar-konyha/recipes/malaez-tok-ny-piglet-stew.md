## Ingredients

- 3-4 week old piglet
- 3-4 deka fresh bacon per kilo of meat, cut into small cubes
- 1-2 teaspoons black ground pepper per kilo of meat
- salt
- meat broth
- green parsley, finely chopped, as much as can be held with five fingers, per kilo of meat
- 1/2 small clove garlic per kilo of meat, finely chopped or ground in wooden mortar
- good paprika
- marjoram

## Directions

1. Cut the 3-4 week old piglet into small pieces and keep in a bowl filled with water
2. For each kilo of meat, render 3-4 deka fresh bacon cut into small cubes until the cracklings turn bread-yellow
3. Add 1-2 teaspoons of black ground pepper per kilo of meat to the fresh hot fat with the cracklings still in it
4. Immediately take the piglet meat from the water and place it in the fat so the pepper does not burn
5. Cover the casserole and let the meat braise
6. After half an hour, if the meat has absorbed the rendered liquid, pour in fat-free meat broth so the meat is always submerged about a quarter of its height
7. At this time, salt the meat and simultaneously add for each kilo of meat as much finely chopped green parsley as can be held with five fingers, and 1/2 small clove of garlic per kilo, finely chopped or ground in a wooden mortar
8. Continue braising until the meat is completely tender, occasionally pouring in broth
9. When the meat is done braising, taste the broth and add more salt if needed
10. Before serving, for 3-4 kilos of meat add 1/2 teaspoon good paprika and a little marjoram, then let the whole thing braise briefly without a cover until it reduces by half, and serve in a warm bowl and on heated plates
