## Ingredients

- 28 dekagrams sugar rubbed lightly with lemon
- 16 dekagrams fine cornstarch
- 14 egg yolks
- 10 egg whites
- 35 dekagrams sugar (for the ice)
- 1 cup strong coffee of the best quality
- 4 deciliters whipped cream

## Directions

1. Pound the sugar rubbed with lemon and sift it; similarly pound and sift the cornstarch
2. In a bowl, beat the sugar with 14 egg yolks for a good quarter hour until it becomes beautifully white and begins to rise
3. Beat 10 egg whites into stiff snow
4. First add the cornstarch flour and stir it in gently, then the egg snow, which must be mixed in very quickly
5. Grease a rimmed ring mold well with butter without any decorations; dust it lightly with fine flour
6. Similarly grease a baking sheet with butter
7. Fill the ring mold with the sponge batter so that it remains empty to a width of two fingers; spread the remaining batter on the baking sheet smoothly and evenly to the thickness of a knife blade with a wide knife
8. Bake both in a warm oven or stove at gentle heat until golden brown like a bread roll
9. Quickly remove the sheet and with a sharp knife cut out two round layers that fit exactly on the ring mold; place them back in the oven for a few moments, then turn out the ring, remove the layers from the sheet with a knife, place both on paper and keep near the warm oven
10. Pound 35 dekagrams sugar finely and sift it; brew a cup-full of strong, best quality coffee and set this, as well as 4 deciliters whipped cream, on ice
11. Place the sponge ring exactly on one of the layers; from the sugar and coffee, which you stir in only spoonful by spoonful, prepare a light ice, but it must not be liquid; cover the entire ring and the second layer, which you keep as a lid, with this
12. Once the ice has dried (which goes very easily on an economical stove, as you place the cake on a sieve near it), whip stiff snow from the chilled cream with a whisk, placing the whisk bowl on ice
13. Once this is done, put in 28 dekagrams sugar and as much ice-chilled coffee until the snow takes on a yellowish color and the coffee flavor becomes predominant
14. Mix the sugar and coffee well with the snow using the whisk, and fill the entire opening of the ring with this snow, which must already have dried; quickly place the lid on top, quickly cover the strip on the lid with the remaining ice, cut neat paper for the cake base, place the cake on it on a table covered with a tablecloth and quickly serve it to the table
15. The coffee snow must be filled into the ring only at the moment when the cake is served to the table, so that the ring does not soften
16. It can also be served without a lid, in which case the snow is filled into the ring and piled high at the moment it is served to the table
17. If the cake is prepared in a season when fresh strawberries can be obtained, the sponge ring can also be covered with strawberry pulp; pass the red strawberries through a fine sieve, place sifted sugar in a bowl, and mix the strawberry spoonful by spoonful with this; if it had a very pale color, one or two drops of cochineal can be mixed in; use care with the addition of strawberry syrup, so that it is not too thin
18. Cover the ring as with the coffee and let it dry; mix the strained strawberry in the bowl with finely pounded sugar for the snow, keep the cream snow on ice, and stir in the strawberry only slowly with the whisk and whip it up slightly again
19. Only so much may be added that the strawberry is still recognizable, so that the mixture is not too thin; give it color with cochineal
20. This cake can also be served without a lid, except that the snow is then filled and piled up at the moment of serving on the tablecloth
