## Ingredients

- emperor pears
- water
- sugar
- lemon juice
- large autumn peaches
- serviceberries
- oranges
- vanilla liqueur
- isinglass

## Directions

1. Cook larger pears such as emperor pears, peel them, cut them in half, and stew them with water, sugar, and lemon juice
2. Boil 10 decagrams of sugar with a coffee cup of water until it threads, peel 5 large autumn peaches, cut them in half, and add them, being careful they do not overcook but remain whole
3. Cook 4 deciliters of serviceberries in water with enough sugar for sweetness
4. Peel the zest from 2 oranges and cut it into long strips, boil this in water until soft
5. In a pan, boil 9 decagrams of sugar with a little water until it threads, drain the orange zest, and with a fork dip it carefully in the thickened sugar so it does not break, bring to a boil for a moment and cool
6. When serving, make the first wreath from the pears, the second from the peaches, pile the serviceberries in the middle, place the orange zest on the edge of the pears, and if well set, stand it up straight with a fork
7. Finally, mix a coffee cup of good vanilla liqueur with half a cup of water, a little sugar, and 1.5 decagrams dissolved isinglass, strain this through cheesecloth, freeze it well, and when set, turn it out and cut small half-moons from it, arranging these on the compote in neat order
