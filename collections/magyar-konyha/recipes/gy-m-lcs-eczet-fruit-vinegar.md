## Ingredients

- rotted apples or rotted pears
- fruit scraps and peels
- good wine vinegar
- 2 deciliters of good wine vinegar per 6 liters
- 2 deciliters of brandy per 6 liters

## Directions

1. Collect rotted apples, rotted pears, and all manner of fruit scraps and peels in a water-tight barrel kept open in the cellar from autumn through winter
2. In spring or when you have time, press the collected fruit and place the juice in a clean vessel
3. Leave it to stand for 2-4 days so that the thick impure part gathers on top and can be removed
4. Place that juice in the vinegar barrel and put it in a moderately warm room, and after a few weeks you will obtain good strong vinegar
5. Alternatively: throw rotted fruit into a barrel standing in a warm location, shake it together and pour 16 liters of water into a 90-liter barrel
6. Bring the fruit temperature to 18-20° R if possible
7. During fermentation, stir the mixture well
8. After 3-4 weeks, depending on how warm or cool the barrel is, draw off the liquid and press out the remainder
9. Place the obtained cloudy liquid in another barrel, and for every 6 liters add 6 deciliters of good wine vinegar and 2 deciliters of brandy, and place the barrel in a warm location
10. Later, separate the fruit vinegar from its sediment
