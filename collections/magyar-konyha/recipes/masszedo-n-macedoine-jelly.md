## Ingredients

- seven lemon juices
- eight deciliters water
- juice of two lemons
- thirty-five dekagrams crushed sugar
- four and one-half dekagrams dissolved isinglass
- green, thinly sliced almonds
- beautiful, large preserved fruit in sugar

## Directions

1. Mix seven lemon juices with eight deciliters of water
2. In this mixture, dissolve thirty-five dekagrams of crushed sugar that has been rubbed on two lemons
3. Mix in four and one-half dekagrams of dissolved isinglass
4. Strain through a taut cloth until clear
5. Pour into a mold to a height of two centimeters and place on ice until it freezes
6. Arrange a dense wreath of green, thinly sliced almonds and beautiful, large sugar-preserved fruit around the edge of the mold
7. Arrange two rows of leaves, one row with tips pointing down and the other pointing up, with the fruit in the middle connecting with the leaves
8. Arrange a star in the same manner in the center of the mold
9. Pour a coffee-cup-sized amount of jelly on top
10. When this sets enough to hold the fruit in place, pour another layer of jelly so that jelly covers one-third of it
11. Layer in sliced quince or quince paste as previously described
12. For each new layer of fruit, initially pour only a small amount of jelly so the fruit does not rise, first securing it with little jelly, then pouring more on top
13. The third layer of jelly should be filled with whole or half sugar-cooked apricots, pour similarly small amounts of jelly on these until they freeze
14. Finally add the remaining jelly
15. Keep on ice until use and unmold as with the others
