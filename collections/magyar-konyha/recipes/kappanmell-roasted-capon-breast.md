## Ingredients

- breast of a fine capon or young turkey
- one and a half bread rolls, skin
- two decilitres of milk
- nine dekagrams of butter
- three whole eggs
- salt
- mace
- carrot slices
- red onion

## Directions

1. Cut out the breast of a fine capon or young turkey. Grate the skin of one and a half bread rolls and cut into thin slices, moisten with two decilitres of milk, add nine dekagrams of butter, place over charcoal and stir continuously, beating in three whole eggs, and stew into a thick paste that should separate from the spoon, then let it cool.
2. Pound the cut capon breast with a little salt and mace finely together in a large mortar and press through a sieve.
3. Braise the rest of the capon in a pot with butter, a few slices of carrot and red onion until light golden, pour in a little broth from time to time, and when the meat is tender, remove the bones and cut into thin slices.
4. Put the forcemeat into a bowl and beat with five egg yolks until frothy, finally mix in the stiff snow of five egg whites and the cut meat, fill into a pot greased with butter, steam for one hour, then turn out and pour over it the broth that remained from braising the capon and has been cleared of all fat.
