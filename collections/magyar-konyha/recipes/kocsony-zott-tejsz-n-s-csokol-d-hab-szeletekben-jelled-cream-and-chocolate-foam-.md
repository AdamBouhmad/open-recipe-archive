## Ingredients

- Two tin molds
- Fresh almond oil
- Quince paste, cut into small cubes
- Green almonds
- 7 deciliters good heavy cream
- 25 dekagrams vanilla sugar
- 1.25 dekagrams dissolved and cooled isinglass
- One large tablet of Milan chocolate
- Quince paste slices preserved in sugar (alternative)

## Directions

1. Line two tin molds very thinly with fresh almond oil
2. Thickly scatter small cubes of quince paste and green almonds in one of the molds
3. Set seven deciliters of good heavy cream on crushed ice in a beating pan and whip it with a whisk into stiff foam
4. Mix in the vanilla sugar and the dissolved and cooled isinglass
5. Soften one large tablet of Milan chocolate in a pan by placing it in a warm but not hot oven
6. Quickly beat the softened chocolate with a new wooden spoon
7. Mix it with half of the cream foam
8. Pour it into the mold not scattered with fruit, shake well, and cover tightly with the lid
9. Wrap in parchment paper and set on crushed ice
10. Mix the pieces of quince paste and almond remaining with the second half of the foam along with any juice that settles from them
11. Pour this into the scattered mold, cover similarly, wrap, and bury in ice for a few hours
12. At serving time, remove the molds, wipe clean, dip quickly into hot water, wipe again, unmold onto a very clean board, and cut into slices as thick as a knife blade with a sharp knife
13. Cover the plate with a cloth and pile the slices high in alternating arrangement
