## Ingredients

- so-called quince-peach fruits, well ripened
- sugar
- water

## Directions

1. Select so-called quince-peaches that are well ripened
2. Peel and halve them
3. If sufficiently softened, push through a sieve; if very firm, cook for a few minutes first
4. For half a kilogram of peaches, use equal amount of sugar with two deciliters water in a preserving pan, cook until it strings and throws large bubbles while boiling
5. Add the peaches, whose juice has been well drained, stirring them in
6. Cook well over charcoal with continuous stirring, then cool
7. Form into balls the size of half an egg
8. Place on white paper thickly sprinkled with sugar, sprinkle with fine sugar, and let stand in a dry, warm place for several days
9. Press flat, sprinkle with sugar again, and when dried, roll thinner each day, repeatedly sprinkling with sugar, until the pieces are wafer-sized
10. When fine and dry, layer in rolls, sprinkle with sugar, and store in a dry place
