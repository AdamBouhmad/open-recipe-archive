## Ingredients

- carrot pulp left over from sugar making
- water
- vinegar mother or vinegar-soaked bread
- 25 liters of liquid
- 7-10 decagrams of tartar
- 4 decagrams of cream of tartar

## Directions

1. Pour water over the carrot pulp left from sugar making so that it covers it completely
2. After 2-3 weeks, press the mixture that has turned to vinegar fermentation and place the juice in a location of 10-15° R temperature
3. To promote sour fermentation, add a small amount of vinegar mother or vinegar-soaked bread, tartar and cream of tartar to 25 liters of liquid
4. Remove the rising foam
5. Once the liquid no longer foams, draw off the vinegar several more times, and if it is not yet strong enough, add a little tartar and place it in open air
6. When the vinegar has the proper sourness, clarify it with egg white, draw it off, and seal it airtight
