## Ingredients

- twenty-eight dekagrams sugar
- sponge cake dough
- butter
- ten dekagrams sugar
- vanilla
- eight deciliters sour cream
- whole fruits for garnish

## Directions

1. Prepare sponge cake dough using twenty-eight dekagrams of sugar as described in the previous recipe
2. Butter a small cake leaf with the ring together
3. Fill it with a few spoonfuls of dough, at most three knife-widths thick, this leaf should be the topmost and smallest
4. Spread the remaining dough onto two cake leaves in the same manner
5. After buttering these, make a ring around the cake leaves from common dough to prevent the sponge cake dough from flowing, apply it to the same thickness as the first, sprinkle with sugar, and bake at gentle heat until light roll-colored
6. When the dough is baked, cut out six leaves, each one a fish-bone width smaller in diameter, so that the one you filled in the cake leaf is the smallest
7. Remove the rings from these
8. Prepare a butter dough from ten dekagrams of sugar and a little vanilla, fill it into a Spanish piping bag, and pipe beautiful Spanish fritters onto the edge of the smallest leaf so that it forms a nice wreath
9. From the remaining butter dough, pipe small fritters the size of Italian hazelnuts onto white paper, place these on a sheet in a completely cooled oven so they remain beautifully white but harden and become crispy
10. Put eight deciliters of sour cream into the whipping bowl, place it on ice, and whip with the whisk until the entire mass becomes stiff foam, then add enough finely broken sugar and vanilla so that it is sweet enough, and quickly stir this in with the whisk
11. Remove the cake slightly earlier from the oven so it cools, fill every leaf with the sour cream foam to a good knife-width thickness so that the foam comes between all six layer-cake leaves
12. Arrange beautiful whole fruits on the top leaf of the cake, and place the small fritters around the edges of the other six leaves in a circle
13. Once the cake is completely assembled and filled, place it on a cake leaf, cover the plate with a tablecloth, and quickly serve it on the table
