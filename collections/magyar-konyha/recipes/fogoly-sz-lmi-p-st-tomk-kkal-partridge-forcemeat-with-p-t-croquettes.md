## Ingredients

- 3 partridges, cleaned
- salt
- thin bacon slices
- fried bread
- good brown broth
- half cup red wine
- partridge breasts
- half goose liver
- fine breadcrumbs
- fat
- butter
- green parsley, chopped
- red onion, chopped
- gravy
- small rolls from baker
- egg, beaten
- breadcrumbs
- fat

## Directions

1. Salt three cleaned partridges and wrap them in thin bacon slices, then roast them.
2. Cut the breasts from two partridges; grind the remaining parts together with the third partridge and fried bread in a mortar until it becomes a paste.
3. Put this in a pot, add a ladle of good brown broth and half a cup of red wine, and simmer until cooked.
4. Cut the reserved partridge breasts into pieces and prepare a separate mixture.
5. Cut the breast meat into cubes; similarly prepare half a goose liver rolled in fine breadcrumbs and fried in fat.
6. Melt a little butter, add finely chopped green parsley and a little red onion; add the cut meat and liver with three to four spoonfuls of buttered gravy and reduce until thick.
7. Have the baker make small rolls the size of a coin; scrape the crust, cut a cover the size of a groschen from the top, and remove the inside, moistening with sour cream but not making it too soft.
8. Dip these rolls in beaten, salted egg and breadcrumbs, then fry golden in fat.
9. Arrange the rolls on a sieve to drain.
10. Push the forcemeat through a fine sieve onto the bottom of a dish, arrange the fried rolls in a circle, fill with the prepared mixture, and top with the lids.
11. Work as quickly as possible so the dish does not cool.
