## Ingredients

- rabbit
- parsley stems
- carrot
- red onion
- water
- wine vinegar
- Hungarian pepper
- thyme
- bay leaf
- lemon zest
- smoked bacon
- flour for thickening

## Directions

1. Clean the rabbit thoroughly and marinate it. Prepare the marinade by chopping parsley stems, carrot, and red onion into slices. In a pot, combine equal parts water and wine vinegar and bring to a boil. Add Hungarian pepper, thyme, bay leaf, and lemon zest in small amounts. Allow to boil well, then pour the hot marinade over the cleaned rabbit and cover well. Once cooled, bring to a boil again and pour off the liquid twice more, then set aside. It is best if the rabbit stays in the marinade for three days. When ready to prepare the rabbit, remove it from the marinade and lard it well. Place it in a roasting pan, add a small amount of onion, parsley, and carrot from the marinade and roast it. While roasting, baste frequently with the marinade liquid. (Do not roast with bay leaf and lemon zest as this will make it taste bitter.) When roasted, pass the vegetables through a sieve and place them in a pot to brown. Crush half a lump of sugar and add it, stirring constantly until it becomes a dark brown. When very dark, pour half a liter of good sour cream over it. Add a slice of lemon zest, a bay leaf, and capers. Pour this sauce over the rabbit and bring to a boil once more.
