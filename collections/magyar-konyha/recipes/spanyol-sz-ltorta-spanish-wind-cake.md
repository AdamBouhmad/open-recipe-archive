## Ingredients

- 50 dekagramm fine sugar
- 2 grams vanilla
- 11 egg whites
- fine broken sugar

## Directions

1. Very finely pound 50 dekagramm of fine sugar with 2 grams of vanilla and sift it.
2. Beat 11 egg whites in a copper bowl to very stiff peaks, whisking the sugar in rapidly and gently.
3. The foam must be so stiff that if the bowl is inverted, it remains motionless, achieved by constantly lifting the whisk high during beating, and whipping faster and harder as it becomes stiffer.
4. Draw four round layers on two sheets of white paper, each slightly smaller in diameter than a pinky finger.
5. Spread the mixture smoothly onto the traced layers to the thickness of a finger using a knife.
6. Using a pastry bag, pipe small pastries around the edges of the layers.
7. For the fourth, smallest, and top layer, pipe four fine ribbons crossing each other, pipe a large tall button in the center, surround it with pea-sized Spanish pastries, and fill the areas not crossed by ribbons with similar nut-sized pastries placed side by side, then pipe similar pastries along the edge.
8. Dust all layers with finely broken sugar through organdine, and bake in a cooled oven; after one to two hours, the paper can be peeled off with a thin knife.
9. Fill the layers with preserved fruit, placing the largest at the bottom, then smaller ones, and the smallest on top decorated with whole preserved fruits.
10. Note that the oven should be neither very hot nor very cold; the best test is if a bare arm can be held in it.
11. This method applies to Spanish wind paste as well.
