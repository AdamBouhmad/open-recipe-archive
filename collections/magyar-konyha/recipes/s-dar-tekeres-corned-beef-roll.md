## Ingredients

- six eggs
- cooked lean corned beef
- fresh sour cream
- butter
- veal roast or chicken breast
- cartifole (artichoke?)
- mushrooms
- crayfish tails
- butter sauce
- egg yolk

## Directions

1. Make thin round omelet slices suitable for rolling from six eggs.
2. Chop cooked lean corned beef finely and mix with enough fresh sour cream to make it spreadable.
3. Spread this mixture knife-thick on the egg slices and roll them up.
4. Trim both ends to make them uniform.
5. Line a round or oval mold buttered with the egg rolls standing upright side by side.
6. Prepare a butter sauce with diced veal roast or chicken breast (removing brown parts), boiled cartifole cut into small pieces (reserving a few smaller rosettes), three small cleaned mushrooms, and 15-20 crayfish tails cut into cubes, braised until the sauce reduces completely.
7. Beat two egg yolks into the sauce and set aside.
8. Fill the mold with the forcemeat mixture and steam.
9. When turning out onto the plate, remove the egg slices from the top and arrange the forcemeat attractively with the cartifole rosettes and crayfish tails.
10. These rolls can also be made with forcemeat containing marrow.
