## Ingredients

- 7 deciliters milk
- 14 dekagrams almonds, soaked and ground
- 14 dekagrams rice
- sugar to taste
- 1 or 2 sheet cakes
- butter
- beaten eggs
- fine breadcrumbs
- fat for frying
- sugar
- preserved cherries

## Directions

1. Bring 7 deciliters of good milk to a boil, place 14 dekagrams of soaked and finely ground almonds in a soup pot, pour hot milk over, and let stand for 15 minutes.
2. Press through a clean linen cloth.
3. Prepare 14 dekagrams rice by cleaning it, rubbing well with a cloth, and grinding very finely in a mortar.
4. Bring the almond milk to a boil, add the rice with enough sugar to taste, and cook to a thick paste with continuous stirring.
5. Butter 1 or 2 sheet cakes and spread the hot paste with a wide knife dipped in milk to the thickness of a small finger.
6. Cool, then dust a board with flour and sugar, warm the cake slightly on the bottom, and unmold the rice.
7. Cut out circles with a medium donut cutter, then cut the center of each ring with a smaller cutter to form a crown.
8. Brush with beaten eggs, coat with fine breadcrumbs, and fry in fat to a bread-brown color.
9. When removed from fat, sprinkle thickly with sugar.
10. Arrange on a plate covered with a kitchen cloth in three rows in a cross pattern, placing a preserved cherry in the opening of each crown and serve hot.
