## Ingredients

- pike or sterlet
- salt
- white vinegar
- water
- red onion
- fresh parsley
- carrot
- lemon zest
- pepper
- cloves
- bay leaf
- thyme
- sturgeon bladder
- aspic
- hard-boiled eggs

## Directions

1. Let the cleaned pike or sterlet lie in salt for an hour.
2. Pour one part white vinegar and three parts water into a pot, add red onion, fresh parsley, carrot, lemon zest, a few peppercorns, cloves, bay leaf and a little thyme.
3. Add the fish and two rings of beaten sturgeon bladder and cook until well done but not falling apart.
4. Then remove and cook the sauce until the sturgeon bladder dissolves.
5. Stretch a cloth over four legs of an upturned chair, pour the sauce over it and strain slowly so it is clear.
6. Place the fish on a plate, pour the sauce over it and place in a cold place so it becomes jelly.
7. Line the back of the fish entirely with aspic.
8. Hard boil two eggs, remove the yolks and press through a sieve; cut fine slices from the egg white and cut half-moons with a small cutter and decorate the entire fish as desired.
