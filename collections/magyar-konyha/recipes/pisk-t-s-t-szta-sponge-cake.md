## Ingredients

- fourteen dekagrams of finely pounded sugar
- four egg yolks
- four egg whites
- ten dekagrams of fine dry flour
- butter
- preserved cherries or rowan berries
- quince paste
- pistachios
- raisins
- sour cream
- egg yolks
- sugar
- vanilla
- preserved apricots
- old white wine
- French reduced stock

## Directions

1. Beat fourteen dekagrams of finely pounded sugar well with four egg yolks; when it begins to foam, add the stiff snow of four egg whites and ten dekagrams of fine dry flour
2. Pour the dough into a buttered pan and bake it slowly
3. When fully baked, carefully trim the brown crust with a thin knife and cut the whole into slices like rusks
4. Prepare the cake one day before using, as it cuts better then
5. Use a mold with an opening in the middle to form a wide, tall crown; butter it well
6. Line the bottom with whole preserved cherries or rowan berries in sugar syrup, then with quince paste cut into small triangles and softened pistachios arranged neatly
7. Build strong walls from the cut sponge cake in the entire mold, cut the remaining sponge cake and a little more quince paste into small cubes, mix with small raisins, fill the mold with this and press it down lightly
8. Half an hour before serving, beat two decilitres of sour cream with five egg yolks, pounded sugar, and vanilla smoothly, pour it on top and set it in a steam bath
9. Before serving, beat two spoons of preserved apricots with the same amount of pounded sugar and four egg yolks well, pour in five tablespoons of white old wine, put it on a low fire and whip with a whisk until it becomes a thick mass
10. Turn the cake out onto a plate, fill the opening with French reduced stock, and arrange the remaining sauce around the edge of the cake in a crown shape with a spoon
