## Ingredients

- half-ripe walnuts
- sugar (equal weight to walnuts)
- cloves
- cinnamon

## Directions

1. Take half-ripe walnuts, which can be cut through very easily.
2. Prick each walnut several times with a feather knife.
3. Place the walnuts immediately in fresh well water, and change the fresh water daily several times, cleaning them of the coarse flavor until the water remains clear.
4. Boil the walnuts in the water for as long as you boil soft-boiled eggs.
5. Take as much sugar as the walnuts.
6. Place the walnuts immediately into the sugar with several split cloves and broken cinnamon, and boil until they are completely softened.
7. Remove the scum from it, then remove the walnuts; boil the syrup a bit more, during which place the walnuts in the glass, and when the syrup has cooled slightly, pour it over them, and place shavings crosswise on top so the syrup is always above the fruit.
8. Finally, tie it with paper, pierced through with a needle.
