## Ingredients

- 180 grams fine sugar
- 8 egg yolks
- 400 ml French champagne
- 1.5 grams dissolved isinglass
- several peeled quinces
- 2 medium quinces
- 140 grams sugar
- 1 glass water
- 2 oranges
- candied cherries
- greengage plums
- lemon

## Directions

1. Beat 180 grams fine sugar and 8 egg yolks with 400 ml French champagne in a pan
2. Place over charcoal fire and beat until thick and risen
3. Mix in 1.5 grams dissolved isinglass
4. Pour into a ring mold and place in broken ice
5. Slice peeled quinces and cut coin-sized round pieces from them
6. Cook 2 medium quinces with 140 grams sugar and 1 glass water until soft
7. Peel 2 oranges, cut into large cubes and sprinkle with sugar
8. Strain candied cherries and greengage plums from their preserving liquid
9. Once the sugar crown is frozen, turn it out into a prepared dish and immediately place on ice
10. Fill the ring opening by layering: quince, cherry, orange, and greengage plum
11. Prepare aspic from 2 oranges and 1 lemon, about 300 ml
12. Pour 2-3 spoonfuls of aspic over the fruit and let freeze
13. Add more aspic in layers, allowing each to freeze before adding the next
14. Once frozen solid, fill the entire opening with remaining aspic
15. Keep on ice until fully frozen and attractive in appearance
