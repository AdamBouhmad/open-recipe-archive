## Ingredients

- 1 suckling pig, one month old
- salt
- garlic
- pig's tongue
- pig's offal (liver, heart, etc.)
- pig's blood
- pepper
- parsley
- onion leaves
- marjoram
- cardamom leaves
- cooked pine nuts, shelled
- pieces of sausage
- lard
- parsley sprigs for garnish
- 1 ripe peeled lemon for the mouth

## Directions

1. Kill the piglet by stabbing with a small knife under the left arm, being careful not to make too large an incision.
2. Collect the blood without adding anything to it, allowing it to coagulate.
3. Scald the pig with boiling water and ash, using the back of a knife to remove hairs from the back.
4. Remove the nails and after thorough washing, if any hairs remain, singe them over straw fire.
5. Make an opening in the belly just large enough to disembowel it.
6. Through this opening, remove the tongue (which should be detached from the mouth with a cut underneath) and the offal.
7. Wash again, then rub inside and out with salt and garlic, allowing the flavors to penetrate.
8. Leave in the same vessel for twenty-four hours.
9. Boil the tongue and offal in salted water along with the coagulated blood.
10. When cooked, chop into thin slices and sauté with salt, pepper, garlic, parsley, onion leaves, marjoram, cardamom leaves (all finely chopped), cooked shelled pine nuts, and pieces of sausage.
11. Cook until the juices have reduced.
12. Stuff the pig's belly with this filling through the opening and sew it shut.
13. Make a hole under the front legs, fold the hind legs toward the back and tie them with a cord.
14. Make a sauce of lard, salt, and garlic; rub the pig with it.
15. Place in a roasting pan and put in the oven with moderate heat.
16. When fully cooked and golden brown, remove from the oven and make a cut in the skin of the neck.
17. Place on a serving platter garnished with parsley sprigs and put a ripe peeled lemon in the pig's mouth.
18. Serve hot, though some prefer it cold.
