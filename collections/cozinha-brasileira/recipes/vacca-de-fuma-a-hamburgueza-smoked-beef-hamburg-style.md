## Ingredients

- piece of beef sirloin
- nitre
- fine salt
- brown sugar, one quarter measure
- two large white onions
- two carrots
- one garlic clove
- two bay leaves
- two cloves
- Indian peppercorns
- blessed herb

## Directions

1. Remove the bones from the beef
2. Rub the meat with nitre and then with fine salt
3. Place in a terrine with a base covered with salt, brown sugar, two large white onions, two carrots, one garlic clove, two bay leaves, two cloves, Indian peppercorns, and blessed herb, all chopped and mixed
4. Cover the meat with this mixture, placing a board and weight on top
5. Cover the terrine with its lid, sealing it to prevent air entry, and keep for seven days in a dry room
6. Turn the meat and leave for another seven days
7. After this time, hang the meat in the chimney over the smoke for four to six weeks
8. When serving, soak this meat in hot water for one hour
9. Then cook it for four to five hours in a pot filled with water, onions, carrots, and a bundle of fragrant herbs
10. Serve with cooked cabbage, sauerkraut, carrots or porridge, potatoes cooked in water and salt, and mashed with sugar and butter
