## Ingredients

- fifteen to twenty pig's ears
- pork skin
- salted water
- juniper berries
- coriander
- bay leaf
- clove
- garlic
- thyme
- marjoram
- sage
- four drams of saltpeter
- white wine
- brandy
- smoked sausage
- meat jelly

## Directions

1. Take fifteen to twenty pig's ears, singe and clean them well.
2. Split them in half, placing them in a clay vessel with some pork skin, and pour salted water over them.
3. Add juniper berries, coriander, bay leaf, clove, garlic, thyme, marjoram, sage, four drams of saltpeter.
4. Cover this terrine with a white cloth, placing a lid on top that closes it as well as possible.
5. Leave them soaking for ten to twelve days.
6. Then, draining them, place them to cook in a pan with water, a bottle of white wine and a cup of brandy.
7. Cook them thus for five hours, and after being well cooked, leave them in their broth until they become almost cold.
8. Drain them and arrange the ears in layers in a mold with some slices of smoked sausage.
9. Fill the mold, placing a lid on top that covers it perfectly, and over it a great weight to press well.
10. Leave everything to cool; in this state, remove from the mold, and serve with meat jelly.
