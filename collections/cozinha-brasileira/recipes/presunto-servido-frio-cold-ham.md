## Ingredients

- ham
- serpol
- bay leaf
- garlic
- twelve onions
- six cloves
- carrots
- parsley
- celery
- one ounce of saltpeter
- water or one bottle of white wine
- dried breadcrumbs or bread crusts
- slices of tongue or pickled cucumber preserves
- gelatin

## Directions

1. Soak the ham for twenty-four hours, tied in cloth
2. Place it in a vessel with serpol, bay leaf, garlic, twelve onions, six cloves, carrots, parsley, celery, and one ounce of saltpeter
3. Cover completely with water or one bottle of white wine
4. Test with a stick for doneness; if it enters easily, the ham is cooked
5. Remove the ham from the fire and let it cool in the same pot until lukewarm
6. Remove it at this point and lift the skin without removing the fat
7. Sprinkle dried breadcrumbs or bread crusts on top
8. Decorate with slices of tongue or pickled cucumber preserves
9. Place gelatin around it
