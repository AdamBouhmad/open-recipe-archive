## Ingredients

- one ham leg
- water
- salt
- four carrots
- four onions
- bay leaves
- serpol
- marjoram
- Indian cloves
- valerian
- blessed herb
- meat stock juice
- watercress
- escarole leaves

## Directions

1. Take the ham leg, remove the hip bone, and give it a strong blow with a large knife on the thigh bone to break it without damaging the meat, carefully removing the broken part of the bone
2. Lightly roast the surface of the skin and soak it in water to remove some salt, which requires forty-eight hours; if the ham is more than one year old, change the water many times
3. Wash it and leave it to drain, placing it in a thick cloth and tying it well
4. Five hours before serving, place it in a large vessel filled with cold water, add four carrots, four onions, some bay leaves, serpol, marjoram, Indian cloves, a little valerian, and blessed herb
5. Leave it to boil over a lively fire, skimming, cover the vessel, and let it cook slowly
6. Half an hour before serving, remove the cloth and let it drain
7. Trim the shank and decorate it; then place it to dry in a mild oven, having covered the ham two or three times with meat stock juice
8. Serve it on a platter on which some watercress branches and escarole leaves (white chicory) have been previously placed
