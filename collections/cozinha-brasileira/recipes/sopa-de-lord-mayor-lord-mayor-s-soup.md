## Ingredients

- 4 pig ears
- 4 pig feet
- 8 jars water
- parsley
- celery
- onions
- cloves
- 1/2 pound butter
- wheat flour
- herbs (parsley, chervil, chives, etc.)
- 1/2 small spoon paprika
- 1/2 jar Port or Madeira wine
- 3 dozen slices of fried beef

## Directions

1. Cook 4 pig ears and 4 pig feet slowly over gentle fire for 4 to 6 hours in 8 jars of water
2. Add a bunch of parsley, celery, and onions garnished with cloves
3. Remove from the fire, strain, and let cool
4. When cool, remove all the fat and cut the ears and feet, after removing the bones, into small pieces
5. In another pan, melt half a pound of butter and add as much wheat flour as is necessary to absorb the butter, keeping it at the heat of the fire for 10 or 12 minutes and stirring constantly
6. Gradually add the broth, letting the mixture boil always before adding new portions of broth
7. When the broth is boiling over, add the chopped ears and feet, 2 spoons of chopped herbs such as parsley, chervil, chives, etc.; half a small spoon of paprika, and half a jar of Port or Madeira wine
8. Remove this broth from the fire and pour it into a tureen, adding 3 dozen slices of fried beef
