## Ingredients

- pork blood
- vinegar
- a dozen onions, chopped
- four bottles of blood
- three pounds of caul fat, chopped
- parsley
- chives, chopped
- salt
- pepper
- nutmeg
- cloves
- bay leaves
- thyme
- marjoram
- one bottle of milk

## Directions

1. Prepare the pork blood by placing a little vinegar in the vessel beforehand and mixing well to prevent the blood from clotting
2. Cook a dozen chopped onions
3. Add four bottles of blood, three pounds of chopped caul fat, parsley, chopped chives, salt, pepper, nutmeg, cloves, some bay leaves, thyme, marjoram, and one bottle of milk
4. Mix everything well so that the fat does not become lumpy
5. Introduce this mixture into well-washed intestines, tying one end
6. When full, tie the other end, leaving a small empty space so they do not burst
7. Cook and keep in hot water, being careful that it does not boil
8. When they begin to harden and when pricked with a pin release fat, they are cooked
9. Remove from heat, let drain, and dry with a cloth
10. If desired, give them a nice gloss by coating them with a piece of lard
11. When you wish to eat them, prick them so they do not burst and place them on the grill over a gentle fire
12. They may also be cut into rounds and fried in fat
