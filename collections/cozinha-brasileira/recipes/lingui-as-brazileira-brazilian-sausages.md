## Ingredients

- meat from the leg and shoulder, cut into small pieces
- salt
- garlic
- India pepper
- Comarí peppers
- parsley
- chives
- marjoram
- bay leaf
- marjoram (segorelha)
- 1 bottle of wine or aguardente
- fine intestine casings
- orange
- corn meal (fubá)
- cold water

## Directions

1. Cut meat from the leg and shoulder into small pieces and season with salt, garlic, India pepper, Comarí peppers, parsley, chives, marjoram, bay leaf, and marjoram.
2. Leave to marinate in this mixture for twenty-four hours.
3. Prepare fine intestine casings by washing with orange and corn meal, then soaking in cold salted water for twenty-four hours.
4. When filling, drain the casings, spread them on cloth to dry, and fill them with the marinated meat pieces.
5. Pack the meat as tightly as possible, pricking the casing occasionally with a pin to release trapped air between the meat layers.
6. Once the casing is full, tie the other end.
7. Place the prepared sausages in smoke for three days.
8. After this time, store in a dry place.
9. Serve fried, grilled, or cooked with rice; they also enter into the composition of various stuffings.
