## Ingredients

- one hen, one duck or one mallard
- half a pound of chopped lard, already fried
- water
- salt
- seasonings
- four hard-boiled eggs
- wheat flour
- eggs

## Directions

1. Cut the hen, duck or mallard into small pieces and sauté in half a pound of chopped lard already fried
2. Add a little water, salt and necessary seasonings, and continue sautéing for more time
3. When cooked, let cool to remove the bones
4. Chop the meat into even finer pieces and add four hard-boiled eggs, making a kind of filling
5. On the other hand, make a dough of wheat flour and eggs, which should be stretched until very thin
6. Cut it into strips, and placing at intervals small pieces of chopped meat, cover them with the other strips of dough, pressing down the places where there is no meat, thus forming small pastries that are separated so they can retain the meat they contain
7. Boil them one after another in pure beef broth
8. Serve mixed with the broth
