## Ingredients

- Four pounds of pork meat, preferably lean loin
- Two pounds of beef loin
- Two pounds of fat, cut into pieces
- Ten ounces of salt
- Five drams of ground cayenne pepper
- Five drams of broken cayenne pepper
- Two and a half drams of whole cayenne pepper
- Twelve drams of powdered saltpeter
- Thick intestines
- String
- Salt
- Saltpeter
- Wine dregs
- Thyme
- Sage
- Bay leaf
- Wood ash

## Directions

1. Chop all meat very well and pound it
2. Add the fat cut into pieces and mix
3. Season with salt, ground cayenne pepper, broken cayenne pepper, whole cayenne pepper, and powdered saltpeter
4. Mix all and leave soaking for twenty-four hours
5. Take well-washed thick intestines and introduce the mixture, pounding it with a round bread so no empty spaces remain
6. Tie both ends and wind strong string around the entire length
7. Place the salami in a large tub with salt and a little saltpeter, putting a weight on top
8. Leave for eight days
9. Hang in smoke to dry
10. Tighten the strings well and coat with wine dregs in which thyme, sage and bay leaf have been boiled
11. Allow to dry and roll in a wooden box surrounded and covered with ash
12. Store in a dry and temperate place
