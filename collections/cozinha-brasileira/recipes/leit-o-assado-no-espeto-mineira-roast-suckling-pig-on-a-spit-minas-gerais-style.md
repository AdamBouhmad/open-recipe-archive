## Ingredients

- 1 suckling pig, cleaned and washed
- salt
- garlic
- lard
- sweet oil (if roasting over coals)

## Directions

1. Split the cleaned suckling pig in half lengthwise, with one half retaining the head.
2. Rub both halves with salt and garlic and leave to marinate for twelve hours.
3. Thread each half onto a spit, passing it between the ribs and skin; for the half with the head, the spit point should exit through the mouth.
4. Tie the leg to the spit with a cord and make a small hole in the skin through which to insert your hand.
5. Place in the oven, resting the spit point on a support with the other end against the oven wall.
6. Turn frequently and baste with a sauce made of lard, salt, and garlic until golden brown.
7. Serve each half on a platter lined with watercress leaves, topped with thin slices of peeled lemon.
8. Alternatively, the pig may be roasted over coals with careful attention to moderate the fire, basting with the sauce but using sweet oil instead of lard.
