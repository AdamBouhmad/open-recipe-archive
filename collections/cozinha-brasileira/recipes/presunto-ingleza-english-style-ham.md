## Ingredients

- ten pounds of salt
- half pound of ground Indian pepper
- four ounces of saltpeter
- water
- salt
- serpol
- bay leaf
- two ounces of ginger
- four ounces of Indian pepper in grain
- four ounces of juniper berries in grain
- marjoram
- sage
- oregano
- four pounds of molasses or muscovado sugar
- wine mixed with vinegar

## Directions

1. After cutting two hams, round them and cut the trimmings around them
2. Prick the skin with the tip of a knife so that the seasoning can penetrate them
3. Place ten pounds of salt, half pound of ground Indian pepper, and four ounces of saltpeter on a table
4. After rubbing them with this mixture, place them in a small tub, putting the rest of the salt on top, and leave them there for seven days
5. After this time, remove them, bring the brine to a boil, composed of water and the blood that came from the ham, skim well, and add serpol, bay leaf, two ounces of ginger, four ounces of Indian pepper in grain, the same amount of juniper berries in grain, marjoram, sage, oregano, and four pounds of molasses or muscovado sugar
6. Place the hams back in the tub, and when the brine is cold, pour it over them, and if it is not sufficient to cover the hams, add water and salt
7. Place a board with any weight on top to keep them in this brine for three weeks
8. After this time, remove them and place them in a press for twelve hours, and after draining, hang them in smoke
9. When they have taken on a beautiful color, rub them with wine mixed with vinegar to protect them from flies, then dry them for preservation
