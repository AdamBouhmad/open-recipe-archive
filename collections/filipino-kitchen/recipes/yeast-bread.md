## Ingredients

- 2 medium sized potatoes
- 4½ cups water
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 4 tablespoons sugar
- 4 tablespoons lard
- 2 cups boiling water
- flour
- potato yeast

## Directions

1. In the evening, boil two potatoes of medium size in four and a half cups of water.
2. Take the potatoes from the water, mash them fine, and return them to the potato water.
3. To the potato water add two teaspoons of salt and four level tablespoons of sugar.
4. When the mixture is lukewarm add the potato yeast.
5. Cover it and set it aside until morning, when it should be light and foamy.
6. In the morning put four tablespoons of sugar and four tablespoons of lard into a large mixing bowl and pour over the ingredients two cups of boiling water.
7. When this liquid has cooled to lukewarm, stir the potato yeast into it, reserving about one and one-fourth cups for a start for the next baking.
8. Stir into the liquid enough sifted flour to make a stiff sponge and beat it well.
9. Cover this sponge and set it in a warm place.
10. When it is light, which should be in about one hour, stir into it enough sifted flour to knead.
11. Knead this dough for about twenty minutes, return it to the mixing bowl and set it in a warm place to rise.
12. When it has doubled in size, which should be in about one hour, mould into loaves or rolls and put into greased pans.
13. Cover and set aside to rise.
14. When the dough has again doubled in size, put into a well regulated oven and bake.
