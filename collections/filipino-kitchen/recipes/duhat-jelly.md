## Ingredients

- duhat fruit
- water
- sugar, equal amount to juice
- lemon juice, 2 tablespoons to 4 cups of duhat juice

## Directions

1. Select the fruit when it is not perfectly ripe. Sort and wash.
2. Add just enough water to barely cover the fruit and cook until the fruit becomes soft and the juice well started.
3. Put the juice into a thin cotton bag, letting the juice drip through it into a porcelain or enamel receptacle. Let it drain for eight or twelve hours. Do not squeeze the bag or the jelly will be cloudy.
4. Measure your juice and an equal amount of sugar.
5. Place the juice only on the fire and let boil thirty minutes.
6. Add sugar and lemon juice. Stir until the sugar is thoroughly dissolved, and let boil ten minutes.
7. Test it by dropping a spoonful on a saucer and allowing it to cool quickly. As soon as it will jell, pour into glasses and set aside to cool.
8. After twenty four hours, seal with paraffin.
9. If the fruit is quite ripe, it will be necessary to boil the juice and sugar for thirty minutes. The time, from ten to thirty minutes, will depend upon the ripeness of the fruit.
