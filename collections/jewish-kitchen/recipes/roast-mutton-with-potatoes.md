## Ingredients

- one shoulder of mutton
- salt
- ginger
- pepper
- flour
- whole mace
- onion
- one cup of water
- potatoes
- currant or cranberry jelly

## Directions

1. Take a shoulder of mutton—must be young and tender—wash the meat well and dry with a clean towel.
2. Rub well with salt, ginger and a speck of pepper, and dredge well with flour.
3. Lay it in a covered roasting-pan.
4. Put a few pieces of whole mace and a few slices of onion on top.
5. Pour a cup of water into the pan.
6. Cover it up tight and set in a hot oven to roast, basting frequently.
7. Allow twenty minutes to the pound for roasting mutton; it should be well done.
8. Add more water if necessary (always add hot water so as not to stop the process of boiling).
9. Skim the gravy well and serve with currant or cranberry jelly.
10. Pare potatoes of uniform size and wash and salt them about three-quarters of an hour before dinner.
11. Lay the potatoes in pan around the roast and sprinkle them with salt and return to the oven to roast.
12. Let them brown nicely.
