## Ingredients

- rind of a lemon, cut very thin
- a small piece of whole cinnamon
- 2 cloves
- a pinch of salt
- a little dried saffron
- 1/2 lb. of loaf sugar
- whites of 2 eggs with the shells
- 1/2 pint of sherry
- 2 wine-glasses of rum
- juice of a large lemon
- 1 bullock's foot or 2 calf's feet

## Directions

1. Through a fine sieve, let it stand till the next morning, then take off all the fat and wipe the top with a clean cloth
2. The liquor will have reduced to a quart; put it in a clean saucepan with the rind of a lemon, cut very thin, not a particle of the white or it will taste bitter
3. Add a small piece of whole cinnamon, 2 cloves, a pinch of salt, a little dried saffron, 1/2 lb. of loaf sugar
4. Beat the whites of 2 eggs with the shells, stir it in well
5. Set it on to boil, keep stirring all the time; when the whites of the eggs set on the top thick move the saucepan from the fire
6. Pour in 1/2 pint of sherry and 2 wine-glasses of rum, with the juice of a large lemon well strained
7. Let it stand at the side of the fire well covered over, so that the strength of the wine and spirit does not evaporate
8. Rinse the jelly bag well in boiling water before you strain the jelly
9. Have it as near as you can to the fire, it soon cools; rinse the mould with cold water, fill it
10. Set it in a very cold place, if for company you must stand it in ice an hour before you are ready to turn it out
