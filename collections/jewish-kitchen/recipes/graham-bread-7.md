## Ingredients

- one cake of compressed yeast
- four tablespoons of light brown sugar or molasses
- one cup of lukewarm water
- one cup of milk (scalded and cooled to lukewarm)
- two tablespoons of melted butter
- four cups of Graham flour
- one cup of white flour (sifted)
- one teaspoon of salt

## Directions

1. Dissolve one cake of compressed yeast and four tablespoons of light brown sugar or molasses in one cup of lukewarm water and one cup of milk which has been scalded and cooled to lukewarm
2. Add two tablespoons of melted butter, then four cups of Graham flour and one cup of white flour (sifted), adding flour gradually, and one teaspoon of salt
3. Knead thoroughly, being sure to keep dough soft
4. Cover and set aside in a warm place to rise for about two hours
5. When double in bulk, turn out on kneading board, mold into loaves, and place in well-greased pans
6. Cover and set to rise again—about one hour or until light
7. Bake one hour, in a slower oven than for white bread
