## Ingredients

- chicken
- one onion
- a bay leaf
- six cloves
- lemon juice
- white celery
- yolks of two hard-boiled eggs
- one teaspoon of salt
- one teaspoon of the finest olive oil
- one teaspoon of prepared mustard
- white pepper
- two teaspoons of white sugar
- white of one egg
- one-half cup of vinegar
- lettuce leaves
- hard-boiled eggs
- olives
- capers

## Directions

1. Place the chicken in boiling water, add one onion, a bay leaf and six cloves.
2. Bring to a boil and let it boil rapidly for five minutes.
3. Reduce the heat to below the boiling point, and let it cook until tender.
4. Let chicken cool in the broth.
5. When the chicken is cold, cut into half inch cubes, removing all the fat and skin.
6. To each pint allow one tablespoon of lemon juice, sprinkle the latter over the prepared chicken and place on ice.
7. When ready to serve, mix the chicken with two-thirds as much white celery, cut into corresponding pieces.
8. Rub the yolks of two hard-boiled eggs as fine as possible, add one teaspoon of salt.
9. Add, a drop at a time, one teaspoon of the finest olive oil.
10. Stir constantly, add one teaspoon of prepared mustard and white pepper, and two teaspoons of white sugar.
11. Whip the white of one egg to a froth and add to the dressing.
12. Add about one-half cup of vinegar last, a spoonful at a time.
13. Put the salad into the dressing carefully, using two silver forks.
14. Line the salad bowl with lettuce leaves, and garnish the top with the whites of hard-boiled eggs chopped up, or cut into half-moons.
15. Garnish this salad with the chopped yolks and whites of hard-boiled eggs, being careful to have the whites and yolks separate.
16. A few olives and capers will add to the decoration.
