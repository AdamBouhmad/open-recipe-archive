## Ingredients

- one small head of cabbage
- salt
- cold water
- salt
- boiling water
- butter
- pepper
- salt

## Directions

1. Cut a small head of cabbage into four parts, cutting down through the stock.
2. Soak for half an hour in a pan of cold water to which has been added one tablespoon of salt; this is to draw out any insects that may be hidden in the leaves.
3. Take from the water and cut into slices.
4. Have a large stewpan half full of boiling water; put in the cabbage, pushing it under the water with a spoon.
5. Add one tablespoon of salt and cook from twenty-five to forty-five minutes, depending upon the age of the cabbage.
6. Turn into a colander and drain for about two minutes.
7. Put in a chopping bowl, and mince.
8. Season with butter, pepper, and more salt if it requires it.
9. Allow one tablespoon of butter to a generous pint of the cooked vegetable.
