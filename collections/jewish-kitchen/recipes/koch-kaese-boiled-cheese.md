## Ingredients

- one quart of fine cottage cheese
- salt
- one-fourth as much caraway seed
- yolks of two eggs
- one even tablespoon of flour
- one-half cup of cold milk
- a lump of butter
- a clean cloth
- beer

## Directions

1. Press one quart of fine cottage cheese through a coarse sieve or colander and set it away in a cool place for a week, stirring it once or twice during that time.
2. When it has become quite strong, stir it smooth with a wooden or silver spoon.
3. Add a saltspoon of salt and one-fourth as much of caraway seed.
4. Add yolks of two eggs and an even tablespoon of flour which has been previously dissolved in about one-half cup of cold milk.
5. Stir the flour and milk until it is a smooth paste, adding a lump of butter, about the size of an egg.
6. Add all to the cheese.
7. Put the cheese on to boil until quite thick, stirring occasionally.
8. Boil altogether about one-half hour, stirring constantly the last ten minutes.
9. The cheese must look smooth as velvet.
10. Pour it into a dish which has been previously rinsed in cold water.
11. Set it away in a cool place.
12. To keep it any length of time, cover it with a clean cloth which has been dipped in and wrung out of beer.
