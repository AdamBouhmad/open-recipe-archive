## Ingredients

- one and one-half cups of flour
- one-quarter teaspoon of salt
- one egg
- one-third cup of warm water
- melted butter
- one quart of sour apples, peeled and cut fine
- one-quarter pound of almonds blanched and chopped
- one-half cup of raisins and currants
- one cup of sugar
- one teaspoon of cinnamon
- bread crumbs

## Directions

1. Into a large mixing bowl place one and one-half cups of flour and one-quarter teaspoon of salt
2. Beat one egg lightly and add it to one-third cup of warm water and combine the two mixtures
3. Mix the dough quickly with a knife; then knead it, place on board, stretching it up and down to make it elastic, until it leaves the board clean
4. Toss it on a well-floured board, cover with a hot bowl and keep in a warm place
5. Lay the dough in the centre of a well-floured tablecloth on the table; roll out a little, brush well with some melted butter
6. With hands under dough, palms down, pull and stretch the dough gently, until it is as large as the table and thin as paper, and do not tear the dough
7. Spread one quart of sour apples, peeled and cut fine, one-quarter pound of almonds blanched and chopped, one-half cup of raisins and currants, one cup of sugar and one teaspoon of cinnamon, evenly over three-quarters of the dough
8. Drop over them a few tablespoons of melted butter
9. Trim edges
10. Roll the dough over apples on one side, then hold cloth high with both hands and the strudel will roll itself over and over into one big roll
11. Trim edges again
12. Twist the roll to fit the greased pan
13. Bake in a hot oven until brown and crisp and brush with melted butter
14. If juicy small fruits or berries are used, sprinkle bread crumbs over the stretched dough to absorb the juices
15. Serve slightly warm
