## Ingredients

- cucumbers
- dill
- whole peppers
- salt
- vinegar

## Directions

1. Examine the cucumbers carefully, discard all that are soft at the ends, and allow them to lay in water overnight
2. In the morning drain, and dry them with a clean towel
3. Put them in a wooden pail or jar, along with the dill, putting first a layer of dill at the bottom then a layer of cucumbers, a few whole peppers, then a layer of dill again, and so on until all are used
4. Lay a clean, white cloth on top, then a plate and a stone to give it weight, so that the pickles will be kept under the brine
5. To a peck of cucumbers use about a cup of salt
6. Dissolve the salt in enough cold water to cover them
7. You may add one or two tablespoons of vinegar to the brine
8. If the cucumbers are small, and if they are kept in a warm place, they will be ready for the table in five or six days
9. Always examine the pickles weekly
10. Take off the cloth, wash it, and remove all the scum that adheres to the pail, and lay a clean cloth over the pickles again
