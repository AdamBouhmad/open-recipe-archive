## Ingredients

- a pound of sugar for each pound of fruit
- quinces
- water

## Directions

1. Weigh a pound of sugar for each pound of fruit
2. Wipe each quince carefully with a coarse linen towel
3. Peel, quarter and core the quinces
4. Put peels and cores in the preserving kettle with just water enough to cover them, and let them simmer with the kettle covered for two hours
5. Strain the liquor through a fine sieve and return it to the kettle
6. Cut the quartered quinces in small pieces and put as many of them in the kettle as the liquor will cover
7. Let them boil gently, with the kettle uncovered, until so tender they may be easily pierced with a broom splint
8. Take them out with a skimmer and lay on flat dishes to cool
9. Repeat this process until all the fruit is properly cooked
10. Put the sugar in the liquor and let it boil gently to a thick syrup
11. Put in as many of the cooked quinces as the syrup will cover and let them cook in the syrup for twenty minutes
12. Skim them out and lay on flat dishes to cool
13. Repeat this process until all the quinces are cooked in the syrup
14. When they are cool put the quinces in glass jars, filling each one half full
15. Let the syrup boil until very thick, stirring it frequently and skimming it clear
16. Pour it through a fine strainer, while very hot, over the fruit
17. As soon as a jar is full, fasten on the cover
