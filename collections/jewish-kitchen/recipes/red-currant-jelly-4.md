## Ingredients

- quite ripe currants
- white sugar

## Directions

1. Strip carefully from the stems some quite ripe currants.
2. Put them into a preserving pan.
3. Stir them gently over a clear fire until the juice flows freely from them.
4. Squeeze the currants and strain the juice through a folded muslin or jelly bag.
5. Pour it into a preserving pan, adding, as it boils, white sugar, in the proportion of one pound of sugar to one pint of juice.
6. Jellies should be continually skimmed till the scum ceases to rise, so that they may be clear and fine.
7. Jams and jellies should be poured into pots when in a boiling state.
