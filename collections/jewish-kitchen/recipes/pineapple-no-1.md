## Ingredients

- Large juicy pineapple
- Fine granulated sugar, allowing one pound to each very large pineapple

## Directions

1. Have your scales at hand, also a sharp-pointed knife and an apple-corer, a slaw-cutter and a large, deep porcelain dish to receive the sliced pineapple
2. Pare the pineapple carefully and dig out all the eyes as you go along
3. Lay the pared pineapple on a porcelain platter
4. Stick an apple-corer right through the centre of the apple, first at one end and then at the other; if it acts stubbornly put a towel around the handle of the corer and twist it, the whole core will come out at once
5. Screw the slaw-cutter to the desired thickness for slicing the pineapple
6. Slice into receiving dish
7. Weigh one pound of fine granulated sugar and sprinkle it all over the apple, and so on until all are pared and sliced
8. Cover the dish until next day
9. Strain all the juice off the apples and boil in a porcelain or bell metal kettle, skimming it well
10. Throw in the sliced pineapples and boil about five minutes
11. Can immediately, filling the cans to overflowing and sealing immediately
12. As the cans grow cold screw tighter and examine daily for three or four days, and screw tighter if possible
