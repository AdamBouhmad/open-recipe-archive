## Ingredients

- two large chickens
- salt
- white, crisp celery
- fine salt
- yolks of six hard-boiled eggs
- white pepper
- four tablespoons of chicken-fat
- one tablespoon of best prepared mustard
- two teaspoons mustard seed
- two teaspoons celery seed
- two tablespoons of white sugar
- one cup of white wine vinegar
- lettuce leaves
- whites of six hard-boiled eggs
- one pint of mayonnaise
- olives

## Directions

1. Boil two large chickens in enough water to cover them, add salt while boiling.
2. When very tender remove from the fire and allow the chickens to cool in the liquor in which they were boiled.
3. When cold skim off every particle of fat, and reserve it to use instead of oil.
4. Cut the chickens up into small bits (do not chop), cut white, crisp celery in half inch pieces, and sprinkle with fine salt, allowing half as much celery as you have chicken.
5. Mix the chicken and celery, using two silver forks to do this.
6. Rub the yolks of six hard-boiled eggs as fine as possible, add one-half teaspoon of salt, white pepper.
7. Add four tablespoons of chicken-fat that has been skimmed off the broth, adding one at a time, stirring constantly.
8. Add one tablespoon of best prepared mustard, two teaspoons mustard seed and celery seed, and two tablespoons of white sugar.
9. Add gradually, stirring constantly, one cup of white wine vinegar.
10. Pour this dressing over the chicken and celery and toss lightly with the silver forks.
11. Line a large salad bowl with lettuce leaves, pour in the salad and garnish the top with the chopped whites of six hard-boiled eggs.
12. Pour a pint of mayonnaise over the salad just before serving.
13. Serve the salad in individual salad dishes, lining each dish with a lettuce leaf, garnish the salad with an olive stuck up in the center of each portion.
