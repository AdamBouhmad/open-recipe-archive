## Ingredients

- one cake compressed yeast
- two cups of lukewarm water
- one cup of milk (scalded and cooled)
- two and one-half cups of rye flour
- one and one-half cups of sifted white flour
- one tablespoon of melted butter or oil
- two and one-half cups of rye flour
- one tablespoon of salt
- white of egg and water
- caraway seeds (optional)

## Directions

1. Dissolve one cake compressed yeast in two cups of lukewarm water and one cup of milk which has been scalded and cooled
2. Add two and one-half cups of rye flour or enough to make a sponge
3. Beat well
4. Cover and set aside in a warm place, free from draught, to rise about two hours
5. When light add one and one-half cups of sifted white flour, one tablespoon of melted butter or oil, two and one-half cups of rye flour to make a soft dough and last one tablespoon of salt
6. Turn on a board and knead or pound it five minutes
7. Place in greased bowl
8. Cover and let rise until double in bulk—about two hours
9. Turn on board and shape into loaves
10. Place in floured shallow pans
11. Cover and let rise again until light—about one hour
12. Brush with white of egg and water, to glaze
13. With sharp knife cut lightly three strokes diagonally across top, and place in oven
14. Bake in slower oven than for white bread
