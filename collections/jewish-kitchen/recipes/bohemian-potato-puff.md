## Ingredients

- potatoes
- salt
- flour
- drippings or goose-fat
- onions

## Directions

1. Pare, wash and boil potatoes until soft enough to mash well.
2. Drain off nearly all the water, leaving just a little; add one teaspoon of salt and return to the stove.
3. Sift one-half cup of flour into the potatoes after returning to the fire and keep covered closely for about five minutes.
4. Remove from the stove and mash them as hard as you can, so as not to have any lumps.
5. Put about two tablespoons of drippings or goose-fat in a spider, chop up some onions very fine and heat them until they become a light brown.
6. Take a tablespoon and dip it in the hot fat, and then cut a spoonful of the potato dough with the same spoon and put it in the spider, and so on until you have used all.
7. Be careful to dip your spoon in the hot fat every time you cut a puff.
8. Let them brown slightly.
