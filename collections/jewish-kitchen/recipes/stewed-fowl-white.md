## Ingredients

- 1 fowl
- 1 onion
- a tooth of garlic
- salt
- pepper
- grated nutmeg
- a little dried saffron
- a few slices of smoked beef or worsht
- 1/2 pint water
- egg sauce
- forced meats balls
- chopped green parsley
- a few white button mushrooms

## Directions

1. Cut a fowl in pieces, the same as if you were carving the pieces for table from a roast fowl.
2. Chop in a stewpan an onion small, with a tooth of garlic, salt and pepper, with grated nutmeg, a little dried saffron, and a few slices of smoked beef or worsht.
3. Lay in the fowl with 1/2 pint water, let it simmer till done.
4. Have ready an egg sauce according to recipe, pour it over the stewed fowl, baste it backwards and forwards with a spoon so that it covers the fowl.
5. Cook a few small forced meats balls with, and after you have put in the sauce, be sure that it does not boil, or you would curdle the sauce.
6. Dish it up nicely, sprinkle over some chopped green parsley.
7. A few white button mushrooms are very nice with this stew.
