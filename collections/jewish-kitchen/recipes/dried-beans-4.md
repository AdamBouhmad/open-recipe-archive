## Ingredients

- beans
- cold water
- soda
- salt
- boiling water

## Directions

1. Look the beans over carefully to remove all dirt and pebbles, then wash clean.
2. Soak them overnight in plenty of cold water.
3. In the morning pour off the water and put them in a stew-pan with cold water enough to cover them generously.
4. Let them come to the boiling point in this water, then drain.
5. If the beans are old and hard, for each quart put a piece of soda about the size of a large bean in the water in which they are soaked overnight, also in the first water in which they are boiled.
6. The scalded and drained beans should be put back in the stew-pan and covered generously with boiling water.
7. Add one tablespoon of salt for one quart of beans.
8. Cook slowly, with the cover partially off the stew-pan until they have reached the required degree of tenderness.
