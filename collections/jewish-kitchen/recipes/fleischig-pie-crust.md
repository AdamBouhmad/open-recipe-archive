## Ingredients

- one-half cup of shortening (drippings and goose, duck or chicken fat, or rendered beef fat)
- one and one-half cups of flour (pastry flour preferred)
- a pinch of salt
- six or eight tablespoons of cold water (ice-water in summer)
- soft drippings for spreading

## Directions

1. With two knives cut the fat into the sifted flour until the shortening is in pieces as small as peas
2. Pour in six or eight tablespoons of cold water; in summer use ice-water; work with the knife until well mixed (never use the hand)
3. Flour a board or marble slab, roll the dough out thin
4. Sprinkle with a little flour and put dabs of soft drippings here and there
5. Fold the dough over and roll out thin again and spread with fat and sprinkle with flour
6. Repeat this and then roll out not too thin and line a pie-plate with this dough
7. For fruit fillings, brush over top of the dough with white of egg slightly beaten, or sprinkle with one tablespoon of bread crumbs to prevent the dough from becoming soggy
8. Put in the filling, brush over the edge of pastry with cold water
9. Lay the second round of paste loosely over the filling; press the edges together lightly, and trim, if needed
10. Cut several slits in the top crust or prick it with a fork before putting it in place
11. Bake from thirty-five to forty-five minutes until crust is a nice brown
