## Ingredients

- sugar
- peaches
- water

## Directions

1. Weigh one pound of sugar for each pound of fruit
2. Brush each peach with a stiff whiskbroom
3. Peel the peaches very thin with a sharp silver knife
4. As fast as the peaches are peeled lay them on porcelain platters
5. Put the peelings in the preserving kettle with enough water to keep from sticking
6. Stand the kettle over rather a quick fire and let the peelings boil with the kettle covered until very soft
7. Then drain them through a colander and pour the juice strained back into the kettle
8. Add sugar to this and let it simmer gently until it is a thick syrup
9. During the time the syrup is cooking it must be frequently stirred and skimmed
10. As soon as the syrup is thick enough, drop in the peaches, twelve at a time if for quart jars, and six at a time if for pint jars
11. Let the peaches cook gently until each one may easily be pierced with a broom splint
12. Then quickly skim them out and lay them on a platter to cool
13. Repeat this process until all the peaches are done
14. Let the syrup cook until thick as molasses
15. Skim it thoroughly
16. When cool put the peaches, one at a time, in the jars with a spoon
17. When the syrup is sufficiently thick, pour it through a strainer over the peaches in the jars until they are full, then seal down quickly and stand them upside down for several hours before putting them in the store-room
