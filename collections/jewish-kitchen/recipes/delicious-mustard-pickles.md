## Ingredients

- about two dozen large, yellow pickles
- silver knife
- salt
- about two quarts of small pickling onions
- horseradish
- whole peppers
- bay leaves
- mustard seed
- allspice
- whole cloves
- best white wine vinegar
- double cheese-cloth

## Directions

1. Pare pickles with a silver knife to prevent them from turning dark
2. Cut lengthwise
3. Remove all the seeds and soft inner pulp with a silver spoon
4. Cut into strips about as long as your finger
5. Sprinkle salt over them and continue until all are cut up
6. Put in a wooden pail or large china bowl overnight
7. Take about two quarts of small pickling onions, scald them with boiling water
8. Remove the skins with a silver knife
9. Salt the same as you did the pickles
10. In the morning take a clean dish towel and dry each piece
11. Lay them in a stone jar in the following manner: First a layer of pickles then a layer of onions
12. Add some horseradish, sliced, between the layers
13. Add a few whole peppers, a very few bay leaves, and sprinkle mustard seed, allspice and whole cloves between each layer
14. Remove the soft little heads of the cloves to prevent the pickles from turning dark
15. Cover all with the best white wine vinegar
16. Put a double cheese-cloth filled with mustard seed on top
17. In two weeks pour off the vinegar carefully and boil, and let it get perfectly cold before pouring over the pickles again
18. You may pack them in small glass jars if you prefer
