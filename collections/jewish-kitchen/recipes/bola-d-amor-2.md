## Ingredients

- two pounds of white sugar
- yolks of twenty eggs
- one pound of sweet almonds
- orange flower-water
- citron

## Directions

1. Clarify two pounds of white sugar
2. To ascertain when it is of a proper consistency, drop a spoonful in cold water, form it into a ball, and try if it sounds when struck against a glass
3. Take the yolks of twenty eggs, mix them up gently and pass them through a sieve
4. Have ready a funnel, the hole of which must be about the size of vermicelli
5. Hold the funnel over the sugar, while it is boiling over a charcoal fire
6. Pour the eggs through, stirring the sugar all the time, and taking care to hold the funnel at such a distance from the sugar, as to admit of the egg dropping into it
7. When the egg has been a few minutes in the sugar, it will be hard enough to take out with a silver fork, and must then be placed on a drainer
8. Continue adding egg to the boiling sugar till enough is obtained
9. Prepare one pound of sweet almonds, finely pounded and boiled in sugar, clarified with orange flower-water only
10. Place in a dish a layer of this paste, over which spread a layer of citron cut in thin slices, and then a thick layer of the egg prepared as above
11. Continue working thus in alternate layers till high enough to look handsome
12. Pile in the form of a cone, and the egg should form the last layer
13. Place in a gentle oven till it becomes a little set, and the last layer slightly crisp
14. Serve in the dish in which it is baked, and ornament with myrtle and gold and silver leaf
