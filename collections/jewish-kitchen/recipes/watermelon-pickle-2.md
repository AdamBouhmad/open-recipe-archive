## Ingredients

- watermelon rind
- one cup of salt
- one gallon of hot water
- one level tablespoon of alum
- one teaspoon each of cloves, allspice, cinnamon and ginger
- one cup of sugar
- one cup of vinegar
- lemon

## Directions

1. Remove the green rind of watermelon and the inside pink portion that is left on after eating it
2. Cut it into two-inch pieces and pour over it a weak brine made in proportion of one cup of salt to a gallon of hot water
3. Let this stand overnight then drain and add clear water and one level tablespoon of alum
4. Boil in this water until the rind has a clear appearance
5. Drain and pour ice water over the rind and allow it to stand a short time
6. In a bag put one teaspoon each of cloves, allspice, cinnamon and ginger and place this in the preserve kettle with the vinegar and sugar
7. Allow one cup of sugar and one cup of vinegar (dilute this with water if too strong) to every pound of rind
8. Thin slices of lemon will give it a pleasant flavor — allow one lemon to about four pounds of rind
9. Bring this syrup to the boiling point and skim
10. Add the melon and cook until tender
11. It is done when it becomes perfectly transparent and can be easily pierced with a broom straw
12. A peach kernel in the cooking syrup will improve the flavor
13. As soon as rinds are cooked they should be put into the containers and covered with the syrup
