## Ingredients

- Cabbage leaves
- White crisp cabbage
- Salt
- Tart apples
- Malaga grapes

## Directions

1. Line the bottom and sides of a clean barrel or keg with cabbage leaves.
2. Cut into fine shreds one or two dozen large heads of white, crisp cabbage.
3. Put in a layer of cabbage, say about four inches deep, and press down firmly and sprinkle with about four tablespoons of salt.
4. Put one or two tart apples, cut up fine, between each layer, or some Malaga grapes.
5. When four layers have been put in, pound with a wooden beetle until the cabbage is quite compact.
6. Add more cabbage, and so on until all has been salted, always pounding down each layer.
7. Cover with cabbage leaves, then a clean cloth, a well-fitting board, and a heavy stone, to act as weight on top of all.
8. Set away in a cool cellar to ferment.
9. In two weeks examine, remove the scum, if any; wash the cloth, board and stone, wash also the sides of the keg or jar, and place all back again.
10. This must be done weekly.
