## Ingredients

- one and one-half cups of flour
- a pinch of salt
- one cup of lukewarm milk
- three-fourths cake of compressed yeast
- warm water
- sugar
- one-half cup of butter
- one cup of sugar
- two eggs
- five cups of sifted flour
- grated peel of a lemon
- one egg
- one-half teaspoon of marmalade, jam or jelly
- rendered butter or vegetable oil
- pulverized sugar

## Directions

1. Take one and one-half cups of flour, a pinch of salt sifted into a deep bowl, one cup of lukewarm milk and three-fourths cake of compressed yeast which has been dissolved in a little warm water and sugar
2. Stir into a dough
3. cover with a towel and set away in a warm place to rise
4. When well risen, take one-half cup of butter, one cup of sugar, a little salt and rub to a cream
5. Add two eggs well beaten, stir all well and add the risen dough, one teaspoon of salt and work in gradually five cups of sifted flour and the grated peel of a lemon
6. Stir the dough till it blisters and leaves the dish perfectly clean at the sides
7. Let the dough rise slowly for about two hours
8. Take a large baking-board, flour well and roll out the dough on it as thin as a double thickness of pasteboard
9. When it is all rolled out, cut with a round cutter the size of a tumbler
10. When all the dough has been cut out, beat up an egg
11. Spread the beaten egg on the edge of each cake (spread only a few at a time for they would get too dry if all were done at once)
12. Then put one-half teaspoon of marmalade, jam or jelly on the cake
13. Put another cake on top of one already spread, having cut it with a cutter a little bit smaller than the one used in the first place
14. This makes them stick better and prevents the preserves coming out while cooking
15. Set all away on a floured board or pan about two inches apart
16. Spread the top of each cake with melted butter and let them rise from one to two hours
17. When ready to fry, heat at least two pounds of rendered butter or any good vegetable oil in a deep iron kettle
18. Try the butter with a small piece of dough
19. If it rises immediately, put in the doughnuts
20. In putting them in, place the side that is up on the board down in the hot butter
21. Do not crowd them in the kettle as they require room to rise and spread
22. Cover them with a lid
23. In a few seconds uncover
24. If they are light brown, turn them over on the other side but do not cover them again
25. When done they will have a white stripe around the centre
26. Take them up with a perforated skimmer, lay on a large platter, sprinkle with pulverized sugar
27. If the butter gets too hot take from the fire a minute
