## Ingredients

- two cups of flour
- pinch of salt
- lukewarm water
- two quarts of sour cherries
- stale bread
- peel of half a lemon
- one cup of sugar
- ground cinnamon
- four ounces of pounded sweet almonds
- melted goose-oil or butter

## Directions

1. Make a dough of two cups of flour, a pinch of salt and a little lukewarm water; do not make it too stiff, but smooth
2. Slap the dough back and forth repeatedly for about fifteen minutes
3. Put the dough in a warm, covered bowl and set it in a warm place for half an hour
4. In the meantime stem and pit two quarts of sour cherries
5. Grate into them some stale bread (about a plateful); also the peel of half a lemon, and mix
6. Add one cup of sugar, some ground cinnamon and about four ounces of pounded sweet almonds, mix all thoroughly
7. Roll out the dough as thin as possible, lay aside the rolling-pin and pull, or rather stretch the dough as thin as tissue paper
8. Walk all around the table, for when well stretched it will cover more than the size of an ordinary table
9. Pull off all of the thick edge, for it must be very thin to be good (save the pieces for another strudel)
10. Pour a little melted goose-oil or butter over this
11. Sprinkle the bread, sugar, almonds, cherries, etc., over it
12. Roll the strudel together into a long roll
13. Have ready a long baking-pan well greased with either butter or goose-fat
14. Fold the strudel into the shape of a pretzel
15. Butter or grease top also and bake a light brown
16. Baste often while baking
17. Eat warm
