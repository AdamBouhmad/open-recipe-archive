## Ingredients

- yellow pear-shaped quinces
- sugar
- quince water
- stick cinnamon
- cloves
- brandy

## Directions

1. Select large yellow, pear-shaped quinces, and peel and quarter them
2. Take out the cores and throw into cold water, until all are pared
3. Then boil until tender, so they can easily be pierced
4. Take them out with a perforated skimmer and weigh
5. Take three-quarters of a pound of sugar to a pound of quinces, and boil in a little over half the quince water
6. Add stick cinnamon and cloves (removing the soft heads)
7. Boil until quite a thick syrup
8. Pack the quinces in jars, add a pint of good brandy to the syrup and pour boiling hot over the quinces and seal immediately
