## Ingredients

- one medium-size, very hard head of red cabbage
- red onions
- salt
- cold water

## Directions

1. Select a medium-size, very hard head of red cabbage
2. Remove the outer leaves and cut the stalk off close to the head
3. Then cut the cabbage in quarters and take out the heart close to the leaves
4. With a very sharp, thin-bladed knife cut the cabbage in shreds as fine as possible
5. After the cabbage is all finely cut let cold water run over it through a colander
6. Put the cabbage in a big kitchen bowl or a stone-crock in layers about two inches thick
7. Over each layer place two or three thin slices of red onions, and sprinkle about four generous tablespoons of salt
8. Repeat this process till all the sliced cabbage is in the jar or bowl
9. Let the last layer be one of salt
10. Pour a pint of cold water over this
11. Cover it with a plate that fits closely and lay a weight of some sort on the plate and stand the bowl in a cool place overnight
12. In the morning pour the cabbage, brine and all, in a large colander to drain
13. Let the cold water from the tap run over it for about five minutes
14. Then return the cabbage to the receptacle in which it was salted
15. A stone-crock is really the best, as the cabbage will keep
