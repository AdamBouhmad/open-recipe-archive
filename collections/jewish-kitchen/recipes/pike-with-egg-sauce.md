## Ingredients

- butter about the size of an egg
- one onion
- celery root
- parsley root
- lemon slices
- water
- salt
- a dozen whole peppers
- ground white pepper
- two eggs
- one dozen almonds
- one tablespoon of cold water
- curly parsley

## Directions

1. Clean the fish thoroughly, and wash it in hot water, wipe dry and salt inside and out
2. Heat the salt so it will penetrate through the meat of the fish in less time
3. Take a kettle, lay in it a piece of butter about the size of an egg
4. Cut up an onion, some celery root, parsley root and a few slices of lemon, lay the fish in, either whole or cut up in slices
5. Boil in enough water to just cover the fish, and add more salt if required
6. Add a dozen whole peppers, black or white
7. Season with ground white pepper
8. Let the fish boil quickly
9. In the meantime beat up the yolks of two eggs, and pound a dozen almonds to a paste
10. Add to the beaten yolks together with a tablespoon of cold water
11. When done remove the fish to a large platter
12. To ascertain whether the fish has cooked long enough, take hold of the fins; if they come out readily your fish has cooked enough
13. Strain the sauce through a sieve, taking out the slices of lemon and with them garnish the top of the fish
14. Add the strained sauce to the beaten eggs, stirring constantly
15. Return the sauce to the kettle, and stir until it boils
16. Remove quickly and pour it over the fish
17. When it is cold garnish with curly parsley
