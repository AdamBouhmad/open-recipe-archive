## Ingredients

- fresh tongue
- cold water
- salt
- one tablespoon of drippings
- goose fat
- onion
- one tablespoon of flour
- pint of tongue liquor
- lemon
- two dozen raisins
- pounded almonds
- stick of cinnamon
- cloves
- four tablespoons of brown sugar
- one-half teaspoon of ground cinnamon
- one tablespoon of molasses
- two tablespoons of vinegar

## Directions

1. Lay the fresh tongue in cold water for a couple of hours and then put it on to boil in enough water to barely cover it, adding salt.
2. Boil until tender, closely covered, from three to four hours steadily.
3. To ascertain when tender run a fork through the thickest part.
4. Pare off the thick skin which covers the tongue and cut into even slices.
5. Sprinkle a little fine salt over each piece.
6. Put one tablespoon of drippings in a kettle or spider.
7. Cut up an onion in it, add a tablespoon of flour and stir, adding gradually about a pint of the liquor in which the tongue was boiled.
8. Cut up a lemon in slices, remove the seeds, and add two dozen raisins, a few pounded almonds, a stick of cinnamon and a few cloves.
9. Sweeten with four tablespoons of brown sugar in which you have put one-half teaspoon of ground cinnamon, one tablespoon of molasses and two tablespoons of vinegar.
10. Let this boil, lay in the slices of tongue and boil up for a few minutes.
