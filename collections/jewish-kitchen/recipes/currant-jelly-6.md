## Ingredients

- half ripe currants
- granulated sugar

## Directions

1. Pick over half ripe currants, leaving stems on.
2. Wash and place in preserving kettle.
3. Pound vigorously with wooden masher until there is juice enough to boil.
4. Boil slowly until fruit turns white and liquid drops slowly from the spoon.
5. Stir to prevent scorching.
6. Remove from fire and dip mixture into the jelly bags, under which large bowls have been placed to catch the drip.
7. Drip overnight.
8. Next morning measure the juice.
9. For every pint of juice allow a pint of granulated sugar, which is put in a flat pan.
10. Put juice in kettle and allow to come to boiling point.
11. Place sugar in oven and heat.
12. When juice boils add sugar and stir until dissolved.
13. When this boils remove from fire and skim. Do this three times.
14. Test liquid with syrup gauge to see if it registers twenty-five degrees, or let it drip from spoon when half cooled to see if it jells.
15. Strain into sterilized jelly glasses.
16. Place glasses on a board in a sunny exposure until it hardens.
17. Cover with melted paraffin one-fourth inch thick.
