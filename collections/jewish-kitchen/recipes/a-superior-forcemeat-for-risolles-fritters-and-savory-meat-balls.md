## Ingredients

- half a pound of the fat of smoked beef
- a pound of lean veal, free from skin, vein, or sinew
- chopped mushrooms
- a little minced parsley
- salt
- pepper
- grated lemon peel
- the crumb of two French rolls soaked in good gravy
- three beaten eggs
- mushroom powder
- a spoonful of ketchup
- a bay leaf
- an onion
- lemon juice

## Directions

1. Scrape half a pound of the fat of smoked beef, and a pound of lean veal, free from skin, vein, or sinew
2. Pound it finely in a mortar with chopped mushrooms, a little minced parsley, salt and pepper, and grated lemon peel
3. Have ready the crumb of two French rolls soaked in good gravy, press out the moisture, and add the crumb to the meat with three beaten eggs
4. If the forcemeat is required to be very highly flavored, the gravy in which the rolls are soaked should be seasoned with mushroom powder
5. Add a spoonful of ketchup, a bay leaf, an onion, pepper, salt, and lemon juice to the panada
6. Add this panada to the pounded meat and eggs
7. Form the mixture into any form required, and either fry or warm in gravy, according to the dish for which it is intended
