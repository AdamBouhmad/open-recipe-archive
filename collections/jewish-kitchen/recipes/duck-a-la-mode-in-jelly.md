## Ingredients

- duckling of about five pounds
- calf's foot
- eight to ten small onions
- young carrots
- bunch of parsley
- water
- salt
- bay leaf
- dry white wine
- brandy
- pepper

## Directions

1. Cook the foot slowly in one quart of water, one teaspoon of salt and a small bay leaf
2. Put aside when the liquor has been reduced to one-half
3. Fry the duck and when well browned wipe off the grease
4. Put in another pan
5. Add the calf's foot with its broth, one glass of dry white wine, a tablespoon of brandy, the carrots, parsley and the onions—the latter slightly browned in drippings
6. Add pepper and salt to taste and cook slowly under a covered lid for one hour
7. Cool off for about an hour
8. Take off the grease
9. Bone and skin the duckling and cut the meat into small pieces
10. Arrange nicely with the vegetables in individual earthenware dishes
11. Cover with the stock and put on the ice to harden
