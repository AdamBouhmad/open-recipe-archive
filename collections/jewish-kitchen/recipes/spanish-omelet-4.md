## Ingredients

- two nice large ripe tomatoes
- one large or two medium-sized white Texas onions
- two sprigs of parsley
- one large green-bell pepper
- olive oil or butter
- salt
- red pepper
- six eggs
- two tablespoons of water

## Directions

1. In a chopping bowl place two nice large ripe tomatoes, first peeling them; one large or two medium-sized white Texas onions, two sprigs of parsley, and one large green-bell pepper, first removing most of its seeds.
2. Chop these ingredients well together quite fine, turn them into a saucepan and let them cook over rather a brisk heat until quite soft.
3. Put no water in this mixture.
4. Add a tablespoon of olive oil or of butter before it begins to cook and season well with salt and red pepper.
5. Make the omelet the same as the plain one, but use water instead of milk in mixing it, and only use two tablespoons of water for the six eggs required.
6. After the eggs are sufficiently beaten, mixed, and in the pan over the fire, and when the edges begin to stiffen, cover the surface of the omelet to within an inch of the edge with the cooked vegetables.
7. Fold the omelet quickly and turn it on a hot platter.
8. Pour around it all the vegetables left in the pan and serve.
