## Ingredients

- a dozen Seville oranges
- salt
- water
- 3 lbs of sugar
- 3 pints of water

## Directions

1. Take a dozen oranges of equal sizes
2. Lay them in salt and water for 6 or 7 days, changing the water each day
3. At the end of the time dry them well
4. Carve each one in different patterns with a small knife
5. Put in a preserving-pan 3 lbs of sugar and set it on the fire with 3 pints of water and let it boil
6. Put in the oranges and let them simmer gently for an hour
7. Take out the oranges and lay them in a sieve to drain
8. Repeat this process for 3 days
9. On the 4th add another pound of sugar to the syrup
10. Put in the oranges and let them boil fast for half an hour
11. The syrup will then be very thick
12. Put the oranges in a glass jar and pour over the syrup
13. Let them get thoroughly cold and fasten it down tight
