## Ingredients

- a peck of green tomatoes
- eight large white onions
- six green-bell peppers
- a pint of salt
- a pint of cold water
- a quart of cider vinegar
- a half pint of tarragon vinegar
- a pound of granulated sugar
- a half pound of yellow mustard seeds
- four bay leaves
- an ounce of stick cinnamon
- six whole cloves

## Directions

1. Wash thoroughly a peck of green tomatoes, eight large white onions and six green-bell peppers
2. Remove the seeds from the peppers
3. Slice all the vegetables very thin
4. Put them in a stone jar
5. Sprinkle a pint of salt over them, add a pint of cold water
6. Cover them with a napkin and let stand overnight
7. In the morning put as much of the pickle as it will hold in a colander
8. Let cold water run over
9. Drain the vegetables a moment, then turn them from the colander into a large preserving kettle
10. Repeat the process till all are in the kettle
11. Then add a quart of cider vinegar, a half pint of tarragon vinegar, a pound of granulated sugar, a half pound of yellow mustard seeds, four bay leaves, an ounce of stick cinnamon (broken in short lengths), six whole cloves
12. Stand the kettle over a slow fire and let the whole simmer for an hour with the cover of the kettle drawn back two inches
13. Stir the mixture frequently
14. At the end of the hour put the pickle in a stone crock or in glass jars
