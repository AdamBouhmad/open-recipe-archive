## Ingredients

- one five-pound haddock
- four large onions
- one sprig of parsley
- two eggs
- pepper
- cinnamon
- salt
- one tablespoon of sugar
- cracker dust
- one sliced onion
- one sprig of parsley
- one small sliced carrot
- pepper
- salt
- cold water
- two eggs

## Directions

1. Cut a five-pound haddock into four-inch slices
2. Cut a big hole into each slice, preserving the backbone and skin
3. Put this meat, cut from the fish, into a wooden tray
4. Add to it four large onions and a sprig of parsley
5. Chop until very fine, then add two eggs, a dash of pepper and cinnamon, a pinch of salt, and a tablespoon of sugar
6. To this add enough cracker dust to stiffen it
7. Put this filling into the holes cut in the fish
8. Take a saucepan, put in one sliced onion, a sprig of parsley, a small sliced carrot, a dash of pepper, and a pinch of salt
9. Put the fish into the saucepan, cover with cold water, and let it boil slowly for one hour
10. At the end of the hour take out the fish, and put on a platter
11. Preserve the water or gravy in which the fish was boiled for the sauce
12. Beat the yolks of two eggs thoroughly
13. Into the beaten yolks slowly pour the gravy in which the fish was boiled, stirring constantly
14. Stand this on the back of the stove to boil for five minutes, stirring constantly so as to prevent burning
