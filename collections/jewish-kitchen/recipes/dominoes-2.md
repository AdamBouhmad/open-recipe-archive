## Ingredients

- sponge cake batter
- currant or cranberry jelly
- ordinary white icing
- dark chocolate icing
- buttered paper

## Directions

1. Make a sponge cake batter, and bake in long tins, not too large
2. The batter should not exceed the depth of one-fourth of an inch
3. Spread it evenly and bake it in a quick oven (line the tins with buttered paper)
4. As each cake is taken from the oven, turn it upside down on a clean board or paper
5. Spread with a thin layer of currant or cranberry jelly, and lay the other cake on top of it
6. With a hot, sharp knife cut into strips like dominoes
7. Push them with the knife about an inch apart
8. Ice them with ordinary white icing, putting a tablespoonful on each piece
9. Set the cakes in a warm place, where the frosting will dry
10. Make a horn of stiff white paper with just a small opening at the lower end
11. Put in one spoon of dark chocolate icing and close the horn at the top
12. By pressing out the icing from the small opening, draw a line of it across the centre of each cake, and then make dots like those on dominoes
13. Keep the horn supplied with the icing
