## Ingredients

- Sliced cabbage
- Red onions
- Salt
- Cold water
- Cider vinegar
- Tarragon vinegar
- Granulated sugar
- Bay leaves
- Allspice
- Peppercorns
- Stick cinnamon

## Directions

1. Place sliced cabbage in layers about two inches thick in a jar or stone-crock.
2. Over each layer place two or three thin slices of red onions, and sprinkle about four generous tablespoons of salt.
3. Repeat this process till all the sliced cabbage is in the jar or bowl.
4. Let the last layer be one of salt.
5. Pour a pint of cold water over this.
6. Cover it with a plate that fits closely and lay a weight of some sort on the plate and stand the bowl in a cool place overnight.
7. In the morning pour the cabbage, brine and all, in a large colander to drain; let the cold water from the tap run over it for about five minutes.
8. Return the cabbage to the receptacle in which it was salted.
9. In a kettle or saucepan over the fire add a pint of good cider vinegar, a gill of tarragon vinegar, a half pint of cold water, a half pound of granulated sugar, four bay leaves, a level tablespoon of allspice, a teaspoon of peppercorns and three ounces of stick cinnamon broken in half-inch pieces.
10. Let this all boil one minute and while boiling hot pour it over the cabbage in the jar.
11. Place a porcelain plate over it; then put the cover of the jar on and let this stand for twenty-four hours.
12. Pour off the vinegar, heat it again till it just boils, pour it over the cabbage, cover it and put it in a cool place.
