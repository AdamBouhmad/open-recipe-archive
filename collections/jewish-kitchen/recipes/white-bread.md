## Ingredients

- one-half cake of compressed yeast
- one teaspoon of salt
- one tablespoon of sugar
- eight cups of sifted flour
- one cup of lukewarm milk or water

## Directions

1. Set the dough at night and bake early in the morning
2. Take one-half cake of compressed yeast, set in a cup of lukewarm milk or water adding a teaspoon of salt and a tablespoon of sugar
3. Let this rise, if it does not, the yeast is not fresh or good
4. Measure eight cups of sifted flour into a deep bread bowl, add one teaspoon of salt
5. Make a depression in the centre, pour in the risen yeast and one cup of lukewarm milk or water
6. In winter be sure that the bowl, flour, milk, in fact everything has been thoroughly warmed before mixing
7. Mix the dough slowly with a wooden spoon and then knead
8. Set to rise again in a warm place free from drafts, and when it has risen to double its former bulk, take a knife, cut through the dough in several places
9. Place this dough on a baking board which has been sprinkled with flour
10. Work with the palm of the hand, always kneading towards the centre of the ball
11. When this leaves the board and the hands perfectly clean the dough may be formed into loaves or rolls
12. Place in pan greased slightly with a good oil, let rise until the imprint of the finger does not remain, and bake
13. Bake forty-five minutes in a moderate oven
