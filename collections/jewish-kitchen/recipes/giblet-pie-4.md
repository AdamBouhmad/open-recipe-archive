## Ingredients

- 1 pound chuck steak
- goose or turkey giblets
- forced meat balls
- 1 onion
- 1 tooth of garlic
- salt
- pepper
- grated nutmeg
- powdered marjoram
- lemon thyme
- 1/4 rind of a lemon
- 1 tablespoonful of flour
- juice of a lemon
- cayenne
- browning
- 2 eggs
- puff paste

## Directions

1. Cut the chuck steak in pieces 2 inches long and square
2. Have the goose or turkey giblets well washed and cut in small pieces, broil the liver first
3. Have ready a few small forced meat balls, well seasoned
4. Chop an onion very small and a tooth of garlic in your stewpan
5. Add salt, pepper, a little grated nutmeg, some powdered marjoram and lemon thyme, and chop a quarter of the rind of a lemon
6. Lay in the steak, then the giblets, set it on the fire, and when it boils draw it to the side to simmer
7. Put in the balls when it boils
8. When done rub in a basin 1 tablespoonful of flour, the juice of a lemon, a little cayenne, and browning to colour
9. Mix it all well together
10. Have 2 eggs boiled hard and cut in quarters
11. Make a good puff paste and line the dish
12. Put in the steak at the bottom, then the giblets, the balls, and the hard egg quarters
13. Cover the dish with the paste and trim the edges neatly
14. Roll out a piece of paste, cut it in two strips, divide each strip in two to make four leaves for the top
15. Make a hole in the centre and form a piece of the dough the shape of a cork, cut the top across twice, put it in the centre of the 4 leaves
16. Wash the pie over with the yolk of an egg
17. Put it in a quick oven
