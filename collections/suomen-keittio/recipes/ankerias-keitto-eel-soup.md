## Ingredients

- 3 lbs. of eel
- 4 quarts of meat or fish stock
- 1 cupful of white wine
- 6 tablespoonfuls of lemon juice
- 1 carrot
- 1 leek
- 1 sprig of parsley
- 1 parsnip
- 1 stalk of celery
- 1 cupful of peas
- 1 cupful of cauliflower
- 1 cupful of asparagus tips
- 10 peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- salt, clove and cinnamon
- ½ cupful of tomato liquor
- 2 tablespoonfuls of butter
- 2 tablespoonfuls of flour
- yolks of 3 eggs
- 1 quart of small pears

## Directions

1. Skin the eels, clean and cut them into one inch pieces.
2. Cook them in 1 quart of fish stock mixed with 3 tablespoonfuls of lemon juice, bay leaf and pepper, being careful not to break the pieces.
3. Leave the fish in the same liquor until next day.
4. Strain the bouillon and put to boil with the vegetables cut into cubes.
5. Add peas, cauliflower and asparagus a little later.
6. Cook the peeled pears in a small quantity of water with lemon juice, white wine, a piece of cinnamon and a few cloves until tender.
7. If the pears are too large, cut them lengthwise into slices.
8. Thicken the soup with butter and flour.
9. Put into it the eels, pears with their own liquor, and the strained tomato liquor.
10. Whip the yolks and pour the soup gently into it.
11. Cream may be used with the soup if wanted.
