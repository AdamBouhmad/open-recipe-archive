## Ingredients

- eel
- salt
- pepper
- lemon juice
- boiling water
- onion
- fish stock
- gelatine
- hard-boiled egg whites
- tomato
- lemon
- lettuce

## Directions

1. Clean the eel, remove the backbone.
2. Cut larger fish into pieces 3 inches long.
3. Sprinkle with salt, pepper and lemon juice and let stand 10 min.
4. Roll in a roll and tie tightly with string.
5. Put on fire in boiling water with salt, pepper and onion and let simmer gently until soft.
6. Remove to cool.
7. Prepare the jelly from the stock in the same way as for salmon aspic.
8. Cut the eel rolls with a knife crosswise into slices and arrange either in a mold to set, like salmon aspic, or pour a little jelly onto a platter, place the fish pieces on top and coat with jelly.
9. Arrange the jellied pieces in a circle on a platter, place pieces of jelly in the center and garnish around with tomato, lemon and lettuce leaves.
10. When boiled in a small amount of broth on a low fire, not much gelatine need be used, as eel contains a large amount of natural gelatine.
